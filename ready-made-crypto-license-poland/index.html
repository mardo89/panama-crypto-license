What Is a Ready Made Poland Crypto License?

A ready made Poland crypto license is a pre-established Polish company that has already been registered with the KNF as a CASP, or is in the advanced stages of the application process. The structure includes a shelf company with a clean history, a registered address, and often a local director or compliance officer in place. The key advantage is speed: you bypass the initial company formation and license application waiting periods, which can take 3-6 months from scratch.

The license is fully MiCA-compliant, meaning it authorises the company to provide crypto services across all EU member states under the passporting regime. Services covered include exchange between crypto and fiat, exchange between crypto assets, operation of trading platforms, custody and administration of crypto assets, and transfer services.

Poland's regulatory environment is business-friendly yet strong, with clear capital requirements and AML obligations. The KNF conducts thorough checks on beneficial owners and management, so a ready made license ensures that the company has already passed these hurdles. License Type and Regulator

The license is a Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) license under MiCA, regulated by the Polish Financial Supervision Authority (Komisja Nadzoru Finansowego, KNF). The KNF is the sole regulatory body for crypto services in Poland, responsible for authorisation, supervision, and enforcement. The CASP license replaces the earlier VASP registration, which was a simpler notification-based system. Under MiCA, CASPs must meet stricter capital, governance, and AML requirements.

Poland was one of the first EU member states to fully implement MiCA, making it a mature jurisdiction for crypto licensing. The KNF has published detailed guidelines on the application process, capital requirements, and ongoing obligations. The license is valid indefinitely, subject to annual compliance reviews and payment of supervision fees.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, Poland applies the standard EU capital tiers based on the type of crypto services offered. The minimum capital requirements are:

  • EUR 50,000 for simple services like exchange and wallet provision.
  • EUR 125,000 for custody services.
  • EUR 150,000 for operating a trading platform.

These amounts must be maintained as equity capital and cannot be withdrawn. For a ready made license, the capital is already contributed and verified by the KNF. When you acquire the company, the capital stays in the company. You may need to increase capital if you plan to offer additional services beyond those already authorised.

Tax Treatment

Poland offers a competitive tax regime for crypto businesses. Corporate income tax (CIT) is a flat 19% (reduced to 9% for small taxpayers with revenue under EUR 2 million). VAT is generally exempt for crypto-to-crypto exchanges, but crypto-to-fiat transactions may be subject to VAT at 23%. Personal income tax on crypto gains for individuals is 19% (flat rate).

Poland has a network of double tax treaties, which can reduce withholding taxes on dividends and interest. The country also offers a tax incentive for qualifying intellectual property (IP box) at 5%, though this is less relevant for most crypto services.

As of 2026, Poland has not introduced a specific crypto tax law; general tax principles apply. The KNF and Ministry of Finance have issued guidance clarifying the treatment of crypto transactions. Consulting24 recommends working with a local tax advisor to optimise your structure.

Allowed Activities

A Poland CASP license authorises the following activities under MiCA:

  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency.
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets.
  • Operation of a trading platform for crypto assets.
  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients.
  • Transfer services for crypto assets.
  • Provision of advice on crypto assets.
  • Portfolio management of crypto assets.

The license does not cover activities that are regulated under other EU frameworks, such as security tokens (MiFID II) or e-money (EMD2). However, stablecoins and utility tokens are within scope. The KNF may impose conditions on the license, such as restricting certain services until the company demonstrates adequate controls.

Compliance and Trust

Ongoing compliance for a Poland CASP includes:

  • AML/KYC procedures in line with the EU's 5th Anti-Money Laundering Directive (5AMLD) and MiCA.
  • Transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting.
  • Annual audit by a certified auditor.

  • Appointment of a local AML officer (can be outsourced).

Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. The KNF conducts periodic inspections. Common Mistakes

Entrepreneurs often make these errors when acquiring a ready made Poland crypto license:

  • Skipping Due Diligence: Not verifying the company's compliance history can lead to hidden liabilities. Always review past KNF communications and audit reports.
  • Ignoring Capital Adequacy: Assuming the existing capital covers your planned services. If you expand services, you may need to inject additional capital.
  • Neglecting Local Director Requirements: The KNF expects a local director or manager. Some ready made companies include this; others do not. Ensure continuity.
  • Overlooking Banking: Even with a license, some banks may still refuse service. Confirm banking arrangements before purchase.
  • Failing to Update AML Policies: The company's AML policies may be outdated. Update them to reflect your business model and current regulations.
