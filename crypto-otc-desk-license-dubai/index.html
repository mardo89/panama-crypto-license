What is a Crypto OTC Desk License in Dubai?

An OTC (Over-the-Counter) desk license in Dubai permits a company to facilitate peer-to-peer trades of virtual assets without using a public exchange. This is critical for high-net-worth individuals and institutions that need to execute large orders without impacting market prices. The license is issued by VARA, Dubai's dedicated virtual assets regulator, and falls under the broader category of a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) license.

OTC desks in Dubai can trade a wide range of cryptocurrencies, including Bitcoin, Ethereum, and stablecoins, as well as offer bespoke liquidity solutions. The regulatory framework ensures transparency, anti-money laundering (AML) compliance, and investor protection. Unlike some jurisdictions, Dubai requires a physical presence, including a local office and a compliance officer resident in the UAE. This adds credibility but also cost. For a lighter regulatory touch, Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 company setup with no dedicated crypto regulator, though it lacks the same institutional trust.

License Type & Regulator

The regulator is the Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA), part of Dubai's Securities and Commodities Authority (SCA) framework. VARA oversees all virtual asset activities in Dubai (excluding DIFC, which has its own regime). The license is a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) license specifically for OTC desk operations.

VARA categorises OTC services under the 'Broker-Dealer' activity class. Applicants must also comply with the UAE's AML/CFT regulations and obtain a commercial license from the relevant free zone (e.g., DMCC, ADGM) or mainland authority. Capital Requirement

VARA imposes a minimum capital requirement based on the type of virtual asset activity. For OTC desk (broker-dealer) services, the minimum capital is AED 250,000 (approximately USD 68,000). This must be deposited in a UAE bank account and maintained throughout the license period.

Higher capital may be required if the firm intends to hold client assets or offer custody services. The capital serves as a buffer for operational risks and is a key part of VARA's prudential requirements. Consulting24 advises on structuring capital to meet regulatory expectations. By comparison, EU MiCA CASP licenses require capital of EUR 50,000 to 150,000 depending on services, as seen in Estonia and Lithuania.

Tax Treatment

Dubai offers a highly favourable tax environment for crypto OTC desks. Key points:

  • 0% corporate income tax on profits from crypto trading activities (subject to the UAE's 9% corporate tax for profits above AED 375,000, but many free zones offer tax holidays or exemptions).
  • No VAT on crypto-to-crypto transactions (VAT may apply to fiat conversions).
  • No capital gains tax on crypto disposals.
  • No withholding tax on dividends or interest.

This tax regime makes Dubai one of the most attractive jurisdictions for crypto OTC desks globally. However, tax advice should be sought on specific structures. For a tax-neutral alternative, Panama also offers 0% tax on offshore income, but without the same regulatory framework.

Allowed Activities

Under the VARA license, an OTC desk can perform the following activities:

  • Buying and selling virtual assets as principal (proprietary trading)
  • Matching buyers and sellers of virtual assets (brokerage)
  • Providing liquidity for large trades
  • Offering price quotes for virtual assets
  • Settlement of trades in fiat or crypto
  • Custody of virtual assets (if separately licensed)

Activities not permitted include operating a public exchange, issuing virtual assets, or offering derivatives without additional licenses. The scope can be expanded via supplementary VARA approvals. For exchange operations, see our exchange license page.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a Dubai OTC desk license involves several stages:

  1. Pre-incorporation: Choose a free zone (e.g., DMCC, ADGM) or mainland jurisdiction. Reserve a company name.
  2. Company setup: Incorporate the legal entity, draft MOA, and appoint directors and shareholders.
  3. VARA initial approval: Submit a business plan, AML/CFT policies, and organisational structure to VARA for in-principle approval.
  4. Compliance documentation: Prepare detailed policies on KYC, transaction monitoring, risk management, and record-keeping.
  5. Capital deposit: Open a corporate bank account and deposit the minimum capital (AED 250,000).
  6. Final VARA review: VARA conducts a thorough due diligence, including background checks on key personnel.
  7. License issuance: Upon approval, pay the supervision fee and receive the VASP license.

Compliance & Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, OTC desks must comply with VARA's ongoing requirements:

  • Quarterly transaction reporting to VARA
  • Maintenance of minimum capital at all times
  • Annual license renewal and payment of supervision fees
  • Appointment of a compliance officer (MLRO) resident in the UAE

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Dubai OTC desk license?

The minimum capital required by VARA for an OTC desk (broker-dealer) license is AED 250,000 (approximately USD 68,000). This must be deposited in a UAE bank account and maintained throughout the license period. Higher capital may be needed if the firm also offers custody services.

How long does it take to get a Dubai OTC desk license?

The typical timeline is 3 to 6 months from application to license issuance. This depends on the completeness of your documentation, VARA's review queue, and the speed of company incorporation in the chosen free zone. Consulting24 can help expedite the process.

Can I operate a crypto OTC desk from Dubai without a license?

No. VARA requires all virtual asset service providers operating in or from Dubai (excluding DIFC) to obtain a VASP license. Operating without a license can result in fines, penalties, and legal action. It is essential to comply with the regulatory framework.

What are the tax benefits of a Dubai OTC desk license?

Dubai offers 0% corporate income tax on crypto trading profits in most free zones, no VAT on crypto-to-crypto transactions, no capital gains tax, and no withholding tax. This makes it one of the most tax-efficient jurisdictions for crypto OTC desks globally.

Do I need a physical office in Dubai for the OTC desk license?

Yes. VARA requires a substantive presence in Dubai, including a physical office space (even a flexi desk is acceptable) and a compliance officer (MLRO) resident in the UAE. This is part of the regulatory requirements for licensing.
