What Is the Crypto NFT Marketplace License Seychelles?

The crypto NFT marketplace license in Seychelles is a regulatory authorization issued by the Financial Services Authority (FSA) under the Securities Act 2007 and the Financial Services Authority Act. It allows businesses to operate a platform where users can buy, sell, and trade non-fungible tokens (NFTs) using cryptocurrencies. The license covers both primary issuance of NFTs (like digital art drops) and secondary market trading.

Unlike some jurisdictions that require separate licenses for each activity, Seychelles offers a consolidated license for digital asset services, including NFT marketplace operations. The framework is designed to be flexible but still demands strong AML/KYC procedures, capital reserves, and regular reporting. As of 2026, the FSA has updated its guidelines to align with FATF recommendations, ensuring international credibility.

Who Needs This License?

Any business that facilitates the trading of NFTs using cryptocurrencies in or from Seychelles must obtain this license. Typical applicants include:

  • NFT marketplaces (primary sales and secondary trading)
  • NFT launchpads and minting platforms
  • Fractional NFT platforms
  • NFT lending or staking services
  • White-label NFT exchange solutions

If your platform allows users to create, list, or exchange NFTs for crypto, you fall under the FSA's definition of a digital asset service provider. Even if your target market is outside Seychelles, operating from Seychelles requires this license. License Type & Regulator

Regulator: Financial Services Authority (FSA)

The FSA is the sole regulator for digital asset services in Seychelles. It issues a single license type for NFT marketplaces under the Securities Act. The license is called a "Digital Asset Service Provider License" (DASP), which covers all activities related to digital assets, including NFTs. There is no separate NFT-specific license; the DASP license is broad enough to cover NFT operations.

The FSA requires licensees to maintain a physical presence in Seychelles, including a registered office and a local director or representative. The regulator also mandates annual audits, AML compliance, and regular reporting. Consulting24 coordinates with local agents to fulfill these requirements on your behalf. Capital Requirement

The FSA does not prescribe a fixed minimum capital for NFT marketplace licenses. Instead, the capital requirement is determined on a case-by-case basis, based on the risk profile and volume of your business. In practice, most applicants are asked to maintain a minimum paid-up capital of EUR 50,000 to EUR 100,000. This capital must be deposited in a Seychelles bank account and can be used for operational expenses.

Consulting24 advises clients on the appropriate capital level for their specific business model. For smaller NFT marketplaces, EUR 50,000 is often sufficient. For platforms handling high transaction volumes, EUR 100,000 or more may be required. The capital must be verified during the application process. Tax Treatment

Seychelles offers a highly favourable tax regime for NFT marketplace operators. The key benefits include:

  • Corporate income tax: 0% for International Business Companies (IBCs) that do not conduct business with Seychelles residents.
  • No capital gains tax on profits from NFT sales.
  • No withholding tax on dividends or interest paid to non-residents.
  • No VAT on digital services provided to overseas clients.

However, if your NFT marketplace serves Seychelles residents, you may be subject to the standard corporate tax rate of 15%. Most offshore NFT platforms are structured as IBCs to enjoy the 0% rate. Allowed Activities Under the License

The DASP license permits a wide range of activities related to NFT marketplaces. These include:

  • Operating a platform for listing and trading NFTs
  • Facilitating primary issuance of NFTs (minting)
  • Providing custodial wallets for NFT storage
  • Offering NFT fractionalisation services
  • Enabling peer-to-peer NFT transfers
  • Integrating fiat-to-crypto on-ramps for NFT purchases

Activities that require additional licensing or are prohibited include: operating as a bank, issuing securities (other than NFTs that qualify as securities), and providing investment advice without a separate license. The FSA reviews each application to ensure the business model falls within the permitted scope. For more on allowed activities, see our exchange license page.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Company Incorporation: Register a Seychelles International Business Company (IBC) with a registered office and local director.
  2. Document Preparation: Prepare a business plan, AML/KYC policies, risk assessment, financial projections, and organisational structure.
  3. Capital Deposit: Open a Seychelles bank account and deposit the required capital.
  4. Submit Application: File the license application with the FSA, including all supporting documents and the application fee.
  5. Regulatory Review: The FSA reviews the application, which may take 2-4 months. They may request additional information or clarifications.
  6. License Issuance: Once approved, the license is issued. You must then comply with ongoing reporting and audit requirements.

Compliance & Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, NFT marketplaces must adhere to ongoing compliance requirements:

  • Submission of annual financial statements to the FSA.
  • Maintenance of a local registered office and director.
  • Reporting of suspicious transactions to the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU).
  • Regular updates to the business plan if activities change.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make several avoidable mistakes when applying for a Seychelles NFT marketplace license:

  • Insufficient documentation: Submitting an incomplete business plan or AML policy leads to delays. Ensure all documents are thorough and professionally prepared.
  • Underestimating capital: Even though no fixed minimum is set, having too little capital can raise red flags. Aim for at least EUR 50,000.
  • Ignoring local presence requirements: The FSA requires a local director and registered office. Using a nominee service is common but must be properly arranged.
  • Poor banking preparation: Start the bank account opening process early, as it can take months. Have all corporate documents ready.
  • Neglecting ongoing compliance: Many licensees treat the license as a one-time event. Annual audits and reporting are mandatory.

Alternatives and Comparisons

Seychelles is not the only jurisdiction for NFT marketplace licensing. Here is how it compares to other popular options:

Seychelles vs. Panama

Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 company setup cost and no specific crypto license requirement for NFT marketplaces, making it a lower-cost entry point. However, Panama lacks a dedicated regulatory framework, which may reduce credibility with partners and banks. Seychelles provides a formal license that signals compliance to users and financial institutions. For more on Panama, see our Panama page.

Seychelles vs. Lithuania

Lithuania, under MiCA, requires a minimum capital of EUR 125,000 for virtual asset service providers, significantly higher than Seychelles' typical EUR 50,000. Lithuania also imposes stricter AML and reporting obligations. Seychelles is more cost-effective and faster to set up, but Lithuania offers EU passporting rights. For a detailed comparison, see our vs Lithuania page.

Seychelles vs. Estonia

Estonia's crypto license regime has tightened, with higher capital requirements (EUR 100,000+) and mandatory local presence. Seychelles remains more accessible for startups, though Estonia's e-Residency program can simplify company management. For more on Estonia, see our Estonia page.

Is there a minimum capital requirement for the license?

Can I serve customers from the EU with a Seychelles license?
