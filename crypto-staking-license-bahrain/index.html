What is a Crypto Staking License in Bahrain?

A crypto staking license Bahrain is a regulatory authorisation issued by the Central Bank of Bahrain (CBB) under its Crypto-Asset Module (Crypto-Asset Rules). It permits a company to operate a staking platform or provide staking-as-a-service for proof-of-stake blockchains. The license covers the custody of clients' crypto assets, the delegation of those assets to validators, and the distribution of staking rewards.

The CBB's framework is one of the most advanced in the region, ensuring that staking providers meet strict requirements for capital, governance, and risk management. As of 2026, the license is mandatory for any firm offering staking services to Bahraini residents or operating from Bahrain. The CBB distinguishes between different types of crypto-asset services, and staking is treated as a separate category requiring specific endorsements. This means that a general exchange license does not automatically permit staking. Key elements of the license include strong AML/CFT controls, a minimum capital requirement of EUR 500,000, and a requirement to maintain a physical presence in Bahrain. The CBB also mandates regular audits and reporting to ensure ongoing compliance. Who Needs a Crypto Staking License in Bahrain?

Any legal entity that intends to offer crypto staking services to clients in Bahrain or from a Bahraini base must obtain a crypto staking license from the CBB. This includes:

  • Staking-as-a-service platforms that pool client assets and delegate them to validators
  • Cryptocurrency exchanges that offer staking to their users as part of their platform
  • Asset managers that stake client funds on proof-of-stake networks like Ethereum, Solana, or Polkadot
  • Wallet providers that integrate staking features, allowing users to stake directly from their wallets
  • Institutional custody providers that offer staking as an additional service

Even if your company is based outside Bahrain, if you target Bahraini clients or market your services to residents, you may need to register or obtain a license. The CBB has extraterritorial reach in certain cases, particularly if the services are deemed to have a local impact. License Type and Regulator

The regulator for crypto staking in Bahrain is the Central Bank of Bahrain (CBB). The CBB's Crypto-Asset Module classifies staking services under the broader category of "Crypto-Asset Services." The specific license type is a "Crypto-Asset Service Provider License" with an endorsement for staking activities. This license is distinct from a regular exchange license and requires additional capital and operational safeguards.

The CBB is known for its rigorous but fair oversight. It requires firms to have a physical presence in Bahrain, including a registered office and key personnel such as a compliance officer and a money laundering reporting officer (MLRO). The regulatory framework aligns with international standards, including FATF recommendations, making it a credible jurisdiction for institutional investors. The CBB also operates a Regulatory Sandbox for innovative fintech solutions, which can be a stepping stone to a full license. Capital Requirement

The CBB imposes a minimum capital requirement for crypto staking license holders. As of 2026, the minimum paid-up capital is EUR 500,000 (or equivalent in BHD). This capital must be maintained at all times and can be in the form of cash or highly liquid assets. The requirement is higher than for simple exchange licenses due to the additional risks associated with staking, such as slashing and market volatility.

Capital must be held in a Bahraini bank account and cannot be used for operational expenses without CBB approval. The CBB may also require that a portion of the capital be held as a security deposit. In comparison, Panama has no specific capital requirement for crypto businesses, making it a lower-cost alternative for startups. However, the higher capital in Bahrain provides greater regulatory credibility, which can be important for attracting institutional clients. Tax Treatment for Crypto Staking in Bahrain

Bahrain offers a highly favourable tax environment for crypto businesses. There is no corporate income tax, no capital gains tax, and no value-added tax (VAT) on crypto transactions. Staking rewards earned by the company are generally tax-free at the corporate level. However, clients may be subject to personal income tax in their home jurisdictions, and it is the responsibility of the staking provider to inform clients of their tax obligations.

Bahrain also has a double taxation treaty network that can benefit international firms. The tax regime is one of the key attractions of the Bahrain crypto license. Additionally, there are no withholding taxes on dividends or interest paid to foreign shareholders. Allowed Activities Under the License

A crypto staking license from the CBB permits the following activities:

  • Receiving and holding clients' crypto assets for staking purposes
  • Delegating staked assets to validators on proof-of-stake blockchains
  • Distributing staking rewards to clients after deducting agreed fees
  • Providing staking-related advisory services to clients
  • Operating a staking pool where multiple clients' assets are combined

The license does not automatically permit exchange trading, custody without staking, or issuing new crypto assets. Additional endorsements are required for those activities. The CBB may also impose conditions on the types of blockchain networks that can be used for staking, focusing on those with proven security and governance. For example, staking on newer or less established networks may require additional risk disclosures. Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining a crypto staking license in Bahrain involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step:

  1. Pre-application consultation: We assess your business model, identify the appropriate license type, and confirm eligibility. This includes a gap analysis of your current compliance framework.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Bahraini company with the Ministry of Industry and Commerce. You will need a local registered address and at least one director resident in Bahrain.
  3. Document preparation: Prepare a detailed business plan, AML/KYC policies, risk management framework, governance structure, and financial projections. The CBB requires these documents to be comprehensive.
  4. Capital deposit: Open a corporate bank account with a licensed Bahraini bank and deposit the minimum capital of EUR 500,000. The bank will conduct its own due diligence.
  5. Submission: Submit the application to the CBB along with all supporting documents. The CBB will acknowledge receipt and begin its review.
  6. Review and interview: The CBB reviews the application and may request an interview with key personnel, including the MLRO and senior management. Be prepared to explain your business model in detail.
  7. Approval and licensing: Upon approval, pay the license fee and receive the official license certificate. The license is valid for one year and must be renewed annually.
  8. Ongoing compliance: Submit regular reports to the CBB, including financial statements, transaction reports, and audit results. Maintain capital requirements and update policies as needed.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

When applying for a crypto staking license in Bahrain, avoid these pitfalls:

  • Underestimating capital requirements: Ensure you have the full EUR 500,000 available in liquid assets and can demonstrate the source of funds.
  • Incomplete documentation: The CBB requires a comprehensive business plan, AML policies, and risk management framework. Missing documents can delay the process by months.
  • Ignoring local presence: You must have a physical office and key personnel in Bahrain. Virtual offices are not accepted.
  • Choosing the wrong license type: Staking requires a specific endorsement; a general exchange license may not suffice, and you may need to apply for a variation.
  • Overlooking ongoing compliance: Regular reporting, audits, and capital maintenance are mandatory. Non-compliance can lead to penalties.
  • Failing to conduct proper due diligence on validators: The CBB expects you to assess the security and reliability of the blockchain networks you use.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto staking license in Bahrain?

The minimum paid-up capital is EUR 500,000 (or equivalent in BHD). This must be maintained at all times and held in a Bahraini bank account. The capital can be in cash or highly liquid assets, and it cannot be used for operational expenses without CBB approval.

How long does it take to get a crypto staking license in Bahrain?

The timeline typically ranges from 6 to 12 months from application to approval. Delays can occur if the application is incomplete or if the CBB requires additional information. Consulting24 helps expedite the process by ensuring all documents are prepared correctly from the start.

Is a physical presence required in Bahrain?

Yes, the CBB requires licensees to have a physical office in Bahrain and key personnel, including a compliance officer and an MLRO. A virtual office is not sufficient. Consulting24 can assist with finding suitable office space and recruiting local staff.

Can I stake any cryptocurrency under a Bahrain license?

The license permits staking on proof-of-stake blockchains that the CBB deems acceptable. The CBB may impose conditions on which networks can be used, focusing on those with proven security and governance. You should disclose the intended networks in your application.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

Licensees must submit regular reports to the CBB, including financial statements, transaction reports, and audit results. They must also maintain AML/CFT policies, conduct periodic risk assessments, and ensure capital requirements are met. Annual license renewal is required.

Does Bahrain tax crypto staking rewards?

No, Bahrain has no corporate income tax, capital gains tax, or VAT on crypto transactions. Staking rewards earned by the company are generally tax-free. However, clients may be subject to tax in their home jurisdictions.

Can I offer staking to clients outside Bahrain?

Yes, the license allows you to serve clients globally, but you must comply with the laws of the client's jurisdiction. The CBB does not restrict the geographic scope, but you should seek legal advice on cross-border compliance.

What happens if I fail to maintain the capital requirement?

Failure to maintain the minimum capital can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. The CBB requires immediate notification if capital falls below the threshold, and you may be given a period to rectify the shortfall.

How does Bahrain compare to Panama for crypto staking?

Bahrain offers a regulated environment with higher capital requirements (EUR 500,000) and a longer timeline (6-12 months), but provides greater regulatory credibility.
