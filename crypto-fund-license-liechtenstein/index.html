What Is the Liechtenstein Crypto Fund License?

The Liechtenstein crypto fund license is a regulatory authorization for managing investment funds that invest primarily in crypto assets. It is governed by the Liechtenstein Financial Market Authority (FMA) under the Investment Undertakings Act (IUG) and the TVTG. The license allows fund managers to establish and operate alternative investment funds (AIFs) or undertakings for collective investment (UCIs) with a crypto focus.

Liechtenstein was one of the first countries to create a dedicated legal framework for blockchain assets, making it a pioneer in the crypto fund space. The license ensures compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) rules, investor protection, and operational transparency. As of 2026, the FMA has refined its expectations, requiring detailed risk management and custody arrangements for digital assets. The license is part of Liechtenstein's strategy to attract fintech and blockchain businesses while maintaining high regulatory standards.

For example, a fund manager launching a venture capital fund that invests in tokenized startups must hold this license. Similarly, a fund focusing on staking and yield farming strategies would need to demonstrate strong risk controls in its application. The FMA also expects clear segregation of client assets and regular reporting on portfolio composition.

Who Needs This License?

The crypto fund license is essential for:

  • Fund managers launching blockchain-based investment funds
  • Asset managers looking to offer crypto-focused products to professional or retail investors
  • Family offices or venture capital firms investing in digital assets on behalf of third parties
  • Existing fund managers expanding into crypto as a new asset class

If you plan to pool investor capital for crypto investments, you need this license. It is not required for single-asset holdings or personal trading. For example, a manager setting up a fund that invests in Bitcoin, Ethereum, and DeFi tokens must hold this license. License Type & Regulator

The license is issued by the Liechtenstein Financial Market Authority (FMA). The primary legal basis is the Investment Undertakings Act (IUG) for fund structures, supplemented by the TVTG for tokenized assets. There are two main types:

  • Internal Fund Manager: Manages a single fund directly. Lower capital requirement (EUR 50,000). This is suitable for a single fund structure where the manager is also the fund itself.
  • External Fund Manager: Manages multiple funds and may delegate activities. Higher capital requirement (EUR 125,000). This is typical for professional fund management companies.

Both require FMA approval and ongoing supervision. The FMA also requires a local registered office, at least one local director (or a local service provider), and substance in Liechtenstein. The regulator conducts fit-and-proper checks on all key personnel. Consulting24 coordinates with local legal partners to ensure compliance with FMA expectations.

In addition, the FMA may require a local compliance officer and a local auditor. The application must include detailed business plans, organizational charts, and risk management policies. The regulator is known for thorough reviews, so preparation is key.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a crypto fund manager in Liechtenstein is typically EUR 50,000 for internal managers and EUR 125,000 for external managers, in line with EU MiFID and AIFMD standards. This capital must be held in liquid assets and maintained throughout the license period. Higher amounts may be required for complex fund structures. For example, a fund with multiple sub-funds or a retail focus may need EUR 150,000 or more. The capital serves as a buffer against operational risks and must be reported to the FMA annually. Consulting24 advises on structuring capital efficiently.

It is important to note that the capital must be fully paid up before the license is granted. Some managers choose to hold the capital in a bank account or in highly liquid securities. The FMA may also require additional capital if the fund engages in high-risk strategies like used trading. We recommend discussing your specific capital needs with a local advisor.

Tax Treatment

Liechtenstein offers a favorable tax regime for investment funds. Fund income is generally exempt from corporate income tax at the fund level; instead, investors are taxed on distributions. The standard corporate tax rate for fund managers is 12.5%. There is no VAT on fund management services. Crypto assets are treated as intangible assets for tax purposes, and capital gains on crypto held by the fund may be tax-exempt if certain conditions are met. However, the tax treatment depends on the fund's structure and investor base. Consulting24 recommends a tax review with a local advisor to optimize your fund's tax position.

For example, a fund that reinvests all income may not trigger tax at the investor level until distributions are made. Additionally, Liechtenstein has double tax treaties with many countries, which can reduce withholding taxes on dividends and interest. It is crucial to structure the fund properly to avoid unintended tax liabilities. We can connect you with tax specialists who understand crypto fund taxation.

Allowed Activities

Under the crypto fund license, you can:

  • Establish and manage alternative investment funds (AIFs) investing in cryptocurrencies, tokens, and blockchain projects
  • Offer fund shares to professional and retail investors (subject to restrictions and additional requirements for retail)
  • Delegate portfolio management to third parties, such as crypto asset managers
  • Provide fund administration and custody services if separately licensed or outsourced to a licensed custodian
  • Engage in staking, lending, or yield farming as part of the fund's investment strategy, provided it is disclosed in the prospectus

Prohibited activities include retail banking, deposit-taking, and unlicensed payment services. The FMA also restricts use and derivatives trading unless specifically authorized. For instance, a fund that wants to use futures or options must apply for a separate authorization or ensure the derivatives are used solely for hedging. The FMA expects clear policies on how the fund handles forks, airdrops, and other blockchain events. Consulting24 can help draft these policies.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Insufficient capital: Failing to maintain minimum capital requirements can lead to rejection or suspension. Ensure capital is held in liquid assets.
  • Poor AML documentation: Incomplete or generic AML policies are a common reason for rejection. Tailor them to your fund's specific activities.
  • Ignoring tax obligations: Not structuring for tax efficiency can result in unexpected liabilities. Consult a tax advisor early.
  • Overlooking substance: The FMA requires local directors, office space, and operational substance. Virtual offices may not suffice.
  • Underestimating timeline: The process can take up to 8 months; plan accordingly and start early.
  • Inadequate investor disclosures: The prospectus must clearly state risks, fees, and investment strategy. Vague disclosures can lead to rejection.
  • Not preparing for FMA queries: The FMA often asks for additional information; having a responsive team can prevent delays.

One common pitfall is using a template AML policy without customizing it to the fund's activities. For instance, a fund that engages in staking must have specific policies on how staking rewards are handled. Consulting24 can review your documents before submission to catch these issues.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between an internal and external fund manager in Liechtenstein?

An internal fund manager manages a single fund and is typically the fund itself, requiring EUR 50,000 capital. An external fund manager manages multiple funds and can delegate activities, requiring EUR 125,000 capital. The choice depends on your fund structure and whether you plan to manage multiple funds.

How long does it take to get a Liechtenstein crypto fund license?

The typical timeline is 4-8 months, including 1-2 months for document preparation and 3-6 months for FMA review. Delays can occur if the FMA requests additional information, so it is best to plan for up to 10 months.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

Ongoing requirements include annual audits, AML/KYC reporting, regulatory filings, capital maintenance, and fit-and-proper updates. The FMA expects quarterly or annual reports depending on the fund size. Non-compliance can lead to fines or license revocation.

Can I use a virtual office for the Liechtenstein fund?

No, the FMA requires a physical registered office and local substance, including at least one local director or service provider. Virtual offices are not sufficient. You can rent a small office or use a licensed service provider for substance.

Is the Liechtenstein crypto fund license recognized in other EU countries?

Yes, under AIFMD, licensed funds can passport into other EEA countries, allowing you to market to professional investors across the EU. This is a major advantage over non-EU jurisdictions like Panama.

What is the tax rate for fund managers in Liechtenstein?

The corporate tax rate for fund managers is 12.5%. Fund-level income is generally exempt from corporate tax; instead, investors are taxed on distributions. Crypto capital gains may be tax-exempt under certain conditions.

Can I include staking and yield farming in the fund's strategy?

Yes, but these activities must be clearly disclosed in the prospectus and risk management framework. The FMA expects detailed policies on how staking rewards are handled and how risks are mitigated. Consulting24 can help draft these policies.

What happens if the FMA rejects my application?

If rejected, the FMA will provide reasons. You may address the deficiencies and resubmit. Common reasons include insufficient capital, poor AML documentation, or lack of substance. Consulting24 can help you prepare a stronger application to minimize rejection risk.

Do I need a local bank account for the fund?

Yes, you need a local bank account for the fund's fiat operations. Liechtenstein banks like LLB and VP Bank offer services to licensed funds. Consulting24 can assist with introductions to crypto-friendly banks.

How does the Liechtenstein license compare to Panama for crypto funds?

Panama offers a simple company setup at EUR 6,000 but no specific fund license, making it suitable for smaller or private funds. Liechtenstein provides a regulated structure with EU passporting, higher credibility, and higher costs (EUR 30,000-70,000). The choice depends on your target investors and regulatory needs.
