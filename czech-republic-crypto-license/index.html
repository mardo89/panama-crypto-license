Czech Republic Crypto License 2026

For years the Czech Republic was the EU's best-kept secret for crypto — a simple trade-licence registration made it among the cheapest and fastest ways to operate legally inside the EU. MiCA changes that, replacing the light regime with full CASP authorization under the Czech National Bank — but Czechia remains one of the more cost-efficient EU jurisdictions.

The old route vs MiCA

Historically, founders set up a Czech s.r.o. and obtained a trade licence covering crypto activities, plus AML registration — quick and inexpensive. With MiCA in force, that model is superseded by CASP authorization supervised by the Czech National Bank. Expect the standard MiCA requirements: own-funds capital, AML/CFT framework, a compliance function, and fit-and-proper management.

Why founders still like Czechia

  • One of the more cost-efficient EU routes to a passportable CASP
  • Central European base with solid banking and talent
  • Competitive corporate tax compared with much of Western Europe
  • No director residency requirement for company formation

Czechia vs Panama: Czechia gives you an EU-passportable CASP at a relatively efficient cost. Panama is faster and cheaper still (€6,000, 2–3 weeks, 0% foreign-income tax, no minimum capital) but isn't EU-regulated. Pick by where your customers are.

Allowed activities under a Czech CASP

  • Operating a crypto-asset trading platform
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for fiat and other crypto-assets
  • Custody and administration of crypto-assets
  • Reception, transmission and execution of orders
  • Placement, advice and portfolio management; transfer services

Tax in the Czech Republic

Czechia's corporate income tax is competitive versus much of Western Europe, and the country offers a stable EU legal environment with strong banking and engineering talent. Combined with relatively efficient setup costs, that makes it a pragmatic base for a passportable CASP. Exact tax outcomes depend on your structure — we model this with you before you commit.

The application process, step by step

  1. Scope & structure — service classes, capital tier, ownership.
  2. Incorporate the Czech s.r.o. and fund the MiCA capital.
  3. Build the file — business plan, governance, AML/CFT, IT/custody, key persons.
  4. Apply for CASP authorization with the Czech National Bank (ČNB).
  5. Authorization, then ongoing supervision and reporting.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Expecting the old, near-instant trade-licence route — MiCA replaced it with full CASP authorization.
  • Under-budgeting capital for exchange/custody (€125k–€150k tiers).
  • Skipping a credible AML function and substance plan.

Who the Czech Republic suits

Czechia fits founders who want EU CASP status and passporting without the higher overheads of Western Europe, and who can meet MiCA capital and substance. If EU access isn't essential and you want to launch fast and lean, a Panama company is typically the quicker, cheaper first step.
