Czech Republic Crypto License: Your EU Gateway Under MiCA

The Czech Republic has become a compelling jurisdiction for crypto asset service providers (CASPs) under the EU's Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) regulation, which is fully in force in 2026. A Czech Republic crypto license is now a MiCA-compliant passport, replacing the old trade-licence VASP route. This means that obtaining a license from the Czech National Bank (CNB) grants you access to the entire European Economic Area (EEA) with a single authorization.

What Is the Czech Republic Crypto License?

The Czech Republic crypto license is an authorization issued by the Czech National Bank (CNB) under the MiCA regulation. It allows companies to provide crypto asset services—such as custody, exchange, and trading—legally across the EU. Unlike the previous system where firms only needed a trade license for VASP activities, MiCA introduces a comprehensive licensing regime with specific capital requirements, governance standards, and investor protections.

Key features include:

  • MiCA CASP classification: The license is a full CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) authorization, not a simple registration.
  • Passporting rights: Once licensed, you can operate in any EU/EEA member state without additional local licenses.
  • Regulatory oversight: The CNB is the primary regulator, ensuring compliance with anti-money laundering (AML), counter-terrorism financing (CTF), and operational resilience rules.

This license is ideal for businesses seeking a solid EU foothold with a relatively fast and cost-effective process compared to other EU jurisdictions like Malta or Luxembourg.

Who Needs a Czech Republic Crypto License?

Any business offering crypto asset services to EU residents must obtain a MiCA CASP license. The Czech Republic license is particularly suitable for:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralized and decentralized platforms) facilitating trading between crypto and fiat or between crypto assets.
  • Custodial wallet providers that hold or control clients' private keys.
  • Brokers and dealers executing orders on behalf of clients.
  • Payment processors enabling crypto-to-fiat conversions.
  • Asset managers offering portfolio management of crypto assets.

If your target market is the EU, a Czech license is a pragmatic choice. It is also attractive for startups and scale-ups that want a reputable EU license without the high costs of jurisdictions like Switzerland or Malta. However, if you need a faster setup for non-EU markets, consider Panama (flat EUR 6,000) or our other jurisdictions.

License Type & Regulator

The Czech Republic crypto license is a MiCA CASP authorization, regulated by the Czech National Bank (CNB). The CNB is the central bank and financial market supervisor, responsible for ensuring that licensed entities comply with MiCA, local AML laws, and prudential requirements.

The license covers all service categories defined in MiCA, including:

  • Class 1: Custody and administration of crypto assets (capital: €50,000)
  • Class 2: Exchange, trading, and execution of orders (capital: €125,000)
  • Class 3: Full service provider including portfolio management (capital: €150,000)

Unlike the old VASP trade license, which required only a simple notification, the new regime demands a detailed application with a business plan, governance structure, risk management policies, and proof of capital. The CNB has a reputation for thorough but fair assessments, with an average processing time of 4-8 months.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the Czech Republic imposes capital requirements based on the type of services offered. The minimum initial capital is:

  • €50,000 for custody and administration services only (Class 1).
  • €125,000 for exchange, trading, and order execution (Class 2).
  • €150,000 for full services including portfolio management (Class 3).

These amounts must be held in liquid assets (e.g., cash or government bonds) and maintained throughout the license period. The capital serves as a buffer to cover operational risks and potential losses. Unlike some jurisdictions, the Czech Republic does not require a local bank account for the capital deposit, but it must be held in an EU bank. Tax Treatment

The Czech Republic offers a favorable tax environment for crypto businesses. Key points:

  • Corporate income tax: 19% on profits (standard rate).
  • VAT: Exempt for crypto-to-fiat exchanges and trading (subject to conditions).
  • Personal income tax: For individuals, crypto gains are taxed at 15% (up to 48x average salary) or 23% above that.
  • No withholding tax on dividends paid to EU residents (under EU directives).

Importantly, the Czech Republic does not impose a specific crypto tax; general tax rules apply. The CNB and Ministry of Finance have issued guidance clarifying that crypto asset transactions are treated as property for tax purposes. This provides clarity and predictability. For detailed tax planning, consult a local tax advisor. Consulting24 can coordinate with Czech tax experts as part of our service.

Allowed Activities

A Czech Republic crypto license under MiCA allows the following activities:

  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients.
  • Operation of a trading platform (exchange) for crypto assets.
  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency or other crypto assets.
  • Execution of orders on behalf of clients.
  • Placement of crypto assets (underwriting).
  • Receiving and transmitting orders for crypto assets.
  • Advice on crypto assets (if applied for).
  • Portfolio management of crypto assets (Class 3).

Activities not covered include issuing asset-referenced tokens (ARTs) or e-money tokens, which require separate licenses under MiCA. The license does not permit gambling or high-risk financial products. All activities must be conducted in compliance with AML/KYC regulations.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a Czech Republic crypto license involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step:

  1. Initial consultation: We assess your business model and determine the appropriate service class and capital requirement.
  2. Company incorporation: Set up a Czech s.r.o. (limited liability company) with a registered office. This typically takes 2-4 weeks.
  3. Document preparation: Prepare the application package, including business plan, AML/KYC policies, risk management framework, governance structure, and financial projections.
  4. Capital deposit: Deposit the required minimum capital in an EU bank account and obtain proof.
  5. Submission: Submit the application to the CNB along with the fee.
  6. CNB review: The CNB reviews the application, may request additional information, and conducts interviews with key personnel. This takes 4-8 months.
  7. License issuance: Upon approval, the CNB issues the license. You can then begin operations across the EU.

Banking & Payment Solutions

One of the biggest challenges for crypto businesses is obtaining banking services. The Czech Republic has a relatively open banking sector, but many traditional banks are cautious. Options include:

  • Local banks: Some Czech banks (e.g., Česká spořitelna, ČSOB) may open accounts for licensed CASPs, but they often require a track record and high compliance standards.
  • Neobanks: Digital banks like Revolut Business or Wise may offer accounts, but they have their own AML policies.
  • EMI partners: Electronic money institutions (EMIs) can provide payment processing and fiat on/off ramps.
