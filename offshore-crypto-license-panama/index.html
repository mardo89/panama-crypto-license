What is the Offshore Panama Crypto License?

The offshore Panama crypto license is a regulatory authorisation for virtual asset service providers (VASPs) to operate from Panama. It is governed by the Superintendencia de Bancos de Panamá (SBP) under the 2021 regulation that brought crypto activities under banking supervision. As of 2026, Panama has not yet implemented a comprehensive crypto law, but the SBP has issued guidelines requiring VASPs to register and comply with AML/CFT obligations.

This license is particularly attractive for offshore operations because it allows companies to serve clients outside Panama without paying local taxes on foreign-sourced income. The flat incorporation and license fee of EUR 6,000 makes it one of the most affordable options globally. There is no minimum capital requirement, and the timeline is typically 3-6 months, depending on the complexity of the business model and the completeness of the application.

License Type and Regulator

The offshore Panama crypto license is a VASP registration under the supervision of the Superintendencia de Bancos de Panamá (SBP). The SBP is the primary financial regulator in Panama, responsible for overseeing banks, trust companies, and, since 2021, virtual asset service providers. The license is not a full banking license but a specific authorisation for crypto-related activities.

There is only one type of VASP license in Panama, covering all permitted activities. However, the SBP may impose conditions based on the business model. The license is valid for an indefinite period, subject to annual renewal and compliance with ongoing AML/CFT obligations.

For comparison, other jurisdictions have multiple license tiers. For example, Lithuania offers three classes of CASP licenses with capital requirements of EUR 50,000, 125,000, and 150,000 under MiCA. Panama's single-class approach simplifies the process. If you are considering other options, see our Lithuania crypto license page.

Capital Requirement

Panama has no minimum capital requirement for the offshore crypto license. This is a major advantage compared to EU jurisdictions where MiCA imposes capital tiers of EUR 50,000, 125,000, or 150,000 depending on the services provided. In Panama, you can start with any amount of capital that is adequate for your business operations.

However, the SBP expects the company to have sufficient financial resources to operate and meet its obligations. A typical recommendation is to have at least EUR 10,000-20,000 in the company bank account to demonstrate financial stability. This is not a legal requirement but a practical consideration for the application review.

Tax Treatment

Panama operates a territorial tax system. Offshore companies that derive income from outside Panama are not subject to local corporate income tax. This means that if your crypto business serves clients abroad, you pay 0% tax in Panama on that income. There is also no capital gains tax, no withholding tax on dividends, and no VAT on services exported.

However, you must file annual tax returns and maintain proper accounting records. Panama has double taxation treaties with a limited number of countries, so you should consider your global tax position. The tax advantages make Panama a popular choice for offshore crypto operations.

Allowed Activities

The Panama crypto license permits the following activities:

  • Exchange of virtual assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of virtual assets for other virtual assets
  • Custody and administration of virtual assets
  • Transfer of virtual assets (including payment services)
  • Participation in and provision of financial services related to an issuer's offer of virtual assets
  • Operation of a trading platform for virtual assets

Activities that are not allowed include issuing virtual assets (unless registered separately), operating as a bank without a banking license, and providing services to sanctioned entities. The SBP may restrict certain activities on a case-by-case basis. For a full list of permitted activities, consult with our experts.

Compliance and Trust

Compliance is critical for maintaining your Panama crypto license. The SBP requires ongoing AML/CFT measures, including:

  • Appointment of a local AML compliance officer
  • Customer due diligence (CDD) and KYC procedures
  • Transaction monitoring and reporting of suspicious activities
  • Annual audit by a licensed auditor
  • Filing of annual reports with the SBP

Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. Consulting24 provides ongoing compliance support to ensure you meet all obligations. Note: this is general guidance, not legal advice. We recommend engaging a local lawyer for specific legal matters.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

When applying for a Panama crypto license, avoid these common pitfalls:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing business plan, AML policies, or source of funds can delay the process.
  • Underestimating compliance: Ongoing AML obligations are not optional; failing to comply can lead to license revocation.
  • Choosing the wrong corporate structure: Ensure the company is properly incorporated with a local registered agent.
  • Ignoring tax obligations in your home country: Panama's territorial tax does not exempt you from taxes elsewhere.
  • Not securing banking early: Bank account opening can take months; start early.

Is there a minimum capital requirement for the Panama crypto license?

No, Panama does not impose a minimum capital requirement for the crypto license. However, you should have sufficient funds to cover operational costs and demonstrate financial stability to the regulator.

How long does it take to get a Panama crypto license?

The typical timeline is 3-6 months from start to approval. This includes company incorporation (1-2 weeks) and the SBP review process (2-4 months). Delays can occur if the application is incomplete.

What activities are allowed under the Panama crypto license?

Allowed activities include exchange of virtual assets for fiat or other virtual assets, custody, transfer services, operation of trading platforms, and participation in token offerings. Issuing virtual assets may require separate registration.

What is the tax rate for an offshore Panama crypto company?

Panama has a territorial tax system. Offshore companies earning income from outside Panama pay 0% corporate income tax. There is no capital gains tax, no withholding tax on dividends, and no VAT on exported services.

Can I operate a crypto exchange in Panama with this license?

Yes, operating a crypto exchange (centralised or peer-to-peer) is one of the permitted activities under the Panama VASP license. You must comply with AML/CFT regulations and obtain a bank account.

Do I need to be a resident of Panama to get the license?

No, there is no residency requirement. Directors and shareholders can be non-residents. However, you must appoint a local registered agent and a local AML compliance officer.
