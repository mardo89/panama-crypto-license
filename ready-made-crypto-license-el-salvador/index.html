What Is a Ready Made El Salvador Crypto License?

A ready made El Salvador crypto license refers to a pre-registered company that already holds the necessary authorizations to operate a virtual asset service provider (VASP) business in El Salvador. Instead of starting from scratch, you purchase an existing entity that has been incorporated and licensed under the country's digital asset laws. This approach significantly reduces the time to market, often from months to weeks.

El Salvador's regulatory framework for crypto is governed by the Banco Central de Reserva (BCR) and the Superintendencia del Sistema Financiero (SSF), which oversee compliance with the Ley de Activos Digitales (Digital Assets Law) enacted in 2023. As of 2026, the regime is fully operational, with clear rules for exchanges, custodians, and payment processors. A ready made license means the entity is already compliant with initial capital requirements and has submitted the mandatory documentation.

Key features of a ready made license include:

  • Pre-incorporated company with a clean legal history.
  • Existing license for specific VASP activities (e.g., exchange, custody).
  • No waiting for incorporation or initial approval - transfer of ownership is handled swiftly.
  • Full compliance with local AML/CFT regulations, including a registered compliance officer.

License Type and Regulator

In El Salvador, the relevant license for crypto activities is the Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) Authorization, issued under the Digital Assets Law. The primary regulators are:

  • Banco Central de Reserva (BCR) - oversees monetary policy and payment systems.
  • Superintendencia del Sistema Financiero (SSF) - supervises financial institutions, including VASPs for compliance and solvency.

The license covers multiple service categories, such as exchange, custody, and transfer of virtual assets. Each category may have specific requirements. As of 2026, the regime is mature, with a standardized application process and ongoing reporting obligations.

It is important to note that El Salvador does not have a separate 'crypto license' per se; the VASP authorization is integrated into the financial regulatory framework. This means licensed entities are subject to similar oversight as traditional financial institutions, including capital adequacy and AML/CFT measures.

Capital Requirement

El Salvador's Digital Assets Law sets minimum capital requirements for VASPs based on the type and volume of activities. As of 2026, the general minimum is $50,000 for basic services (e.g., exchange and wallet), with higher tiers for custody and complex operations (up to $250,000). A ready made license entity will already have this capital paid up and verified.

Capital must be maintained in a local bank account or in liquid assets approved by the SSF. The regulator may require additional capital if the business scales or offers services to a large number of users. Tax Treatment in El Salvador

El Salvador offers a highly favorable tax regime for crypto businesses. Key points:

  • Zero capital gains tax on Bitcoin and other digital assets (since 2021).
  • Corporate income tax at a standard rate of 30% (no special crypto exemption).
  • VAT of 13% applies to services, but crypto-to-crypto transactions may be exempt.
  • No withholding tax on dividends paid to non-residents (if certain conditions met).

It is crucial to obtain local tax advice, as the interaction between crypto activities and traditional tax rules can be complex. Consulting24 coordinates with local tax experts to ensure your structure is optimized.

Allowed Activities Under the License

A ready made El Salvador crypto license authorizes the following activities (subject to the specific license scope):

  • Exchange of virtual assets for fiat currency or other virtual assets.
  • Custody and administration of virtual assets on behalf of clients.
  • Transfer of virtual assets (including payment services).
  • Operation of a trading platform (order book or automated market maker).
  • Issuance of stablecoins or tokenized assets (with additional approval).

Activities such as mining, staking, or DeFi protocol operation may not require a license if no customer funds are involved. However, any activity that involves holding or transferring third-party assets is regulated. The license does not cover securities offerings or banking activities.

Compliance and Trust Considerations

Operating under a ready made license does not exempt you from ongoing compliance. El Salvador requires VASPs to implement strong AML/CFT programs, including:

  • Customer due diligence (CDD) and enhanced due diligence (EDD) for high-risk clients.
  • Transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting.
  • Appointment of a local compliance officer (can be outsourced).
  • Annual audits by an approved external auditor.
  • Reporting to the SSF on a quarterly basis.
