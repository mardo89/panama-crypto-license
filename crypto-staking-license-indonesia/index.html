Overview: What is a Crypto Staking License in Indonesia?

Indonesia regulates crypto assets as commodities under Bappebti. A crypto staking license Indonesia refers to the registration and approval required to offer staking services as part of a crypto asset trading platform. Staking, where users lock tokens to support blockchain networks and earn rewards, is treated as a value-added service. The license is essentially a Crypto Asset Physical Trader License (PAK) that permits staking alongside trading, custody, and other activities.

Bappebti's Regulation No. 8 of 2021 and subsequent updates set the legal basis. As of 2026, the framework is mature, with clear capital requirements and operational rules. Consulting24 advises on the entire process, from entity setup to regulatory submission. The process involves registering a local entity (PT), preparing detailed documentation in Indonesian language, and meeting strict capital and compliance standards. For example, a platform offering staking for tokens like Solana or Cardano must ensure those tokens are on Bappebti's official whitelist. The license is mandatory for any entity facilitating staking for Indonesian residents, including foreign platforms targeting the market.

Who Needs a Crypto Staking License in Indonesia?

Any platform offering crypto staking services to Indonesian residents must obtain the license. This includes:

  • Centralized exchanges (CEX) with staking pools, such as Binance or local exchanges like Tokocrypto
  • Decentralized finance (DeFi) platforms that intermediate staking, for example, those offering liquidity staking derivatives
  • Wallet providers offering staking-as-a-service, like Trust Wallet or Exodus if they target Indonesian users
  • Brokerage firms including staking in their product suite

Even foreign entities targeting Indonesian users must register locally. The obligation applies to any business that facilitates staking, whether directly or through smart contracts. For example, a global exchange listing staking for Indonesian users must have a local entity and PAK license. Non-compliance can result in fines, blocking of services by the Ministry of Communication, and criminal penalties. License Type & Regulator

The regulator is the Commodity Futures Trading Regulatory Agency (Bappebti), under the Ministry of Trade. The license is a Crypto Asset Physical Trader License (PAK). Staking is not a separate license but an allowed activity under the PAK. Bappebti sets capital, technology, and compliance standards. The regulator also requires that all crypto assets traded or staked be listed on the official whitelist maintained by Bappebti. As of 2026, the whitelist includes over 300 tokens, with periodic updates.

Capital Requirement

Bappebti requires a minimum paid-up capital of IDR 1 trillion (approximately EUR 60,000 as of 2026). This capital must be deposited in a local bank account and cannot be withdrawn without regulatory approval. The capital ensures operational stability and client protection. Additionally, the company must maintain a minimum net worth after licensing, typically 80% of the paid-up capital. For example, if the company incurs losses, it must replenish capital to meet the threshold.

Tax Treatment for Crypto Staking in Indonesia

Indonesia imposes a value-added tax (VAT) of 0.11% on crypto asset transactions, including staking rewards. Additionally, income tax (PPh) of 0.1% applies to gains from trading. Staking rewards are treated as income and subject to progressive corporate income tax rates (up to 22% for corporations, or up to 30% for individuals). For example, a corporate entity earning staking rewards of IDR 500 million annually would pay approximately IDR 110 million in corporate income tax, plus VAT on each transaction.

Consulting24 recommends engaging a local tax advisor to optimize structures. Panama offers a territorial tax system, meaning no tax on foreign-source income, including staking rewards from international users. This can result in significant tax savings for global operations. For EU licenses, tax treatment varies by country; for instance, Lithuania taxes corporate income at 15% on global profits.

Allowed Activities Under the License

The PAK license permits the following activities:

  • Crypto asset trading (spot, margin)
  • Custody of client assets
  • Staking services (proof-of-stake tokens like Ethereum, Solana, Polkadot)
  • Token listing and delisting (subject to Bappebti whitelist)
  • Provision of crypto wallets (hot and cold storage)

Staking is explicitly allowed, but the platform must disclose risks and ensure client funds are segregated. DeFi staking pools may require additional approval from Bappebti. The license does not cover derivatives or futures trading, which require separate approval from Bappebti or the Financial Services Authority (OJK). For example, offering staking derivatives like liquid staking tokens may be considered a separate product. Consulting24 can advise on the scope of your intended activities.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Company setup: Establish a local entity (PT) with minimum capital. This includes notarization, deed of establishment, and approval from the Ministry of Law and Human Rights. The process takes 2-4 weeks.
  2. Document preparation: Business plan, AML/KYC policies, technology infrastructure details, financial projections, and risk management framework. All documents must be in Indonesian language. Consulting24 assists with templates and translation.
  3. Capital deposit: Deposit IDR 1 trillion in a local bank and obtain a certificate of deposit. The bank must be approved by Bappebti.
  4. Submission: File application with Bappebti, including all documents and fee payment. The regulator may request additional information within 30 days.
  5. Review: Bappebti assesses compliance, may request clarifications. This stage can take 3-6 months, including a technology audit by an approved auditor.
  6. Approval: License granted, followed by ongoing reporting obligations. The company must also register with the Trade Ministry and obtain a business identification number (NIB).

Bappebti also conducts periodic inspections.

Common Mistakes When Applying

  • Underestimating capital requirements: Many applicants fail to deposit the full IDR 1 trillion on time, causing delays or rejection.
  • Incomplete AML/KYC documentation: Missing policies or procedures lead to requests for additional information, extending the timeline.
  • Not segregating client assets: Bappebti requires strict separation of client and company funds, often through a trust account.
  • Ignoring local tax obligations: Failure to register for VAT and income tax can cause penalties and jeopardize the license.
  • Choosing the wrong entity structure: A PT with foreign ownership may need local shareholders and meet minimum local ownership requirements (e.g., 15% local).
  • Not preparing for the technology audit: The platform must meet security standards; failing the audit can halt the process.
