What is a Crypto Fund License Malta?

A crypto fund license Malta is a regulatory authorisation issued by the MFSA that permits a fund manager to operate a collective investment scheme (CIS) investing primarily in virtual financial assets (VFAs). This license is required under the Investment Services Act (Cap. 370) and the VFA Act. The license covers both Professional Investor Funds (PIFs) and Alternative Investment Funds (AIFs) that invest in crypto assets.

Key features of the regime include:

  • Regulator: Malta Financial Services Authority (MFSA)
  • Legal basis: Investment Services Act, VFA Act, and MFSA Rulebooks
  • Fund types: Professional Investor Funds (PIFs), Alternative Investment Funds (AIFs), and UCITS (rare for crypto)
  • Minimum capital: EUR 125,000 for a PIF, EUR 150,000 for an AIF (as per MiCA tiers)
  • Tax treatment: 5% effective tax rate on fund income (subject to conditions)

Malta's framework is designed to provide investor protection while fostering innovation. The MFSA requires a substance presence in Malta, including at least two directors resident in Malta or EEA, and a local compliance officer.

Who Needs a Crypto Fund License in Malta?

You need this license if you are setting up a fund that:

  • Invests in virtual financial assets (e.g., Bitcoin, Ethereum, security tokens)
  • Is open to professional or retail investors (depending on fund type)
  • Is managed by a company based in Malta or targeting EU investors

Typical applicants include:

  • Fund managers launching a crypto hedge fund or venture capital fund
  • Existing asset managers expanding into digital assets
  • Family offices seeking a regulated structure for crypto investments
  • Blockchain projects raising capital through tokenised funds

If your fund only invests in utility tokens or non-financial crypto assets, you may still fall under the VFA Act. The MFSA determines classification on a case-by-case basis. License Type & Regulator

The crypto fund license Malta is issued by the Malta Financial Services Authority (MFSA). The MFSA is the single regulator for financial services in Malta, including investment services, banking, and insurance. For crypto funds, the MFSA applies the VFA Rulebook and the Investment Services Rules for Fund Managers.

There are two main license types:

License TypeFund TypeInvestor Type
PIF (Professional Investor Fund)Collective investment schemeProfessional investors only (min. EUR 100,000 investment)
AIF (Alternative Investment Fund)Alternative investment fundProfessional and retail investors (subject to additional rules)

Most crypto fund managers opt for a PIF because it has lighter regulatory requirements. The AIF is suitable if you want to admit retail investors. Both require a licensed fund manager (the 'Fund Manager') and a licensed custodian (the 'Custodian'). The MFSA also requires the appointment of a VFA Agent if the fund invests in VFAs.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA-aligned rules (fully in force in 2026), the minimum capital for a crypto fund manager in Malta is:

  • EUR 125,000 for a Professional Investor Fund (PIF)
  • EUR 150,000 for an Alternative Investment Fund (AIF) that admits retail investors
  • EUR 50,000 for a small AIF (assets under management below EUR 100 million)

This capital must be held in liquid assets (cash or cash equivalents) and maintained at all times. The MFSA may require additional capital based on the risk profile of the fund. For example, if the fund uses use or invests in highly volatile assets, a higher capital buffer may be imposed. The capital requirement is separate from the fund's own assets; it is the fund manager's own capital.

Note: These figures are the minimums. In practice, the MFSA expects the manager to have sufficient financial resources to cover operational expenses for at least 12 months. Consulting24 can help you model the required capital for your specific fund.

Tax Treatment

Malta offers a highly favourable tax regime for investment funds. The effective tax rate for a Malta fund can be as low as 5% due to the full imputation system. Here is how it works:

  • The fund pays tax at the standard corporate rate of 35% on its income.
  • When the fund distributes dividends to its shareholders, the shareholders (if non-resident) may claim a refund of 6/7 of the tax paid, reducing the effective rate to 5%.
  • Alternatively, if the fund elects to be a 'prescribed fund', it may be exempt from tax on capital gains and certain income.

Additionally, Malta has no withholding tax on dividends paid to non-resident investors, and no capital gains tax for non-resident investors on the sale of fund units. The tax regime is one of the main reasons fund managers choose Malta. However, the fund must have substance in Malta (management and control exercised in Malta) to benefit. Allowed Activities

A Malta crypto fund license permits the following activities:

  • Managing a collective investment scheme that invests in virtual financial assets (VFAs)
  • Marketing fund units to professional and/or retail investors (depending on fund type)
  • Dealing in VFAs for the account of the fund (as a portfolio management activity)
  • Providing investment advice to the fund (ancillary service)
  • Safe-keeping of fund assets (if the fund manager also acts as custodian, though usually a separate custodian is required)

Activities that are NOT allowed without additional licensing:

  • Operating a crypto exchange (requires a VFA Exchange license)
  • Providing custody services to third parties (requires a VFA Custodian license)
  • Issuing stablecoins or other VFAs (requires a VFA Issuer license)

The fund itself must be a 'prescribed fund' or 'professional investor fund' as defined by the MFSA. The fund's investment strategy must be clearly defined in the offering document. Consulting24 can help you define the scope of activities to match the license.

Banking & Payments

Opening a bank account for a Malta crypto fund can be challenging due to the perceived risk of crypto assets. However, Malta has several banks that accept crypto fund clients, especially if the fund is regulated by the MFSA. Options include:

  • Local Maltese banks (e.g., Bank of Valletta, HSBC Malta, APS Bank) - they require a full compliance review and may take 2-4 months to open an account.
  • EMI (Electronic Money Institutions) - faster but may have limitations on transaction sizes.
  • Payment processors specialising in crypto (e.g., Coinbase Commerce, BitPay) for accepting subscriptions in crypto.

We recommend starting the banking process early, as it can be a bottleneck. Benefits of a Malta Crypto Fund License

Malta offers several advantages for crypto fund managers:

  • EU passporting: As an EU member state, a Malta-licensed fund can market to professional investors across the EU under the AIFMD passport (for AIFs) or national private placement regimes.
  • Tax efficiency: Effective tax rate as low as 5% on fund income, and no withholding tax on dividends to non-residents.
  • Reputation: Malta is a well-regulated jurisdiction with a proactive regulator, enhancing investor confidence.
  • Established ecosystem: Malta has a mature professional services sector, including lawyers, auditors, and compliance experts familiar with crypto.
  • Flexible fund structures: Options for PIF, AIF, and even umbrella funds with multiple sub-funds.

Compliance & Trust

Compliance is critical for maintaining a Malta crypto fund license. Key requirements include:

  • AML/CFT: The fund must implement strong anti-money laundering policies, including customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting. The MFSA conducts periodic inspections.
  • Reporting: Annual audited financial statements, quarterly regulatory reports, and immediate notification of material changes.
  • Substance: The fund manager must have adequate physical presence in Malta (office, staff, directors). The MFSA expects at least two directors who are resident in Malta or the EEA.
  • VFA Agent: A licensed VFA Agent must be appointed to ensure compliance with the VFA Act.
  • Risk management: The fund must have a risk management framework covering market, liquidity, operational, and custody risks.

General guidance, not legal advice: The MFSA takes a principles-based approach, so demonstrating good faith and transparency is key. Consulting24 advises on setting up a compliance framework that meets MFSA expectations. We also recommend engaging a local compliance consultant for ongoing support.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

When applying for a Malta crypto fund license, avoid these pitfalls:

  • Insufficient substance: The MFSA requires real presence in Malta. Having only a registered address and no local directors or staff will lead to rejection.
  • Poor business plan: Your business plan must be detailed, realistic, and show how the fund will generate revenue. Vague plans are a red flag.
  • Ignoring AML/CFT: The MFSA expects a full AML/CFT framework from day one. Do not treat it as an afterthought.
  • Underestimating costs: Many applicants underestimate the total cost (legal, compliance, substance). Budget at least EUR 100,000 for the first year.
  • Choosing the wrong fund type: A PIF is simpler but restricts investors to professionals. If you later want retail investors, you may need to reapply as an AIF.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Malta crypto fund license?

The minimum capital is EUR 125,000 for a Professional Investor Fund (PIF) and EUR 150,000 for an Alternative Investment Fund (AIF) that admits retail investors. For small AIFs (AUM below EUR 100 million), the minimum is EUR 50,000. This capital must be held in liquid assets.

How long does it take to get a Malta crypto fund license?

The MFSA typically processes applications within 4 to 8 months. The timeline depends on the complexity of the fund, completeness of the application, and the MFSA's workload. Pre-submission meetings can help speed up the process.

Can I passport a Malta crypto fund to other EU countries?

Yes, if you obtain an AIF license, you can passport the fund to professional investors across the EU under the AIFMD passport. PIFs may also be passported under national private placement regimes, but this is more limited.

What is the tax rate for a Malta crypto fund?

The effective tax rate can be as low as 5% due to Malta's full imputation system. The fund pays 35% corporate tax, but non-resident investors can claim a refund of 6/7 of the tax paid on distributed dividends. Capital gains are generally exempt for non-residents.

Do I need to have physical presence in Malta?

Yes, the MFSA requires substance, including at least two directors resident in Malta or the EEA, a local compliance officer, and a physical office. The fund manager must have its mind and management in Malta.

What is a VFA Agent and do I need one?

A VFA Agent is a licensed person or entity that assists with compliance under the VFA Act. You need a VFA Agent if your fund invests in virtual financial assets. The agent helps with regulatory filings and ensures adherence to AML/CFT rules.

Can I convert an existing fund into a Malta crypto fund?

Yes, it is possible to redomicile an existing fund to Malta or establish a new Maltese fund. The process involves transferring assets and obtaining MFSA approval. Consulting24 can advise on the best approach.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

Ongoing requirements include annual audited financial statements, quarterly regulatory reports, AML/CFT audits, and maintaining substance. The MFSA conducts periodic inspections. You must also appoint a compliance officer and MLRO.

Is Malta a good jurisdiction for a crypto fund compared to Panama?

Malta offers EU passporting and a low effective tax rate, but is more expensive and slower than Panama. Panama costs EUR 6,000 flat with no capital requirement and 0% tax, but no EU passport. Your choice depends on your target market and budget.
