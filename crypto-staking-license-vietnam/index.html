Overview: What Is a Crypto Staking License in Vietnam?

A crypto staking license in Vietnam is not a single formal license but a combination of regulatory approvals and registrations required to operate a staking business. Staking involves locking up cryptocurrencies to support a blockchain network and earn rewards. In Vietnam, the State Bank of Vietnam (SBV) and the Ministry of Finance (MoF) have not issued specific regulations for staking. Instead, activities may fall under existing laws on virtual assets, securities, or investment advisory. As of 2026, there is no dedicated licensing regime, meaning businesses must rely on legal opinions and registrations like an Enterprise Registration Certificate and, if dealing with securities tokens, a securities license. Who Needs a Crypto Staking License in Vietnam?

Any entity that offers staking services to customers in Vietnam likely needs to comply with local regulations. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges that provide staking pools for users.
  • Independent staking-as-a-service providers.
  • DeFi protocols that onboard Vietnamese residents.
  • Funds that stake assets on behalf of investors.

If your business holds customer assets, manages staking rewards, or provides advice on staking strategies, you may be subject to licensing requirements under the Law on Credit Institutions or the Securities Law. License Type & Regulator

As of 2026, Vietnam has no single regulator for crypto staking. The relevant authorities include:

  • State Bank of Vietnam (SBV): Oversees payment services and may classify staking as a payment intermediary if it involves fiat currency.
  • Ministry of Finance (MoF): Regulates securities and investment services. If staking rewards are considered securities, a securities license may be required.
  • Ministry of Information and Communications (MIC): May have oversight if the service involves data processing or telecoms.

Most staking businesses register as a limited liability company or joint stock company under the Enterprise Law. They then apply for an Enterprise Registration Certificate. If they handle customer assets, they may need a license under the Law on Credit Institutions. Capital Requirement

Vietnam does not impose a specific minimum capital requirement for crypto staking businesses. However, the Enterprise Law requires companies to have charter capital sufficient to cover their intended operations. For a limited liability company, the charter capital is declared by the founders and must be adequate for the business scope. In practice, authorities may expect at least EUR 10,000 - 20,000 for a service company. If a securities license is needed, the minimum capital is higher (e.g., EUR 100,000+). Consulting24 advises on setting appropriate capital levels based on your business plan and the risk of being classified as a securities firm.

Tax Treatment

Vietnam taxes corporate income at a standard rate of 20%. For crypto staking, the tax treatment of rewards is unclear. The General Department of Taxation has not issued specific guidance. Generally, income from staking may be treated as:

  • Corporate income: Taxable at 20% if the business is considered to be providing a service.
  • Capital gains: If staking rewards are seen as capital appreciation, they may be taxed as capital gains (subject to 20% corporate rate).
  • Value-added tax (VAT): Staking fees may be subject to 10% VAT if considered a service.

Given the ambiguity, many staking businesses choose to operate from jurisdictions with clear tax rules, such as Panama (territorial tax, no tax on foreign-source income) or Estonia (0% tax on retained profits). Consulting24 can help you compare tax outcomes across jurisdictions.

Allowed Activities

Under current Vietnamese law, the following activities are generally allowed for a registered company, subject to legal opinion:

  • Operating a staking pool for proof-of-stake blockchains (e.g., Ethereum, Solana, Cardano).
  • Offering staking-as-a-service to non-resident clients (if no solicitation in Vietnam).
  • Providing technical staking infrastructure (nodes, validators).
  • Managing staking rewards distribution (if not deemed collective investment).

Prohibited or restricted activities include:

  • Accepting deposits from the public (banking activity).
  • Issuing tokens that qualify as securities without a license.
  • Guaranteeing fixed returns (may be considered a deposit).

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Business structure choice: Decide between a limited liability company (LLC) or joint stock company (JSC). Most foreign founders choose LLC.
  2. Company registration: Submit an application for an Enterprise Registration Certificate to the Department of Planning and Investment. Provide charter capital, business lines, and shareholder details.
  3. Legal opinion: Engage a local law firm to draft an opinion on whether staking is permissible and whether any additional licenses are needed.
  4. AML/KYC program: Implement a compliance program aligned with Vietnam's AML law and FATF recommendations.
  5. Bank account: Open a corporate bank account in Vietnam or a jurisdiction like Panama for international operations.
  6. Tax registration: Register for corporate income tax and VAT with the tax authority.
  7. Ongoing compliance: File annual reports, tax returns, and maintain records of staking transactions.

Banking & Payments

Opening a bank account for a crypto staking business in Vietnam is challenging. Most local banks are cautious about crypto-related activities due to the SBV's stance that cryptocurrencies are not legal tender. However, some banks may accept accounts for non-crypto operational expenses (rent, salaries) if the business does not process crypto transactions through the bank. For crypto-to-fiat conversions, many firms use third-party payment processors or offshore accounts. Benefits of a Crypto Staking License in Vietnam

  • Growing market: Vietnam has a high crypto adoption rate, providing a large user base for staking services.
  • Low initial costs: Company registration is inexpensive compared to EU jurisdictions.
  • Skilled workforce: Access to technical talent for blockchain development.

Compliance & Trust

To build trust with customers and regulators, your staking business must demonstrate strong compliance. Key elements include:

  • Anti-Money Laundering (AML): Implement customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting.
  • Know Your Customer (KYC): Verify the identity of all customers, especially if they are Vietnamese residents.
  • Data protection: Comply with Vietnam's Law on Cybersecurity and Personal Data Protection.
  • Transparent disclosures: Clearly communicate staking rewards, risks, and lock-up periods.

Common Mistakes

Founders often make these errors when entering the Vietnamese market:

  • Assuming no regulation: Believing that because there is no specific license, no compliance is needed. This can lead to fines or closure.
  • Using personal accounts: Mixing business and personal funds for staking operations, which violates AML rules.
  • Ignoring tax obligations: Not registering for VAT or corporate tax, risking penalties.
  • Overpromising returns: Guaranteeing fixed staking yields may be seen as an unlicensed deposit-taking activity.
