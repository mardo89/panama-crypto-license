What Is the Crypto Wallet Custody License in the Philippines?

The crypto wallet custody license in the Philippines is formally called the Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) registration, regulated by the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP). It authorises companies to offer services such as custody, safekeeping, and management of virtual assets on behalf of clients. This includes hot and cold wallet storage, private key management, and transaction facilitation.

Unlike some jurisdictions that separate exchange and custody licenses, the BSP covers both under the VASP umbrella. However, for pure custody services, the application focuses on security, operational resilience, and client asset segregation. The license is mandatory for any entity providing custodial wallet services to Philippine residents or operating from the Philippines.

A key distinction is that the BSP treats virtual assets as a form of payment and not as securities, which falls under the SEC's purview. This means custody services are primarily regulated for financial stability and consumer protection, not investment product rules. The VASP registration also requires adherence to the BSP's digital payment framework, ensuring interoperability with the national payment system.

Who Needs This License?

Any business that provides custodial wallet services for cryptocurrencies or other virtual assets in the Philippines must obtain a VASP license. This includes:

  • Crypto wallet providers offering hosted wallets (e.g., multi-currency wallets for retail users)
  • Exchanges that also offer custody services (e.g., Binance-style platforms with wallet features)
  • Institutional custodians serving funds and asset managers (e.g., Fireblocks-like services for hedge funds)
  • Fintech companies integrating crypto storage into their apps (e.g., GCash adding crypto wallets)

Even if your company is based abroad, if you target Philippine clients or have a local office, the BSP requires registration. For example, a Singapore-based custodian offering services to OFWs (overseas Filipino workers) would need a VASP license. License Type and Regulator

The Philippines crypto wallet custody license is a VASP registration under the BSP, governed by Circular No. 1108 (2021) and subsequent amendments. The BSP is the central bank and primary financial regulator. Unlike the SEC's oversight of securities tokens, the BSP focuses on payment and custody services for virtual assets.

As of 2026, the BSP has not introduced a separate custody license; the VASP registration covers both exchange and custody. However, the BSP may impose additional conditions for custodians, such as higher capital or insurance requirements. For instance, custodians must maintain a minimum of PHP 50 million capital, while pure exchanges may have a lower threshold (PHP 25 million). The BSP also requires custodians to have a qualified IT security officer and a business continuity plan.

Capital Requirement

The BSP imposes a minimum capital requirement for VASP applicants. As of 2026, the minimum paid-up capital is PHP 50 million (approximately USD 900,000) for companies offering custody services. This is a significant threshold, reflecting the BSP's focus on financial stability and consumer protection.

Capital must be maintained in liquid assets and cannot be withdrawn without BSP approval. For foreign-owned entities, additional capital may be required under the Foreign Investment Act. For example, if the foreign ownership exceeds 40%, the capital must be at least PHP 100 million (approx. USD 1.8 million). It is important to note that the capital must be deposited in a Philippine bank and evidenced by a certificate of deposit. The BSP may also require a surety bond or insurance policy for custodial services, adding to the capital outlay.

Tax Treatment

Philippines tax treatment for crypto custody services is evolving. Generally, income from custody fees is subject to corporate income tax at 25% (or 20% for small businesses with net taxable income under PHP 5 million and total assets under PHP 100 million). VAT at 12% applies to services rendered in the Philippines.

Virtual asset transactions may also be subject to capital gains tax if the BSP classifies them as securities. However, pure custody fees are typically treated as ordinary service income. The Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) has issued guidelines requiring crypto businesses to report all transactions, and failure to do so can result in penalties.

Consulting24 recommends engaging a local tax advisor for specific obligations. For example, if you hold client assets in cold storage, the tax treatment of the underlying assets may differ from the custody fee. Transfer pricing rules also apply if the Philippine entity transacts with related parties abroad.

Allowed Activities

Under the VASP license, custodians can perform the following activities:

  • Safekeeping and administration of virtual assets (including multi-signature wallets)
  • Private key generation and management (with hardware security modules)
  • Transaction settlement and clearing (on-chain or off-chain)
  • Cold and hot wallet storage (with defined security protocols)
  • Client asset segregation and reporting (monthly statements)

Activities not allowed include lending client assets without explicit consent, operating a crypto exchange without separate registration (though same VASP covers both), and offering unregistered securities. The BSP prohibits custodians from commingling client assets with corporate funds. Additionally, custodians cannot engage in proprietary trading using client assets.

Some custodians may also offer staking services, but this requires additional BSP approval as it involves generating yield on client assets. Consulting24 can advise on the scope of activities and any supplementary licenses needed.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining the Philippines crypto wallet custody license involves several stages:

  1. Pre-incorporation: Choose a company structure (domestic or foreign-owned) and register with the SEC. This includes drafting articles of incorporation and bylaws.
  2. AML/CTF program: Develop a compliant program with policies, risk assessment, and reporting procedures. Must include a designated AML officer.
  3. Capital deposit: Deposit the minimum paid-up capital in a Philippine bank and obtain a certificate of deposit.
  4. Submit application: File with the BSP including business plan, financial statements, AML program, and organizational chart. The BSP charges a non-refundable filing fee of PHP 100,000 (approx. USD 1,800).
  5. BSP review: The BSP evaluates the application, may request additional information, and conducts an on-site inspection of premises and IT systems.
  6. Approval: If approved, the BSP issues a Certificate of Registration as a VASP. The license is valid indefinitely but subject to annual renewal.

Compliance and Trust

Compliance is ongoing. VASPs must submit regular reports to the BSP, including transaction monitoring, suspicious activity reports (SARs), and audited financial statements. The BSP conducts periodic examinations, typically every 1-2 years. Non-compliance can result in fines up to PHP 1 million per violation or license revocation.

General guidance: Implement strong KYC/AML procedures, maintain segregated client accounts, and ensure cybersecurity measures such as multi-factor authentication and encryption. The BSP also requires custodians to have a business continuity plan and disaster recovery site.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Applicants often make these errors:

  • Underestimating capital requirements: The PHP 50 million minimum is strict and must be fully paid up before application.
  • Incomplete AML program: Must be tailored to the business model, not a generic template. Include transaction monitoring tools and SAR procedures.
  • Ignoring local director requirements: At least one resident director may be needed, and the AML officer must be a Philippine resident.
  • Poor documentation: Business plans must be detailed, including financial projections, risk management, and IT security architecture.

Alternatives and Comparison

If the Philippines capital requirement is too high, consider alternatives:

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto wallet custody license in the Philippines?

The BSP requires a minimum paid-up capital of PHP 50 million (approx. USD 900,000) for VASP applicants offering custody services. This must be maintained in liquid assets and deposited in a Philippine bank. Foreign-owned entities may need higher capital under the Foreign Investment Act.

How long does it take to get a Philippines VASP license?

The BSP processing time is typically 6-12 months after submission of complete documents. Preparation of requirements takes an additional 2-4 months. The total timeline from start to approval is usually 8-16 months, depending on the complexity of the application and BSP workload.

Can a foreign-owned company apply for a Philippines crypto custody license?

Yes, but foreign ownership may require additional capital under the Foreign Investment Act. At least one resident director is often required, and the AML officer must be a Philippine resident. Consulting24 advises on structuring to meet these requirements.

What activities are allowed under the VASP license?

Custody, safekeeping, private key management, transaction settlement, and client asset segregation. Lending client assets without consent is prohibited. Staking may require additional approval. The license also covers exchange services if applicable.

Is there a separate license for crypto wallet custody vs exchange?

No, the BSP's VASP registration covers both exchange and custody services. However, the BSP may impose additional conditions for custodians, such as higher capital or insurance requirements. Pure custodians must still register as VASPs.

What are the tax rates for crypto custody services in the Philippines?

Corporate income tax is 25% (20% for small businesses with net taxable income under PHP 5 million and assets under PHP 100 million). VAT at 12% applies to services. Pure custody fees are treated as ordinary service income. Consult a local tax advisor for specifics.

Can I use a Panama company to offer custody services in the Philippines?

A Panama company does not automatically allow you to offer custody services to Philippine residents. The BSP requires any entity targeting Philippine clients to register as a VASP. Panama is suitable for non-Philippine clients or B2B services. Consulting24 can advise on the best structure.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements after obtaining the license?

VASPs must submit quarterly transaction reports, annual audited financial statements, and suspicious activity reports. The BSP conducts periodic examinations. Non-compliance can result in fines or license revocation. Ongoing costs include compliance officer salary and audit fees.

Is the Philippines VASP license recognized in other ASEAN countries?

No, the VASP license is specific to the Philippines. However, the BSP is a member of the ASEAN Financial Innovation Network, which promotes interoperability. For cross-border operations, you may need additional licenses in each country. Consulting24 can advise on multi-jurisdictional strategies.

What happens if I operate without a VASP license?

Operating without a license is illegal and can result in criminal penalties, including fines up to PHP 5 million and imprisonment. The BSP actively monitors unlicensed VASPs and has shut down several platforms. It is essential to obtain the license before offering services.
