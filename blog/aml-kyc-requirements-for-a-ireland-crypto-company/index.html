What Are AML/KYC Requirements for an Ireland Crypto Company?

AML (Anti-Money Laundering) and KYC (Know Your Customer) requirements are regulatory obligations designed to prevent financial crime. For an Ireland crypto company, these requirements involve verifying customer identities, monitoring transactions, and reporting suspicious activities to the authorities. The Central Bank of Ireland enforces these rules under the Criminal Justice (Money Laundering and Terrorist Financing) Acts 2010-2021, as amended, and MiCA.

Key components include:

  • Customer due diligence (CDD) including identity verification and beneficial ownership checks.
  • Ongoing monitoring of transactions for unusual patterns.
  • Risk-based approach: assessing and mitigating AML/CFT risks.
  • Appointment of a designated person (MLRO) for reporting.
  • Maintaining records for at least five years.

These requirements apply to all CASPs registered in Ireland, including exchanges, wallet providers, and custodian services. For example, a crypto exchange must verify each customer's identity before allowing trades, screen against sanctions lists, and monitor for high-risk transactions such as large transfers from high-risk jurisdictions. The AML/KYC requirements for an Ireland crypto company are not just a one-time setup; they require ongoing compliance, including periodic reviews and updates to risk assessments. A practical example: if a customer deposits over EUR 10,000 in crypto, the exchange must perform enhanced due diligence (EDD) and verify the source of funds. Similarly, transactions involving unhosted wallets (private wallets) may trigger additional scrutiny under MiCA's travel rule. The Central Bank expects CASPs to have automated monitoring systems that flag transactions above certain thresholds or from sanctioned countries. For instance, if a customer in Iran tries to trade, the system must block the transaction and file a suspicious transaction report (STR) within 24 hours. Failure to comply can result in fines up to EUR 1 million or 10% of annual turnover, whichever is higher, under the Criminal Justice Act.

License Type and Regulator

The license for an Ireland crypto company is a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) registration, now superseded by the MiCA CASP authorization. The Central Bank of Ireland is the competent authority. Under MiCA, CASPs must comply with capital requirements (EUR 50,000 to EUR 150,000 depending on services), governance, and AML/CFT obligations.

There is no separate 'crypto license' per se; it is an authorization to operate as a CASP. The AML/KYC requirements for an Ireland crypto company are embedded in the authorization process. The regulator conducts a thorough assessment of the applicant's policies, procedures, and personnel. For instance, the Central Bank will review your AML policy document, risk assessment methodology, and the qualifications of your MLRO. The MLRO must be a senior employee with at least three years of AML experience and must be based in Ireland. The Central Bank also requires a board-level director to be responsible for compliance. Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the capital requirement for a CASP in Ireland depends on the services offered:

  • EUR 50,000 for simple services (e.g., custody of crypto assets).
  • EUR 125,000 for exchange services (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto).
  • EUR 150,000 for more complex services (e.g., trading platforms).

This capital must be held as own funds and is separate from operational costs. The AML/KYC requirements for an Ireland crypto company do not directly affect capital, but the regulator will assess the adequacy of capital for AML/CFT compliance. For example, if you plan to offer custody services, you need EUR 50,000 in capital, but you also need to budget for AML software and personnel. For comparison, Panama has no minimum capital requirement, making it attractive for startups. Consulting24 can help you choose the right jurisdiction based on your capital availability. If you have limited capital (under EUR 50,000), Panama or Estonia (which has no minimum capital for the license itself, but requires a bank account) may be more suitable. If you have EUR 150,000 or more, Ireland's EU passporting may justify the cost.

Tax Treatment

Ireland's corporate tax rate is 12.5% for trading income, but crypto activities may be treated differently. The Revenue Commissioners (Irish tax authority) classify crypto as intangible assets, subject to capital gains tax (33%) on disposals. VAT is not applicable on crypto-to-fiat exchanges, but services may be subject to 23% VAT.

The AML/KYC requirements for an Ireland crypto company do not directly affect tax, but proper record-keeping is essential for tax compliance. For instance, you must maintain records of all transactions for at least five years, which also supports tax audits. Consulting24 advises on structuring to optimize tax efficiency. For jurisdictions with simpler tax regimes, consider Panama (territorial tax, no capital gains on foreign income). In Panama, if your crypto income is earned outside Panama, it is tax-free. In Ireland, even if your customers are outside Ireland, you may still be subject to Irish tax if your company is tax resident there. A common strategy is to incorporate in Ireland for EU presence but manage tax residency elsewhere, but this requires careful planning. Allowed Activities

Under MiCA, an Ireland crypto company can perform the following activities once authorized:

  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients.
  • Operation of a trading platform for crypto assets.
  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency or other crypto assets.
  • Execution of orders for crypto assets on behalf of clients.
  • Placing of crypto assets.
  • Receiving and transmitting orders for crypto assets.
  • Providing advice on crypto assets.
  • Portfolio management of crypto assets.

All activities must comply with AML/KYC requirements for an Ireland crypto company, including transaction monitoring and reporting. For example, if you operate a trading platform, you must monitor for wash trading or market manipulation and report suspicious activity to the Central Bank. Additionally, under MiCA's travel rule, you must collect and share information about the originator and beneficiary of crypto transfers exceeding EUR 1,000. This means your platform must have systems to capture and transmit this data to counterparty CASPs. If you offer custody, you must also segregate client assets from your own corporate assets and maintain insurance or a similar guarantee. The Central Bank may also impose activity-specific conditions, such as limits on use for derivatives exchanges.

Step-by-Step Process to Obtain Authorization

  1. Pre-application preparation: Develop AML/KYC policies, risk assessment, and business plan. Engage legal counsel. This step typically takes 2-3 months. You must also appoint an MLRO and a board member responsible for compliance.
  2. Company formation: Incorporate a private limited company (LTD) with the Companies Registration Office. You need a registered office in Ireland and at least one director (can be non-resident). The company must have a minimum of two directors if the sole director is not an EU resident.
  3. Submit application: File with the Central Bank of Ireland, including detailed documentation on AML/KYC procedures, governance, and capital. The application fee is non-refundable. Prepare for a pre-application meeting where the Central Bank will ask about your business model and compliance plans.
  4. Regulatory review: The Central Bank assesses the application, may request additional information, and conducts interviews with key personnel. This can take 3-6 months. During this period, you may need to provide evidence of your AML software implementation and staff training.
  5. Authorisation: If approved, you receive CASP status. Ongoing supervision includes periodic AML/CFT audits and annual reporting. You must submit an annual AML/CFT report to the Central Bank and undergo on-site inspections every 2-3 years.

Banking and Payments

Opening a bank account for an Ireland crypto company is challenging due to de-risking by traditional banks. Many Irish banks are cautious about crypto clients. Alternatives include:

  • EMI (Electronic Money Institution) accounts in Ireland or Lithuania.
  • Payment service providers like Revolut Business or Airwallex.
  • Crypto-friendly banks in other EU countries, such as those in Lithuania or Estonia.

The AML/KYC requirements for an Ireland crypto company extend to banking partners, who will conduct their own due diligence. For example, a bank may require proof of your CASP authorization and your AML policy before opening an account. Some banks may also ask for a minimum deposit of EUR 10,000 and a business plan. In Panama, banking is also relatively straightforward: many international banks accept crypto companies, and there is no requirement for a local bank account if you use an EMI. However, for Ireland, expect to spend 2-4 months just to secure a banking relationship. A common workaround is to use a Lithuanian EMI for fiat handling while maintaining an Irish registered office for regulatory compliance.

Compliance and Trust

Compliance with AML/KYC requirements for an Ireland crypto company builds trust with customers and partners. The Central Bank expects a risk-based approach, including:

  • Customer identification and verification (CDD).
  • Beneficial ownership checks.
  • Transaction monitoring for suspicious activity.
  • Ongoing due diligence on high-risk customers.
  • Annual AML/CFT audit by an external auditor.

For example, if you have customers from high-risk third countries (e.g., Iran, North Korea), you must apply enhanced due diligence, including verifying the source of funds and obtaining senior management approval for the business relationship. The Central Bank also requires you to screen all customers against EU sanctions lists and politically exposed persons (PEPs) databases. Failure to do so can result in fines and reputational damage. Consulting24 can help you design a compliance framework that meets these standards. We recommend using automated AML software like Chainalysis or Elliptic for transaction monitoring, and identity verification providers like Onfido or Jumio for CDD. The cost of these tools is typically EUR 5,000-20,000 per year, depending on transaction volume. For smaller companies, manual monitoring may be acceptable initially, but the Central Bank expects a scalable solution as you grow.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make these mistakes when addressing AML/KYC requirements for an Ireland crypto company:

  • Underestimating the timeline: Many expect approval in 3 months, but 6-12 months is typical. Plan your launch accordingly.
  • Inadequate AML policies: Copying generic templates without tailoring to your business model. The Central Bank expects a specific risk assessment for your services and customer base.
  • Ignoring the travel rule: Under MiCA, you must implement systems to collect and transmit originator/beneficiary information for transfers over EUR 1,000. This is often overlooked until the last minute.
  • Not appointing a local MLRO: The MLRO must be based in Ireland and have relevant experience. Outsourcing to a non-resident is not accepted.
  • Insufficient capital: Some applicants try to meet the minimum capital but forget to budget for operational costs. The Central Bank will assess whether you have enough funds to run the business for at least 12 months.
  • Choosing the wrong jurisdiction: Ireland is not for everyone. If your budget is under EUR 50,000, consider Panama or Lithuania instead. Consulting24 can help you avoid these pitfalls.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for an Ireland crypto company under MiCA?

The minimum capital depends on the services offered: EUR 50,000 for custody, EUR 125,000 for exchange, and EUR 150,000 for trading platforms. This capital must be held as own funds and is separate from operational costs.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Ireland?

Typically 6 to 12 months from application to approval. The timeline depends on the completeness of your application and the Central Bank's workload. Pre-application preparation can add 2-3 months.

Can I operate an Ireland crypto company without a physical office?

You need a registered office in Ireland, but it can be a virtual office provided by a service provider. However, the Central Bank expects a substantive presence, including a local MLRO and compliance team.

What are the AML/KYC requirements for an Ireland crypto company?

You must implement customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, suspicious activity reporting, and record-keeping. You need an AML policy, a risk assessment, and an MLRO. The requirements align with MiCA and 5AMLD.

Is Ireland a good jurisdiction for a small crypto startup?

Ireland is better suited for well-funded startups due to high costs (EUR 28,000-83,000) and capital requirements (EUR 50,000-150,000). Smaller startups may prefer Panama (EUR 6,000 flat) or Lithuania.

Do I need to pay tax on crypto gains in Ireland?

Yes, crypto gains are subject to capital gains tax at 33% for individuals and 12.5% corporate tax for companies on trading income. VAT may apply to services at 23%.

Can Consulting24 guarantee approval for an Ireland crypto license?

No, we cannot guarantee approval. The Central Bank makes the final decision. We help prepare a strong application to maximize your chances, based on our experience with 500+ licenses.

What happens if I fail to comply with AML/KYC requirements in Ireland?

Non-compliance can result in fines up to EUR 1 million or 10% of annual turnover, suspension of license, or criminal prosecution. The Central Bank conducts periodic audits and on-site inspections.

Can I use a non-Irish bank account for my Ireland crypto company?

Yes, you can use an EMI or bank in another EU country, but you must have a local bank account for regulatory fees and some operational expenses. Many companies use Lithuanian EMIs for fiat handling.

What activities are allowed under an Ireland CASP license?

Allowed activities include custody, exchange, trading platform operation, order execution, advice, portfolio management, and transfer services. All must comply with MiCA and AML/KYC rules.
