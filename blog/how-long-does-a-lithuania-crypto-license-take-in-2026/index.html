What Is the Lithuania Crypto License?

In 2026, the Lithuania crypto license is a Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) authorisation under the Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) regulation, which is fully in force across the European Union. It replaces the previous virtual currency exchange operator and depository wallet operator licences. The Bank of Lithuania is the competent authority responsible for licensing and supervision.

This license allows companies to offer services such as crypto exchange, custody, transfer, and advisory within Lithuania and across the EU under the single passport. The regime requires a physical presence in Lithuania, including a registered office and local management. Minimum capital requirements start at EUR 50,000 for basic services and go up to EUR 150,000 for more complex offerings.

MiCA harmonises rules across the EU, meaning a Lithuania CASP license is recognised in all 27 member states. This is a major advantage for founders targeting the European market. However, the application process is rigorous, requiring detailed documentation on AML/CFT policies, governance, and financial soundness. License Type and Regulator

The license is a single Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) authorisation under MiCA, issued by the Bank of Lithuania (Lietuvos bankas). There is no separate exchange or wallet licence; one licence covers all CASP activities you register for. The Bank of Lithuania is known for its thorough but fair approach, focusing on AML/CFT compliance, governance, and financial soundness.

Under MiCA, the license allows passporting to all EU/EEA member states, making it a gateway to a market of over 450 million people. The regulator expects applicants to have a clear business plan, strong internal controls, and a local presence. The application process includes background checks on shareholders and management, and the regulator may request an on-site inspection.

It is important to note that the Bank of Lithuania does not issue licenses for crypto derivatives or securities tokens, which fall under MiFID II. If your business involves such products, you may need a different authorisation. Consulting24 can advise on the appropriate license structure for your specific offerings.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for a CASP in Lithuania depends on the types of services offered:

  • EUR 50,000 for basic services (e.g., execution of orders, placement of crypto assets)
  • EUR 125,000 for services involving custody and administration of crypto assets
  • EUR 150,000 for services that combine custody with trading or other higher-risk activities

This capital must be maintained as own funds and cannot be used for operational expenses. Proof of capital is required at application and must be held in a Lithuanian bank account. Consulting24 can help you open a bank account in Lithuania or recommend alternatives like EMI accounts.

It is important to note that the capital requirement is a minimum; the regulator may require higher capital based on your business plan and risk profile. Tax Treatment

Lithuania offers a competitive tax environment for crypto businesses. The corporate income tax rate is 15%, but a reduced 5% rate applies for small companies (up to EUR 300,000 revenue and fewer than 10 employees). Crypto-to-crypto trades are generally exempt from VAT. However, profits from crypto trading are subject to corporate income tax.

Personal income tax on crypto gains for individuals is 15%. Lithuania does not impose withholding tax on dividends paid to non-residents, making it attractive for international founders. Always consult a tax advisor for your specific situation.

Lithuania also has a favourable tax treatment for capital gains on crypto assets held for more than one year, which may be exempt from tax under certain conditions. The tax authority (VMI) has issued guidance on crypto taxation, which is generally business-friendly. Consulting24 can connect you with local tax advisors who specialise in crypto.

Allowed Activities Under the License

With a Lithuania CASP license, you can perform the following activities as defined by MiCA:

  • Providing custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Operating a trading platform for crypto assets
  • Exchanging crypto assets for fiat currency
  • Exchanging crypto assets for other crypto assets
  • Executing orders for crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Placing crypto assets
  • Receiving and transmitting orders for crypto assets
  • Providing advice on crypto assets
  • Managing crypto asset portfolios
  • Transferring crypto assets on behalf of clients

You must specify which activities you intend to offer in your application. The license does not cover derivatives or securities tokens, which fall under MiFID II. If your business involves such products, you may need a separate license or a different jurisdiction.

It is also possible to offer ancillary services such as staking or lending, but these may require additional permissions under MiCA or other regulations. Consulting24 can help you define your service scope to ensure full compliance.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a Lithuania crypto license in 2026 involves these steps:

  1. Company incorporation in Lithuania with a registered office and local director.
  2. AML/KYC policy development tailored to your business model, including risk assessment and transaction monitoring.
  3. Preparation of business plan, financial projections, and risk management framework.
  4. Submission of application to the Bank of Lithuania via their electronic system, including all supporting documents.
  5. Review and due diligence by the regulator, including background checks on shareholders and management.
  6. Possible requests for additional information or clarifications, which can extend the timeline.
  7. On-site inspection (if required) by the Bank of Lithuania to verify physical presence and systems.
  8. License issuance after approval, followed by ongoing supervision.

  • Appoint a local AML compliance officer.
  • Undergo periodic audits and inspections by the regulator.
  • Report any changes in management, ownership, or business activities.
