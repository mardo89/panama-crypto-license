Choosing Between Direct Delivery and Advise-and-Coordinate for Your Crypto Licence

By Mardo Soo, Founder & CEO, Consulting24 (X24Consulting OÜ) · Updated 2026-07-21

Short answer: Compare direct delivery vs advise-and-coordinate for your crypto licence. Consulting24 delivers in Estonia, Lithuania, Panama and coordinates globally.

When you decide to obtain a crypto licence, one of the first strategic choices is whether to work with a firm that delivers the licence directly in your target jurisdiction or one that advises and coordinates with local partners. Consulting24 offers both models: direct delivery in Panama, Estonia, and Lithuania, and advise-and-coordinate services for all other jurisdictions. This page helps you evaluate which approach suits your project, timeline, and budget.

Direct delivery means we handle the entire application process internally, from company setup to regulatory submission, using our in-house team and established relationships. Advise-and-coordinate means we guide you through the requirements, connect you with vetted local lawyers and agents, and oversee the process, but the actual filings are executed by third parties. Both models have distinct advantages, and your choice depends on factors like jurisdiction complexity, your internal resources, and risk tolerance.

This comparison is essential for founders who want to avoid costly mistakes. A wrong choice can lead to delays, unexpected costs, or even rejection. We have seen projects where a founder chose a jurisdiction solely based on low headline fees, only to discover that banking was impossible or the licence scope was too narrow. By understanding the trade-offs, you can make an informed decision.

What Is Direct Delivery vs Advise-and-Coordinate?

Direct delivery is a full-service model where the consulting firm takes end-to-end responsibility for obtaining your crypto licence. In our case, we deliver directly in Estonia, Lithuania, and Panama. This means we incorporate your company, prepare all documentation, submit to the regulator, and manage communications until the licence is granted. You have a single point of contact and a fixed price. For example, a Panama licence costs a flat EUR 6,000 with no hidden fees, and we handle everything from company registration to regulatory approval.

Advise-and-coordinate is a hybrid model where we act as your strategic advisor. We assess your business model, recommend the best jurisdiction, and create a roadmap. Then we coordinate with local legal and compliance partners who handle the actual application. This model is ideal for jurisdictions where we do not have a direct delivery setup, such as Dubai, Malta, Switzerland, or the Cayman Islands. You benefit from our expertise without us being physically present. For instance, if you need a VARA licence in Dubai, we will connect you with a licensed local agent, review their work, and ensure the application meets regulatory standards.

Both models have their place. Direct delivery is faster and more predictable, while advise-and-coordinate offers access to a wider range of jurisdictions. The key is to match the model to your specific needs.

License Type & Regulator

For direct delivery jurisdictions, the licence types are well-defined. In Estonia, the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) issues a Virtual Currency Service Provider licence. In Lithuania, the Bank of Lithuania grants a CASP licence under MiCA. In Panama, the regulator is the Superintendencia de Bancos de Panamá (SBP) for the Crypto Asset Service Provider licence. Each regulator has specific requirements for AML policies, corporate governance, and financial soundness.

For advise-and-coordinate jurisdictions, each has its own regulator and licence type. For example, the Dubai Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA) issues a VASP licence, while the Malta Financial Services Authority (MFSA) grants a Class 3 CASP licence. Switzerland's FINMA issues a fintech licence or a banking licence depending on the activity. We help you navigate these differences and select the right licence for your activities. For instance, if you plan to offer custody services, we will guide you to jurisdictions that explicitly allow it under their licence scope.

Understanding the regulator's expectations is critical. In direct delivery, we know exactly what documents they require and how to present them. In advise-and-coordinate, we rely on local partners who have direct experience with the regulator.

Capital Requirement

Capital requirements depend on the jurisdiction and licence class. Under MiCA, which is fully in force in 2026, EU member states apply capital tiers: EUR 50,000 for basic services (e.g., transfer services), EUR 125,000 for custody, and EUR 150,000 for exchange. Lithuania follows this exactly. Estonia also aligns with MiCA but may have additional own funds requirements, typically EUR 50,000-125,000 depending on the service.

Panama has no statutory minimum capital for crypto licences, but the regulator expects sufficient financial resources to cover operational costs. Typically, we recommend at least EUR 10,000 in paid-up capital. This makes Panama attractive for startups with limited funds. For advise-and-coordinate jurisdictions, requirements vary widely: Dubai VARA requires AED 100,000-500,000 (approx. EUR 25,000-125,000) depending on the category, while Malta requires EUR 125,000 for a Class 3 licence. Switzerland has no fixed minimum but expects adequate capitalisation, often EUR 100,000+.

Capital must be held in a bank account and may be locked for a period. We help you structure this efficiently.

Tax Treatment

Tax treatment is a critical factor. Panama offers territorial taxation: income sourced outside Panama is tax-free, and there is no VAT on crypto services. Corporate tax is 25% only on Panama-source income, but most crypto businesses can structure to minimise local tax. For example, if your customers are outside Panama, your profits are tax-free. This is a major advantage over EU jurisdictions.

Estonia has a unique corporate income tax system: profits are taxed only when distributed (20% on dividends). This allows you to reinvest profits tax-free. Lithuania has a standard 15% corporate tax, with potential exemptions for small enterprises (0% for the first year if revenue under EUR 300,000). For advise-and-coordinate jurisdictions, tax regimes differ: Switzerland has cantonal tax rates from 12-20%, Malta has 5% effective tax after imputation, and Dubai has 0% corporate tax for qualifying activities. However, you must consider substance requirements and economic presence.

We recommend consulting a tax advisor for your specific situation, as tax laws are complex and subject to change.

Allowed Activities

The scope of allowed activities depends on the licence. In Panama, the Crypto Asset Service Provider licence covers exchange between crypto and fiat, crypto-to-crypto exchange, custody, and transfer services. It does not cover derivatives or investment advice unless separately licensed. For example, if you plan to offer staking or lending, you need to check if these fall under custody or require additional authorisation.

In Lithuania and Estonia, MiCA-compliant licences cover a broad range: operating a trading platform, exchanging crypto for fiat, custody, and transfer services. Under MiCA, you can also provide advice and portfolio management with additional authorisation. For advise-and-coordinate jurisdictions, always verify the exact scope with local regulators. For instance, a Dubai VASP licence covers exchange, custody, and transfer, but not derivatives unless you obtain a separate category.

We help you map your business activities to the licence scope to ensure full compliance.

Step-by-Step Process: Direct Delivery vs Advise-and-Coordinate

Direct Delivery (e.g., Panama):

  1. Initial consultation and business model assessment. We identify the right licence type and required documents.
  2. Company incorporation in Panama (we handle all paperwork, including registration with the Public Registry and obtaining a tax ID).
  3. Preparation of compliance documents: AML/KYC policies, business plan, risk assessment, and internal procedures.
  4. Submission to the Superintendencia de Bancos de Panamá. We file the application and pay the fee.
  5. Regulatory review and potential clarifications (we manage communication and respond to queries within 24 hours).
  6. Licence issuance and post-licence compliance support, including ongoing AML reporting.

Advise-and-Coordinate (e.g., Dubai):

  1. Strategic advisory: jurisdiction selection, licence type, timeline, and cost estimate.
  2. Introduction to local legal partner in Dubai. We vet them for quality and reliability.
  3. We review and refine the application prepared by the local partner, ensuring it meets VARA standards.
  4. We oversee the submission to VARA and provide ongoing guidance on regulatory expectations.
  5. Local partner handles any regulatory interviews or site visits. We prepare you for these.
  6. Licence granted; we assist with ongoing compliance coordination, such as annual audits and AML reporting.

In both models, we provide a dedicated project manager who updates you weekly.

For advise-and-coordinate jurisdictions, banking is handled by the local partner. For example, in Dubai, we coordinate with banks like Mashreq and RAKBANK that understand VARA licences. In Switzerland, cantonal banks like Zürcher Kantonalbank are often crypto-friendly. We cannot guarantee account opening, but we significantly de-risk the process by preparing a strong application and using our relationships.

We recommend starting the banking process early, as it can take 4-8 weeks.

Common Mistakes

Founders often make these mistakes when choosing between models:

  • Choosing a jurisdiction solely based on cost without considering banking or tax. For example, a cheap licence in Estonia may have high ongoing compliance costs.
  • Assuming advise-and-coordinate is cheaper - local partner fees can add up to EUR 5,000-15,000.
  • Underestimating the time needed for advise-and-coordinate due to coordination overhead. A Dubai licence can take 6 months.
  • Not checking whether the consulting firm has direct delivery in the target jurisdiction. Some firms claim to deliver everywhere but outsource everything.
  • Ignoring post-licence compliance costs. In Lithuania, annual AML audits can cost EUR 3,000-5,000.

To avoid these, always request a detailed breakdown of costs and timelines before committing. We provide a free consultation to discuss your specific needs.
