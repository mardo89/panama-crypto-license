What Is a Crypto License in Slovakia?

A crypto license in Slovakia is an authorization from the National Bank of Slovakia (NBS) to provide crypto-asset services under MiCA. It allows companies to operate legally within the EU, offering services such as exchange, custody, and wallet provision. The license is mandatory for any business that handles crypto assets for clients in Slovakia or across the EU.

Slovakia's regulatory framework is now fully aligned with MiCA, meaning that license holders can passport their services to other EU member states. This makes Slovakia an attractive hub for crypto businesses seeking a single license with EU-wide reach. The cost of obtaining this license is a key factor in choosing Slovakia over other jurisdictions.

Who Needs a Crypto License in Slovakia?

Any company that provides crypto-asset services to Slovak residents or operates from Slovakia must obtain a license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (buying/selling crypto for fiat or other crypto)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Brokers and dealers in crypto assets
  • Platforms offering crypto lending or staking
  • Payment services using crypto

If your business involves any of these activities, you need to apply for a license with the NBS. Failure to do so can result in fines, legal action, and reputational damage. Even if you are based outside Slovakia but target Slovak customers, you may need to comply with MiCA regulations.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator for crypto licenses in Slovakia is the National Bank of Slovakia (NBS). Under MiCA, there is a single license type for crypto-asset service providers (CASPs). This license covers all services defined in MiCA, including exchange, custody, and transfer services. There are no separate categories; instead, the license scope depends on the services you register for.

The NBS is responsible for supervising compliance with MiCA, including anti-money laundering (AML) obligations, capital requirements, and consumer protection. The application process involves submitting detailed documentation, including business plans, AML policies, and governance structures. The NBS has up to 6 months to review applications, though the timeline can vary.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the capital requirement for crypto asset service providers in Slovakia is tiered based on the services offered. The minimum capital must be maintained at all times and can be in the form of equity, reserves, or a combination. The tiers are:

  • Tier 1: EUR 125,000 for services including exchange, custody, and transfer of crypto assets.
  • Tier 2: EUR 50,000 for services such as advisory, portfolio management, and transfer of crypto assets without custody.
  • Tier 3: EUR 150,000 for services that involve both exchange and custody (full-service platforms).

These capital requirements are designed to ensure financial stability and protect clients. The capital must be held in a bank account in Slovakia or another EU member state, and you must provide proof of capital as part of the application. Tax Treatment of Crypto in Slovakia

Slovakia has a favorable tax regime for crypto businesses. Corporate income tax is a flat 21% on profits. Capital gains from crypto trading are subject to the same rate, but there are exemptions for long-term holdings (held for more than 1 year) if certain conditions are met. VAT is not applicable to crypto transactions as they are considered exempt financial services.

It is important to note that tax laws can change, and you should consult with a local tax advisor. Slovakia also has double taxation treaties with many countries, which can reduce withholding taxes on cross-border payments. For a comprehensive tax strategy, contact Consulting24 for a consultation.

Allowed Activities Under the License

A Slovak crypto license allows you to conduct a wide range of activities as defined by MiCA. These include:

  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets
  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Operation of a trading platform for crypto assets
  • Transfer of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Provision of crypto asset advisory services
  • Portfolio management of crypto assets

You can combine multiple services under one license, but each service must be declared in your application. The NBS may impose additional conditions depending on the scope of your activities. For example, if you offer staking or lending, you may need to comply with additional consumer protection rules.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining a crypto license in Slovakia involves several steps. Here is a typical process:

  1. Pre-application preparation: Define your business model, services, and corporate structure. Prepare a detailed business plan, AML/CFT policies, risk assessment, and governance framework.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a company in Slovakia (or use an existing one) with a registered office and local directors. The company must meet the capital requirements.
  3. Submit application to NBS: File the application with all required documents, including proof of capital, background checks on directors, and AML procedures. Pay the application fee.
  4. Review and due diligence: The NBS reviews the application, conducts background checks, and may request additional information. This stage typically takes 3-5 months.
  5. License issuance: Once approved, you receive a license. You must then comply with ongoing reporting and AML obligations.

Compliance and Trust - Ongoing Obligations

After obtaining the license, you must comply with ongoing requirements to maintain it. These include:

  • Annual AML/CFT audits and reporting to the NBS
  • Maintaining minimum capital at all times
  • Appointing a local AML officer and compliance officer
  • Submitting periodic financial reports and transaction monitoring
  • Updating policies and procedures as regulations evolve

Non-compliance can lead to fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. It is crucial to have a strong compliance framework from day one. Frequently asked questions

What is the total cost of a crypto license in Slovakia in 2026?

The total first-year cost is approximately EUR 152,000 to 180,000, including application fees, legal fees, and minimum capital requirements. Ongoing annual costs are around EUR 10,000 to 20,000 for compliance and reporting.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Slovakia?

The application process typically takes 4 to 6 months from submission to license issuance. The timeline depends on the completeness of your application and the NBS's review workload.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a crypto license in Slovakia?

Under MiCA, the minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for basic services, EUR 125,000 for exchange and custody, and EUR 150,000 for full-service platforms. The capital must be maintained at all times.

Can I passport my Slovak crypto license to other EU countries?

Yes, a Slovak crypto license allows you to provide services across the EU under the MiCA passporting regime. You can serve clients in any EU member state without needing additional licenses.

What activities are allowed under a Slovak crypto license?

Allowed activities include exchange, custody, transfer, advisory, portfolio management, and operation of trading platforms for crypto assets. You must declare the services you intend to offer in your application.

Do I need to have a physical office in Slovakia?

Yes, you need a registered office in Slovakia. The NBS requires a local presence, including a registered address and, in some cases, local directors or compliance officers.

What are the tax rates for crypto businesses in Slovakia?

Corporate income tax is a flat 21% on profits. Crypto transactions are exempt from VAT. Capital gains from crypto may be exempt if held for more than one year, subject to conditions.
