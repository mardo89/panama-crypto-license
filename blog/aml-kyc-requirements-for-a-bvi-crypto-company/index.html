Overview: What Are AML/KYC Requirements for a BVI Crypto Company?

AML (Anti-Money Laundering) and KYC (Know Your Customer) are regulatory measures designed to prevent financial crimes such as money laundering, terrorist financing, and fraud. For a BVI crypto company, these requirements are mandated by the BVI FSC under the Anti-Money Laundering and Terrorist Financing Code of Practice (the Code). The Code applies to all virtual asset service providers (VASPs) licensed in the BVI.

Key components include:

  • Customer due diligence (CDD) - verifying customer identity and beneficial ownership.
  • Enhanced due diligence (EDD) for high-risk customers or transactions.
  • Ongoing monitoring of transactions and business relationships.
  • Record-keeping of all documents and transactions for at least five years.
  • Reporting suspicious transactions to the BVI Financial Investigation Agency (FIA).

These rules are not optional; they form the backbone of your compliance program. Failure to implement them can result in fines, license revocation, or criminal liability. The BVI FSC expects a risk-based approach, meaning your procedures must be proportionate to the size and nature of your business. For example, a small exchange with low transaction volumes may apply simplified due diligence for low-risk customers, while a large platform handling high-value transfers must implement strong transaction monitoring and EDD for all customers.

Who Needs to Comply with AML/KYC in the BVI?

Any entity that holds a BVI crypto license must comply with AML/KYC requirements. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralized and decentralized).
  • Custodial wallet providers.
  • Brokers and dealers in virtual assets.
  • Any business that facilitates the exchange, transfer, or safekeeping of cryptocurrencies.

If you are a founder considering a BVI license, you must appoint a compliance officer, establish written AML policies, and ensure all staff are trained. The BVI FSC expects a risk-based approach, meaning your procedures must be proportionate to the size and nature of your business. Additionally, if your business involves tokenized securities, you may need additional licensing under the Securities and Investment Business Act, which carries its own AML obligations.

License Type and Regulator

The BVI FSC is the sole regulator for crypto activities in the territory. It issues a single type of license: the Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) license. This license covers all crypto-related services, including exchange, transfer, and custody. Unlike some jurisdictions that have multiple license tiers (e.g., Lithuania's CASP under MiCA), BVI offers a unified license.

The regulatory framework is based on the VASP Act of 2022, which was updated in 2024 to align with FATF recommendations. The FSC conducts on-site inspections and reviews compliance reports annually. For a detailed breakdown of the application process, see our application process guide. The FSC also requires that the beneficial owners and directors of the applicant company be fit and proper, meaning they must have no criminal record and demonstrate competence in compliance matters.

Capital Requirement

The BVI FSC does not impose a statutory minimum capital requirement for VASP licenses. However, you must demonstrate sufficient financial resources to operate your business, including adequate liquidity and solvency. The FSC assesses this on a case-by-case basis, considering your business plan, risk profile, and projected transaction volumes.

In practice, most applicants maintain at least EUR 50,000 in capital to cover operational expenses and compliance costs. This is significantly lower than EU jurisdictions, where MiCA mandates minimum capital of EUR 50,000 to EUR 150,000 depending on service type. For a jurisdiction with clear capital rules, see our Lithuania crypto license page. The FSC may also require a security deposit or bond in some cases, though this is not standard. Always confirm with the FSC or your advisor.

Tax Treatment for BVI Crypto Companies

The BVI offers a zero-tax regime for offshore companies. There is no corporate income tax, capital gains tax, or VAT on crypto transactions. However, you must pay an annual license fee to the FSC (typically EUR 1,000 to 3,000) and maintain a registered agent in the BVI.

This tax neutrality makes the BVI attractive for crypto businesses that primarily operate internationally. However, you must consider the tax laws in your home country. For example, if you are a resident of a high-tax jurisdiction, you may still be liable for taxes there. Consulting24 advises clients on structuring to optimize tax efficiency. Compare this with Panama, which also has territorial taxation - see our Panama page for details. The BVI also has no withholding tax on dividends or interest, which is beneficial for profit repatriation.

Allowed Activities Under a BVI Crypto License

A BVI VASP license permits the following activities:

  • Exchange between virtual assets and fiat currencies.
  • Exchange between one or more forms of virtual assets.
  • Transfer of virtual assets (on behalf of customers).
  • Safekeeping and administration of virtual assets (custody).
  • Participation in and provision of financial services related to an issuer's offer or sale of virtual assets.

Notably, the BVI license does not allow activities that are considered securities or derivatives under BVI law. If your business involves tokenized securities, you may need additional licensing under the Securities and Investment Business Act. For a broader view of what different jurisdictions allow, explore our jurisdictions overview. The license also does not cover mining or staking services unless they involve custody or exchange.

Step-by-Step Process to Obtain a BVI Crypto License

Here is a typical process:

  1. Pre-incorporation: Choose a company name and appoint a registered agent in the BVI.
  2. Company incorporation: Register your BVI business company (BC) with the BVI Registry of Corporate Affairs.
  3. Prepare AML/KYC policies: Draft a compliance manual, risk assessment, and procedures. This is the most critical step.
  4. Appoint compliance officer: Designate a qualified individual to oversee AML compliance.
  5. Submit license application: File the VASP application with the FSC, including business plan, financial projections, and AML documentation.
  6. FSC review: The FSC may request additional information or clarifications. This stage takes 2-4 months.
  7. License issuance: Upon approval, you receive your VASP license. You must then implement ongoing reporting.

Banking and Payments for BVI Crypto Companies

Opening a bank account for a BVI crypto company can be challenging due to the perceived risk. Many traditional banks are reluctant to serve VASPs. However, there are options:

  • International banks with a crypto-friendly stance (e.g., in the Caribbean or Europe).
  • EMI (Electronic Money Institution) accounts.
  • Crypto-friendly payment processors.

You will need to provide your AML/KYC policies, license certificate, and proof of compliance. Some banks also require a minimum deposit, typically EUR 10,000 to 50,000. Benefits of a BVI Crypto License

The BVI offers several advantages for crypto businesses:

  • Zero corporate tax - maximize your profits.
  • Fast licensing process (3-6 months).
  • No minimum capital requirement.
  • Flexible regulatory approach - the FSC is pragmatic and business-friendly.
  • Established legal system based on English common law.
  • Confidentiality - beneficial ownership information is not publicly accessible.

These benefits make the BVI a strong contender for startups and mid-sized exchanges. However, you must weigh them against the stricter AML/KYC obligations that come with operating in a jurisdiction that follows FATF recommendations. The BVI also has a well-developed legal infrastructure for dispute resolution, which is a plus for international operations.

Compliance and Trust: Building a strong AML Program

To maintain your license and build trust with partners, your AML program must include:

  • Customer due diligence (CDD): Verify identity using government-issued IDs, proof of address, and beneficial ownership structure.
  • Enhanced due diligence (EDD): For politically exposed persons (PEPs) or high-risk jurisdictions.
  • Transaction monitoring: Use software to flag unusual activity (e.g., large transfers, rapid movement).
  • Record-keeping: Keep all records for at least 5 years after the business relationship ends.
  • Reporting: File suspicious transaction reports (STRs) with the FIA within 15 days.
  • Training: Train staff annually on AML/KYC procedures.

Note: This is general guidance, not legal advice. You should engage a local compliance consultant for your specific setup. The FSC also expects you to conduct regular independent audits of your AML program. Many firms use third-party auditors to ensure compliance and identify gaps.

Common Mistakes in AML/KYC Compliance for BVI Companies

Avoid these pitfalls:

  • Inadequate CDD: Failing to verify beneficial owners behind corporate structures.
  • Ignoring EDD: Not applying enhanced checks for high-risk customers.
  • Poor record-keeping: Not storing documents securely or for the required period.
  • Lack of training: Staff unaware of AML obligations.
  • Delayed reporting: Not filing STRs promptly.
  • Over-reliance on software: Automated systems can miss nuanced risks.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between AML and KYC in the BVI?

AML (Anti-Money Laundering) refers to the overall framework of laws, policies, and procedures to prevent money laundering. KYC (Know Your Customer) is a subset of AML that focuses on verifying customer identity and assessing risk. In the BVI, both are mandated by the Code and enforced by the FSC.

Do I need a compliance officer for my BVI crypto company?

Yes, the BVI FSC requires every licensed VASP to appoint a compliance officer. This person must be based in the BVI or have a local representative, and must have adequate knowledge of AML regulations. The compliance officer is responsible for implementing and monitoring the AML program.

How long must I keep AML records in the BVI?

The BVI Code requires that all AML/KYC records, including CDD documents and transaction records, be kept for at least five years after the business relationship ends or after the transaction is completed. This applies even if the license is surrendered or revoked.

Can I use a third-party for KYC verification in the BVI?

Yes, you can rely on a third party for CDD, but you remain ultimately responsible for compliance. The third party must be regulated and have adequate AML procedures. You must also obtain the relevant documentation from them and ensure they meet BVI standards.

What happens if I fail to report a suspicious transaction in the BVI?

Failure to report a suspicious transaction to the FIA can result in criminal penalties, including fines and imprisonment. The BVI takes non-compliance seriously, and the FSC may also revoke your license. It is crucial to have clear reporting procedures in place.

Is there a minimum capital requirement for a BVI crypto license?

No, the BVI FSC does not set a statutory minimum capital requirement. However, you must demonstrate that you have sufficient financial resources to operate. In practice, most applicants maintain at least EUR 50,000 to cover costs and show solvency.

Can a BVI crypto company bank in the BVI?

Local BVI banks are often reluctant to open accounts for crypto companies due to regulatory risk. Most BVI crypto firms use international banks or EMIs in other jurisdictions, such as Singapore, Switzerland, or the EU. Consulting24 can help identify suitable banking partners.

What is the tax rate for a BVI crypto company?

The BVI has zero corporate income tax, capital gains tax, and VAT for offshore companies. However, you must pay an annual license fee to the FSC (typically EUR 1,000 to 3,000) and maintain a registered agent. Your home country tax obligations still apply.

How does the BVI compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Panama offers a fixed cost of EUR 6,000 and faster processing (2-4 months), while the BVI has no minimum capital and zero tax. Panama's regulatory framework is newer, while the BVI has a longer track record. Both are attractive for different reasons. See our Panama page for details.

Can I transfer my BVI license to another jurisdiction?

No, a BVI VASP license is non-transferable. If you want to operate in another jurisdiction, you must apply for a new license there. However, you can restructure your business and apply for a license in a new jurisdiction while maintaining the BVI entity for other purposes.
