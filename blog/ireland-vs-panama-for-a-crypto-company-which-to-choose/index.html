What Is the Ireland vs Panama Choice for Crypto Companies?

The decision between Ireland and Panama hinges on your business model, target market, and risk appetite. Ireland is a regulated EU jurisdiction where crypto firms must obtain authorisation from the Central Bank of Ireland. The regime is comprehensive, covering anti-money laundering (AML), consumer protection, and prudential requirements. Panama, on the other hand, has no specific crypto licensing law; companies can legally conduct crypto activities under a standard Panama corporation, with no dedicated regulatory oversight.

This fundamental difference shapes every aspect of the comparison: cost, timeline, capital requirements, tax treatment, and allowed activities. Ireland provides passporting rights across the EU, while Panama offers operational freedom with minimal bureaucracy. For founders prioritising speed and low cost, Panama is attractive; for those needing EU credibility and access to European banks, Ireland may be necessary.

Consider a concrete example: a startup launching a crypto exchange targeting EU retail investors will likely need Irish VASP registration to comply with MiCA and access EU payment rails. In contrast, a proprietary trading firm focusing on Asian markets may prefer Panama's low overhead and tax neutrality. Consulting24 helps you map your specific use case to the right jurisdiction.

Who Needs a Crypto License in Ireland vs Panama?

In Ireland, any entity offering exchange services between virtual assets and fiat currencies, custody of virtual assets, or transfer services must register as a VASP with the CBI. This applies to both Irish-incorporated companies and EU branches. In Panama, there is no mandatory crypto license. However, businesses that engage in money transmission or exchange may need to consider voluntary compliance with AML guidelines to access banking services.

Typical profiles for Ireland include: EU-focused exchanges, custodians, and payment processors seeking regulatory legitimacy. Panama suits: startups testing products, token issuers, and trading firms that do not require EU passporting. Consulting24 helps founders assess which profile fits, and for those choosing Panama, we handle company setup directly.

For example, a DeFi protocol issuing governance tokens may operate from Panama without a license, but if it plans to offer custody to EU residents, Irish registration becomes necessary. We can guide you through this decision matrix.

License Type and Regulator

Ireland: The regulator is the Central Bank of Ireland (CBI). Crypto firms must obtain a VASP registration under the Criminal Justice (Money Laundering and Terrorist Financing) Acts, now aligned with MiCA. The license is a single authorisation for specified services, with capital requirements based on service class.

Panama: No specific crypto regulator. Companies are formed under the Panama General Corporation Law (Law 32 of 1927) and supervised by the Public Registry and the Superintendency of Banks (for banking activities). Crypto businesses operate as general corporations with no dedicated licensing.

For a full list of regulated jurisdictions, see our jurisdictions page. In Ireland, the CBI conducts a thorough fitness and propriety assessment of directors and beneficial owners. Panama has no such requirement, making it faster but less transparent.

Capital Requirements

Under MiCA, Ireland imposes capital tiers: EUR 50,000 for certain services (e.g., custody), EUR 125,000 for exchange services, and EUR 150,000 for full-service platforms. These must be maintained as own funds. Panama has no statutory capital requirement for crypto businesses. A standard Panama corporation typically has a minimum paid-up capital of USD 10,000 (approx. EUR 9,200) but this is not enforced for crypto activities. For founders seeking low entry cost, Panama is clearly advantageous.

Example: A crypto custodian in Ireland must lock up EUR 50,000 in capital from day one, while a Panama-based custodian can allocate that capital to operations or liquidity. This difference can be decisive for early-stage companies.

Tax Treatment

Ireland: Corporation tax is 12.5% on trading income. Capital gains tax is 33%. VAT applies to crypto services at 23% unless exempt. Ireland has a broad tax treaty network and a participation exemption for dividends. Crypto transactions may be subject to stamp duty and withholding taxes.

Panama: Territorial taxation - only income sourced in Panama is taxed. Offshore income (including most crypto trading) is tax-free. No capital gains tax, no VAT. Annual flat tax for corporations is approximately USD 300 (EUR 275). Panama offers significant tax advantages for non-domestic operations.

Consulting24 advises on tax structuring; for Panama, we coordinate with local accountants. For a Panama company earning all revenue from non-Panamanian clients, effective tax rate can be near zero, compared to Ireland's 12.5% minimum.

Allowed Activities

Ireland permits a wide range of crypto activities under VASP registration: exchange, custody, transfer, and advisory services. However, derivatives and certain DeFi protocols may require additional authorisation under MiFID. Panama imposes no restrictions on crypto activities beyond general corporate law and AML obligations. Businesses can trade, mine, stake, lend, or issue tokens without prior approval. This flexibility makes Panama ideal for innovative or experimental models.

For instance, a Panama company can run a crypto mining farm, operate a peer-to-peer lending platform, or issue a utility token without seeking regulatory clearance. In Ireland, each activity must be scoped within the VASP authorisation, limiting agility.

Step-by-Step Process

Ireland: 1. Pre-application: prepare business plan, AML policies, risk assessment. 2. Submit VASP application to CBI with supporting documents. 3. CBI review (6-12 months) including interviews. 4. Obtain authorisation. 5. Ongoing compliance reporting.

Panama: 1. Choose company name and structure. 2. Engage Consulting24 for incorporation. 3. Submit documents to Public Registry. 4. Receive corporate kit (2-4 weeks). 5. Open bank account (may require additional due diligence). No regulatory approval needed for crypto operations.

For detailed steps on Panama, see our company setup page. The Irish process is intensive: expect multiple rounds of questions from the CBI, and possibly a pre-application meeting. Panama's process is administrative and predictable.

Banking and Payments

Ireland: VASP registration facilitates access to EU banks, but many still apply strict due diligence. Crypto-friendly banks include Revolut Business and some challenger banks. Panama: Banking is more accessible for corporations, but crypto businesses face scrutiny. Banks like Banco General and Towerbank may accept crypto firms with proper compliance. Consulting24 assists with introductions to crypto-friendly banking partners in both jurisdictions.

In practice, a Panama company may need to present a compliance manual and proof of source of funds to open an account. Ireland's VASP status can streamline account opening with EU banks, but not all banks are willing. We recommend preparing a banking package in advance for either jurisdiction.

Benefits of Each Jurisdiction

Ireland benefits: EU passporting, strong regulatory reputation, access to EU payment systems, skilled workforce, and government support for fintech. Panama benefits: Low cost (EUR 6,000 flat), no capital requirement, territorial tax, quick setup (2-4 weeks), no specific crypto license, and operational privacy. For founders seeking speed and low overhead, Panama is hard to beat. For those needing EU market access, Ireland is the standard.

Panama offers less regulatory burden, but this can reduce trust with some partners. To mitigate, Panama companies often adopt voluntary AML/KYC procedures. Frequently asked questions

Is a crypto license required in Panama?

No, Panama has no specific crypto licensing regime. Crypto businesses can operate under a standard Panama corporation with no regulatory approval. However, voluntary AML compliance is recommended for banking access.

How long does it take to set up a crypto company in Panama?

Company incorporation takes 2-4 weeks. There is no additional licensing timeline. Total time to operational can be as short as one month, depending on banking setup.

What is the minimum capital for a crypto company in Ireland?

Under MiCA, Ireland requires minimum capital of EUR 50,000 for custody services, EUR 125,000 for exchange, and EUR 150,000 for full-service platforms. These must be maintained as own funds.

Can a Panama crypto company bank in the EU?

EU banks may be reluctant to open accounts for Panama companies due to perceived risk. Some fintech banks like Revolut may accept, but expect enhanced due diligence. Consulting24 can assist with banking introductions.

What is the corporate tax rate for crypto companies in Panama?

Panama has a territorial tax system: only income sourced in Panama is taxed. Offshore crypto income is tax-free. The annual flat tax for corporations is approximately USD 300 (EUR 275).

Does Ireland allow crypto derivatives trading?

Crypto derivatives may require additional authorisation under MiFID II, beyond the VASP registration. Firms should consult with legal advisors to determine if a top-up license is needed.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations in Ireland?

VASP holders must submit annual AML reports, undergo periodic audits, and notify the CBI of any material changes. Compliance costs can range from EUR 5,000 to EUR 15,000 per year.

Can I switch from Panama to Ireland later?

Yes, you can incorporate an Irish subsidiary and migrate operations. Consulting24 can assist with the transition, including VASP application and restructuring.

Is Panama considered a tax haven?

Panama is often labelled a tax haven due to its territorial tax system and low rates. However, it has tax information exchange agreements with many countries. Consult a tax professional for your specific situation.

What is the success rate for Irish VASP applications?

The CBI is rigorous; many applications are rejected or withdrawn. Success depends on the quality of documentation and business model. Consulting24 can help prepare a strong application.

Does Panama have any crypto-specific regulations?

As of 2026, Panama has not enacted crypto-specific laws. However, the Superintendency of Banks has issued guidance on AML for virtual assets. No license is required.

How does Panama compare to other low-cost jurisdictions like the Cayman Islands?

Panama is cheaper (EUR 6,000 flat vs Cayman's higher setup costs) and faster. However, Cayman has a more established financial services framework. See our Cayman Islands page for details.
