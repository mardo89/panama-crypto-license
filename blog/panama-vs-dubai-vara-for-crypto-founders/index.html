Overview: Panama vs Dubai VARA for Crypto Founders

Panama and Dubai represent two different approaches to crypto regulation. Panama is a civil law jurisdiction with no dedicated crypto law but a functional framework under the Superintendence of Banks and the Securities Commission. Companies providing virtual asset services must register and comply with AML/CFT rules. The key advantage is simplicity: a Panama corporation can be set up for a flat €6,000, with no minimum capital requirement and no corporate income tax on offshore income.

Dubai VARA, established in 2022, is a dedicated regulator for virtual assets. It offers several license categories (e.g., Broker-Dealer, Exchange, Advisory) with specific capital requirements. VARA is part of the UAE's broader crypto hub strategy, attracting major exchanges and fintechs. However, costs and compliance are higher: licensing fees start from approximately AED 50,000 (€12,500) plus capital requirements of AED 500,000 (€125,000) or more, depending on the activity.

Who Needs a Crypto License in Panama or Dubai?

Both jurisdictions cater to different profiles:

  • Panama: Ideal for startups and mid-size firms that want a cost-effective, tax-efficient base with minimal red tape. Suitable for crypto exchanges, payment processors, OTC desks, and blockchain consultancies that operate internationally and do not target the local market.
  • Dubai VARA: Best for companies seeking a prestigious, well-regulated environment with access to the UAE's financial ecosystem. Suitable for exchanges, custodians, DeFi platforms, and token issuers that want to serve the UAE market or use Dubai as a gateway to the Middle East and Asia.

License Type & Regulator

Panama: There is no single 'crypto license' in Panama. Instead, companies register as a 'Virtual Asset Service Provider' (VASP) with the Superintendencia del Mercado de Valores (SMV) or the Superintendencia de Bancos (SBP), depending on the activity. The framework is based on AML/CFT Law 23 of 2015 and recent updates. No specific capital requirement, but you must comply with AML procedures.

Dubai VARA: The Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA) is the sole regulator for virtual assets in Dubai (excluding DIFC). License types include: Broker-Dealer, Exchange, Custody, Advisory, and more. Each has specific capital and governance requirements. VARA is known for its rigorous application process and ongoing compliance obligations.

Capital Requirement

Panama: No statutory minimum capital for VASP registration. However, to demonstrate substance, we recommend at least $10,000 paid-up capital. The company must have a local registered agent and physical address.

Dubai VARA: Capital requirements vary by license type. For example, a Broker-Dealer license requires AED 500,000 (€125,000) minimum capital; an Exchange license may require AED 1,000,000 (€250,000). Capital must be held in a UAE bank account and cannot be withdrawn without approval.

For founders with limited capital, Panama is clearly more accessible. Dubai VARA is suited for well-funded ventures. Learn more about requirements on our requirements page.

Tax Treatment

Panama: Territorial taxation-only income sourced in Panama is taxed. Offshore income (from non-Panamanian clients) is tax-free. No capital gains tax, no VAT, and no withholding tax on dividends. This makes Panama a classic tax haven for international businesses.

Dubai (UAE): The UAE introduced a 9% federal corporate tax in 2023 for profits exceeding AED 375,000 (€93,000). Free zone companies can still benefit from 0% tax if they comply with substance requirements and do not trade with the mainland. VAT at 5% applies to most services. Dubai VARA-licensed entities are subject to these taxes.

For tax efficiency, Panama generally wins for offshore operations. Dubai offers a strong regulatory brand but higher tax burden.

Allowed Activities Under Each Regime

Panama: Permitted activities include: exchange of virtual assets, wallet services, OTC trading, payment processing, token issuance (non-security), and blockchain consulting. Prohibited: securities-related tokens (unless registered with SMV), gambling, and unlicensed banking.

Dubai VARA: License categories cover: Broker-Dealer (order matching), Exchange (matching engine), Custody, Advisory, Lending/Borrowing, and Token Issuance (subject to approval). VARA also regulates DeFi platforms and NFTs. Activities must be conducted within the UAE or with UAE residents.

Both jurisdictions allow a wide range of activities, but Dubai VARA is more prescriptive and requires separate licenses for each activity.

Step-by-Step Process for Panama and Dubai VARA

Panama (via Consulting24):

  1. Company incorporation (2-3 days): name reservation, incorporation, registered agent.
  2. Open a corporate bank account (2-4 weeks).
  3. Prepare AML/CFT policies and appoint a compliance officer.
  4. Register as VASP with SMV or SBP (2-3 months).
  5. Ongoing: annual renewal, AML reporting.

Dubai VARA:

  1. Company setup in mainland or free zone (e.g., DMCC) (2-4 weeks).
  2. Submit VARA application: business plan, AML policies, capital proof, shareholder background.
  3. VARA review and interview (2-4 months).
  4. Obtain license and open bank account.
  5. Compliance: quarterly reporting, annual audit, regulatory fees.

Banking & Payment Processing

Panama: Banking is relatively open for crypto companies. Major banks like Banco General and BAC Credomatic accept VASP clients, though due diligence is thorough. Alternative payment processors (e.g., PSPs) are available. Panama has a US dollarised economy, eliminating currency risk.

Dubai VARA: Banking is more challenging. Most UAE banks (e.g., Emirates NBD, Mashreq) require VARA license approval before opening accounts. Some digital banks like Zand and Al Maryouj are more crypto-friendly. Payment processing via local acquirers is possible but subject to VARA oversight.

Panama offers faster, easier banking for crypto founders, especially those targeting international markets. For more on banking, visit our company setup page.

Benefits of Panama vs Dubai VARA

  • Panama: Low cost (€6,000 flat), no minimum capital, 0% tax on offshore income, quick setup (2-3 months), US dollar economy, no exchange controls, privacy.
  • Dubai VARA: Strong regulatory reputation, access to UAE market, clear legal framework, potential for free zone tax benefits, high-quality infrastructure, networking opportunities.

Your choice depends on whether you prioritise cost efficiency or regulatory prestige. Many founders start with Panama and later expand to Dubai.

Compliance & Trust Considerations

Panama: Compliance is based on AML/CFT Law 23. You must appoint a local compliance officer, conduct KYC/CDD, and file annual reports. The SMV and SBP conduct inspections. While Panama has a reputation for lax enforcement, recent updates have tightened oversight. Consulting24 ensures your setup meets all legal requirements.

Dubai VARA: VARA is a proactive regulator with strict compliance standards. Licensees must submit monthly transaction reports, undergo annual audits, and maintain capital adequacy. Non-compliance can result in fines or license revocation. VARA's rigorous approach enhances trust with partners and customers.

Common Mistakes When Choosing Between Panama and Dubai VARA

  • Underestimating costs: Dubai VARA's total cost (setup + capital + ongoing) can exceed €200,000 in the first year. Panama is a fraction of that.
  • Ignoring substance requirements: Both jurisdictions require local presence (office, staff, bank account). Panama's requirements are lighter but still mandatory.
  • Choosing based on tax alone: Tax efficiency is important, but regulatory fit and market access matter equally.
  • Assuming Dubai VARA is faster: The process often takes 6+ months, while Panama can be done in 2-3 months.
  • Not checking banking compatibility: Some banks refuse crypto clients. Verify before committing.
