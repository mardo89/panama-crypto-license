Overview: Crypto Banking and Payment Rails in Spain

Spain's crypto banking and payment ecosystem has matured significantly by 2026. The Bank of Spain regulates virtual asset service providers (VASPs) under a mandatory registration regime, while payment institutions (PIs) and electronic money institutions (EMIs) fall under the Bank of Spain's supervision for fiat services. MiCA's full implementation harmonises crypto-asset services across the EU, but Spain maintains additional requirements for anti-money laundering (AML) and banking partnerships.

Key players include traditional banks like BBVA and Santander, which now offer limited crypto services, and fintechs like Monei and N26 that provide crypto-friendly accounts. Payment rails such as SEPA Instant and Bizum are widely integrated for fast euro transfers. For crypto founders, the main challenge is securing a banking partner willing to support crypto-related transactions, as many banks still apply strict due diligence. However, the regulatory clarity in Spain makes it a viable choice for long-term operations.

By 2026, Spain has also implemented the EU's Transfer of Funds Regulation (TFR), requiring VASPs to collect and share information on crypto transfers. This adds compliance overhead but aligns with global standards, making Spanish-licensed entities more trustworthy for banking partners.

Who Needs a Crypto Banking and Payment Solution in Spain?

Any business that handles both crypto and fiat currencies needs strong banking and payment rails in Spain. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges and trading platforms that convert crypto to fiat
  • Payment service providers offering crypto-to-fiat conversion for merchants
  • Merchants that accept crypto payments and need to settle in euros
  • DeFi projects with fiat on-ramps or off-ramps
  • Funds and asset managers dealing with digital assets and requiring fiat custody
  • Remittance companies using crypto for cross-border transfers

If your business model involves receiving euros from customers or paying suppliers in fiat, you need a Spanish bank account or an EMI licence. Without proper banking integration, you risk delays, high fees, or account closures. For example, a crypto exchange that only uses a foreign bank account may face longer settlement times (2-3 days) compared to a local SEPA Instant account (seconds).

License Type and Regulator

In Spain, the primary regulator for crypto banking and payment services is the Bank of Spain. Crypto service providers must register as VASPs with the Bank of Spain, while payment institutions (PIs) and electronic money institutions (EMIs) are also authorised by the Bank of Spain under EU directives. The Spanish Securities Market Commission (CNMV) regulates crypto derivatives, but for spot services, the Bank of Spain is the key authority.

For crypto payment rails specifically, you may need a combination of a VASP registration and a PI or EMI licence if you handle fiat money. The Bank of Spain also oversees AML compliance and requires all registered entities to have a physical presence in Spain, including a compliance officer and adequate systems. As of 2026, MiCA applies fully, meaning that if you hold a licence in one EU member state, you can passport services across the EU. However, Spain has not yet transposed MiCA into national law; the existing VASP registration continues until the Spanish transposition is complete, expected by late 2026. After transposition, a new CASP licence will replace the VASP register, with capital requirements of EUR 50,000 to EUR 150,000 depending on services.

Capital Requirement

Spain imposes minimum capital requirements for payment institutions and electronic money institutions under EU law. For a PI licence, the minimum initial capital is EUR 125,000, while for an EMI licence it is EUR 350,000. VASP registration does not have a statutory minimum capital, but the Bank of Spain expects adequate financial resources, typically at least EUR 50,000 in practice. If you combine a VASP with a PI licence, you must meet the higher capital requirement.

For a pure VASP without fiat handling, the capital requirement is lower but still requires evidence of sufficient funds to cover operational costs for at least 12 months. These figures are for 2026 and are subject to change after MiCA transposition, which will introduce tiered capital: EUR 50,000 for simple custody, EUR 125,000 for exchange services, and EUR 150,000 for trading platforms. Tax Treatment of Crypto Banking and Payment Income

Spain taxes corporate income at a standard rate of 25% for resident companies. Crypto-related income, including trading fees, payment processing fees, and interest from crypto lending, is treated as ordinary business income and subject to corporate tax. VAT is generally not applicable on crypto-to-crypto transactions, but fiat-to-crypto conversions may be subject to VAT in certain cases, such as when a commission is charged.

For individuals, capital gains on crypto are taxed at progressive rates (19% to 23% in 2026). The Spanish tax authority (AEAT) has increased scrutiny on crypto transactions, and all VASPs must report customer transactions annually. Founders should engage a local tax advisor to ensure compliance with reporting obligations, including Form 720 for foreign assets if applicable. Spain also has a wealth tax that may apply to high-net-worth individuals holding crypto.

Allowed Activities Under a Spanish Crypto Banking and Payment Licence

With a VASP registration and a PI/EMI licence, you can conduct the following activities in Spain:

  • Exchange of crypto for fiat and vice versa
  • Custody of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Payment services including credit transfers, direct debits, and card issuing
  • Issuance of electronic money
  • Merchant acquiring for crypto payments
  • Remittance services using crypto
  • Conversion of crypto to fiat and transfer to bank accounts

Activities not covered include derivatives trading (requires CNMV authorisation) and deposit-taking (reserved for banks). You must also comply with AML/KYC requirements and report suspicious transactions to the Financial Intelligence Unit (SEPBLAC). The Bank of Spain may also require you to limit use or restrict certain high-risk activities.

Step-by-Step Process to Set Up Crypto Banking and Payment Rails in Spain

The process involves several stages. Here is a typical step-by-step approach:

  1. Company incorporation: Register a Spanish limited liability company (S.L.) with a registered office and a director. Obtain a NIF (tax ID). The company must have a physical address in Spain.
  2. VASP registration: Prepare a compliance manual, AML policies, and a business plan. Submit the application to the Bank of Spain. This requires a physical presence in Spain, including a compliance officer. The Bank of Spain will review the application and may request additional information.
  3. PI/EMI licence application (if needed): This is a more rigorous process requiring a detailed programme of operations, capital proof, and governance structure. The Bank of Spain will conduct a fit-and-proper test on directors. The application must include a business plan for three years, showing projected volumes and profitability.
  4. Bank account opening: Approach banks with a clear compliance framework. Be prepared for extensive due diligence. Some crypto-friendly banks include Banco Sabadell and BNP Paribas Spain. You may need to provide your VASP registration number and AML policies.
  5. Payment rail integration: Connect to SEPA, Bizum, or card networks via a payment processor. You may need to partner with an acquirer or use a Banking-as-a-Service (BaaS) provider. For SEPA Instant, you can become a direct member or use a sponsor bank.
  6. Go-live: Once all approvals are in place, you can launch your services. Ongoing reporting to the Bank of Spain is required, including annual audits and transaction reports.

Bizum is popular for peer-to-peer payments and can be integrated via APIs. Benefits of Using Spain for Crypto Banking and Payment

Spain offers several advantages for crypto banking and payment infrastructure:

  • Clear regulatory framework under Bank of Spain and MiCA alignment
  • Access to SEPA and Eurozone payment systems, enabling fast and low-cost transfers
  • Growing fintech ecosystem with talent and support, especially in Madrid and Barcelona
  • Competitive tax regime for corporate income (25%), lower than Germany (30%) or France (28%)
  • Passporting rights to other EU markets once MiCA licence is obtained
  • Strong banking infrastructure with multiple crypto-friendly options

Compared to other jurisdictions, Spain is more established than some Eastern European hubs but slower than Malta or Lithuania. For founders seeking speed, Estonia or Panama may be better options. However, for long-term EU operations, Spain provides a solid base.

Compliance and Trust Considerations

Compliance is paramount in Spain. The Bank of Spain expects strong AML/KYC procedures, transaction monitoring, and regular audits. You must appoint a compliance officer resident in Spain. Data protection under GDPR applies, and you must register with the Spanish Data Protection Agency (AEPD) if processing personal data. Additionally, the TFR requires you to collect and verify beneficiary information for crypto transfers above EUR 1,000.

Trust is built through transparency. Display your registration number on your website, publish your compliance policies, and consider obtaining an ISO 27001 certification for information security. Many banks will require proof of compliance before opening accounts. Consulting24 recommends engaging a local compliance consultant to ensure ongoing adherence. Common Mistakes When Setting Up Crypto Banking and Payment in Spain

Founders often make these errors:

  • Underestimating the time required for VASP registration (3-6 months minimum)
  • Choosing a bank without prior agreement on crypto activity, leading to account closure
  • Neglecting to appoint a local compliance officer, which is a legal requirement
  • Failing to prepare a detailed business plan for the PI/EMI licence, resulting in rejection
  • Ignoring tax reporting obligations for customers, leading to fines
  • Not integrating SEPA Instant from the start, causing slow settlements
  • Overlooking the need for a legal entity in Spain before applying for licences

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a VASP registration and a PI/EMI licence in Spain?

A VASP registration with the Bank of Spain allows you to provide crypto services (exchange, custody) but not handle fiat money directly. A PI or EMI licence is required to offer payment services or issue electronic money. Many crypto firms need both to operate fully compliant payment rails.

How long does it take to get a VASP registration in Spain?

The Bank of Spain typically processes VASP applications within 3 to 6 months. Delays can occur if the application is incomplete or if additional information is requested. It is advisable to engage a local advisor to prepare a thorough application.

Can I passport a Spanish crypto licence to other EU countries?

Yes, once Spain transposes MiCA (expected by late 2026), a Spanish CASP licence will allow passporting across the EU. Currently, VASP registration does not automatically grant passporting rights, but you can apply for licences in other member states separately.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations for a crypto payment firm in Spain?

You must conduct ongoing AML/KYC checks, monitor transactions, report suspicious activities to SEPBLAC, and submit annual audits to the Bank of Spain. You also need to comply with GDPR and TFR requirements for data collection. A local compliance officer is mandatory.

Is it possible to open a bank account for a crypto business in Spain?

Yes, but it is challenging. Banks like Banco Sabadell and BNP Paribas Spain are more open to crypto businesses if you have a VASP registration and strong compliance policies. You may need to provide a detailed business plan and proof of AML procedures.

What is the tax rate for crypto companies in Spain?

Corporate income tax is 25% for resident companies. Crypto-related income is treated as ordinary business income. VAT may apply to fiat-to-crypto conversion fees. Individual capital gains are taxed at 19-23% depending on the amount.

Can I use a Spanish EMI licence for crypto payment processing?

Yes, an EMI licence allows you to issue electronic money and process payments. Combined with a VASP registration, you can offer crypto-to-fiat conversion and payment services. The EMI licence requires a minimum capital of EUR 350,000.

What are the alternatives to Spain for crypto banking and payment rails?

Popular alternatives include Panama (fast and low cost, flat EUR 6,000), Lithuania (EU passporting, moderate cost), and Estonia (low cost but challenging banking). Consulting24 delivers directly in Panama, Estonia, and Lithuania, and advises on others.

Does Spain require a physical office for crypto licensing?

Yes, the Bank of Spain requires a physical presence in Spain, including a registered office and a compliance officer. This can be a serviced office or a shared space, but it must be a genuine operational base.

What happens if I operate without a licence in Spain?

Operating without registration is illegal and can result in fines, account freezes, and criminal charges. The Bank of Spain actively monitors unlicensed activity and may issue cease-and-desist orders. Always obtain the necessary licences before offering services.
