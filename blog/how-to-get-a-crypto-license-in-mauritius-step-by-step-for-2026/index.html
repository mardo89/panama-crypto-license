What Is a Mauritius Crypto License?

A Mauritius crypto license is a regulatory authorisation issued by the Financial Services Commission (FSC) under the VAITOS Act. It allows businesses to operate as a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) or Digital Security Token Service Provider (DSTSP). The license covers activities such as exchange, custody, transfer, and advisory services related to virtual assets. Unlike some jurisdictions that require separate licenses for each activity, Mauritius offers a single license that can be tailored to your business model. This makes it an attractive option for startups and established firms looking for a comprehensive regulatory framework in Africa and Asia.

The VAITOS Act defines virtual assets broadly as any digital representation of value that can be traded or transferred digitally, excluding digital representations of fiat currencies and securities already regulated under the Securities Act. The FSC also publishes guidelines on the treatment of stablecoins and utility tokens, providing clarity for issuers. As of 2026, the FSC has not imposed a ban on privacy coins or decentralised finance protocols, but it requires applicants to demonstrate adequate risk controls. The license is valid for one year and must be renewed annually, with a renewal fee of approximately EUR 2,000 to EUR 4,000. Compared to other jurisdictions, the Mauritius license is relatively straightforward, with clear guidelines published by the FSC. Who Needs a Mauritius Crypto License?

Any entity that provides virtual asset services in or from Mauritius must hold a license from the FSC. This includes:

  • Virtual asset exchanges (centralised and decentralised)
  • Custodians of virtual assets
  • Brokers and dealers in virtual assets
  • Payment processors using virtual assets
  • Advisors and portfolio managers dealing with virtual assets
  • Issuers of initial token offerings (ITOs)

If you plan to serve customers in Mauritius or use Mauritius as your base of operations, you need a license. The FSC also requires that the beneficial owners and directors meet fit and proper criteria. For businesses already licensed in other jurisdictions, the Mauritius license can serve as a complementary authorisation, especially for those targeting African markets. For example, a crypto exchange already licensed in Lithuania under MiCA might seek a Mauritius license to expand into Sub-Saharan Africa without the higher capital requirements of the EU. The FSC does not require a physical office in Mauritius, but you must have a registered agent and a local resident director (or a management company). License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Financial Services Commission (FSC) of Mauritius. Under the VAITOS Act, the FSC issues a single licence category: the Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) License. However, the license scope can include Digital Security Token (DST) activities if the business deals with security tokens. The FSC also oversees compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) obligations under the Financial Intelligence and Anti-Money Laundering Act. The license is valid for one year and must be renewed annually. Compared to other jurisdictions, the Mauritius license is relatively straightforward, with clear guidelines published by the FSC.

The FSC has a dedicated VASP unit that handles applications and supervision. As of 2026, the FSC has not yet implemented a sandbox regime, but it offers pre-application meetings to discuss business models and regulatory expectations. The regulator is known for being approachable and responsive, often providing feedback within 2 weeks of submission. For businesses that require a faster timeline, the FSC offers an expedited review for an additional fee (typically EUR 1,000), reducing the review period to 2-3 months. This is a key advantage over jurisdictions like Switzerland, where the FINMA review can take 6-12 months. Consulting24 can coordinate these meetings on your behalf.

Capital Requirement

Mauritius does not impose a statutory minimum capital requirement for VASP licenses. However, the FSC expects applicants to have sufficient financial resources to operate their business and meet regulatory obligations. In practice, a minimum of EUR 50,000 in paid-up capital is recommended to demonstrate financial substance. This is lower than many EU jurisdictions, where MiCA mandates EUR 125,000 or more depending on service type. The FSC reviews the business plan and financial projections to assess adequacy. For companies also holding a Category 1 Global Business Licence (GBL), additional capital may be required under the Companies Act.

The FSC may ask for a bank statement showing the capital is held in a Mauritian bank account. You can use a local management company to open an account before incorporation. The capital must be maintained throughout the license period and cannot be withdrawn without FSC approval. For comparison, under MiCA, a custodian wallet provider needs EUR 125,000, while an exchange needs EUR 150,000. Tax Treatment

Mauritius offers a flat corporate tax rate of 15% on net profits. There is no capital gains tax, no withholding tax on dividends, and no inheritance tax. Virtual asset transactions are treated as ordinary business income, subject to the same rate. The country has an extensive network of double taxation treaties (over 40) that can reduce withholding taxes on cross-border payments. For businesses that hold a GBL, tax incentives may apply, such as an 80% partial exemption on certain foreign-source income, effectively reducing the rate to 3%. This makes Mauritius one of the most tax-efficient jurisdictions for crypto businesses.

For example, a crypto trading firm based in Mauritius that earns profits from trading on international exchanges would pay only 15% corporate tax, and if it qualifies for the GBL partial exemption, the effective rate drops to 3%. Additionally, Mauritius does not impose VAT on virtual asset transactions, though standard VAT of 15% applies to advisory services. The tax authority, the Mauritius Revenue Authority (MRA), has issued guidance confirming that crypto-to-crypto trades are not subject to VAT. This clarity is a major advantage over jurisdictions like Poland, where VAT treatment of crypto remains ambiguous. Allowed Activities

A Mauritius VASP license permits the following activities:

  • Exchange between virtual assets and fiat currencies
  • Exchange between one or more forms of virtual assets
  • Transfer of virtual assets
  • Custody and administration of virtual assets
  • Participation in and provision of financial services related to an issuer’s offer or sale of a virtual asset
  • Advisory services on virtual assets
  • Operation of a virtual asset trading platform

The license can also cover Digital Security Tokens if the issuer complies with the Securities Act. The FSC does not restrict the type of virtual assets you can support, but you must conduct due diligence on each asset. Activities not permitted include unlicensed banking, insurance, or gambling services. The FSC also prohibits the issuance of algorithmic stablecoins without prior approval. Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to get a crypto license in Mauritius involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application preparation: Define your business model, prepare a detailed business plan, and gather documents (passports, proof of address, CVs, bank references, source of wealth). This stage typically takes 2-4 weeks.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a company in Mauritius with the Registrar of Companies. You will need a registered office and a local resident director (or a management company). Incorporation takes 1-2 weeks.
  3. Submit application to FSC: File the VASP license application with the FSC, including all required forms, the business plan, AML/CTF policies, and due diligence reports. The FSC charges a non-refundable application fee of EUR 1,500.
  4. FSC review: The FSC assesses the application, conducts background checks, and may request additional information. This stage takes 2-4 months. The FSC may also request a meeting with the beneficial owners.
  5. Approval and license issuance: Once approved, you pay the license fee (EUR 1,500 to EUR 3,000) and receive the certificate. The license is valid for one year.
  6. Post-licence compliance: Appoint a compliance officer, file annual returns, conduct AML audits, and renew the license annually. The first renewal is due 12 months from issuance.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

After obtaining the license, you must comply with ongoing obligations under the VAITOS Act and AML/CTF regulations. These include:

  • Appointing a compliance officer resident in Mauritius
  • Implementing AML/CTF policies and procedures
  • Conducting customer due diligence (CDD) and enhanced due diligence (EDD) for high-risk clients
  • Reporting suspicious transactions to the Financial Intelligence Unit
  • Maintaining records for at least 5 years
  • Submitting annual audited financial statements and compliance reports to the FSC
  • Renewing the license annually and paying the renewal fee
  • Notifying the FSC of any material changes (e.g., change of directors, shareholding, business model)

Non-compliance can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. The FSC conducts periodic inspections and may request ad-hoc reports. Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital required for a Mauritius crypto license?

Mauritius does not have a statutory minimum capital requirement. However, the FSC expects applicants to have sufficient financial resources. In practice, we recommend at least EUR 50,000 in paid-up capital to demonstrate substance. This is lower than MiCA jurisdictions, which require EUR 125,000 or more.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Mauritius?

The typical timeline is 3 to 6 months from submission to approval. The FSC reviews applications within 2-4 months, but delays can occur if documents are incomplete. Engaging a local consultant can help streamline the process. Expedited review is available for an additional fee.

What activities are covered under a Mauritius VASP license?

The license covers exchange (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto), custody, transfer, advisory services, and operation of a trading platform. It can also include digital security tokens if the issuer complies with securities laws. The license is single and can be tailored to your business model.

Can I use a Mauritius license to serve customers in the EU?

No, a Mauritius license is not recognised in the EU under MiCA. To serve EU customers, you need a CASP license from an EU member state. However, Mauritius is ideal for serving African and Asian markets. You can hold both a Mauritius license and an EU license for global coverage.

What are the tax benefits of a Mauritius crypto license?

Mauritius offers a flat corporate tax rate of 15%, no capital gains tax, and no withholding tax on dividends. With a Global Business Licence, foreign-source income can be 80% exempt, reducing the effective rate to 3%. The country also has over 40 double taxation treaties.

Do I need a physical office in Mauritius?

No, you do not need a physical office. You must have a registered agent and a local resident director (or a management company). The registered agent provides a registered address and handles regulatory correspondence. This makes Mauritius accessible to remote teams.

How much does a Mauritius crypto license cost in total?

The total initial cost is approximately EUR 8,500 to EUR 17,000, including FSC fees, due diligence, legal setup, and company incorporation. Annual renewal costs EUR 2,000 to EUR 4,000. What are the ongoing compliance obligations after obtaining the license?

You must appoint a compliance officer, implement AML/CTF policies, conduct CDD/EDD, report suspicious transactions, maintain records for 5 years, submit annual audited financial statements, and renew the license annually. Non-compliance can lead to fines or license revocation.
