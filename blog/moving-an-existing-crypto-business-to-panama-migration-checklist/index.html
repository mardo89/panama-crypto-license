What Does Moving an Existing Crypto Business to Panama Mean?

Moving an existing crypto business to Panama means re-domiciling your legal entity or setting up a new Panamanian company and transferring operations, assets, and customers. Panama does not have a dedicated crypto law but regulates virtual asset service providers (VASPs) under anti-money laundering (AML) rules. No licence is mandatory today for custody, exchange, or transfer of virtual assets in Panama (a framework is pending).

Key steps include:

  • Incorporating a Panama company (cost EUR 6,000 flat fee via Consulting24).
  • Registering a Panama company and putting an AML/CTF programme in place.
  • Meeting capital requirements (no minimum capital for the license itself, but adequate capital for operations).
  • Opening a corporate bank account (challenging but possible with proper compliance).
  • Migrating existing users and smart contracts.

Panama offers territorial taxation: only income sourced in Panama is taxed. Crypto trading gains are generally tax-free if the source is outside Panama. This makes it attractive for international crypto businesses. For example, a crypto exchange serving clients worldwide would pay no Panama tax on trading fees earned from non-Panamanian users. However, if you have local clients or an office in Panama, part of your income may be taxable. Proper structuring is essential.

Who Needs a Panama Crypto License?

Any entity that provides virtual asset services in or from Panama needs a license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralized and peer-to-peer).
  • Wallet providers (custodial and non-custodial if they hold keys).
  • Payment processors handling crypto.
  • OTC desks.
  • Token issuers (if they provide secondary market services).

If you are moving an existing crypto business that offers any of these services, you must apply for a VASP license. Panama does not distinguish between different service types under one license, but the regulator may impose conditions based on your business model. Non-custodial services (like a non-custodial wallet) may not require a license, but legal advice is recommended. For example, a non-custodial wallet provider that never holds private keys is generally outside AML obligated-entity scope, whereas a service that holds funds or facilitates transfers should maintain an AML/CTF programme. Always clarify with a compliance expert.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator for crypto licensing in Panama is the Panama's AML framework. It oversees non-financial entities, including VASPs, under Law 23 of 2015 (amended) and Executive Decree 163 of 2020. The license is a single category: Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP).

There is no separate license for exchanges vs. wallets. However, banking partners may require additional compliance measures depending on the services offered. The license is not a MiCA-equivalent; Panama is not an EU member. For EU operations, consider Lithuania or Estonia. Panama's regulatory framework is lighter than MiCA, which means lower compliance costs but also less harmonization. Beneficial-owner background checks (KYC) are standard for company formation and banking. Directors and shareholders must provide police clearance certificates and proof of funds.

Capital Requirement

Panama does not impose a minimum capital requirement for VASP licenses. However, banks and payment partners expect the company to have adequate capital to cover operational risks. In practice, most applicants set up with a minimum paid-in capital of USD 10,000-50,000. This is significantly lower than EU jurisdictions under MiCA, which require EUR 50,000 to 150,000 depending on service class.

If you are moving from an EU country, you may need to adjust your capital structure. Panama's flexibility is a key advantage for startups and SMEs. For example, a small exchange can start with USD 20,000 in capital, while a larger platform might need USD 100,000 to demonstrate financial stability. The capital must be deposited in a Panama bank account and can be used for operational expenses. There is no fixed capital requirement, but banks may review your capital adequacy during onboarding.

Tax Treatment in Panama

Panama operates a territorial tax system. Only income sourced within Panama is taxed. For a crypto business, this means:

  • Gains from trading crypto on international exchanges are generally tax-free.
  • Income from services provided to non-Panamanian clients is not taxed.
  • No capital gains tax on crypto assets held as investments.
  • No withholding tax on dividends paid to non-resident shareholders.
  • Corporate income tax rate: 25% on Panama-sourced income only.

This makes Panama one of the most tax-efficient jurisdictions for crypto businesses. However, you must file annual tax returns and maintain proper accounting. Transfer pricing rules apply if you transact with related parties. Consult a tax advisor for your specific situation. For example, if your Panama company earns fees from a US client, those fees are not Panama-sourced and thus tax-free. But if you have employees in Panama, their salaries are subject to payroll taxes. Also, Panama has a 7% VAT (ITBMS) on services provided locally, but exports of services are zero-rated.

Allowed Activities Under the Panama Crypto License

The Panama VASP license covers a broad range of activities:

  • Exchange of virtual assets for fiat or other virtual assets.
  • Transfer of virtual assets (custodial and non-custodial).
  • Custody and wallet services.
  • Payment services using virtual assets.
  • Participation in ICOs/STOs (if acting as a platform).

Activities that are not allowed without additional licensing: banking, securities brokerage, and derivatives trading. If your business involves these, you may need a separate license from the Superintendencia del Mercado de Valores (SMV). For example, offering crypto futures or options would likely trigger securities regulation. Similarly, if you lend customer crypto and pay interest, that may be considered a banking activity. Banks and payment partners typically restrict certain high-risk activities like privacy coins or mixers. Always check with the regulator before expanding services.

Banking and Payment Integration

Opening a corporate bank account in Panama for a crypto business is challenging but possible. Traditional banks often reject crypto-related companies due to perceived risk. Recommended approaches:

  • Use a crypto-friendly local bank (e.g., Banco General, but subject to compliance).
  • Consider international payment processors like Airwallex or Currencycloud.
  • Use a neobank or EMI (e.g., Mercury, Brex) for USD accounts.
  • Use stablecoin rails for operational liquidity.

Consulting24 can recommend banking partners based on your business profile. Expect to provide detailed AML policies and proof of license application. Some banks require a minimum deposit of USD 5,000-10,000 and may charge monthly fees of USD 50-200. For payment integration, you can use fiat on-ramps like MoonPay or Simplex, or crypto payment gateways like Coinbase Commerce. Panama's use of USD as legal tender eliminates currency risk. If you need multi-currency accounts, consider EMIs that support EUR, GBP, and USD.

Benefits of Moving to Panama

Panama offers several advantages for crypto businesses:

  • Low cost: flat EUR 6,000 company setup and license support.
  • No minimum capital requirement.
  • Territorial tax: no tax on foreign-source income.
  • No capital gains tax on crypto.
  • Stable USD-pegged economy (Panama uses USD as legal tender).
  • Strategic location with time zone close to US.
  • Growing crypto ecosystem with supportive regulator.

Compared to EU jurisdictions like Lithuania or Estonia, Panama is more cost-effective and tax-friendly. However, it lacks the regulatory clarity of MiCA. For businesses targeting EU customers, a dual structure (Panama + Lithuania) may be optimal. Panama also offers a fast incorporation process (1-2 weeks) and no foreign exchange controls. You can repatriate profits freely. The country has a modern legal system based on civil law, and English is widely used in business.

Compliance and Trust Considerations

Compliance is critical for a successful migration. Panama requires:

  • Appointment of a local AML compliance officer.
  • Registration with the Financial Analysis Unit (UAF).
  • Implementation of KYC, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting.
  • Annual audit by a certified public accountant.

Failure to comply can result in fines or license revocation. This is general guidance, not legal advice. Consult a compliance specialist. Panama is tightening AML enforcement generally, so maintaining strong compliance is essential for trust with partners and banks. For example, you must file suspicious transaction reports (STRs) within 24 hours of detection. The UAF may conduct periodic inspections. Non-compliance can lead to fines of up to USD 1 million or imprisonment for serious violations. To build trust, consider obtaining an ISO 27001 certification for information security and undergoing a voluntary audit by a reputable firm.

Common Mistakes When Moving a Crypto Business to Panama

Avoid these pitfalls:

  • Underestimating the timeline: license approval can take 6 months or more.
  • Insufficient AML documentation: banks and compliance reviews expect detailed policies.
  • Ignoring tax residency: if you are a director, you may become tax resident in Panama.
  • Not planning for banking: start the bank account process early.
  • Assuming no ongoing costs: annual compliance and registered agent fees apply.
  • Failing to notify existing customers of the migration.

Learn from others' experiences by reading our blog for case studies. Another common mistake is not properly structuring the ownership. If you are a US citizen, you may still be subject to US tax on worldwide income, regardless of where the company is based. Also, some founders try to operate without a license, which can lead to fines or closure. Always apply for the license before offering services from Panama. Finally, do not neglect data privacy: Panama has a data protection law (Law 81 of 2019) that requires registration of databases and consent for processing personal data.

Frequently asked questions

Can I move my existing crypto business to Panama without a license?

No. Panama does not currently require a VASP licence, so there is no crypto licence to obtain; you operate through a Panama company with an AML/CTF programme. Operating without a license can result in fines or closure. Non-custodial services may be exempt, but legal advice is recommended.

How long does it take to get a Panama crypto license?

The typical timeline is 3-6 months from application to approval. This includes company incorporation (1-2 weeks), document preparation, and regulatory review. Delays can occur if documents are incomplete or if background checks take longer.

What is the total cost for moving a crypto business to Panama?

Consulting24's flat fee is EUR 6,000, which includes company incorporation and license application support. Government fees are approximately USD 1,500-2,000. Total estimated cost is EUR 7,500-8,000. Ongoing costs include annual fees and compliance expenses.

Is Panama tax-free for crypto businesses?

Panama has a territorial tax system: only income sourced in Panama is taxed. Crypto gains from international activities are generally tax-free. However, you must file annual returns and pay 25% corporate tax on Panama-sourced income. No capital gains tax on crypto.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a Panama VASP license?

Panama does not impose a minimum capital requirement. However, banks and payment partners expect adequate capital for operations. Most applicants set up with USD 10,000-50,000. This is much lower than EU MiCA requirements.

Can I open a bank account in Panama for my crypto business?

Yes, but it is challenging. Traditional banks often reject crypto companies. Recommended options include crypto-friendly local banks, international payment processors, or neobanks. Consulting24 can provide introductions based on your profile.

What activities are allowed under the Panama VASP license?

The license covers exchange, transfer, custody, wallet services, and payment services using virtual assets. Banking, securities brokerage, and derivatives trading require separate licenses. Always check with the regulator for your specific services.

Do I need to be physically present in Panama to get a license?

No, you do not need to be physically present. However, you must appoint a local registered agent and a local AML compliance officer. Consulting24 can help arrange these services. Directors and shareholders can be non-residents.

How does Panama compare to EU jurisdictions under MiCA?

Panama is cheaper (EUR 6,000 vs. EUR 5,000-10,000), has no minimum capital, and offers territorial tax (0% on foreign income). However, it does not provide EU passporting. For EU market access, Lithuania or Estonia are better. A dual structure may be optimal.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations after getting the license?

You must appoint an AML compliance officer, register with the UAF, implement KYC and transaction monitoring, file annual reports, and undergo annual audits. Non-compliance can lead to fines or license revocation. Ongoing costs include registered agent fees and compliance officer salary.

Can I migrate my existing users and smart contracts to Panama?

Yes, but it requires careful planning. You need to update terms of service, notify users, and possibly redeploy smart contracts. Ensure continuity of service during the migration. Consulting24 can help coordinate the transition.

Is Panama a good jurisdiction for DeFi protocols?

Panama can be suitable for DeFi protocols that provide custodial services. Non-custodial protocols may not require a license, but legal advice is recommended. Panama's tax regime is favorable for international DeFi projects. However, regulatory clarity is limited compared to some EU jurisdictions.
