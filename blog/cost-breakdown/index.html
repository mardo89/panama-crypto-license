What Is the Panama Crypto License Cost?

The Panama crypto license cost refers to the total expense of incorporating a Panamanian company that can legally operate a crypto-related business. Unlike many jurisdictions that require separate licensing fees, Panama uses a corporate structure with a defined activity clause. The setup cost is a flat EUR 6,000, which includes:

  • Company formation and registration with the Public Registry
  • Notarization of documents for one person
  • Appointment of two directors (as required by law)
  • Introductions to seven payment providers for banking and payment processing
  • Registered agent service for the first year

Additional persons beyond the first cost EUR 250 each. This all-inclusive pricing makes the Panama crypto license cost one of the most transparent in the world. There are no surprise fees for document preparation or filing. The fee covers everything needed to get your company operational.

For example, if you need three directors instead of two, the total setup cost becomes EUR 6,250. If you also require notarization for two people, that is EUR 250 extra. These add-ons are clearly priced, so you can budget accurately. Most founders find the standard package sufficient.

Who Needs a Panama Crypto License?

Panama's corporate framework is ideal for founders who want to operate a crypto business with minimal regulatory overhead. You might need a Panama crypto license if you are:

  • Starting a crypto exchange or trading platform
  • Launching a crypto wallet or payment processor
  • Providing crypto custody services
  • Building a DeFi platform or tokenization project
  • Offering blockchain consulting or development services
  • Operating a crypto mining or staking operation

Panama does not require a separate license for most crypto activities as long as the company's purpose clause includes digital assets. This makes it a fast and affordable option compared to jurisdictions like Lithuania or Estonia, where specific licenses with higher capital requirements apply. For example, a Lithuanian CASP license requires EUR 125,000 in capital and takes months to obtain. In Panama, you can be operational in weeks with no capital outlay.

However, if your business involves holding fiat currency for customers (e-money), you may need additional licensing from the Superintendencia de Bancos. For pure crypto-to-crypto activities, Panama is sufficient.

License Type and Regulator

Panama does not have a dedicated crypto license per se. Instead, you incorporate a Panamanian corporation (Sociedad Anónima) with a corporate purpose that includes digital assets, virtual currencies, or blockchain services. The regulator is the Panama's AML supervisor for non-financial businesses, which oversees non-financial entities for AML compliance. However, for pure crypto activities (not involving fiat custody), no specific regulatory approval is needed. This structure offers flexibility and speed.

The corporate purpose can be broad, such as "to engage in the exchange, custody, and management of digital assets, cryptocurrencies, and blockchain technology." This allows you to pivot or expand your services without amending the articles. Panama's AML supervisor for non-financial businesses may apply only if your activity falls into a supervised category (casinos, real-estate, etc.); for most crypto startups this is not triggered.

Capital Requirement

Panama has no minimum capital requirement for a standard corporation. You can start with a nominal capital of USD 10,000 (often stated in the articles) but without any need to deposit or prove the funds. This zero-capital requirement is a major advantage over EU jurisdictions where MiCA imposes capital tiers of EUR 50,000, 125,000, or 150,000 depending on services offered. Panama's flexibility allows founders to allocate capital to business growth rather than regulatory compliance.

For example, if you are launching a crypto exchange, you would need EUR 125,000 in capital in Lithuania. In Panama, you can start with EUR 0 in capital and use that money for marketing, development, or liquidity. This is especially beneficial for early-stage startups that need to preserve cash.

There is also no requirement to maintain a minimum balance in the corporate bank account. You can deposit only what you need for operations.

Tax Treatment

Panama operates on a territorial tax system: income earned outside Panama is not taxed. For crypto businesses that serve international clients, this can result in zero corporate income tax. There is no VAT on services, and no capital gains tax on crypto transactions. The only annual cost is the franchise tax of approximately USD 300. This tax regime is highly attractive compared to jurisdictions like Malta or Switzerland, where corporate taxes can reach 12-18% even with incentives.

To benefit from territorial taxation, your business must ensure that all income-generating activities occur outside Panama. This is straightforward for online crypto businesses with no physical presence in Panama. You should maintain records showing that clients are abroad and that management decisions are made outside Panama. Consulting24 can advise on structuring to optimize tax efficiency.

There are no withholding taxes on dividends paid to non-resident shareholders, and no estate or inheritance taxes. Panama also does not tax foreign-source income, even if remitted to Panama.

Allowed Activities

A Panamanian corporation with a broad crypto purpose clause can engage in the following activities:

  • Cryptocurrency exchange (centralized or peer-to-peer)

However, activities that involve holding fiat currency for customers (e-money) may require additional licensing from the Superintendencia de Bancos. Similarly, if you offer services to Panamanian residents, you may need to comply with local regulations. Most pure crypto activities fall outside this scope. Step-by-Step Process to Obtain a Panama Crypto License

  1. Choose a company name and check availability with the Public Registry. We recommend having 2-3 alternatives.
  2. Engage a registered agent (included in our EUR 6,000 fee) to prepare incorporation documents.
  3. Draft the corporate purpose to include digital asset activities. We provide a template that covers common crypto activities.
