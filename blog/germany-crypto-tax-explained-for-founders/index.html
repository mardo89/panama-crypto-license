What Is Germany Crypto Tax?

Germany crypto tax explained means understanding how the German tax system treats gains from cryptocurrency transactions. For private individuals (non-professional traders), capital gains from selling crypto are tax-free if held for more than one year. If sold within one year, gains are subject to personal income tax rates (up to 45% plus solidarity surcharge). For business entities, crypto gains are treated as ordinary business income and taxed at corporate rates (15% plus trade tax, typically 30-33% effective). Mining, staking, and lending income are also taxable depending on the activity and status of the taxpayer.

Key definitions: "Private sale" refers to occasional trading; "professional trading" applies if you trade frequently or with borrowed funds. The tax office uses criteria like transaction volume, use of derivatives, and intention to profit. Founders must decide whether to operate as a private investor or through a corporate structure, as each path has distinct tax implications.

For example, a founder who trades crypto daily on margin is likely classified as a professional trader, meaning all gains are immediately taxable as business income. In contrast, an investor who buys and holds for 18 months pays 0% tax on gains. Understanding this distinction is critical for tax planning. Additionally, losses from crypto can offset gains, but only if properly documented with transaction records, wallet addresses, and timestamps. The German tax office requires detailed reporting for each transaction, which can be burdensome for high-frequency traders.

License Type and Regulator

Germany regulates crypto custody and trading under the Banking Act (Kreditwesengesetz - KWG) and the German Federal Financial Supervisory Authority (BaFin). As of 2026, the EU Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA) is fully in force, and BaFin acts as the competent authority for CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) licenses. The main license types are:

  • Crypto Custody License (BaFin): for safekeeping and administration of crypto assets.
  • Proprietary Trading License: for firms trading crypto for their own account.
  • Exchange License: for operating a trading platform.

Each license requires a minimum capital of EUR 50,000 (custody), EUR 125,000 (exchange), or EUR 150,000 (combined services). The application process is rigorous, taking 6-12 months. Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, capital requirements for German CASP licenses are tiered by service class:

  • EUR 50,000 for custody and transfer services.
  • EUR 125,000 for exchange and trading services.
  • EUR 150,000 for combined services (e.g., custody + exchange).

Capital must be held in liquid assets and maintained throughout the license period. For founders, this is a significant upfront cost. In contrast, Panama has no minimum capital requirement for crypto license, making it more accessible for startups. Germany crypto tax explained for founders: corporate tax rates are 15% federal plus trade tax (14-17% average), resulting in an effective rate of 30-33%. VAT is generally 19% on services, but crypto trading may be exempt under certain conditions. For private investors, the one-year holding period rule applies. Staking and lending income is treated as miscellaneous income and taxed at personal rates. Losses from crypto can offset gains, but strict documentation is required.

Founders should consider structuring their business to minimize tax. For example, holding crypto in a Panama company can defer German taxation until repatriation. Allowed Activities

A German CASP license permits the following activities under MiCA:

  • Custody and administration of crypto assets.
  • Operation of a trading platform.
  • Exchange of crypto for fiat or other crypto.
  • Execution of orders on behalf of clients.
  • Placement of crypto assets.
  • Transfer services for crypto assets.
  • Advice on crypto assets.
  • Portfolio management of crypto assets.

Each activity requires separate authorization and capital allocation. Founders must specify their service scope in the application. Frequently asked questions

What is the holding period for tax-free gains in Germany?

For private investors, gains from selling crypto are tax-free if held for more than one year. If sold within one year, gains are taxed at personal income tax rates (up to 45% plus solidarity surcharge). Professional traders and businesses do not benefit from this holding period.

Are staking and lending income taxable in Germany?

Yes, staking and lending income is treated as miscellaneous income for private investors and taxed at personal rates. For businesses, it is ordinary business income. You must report all rewards and interest in your tax return.

Can I offset crypto losses against gains in Germany?

Yes, losses from crypto can offset gains from the same type of income. For private investors, losses are limited to EUR 20,000 per year and can be carried forward. Proper documentation is essential.

What is the corporate tax rate for crypto companies in Germany?

The effective corporate tax rate is 30-33%, consisting of 15% federal tax plus trade tax (14-17% depending on municipality). This applies to all business income, including crypto gains.

Do I need a German crypto license to operate a crypto exchange?

Yes, operating a crypto exchange in Germany requires a BaFin license under MiCA. The minimum capital is EUR 125,000 for exchange services. Without a license, you risk fines and criminal penalties.

How long does it take to get a German crypto license?

The process typically takes 6-12 months, including preparation, documentation, and BaFin review. Faster alternatives like Panama (4-6 weeks) or Lithuania (3-6 months) are available.

Is Panama a better option than Germany for crypto licensing?

Panama offers a flat fee of EUR 6,000, no minimum capital, 0% tax on foreign-source income, and a 4-6 week timeline. It is ideal for international businesses not targeting the EU market. Germany offers EU passporting but at higher cost and time.

What are the ongoing compliance costs for a German crypto license?

Annual compliance costs range from EUR 10,000 to EUR 30,000, including AML officer fees, auditing, and reporting. This is higher than Panama, where ongoing costs are minimal.

Can I use a German crypto license to serve customers in other EU countries?

Yes, under MiCA, a German CASP license allows you to passport services across all EU member states without additional licensing. This is a key advantage over non-EU jurisdictions.

What happens if I trade crypto as a private investor but the tax office deems me a professional trader?

If the tax office reclassifies you as a professional trader, all gains become immediately taxable as business income, and you lose the one-year holding period benefit. You may also face penalties for underreporting.

Does Germany tax crypto-to-crypto trades?

Yes, crypto-to-crypto trades are taxable events. The gain is calculated as the difference between the fair market value at the time of trade and the acquisition cost. This applies to both private and professional traders.
