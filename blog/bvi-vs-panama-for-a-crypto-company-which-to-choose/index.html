Overview: BVI vs Panama for Crypto Companies

The British Virgin Islands (BVI) and Panama are both renowned offshore financial centres. BVI has long been a favourite for holding companies and investment funds, while Panama offers a strong services sector and the US dollar as its official currency. For crypto companies, the key differences lie in licensing requirements, capital costs, tax treatment, and the ease of opening bank accounts.

In BVI, crypto activities fall under the Securities and Investment Business Act (SIBA) and the Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) regime, which is still evolving. Panama, by contrast, has a more established crypto licensing framework under the Superintendencia de Bancos de Panamá (SBP) and the Superintendencia de Valores (SMV). As of 2026, Panama offers a flat fee of EUR 6,000 for a crypto license, making it one of the most cost-effective regulated options globally.

Both jurisdictions are tax-neutral, but Panama's regulatory clarity and lower costs often tip the balance for crypto startups. BVI remains popular for fund structures and holding companies, but for active crypto service providers, Panama's framework is more straightforward.

Who Needs a Crypto License in BVI or Panama?

Any business that provides virtual asset services - such as exchange, custody, transfer, or payment processing - needs a license in both jurisdictions. The specific trigger activities include:

  • Operating a cryptocurrency exchange (centralised or decentralised)
  • Providing custodial wallet services
  • Facilitating crypto-to-fiat conversions
  • Running an initial coin offering (ICO) or security token offering (STO)
  • Offering crypto lending or staking services

If your business involves any of these, you must obtain the appropriate license. In BVI, the Financial Services Commission (FSC) oversees VASPs. In Panama, the SBP and SMV share regulatory oversight depending on the activity. It is important to note that even if your business is based elsewhere, you may need a license in the jurisdiction where your customers are located. Both BVI and Panama require a local presence, so you must have a registered office and, in Panama, a local agent.

Panama’s framework is more streamlined, with a single flat fee and clear application process. Both regulators expect strong compliance measures, but Panama’s process is generally faster and more predictable.

For BVI, the FSC has issued guidance on VASPs but has not yet finalised a dedicated regulatory framework. This creates some uncertainty. Panama, on the other hand, has a clear law (Law 23 of 2015, as amended) that covers virtual asset service providers. The regulator publishes a list of licensed entities, adding transparency.

Capital Requirement

BVI does not prescribe a statutory minimum capital for VASPs, but the FSC expects a company to have sufficient financial resources to cover operational and compliance costs. In practice, a minimum of USD 50,000 is often recommended to demonstrate substance.

Panama, on the other hand, has no explicit minimum capital requirement for crypto licensees under the flat-fee regime. However, the regulator expects the company to maintain adequate funds for its activities. This makes Panama more accessible for startups with limited capital.

For comparison, EU jurisdictions under MiCA require minimum capital of EUR 50,000 to EUR 150,000 depending on the service. Panama's lack of a fixed minimum is a significant advantage for early-stage crypto companies.

Tax Treatment

Both BVI and Panama are tax-neutral jurisdictions. BVI imposes no corporate income tax, no capital gains tax, and no withholding tax on dividends or interest. Panama taxes only income sourced within Panama (territorial taxation), meaning crypto profits from international clients are generally tax-free. However, Panama does have a 7% VAT on local services, and companies must file annual tax returns. Neither jurisdiction taxes cryptocurrency gains if the income is sourced outside the country.

For crypto companies, this tax treatment is highly favourable. However, founders must consider their home country tax obligations. Consulting24 always recommends consulting a tax advisor to ensure compliance with global tax rules.

It is worth noting that Panama has a network of double taxation treaties, though limited. BVI has no treaties. Both jurisdictions require economic substance, meaning you must have real operations (office, staff) to benefit from the tax regime.

Allowed Activities Under Each License

In BVI, the VASP license permits exchange, custody, transfer, and payment services. It does not cover derivatives or securities unless the company also holds a SIBA license for securities. In Panama, the crypto license covers a broader range of activities, including exchange, custody, payment processing, and token issuance. Panama’s framework is more flexible, allowing businesses to scale without needing multiple licenses.

Both jurisdictions prohibit unlicensed money transmission and require AML programs. Panama’s license explicitly permits fiat on-ramps and off-ramps, which is a significant advantage for exchange platforms.

For example, a Panama-licensed exchange can offer crypto-to-fiat trading pairs directly, while a BVI-licensed exchange may need additional approvals. This makes Panama more attractive for retail-facing platforms.

Banking and Payment Services

Access to banking is a major challenge for crypto companies. In BVI, many international banks are hesitant to open accounts for VASPs due to perceived risks. Some local banks accept crypto clients but require high minimum balances and rigorous due diligence.

Panama has a more developed banking sector that is generally open to licensed crypto companies. The country’s use of the US dollar eliminates currency risk. Several Panamanian banks offer accounts to crypto license holders, and payment processors like Nequi and Yappy facilitate local transactions. For BVI, we can recommend banks that accept VASPs, but options are limited. Panama's banking landscape is more favourable, with multiple banks actively seeking licensed fintech clients. Additionally, Panama has a growing network of crypto-friendly payment gateways.

Benefits of Panama Over BVI for Crypto

  • Lower cost: Flat EUR 6,000 license fee vs BVI’s variable and higher costs.
  • Faster timeline: 3-6 months vs 6-12 months.
  • No minimum capital: More accessible for startups.
  • Better banking: Easier to open accounts.
  • US dollar economy: Removes forex risk.
  • Clearer regulation: Panama’s framework is more mature and predictable.

For these reasons, Panama is often the preferred choice for crypto founders seeking a cost-effective, regulated base. BVI may still be suitable for specific structures, such as investment funds or holding companies, but for active crypto service providers, Panama offers a better value proposition.

Compliance and Trust Considerations

Both jurisdictions require strong AML/KYC programs. Panama’s regulator expects a compliance officer, transaction monitoring, and regular reporting. Trust is built through transparency. Panama publishes a list of licensed VASPs, which adds credibility. BVI does not have a public register. For businesses that need to demonstrate regulatory compliance to partners or customers, Panama's transparency is a clear advantage.

Common Mistakes When Choosing Between BVI and Panama

Founders often make these errors:

  • Ignoring substance requirements: Both jurisdictions expect real operations (office, staff). A mailbox company will not pass due diligence.
  • Underestimating compliance costs: Ongoing AML monitoring and audits can be significant. Budget at least EUR 5,000-10,000 annually for compliance in Panama.
  • Choosing based solely on tax: While both are tax-friendly, banking access and regulatory clarity matter more for crypto.
  • Not checking home country tax: Your personal tax residency may trigger additional obligations. For example, US citizens must report worldwide income regardless of where the company is based.
  • Overlooking the timeline: BVI can take a year or more, which may delay your go-to-market. Panama is faster.

Consulting24 advises clients to evaluate all factors before deciding. A consultation can help you avoid these pitfalls.

Frequently asked questions

What is the main difference between BVI and Panama for a crypto company?

The main difference is cost and regulatory clarity. Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 license fee, no minimum capital, and a faster 3-6 month timeline. BVI has variable costs, typically higher, and a longer 6-12 month timeline. Panama's framework is more mature and predictable, making it easier for startups.

Is Panama's crypto license recognised internationally?

Panama's license is issued by the SBP and SMV, which are reputable regulators. It is recognised by international banks and partners, though acceptance varies. Panama is not on the FATF grey list as of 2026, which adds credibility. However, you should verify with your specific counterparties.

Can I operate a crypto exchange from BVI without a license?

No. BVI requires a VASP license under SIBA for any virtual asset service. Operating without a license can lead to penalties and reputational damage. Always obtain the proper license before offering services.

What are the ongoing compliance costs for a Panama crypto license?

Annual renewal fees are EUR 3,000-5,000. Additionally, you must maintain a compliance officer (internal or outsourced), conduct regular AML audits, and file reports. Total ongoing compliance costs typically range from EUR 8,000-15,000 per year, depending on transaction volume.

Does Panama require a physical office for a crypto license?

Yes. Panama requires a registered office and a local agent. The regulator expects substance, meaning you should have a physical presence (office, staff) to demonstrate real operations. A virtual office may not suffice.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Panama?

Typically 3-6 months from application submission. The timeline depends on the completeness of your application and the regulator's workload. Consulting24 can help expedite the process by preparing all documents correctly upfront.

Can I use Panama as a base for a global crypto business?

Yes. Panama's territorial tax system means only Panama-sourced income is taxed, so profits from international clients are tax-free. Combined with the US dollar economy and good banking access, Panama is an excellent base for global crypto operations.

What happens if I don't meet the substance requirements in BVI or Panama?

Both regulators can revoke your license or impose fines if you fail to demonstrate substance. This includes having a physical office, local staff, and active operations. A shell company will not pass regulatory scrutiny.

Is BVI suitable for a crypto hedge fund?

BVI is more suited for investment funds and holding structures due to its established fund regime. For active crypto trading or exchange services, Panama is generally more practical due to lower costs and clearer regulation.

Can Consulting24 help with BVI licensing?

Consulting24 advises and coordinates for BVI licensing but does not deliver directly. We work with local partners to handle the application. For direct delivery, we recommend Panama, Estonia, or Lithuania.

What are the tax implications for US citizens using Panama?

US citizens are taxed on worldwide income regardless of where the company is based. Panama's territorial tax does not exempt US citizens from US taxes. You should consult a US tax advisor to understand your obligations.

How does Panama compare to EU jurisdictions under MiCA?

EU jurisdictions under MiCA require minimum capital of EUR 50,000-150,000 and have stricter compliance requirements. Panama has no minimum capital and lower costs, making it more accessible. However, EU licenses may offer broader recognition within the EU market.
