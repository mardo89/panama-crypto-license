Overview: Malta vs Panama for Crypto

Malta was an early mover in crypto regulation, enacting the Virtual Financial Assets (VFA) Act in 2018. The Malta Financial Services Authority (MFSA) oversees VFA service providers. Panama, on the other hand, has no specific crypto law but operates a business-friendly environment with no central bank restrictions on crypto activities. Both jurisdictions offer unique advantages, but the choice depends on your business model, target market, and compliance appetite.

Malta is an EU member state, meaning it must align with MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation), which is fully in force in 2026. This imposes capital requirements of EUR 50,000 to EUR 150,000 depending on services. Panama is not in the EU and offers more flexibility, with no minimum capital for crypto activities and a flat EUR 6,000 company setup fee through Consulting24. For founders who need to serve EU clients, Malta provides a regulated pathway with passporting rights. For those seeking speed and low overhead, Panama is often the better choice.

Who Needs a Malta or Panama Crypto License?

If you plan to operate a crypto exchange, wallet service, or custodial service within the EU, Malta's VFA license is a strong option. It provides regulatory clarity and passporting rights across the EU under MiCA. This is ideal for companies targeting European clients who require a licensed counterparty. For example, a European crypto exchange that wants to offer trading to German, French, and Spanish users would benefit from Malta's license.

Panama is better suited for founders who want a cost-effective, fast setup with minimal ongoing compliance. It works well for crypto trading firms, proprietary trading, or businesses that do not require an EU passport. Panama is also a hub for Latin American markets. A typical client is a crypto asset manager serving non-EU clients, or a DeFi protocol that wants a corporate wrapper without heavy regulation. Consulting24 helps you evaluate your needs. If your primary market is the EU, Malta may be worth the higher cost. If you need speed and low overhead, Panama is often the better choice.

License Type and Regulator

Malta: The regulator is the Malta Financial Services Authority (MFSA). The license is a VFA Service Provider license under the VFA Act. It covers exchange, wallet, and custodial services. Since MiCA is fully in force, Maltese VFA licenses are now MiCA-compliant, allowing passporting across the EU. The MFSA also supervises compliance with AML/CFT rules and requires regular reporting.

Panama: There is no specific crypto license. The Panama approach is to use a standard international corporation (Sociedad Anónima) with a corporate purpose that includes crypto activities. The regulator is the Superintendencia de Bancos de Panamá for banking, but crypto businesses are not directly supervised. Consulting24 sets up the company with a compliant structure, including AML policies if the business involves financial intermediation. This means Panama offers more operational freedom but less regulatory clarity.

Capital Requirement

Malta: Under MiCA, capital requirements are tiered: EUR 50,000 for basic services (e.g., wallet), EUR 125,000 for exchange services, and EUR 150,000 for custodial services. These must be maintained as liquid capital. The capital must be held in a bank account and cannot be used for operational expenses. This is a significant upfront cost for startups.

Panama: No minimum capital requirement for crypto activities. The standard paid-in capital for a Panama corporation is USD 10,000 (approx EUR 9,000), but this can be lower. There is no regulatory capital tied to crypto services. This means you can allocate more funds to business development. For many startups, Panama's zero capital requirement is a major advantage. However, if you need to hold client assets, Malta's capital requirements provide credibility and trust with partners.

Tax Treatment

Malta: Corporate tax rate is 35%, but effective rates can be as low as 5% through refund mechanisms. Crypto gains are treated as income or capital gains depending on the activity. VAT is not charged on crypto-to-fiat exchanges. Malta has a wide double tax treaty network, which can reduce withholding taxes on dividends and interest. However, the high nominal rate may deter some founders.

Panama: Territorial taxation: only income sourced in Panama is taxed. Crypto trading profits from outside Panama are tax-free. No capital gains tax, no VAT on crypto. Panama also has no central bank restrictions on crypto. This makes Panama highly attractive for tax optimization. For example, a crypto trading firm that generates profits from international clients would pay zero tax in Panama. Consulting24 recommends Panama for founders seeking tax efficiency. Malta is better if you need EU market access despite higher tax.

Allowed Activities

  • Malta: VFA exchange, wallet, custodial services, investment advice in VFAs, portfolio management. Activities must be pre-approved by MFSA. Prohibited: unlicensed ICOs, derivatives on VFAs without additional licenses. The MFSA also restricts certain DeFi activities unless they fit within the VFA framework.
  • Panama: All crypto activities are permitted: exchange, trading, mining, wallet, DeFi, NFT marketplace, payment processing. No specific restrictions, but businesses must comply with AML laws if engaging in financial intermediation. This flexibility allows innovative models like DAOs and tokenized asset platforms. However, the lack of regulation means you have less legal protection in disputes.

Panama offers more flexibility for innovative business models. Malta is more restrictive but provides legal certainty.

Banking and Payments

Malta: Banks are cautious but some offer accounts for licensed VFA firms. Revolut and other fintechs also serve Maltese companies. Payment processing is straightforward within SEPA. However, banks may require additional compliance checks, and account opening can take 1-2 months. Some traditional banks still avoid crypto, so fintech solutions are common.

Panama: Banking is more accessible. Many international banks accept Panama corporations. However, crypto-related accounts may still face scrutiny. Consulting24 assists with bank introductions. Panama uses the US dollar as official currency, eliminating FX risk. This is a major advantage for global operations. Some banks in Panama have specific crypto-friendly policies, but you should expect to provide a detailed business plan and proof of source of funds. Both jurisdictions have challenges, but Panama's dollarized economy is a plus.

Compliance and Trust

Malta: High compliance burden: ongoing AML/CFT reporting, audited financial statements, and regular MFSA supervision. This builds trust with partners and clients. Malta is not on the FATF grey list and is considered a compliant jurisdiction. The MFSA conducts on-site inspections and requires annual audits. This level of oversight can be a selling point for institutional clients.

Panama: Lower compliance requirements. No specific crypto oversight, but general AML laws apply. Panama was previously on FATF grey lists but has been removed as of 2024. Some partners may view Panama as higher risk due to lack of regulation. However, for many crypto businesses, this is acceptable. You should still implement strong AML policies to protect your business. This is general guidance, not legal advice. Consulting24 recommends consulting a lawyer for your specific situation.

Common Mistakes

  • Choosing Malta without EU market need: If your clients are not in the EU, the high cost may not be justified. You could use Panama and save significantly.
  • Underestimating Panama's banking challenges: Some banks reject crypto-related companies. Prepare a solid business plan and consider using fintech banks like Mercury or Brex.
  • Ignoring tax implications in home country: Even if Panama does not tax foreign income, your home country may. Get cross-border tax advice to avoid double taxation.
  • Not checking MiCA passporting details: Malta's license allows EU-wide services, but you must comply with each country's marketing rules. For example, Germany requires additional notifications.
  • Overlooking ongoing compliance costs: Malta's annual compliance fees can be EUR 10,000 or more. Factor this into your budget.

Frequently asked questions

What is the main difference between Malta and Panama for a crypto company?

Malta offers an EU-regulated VFA license with MiCA passporting, higher costs (EUR 50,000+ capital), and longer timelines (6-12 months). Panama provides a fast, low-cost setup (EUR 6,000 flat) with no specific crypto regulation and territorial tax, but no EU passport.

Which jurisdiction is cheaper for a crypto startup?

Panama is significantly cheaper. The flat fee is EUR 6,000 with no minimum capital. Malta requires EUR 50,000 to 150,000 in capital plus professional fees of EUR 10,000-30,000. Ongoing costs are also lower in Panama (EUR 1,000-2,000 per year vs EUR 5,000-15,000 in Malta).

Can I use a Panama company to serve EU clients?

Yes, but you must comply with each EU country's regulations. Without an EU license, you may be restricted from marketing to EU residents. Some EU countries require a local license or registration. Panama is best for non-EU clients.

Does Malta's VFA license allow passporting under MiCA?

Yes, as of 2026, Maltese VFA licenses are MiCA-compliant and allow passporting across the EU. This means you can provide services in any EU member state without additional licensing, but you must notify the local regulator.

What is the timeline for getting a Malta crypto license?

Typically 6-12 months from application to approval. The MFSA reviews applications thoroughly, and delays can occur if documents are incomplete. Pre-application meetings can help streamline the process.

Is Panama a tax haven for crypto companies?

Panama has territorial taxation, meaning only Panama-sourced income is taxed. Crypto profits from international clients are tax-free. However, your home country may still tax you. Always seek cross-border tax advice.

Can I open a bank account for a Panama crypto company?

Yes, but some banks are cautious about crypto. Consulting24 assists with bank introductions. Options include local Panama banks, international banks like Mercury, and fintech platforms. Expect to provide a detailed business plan.

What activities are allowed under a Malta VFA license?

Allowed activities include operating a VFA exchange, wallet services, custodial services, investment advice, and portfolio management. Activities must be pre-approved. Unlicensed ICOs and derivatives on VFAs are prohibited.

Does Panama have any compliance requirements for crypto?

Panama has general AML laws that apply to financial intermediation. If your business involves exchanging crypto for fiat or custody, you must register with the Superintendencia de Bancos and implement AML policies. Otherwise, no specific crypto compliance is required.

Which jurisdiction is better for a DeFi project?

Panama is more flexible for DeFi projects as there are no specific restrictions. Malta's VFA framework may not easily accommodate DeFi models. Panama allows you to operate a DAO or tokenized platform without regulatory hurdles.
