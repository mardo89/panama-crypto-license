What Is a Seychelles Crypto License?

A Seychelles crypto license, officially known as a Securities Dealer License (for digital assets) under the Securities Act 2007, or a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) registration under the Virtual Asset Service Provider Act 2024, allows companies to legally operate cryptocurrency exchanges, wallet services, and other crypto-related activities. The Seychelles Financial Services Authority (FSA) is the regulatory body overseeing these licenses. The process is designed to be efficient, but the exact duration depends on the license type and the quality of your application.

For most crypto founders, the VASP license is the most relevant option in 2026. It covers a range of activities including exchange, transfer, and custody of virtual assets. The timeline for approval typically ranges from 2 to 4 months for a straightforward application, but can extend to 6 months or more if additional information is required. The FSA has been modernizing its processes, and as of 2026, the average processing time for a complete VASP application is around 10-12 weeks. However, delays often occur due to incomplete documentation or the need for clarifications on the business model.

To put this in perspective, a Seychelles crypto license is generally faster than EU jurisdictions like Lithuania or Estonia, where MiCA-driven processes can take 6-12 months. However, it is slower than Panama, where company setup is a flat EUR 6,000 and no specific crypto license is required for certain activities. Consulting24 helps you weigh these trade-offs.

Who Needs a Seychelles Crypto License?

Any business offering virtual asset services to Seychellois residents or operating from within Seychelles must obtain a license. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (centralized and decentralized)
  • Wallet providers (custodial and non-custodial)
  • Payment processors using crypto
  • Token issuers (if the token is considered a security)
  • Brokers and dealers in digital assets
  • OTC trading desks
  • Staking and lending platforms (subject to additional approval)

If your target market includes Africa, Asia, or the Middle East, Seychelles can be a strategic base due to its time zone (UTC+4) and business-friendly environment. Many founders also choose Seychelles for its low tax regime and confidentiality. However, if your primary market is in the EU, you may need to consider a license in an EU member state such as Lithuania or Estonia to comply with MiCA. Capital Requirement

The Seychelles Financial Services Authority does not impose a statutory minimum capital requirement for VASP registrations in 2026. However, applicants must demonstrate sufficient financial resources to cover operational expenses and potential liabilities. In practice, the FSA expects a minimum paid-up capital of around EUR 50,000 for a standard VASP license, though this is not codified in law. The exact amount is determined on a case-by-case basis based on the business plan and risk assessment.

For a Securities Dealer License, the capital requirement is higher, typically EUR 100,000 or more. The FSA will review your financial projections to ensure you have enough runway for at least 12 months of operations. Consulting24 advises clients to prepare a detailed budget and financial projections to satisfy the FSA's requirements. For a fixed-price alternative with no specific capital requirement, consider Panama where the company setup cost is EUR 6,000 and no specific crypto capital requirement exists. However, note that Panama does not offer a dedicated crypto license, so your activities may be limited to non-regulated services.

Tax Treatment in Seychelles

Seychelles offers a highly favorable tax regime for crypto businesses. Key points:

  • No corporate income tax on offshore companies (International Business Company or IBC)
  • No capital gains tax
  • No withholding tax on dividends, interest, or royalties
  • VAT (GST) at 15% applies only to local services; crypto services to non-residents are exempt
  • No exchange controls

To benefit from the 0% corporate tax, the company must be structured as an IBC and not conduct business with Seychellois residents. This makes Seychelles an attractive base for international crypto operations. However, founders must ensure compliance with tax laws in their home country. For example, if you are a tax resident in a high-tax country, you may still be liable for taxes there. Consulting24 can coordinate with local tax advisors to structure your entity optimally. For a jurisdiction with a clear tax framework for crypto, consider Lithuania, which has a 15% corporate tax but offers double tax treaties with many countries.

Allowed Activities Under a Seychelles Crypto License

A Seychelles VASP license permits the following activities:

  • Operating a cryptocurrency exchange (order book, peer-to-peer, or automated market maker)
  • Providing wallet services (custodial and non-custodial)
  • Facilitating crypto-to-crypto and crypto-to-fiat conversions
  • Transferring virtual assets on behalf of clients
  • Staking and lending services (subject to additional approval)
  • OTC trading
  • Issuance of stablecoins (subject to FSA approval)

Activities not allowed without a separate license include issuing securities tokens (requires Securities Dealer License) and operating a crypto ATM network (requires specific authorization). The FSA regularly updates the list of permitted activities, so it is advisable to confirm with a compliance expert. Step-by-Step Process to Obtain a Seychelles Crypto License

The process can be broken down into the following steps:

  1. Company incorporation: Register a Seychelles IBC or local company. This takes 1-2 weeks. You will need a registered office and a local agent.
  2. Prepare documentation: Draft business plan, AML/KYC policies, risk assessment, and compliance manual. Engage a local compliance officer. This stage typically takes 2-4 weeks.
  3. Submit application: File the VASP registration with the FSA, including all supporting documents and the application fee. The FSA will acknowledge receipt within a few days.
  4. FSA review: The FSA reviews the application and may request additional information. This stage takes 4-8 weeks. In 2026, the FSA aims to process complete applications within 10 weeks.
  5. Approval & license issuance: Once satisfied, the FSA issues the license. You must then pay the annual license fee. The license is valid for one year and must be renewed annually.
  6. Post-licensing compliance: Submit periodic reports, conduct AML audits, and maintain a physical presence (registered office) in Seychelles. Consulting24 offers ongoing compliance support.

Total timeline: typically 2-4 months from incorporation to license issuance. For a faster alternative, consider Panama where company setup is a flat fee and no specific crypto license is required for certain activities. However, Panama may not provide the regulatory clarity that some partners require.

Banking & Payment Processing for Seychelles Crypto Businesses

Opening a bank account for a Seychelles crypto company can be challenging due to the perceived risk. However, several international banks and payment processors accept Seychelles entities, especially if the company holds a VASP license. Options include:

  • Banks in Seychelles (e.g., Bank of Baroda, Nouvobanq) with a local presence
  • EMI (Electronic Money Institutions) like TransferWise (now Wise), Revolut Business, or Payoneer
  • Cryptocurrency-friendly banks in Mauritius, Singapore, or the UAE
  • Payment processors like Stripe (for fiat) and BitPay (for crypto)

To open an account, you typically need to provide the license, business plan, AML policies, and proof of directors' identity. Some banks may require a minimum deposit or turnover. Compliance & Trust: What Regulators Expect

To maintain your Seychelles crypto license, you must adhere to ongoing compliance requirements:

  • Appoint a local compliance officer (can be outsourced)
  • Implement strong AML/KYC procedures
  • Submit annual audited financial statements
  • Report suspicious transactions to the FSA
  • Maintain a physical registered office in Seychelles
  • Renew the license annually and pay the fee
  • Undergo periodic inspections by the FSA

Failure to comply can result in fines or license revocation. In 2026, the FSA has increased its enforcement activities, so it is crucial to stay compliant. Common Mistakes to Avoid When Applying

Founders often make these errors, which delay the process:

  • Submitting incomplete documentation: missing business plan or AML policy
  • Underestimating the fit and proper test: directors with criminal records or poor credit history may be rejected
  • Choosing the wrong license type: applying for a Securities Dealer License when a VASP is sufficient
  • Not engaging a local agent: the FSA requires a licensed agent for submission
  • Ignoring post-licensing compliance: failing to submit reports on time
  • Overlooking the need for a local compliance officer: this is a mandatory requirement
  • Providing unrealistic financial projections: the FSA expects conservative estimates

Frequently asked questions

How long does a Seychelles crypto license take in 2026?

Typically 2-4 months from company incorporation to license issuance. The FSA aims to process complete VASP applications within 10 weeks. Delays can occur if documentation is incomplete or if the FSA requests additional information.

What is the cost of a Seychelles crypto license?

Total initial costs range from EUR 12,000 to EUR 28,000, including company incorporation, application fee, legal setup, and first-year license fee. Annual renewal costs are around EUR 5,000-10,000. Exact figures depend on your business complexity.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a Seychelles VASP license?

There is no statutory minimum, but the FSA expects around EUR 50,000 in paid-up capital to demonstrate financial stability. The amount is assessed case-by-case based on your business plan.

Can I operate a crypto exchange with a Seychelles license?

Yes, a VASP registration allows you to operate a cryptocurrency exchange, including order book, P2P, and AMM models. You can also offer wallet services, crypto-to-fiat conversions, and transfers.

Does Seychelles have corporate tax for crypto businesses?

If structured as an International Business Company (IBC) and not serving Seychellois residents, the corporate tax rate is 0%. There is no capital gains tax or withholding tax.

Do I need to live in Seychelles to get a license?

No, there is no residency requirement for directors or shareholders. However, you must appoint a local compliance officer and maintain a registered office in Seychelles.

How does Seychelles compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Panama offers a faster and cheaper company setup (EUR 6,000 flat) with no specific crypto license, but lacks regulatory clarity. Seychelles provides a regulated VASP license that may be required by partners or banks.

Can I use a Seychelles license to serve EU customers?

No, a Seychelles license does not grant passporting rights in the EU. To serve EU customers, you need a MiCA-compliant license from an EU member state like Lithuania or Estonia.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements for a Seychelles crypto license?

You must appoint a local compliance officer, implement AML/KYC procedures, submit annual audited financial statements, report suspicious transactions, and renew the license annually. Failure to comply can lead to fines or revocation.

Is it difficult to open a bank account for a Seychelles crypto company?

It can be challenging due to perceived risk, but having a VASP license helps. Options include local Seychelles banks, EMIs like Wise or Revolut, and crypto-friendly banks in Mauritius or Singapore. Consulting24 can assist with documentation.

What activities are not allowed under a Seychelles VASP license?

Issuing security tokens requires a separate Securities Dealer License. Operating crypto ATMs needs specific authorization. Staking and lending may require additional approval from the FSA.
