What Is the Crypto Company Tax Regime in Cyprus?

Cyprus does not have a separate tax regime for crypto companies. Instead, crypto businesses are taxed under the standard corporate tax framework, with specific clarifications from the Cyprus Tax Department. The key features include:

  • Corporate income tax: 12.5% on net profits, one of the lowest in the EU.
  • VAT: Crypto-to-fiat exchanges are subject to VAT at 19%, but crypto-to-crypto transactions are exempt under EU law (as per the Hedqvist ruling).
  • Capital gains tax: 0% on gains from disposal of shares, securities, and crypto assets held as investments (subject to conditions).
  • Withholding tax: 0% on dividends and interest paid to non-residents.

This regime makes Cyprus attractive for crypto founders, especially those involved in trading, investment, or holding crypto assets. However, the classification of crypto activities (e.g., mining, staking, DeFi) can affect tax treatment. For example, mining rewards are treated as trading income, while staking returns may be considered investment income. Consulting24 advises that you seek professional tax advice tailored to your specific business model. The Cyprus Tax Department has issued circulars on crypto taxation, but some areas remain grey. A tax ruling can provide certainty.

License Type and Regulator for Crypto Companies in Cyprus

Cyprus regulates crypto asset services under the Investment Services and Activities and Regulated Markets Law (Law 87(I)/2017), as amended to implement the EU's Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA). The regulator is the Cyprus Securities and Exchange Commission (CySEC). As of 2026, MiCA is fully in force, meaning all crypto asset service providers (CASPs) must obtain a license from CySEC. The license types include:

  • Class 1: Reception and transmission of orders, execution of orders on behalf of clients, dealing on own account.
  • Class 2: Portfolio management, investment advice, underwriting, placing.
  • Class 3: Operation of a multilateral trading facility (MTF) or organized trading facility (OTF).

For crypto-specific services (e.g., exchange, custody, transfer), MiCA introduces a harmonized CASP license. The minimum capital requirement under MiCA is EUR 50,000 for basic services, EUR 125,000 for custodians, and EUR 150,000 for exchange operators. CySEC oversees compliance with AML/CFT rules and ongoing reporting. The application process is rigorous, requiring detailed business plans, AML policies, and governance structures. Capital Requirements for Crypto Companies in Cyprus

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirements for CASPs in Cyprus are tiered by service type:

  • EUR 50,000 for services such as reception and transmission of orders, execution of orders, and dealing on own account.
  • EUR 125,000 for custody and administration of crypto assets.
  • EUR 150,000 for operating a trading platform (exchange).

These amounts must be maintained as own funds, and additional capital may be required based on operational risk. For companies that are not CASPs (e.g., pure investment holding), no specific capital requirement applies, but the standard company capital (typically EUR 1,000) is sufficient. However, if you plan to apply for a license later, it is wise to capitalise the company adequately from the start. Tax Treatment of Crypto Activities in Cyprus

The Cyprus Tax Department has issued guidelines on the tax treatment of crypto assets. Key points include:

  • Corporate tax: Profits from trading crypto as a business are subject to 12.5% corporate tax. Capital gains from disposal of crypto held as an investment are generally tax-free (0% capital gains tax).
  • VAT: Exchange of crypto for fiat is subject to VAT (19%), while crypto-to-crypto exchanges are exempt. Mining and staking rewards are considered income and subject to corporate tax.
  • Withholding tax: No withholding tax on dividends, interest, or royalties paid to non-residents.
  • Transfer pricing: Transactions with related parties must be at arm's length, with documentation required.
  • IP regime: 80% exemption on qualifying intellectual property income, which can apply to proprietary trading algorithms or software.

It is crucial to distinguish between capital gains and trading income. For example, if you buy and hold Bitcoin for appreciation, gains are tax-free. But if you trade frequently, profits are taxable as trading income. The tax treatment of DeFi activities (lending, liquidity mining) is still evolving; Consulting24 recommends obtaining a tax ruling from the Cyprus Tax Department for clarity. For businesses that want zero tax on offshore income, Panama may be a better fit.

Allowed Activities for Crypto Companies in Cyprus

Under the MiCA framework, licensed CASPs in Cyprus can engage in a wide range of activities, including:

  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat or other crypto assets.

  • Operation of a crypto trading platform.
  • Reception and transmission of orders.

  • Providing transfer services for crypto assets.
  • Advising on crypto investments (if Class 2).

Non-licensed activities (e.g., pure mining, software development) do not require a license but are still subject to tax. Cyprus also allows the issuance of tokens (STOs, ICOs) under certain conditions, subject to prospectus requirements if the offering is public. Step-by-Step Process to Set Up a Crypto Company in Cyprus

  1. Incorporate a company: Choose a name, register with the Registrar of Companies, and obtain a tax ID. Minimum share capital is EUR 1,000. You will need a registered office in Cyprus.
  2. Prepare the license application: Draft a business plan, AML policies, risk assessment, and governance framework. Engage a local compliance consultant. This step can take 2-4 months.
  3. Submit to CySEC: File the application with supporting documents. Pay the application fee (EUR 5,000-10,000). CySEC will acknowledge receipt and start the review.
  4. Await review: CySEC will review and may request additional information. This stage takes 6-12 months. During this period, you may need to appoint a local AML compliance officer.
  5. Obtain license: Once approved, you will receive a CASP license. You must maintain ongoing compliance, including annual audits and reporting.
  6. Set up banking: Open a corporate bank account in Cyprus or an EU bank. Many banks require a license before opening an account. This can take another 2-6 months.

Banking and Payment Solutions for Crypto Companies in Cyprus

Banking is a common challenge for crypto companies. In Cyprus, several banks accept crypto businesses, but they require a valid CySEC license and a strong compliance framework. Options include:

  • Traditional banks: Bank of Cyprus, Hellenic Bank (with strict due diligence). These banks often require a minimum deposit and charge higher fees for crypto-related accounts.
  • EMIs: Electronic money institutions like Revolut, Monese, or specialised crypto-friendly EMIs such as BCB Group or Clear Junction. EMIs are often faster to onboard but may have transaction limits.
  • Payment processors: Services like Coinbase Commerce or BitPay for merchant solutions. These are useful for accepting crypto payments from customers.

Expect longer onboarding times (2-6 months) and higher fees. Banks will request detailed information about your business model, source of funds, and AML procedures. Compliance and Trust: Staying on the Right Side of the Law

Compliance is non-negotiable. Cyprus has implemented the EU's AML Directive and MiCA requirements. Key obligations include:

  • Customer due diligence (CDD) and KYC procedures for all clients.
  • Transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting to the Cyprus Financial Intelligence Unit.
  • Annual audits by an external auditor, with audit reports submitted to CySEC.
  • Ongoing reporting to CySEC, including financial statements, compliance reports, and changes in management.
  • Appointment of a local AML compliance officer who is resident in Cyprus.

Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. It is essential to have strong internal policies and procedures. Common Mistakes When Setting Up a Crypto Company in Cyprus

Founders often make several mistakes when entering the Cyprus market. Avoid these pitfalls:

  • Underestimating the timeline: Many expect licensing in 3 months, but 6-12 months is realistic. Plan your budget and operations accordingly.
  • Insufficient capital: The minimum capital under MiCA is just the start. You may need additional funds for operational expenses during the licensing period.
  • Poor AML documentation: CySEC rejects applications with weak AML policies. Invest in professional compliance support.
  • Ignoring tax residency: If management is not in Cyprus, the company may be tax resident elsewhere, negating the tax benefits.
  • Overlooking banking: Start the banking process early, as it can take months. Some founders have to delay operations due to lack of a bank account.
  • Choosing the wrong license class: Applying for a Class 3 license when you only need Class 1 can increase costs and capital requirements unnecessarily.

Frequently asked questions

What is the corporate tax rate for crypto companies in Cyprus?

The corporate tax rate is 12.5% on net profits, one of the lowest in the EU. This applies to trading income from crypto activities. Capital gains from disposal of crypto assets held as investments are tax-free.

Is VAT applicable to crypto transactions in Cyprus?

Yes, but only for crypto-to-fiat exchanges, which are subject to 19% VAT. Crypto-to-crypto exchanges are exempt under EU law, following the Hedqvist ruling. Mining and staking rewards are not subject to VAT but are taxable as income.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a crypto exchange license in Cyprus?

Under MiCA, the minimum capital for operating a crypto exchange (trading platform) is EUR 150,000. Custody services require EUR 125,000, and basic services like order execution require EUR 50,000. These funds must be maintained as own funds.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Cyprus?

The licensing process typically takes 6-12 months from application submission to approval. This includes the time for CySEC to review the application, request additional information, and conduct assessments. The timeline can vary based on application completeness.

Can I operate a crypto exchange without a license in Cyprus?

No, as of 2026, all crypto asset service providers (CASPs) must be licensed by CySEC under MiCA. Operating without a license is illegal and can result in penalties. Non-licensed activities like mining or software development are allowed without a license.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations for a Cyprus crypto license?

Ongoing obligations include annual audits, submission of financial and compliance reports to CySEC, transaction monitoring, suspicious activity reporting, and maintaining AML/CFT policies. You must also appoint a local AML compliance officer.

Is Cyprus a good jurisdiction for DeFi projects?

Cyprus can be suitable for DeFi projects, but the tax treatment of DeFi activities (lending, liquidity mining) is still evolving. It is advisable to obtain a tax ruling from the Cyprus Tax Department. Licensing may be required if you offer custody or exchange services.

What are the advantages of Panama over Cyprus for crypto companies?

Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 company setup, 0% corporate tax on offshore income, no capital gains tax, and faster licensing (4-8 weeks). However, it does not provide EU passporting. Panama is ideal for non-EU focused businesses seeking low costs and speed.

What is the tax treatment of staking rewards in Cyprus?

Staking rewards are generally treated as income and subject to corporate tax at 12.5%. If the staking is part of a business activity, it is taxable as trading income. If it is passive investment, it may be considered investment income, but the tax treatment is not fully clarified.

Are there any double tax treaties that benefit crypto companies in Cyprus?

Yes, Cyprus has over 60 double tax treaties, which can reduce withholding taxes on dividends, interest, and royalties. This is beneficial for crypto companies with international operations. The treaties follow the OECD model.

What is the difference between Class 1, 2, and 3 licenses in Cyprus?

Class 1 covers reception/transmission of orders, execution, and dealing on own account. Class 2 includes portfolio management and investment advice. Class 3 covers operation of trading facilities. The capital requirements and scope of activities differ, with Class 3 being the most comprehensive.
