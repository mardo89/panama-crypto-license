What is a Seychelles crypto license?

The Seychelles crypto license, officially referred to as a Securities Dealer License for Digital Assets, is issued by the Financial Services Authority (FSA). It authorises businesses to operate a digital asset exchange, custody services, or other virtual asset activities within the Seychelles International Business Companies (IBC) framework. The regime is based on the Securities Act 2007 and the FSA's guidelines on digital assets.

Unlike EU member states under MiCA, Seychelles does not impose a single passport regime. However, its licensing process is relatively streamlined, with no minimum capital requirement for certain activities. The license is attractive for startups and SMEs looking for a credible offshore base. The FSA has issued over 50 such licenses as of 2026, with a growing interest from Asian and African markets. The license does not confer any EU passporting rights, so businesses targeting European clients may need to consider a MiCA-compliant jurisdiction like Lithuania as a complement.

Who needs a Seychelles crypto license?

Any entity that provides virtual asset services to clients in or from Seychelles typically requires a license. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (centralised and decentralised)
  • Digital asset custodians
  • Brokerage services for crypto
  • Payment processors using virtual assets
  • Initial coin offering (ICO) issuers (subject to additional requirements)

If your business model involves holding client funds or executing trades, you likely fall under the FSA's purview. Consulting24 recommends a free consultation to confirm your specific obligations. Even if your target market is outside Seychelles, having a Seychelles IBC with a license may be sufficient if you do not solicit clients from within Seychelles. However, the FSA may still require a license if you have a place of business in Seychelles.

License type and regulator

The regulator is the Financial Services Authority (FSA) of Seychelles. The primary license is the Securities Dealer License, which covers dealing in digital assets as securities. The FSA also issues a separate license for fund managers and investment advisers dealing in crypto.

Key features of the license include:

  • No specific minimum capital requirement (though the FSA expects adequate financial resources)
  • Annual renewal with ongoing compliance obligations
  • Requirement for a local registered office and resident agent
  • Anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) policies

The FSA conducts a fit and proper test on directors and shareholders. The license is non-transferable and must be renewed annually. The FSA also requires the appointment of a compliance officer who is a resident of Seychelles or a nearby jurisdiction. The regulator has the power to conduct on-site inspections and request information at any time.

Capital requirement

Seychelles does not impose a statutory minimum capital requirement for crypto license applicants. However, the FSA expects the applicant to demonstrate sufficient financial resources to cover operational expenses and liabilities. In practice, the regulator may require evidence of at least EUR 50,000 in working capital, especially if the business will handle client assets.

This contrasts with EU jurisdictions like Lithuania, where capital requirements range from EUR 50,000 to 150,000 under MiCA. For a jurisdiction with no set minimum, Panama also does not mandate a minimum capital. The FSA may also look at the nature of the business: a simple exchange may need less capital than a custody service. It is advisable to have at least EUR 100,000 in liquid assets to satisfy the FSA's expectations. Consulting24 can help you prepare a financial plan that meets the regulator's standards.

Tax treatment

Seychelles International Business Companies (IBCs) are exempt from corporate income tax on foreign-sourced income. Since most crypto businesses serve international clients, the effective tax rate can be 0%. However, if the company derives income from Seychelles sources, it may be subject to local tax at standard rates (15-25%).

It is crucial to structure operations to avoid creating a taxable presence in Seychelles. Consulting24 advises clients on tax-efficient structuring, always in coordination with a qualified tax advisor. The IBC also benefits from no withholding tax on dividends, no capital gains tax, and no stamp duty. However, the company must file an annual return with the Seychelles Revenue Commission. For a simpler tax structure, many founders choose Panama, which also operates a territorial tax system with 0% tax on foreign income.

Allowed activities

A Seychelles crypto license permits the following activities:

  • Operating a digital asset exchange (order book or peer-to-peer)
  • Providing custody services for virtual assets
  • Brokerage and dealing in digital assets
  • Investment advisory related to crypto
  • Issuing and managing stablecoins (subject to additional approval)

Activities not permitted include gambling-related tokens or services that violate Seychelles' AML laws. The FSA may also restrict certain high-risk tokens, such as privacy coins or those associated with money laundering. The license does not cover derivatives or securities tokens unless specifically approved. If you plan to offer margin trading or futures, you may need a separate license. The FSA also requires that all transactions be recorded and reported to the regulator on a quarterly basis.

Step-by-step application process

Follow these steps to apply for a Seychelles crypto license:

  1. Incorporate a Seychelles IBC with a registered office and resident agent.
  2. Prepare documentation: business plan, AML/CTF policies, risk assessment, financial projections, and personal details of directors and shareholders.
  3. Submit application to the FSA with the required fee.
  4. Undergo due diligence - the FSA will check the background of key individuals, including criminal records and financial history.
  5. Receive approval in principle - usually within 2-3 months.
  6. Fulfill post-approval conditions (e.g., appoint a compliance officer, implement systems, and provide a bank account).
  7. Obtain final license and commence operations.

Banking and payments

Opening a bank account for a Seychelles IBC can be challenging due to perceived offshore risks. Options include:

  • Local Seychelles banks (e.g., Nouvobanq, Bank of Baroda) - these may require a minimum deposit and have strict compliance.
  • International banks with Seychelles presence - but many have closed their crypto-friendly accounts.
  • EMI accounts (e.g., Payoneer, TransferWise) - but these may not accept crypto-related businesses.

Many founders pair their Seychelles license with a payment processor or crypto-friendly bank in another jurisdiction, such as Lithuania or Estonia. Consulting24 recommends exploring banking options early in the process, as it can take 2-3 months to open an account. Some EMIs like Mercury or Brex may accept Seychelles IBCs if they have a US presence. Alternatively, consider using a crypto payment gateway like BitPay or CoinGate.

Compliance and trust considerations

While Seychelles offers a light-touch regime, it is not a zero-compliance jurisdiction. License holders must:

  • Submit annual returns and audited financial statements
  • Maintain AML/CTF procedures
  • Report suspicious transactions
  • Appoint a resident agent
  • Keep records of all transactions for at least 7 years

Failure to comply can result in license revocation or fines. Consulting24 advises clients to treat compliance seriously. This is general guidance, not legal advice. The FSA has increased its enforcement in recent years, and non-compliance can lead to public warnings or blacklisting. We recommend engaging a local compliance officer and conducting regular internal audits. The Seychelles IBC must also file an annual return with the Registrar of Companies.

Frequently asked questions

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Seychelles?

The typical timeline is 3 to 6 months from application submission to final approval. This includes company incorporation, document preparation, and FSA review. Delays can occur if the application is incomplete or if due diligence raises concerns. The FSA aims to process applications within 12 weeks, but this can extend if additional information is required.

What is the minimum capital for a Seychelles crypto license?

There is no statutory minimum capital requirement. However, the FSA expects the applicant to have sufficient financial resources. In practice, demonstrating at least EUR 50,000 in working capital is advisable. For custody services, the FSA may expect a higher amount, such as EUR 100,000, to cover potential liabilities.

Can I operate a crypto exchange with a Seychelles license?

Yes, the Securities Dealer License covers operating a digital asset exchange, including order book and peer-to-peer platforms. Custody and brokerage services are also permitted. However, if you plan to offer derivatives or margin trading, you may need a separate license or additional approval from the FSA.

Is Seychelles tax-free for crypto businesses?

Seychelles IBCs are exempt from corporate tax on foreign-sourced income. Since most crypto businesses serve international clients, the effective tax rate can be 0%. However, local-source income is taxed at standard rates (15-25%). It is important to structure operations to avoid a taxable presence in Seychelles.

Do I need a physical office in Seychelles?

You need a registered office address and a resident agent in Seychelles, but a physical office with staff is not required. The registered office can be provided by a service provider. However, the FSA may require that the company's books and records be kept in Seychelles or be accessible from there.

Can I use a Seychelles license to serve EU clients?

A Seychelles license does not provide EU passporting rights. If you want to serve EU clients directly, you may need a MiCA-compliant license in an EU member state like Lithuania. However, you can use a Seychelles entity as a holding company or for non-EU clients. Consulting24 can advise on a multi-jurisdictional structure.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations for a Seychelles crypto license?

License holders must submit annual returns, audited financial statements, and maintain AML/CTF procedures. They must also report suspicious transactions and appoint a resident agent. The FSA may conduct periodic inspections. Failure to comply can result in fines or license revocation. Annual renewal fees are typically EUR 2,000-5,000.

How does Seychelles compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 fee with no minimum capital and no ongoing compliance burden, making it simpler and cheaper. Seychelles has a more established regulatory framework but higher ongoing costs. For most founders, Panama is the better value. Consulting24 delivers directly in Panama and can set up your license quickly.

Can I apply for a Seychelles crypto license if I am not a resident?

Yes, non-residents can apply. The company must have a registered office and resident agent in Seychelles, but directors and shareholders can be from anywhere. However, the FSA may require that at least one director be a resident of Seychelles or a nearby jurisdiction. Consulting24 can help you find a suitable local director.

What documents are needed for the application?

You will need a business plan, AML/CTF policies, risk assessment, financial projections, personal details of directors and shareholders (including passports, proof of address, and CVs), and a description of the proposed activities. All documents must be in English or officially translated. The FSA may also request bank references and audited financial statements if the company has been operating elsewhere.
