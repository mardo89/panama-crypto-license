What Is the Irish Crypto License?

The Irish crypto license is a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) registration mandated by the Central Bank of Ireland under the Criminal Justice (Money Laundering and Terrorist Financing) Act 2010, as amended, and now aligned with MiCA. It is not a standalone "crypto license" per se but a registration that allows firms to exchange virtual assets for fiat or other virtual assets, transfer virtual assets, and provide custodial wallet services. Since MiCA's full application in 2026, the regime has been harmonised with EU-wide standards, including capital requirements and conduct rules.

Firms already registered under the previous anti-money laundering (AML) framework must now comply with MiCA's operational resilience, governance, and disclosure obligations. The Central Bank of Ireland expects applicants to demonstrate strong AML/CFT controls, fit and proper management, and adequate financial resources. This makes Ireland a credible but demanding jurisdiction for crypto businesses seeking EU market access.

Who Needs an Irish Crypto License?

Any entity providing crypto asset services in or from Ireland must be registered as a VASP. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (centralised and decentralised if offering matching services)
  • Peer-to-peer trading platforms
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Brokers and dealers in virtual assets
  • Payment service providers handling crypto-fiat conversions
  • Issuers of crypto assets (if not exempt under MiCA)

If your business targets Irish residents or operates from an Irish office, you fall under the Central Bank's remit. Even firms based elsewhere in the EU may need to register in Ireland if they have a branch or significant marketing activities there. License Type and Regulator

The primary regulator is the Central Bank of Ireland (CBI). Under MiCA, the license type is a "CASP" (Crypto Asset Service Provider) authorisation, which replaces the previous VASP registration for most services. However, firms that only provide services not covered by MiCA (e.g., certain utility tokens) may still require VASP registration. The CBI has issued guidance that all existing VASPs must transition to CASP status by the MiCA deadline.

The CBI is known for a rigorous assessment process. It expects applicants to have a physical presence in Ireland, including a registered office and senior management based locally. The regulator also requires a detailed business plan, AML policies, risk assessments, and audited financial statements. The cost of obtaining a crypto license in Ireland varies based on complexity and legal fees. Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, capital requirements depend on the type of crypto asset services provided. The minimum capital thresholds are:

  • EUR 50,000 for providing custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • EUR 125,000 for operating a trading platform or exchanging crypto assets for fiat
  • EUR 150,000 for placing crypto assets without a firm commitment basis

These are initial capital requirements that must be maintained as own funds. The Central Bank may also impose additional capital based on operational risk assessments. For VASP-only firms not fully transitioning to CASP, the previous AML regime did not mandate a minimum capital, but the CBI expected proportional resources. As of 2026, all active firms must meet MiCA capital tiers. Tax Treatment

Ireland's corporate tax rate is 25% for trading income (standard rate) and 12.5% for non-trading income, but crypto-specific tax treatment is still evolving. The Revenue Commissioners treat crypto assets as intangible assets for corporation tax purposes. Gains on disposal are generally subject to capital gains tax (33% for individuals, 25% for companies if trading). VAT does not apply to crypto-to-fiat exchanges under EU law, but advisory fees may be VATable.

Ireland has no stamp duty on crypto transfers, and no wealth tax. However, the tax regime is not as favourable as Panama, which has no capital gains tax and no corporate tax on offshore income. For tax-efficient structures, many founders choose Panama as a holding jurisdiction while operating elsewhere. Consulting24 can advise on the tax implications of each route.

Allowed Activities

A licensed Irish VASP or CASP can perform the following activities:

  • Exchange of virtual assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of virtual assets for other virtual assets
  • Transfer of virtual assets (on behalf of clients)
  • Custody and administration of virtual assets
  • Participation in and provision of financial services related to an issuer's offer or sale of crypto assets
  • Operation of a trading platform for crypto assets

Activities not covered by MiCA, such as decentralised finance (DeFi) protocols without a central operator, may fall outside the licensing requirement. However, the CBI has warned that any entity exercising control or influence over a DeFi protocol may still need authorisation. The regulator also prohibits unlicensed cross-border marketing to Irish residents. Consulting24 can help you map your service offerings to the correct license type.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Applying for an Irish crypto license involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application preparation: Develop a detailed business plan, AML/CFT policies, risk assessment, and governance framework. Engage a local compliance advisor.
  2. Submit application to CBI: Complete the online application form via the CBI's portal, including all supporting documents (e.g., audited financials, fit and proper declarations for directors, organisational chart).
  3. Initial review: The CBI acknowledges receipt and may request clarification within 30 days. Respond promptly.
  4. In-depth assessment: The CBI evaluates your business model, AML controls, capital adequacy, and management fitness. This can take 3-6 months.
  5. On-site inspection (if applicable): The CBI may conduct a physical visit to your Irish office.
  6. Decision: Approval or rejection. If approved, you receive a VASP or CASP authorisation subject to ongoing supervision.

The entire process typically takes 6-12 months. Consulting24 does not file Irish applications directly but can coordinate with local legal partners if you choose this route. For a faster and cheaper alternative, consider Estonia (2-3 months) or Panama (2-3 months).

  • Specialist crypto-friendly banks like Bank Frick (Liechtenstein) or Clear Junction
  • Irish branches of international banks with dedicated fintech teams

Benefits of an Irish Crypto License

Despite the complexity, an Irish crypto license offers several advantages:

  • EU passporting: Once authorised, you can provide services across all EU/EEA member states without additional licenses.
  • Regulatory credibility: The CBI is a respected regulator, which can enhance trust with partners and customers.
  • Access to talent: Ireland has a strong fintech ecosystem and English-speaking workforce.
  • Stable legal environment: Common law system with clear property rights.

However, the high cost and long timeline may not suit early-stage startups. Consulting24 often recommends starting in a simpler jurisdiction like Panama (EUR 6,000 flat) and later expanding to Ireland if needed.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, firms must comply with ongoing requirements:

  • Annual AML/CFT audits and reporting to the CBI
  • Maintain minimum capital at all times
  • Submit periodic financial and operational reports
  • Conduct regular risk assessments and update policies
  • Ensure all directors and beneficial owners remain fit and proper
  • Comply with MiCA's disclosure and transparency rules

The CBI has the power to impose fines, suspend licenses, or require remedial actions. Common Mistakes When Applying

Founders often make these errors when pursuing an Irish crypto license:

  • Underestimating capital requirements: Many assume no minimum capital, but MiCA tiers apply.
  • Incomplete AML documentation: The CBI expects detailed policies, not generic templates.
  • Lack of local presence: The regulator insists on a physical office and local management.
  • Ignoring tax obligations: Failing to register for Irish corporation tax or VAT can delay approval.
  • Choosing the wrong license type: Some firms apply for VASP when they need CASP, or vice versa.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between VASP and CASP in Ireland?

VASP (Virtual Asset Service Provider) registration was the pre-MiCA AML-focused regime. CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) is the new MiCA authorisation that includes additional capital, governance, and conduct requirements. As of 2026, all firms must transition to CASP if they offer MiCA-covered services.

Can a non-EU company apply for an Irish crypto license?

Yes, but the company must establish a physical presence in Ireland, including a registered office and local directors or managers. The Central Bank expects the entity to be incorporated in Ireland or have a branch there. Consulting24 can help with company setup.

How long does the Irish crypto license application take?

Typically 6 to 12 months from submission to decision. The timeline depends on the completeness of your application and the CBI's workload. Delays often occur if additional information is requested.

What are the minimum capital requirements for a CASP in Ireland?

Under MiCA, capital requirements are EUR 50,000 for custody services, EUR 125,000 for exchange services, and EUR 150,000 for placing crypto assets without a firm commitment. These must be maintained as own funds.

Is Ireland a good jurisdiction for crypto startups?

Ireland offers EU passporting and regulatory credibility, but the high cost (EUR 35,000-100,000) and long timeline make it less suitable for early-stage startups. Many founders start in Panama or Lithuania and expand later.

What taxes apply to crypto businesses in Ireland?

Corporate tax is 25% on trading income. Capital gains tax is 33% for individuals and 25% for companies on crypto disposals. VAT does not apply to crypto-fiat exchanges. Advisory fees may be subject to VAT at 23%.

Can I use a Panama company to serve EU customers?

A Panama company does not have an EU passport. To serve EU customers directly, you need a license in an EU member state like Ireland, Lithuania, or Estonia. Panama is best for non-EU markets or as a holding company.

Does the Central Bank of Ireland require a physical office?

Yes, the CBI expects a physical registered office and local management in Ireland. Virtual offices are not accepted. You must have a substantive presence.

What happens if I operate without a license in Ireland?

Operating without a license is a criminal offence. The CBI can impose fines, issue cease-and-desist orders, and refer cases for prosecution. Unlicensed firms also face reputational damage and banking difficulties.
