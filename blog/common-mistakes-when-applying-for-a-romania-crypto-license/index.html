What is a Romania crypto license?

A Romania crypto license, officially a CASP authorization under MiCA, allows companies to provide crypto services legally within Romania and across the EU. It is regulated by the ASF and requires compliance with EU-wide standards on capital, governance, and anti-money laundering (AML). The license covers specific service categories defined in MiCA, including custody, exchange, and trading.

Common mistakes often stem from misunderstanding the scope of MiCA. Some founders assume it is a simple registration, but it is a full authorization process with rigorous checks. Others confuse it with the old national VASP regimes that no longer apply. In 2026, MiCA is fully in force, and Romania has transposed it into national law. A frequent error is failing to distinguish between a CASP license and a payment institution license, which leads to applying for the wrong authorization. Who needs a Romania crypto license?

Any business offering crypto services in Romania or targeting Romanian clients needs a license. This includes exchanges, wallet providers, custodians, and brokers. Even foreign companies that actively market to Romanian residents must obtain authorization. The ASF applies a substance-over-form approach, so passive cross-border services may still trigger licensing requirements.

A common mistake is assuming that a license from another EU country automatically covers Romania. While MiCA allows passporting, the initial authorization must be obtained in the home member state. Some firms try to use a non-EU license or an Estonian license without proper passporting, which is non-compliant. Others mistakenly believe that operating through a Romanian agent or representative office exempts them from licensing. License type and regulator

The regulator is the Autoritatea de Supraveghere Financiară (ASF), Romania's financial market authority. The license type is a CASP authorization under MiCA, classified by service: custody, exchange, trading, or advisory. Each class has specific requirements, and you must apply for the exact categories you intend to offer. The ASF also requires a minimum of two key persons, including a compliance officer and a board member with relevant experience.

Mistakes include applying for the wrong service class or failing to update the license when adding new services. Some founders attempt to bundle all services under one application without proper segregation, leading to rejection. The ASF expects clarity on which MiCA service categories you intend to offer. Another error is not providing sufficient evidence of the key persons' qualifications, such as CVs and professional references. Capital requirement

Under MiCA, capital requirements are tiered: EUR 50,000 for simple custody/wallet services, EUR 125,000 for exchanges, and EUR 150,000 for trading platforms or complex services. This capital must be maintained at all times and cannot be used for operational expenses. The ASF requires proof that the capital is fully paid up and from a legitimate source.

A frequent mistake is using borrowed or unverified funds. The ASF requires proof of source of funds and that capital is fully paid up. Some applicants try to use crypto assets as capital, which is not accepted. Only fiat currency in a bank account qualifies. Another error is not maintaining the capital after approval; the ASF conducts periodic checks. Tax treatment

Romania taxes crypto gains as income: 10% for individuals, 16% for corporate profits. VAT may apply to certain services like advisory fees. The tax regime is relatively favourable compared to other EU states, but it requires careful reporting. Crypto-to-crypto trades are considered taxable events, and losses can be offset against gains under certain conditions.

Mistakes include not registering for tax or failing to file crypto transactions properly. Some founders assume that crypto-to-crypto trades are tax-free, but Romania treats them as taxable events. Others neglect VAT obligations for services provided to EU clients. Consulting24 recommends engaging a local tax advisor to ensure full compliance. For a simpler tax environment, some founders choose Panama, which has no capital gains tax on crypto.

Allowed activities

A Romania CASP license permits: custody and administration of crypto assets, operation of a trading platform, exchange of crypto for fiat or other crypto, execution of orders, placement of crypto, and advisory services. Each activity requires separate authorization, and you must list them explicitly in your application. The ASF also allows ancillary services like staking if they are directly related to custody, but this is subject to interpretation.

Common mistakes include offering unlicensed services like staking or lending without permission. The ASF has strict boundaries; any deviation can result in fines or license revocation. Some founders assume that because a service is not explicitly prohibited, it is allowed. This is not the case. Always check the exact scope before launching. Step-by-step application process

  1. Pre-application: business plan, AML policies, governance documents, key person appointments.
  2. Submit application to ASF with all required documents, including proof of capital and bank account.
  3. ASF reviews (3-6 months), may request additional information or clarifications.
  4. Approval and issuance of license, followed by publication in the official gazette.
  5. Ongoing compliance: quarterly reporting, annual audits, capital maintenance, and AML updates.

Mistakes often occur in step 1: incomplete documentation or weak AML procedures. Some founders skip the pre-application phase and rush to submit, leading to rejection. Others fail to prepare for the ASF's due diligence on key persons, such as background checks. Consulting24 coordinates the entire process, ensuring completeness and readiness. Banking and payments

Opening a bank account for a crypto business in Romania is challenging but possible. Major banks like Banca Transilvania and BRD accept CASPs with proper documentation, but they often require a physical presence and a strong business case. Some applicants fail because they do not have a local bank account ready before applying.

A common mistake is not securing banking before applying. The ASF may require proof of a bank account as part of the application. Compliance and trust

Compliance is the backbone of a successful application. The ASF expects strong AML/KYC procedures, regular audits, and a qualified compliance officer. Many applications fail due to weak compliance frameworks, such as outdated AML policies or lack of transaction monitoring systems. The ASF also requires a risk assessment specific to your business model.

Common mistakes include appointing an inexperienced compliance officer or outsourcing to a non-EU provider. The ASF requires the compliance officer to be based in Romania or the EU. Common mistakes to avoid

  • Incomplete application forms or missing documents, especially the business plan and AML policy.
  • Insufficient capital or unverified source of funds, leading to rejection.
  • Weak AML/KYC policies not aligned with MiCA standards, such as lacking transaction monitoring.
  • Choosing the wrong service class or failing to list all intended services.
  • Ignoring tax obligations, including VAT and corporate income tax.
  • Not securing a bank account in advance, causing delays.
  • Underestimating timeline and costs, leading to budget overruns.
  • Appointing key persons without relevant experience or EU residency.

Frequently asked questions

What is the most common mistake when applying for a Romania crypto license?

The most common mistake is submitting an incomplete application, especially lacking a strong AML policy or proof of capital source. Many founders rush the process without proper preparation, leading to rejection or long delays.

How long does it take to get a Romania crypto license?

Typically 3-6 months from submission to approval. Delays often occur due to missing documents or ASF requests for additional information. Pre-application preparation can shorten the timeline.

What is the capital requirement for a Romania crypto license?

Under MiCA, capital is EUR 50,000 for custody, EUR 125,000 for exchange, and EUR 150,000 for trading platforms. This must be held in fiat in a bank account and maintained throughout the license period.

Can I use a crypto license from another EU country to operate in Romania?

Yes, via MiCA passporting, but you must be authorized in your home member state first. You cannot simply use a non-EU license. The passporting process requires notification to the ASF.

Is a Romania crypto license expensive?

Total costs range from EUR 15,000 to 50,000+ including fees, capital, and professional services. Exact pricing depends on your business model and service categories. Panama is a cheaper alternative at EUR 6,000 flat.

Do I need a physical office in Romania?

Yes, the ASF requires a registered office and local presence, including a compliance officer based in Romania or the EU. A virtual office may not suffice; you need a physical address for regulatory correspondence.

What activities are not allowed under a Romania CASP license?

Activities like crypto lending, staking as a separate service, or derivatives are not covered by a standard CASP license. You may need additional authorization or a different license type.

Can Consulting24 guarantee approval of my Romania crypto license?

No, we cannot guarantee approval. We provide expert guidance to maximize your chances, but the final decision rests with the ASF. Our track record shows high success rates.

How does Romania compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Panama is cheaper (EUR 6,000 flat) and faster, but it is non-EU and does not offer passporting. Romania provides EU access but higher costs and compliance. Your choice depends on your target market.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements after obtaining a Romania crypto license?

Ongoing requirements include regular AML audits, capital maintenance, transaction reporting, and annual financial statements. Non-compliance can lead to fines or license revocation.

Can I apply for a Romania crypto license if my company is registered outside the EU?

Yes, but you must establish a subsidiary in Romania with a local registered office and key persons. The ASF requires the licensed entity to be incorporated in Romania.

What documents are needed for a Romania crypto license application?

Key documents include a detailed business plan, AML/KYC policies, governance documents, proof of capital, CVs of key persons, and a bank account statement. Incomplete documentation is a common mistake.
