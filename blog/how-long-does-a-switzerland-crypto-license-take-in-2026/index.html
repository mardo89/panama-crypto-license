What Is a Switzerland Crypto License?

A Switzerland crypto license is a regulatory authorization issued by FINMA for businesses that provide crypto-related financial services. It is not a single license but a classification under Swiss financial market law. Depending on your activities, you may need a banking license, a securities dealer license, or a fintech license. The most common route for crypto exchanges and custodians is the "Fintech license" or a "securities dealer license" for trading platforms.

Switzerland has a progressive approach to crypto, but the licensing process is rigorous. FINMA focuses on investor protection, anti-money laundering (AML) compliance, and operational integrity. The timeline reflects this thoroughness. For example, a crypto exchange handling over CHF 1 million in client assets will likely need a full license, while a small wallet provider may only require AML registration. The distinction is critical and affects both cost and time.

In practice, a company offering custodial services for 10,000 users with average holdings of CHF 500 each would exceed CHF 1 million in client assets, triggering full licensing. Conversely, a small non-custodial wallet app with no control over private keys might only need AML registration. Who Needs a Switzerland Crypto License?

Any business that professionally holds client crypto assets, operates a crypto exchange, or provides crypto custody services in or from Switzerland needs a license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralized and decentralized platforms)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Token issuers offering secondary market trading
  • Brokers and dealers in crypto assets
  • Payment service providers using crypto

If your business involves more than CHF 1 million in client assets or has a high transaction volume, FINMA will likely require full licensing. Smaller operations may qualify for a simpler registration under the AML Act. For instance, a small peer-to-peer exchange with low volume might only need to register with the Money Laundering Reporting Office (MROS). However, any activity that resembles professional financial services triggers licensing. Consulting24 can help assess your specific situation and recommend the appropriate route.

Real-world example: a Swiss-based DeFi protocol that allows users to swap tokens via a non-custodial smart contract may still be considered a securities exchange if the tokens qualify as securities under Swiss law. FINMA has issued guidance classifying certain DeFi platforms as needing a license. Always get a legal review of your business model.

License Type and Regulator

The primary regulator is FINMA (Financial Market Supervisory Authority). The main license types are:

  • Fintech License: For businesses accepting public deposits up to CHF 100 million, but not engaging in traditional lending. Capital requirement: CHF 1.2 million (approx. EUR 1.2 million).
  • Securities Dealer License: For trading platforms and brokers dealing in crypto assets classified as securities. Capital requirement: CHF 1.6 million (approx. EUR 1.6 million).
  • Banking License: Full banking license for crypto banks. Capital requirement: CHF 10 million (approx. EUR 10 million).

Most crypto companies apply for the Fintech license or securities dealer license. FINMA also requires a minimum of two board members resident in Switzerland. The choice depends on your business model: a custody provider may use the Fintech license, while an exchange trading tokenized securities needs the securities dealer license. The application must include a detailed business plan, risk management framework, and AML policies. FINMA's review is iterative, often requiring multiple rounds of questions.

For example, if you operate a platform that lists tokenized real estate, FINMA may classify those tokens as securities, pushing you toward the securities dealer license. A simple crypto-to-crypto exchange with no fiat on-ramp might qualify for the Fintech license if it holds client assets. Capital Requirement

For the Fintech license, the minimum capital is CHF 1.2 million (approx. EUR 1.2 million). For a securities dealer license, it is CHF 1.6 million (approx. EUR 1.6 million). This capital must be fully paid up and held in a Swiss bank account. It cannot be in crypto assets. The capital is intended to cover operational risks and ensure solvency.

In comparison, Panama requires no minimum capital for its crypto license, making it a more accessible option for startups. Lithuania under MiCA requires EUR 125,000 for exchange and custody services. The high Swiss capital requirement is a barrier for many early-stage companies. However, it also signals financial stability to partners and clients.

Practical consideration: the capital must be deposited before FINMA grants the license, meaning you need to raise or have the funds ready. Some founders use a bridge loan from a Swiss bank, but this adds interest costs. Once licensed, you can use the capital for operational expenses, subject to FINMA approval. Tax Treatment

Switzerland has a favorable tax regime for crypto companies. Corporate income tax rates vary by canton but typically range from 11% to 21%. Crypto assets are treated as assets for wealth tax purposes, and capital gains from private crypto investments are generally tax-free for individuals. For businesses, profits from crypto trading are subject to corporate tax. VAT is not applied to crypto transactions in most cases. However, detailed tax planning is essential.

For example, a crypto exchange based in Zug (Crypto Valley) may benefit from a lower corporate tax rate of around 11.9%. But you must also consider cantonal and communal taxes. Transfer pricing and cross-border tax issues require expert advice. Consulting24 can coordinate with Swiss tax advisors to optimize your structure.

One nuance: if your company holds crypto as inventory, unrealized gains may be taxed in some cantons. Most cantons only tax realized gains, but check with a local advisor. The Swiss tax authorities have issued specific guidelines for crypto mining, staking, and airdrops, which are treated as income at the time of receipt. Allowed Activities

A Switzerland crypto license allows you to:

  • Operate a crypto exchange (buy/sell crypto against fiat or other crypto)
  • Provide crypto custody services
  • Issue and trade tokenized securities (with additional licensing)
  • Offer crypto payment services
  • Accept public deposits up to CHF 100 million (Fintech license)

Activities not allowed include traditional lending (unless you have a banking license) and operating a decentralized exchange without a license (FINMA may still regulate it). For instance, if you run a DEX with an automated market maker, FINMA may consider it a securities exchange if the tokens qualify as securities. Always check with a legal expert.

Specific example: a Swiss-based company offering crypto-backed loans to retail clients would need a banking license because it involves lending. A company that simply facilitates peer-to-peer lending using smart contracts may also fall under banking regulations. Consulting24 can help you map your activities to the correct license category.

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Pre-application assessment: Determine the right license type and prepare documentation. (1-2 months)
  2. Company setup: Incorporate a Swiss company (AG or GmbH) with minimum capital. (1-2 weeks)
  3. Draft business plan: Include AML/KYC policies, risk management, and financial projections. (1-2 months)
  4. Submit application to FINMA: Provide all required documents, including organizational charts, compliance manuals, and audited financial statements. (1 month)
  5. FINMA review and feedback: FINMA will ask questions and request additional information. (3-6 months)
  6. Approval and licensing: Once satisfied, FINMA issues the license. (1 month)

During the review phase, FINMA often requests clarifications on risk management. For example, they may ask for a detailed description of your cybersecurity measures or your business continuity plan. Opening a bank account in Switzerland for a crypto company is challenging but possible. Many traditional banks are cautious. Specialized crypto-friendly banks include Sygnum, SEBA Bank, and some cantonal banks like Zuger Kantonalbank. You will need to present your FINMA license or at least a pending application. A Swiss bank account is required to hold the minimum capital. For payment processing, you can integrate with crypto payment gateways or use traditional acquiring banks.

Frequently asked questions

How long does a Switzerland crypto license take in 2026?

The typical timeline is 6 to 12 months from application to approval. This includes 1-2 months for pre-application preparation, 3-6 months for FINMA review, and 1 month for final approval. Complex cases may take longer.

What is the minimum capital for a Switzerland crypto license?

For the Fintech license, CHF 1.2 million (approx. EUR 1.2 million). For a securities dealer license, CHF 1.6 million (approx. EUR 1.6 million). The capital must be in fiat and held in a Swiss bank account.

Can I use crypto assets to meet the capital requirement?

No, FINMA requires the capital to be in fiat currency (CHF, EUR, or USD). Crypto assets are not accepted as capital. The funds must be deposited in a Swiss bank account before license approval.

What is the cost of a Switzerland crypto license?

Total costs excluding capital range from EUR 40,000 to EUR 100,000, including FINMA application fees (EUR 10,000-20,000) and legal/advisory fees (EUR 30,000-80,000). Exact pricing depends on your business model.

Do I need a Swiss resident director?

Yes, FINMA requires that at least two board members be resident in Switzerland. One of them must be a Swiss citizen or hold a valid Swiss work permit. This is a key requirement for licensing.

What activities are allowed under a Switzerland crypto license?

You can operate a crypto exchange, provide custody services, issue and trade tokenized securities (with additional licensing), offer crypto payment services, and accept public deposits up to CHF 100 million under the Fintech license.

Is a Switzerland crypto license recognized in the EU?

No, Switzerland is not an EU member. A Swiss license does not automatically grant passporting rights in the EU. For EU market access, consider a license under MiCA in an EU member state like Lithuania.

How does the Switzerland license compare to Panama?

Panama offers a flat fee of EUR 6,000, no minimum capital, and a timeline of 2-3 months. Switzerland costs EUR 40,000-100,000 plus CHF 1.2 million capital and takes 6-12 months. Panama is faster and cheaper, but Switzerland offers higher prestige.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

You must conduct annual audits, submit quarterly AML reports, appoint a Swiss-based AML officer, and notify FINMA of any material changes. Non-compliance can result in fines or license revocation.
