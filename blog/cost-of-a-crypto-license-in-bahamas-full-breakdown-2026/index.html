What Is a Bahamas Crypto License?

The Bahamas crypto license, issued under the DARE Act, allows businesses to operate digital asset exchanges, custody services, and other virtual asset activities. The Securities Commission of The Bahamas (SCB) is the regulator. The license is mandatory for any entity providing virtual asset services to or from the Bahamas.

There are two main categories: a full license for exchanges and a shorter-term registration for smaller operators. The cost of a crypto license in the Bahamas varies depending on the type and complexity of the business. As of 2026, the framework is fully operational, with a growing number of licensed firms including major international players. The SCB has published detailed guidelines on licensing, which are regularly updated to align with international standards such as FATF recommendations.

For founders targeting the Caribbean or Latin American markets, the Bahamas offers a stable regulatory environment with a clear path to compliance. Compared to EU jurisdictions where MiCA is fully in force with capital tiers of EUR 50,000, 125,000, or 150,000, the Bahamas provides greater flexibility in capital requirements. This makes it an attractive option for startups and established firms alike.

Who Needs a Bahamas Crypto License?

Any business offering virtual asset services in or from the Bahamas must obtain a license. This includes:

  • Digital asset exchanges (centralized and decentralized)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Virtual asset transfer services
  • Issuers of digital tokens (subject to exemptions)
  • Brokers and dealers in virtual assets
  • Any entity facilitating trading or exchange of virtual assets for fiat or other virtual assets

Founders targeting the Caribbean or Latin American markets often consider the Bahamas. Compared to Lithuania or Estonia, the Bahamas offers a tax-neutral environment and a clear regulatory path. Capital Requirement

The Bahamas does not impose a fixed minimum capital requirement under DARE. Instead, the SCB assesses capital adequacy on a case-by-case basis, considering the business model and risk profile. Typically, applicants need to demonstrate sufficient capital to cover operational costs for at least 12 months, often in the range of $100,000 - $500,000. This is more flexible than EU jurisdictions where capital tiers are EUR 50,000 / 125,000 / 150,000 under MiCA.

For founders seeking lower capital requirements, Panama offers a simpler setup with no minimum capital for the license itself. The SCB may also require a surety bond or insurance depending on the activities. Consulting24 advises clients on structuring their capital to meet SCB expectations without overcapitalizing.

Tax Treatment

The Bahamas is a tax-neutral jurisdiction. There is no corporate income tax, capital gains tax, VAT, or withholding tax. This makes it highly attractive for crypto businesses. However, businesses must pay annual license fees and comply with economic substance requirements if they have physical presence. The cost of a crypto license in the Bahamas is offset by the tax savings compared to high-tax jurisdictions.

For example, a company earning $1 million in profits would pay zero corporate tax in the Bahamas, whereas in Lithuania it would pay 15% ($150,000) and in Estonia 20% on distributed profits. Allowed Activities

A Bahamas crypto license permits the following activities:

  • Operating a digital asset exchange (order book, AMM, etc.)
  • Providing custodial wallet services
  • Facilitating virtual asset transfers
  • Issuing digital tokens (with additional approval)
  • Brokerage and dealing in virtual assets
  • Investment management of virtual assets

Activities not allowed include unregistered securities offerings and services to sanctioned entities. The SCB provides a clear list of prohibited activities. Licensees must also comply with the DARE Act's provisions on consumer protection and market integrity. For example, exchanges must maintain transparent order books and segregate client assets.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Applying for a Bahamas crypto license involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application: Engage local legal counsel and prepare business plan, AML policies, and financial projections. This stage takes 1-2 months.
  2. Submission: Submit application to the SCB with all supporting documents and fee. The SCB acknowledges receipt within 5 working days.
  3. Review: The SCB reviews the application, may request additional information, and conducts background checks on directors and shareholders. This stage takes 2-4 months.
  4. Approval: If approved, the license is issued with conditions. The entire process typically takes 4-8 months. Some applications may be expedited if all documents are in order.

Compliance and Trust

Compliance with AML/CFT regulations is mandatory. Licensees must appoint a Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO), conduct customer due diligence, and submit regular reports to the SCB. The regulator conducts periodic inspections. Failure to comply can result in fines or license revocation. This is general guidance, not legal advice.
