What Is Crypto Company Tax in Abu Dhabi?

Crypto company tax in Abu Dhabi refers to the corporate tax obligations for businesses engaged in virtual asset activities within the emirate. As of 2026, the UAE has implemented a federal corporate tax of 9% on profits above AED 375,000, but free zones that meet certain criteria can still benefit from a 0% rate. Abu Dhabi's key free zones for crypto-Abu Dhabi Global Market (ADGM) and the Abu Dhabi Free Zone-offer a 0% corporate tax for qualifying entities, including those holding a Financial Services Permission (FSP) for crypto activities.

There is no capital gains tax on crypto-to-crypto trades, no VAT on cryptocurrency transactions (VAT is 0% on digital asset transfers), and no withholding tax on dividends or interest paid to non-residents. However, VAT at 5% applies to advisory and management services. Founders must also comply with economic substance requirements and maintain proper accounting records. The tax year is the calendar year, and annual returns must be filed.

This regime is designed to attract blockchain innovators by removing tax drag, allowing companies to reinvest profits. It is important to distinguish between the free zone regime and the mainland UAE regime, where the 9% corporate tax applies. Most crypto companies opt for a free zone setup to enjoy the 0% rate.

Who Needs a Crypto License in Abu Dhabi?

Any business that provides virtual asset services in or from Abu Dhabi must obtain a license from the relevant regulator. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralized and decentralized)
  • Digital asset custodians and wallet providers
  • Token issuers (ICOs, STOs, utility tokens)
  • DeFi platforms and liquidity providers
  • Crypto payment processors and merchant services
  • Brokerage and trading desks
  • Advisory and consulting firms focused on digital assets

If your company is incorporated in a free zone and provides services only to non-UAE residents, you may still need a license if you have any presence or marketing in the UAE. The Financial Services Regulatory Authority (FSRA) in ADGM and the Abu Dhabi Free Zone Authority regulate these activities. Founders should also consider Lithuania or Panama as lower-cost alternatives for certain activities.

License Type and Regulator

In Abu Dhabi, the primary regulator for crypto activities is the Financial Services Regulatory Authority (FSRA) of the Abu Dhabi Global Market (ADGM). The ADGM is an international financial centre that operates under English common law. The FSRA issues a Financial Services Permission (FSP) for virtual asset activities. There are several categories:

  • Category 3A: Operating a Multilateral Trading Facility (MTF) for crypto assets (exchange license). Capital requirement: AED 15 million (approx. EUR 3.7 million).
  • Category 3C: Custody and wallet services. Capital: AED 5 million (approx. EUR 1.2 million).
  • Category 4: Advising on crypto investments. Capital: AED 2 million (approx. EUR 490,000).
  • Category 5: Arranging deals in crypto assets (brokerage). Capital: AED 1 million (approx. EUR 245,000).

For smaller operations, the Abu Dhabi Free Zone (ADFZ) offers a Virtual Asset License with lower capital requirements (around AED 500,000 / EUR 122,000) but with restrictions on services. The FSRA is the most reputable regulator in the region, aligning with international standards like FATF recommendations. For a comparison with other regulators, see our Estonia and Malta pages.

Capital Requirement for Crypto License in Abu Dhabi

The minimum capital requirement depends on the license category and regulator. For ADGM:

  • Category 3A (Exchange): AED 15 million (EUR 3.7 million)
  • Category 3C (Custody): AED 5 million (EUR 1.2 million)
  • Category 4 (Advisory): AED 2 million (EUR 490,000)
  • Category 5 (Arranging): AED 1 million (EUR 245,000)

For ADFZ Virtual Asset License: minimum paid-up capital of AED 500,000 (EUR 122,000). Capital must be deposited in a UAE bank account and may be in fiat or certain stablecoins. The funds must be maintained throughout the license period. This is a significant barrier for startups compared to Lithuania (EUR 125,000 for CASP) or Panama (no minimum capital). However, the capital can be used for operational expenses once the license is granted.

Tax Treatment of Crypto Companies in Abu Dhabi

Abu Dhabi offers one of the most favourable tax regimes for crypto companies globally:

  • Corporate tax: 0% for qualifying free zone entities (ADGM, ADFZ). Mainland companies pay 9% on profits above AED 375,000.
  • Capital gains tax: 0% on crypto asset disposals, provided the company is a free zone entity.
  • Withholding tax: 0% on dividends, interest, and royalties paid to non-residents.
  • VAT: 0% on crypto transactions (transfer of digital assets). 5% on management and advisory fees.
  • Personal income tax: 0% for employees and directors.
  • Economic substance: Free zone entities must demonstrate adequate physical presence, staff, and expenditure in the UAE.

This regime is ideal for crypto businesses that want to maximize retained earnings. However, founders must ensure they meet the free zone conditions to maintain the 0% rate. For a detailed comparison, see our vs Lithuania page.

Allowed Activities Under an Abu Dhabi Crypto License

Depending on the license category, permitted activities include:

  • Operating a crypto exchange (order book, matching engine)
  • Providing custody and wallet services
  • Brokerage and dealing in crypto assets
  • Investment advisory on digital assets
  • Token issuance and management (STO, ICO)
  • Staking and lending services
  • Payment processing using crypto
  • Mining pool management

Activities not typically allowed: derivatives (unless separately regulated), gambling-related tokens, and services involving privacy coins (e.g., Monero) without enhanced due diligence. The FSRA requires all activities to be conducted in compliance with AML/CFT regulations. For a broader list of jurisdictions, visit our jurisdictions page.

Step-by-Step Process to Get a Crypto License in Abu Dhabi

  1. Pre-consultation: Define your business model and select the appropriate free zone (ADGM vs ADFZ). Consulting24 can help assess fit.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a legal entity in the chosen free zone. This includes name reservation, memorandum, and shareholder documents.
  3. Prepare business plan: Detailed description of services, target market, risk management, and compliance framework.
  4. Submit application: File with FSRA (for ADGM) or ADFZ authority. Include capital proof, AML policies, and governance documents.
  5. Regulatory review: The regulator may request additional information or interviews with key personnel. Expect 2-4 months for ADGM, 1-2 months for ADFZ.
  6. Approval and payment: Upon approval, pay the license fee and regulatory fees.
  7. Post-licensing: Set up bank account, implement AML systems, and file ongoing reports.

Banking and Payment Processing for Crypto Companies in Abu Dhabi

Abu Dhabi has a mature banking sector, but crypto companies still face challenges opening accounts. Major banks like First Abu Dhabi Bank (FAB), ADCB, and Mashreq have crypto-friendly policies, but require a valid license and strong compliance history. Some digital banks like Zand and Al Maryah Community Bank offer accounts to licensed fintechs. For payment processing, companies can integrate with local PSPs like Network International or use global providers like Stripe (via UAE entity).

It is advisable to open a corporate account before applying for the license, as proof of bank account may be required. Consulting24 can introduce you to banking partners. For a simpler banking setup, Panama offers a faster process with no minimum capital.

Compliance and Trust: Staying on the Right Side of the Regulator

Compliance is non-negotiable in Abu Dhabi. The FSRA requires:

  • AML/CFT policies and procedures
  • Appointment of a Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO)
  • Regular transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting
  • Annual audit by a licensed auditor
  • Economic substance reporting (physical office, employees, expenditure in UAE)
  • Cybersecurity and data protection measures

Failure to comply can result in fines, license suspension, or revocation. Consulting24 advises clients on building a compliance framework. This is general guidance, not legal advice. Always consult a qualified lawyer for your specific situation.

Common Mistakes When Applying for an Abu Dhabi Crypto License

  • Underestimating capital requirements: Many startups fail to raise the required capital (AED 1-15 million).
  • Incomplete business plan: Regulators expect detailed risk assessments and revenue models.
  • Ignoring economic substance: Having a mailbox office is not enough; you need real operations in the UAE.
  • Choosing the wrong free zone: ADGM is more prestigious but costly; ADFZ may be sufficient for smaller operations.
  • Delaying bank account opening: Without a local bank account, you cannot meet capital deposit requirements.

Learn from others' mistakes and consult experts. For a simpler route, consider Panama where the process is straightforward and costs are fixed.

Frequently asked questions

What is the corporate tax rate for crypto companies in Abu Dhabi?

Qualifying free zone entities in ADGM or ADFZ enjoy a 0% corporate tax rate. Mainland UAE companies pay 9% on profits above AED 375,000. There is no capital gains tax on crypto disposals.

How much does a crypto license cost in Abu Dhabi?

Costs vary by free zone: ADGM first-year total is typically EUR 80,000-145,000, while ADFZ is EUR 40,000-75,000. This includes application, license, regulatory fees, office rent, and consulting. Panama is a flat EUR 6,000.

What is the minimum capital for a crypto license in Abu Dhabi?

For ADGM, minimum capital ranges from AED 1 million (EUR 245k) for brokerage to AED 15 million (EUR 3.7M) for an exchange. ADFZ requires AED 500,000 (EUR 122k). Panama has no minimum capital.

Do I need to pay VAT on crypto transactions in Abu Dhabi?

No, VAT is 0% on the transfer of digital assets. However, advisory and management fees are subject to 5% VAT. You must register for VAT if your taxable supplies exceed AED 375,000.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Abu Dhabi?

ADGM typically takes 4-8 months, while ADFZ can be 2-4 months. This is longer than Panama (2-4 weeks) or Lithuania (3-6 months).

Can I use a crypto license from Abu Dhabi to serve clients in the EU?

No, an Abu Dhabi license does not automatically grant EU passporting. You would need a separate license under MiCA in an EU member state. However, you can serve clients outside the EU under the Abu Dhabi license.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements for an Abu Dhabi crypto license?

You must file annual audited financial statements, maintain AML/CFT procedures, appoint an MLRO, report suspicious transactions, and demonstrate economic substance (physical office, employees, expenditure).

Is Abu Dhabi better than Dubai for crypto licensing?

Abu Dhabi (ADGM) is more established with a clearer regulatory framework. Dubai's VARA also offers licenses but has different requirements. Both have 0% tax for free zones. The choice depends on your business model and target market.

Can I set up a crypto company in Abu Dhabi without a local partner?

Yes, free zones allow 100% foreign ownership. You do not need a local sponsor. However, you must have a physical office and meet economic substance requirements.

What is the cheapest alternative to Abu Dhabi for a crypto license?

Panama offers a crypto license for a flat EUR 6,000 with no minimum capital and 0% tax on foreign-source income. It is the most cost-effective option for many founders.
