What is a Panama OTC Desk?

An OTC (over-the-counter) desk facilitates large-volume crypto trades directly between buyers and sellers, bypassing public exchanges. Panama OTC desks are typically structured as Panamanian corporations (Sociedad Anónima) that execute trades for institutional clients, high-net-worth individuals, or other crypto businesses. The desk earns a spread or commission on each trade. Unlike exchange-based trading, OTC desks offer price improvement, reduced slippage, and privacy for block trades.

Key characteristics of a Panama OTC desk:

  • No specific crypto license required - operates under general corporate and AML laws.
  • No capital gains tax on crypto-to-crypto or crypto-to-fiat trades.
  • Ability to open bank accounts in Panama, the US, or international banks.
  • Client onboarding follows AML/CFT obligations under Panamanian Law 23 of 2015.
  • Trades are settled bilaterally, often with netting agreements for frequent counterparties.

Panama OTC desks can handle any cryptocurrency and major fiat currencies (USD, EUR, GBP). Many desks also offer stablecoin settlement via USDC or USDT to speed up transactions.

License Type & Regulator

Panama does not have a dedicated crypto license. OTC desks operate under the general corporate framework regulated by the Public Registry of Panama and the Superintendency of Banks (for banking activities). However, any entity engaging in financial activities must comply with AML/CFT regulations enforced by the Financial Analysis Unit (UAF). The UAF is the primary regulator for anti-money laundering oversight.

For OTC desks, the key regulatory obligations are:

  • Register as a corporation with the Public Registry.
  • Appoint a resident agent and maintain a registered office.
  • File annual tax returns (even if no tax is due).
  • Implement AML policies per Law 23 of 2015 (customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, suspicious activity reporting).
  • Register with the UAF if the desk engages in certain financial activities (e.g., money transmission).

There is no license fee or capital requirement for crypto OTC activities. However, if the desk also handles client funds or acts as a custodian, additional considerations apply. Capital Requirement

Panama imposes no minimum capital requirement for corporations. You can register a company with a nominal capital of USD 10,000 (or any amount). There is no requirement to deposit capital into a bank account. This makes Panama one of the most capital-efficient jurisdictions for OTC desks. In practice, many desks start with as little as USD 50,000 in trading capital.

Compare with EU jurisdictions under MiCA: Lithuania requires EUR 125,000 for exchange services, Estonia EUR 100,000, and Czech Republic EUR 50,000. Panama's zero capital requirement is a significant advantage for bootstrapped OTC desks. However, banks may require a minimum deposit to open an account, typically USD 5,000 to USD 25,000.

Tax Treatment

Panama operates on a territorial tax system. Income sourced outside Panama is not taxed. For an OTC desk, if trades are executed with non-Panamanian counterparties and funds are held offshore, no Panamanian income tax applies. Additionally:

  • No capital gains tax on crypto.
  • No VAT on crypto transactions.
  • No withholding tax on dividends paid to foreign shareholders.
  • Annual flat tax of USD 300 (for companies with income under USD 1 million).

However, if the desk has a physical office in Panama or employs local staff, payroll taxes apply. Consulting24 recommends a structure where the desk is a Panamanian corporation but operations are managed from abroad to maximize tax efficiency. For example, trading decisions and trade execution can occur outside Panama, while the company is registered in Panama. This is a common structure for crypto OTC desks.

Allowed Activities

A Panama OTC desk can engage in the following activities without a specific license:

  • Purchase and sale of cryptocurrencies (BTC, ETH, stablecoins, altcoins).
  • Fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-fiat conversions.
  • Block trades and negotiated settlements.
  • Liquidity provision to other desks or exchanges.
  • Escrow services (if structured properly).
  • Market making for token issuers.

Activities that may require additional licensing: holding client funds as custodian, offering derivatives or margin trading, operating a public exchange, or issuing tokens. If your OTC desk plans to offer custodial services, consult with Consulting24 to determine whether a Malta or Switzerland license might be more appropriate. For pure OTC trading, Panama is usually sufficient.

Banking & Payment Rails

Banking is often the biggest challenge for crypto OTC desks. Panama offers several options:

  • Local Panamanian banks: Banco General, BAC Credomatic, and Banistmo. They accept crypto-related businesses but require a strong compliance framework and may ask for a minimum deposit (e.g., USD 5,000). Some banks may require a personal guarantee from directors.
  • US banks: Mercury, Silicon Valley Bank (for qualified firms), or community banks. These often require a US entity or a US-based director. Mercury is popular for crypto startups.
  • International banks: Banks in Switzerland, Liechtenstein, or Singapore may open accounts for Panama-incorporated desks. These are more expensive but offer greater stability.
  • Payment processors: Circle (USDC settlement), Wyre, or local fintechs for faster fiat movement. Circle allows instant USD settlement via USDC.

Compliance & Trust

Even without a specific crypto license, Panama OTC desks must comply with AML/CFT regulations. Failure to do so can result in fines, bank account closures, or criminal liability. Key compliance steps:

  • Implement a KYC/AML program per Law 23 of 2015.
  • Conduct customer due diligence (CDD) and enhanced due diligence (EDD) for high-risk clients.
  • Monitor transactions and file suspicious activity reports (SARs) with the UAF.
  • Maintain records for at least 5 years.
  • Appoint a compliance officer (can be outsourced).
  • Conduct periodic reviews of client risk profiles.

Consulting24 provides a compliance package that includes a custom AML manual, training, and ongoing support. Note: This is general guidance, not legal advice. You should engage a local attorney for specific compliance obligations. For more on compliance, see our blog.

Common Mistakes

  • Ignoring AML compliance: Banks will close accounts if you lack proper KYC. Always have a compliance manual in place before approaching banks.
  • Using personal accounts for business: This is a red flag for banks and regulators. Use a corporate account.
  • Not registering for tax: Even if no tax is due, you must file annual returns. Failure leads to penalties and potential dissolution.
  • Choosing the wrong bank: Some banks are hostile to crypto. Use our introductions to avoid wasted time.
  • Overcomplicating the structure: A simple Panamanian corporation is often sufficient. Avoid unnecessary holding companies or trusts unless you have specific asset protection needs.
  • Neglecting to screen counterparties: Always perform due diligence on trading partners to avoid sanctions or fraud risks.
