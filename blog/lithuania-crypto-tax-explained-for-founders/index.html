Overview: Lithuania's Crypto Tax Regime in 2026

Lithuania applies a straightforward tax framework to crypto activities. The key principle is that crypto is treated as a financial asset, not as a currency. This means that gains from selling or exchanging crypto are subject to corporate income tax (CIT) at the standard rate of 15% for companies, or 5% for small enterprises (those with fewer than 10 employees and annual income under EUR 300,000). Individuals pay a 15% capital gains tax on crypto profits, but for founders operating through a company, the corporate rate applies.

Lithuania does not levy VAT on the exchange of crypto for fiat currency or on the transfer of crypto between wallets, following the European Court of Justice's 2015 decision. However, VAT may apply to other services like mining or advisory fees. The tax year is the calendar year, and companies must file annual CIT returns by June 1 of the following year. Who Needs to Understand Lithuania Crypto Tax?

This tax guide is essential for founders of crypto businesses that hold a Lithuanian CASP licence or are considering obtaining one. Specifically, it applies to:

  • Companies registered in Lithuania that operate crypto exchanges, wallet services, or custody solutions.
  • Foreign founders who have established a Lithuanian subsidiary to access the EU market under the MiCA framework.
  • Individuals who are tax residents in Lithuania and trade crypto personally, though the focus here is on corporate taxation.

If you are exploring other jurisdictions, such as Estonia or Panama, the tax rules differ significantly. Lithuania's regime is particularly attractive for EU-focused businesses due to its low corporate tax and clear guidance from the State Tax Inspectorate (VMI).

Licence Type and Regulator Context

Lithuania's crypto licensing is now fully aligned with MiCA. The regulator is the Bank of Lithuania, which issues CASP licences. Licence holders must comply with AML/CFT rules, capital requirements, and reporting obligations. The tax treatment is separate from licensing but intertwined: the VMI expects all crypto transactions to be recorded and reported accurately. Capital Requirement and Tax Implications

Under MiCA, Lithuanian CASP licences require minimum capital of EUR 50,000 for basic services (e.g., exchange), EUR 125,000 for custody, and EUR 150,000 for combined services. This capital is held as equity and is not directly taxable. However, the funds used to capitalise the company may have tax implications if sourced from abroad (e.g., transfer pricing rules). Interest earned on capital reserves is taxable as income. Founders should structure their capital contribution carefully to avoid unintended tax charges.

Tax Treatment of Crypto Transactions

Lithuania's VMI has issued detailed guidance on how to treat various crypto transactions:

  • Exchange of crypto to fiat: Taxable event. The gain is the difference between the sale price and the acquisition cost.
  • Crypto-to-crypto trades: Taxable event. Each trade is treated as a disposal of one asset and acquisition of another.
  • Mining rewards: Taxed as income at the market value when received. Subsequent sale of mined coins triggers capital gains tax.
  • Staking and lending: Rewards are taxed as income. The principal is not taxed until disposed.
  • Gifts and donations: Generally not taxable for the recipient if no consideration, but the donor may be subject to gift tax (if individual) or CIT (if business).

All transactions must be recorded in EUR using the exchange rate at the time of the transaction. The VMI accepts FIFO (first-in, first-out) and average cost methods for calculating gains.

VAT and Crypto in Lithuania

As per EU law, Lithuania exempts from VAT the exchange of virtual currencies for fiat money and the transfer of virtual currencies between wallets. This exemption applies to both the platform operator and the user. However, other services such as wallet maintenance fees, advisory services, or advertising may be subject to the standard 21% VAT. Mining services are generally considered outside the scope of VAT if the miner receives crypto as consideration. Founders should consult a tax advisor to determine their specific VAT obligations.

Allowed Activities and Their Tax Treatment

A Lithuanian CASP licence permits a range of activities, each with distinct tax implications:

  • Exchange services: Income from spreads and commissions is taxable as business income. No VAT on the exchange itself.
  • Custody and wallet services: Fees are subject to CIT and may be VATable if they are not directly related to the transfer of crypto.
  • Transfer services: Transfer fees are taxable income; the transfer itself is VAT-exempt.
  • Staking as a service: Rewards are income for the company; if passed to clients, the company may deduct the payout.

It is crucial to segregate income streams and apply the correct tax treatment to each. For a list of permitted activities, see our exchange licence page.

Step-by-Step Tax Compliance Process

Ensuring tax compliance in Lithuania involves several steps:

  1. Register for taxes: Upon company incorporation, register with the VMI for CIT, VAT (if applicable), and social tax.
  2. Set up accounting: Maintain records of all crypto transactions in EUR, including date, value, counterparty, and nature.
  3. Calculate gains/losses: Use FIFO or average cost method. Keep a ledger of all disposals.
  4. File annual CIT return: Due by June 1. Include all income and deductible expenses.
  5. Prepare transfer pricing documentation: If transacting with related parties, document arm's length pricing.
  6. Submit VAT returns: Quarterly if turnover exceeds EUR 55,000; otherwise annually.
  7. Pay any tax due: CIT is paid in advance quarterly instalments based on the previous year's liability.

Banking and Payments: Tax Reporting Considerations

Lithuanian banks are generally crypto-friendly, but they require strong AML/KYC procedures. For tax purposes, all fiat inflows and outflows must be traceable. The VMI may request bank statements to verify income. It is advisable to maintain separate accounts for operational expenses and client funds. When repatriating profits to non-resident shareholders, withholding tax may apply (typically 15%, reduced under tax treaties). Proper documentation of the beneficial owner is essential.

Benefits of Lithuania's Crypto Tax Regime

Lithuania offers several tax advantages for crypto founders:

  • Low corporate tax: 15% standard, 5% for small companies - one of the lowest in the EU.
  • No VAT on crypto exchange: Reduces compliance burden and cost for users.
  • Clear guidance: The VMI has published specific tax rulings for crypto, reducing uncertainty.
  • No capital gains tax for companies: Gains are treated as ordinary income, which can be offset by losses.
  • Double tax treaty network: Over 50 treaties to avoid double taxation.

Compliance and Trust: Avoiding Tax Pitfalls

Tax compliance is not just about filing returns; it is about building trust with regulators and partners. Common compliance requirements include:

  • Maintaining a transaction history for at least 10 years.
  • Reporting large transactions (over EUR 15,000) to the Financial Crime Investigation Service.
  • Ensuring transfer pricing documentation is in place for cross-border related-party transactions.

Common Mistakes in Lithuania Crypto Tax

Founders often make these errors:

  • Not recording crypto-to-crypto trades: Each trade is a taxable event; missing records lead to inaccurate filings.
  • Ignoring transfer pricing: Transactions with parent companies or affiliates must be at arm's length.
  • Confusing VAT exemption scope: Not all services are exempt; advisory and management fees are VATable.
  • Using wrong cost basis method: FIFO is the default; switching to average cost requires VMI approval.
  • Forgetting to file quarterly CIT prepayments: Even if no tax is due, nil returns may be required.

Avoid these by working with an experienced crypto tax accountant.

Frequently asked questions

What is the corporate tax rate for crypto companies in Lithuania?

The standard corporate income tax rate is 15%. Small companies with fewer than 10 employees and annual income under EUR 300,000 can benefit from a reduced 5% rate. This applies to all business income, including crypto trading profits.

Is VAT charged on crypto transactions in Lithuania?

No. The exchange of virtual currencies for fiat money and transfers between wallets are exempt from VAT, following EU law. However, other services like advisory or management fees may be subject to the standard 21% VAT.

Are crypto-to-crypto trades taxable in Lithuania?

Yes. Each crypto-to-crypto trade is treated as a disposal of one asset and acquisition of another. The gain or loss must be calculated in EUR and reported on the corporate tax return. FIFO or average cost methods are accepted.

What is the tax treatment of staking rewards?

Staking rewards are taxed as ordinary income at the market value when received. If the company then sells the staked coins, any subsequent gain or loss is treated as capital gain or loss. The cost basis is the value at the time of receipt.

Do I need to file quarterly tax returns in Lithuania?

Companies must pay corporate income tax in advance quarterly instalments based on the previous year's liability. VAT returns are quarterly if turnover exceeds EUR 55,000. Annual CIT return is due by June 1.

Can I use losses from crypto trading to offset other income?

Yes. Capital losses from crypto disposals can be offset against capital gains from other assets. If losses exceed gains, they can be carried forward for up to 5 years. However, losses cannot be offset against non-capital income.

What records must I keep for tax purposes?

You must keep records of every crypto transaction, including date, value in EUR, counterparty, and nature (buy, sell, transfer, fee). Records should be retained for at least 10 years. The VMI may request them during an audit.

How does Lithuania tax mining income?

Mining rewards are taxed as income at the fair market value of the coins when received. The miner can deduct expenses such as electricity and hardware. Subsequent sale of mined coins triggers capital gains tax on the difference from the value at receipt.

Is there a tax treaty between Lithuania and my home country?

Lithuania has double tax treaties with over 50 countries. These treaties can reduce or eliminate withholding tax on dividends, interest, and royalties. Check the specific treaty with your country to determine the applicable rate.

What happens if I fail to report crypto income?

Failure to report can result in penalties up to 50% of the underpaid tax, plus interest. In serious cases, criminal charges may apply. It is crucial to engage a tax advisor to ensure full compliance.
