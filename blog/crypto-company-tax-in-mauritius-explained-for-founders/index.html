What Is Crypto Company Tax in Mauritius?

The term crypto company tax in Mauritius refers to the corporate income tax applied to businesses engaged in crypto-related activities, such as trading, mining, staking, or providing exchange services. Mauritius does not have a specific crypto tax law; instead, crypto income is treated as ordinary business income under the Income Tax Act. The standard corporate tax rate is 15%, but companies holding a Global Business Corporation (GBC) license can benefit from an effective tax rate of 3% after applying a deemed foreign tax credit of 80%.

This regime makes Mauritius one of the most tax-efficient jurisdictions for crypto businesses, especially when combined with its network of double taxation treaties and stable legal environment. However, the tax treatment depends on the type of crypto activity and the company's structure. For example, capital gains from crypto are generally not taxed separately, but trading profits are taxable as income. The effective 3% rate applies only to foreign-source income, which is the typical case for GBCs. To illustrate, if your crypto exchange generates EUR 1 million in trading fees from non-Mauritian clients, the tax payable would be around EUR 30,000, compared to EUR 150,000 in a jurisdiction with a 15% rate. This can significantly impact your bottom line, especially for high-volume operations.

License Type and Regulator

Mauritius does not have a dedicated crypto license. Instead, crypto businesses typically operate under a Global Business Corporation (GBC) license issued by the Financial Services Commission (FSC). The GBC regime is designed for companies conducting business outside Mauritius. For crypto activities that involve custody or exchange of virtual assets, the FSC may require a Virtual Asset and Initial Token Offering Services (VAITOS) license under the Financial Services Act. However, as of 2026, the VAITOS regime is not yet fully operational; the FSC has issued guidelines but no formal licensing framework. Most crypto businesses use the GBC structure.

The regulator for all financial services, including crypto, is the Financial Services Commission (FSC) Mauritius. Companies must comply with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorist financing (CTF) regulations under the Financial Intelligence and Anti-Money Laundering Act. The FSC expects GBCs to have a physical office in Mauritius, at least one resident director, and adequate compliance infrastructure. Failure to meet these substance requirements can lead to license revocation.

Capital Requirement

There is no statutory minimum capital requirement for a GBC in Mauritius. However, the FSC expects a GBC to have adequate capital to cover its operational expenses and liabilities. Typically, a minimum share capital of USD 1 (or equivalent) is sufficient for incorporation, but for substance purposes, the company should maintain sufficient funds to meet its obligations. For crypto businesses, the FSC may require a higher capital base if the company holds client assets or engages in high-risk activities. As a rule of thumb, founders should budget at least EUR 50,000 in working capital to demonstrate substance. This is not a fixed requirement but a practical guideline. In contrast, under MiCA in the EU, capital requirements are tiered: EUR 50,000 for basic services, EUR 125,000 for exchange and custody, and EUR 150,000 for trading platforms. Mauritius offers more flexibility in this regard.

Tax Treatment of Crypto Activities

Under Mauritius tax law, crypto income is treated as ordinary income. The effective tax rate for a GBC is 3% after the deemed foreign tax credit. Key points:

  • Trading profits: Taxable at 3% effective rate.
  • Mining and staking rewards: Taxable as income at the time of receipt, valued at fair market value.
  • Capital gains: Not taxed separately; gains from sale of crypto held as investment may be considered capital in nature, but the FSC treats most crypto gains as income.
  • Dividends and interest: Taxable at 3% if derived from foreign sources.
  • Withholding tax: No withholding tax on dividends paid to non-residents.
  • VAT: No VAT on crypto transactions; crypto is treated as an exempt supply.

Mauritius has a territorial tax system: only income sourced in Mauritius is taxed. Since GBCs earn income from outside Mauritius, the effective tax is low. However, the tax authorities may scrutinize transfer pricing and substance requirements. For example, if your company has a single director in Mauritius but all operations are offshore, the tax office may argue that the income is sourced in Mauritius and apply the full 15% rate. Proper substance planning is essential.

A GBC in Mauritius can engage in a wide range of crypto activities, including:

However, activities that involve serving Mauritius residents or holding client assets may require additional licensing under the VAITOS regime once operational. For now, most businesses operate under the GBC license with AML compliance. It is important to note that the FSC may impose restrictions on certain high-risk activities, such as privacy coins or unregistered securities. Step-by-Step Process to Set Up a Crypto Company in Mauritius

  1. Choose a service provider: Engage a licensed management company or law firm in Mauritius.
  2. Incorporate the company: Register a GBC with the Registrar of Companies. Submit memorandum and articles, registered address, and director details.
  3. Apply for a GBC license: Submit application to the FSC with business plan, AML policies, and proof of substance (office lease, local staff).
  4. Open a bank account: Apply for a corporate bank account in Mauritius or abroad. Note: banking for crypto is challenging; consider payment processors like EMI licenses.
  5. Comply with AML: Appoint a compliance officer, register with the Financial Intelligence Unit, and implement AML procedures.
  6. Ongoing compliance: File annual returns, audited financial statements, and tax returns. Maintain substance (local office, at least one director resident in Mauritius).

Banking and Payment Solutions for Crypto Companies

Banking is one of the biggest challenges for crypto companies in Mauritius. Traditional banks are often reluctant to open accounts for crypto businesses due to perceived risk. However, some local banks like MCB and SBM have opened to regulated entities. Alternatives include:

  • Payment service providers with EMI licenses (e.g., in Lithuania or Estonia).
  • Cryptocurrency-friendly banks in Switzerland or Singapore.
  • Digital banks like Revolut Business (subject to compliance).

Common Mistakes When Setting Up a Crypto Company in Mauritius

  • Insufficient substance: Not having a local office or director can lead to license denial.
  • Ignoring AML requirements: The FSC is strict; non-compliance can shut down operations.
  • Underestimating costs: Annual compliance fees can exceed EUR 10,000, including audit and AML officer fees.
  • Choosing the wrong structure: Some activities may require a VAITOS license, which is not yet available.
  • Banking delays: Assuming a bank account will be easy to open; many crypto companies face months of delays.
