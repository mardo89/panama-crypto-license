Overview: Cyprus vs Panama for Crypto Licensing

Cyprus, an EU member state, has fully implemented the Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA) as of 2026. Its crypto asset service provider (CASP) regime requires firms to hold capital based on service classes: EUR 50,000 for simple services (e.g., custody), EUR 125,000 for exchanges, and EUR 150,000 for combined services. The Cyprus Securities and Exchange Commission (CySEC) is the regulator.

Panama, by contrast, is a non-EU jurisdiction with no specific crypto licensing law as of 2026. Instead, crypto companies operate under general corporate and AML frameworks. The cost for a Panama company setup through Consulting24 is a flat EUR 6,000, with no minimum capital requirement for standard operations. Panama offers a territorial tax system, meaning income earned outside Panama is tax-free. This makes it attractive for international crypto businesses.

Both jurisdictions have their merits. Cyprus provides regulatory clarity and EU passporting, while Panama offers cost efficiency and tax advantages. The choice depends on your business model, target market, and risk tolerance. For a broader view of options, explore our jurisdictions page.

Who Needs a Crypto License in Cyprus or Panama?

Any business offering crypto asset services needs to consider licensing. In Cyprus, under MiCA, services such as exchange between crypto and fiat, exchange between crypto assets, custody, transfer services, and portfolio management require a CASP license. This applies to both local and foreign firms serving EU clients.

In Panama, there is no formal crypto license. However, if you handle fiat currency or provide services to Panamanian residents, you must comply with general AML laws and register with the Financial Analysis Unit (UAF). Many crypto firms choose Panama for non-EU operations, using it as a holding or operating company while obtaining licenses elsewhere for EU access.

Typical clients for Cyprus: EU-facing exchanges, custodians, DeFi platforms, and payment processors. License Type & Regulator

Cyprus: The license is a Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) authorization under MiCA, regulated by CySEC. It is a full license with mandatory compliance requirements. The regime categorizes services into three tiers with corresponding capital requirements.

Panama: There is no dedicated crypto license. Companies operate under a standard corporate structure (e.g., Sociedad Anónima) and must register for AML purposes if engaging in financial activities. The regulator for AML is the Superintendencia de Bancos de Panamá (SBP) and the UAF. However, for most crypto businesses, no specific license is required, only general corporate compliance.

Capital Requirement

Cyprus: Under MiCA, minimum capital is tied to service classes: EUR 50,000 for custody and transfer; EUR 125,000 for exchange services; EUR 150,000 for combined services. This capital must be maintained and can be in cash or liquid assets. It serves as a buffer for operational risks and must be evidenced at application.

Panama: There is no minimum capital requirement for a standard corporation. You can start with as little as USD 10,000 in authorized capital, but no paid-up capital is required. This makes Panama highly accessible for startups. However, some banks may require a minimum deposit to open an account, typically USD 5,000 to USD 25,000.

If you are considering Panama for a crypto company, the absence of capital requirements reduces upfront costs significantly. But you may still need to demonstrate financial stability for banking relationships. For a deeper dive into capital rules across jurisdictions, see our requirements page.

Tax Treatment

Cyprus: Corporate tax rate is 12.5% on worldwide income. Capital gains from crypto trading may be taxable depending on the nature of the activity (trading vs investment). VAT is not applied to crypto-to-crypto exchanges but may apply to fiat conversions. Cyprus has a wide network of double tax treaties, which can reduce withholding taxes on dividends and interest.

Panama: Territorial tax system: only income sourced in Panama is taxed. For a crypto company, if your clients and operations are outside Panama, you pay 0% corporate tax. There is no capital gains tax on offshore income. Panama also has no VAT on services. This is a major advantage for international crypto businesses. However, you must be careful not to create a taxable presence in Panama through local operations or management.

Important: Tax residency rules apply. If you manage the company from Panama, you may become tax resident. Consulting24 recommends consulting a tax advisor for your specific situation. For more on tax planning, read our blog.

Allowed Activities

Cyprus (CASP license): You can operate a crypto exchange (fiat and crypto pairs), custody wallet, transfer services, and portfolio management. DeFi protocols and certain token issuances may require additional approvals. Stablecoin issuance is regulated under MiCA. The license covers a broad range of services but does not automatically permit all activities; each service class must be applied for.

Panama: No specific restrictions on crypto activities. You can run an exchange, wallet, mining pool, DeFi platform, or token project. However, if you deal with fiat currency, you need a money transmitter license (which is difficult to obtain). Most Panama crypto companies operate purely in crypto or use third-party payment processors for fiat. Panama also allows token issuance under general securities laws if tokens are deemed securities.

Step-by-Step Process

Cyprus CASP License

  1. Pre-application: Prepare business plan, AML policies, and capital proof. Engage a local compliance consultant.
  2. Submit application to CySEC with detailed documentation, including organisational structure and source of funds.
  3. CySEC reviews (6-12 months), may request changes or additional information. Expect at least one round of questions.
  4. On approval, pay license fee and maintain capital in a Cyprus bank account.
  5. Ongoing: submit periodic reports, comply with AML audits, and undergo annual reviews.

Panama Company Setup

  1. Choose company name and structure (Sociedad Anónima).
  2. Engage a registered agent (e.g., Consulting24) to file incorporation with the Public Registry.
  3. Obtain corporate documents (articles, tax ID, etc.). This typically takes 1-2 weeks.
  4. Open a bank account (may require crypto-friendly bank). Prepare a business plan and proof of source of funds.
  5. Register for AML if engaging in financial activities. This involves filing a registration with the UAF and appointing a compliance officer.

Banking & Payments

Banking is a challenge for crypto companies in both jurisdictions. In Cyprus, traditional banks are cautious, but some (e.g., Hellenic Bank) work with licensed CASPs. You may also use EMI accounts from providers like Revolut Business or Payoneer. However, expect strict due diligence and potential account freezes if transactions are flagged.

In Panama, banking is more open to international companies, but crypto-related accounts are still rare. Many Panama firms use payment processors like Airwallex or Mercury, or crypto-to-fiat gateways like Binance Pay. Panama's banking secrecy laws can be an advantage for privacy, but they also mean banks are wary of high-risk industries. Some Panama banks require a minimum deposit of USD 10,000 and a personal visit.

Benefits

Cyprus: EU passporting allows you to serve clients across all EU member states without additional licenses. Regulatory clarity under MiCA gives confidence to partners and investors. Cyprus has a skilled workforce, good infrastructure, and a reputable legal system. The 12.5% corporate tax rate is competitive within the EU.

Panama: Low cost (EUR 6,000 flat), no minimum capital, territorial tax (0% on offshore income), fast setup (1-2 weeks), no crypto license required, privacy, and strategic location for Americas. Panama also has a stable dollarised economy (uses USD as currency) and no exchange controls.

For many founders, Panama offers the fastest and cheapest way to incorporate a crypto company, while Cyprus provides the regulatory stamp needed for EU operations. The best choice depends on your market focus. If you are also considering other EU options, compare with our Estonia crypto license or Lithuania crypto license.

Compliance & Trust

Cyprus CASP license signals high compliance standards to partners and customers. You must implement strong AML/KYC, report suspicious transactions, and undergo regular audits. This builds trust but requires ongoing investment in compliance staff or outsourcing. Non-compliance can lead to fines or license revocation.

Panama has no specific crypto compliance regime, but you must follow general AML laws. Some crypto firms choose to voluntarily comply with FATF recommendations to enhance credibility. Without a license, you may face challenges with banks and partners who require regulatory oversight. However, Panama's corporate registry is not public, offering privacy that some founders value.

Common Mistakes

1. Assuming Panama is automatically tax-free: You must ensure no income is sourced in Panama. If you have local clients or employees, you may be taxed. 2. Overlooking AML registration in Panama: Even without a license, you may need to register with UAF if you handle crypto-to-fiat transactions. 3. Choosing Cyprus without sufficient capital: The EUR 50,000-150,000 requirement is real and must be maintained. 4. Ignoring banking challenges: Start banking applications early; in Cyprus, banks may require a local director. 5. Not considering future MiCA expansion: If you plan to serve EU clients, Cyprus may be necessary, but Panama can be a holding company. 6. Rushing the application: In Cyprus, incomplete applications cause delays. In Panama, failing to file annual returns can lead to dissolution.

Alternatives: Comparison with Other Jurisdictions

Beyond Cyprus and Panama, consider Estonia (Estonia crypto license), Lithuania (Lithuania crypto license), and Dubai (Dubai crypto license). Frequently asked questions

What is the main difference between Cyprus and Panama for crypto licensing?

Cyprus requires a CASP license under MiCA with capital from EUR 50,000 to 150,000, regulated by CySEC. Panama has no specific crypto license; companies operate under general corporate law with no minimum capital. Cyprus offers EU passporting; Panama offers territorial tax (0% on offshore income) and faster setup.

How much does it cost to set up a crypto company in Panama?

Consulting24 offers a flat fee of EUR 6,000 for Panama company setup, including incorporation, registered agent, and initial documentation. There are no government license fees. Annual compliance costs are typically EUR 1,000-2,000. This is significantly lower than Cyprus, where setup and license application can cost EUR 20,000-40,000.

What is the timeline for getting a Cyprus CASP license?

The Cyprus CASP license application process takes 6 to 12 months. This includes preparing documentation, submitting to CySEC, and responding to queries. The timeline can be shorter if you have all documents ready and a clean background. In contrast, Panama company setup takes 1-2 weeks.

Do I need a physical office in Cyprus or Panama?

For Cyprus, you need a registered office and may need a local director or compliance officer. For Panama, a registered agent address is sufficient; no physical office is required. However, for banking, you may need a local presence or a virtual office.

Can I use Panama to serve EU clients?

Yes, but you must comply with EU regulations if you actively market to EU residents. Panama does not provide EU passporting. Many firms use Panama as a holding company and obtain a separate EU license (e.g., in Lithuania or Estonia) for EU operations. Consulting24 can advise on this structure.

What are the tax implications for a crypto company in Panama?

Panama has a territorial tax system: only income sourced in Panama is taxed. If your crypto company's clients and operations are outside Panama, you pay 0% corporate tax. There is no capital gains tax on offshore income. However, if you have local employees or clients, you may be subject to Panama tax. Consult a tax advisor.

Is it possible to get a bank account for a Panama crypto company?

Yes, but it can be challenging. Many Panama banks are cautious of crypto. Some options include Banco General, Towerbank, or international EMIs like Mercury or Airwallex. Consulting24 can introduce you to banking partners. You may need a minimum deposit of USD 5,000-25,000 and a business plan.

What activities are allowed under a Cyprus CASP license?

Under MiCA, a Cyprus CASP license allows exchange between crypto and fiat, exchange between crypto assets, custody and administration, transfer services, and portfolio management. DeFi and token issuance may require additional approvals. Stablecoin issuance is regulated separately.

Can I switch from Panama to Cyprus later?

Yes, many firms start in Panama for speed and low cost, then later apply for a Cyprus CASP license when they need EU passporting. Consulting24 can help with both setups and ensure a smooth transition. However, you must meet Cyprus capital requirements at that time.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements for a Cyprus CASP?

Annual compliance costs are EUR 10,000-20,000. You must submit periodic reports to CySEC, conduct AML audits, maintain capital, and appoint a compliance officer. Non-compliance can result in fines or license suspension. Panama has lighter compliance but still requires annual filings and AML registration if applicable.
