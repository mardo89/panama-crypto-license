What Is the Romanian Crypto License (MiCA CASP)?

Under MiCA, which is fully in force across the EU in 2026, Romania requires any company offering crypto asset services to obtain authorisation as a CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider). The Romanian Financial Supervisory Authority (ASF) is the designated competent authority for licensing and supervision. The license covers services such as custody, exchange, transfer, and advisory. Unlike the pre-MiCA VASP register model, the Romanian CASP license imposes minimum capital requirements based on the service class: EUR 50,000 for basic services, EUR 125,000 for exchange and custody, and EUR 150,000 for more complex offerings. The cost of a crypto license in Romania includes these capital requirements plus application fees, legal setup, and ongoing compliance.

Who Needs a Romanian Crypto License?

Any legal entity established in Romania that provides one or more crypto asset services to clients on a professional basis must hold a CASP license. This includes:

  • Exchanges offering fiat-to-crypto or crypto-to-crypto trading.
  • Custodial wallet providers.
  • Brokers and dealers in crypto assets.
  • Platforms for crypto asset transfers.
  • Advisors and portfolio managers for crypto assets.

If your target market includes Romanian residents or you plan to operate from Romania, you need this license. However, if you are looking for a faster, lower-cost entry, jurisdictions like Panama offer a flat EUR 6,000 license with no minimum capital and a simpler process. License Type & Regulator

The license is a MiCA CASP authorisation, regulated by the Autoritatea de Supraveghere Financiară (ASF) in Romania. The ASF is the integrated financial regulator overseeing insurance, capital markets, and now crypto assets. The application process is handled through the ASF's dedicated fintech unit. There is no separate 'crypto license' distinct from the CASP; it is a single authorisation covering all permitted activities. The ASF coordinates with the European Banking Authority (EBA) and other EU regulators under MiCA. For comparison, Panama's regulator is the Superintendencia de Bancos de Panamá (SBP) for financial activities, but crypto licensing is handled under Law 23 of 2015 for securities, with a simpler regime.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital for CASPs is tiered by the type of services offered. For Romania, the ASF applies the following tiers as of 2026:

  • EUR 50,000 for custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients (excluding exchange services).
  • EUR 125,000 for exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency or other crypto assets, and for placing crypto assets.
  • EUR 150,000 for operating a trading platform for crypto assets.

These capital requirements are permanent and must be maintained at all times. They can be held as cash or high-quality liquid assets. In contrast, Panama has no minimum capital requirement for its crypto license, which is a significant advantage for early-stage ventures. Tax Treatment of Crypto in Romania

Romania applies a corporate income tax of 16% on profits from crypto asset services. Additionally, a 10% tax on certain crypto-to-fiat conversions for individuals may apply, but for licensed CASPs, the corporate regime is standard. VAT is generally not applicable to crypto services under EU law, but professional advice is recommended. Romania also requires transfer pricing documentation for related-party transactions. Compared to Panama, which has a territorial tax system and no capital gains tax on crypto, Romania's tax burden is higher. Panama's corporate tax is 25% but only on Panama-source income; offshore crypto income is exempt. For a full tax comparison, see our cost guide.

Allowed Activities Under the Romanian License

A Romanian CASP license permits the following activities as defined in MiCA:

  • Providing custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients.
  • Operation of a trading platform for crypto assets.
  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency.
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets.
  • Execution of orders for crypto assets on behalf of clients.
  • Placing of crypto assets.
  • Receiving and transmitting orders for crypto assets on behalf of clients.
  • Providing advice on crypto assets.
  • Providing portfolio management on crypto assets.
  • Providing transfer services for crypto assets on behalf of clients.

All activities are subject to strict AML/KYC requirements under the EU's AML package. The license does not cover crypto asset issuance (e.g., ICOs) which may require a separate white paper approval. Panama's license covers similar activities but with more flexibility and fewer restrictions.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a Romanian crypto license involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates throughout, ensuring compliance with ASF requirements.

  1. Pre-application preparation: Company incorporation in Romania, appointment of directors and AML officer, preparation of business plan and risk assessment.
  2. Documentation: Drafting AML/KYC policies, internal controls, IT security protocols, and governance framework.
  3. Capital deposit: Opening a bank account in Romania and depositing the required minimum capital.
  4. Submission: Filing the application with the ASF, including all supporting documents and payment of the application fee.
  5. Review and interview: ASF reviews the application, may request additional information, and conducts an interview with management.
  6. Approval and licensing: Upon approval, the license is issued. The company must then register with the National Trade Register Office.
  7. Ongoing compliance: Annual reporting, AML audits, and supervision fees.

Benefits of the Romanian Crypto License

Despite the higher cost, the Romanian CASP license offers several advantages:

  • EU passporting: Once licensed, you can provide services across all EU member states without additional authorisation.
  • Regulatory clarity: MiCA provides a harmonised framework, reducing legal uncertainty.
  • Access to EU banking: Licensed entities find it easier to open bank accounts within the EU.
  • Credibility: A regulated status enhances trust with clients and partners.
  • Skilled workforce: Romania has a strong IT talent pool and relatively low operational costs compared to Western Europe.

Compliance & Trust: Ongoing Obligations

Maintaining a Romanian crypto license requires continuous compliance. Key obligations include:

  • Appointment of a local AML compliance officer (can be outsourced).
  • Annual AML audit by an external auditor.
  • Submission of annual financial statements and regulatory reports to ASF.
  • Maintenance of minimum capital at all times.
  • Notification of any material changes (e.g., change of management, new services).
  • Adherence to EU travel rule and sanctions screening.

Non-compliance can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. This is general guidance, not legal advice. Always consult with a qualified legal expert for your specific situation. Panama's compliance is lighter, with annual renewal and basic AML requirements.

Common Mistakes When Applying for a Romanian Crypto License

Many applicants face delays or rejection due to avoidable errors. Common pitfalls include:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing business plan, risk assessment, or AML policies.
  • Underestimating capital requirements: Choosing a service class without adequate capital.
  • Unrealistic business projections: ASF scrutinises financial forecasts.
  • Lack of local presence: Not having a physical office or local management.
  • Ignoring travel rule compliance: Failure to implement systems for crypto transfer data sharing.

Frequently asked questions

What is the total cost of a crypto license in Romania in 2026?

The total first-year cost (excluding minimum capital) is estimated between EUR 15,000 and EUR 38,000, including government fees, legal fees, and setup. The minimum capital requirement ranges from EUR 50,000 to EUR 150,000 depending on services. Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 all-inclusive license with no capital requirement.

How long does it take to get a Romanian crypto license?

The typical timeline is 6 to 12 months from application submission to approval. This can vary based on the completeness of your application and ASF workload. Panama's license can be obtained in 2-3 months.

What are the minimum capital requirements for a Romanian CASP?

Under MiCA, capital requirements are EUR 50,000 for custody only, EUR 125,000 for exchange services, and EUR 150,000 for operating a trading platform. These amounts must be maintained at all times.

Can I passport my Romanian license to other EU countries?

Yes, a Romanian CASP license allows you to provide services across the EU under the MiCA passporting regime without additional authorisation in each member state.

What taxes apply to a Romanian crypto license holder?

Corporate income tax is 16% on profits. There is no VAT on crypto services. Individual crypto gains may be taxed at 10% in certain cases. Panama has no tax on offshore crypto income.

Is it mandatory to have a local office in Romania?

Yes, you need a registered office and a physical presence in Romania. This can be a serviced office. Panama also requires a local registered agent but no physical office.

What are the main differences between Romania and Panama for crypto licensing?

Romania has higher costs (EUR 15k-38k plus capital), longer timeline (6-12 months), and 16% tax, but offers EU passporting. Panama has a flat EUR 6,000 fee, no capital, 2-3 months timeline, and zero tax on offshore income, but no EU passport.
