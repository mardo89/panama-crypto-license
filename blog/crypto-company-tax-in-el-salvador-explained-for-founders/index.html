What Is Crypto Company Tax in El Salvador?

Crypto company tax in El Salvador refers to the specific tax obligations and incentives that apply to businesses dealing with cryptocurrencies. The country has positioned itself as a pro-Bitcoin hub, with laws that exempt Bitcoin and other crypto assets from capital gains tax. However, income derived from crypto-related activities (e.g., trading, mining, payments) is subject to standard corporate income tax at 30%, with a reduced rate of 25% for companies with annual revenue under USD 150,000. There is no VAT on crypto transactions, and no withholding tax on dividends paid to non-residents. This regime makes El Salvador attractive for crypto startups, but careful structuring is needed to avoid double taxation. For example, a company that trades crypto for its own account pays 0% capital gains, but if it charges fees for exchange services, those fees are taxable as ordinary income. The lack of a double tax treaty network means founders must rely on domestic tax credits in their home country. Consulting24 advises on structuring to minimize global tax exposure.

License Type and Regulator

El Salvador does not have a dedicated crypto license regime as of 2026. Instead, crypto companies operate under general business laws and must register with the National Registry (CNR) and obtain a tax ID (NIT). The Central Reserve Bank (BCR) oversees Bitcoin-related activities under the Bitcoin Law, but there is no licensing body akin to Lithuania's Bank of Lithuania or Estonia's FIU. Tax Treatment

The tax treatment in El Salvador is one of the most favorable for crypto companies. Key points:

  • Capital gains: 0% on Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies (per Bitcoin Law).
  • Corporate income tax: 30% standard; 25% for annual revenue under USD 150,000.
  • VAT: No VAT on crypto transactions; standard VAT of 13% applies to other goods/services.
  • Withholding tax: 0% on dividends paid to non-residents.
  • Transfer pricing: Rules apply for related-party transactions.

This regime can lead to significant savings compared to EU countries where crypto gains are often taxed as income. However, founders must consider their home country's tax laws. For a territorial tax system, Panama offers 0% tax on foreign-source income. Compare on our vs Lithuania page. Note that El Salvador does not tax unrealized gains, which is a key advantage for holding crypto long-term.

  • Payment processing using Bitcoin
  • Mining and staking

However, activities involving fiat currency may require additional registration with the BCR. For a fully regulated environment with a clear license, consider our exchange license page or Lithuania. In El Salvador, you can also operate a Bitcoin ATM network without a license, but you must register as a money service business if you handle fiat conversions.

Step-by-Step Process to Set Up a Crypto Company in El Salvador

  1. Choose company type: Typically a Sociedad Anónima (SA) or Sociedad de Responsabilidad Limitada (SRL).
  2. Reserve company name: With the National Registry (CNR).
  3. Draft statutes: Legal document outlining share structure, directors, etc.
  4. Notarize and register: Submit to CNR; pay registration fees.
  5. Obtain NIT: Tax identification number from the Ministry of Finance.
  6. Open bank account: In a local bank or international bank with presence in El Salvador.
  7. Register for VAT (if applicable): For non-crypto services.
  8. Comply with AML: Register as a service provider if handling third-party funds.

Common Mistakes When Structuring a Crypto Company in El Salvador

  • Ignoring home country tax: El Salvador's 0% capital gains does not exempt you from tax in your country of residence.
  • Not registering for VAT: If you provide non-crypto services, VAT applies.
  • Using personal accounts for business: Commingling funds can lead to legal issues.
  • Assuming no compliance: AML registration is mandatory for certain activities.
  • Overlooking transfer pricing: Related-party transactions must be at arm's length.

Avoid these pitfalls by consulting with experts. Our blog has more insights. Another common mistake is failing to file annual tax returns on time, which can result in penalties and loss of the reduced tax rate.

Frequently asked questions

What is the corporate tax rate for crypto companies in El Salvador?

The standard corporate income tax rate is 30%. For companies with annual revenue under USD 150,000, a reduced rate of 25% applies. There is no capital gains tax on Bitcoin or other cryptocurrencies.

Is there VAT on crypto transactions in El Salvador?

No, crypto transactions are exempt from VAT. However, if your company provides non-crypto goods or services, the standard 13% VAT applies. It is important to separate crypto income from other revenue streams for tax reporting.

Do I need a license to operate a crypto exchange in El Salvador?

No specific crypto license is required. You must register your company with the CNR and obtain a NIT. If you handle fiat currency, you may need to register with the BCR as a money service business. For a regulated alternative, consider Lithuania's CASP license.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a crypto company in El Salvador?

There is no legal minimum capital requirement. However, you must demonstrate sufficient funds to cover initial expenses. In practice, USD 1,000-2,000 is acceptable. This is a key advantage over EU jurisdictions where capital requirements start at EUR 50,000.

How long does it take to set up a crypto company in El Salvador?

Typically 3-6 weeks from start to finish. This includes company incorporation, tax registration, and bank account opening. Panama offers a faster setup at a flat fee of EUR 6,000, often completed in 2-3 weeks.

Are dividends taxed in El Salvador for non-residents?

No, dividends paid to non-residents are not subject to withholding tax. This makes El Salvador attractive for repatriating profits. However, your home country may tax the dividends, so check your local tax treaty provisions.

Can I use El Salvador to avoid taxes in my home country?

No, you must comply with your home country's tax laws. El Salvador's 0% capital gains tax does not override your residence country's rules. You may need to pay tax on worldwide income if you are a tax resident elsewhere. Consult a tax advisor.

Is El Salvador on the FATF grey list?

No, as of 2026, El Salvador is not on the FATF grey list. The country has implemented AML/CFT measures, including registration requirements for crypto businesses. This enhances its reputation for compliance.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements for a crypto company in El Salvador?

You must file annual tax returns, maintain proper accounting records, and comply with AML obligations if you handle third-party funds. There is no requirement for a physical office or local director, but having a local agent is recommended.

How does El Salvador compare to Panama for crypto companies?

Panama offers 0% corporate tax on foreign-source income and a flat setup fee of EUR 6,000. El Salvador has 0% capital gains but 25-30% corporate tax on active income. Panama is simpler for international businesses; El Salvador is better for companies holding crypto long-term.
