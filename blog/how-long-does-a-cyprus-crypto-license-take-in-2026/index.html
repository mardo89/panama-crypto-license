What Is a Cyprus Crypto License?

The Cyprus crypto license, officially a Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) registration under the Cyprus Securities and Exchange Commission (CySEC), is required for any entity offering crypto-related services in or from Cyprus. Since MiCA came into full force in 2025, Cyprus has transposed the EU regulation into national law, replacing its earlier innovative framework.

Key services covered include:

  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat or other crypto assets
  • Custody and administration of crypto assets
  • Operation of a trading platform
  • Transfer services for crypto assets
  • Advice and portfolio management

The license is valid across the EU under the passporting regime, making Cyprus a gateway to the European market. For example, a Cyprus-licensed exchange can serve clients in Germany, France, and Spain without applying for separate licenses. However, the application process is rigorous and requires a substantial commitment of time and capital.

Who Needs a Cyprus Crypto License?

Any legal entity established in Cyprus that provides crypto asset services to clients in the EU must obtain a CASP license from CySEC. This includes startups, established exchanges, custodians, and DeFi projects that fall under MiCA's scope.

You need a license if you:

  • Operate a crypto exchange (centralized or decentralized if you control the platform)
  • Offer wallet services or custody
  • Provide crypto-to-crypto or crypto-to-fiat conversion
  • Run a crypto ATM network
  • Offer portfolio management or advice on crypto assets

If your business is based outside the EU but targets EU clients, you must either establish a Cyprus entity or passport from another EU member state. Consulting24 can help you structure your entity and application. For example, a US-based exchange wanting to serve EU customers would need to set up a Cyprus subsidiary and apply for the license. Non-EU firms often find the process more complex due to additional documentation requirements.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Cyprus Securities and Exchange Commission (CySEC). Under MiCA, there is a single CASP license category, but capital requirements vary by service class.

The license types are not separate; instead, you apply for authorisation to provide specific services. CySEC assesses your application based on the scope of activities. For instance, if you only offer crypto-to-fiat exchange, you apply for that service class. If you also offer custody, you need to meet the higher capital requirement.

For comparison, Lithuania and Estonia also operate under MiCA, but their timelines and costs differ. Cyprus is known for its thorough but efficient process. CySEC has a dedicated fintech innovation hub that provides guidance to applicants, which can help streamline the process.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for CASPs in Cyprus is tiered based on the services provided:

  • Tier 1 (EUR 50,000): For services like exchange of crypto for fiat, transfer services, and advice.
  • Tier 2 (EUR 125,000): For custody and administration of crypto assets.
  • Tier 3 (EUR 150,000): For operating a trading platform.

These amounts must be held as own funds and maintained at all times. Additional capital may be required based on operational risks. For example, a platform with high trading volumes might need extra capital to cover potential losses. Compare this with Panama, which has no minimum capital requirement, making it more accessible for startups. In Cyprus, the capital must be in liquid form, such as cash or government bonds, and cannot be used for operational expenses.

Tax Treatment for Crypto Businesses in Cyprus

Cyprus offers a favourable corporate tax regime. The standard corporate income tax rate is 12.5%, one of the lowest in the EU. Capital gains from the sale of crypto assets are generally exempt from tax, but trading profits are taxable as ordinary income. This means that if you hold crypto as an investment and sell at a profit, you may not owe tax, but if you trade frequently, profits are subject to corporate tax.

VAT treatment: Crypto-to-fiat exchanges are exempt from VAT, while crypto-to-crypto exchanges may be subject to VAT depending on the nature. Cyprus has not introduced a specific crypto tax yet, but general tax principles apply. For example, fees charged for exchange services are subject to VAT at the standard rate of 19% unless an exemption applies.

Allowed Activities Under the Cyprus Crypto License

Once licensed, you can provide the following services to EU clients:

  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets
  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Operation of a crypto trading platform
  • Transfer services for crypto assets
  • Advice on crypto assets
  • Portfolio management of crypto assets

You cannot engage in activities outside the scope of your license. CySEC requires regular reporting and compliance with AML/CFT obligations. For instance, if you are licensed for exchange only, you cannot offer custody services without a separate authorization. The license also requires you to have adequate systems in place to protect client assets and prevent market abuse.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Pre-application preparation: Engage a local legal advisor, prepare business plan, compliance policies, AML procedures, and risk assessment. This stage typically takes 1-2 months.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Cyprus limited company with a physical office and local directors (if required). The incorporation can be done in 1-2 weeks.
  3. Submit application to CySEC: Complete the CASP application form, provide all supporting documents, and pay the fee. CySEC will acknowledge receipt within 10 business days.
  4. Review and queries: CySEC reviews the application within 3 months. They may request additional information or clarifications, which can extend the timeline. Respond promptly to avoid delays.
  5. On-site inspection (if needed): CySEC may conduct an on-site visit to verify your operations. This is more common for complex applications.
  6. License issuance: Once satisfied, CySEC grants the license. You can then commence operations. The total time from start to finish is typically 6-12 months.

Banking and Payment Services for Cyprus Crypto Firms

Opening a bank account for a crypto business in Cyprus can be challenging due to perceived risks. However, several banks in Cyprus are crypto-friendly, especially after MiCA implementation. Expect to provide your license application or approval, detailed business plan, source of funds, and AML policies. Banks may also require a minimum deposit and charge higher fees for crypto-related accounts.

Alternative payment providers and EMI licenses are also available. For example, you can partner with an EMI that specializes in crypto payments to offer fiat on/off ramps. Benefits of a Cyprus Crypto License

  • EU passporting: Once licensed, you can operate across all EU member states without additional licenses.
  • Low corporate tax: 12.5% corporate income tax, with exemptions on capital gains.
  • Reputable regulator: CySEC is well-regarded and provides regulatory clarity.
  • Access to EU market: Over 450 million potential customers.
  • Established legal framework: Cyprus has a common law system and a strong financial services sector.

However, the cost and timeline are higher than non-EU alternatives like Panama. For businesses that need EU access, the investment can be worthwhile. Additionally, Cyprus has a skilled workforce and good infrastructure for fintech companies.

Compliance and Trust Considerations

Maintaining a Cyprus crypto license requires ongoing compliance. You must appoint a local AML compliance officer, conduct regular audits, submit periodic reports to CySEC, and adhere to MiCA's operational requirements. Failure to comply can result in fines or license revocation. For example, if you fail to report suspicious transactions, CySEC may impose penalties.

General guidance, not legal advice: ensure your compliance framework is strong from day one. This includes having a strong KYC process, transaction monitoring, and regular staff training. Common Mistakes When Applying for a Cyprus Crypto License

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing business plans, AML policies, or risk assessments cause delays. Ensure all documents are complete and professionally prepared.
  • Underestimating capital requirements: Ensure you have the required capital in place before applying. CySEC may ask for proof of funds.
  • Ignoring local presence: CySEC expects a physical office and local management. Renting a virtual office is not sufficient.
  • Not engaging experienced advisors: The process is complex; professional help is essential. Many applicants try to save costs but end up with rejections.
  • Overlooking ongoing costs: Budget for annual compliance, audit, and legal fees. These can add up to EUR 20,000-50,000 per year.

Frequently asked questions

How long does a Cyprus crypto license take in 2026?

The process typically takes 6 to 12 months from application submission to license grant. CySEC has 3 months to review, but requests for additional information can extend the timeline. Most applicants receive a decision within 9 months.

What is the cost of a Cyprus crypto license?

Total costs range from EUR 80,000 to over 230,000, including application fees (EUR 10,000-30,000), professional fees (EUR 20,000-50,000), and minimum capital (EUR 50,000-150,000). Ongoing compliance costs add EUR 20,000-50,000 per year.

What are the capital requirements for a Cyprus CASP?

Capital requirements are tiered: EUR 50,000 for basic services, EUR 125,000 for custody, and EUR 150,000 for trading platforms. These must be maintained as own funds.

Can I passport a Cyprus crypto license to other EU countries?

Yes, under MiCA, a Cyprus CASP license allows you to provide services across all EU member states without additional licenses. This is a key advantage for businesses targeting the European market.

Is Cyprus tax-friendly for crypto businesses?

Yes, Cyprus has a 12.5% corporate tax rate, one of the lowest in the EU. Capital gains on crypto are generally exempt, but trading profits are taxable. VAT exemptions apply to certain crypto services.

What are the main differences between Cyprus and Panama?

Panama offers a flat fee of EUR 6,000, no capital requirement, and a 2-3 month timeline, but no EU passport. Cyprus costs EUR 80,000-230,000, requires EUR 50,000-150,000 capital, takes 6-12 months, and provides EU passporting.

Do I need a physical office in Cyprus?

Yes, CySEC requires a physical office in Cyprus with local management. A virtual office is not sufficient. You must also have a local AML compliance officer.

What documents are needed for a Cyprus crypto license application?

You need a business plan, AML/CFT policies, risk assessment, organizational structure, financial projections, and proof of capital. All documents must be in English or Greek and professionally prepared.
