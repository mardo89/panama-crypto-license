What Is the Czech Republic Crypto License?

The Czech Republic crypto license is an authorization from the Czech National Bank (CNB) for virtual asset service providers (VASPs). Under MiCA, which is fully in force in 2026, the license allows companies to offer crypto services such as exchange, custody, and wallet services across the EU under passporting rights. The regime replaced the previous registration-only system with a full licensing process, including minimum capital requirements and AML/CFT compliance.

Key features include:

  • Regulated by the CNB under Act No. 253/2008 Coll. (AML Act) and MiCA.
  • License categories: CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) with sub-classes for different services.
  • Minimum capital tiers: EUR 50,000 for basic services, EUR 125,000 for custody, EUR 150,000 for exchange and trading platforms.
  • Passporting rights across all EU/EEA member states.

This license is essential for any company that wants to operate legally in the Czech crypto market and expand across Europe without obtaining separate licenses in each country. The CNB has been proactive in adopting MiCA, making the Czech Republic a relatively stable jurisdiction for crypto businesses. However, the application process is rigorous and requires thorough preparation.

Who Needs a Czech Republic Crypto License?

Any entity providing crypto asset services in or from the Czech Republic must obtain a license. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto).
  • Custodial wallet providers.
  • Brokers and dealers in crypto assets.
  • Operators of crypto ATMs.
  • Platforms for initial coin offerings (ICOs) or token issuance.

Foreign companies targeting Czech clients or establishing a presence in the country also need the license. If you are already licensed in another EU member state under MiCA, you can passport into the Czech Republic without a separate local license. For non-EU founders, setting up a Czech company and obtaining the license is the standard path. License Type & Regulator

The regulatory authority is the Czech National Bank (CNB), which oversees all financial services including crypto assets. The license type is a CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) license under MiCA. The CNB classifies services into several categories, each with its own capital requirement and compliance obligations.

Main service classes:

If your business model involves multiple service classes, you must meet the highest applicable capital requirement. Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement under MiCA for the Czech Republic is tiered by service class:

  • EUR 50,000 for basic services (order execution, advice).
  • EUR 125,000 for custody services.
  • EUR 150,000 for exchange platforms and trading.

Capital must be held in liquid assets (cash or equivalents) and maintained throughout the license period. The CNB may require higher capital based on risk assessment. For companies offering multiple services, the highest applicable tier applies. Consulting24 can help structure your capital efficiently. For example, if you offer both custody and exchange services, you need EUR 150,000. This capital cannot be used for operational expenses; it must remain as a reserve. Compare this with Panama, which has no capital requirement for crypto businesses.

Tax Treatment for Crypto Businesses in the Czech Republic

The Czech Republic has a progressive corporate income tax rate of 19% (2026). Cryptocurrency transactions are treated as intangible assets for tax purposes. Key points:

  • Profits from crypto trading are subject to corporate tax.
  • VAT: Crypto-to-fiat exchanges are exempt from VAT; crypto-to-crypto may be subject to VAT in certain cases.
  • Withholding tax on dividends: 15% (reduced under tax treaties).
  • No specific crypto tax regime, but the CNB provides guidance.

For international founders, the Czech Republic has a wide network of double taxation treaties. Consulting24 recommends a tax review with a local advisor. Compare with Panama, which has no corporate tax on foreign-source income. However, the Czech Republic's tax treaties can reduce withholding taxes on dividends paid to foreign shareholders. Also, the Czech Republic does not impose a wealth tax or net worth tax on crypto holdings.

Allowed Activities Under the License

The Czech Republic crypto license permits the following activities under MiCA:

  • Reception and transmission of orders for crypto assets.
  • Execution of orders on behalf of clients.
  • Operation of a trading platform (order-book or AMM).
  • Custody and administration of crypto assets.
  • Exchange services (fiat-to-crypto, crypto-to-crypto).
  • Placement of crypto assets (including ICOs).
  • Advice on crypto assets.

Activities not covered include issuance of e-money or payment services, which require separate licenses. The license does not cover derivatives or securities tokens (subject to MiFID). If your business involves stablecoins or tokenized assets, check with the CNB whether they fall under MiCA or other regulations. Consulting24 can help you scope your activities correctly.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining the Czech Republic crypto license involves several stages. Consulting24 advises on this process and can coordinate with local partners.

  1. Pre-application preparation: Define your business model, service classes, and prepare a detailed business plan including financial projections for three years.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Czech limited liability company (s.r.o.) with a registered office and local director (if required). The minimum share capital is CZK 1 (about EUR 0.04) but for licensing you need the higher capital.
  3. AML/KYC program: Develop policies for customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting. The CNB expects a risk-based approach.
  4. Capital deposit: Deposit the required minimum capital in a Czech bank account. The bank will issue a confirmation letter.
  5. Application submission: Submit the application to the CNB with all supporting documents (business plan, AML policies, financial projections, fit-and-proper forms for management, and proof of capital).
  6. CNB review: The CNB reviews the application within 3-6 months. They may request additional information or clarifications. In practice, expect 4-8 months total.
  7. License grant: Upon approval, you receive the CASP license and can start operations. You must then register with the trade licensing office.

For a faster track, consider Estonia or Lithuania, where Consulting24 delivers directly with established processes. Estonia's application typically takes 2-4 months.

Banking & Payment Partners

Opening a bank account for a crypto business in the Czech Republic can be challenging. Traditional banks often have restrictive policies. Options include:

  • Local Czech banks (e.g., CSOB, Komercni Banka) - require a strong compliance record and may take months. They often ask for the license before opening an account.
  • EMI (Electronic Money Institutions) like Revolut, Wise, or local EMI partners - faster but with transaction limits and higher fees.
  • Specialized crypto-friendly banks in the EU (e.g., Bank Frick, Clear Junction) - often require a license and have higher minimum deposits.

Benefits of the Czech Republic Crypto License

Key advantages include:

  • EU passporting: Serve all 27 EU member states from one license.
  • Stable regulatory environment under MiCA.
  • Relatively fast processing (4-8 months) compared to some other EU jurisdictions like Germany or France.
  • No requirement for a local physical office (registered address suffices).
  • Low corporate tax rate (19%) compared to other EU countries like France (25%) or Germany (30%+).
  • Access to a skilled workforce and tech talent in Prague.

However, the capital requirement and compliance costs are higher than in non-EU jurisdictions like Panama, where no license is needed for crypto activities. Panama also offers zero tax on foreign-source income, which can be attractive for global operations.

Compliance & Trust: Ongoing Obligations

After obtaining the license, you must maintain ongoing compliance:

  • Annual AML audit and reporting to the CNB.
  • Regular financial reporting (annual accounts).
  • Transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting (STRs).
  • Fit-and-proper updates for management changes.
  • Capital maintenance at all times.
  • Annual license renewal fee (typically EUR 1,000-3,000).

Failure to comply can result in fines or license revocation. This is general guidance, not legal advice. Consulting24 recommends engaging a local compliance officer or outsourcing to a compliance firm. For a simpler compliance burden, Panama offers a lighter regulatory touch with no annual reporting requirements for crypto activities. However, if you need EU market access, the Czech Republic is a solid choice.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make these errors:

  • Underestimating the capital requirement - ensure you have the full amount in liquid assets. Some founders try to use crypto as capital, but the CNB requires fiat or equivalents.
  • Incomplete AML policies - the CNB is strict on AML documentation. Generic templates are rejected.
  • Choosing the wrong service class - leads to reapplication delays. For example, if you offer both exchange and custody, you need Class 3 capital.
  • Ignoring tax obligations - the CNB and tax authority collaborate. Unreported crypto income can trigger audits.
  • Not planning for banking - start the account opening process early. Some founders wait until after the license, which delays operations.
  • Assuming passporting is automatic - you must notify the CNB and host member states.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between the Czech Republic crypto license and the old registration?

The old registration was a simple notification to the CNB with no capital requirement. The new CASP license under MiCA requires minimum capital (EUR 50,000-150,000), a full AML program, and fit-and-proper tests. It also grants EU passporting rights, which the old registration did not.

Can I use the Czech license to serve clients in other EU countries?

Yes, under MiCA passporting rights, you can serve clients in all EU/EEA member states without obtaining separate licenses. You must notify the CNB and the host member state's regulator. This is one of the main advantages of the Czech license.

What are the ongoing costs after obtaining the license?

Ongoing costs include annual AML audit (EUR 2,000-5,000), compliance officer salary or outsourcing (EUR 1,000-3,000 per month), annual license renewal fee (EUR 1,000-3,000), and accounting/legal fees (EUR 3,000-6,000). Total annual costs typically range from EUR 10,000 to 30,000.

Is it possible to get a Czech crypto license without a local director?

The CNB does not explicitly require a local director, but having a local resident director can facilitate communication and compliance. Many founders appoint a local nominee director through a service provider. Consulting24 can help arrange this.

How long does it take to open a bank account for a Czech crypto company?

Opening a bank account can take 2-4 months. Traditional banks often require the license before opening an account. EMIs like Revolut can open an account faster (1-2 weeks) but may have transaction limits. Start the process early to avoid delays.

What happens if I change my business model after obtaining the license?

You must notify the CNB of any material changes to your business model, including new services. If the change requires a higher capital tier, you must deposit additional capital and may need to submit a modified application. Failure to notify can result in penalties.

Can I use the Czech license for DeFi services?

DeFi services that involve custody, exchange, or order execution may fall under MiCA and require a license. Fully decentralized protocols without a central operator may not be covered. The CNB assesses each case individually. Consulting24 recommends a legal review of your DeFi model.

What are the penalties for non-compliance?

The CNB can impose fines up to EUR 5 million or 10% of annual turnover for serious breaches. License revocation is also possible. Ongoing compliance is essential. This is general guidance, not legal advice.

Is the Czech Republic crypto license worth the cost compared to Panama?

It depends on your target market. If you need EU passporting and access to European clients, the Czech license is valuable despite higher costs. If your clients are outside the EU or you want minimal regulation, Panama's flat EUR 6,000 setup with no license is more cost-effective.

Can Consulting24 guarantee approval of my Czech license application?

No, we cannot guarantee approval. The CNB makes the final decision. However, we help you prepare a complete and accurate application to maximize your chances. Our track record shows that thorough preparation leads to higher success rates.
