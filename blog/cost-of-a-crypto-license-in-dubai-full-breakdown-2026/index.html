What Is a Crypto License in Dubai?

A crypto license in Dubai, issued by VARA, allows companies to legally operate virtual asset services within or from Dubai (excluding the Dubai International Financial Centre, which has its own regime). The license covers activities such as exchange, custody, advisory, and payment services. VARA's framework is comprehensive and aligns with international standards, including FATF recommendations.

Obtaining a license involves meeting strict requirements on capital, governance, AML/CFT controls, and technology security. The total cost includes government fees, compliance setup, and professional service fees. Dubai is often chosen for its tax advantages (0% corporate and personal income tax) and strategic location, but the upfront investment is significant compared to other hubs. For example, a Category 1 exchange license can easily exceed USD 300,000 in first-year costs, whereas a Panama license is a flat EUR 6,000. This makes Dubai more suitable for well-funded enterprises rather than early-stage startups.

Who Needs a Crypto License in Dubai?

Any entity providing virtual asset services in or from Dubai must be licensed by VARA. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (spot, derivatives)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Broker-dealers and market makers
  • Payment and remittance services using virtual assets
  • Advisory and portfolio management services

Even if you operate online and target customers globally, if your management or operations are based in Dubai, a license is mandatory. Foreign companies seeking to serve UAE residents also need a local license. For those with a smaller budget, Panama offers a simpler and cheaper alternative at a flat EUR 6,000. Additionally, if your business model is primarily non-custodial or you offer only software services, you may not need a VARA license, but legal advice is essential to confirm.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA), established under Dubai Law No. 4 of 2022. VARA issues several license types depending on the services offered:

  • Category 1: Exchange services (trading platforms) - highest capital and compliance requirements.
  • Category 2: Custody and wallet services - requires insurance and secure storage.
  • Category 3: Advisory and management services - lower capital but still rigorous AML.
  • Category 4: Payment and remittance services - includes stablecoin transfers.

Each category has specific capital and operational requirements. VARA also requires a local presence, including a physical office in Dubai. The regulatory framework is one of the most advanced globally, but also one of the most demanding in terms of compliance costs. For a comparison with other regulators, see our jurisdictions page.

Capital Requirement

VARA mandates minimum paid-up capital based on license category:

  • Category 1 (Exchange): USD 250,000
  • Category 2 (Custody): USD 100,000
  • Category 3 (Advisory): USD 50,000
  • Category 4 (Payment): USD 100,000

Capital must be deposited in a UAE bank account and cannot be withdrawn without VARA approval. This is a significant barrier for smaller firms. In contrast, jurisdictions like Panama have no minimum capital requirement, making them more flexible for early-stage projects. Additionally, the capital must be maintained throughout the license period, and any reduction requires prior approval. For firms with limited funding, this can tie up resources that could otherwise be used for growth.

Tax Treatment

Dubai offers a highly favorable tax environment:

  • 0% corporate income tax for most free zone companies (mainland may have 9% from 2023 onwards, but free zones remain 0% for qualifying income)
  • 0% personal income tax
  • No capital gains tax
  • No withholding tax on dividends and interest
  • VAT at 5% on most services

However, crypto transactions may be subject to VAT if they involve goods or services. VARA requires licensed entities to maintain proper tax records. Overall, Dubai's tax regime is a major draw, but the high licensing costs offset some benefits for smaller businesses. For a tax comparison with other jurisdictions, see our Lithuania comparison.

Allowed Activities

A VARA license permits the following activities, depending on the category:

  • Operating a virtual asset exchange (order book, OTC, DEX)
  • Custody and safekeeping of virtual assets
  • Virtual asset transfers and payments
  • Portfolio management and advisory
  • Token issuance and listing services
  • Staking and lending (subject to additional approval)

Activities not covered include derivatives (unless separately licensed by the Securities and Commodities Authority) and gambling-related tokens. VARA also prohibits anonymous transactions and requires full KYC/AML compliance. For a broader list of activities allowed in other jurisdictions, check our blog.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Pre-application: Engage a local consultant (e.g., Consulting24) to assess suitability and prepare documents.
  2. Company incorporation: Set up a legal entity in a suitable free zone or mainland Dubai. Obtain initial approval.
  3. Prepare compliance documentation: Draft AML/CFT policies, risk assessment, business plan, and governance framework.
  4. Submit application to VARA: Pay the non-refundable application fee and submit all documents through the VARA portal.
  5. VARA review and interview: VARA may request additional information or conduct interviews with key personnel.
  6. Capital deposit: Deposit the required capital in a UAE bank account and provide proof.
  7. Physical office setup: Lease a physical office and obtain a tenancy contract.
  8. Final approval and license issuance: Once all conditions are met, VARA issues the license. Annual renewal required.

Banking and Payment Services

Opening a bank account for a crypto company in Dubai can be challenging due to perceived risks. Options include:

  • Traditional UAE banks (e.g., Emirates NBD, Mashreq) with strict compliance requirements
  • Digital banks and fintech platforms (e.g., Zand, Al Maryah Community Bank)
  • International banks with UAE presence

Many banks require a minimum balance of USD 50,000 or more and a proven track record. For payment processing, licensed crypto firms can integrate with local payment gateways. Consulting24 can recommend banking partners based on your profile. In contrast, Panama offers more accessible banking for crypto firms, with no minimum balance requirements in many cases. Additionally, Panama's banking system is more open to international clients, making it easier to set up merchant accounts.

Compliance and Trust

VARA enforces strict compliance requirements, including:

  • Full KYC/AML procedures for all customers
  • Transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting
  • Regular audits and reporting to VARA
  • Cybersecurity standards (ISO 27001 recommended)
  • Insurance for custodial services (minimum coverage as per VARA)

Non-compliance can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. Engaging a compliance consultant is highly recommended. This is general guidance, not legal advice. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Underestimating costs: Many founders budget only for government fees, ignoring legal, compliance, and office costs. Example: one client budgeted USD 80,000 but ended up spending USD 200,000.
  • Inadequate compliance preparation: VARA requires detailed policies; generic templates are often rejected. Invest in custom documentation.
  • Choosing the wrong license category: Selecting a category that doesn't match your business model can lead to delays. For instance, a custody business applying as an exchange may face additional scrutiny.
  • Delaying bank account opening: Bank account setup can take months; start early. Some banks require a personal meeting in Dubai.
  • Ignoring ongoing obligations: Annual fees, audits, and reporting are mandatory and costly. Budget at least USD 50,000 per year for compliance maintenance.

Frequently asked questions

What is the total cost of a crypto license in Dubai in 2026?

The total first-year cost ranges from USD 100,000 to USD 500,000, depending on the license category, professional fees, and office rent. This excludes the capital requirement which must be deposited separately. For a precise quote, contact Consulting24.

How long does it take to get a Dubai crypto license?

The typical timeline is 3 to 6 months from application to issuance, assuming all documents are complete. Delays can occur if additional information is requested or if bank account opening takes longer.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a VARA license?

Minimum capital varies by category: USD 250,000 for exchange, USD 100,000 for custody and payment, and USD 50,000 for advisory. Capital must be deposited in a UAE bank account and cannot be withdrawn without VARA approval.

Can I operate a crypto exchange from Dubai without a license?

No. Operating a virtual asset exchange without a VARA license is illegal and can result in fines, closure, or criminal penalties. All virtual asset service providers must be licensed.

What taxes will I pay in Dubai as a crypto company?

Most free zone companies pay 0% corporate income tax. There is no personal income tax, capital gains tax, or withholding tax. VAT at 5% applies to most services. Crypto transactions may be subject to VAT if they involve goods or services.

Is it difficult to open a bank account for a crypto company in Dubai?

Yes, it can be challenging due to perceived risks. Many banks require a minimum balance of USD 50,000 or more, a proven track record, and a physical presence. Digital banks like Zand offer alternatives. Consulting24 can recommend suitable banks.

What are the main advantages of a Dubai crypto license over Panama?

Dubai offers a world-class regulatory framework, 0% tax, and strategic location. However, Panama is much cheaper (EUR 6,000 flat) with no capital requirement and faster setup. For well-funded firms, Dubai provides prestige; for startups, Panama is more accessible.

What happens if I don't comply with VARA regulations?

Non-compliance can lead to fines, license suspension, or revocation. VARA conducts regular audits and expects full cooperation. It is essential to maintain strong AML/CFT controls and submit timely reports.

Can I apply for a VARA license if my company is outside the UAE?

Yes, but you must set up a local entity in Dubai (mainland or free zone) first. The license is issued to the local entity, and you must have a physical office and local management.
