Overview: What Are Substance Requirements for Offshore Crypto Licences?

Substance requirements, often called economic substance rules, are regulatory standards that ensure a company has a real physical and operational presence in the jurisdiction where it is licensed. For offshore crypto licences, these rules prevent tax evasion, money laundering, and the misuse of shell companies. In 2026, most reputable offshore hubs enforce substance tests, especially after the EU's Anti-Tax Avoidance Directive and global pressure from the OECD. The Financial Action Task Force (FATF) also recommends substance as a key factor in assessing a jurisdiction's AML regime.

Typically, substance includes having a registered office (not just a virtual address), employing local staff (with employment contracts and payroll), holding board meetings locally, and maintaining bank accounts and operational records in the jurisdiction. The exact requirements vary by country, but the principle is consistent: your crypto business must be more than a mailbox. For example, in Panama, the Superintendencia de Bancos expects to see a physical office with a sign, a local manager, and evidence of active business transactions within six months of licence issuance. In Lithuania, the Bank of Lithuania mandates that at least one director be a resident of the EEA and that key compliance functions are performed locally. Estonia's Financial Intelligence Unit requires a local contact person and a registered address, but the substance bar is lower than Lithuania's.

Who Needs to Meet Substance Requirements?

Any company applying for or holding an offshore crypto licence must demonstrate substance. This applies to:

  • New applicants seeking a licence in Panama, Estonia, Lithuania, or other jurisdictions.
  • Existing licence holders undergoing renewal or regulatory review.
  • Companies restructuring to relocate their licence to a new jurisdiction.
  • Founders who want to avoid being flagged as a shell company by banks or partners.

If you plan to operate a crypto exchange, wallet service, or custody platform from an offshore base, substance is non-negotiable. Even jurisdictions with lighter initial requirements, like Panama, now expect real operations within a reasonable timeframe. For instance, a Panama crypto licence holder that fails to hire local staff within the first year may face a regulatory warning or licence suspension. Similarly, in Estonia, the FIU conducts periodic checks and may request proof of substance, such as employment contracts and rental agreements. In Lithuania, substance is a core part of the CASP licence application, and the Bank of Lithuania often asks for detailed business plans showing how local staff will be deployed.

Capital Requirement for Substance Compliance

Minimum capital requirements are part of substance because they demonstrate financial commitment. Under MiCA, EU member states like Lithuania apply capital tiers: EUR 50,000 for basic services (e.g., custody), EUR 125,000 for exchange services, and EUR 150,000 for trading platforms. Panama has no statutory minimum capital, but the regulator expects sufficient funds to cover operational costs for at least 6 months. Estonia requires a minimum share capital of EUR 12,000 (paid-up) for VASP licences. These capital amounts must be maintained and evidenced in local bank accounts, reinforcing your substance profile. For example, a Lithuania CASP applicant must deposit the required capital in a Lithuanian bank and provide proof of the deposit as part of the application. Failure to maintain the capital can lead to licence suspension. For more on capital requirements, see our requirements page.

Tax Treatment and Substance

Substance requirements are closely tied to tax residency. In Panama, the territorial tax system means only locally-sourced income is taxed, but substance ensures you are not deemed a tax resident elsewhere. Estonia taxes distributed profits (0% on retained earnings), but substance is needed to avoid being classified as a controlled foreign company by your home country. Lithuania applies a 15% corporate tax, with reduced rates for small companies, and substance helps you claim treaty benefits. Proper substance planning can reduce your effective tax rate while staying compliant with global standards. For instance, a Panama-based crypto company with real substance can avoid being taxed in its founders' home country under most tax treaties. However, without substance, tax authorities may treat the company as a shell and tax its income directly. Consulting24 advises on tax-efficient structuring as part of our licensing service.

Allowed Activities Under Offshore Crypto Licences

Each licence permits specific activities. For example, Panama's licence covers crypto exchange, custody, and wallet services. Estonia's VASP licence allows exchange between virtual and fiat currencies, transfer services, and wallet provision. Lithuania's CASP licence aligns with MiCA categories: custody, exchange, execution, and advisory. Substance requirements apply to all activities; you must have staff competent in each service line. If you plan to offer multiple services, your substance must scale accordingly-more staff, larger office, and enhanced compliance systems. For example, a company offering both exchange and custody services in Lithuania needs separate compliance officers for each function and a larger office to house them. In Panama, a multi-service licence may require additional local staff with relevant expertise. Always check the specific activity list in your licence application.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum staff requirement for a Panama crypto licence?

Panama does not specify a minimum number of staff, but the regulator expects at least one local employee (e.g., a manager or compliance officer) and a physical office with a sign. Most successful applicants hire at least two local staff: a director and an administrative assistant. The key is to demonstrate that the company has real operational capacity in Panama.

Can I use a virtual office to meet substance requirements in Estonia?

Estonia's FIU accepts a registered address, but a virtual office alone may not be sufficient if it lacks a physical presence. For substance, you need a real office where business activities occur. Co-working spaces are allowed if you have a dedicated desk and meeting room. However, for higher-risk licences, a private office is recommended.

How does the Bank of Lithuania verify substance for CASP licences?

The Bank of Lithuania conducts on-site inspections and requests documents such as employment contracts, office lease agreements, and board meeting minutes. They also check that the local director is actively involved in decision-making. Substance is verified both during the application and periodically after licensing.

What happens if I fail to maintain substance after getting a licence?

Failure to maintain substance can lead to licence suspension, revocation, or fines. For example, in Panama, the SBP may issue a warning and give a deadline to comply. In Lithuania, the Bank of Lithuania can revoke the CASP licence if substance is not demonstrated within a specified period. Ongoing compliance is essential.

Is there a difference in substance requirements for custody vs. exchange licences?

Yes, the substance requirements scale with the complexity of services. Custody services typically require more strong security and operational staff, while exchange services may need additional compliance and trading personnel. Under MiCA, capital requirements also differ: EUR 50,000 for custody, EUR 125,000 for exchange, and EUR 150,000 for trading platforms.

Can I outsource substance functions like compliance to a third party?

Some jurisdictions allow outsourcing of specific functions, but the ultimate responsibility for substance lies with the licence holder. For example, you can hire an external compliance officer, but that person must be locally based and actively engaged. Outsourcing does not relieve you of the need for a physical office and local staff.

How long does it take to establish substance in Panama?

The typical timeline is 3-6 months from company incorporation to having a fully operational office with staff. This includes renting an office, hiring employees, opening a bank account, and setting up compliance systems. What are the annual substance costs for a Lithuania CASP licence?

Annual substance costs for a Lithuania CASP licence typically range from EUR 20,000 to EUR 35,000, covering office rent, local director salary, compliance officer, and administrative expenses. Exact costs depend on the size of your team and office. Consulting24 provides a detailed budget during consultation.

Do I need to be physically present in the jurisdiction to meet substance?

Not necessarily, but you must have local staff who are physically present. As a founder, you can be based elsewhere, but the local director and key personnel must reside in the jurisdiction. Some regulators, like the Bank of Lithuania, require the local director to be an EEA resident and to attend board meetings in person.

Can I switch jurisdictions after obtaining a licence?

Yes, but switching jurisdictions requires a new licence application and establishing substance in the new location. You cannot simply transfer an existing licence.
