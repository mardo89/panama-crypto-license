What Is the Kazakhstan Crypto License?

The Kazakhstan crypto license is a regulatory authorization issued by the Astana Financial Services Authority (AFSA) under the AIFC. It allows companies to operate as a Digital Asset Trading Facility (DATF) or provide other crypto services within the AIFC's legal framework. The AIFC operates on English common law principles, offering a familiar environment for international firms. The license is mandatory for any entity conducting crypto exchange, custody, or related activities within the AIFC. As of 2026, the AIFC has aligned with global standards but is not part of the EU's MiCA regime. The application process involves a detailed business plan, compliance policies, and a minimum capital requirement. What Is Panama for Crypto Companies?

Panama is not a crypto licensing jurisdiction. Instead, founders set up a standard Panama corporation (Sociedad Anónima) and conduct crypto activities under general business law. There is no specific regulator for crypto, though the Superintendencia de Bancos de Panamá (SBP) oversees banking and anti-money laundering compliance. Panama's territorial tax system means income earned outside Panama is not taxed, making it attractive for international crypto businesses. The cost is a flat EUR 6,000 for company setup through Consulting24, with no ongoing license fees. However, Panama lacks a formal crypto license, which can be a drawback for founders seeking regulatory clarity or institutional partnerships. This makes Panama ideal for early-stage projects, trading firms, or companies that operate outside regulated markets. For more details, see our cost guide.

Who Needs a Crypto License in Each Jurisdiction?

Kazakhstan: Any entity that operates a digital asset exchange, provides custodial wallet services, or facilitates the trading of cryptocurrencies within the AIFC must hold an AFSA license. This includes both centralized and decentralized platforms if they have a physical presence in the AIFC. The license is also required for broker-dealers and investment advisors dealing in digital assets. Founders targeting institutional clients or seeking a regulated environment should consider Kazakhstan.

Panama: No license is needed for crypto activities per se. However, any business that processes payments or holds customer funds may need to comply with AML/CFT regulations under Panama's Law 23 of 2015. Companies that offer exchange services between crypto and fiat may face banking scrutiny but are not required to obtain a crypto license. Panama suits founders who want minimal regulatory overhead and are comfortable operating without a formal license. For a broader comparison, see our vs Lithuania page.

Capital Requirement

Kazakhstan: The minimum capital requirement for a DATF license is typically around USD 100,000 (or equivalent in KZT), depending on the services offered. This must be maintained as paid-up capital. Higher capital may be required for custodial services or if the company plans to hold client assets. The exact amount is determined by AFSA during the application process.

Panama: There is no minimum capital requirement for a Panama corporation. The standard paid-in capital is USD 10,000, but this can be as low as USD 1,000. No capital needs to be deposited in a bank account; it is a statutory declaration. This makes Panama far more accessible for startups with limited funds. For more on capital, see our requirements guide.

Tax Treatment

Kazakhstan: Companies in the AIFC enjoy a 0% corporate income tax rate on income derived from activities within the AIFC until 2066. However, VAT and social taxes may apply. The AIFC also offers a 0% withholding tax on dividends and interest paid to non-residents. This is a significant advantage for international businesses.

Panama: Panama operates a territorial tax system: income earned outside Panama is not taxed. Only income sourced in Panama is subject to tax, at a rate of 25% for corporations. Crypto trading profits from international clients are generally considered foreign-source and thus tax-free. There is no capital gains tax on crypto. However, Panama has a 7% VAT on local services. Both jurisdictions offer tax benefits, but Panama's territorial system is simpler for global operations. Consult a tax advisor for your specific situation.

Allowed Activities

Kazakhstan (under AIFC): The DATF license permits operation of a digital asset exchange, custody of digital assets, brokerage, and advisory services. Staking, lending, and DeFi activities may be allowed subject to AFSA approval. The AIFC prohibits activities that are not explicitly authorized, so innovation requires prior clearance.

Panama: Any lawful crypto activity is allowed, including exchange, trading, mining, custody, and DeFi. There are no restrictions on token issuance, but securities laws may apply if tokens are deemed securities. Panama's flexibility is a double-edged sword: it allows almost anything, but without regulatory clarity, banks may refuse services. Banking & Payments

Kazakhstan: Banking within the AIFC is relatively straightforward. Several AIFC-licensed banks offer accounts to crypto companies. However, correspondent banking relationships may be limited for fiat on/off ramps. Companies often use a combination of local banks and international payment processors.

Panama: Banking is a major challenge. Most Panamanian banks are wary of crypto due to AML concerns. Founders often need to use international banks or fintechs that support crypto-friendly accounts. Benefits of Each Jurisdiction

  • Kazakhstan: Regulated environment builds trust with partners and investors; 0% corporate tax in AIFC; common law legal system; access to Central Asian markets; clear regulatory framework for digital assets.
  • Panama: Low cost (EUR 6,000 flat); no crypto license required; territorial tax (foreign income tax-free); fast setup (1-2 weeks); no minimum capital; privacy (bearer shares allowed but discouraged).

Choose Kazakhstan if you need a regulated license for institutional clients. Choose Panama for speed, cost savings, and simplicity.

Compliance & Trust

Kazakhstan: AFSA requires strong AML/CFT programs, including transaction monitoring, suspicious activity reporting, and annual audits. The regulator conducts periodic inspections. Non-compliance can result in fines or license revocation. This high compliance burden ensures trust but requires dedicated resources.

Panama: Compliance is lighter but not absent. Panama's Law 23 requires all businesses to implement AML measures. Crypto companies must register with the Financial Analysis Unit (UAF) if they engage in certain activities. However, enforcement is less stringent than in Kazakhstan. For companies seeking high trust, Panama's lack of a license may be a drawback. Always consult legal experts for compliance advice. This is general guidance, not legal advice.

Common Mistakes When Choosing Between Kazakhstan and Panama

  • Underestimating timeline: Kazakhstan's 3-6 month process is often longer than expected. Panama's 1-2 weeks is faster but banking can delay operations.
  • Ignoring tax implications: While AIFC offers 0% tax, profits from outside the AIFC may be taxed in other jurisdictions. Panama's territorial system is simpler but requires careful structuring.
  • Overlooking banking: Both jurisdictions have banking challenges. Plan ahead and secure a banking relationship before incorporation.
  • Assuming Panama is unregulated: Panama still has AML obligations. Ignoring them can lead to fines or account closures.
  • Choosing based on cost alone: Panama is cheaper, but if your business needs a license, Kazakhstan may be necessary. Consider your target market and partners.

What is the main difference between Kazakhstan and Panama for a crypto company?

Kazakhstan offers a formal crypto license under the AIFC with 0% tax but higher costs and longer timelines. Panama has no crypto license, lower costs (EUR 6,000 flat), and faster setup, but lacks regulatory clarity.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a crypto license in Kazakhstan?

The minimum capital for a DATF license in Kazakhstan is typically USD 100,000, though it may vary based on services. This must be maintained as paid-up capital. Panama has no minimum capital requirement.

Can I operate a crypto exchange in Panama without a license?

Yes, Panama does not require a specific crypto license. However, you must comply with AML regulations under Law 23 of 2015. Banking may be difficult without a license, but operations are legally permitted.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Kazakhstan?

The AFSA application process typically takes 3-6 months from submission to approval. This includes company incorporation, document preparation, and regulatory review. Panama company setup takes 1-2 weeks.

What are the tax benefits of Panama for crypto companies?

Panama's territorial tax system means income earned outside Panama is not taxed. Crypto trading profits from international clients are generally tax-free. There is no capital gains tax on crypto. Only Panama-sourced income is taxed at 25%.

Yes, companies within the AIFC enjoy a 0% corporate income tax rate on AIFC-sourced income until 2066. However, VAT and social taxes may apply. This makes Kazakhstan attractive for high-margin crypto businesses.

Which jurisdiction is better for a startup with limited funds?

Panama is better for startups due to its low cost (EUR 6,000 flat), no minimum capital, and fast setup. Kazakhstan's higher capital and ongoing costs may be prohibitive for early-stage companies.

Do I need a physical office in Kazakhstan or Panama?

Kazakhstan requires a registered office in the AIFC. Panama requires a registered agent address but no physical office. Virtual offices are acceptable in both jurisdictions.
