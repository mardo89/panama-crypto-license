What Are Crypto Banking and Payment Rails?

Crypto banking and payment rails refer to the infrastructure that enables the transfer, settlement, and conversion of digital assets between parties. This includes payment gateways, merchant services, fiat on-ramps and off-ramps, and custodial wallets. In Mauritius, these activities fall under the purview of the FSC and require a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) license under the VAITOS Act.

Mauritius aims to attract fintech innovators by providing a clear legal framework. The FSC classifies virtual asset activities into categories such as exchange, transfer, custody, and advisory services. For payment rails specifically, the license covers the operation of a virtual asset exchange platform and the facilitation of transfers.

Key features of the Mauritian regime include a risk-based approach, mandatory AML/CFT compliance, and fit-and-proper requirements for directors and shareholders. The regulator expects applicants to demonstrate strong internal controls and cybersecurity measures.

Who Needs a Crypto Banking and Payment License in Mauritius?

Any business that provides services involving the exchange, transfer, or custody of virtual assets for clients must obtain a VASP license from the FSC. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (centralized and peer-to-peer)
  • Payment processors and merchant service providers
  • Wallet providers (custodial and non-custodial if they hold private keys)
  • Fiat-to-crypto on-ramp/off-ramp services
  • OTC trading desks

Founders who plan to offer crypto banking services-such as interest accounts, lending, or staking-should also check if these activities are covered under the VAITOS Act or require additional licensing from the Bank of Mauritius. License Type and Regulator

The regulator for crypto banking and payment rails in Mauritius is the Financial Services Commission (FSC). The applicable license is the Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) license, issued under the Virtual Asset and Initial Token Offering Services Act 2021 (VAITOS Act). There is no separate license for payment rails; the VASP license covers all virtual asset services.

The FSC distinguishes between different categories of VASP activities, but the license is a single authorization that covers all permitted activities. Applicants must specify the activities they intend to conduct, such as exchange, transfer, or custody. The license is valid for one year and must be renewed annually.

Mauritius also has a parallel regime for security tokens under the Securities Act, but for most crypto payment businesses, the VAITOS Act is the relevant framework.

Capital Requirement for Mauritius VASP License

The FSC imposes a minimum capital requirement for VASP license applicants. As of 2026, the minimum paid-up capital is EUR 50,000 (or equivalent in USD or MUR). This capital must be maintained at all times and can be in the form of cash or liquid assets.

Higher capital may be required if the applicant plans to offer custodial services or operate a large exchange. The FSC assesses capital adequacy on a case-by-case basis, considering the volume of transactions and risk profile. Consulting24 recommends applicants prepare a detailed business plan showing projected volumes and capital needs.

In comparison, under MiCA in the EU, capital requirements range from EUR 50,000 to 150,000 depending on the service class. Panama has no specific capital requirement for crypto businesses, but a minimum share capital of USD 10,000 is typical for an offshore company.

Tax Treatment for Crypto Businesses in Mauritius

Mauritius offers a competitive tax regime for international businesses. The standard corporate income tax rate is 15%, but companies can benefit from an 80% partial exemption on certain types of income, effectively reducing the rate to 3%. This exemption applies to income from foreign sources, including virtual asset services provided to non-residents.

Value Added Tax (VAT) at 15% applies to taxable supplies, but crypto-related services may be exempt or zero-rated depending on the nature. The Mauritius Revenue Authority (MRA) has issued guidelines clarifying that virtual asset transactions are generally subject to VAT only when provided for a fee.

Capital gains tax is not imposed in Mauritius, which is favorable for crypto trading profits. However, income from mining or staking may be treated as ordinary income. Consulting24 recommends clients obtain a tax ruling from the MRA to confirm their specific tax position.

Allowed Activities Under the Mauritius VASP License

The VASP license in Mauritius permits a range of virtual asset services, including:

  • Exchange between virtual assets and fiat currencies
  • Exchange between one or more forms of virtual assets
  • Transfer of virtual assets (on behalf of clients)
  • Custody and administration of virtual assets (including private keys)
  • Participation in and provision of financial services related to an issuer's offer or sale of a virtual asset

The license does not cover activities that fall under the Banking Act or the Securities Act unless separately licensed. For example, lending virtual assets or offering interest-bearing accounts may require a banking license from the Bank of Mauritius. Step-by-Step Application Process for Mauritius VASP License

Obtaining a VASP license in Mauritius involves several stages. Consulting24 coordinates with local partners to guide clients through each step.

  1. Pre-application assessment: Review business model, identify required activities, and check eligibility.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a company in Mauritius with a physical office and local directors (if required).
  3. Document preparation: Compile business plan, AML/CFT policies, risk assessment, organizational structure, and fit-and-proper declarations for directors and shareholders.
  4. Submission: File the application with the FSC, including all supporting documents and the application fee.
  5. Review and queries: The FSC reviews the application and may request additional information or clarifications.
  6. Approval and licensing: Once satisfied, the FSC issues the license. The company must then pay the annual license fee.
  7. Post-licensing compliance: Ongoing AML reporting, annual audits, and regulatory filings.

The entire process typically takes 4-8 months. Consulting24 offers advisory services to streamline the process, but does not directly file applications in Mauritius.

Banking and Payment Partners in Mauritius

One of the biggest challenges for crypto businesses is accessing traditional banking services. In Mauritius, several banks are open to working with licensed VASPs, provided they have strong AML procedures. Notable banks include MCB (Mauritius Commercial Bank), SBM Bank, and AfrAsia Bank.

These banks offer corporate accounts, fiat currency accounts in multiple currencies, and payment processing services. However, they require a valid VASP license and may impose transaction limits or enhanced due diligence. Consulting24 recommends approaching banks early in the licensing process to understand their requirements.

For payment rails specifically, Mauritius has a modern payment infrastructure including the Mauritius Automated Clearing and Settlement System (MACSS) for real-time gross settlement. Licensed VASPs can integrate with local payment gateways to offer fiat on-ramp/off-ramp services.

Compliance and Trust: What Regulators Expect

Regulators in Mauritius expect VASPs to maintain high standards of compliance. Key requirements include:

  • AML/CFT policies aligned with FATF recommendations
  • Customer due diligence (CDD) and enhanced due diligence (EDD) for high-risk clients
  • Transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting
  • Annual audits by an approved auditor
  • Fit-and-proper criteria for directors and shareholders (including police clearance and financial background checks)
  • Cybersecurity measures and data protection compliance

Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. Consulting24 emphasizes that this is general guidance, not legal advice. We recommend engaging local counsel for compliance setup.

Common Mistakes When Applying for a Mauritius VASP License

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing business plans, AML policies, or risk assessments cause delays.
  • Underestimating capital requirements: Some applicants assume lower capital than the EUR 50,000 minimum.
  • Ignoring fit-and-proper checks: Directors with adverse backgrounds may be rejected.
  • Overlooking local presence: The FSC requires a physical office in Mauritius and may require local directors.
  • Not engaging local experts: Attempting to file without local legal support often leads to rejection.

Frequently asked questions

What is the primary regulator for crypto banking in Mauritius?

The Financial Services Commission (FSC) is the primary regulator for virtual asset service providers under the VAITOS Act. It oversees licensing, compliance, and supervision.

Is a separate license needed for payment rails in Mauritius?

No, the VASP license covers payment rails as part of virtual asset transfer and exchange services. However, if you offer fiat payment services, you may need a payment services license from the Bank of Mauritius.

What is the minimum capital for a Mauritius VASP license?

The minimum paid-up capital is EUR 50,000 (or equivalent). Higher capital may be required for custodial services or high-volume operations.

How long does it take to get a VASP license in Mauritius?

Typically 4 to 8 months from application to approval, depending on completeness and FSC workload. Budget for at least 6 months.

Can foreign companies apply for a Mauritius VASP license?

Yes, but the applicant must incorporate a company in Mauritius with a physical office and local directors or representatives. The FSC requires a local presence.

What are the tax benefits for crypto businesses in Mauritius?

The effective corporate tax rate can be as low as 3% on foreign-source income due to an 80% partial exemption. Capital gains are not taxed.

Is Panama a better alternative than Mauritius for crypto banking?

Panama is faster and cheaper (EUR 6,000 fixed) with no dedicated crypto license, but lacks the regulatory certainty of Mauritius. It depends on your business needs and target market.

What activities are prohibited under the Mauritius VASP license?

Activities that require a banking license (e.g., lending, deposit-taking) or securities license (e.g., security token offerings) are not covered. Check with the FSC for specific activities.

How do I open a bank account for a crypto business in Mauritius?
