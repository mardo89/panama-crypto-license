What Is a Kazakhstan Crypto Company?

A Kazakhstan crypto company is a legal entity registered within the AIFC, authorised to provide digital asset services under AFSA regulation. The AIFC is a special economic zone in Nur-Sultan (formerly Astana), designed to attract fintech and financial services. Companies operating outside the AIFC are subject to different, less developed rules. The primary license type is the 'Crypto Asset Service Provider' (CASP) license, which covers activities such as custody, exchange, and trading. Kazakhstan's approach is modelled on international standards, including FATF recommendations, and is considered one of the more structured regulatory regimes in the region.

Who Needs a Kazakhstan Crypto License?

Any business planning to offer crypto-related services within or from Kazakhstan must obtain an AIFC license. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (centralised and peer-to-peer platforms)
  • Digital asset custodians and wallet providers
  • Token issuance platforms and ICO/STO facilitators
  • Brokerage and dealing services in crypto assets
  • Investment management involving digital assets

Foreign companies targeting Kazakhstani clients also need to register locally. The AIFC provides a clear legal framework, but the licensing process is rigorous and requires substantial documentation, including a detailed business plan, AML/KYC policies, and proof of capital adequacy.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator for crypto activities in Kazakhstan's AIFC is the Astana Financial Services Authority (AFSA). AFSA issues a single license category: the 'Crypto Asset Service Provider' (CASP) license. This license covers multiple activities under one umbrella, but applicants must specify which services they intend to provide. AFSA conducts a thorough assessment of the applicant's fitness and propriety, including background checks on directors and shareholders. The regulatory framework is aligned with the Financial Action Task Force (FATF) standards and includes requirements for market conduct, client asset protection, and reporting.

For businesses operating outside the AIFC, the National Bank of Kazakhstan (NBK) has some oversight, but the AIFC regime is the only fully developed licensing pathway for crypto firms as of 2026.

Capital Requirement

AFSA imposes a minimum capital requirement for CASP license holders. The amount varies by the type of services provided:

  • Basic services (e.g., custody, simple exchange): Minimum capital of USD 100,000.
  • Advanced services (e.g., trading, brokerage, market making): Minimum capital of USD 200,000.
  • Combined services: The higher of the applicable thresholds, up to USD 200,000.

This capital must be maintained in liquid form (cash or cash equivalents) and held in a Kazakhstani bank account. It serves as a buffer against operational risks and demonstrates financial stability. Tax Treatment for Crypto Companies in Kazakhstan

Kazakhstan offers a favourable tax regime within the AIFC. Key points:

  • Corporate income tax: 0% on income derived from AIFC-regulated activities for the first 10 years (subject to conditions). After that, a reduced rate of 6% applies.
  • VAT: 0% on most financial services, including crypto transactions.
  • Withholding tax: 0% on dividends and interest paid to non-residents, provided the recipient is not in a tax haven.
  • Personal income tax: 0% for AIFC employees on qualifying income (e.g., salaries from AIFC entities).

These incentives make Kazakhstan attractive for crypto firms, but the tax benefits are tied to strict substance requirements (e.g., having a physical office, local staff, and board meetings in the AIFC). Non-compliance can result in loss of benefits. For a broader tax comparison, see our Lithuania vs Kazakhstan page.

Allowed Activities Under the AIFC License

The CASP license permits a range of activities, including:

  • Operating a cryptocurrency exchange (order book or OTC)
  • Digital asset custody and safekeeping
  • Brokerage and dealing in crypto assets
  • Investment management of crypto portfolios
  • Token issuance and advisory services
  • Staking and lending of digital assets (subject to additional approval)

Activities that are not permitted include offering derivatives or securities tokens without a separate license, and providing services to sanctioned entities. The AIFC also restricts the use of privacy coins and requires compliance with travel rule obligations.

Step-by-Step Licensing Process

The process to obtain a Kazakhstan crypto license involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application preparation (1-2 months): Engage local advisors, prepare business plan, AML/KYC policies, risk management framework, and governance documents.
  2. Company incorporation (2-4 weeks): Register a legal entity within the AIFC (typically a Private Company or Special Purpose Company).
  3. Application submission: Submit the completed application form, supporting documents, and fee to AFSA.
  4. AFSA review (3-6 months): AFSA assesses the application, may request additional information, and conducts interviews with key personnel.
  5. Approval and licensing: If successful, AFSA issues a conditional or full license. Conditions may include capital deposit, insurance, or IT security audits.
  6. Post-licensing compliance: Ongoing reporting, annual audits, and regulatory filings.

Banking and Payment Services

Opening a bank account in Kazakhstan for a crypto company can be challenging. Most local banks are cautious due to AML concerns. Options include:

  • AIFC-registered banks: A few banks within the AIFC are more crypto-friendly, such as the Eurasian Bank and Halyk Bank (with restrictions).
  • International banks: Some foreign banks with presence in Kazakhstan may open accounts, but require strong compliance documentation.
  • EMI accounts: Electronic money institutions in the AIFC can provide payment services, but may not offer full banking facilities.

For comparison, Panama offers a more straightforward banking environment for crypto firms, with several local banks accepting crypto-related businesses. See our company setup page for more details.

Benefits of a Kazakhstan Crypto License

Key advantages include:

  • Tax incentives: 0% corporate tax for 10 years, and 0% VAT on crypto services.
  • Regulatory clarity: A well-defined legal framework under English common law.
  • Strategic location: Gateway to Central Asian markets and the Belt and Road Initiative.
  • Access to the AIFC ecosystem: Networking with fintech firms, investors, and service providers.
  • International recognition: AFSA is a member of the International Association of Insurance Supervisors and aligns with FATF.

However, the high capital requirement and longer timeline may deter smaller startups. For a lower-cost alternative, consider Panama at a flat EUR 6,000.

Compliance and Trust Considerations

Operating a crypto company in Kazakhstan requires strong compliance. AFSA expects:

  • Comprehensive AML/CFT policies, including customer due diligence and transaction monitoring.
  • Appointment of a local Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO).
  • Regular audits by an approved external auditor.
  • Submission of annual financial statements and compliance reports.
  • IT security standards, including data protection and cyber resilience.

Failure to comply can result in fines, license suspension, or revocation. This is general guidance and not legal advice; consult a qualified lawyer for your specific situation. For more on compliance, visit our requirements page.

Common Mistakes When Applying

Founders often make these errors:

  • Underestimating the timeline: Expect 6-8 months, not 3.
  • Insufficient capital proof: AFSA requires evidence of liquid funds, not just commitments.
  • Weak AML policies: Generic templates are rejected; policies must be specific to the business model.
  • Lack of local substance: A virtual office is not enough; physical presence and local staff are mandatory.
  • Ignoring travel rule compliance: Kazakhstan requires VASP-to-VASP data sharing for transactions over USD 1,000.

Frequently asked questions

What is the total cost to set up a crypto company in Kazakhstan?

The total first-year cost, including application fees, legal fees, and office setup, typically ranges from USD 30,000 to USD 55,000, plus a capital deposit of USD 100,000 to USD 200,000. Exact figures depend on the services offered and your specific situation.

How long does it take to get a Kazakhstan crypto license?

The licensing process usually takes 4 to 8 months from application submission to approval. This includes a pre-application phase for document preparation and AFSA's review period, which can extend if additional information is requested.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a Kazakhstan crypto license?

The minimum capital requirement is USD 100,000 for basic services like custody and simple exchange, and USD 200,000 for advanced services such as trading or brokerage. This capital must be held in liquid form in a Kazakhstani bank account.

Are there any tax benefits for crypto companies in Kazakhstan?

Yes, within the AIFC, crypto companies enjoy 0% corporate income tax for the first 10 years, 0% VAT on financial services, and 0% withholding tax on dividends to non-residents. Personal income tax for AIFC employees is also 0% on qualifying income.

Can I operate a crypto exchange in Kazakhstan without an AIFC license?

No, operating a crypto exchange or any crypto asset service without an AIFC license is not permitted. The AIFC is the sole regulatory body for digital asset services in Kazakhstan, and unlicensed activity can result in penalties.

What activities are allowed under a Kazakhstan crypto license?

Allowed activities include operating a crypto exchange, custody, brokerage, investment management, token issuance, and advisory services. Staking and lending may be permitted with additional approval. Derivatives and securities tokens require separate licenses.

Is it difficult to open a bank account for a crypto company in Kazakhstan?

Yes, it can be challenging due to banks' AML concerns. Some AIFC-registered banks are more accommodating, but they require strong compliance documentation. International banks with local presence may also open accounts, but the process is rigorous.

How does Kazakhstan compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Panama offers a much lower cost (EUR 6,000 flat) and faster setup (2-3 months) with no minimum capital requirement. Kazakhstan has higher costs (USD 30,000+) and a longer timeline, but provides tax incentives and access to Central Asian markets.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements for a Kazakhstan crypto license?

Ongoing requirements include annual AML/CFT audits, submission of financial statements, appointment of a local MLRO, transaction monitoring, and travel rule compliance. Non-compliance can lead to fines or license revocation.
