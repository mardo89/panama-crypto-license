What Is a Crypto License in Singapore?

Singapore does not issue a standalone "crypto license" per se. Instead, crypto service providers must obtain a Major Payment Institution (MPI) licence under the Payment Services Act (PSA), which regulates Digital Payment Token (DPT) services. This licence covers activities like operating a crypto exchange, providing custodial wallet services, and facilitating crypto transfers.

The MAS revised the PSA in 2024-2025 to align with international standards, including enhanced anti-money laundering (AML) requirements and a mandatory segregation of customer assets. As of 2026, all DPT service providers must hold an MPI licence to operate legally. The regime is strict but offers a clear path for compliant businesses.

Key features of the Singapore crypto licence:

  • Regulated by the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS)
  • Covered under the Payment Services Act (PSA)
  • Requires a minimum capital of S$250,000 for DPT services
  • Includes ongoing compliance, audit, and reporting obligations

Who Needs a Crypto License in Singapore?

Any business that provides any of the following services in or from Singapore must obtain an MPI licence:

  • Operating a digital payment token (crypto) exchange
  • Providing custodial wallet services for DPTs
  • Facilitating the transfer of DPTs (including cross-border)
  • Merchant acquisition services for DPTs
  • Issuing DPTs (e.g., stablecoins under MAS regulation)

Even if your company is registered outside Singapore but offers services to Singapore residents, you may be subject to licensing. The MAS takes a territorial approach. Many founders choose to incorporate a Singapore entity and apply for the MPI licence to access the Asian market legally.

If your business model involves any of the above, you need to start the licensing process. For those who find the Singapore process too lengthy or costly, alternatives like Panama offer a flat fee of EUR 6,000 and a faster timeline.

License Type & Regulator

The relevant licence is the Major Payment Institution (MPI) licence under the Payment Services Act. The regulator is the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS), Singapore's central bank and integrated financial regulator.

There are two tiers of licences under the PSA:

Licence TypeThresholdCapital Requirement
Standard Payment Institution (SPI)Monthly transaction volume < S$3 millionS$100,000
Major Payment Institution (MPI)Monthly transaction volume >= S$3 millionS$250,000 (for DPT services)

Most crypto businesses will require the MPI licence due to the volume of transactions. The MAS also imposes a minimum capital of S$250,000 specifically for DPT services, which is higher than the general MPI capital requirement of S$100,000. This figure is set by the MAS and is non-negotiable.

Capital Requirement

The MAS mandates a minimum capital of S$250,000 for MPI licence holders that provide DPT services. This capital must be maintained at all times and can be in the form of cash or liquid assets. The capital requirement is designed to ensure financial stability and operational resilience.

Additionally, the MAS may require higher capital based on the risk profile and transaction volume of the applicant. For example, if your platform handles large volumes, the MAS might impose a higher capital buffer. The capital must be held in a Singapore bank account and cannot be used for operational expenses without regulatory approval.

For comparison, Estonia requires €12,000-€100,000 depending on the licence type, and Lithuania requires €125,000 for exchange services. Panama has no minimum capital requirement, making it an attractive alternative for startups.

Tax Treatment

Singapore offers a favourable tax regime for crypto businesses. The Inland Revenue Authority of Singapore (IRAS) treats cryptocurrency as property for tax purposes. Key points:

  • Corporate tax: Standard rate of 17%. New companies may enjoy tax exemptions for the first three years (up to S$125,000 of chargeable income).
  • GST: As of 2026, the supply of DPTs is exempt from Goods and Services Tax (GST). However, other services (like advisory fees) may be subject to 9% GST.
  • Capital gains: No capital gains tax in Singapore, which is beneficial for long-term holdings.
  • Withholding tax: Payments to non-residents may be subject to withholding tax at 10-15% depending on the nature.

Singapore does not tax foreign-sourced income remitted to Singapore, provided the income has been taxed in the source jurisdiction. This makes it a tax-efficient hub for international crypto operations. However, proper tax structuring is essential. For a zero-tax alternative, Panama offers territorial taxation (no tax on foreign income) with no capital gains tax.

Allowed Activities

An MPI licence in Singapore permits the following regulated activities:

  • Operating a digital payment token exchange (buying/selling DPTs)
  • Providing custodial wallet services for DPTs
  • Facilitating DPT transfers (including cross-border remittances)
  • Merchant acquisition for DPT payments
  • Issuing DPTs (e.g., stablecoins, subject to additional MAS guidelines)

The licence does not cover:

  • Securities or derivatives trading (requires Capital Markets Services licence)
  • DeFi protocols that are not operated by a central entity
  • Non-fungible tokens (NFTs) unless they qualify as DPTs

Step-by-Step Process

Here is the step-by-step process to get a crypto license in Singapore in 2026:

  1. Pre-incorporation consultation: Engage a compliance advisor (like Consulting24) to assess your business model and readiness.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Singapore private limited company with ACRA. Minimum paid-up capital of S$1 (but S$250,000 is required for the licence).
  3. Prepare AML/CFT policies: Draft comprehensive policies covering customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, suspicious reporting, and record keeping.
  4. Appoint key personnel: At least one director must be a Singapore resident. Compliance officer, MLRO, and auditor must be appointed.
  5. Submit pre-application: File a pre-application with MAS to confirm eligibility and scope.
  6. Formal application: Submit the full MPI licence application via the MAS's online portal, including all supporting documents (business plan, risk assessment, financial projections).
  7. MAS review: The MAS reviews the application, which may take 3-6 months. They may request clarifications or additional information.
  8. Approval and post-licence: Once approved, you must maintain compliance, submit regular reports, and undergo annual audits.

Banking & Payments

Opening a bank account for a crypto business in Singapore is challenging but possible. Major banks like DBS, OCBC, and UOB have strict AML policies and may require a minimum deposit of S$50,000-S$100,000. Some banks require a relationship manager and a physical presence.

Alternative options include digital banks like Aspire or neobanks that are more crypto-friendly. Additionally, payment service providers like Stripe and Xfers can be used for fiat on/off ramps.

For the MPI licence, you must maintain a Singapore bank account for the minimum capital. Consulting24 can introduce you to banking partners. If banking is a major hurdle, Panama offers a more accessible banking environment for crypto firms.

Obtaining an MPI licence in Singapore offers several advantages:

  • International reputation: Singapore is a globally respected financial centre, enhancing credibility with partners and investors.
  • Tax efficiency: Low corporate tax rate (17%) and no capital gains tax.

Compliance & Trust

Compliance is the cornerstone of the Singapore crypto licence. The MAS requires:

  • Appointment of a resident director, compliance officer, and money laundering reporting officer (MLRO).
  • Implementation of strong AML/CFT policies, including transaction monitoring and suspicious transaction reporting.
  • Segregation of customer assets from company assets, held in trust accounts.
  • Regular audits by an approved auditor.
  • Annual submission of audited financial statements and compliance reports.

Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the licence. Consulting24 advises clients on building a compliant framework. This is general guidance, not legal advice. For a less demanding compliance environment, Panama requires no mandatory resident director and has simpler reporting obligations.

Common Mistakes

Founders often make these mistakes when applying for a Singapore crypto licence:

  • Underestimating the timeline: The process can take 12+ months. Start early and plan for delays.
  • Incomplete AML policies: The MAS is strict on AML. Generic policies will be rejected.
  • Insufficient capital: The S$250,000 must be fully paid up and maintained.
  • Ignoring tax obligations: Even though DPT is GST-exempt, other services may be taxable.
  • Choosing the wrong licence type: Some apply for SPI when they need MPI, leading to reapplication.

Frequently asked questions

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Singapore?

The process typically takes 6 to 12 months from incorporation to approval. The MAS review period alone can take 3-6 months. Delays often occur if the application is incomplete or if additional information is requested.

What is the minimum capital for a Singapore crypto license?

The MAS requires a minimum capital of S$250,000 for Major Payment Institution licence holders providing Digital Payment Token services. This must be maintained at all times and held in a Singapore bank account.

Can a foreigner apply for a Singapore crypto license?

Yes, but the company must have at least one director who is a Singapore resident (citizen, permanent resident, or Employment Pass holder). Foreigners can incorporate a company and appoint a local director.

What activities are covered under the Singapore crypto license?

The MPI licence covers operating a DPT exchange, custodial wallet services, DPT transfers, merchant acquisition, and DPT issuance. It does not cover securities or derivatives trading.

Is there a tax on crypto transactions in Singapore?

Singapore does not impose capital gains tax. Corporate tax is 17% on profits. The supply of DPTs is exempt from GST. Foreign-sourced income may be exempt if taxed abroad.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

Licensees must appoint a compliance officer and MLRO, conduct annual audits, submit periodic reports to MAS, maintain AML/CFT policies, and segregate customer assets. Non-compliance can lead to fines or licence revocation.

Can I use a Singapore crypto license to operate in other countries?

The licence is valid only for activities in or from Singapore. To operate in other jurisdictions, you may need separate licences. However, the Singapore licence enhances credibility globally.

How much does it cost to apply for a Singapore crypto license?

Total costs including legal, compliance, and application fees typically range from S$100,000 to S$200,000. The MAS application fee itself is S$1,000 to S$10,000. Ongoing annual compliance costs are around S$20,000 to S$50,000.

What happens if my application is rejected?

If the MAS rejects your application, you may appeal or reapply after addressing the deficiencies. Common reasons for rejection include incomplete documentation, insufficient AML policies, or concerns about the business model.

Is Panama a better alternative to Singapore for crypto licensing?

Panama offers a flat fee of EUR 6,000, no minimum capital, and a 2-3 month timeline, making it ideal for startups and cost-conscious firms. Singapore is better for those needing high regulatory credibility and access to Asian markets.
