What Is the Singapore vs Panama Choice for Crypto Companies?

The decision between Singapore and Panama for a crypto company boils down to two fundamentally different approaches to regulation and business environment. Singapore offers a highly regulated, prestigious, but expensive and time-consuming license under the Payment Services Act (PSA). Panama, on the other hand, provides a low-cost, flexible corporate structure with no dedicated crypto law, making it attractive for startups and projects that prioritise speed and minimal upfront costs.

Key differences include:

  • Regulatory clarity: Singapore has a clear, enforced regime; Panama has no specific crypto regulation, relying on general AML obligations.
  • Cost: Singapore license application and compliance can exceed EUR 100,000; Panama company setup is a flat EUR 6,000.
  • Timeline: Singapore takes 6-12 months; Panama can be done in 2-4 weeks.
  • Tax: Singapore has a territorial tax system with incentives; Panama taxes only locally sourced income.

Your choice depends on whether you need a regulated badge for institutional partnerships or a lean, fast setup for a growing venture. For more details on other options, explore our jurisdictions page.

Who Needs a Crypto License in Singapore or Panama?

In Singapore, any business providing digital payment token (DPT) services - including exchange, transfer, and custody - must hold an MPI license from MAS. This applies to both local and foreign firms serving Singapore residents. Exemptions exist for limited operations, but most commercial crypto activities require licensing.

In Panama, there is no specific crypto license. Companies can legally operate crypto-related activities under a standard corporation, provided they comply with general AML laws (Law 23 of 2015) and register with the Financial Analysis Unit (UAF) if engaging in money service activities. This makes Panama suitable for:

  • Startups testing a crypto business model
  • Projects that do not target Singapore or EU residents
  • Companies seeking a holding or treasury entity for crypto assets

If your target market is institutional or requires a regulated status, Singapore may be necessary. For a lighter, faster setup, Panama is often the better fit. Consulting24 helps you assess your specific needs and choose the right jurisdiction.

Capital Requirements

Singapore: The MPI license requires a base capital of SGD 250,000 (approximately EUR 170,000). If you provide e-money issuance or domestic money transfer, higher tiers apply. This capital must be maintained at all times and is subject to MAS's audit.

Panama: There is no statutory minimum capital for a standard corporation. However, for credibility and banking purposes, a paid-up capital of at least USD 10,000 (EUR ~9,300) is recommended. No capital is required to be locked with a regulator.

The difference is stark: Singapore demands significant committed capital, while Panama allows you to allocate funds to growth instead. For many founders, Panama's low barrier is a decisive advantage. For more on capital requirements across jurisdictions, visit our requirements page.

Tax Treatment for Crypto Companies

Singapore: Territorial taxation - only income sourced in Singapore is taxed. Corporate tax rate is 17%, with incentives for fintech and startups (e.g., tax exemption for new companies on first SGD 100,000 of chargeable income). Capital gains are not taxed, which benefits crypto trading profits. GST (VAT) is exempt on DPT transactions.

Panama: Territorial tax system - only income generated within Panama is taxed. Offshore income (including crypto trading with non-Panamanian counterparties) is tax-free. Corporate tax rate is 25% on local-source income. No capital gains tax, no VAT on crypto services. Panama also offers a 0% tax on foreign-source dividends.

Both jurisdictions offer favourable tax treatment for crypto, but Panama's territorial system is broader, exempting most international crypto activities. Singapore's incentives are more targeted but require substance and compliance. For a detailed comparison, see our blog on crypto taxation.

Allowed Activities Under Each Regime

Singapore (MPI license): Allows:

  • Digital payment token exchange (spot trading)
  • Cross-border and domestic money transfer
  • Merchant acquisition
  • E-money issuance
  • Digital payment token custody

Panama (no license): Allows any crypto activity not requiring a financial license, including:

  • Cryptocurrency exchange (P2P, OTC)
  • Wallet services (non-custodial)
  • Mining and staking
  • Token issuance (subject to securities laws)
  • DeFi protocols (if no regulated financial activity)

Singapore's license is comprehensive but limited to specific services. Panama offers more freedom but with less regulatory clarity. If you plan to offer services to Singapore residents, you must have an MPI license. For global operations without a Singapore nexus, Panama is more permissive.

Step-by-Step Process: Singapore vs Panama

Singapore MPI License Process

  1. Pre-application: Engage a compliance consultant (e.g., Consulting24 advises) and prepare business plan, AML policy, risk assessment.

Banking and Payment Services

Singapore: Banking is relatively accessible for licensed MPI holders. Major banks like DBS, OCBC, and UOB offer accounts to crypto firms, but due diligence is thorough. Some digital banks (e.g., Revolut) also provide services. Payment gateways are widely available.

Panama: Banking is more challenging for crypto companies. Traditional Panamanian banks are conservative. However, international banks with Panama presence (e.g., Banco General, Towerbank) may open accounts for well-structured companies. Consulting24 assists with introductions to crypto-friendly banks and payment processors. Alternatively, many Panama crypto firms use EMI (electronic money institution) accounts in Europe or the US.

Singapore offers better banking integration, but Panama's lower costs may offset the banking friction. For guidance on banking, see our company setup page.

Benefits of Singapore vs Panama

Singapore benefits:

  • Strong regulatory reputation - facilitates partnerships with banks, exchanges, and institutional investors.
  • Access to Asian markets and Singapore's fintech ecosystem.
  • Stable political and legal environment.
  • Tax incentives for fintech and innovation.

Panama benefits:

  • Low cost and fast setup - EUR 6,000 flat, 2-4 weeks.
  • No crypto license requirement - minimal regulatory burden.
  • Territorial tax - offshore crypto income tax-free.
  • Flexible corporate structure (bearer shares, nominee directors).
  • Privacy - no public register of beneficial owners (until 2026 reforms).

Your choice depends on whether you prioritise regulatory credibility (Singapore) or cost and speed (Panama). Many founders start in Panama and later obtain a license in Singapore or Lithuania for expansion.

Compliance and Trust Considerations

Singapore: MAS imposes strict AML/CFT obligations, including transaction monitoring, suspicious transaction reporting (STR), and regular audits. Non-compliance can result in fines or license revocation. The regime is mature and respected globally.

Panama: AML compliance is required under Law 23 of 2015, but enforcement is lighter. Crypto companies must register with the UAF if they engage in money service activities. However, Panama is on the FATF grey list (as of 2026), which may affect correspondent banking relationships. Compliance is essential but less onerous than Singapore.

For founders who prioritise trust and institutional acceptance, Singapore is superior. Panama suits those who value speed and low overhead and are willing to manage the grey list risk. Consulting24 provides compliance support for both jurisdictions - general guidance, not legal advice.

Common Mistakes When Choosing Between Singapore and Panama

  1. Underestimating Singapore costs: Many founders are shocked by the total cost (EUR 100k+) and timeline (6-12 months). Plan accordingly.
  2. Ignoring Panama's grey list status: As of 2026, Panama is on the FATF grey list. This can complicate banking and partnerships. Stay informed.
  3. Assuming no regulation in Panama: While no crypto license exists, AML obligations apply. Ignoring them can lead to fines.
  4. Overestimating Singapore's ease: MAS is rigorous. Application rejection rates are high without expert help.
  5. Failing to consider target market: If your clients are in Singapore, you need an MPI license. Panama is not a substitute for regulated markets.

Why Choose Panama Over Singapore?

Panama is often the better choice for early-stage crypto projects, bootstrapped startups, and companies that do not require a regulated status for their business model. The flat EUR 6,000 cost, 2-4 week setup, and territorial tax regime make it one of the most cost-effective jurisdictions globally. You can start operating quickly and reinvest savings into product development.

Moreover, Panama's flexibility allows you to pivot your business model without regulatory constraints. If later you need a regulated license (e.g., to partner with EU banks), you can obtain one in Lithuania or Estonia while maintaining your Panama holding company. For many founders, the question is not whether Panama is better than Singapore in absolute terms, but whether the regulatory premium of Singapore is worth the cost. For most early-stage projects, it is not.

Frequently asked questions

Is Panama good for crypto companies in 2026?

Yes, Panama remains a popular choice due to its low cost (EUR 6,000 flat), fast setup (2-4 weeks), and territorial tax system. However, it is on the FATF grey list, which may affect banking. It is best for projects that do not require a regulated license.

What is the minimum capital for a crypto company in Singapore?

The MPI license requires a base capital of SGD 250,000 (approx. EUR 170,000). Higher tiers apply for certain activities. This capital must be maintained and is subject to MAS audit.

Can I operate a crypto exchange from Panama without a license?

Yes, Panama has no specific crypto license. You can operate a crypto exchange under a standard corporation, provided you comply with AML laws and register with the UAF if required. However, you cannot serve Singapore or EU residents without their respective licenses.

How long does it take to get a Singapore crypto license?

The MAS application process typically takes 6-12 months. This includes pre-application preparation, submission, review, and any conditions. Consulting24 advises on the process.

What taxes do crypto companies pay in Panama?

Panama taxes only locally sourced income at 25%. Offshore income, including crypto trading with non-Panamanian counterparties, is tax-free. No capital gains tax or VAT on crypto services.

Is Singapore's crypto license worth the high cost?

For projects targeting institutional clients or needing a strong regulatory badge, yes. Singapore offers credibility and access to Asian markets. For early-stage startups, the cost may be prohibitive.

Can I use Panama as a holding company for a crypto group?

Yes, Panama is commonly used as a holding jurisdiction due to its tax neutrality and flexibility. You can hold IP, treasury, or investment assets in a Panama corporation while operating in regulated markets elsewhere.

Does Panama require a physical office for a crypto company?

No, Panama does not require a physical office for a corporation. A registered agent address is sufficient. However, for bank account opening, a local presence may be helpful.

What are the AML obligations for a Panama crypto company?

If you engage in money service activities (e.g., exchange, transfer), you must register with the UAF and implement AML policies including customer due diligence, record keeping, and suspicious transaction reporting.

How does Panama compare to Lithuania for crypto?

Lithuania offers an EU-regulated CASP license with capital from EUR 50,000, but costs are higher (approx. EUR 15-25k setup). Panama is cheaper and faster but lacks EU passporting. Choose based on target market.
