What Are AML/KYC Requirements for a El Salvador Crypto Company?

AML (anti-money laundering) and KYC (know-your-customer) requirements are regulatory obligations designed to prevent financial crimes such as money laundering and terrorist financing. For a El Salvador crypto company, these rules mandate verifying customer identities, monitoring transactions, and reporting suspicious activity to the Financial Investigation Unit (FIU) of El Salvador.

The central regulator is the Central Reserve Bank of El Salvador (BCR), which oversees compliance under the Law on Supervision and Regulation of the Bitcoin Ecosystem (Ley Bitcoin) and related AML decrees. Companies must implement a written AML program, appoint a compliance officer, and conduct customer due diligence (CDD) including enhanced due diligence (EDD) for high-risk clients.

Key components of an AML program include:

  • Written policies and procedures approved by senior management.
  • A designated compliance officer with local residency.
  • Ongoing employee training on AML/KYC.
  • Independent audit function to test the program annually.

Transaction monitoring systems must flag unusual patterns, such as rapid movements or high-value transfers. For example, a crypto exchange must screen all transactions against sanctions lists maintained by the UN and OFAC. Record-keeping requirements mandate storing customer identification data and transaction records for at least five years. The BCR also expects companies to conduct periodic reviews of their AML programs, at least every 12 months, and to update policies as new risks emerge. Failure to maintain strong AML controls can result in fines up to $500,000 or license revocation.

Who Needs to Comply?

Any entity providing virtual asset services in El Salvador must comply with AML/KYC regulations. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (both centralized and decentralized platforms operating within the country).
  • Bitcoin wallet providers offering custodial or non-custodial services.
  • Payment processors handling crypto transactions.
  • ATMs and kiosks dealing in digital assets.
  • Any business facilitating the transfer, custody, or exchange of virtual assets.

Even foreign companies targeting El Salvadoran customers may need to register and comply. For instance, a foreign exchange that allows El Salvador residents to trade must either obtain a local license or restrict access. The BCR has also clarified that decentralized finance (DeFi) platforms that are controlled by a central entity or have a governance token may fall under the definition of a VASP and thus require licensing. Non-custodial wallet providers that do not hold customer funds are generally exempt, but they should still seek legal advice to confirm their status.

License Type and Regulator

In El Salvador, crypto companies must obtain a license from the Central Reserve Bank (BCR) under the Bitcoin Law. The license is specifically for “Virtual Asset Service Providers” (VASPs). The BCR is the primary regulator, assisted by the Financial Investigation Unit (FIU) for AML enforcement.

The license type is a single category covering all crypto activities, but the scope of permitted services is defined in the application. There is no separate exchange or custody license; one license covers all allowed activities. The BCR evaluates applicants based on business model, financial health, and AML controls. As of 2026, the BCR has published detailed guidelines on application requirements, including a mandatory compliance manual. The regulator also conducts on-site inspections for high-risk applicants and may require a personal interview with the compliance officer. Capital Requirement

El Salvador does not impose a statutory minimum capital requirement for crypto licenses. However, the BCR expects companies to have sufficient financial resources to operate and implement AML/KYC controls. A practical minimum is around $50,000 in paid-up capital, but this is not codified. For comparison, EU member states under MiCA require EUR 50,000 to 150,000 depending on service class. Always confirm with your legal advisor.

In practice, the BCR may ask for a business plan demonstrating adequate funding for at least 12 months of operations. This includes salaries, technology costs, and compliance expenses. Some applicants have been asked to provide a bank guarantee or proof of liquid assets. For example, a startup exchange with 5 employees should budget at least $200,000 for the first year, including capital reserves. The BCR also considers the company's ability to cover potential losses from fraud or operational failures. Tax Treatment

El Salvador has no capital gains tax on Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies, making it attractive for crypto businesses. However, income tax applies at a standard rate of 30% for corporations. VAT is 13% on services, but crypto transactions may be exempt under certain conditions. The government has also introduced a tax amnesty for crypto companies that register before a certain date. Consult a tax expert for your specific situation.

For example, a crypto exchange earning fees from trades would pay 30% corporate income tax on net profits. But if the exchange holds Bitcoin as an investment, any appreciation is not taxed until converted to fiat. This creates a tax-efficient environment for long-term holders. Additionally, dividends paid to shareholders are subject to a 5% withholding tax. The tax amnesty, valid until December 2026, waives penalties for past non-compliance if companies register and pay a reduced fee. However, transfer pricing rules apply to related-party transactions, and the tax authority (DGII) has increased audits of crypto firms. Allowed Activities

Under the Bitcoin Law, licensed companies can perform the following activities:

  • Exchange between virtual assets and fiat currency.
  • Exchange between one or more forms of virtual assets.
  • Transfer of virtual assets.
  • Safekeeping and administration of virtual assets.
  • Participation in financial services related to virtual assets.

These activities are broadly defined, allowing for innovation. However, any activity not explicitly permitted requires prior approval from the BCR. For instance, operating a crypto derivatives platform may need additional authorization. The BCR has indicated it will issue further guidance on DeFi and staking services in 2026. Also, lending and borrowing of virtual assets are not explicitly covered, so companies must seek a specific ruling. The license does not permit activities that require a banking license, such as accepting deposits. For a more comprehensive list of permitted activities, see our exchange license page.

Step-by-Step Process to Obtain a License

  1. Pre-incorporation: Register a company in El Salvador with a legal address and director. This typically takes 1-2 weeks. You will need a local registered agent and a minimum of two directors (one can be a foreign entity).
  2. Develop AML/KYC policies: Draft a comprehensive AML program including customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and reporting procedures. This should be tailored to your business model. The program must cover risk assessment, customer identification, ongoing monitoring, and record keeping.
  3. Appoint a compliance officer: This person must be resident in El Salvador and have relevant experience. They will be the point of contact for the BCR. The compliance officer should have at least 3 years of experience in AML compliance and pass a background check.
  4. Submit application: File with the BCR, including business plan, AML policies, financial statements, and background checks on directors. The BCR charges a non-refundable fee of approximately $1,000. The application must be in Spanish and include certified translations of foreign documents.
  5. Regulatory review: The BCR assesses the application and may request additional information. This stage can take 1-2 months. The BCR may also conduct an interview with the compliance officer and beneficial owners.
  6. Approval and licensing: Once approved, you receive the license and can commence operations. You must then register with the FIU for AML supervision and submit an initial compliance report within 30 days.

Common Mistakes

  • Inadequate AML policies: Many applicants submit generic policies that do not meet local requirements. The BCR expects detailed procedures tailored to your services.
  • Ignoring ongoing compliance: After obtaining the license, some companies neglect reporting obligations. This can lead to fines or revocation.
  • Poor record keeping: Failure to maintain records for the required 5 years is a common violation.
  • Not appointing a local compliance officer: The BCR requires a resident compliance officer; outsourcing to a foreign firm is not accepted.
  • Underestimating costs: Many startups underestimate the total cost of compliance, including software and audits.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital requirement for a crypto license in El Salvador?

El Salvador does not have a statutory minimum capital requirement. However, the BCR expects companies to have sufficient funds to operate and implement AML controls. A practical minimum is around $50,000 in paid-up capital, but this is not legally mandated. Always confirm with your advisor.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in El Salvador?

The process typically takes 3-6 months from company incorporation to license approval. The BCR aims to review complete applications within 90 days, but delays can occur if additional information is needed. Pre-application preparation can shorten the timeline.

What are the tax benefits of a crypto company in El Salvador?

El Salvador has no capital gains tax on Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies. Corporate income tax is 30%, and VAT is 13% on services. Crypto transactions may be VAT-exempt. A tax amnesty is available for companies registering before December 2026.

Can a foreigner obtain a crypto license in El Salvador?

Yes, foreigners can obtain a license. The company must have a local legal address and a compliance officer resident in El Salvador. Directors can be foreign. The BCR will conduct background checks on all beneficial owners.

What activities are allowed under an El Salvador crypto license?

Allowed activities include exchange between virtual assets and fiat, exchange between virtual assets, transfer of virtual assets, safekeeping and administration, and participation in financial services related to virtual assets. Derivatives and lending require additional approval.

Is it difficult to open a bank account for a crypto company in El Salvador?

It can be challenging due to perceived risks. Some banks are willing to serve licensed VASPs, but due diligence is thorough. State-owned BANDESAL and private banks like Banco Agrícola are options. International banks may also be considered.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations after obtaining the license?

Ongoing obligations include filing suspicious activity reports within 24 hours, conducting annual AML program reviews, training staff, and maintaining records for 5 years. The BCR may conduct unannounced inspections.

Can I use a generic AML template for my El Salvador crypto license application?

No, the BCR expects a tailored AML program specific to your business model. Generic templates are often rejected. You must include risk assessment, customer due diligence procedures, transaction monitoring, and reporting protocols.

How does El Salvador's crypto license compare to Panama's company setup?

Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 setup fee with no corporate income tax on offshore income, but no specific crypto license is required for some activities. El Salvador has a licensing process with tax advantages for Bitcoin. Consulting24 delivers Panama directly.

What happens if I fail to comply with AML regulations in El Salvador?

Non-compliance can result in fines up to $500,000, license revocation, or criminal charges. The FIU actively monitors compliance. It is crucial to maintain strong AML controls and seek professional advice.
