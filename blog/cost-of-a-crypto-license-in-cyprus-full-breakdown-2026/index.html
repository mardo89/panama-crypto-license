What Is a Crypto License in Cyprus?

A crypto license in Cyprus, now issued under the EU MiCA framework, allows companies to provide crypto-asset services such as exchange, custody, and wallet services. The Cyprus Securities and Exchange Commission (CySEC) is the competent authority. The license is a CASP (Crypto-Asset Service Provider) authorization, which replaced the previous national registration regime in 2025.

Under MiCA, Cyprus offers a single passportable license across all EU member states. This means a Cyprus CASP can operate throughout the EU without additional licenses in each country. The cost of a crypto license in Cyprus includes regulatory fees, capital requirements, and professional service fees. For example, a company offering exchange services to French and German clients can do so from a Cyprus base, saving time and money on multiple national registrations.

MiCA also imposes uniform standards for consumer protection, market abuse prevention, and operational resilience. This regulatory clarity attracts serious projects. However, the cost of compliance is higher than in non-EU jurisdictions, which is why many founders compare Cyprus with Panama or other jurisdictions.

Who Needs a Crypto License in Cyprus?

Any business offering crypto-asset services to EU residents must obtain a CASP license under MiCA. This includes exchanges, wallet providers, custodians, and platforms facilitating crypto transactions. Founders targeting the European market should consider Cyprus due to its efficient regulatory process and competitive costs.

If you are a startup or an established exchange, the cost of a crypto license in Cyprus should be weighed against the benefits of EU passporting. For those seeking a simpler and faster setup, Panama offers a flat fee of EUR 6,000 with no minimum capital, though without EU passporting. A typical candidate for Cyprus is a mid-sized exchange with a clear EU customer base, or a custody provider needing regulatory credibility to attract institutional clients.

We also see DeFi projects and token issuers applying for CASP licenses to offer ancillary services. The key is that if you touch EU clients' crypto or fiat, you need authorization. Consulting24 advises on whether Cyprus or another jurisdiction fits your business model.

License Type and Regulator

The license is a Crypto-Asset Service Provider (CASP) authorization under MiCA, regulated by CySEC. CySEC is a well-respected EU regulator with a track record of supervising investment firms. The license covers several service categories, each with specific capital requirements.

  • Class 1: Services without client money or crypto custody (e.g., order execution only) - minimum capital EUR 50,000.
  • Class 2: Services involving custody of client crypto assets or money (e.g., exchange with wallet) - minimum capital EUR 125,000.
  • Class 3: Services that involve both custody and trading on own account (e.g., market making) - minimum capital EUR 150,000.

These tiers are set by MiCA and are uniform across all EU member states. The cost of a crypto license in Cyprus is influenced by the class you apply for. Choosing the right class is critical: applying for Class 3 when you only need Class 1 locks up unnecessary capital. CySEC also assesses the fitness and propriety of directors and shareholders, requiring detailed personal declarations.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement depends on the services you provide. As noted, Class 1 requires EUR 50,000, Class 2 EUR 125,000, and Class 3 EUR 150,000. This capital must be maintained at all times and can be in the form of cash or highly liquid assets. The capital requirement is a significant part of the cost of a crypto license in Cyprus, as it must be locked in the company.

For comparison, Panama has no minimum capital requirement, making it a lower-cost entry point. However, Panama does not offer EU passporting. Some founders choose to incorporate in Panama for the main operating entity and use a Cyprus-licensed subsidiary for EU clients, balancing capital costs with market access.

Capital can be released only with CySEC approval, typically after the license is surrendered. This means the capital is not an expense but a long-term commitment. Planning for this liquidity is essential.

Tax Treatment

Cyprus has one of the most favourable corporate tax regimes in the EU, with a standard corporate tax rate of 12.5%. Additionally, capital gains from the sale of securities (including crypto assets held as investments) are generally exempt from tax. VAT is not applicable to crypto transactions under current EU rules. However, profits from trading crypto as a business are subject to corporate tax.

There is also a special regime for non-domiciled residents, offering tax exemptions on dividends and interest. This can significantly reduce the effective tax burden for founders. The cost of a crypto license in Cyprus should be considered alongside these tax advantages. For example, a founder paying themselves dividends could see an effective tax rate below 10% with proper structuring.

Transfer pricing rules apply for related-party transactions, and Cyprus has a wide network of double tax treaties. This makes it attractive for international groups. We recommend a tax advisor to optimize your structure.

Allowed Activities

A Cyprus CASP license permits the following activities under MiCA:

  • Providing custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • Operating a trading platform for crypto-assets
  • Exchanging crypto-assets for fiat currency
  • Exchanging crypto-assets for other crypto-assets
  • Executing orders for crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • Placing crypto-assets
  • Receiving and transmitting orders for crypto-assets
  • Providing advice on crypto-assets
  • Managing crypto-assets on behalf of clients

These activities are passportable across the EU. The cost of a crypto license in Cyprus covers the authorization for one or more of these services. You can apply for multiple service categories under one license, but each may increase the capital requirement if they fall into higher classes. For instance, adding custody to a Class 1 exchange would move you to Class 2.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a Cyprus CASP license involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application preparation: Engage legal and compliance advisors. Prepare business plan, AML policies, and governance documents. This stage typically takes 1-2 months.
  2. Company incorporation: Set up a Cyprus limited company with a physical office and local directors (or at least one local director). This requires a registered address and a company secretary.
  3. Capital deposit: Deposit the required minimum capital in a Cyprus bank account. The bank will require proof of source of funds.
  4. Submit application: File the application with CySEC, including all supporting documents and the application fee. CySEC may request clarifications.
  5. Review and interviews: CySEC reviews the application, may request additional information, and conducts interviews with key personnel. This stage can take 3-6 months.
  6. Approval and licensing: Upon approval, pay the annual supervisory fee and receive the license. The license is valid indefinitely subject to ongoing compliance.

Each step contributes to the overall cost of a crypto license in Cyprus. We advise and coordinate applications in Cyprus as part of our services. For a simpler process, Panama offers a streamlined setup with no local director requirement and faster incorporation.

Banking and Payment Services

Opening a bank account in Cyprus for a crypto company can be challenging but is feasible with proper documentation. Major banks like Bank of Cyprus and Hellenic Bank accept licensed crypto firms, though they conduct thorough due diligence. They typically require the license approval letter, a detailed business plan, and proof of source of funds. The process can take 2-4 months.

Alternatively, EMI (Electronic Money Institution) accounts are available from providers like Revolut Business or Monese, which are more crypto-friendly. However, EMIs may have lower transaction limits. The cost of a crypto license in Cyprus includes the effort to secure banking relationships. We can assist with introductions to compliant banking partners.

For a simpler banking setup, Panama offers a more flexible banking environment with less scrutiny on crypto-related businesses. Many Panama-licensed companies use international EMIs or local banks with minimal friction.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, you must comply with ongoing requirements:

  • Annual AML audit and submission of compliance reports.
  • Maintain minimum capital at all times.
  • Appoint a local compliance officer and MLRO.
  • Submit periodic financial and operational reports to CySEC.
  • Undergo regular inspections by CySEC.

Annual compliance costs typically range from EUR 10,000 to EUR 25,000, adding to the total cost of a crypto license in Cyprus. This includes the compliance officer's salary (if in-house) or outsourced services, audit fees, and regulatory filing costs. This is general guidance, not legal advice. We recommend budgeting for at least EUR 20,000 per year for a small operation.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often underestimate the complexity of the application. Common pitfalls include:

  • Insufficiently detailed business plan or AML policies. CySEC expects granular descriptions of your operations, risk assessment, and transaction flows.
  • Underestimating the timeline and running out of funds. Many projects fail because they cannot sustain the 6-12 month process without revenue.
  • Choosing the wrong license class, leading to higher capital than needed. For example, a simple exchange without custody can use Class 1, saving EUR 75,000 compared to Class 2.
  • Failing to secure banking before applying. Some banks require the license to open an account, creating a chicken-and-egg problem. We advise identifying a banking partner early.
  • Not engaging experienced local advisors. Cyprus has specific corporate governance requirements, such as having at least one local director.

Avoiding these mistakes can reduce the effective cost of a crypto license in Cyprus by preventing delays and rejections. Consulting24's experience across 500+ applications helps you navigate these issues.

Alternatives and Comparisons

Cyprus vs. Panama: Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 fee, no minimum capital, and a 2-3 month process. However, it does not provide EU passporting. For EU-focused businesses, Cyprus may be worth the higher cost. Panama is ideal for non-EU markets or as a holding company.

Cyprus vs. Lithuania: Lithuania also offers a CASP license under MiCA with similar capital requirements but a slightly faster process (4-6 months). The cost is comparable. Lithuania has a 15% corporate tax rate. Compare Cyprus and Lithuania.

Cyprus vs. Estonia: Estonia no longer issues new crypto licenses under its old regime; it now follows MiCA. The process is similar to Cyprus. Estonia's e-residency program can be an advantage for remote setup.

Frequently asked questions

What is the total cost of a crypto license in Cyprus?

The total cost excluding capital is typically EUR 28,000 to EUR 58,000, including application fees, legal fees, and compliance setup. Minimum capital ranges from EUR 50,000 to EUR 150,000 depending on service class. Exact figures depend on your specific services and advisor rates. Contact us for a personalized quote.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Cyprus?

The process typically takes 6 to 12 months from application to approval. This includes company incorporation, document preparation, CySEC review, and interviews. Delays can occur if the application is incomplete or if CySEC requests additional information. Planning for at least 9 months is wise.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a Cyprus CASP license?

Under MiCA, the minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for Class 1 (no custody), EUR 125,000 for Class 2 (custody), and EUR 150,000 for Class 3 (custody and own account trading). This capital must be maintained at all times and held in liquid assets.

Can I passport my Cyprus crypto license to other EU countries?

Yes, a Cyprus CASP license is passportable across all EU member states under MiCA. This means you can provide services in any EU country without additional licensing, though you may need to register with local regulators for AML purposes.

What are the ongoing compliance costs for a Cyprus crypto license?

Annual compliance costs typically range from EUR 10,000 to EUR 25,000, including AML audits, compliance officer fees, regulatory filings, and legal support. This is in addition to the annual CySEC supervisory fee of EUR 3,000 to EUR 8,000.

Is Cyprus a good jurisdiction for crypto startups?

Cyprus is suitable for startups with sufficient funding (EUR 100,000+ for capital and fees) and a clear EU market focus. The tax regime is favourable, but the timeline and capital requirements may be challenging for early-stage projects. Panama or Lithuania may be better for smaller budgets.

Do I need a physical office in Cyprus?

Yes, you need a registered office in Cyprus, and at least one local director is required. The office can be a serviced space, but it must be a physical address where records are kept. Virtual offices are not accepted for the license application.

What are the tax benefits of a Cyprus crypto license?

Cyprus has a 12.5% corporate tax rate, exemptions on capital gains from securities (including crypto), and a non-domicile regime that exempts dividends and interest for qualifying individuals. This can result in an effective tax rate below 10% for founders.
