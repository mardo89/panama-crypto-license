What Is Crypto Banking in Panama?

Crypto banking in Panama refers to the ability for cryptocurrency businesses to operate under a local license that allows activities such as exchange, custody, and payment processing. Panama does not have a specific crypto law but uses a general business license combined with anti-money laundering (AML) compliance. The regulator is the Superintendencia de Bancos de Panamá (SBP) for banking activities and the Superintendencia del Mercado de Valores (SMV) for securities. However, crypto businesses typically operate under a commercial license (Sociedad Anónima) with a registered agent and must comply with AML/CFT obligations under Law 23 of 2015.

Founders often choose Panama for its territorial tax system: income earned outside Panama is not taxed. This makes it attractive for global crypto operations. The process is relatively fast, with company incorporation taking 2-4 weeks and license approval 3-6 months. Consulting24 handles the entire setup, including banking introductions. For example, a typical client might incorporate a Panamanian company to run a crypto exchange serving clients in Latin America and Asia, paying zero tax on those revenues while benefiting from the US dollar economy.

Who Needs a Crypto Banking License in Panama?

Any business that wants to offer cryptocurrency services to clients in or from Panama should consider a local license. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (centralized and decentralized)
  • Payment processors handling crypto-to-fiat conversions
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • OTC trading desks
  • Token issuers (if considered securities)

If your target market is Latin America, Panama is a strategic base. However, if your clients are primarily in the EU, you might compare Panama with Lithuania or Estonia, where MiCA regulations apply. License Type and Regulator

Panama does not have a dedicated 'crypto license'. Instead, businesses obtain a general commercial license (Sociedad Anónima) and register as a Financial Technology Company (Empresa de Tecnología Financiera) under the supervision of the Superintendencia de Bancos de Panamá (SBP) if they engage in payment services. For crypto exchange and custody, the regulator is the Superintendencia del Mercado de Valores (SMV) if tokens are classified as securities. Most crypto businesses operate under the SBP's fintech sandbox or as a non-regulated entity with AML compliance.

The key regulator for crypto banking in Panama is the Superintendencia de Bancos de Panamá (SBP). They oversee compliance with Law 23 of 2015 on AML/CFT. Capital Requirement

Panama does not have a statutory minimum capital for commercial companies. However, for fintech companies applying for a license under the SBP, a minimum capital of USD 250,000 (approximately EUR 230,000) is typically required. This capital must be deposited in a Panamanian bank and may be used for operational expenses. For companies that only provide crypto exchange without payment services, the capital requirement may be lower, but it is advisable to have at least USD 100,000 to demonstrate financial soundness.

Consulting24 can advise on the exact capital needed based on your business plan. Note that this is a regulatory expectation, not a fixed law, so exact amounts are confirmed during consultation. For example, a simple exchange may require USD 100,000, while a payment processor handling client funds may need the full USD 250,000. This capital is not locked; it can be used for salaries, rent, and other operational costs.

Tax Treatment

Panama operates a territorial tax system. Income earned outside Panama is not subject to Panamanian income tax. For crypto businesses, this means that if your clients are non-residents and your revenue comes from abroad, you pay 0% tax in Panama. However, income sourced within Panama (e.g., from Panamanian residents) is taxed at a standard corporate rate of 25%.

There is no capital gains tax on crypto trading profits if the gains are considered offshore. Additionally, Panama does not tax dividends paid to non-resident shareholders. This makes Panama one of the most tax-efficient jurisdictions for crypto banking. Consulting24 can connect you with local tax advisors to structure your operations optimally. For instance, a company that earns trading fees from global clients pays no Panamanian tax, while a company serving local clients would pay 25% on that portion.

Allowed Activities

Under a Panamanian fintech license, you can conduct the following activities:

  • Cryptocurrency exchange (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto)
  • Digital wallet services (custodial and non-custodial)
  • Payment processing with crypto conversion
  • OTC trading
  • Staking and lending (if not considered securities)

Activities that require additional licensing include issuing tokens that qualify as securities (regulated by SMV) and banking services like accepting deposits (requires a full banking license). Consulting24 helps you define your activity scope to ensure compliance. For example, a company offering staking as a service may need to verify that the staked tokens are not classified as securities under Panamanian law.
