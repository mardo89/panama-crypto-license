What Is Crypto Banking in Georgia?

Crypto banking in Georgia refers to the provision of banking-like services for digital assets, including custody, exchange, payment processing, and lending. Unlike jurisdictions with dedicated crypto licenses, Georgia treats crypto activities under its existing legal framework. The NBG oversees payment services and virtual asset service providers (VASPs) under the Law on Payment System and Payment Services. As of 2026, there is no specific "crypto bank" license; instead, firms register as VASPs or obtain a payment institution license to offer crypto-related services.

Key characteristics include:

  • Regulation by the NBG for payment services and anti-money laundering (AML) compliance.
  • No minimum capital requirement for VASP registration, but payment institutions require capital based on service scope.
  • Corporate tax rate of 15% with possible exemptions for reinvested profits.
  • Growing but cautious regulatory approach, with ongoing alignment with EU standards.

For founders considering Georgia, it is important to distinguish between a VASP registration and a full payment institution license. The VASP route is simpler and cheaper, but it limits the scope of services. A payment institution license allows broader activities but comes with higher capital and compliance costs. Any business offering crypto-related financial services in Georgia must comply with local regulations. This includes:

  • Brokers and dealers in digital assets.
  • Lending and staking platforms.

If you plan to serve Georgian residents or establish a physical presence, you need to register with the NBG or obtain a payment institution license. License Type and Regulator

The primary regulator for crypto activities in Georgia is the National Bank of Georgia (NBG). The NBG oversees payment services and VASPs under the Law on Payment System and Payment Services. As of 2026, there are two main pathways:

  • VASP Registration: For basic crypto services like exchange and custody. No minimum capital, but AML compliance is mandatory.
  • Payment Institution License: For firms offering payment services (including crypto payments). Requires minimum capital of GEL 350,000 (approx. EUR 120,000) and more extensive compliance.

Additionally, the Georgian Financial Supervisory Agency (FSA) regulates capital markets, but crypto securities fall under securities laws. For most crypto startups, the VASP route is the entry point. However, if you plan to offer payment services or issue electronic money, the payment institution license is necessary. Capital Requirement

Georgia does not impose a minimum capital requirement for VASP registration. However, for a Payment Institution License, the minimum capital is GEL 350,000 (approx. EUR 120,000). This capital must be maintained at all times. For firms offering only crypto exchange or custody, the VASP route is capital-light, making it attractive for early-stage startups. Tax Treatment

Georgia offers a competitive tax regime for businesses. Key points:

  • Corporate income tax: 15% on distributed profits (reinvested profits are tax-exempt).
  • Withholding tax on dividends: 5% for residents, 10% for non-residents (subject to treaties).
  • VAT: 18% on services, but crypto transactions may be exempt if classified as financial services.
  • No capital gains tax on crypto held for personal investment (business gains are taxed as corporate income).

This tax structure can be advantageous for crypto firms that reinvest profits. However, tax treatment of specific crypto activities (e.g., staking, DeFi) is not fully clarified; professional advice is recommended. For example, a crypto exchange that reinvests its profits into expansion may pay zero corporate tax in Georgia. In contrast, Panama offers 0% tax on offshore income, which may be simpler for international operations.

Allowed Activities

Under the VASP registration, allowed activities include:

  • Exchange of crypto for fiat or other crypto.
  • Custodial wallet services.
  • Transfer of crypto assets.
  • Participation in ICOs/STOs (subject to securities laws).

With a Payment Institution License, you can also offer payment services, including crypto payment processing and issuing electronic money. However, activities like crypto lending, staking, and derivatives may fall under securities regulation and require additional licensing from the FSA. As of 2026, the NBG is working on a comprehensive crypto law, expected to clarify these boundaries. For now, founders should seek legal advice before offering complex products. Step-by-Step Process

  1. Company Incorporation: Register a legal entity (e.g., LLC) in Georgia. This takes 1-2 weeks and costs around EUR 500-1,000.
  2. AML/KYC Program: Develop internal policies, appoint a compliance officer, and register with the AML monitoring service.
  3. VASP Application: Submit application to NBG with business plan, AML policies, and shareholder details. Processing takes 2-4 months.
  4. Capital Deposit: If applying for a Payment Institution License, deposit the minimum capital in a Georgian bank account.
  5. Bank Account: Open a corporate bank account (can be challenging; see Banking section).
  6. Launch: Once approved, commence operations. Ongoing reporting to NBG is required.

Banking and Payments

Opening a bank account for a crypto business in Georgia can be challenging. Most traditional banks are cautious due to AML concerns. Options include:

  • Local banks like TBC Bank and Bank of Georgia, which may accept crypto firms with strong compliance.
  • International payment processors or EMI licenses (e.g., from Lithuania) for fiat on/off ramps.
  • Crypto-friendly banks in other jurisdictions (e.g., Switzerland).

Many founders opt to combine a Georgian VASP registration with a payment institution license in Lithuania or Poland to access SEPA and faster payments.
