What Is the Cayman Islands Crypto License?

The Cayman Islands crypto license, officially known as a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) license, is a regulatory approval issued by the Cayman Islands Monetary Authority (CIMA). It allows businesses to conduct activities such as exchanging virtual assets, transferring virtual assets, and providing custodial wallet services. The legal framework is based on the Virtual Asset (Service Providers) Act, 2020 (VASP Act), which aligns with international standards set by the Financial Action Task Force (FATF).

There are two main categories of VASP licenses in the Cayman Islands: a full license for entities that conduct virtual asset services from within the islands, and a simple license for those that operate from outside but target Cayman residents. The full license is more common for international crypto businesses. The license is valid for one year and must be renewed annually. Who Needs a Cayman Islands Crypto License?

A Cayman Islands crypto license is required for any entity that provides virtual asset services in or from the Cayman Islands. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (centralized and decentralized)
  • Virtual asset custodians and wallet providers
  • Virtual asset transfer services (e.g., payment processors)
  • Virtual asset trading platforms
  • Any other business that facilitates the exchange, transfer, or storage of virtual assets

If your company is incorporated in the Cayman Islands or has a physical presence there, and you offer these services to clients, you must obtain a VASP license. The requirements also apply to entities that market their services to Cayman residents. License Type and Regulator

The Cayman Islands crypto license is regulated by the Cayman Islands Monetary Authority (CIMA). CIMA is a well-respected financial regulator that oversees banking, insurance, securities, and now virtual assets. The VASP Act distinguishes between two license classes:

License TypeDescription
Full VASP LicenseFor entities that conduct virtual asset services from within the Cayman Islands. Requires a physical office and local staff.
Simple VASP LicenseFor entities that operate from outside the Cayman Islands but provide services to Cayman residents. Lower compliance requirements.

Capital Requirement

The Cayman Islands VASP Act does not prescribe a specific minimum capital requirement for all license types. However, CIMA expects that the applicant maintains adequate financial resources proportionate to the nature, scale, and complexity of its business. In practice, a minimum capital of at least USD 100,000 (approximately EUR 92,000) is typically recommended for a full license. For simple licenses, a lower amount may be acceptable.

This is a significant difference from EU jurisdictions under MiCA, which impose tiered capital requirements of EUR 50,000, 125,000, or 150,000 depending on the services provided. Tax Treatment

The Cayman Islands is known for its tax-neutral regime. There is no corporate income tax, no capital gains tax, no withholding tax, and no value-added tax (VAT). Instead, the government relies on fees and indirect taxes. This makes the Cayman Islands highly attractive for crypto businesses seeking to minimize their tax burden.

However, it is important to note that the Cayman Islands has implemented economic substance requirements for certain entities. VASP licensees must demonstrate that they have adequate physical presence, staff, and expenditure in the islands. Failure to comply can result in penalties. Allowed Activities Under the License

A Cayman Islands VASP license permits the following activities:

  • Exchange between virtual assets and fiat currencies
  • Exchange between one or more forms of virtual assets
  • Transfer of virtual assets (on behalf of clients)
  • Safekeeping and administration of virtual assets (custody)
  • Participation in and provision of financial services related to an issuer's offer or sale of a virtual asset

The license does not cover activities like mining or the development of blockchain software unless they involve the provision of virtual asset services.   2. Company incorporation: You must incorporate a Cayman Islands exempted company or LLC. This takes 1-2 weeks.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, VASPs in the Cayman Islands must comply with ongoing obligations:

  • Annual renewal of the license with CIMA (fee applies).
  • Submission of audited financial statements within 6 months of the financial year-end.
  • Maintenance of AML/CFT policies and procedures, including transaction monitoring and reporting suspicious activity.
  • Appointment of a local compliance officer and money laundering reporting officer (MLRO).
  • Economic substance reporting (if applicable).
