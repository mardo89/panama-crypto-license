What Is a Dubai Crypto License?

A Dubai crypto license, issued by VARA, allows businesses to operate virtual asset services within or from Dubai (excluding the Dubai International Financial Centre, which has its own regime). The license covers activities such as exchange, custody, advisory, and more. VARA was established in 2022 and has since built a comprehensive regulatory framework that is fully operational in 2026.

Unlike some other jurisdictions, Dubai requires a physical presence, including a local office and staff. The process involves multiple stages: initial approval, licensing, and operational compliance. The timeline from application to approval can range from 3 to 12 months, depending on the complexity of your business model and the completeness of your documentation. For example, a simple advisory firm may be approved faster than a full exchange with multiple trading pairs. The key is to have all documents ready from the start, including a detailed business plan, AML policies, and proof of source of funds for all shareholders.

Who Needs a Dubai Crypto License?

Any entity that intends to provide virtual asset services in Dubai (mainland or free zones, excluding DIFC) must obtain a VARA license. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (spot, derivatives, OTC)
  • Custodians and wallet providers
  • Broker-dealers and market makers
  • Advisory and asset management firms
  • Payment service providers using virtual assets

If you plan to serve clients in the UAE or base your operations in Dubai, you need a license. Even if you are already licensed elsewhere, a Dubai license may be required to operate in the region. For instance, a company holding an Estonia crypto license cannot serve UAE clients without a local license. VARA has been active in enforcement, and operating without a license can lead to fines of up to AED 10 million.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA), part of the Dubai World Trade Centre Authority. VARA issues four main license categories:

CategoryActivities Covered
Category 1Exchange, broker-dealer, market maker
Category 2Custody, advisory, asset management
Category 3Payment services, remittances
Category 4Other virtual asset services (e.g., token issuance)

Each category has specific capital requirements and operational conditions. VARA also requires a local office and a compliance officer. For example, a Category 1 license requires a physical trading floor and a minimum of three staff with relevant experience. The regulator conducts background checks on all key personnel, including shareholders and directors.

Capital Requirement

Dubai's VARA imposes minimum capital requirements based on license category. As of 2026, these are:

  • Category 1: AED 5 million (approx. EUR 1.25 million)
  • Category 2: AED 2 million (approx. EUR 500,000)
  • Category 3: AED 1 million (approx. EUR 250,000)
  • Category 4: AED 500,000 (approx. EUR 125,000)

Capital must be held in a local bank account and cannot be withdrawn without regulator approval. This is significantly higher than Panama, which has no minimum capital requirement for its crypto license. For example, a startup exchange would need to raise at least EUR 1.25 million just for capital, plus operational costs, making Dubai suitable only for well-funded entities.

Tax Treatment

Dubai offers a favorable tax environment. There is no corporate tax for most free zone companies, and no personal income tax. However, as of 2026, a 9% federal corporate tax applies to profits exceeding AED 375,000 (approx. EUR 95,000), but free zone companies that meet certain conditions may still be exempt. VAT is 5% on taxable supplies. There are no capital gains or withholding taxes on crypto transactions.

Allowed Activities

A Dubai crypto license permits a range of activities, depending on the category. Common allowed activities include:

  • Operating a virtual asset exchange (spot and derivatives)
  • Custody and safekeeping of virtual assets
  • Broker-dealer services
  • Market making
  • Advisory and asset management
  • Payment services using virtual assets
  • Token issuance and initial exchange offerings (IEOs)

Prohibited activities include services related to privacy coins, gambling, and any activity that violates UAE anti-money laundering laws. For example, dealing with Monero or Zcash is not allowed. Additionally, any token classified as a security under UAE law requires separate approval from the Securities and Commodities Authority (SCA).

Step-by-Step Process

Obtaining a Dubai crypto license involves several stages. Here is a typical timeline:

  1. Pre-application (1-2 months): Business plan, legal structure, compliance policies, and documentation. This includes drafting AML/KYC procedures, risk assessments, and appointing a compliance officer.
  2. Initial approval (2-3 months): Submit application to VARA, which reviews the business model and key personnel. Approval in principle is granted, often subject to conditions such as office setup.
  3. Licensing (3-6 months): Fulfill conditions: set up office, hire staff, implement AML/KYC procedures, and submit final documentation. VARA conducts an on-site inspection to verify physical presence and systems.
  4. Final approval (1-2 months): License issued after all conditions are met. Total: 6-12 months.

Common Mistakes

Frequently asked questions

How long does a Dubai crypto license take in 2026?

The total process typically takes 6 to 12 months. Initial approval takes 2-3 months, followed by 3-6 months for full licensing, and final approval 1-2 months. The timeline depends on the complexity of your business and the completeness of your application. For example, a simple advisory license may take 4-6 months, while a full exchange can take up to a year.

What is the cost of a Dubai crypto license?

First-year costs range from EUR 70,000 to 165,000, including application fees, license fees, office rent, and compliance costs. Exact figures depend on the license category and free zone. A consultation is needed for a precise quote. In comparison, Panama costs a flat EUR 6,000.

What are the capital requirements for a Dubai crypto license?

Minimum capital ranges from AED 500,000 (approx. EUR 125,000) for Category 4 to AED 5 million (approx. EUR 1.25 million) for Category 1. Capital must be held in a local bank account and cannot be withdrawn without regulator approval. Panama has no capital requirement.

Can I operate a crypto exchange in Dubai without a license?

No. Operating without a VARA license is illegal and can result in fines up to AED 10 million and imprisonment. All virtual asset service providers must be licensed. Even if you are based in a free zone, you need a license from VARA.

Is a physical office required for a Dubai crypto license?

Yes. VARA requires a physical presence in Dubai, including a local office and staff. The office must be in a free zone or mainland area approved by VARA. Remote operations are not allowed. This adds to the cost and timeline.

What is the difference between VARA and DIFC crypto licenses?

VARA regulates virtual assets in Dubai mainland and free zones (except DIFC). DIFC has its own regulator, the Dubai Financial Services Authority (DFSA), which issues licenses for crypto activities within the DIFC. DIFC licenses are more expensive and have higher capital requirements.

Can I use a Dubai license to serve clients in other countries?

A Dubai license allows you to serve clients in the UAE. For clients outside the UAE, you may need additional licenses in their jurisdictions. Dubai does not have passporting rights like the EU under MiCA. For global reach, Panama or Lithuania may be better options.

How does Panama compare to Dubai for crypto licensing?

Panama offers a flat fee of EUR 6,000, no capital requirement, and a timeline of 2-4 weeks. Dubai costs EUR 70,000-165,000, requires capital of EUR 125,000-1.25 million, and takes 6-12 months. Panama is better for startups, while Dubai offers market access to the Middle East.

What are the ongoing compliance costs for a Dubai crypto license?

Annual costs include license renewal (EUR 30,000-60,000), office rent (EUR 15,000-40,000), compliance officer salary (EUR 30,000-60,000), and audit fees (EUR 10,000-20,000). Total ongoing costs can be EUR 85,000-180,000 per year.
