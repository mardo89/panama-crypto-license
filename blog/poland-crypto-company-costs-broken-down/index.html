Poland crypto company costs refer to the total expenditure required to establish and operate a crypto asset service provider (CASP) in Poland under the 2026 MiCA framework. These costs include company registration, license application fees, minimum capital, legal and compliance setup, banking integration, and ongoing operational expenses.

Who Needs a Poland Crypto License?

Any entity planning to offer crypto asset services in Poland or to Polish residents must obtain a CASP license under MiCA. This includes exchanges, wallet providers, custodians, and brokers. The license is mandatory for both domestic and foreign companies targeting the Polish market.

Key beneficiaries include:

  • Startups launching a crypto exchange or trading platform
  • Fintech firms integrating crypto payments
  • Institutional investors setting up custody services
  • DeFi projects transitioning to regulated models
  • Blockchain developers offering wallet or staking services

If you are exploring multiple jurisdictions, Poland offers a solid EU framework, but you should also compare with Panama for a more cost-effective and faster setup.

License Type and Regulator

In Poland, the crypto license is a CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) authorization under MiCA. The regulatory authority is the Polish Financial Supervision Authority (Komisja Nadzoru Finansowego, KNF). The KNF oversees compliance with capital requirements, AML/CFT rules, and consumer protection standards.

The license covers multiple service classes, each with specific capital thresholds. The main categories include:

  • Class 1: Custody and administration of crypto assets (EUR 125,000)
  • Class 2: Operation of a trading platform (EUR 150,000)
  • Class 3: Exchange of crypto for fiat or other crypto (EUR 50,000)
  • Class 4: Execution of orders on behalf of clients (EUR 50,000)
  • Class 5: Placement of crypto assets (EUR 50,000)
  • Class 6: Reception and transmission of orders (EUR 50,000)
  • Class 7: Advice on crypto assets (EUR 50,000)

These figures are set by MiCA and are subject to adjustment. Always verify with the KNF or your legal advisor.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a Poland CASP depends on the service classes you apply for. Under MiCA, the tiers are:

  • EUR 50,000 for most service classes (exchange, order execution, advice, etc.)
  • EUR 125,000 for custody and administration
  • EUR 150,000 for operating a trading platform

This capital must be held in a bank account in Poland or another EU member state and cannot be used for operational expenses. It serves as a safeguard for clients. The capital must be maintained throughout the license period. If you apply for multiple classes, the highest capital requirement applies.

If you are looking for a jurisdiction with no minimum capital requirement, Panama offers a more flexible structure.

Tax Treatment

Poland's tax treatment of crypto assets is evolving. As of 2026, corporate income tax (CIT) is 19% (9% for small taxpayers). Crypto trading profits are generally taxable as business income. VAT is not applicable on crypto-to-crypto transactions, but fiat conversions may be subject to VAT. Personal income tax (PIT) for individuals is 17-32%.

Key considerations:

  • Capital gains from crypto are taxed at 19% flat rate for individuals (if held as investment).
  • Business income from crypto services is taxed under standard CIT/PIT rates.
  • Losses can be offset against future profits.
  • Transfer pricing rules apply for related-party transactions.
  • Withholding tax on dividends is 19% (may be reduced under tax treaties).

Allowed Activities

A Poland CASP license permits a wide range of activities under MiCA, including:

  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Operation of a crypto trading platform
  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency or other crypto assets
  • Execution of orders on behalf of clients
  • Placement of crypto assets
  • Reception and transmission of orders
  • Providing advice on crypto assets

Activities not covered include crypto mining, non-fungible tokens (NFTs) as collectibles, and decentralized finance (DeFi) protocols if they do not involve client funds. Always confirm with the KNF.

Step-by-Step Process

Obtaining a Poland crypto license involves several stages:

  1. Company formation: Register a limited liability company (sp. z o.o.) with a registered office in Poland. This typically takes 1-2 weeks.
  2. Prepare documentation: Draft AML/CFT policies, business plan, risk assessment, and governance framework. This can take 4-8 weeks.
  3. Open a bank account: Deposit the minimum capital in a Polish bank. This step may require a physical visit.
  4. Submit application: File with the KNF, including all supporting documents and fee payment. The KNF will acknowledge receipt within 14 days.
  5. Regulatory review: The KNF assesses the application, which may take 3-6 months. They may request clarifications or additional documents.
  6. Interview and approval: Key personnel may be interviewed. Upon approval, the license is granted.
  7. Ongoing compliance: Submit regular reports, undergo audits, and maintain capital.

Banking and Payments

Opening a bank account for a crypto company in Poland can be challenging due to AML concerns. Many traditional banks are cautious. Options include:

  • Polish banks like PKO BP, mBank, or Santander (with strong compliance documentation).
  • EMI (Electronic Money Institutions) like Revolut or Paysera for operational accounts.
  • International banks with a presence in Poland.

Requirements typically include a valid license (or proof of application), AML policies, and personal identification of directors. Some banks require a minimum deposit of EUR 10,000. Consulting24 can assist with introductions to crypto-friendly banking partners.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make these errors when pursuing a Poland crypto license:

  • Underestimating capital requirements: Ensure you have the correct tier for your services. Applying for a trading platform without EUR 150,000 capital will delay the process.
  • Incomplete AML documentation: The KNF scrutinizes AML policies heavily. Missing a single procedure can lead to rejection.
  • Choosing the wrong service class: Applying for too many classes increases costs and complexity. Focus on what you actually need.
  • Ignoring tax implications: Plan for both corporate and personal taxes. Unexpected tax bills can strain cash flow.
  • Rushing the process: Allow sufficient time for regulatory review. Submitting incomplete applications wastes time.
  • Neglecting banking relationships: Start the bank account process early, as it can take months.

Frequently asked questions

What is the total cost to set up a crypto company in Poland?

The total cost typically ranges from EUR 15,000 to EUR 30,000, excluding the minimum capital deposit (EUR 50,000 to EUR 150,000). This includes company registration, legal fees, application fees, and initial compliance setup. Exact figures depend on the service classes you apply for and the complexity of your business model.

How long does it take to get a Poland crypto license?

The process usually takes 6 to 12 months from company formation to license approval. The KNF's review period is 3-6 months, but delays can occur if documentation is incomplete. Working with experienced advisors can help streamline the process.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a Poland CASP?

Under MiCA, the minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for most service classes, EUR 125,000 for custody, and EUR 150,000 for operating a trading platform. The capital must be held in a bank account and cannot be used for operations.

Can I use a Poland crypto license to serve clients across the EU?

Yes, a Poland CASP license allows you to passport services across all EU member states under MiCA. This means you can serve clients in other EU countries without needing additional licenses, though you must comply with local consumer protection laws.

What are the ongoing compliance costs for a Poland crypto company?

Annual compliance costs typically range from EUR 5,000 to EUR 15,000, including audit fees, AML/CFT reporting, and compliance officer salaries. These costs can vary based on transaction volume and the complexity of your operations.

Is Poland's tax regime favorable for crypto companies?

Poland's corporate tax rate is 19% (9% for small taxpayers), which is moderate. Crypto trading profits are taxed as business income. There is no VAT on crypto-to-crypto transactions. However, personal capital gains from crypto are taxed at 19% flat rate.

What activities are not allowed under a Poland CASP license?

Activities not covered include crypto mining, NFTs as collectibles, and DeFi protocols that do not involve client funds. Additionally, services that fall outside the defined CASP categories may require separate authorization. Always check with the KNF.

How does Poland compare to Lithuania for crypto licensing?

Lithuania has a lower corporate tax rate (15%) and a faster timeline (3-6 months), but its capital requirement is fixed at EUR 125,000. Poland offers more flexibility in capital tiers but has higher overall costs. Both allow EU passporting.

Can I open a bank account for a crypto company in Poland easily?

Opening a bank account can be challenging due to AML concerns. Many Polish banks require a valid license or proof of application, strong compliance documentation, and personal identification of directors. EMIs like Revolut may offer easier alternatives.

What happens if I fail to comply with Poland's crypto regulations?

Non-compliance can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. The KNF may also impose penalties for AML breaches. It is essential to maintain ongoing compliance with MiCA and local laws. Consulting24 can help with compliance advisory.

Is Panama a better alternative to Poland for crypto licensing?

Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 fee, no minimum capital, and a 2-3 month timeline. It also has 0% tax on foreign-source income. However, Panama does not provide EU passporting. For cost-sensitive startups, Panama is often more attractive.
