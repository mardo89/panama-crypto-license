What Is a Crypto License in the Czech Republic?

A crypto license in the Czech Republic is an authorization granted by the Czech National Bank (CNB) to provide crypto-asset services under MiCA. As of 2026, all EU member states have transposed MiCA into national law, replacing previous fragmented regimes. The Czech license allows firms to operate across the EU via passporting, covering services such as exchange, custody, and transfer of crypto-assets.

The CNB oversees compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and prudential requirements. Unlike some jurisdictions, the Czech Republic has a well-defined application process with clear cost structures. For founders, this means predictable expenses and a regulated environment that enhances trust with partners and clients. The license is a single authorization that can include multiple service classes, each with specific capital tiers. The CNB is known for its thorough but fair approach, with application review typically taking 3-6 months. Key regulatory references include Act No. 253/2008 Coll. on AML (as amended), MiCA Regulation (EU) 2023/1114, and CNB decree on prudential requirements for CASPs.

Who Needs a Crypto License in the Czech Republic?

Any entity offering crypto-asset services in or from the Czech Republic must hold a CASP license. This includes crypto exchanges (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto), custodial wallet providers, brokers and dealers in crypto-assets, platforms facilitating crypto transfers, and advisors or portfolio managers dealing with crypto. If you plan to serve Czech clients or passport services to other EU states, licensing is mandatory.

Even non-EU firms targeting the European market often establish a Czech entity to benefit from the single market passport. For example, a US-based exchange wanting to offer services in Germany, France, and Italy can set up in Prague and passport across the EU. However, the capital and compliance costs can be significant. Capital Requirements

Under MiCA, capital requirements are tiered based on the type of service. For the Czech Republic, the CNB applies the standard EU thresholds:

  • Tier 1 (EUR 50,000): Custody and transfer services
  • Tier 2 (EUR 125,000): Exchange services (including order matching)
  • Tier 3 (EUR 150,000): Services involving client money or crypto (e.g., margin trading)

Capital must be held as equity or equivalent, and cannot be withdrawn during operation. This is a significant cost compared to Panama, which has no minimum capital requirement. For more details, visit our requirements page. The capital must be maintained at all times; if it falls below the threshold, the CNB may suspend or revoke the license.

Tax Treatment of Crypto in the Czech Republic

The Czech Republic has a favourable tax regime for crypto. Corporate income tax is a flat 19% on profits. Crypto-to-crypto trades are generally exempt from VAT, and capital gains from crypto held for more than 3 years are tax-free for individuals. For companies, crypto is treated as intangible assets or inventory depending on business model.

Key points:

  • No withholding tax on dividends (subject to conditions)
  • Transfer pricing rules apply for related-party transactions
  • VAT exemption for exchange and transfer services

Always consult a tax advisor. For a jurisdiction with zero corporate tax, consider Panama. Learn more on our blog. The Czech tax authorities have issued guidance on crypto taxation, which provides clarity for businesses.

Allowed Activities Under the License

A Czech CASP license permits the following activities under MiCA:

  • Exchange of crypto-assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for other crypto-assets
  • Custody and administration of crypto-assets
  • Transfer of crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • Placing of crypto-assets (underwriting)
  • Execution of orders on behalf of clients

Additional services like staking or lending may require separate authorization. The license does not cover security tokens or e-money, which fall under other regimes. For a broader scope, Panama allows all crypto activities without restriction. Compare on our Panama page.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining a crypto license in the Czech Republic involves these steps:

  1. Pre-incorporation: Register a Czech company (s.r.o.) with a registered address and director.
  2. Prepare documentation: Draft AML/KYC policies, risk assessment, business plan, and internal controls.
  3. Appoint local agents: AML officer, compliance officer, and board members (may be non-resident).
  4. Submit application: File with CNB via their electronic system, including all supporting documents and proof of capital.
  5. CNB review: The regulator assesses fit and proper tests, AML compliance, and capital adequacy. They may request additional information.
  6. Approval: Once granted, you can begin operations. You must notify CNB of any material changes.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

After licensing, firms must maintain ongoing compliance:

  • Annual AML audit and reporting to CNB
  • Quarterly transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reports
  • Capital maintenance (cannot fall below minimum)
  • Fit and proper updates for key personnel
  • Data protection under GDPR

Non-compliance can result in fines or license revocation. Consulting24 provides compliance advisory for Czech license holders. For a simpler compliance regime, Panama requires only annual renewal. Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make these errors when applying for a Czech crypto license:

  • Underestimating capital: Ensure you have the full amount in liquid assets before applying.
  • Poor AML documentation: CNB scrutinises AML policies; generic templates are rejected.
  • Ignoring fit and proper tests: Directors with criminal records or bankruptcies may be disqualified.
  • Delaying bank account opening: Start the process early to avoid delays.
  • Not planning for ongoing costs: Budget for annual compliance and audits.

What is the total cost of a crypto license in the Czech Republic in 2026?

What are the capital requirements for a Czech crypto license?

Capital is tiered under MiCA: EUR 50,000 for custody/transfer, EUR 125,000 for exchange services, and EUR 150,000 for services involving client funds. Capital must be maintained at all times.
