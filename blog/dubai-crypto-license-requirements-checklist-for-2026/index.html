What Is the Dubai Crypto License?

The Dubai crypto license, formally a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) license, is issued by the Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA) for mainland Dubai operations. It allows companies to engage in regulated virtual asset activities such as exchange, custody, transfer, and advisory services. VARA was established in 2022 and has since built a comprehensive rulebook covering issuance, trading, and settlement of virtual assets.

Unlike some jurisdictions that offer a single license for all activities, Dubai's regime is activity-based, meaning you must apply for specific categories. The license is mandatory for any business providing virtual asset services in or from Dubai (excluding the Dubai International Financial Centre, which has its own framework). As of 2026, VARA continues to refine its regulations, aligning with global standards while maintaining a pro-innovation stance.

Who Needs a Dubai Crypto License?

Any entity that offers virtual asset services in or from Dubai must obtain a VARA license. This includes:

  • Virtual asset exchanges (centralized and decentralized platforms)
  • Custody and wallet service providers
  • Broker-dealers and market makers
  • Payment and remittance services using virtual assets
  • Advisory and portfolio management services for virtual assets
  • Token issuers and ICO/STO platforms

If your business involves any of these activities and you plan to have a physical presence in Dubai, you must comply. Even if you are a foreign entity targeting Dubai residents, you may need a license depending on the nature of your services. License Types and Regulator

The regulator for Dubai crypto licenses is the Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA), operating under Dubai Law No. 4 of 2022. VARA issues licenses for specific virtual asset activities, each with its own set of requirements. The main license categories include:

License CategoryActivities Covered
Exchange LicenseOperating a virtual asset exchange, matching orders, trading platform
Custody LicenseSafekeeping and administration of virtual assets on behalf of clients
Transfer LicenseTransferring virtual assets between wallets or accounts
Advisory LicenseProviding investment advice or portfolio management for virtual assets
Issuance LicenseIssuing new virtual assets (tokens, coins) through ICOs or STOs

Each license requires a separate application and compliance with VARA's rulebook. Some activities may be bundled under a single license if they are closely related. VARA also imposes a mandatory contribution to a risk mitigation fund for certain license types.

Capital Requirement

Dubai does not have a single minimum capital requirement; instead, VARA mandates that licensees maintain adequate financial resources based on the risk profile and scale of activities. As of 2026, the typical minimum paid-up capital ranges from EUR 50,000 to EUR 150,000, depending on the license category. For example, an exchange license may require EUR 100,000, while a custody license might need EUR 150,000.

Capital must be held in a bank account in Dubai and can be in fiat or virtual assets (subject to valuation rules). VARA also requires licensees to maintain a minimum level of liquid assets to cover operational expenses for at least six months. This is similar to the EU's MiCA framework, which sets capital tiers of EUR 50,000, 125,000, or 150,000 based on service class. For a cost-effective alternative, Panama has no minimum capital requirement, only a nominal share capital of EUR 1.

Tax Treatment

Dubai offers a highly favorable tax environment for crypto businesses. The key tax features include:

  • 0% corporate income tax on profits from virtual asset activities (as of 2026, subject to general corporate tax rules for mainland companies, but virtual asset income may be exempt under specific conditions)
  • No value-added tax (VAT) on virtual asset transactions (VAT is 5% on most goods and services, but crypto transactions are often VAT-exempt)
  • No withholding tax on dividends or interest paid to non-residents
  • No capital gains tax on disposal of virtual assets

However, companies must still comply with transfer pricing rules and economic substance requirements. It is advisable to obtain a tax ruling from the Federal Tax Authority. Compared to EU jurisdictions like Lithuania, which has a 15% corporate tax and 20% capital gains tax on crypto, Dubai's tax benefits are significant. Panama also offers territorial taxation with 0% tax on offshore income, making it another attractive option.

Allowed Activities

A Dubai crypto license permits a wide range of virtual asset activities, subject to the specific license category. The allowed activities include:

  • Operating a virtual asset exchange (order book, OTC, P2P)
  • Custody and safekeeping of virtual assets
  • Transfer of virtual assets between users
  • Brokerage and market making
  • Portfolio management and advisory services
  • Token issuance (ICO, STO, IEO)
  • Virtual asset payment services

Activities that are explicitly prohibited include offering derivatives or used products without a separate license, and engaging in activities that violate anti-money laundering (AML) rules. VARA also restricts certain activities for retail investors, such as high-risk tokens. For a more permissive regime, Panama allows all crypto activities without specific restrictions, as long as the company complies with AML laws.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining a Dubai crypto license involves several stages. Here is a step-by-step overview:

  1. Pre-incorporation: Choose a legal structure (typically a Dubai mainland LLC) and reserve a trade name. Engage a local registered agent.
  2. Company setup: Incorporate the company with the Department of Economic Development (DED). Obtain initial approval from VARA.
  3. Office lease: Secure a physical office space in Dubai (flexi desk or dedicated office). VARA requires a physical presence.
  4. Compliance documentation: Prepare AML/KYC policies, risk assessment, business plan, and financial projections. Appoint a compliance officer and a Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO).
  5. Submit application: File the VARA license application with all supporting documents. Pay the application fee.
  6. VARA review: VARA conducts due diligence on shareholders, directors, and beneficial owners. This may include interviews and requests for additional information.
  7. License issuance: Upon approval, pay the annual license fee and receive the VASP license. The license is valid for one year and must be renewed annually.
  8. Post-licensing: Set up a corporate bank account, implement transaction monitoring systems, and submit periodic reports to VARA.

Banking and Payment Solutions

Opening a bank account in Dubai for a crypto business can be challenging due to perceived risks. However, several banks in Dubai are crypto-friendly, particularly for licensed VASPs. Options include:

  • Local banks: Mashreq Bank, Emirates NBD (with certain conditions)
  • International banks: Standard Chartered, HSBC (often require a minimum balance of EUR 50,000)
  • Digital banks: Zand Bank, Al Maryah Community Bank (more flexible for fintechs)

Requirements typically include a valid VARA license, a physical presence in Dubai, and a detailed business plan. Some banks may also require a minimum deposit of EUR 100,000. For payment processing, companies can integrate with crypto payment gateways like Coinbase Commerce or BitPay. In contrast, Panama offers easier banking for crypto companies, with no requirement for a local bank account and access to international payment processors.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, VARA imposes strict ongoing compliance requirements. These include:

  • AML/CFT compliance: Implement KYC procedures, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting. Appoint a compliance officer and MLRO.
  • Audit and reporting: Submit annual audited financial statements and periodic reports to VARA on transactions, risk exposure, and capital adequacy.
  • Capital maintenance: Maintain the required minimum capital at all times.
  • Risk management: Maintain a risk mitigation fund (if applicable) and have business continuity plans.
  • Consumer protection: Disclose risks to clients, handle complaints, and ensure fair treatment.

Non-compliance can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. It is advisable to engage a compliance consultant to manage these obligations. This general guidance is not legal advice; always consult with a qualified lawyer. For a less burdensome compliance regime, Panama requires only basic AML compliance and annual renewal of the license.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make several mistakes when applying for a Dubai crypto license. Avoid these pitfalls:

  • Incomplete documentation: Failing to provide a detailed business plan, risk assessment, or AML policies can delay or reject the application.
  • Underestimating costs: The total cost can exceed EUR 100,000 in the first year. Budget accordingly.
  • Ignoring local presence: VARA requires a physical office and a local manager. Virtual offices are not accepted.
  • Choosing the wrong license category: Applying for a license that does not cover all intended activities may require a separate application later.
  • Neglecting compliance: Ongoing compliance is as important as the initial application. Non-compliance can lead to license revocation.

Frequently asked questions

What are the Dubai crypto license requirements for 2026?

The requirements include a VARA license application, company incorporation in mainland Dubai, physical office space, minimum capital of EUR 50,000-150,000, AML/KYC policies, appointment of a compliance officer, and submission of a detailed business plan. The process takes 3-6 months.

How much does a Dubai crypto license cost?

The total first-year cost typically ranges from EUR 48,000 to EUR 115,000, including application fees, annual license fee, company setup, office rent, and advisory fees. Exact pricing is confirmed during a consultation.

Is a physical office required for a Dubai crypto license?

Yes, VARA requires a physical office in Dubai. Flexi desks or dedicated office spaces are acceptable. Virtual offices are not permitted. This is a key difference from Panama, which allows a virtual office.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a Dubai crypto license?

The minimum paid-up capital ranges from EUR 50,000 to EUR 150,000 depending on the license category. For example, an exchange license may require EUR 100,000, while a custody license may need EUR 150,000.

Can I apply for a Dubai crypto license remotely?

Yes, you can engage a local registered agent to handle the application process remotely. However, you will need to visit Dubai for certain steps, such as opening a bank account and signing documents. Consulting24 can coordinate the process.

What are the tax benefits of a Dubai crypto license?

Dubai offers 0% corporate income tax on virtual asset income (subject to conditions), no VAT on crypto transactions, and no capital gains tax. This makes it one of the most tax-efficient jurisdictions for crypto businesses.

How does Dubai compare to Panama for a crypto license?

Panama is faster (4-6 weeks) and cheaper (EUR 6,000 flat) with no minimum capital, while Dubai offers regulatory clarity and tax benefits but costs EUR 48,000-115,000 and takes 3-6 months. Panama is better for startups; Dubai for established firms needing a regulated hub.

What activities are allowed under a Dubai crypto license?

Allowed activities include operating a virtual asset exchange, custody, transfer, brokerage, advisory, token issuance, and payment services. Derivatives and used products require a separate license. Prohibited activities include those violating AML rules.

How long is the Dubai crypto license valid?

The license is valid for one year and must be renewed annually. Renewal requires submission of updated compliance reports, audited financial statements, and payment of the annual fee.

What happens if I operate without a Dubai crypto license?

Operating without a license is illegal and can result in fines, business closure, and potential criminal liability. VARA has enforcement powers and actively monitors the market. Always obtain the license before starting operations.
