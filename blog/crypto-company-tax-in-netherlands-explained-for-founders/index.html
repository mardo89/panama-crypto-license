What Is Crypto Company Tax in Netherlands?

The Netherlands does not have a specific 'crypto tax' law. Instead, crypto companies are subject to the general Dutch corporate income tax (CIT) regime, VAT rules, and payroll taxes. The tax treatment depends on the nature of your crypto activities: trading, mining, staking, DeFi services, or payment processing.

Key points:

  • Corporate income tax: profits from crypto activities are taxed at standard CIT rates (15% on first EUR 200,000 profit, 25.8% above that as of 2026).
  • VAT: most crypto-to-fiat exchange services are VAT-exempt under EU law, but advisory and software services may be subject to 21% VAT.
  • Payroll tax: if you employ staff, you must withhold wage tax and social security contributions.
  • Transfer pricing: transactions with related parties must be at arm's length.

The Dutch tax authority (Belastingdienst) has issued specific guidance on the classification of crypto assets, which influences tax treatment. For example, cryptocurrencies are generally treated as 'intangible assets' for CIT purposes, meaning gains are taxable and losses deductible.

License Type and Regulator for Crypto Companies in Netherlands

As of 2026, the Netherlands requires crypto service providers to register with De Nederlandsche Bank (DNB) under the Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorist Financing Act (Wwft). This is a registration regime, not a full license, but it imposes strict compliance obligations.

The registration applies to:

  • Exchanges between virtual currencies and fiat currencies.
  • Custodial wallet providers.
  • Providers of services for the transfer of virtual currencies.

Additionally, if your company operates under the EU Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA), you may need a CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) license, which is regulated by the Dutch Authority for the Financial Markets (AFM) and DNB jointly. MiCA is fully in force in 2026, so new entrants must comply with capital requirements: EUR 50,000 for simple exchange services, EUR 125,000 for custody and transfer, and EUR 150,000 for more complex services.

Capital Requirements for Crypto Companies in Netherlands

There is no specific minimum capital requirement for a Dutch BV under the Wwft registration. However, MiCA imposes capital tiers as mentioned: EUR 50,000 / 125,000 / 150,000 depending on the service class. Additionally, the DNB expects adequate financial resources to cover operational risks and compliance costs.

In practice, you should have sufficient capital to cover at least 6-12 months of operating expenses, including rent, salaries, and compliance. Many banks and payment partners also require a minimum capital of EUR 100,000 or more to open a business account for crypto companies.

Tax Treatment of Crypto Companies in Netherlands

Corporate income tax (CIT): Dutch resident companies are taxed on worldwide income. The CIT rate for 2026 is 15% on the first EUR 200,000 of taxable profit, and 25.8% on the excess. Crypto profits (trading gains, fees, mining rewards) are included in taxable profit.

VAT: Exchange of crypto for fiat is VAT-exempt under EU law. However, services like crypto consultancy, software development, and advertising are subject to 21% VAT. If you provide services to EU customers, you may need to charge VAT based on the customer's location.

Payroll tax: If you have employees, you must withhold wage tax (loonbelasting) and social security contributions. The Netherlands has a progressive wage tax rate up to 49.5% for high incomes.

Dividend withholding tax: 15% on dividends paid to shareholders, unless reduced by a tax treaty.

Tax incentives: The Netherlands offers the innovation box regime (effective 9% tax on qualifying R&D income) and the 30% ruling for expats, which can reduce tax on salary. Crypto companies engaged in blockchain development may qualify.

Allowed Activities for Crypto Companies in Netherlands

Under the Wwft registration, the following activities are allowed:

  • Exchange between virtual currencies and fiat currencies.
  • Custodial wallet services.
  • Transfer of virtual currencies (including payment services).

With a MiCA CASP license, additional activities include:

  • Execution of orders for crypto assets on behalf of clients.
  • Placing of crypto assets.
  • Receiving and transmitting orders for crypto assets.
  • Advice on crypto assets.
  • Portfolio management of crypto assets.

Activities not covered by these regimes (e.g., pure software development, mining without custody) do not require registration but may still be subject to tax and general business regulations.

Step-by-Step Process to Set Up a Crypto Company in Netherlands

  1. Business planning: Define your activities, target market, and corporate structure. Consider tax implications and choose the right legal form (usually BV).
  2. Company incorporation: Engage a Dutch notary to draft the deed of incorporation. Register with the KvK and obtain a VAT number.
  3. Open a business bank account: This is challenging for crypto companies. Consulting24 can assist with introductions to crypto-friendly banks in the Netherlands or alternative payment processors.
  4. DNB registration: Prepare AML/KYC policies, appoint a compliance officer, and submit the application. This includes a business plan, organisational structure, and background checks on directors.
  5. MiCA CASP license (if required): Submit a more detailed application to the AFM/DNB, including capital proof, risk management framework, and IT security measures.
  6. Ongoing compliance: File annual accounts, submit tax returns, and comply with AML obligations. Engage an external auditor if required.

Banking and Payment Solutions for Crypto Companies in Netherlands

Opening a bank account for a crypto company in the Netherlands is notoriously difficult. Most traditional banks (ING, ABN AMRO, Rabobank) are cautious and often refuse crypto-related businesses. However, some niche banks and payment institutions are more open, such as Bunq, Knab, and certain international banks with a Dutch presence.

Alternative solutions include:

  • EMI (Electronic Money Institution) accounts: services like PayDo, MisterTango, or SatchelPay.
  • Crypto-friendly banks in other EU countries: e.g., Bank Frick (Liechtenstein), ClearBank (UK), or Transact24.
  • Using a Lithuanian EMI if you also have a Lithuanian license.

Compliance and Trust: Staying on the Right Side of Dutch Regulators

Compliance is non-negotiable in the Netherlands. The DNB and AFM conduct regular inspections and impose fines for non-compliance. Key requirements include:

  • AML/KYC procedures: Customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious transaction reporting.
  • Appointment of a compliance officer (can be outsourced).
  • Annual audits by an external accountant.
  • Data protection under GDPR.
  • IT security standards (e.g., ISO 27001).

Common Mistakes When Setting Up a Crypto Company in Netherlands

  1. Underestimating the timeline: DNB registration can take 6 months or more. Many founders start too late.
  2. Inadequate AML policies: Dutch regulators expect detailed, operational AML procedures, not generic templates.
  3. Ignoring VAT: Failing to charge VAT on taxable services can lead to back taxes and penalties.
  4. Not securing banking early: Account opening can take months; start the process immediately after incorporation.
  5. Overlooking substance requirements: The DNB expects real economic substance in the Netherlands (office, management, employees).

Frequently asked questions

What is the corporate tax rate for crypto companies in the Netherlands?

The corporate income tax rate for 2026 is 15% on the first EUR 200,000 of taxable profit and 25.8% on the excess. Crypto profits are taxed as ordinary income.

Do I need a license to operate a crypto exchange in the Netherlands?

Yes, you must register with De Nederlandsche Bank (DNB) under the Wwft. If you provide services covered by MiCA, you may also need a CASP license from the AFM/DNB.

Is crypto trading VAT-exempt in the Netherlands?

Yes, the exchange of crypto for fiat currency is VAT-exempt under EU law. However, other services like consultancy or software development are subject to 21% VAT.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a crypto company in the Netherlands?

There is no statutory minimum capital for a BV, but MiCA requires EUR 50,000 to 150,000 depending on services. In practice, you need sufficient capital to cover operational costs.

How long does it take to get a DNB registration?

The registration process typically takes 3 to 6 months, but can be longer if the application is incomplete or complex. Consulting24 recommends starting early.

Can I open a bank account for my crypto company in the Netherlands?

It is challenging but possible. Some banks like Bunq or Knab may accept crypto companies, but requirements are strict. Consulting24 can help with introductions.

What are the ongoing compliance costs for a Dutch crypto company?

Annual compliance costs typically range from EUR 10,000 to 30,000, including AML officer fees, audits, and legal support. Exact costs depend on business size.

Is the Netherlands a good jurisdiction for crypto startups compared to Panama?

The Netherlands offers a stable EU regulatory framework but higher costs and longer timelines. Panama provides zero-tax on foreign income, faster setup, and lower costs at EUR 6,000 flat via Consulting24.

Does the Netherlands have a tax treaty with the US?

Yes, the Netherlands has a double tax treaty with the US that can reduce withholding taxes on dividends, interest, and royalties. Consult a tax advisor for specifics.

Can I outsource the compliance officer role for my Dutch crypto company?

Yes, you can outsource the compliance officer to a qualified third party, but the ultimate responsibility remains with the company's board. Consulting24 can recommend trusted providers.
