What Is the Czech Crypto License?

The Czech crypto license is a mandatory authorisation for any entity providing crypto asset services in or from the Czech Republic. As of 2026, it is fully aligned with MiCA, meaning that the license is recognised across the EU via passporting. The Czech National Bank (CNB) is the competent authority responsible for licensing and supervision. The license covers a range of activities such as custody, exchange, transfer services, and advisory. It replaces the previous registration-only regime and introduces stricter requirements on capital, governance, and AML/CFT compliance.

For founders, this means that operating without a license is illegal and can result in fines or criminal penalties. The license is a single authorisation that allows you to serve clients across the EU, making it a strategic choice for pan-European operations. However, it also comes with ongoing obligations such as regular reporting, audits, and maintaining a local presence.

Consulting24 advises that while the Czech license is strong, the timeline and costs are competitive compared to other EU hubs like Lithuania or Malta. You can read more about the Czech Republic crypto license on our dedicated page.

Who Needs a Crypto License in Czech Republic?

Any legal person (company) that intends to provide crypto asset services on a professional basis in the Czech Republic must obtain a license from the CNB. This includes both domestic and foreign entities. The key criterion is that the services are offered to clients located in the Czech Republic or elsewhere in the EU. Even if your company is registered in another EU country, you may need to passport your license or apply directly if you have a branch in the Czech Republic.

Specifically, the following entities need a license:

  • Crypto exchanges (trading platforms)
  • Wallet providers (custodial and non-custodial if they hold private keys)
  • Payment processors using crypto
  • Brokers and dealers in crypto assets
  • Advisors on crypto investments
  • Issuers of crypto assets (if not exempt under MiCA)

License Type and Regulator

The license is a single authorisation under MiCA, known as a Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) license. The regulator is the Czech National Bank (CNB), which is also the central bank and financial market supervisor. The CNB has a dedicated fintech section that handles licensing and supervision. The license is divided into classes based on the services provided, each with its own capital requirement:

Service ClassMinimum Capital (EUR)
Custody and administration of crypto assetsEUR 125,000
Operation of a trading platformEUR 150,000
Exchange of crypto assets for fiat or other cryptoEUR 50,000
Execution of orders on behalf of clientsEUR 125,000
Transfer servicesEUR 50,000
Advisory servicesEUR 50,000

The CNB also requires that the company has a physical office in the Czech Republic, local management, and adequate AML/CFT measures. The license is valid indefinitely but subject to ongoing supervision.

Capital Requirement

As per MiCA, the minimum capital requirement depends on the type of services offered. For the Czech Republic, the CNB applies the following tiers:

  • EUR 50,000 for exchange services, transfer services, and advisory
  • EUR 125,000 for custody, order execution, and certain other services
  • EUR 150,000 for operating a trading platform

If your company provides multiple services, the highest capital requirement applies. The capital must be fully paid up in cash or liquid assets and maintained at all times. Additionally, the company must have a strong capital adequacy framework to cover operational risks. This is a significant commitment compared to non-EU jurisdictions like Panama, which has no minimum capital requirement for crypto companies. For many startups, the capital requirement can be a barrier, but it also signals credibility to clients and partners.

Tax Treatment

The Czech Republic has a standard corporate income tax rate of 19% (as of 2026). Crypto transactions are treated similarly to other financial instruments for tax purposes. VAT is generally not applicable on crypto-to-crypto trades, but crypto-to-fiat exchanges may be subject to VAT. Capital gains from crypto are taxed as income, and losses can be offset. There is no specific crypto tax regime, but the CNB and Ministry of Finance have issued guidelines. For companies holding crypto as inventory, mark-to-market accounting may apply. Consulting24 recommends working with a local tax advisor to ensure compliance. Compared to Panama, which has no capital gains tax and territorial taxation, the Czech tax burden is higher. However, the Czech license provides access to the EU market, which can outweigh the tax costs for many businesses.

Allowed Activities Under the License

The Czech CASP license permits a wide range of activities, all subject to the conditions of the license. These include:

  • Providing custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Operating a crypto trading platform (order book or automated matching)
  • Exchanging crypto assets for fiat currency or other crypto assets
  • Executing orders for clients
  • Providing transfer services for crypto assets
  • Providing advice on crypto assets
  • Underwriting and placing crypto assets (subject to additional rules)

Activities not covered include issuing crypto assets (which may require a separate prospectus or be exempt under MiCA) and decentralised finance (DeFi) protocols that are truly non-custodial. The CNB has indicated that DeFi activities may fall outside the scope of licensing if they are fully automated and non-custodial, but this is assessed on a case-by-case basis. Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a Czech crypto license involves several stages. Consulting24 can coordinate the entire process, but here is a general outline:

  1. Pre-application assessment: Determine the exact services and capital requirement. Engage a local agent (Consulting24 can recommend).
  2. Company incorporation: Set up a Czech limited liability company (s.r.o.) with a registered office and local directors. Minimum share capital is CZK 1 (approx. EUR 0.04) but must meet MiCA capital requirements.
  3. AML/CFT program: Develop policies, risk assessment, and appoint an AML officer. Implement KYC/AML software.
  4. Prepare application documents: Business plan, financial projections, organisational structure, capital proof, and governance documents.
  5. Submit application to CNB: The CNB has up to 3 months to review, but the process often takes 6-12 months due to back-and-forth.
  6. On-site inspection: The CNB may conduct an on-site visit to verify premises and systems.
  7. Approval and license issuance: Once approved, the company is added to the CNB's register of CASPs.
  8. Ongoing compliance: Submit regular reports, undergo audits, and maintain capital.

Banking and Payment Services

Opening a bank account for a crypto company in the Czech Republic can be challenging, as many traditional banks are cautious. However, the CNB's licensing provides credibility, and several banks are open to licensed CASPs. Options include:

  • Local banks (e.g., CSOB, Komercni Banka) - require full license and local presence.
  • Neobanks and fintechs (e.g., Revolut, Wise) - may offer accounts but with restrictions.
  • Payment institutions licensed in the EU - can provide fiat on/off ramps.

Benefits of a Czech Crypto License

Obtaining a crypto license in the Czech Republic offers several advantages:

  • EU passporting: Once licensed, you can serve clients across all EU member states without additional licenses.
  • Regulatory clarity: MiCA provides a clear legal framework, reducing uncertainty.
  • Reputation: A CNB license signals trustworthiness to clients, investors, and partners.
  • Access to banking: Licensed firms find it easier to open bank accounts and obtain payment services.
  • Skilled workforce: Czech Republic has a strong tech and fintech talent pool.
  • Cost-effective compared to other EU hubs: The total cost is lower than in Malta, Switzerland, or Cyprus.

Compliance and Trust

Compliance is at the heart of the Czech crypto license. The CNB requires a strong AML/CFT framework, including:

  • Customer due diligence (CDD) and enhanced due diligence (EDD) for high-risk clients
  • Transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting
  • Record keeping for at least 5 years
  • Appointment of a local AML officer
  • Regular independent audits

Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. Consulting24 provides ongoing compliance support to ensure you meet all obligations. Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make these mistakes when applying for a Czech crypto license:

  • Underestimating the timeline: The process can take 6-12 months; plan accordingly.
  • Insufficient capital: Ensure the capital is fully paid up and maintained.
  • Poor AML/CFT documentation: The CNB scrutinises policies; generic templates are not enough.
  • Lack of local presence: A physical office and local management are mandatory.
  • Ignoring tax obligations: Crypto transactions are taxable; failing to report can lead to penalties.
  • Choosing the wrong service class: Over- or under-licensing can cause delays or compliance issues.

Frequently asked questions

How long does it take to get a Czech crypto license?

The typical timeline is 6 to 12 months from application submission to approval. The Czech National Bank has up to 3 months to review, but additional requests for information can extend the process. Consulting24 can help expedite by ensuring a complete application.

What is the minimum capital for a Czech crypto license?

The minimum capital depends on the services: EUR 50,000 for exchange/transfer/advisory, EUR 125,000 for custody/order execution, and EUR 150,000 for operating a trading platform. The highest requirement applies if you offer multiple services.

Can I passport the Czech license to other EU countries?

Yes, under MiCA, a Czech CASP license is valid across the entire EU through passporting. You can serve clients in any member state without additional licensing, though you may need to notify the host regulator.

Is a physical office required in the Czech Republic?

Yes, you must have a registered office and a physical presence in the Czech Republic. The CNB may conduct on-site inspections. Consulting24 can help arrange office space and local representation.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations?

Ongoing obligations include AML/CFT compliance, transaction monitoring, regular reporting to the CNB, annual audits, and maintaining minimum capital. You must also update policies as regulations change.

Can I operate a crypto exchange without a license?

No, operating a crypto exchange in or from the Czech Republic without a license is illegal and can result in fines or criminal charges. All CASPs must be licensed by the CNB.

How does the Czech license compare to Panama?

Panama has no specific crypto license and no capital requirement, with a flat company setup cost of EUR 6,000. However, it does not offer EU passporting. The Czech license is more expensive and time-consuming but provides EU market access.

What is the corporate tax rate for crypto companies in Czech Republic?

The standard corporate income tax rate is 19% as of 2026. Crypto transactions are taxed as income, and capital gains are included. VAT may apply to crypto-to-fiat exchanges. Consult a tax advisor for specifics.
