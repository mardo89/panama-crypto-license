What Is Panama Crypto Company Registration?

Panama crypto company registration refers to the process of incorporating a Panamanian Sociedad Anonima (SA) - a standard corporation - to conduct crypto-related business activities. Unlike many jurisdictions, Panama does not have a dedicated crypto license or regulatory framework for digital assets as of 2026. Instead, crypto businesses operate under general commercial law, which makes registration simpler and faster than in regulated regimes like Estonia or Lithuania.

Key characteristics of a Panama crypto company:

  • No minimum capital requirement (typically USD 10,000 but not paid in).
  • No requirement for local directors or shareholders (nominee services available).
  • Taxation: territorial - only income sourced within Panama is taxed (0% on foreign-source income, including crypto trading profits).
  • Bearer shares are not allowed; registered shares only.

This structure is ideal for founders who want a legal entity to hold assets, process transactions, or operate a global crypto business without heavy regulatory overhead. However, it is not a substitute for a Lithuania crypto license or Estonia crypto license if you need to serve EU customers under MiCA. For a comparison of jurisdictions, see our jurisdictions page.

License Type & Regulator

Panama does not have a specific crypto license. The regulatory body for financial activities is the Superintendencia de Bancos de Panamá (SBP), but crypto businesses are not currently regulated by the SBP unless they involve traditional banking. Instead, a Panama crypto company operates under the general corporate law, regulated by the Public Registry (Registro Público) for incorporation and the Ministry of Commerce for commercial licenses.

There is no separate crypto license to obtain. The company is simply registered as a Sociedad Anonima (SA) with a corporate purpose that includes crypto activities, such as:

  • Digital asset trading, exchange, and custody.
  • Blockchain development and consulting.
  • Cryptocurrency mining and staking.
  • Issuance of tokens (subject to securities laws if applicable).

Because there is no dedicated regulator, due diligence on your business partners and banking relationships is critical. Many payment providers and banks will require a compliance review. Capital Requirement

Panama has no minimum paid-up capital requirement for a Sociedad Anonima. The standard authorized capital is USD 10,000, divided into 100 shares of USD 100 each, but this amount does not need to be deposited in a bank account. You can set a higher or lower authorized capital as needed. The capital is purely a nominal figure on the incorporation documents.

This is a significant advantage over EU jurisdictions where MiCA imposes capital tiers: EUR 50,000 for certain services, EUR 125,000 for custody, and EUR 150,000 for exchange and trading. Panama’s zero capital requirement makes it one of the most accessible jurisdictions for bootstrapped startups. However, if you need to build trust with partners, you may choose to show a higher capital. For more on capital requirements in other countries, see our requirements page.

Tax Treatment

Panama operates a territorial tax system. This means that only income derived from sources within Panama is subject to Panamanian income tax. Foreign-source income - including profits from cryptocurrency trading, exchange fees, and capital gains from crypto assets - is taxed at 0%. There is also no capital gains tax, no VAT on services (only on goods), and no withholding tax on dividends paid to non-residents.

Key tax points for crypto companies:

  • Corporate income tax: 25% on Panama-source income only.
  • Foreign-source crypto income: 0% tax.
  • No CFC rules (controlled foreign corporation) - but check your home country rules.
  • No exchange controls - free movement of capital.
  • Annual license fee (patente) is minimal (approx. USD 300-600).

Important: You must still comply with tax reporting in your country of residence. Panama does not automatically exchange tax information with all countries (it is not a signatory to the CRS with all jurisdictions). However, as of 2026, Panama has committed to the OECD’s BEPS framework. Always consult a tax advisor. For a comparison with EU tax regimes, see vs Lithuania.

Allowed Activities

A Panama crypto company can engage in a wide range of crypto-related activities, as long as they do not violate Panamanian law (e.g., money laundering, fraud). Typical allowed activities include:

  • Cryptocurrency exchange (centralized or peer-to-peer).
  • Crypto custody and wallet services.
  • Blockchain development and consulting.
  • Token issuance (subject to securities laws if tokens are deemed securities).
  • Mining and staking operations.
  • Payment processing for crypto merchants.
  • DeFi protocol operations.

Activities that may require additional licenses: if you engage in traditional banking (taking deposits), you need a banking license from the SBP. If you issue securities (e.g., STOs), you may need approval from the Superintendencia del Mercado de Valores (SMV). For most crypto businesses, the SA structure suffices. Banking & Payments

Opening a bank account or payment provider account for a Panama crypto company can be challenging due to AML concerns. Panama has a well-developed banking sector, but traditional banks often shy away from crypto. However, there are options:

  • EMI accounts (Electronic Money Institutions) - e.g., accounts in the UK, Lithuania, or other EU jurisdictions. These are often crypto-friendly.
  • Panamanian banks - some banks accept crypto companies if they have a clear compliance framework and a physical presence. Consulting24 can introduce you to banks we work with.
  • Payment processors like Stripe, though they may have restrictions on crypto businesses.

Compliance & Trust

While Panama offers a light regulatory touch, you must still comply with international AML standards to maintain banking relationships and avoid legal issues. Key compliance steps:

  • Conduct KYC on your customers (know your customer).
  • Implement AML policies and procedures.
  • Register with the Panamanian Financial Analysis Unit (UAF) if you engage in certain financial activities (though crypto is not yet explicitly covered).
  • Keep proper accounting records.
  • File annual corporate taxes (even if zero tax due).

Common Mistakes

Avoid these pitfalls when registering a Panama crypto company:

  • Not understanding territorial tax: you still need to report income in your home country. Panama’s 0% tax on foreign income does not exempt you from taxes elsewhere.
  • Using bearer shares: not allowed since 2015. Use registered shares only.
  • Ignoring substance requirements: if you are tax resident in another country, you may need substance in Panama to avoid being seen as a shell company.
  • Choosing the wrong bank: not all banks accept crypto. Plan ahead.
  • Delaying compliance: even without a license, you need AML policies to open accounts.
