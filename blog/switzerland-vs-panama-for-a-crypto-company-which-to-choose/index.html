Overview: Switzerland vs Panama for a Crypto Company

Switzerland and Panama represent two ends of the spectrum for crypto company formation. Switzerland, particularly the canton of Zug (Crypto Valley), has a well-defined legal framework for blockchain businesses. The Swiss Financial Market Supervisory Authority (FINMA) regulates crypto activities under the Banking Act and the Anti-Money Laundering Act. Panama, on the other hand, has no specific crypto license but allows crypto businesses to operate under general corporate and AML laws. It offers a flat EUR 6,000 company setup cost, no capital gains tax, and no minimum capital requirement.

For founders, the choice often comes down to reputation versus cost. Switzerland offers a gold-standard regulatory environment, but with high costs and complexity. Panama offers speed and affordability, but with less regulatory certainty and limited banking options. This article explores each aspect in depth.

Who Needs a Crypto License in Switzerland vs Panama?

In Switzerland, any company that provides services related to cryptocurrencies, such as exchange, custody, or trading, may need a FINMA license or a simple registration as a financial intermediary. The threshold depends on the volume and nature of activities. Panama does not have a dedicated crypto license, but companies must comply with AML regulations if they engage in money transmission or exchange services. Many crypto businesses in Panama operate under a general corporate structure, with legal opinions confirming no specific license is required.

Typically, the following entities need regulatory attention:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (centralized and decentralized)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Payment processors using crypto
  • Token issuers (ICOs, STOs)
  • Mining pool operators

Switzerland imposes stricter rules on custodians and exchanges, while Panama is more permissive but requires strong internal AML controls.

License Type & Regulator

Switzerland: The primary regulator is FINMA. Crypto companies may need a banking license if they accept public deposits, or a securities dealer license if they trade tokens classified as securities. For most crypto exchanges and custodians, a Switzerland crypto license is not a single license but a combination of authorizations under the Financial Institutions Act (FinIA) and the Anti-Money Laundering Act (AMLA). In practice, many firms register as a self-regulatory organization (SRO) member for AML compliance.

Panama: There is no specific crypto license. The regulator is the Superintendencia de Bancos de Panamá (SBP) for banking, and the Unidad de Análisis Financiero (UAF) for AML. Crypto companies are treated as regular corporations and must register with the Public Registry and obtain a business license from the municipality. Capital Requirement

Switzerland: If you need a banking license, minimum capital is CHF 100,000 (approx. EUR 100,000). For a simple AML registration, there is no formal minimum capital, but FINMA expects sufficient financial resources for the business. In practice, many firms maintain at least CHF 50,000 in equity.

Panama: There is no minimum capital requirement for a standard corporation. You can incorporate with USD 10,000 authorized capital, but no paid-up capital is needed. This makes Panama highly accessible for startups. However, banks may require a minimum deposit to open an account, typically USD 5,000 - 25,000.

Tax Treatment

Switzerland: Corporate income tax rates vary by canton, typically 12-18% on profits. Capital gains from crypto trading are generally tax-free for private individuals, but professional traders may be subject to income tax. There is no withholding tax on dividends for Swiss companies. VAT is 7.7% on most services, but crypto transactions may be exempt.

Panama: Territorial taxation: only income sourced in Panama is taxed. Offshore income (including most crypto trading) is tax-free. No capital gains tax, no VAT, and no withholding tax on dividends. This makes Panama extremely attractive for crypto companies that earn revenue from non-Panamanian clients. The only tax is a 4.5% dividend tax if profits are distributed from Panama-sourced income.

Allowed Activities

Switzerland: Most crypto activities are allowed, but regulated. You can operate a crypto exchange, custody service, payment processor, or token issuer. However, activities that involve accepting public deposits (e.g., lending) require a banking license. ICOs are allowed but subject to FINMA guidance. Derivatives trading requires a securities dealer license.

Panama: All crypto activities are permitted except those that require a banking license (e.g., taking deposits from the public). You can operate an exchange, wallet, payment processor, mining, or DeFi platform. Panama has no specific laws prohibiting crypto, but you must comply with AML obligations. The government has issued a law recognizing digital assets as a means of payment, but it is not fully implemented.

Banking & Payments

Switzerland: Banking is relatively easier than in many EU countries. Several banks, including SEBA Bank, Sygnum, and some cantonal banks, accept crypto companies. However, they require a thorough due diligence process and may ask for a minimum deposit of CHF 100,000 or more. Payment processors like UBS and PostFinance also serve crypto firms.

Panama: Banking is more challenging. Traditional Panamanian banks are cautious of crypto. However, international banks and some local banks accept crypto companies with proper documentation. Consulting24 can introduce you to banks that have worked with crypto firms. Alternatively, you can use payment processors like AirTM or crypto-friendly neobanks.

Benefits of Each Jurisdiction

Switzerland:

  • High reputation and regulatory clarity
  • Access to a mature crypto ecosystem (Crypto Valley)
  • Stable legal system and political neutrality
  • Favorable tax treatment for capital gains
  • Strong investor confidence

Panama:

  • Extremely low setup cost (EUR 6,000 flat)
  • No minimum capital
  • Territorial tax system (no tax on offshore income)
  • Fast incorporation (2-4 weeks)
  • No specific crypto license required

For many startups, Panama offers a faster and cheaper path to market. For established firms seeking prestige and regulatory certainty, Switzerland is a strong choice.

Compliance & Trust

Switzerland: FINMA is a respected regulator. Compliance is rigorous: you need AML policies, KYC procedures, and regular audits. The cost of compliance is high, but it builds trust with partners and customers. Many exchanges and custodians choose Switzerland to signal quality.

Panama: Compliance is lighter but still required. You must register with the UAF if you engage in money transmission. Many crypto companies operate under a legal opinion that no license is needed, but this carries risk. To mitigate, we recommend implementing voluntary AML/KYC procedures. Panama's reputation has improved in recent years, but it is not as strong as Switzerland's.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Switzerland: Underestimating the cost and time for FINMA authorization. Many startups run out of cash before approval. Also, failing to properly classify tokens under FINMA guidelines can lead to rejection.
  • Panama: Assuming no compliance is needed. Even without a license, you must follow AML laws. Another mistake is using Panama only for tax evasion, which is illegal. Ensure your business has substance (local director, office, bank account).
  • Both: Neglecting banking. In both jurisdictions, opening a bank account can be the hardest part. Start the process early.

Alternatives and Comparisons

Besides Switzerland and Panama, other popular jurisdictions include:

  • Estonia: Offers a crypto license with a digital nomad-friendly environment. Cost is around EUR 3,000 - 5,000, but requires a local director and physical presence. Capital requirement is EUR 12,000 (for exchange) or EUR 100,000 (for wallet).
  • Lithuania: A fast-growing hub with a crypto license under the AML framework. No minimum capital for some activities, but requires a local presence. Cost is around EUR 2,500 - 5,000.
  • Dubai: VARA regulates crypto in the UAE. High costs (EUR 50,000+) but zero tax on personal income. Consulting24 does not provide this license; we offer comparison-only guidance.

For many crypto founders, Panama is the better choice because of its low cost and tax efficiency.
