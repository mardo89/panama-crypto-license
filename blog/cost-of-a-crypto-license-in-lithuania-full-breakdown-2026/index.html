What Is the Cost of a Crypto License in Lithuania?

The cost of a crypto license in Lithuania in 2026 includes several components: state fees, minimum capital, legal and compliance setup, ongoing annual costs, and optional services like banking assistance. Under MiCA, the license is officially a CASP (Crypto-Asset Service Provider) authorization issued by the Bank of Lithuania. The total first-year cost typically ranges between EUR 25,000 and EUR 45,000, depending on the scope of services and complexity of your business model.

This figure is significantly lower than in countries like Malta or Switzerland, but higher than the flat EUR 6,000 for a Panama license. However, Lithuania offers EU passporting rights, which Panama cannot provide. Below we dissect each cost element in detail.

To put it in perspective, a typical exchange startup with custody and trading services will incur around EUR 35,000 in first-year costs (excluding the capital requirement). This includes legal drafting of AML policies, business plan development, and initial compliance setup. Ongoing annual costs for compliance, audit, and registered office add another EUR 15,000 to EUR 25,000. For a detailed cost comparison across jurisdictions, see our cost overview.

Who Needs a Crypto License in Lithuania?

Any business offering crypto-asset services to residents of Lithuania or other EU member states must hold a CASP license. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto)
  • Wallet providers (custodial and non-custodial)
  • Brokerage services
  • Automated market makers and liquidity providers
  • Token issuance platforms (if acting as an intermediary)
  • OTC desks and payment processors handling crypto

If you are a non-EU company targeting European clients, Lithuania is a popular entry point. For a comparison of requirements across jurisdictions, see our jurisdictions overview. Even if your business is based outside the EU, if you actively solicit EU clients, you need a license. The Bank of Lithuania has been proactive in enforcing this, issuing fines to unlicensed entities.

License Type and Regulator

The license is a Crypto-Asset Service Provider (CASP) authorization under the Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) regulation, which is fully in force in 2026. The competent authority is the Bank of Lithuania (Lietuvos bankas). The license covers one or more of the following service classes:

  • Class 1: Custody and administration of crypto-assets (capital: EUR 125,000)
  • Class 2: Operation of a trading platform (capital: EUR 150,000)
  • Class 3: Exchange of crypto-assets for fiat currency (capital: EUR 125,000)
  • Class 4: Exchange of crypto-assets for other crypto-assets (capital: EUR 125,000)
  • Class 5: Execution of orders on behalf of clients (capital: EUR 125,000)
  • Class 6: Placement of crypto-assets (capital: EUR 125,000)
  • Class 7: Reception and transmission of orders (capital: EUR 50,000)
  • Class 8: Advice on crypto-assets (capital: EUR 50,000)

The capital requirement is determined by the highest class applied for. For example, if you apply for Class 2, you need EUR 150,000 regardless of other classes. This tiered system aligns with MiCA's risk-based approach. The Bank of Lithuania is known for thorough but fair assessments, and it maintains a public register of all licensed CASPs.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital for a CASP in Lithuania is tiered by service class as detailed above. For most exchange and wallet services, the requirement is EUR 125,000. If you operate a trading platform (order book), the requirement is EUR 150,000. This capital must be fully paid up in cash and held in a bank account in Lithuania. It can be used for operational expenses, but the company must maintain the capital at all times.

Compared to the flat EUR 6,000 for a Panama license, Lithuania's capital requirement is substantial. However, EU passporting justifies the investment for businesses targeting the European market. The capital serves as a buffer for client protection and operational stability. Some founders choose to raise venture capital specifically to meet this requirement. Tax Treatment

Lithuania offers a competitive corporate tax rate of 15% (reduced to 0% for small companies with less than EUR 300,000 revenue and fewer than 10 employees in their first year, then 5% on distributed profits). Crypto-to-crypto trades are generally not subject to VAT. Capital gains from crypto are treated as ordinary income and taxed at the corporate rate.

For individuals, crypto gains are taxed at 15% if held for less than 3 years, and 0% thereafter. This makes Lithuania attractive for both companies and founders. Always consult a local tax advisor for your specific situation. The tax environment is stable and predictable, which is a key advantage over jurisdictions with ambiguous tax treatment. Allowed Activities Under the License

A Lithuanian CASP license permits the following activities within the EU:

  • Operating a crypto exchange (fiat/crypto and crypto/crypto)
  • Providing custodial wallet services
  • Brokerage and order execution
  • Receiving and transmitting orders
  • Advising on crypto-assets
  • Placement of crypto-assets (underwriting)

Activities not covered include operating a crypto ATM (requires separate registration) and issuing stablecoins (subject to e-money regulations). The license does not permit dealing in securities tokens unless you also hold a MiFID license. If your business model includes token issuance or DeFi services, you may need additional authorizations. The Bank of Lithuania provides guidance on scope, but it is wise to engage a legal advisor early.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Pre-application preparation: Draft a detailed business plan, AML/KYC policies, risk assessment, and internal procedures. Engage a local compliance officer.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a private limited liability company (UAB) in Lithuania with the required share capital. Obtain a registered address and appoint directors.
  3. Submit application: File the application with the Bank of Lithuania via their electronic system, including all supporting documents and the state fee payment.
  4. Review and queries: The regulator reviews the application and may request clarifications or additional documents. Respond promptly to avoid delays.
  5. On-site inspection (if required): The Bank of Lithuania may conduct an on-site visit to verify your physical presence and compliance readiness.
  6. License issuance: Upon approval, you receive a CASP license and are added to the public register.
  7. Post-licensing: Submit ongoing reports (AML, financial, transaction monitoring) and maintain compliance.

For a detailed guide, see our application process page. Each step can take 2-4 weeks, so planning is essential. We recommend starting the compliance documentation well before incorporation.

Banking and Payment Services

Opening a business bank account in Lithuania for a crypto company can be challenging. Major banks like Swedbank and SEB are cautious. Some founders opt for EMI accounts from providers like Revolut or Paysera, which offer faster onboarding but limited transaction volumes. If you need high-volume fiat processing, a traditional bank account is preferable. The Bank of Lithuania requires that the licensed entity have a bank account in Lithuania for capital maintenance. Benefits of a Lithuanian Crypto License

  • EU passporting: Serve clients across all 27 EU member states without additional licenses.
  • Regulatory clarity: MiCA provides a clear legal framework, reducing uncertainty.
  • Competitive tax regime: Low corporate tax and favorable treatment of crypto gains.
  • Fast processing: The Bank of Lithuania is known for efficient handling compared to other EU regulators.
  • Access to SEPA: Easy euro payments and integration with European banking.
  • Reputation: A Lithuanian license is widely recognized and respected by partners and investors.

These benefits make Lithuania one of the top choices for crypto businesses in Europe. However, the capital requirement can be a barrier for early-stage startups. For those, the Panama license offers a lower-cost entry point.

Maintaining a Lithuanian license requires ongoing compliance: annual AML audits, transaction monitoring, and reporting to the Bank of Lithuania. Non-compliance can lead to fines or revocation. The Bank of Lithuania conducts periodic reviews and may request data on suspicious transactions. Having a strong compliance framework from day one is essential. Many licensees hire a dedicated compliance officer or use a third-party service. The cost of compliance is a significant ongoing expense, but it protects your license and reputation.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Underestimating capital requirements: Ensure you have the full capital ready before applying.
  • Incomplete AML policies: The Bank of Lithuania scrutinizes AML procedures heavily.
  • Ignoring local presence: You must have a physical office and local compliance officer.
  • Delayed responses: Slow replies to regulator queries can extend the timeline.
  • Choosing the wrong service class: Applying for a class that doesn't match your business model can cause rejection.
  • Neglecting ongoing reporting: Failure to submit annual reports can lead to license suspension.

Frequently asked questions

What is the total cost of a crypto license in Lithuania in 2026?

The total first-year cost ranges from EUR 25,000 to EUR 45,000, excluding the minimum capital requirement of EUR 125,000 to EUR 150,000. This includes state fees, legal setup, compliance, and ongoing annual costs. For a fixed-fee alternative, Panama costs EUR 6,000 flat.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Lithuania?

The application process typically takes 3 to 6 months from submission to approval. The Bank of Lithuania has a statutory 3-month review period, but delays can occur if additional information is requested. Preparation beforehand can take 4-8 weeks.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a Lithuanian CASP license?

Under MiCA, the minimum capital is tiered: EUR 50,000 for advisory and order transmission services, EUR 125,000 for most exchange and custody services, and EUR 150,000 for operating a trading platform. The requirement is based on the highest service class applied for.

Can I use a Lithuanian license to serve clients in other EU countries?

Yes, a Lithuanian CASP license allows you to passport your services across all 27 EU member states without needing additional licenses. This is a key advantage over non-EU jurisdictions like Panama.

What are the tax benefits of a Lithuanian crypto license?

Lithuania offers a corporate tax rate of 15%, reduced to 0% for small companies in their first year (under EUR 300,000 revenue and fewer than 10 employees). Crypto-to-crypto trades are VAT-exempt, and individual crypto gains are tax-free after 3 years.

Is a physical office required in Lithuania?

Yes, you must have a registered office address in Lithuania. This can be a serviced office provided by a registered agent. A physical presence is required for regulatory inspections and compliance.

What activities are not allowed under a Lithuanian CASP license?

The license does not cover operating crypto ATMs (separate registration needed), issuing stablecoins (requires e-money license), or dealing in security tokens (requires MiFID license). It also does not permit unregistered DeFi protocols.

How does Lithuania compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Lithuania offers EU passporting but has higher costs (EUR 25,000-45,000 plus EUR 125,000 capital) and longer timelines (3-6 months). Panama costs EUR 6,000 flat, no capital requirement, and setup in 4-6 weeks, but no EU passporting.

What ongoing compliance is required after obtaining the license?

You must submit annual AML audits, transaction monitoring reports, and financial statements to the Bank of Lithuania. A local compliance officer is mandatory. Non-compliance can result in fines or license revocation.
