What Is a Crypto License in Mauritius and Panama?

A crypto license allows a company to legally operate virtual asset services such as exchange, custody, or payment processing. In Mauritius, the Financial Services Commission (FSC) issues licenses under the Virtual Asset and Initial Token Offering Services Act 2021 (VAITOS). Panama, by contrast, does not have a dedicated crypto licensing law as of 2026. Instead, crypto businesses operate under general corporate and anti-money laundering (AML) regulations, often using a Panama foundation or corporation.

Key differences:

  • Mauritius: Formal license with regulatory oversight, mandatory AML/CFT compliance, and capital requirements.
  • Panama: No specific crypto license; businesses rely on existing legal structures and voluntary compliance.
  • Both jurisdictions offer tax advantages, but Panama has no corporate income tax on offshore income.

For founders seeking regulatory certainty, Mauritius provides a clear framework. For those prioritizing low cost and simplicity, Panama is attractive. Consulting24 can help you set up in Panama directly or coordinate Mauritius applications.

Who Needs a Crypto License in These Jurisdictions?

Any entity offering virtual asset services to the public needs a license in Mauritius. This includes exchanges, wallet providers, custodians, and token issuers. Panama does not require a specific license, but businesses that engage in money transmission or hold customer funds may need to register with the Superintendencia de Bancos de Panama or comply with AML laws.

Typical clients include:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralized and decentralized)
  • OTC desks
  • Wallet and custody providers
  • Token issuers and ICO organizers
  • Payment processors using crypto

License Type and Regulator

Mauritius: The Financial Services Commission (FSC) issues a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) license under VAITOS. There is one license type covering all virtual asset activities. The FSC is a well-respected regulator with a risk-based approach.

Panama: No dedicated crypto regulator. The Superintendencia de Bancos oversees banking, and the Superintendencia de Valores regulates securities. Crypto businesses operate under general corporate law. However, a new crypto bill (Ley de Criptoactivos) has been proposed but not yet enacted as of 2026.

Capital Requirement

Mauritius: The FSC requires a minimum capital of MUR 2 million (approx. EUR 45,000) for VASP licenses. This must be maintained as paid-up capital. Higher amounts may be required based on business volume.

Panama: No minimum capital requirement for a standard corporation. You can set up with a nominal capital of USD 10,000 (EUR 9,000) or less. However, banks may require a minimum deposit to open an account.

Capital comparison:

  • Mauritius: EUR 45,000 minimum (strict)
  • Panama: No minimum (flexible)

For founders with limited capital, Panama is more accessible. If you need a regulated license, Mauritius may be worth the investment. Consulting24 can advise on capital planning for both routes.

Tax Treatment

Both jurisdictions offer favorable tax regimes, but with differences.

Mauritius: Corporate income tax is 15% on worldwide income, but foreign-source income is generally exempt. No capital gains tax. VAT at 15% applies to local services. A partial exemption regime for offshore companies may reduce effective rate to 3-5%.

Panama: Territorial taxation: only income sourced in Panama is taxed. Offshore income (including crypto trading) is tax-free. No capital gains tax, no VAT on services. Corporate income tax is 25% on local income, but most crypto businesses can structure to have zero tax.

Allowed Activities

Mauritius: The VASP license allows:

  • Exchange between virtual assets and fiat
  • Exchange between virtual assets
  • Transfer of virtual assets
  • Custody and administration of virtual assets
  • Participation in ICOs

Panama: No restrictions on crypto activities as long as they do not violate AML laws. You can run an exchange, wallet, mining pool, DeFi protocol, or NFT marketplace. However, if you hold customer funds, you may need a money transmitter license.

Mauritius is more restrictive and requires compliance with the FSC's rules. Panama offers more freedom but less legal protection. For high-risk activities like DeFi, Panama may be preferable. Consulting24 can help you structure your business to fit either jurisdiction.

Step-by-Step Process

Mauritius:

  1. Incorporate a company with a registered agent.
  2. Prepare a business plan, AML policy, and risk assessment.
  3. Submit application to FSC with required documents.
  4. Pay application fee and undergo due diligence.
  5. Receive license (3-6 months).
  6. Appoint a local compliance officer and auditor.

Panama:

  1. Choose a company type (corporation or foundation).
  2. Register with the Public Registry via a law firm.
  3. Obtain a digital signature and tax ID (RUC).
  4. Open a bank account (may require physical presence).
  5. Start operations immediately; no license needed.

Banking and Payments

Banking is a major challenge for crypto companies in both jurisdictions.

Mauritius: Local banks are cautious but some accept VASP license holders. You may need to present your license and compliance framework. International banks like Standard Bank and Absa operate in Mauritius. Alternative payment processors like EMI accounts are available.

Panama: Banking is more accessible but still requires due diligence. Many international banks (e.g., Banco General, Towerbank) accept crypto companies if they have a clean reputation. Panama also has a growing fintech ecosystem with digital banks.

Tips:

  • Prepare a thorough AML/KYC policy.
  • Provide proof of business model and source of funds.
  • Consider multi-currency accounts for fiat on/off ramps.

  • Double taxation treaties with many countries.

Panama benefits:

  • Low cost and fast setup.
  • No tax on offshore income.
  • No license requirement (less bureaucracy).
  • Stable USD-based economy.

Compliance and Trust

Compliance is non-negotiable in both jurisdictions, though the level differs.

Mauritius: FSC requires a strong AML/CFT program, including customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting. You must appoint a compliance officer and undergo annual audits. Non-compliance can lead to fines or license revocation.

Panama: No specific crypto compliance requirements, but general AML laws apply if you handle funds. You should still implement voluntary KYC/AML to avoid legal risks and bank account closures. Panama has a reputation for lax enforcement, but international pressure is increasing.

  • Not considering target market: If you serve EU clients, Mauritius may not comply with MiCA.
  • Using unregistered agents: Always work with reputable local partners.
