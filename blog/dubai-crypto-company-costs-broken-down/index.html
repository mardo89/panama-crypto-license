What Are Dubai Crypto Company Costs?

Dubai crypto company costs include government application fees, license fees, regulatory capital, office space, and professional service fees. In 2026, the total for a VARA license typically ranges from USD 50,000 to over USD 150,000 depending on the license category and service type. This is significantly higher than the EUR 6,000 flat fee for a Panama crypto company. However, Dubai offers a regulated environment with a clear legal framework and tax advantages.

Costs vary based on whether you apply for a full license or a preparatory license, and whether you operate in the Dubai International Financial Centre (DIFC) or in mainland Dubai under VARA. For example, a full exchange license may cost USD 150,000 in application and first-year fees, while a preparatory license might be around USD 30,000. Who Needs a Dubai Crypto License?

Any entity providing virtual asset services in or from Dubai must be licensed by VARA. This includes:

  • Exchanges (spot, derivatives, OTC)
  • Custody and wallet providers
  • Payment and remittance services using virtual assets
  • Token issuance and ICO/STO platforms
  • DeFi protocols if they have a central operator in Dubai

License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA), established under Dubai Law No. 4 of 2022. VARA offers several license categories:

  • Preparatory License: Allows a company to establish a presence and prepare for full operations (cost around USD 30,000-50,000).
  • Full License: For operating a virtual asset service. Sub-categories include Exchange, Custody, Payment, and Advisory.
  • DIFC License: For firms operating within the Dubai International Financial Centre, regulated by the DFSA. This is separate from VARA.

Most founders opt for a VARA full license. The application process is rigorous and includes a business plan, compliance framework, and fit-and-proper tests for directors. For instance, an exchange license requires demonstrating strong AML/KYC procedures and a minimum of two years of operational experience for key personnel.

Capital Requirement

VARA imposes minimum capital requirements based on the license category. As of 2026, these are:

  • Preparatory license: No minimum capital, but must show sufficient funds for setup.
  • Full license (Exchange): USD 150,000 minimum paid-up capital.
  • Full license (Custody): USD 100,000 minimum.
  • Full license (Payment): USD 50,000 minimum.

Capital must be held in a UAE bank account or escrow. This is a significant commitment compared to Panama's EUR 6,000 flat fee which includes company formation with no separate capital requirement. For a cost-effective alternative, see our company setup page.

Tax Treatment

Dubai offers a 0% corporate tax for most virtual asset activities, provided the company is not part of a multinational group with UAE nexus. There is no VAT on virtual asset transactions. Personal income tax is also 0%. However, from 2023, a 9% corporate tax applies to profits exceeding AED 375,000 (approx USD 102,000) for certain businesses. Crypto companies may be exempt if they are regulated by VARA and do not conduct 'qualifying' activities. Always confirm with a tax advisor.

In contrast, Panama taxes only local-source income, and Estonia has 0% corporate tax on retained profits. Compare all options on our vs Lithuania page.

For example, a Dubai crypto company earning USD 500,000 in profit might pay 9% tax on the excess over AED 375,000, whereas a Panama company would pay 0% if the income is from foreign sources. This makes Panama attractive for globally oriented businesses.

Allowed Activities Under a VARA License

A VARA full license permits the following activities (subject to license scope):

  • Operating a virtual asset exchange (order book, OTC, P2P)
  • Providing custody and wallet services
  • Facilitating virtual asset payments and remittances
  • Token issuance and management (subject to prospectus rules)
  • Advisory and portfolio management on virtual assets

DeFi protocols and NFT marketplaces may require a specific license. Activities not allowed include unlicensed lending, gambling, and privacy coin trading. For a broader list of jurisdictions and their allowed activities, visit our jurisdictions page.

For example, a DeFi lending platform would need to apply for a separate license or partner with a licensed entity. In contrast, Panama allows a wider range of activities under its general corporate framework, though specific crypto regulation is limited.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Pre-application: Submit a preliminary application to VARA with company details and business plan.
  2. Fit and proper test: Directors and shareholders undergo background checks.
  3. Compliance review: VARA reviews your AML/KYC policies, risk assessment, and operational plan.
  4. License approval: If preparatory, you get a license to set up. If full, you must also secure office space and bank account.
  5. Operational launch: After final approval, you can start regulated activities.

Banking and Payment Services

Opening a bank account for a Dubai crypto company is challenging. Most traditional UAE banks are cautious. Options include:

  • Rakbank (Ras Al Khaimah) - accepts crypto firms with VARA license.
  • Mashreq Neo - digital account for startups.
  • Wio Bank - digital banking for SMEs.
  • International banks like Barclays or HSBC (with strong compliance).

Alternatively, use payment processors like BitOasis or CoinMENA for fiat on-ramps. For a simpler banking experience, Panama offers a more open environment with no capital requirements. See our cost page for details.

For example, a Dubai crypto exchange may need to open multiple bank accounts for operational flexibility, which can take 3-6 months. In Panama, bank account opening is typically faster and less restrictive.
