What Is the Seychelles Crypto License?

The Seychelles crypto license is a regulatory approval issued by the Financial Services Authority (FSA) for businesses engaged in virtual asset services. This includes cryptocurrency exchanges, wallet providers, and other digital asset intermediaries. The regime was introduced under the Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) framework, aligning with international standards from the Financial Action Task Force (FATF).

As of 2026, Seychelles does not have a comprehensive crypto-specific law like MiCA in the EU. Instead, it operates under the Financial Services Authority Act and the Anti-Money Laundering (AML) regulations. The license is essentially a VASP registration that allows companies to offer crypto services legally from Seychelles. The jurisdiction is popular for its tax neutrality and ease of incorporation.

Key features of the license include: no minimum capital requirement for certain activities, no corporate tax on foreign-sourced income, and a streamlined application process. However, the FSA requires strong AML/KYC policies, a local registered office, and a resident director or agent. The license does not cover all activities; for example, derivatives or securities tokens may fall under different regulations.

Who Needs a Crypto License in Seychelles?

Any business that provides virtual asset services to clients or conducts exchange activities involving cryptocurrencies must obtain a license. This includes:

  • Centralized and decentralized cryptocurrency exchanges
  • Peer-to-peer trading platforms
  • Wallet providers (custodial and non-custodial)
  • Payment processors dealing with crypto
  • Initial coin offering (ICO) issuers (if acting as a platform)

If you are a founder looking to launch a crypto exchange or wallet, Seychelles may be suitable if your target market is outside the EU or US, and you prefer a low-tax jurisdiction. However, note that Seychelles is not a member of the EU, so MiCA does not apply. This can be an advantage for those wanting to avoid the higher capital requirements of EU licenses.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator for crypto licenses in Seychelles is the Financial Services Authority (FSA). The FSA issues a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) license under the Financial Services Authority Act. There is currently only one type of license for all virtual asset activities, but the FSA may impose conditions based on the specific services offered.

Unlike some jurisdictions (e.g., Lithuania with its three-tier system), Seychelles does not differentiate between exchange, wallet, or transfer services in the license itself. However, the scope of activities must be clearly defined in the application. The FSA reviews each case individually and may require additional capital or compliance measures for higher-risk activities.

It is important to note that Seychelles is not a member of the EU or EEA, so the license does not automatically grant passporting rights. If you plan to serve European clients, you may need to comply with MiCA separately or choose an EU jurisdiction. For a comparison of EU vs. offshore licenses, see our comparison page.

Capital Requirement

Seychelles does not impose a statutory minimum capital requirement for VASP licenses. However, the FSA expects the company to have sufficient financial resources to operate and meet its obligations. In practice, applicants should demonstrate at least EUR 10,000 to EUR 50,000 in paid-up capital, depending on the scale of operations. This is significantly lower than EU jurisdictions where MiCA mandates EUR 50,000 to 150,000 depending on service class.

For comparison, Lithuania requires EUR 125,000 for exchange and wallet services, while Estonia has no formal minimum but expects at least EUR 12,000. Panama has no minimum capital requirement at all. The low capital requirement in Seychelles makes it attractive for startups, but the FSA may still ask for proof of funds and a business plan.

Tax Treatment

Seychelles offers a territorial tax system: companies are taxed only on income sourced within Seychelles. Since most crypto businesses generate income from clients abroad, the effective tax rate can be 0%. There is no capital gains tax, no withholding tax on dividends, and no VAT on digital services. This makes Seychelles one of the most tax-efficient jurisdictions for crypto operations.

However, to benefit from this, the company must be managed and controlled outside Seychelles. The company must also file annual returns and financial statements with the FSA. It is crucial to maintain proper substance (e.g., a local registered office, a resident agent, and board meetings) to avoid being classified as a tax resident elsewhere.

In contrast, EU jurisdictions impose corporate income tax (e.g., 15% in Lithuania, 20% in Estonia on distributed profits). Panama also has territorial taxation with no tax on foreign income. For a detailed tax comparison, see our cost analysis page.

Allowed Activities Under the License

The Seychelles VASP license permits a range of activities, including:

  • Operating a cryptocurrency exchange (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto)
  • Providing custodial wallet services
  • Facilitating peer-to-peer transfers of virtual assets
  • Issuing and redeeming stablecoins (subject to conditions)
  • Offering crypto payment processing

Activities that are not covered include: dealing in security tokens (regulated under securities law), operating a crypto ATM without additional approval, and offering derivatives or margin trading. If you plan to offer such services, you may need a separate license or a different jurisdiction.

The FSA may also restrict serving clients from certain high-risk countries. You should ensure your business model aligns with the license scope. For a full list of permitted activities, consult the FSA guidelines or engage a compliance advisor.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining a crypto license in Seychelles involves the following steps:

  1. Company Incorporation: Register a Seychelles International Business Company (IBC) or a Special License Company (CSL) with a local registered office and resident agent.
  2. Prepare Documentation: Draft AML/KYC policies, a business plan, financial projections, and corporate documents (e.g., memorandum and articles of association).
  3. Submit Application: File the VASP application with the FSA, including all supporting documents and the application fee.
  4. Due Diligence: The FSA conducts background checks on directors, shareholders, and beneficial owners. Provide certified copies of passports, proof of address, and CVs.
  5. Compliance Review: The FSA reviews your AML policies and operational procedures. You may need to appoint a local compliance officer.
  6. Approval and License Issuance: Once satisfied, the FSA issues the license. The total process takes 3-6 months.

The FSA may conduct on-site inspections and request transaction monitoring reports.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make these errors when applying for a Seychelles crypto license:

  • Underestimating compliance: The FSA requires detailed AML policies. Generic templates are often rejected.
  • Ignoring substance requirements: A shell company with no local presence may be denied or face delays.
  • Choosing the wrong company type: An IBC may not be suitable for all activities; a CSL may be required for certain services.
  • Not budgeting for ongoing costs: Annual fees for the license, registered agent, and compliance can add up to EUR 5,000-10,000.
  • Assuming zero tax without planning: You must ensure the company is managed outside Seychelles to benefit from territorial taxation.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital required for a crypto license in Seychelles?

There is no statutory minimum capital requirement, but the FSA expects sufficient financial resources. Practically, applicants should have at least EUR 10,000-50,000 in paid-up capital to demonstrate substance.

How long does it take to get a Seychelles crypto license?

The process typically takes 3 to 6 months from company incorporation to license approval. Delays can occur if the application is incomplete or if the FSA requires additional information.

Is Seychelles tax-free for crypto businesses?

Seychelles has a territorial tax system: only income sourced within Seychelles is taxed. Since most crypto income is from abroad, the effective tax rate can be 0%. There is no capital gains tax or VAT on digital services.

Can I serve EU clients with a Seychelles license?

Yes, but you must comply with EU regulations, including MiCA if your clients are in the EU. The Seychelles license does not provide passporting rights. You may need to register as a CASP in an EU member state.

What activities are not allowed under the Seychelles VASP license?

The license does not cover security tokens, derivatives, margin trading, or crypto ATM operations without additional approval. These activities may require a separate securities license or a different jurisdiction.

Do I need a physical office in Seychelles?

You need a registered office and a resident agent. A physical office is not mandatory, but having one can strengthen your application and help with banking relationships.

How much does a Seychelles crypto license cost in total?

Total first-year costs typically range from EUR 9,000 to 16,500, including incorporation, application fees, legal advisory, and AML policy drafting. Ongoing annual costs are around EUR 5,000-10,000.

What are the AML requirements for a Seychelles crypto license?

You must implement strong AML/KYC policies, including customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting. The FSA requires these policies to be submitted with the application.

Can I use a Seychelles IBC for crypto activities?

Yes, an International Business Company (IBC) is commonly used. However, for certain regulated activities, a Special License Company (CSL) may be required. Consult with an advisor to choose the right entity.

Is the Seychelles crypto license recognized internationally?

The license is recognized by the FSA and complies with FATF standards. However, some countries may not accept it for regulatory equivalence. It is important to check the requirements of your target markets.
