What Is a Crypto License in Italy?

A crypto license in Italy authorizes a company to offer crypto-asset services such as exchange, custody, and wallet management. Since MiCA came fully into force in 2025, Italian regulation aligns with the EU-wide regime. The license is issued by the Banca d'Italia after consultation with CONSOB, the securities regulator. It replaces the previous optional registration system and imposes stricter capital, governance, and reporting standards.

For founders, this means a clear, harmonized framework but also higher upfront costs compared to pre-MiCA regimes. The cost of a crypto license in Italy reflects the strong compliance infrastructure required. Companies must be established in Italy or another EU member state and passport their license. The process involves a detailed application, proof of capital, and a fit-and-proper assessment of management. Unlike some jurisdictions that only register entities, Italy conducts a thorough review of your business plan, AML policies, and the background of all beneficial owners. This scrutiny adds time but also credibility to your license.

Who Needs an Italian Crypto License?

Any business providing crypto-asset services to Italian residents or operating from Italy needs this license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralized and decentralized)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Brokers and dealers in crypto-assets
  • Platforms offering crypto lending or staking
  • Payment processors using crypto

Even if your company is based elsewhere, if you target Italian clients, you must either obtain an Italian license or passport an existing EU CASP license. The cost of a crypto license in Italy is therefore relevant for any firm with Italian users. License Type and Regulator

Italy issues a single type of license for crypto-asset service providers (CASP) under MiCA. The regulators are:

  • Banca d'Italia - primary regulator, responsible for authorization and supervision
  • CONSOB - oversees market conduct and investor protection

There are no separate categories for different services; the license covers all MiCA services, but capital requirements vary by service class. The cost of a crypto license in Italy is driven by the scope of services you intend to offer. For example, operating an exchange requires higher capital than mere custody. Consulting24 can advise on structuring your service list to optimize costs. The application is submitted through the Banca d'Italia's dedicated portal, and the process includes a preliminary assessment of your documentation before formal submission.

Capital Requirement Details

Under MiCA, capital requirements are tiered by service class. Italy applies the EU minimums exactly:

  • EUR 50,000 - for custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • EUR 125,000 - for exchange of crypto-assets for fiat currency or other crypto-assets (without custody)
  • EUR 150,000 - for operating a trading platform or providing all services including custody

Capital must be held in liquid assets, typically a bank deposit or government bonds. It is not a fee but a solvency requirement. The cost of a crypto license in Italy thus includes locking up capital that cannot be used for operations. This is a key difference from jurisdictions like Panama, which has no minimum capital. Consulting24 can help you structure your capital efficiently, for example by using a combination of share capital and reserves. Note that the capital must be maintained throughout the license period; if you incur losses, you may need to top it up.

Tax Treatment for Crypto Businesses in Italy

Italy taxes crypto businesses under corporate income tax (IRES) at 24% and regional tax (IRAP) at about 3.9%. VAT is not applicable on crypto-to-fiat exchanges under EU law. Capital gains from crypto trading are taxed at 26% for individuals, but businesses are subject to ordinary corporate rates.

There are no specific crypto tax incentives, but the regime is stable. The cost of a crypto license in Italy should factor in tax advisory fees to optimize structure. For comparison, Panama offers territorial taxation (no tax on foreign-source income), making it a popular alternative. Consulting24 advises on tax-efficient holding structures across jurisdictions. For example, a common strategy is to hold the IP and treasury in Panama while operating the licensed entity in Italy, reducing the overall tax burden.

Allowed Activities Under the Italian Crypto License

The license permits the following services as defined by MiCA:

  • Providing custody and administration of crypto-assets
  • Operating a trading platform for crypto-assets
  • Exchanging crypto-assets for fiat currency
  • Exchanging crypto-assets for other crypto-assets
  • Execution of orders on behalf of clients
  • Placing crypto-assets
  • Receiving and transmitting orders
  • Providing advice on crypto-assets
  • Portfolio management of crypto-assets
  • Transfer services for crypto-assets

You can apply for one or multiple classes. The cost of a crypto license in Italy increases with the scope of services due to higher capital and compliance demands. Consulting24 helps you define your service list to match your business plan. For instance, if you only plan to offer custody, you can apply for Class 1 and save on capital. However, if you later want to add exchange services, you will need to apply for a variation and increase capital.

The cost of a crypto license in Italy includes banking setup fees (EUR 1,000-3,000) and monthly account fees (EUR 100-500). For fiat on-ramps, you need a SEPA-compliant account. Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

After obtaining the license, you must comply with ongoing requirements:

  • Annual AML audit by an external auditor
  • Appointment of an AML officer (resident in EU)
  • Quarterly reporting to Banca d'Italia
  • Capital maintenance (must always meet minimum)
  • Annual financial statements audited
  • Transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting

What is the total cost of a crypto license in Italy in 2026?

The total first-year cost (excluding capital) ranges from EUR 30,000 to 65,000, including application fees, legal setup, and ongoing compliance. The minimum capital requirement is between EUR 50,000 and 150,000 depending on services. Exact costs depend on your business scope and structure.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Italy?

The timeline is typically 4 to 8 months from application to approval. This includes company incorporation, document preparation, regulatory review, and final registration. Delays can occur if the regulator requests additional information.

What are the capital requirements for an Italian crypto license?

Under MiCA, capital is tiered: EUR 50,000 for custody only, EUR 125,000 for exchange without custody, and EUR 150,000 for full services including custody and trading platforms. Capital must be held in liquid assets and maintained throughout the license.

Can I passport an Italian crypto license to other EU countries?

Yes. Once licensed in Italy, you can passport your CASP license to any other EU member state, allowing you to serve clients across the EU without additional licensing. This is a key advantage of the MiCA framework.

Annual compliance costs range from EUR 10,000 to 20,000, covering AML audits, AML officer salary, quarterly reporting, and financial audits. The annual supervisory fee is EUR 2,000-5,000 based on revenue.
