What Are AML/KYC Requirements for a Czech Republic Crypto Company?

AML/KYC requirements refer to the legal obligations that crypto companies must follow to prevent money laundering and terrorist financing. In the Czech Republic, these are governed by the Act No. 253/2008 Sb. on Money Laundering and the EU's MiCA regulation, fully in force since 2025. KYC (Know Your Customer) means verifying the identity of your clients, while AML (Anti-Money Laundering) involves ongoing monitoring and reporting suspicious transactions.

For a Czech crypto company, you must implement a risk-based approach. This includes customer identification, beneficial ownership checks, transaction monitoring, and record keeping. The regulator expects you to have written internal policies, appoint a compliance officer, and file reports with the FAÚ. Failure to comply can result in fines, licence revocation, or criminal liability.

Under MiCA, the requirements are harmonised across the EU, but the Czech Republic adds specific local obligations. For example, you must register with the CNB as a CASP and comply with the FAÚ's guidance on virtual currency. The rules apply to all crypto activities, including exchange, custody, transfer, and advisory services. Consulting24 can help you draft custom AML policies that meet both EU and Czech standards.

Who Needs to Comply with These Requirements?

Any entity providing crypto asset services in the Czech Republic must comply. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (buying/selling crypto for fiat or other crypto)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Brokers and dealers in crypto assets
  • ATMs and kiosks
  • Issuers of crypto assets (if offering services)
  • Any other service defined under MiCA as a CASP

If you are a foreign company targeting Czech clients, you may also need to register or establish a local presence. The rules apply to both legal entities and individuals operating as sole traders. Consulting24 can help you assess whether your business model falls under these requirements.

Even if you only offer services online to Czech residents, you likely need a Czech licence or passport under MiCA. The CNB has been active in enforcing against unregistered providers. License Type and Regulator

The Czech Republic issues a Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) license under the MiCA framework. The primary regulator is the Czech National Bank (CNB), which oversees licensing and supervision. The Financial Analytical Office (FAÚ) handles AML enforcement and receives suspicious transaction reports.

Unlike the old registration-only regime, MiCA requires a full authorisation. The licence covers multiple service classes, each with specific capital requirements. The CNB assesses your AML policies, governance, and operational resilience before granting approval. The CNB is known for a thorough but fair process. They expect detailed business plans, risk assessments, and proof of adequate systems. The licence is valid across the EU under passporting, allowing you to serve clients in other member states without additional licensing. However, the process can take 6-12 months. Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, capital requirements are tiered by service class. For a Czech CASP, the minimum capital is:

  • EUR 50,000 for simple services like custody or transfer
  • EUR 125,000 for exchange services (crypto to fiat or crypto to crypto)
  • EUR 150,000 for more complex services like trading platforms or dealing on own account

These amounts must be held as own funds and are subject to ongoing maintenance. The CNB may require higher capital based on risk assessment. Consulting24 can help you determine which tier applies to your business model.

Capital must be fully paid up before application. You can use fiat or certain liquid assets, but crypto is not accepted. The capital requirement is a significant barrier for startups. In contrast, Panama has no minimum capital requirement for crypto companies, making it a more accessible option. Our cost page compares expenses across jurisdictions.

Tax Treatment

The Czech Republic taxes crypto profits as capital gains for individuals (15% flat rate) and as corporate income for companies (19% rate). VAT is not applied to crypto transactions, but mining may be subject to business income tax. The tax treatment aligns with EU guidelines, but you must report all transactions to the tax office.

For companies, holding crypto as inventory or investment affects accounting. Transfer pricing rules apply if dealing with related parties. Consulting24 recommends working with a local tax advisor to ensure compliance. For a tax-neutral alternative, consider Panama, which has no capital gains tax on crypto.

Additionally, the Czech Republic has a withholding tax on dividends (15%) and a social security system for employees. If you plan to operate as a lean startup, these costs add up. Panama offers a territorial tax system, meaning only locally sourced income is taxed. Our comparison page details tax differences between jurisdictions.

Allowed Activities Under the Licence

A Czech CASP licence permits the following activities under MiCA:

  • Providing custody and administration of crypto assets
  • Operating a trading platform (exchange)
  • Exchanging crypto for fiat or other crypto
  • Executing orders on behalf of clients
  • Placing crypto assets (underwriting)
  • Transfer services (sending/receiving crypto)
  • Advising on crypto assets
  • Portfolio management

Each activity requires separate authorisation. You cannot offer services outside your licence scope. The CNB also restricts certain high-risk activities, such as anonymity-enhanced coins, unless additional controls are in place.

If your business model involves multiple services, you need to apply for each class. This increases capital requirements and complexity. Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining a Czech crypto licence involves these steps:

  1. Pre-application assessment: Review your business model and determine the required service classes and capital.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Czech entity (usually a s.r.o.) with a registered office and local director.
  3. AML policy development: Draft internal policies covering KYC, transaction monitoring, record keeping, and suspicious activity reporting.
  4. Appoint a compliance officer: This person must be resident in the Czech Republic and have relevant experience.
  5. Prepare application: Compile the required documents, including business plan, risk assessment, governance structure, and financial projections.
  6. Submit to CNB: File the application electronically. The CNB has up to 6 months to decide.
  7. Post-licence compliance: Implement ongoing reporting, audits, and AML training.

Compliance and Trust Considerations

Compliance with AML/KYC rules is not optional. The FAÚ conducts regular inspections and can impose fines up to CZK 50 million (approx. EUR 2 million) for breaches. You must maintain records for 10 years, conduct annual AML audits, and train staff. A compliance officer must be appointed and registered with the CNB.

  • No local presence: The CNB expects a physical office and local compliance officer.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Czech crypto licence?

Under MiCA, the minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for custody/transfer, EUR 125,000 for exchange services, and EUR 150,000 for trading platforms. These amounts must be held as own funds and are subject to CNB approval.

How long does it take to get a Czech crypto licence?

The process typically takes 6-12 months from application to approval. This includes company incorporation, AML policy drafting, and CNB review. Consulting24 can coordinate with local partners to expedite the process.

Can I use a Czech licence to serve clients in other EU countries?

Yes, once licensed, you can passport your services across the EU under MiCA. You need to notify the CNB of your intent to operate in other member states, but no additional licence is required.

What are the ongoing compliance costs for a Czech crypto company?

Annual costs include compliance officer salary (EUR 3,000-6,000), AML audits (EUR 2,000-5,000), and reporting fees. Total ongoing compliance can be EUR 10,000-20,000 per year.

Is it possible to get a Czech crypto licence without a local office?

No, the CNB requires a physical registered office in the Czech Republic and a local compliance officer. Virtual offices are not accepted. Consulting24 can help you set up a compliant office space.

What happens if I don't comply with AML/KYC rules in the Czech Republic?

Non-compliance can result in fines up to CZK 50 million (approx. EUR 2 million), licence revocation, and criminal liability. The FAÚ conducts regular inspections and can freeze assets.

Can I convert my existing Czech registration to a MiCA licence?

Yes, if you were previously registered under the old VASP regime, you must transition to a full CASP licence under MiCA. The CNB has a transition period, but as of 2026, all providers must hold a MiCA licence.

What are the tax rates for crypto companies in the Czech Republic?

Corporate income tax is 19%. Capital gains for individuals are taxed at 15%. VAT is not applied to crypto transactions. Mining income is treated as business income and taxed accordingly.

Is Panama a better option than the Czech Republic for a crypto startup?

For many startups, Panama offers a faster and cheaper alternative with no crypto licence requirement, no capital gains tax, and a flat EUR 6,000 company setup. However, Panama does not provide EU passporting. Consulting24 delivers Panama setups directly.
