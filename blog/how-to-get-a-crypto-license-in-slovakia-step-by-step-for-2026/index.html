What Is a Crypto License in Slovakia?

A crypto license in Slovakia is a regulatory authorization issued by the National Bank of Slovakia (NBS) under the EU's Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA). It allows companies to offer crypto-asset services such as exchange, wallet custody, transfer, and advisory. The license is mandatory for any entity providing these services to Slovak residents or operating from Slovakia.

Slovakia's regime is part of the EU's harmonized framework, meaning a Slovak CASP license can be passportable across the European Economic Area. This makes it attractive for founders targeting the EU market. The license requires compliance with AML, capital adequacy, and consumer protection rules. Unlike some pre-MiCA regimes, there is no grandfathering for existing VASPs; all must obtain a full CASP license by 2026.

Who Needs a Crypto License in Slovakia?

Any legal entity offering crypto-asset services to Slovak clients or operating a crypto business from Slovakia needs a license. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (centralized and decentralized)
  • Wallet providers (custodial and non-custodial if they hold private keys)
  • Brokers and dealers in crypto assets
  • Crypto ATMs operators
  • Advisory and portfolio management services for crypto

DeFi protocols and NFT platforms may also fall under MiCA if they offer regulated services. For example, a DeFi lending platform that takes custody of user funds would likely require a CASP license. License Type and Regulator

The license is called a Crypto-Asset Service Provider (CASP) license under MiCA. The regulator is the National Bank of Slovakia (Národná banka Slovenska, NBS). The NBS is responsible for authorization, supervision, and enforcement.

There is only one license category for all crypto services, but the capital requirement varies by the services offered. The NBS also requires a local registered office and a compliance officer based in Slovakia. The license is valid throughout the EU via passporting. The NBS is known for thorough due diligence, so applicants should expect detailed scrutiny of their business plan and AML policies.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the capital requirement for CASPs in Slovakia is tiered based on the type of services provided:

  • EUR 50,000 for advisory, portfolio management, and transfer services (if no custody)
  • EUR 125,000 for exchange services (custodial or non-custodial)
  • EUR 150,000 for custody of client crypto assets

If you offer multiple services, the highest tier applies. The capital must be held in a Slovak bank account and cannot be used for operational expenses. It serves as a buffer for client protection. This is a significant upfront cost compared to Panama, which has zero capital requirement. For example, a startup offering exchange and custody would need EUR 150,000 in capital before even starting operations.

Tax Treatment

Slovakia has a relatively favorable tax regime for crypto. As of 2026, capital gains from crypto held for more than one year are tax-exempt for individuals. For companies, crypto gains are taxed as corporate income at a flat rate of 21% (15% for small businesses). VAT does not apply to crypto transactions under EU law.

However, Slovakia does not have a specific crypto tax exemption like Panama, where crypto gains are tax-free for both individuals and companies. For tax optimization, many founders choose Panama as a holding or operating entity. For example, a Slovak CASP could be used for EU operations while a Panama entity handles non-EU clients to minimize tax.

Allowed Activities

With a Slovak CASP license, you can offer the following services under MiCA:

  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets
  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Transfer services (sending/receiving crypto)
  • Execution of orders on behalf of clients
  • Placement of crypto assets (underwriting)
  • Advisory services on crypto assets
  • Portfolio management of crypto assets

Non-custodial wallet providers may be exempt if they never hold private keys. DeFi platforms should seek legal advice on their specific model. For example, a DEX that only facilitates peer-to-peer trading without taking custody may not need a license, but if it holds user funds in an escrow, it likely does.

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Pre-application preparation: Define your business model, services, and target market. Prepare a detailed business plan, AML/KYC policies, risk management framework, and internal controls. This step typically takes 1-2 months.
  2. Company incorporation: Set up a Slovak limited liability company (s.r.o.) with a registered office in Slovakia. Appoint a local director or compliance officer. This can be done in 1-2 weeks.
  3. Capital deposit: Open a corporate bank account in Slovakia and deposit the required minimum capital. The bank must be regulated by NBS. This step can take 1-3 months due to bank due diligence.
  4. Application submission: Submit the application to NBS with all required documents, including policies, financial projections, and personal questionnaires for directors. The NBS will acknowledge receipt within 10 days.
  5. Regulatory review: NBS reviews the application, may request additional information, and conducts interviews with management. This typically takes 3-6 months. In some cases, the NBS may request a third-party audit of your AML systems.
  6. License issuance: If approved, NBS issues the CASP license. You can then start operations. Ongoing compliance includes regular reporting, AML audits, and capital maintenance.

Banking and Payments

Opening a bank account for a crypto company in Slovakia can be challenging. Most traditional banks are cautious. However, some Slovak banks accept CASP license holders, especially after MiCA implementation. You may also consider EMI (electronic money institution) accounts or specialized crypto-friendly banks in Lithuania or Estonia.

For payment processing, you can integrate with fiat on-ramps like Stripe or Binance Pay. However, many payment processors still view crypto as high-risk, so expect higher fees and longer onboarding. Panama offers easier banking with no capital requirement, and many international banks accept Panama companies. For example, a Panama entity can open accounts with banks in Switzerland or the UAE more easily.

Compliance and Trust

Compliance is ongoing. You must:

  • Conduct AML/KYC on all clients
  • Report suspicious transactions to the Financial Intelligence Unit
  • Maintain capital adequacy
  • Submit annual audited financial statements
  • Undergo periodic regulatory inspections

Failure to comply can result in fines or license revocation. For example, inadequate AML screening could lead to penalties of up to EUR 1 million or 10% of annual turnover. This is general guidance, not legal advice. Consulting24 helps you build a compliant framework from day one.

  • Ignoring local presence: A virtual office may not satisfy the registered office requirement.
  • Choosing the wrong bank: Some banks refuse crypto companies even with a license.
  • Not planning for passporting: If you target multiple EU countries, ensure your compliance covers all.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital required for a crypto license in Slovakia?

The minimum capital depends on your services: EUR 50,000 for advisory and transfer services, EUR 125,000 for exchange services, and EUR 150,000 for custody services. If you offer multiple services, the highest tier applies. The capital must be deposited in a Slovak bank account before application.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Slovakia?

The timeline is typically 6 to 12 months from application to issuance. This includes company incorporation (1-2 weeks), bank account opening (1-3 months), and regulatory review (3-6 months). Delays can occur if the NBS requests additional information.

Can I passport a Slovak CASP license to other EU countries?

Yes, under MiCA, a Slovak CASP license is passportable across the European Economic Area (30 countries). You can operate in other EU member states without obtaining a separate license, but you must notify the NBS and comply with local AML rules.

What are the tax implications for crypto companies in Slovakia?

Corporate income tax on crypto gains is 21% (15% for small businesses). Individuals holding crypto for more than one year pay 0% capital gains tax. VAT does not apply to crypto transactions. Panama offers 0% tax on crypto gains for both individuals and companies.

Is a Slovak crypto license suitable for DeFi protocols?

It depends on the specific model. If the protocol takes custody of user funds or offers exchange services, it likely requires a CASP license. Non-custodial DeFi platforms may be exempt. Legal advice is recommended to determine your obligations.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements after obtaining a Slovak crypto license?

You must conduct AML/KYC on all clients, report suspicious transactions, maintain capital adequacy, submit annual audited financial statements, and undergo periodic regulatory inspections. Non-compliance can result in fines or license revocation.

Can I use a virtual office for the registered address in Slovakia?

No, the NBS requires a physical registered office in Slovakia. A virtual office or PO box is not sufficient. You must have a local presence, including a compliance officer based in Slovakia.

How does Slovakia compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 license with no capital requirement, 0% tax on crypto gains, and a 2-3 month timeline. Slovakia requires EUR 50,000-150,000 in capital, 21% corporate tax, and takes 6-12 months. Panama is more cost-effective for startups, while Slovakia offers EU passporting.

What types of crypto services are allowed under a Slovak CASP license?

Allowed services include exchange (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto), custody, transfer, execution of orders, placement, advisory, and portfolio management. Non-custodial wallets may be exempt if they never hold private keys.

Do I need to be physically present in Slovakia to apply for a license?

No, but you must have a local registered office and a compliance officer based in Slovakia. The NBS may conduct interviews with management, which can be done remotely. Consulting24 can help coordinate the local requirements.
