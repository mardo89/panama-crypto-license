What Is a Crypto License in Spain?

A crypto license in Spain, officially a CASP registration, is required to provide services such as exchange, custody, and transfer of crypto assets. Under MiCA, which is fully applicable from 2026, the Bank of Spain is the competent authority. The license demonstrates compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) rules, consumer protection, and operational standards. It is mandatory for any entity offering crypto services to Spanish residents.

Spain's regime is not a simple registration; it is a full authorization process that includes prudential supervision. The Bank of Spain evaluates the fitness of management, the strength of IT systems, and the adequacy of capital. Unlike pre-MiCA VASP registers that had no minimum capital, the 2026 CASP regime imposes real financial requirements. This shift brings Spain in line with other EU member states and raises the bar for market entry.

For businesses already operating in Spain without a license, the transition period under MiCA has ended. New applicants must follow the full CASP process. Consulting24 can help you understand whether your existing setup meets the new standards or if a fresh application is needed.

Who Needs a Crypto License in Spain?

Any business that provides crypto asset services in Spain must obtain a license. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (centralized and decentralized)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Brokers facilitating crypto trades
  • Platforms offering crypto-to-crypto or fiat-to-crypto conversions
  • Payment processors using crypto

If your business involves any of these activities, you need to register with the Bank of Spain. Exemptions exist for non-custodial software providers and certain utility tokens, but most commercial operations require authorization.

Additionally, foreign companies targeting Spanish customers must also obtain a license. The Bank of Spain takes a broad view of 'providing services in Spain' - if your website is in Spanish or you market to Spanish residents, you likely fall under their jurisdiction. License Type and Regulator

The license is a CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) registration under MiCA. The regulator is the Bank of Spain (Banco de España), which oversees compliance with AML and prudential requirements. Spain also has a separate register for virtual asset service providers (VASPs) under the AML framework, but from 2026, the CASP regime is the primary route. The Bank of Spain works alongside the CNMV (securities regulator) for certain activities.

The Bank of Spain has a dedicated unit for fintech and crypto supervision. They conduct thorough background checks on shareholders and management, including criminal record checks and financial probity assessments. The regulator also requires a detailed business plan showing projected volumes, revenue streams, and risk management frameworks.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, capital requirements are tiered by service class:

  • EUR 50,000 for simple services (e.g., custody of client funds)
  • EUR 125,000 for exchange services (fiat-to-crypto or crypto-to-crypto)
  • EUR 150,000 for more complex services (e.g., trading platforms with order matching)

These amounts must be held as own funds. Spain may impose additional buffers for higher-risk activities. Always confirm with a local adviser.

The capital must be fully paid up before application. It can be held in a Spanish bank account or an EU bank account. The Bank of Spain may ask for proof of source of funds. Consulting24 recommends having the capital ready in a regulated financial institution before starting the application. For comparison, Panama has no minimum capital requirement.

Tax Treatment

Spain taxes crypto as property. Capital gains from crypto trading are subject to personal income tax (19-26%) for individuals. Corporate entities pay the standard corporate tax rate of 25% on profits. VAT does not apply to crypto-to-fiat exchanges, but mining and staking rewards are treated as income. Spain has a mandatory reporting requirement for crypto holdings above EUR 50,000. Proper tax structuring is essential; Consulting24 can recommend local tax experts.

For corporate entities, losses can be offset against future gains, but there are limitations. Spain also has a wealth tax that may apply to crypto holdings above a certain threshold. The tax treatment of DeFi activities is still evolving. Consulting24 works with tax advisors who specialize in crypto to help you optimize your structure.

Allowed Activities Under a Spanish Crypto License

A Spanish CASP license permits:

  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets
  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Execution of orders on behalf of clients
  • Placing of crypto assets (underwriting)
  • Transfer services (sending crypto on behalf of clients)

Activities like crypto derivatives or securities tokens may require additional authorization from the CNMV.

The license does not cover mining pools, non-custodial software, or pure advisory services. If you plan to offer staking services, you need to clarify with the Bank of Spain whether it falls under custody or a separate activity. Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Pre-application preparation: Draft business plan, AML/KYC policies, risk assessment, and operational procedures.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Spanish company (S.L. or S.A.) with a registered office in Spain.
  3. Appoint a compliance officer: Must be a Spanish resident with relevant experience.
  4. Submit application to Bank of Spain: Provide all documentation, including proof of capital, shareholder background checks, and IT security measures.
  5. Review and interview: The Bank may request additional information or hold interviews with management.
  6. Approval and registration: Once approved, you are added to the CASP register. The process takes 3-6 months.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

After obtaining the license, you must:

  • Submit regular AML reports to the Bank of Spain
  • Conduct annual audits by an external auditor
  • Maintain minimum capital at all times
  • Update policies for new regulations
  • Report suspicious transactions

Non-compliance can lead to fines or license revocation. This is general guidance, not legal advice. Always consult a qualified lawyer.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Incomplete AML documentation: The Bank of Spain is strict on AML policies. Ensure they are detailed and tailored to your business.
  • Underestimating timeline: The process can take up to 6 months. Plan accordingly.
  • Ignoring beneficial ownership rules: All shareholders must be identified and vetted.
  • Not having a local presence: A registered office and local compliance officer are mandatory.
  • Overlooking IT security requirements: The Bank of Spain expects a strong cybersecurity framework. Failing to provide a penetration test report is a common reason for delays.

Frequently asked questions

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Spain?

The process typically takes 3 to 6 months from application to approval, depending on the completeness of your documentation and the Bank of Spain's workload. Complex cases may take up to 9 months.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a crypto license in Spain?

Under MiCA, the minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for basic services, EUR 125,000 for exchange services, and EUR 150,000 for more complex activities. These amounts must be held as own funds.

Can I passport a Spanish crypto license to other EU countries?

Yes, a Spanish CASP license allows you to provide services across the EU under MiCA passporting, subject to notification to the Bank of Spain and the host member state.

Is a Spanish crypto license expensive?

Total costs including legal fees, application fees, and software can range from EUR 10,000 to EUR 30,000, with annual compliance costs of EUR 10,000 to EUR 25,000.

Do I need a physical office in Spain?

Yes, you need a registered office in Spain and a local compliance officer. Virtual offices may be acceptable if they meet regulatory requirements, but a physical presence is preferred.

What activities are covered by a Spanish crypto license?

The license covers exchange, custody, order execution, transfer services, and placing of crypto assets. Derivatives and securities tokens may need additional authorization from the CNMV.

How does Spain tax crypto gains?

Individuals pay capital gains tax (19-26%) on crypto profits. Corporate profits are taxed at 25%. Crypto held for over one year may qualify for reduced rates. Mining and staking are taxed as income.

Can I apply for a Spanish crypto license as a foreign company?

Yes, but you must incorporate a Spanish subsidiary (e.g., S.L.) and meet all local requirements, including having a local compliance officer and registered office.

What happens if I don't comply with Spanish crypto regulations?

Non-compliance can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. The Bank of Spain actively monitors and enforces rules, including conducting on-site inspections.

Is Panama a better option than Spain for a crypto license?

Panama offers a faster, cheaper option (EUR 6,000 flat) with no minimum capital, but it does not provide EU market access. Your choice depends on your target market.

Do I need to have a Spanish bank account to apply?

Not strictly, but having a Spanish bank account can facilitate the process. The Bank of Spain may ask for proof of capital held in an EU bank account.

Can Consulting24 guarantee approval of my Spanish license?

No, we cannot guarantee approval as the decision rests with the Bank of Spain. However, our thorough preparation and coordination with local experts significantly increase your chances.
