What Is the El Salvador Crypto License?

The El Salvador crypto license, officially a Digital Assets Service Provider (DASP) authorization, is issued by the National Digital Assets Commission (CNAD). It allows companies to legally offer crypto-related services such as exchange, custody, trading, and token issuance. The license is governed by the Digital Assets Law, which came into force in 2023 and is fully operational in 2026.

Key features include:

  • Regulatory clarity for Bitcoin and other digital assets
  • Tax exemptions on capital gains from digital assets
  • No foreign exchange controls
  • Access to a growing ecosystem of crypto-friendly banks and payment providers

El Salvador's approach is distinct from the EU's MiCA framework, offering a more flexible regime with lower capital requirements for certain activities. The license is not a passport within the EU, but it provides a strong base for operations in Latin America and beyond.

Who Needs an El Salvador Crypto License?

Any business that provides digital asset services to residents of El Salvador or operates from within the country must obtain a license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralized and decentralized)
  • Digital wallet providers
  • Custodial services
  • Token issuers (including security token offerings)
  • Payment processors using digital assets
  • Brokerage and trading platforms

Foreign companies targeting the Salvadoran market or seeking a regulatory base in Latin America also need to apply. The license is particularly attractive for startups and established firms looking for a cost-effective, crypto-friendly jurisdiction with a clear legal framework. For example, a Bitcoin-focused exchange can use the legal tender status to offer smooth BTC/fiat pairs without additional licensing burdens.

License Type & Regulator

The regulator is the National Digital Assets Commission (Comisión Nacional de Activos Digitales, CNAD), an independent body under the Ministry of Economy. The license is a single authorization covering multiple activities, but applicants must specify which services they intend to provide.

There are two main categories:

  • Class A: For service providers dealing with third-party assets (e.g., exchanges, custodians). Requires higher capital of $50,000.
  • Class B: For issuers of digital assets (e.g., tokenization). Lower capital of $25,000 but stricter disclosure and reporting rules.

The CNAD also oversees compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) regulations, which align with FATF recommendations. The commission has the authority to conduct on-site inspections and impose sanctions for non-compliance.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for an El Salvador crypto license is $50,000 for Class A service providers and $25,000 for Class B issuers. This capital must be deposited in a Salvadoran bank account and cannot be withdrawn without regulatory approval. The funds serve as a solvency buffer and must be maintained throughout the license period.

Compared to the EU's MiCA capital tiers (EUR 50,000 to 150,000), El Salvador's requirement is competitive, especially for smaller startups. However, the capital must be in cash; in-kind contributions are not accepted. Proof of deposit is required at the application stage.

Tax Treatment

One of the biggest advantages of the El Salvador crypto license is the favorable tax regime. Under the Digital Assets Law, income from digital assets is exempt from capital gains tax. Additionally, no withholding tax applies to dividends or interest from digital asset activities. This exemption applies to both residents and non-residents operating through a Salvadoran entity.

However, corporate income tax (30%) applies to other business activities, such as consulting fees or fiat-based services. Value-added tax (VAT) is 13% on services, but crypto transactions may be exempt if they qualify as financial services. Companies should seek local tax advice to optimize their structure, especially if they have mixed revenue streams.

Allowed Activities

Licensed entities can engage in a wide range of activities, including:

  • Exchange of digital assets for fiat or other digital assets
  • Custody and wallet services
  • Brokerage and trading
  • Token issuance (including STOs and ICOs)
  • Payment processing using digital assets
  • Mining and staking services

Notably, El Salvador allows Bitcoin as legal tender, so businesses can integrate Bitcoin payments without additional licensing. The CNAD also permits decentralized finance (DeFi) activities under certain conditions, provided they meet AML requirements. For example, a DeFi lending protocol must implement KYC for users if it involves custodial elements.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining an El Salvador crypto license involves the following steps:

  1. Pre-incorporation: Register a company in El Salvador (Sociedad Anónima de Capital Variable). This takes 1-2 weeks and costs around $1,000 in legal fees.
  2. Document preparation: Gather business plan, AML policies, financial projections, and background checks for directors. The AML policy must cover customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting.
  3. Submit application: File with the CNAD along with the application fee. The application form requires detailed information on ownership structure and service types.
  4. Regulatory review: The CNAD assesses compliance and may request additional information (2-4 months). Common requests include clarification on source of funds or risk management procedures.
  5. Capital deposit: Provide proof of minimum capital in a local bank account. The bank will issue a certificate of deposit.
  6. License issuance: Upon approval, pay the issuance fee and receive the license. The license is valid for one year and renewable annually.
  7. Ongoing compliance: Submit periodic reports (quarterly transaction reports, annual audit) and undergo CNAD inspections.

Banking & Payments

El Salvador has a developing but functional banking sector for crypto businesses. Several banks accept deposits from licensed DASPs, including Banco Agricola and Banco Cuscatlán. However, some international banks remain cautious due to AML concerns. It is advisable to open accounts with local banks that have experience with crypto clients.

Payment providers like Strike and Bitrefill offer integration with the Lightning Network, enabling fast, low-cost Bitcoin transactions. Benefits of an El Salvador Crypto License

Compliance & Trust

Licensed entities must adhere to strict AML/CTF obligations, including customer due diligence (CDD), transaction monitoring, and reporting suspicious activities to the Financial Investigation Unit (FIU). The CNAD conducts regular audits and can impose fines or revoke licenses for non-compliance. For example, failure to report a suspicious transaction can result in a fine of up to $100,000.

While the regime is progressive, it is still evolving. Founders should engage local legal counsel to ensure full compliance. This guide provides general guidance, not legal advice. Common Mistakes to Avoid

Many applicants face delays due to:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing business plans or AML policies. Ensure all documents are translated into Spanish if required.
  • Underestimating capital requirements: Failing to demonstrate the deposit in a timely manner.
  • Ignoring local presence: The CNAD expects a physical office and a local director. Virtual offices may not be accepted.
  • Poorly drafted compliance policies: Must align with FATF standards and be specific to your business model.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for an El Salvador crypto license?

The minimum capital is $25,000 for Class B (issuers) and $50,000 for Class A (service providers). This must be deposited in a Salvadoran bank account and maintained throughout the license period. The capital cannot be withdrawn without CNAD approval.

How long does it take to get an El Salvador crypto license?

The typical timeline is 3 to 6 months from application submission to approval. Delays can occur if documentation is incomplete or if the CNAD requests additional information. Using a local advisor can help streamline the process.

Is El Salvador part of the EU's MiCA framework?

No, El Salvador is not an EU member and is not subject to MiCA. Its Digital Assets Law is a standalone regime, offering more flexibility and lower capital requirements. However, it does not provide EU passporting rights.

Can I use a virtual office for the El Salvador license?

No, the CNAD expects a physical office in El Salvador. A virtual office may not satisfy the local presence requirement. You should lease a physical space or use a serviced office provider.

What taxes apply to crypto businesses in El Salvador?

Capital gains from digital assets are exempt from tax. Corporate income tax of 30% applies to other income. VAT is 13% but may not apply to crypto transactions classified as financial services. Local tax advice is recommended.

Does El Salvador require a local director?

Yes, the CNAD typically requires at least one local director who is a resident of El Salvador. This director can be a nominee if you do not have a local presence, but they must meet fit and proper criteria.

Can I apply for the license remotely?

The application process can be initiated remotely, but you will need to visit El Salvador for certain steps, such as opening a bank account and meeting with the CNAD if required. A local representative can handle some tasks.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations?

Licensees must submit quarterly transaction reports, annual audited financial statements, and undergo regular CNAD inspections. AML/CTF policies must be updated regularly, and suspicious activity reports must be filed promptly.

How does El Salvador compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Panama does not have a specific crypto license, making it a more flexible but less regulated environment. El Salvador offers a clear legal framework with tax benefits. For a flat fee of EUR 6,000, Consulting24 can set up a Panama company.

Can I offer services to EU clients with an El Salvador license?

No, an El Salvador license does not provide EU passporting. To serve EU clients, you would need a MiCA-compliant license in an EU member state like Lithuania or Estonia. Consulting24 advises on both options.
