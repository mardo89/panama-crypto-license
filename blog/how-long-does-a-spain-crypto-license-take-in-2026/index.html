What Is a Spain Crypto License in 2026?

Under MiCA, which is fully in force in 2026, a Spain crypto license is essentially a CASP authorisation granted by the Bank of Spain (Banco de España) and the National Securities Market Commission (CNMV). The license allows firms to provide crypto-asset services such as custody, exchange, and transfer within Spain and across the EU. Unlike the pre-MiCA regime where Spain only required a simple VASP registration with no minimum capital, the new framework imposes strict capital requirements, operational standards, and ongoing compliance obligations.

The Spanish regulator has been proactive in implementing MiCA, and as of 2026, all crypto service providers must hold a CASP license. The timeline for obtaining this license is influenced by the applicant's readiness, the complexity of services offered, and the regulator's review capacity. In practice, the total process from preparation to approval typically takes 6 to 12 months, but we break down each phase below.

Who Needs a Spain Crypto License?

Any entity that provides crypto-asset services in Spain or to Spanish residents must obtain a CASP license. This includes exchanges, wallet providers, custodians, brokers, and platforms facilitating crypto transfers. If you are a startup or an established firm planning to offer any of the following services, you need a license:

  • Exchange of crypto-assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for other crypto-assets
  • Custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • Execution of orders for crypto-assets
  • Placement of crypto-assets
  • Transfer services for crypto-assets
  • Advice on crypto-assets

Even if your company is based outside Spain, if you target Spanish customers, you must comply. The Spanish regulator has made it clear that passporting from other EU countries is possible under MiCA, but local requirements may still apply. License Type and Regulator

The Spain crypto license in 2026 is a CASP authorisation under MiCA. The primary regulators are:

  • Banco de España - responsible for anti-money laundering (AML) supervision and prudential oversight.
  • CNMV (Comisión Nacional del Mercado de Valores) - oversees conduct of business and investor protection.

Both regulators work together in a joint process. The application is submitted through the Banco de España's portal, but the CNMV also reviews the business plan and governance. The license is valid across the EU under the single passport, but you must maintain a registered office in Spain and have a local compliance officer.

There is no separate “crypto exchange license” in Spain; all services fall under the CASP umbrella. However, the capital requirement varies by service class, as detailed below.

Capital Requirement for a Spain Crypto License

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for a CASP license in Spain depends on the services provided. The tiers are as follows:

  • EUR 50,000 - for services that do not involve custody of client assets (e.g., order execution, advice).
  • EUR 125,000 - for services that involve custody of crypto-assets (e.g., wallet providers, custodians).
  • EUR 150,000 - for services that include both custody and exchange (e.g., full-service exchanges).

These capital requirements must be maintained at all times as part of the ongoing prudential obligations. The capital can be in the form of cash or highly liquid assets. In addition, firms must have professional indemnity insurance or a similar guarantee covering the value of assets under custody. Tax Treatment for Crypto in Spain

Spain has a relatively clear tax framework for crypto-assets. For companies, profits from crypto activities are subject to the standard corporate income tax rate of 25%. Capital gains from crypto trading are treated as ordinary income. VAT is not applicable on crypto-to-fiat exchanges, but other services may be subject to VAT. Additionally, Spain imposes a wealth tax on crypto holdings above certain thresholds, though this primarily affects individuals.

For crypto founders, it is crucial to structure the company and transactions to optimise tax liability. Spain has a network of double tax treaties, but the tax treatment can be complex. Consulting24 recommends working with a local tax advisor to ensure compliance. For a simpler tax environment, some founders compare Spain with Panama, which offers territorial taxation (no tax on foreign-source income) and a flat EUR 6,000 company setup fee.

Allowed Activities Under a Spain Crypto License

A Spain CASP license permits the following activities within the EU:

  • Exchange of crypto-assets for fiat or other crypto-assets
  • Custody and administration of crypto-assets
  • Execution of orders on behalf of clients
  • Placement of crypto-assets (underwriting or selling)
  • Transfer services for crypto-assets
  • Advice on crypto-assets
  • Management of crypto-asset portfolios

Notably, the license does not cover the issuance of crypto-assets (which may require a separate prospectus) or decentralised finance (DeFi) operations that are fully automated. The regulator expects all services to be provided with a clear segregation of client assets and strong AML controls. If you plan to offer services beyond the standard list, you should seek pre-application guidance from the regulator or an advisor.

  2. Company incorporation in Spain - You need a legal entity registered in Spain with a local registered address and directors.

Compliance and Trust: Ongoing Obligations

Once you hold a Spain crypto license, you must comply with ongoing requirements:

  • Periodic reporting - Submit quarterly and annual reports to the Bank of Spain on transaction volumes, client assets, and financial health.
  • AML/CTF compliance - Maintain strong AML procedures, conduct regular staff training, and file suspicious transaction reports.
  • Capital maintenance - Always hold the minimum capital requirement and notify the regulator of any significant changes.
  • Audits - An annual audit by an approved auditor is mandatory.
  • Changes in management - Any change in directors or beneficial owners must be approved by the regulator.

Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. Consulting24 provides ongoing compliance support to ensure you meet all obligations. This is general guidance, not legal advice. You should engage a local legal advisor for specific compliance matters.

Common Mistakes When Applying for a Spain Crypto License

Many applicants face delays or rejections due to avoidable errors. Common mistakes include:

  • Incomplete or inconsistent documentation - The application must be thorough. Missing details on beneficial ownership or source of funds can cause delays.
  • Underestimating capital requirements - Ensure you have the correct capital tier for your services and that the funds are fully traceable.
  • Inadequate AML policies - The regulator expects policies that are tailored to your business, not generic templates.
  • Poor governance structure - Directors and compliance officers must have relevant experience and be fit and proper.
  • Ignoring local presence requirements - You need a physical office in Spain and a local compliance officer.
