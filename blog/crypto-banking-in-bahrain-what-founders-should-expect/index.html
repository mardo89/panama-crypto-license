What Is Crypto Banking in Bahrain?

Crypto banking in Bahrain refers to the regulated provision of crypto asset services under the Central Bank of Bahrain's (CBB) Crypto Asset Module (CRA). This framework, established in 2019 and updated regularly, allows licensed entities to offer services such as custody, trading, and payment services for crypto assets. Unlike some jurisdictions that treat crypto as a commodity, Bahrain classifies crypto assets as a form of digital currency, subject to specific regulations.

The CBB's approach is principles-based, focusing on risk management, consumer protection, and anti-money laundering (AML) compliance. As of 2026, the framework is fully operational, with several licensed crypto banks and exchanges operating from the Bahrain FinTech Bay in Manama. The license is officially called a "Crypto Asset Services License" under the CRA Module, and it covers multiple service categories.

For founders, this means a clear path to offering services like crypto-to-fiat exchange, custody, and payment processing within a regulated environment. The CBB also requires a physical presence in Bahrain, including a registered office and local management. This requirement is more demanding than Panama, where a virtual office may suffice, but it provides a stronger regulatory standing.

Who Needs a Crypto Banking License in Bahrain?

Any entity that intends to provide crypto asset services to customers in Bahrain or from Bahrain requires a license from the CBB. This includes:

  • Exchanges offering crypto-to-fiat or crypto-to-crypto trading
  • Custodians holding crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Payment service providers using crypto for transfers or merchant services
  • Brokers facilitating crypto transactions
  • Portfolio managers dealing in crypto assets

Founders based outside Bahrain but targeting Bahraini customers also need a license, as the CBB applies a territorial scope. If you are already licensed in another jurisdiction, you may still need a Bahrain license for local operations. Consulting24 can advise on whether your business model triggers licensing requirements. For example, a crypto exchange serving users in the Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) region would likely need a Bahrain license if it markets to Bahraini residents.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Central Bank of Bahrain (CBB), specifically the Financial Institutions Supervision Directorate. The license is the Crypto Asset Services License, which falls under the CBB's Rulebook Volume 6 (Crypto Assets). There are two main categories:

CategoryAllowed Services
Category 1Operating a crypto asset exchange, matching orders, and providing custody
Category 2Providing crypto asset services without operating an exchange (e.g., custody only, advisory)

Each category has specific capital requirements and operational conditions. The CBB also distinguishes between "crypto assets" and "crypto tokens" (e.g., utility tokens), with different rules for each. As of 2026, the CBB has not yet adopted the EU's MiCA framework but is monitoring it. This means Bahrain's rules are independent, offering flexibility but also requiring careful interpretation.

Capital Requirement

The CBB sets minimum capital requirements based on the category and type of services. As of 2026:

  • Category 1 (exchange): Minimum capital of EUR 500,000 (or equivalent in BHD)
  • Category 2 (non-exchange services): Minimum capital of EUR 200,000
  • Higher capital may be required for more complex operations or if the entity also holds client funds

These figures are significantly higher than Panama's EUR 6,000 flat fee (which does not include capital requirements). However, Bahrain's capital requirement is comparable to other regulated jurisdictions like Lithuania (EUR 125,000 - 150,000 under MiCA). The capital must be maintained at all times and can be in the form of cash or liquid assets. For example, a Category 1 exchange would need to deposit at least EUR 500,000 in a Bahraini bank account and maintain that balance throughout the license period.

Tax Treatment

Bahrain offers a highly favorable tax environment for crypto businesses:

  • No corporate income tax on profits (except for oil and gas companies)
  • No value-added tax (VAT) on most services, including crypto transactions
  • No capital gains tax on crypto asset disposals
  • No withholding tax on dividends or interest

This makes Bahrain one of the most tax-efficient jurisdictions for crypto banking. However, founders should consider their home country's tax rules and any double taxation agreements. For example, a founder resident in a high-tax country may still face taxation on worldwide income, but the absence of local taxes reduces the overall burden. Consulting24 recommends consulting a tax advisor familiar with Bahraini law to structure operations optimally.

Allowed Activities

Under the Crypto Asset Services License, the following activities are permitted:

  • Operating a crypto asset exchange (order book or market making)
  • Custody of crypto assets
  • Brokerage services (matching buyers and sellers)
  • Payment services using crypto (including merchant acquiring)
  • Portfolio management of crypto assets
  • Advisory services on crypto assets

Activities that are NOT allowed include issuing unregulated tokens (unless approved by the CBB) and offering derivatives on crypto assets without a separate license. The CBB also prohibits using crypto assets for anonymous transactions; all services must comply with AML/KYC requirements. For example, a licensed exchange cannot offer privacy coins like Monero without explicit approval.

Step-by-Step Process

Obtaining a crypto banking license in Bahrain involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application preparation: Define your business model, select the appropriate category, and prepare a detailed business plan and AML policy.
  2. Engage a local sponsor or agent: Foreign entities must have a local partner or register a Bahraini company with a local director.
  3. Submit application: File the application with the CBB, including all required documents (financial statements, shareholder details, compliance manual).
  4. Review and due diligence: The CBB conducts a thorough review, including background checks on key personnel and shareholders.
  5. In-principle approval: If satisfied, the CBB grants an in-principle approval, allowing you to set up the legal entity and operational infrastructure.
  6. Final approval and license issuance: After meeting all conditions (e.g., capital deposit, office setup, system testing), the CBB issues the final license.

One of the key challenges for crypto businesses is access to traditional banking. Bahrain has a proactive approach: several local banks (e.g., Bank ABC, Al Salam Bank) offer accounts to licensed crypto entities. The CBB also encourages banks to provide services to crypto firms, provided they comply with AML regulations.

Payment integration is straightforward: licensed entities can connect to the local payment systems (BenefitPay) and international networks (SWIFT). For crypto-to-fiat conversions, the CBB requires that fiat is held in segregated client accounts. Consulting24 can help you identify banking partners that are crypto-friendly. Compared to Panama, where banking access can be more challenging due to correspondent banking restrictions, Bahrain offers a more open environment.

Compliance and Trust

Compliance is critical for maintaining a Bahrain crypto license. The CBB requires:

  • AML/KYC procedures aligned with FATF recommendations
  • Regular reporting on transactions and suspicious activities
  • Audit by an approved external auditor
  • Appointment of a compliance officer and a money laundering reporting officer (MLRO)

Non-compliance can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. Consulting24 advises on setting up a strong compliance framework. Note: this is general guidance, not legal advice. Always consult a qualified lawyer for your specific situation. For example, the CBB expects transaction monitoring systems to detect unusual patterns, and failure to report suspicious transactions can lead to penalties.

Common Mistakes

Founders often make these errors when applying for a Bahrain crypto license:

  • Underestimating capital requirements: The EUR 500,000 minimum for exchanges is a hard requirement; many applicants fail to demonstrate sufficient liquid capital.
  • Incomplete AML documentation: The CBB scrutinizes AML policies; generic templates are rejected.
  • Ignoring local presence rules: A registered office and local management are mandatory; remote operations are not permitted.
  • Overlooking ongoing compliance costs: Annual fees, audit costs, and compliance salaries add up; budget for at least EUR 20,000 per year.
  • Assuming fast approval: The 6-12 month timeline is realistic; rushing the application leads to errors.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto exchange license in Bahrain?

For Category 1 (exchange), the minimum capital is EUR 500,000. For Category 2 (non-exchange services), it is EUR 200,000. These amounts must be maintained in liquid assets.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Bahrain?

The typical timeline is 6 to 12 months from application to final approval. Delays can occur if documents are incomplete or if the CBB requires additional information.

Does Bahrain tax crypto profits?

No. Bahrain has no corporate income tax (except for oil and gas), no VAT on crypto services, and no capital gains tax. This makes it highly tax-efficient for crypto businesses.

Can I operate a crypto exchange in Bahrain without a license?

No. Any entity providing crypto asset services to Bahraini residents or from Bahrain must obtain a Crypto Asset Services License from the CBB. Unlicensed operations are illegal.

What are the ongoing costs after obtaining a Bahrain crypto license?

Annual license fees range from EUR 10,000 to 25,000. Additionally, you need to budget for compliance salaries, audit costs, and office rent, totaling at least EUR 20,000 per year.

Do I need a local office in Bahrain?

Yes. The CBB requires a physical presence, including a registered office and local management. A virtual office is not sufficient.

Is Bahrain's crypto license recognized internationally?

Bahrain is a FATF member, so its license meets global AML standards. However, recognition depends on the counterparty jurisdiction. It is well-regarded in the GCC and Middle East.

Can I use a Bahrain license to serve customers in other countries?

Yes, but you must comply with the laws of the customers' countries. The CBB license allows you to operate from Bahrain, but you may need additional registrations elsewhere.

How does Bahrain compare to Panama for crypto banking?

Panama is cheaper (EUR 6,000 flat) and faster (2-3 months), but has no dedicated crypto regulator. Bahrain offers a comprehensive regulatory framework and tax advantages, but costs more and takes longer.

What happens if I fail to comply with CBB regulations?

Non-compliance can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. The CBB conducts regular audits and expects strict adherence to AML/KYC rules.
