What Is a Switzerland Crypto Company and Its Costs?

A Switzerland crypto company is typically a corporation (AG or GmbH) that engages in blockchain-related activities such as trading, custody, or token issuance. The costs include company formation, regulatory licensing, ongoing compliance, and operational expenses. In 2026, the Swiss Financial Market Supervisory Authority (FINMA) requires a license for most crypto activities under the Financial Services Act (FinSA) and Anti-Money Laundering Act (AMLA).

Key cost components include:

  • Company registration and notary fees: CHF 5,000-10,000
  • Regulatory application fees: CHF 10,000-50,000
  • Minimum capital requirements: CHF 100,000-500,000 depending on activity
  • Annual compliance costs: CHF 20,000-100,000
  • Office and staff costs: variable

Total first-year costs typically range from CHF 150,000 to over CHF 500,000. For a cost-effective alternative, explore our Panama crypto license at a flat EUR 6,000.

To give a concrete example, a startup offering crypto custody and exchange services would need a banking license, requiring CHF 500,000 capital and likely CHF 200,000 in first-year legal and compliance fees. In contrast, a token issuance project might only need a FinTech license with CHF 100,000 capital and CHF 100,000 in setup costs. These figures highlight the importance of matching your business model to the right license type.

License Type and Regulator

In Switzerland, the regulator is FINMA (Swiss Financial Market Supervisory Authority). Crypto activities fall under two main license categories:

  • FinTech License: For deposit-taking up to CHF 100 million, with lighter requirements. Minimum capital CHF 100,000.
  • Banking License: For full-scale banking activities including crypto custody and trading. Minimum capital CHF 500,000.

Additionally, asset managers and advisors need a license under the Financial Services Act (FinSA). For pure crypto exchange or custody, a banking license is typically required. The application process is rigorous, taking 6-12 months. Capital Requirement

Switzerland imposes minimum capital requirements based on the license type:

  • FinTech License: CHF 100,000 (approx. EUR 95,000) - must be fully paid in cash.
  • Banking License: CHF 500,000 (approx. EUR 475,000) - for full banking activities.
  • Asset Manager: No fixed minimum but must have sufficient equity for operations.

Capital must be deposited in a Swiss bank account and cannot be withdrawn without FINMA approval. This is a significant barrier for startups. Tax Treatment

Switzerland has a favorable tax regime for crypto companies. Key points:

  • Corporate income tax: Effective rate 12-21% depending on canton (e.g., Zug ~12%, Zurich ~21%).
  • VAT: 7.7% standard; crypto transactions may be exempt if considered payment tokens.
  • Wealth tax: Cantonal, typically 0.1-0.5% on net assets.
  • Stamp duty: 1% on equity issuance over CHF 1 million.

Switzerland does not tax capital gains for private individuals, but corporate gains are taxed. For tax optimization, many founders combine a Swiss holding company with an operating entity in a lower-tax jurisdiction like Panama, which has 0% corporate tax on foreign income. See our comparison with Lithuania for more details. For instance, a Swiss company earning CHF 1 million in profit would pay around CHF 120,000 in tax in Zug, while a Panama company would pay 0% on non-Panama-source income.

Banking and Payments

Switzerland has a well-developed banking sector, but crypto companies face challenges opening accounts. Many traditional banks (UBS, Credit Suisse) are cautious. Specialized crypto-friendly banks include:

  • SEBA Bank - licensed crypto bank
  • Sygnum Bank - digital asset bank
  • FlowBank - offers crypto services

Account opening requires a valid license and due diligence. Minimum deposits range from CHF 50,000-200,000. For payment processing, Swiss companies can use SEPA and SWIFT. Compliance and Trust

Switzerland has a strong compliance framework. Ongoing obligations include:

  • Reporting: Periodic reports to FINMA on financial health and compliance.
  • Capital adequacy: Maintain minimum capital at all times.

What is the minimum capital required for a Swiss crypto license?

For a FinTech license, CHF 100,000; for a banking license, CHF 500,000. Capital must be fully paid in cash and deposited in a Swiss bank account. It cannot be withdrawn without FINMA approval.

Licensed activities include operating a crypto exchange, custody services, token issuance, crypto lending, asset management, and payment systems. Activities must be within the license scope; unlicensed deposit-taking is prohibited.
