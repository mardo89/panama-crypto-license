Overview of Crypto Taxation in the Czech Republic

The Czech Republic treats cryptocurrency as an asset for tax purposes, not as legal tender. This means that transactions involving crypto are generally subject to income tax or capital gains tax, depending on the nature of the activity. The key legislation is the Income Tax Act, which has been updated to address digital assets. For individuals, crypto held for more than three years is exempt from capital gains tax, provided the total annual income from disposals does not exceed CZK 100,000 (approximately EUR 4,000). For businesses, crypto is treated as inventory or intangible assets, and profits are taxed as corporate income.

VAT treatment is also important: the European Court of Justice ruled that exchanges of cryptocurrency for fiat are exempt from VAT, and the Czech Republic follows this principle. However, services like mining or staking may be subject to VAT if they are considered a supply of services. The tax year is the calendar year, and residents are taxed on worldwide income, while non-residents are taxed only on Czech-source income. Who Needs to Pay Czech Republic Crypto Tax?

Any individual or legal entity that is a tax resident of the Czech Republic and engages in crypto transactions may be liable for tax. This includes:

  • Individuals who buy, sell, or trade crypto as an investment or business
  • Companies that accept crypto as payment, mine, or operate exchanges
  • Founders receiving tokens as payment or investment

Non-residents are only taxed on crypto activities that have a source in the Czech Republic, such as mining using Czech-based servers or operating a business from a Czech permanent establishment. It is important to distinguish between personal investment (subject to capital gains) and business activity (subject to income tax). The Czech tax authorities look at frequency, volume, and intention to determine the nature. For example, a founder who trades daily on a Czech exchange may be deemed to be carrying on a business, while a holder who sells once after three years is likely an investor. License Type and Regulator

The Czech National Bank (ČNB) is the primary regulator for crypto activities under the EU Anti-Money Laundering Directive (AMLD) and the Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA), which is fully in force as of 2026. Under MiCA, companies offering services like exchange, custody, or wallet management must obtain a license from the ČNB. The license types are:

  • Class 1: Minor services (e.g., advisory) - capital requirement EUR 50,000
  • Class 2: Custody and exchange - capital requirement EUR 125,000
  • Class 3: Full services including trading platform - capital requirement EUR 150,000

These capital requirements are in line with MiCA standards. For founders, this means that if you plan to operate a crypto business in the Czech Republic, you need to budget for licensing and compliance costs. Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the Czech Republic imposes minimum capital requirements based on the type of crypto services offered. As noted, these are EUR 50,000 for minor services, EUR 125,000 for custody and exchange, and EUR 150,000 for full services. This capital must be maintained at all times and can be in the form of cash or liquid assets. The ČNB may also require additional capital based on operational risk assessments.

Tax Treatment of Crypto in the Czech Republic

The tax treatment depends on whether you are an individual or a business. For individuals:

  • Capital gains: Exempt if held for more than 3 years and annual gains under CZK 100,000.
  • Otherwise, gains are added to income and taxed at progressive rates (15% up to CZK 1.9 million, 23% above).

For businesses:

  • Corporate income tax rate is 19% (2026).
  • VAT: Exempt for exchange of crypto to fiat; mining may be subject to VAT if considered a service.

Founders should keep detailed records of all transactions, including dates, amounts, and counterparties. The tax authorities have been increasing scrutiny, so accurate reporting is essential. For example, if you receive crypto as payment for services, you must record the fair market value at the time of receipt. Consulting24 recommends working with a local tax advisor. For a jurisdiction with zero capital gains tax on crypto, consider Panama.

Allowed Activities Under the Czech Crypto License

Once licensed by the ČNB, a CASP can perform the following activities under MiCA:

  • Exchange of crypto for fiat or other crypto
  • Custody and administration of crypto on behalf of clients
  • Operation of a trading platform
  • Transfer services (sending crypto on behalf of clients)
  • Advice on crypto assets

These activities are subject to ongoing compliance, including AML/KYC procedures, reporting to the regulator, and maintaining adequate capital. The license is valid across the EU under the MiCA passport, allowing you to serve clients in other member states without additional licensing. However, you must still comply with local tax and consumer protection laws in each country. Step-by-Step Process for Obtaining a Czech Crypto License

The application process involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates the process for founders, though we do not file directly in the Czech Republic.

  1. Pre-application preparation: Define the scope of services, prepare business plan, AML policies, and governance documents. This typically takes 1-2 months.
  2. Capital deposit: Ensure the required minimum capital is in a Czech bank account. You may need to open a corporate bank account first, which can be challenging.
  3. Submit application: File with the Czech National Bank, including all required documentation. The ČNB may request additional information.
  4. Review and due diligence: The ČNB assesses the application, including background checks on directors and beneficial owners. This stage can take 3-6 months.
  5. Approval and licensing: If approved, the license is issued. This can take 3 to 18 months depending on complexity.

Banking and Payment Services for Crypto Firms

Finding a bank in the Czech Republic that works with crypto companies can be challenging. Many traditional banks are cautious due to AML concerns. However, some banks, such as Equa Bank (now part of Raiffeisenbank) and Fio Banka, have been known to accept crypto firms, but often with strict due diligence. You may need to provide the license approval, business plan, and proof of AML procedures.

Alternatively, founders often use payment service providers (PSPs) or neobanks that specialize in crypto-friendly banking. These include companies like Revolut Business, Coinbase Commerce, or dedicated crypto payment processors. For companies licensed under MiCA, the regulatory approval can help open bank accounts, as the license demonstrates compliance. It is also possible to use an EMI (Electronic Money Institution) license for payment services. Compliance is critical in the Czech Republic. The ČNB expects licensed firms to have strong AML/KYC procedures, report suspicious transactions, and maintain accurate records. Failure to comply can result in fines, revocation of the license, or even criminal charges. The regulator conducts periodic inspections and may request audits.

Founders should also be aware of the tax reporting obligations. The Czech tax authorities have access to information from exchanges and banks, so underreporting is risky. Founders often make these mistakes:

  • Failing to register for VAT when providing taxable services.

Frequently asked questions

What is the tax rate for crypto gains in the Czech Republic for individuals?

For individuals, crypto gains are taxed as capital gains or income. If held for more than 3 years and annual gains are under CZK 100,000, they are exempt. Otherwise, gains are added to income and taxed at progressive rates: 15% up to CZK 1.9 million and 23% above. This applies to Czech tax residents.

Is crypto mining taxable in the Czech Republic?

Yes, mining is generally considered a business activity and is subject to corporate income tax at 19% if done regularly. VAT may also apply if mining is considered a supply of services. The tax authorities look at the frequency and scale of mining to determine if it is a business.

Do I need a license to operate a crypto exchange in the Czech Republic?

Yes, as of 2026 under MiCA, any crypto-asset service provider (CASP) must obtain a license from the Czech National Bank. The license class depends on the services offered, with capital requirements ranging from EUR 50,000 to EUR 150,000.

How long does it take to get a Czech crypto license?

The timeline varies by service class and application complexity. Typically, it takes 3 to 6 months for Class 1, 6 to 12 months for Class 2, and 9 to 18 months for Class 3. This includes preparation, submission, and regulatory review.

Can I use a Czech crypto license to serve clients in other EU countries?

Yes, under the MiCA passport, a Czech license allows you to offer services across the EU without additional licensing in each member state. However, you must comply with local tax and consumer protection laws.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements for a Czech crypto license?

Ongoing requirements include maintaining AML/KYC procedures, reporting suspicious transactions, submitting periodic reports to the ČNB, and maintaining the required capital. The regulator may conduct inspections and audits.

Is there a minimum capital requirement for a crypto license in the Czech Republic?

Yes, under MiCA, the minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for Class 1, EUR 125,000 for Class 2, and EUR 150,000 for Class 3. This must be maintained at all times in liquid assets.

What is the corporate tax rate for crypto businesses in the Czech Republic?

The corporate income tax rate is 19% as of 2026. This applies to profits from crypto activities, including trading, mining, and exchange operations. VAT exemptions apply to crypto-to-fiat exchanges.

Can I avoid Czech crypto tax by holding for more than 3 years?

For individuals, holding crypto for more than 3 years can exempt gains from tax, but only if total annual gains from disposals are under CZK 100,000. If gains exceed this threshold, the entire amount is taxable. This exemption does not apply to businesses.

What are the alternatives to the Czech Republic for crypto licensing?

Do I need a local bank account for a Czech crypto license?

Yes, you will need a Czech bank account to deposit the required capital and for operational transactions. Opening such an account can be challenging, but a license approval often helps. Consulting24 can assist with banking in jurisdictions we serve directly.

What happens if I operate a crypto business in the Czech Republic without a license?

Operating without a license is illegal and can result in fines, criminal charges, and confiscation of assets. The ČNB actively monitors unlicensed activities. It is essential to obtain the proper license or choose a jurisdiction with a simpler regime like Panama.
