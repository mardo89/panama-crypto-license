What Is Crypto Banking in Hong Kong?

Crypto banking in Hong Kong refers to the provision of digital asset custody, trading, and payment services by licensed entities. Under the 2026 framework, the SFC regulates virtual asset trading platforms (VATPs) and crypto custodians, while the HKMA oversees stablecoin issuers and banks offering crypto services. The regime is aligned with international standards, including FATF recommendations.

Key features include mandatory licensing for any business dealing with crypto assets, strict AML/KYC requirements, and capital adequacy rules. Unlike some jurisdictions, Hong Kong does not have a separate crypto banking license; instead, firms must obtain a VATP license or a banking license with crypto permissions. This dual-track approach creates opportunities but also adds complexity.

For example, a platform offering both spot trading and custody must comply with both SFC and HKMA rules if it also handles stablecoins. This layered regulation can be challenging for startups. In contrast, Panama offers a single crypto license that covers a broad range of activities with minimal regulatory overlap.

Who Needs a Crypto Banking License in Hong Kong?

Any entity that provides crypto custody, trading, or payment services to Hong Kong residents must be licensed. This includes:

  • Centralized exchanges offering spot or derivative trading
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Stablecoin issuers
  • Payment processors facilitating crypto transactions

Founders planning to serve institutional clients or retail investors must comply. The SFC has a clear licensing roadmap, and operating without a license can lead to severe penalties. For startups, the high compliance burden often makes Hong Kong less attractive compared to Panama, where the licensing process is faster and cheaper.

Even decentralized finance (DeFi) platforms may fall under the SFC's purview if they offer centralized control or custody. The SFC has issued guidance that any platform with control over user assets must be licensed. This broad interpretation means many DeFi projects need to consider licensing.

License Type and Regulator

The primary regulator for crypto banking in Hong Kong is the Securities and Futures Commission (SFC). The relevant license is the Virtual Asset Trading Platform (VATP) license, which covers both centralized and decentralized platforms. For stablecoin issuers, the HKMA requires a separate license under the Stablecoin Ordinance.

The SFC imposes strict fit-and-proper criteria for directors and shareholders. Additionally, the HKMA regulates banks that offer crypto services, requiring them to maintain separate capital buffers. Capital Requirement

The SFC requires VATP licensees to maintain a minimum paid-up capital of HKD 5 million (approx. EUR 590,000) for trading platforms. For custodians, the requirement is HKD 2 million (approx. EUR 235,000). Additionally, firms must hold liquid assets equal to at least 12 months of operating expenses.

These capital requirements are high compared to other jurisdictions. For example, in Panama, there is no minimum capital requirement for crypto licenses, making it a more accessible option for startups. Tax Treatment of Crypto in Hong Kong

Hong Kong operates a territorial tax system: only income sourced in Hong Kong is taxable. Crypto trading profits are generally considered capital gains and are not taxed if the trading is conducted offshore. However, if a company is deemed to be carrying on business in Hong Kong, profits from crypto activities may be subject to profits tax at 16.5%.

Stablecoin interest and custody fees are taxable as ordinary income. The Inland Revenue Department has issued guidelines clarifying that crypto-to-crypto trades are taxable events. For founders, proper tax structuring is essential. In contrast, Panama taxes only locally sourced income, and crypto gains are exempt, offering a more favorable tax environment.

For example, a Hong Kong-based exchange earning trading fees from overseas clients may still be taxed if the trading platform is operated from Hong Kong. Founders should consult tax advisors to optimize their structure. Consulting24 can provide referrals to tax experts familiar with crypto.

Allowed Activities Under a Hong Kong Crypto License

A VATP license permits the following activities:

  • Operating a crypto exchange (spot and derivatives)
  • Providing custody services
  • Facilitating crypto-to-fiat conversions
  • Staking and lending (with additional approval)

Activities not allowed include unlicensed stablecoin issuance and offering complex derivatives to retail investors. The SFC also restricts margin trading for retail clients. For founders seeking a broader scope of activities, jurisdictions like Panama offer more flexibility with fewer restrictions.

For instance, a platform wanting to offer used trading to retail users would face limitations in Hong Kong. In Panama, such activities are generally permitted under the crypto license, subject to general business regulations.

Step-by-Step Process for Obtaining a Hong Kong Crypto License

  1. Pre-application assessment: Engage legal counsel to review business model and eligibility.
  2. Prepare documentation: Draft business plan, AML policy, risk management framework, and financial projections.
  3. Submit application: File with the SFC along with the application fee.
  4. Review and interview: The SFC may request additional information and interview key personnel.
  5. On-site inspection: Regulators may visit your premises to verify compliance.
  6. License grant: If approved, you receive the VATP license with conditions.
  7. Ongoing compliance: Submit periodic reports and maintain capital requirements.

This process typically takes 6-12 months. For a faster alternative, consider the application process in Panama, which is streamlined and can be completed in weeks.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a VATP license and a banking license in Hong Kong?

A VATP license from the SFC allows operation of a crypto trading platform and custody services. A banking license from the HKMA is needed for banks to offer crypto services directly. Most crypto firms apply for a VATP license. The two are separate and require different compliance frameworks.

Can a foreign company apply for a Hong Kong crypto license?

Yes, but the company must have a physical presence in Hong Kong, including a registered office and local management. The SFC requires key personnel to be based in Hong Kong. Foreign entities often set up a Hong Kong subsidiary to apply.

How long does it take to get a Hong Kong crypto license in 2026?

The process typically takes 6 to 12 months from application to approval. Delays can occur if the SFC requests additional information or if the applicant fails to meet fit-and-proper criteria. Pre-application preparation can take an additional 3-6 months.

What are the ongoing compliance costs for a Hong Kong crypto license?

Annual compliance costs range from EUR 20,000 to 50,000, including audit fees, compliance officer salary, and transaction monitoring systems. These costs are higher than in Panama, where ongoing compliance is minimal.

Is Hong Kong tax-friendly for crypto businesses?

Hong Kong's territorial tax system means only locally sourced income is taxed at 16.5%. Offshore crypto trading profits may be exempt, but careful structuring is needed. Panama offers a more favorable tax regime with no tax on foreign-source income.

Can I use a Panama crypto license to bank in Hong Kong?

Yes, some founders incorporate in Panama and then open corporate bank accounts in Hong Kong. Panama's regulatory reputation can facilitate banking relationships. Consulting24 can assist with this dual structure.

What happens if I operate without a license in Hong Kong?

Operating without a license is illegal and can result in fines, imprisonment, and seizure of assets. The SFC actively enforces against unlicensed platforms. It is essential to obtain proper licensing before offering services to Hong Kong residents.

Does Hong Kong regulate stablecoins?

Yes, the HKMA regulates stablecoin issuers under the Stablecoin Ordinance. Issuers must obtain a separate license and meet capital and reserve requirements. This adds another layer of regulation for stablecoin projects.

How does Hong Kong compare to Singapore for crypto licensing?

Both have high standards, but Hong Kong's VATP license is more straightforward than Singapore's Payment Services Act. Singapore requires a Major Payment Institution license for crypto, which has higher capital requirements. Hong Kong is often seen as more crypto-friendly.
