What Is the Cost of a Crypto License in Estonia?

The cost of a crypto license in Estonia in 2026 is not a single number. It includes state fees, minimum capital, and ongoing compliance costs. The total initial outlay typically ranges from EUR 50,000 to over EUR 150,000, depending on the services you intend to offer. This is a significant increase from the pre-MiCA era when no minimum capital was required. The main cost drivers are:

  • Minimum capital requirement (EUR 50,000 to 150,000)
  • State application fee (approximately EUR 3,000 to 5,000)
  • Legal and consulting fees (EUR 10,000 to 20,000)
  • Bank account setup and compliance (EUR 2,000 to 5,000)
  • Annual supervisory fees and AML compliance costs

It is important to note that the capital requirement must be maintained as equity, not borrowed. The exact cost of a crypto license in Estonia for your specific business can be confirmed during a consultation with our experts. For example, a startup offering only exchange services would need at least EUR 125,000 in capital, plus around EUR 15,000 in fees, totaling EUR 140,000 initial outlay. In contrast, Panama's flat fee of EUR 6,000 with no capital requirement can be a fraction of that cost.

Who Needs an Estonian Crypto License?

Any business that provides crypto asset services in or from Estonia needs a license. This includes exchanges, wallet providers, custodians, and brokers. The license is mandatory for both Estonian companies and foreign entities targeting the Estonian market. If you plan to offer services to EU residents, an Estonian CASP license allows passporting across the EU under MiCA. The cost of a crypto license in Estonia is an investment for serious operators who want regulatory credibility and access to the European market. For smaller projects or those testing the waters, Panama might be a more affordable entry point at a flat EUR 6,000. For example, a small trading platform with limited volume may find Panama's no-capital requirement and lower ongoing costs more suitable.

License Type and Regulator

The Estonian crypto license is now a CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) license under MiCA. The regulator is the Financial Supervisory Authority (Finantsinspektsioon). There is no longer a separate VASP registration; all crypto service providers must be licensed as CASPs. The license categories are based on the services provided, each with a corresponding capital tier. The cost of a crypto license in Estonia is influenced by which category you apply for. The regulator is known for thorough due diligence and expects strong AML/KYC procedures. For instance, applying for a Tier 3 license (custodian services) requires EUR 150,000 capital and more detailed operational policies, increasing both upfront and ongoing costs.

Capital Requirement

The capital requirement is the largest component of the cost of a crypto license in Estonia. Under MiCA, the minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for simple services (e.g., order execution), EUR 125,000 for exchange and wallet services, and EUR 150,000 for custodial services. This capital must be held in an Estonian bank account and cannot be withdrawn without regulatory approval. It serves as a buffer for operational risks. For comparison, Panama has no minimum capital requirement, making it a lower-cost entry point. For a business starting with limited funds, Panama's EUR 6,000 flat fee is far more accessible than Estonia's EUR 125,000 capital plus fees.

Tax Treatment

Estonia has a unique corporate tax system: profits are not taxed until distributed as dividends. The corporate income tax rate is 20% on distributed profits (effective rate 20/80 = 25% on gross dividend). Crypto transactions are treated as business income and subject to standard corporate tax rules. VAT is not applicable on crypto-to-fiat exchanges in most cases. The cost of a crypto license in Estonia should be considered alongside the tax benefits of profit deferral. However, for founders seeking tax simplicity, Panama offers territorial taxation (no tax on foreign-source income) and no capital gains tax on crypto. For example, a crypto trading firm that reinvests profits can defer Estonian tax indefinitely, but if it distributes dividends, the effective rate is 25%. Panama's zero tax on foreign income can be more attractive for non-resident owners.

Allowed Activities Under the License

With an Estonian CASP license, you can offer the following services: exchange between crypto and fiat, exchange between crypto assets, custody and administration of crypto assets, operation of a trading platform, transfer services, and advice on crypto assets. The cost of a crypto license in Estonia covers all these activities, but you must specify which ones you intend to provide. Each activity may have additional compliance requirements. Panama's license is more flexible, allowing a wide range of activities without the need for multiple license categories. For instance, a business wanting to offer both exchange and custody services in Estonia would need a Tier 3 license (EUR 150,000 capital), while Panama's single license covers both at a flat EUR 6,000.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Company Setup: Register an Estonian company (e-residency is popular) with a physical address and local management.
  2. Prepare Documentation: Business plan, AML/KYC policies, risk assessment, financial projections, and governance structure.
  3. Capital Deposit: Open an Estonian bank account and deposit the minimum capital.
  4. Submit Application: File with Finantsinspektsioon, including all documents and the state fee.
  5. Regulatory Review: The regulator assesses the application, may request changes, and conducts interviews.
  6. Approval: Once approved, you receive the CASP license and can begin operations.

Frequently asked questions

What is the total cost of a crypto license in Estonia in 2026?

The total cost ranges from EUR 70,000 to over EUR 180,000, including minimum capital (EUR 50,000-150,000), state fees (EUR 3,000-5,000), and professional fees (EUR 10,000-20,000). The exact amount depends on the license tier and services offered.

Is the capital requirement refundable?

The minimum capital must be maintained as equity in the company. It is not refundable upon exit but can be returned after regulatory approval if the license is surrendered or revoked.

How long does it take to get an Estonian crypto license?

The application process typically takes 3-6 months. Delays can occur if the regulator requests additional documentation or if the application is incomplete.

Can I use e-residency to apply for an Estonian crypto license?

Yes, e-residency allows you to register a company remotely, but you still need a local director and physical address. The license application can be handled digitally.

What are the ongoing compliance costs after obtaining the license?

Annual compliance costs include supervisory fees (EUR 2,000-10,000), AML audits, and reporting. Total annual costs typically range from EUR 5,000 to EUR 20,000.

Does the Estonian crypto license allow passporting across the EU?

Yes, under MiCA, an Estonian CASP license allows you to provide services across all EU member states without additional licensing, subject to notification requirements.

What happens if I fail to maintain the minimum capital?

The regulator can impose fines, suspend, or revoke the license. The capital must be maintained at all times as a condition of the license.

Can I apply for a lower capital tier if I offer multiple services?

No, the capital tier is determined by the highest-risk service you offer. For example, if you offer both exchange and custody, you need the Tier 3 capital of EUR 150,000.

Is Panama a good alternative to Estonia for a crypto license?

Yes, Panama offers a flat fee of EUR 6,000, no minimum capital, territorial tax, and faster processing (4-6 weeks). It is ideal for startups and businesses not targeting the EU market.
