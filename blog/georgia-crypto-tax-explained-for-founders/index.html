What Is Georgia's Crypto Tax Regime?

Georgia offers a unique tax environment for crypto founders. The country does not tax capital gains on cryptocurrency for individuals, and businesses can benefit from a low corporate tax rate. The key features include:

  • 0% capital gains tax on crypto-to-fiat conversions for individuals
  • Corporate income tax of 15% on distributed profits only
  • No VAT on crypto transactions
  • Special tax regime for IT companies: 5% withholding tax on dividends

This makes Georgia one of the most tax-efficient jurisdictions for crypto founders in 2026. For example, a founder who trades crypto frequently and realizes gains of EUR 500,000 annually would pay zero personal tax on those gains in Georgia, whereas in many EU countries they would face 20-30% capital gains tax. Businesses that reinvest profits can defer corporate tax indefinitely, as tax is only triggered on distributions. The IT regime is particularly attractive for blockchain startups: if the company qualifies as an IT company, dividends paid to shareholders are taxed at just 5% instead of the standard 15% corporate income tax. This can result in significant savings compared to jurisdictions like Estonia where the corporate tax rate is 20% on distributed profits.

License Type and Regulator

Georgia does not have a specific crypto license regime as of 2026. Instead, crypto businesses operate under general business laws and are supervised by the National Bank of Georgia (NBG) for anti-money laundering (AML) compliance. The NBG requires crypto exchanges and custodial wallet providers to register and comply with AML/CFT regulations.

For founders, this means you can set up a company and start operations without a costly licensing process, but you must register with the NBG and implement AML procedures. This is simpler than obtaining a full license in jurisdictions like Lithuania or Estonia, which require a VASP license under MiCA with minimum capital of EUR 50,000 to 150,000. However, the NBG's AML registration is not a free pass: you must submit a detailed business plan, risk assessment, and AML policies. The NBG may also conduct on-site inspections. Failure to register can result in fines of up to GEL 500,000 (approx EUR 160,000) and criminal liability for the directors. Therefore, while the process is less burdensome than a full license, it still requires serious compliance commitment.

Capital Requirement

Georgia does not impose a minimum capital requirement for crypto businesses. You can register a company with a nominal capital of GEL 1 (approximately EUR 0.30). However, for operational credibility and to open a bank account, a higher capital may be required by banks, typically around EUR 10,000 to 50,000.

This is much lower than the EU's MiCA capital requirements, which range from EUR 50,000 to 150,000 depending on the service class. For founders on a budget, Georgia offers a low-cost entry point. For example, a small crypto consultancy or a solo trader can start with just the registration fee and a minimal bank deposit. However, if you plan to operate a high-volume exchange, banks may ask for a larger capital buffer to demonstrate financial stability. Some banks also require a minimum average balance of EUR 5,000 to avoid monthly fees. It is advisable to discuss capital requirements with your bank before incorporation.

Tax Treatment

Georgia's tax treatment for crypto is straightforward:

  • Individuals: No capital gains tax on crypto. Crypto-to-crypto trades are also tax-free. Only when crypto is converted to fiat and transferred to a bank account may there be a tax event, but gains are exempt.
  • Businesses: Corporate income tax is 15% on distributed profits (profits that are paid out as dividends). Retained earnings are tax-free. Additionally, IT companies can opt for a 5% withholding tax on dividends.
  • VAT: Crypto transactions are exempt from VAT.

This regime is highly favourable compared to many European countries where capital gains tax can exceed 30%. For example, a founder who earns EUR 1 million in crypto trading profits as an individual would pay zero tax in Georgia, whereas in France or Germany the tax could be over EUR 300,000. For businesses, the ability to defer tax by retaining profits is a powerful tool for growth. The IT company regime is particularly beneficial for blockchain startups: if the company qualifies (e.g., by having a software development focus), dividends can be taxed at 5% instead of 15%. This can result in an effective tax rate of 5% on all profits distributed, which is lower than most corporate tax rates worldwide.

Allowed Activities

Under Georgian law, the following crypto activities are allowed without a specific license, subject to AML registration:

  • Crypto exchange (centralized and peer-to-peer)
  • Crypto wallet services (custodial and non-custodial)
  • Mining and staking
  • Initial coin offerings (ICOs) and token sales
  • DeFi protocols and lending platforms

However, activities that involve derivatives or securities may fall under securities laws and require additional authorization from the National Bank of Georgia. It is advisable to consult with local legal experts. For example, if you offer crypto futures or options, the NBG may classify these as financial instruments and require a separate license under the Securities Market Law. Similarly, tokenized assets that represent equity or debt may be treated as securities. In practice, most crypto spot trading and wallet services are covered by the AML registration. Step-by-Step Process

To set up a crypto business in Georgia, follow these steps:

  1. Company registration: Choose a legal form (LLC is most common) and register with the National Agency of Public Registry. You will need a local registered address and a director (can be foreign). The registration fee is around EUR 200-500 and takes 1-2 weeks.
  2. Open a bank account: Approach a Georgian bank (e.g., TBC Bank, Bank of Georgia) with your company documents. Be prepared to explain your business model and source of funds. Some banks require a minimum deposit of EUR 10,000. The process takes 1-4 weeks.
  3. Register with the NBG: Submit an AML registration application. This includes providing information about beneficial owners, AML policies, and a risk assessment. The NBG will review and may request additional information. The process takes 2-4 weeks.
  4. Implement AML procedures: Appoint an AML officer, set up transaction monitoring, and establish customer due diligence processes. You may need to purchase AML software or hire a compliance consultant.
  5. Begin operations: Once registered, you can start your crypto business. Ensure ongoing compliance with NBG reporting requirements, including annual AML audits and suspicious transaction reports.

Banking and Payments

Georgia has a well-developed banking sector with several banks that are crypto-friendly. Major banks like TBC Bank and Bank of Georgia accept crypto businesses, though they may require a thorough due diligence process. Some banks may ask for a minimum deposit or turnover to maintain the account.

For international payments, Georgia uses the SWIFT system and is connected to the European banking network. You can receive and send euros and US dollars easily. However, some banks may have restrictions on transactions from high-risk jurisdictions. For example, TBC Bank may require additional documentation for transfers from countries like Iran or North Korea. It is advisable to open a bank account before registering with the NBG, as some banks require proof of registration. In practice, many founders open a personal account first and then convert it to a business account after company formation. Compliance and Trust

While Georgia's tax regime is favourable, compliance with AML regulations is critical. The NBG has increased scrutiny on crypto businesses, and failure to comply can result in fines or revocation of registration. Key compliance requirements include:

  • Customer due diligence (CDD) for all clients
  • Transaction monitoring for suspicious activity
  • Reporting of large transactions (over GEL 50,000) to the Financial Monitoring Service
  • Annual AML audit

This is general guidance, not legal advice. We recommend engaging a local compliance expert to ensure full adherence to Georgian laws. For example, you must verify the identity of each customer using official documents, and maintain records for at least 5 years. Transaction monitoring software can help detect unusual patterns, such as rapid movements of large sums. The annual AML audit must be conducted by an accredited auditor. Non-compliance can lead to fines of up to GEL 500,000 and imprisonment for directors. Consulting24 can coordinate with local compliance firms to set up your AML framework.

Common Mistakes

Founders often make these mistakes when setting up in Georgia:

  • Ignoring AML registration: Operating without NBG registration can lead to legal issues.
  • Not maintaining a local presence: The tax authority may challenge your tax residency if you have no substance in Georgia.
  • Using personal accounts for business: Always use a separate business bank account.
  • Overlooking double tax treaties: Georgia has treaties with many countries; ensure you understand how they affect your tax liability.
  • Assuming no tax on crypto-to-crypto trades: While gains are exempt, record-keeping is still important for audit purposes.

Frequently asked questions

Do I have to pay tax on crypto gains in Georgia?

No, individuals are not subject to capital gains tax on crypto in Georgia. Gains from crypto-to-fiat conversions are exempt. However, if you are a tax resident, you must still declare your assets. Businesses pay corporate tax only on distributed profits.

Is there a crypto license required in Georgia?

Georgia does not have a specific crypto license. However, crypto exchanges and custodial wallet providers must register with the National Bank of Georgia for AML compliance. This is a simpler process than obtaining a full license in other jurisdictions.

What is the minimum capital for a crypto company in Georgia?

There is no statutory minimum capital. You can register with GEL 1 (approx EUR 0.30). However, banks may require a deposit of EUR 10,000 to 50,000 to open a business account.

How long does it take to set up a crypto business in Georgia?

Company registration takes 1-2 weeks. Bank account opening takes 1-4 weeks. NBG AML registration takes 2-4 weeks. Total timeline is 4-10 weeks, depending on the complexity.

Can I open a bank account for my crypto company in Georgia?

Yes, major banks like TBC Bank and Bank of Georgia accept crypto businesses. They will conduct due diligence and may require a minimum deposit. Consulting24 can help you prepare the application.

Is Georgia compliant with EU tax standards?

Georgia is not an EU member but has a double tax treaty with many EU countries. It is not on the EU blacklist of non-cooperative jurisdictions. However, founders should ensure substance to avoid tax evasion allegations.

What happens if I don't register with the NBG?

Operating without NBG registration is illegal and can result in fines of up to GEL 500,000 and criminal liability for directors. It is essential to register before starting operations.

Can I use Georgia for a crypto exchange?

Yes, crypto exchanges are allowed subject to AML registration. You must implement KYC, transaction monitoring, and report suspicious activities. There is no specific licensing for exchanges.

How does Georgia compare to Panama for crypto?

Both have 0% capital gains tax. Panama has no corporate tax on foreign-source income, while Georgia taxes distributed profits at 15%. Panama setup via Consulting24 is EUR 6,000 flat and takes 2-4 weeks. Georgia is cheaper but requires more coordination.

Do I need to be a resident of Georgia to set up a company?

No, you can be a non-resident director. However, to benefit from tax residency, you must spend at least 183 days per year in Georgia or have your centre of vital interests there.

What ongoing compliance is required?

You must file annual financial statements, conduct an annual AML audit, and report suspicious transactions to the Financial Monitoring Service. Retained earnings are tax-free, but dividends are taxed at 15% (or 5% for IT companies).
