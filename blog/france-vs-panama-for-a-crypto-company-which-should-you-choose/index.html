Overview: France vs Panama for a Crypto Company

France and Panama represent two ends of the crypto licensing spectrum. France, as an EU member, has fully implemented MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation) since 2025. The French financial regulator, the Autorité des Marchés Financiers (AMF), now issues CASP (Crypto-Asset Service Provider) licenses with tiered capital requirements of EUR 50,000, 125,000, or 150,000 depending on services offered. Panama, by contrast, operates under a 2021 law (Law 23) that regulates virtual asset service providers (VASPs) through the Superintendencia de Bancos de Panamá (SBP). Panama's license costs a flat EUR 6,000 with no minimum capital requirement, making it one of the most affordable and fastest options globally.

For founders, the choice hinges on target market, budget, and compliance appetite. France suits those prioritizing EU passporting and institutional trust, while Panama appeals to cost-conscious startups and global operators. The regulatory regime in 2026 is settled: MiCA is fully in force, and Panama's Law 23 is well established. Both jurisdictions require strong AML programs, but the cost and complexity differ significantly.

Who Needs a Crypto License in France or Panama?

Any business offering crypto services such as exchange, custody, wallet management, or token issuance needs authorization. In France, the AMF requires CASP registration for all service providers, including those already registered under the previous optional regime. In Panama, Law 23 mandates licensing for any entity providing virtual asset services to or from Panama, including exchanges, custodians, and payment processors.

Typical applicants include:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralized and decentralized)
  • Wallet providers and custodians
  • Token issuers and ICO/STO platforms
  • Payment processors using crypto
  • DeFi protocols with intermediary functions

Founders should also consider their target market: if you plan to serve EU residents, a French license provides passporting rights. For global operations without EU focus, Panama offers more flexibility and lower costs.

License Type & Regulator

France: The AMF issues CASP licenses under MiCA. The license covers services like exchange, custody, and transfer. The AMF is the primary regulator, with ACPR (Prudential Supervision Authority) handling anti-money laundering (AML) oversight. MiCA introduces a harmonized framework across the EU, meaning a French CASP license allows you to operate in all 27 member states via passporting.

Panama: The SBP regulates VASPs under Law 23. The license is a single category covering all virtual asset services. The SBP focuses on AML/CFT compliance and consumer protection. Panama's regulatory approach is more flexible, with no prescriptive capital tiers or governance requirements. However, the SBP has become more rigorous in recent years, requiring detailed business plans and AML policies.

Both regulators require strong AML programs, but France imposes stricter capital and governance requirements due to MiCA. Consulting24 helps prepare documentation that meets both standards.

Capital Requirement

France: Under MiCA, CASP licenses require minimum capital of EUR 50,000 for simple services (e.g., custody), EUR 125,000 for exchange services, and EUR 150,000 for combined services. This capital must be maintained throughout the license period. For example, if you offer both custody and exchange, you need EUR 150,000 in paid-up capital. This can be a barrier for early-stage startups.

Panama: No minimum capital requirement. Companies can start with minimal paid-in capital, often just the registration amount (e.g., USD 10,000). This makes Panama highly attractive for bootstrapped startups. You can later increase capital as the business grows. Consulting24 advises on structuring capital efficiently in both jurisdictions, ensuring you meet regulatory thresholds without overcapitalizing.

Tax Treatment

France: Corporate income tax at 25% (standard rate). Crypto gains are taxed as capital gains or business income depending on activity. VAT applies to certain services. France has tax treaties with many countries, but the overall burden is higher than Panama. For example, if your crypto exchange earns EUR 1 million in profit, you would pay approximately EUR 250,000 in corporate tax.

Panama: Territorial taxation only income sourced in Panama is taxed. Offshore crypto companies pay 0% tax on foreign income. No capital gains tax, no VAT, and no withholding tax on dividends. This is a major advantage for global operations. If your customers are outside Panama, your tax liability is effectively zero. However, you must ensure your business is structured to avoid creating a taxable presence in other countries. Consulting24 recommends Panama for tax efficiency, but advises consulting a tax professional for your specific situation.

Allowed Activities

France: CASP license allows exchange (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto), custody, transfer, and advisory services. Token issuance requires additional prospectus compliance. DeFi and non-custodial services may fall outside CASP scope. For example, if you run a decentralized exchange with no intermediary, you might not need a CASP license, but the AMF has indicated it will assess on a case-by-case basis.

Panama: VASP license covers exchange, custody, wallet services, payment processing, and token issuance. The scope is broader and more flexible, including DeFi and NFT platforms. Panama does not restrict specific business models. For instance, a DeFi protocol that facilitates lending and borrowing can obtain a VASP license. France: Banking is accessible but requires compliance with AML checks. Many French banks accept crypto companies with a CASP license. Payment processing via SEPA is straightforward. For example, you can open a business account with BNP Paribas or Société Générale, though they may require a minimum deposit and charge monthly fees. Some neobanks like Revolut Business also support crypto companies.

Panama: Banking is more challenging due to international AML scrutiny. However, Panama offers a range of international banks and payment processors.   • Panama: Low cost (EUR 6,000 flat), fast timeline (4-6 weeks), no minimum capital, territorial tax, flexible activity scope. Panama is ideal for startups that want to launch quickly and minimize costs.

Compliance costs are higher in France due to MiCA's detailed requirements, such as mandatory annual audits, transaction monitoring systems, and a local compliance officer. In Panama, the compliance burden is lighter, but you still need to implement AML policies and submit periodic reports.

Common Mistakes

  • Underestimating capital needs: France's EUR 50,000+ capital requirement surprises many startups. Ensure you have sufficient funds before applying.
  • Ignoring tax implications: France's 25% corporate tax vs Panama's 0% offshore tax is a key differentiator. Plan your tax structure early.
  • Rushing the application: Incomplete documentation causes delays in both jurisdictions. Use a checklist to ensure all required documents are submitted.
  • Choosing based on cost alone: Consider market access and regulatory burden. A cheap license in Panama may not suit a business targeting EU clients.
  • Neglecting ongoing compliance: Both jurisdictions require regular reporting. Failure to comply can result in fines or license revocation.

Frequently asked questions

Which is cheaper: France or Panama for a crypto license?

Panama is significantly cheaper. The license costs a flat EUR 6,000 with no minimum capital. France requires EUR 50,000+ capital and higher ongoing costs. For budget-conscious startups, Panama is the clear winner.

Can I use a French CASP license to operate across the EU?

Yes, under MiCA, a French CASP license allows passporting to all EU member states. Panama's license does not provide EU access. If your target market is Europe, France is better.

How long does it take to get a Panama crypto license?

Typically 4-6 weeks with Consulting24. France takes 6-12 months due to AMF's thorough review. Panama's speed is a major advantage.

What are the tax benefits of Panama for crypto companies?

Panama uses territorial taxation only Panama-sourced income is taxed. Offshore crypto companies pay 0% on foreign income, no capital gains tax, and no VAT. France taxes worldwide income at 25%.

Do I need to be physically present in Panama to get a license?

No. Consulting24 handles everything remotely. You can incorporate and apply for the license without traveling. However, you may need a local agent, which we provide.

What happens if I already have a French registration under the old regime?

Under MiCA, existing registrations must be upgraded to a full CASP license. The AMF has set a transition period, but as of 2026, you should apply for a CASP license to continue operating legally.

Can Panama's license be used for DeFi projects?

Yes, Panama's VASP license is broad and covers DeFi protocols that provide intermediary services. However, fully decentralized protocols without a central operator may not require a license. Consulting24 can help assess your specific case.

What are the ongoing compliance costs in Panama?

Ongoing costs are typically EUR 3,000-5,000 per year, including annual reports, AML audits, and agent fees. This is much lower than France's EUR 15,000-30,000.

Is a French CASP license recognized outside the EU?

While not automatically recognized, a French license enhances credibility with international partners and regulators. It signals strong compliance and governance.

Can I switch from Panama to France later?

Yes, you can apply for a French CASP license later, but you would need to meet the capital and compliance requirements. Consulting24 can help with the transition.
