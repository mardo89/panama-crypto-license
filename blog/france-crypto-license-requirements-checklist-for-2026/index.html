What Is the France Crypto License?

The France crypto license, officially known as the registration as a Digital Asset Service Provider (DASP or PSAN in French), is required for any company offering crypto-related services in or from France. Since MiCA came fully into force in 2026, this licence now aligns with the EU-wide CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) regime, superseding the previous optional PSAN framework.

The regulator is the Autorité des Marchés Financiers (AMF), which oversees all crypto asset service providers. The licence covers activities such as custody, exchange, brokerage, and advisory services. The requirements are stringent, reflecting France's proactive approach to investor protection and anti-money laundering (AML).

Key features of the French regime include mandatory registration with the AMF, compliance with AML/CFT obligations, and adherence to prudential requirements under MiCA. The licence is mandatory for any entity providing covered services to French residents, regardless of where the company is incorporated.

Who Needs a France Crypto License?

Any business that provides one or more of the following services to French clients must obtain a France crypto license:

  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets
  • Operation of a trading platform
  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Placement of crypto assets (underwriting)
  • Reception and transmission of orders for crypto assets
  • Portfolio management of crypto assets
  • Advice on crypto assets

This includes both French-incorporated companies and foreign entities that actively target French customers. Even if your company is registered in another EU member state, you may need to comply with French rules if you market services to France. The AMF has been vigilant in enforcing this.

Startups, exchanges, wallet providers, and DeFi projects that offer any of these services should assess their licensing needs early. Failure to register can result in fines, criminal sanctions, and reputational damage.

License Type and Regulator

The France crypto license is a single licence type under MiCA, but with different authorisation classes based on services offered. The regulator is the Autorité des Marchés Financiers (AMF), France's financial markets authority. The AMF works closely with the ACPR (Prudential Supervision and Resolution Authority) for AML/CFT oversight.

Under MiCA, the licence is called a CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) authorisation. Companies must apply to the AMF and meet all MiCA requirements, including:

  • Registered office in France (or an EU branch for non-EU firms)
  • Fit and proper management
  • AML/CFT policies and procedures
  • Prudential safeguards (minimum capital or insurance)
  • IT security and business continuity plans
  • Transparent fee and conflict of interest policies

The AMF maintains a public register of all licensed DASPs. As of 2026, all existing French DASPs have been transitioned to the MiCA CASP regime.

Capital Requirements

Under MiCA, capital requirements for a France crypto license depend on the type of services provided. The minimum capital thresholds are as follows:

  • EUR 50,000 for services that do not involve custody of client assets (e.g., advisory, order transmission)
  • EUR 125,000 for services involving custody or exchange of crypto assets
  • EUR 150,000 for operating a trading platform

These amounts must be held as own funds, typically in the form of share capital or reserves. The AMF may require higher capital if the company's risk profile warrants it. Additionally, companies must maintain professional indemnity insurance or a combination of capital and insurance to cover potential liabilities.

It is important to note that these are minimums; the actual capital required may be higher based on your business plan and risk assessment. Consulting24 advises clients on structuring capital efficiently.

Tax Treatment for Crypto Businesses in France

France has a specific tax regime for crypto businesses. Corporate income tax (CIT) applies at the standard rate of 25% (reduced to 15% for SMEs on the first €42,500 of profit). Capital gains from crypto asset trading are subject to CIT, but there are exemptions for certain transactions.

Value Added Tax (VAT) is generally not applied to crypto-to-fiat exchanges or crypto-to-crypto trades, following the European Court of Justice ruling. However, fees for services like custody or advisory may be subject to VAT at 20%.

France also imposes a flat tax of 30% on capital gains for individuals (not corporate entities), which includes 17.2% social contributions. For corporate entities, gains are treated as ordinary income. Transfer pricing rules apply for related-party transactions.

Tax compliance is rigorous; companies must file annual returns and maintain detailed records. Consulting24 recommends engaging a French tax advisor to navigate the specific rules.

Allowed Activities Under the France Crypto License

The France crypto license under MiCA covers a broad range of activities. The following are explicitly allowed:

  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Operation of a crypto trading platform (order-book or AMM-based)
  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency or other crypto assets
  • Execution of orders on behalf of clients
  • Placement of crypto assets (including initial exchange offerings)
  • Reception and transmission of orders for crypto assets
  • Portfolio management of crypto assets
  • Advice on crypto assets
  • Transfer services for crypto assets (new under MiCA)

Activities that are not covered include pure mining, non-custodial software development, and decentralised finance (DeFi) protocols that do not involve custody or intermediation. However, if a DeFi project provides any of the above services, it may fall under the licence requirement.

Step-by-Step Process to Obtain a France Crypto License

Obtaining a France crypto license involves a structured process. Below is the step-by-step approach, which Consulting24 coordinates for clients:

  1. Pre-assessment: Evaluate your business model, services, and target market to determine the exact licence class and capital required.
  2. Company incorporation: Establish a French company (e.g., SAS or SARL) with a registered office in France. This typically takes 2-4 weeks.
  3. Compliance documentation: Draft AML/CFT policies, risk assessment, business continuity plan, IT security policies, and conflict of interest policy. This is the most time-consuming step (2-4 months).
  4. Capital deposit: Deposit the required minimum capital into a French bank account or escrow.
  5. Application submission: Submit the application to the AMF through their online portal, including all supporting documents and the application fee.
  6. AMF review: The AMF reviews the application, may request additional information, and conducts interviews with management. This takes 3-6 months.
  7. Approval and registration: Once approved, the company is registered as a DASP/CASP on the AMF public register.
  8. Ongoing compliance: Submit annual reports, maintain AML/CFT procedures, and notify the AMF of any material changes.

Compliance and Trust: Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, French crypto firms must maintain rigorous compliance. Key ongoing obligations include:

  • AML/CFT: Implement transaction monitoring, customer due diligence (CDD), and suspicious transaction reporting (STR) to Tracfin.
  • Annual reporting: Submit audited financial statements and compliance reports to the AMF.
  • Capital maintenance: Keep minimum capital at all times; notify the AMF if capital falls below thresholds.
  • IT security: Conduct regular penetration testing and maintain business continuity plans.
  • Changes in control: Prior approval required for any change in ownership or management.

Common Mistakes When Applying for a France Crypto License

Many applicants face delays or rejection due to avoidable errors. Common mistakes include:

  • Incomplete AML policies: The AMF expects detailed, tailored AML procedures, not generic templates.
  • Underestimating capital: Choosing the wrong capital tier or failing to prove source of funds.
  • Poor management fit: Key personnel with criminal records or lack of crypto experience may disqualify the application.
  • Ignoring IT security: The AMF requires strong cybersecurity measures, including cold storage for custody.
  • Marketing before approval: Offering services to French clients before obtaining the licence can lead to sanctions.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a France DASP and a MiCA CASP licence?

As of 2026, the France DASP (PSAN) licence has been fully replaced by the MiCA CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) authorisation. The AMF now issues CASP licences that comply with EU-wide standards. Existing DASPs have been transitioned to the new regime.

How long does it take to get a France crypto license?

The typical timeline is 6 to 12 months from application submission to approval, assuming complete documentation. The AMF review process takes 3-6 months, and preparation of compliance documents can take 2-4 months.

What is the minimum capital for a France crypto license?

Under MiCA, the minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for advisory services, EUR 125,000 for custody or exchange, and EUR 150,000 for operating a trading platform. These amounts must be held as own funds.

Can a non-EU company obtain a France crypto license?

Yes, but the company must establish a registered office in France or an EU branch. The AMF requires a physical presence and local management. Non-EU firms may also need to appoint a local representative.

Does France tax crypto gains for companies?

Yes, corporate income tax applies at 25% (15% for SMEs on first €42,500 profit). Capital gains from crypto trading are taxable. VAT is generally not applied to crypto exchanges but may apply to service fees.

What activities are not covered by the France crypto license?

Pure mining, non-custodial software development, and decentralised finance protocols that do not provide custody or intermediation are typically not covered. However, any activity that falls under the CASP definition requires a licence.

Is it possible to get a France crypto license without a physical office?

No, the AMF requires a registered office in France. A virtual office is not sufficient. You need a physical address and local management to meet the fit and proper requirements.

How does the France crypto license compare to Panama?

France offers EU passporting but costs €30,000-85,000 and takes 6-12 months. Panama costs a flat €6,000, takes 4-6 weeks, and has no capital requirement, but does not provide EU passporting. Panama is ideal for speed and low cost.

What are the ongoing compliance costs for a France crypto license?

Ongoing costs include annual AML/CFT audits, compliance officer salaries, IT security testing, and AMF reporting fees. Expect €10,000-30,000 per year depending on the size of the business.
