Overview: Crypto Banking and Payment in the Czech Republic

The Czech Republic has positioned itself as a crypto-friendly jurisdiction within the EU. The central bank (CNB) has historically taken a permissive stance, and the country's AML Act requires crypto asset service providers to register. However, banking relationships remain the primary challenge. As of 2026, MiCA provides a harmonised framework, but local banks still apply their own risk assessments. This means that even with a valid CASP authorisation, securing a business bank account can take months.

Key aspects of the Czech crypto banking environment include:

  • Banks such as Raiffeisenbank, Ceska sporitelna, and some fintechs offer services to licensed crypto firms, but each has its own compliance thresholds.
  • Payment rails (SEPA, SWIFT) are standard, but crypto-specific payment processing may require additional partnerships with payment processors like Coinbase Commerce or BitPay.
  • The CNB has issued guidance on AML compliance for crypto transactions, which banks rely on heavily when conducting due diligence.

For founders, the practical challenge is securing a business bank account that supports crypto-related transactions. Many banks require a minimum of 6 months of operation, audited financials, and a detailed AML program. License Type and Regulator

In the Czech Republic, crypto asset service providers must register with the Czech National Bank (CNB) under the AML Act. However, from 2026, MiCA applies: CASPs need authorisation from the CNB as the competent authority. The licence type is a 'CASP authorisation' under MiCA, replacing the previous registration regime. This means that all crypto businesses offering services to EU residents must comply with MiCA's harmonised standards.

The regulator is the Czech National Bank (CNB). It oversees compliance with AML, prudential, and conduct requirements. The CNB has a pragmatic reputation but conducts thorough background checks on beneficial owners and management. The application process includes a review of the business plan, AML policies, risk assessment, and governance structure. The CNB may also conduct on-site inspections.

For comparison, Estonia and Lithuania also offer MiCA-compliant licences, but the Czech process may be more familiar for local founders. However, Estonia and Lithuania have more streamlined processes and lower professional fees. Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, capital requirements depend on the services offered. For the Czech Republic, as an EU member, the tiers are:

  • EUR 50,000 for simple services (e.g., custody of crypto assets, wallet provision).
  • EUR 125,000 for exchange services (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto).
  • EUR 150,000 for more complex services (e.g., trading platform, portfolio management, order execution).

These figures are mandatory and must be held as own funds in a bank account or liquid assets. The CNB may require additional capital based on risk assessment, such as higher operational risk or volume projections. The capital must be maintained at all times, and any shortfall must be reported immediately.

For comparison, Panama has no minimum capital requirement, making it a lower barrier for startups. However, Panama's licence does not provide EU passporting rights, so it is best for non-EU operations. Consulting24 can help you structure your capital efficiently.

Tax Treatment of Crypto in the Czech Republic

The Czech Republic treats crypto as a means of payment and an investment asset. Key tax points:

  • Corporate income tax: 19% on profits from crypto trading, mining, and other business activities.
  • VAT: Crypto transactions are exempt from VAT (as per EU court rulings), but advisory services may be subject to VAT.
  • Personal income tax: 15% for individuals, with a holding period exemption after 3 years (no capital gains tax on crypto held for more than 3 years).

However, tax treatment can vary by activity (mining, staking, lending). For example, staking rewards may be considered income at the time of receipt. Professional advice is essential to avoid penalties. Panama offers territorial taxation, meaning no tax on foreign-source income, which can be more advantageous for international operations. Allowed Activities Under the Czech Crypto Licence

A Czech CASP authorisation allows the following activities under MiCA:

  • Custody and administration of crypto assets
  • Operation of a trading platform
  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets
  • Execution of orders on behalf of clients
  • Placing of crypto assets
  • Receiving and transmitting orders for crypto assets
  • Providing advice on crypto assets
  • Portfolio management of crypto assets

These activities mirror the MiCA categories. The CNB may impose conditions, such as limits on use or client types. For a broader scope, exchange licences in other jurisdictions may offer different flexibilities. For instance, Panama's licence allows all crypto activities without service class restrictions, making it more versatile for multi-service platforms.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process for obtaining a Czech crypto licence under MiCA involves:

  1. Pre-application: Prepare business plan, AML policies, risk assessment, governance documents, and financial projections. Engage a local legal advisor (Consulting24 can recommend partners).
  2. Submit application to CNB with all required documents, including proof of capital, ownership structure, and compliance manuals.
  3. CNB review: Typically 3-6 months, with requests for additional information. The CNB may interview management.
  4. On-site inspection (if required) to verify operational readiness.
  5. Decision: Approval or rejection. If approved, you must maintain ongoing compliance with reporting and capital requirements.

Banking and Payment Solutions

Securing banking is often the hardest part. In the Czech Republic, banks are cautious but some work with licensed crypto firms. Options include:

  • Raiffeisenbank: Known to accept crypto clients with proper compliance, but requires a minimum deposit and a proven track record.
  • Ceska sporitelna: May open accounts for CASPs, but often requires a local presence and audited financials.
  • Fintechs like Revolut Business or Wise for multi-currency accounts, though they may restrict crypto-related transactions.

Payment rails: SEPA for euro transfers, SWIFT for international. Crypto-specific payment processors like Coinbase Commerce or BitPay can be integrated for merchant services. However, some banks may block transactions to crypto exchanges. To mitigate this, consider opening a dedicated account with a crypto-friendly bank in Lithuania or Estonia, where banking is more established.

Compliance and Trust: What Banks Look For

Banks assess crypto firms on AML/CFT compliance, source of funds, and business model. To improve banking approval:

  • Have a strong AML program (policies, KYC, transaction monitoring, and screening). Use a reputable compliance software like Chainalysis or Elliptic.
  • Provide audited financial statements and a clear business plan with projected volumes and revenue.
  • Show a transparent ownership structure with no politically exposed persons (PEPs) or sanctions links.
  • Maintain a minimum balance and demonstrate regular transaction flow.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Czech crypto licence under MiCA?

Under MiCA, the minimum capital depends on the services offered: EUR 50,000 for custody, EUR 125,000 for exchange, and EUR 150,000 for complex services like trading platforms. These are own funds that must be held at all times. The CNB may require additional capital based on risk assessment.

How long does it take to get a Czech crypto licence?

The application process typically takes 3-6 months from submission to decision. This includes document review, background checks, and possible on-site inspections. Preparation of documents can take an additional 1-3 months. In contrast, Panama's licence takes 4-6 weeks.

Can I open a bank account in the Czech Republic with a crypto licence?

Yes, but it is challenging. Banks like Raiffeisenbank and Ceska sporitelna may open accounts for licensed CASPs, but they require a strong compliance program, audited financials, and a proven track record. The process can take 3-6 months after obtaining the licence.

What are the tax rates for crypto businesses in the Czech Republic?

Corporate income tax is 19% on profits from crypto activities. VAT is exempt on crypto transactions. Personal income tax is 15%, with a 3-year holding period exemption for capital gains. However, tax treatment varies by activity, so professional advice is recommended.

Is the Czech Republic better than Panama for crypto banking?

It depends on your target market. The Czech Republic offers EU passporting under MiCA, but banking is more difficult and costly. Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 fee, no capital requirement, and easier banking, but no EU access. Consulting24 can help you decide based on your business needs.

What activities are allowed under a Czech CASP authorisation?

All MiCA activities are allowed, including custody, exchange (fiat and crypto), trading platform operation, order execution, advice, and portfolio management. The CNB may impose conditions on certain activities. For a full list, see the 'Allowed Activities' section above.

Do I need a local office in the Czech Republic for the licence?

Yes, you need a registered office and a local presence for compliance and management. The CNB requires that key functions like AML compliance are based in the Czech Republic. This can be a rented office or a virtual office with a physical address.

What happens if I don't maintain the minimum capital?
