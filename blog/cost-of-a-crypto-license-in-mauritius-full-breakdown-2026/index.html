What Is a Crypto License in Mauritius?

A crypto license in Mauritius is a regulatory authorization issued by the Financial Services Commission (FSC) under the Virtual Asset and Initial Token Offering Services Act (VAITOS) 2021. It allows businesses to operate virtual asset services, including exchange, custody, and advisory services. The license is mandatory for any entity providing virtual asset services in or from Mauritius.

The FSC has established a sandbox regime and a full license framework. As of 2026, the full license is the standard route for established operators. The cost of a crypto license in Mauritius includes application fees, annual fees, capital requirements, and compliance costs. This license is recognized for its strong AML/CFT standards and is attractive for businesses targeting African and Asian markets.

For example, a crypto exchange serving clients in South Africa or India may find Mauritius a stable base with clear rules. The VAITOS framework covers both custodial and non-custodial services, and the FSC has issued guidelines on token classification. Compared to some other jurisdictions, Mauritius offers a relatively fast process, typically 3-6 months for a full license. The cost of a crypto license in Mauritius can be a significant investment, but it provides legal certainty and access to a growing fintech hub.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Financial Services Commission (FSC) of Mauritius. The main license types under VAITOS are:

  • Class 1 License: For virtual asset exchange services and custody.
  • Class 2 License: For advisory, portfolio management, and other non-custodial services.
  • Sandbox License: A temporary license for innovative projects, typically valid for 12-24 months.

The full license (Class 1 or 2) is the standard for ongoing operations. The cost of a crypto license in Mauritius differs by class, with Class 1 requiring higher capital and fees due to custodial risks. The FSC also requires that key personnel, such as directors and compliance officers, be fit and proper, with background checks conducted. For a Class 1 license, you may need to appoint a local auditor and a money laundering reporting officer (MLRO). The sandbox license is cheaper but limited in scope and duration. Many founders start with a sandbox to test their business model before applying for a full license, which can reduce initial costs.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement depends on the license class. For Class 1 (custodial services), the FSC requires at least EUR 50,000 to EUR 100,000 in paid-up capital. For Class 2 (non-custodial), the requirement is lower, typically EUR 20,000 to EUR 50,000. Capital must be maintained at all times and can be in fiat or liquid assets.

This capital is a key component of the cost of a crypto license in Mauritius. It must be deposited in a local bank account and cannot be withdrawn without FSC approval. For example, a Class 1 exchange must hold sufficient capital to cover potential losses from custody breaches. Some founders choose to overcapitalize to demonstrate financial strength, which can speed up the approval process. However, tying up capital in Mauritius may be a disadvantage compared to Panama, where no minimum capital is required for crypto activities.

Tax Treatment

Mauritius offers a favorable tax regime for crypto businesses. The corporate tax rate is 15%, but there are incentives for licensed companies. Virtual asset transactions are subject to the same tax rules as other financial services. VAT is not applicable on crypto transactions in most cases.

Mauritius has a territorial tax system, meaning only income sourced in Mauritius is taxed. This can be advantageous for international crypto businesses. The cost of a crypto license in Mauritius should be weighed against potential tax savings. Always consult a tax advisor for your specific situation. For example, if your exchange earns fees from clients outside Mauritius, those fees may not be taxable in Mauritius. Additionally, Mauritius has a network of double taxation treaties with African and Asian countries, which can reduce withholding taxes on dividends and interest. However, the tax treatment of crypto-to-crypto trades is still evolving, and you should seek professional advice to ensure compliance.

Allowed Activities Under the License

A Mauritius crypto license permits a range of activities, including:

  • Operating a virtual asset exchange (order book or P2P)
  • Providing virtual asset custody
  • Brokerage and dealing in virtual assets
  • Portfolio management and advisory
  • Issuing initial token offerings (ITOs)
  • Transfer services for virtual assets

Activities not permitted include unlicensed derivatives trading and services to sanctioned entities. The cost of a crypto license in Mauritius covers these activities, but each service line may require separate regulatory approval. For instance, if you plan to offer staking services, you may need to clarify with the FSC whether this falls under custody or a separate activity. The license also allows you to hold client funds in segregated accounts, which is a trust-building feature. However, you cannot engage in proprietary trading unless explicitly authorized. The FSC expects licensees to have clear policies on conflicts of interest and client asset protection.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a crypto license in Mauritius involves several steps. Here is a typical timeline:

  1. Pre-application (1-2 months): Company incorporation in Mauritius, preparation of business plan, AML/CFT policies, and capital deposit.
  2. Submission (1 month): Submit application to FSC with all required documents, including due diligence on directors and shareholders.
  3. Review (2-4 months): FSC reviews the application, may request additional information, and conducts background checks.
  4. Approval (1 month): If satisfied, FSC issues the license. Post-approval, you must pay the annual fee and maintain compliance.

Some founders choose to use a licensed management company to provide these services, which adds to the cost but simplifies compliance.

Compliance and Trust Requirements

License holders must comply with ongoing AML/CFT obligations, including:

Non-compliance can lead to fines or license revocation. The cost of a crypto license in Mauritius includes these compliance costs, typically EUR 5,000-15,000 annually. Frequently asked questions

What is the total cost of a crypto license in Mauritius for 2026?

The total first-year cost ranges from EUR 25,000 to EUR 125,000 depending on the license class. This includes application fees, annual fees, capital deposit, legal setup, and compliance costs. For Class 2 (non-custodial), expect EUR 25,000-70,000; for Class 1 (custodial), EUR 60,000-125,000. Exact figures depend on your business model and professional fees.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Mauritius?

The typical timeline is 3-6 months from application submission to approval. The sandbox route may be faster (2-4 months). Delays can occur if the FSC requests additional information or if your documentation is incomplete. Planning for 6 months is advisable.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a Mauritius crypto license?

For Class 1 (custodial services), the minimum capital is EUR 50,000-100,000. For Class 2 (non-custodial), it is EUR 20,000-50,000. Capital must be paid up and maintained in a local bank account. It cannot be withdrawn without FSC approval.

What activities are allowed under a Mauritius crypto license?

Allowed activities include operating a virtual asset exchange, custody, brokerage, advisory, portfolio management, ITO issuance, and transfer services. Derivatives trading and services to sanctioned entities are not permitted. Each activity may require separate approval from the FSC.

Is Mauritius a good jurisdiction for crypto businesses targeting Africa?

Yes, Mauritius is strategically located for African and Asian markets. It has a stable political environment, a favorable tax regime, and a clear regulatory framework under VAITOS. Its AML standards are recognized regionally, which can facilitate banking and partnerships in Africa.

What are the ongoing compliance costs for a Mauritius crypto license?

Ongoing compliance costs typically range from EUR 5,000 to EUR 15,000 annually. This includes annual license fees, audit fees, MLRO services, and AML software. You must also submit annual returns to the FSC and maintain records for at least 5 years.

Can I use a Mauritius crypto license to serve clients in Europe?

No, a Mauritius license does not provide passporting rights into the EU. For European clients, you would need a MiCA license from an EU member state like Lithuania or Estonia. Mauritius is better suited for non-EU markets, especially Africa and Asia.

What documents are required for a Mauritius crypto license application?

You need a detailed business plan, AML/CFT policies, due diligence on directors and shareholders, proof of capital deposit, and a registered office in Mauritius. The FSC may also require a local director and a compliance officer. All documents must be in English.

How does the cost of a Mauritius crypto license compare to Panama?

Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 company setup with no dedicated crypto license, making it much cheaper. However, Panama lacks the regulatory clarity of Mauritius. For businesses needing a clear legal framework and regional access, Mauritius may be worth the higher cost.
