Overview: What Is a Georgia Crypto Company?

A Georgia crypto company is a legal entity registered in Georgia that holds a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) license from the National Bank of Georgia. This license allows businesses to offer services such as crypto exchange, custody, and wallet management. Georgia's regulatory framework is aligned with international standards, including FATF recommendations, and offers a stable, low-tax environment.

The NBG oversees licensing and compliance, requiring firms to meet specific capital thresholds, implement AML/KYC procedures, and appoint local representatives. Georgia is not part of the EU, so it is not subject to MiCA, but its regime is designed to be competitive and compliant with global norms. For example, a crypto exchange operating from Tbilisi can serve clients across the Caucasus and Central Asia while benefiting from a 15% tax rate on distributed profits only.

Compared to Panama, which has no dedicated crypto license, Georgia offers a regulated framework that can enhance credibility with banks and partners. However, Panama's flat EUR 6,000 company setup cost is significantly lower, making it attractive for startups that do not require a license.

Who Needs a Georgia Crypto License?

Any business that provides virtual asset services in or from Georgia must obtain a VASP license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralized and decentralized)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Brokerage services
  • Payment processors handling crypto
  • ATMs and kiosks
  • OTC desks

If you plan to serve Georgian residents or operate from Georgia, you need a license. Even if your target market is international, registering in Georgia can be advantageous due to its tax regime and regulatory clarity. For instance, a European startup targeting Central Asian markets might find Georgia a strategic base with lower costs than EU jurisdictions.

License Type & Regulator

The regulator is the National Bank of Georgia (NBG). The license type is a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) License. There is one main license category, but the NBG may impose conditions based on the services offered. The license is valid for an indefinite period, subject to ongoing compliance.

Georgia's regime is not part of MiCA, but it is recognized by international bodies. The NBG requires regular reporting, AML compliance, and fit-and-proper tests for directors and beneficial owners. For example, the NBG may require a higher capital buffer if you plan to offer custody services with high transaction volumes.

In contrast, under MiCA (fully in force in 2026), EU member states like Lithuania apply capital tiers of EUR 50,000, 125,000, or 150,000 by service class. Georgia's single tier simplifies planning.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a Georgia VASP license is EUR 50,000 (or equivalent in GEL). This capital must be fully paid up and maintained throughout the license period. The NBG may require higher capital for higher-risk activities, such as operating an exchange with high transaction volumes or offering custody services.

This is lower than many EU jurisdictions under MiCA, where capital tiers range from EUR 50,000 to 150,000 depending on services. Georgia's requirement is competitive, especially for startups. For example, a small exchange with monthly volume under EUR 1 million can meet the EUR 50,000 threshold, while a larger platform might need EUR 100,000 or more based on NBG discretion.

In Panama, there is no capital requirement for company setup, but banks may require deposits. Consulting24 can advise on structuring your capital efficiently.

Tax Treatment

Georgia offers a favorable tax regime for crypto businesses. The corporate income tax is a flat 15%, but with a unique system: profits are only taxed when distributed (as dividends). Retained earnings are tax-free. This can significantly reduce the effective tax rate for reinvesting companies.

VAT is 18%, but crypto transactions may be exempt depending on the service. Personal income tax on dividends is 5%. Georgia has no capital gains tax on crypto assets held by individuals, but business income from crypto is subject to corporate tax. Always consult a tax advisor for your specific situation.

For example, a Georgia crypto company that earns EUR 200,000 in profit and reinvests EUR 150,000 pays zero tax on that retained amount. Only the distributed EUR 50,000 is taxed at 15%. Compare this to Panama, which taxes only Panama-source income (foreign-source income is 0%), but has no dedicated crypto license.

Allowed Activities

A Georgia VASP license permits the following activities:

  • Exchange of virtual assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of virtual assets for other virtual assets
  • Custody and administration of virtual assets
  • Transfer of virtual assets
  • Participation in ICOs/STOs (with additional approval)
  • Operation of crypto ATMs

Activities not covered include derivatives and securities (regulated separately). The NBG may impose restrictions on certain high-risk services, such as privacy coins or high-volume trading. For example, if you plan to offer margin trading, the NBG may require additional capital or prohibit the service entirely.

Step-by-Step Process

The process to obtain a Georgia crypto license involves several stages:

  1. Pre-incorporation: Choose a company name, prepare constitutional documents, and appoint directors/shareholders.
  2. Company registration: Register with the National Agency of Public Registry (NAPR) and obtain a tax ID.
  3. Prepare AML policies: Develop internal AML/CFT procedures, appoint an AML officer, and implement KYC processes.
  4. Submit license application: File with the NBG, including business plan, financial projections, and proof of capital.
  5. NBG review: The NBG conducts due diligence on directors and beneficial owners. This may take 2-4 months.
  6. License issuance: Upon approval, the license is granted. You must then maintain ongoing compliance.

  • Fit-and-proper tests for directors

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Georgia crypto license?

The minimum capital requirement is EUR 50,000 (or equivalent in GEL). This must be fully paid up and maintained throughout the license period. Higher capital may be required for higher-risk activities, such as exchange services with high transaction volumes.

How long does it take to get a Georgia crypto license?

The typical timeline is 3 to 6 months from application to license issuance. This includes company registration, document preparation, and NBG review. Delays can occur if documents are incomplete or if the NBG requires additional information.

What are the ongoing costs for a Georgia crypto company?

Annual compliance costs typically range from EUR 2,000 to 5,000, including AML audits, reporting, and AML officer fees. Additionally, you must maintain the minimum capital of EUR 50,000. Professional fees for legal and accounting may add EUR 1,000-3,000 per year.

Is Georgia part of the EU or subject to MiCA?

No, Georgia is not part of the EU and is not subject to MiCA. Its regulatory framework is independent, based on FATF recommendations. This can be an advantage for businesses seeking a non-EU regime with lower capital requirements.

Can I serve EU clients with a Georgia crypto license?

Yes, but you must comply with EU AML regulations if you actively market to EU residents. MiCA does not apply to non-EU entities, but individual EU member states may require registration. Consulting24 can advise on cross-border compliance.

What are the tax benefits of a Georgia crypto company?

Corporate tax is 15% only on distributed profits; retained earnings are tax-free. There is no capital gains tax on crypto for individuals. Dividends are taxed at 5%. This can result in an effective tax rate below 5% for reinvesting companies.

Do I need a local director in Georgia?

Can I open a bank account for a Georgia crypto company?

How does Georgia compare to Panama for crypto?

Panama has no crypto license, offering a flat EUR 6,000 company setup with 0% tax on foreign-source income. Georgia provides a regulated license with EUR 50,000 capital and 15% tax on distributed profits. Choose Panama for simplicity, Georgia for regulatory credibility.

What activities are not allowed under a Georgia VASP license?

Derivatives, securities, and banking services are not covered. The NBG may also restrict privacy coins or high-risk services. Additional approvals are needed for ICOs/STOs.
