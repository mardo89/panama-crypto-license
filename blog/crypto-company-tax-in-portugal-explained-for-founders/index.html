What Is Crypto Company Tax in Portugal?

Crypto company tax in Portugal refers to the corporate income tax (IRC) and other taxes applicable to businesses that deal with cryptocurrencies. Portugal has not introduced a specific crypto tax law for companies; instead, existing tax rules apply. As of 2026, the Portuguese Tax Authority (Autoridade Tributária) has issued guidelines clarifying that crypto asset transactions are generally subject to standard corporate tax rates.

The standard IRC rate is 21% on taxable profits, plus a municipal surcharge (derrama) of up to 1.5%, and a state surcharge for profits above €1.5 million. However, certain crypto activities may qualify for exemptions or reduced rates, especially if they involve holding crypto as long-term investments or if the company is classified as a non-habitual resident (NHR) entity.

License Type & Regulator

Portugal does not have a specific crypto license regime for companies. Instead, crypto businesses must comply with anti-money laundering (AML) regulations and register with the Bank of Portugal (Banco de Portugal) as a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP). This registration is mandatory for exchanges, custodian wallet providers, and other entities that facilitate crypto transactions.

The regulator is the Bank of Portugal, which oversees compliance with AML/CFT obligations. There is no minimum capital requirement for VASP registration, but the company must have adequate internal controls. As of 2026, the EU's Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) regulation is fully in force, and Portugal has implemented MiCA, meaning that crypto companies may need to comply with additional licensing requirements depending on the services offered.

Capital Requirement

Portugal does not impose a statutory minimum capital for limited liability companies (Lda). The typical share capital is €1 per shareholder, though it is advisable to have at least €5,000 for credibility. For VASP registration, there is no explicit capital requirement, but the Bank of Portugal expects the company to have sufficient financial resources to operate.

In contrast, under MiCA, companies providing certain crypto services may need to meet capital requirements of EUR 50,000, EUR 125,000, or EUR 150,000 depending on the service class. Since Portugal has implemented MiCA, these capital tiers apply to new VASP registrations as of 2026.

Tax Treatment of Crypto Companies in Portugal

The tax treatment of crypto companies in Portugal depends on the nature of the activities. General corporate income tax (IRC) applies at 21% on profits, but there are important nuances:

  • Trading income: Profits from short-term trading of crypto are fully taxable as ordinary business income.
  • Investment income: Capital gains from holding crypto for more than one year may be exempt under the participation exemption regime, provided certain conditions are met (e.g., the company holds at least 10% of the crypto asset issuer).
  • Mining and staking: Income from mining or staking is generally taxable as business income.
  • VAT: Crypto transactions are exempt from VAT under the EU's VAT directive.
  • Withholding tax: No withholding tax on dividends paid to non-residents if the company qualifies for the NHR regime.

Portugal also offers a tax regime for non-habitual residents (NHR), which can provide a 10-year exemption on certain foreign-source income. This can be advantageous for founders who relocate to Portugal. Consulting24 can advise on how to structure your company to benefit from these rules.

Allowed Activities for Crypto Companies in Portugal

Under Portuguese law and MiCA, crypto companies can engage in the following activities after VASP registration:

  • Exchange of virtual assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of virtual assets for other virtual assets
  • Transfer of virtual assets
  • Custody and administration of virtual assets
  • Participation in and provision of financial services related to an issuer's offer of virtual assets

Activities that are not allowed without a license include operating a crypto exchange without registration, offering derivatives based on crypto, and engaging in activities that fall under MiCA's stricter requirements for asset-referenced tokens (ARTs) or e-money tokens (EMTs).

Step-by-Step Process for Setting Up a Crypto Company in Portugal

  1. Choose your company structure: Typically a limited liability company (Lda) or a public limited company (SA).
  2. Reserve your company name: Check availability with the Commercial Registry.
  3. Open a bank account: Deposit the share capital (minimum €1, but recommended higher).
  4. Draft and notarize the articles of association: Engage a Portuguese lawyer.
  5. Register with the Commercial Registry: Obtain the NIPC (tax identification number).
  6. Register for taxes: Obtain a VAT number and register with social security.
  7. Apply for VASP registration: Submit AML policies, business plan, and compliance documentation to the Bank of Portugal.
  8. Implement AML/CFT procedures: Appoint a compliance officer and set up transaction monitoring.
  9. Ongoing compliance: File annual accounts, tax returns, and AML reports.

Compliance & Trust: What Founders Must Know

Compliance is critical for crypto companies in Portugal. The Bank of Portugal expects strong AML/CFT measures, including customer due diligence (CDD), transaction monitoring, and reporting of suspicious activities. Failure to comply can result in fines up to €5 million or more.

As of 2026, MiCA adds additional requirements for CASPs, including capital adequacy, governance, and disclosure. Founders should work with experienced compliance advisors to ensure their operations meet all legal standards.

Frequently asked questions

What is the corporate tax rate for crypto companies in Portugal?

The standard corporate income tax (IRC) rate is 21% on taxable profits. Municipal surcharges (derrama) can add up to 1.5%, and a state surcharge applies for profits above €1.5 million. Effective rates are typically 21% to 22.5%.

Do crypto companies need a license in Portugal?

Yes, crypto companies must register with the Bank of Portugal as a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) under AML/CFT regulations. As of 2026, MiCA also applies, requiring additional compliance for certain services.

Is crypto trading income taxable in Portugal?

Yes, profits from short-term trading of crypto are fully taxable as ordinary business income at the corporate tax rate. Long-term capital gains may qualify for the participation exemption if conditions are met.

What is the minimum capital for a crypto company in Portugal?

There is no statutory minimum capital for a limited liability company (Lda). However, under MiCA, capital requirements of €50,000 to €150,000 may apply depending on the services offered.

Can I get a tax exemption for crypto gains in Portugal?

Yes, under the participation exemption regime, capital gains from holding crypto for more than one year may be exempt if the company holds at least 10% of the crypto asset issuer. The NHR regime also offers exemptions for foreign-source income.

How long does it take to set up a crypto company in Portugal?

Company incorporation takes 1-2 weeks. VASP registration with the Bank of Portugal can take 3-6 months. Total timeline is typically 4-8 months.

What are the ongoing compliance costs for a crypto company in Portugal?
