What Is a Poland Crypto License in 2026?

Under MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation), which is fully in force across the EU in 2026, Poland issues a CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) license. This replaces the previous national VASP register. The license allows companies to legally offer crypto services such as exchange, custody, and wallet services to EU customers. Poland's regulator, the Polish Financial Supervision Authority (KNF), is the competent authority for authorisation. The process is harmonised across the EU, but national implementation details vary. For example, Poland requires that the company's management board members have clean criminal records and relevant experience. The KNF also expects a detailed business plan covering at least three years of operations, including financial projections and risk management strategies. Compared to the old VASP regime, MiCA brings stricter capital requirements and conduct of business rules, but also provides a clear pathway to passport services across the EU.

Who Needs a Poland Crypto License?

Any company that provides crypto services to clients in Poland or other EU member states needs a CASP license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Brokerage platforms
  • ATMs operators
  • DeFi platforms that offer regulated services (e.g., custody or exchange)

If your business is based outside the EU but serves EU customers, you typically need to establish a legal entity in an EU member state and obtain a CASP license there. Poland is a popular choice due to its relatively efficient process and clear regulatory framework. However, note that pure DeFi protocols that are fully decentralised may fall outside the scope of MiCA, but the KNF assesses each case individually. For example, a platform that holds private keys for users would likely require a license.

License Type and Regulator

The license is a CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) authorisation under MiCA. The regulator is the Polish Financial Supervision Authority (Komisja Nadzoru Finansowego, KNF). The KNF is responsible for reviewing applications, ensuring compliance with AML/CFT rules, and supervising licensed entities. The license is valid across the EU under the passporting regime, meaning once you obtain it in Poland, you can serve clients in other EU countries without additional licensing. The KNF has published guidelines on the application process, including required documents and forms. It is advisable to engage a local legal advisor who is familiar with the KNF's expectations. The regulator also conducts on-site inspections and may request additional information during the review period.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, capital requirements are tiered by service class. For Poland in 2026, the minimum capital is:

  • EUR 50,000 for simple services (e.g., custody of crypto assets)
  • EUR 125,000 for exchange services (e.g., trading platforms)
  • EUR 150,000 for more complex services (e.g., full-service exchanges with custody)

These figures are set by MiCA and are non-negotiable. The capital must be paid up and held in a bank account or qualifying assets. Proof of capital is required at the application stage. For example, if you plan to offer both custody and exchange, you need EUR 150,000. The capital must be maintained throughout the license period. You cannot use borrowed funds; the capital must be own funds. The KNF may also require additional capital based on your risk profile.

Tax Treatment

Poland's tax treatment of crypto activities follows general corporate tax rules. Corporate income tax (CIT) is 19% (or 9% for small taxpayers with revenue under EUR 2 million). Crypto-to-crypto transactions are generally subject to CIT on gains. VAT does not apply to crypto exchange services under EU case law. Personal income tax on crypto gains is 19% (flat rate). It is essential to consult a local tax advisor, as the tax landscape can change. For example, Poland has introduced reporting obligations for crypto transactions under DAC8. Additionally, if your company is tax resident in Poland, you must file annual CIT returns and may be subject to transfer pricing rules if you transact with related parties.

Allowed Activities Under the License

A Poland CASP license permits the following activities:

  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets
  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Operation of a trading platform
  • Placement of crypto assets (underwriting)
  • Reception and transmission of orders
  • Advice on crypto assets

All activities must comply with MiCA's conduct of business rules, including transparency, conflict of interest management, and client asset protection. For example, if you operate a trading platform, you must have rules for order execution and price transparency. You cannot engage in market manipulation or insider trading. The KNF may also restrict certain activities if they pose higher risks.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Pre-application preparation: Prepare your business plan, AML/KYC policies, risk assessment, and internal procedures. Engage a local compliance consultant. This step typically takes 4-8 weeks.
  2. Company incorporation: Set up a Polish limited liability company (Sp. z o.o.) with the required share capital. This takes 2-4 weeks. You will need a registered office address in Poland.
  3. Drafting documentation: Prepare the application form, program of operations, governance arrangements, and financial projections. This can take 2-4 weeks.
  4. Submission to KNF: Submit the complete application along with the fee. The KNF acknowledges receipt and begins the review. The clock starts ticking.
  5. Review and queries: The KNF may ask for additional information or clarifications. Respond promptly to avoid delays. This phase can take 4-8 weeks.
  6. Decision: The KNF issues a decision within 3 months of a complete application (can be extended by 3 months in complex cases). If approved, you receive your CASP license.
  7. Post-licensing: Once approved, you must comply with ongoing reporting and supervision requirements. This includes annual audits, transaction reporting, and AML updates.

Banking and Payment Services

Opening a bank account for a crypto business in Poland can be challenging, as many traditional banks are cautious. However, several banks in Poland accept crypto clients with proper licensing, such as mBank and Alior Bank. You may also consider EMI (Electronic Money Institution) accounts or specialised crypto-friendly banks like Bank Frick (Liechtenstein) or ClearBank (UK). The KNF requires that you have a bank account in Poland for capital requirements. Consulting24 can help you identify suitable banking partners. For comparison, Panama offers more flexible banking options, but with a different regulatory framework. In Poland, you may need to provide your license and AML policies to the bank. Some banks also require a minimum deposit or revenue threshold.

Benefits of a Poland Crypto License

  • Regulatory clarity: MiCA provides a clear legal framework, reducing uncertainty.
  • Efficient process: Poland's KNF is known for a relatively streamlined process compared to some other EU regulators like BaFin in Germany.

Compliance and Trust

Maintaining a Poland crypto license requires ongoing compliance with MiCA and AML/CFT regulations. This includes regular reporting to the KNF, independent audits, and customer due diligence. Failure to comply can result in fines or license revocation. For example, in 2025, the KNF fined several entities for inadequate AML controls. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Incomplete application: Missing documents or unclear information can cause significant delays. For example, failing to include a risk assessment is a common oversight.
  • Underestimating capital requirements: Ensure you have the required capital ready and properly documented. The KNF may ask for proof of source of funds.
  • Ignoring AML requirements: A weak AML framework is a common reason for rejection. Your AML policies must cover all relevant risk areas.
  • Choosing the wrong service class: Your license must cover all intended activities; adding services later requires a variation, which costs time and money.
  • Not planning for banking: Secure a bank account early, as it can take time. Some banks require a minimum of 3 months to open an account.

Frequently asked questions

How long does a Poland crypto license take in 2026?

Typically 3 to 6 months from submission of a complete application to the KNF. The timeline depends on the complexity of your services and the quality of your documentation. Incomplete applications can add 2-3 months.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a Poland crypto license?

Under MiCA, the minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for custody services, EUR 125,000 for exchange services, and EUR 150,000 for full-service platforms. These amounts must be paid up and maintained in a bank account.

Can I serve clients in other EU countries with a Poland crypto license?

Yes. A Poland CASP license is passportable across the EU, allowing you to provide services in all member states without additional licensing. You must notify the KNF of your intent to passport.

What are the main costs involved in obtaining a Poland crypto license?

Costs include the application fee (EUR 2,000-5,000), company setup (EUR 1,000-3,000), AML/KYC compliance setup (EUR 5,000-15,000), and legal/consulting fees (EUR 10,000-30,000). Total estimated cost is EUR 18,000-53,000.

Is a Poland crypto license valid indefinitely?

No. The license is valid as long as you remain compliant. You must renew annually with the KNF and pay ongoing supervision fees. Failure to comply can lead to suspension or revocation.

Can I apply for a Poland crypto license if my company is not based in Poland?

You must first establish a legal entity in Poland (e.g., a Sp. z o.o.) before applying. The entity must have a registered office and management in Poland. Consulting24 can assist with company setup.

What documents are required for the application?

Key documents include a business plan, AML/KYC policies, risk assessment, governance arrangements, financial projections, and proof of capital. The KNF provides a checklist. Engaging a local advisor is recommended.

How does Poland compare to Panama for a crypto license?

Poland offers EU passporting but requires higher capital and longer timeline. Panama has no specific crypto license, lower cost (EUR 6,000 flat), and faster setup (2-4 weeks), but no EU access. Choose based on your target market.

What happens if my application is rejected?

The KNF will provide reasons for rejection. You can appeal or reapply after addressing the issues. Common reasons include weak AML controls or insufficient capital. Consulting24 can help you prepare a stronger application.

Do I need to be physically present in Poland during the application?

Not necessarily, but your management board members may need to appear before the KNF. You can appoint a local representative. Consulting24 handles the process remotely with local partners.
