What is a Slovakia crypto license?

Slovakia, as an EU member state, has fully implemented the Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) regulation as of 2026. A Slovakia crypto license is a CASP (Crypto-Asset Service Provider) authorization that allows companies to legally offer crypto services within the EU. The license is issued by the National Bank of Slovakia (Národná banka Slovenska, NBS). It covers activities such as exchange, custody, and transfer services.

Unlike some EU countries that previously operated under lighter regimes, Slovakia now enforces strict MiCA rules, including capital requirements of EUR 50,000, 125,000, or 150,000 depending on the service class. Understanding this framework is the first step to a successful application. Many applicants mistakenly assume the old VASP register model still applies, which leads to incomplete documentation. For example, some submit only a basic AML policy without a risk assessment, which NBS will reject outright.

Who needs a Slovakia crypto license?

Any company offering crypto-asset services in or from Slovakia needs a license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Brokers and dealers in crypto assets
  • Payment processors using crypto
  • ATMs operators

If your business involves holding client funds or assets, or facilitating transactions, you likely fall under CASP requirements. Even if you are based outside Slovakia but serve Slovak customers, you may need to register or obtain a license. A common mistake is assuming that a foreign license will suffice, but MiCA requires local authorization for each EU market. For instance, a company with a license from Malta cannot automatically serve Slovak clients without a branch or passporting notification, which adds time and cost.

If your business is purely non-EU, a Panama license may be more suitable. Panama offers global flexibility without EU compliance burdens.

License type and regulator

The Slovakia crypto license is a single CASP authorization under MiCA. The regulator is the National Bank of Slovakia (NBS), which oversees all financial services, including crypto. The license is divided into classes based on services offered, each with specific capital and operational requirements.

Key points:

  • Class 1: Basic services (e.g., exchange) - EUR 50,000 capital
  • Class 2: Custody and transfer - EUR 125,000
  • Class 3: Full services including trading platform - EUR 150,000

NBS also requires strong AML/KYC procedures, a local registered office, and fit-and-proper management. The regulator is known for thorough vetting, so preparation is critical. Many applicants underestimate the importance of a detailed business plan that aligns with the chosen class. For example, a startup planning to offer both exchange and custody should apply for Class 2 or 3, not Class 1, or risk rejection and a costly resubmission.

Capital requirement

Under MiCA, Slovakia imposes minimum capital requirements based on service type. These must be maintained as own funds, not as client money. The tiers are:

  • EUR 50,000 for exchange-only services
  • EUR 125,000 for custody or transfer services
  • EUR 150,000 for combined services including operating a trading platform

Additionally, NBS may require higher capital if your business volume is large. Capital must be in liquid assets and held in a Slovak bank account. Many applicants underestimate the need to prove the source of funds, a common mistake. Ensure you have clear documentation of where the capital comes from, such as bank statements or investment records. For example, if the capital is a loan from a related party, NBS may require a formal loan agreement and evidence of the lender's source of funds.

Tax treatment

Slovakia has a corporate tax rate of 21% (2026). Crypto transactions are treated as income or capital gains, subject to standard corporate tax. VAT does not apply to crypto-to-crypto exchanges, but fiat-to-crypto may be exempt. Personal income tax on crypto gains is 19% or 25% depending on income level.

One common mistake is failing to register for VAT if providing taxable services (e.g., advisory). Also, ensure proper accounting for each transaction. Consulting24 recommends working with a Slovak tax advisor to avoid penalties. For a simpler tax environment, consider Panama, which has no capital gains tax on crypto. Additionally, Panama does not tax foreign-source income, making it attractive for international operations.

Allowed activities

A Slovakia crypto license permits the following activities under MiCA:

  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets
  • Provision of custodial wallets
  • Transfer services on behalf of clients
  • Operation of a crypto trading platform

Activities not allowed include derivative products (which require a MiFID license) and unregulated gambling. Ensure your business model fits within these boundaries to avoid rejection. A common mistake is applying for a license that does not cover all intended services, requiring a costly amendment later. For example, if you plan to add custody later, you must apply for a higher class upfront or go through a separate modification process.

Step-by-step application process

Here is the typical process for obtaining a Slovakia crypto license:

  1. Pre-assessment: Review your business model and determine the license class. Consulting24 offers a free initial consultation.
  2. Company setup: Incorporate a Slovak company (usually an s.r.o.) with a registered office. Minimum capital is EUR 5,000 for the company itself.
  3. Documentation: Prepare AML/KYC policies, business plan, risk assessment, and fit-and-proper declarations for management.
  4. Capital deposit: Deposit the required own funds in a Slovak bank account and obtain a confirmation letter.
  5. Submit application: File with NBS including all documents and pay the fee.
  6. Review and interview: NBS may request additional information or interview management. This can take 3-6 months.
  7. Approval: Once approved, you receive the CASP license. You must start operations within 12 months.

Common mistakes include incomplete documentation and underestimating the time needed for bank account opening. Starting early with expert help can shorten the timeline. For example, having a pre-vetted compliance package ready before incorporation can save 1-2 months.

Banking and payments

Opening a bank account for a crypto company in Slovakia is challenging. Most traditional banks are cautious. Common mistakes include applying without a proper compliance package or choosing the wrong bank. We recommend approaching banks that accept crypto clients, such as some challenger banks or payment institutions. Examples include Revolut Business or a few local banks like Tatra banka, but they often require a license first.

Alternatives include using an EMI (Electronic Money Institution) or a crypto-friendly bank in Lithuania or Estonia. Compliance and trust

Ongoing compliance is mandatory. NBS requires annual audits, periodic AML training, and transaction monitoring. Common mistakes include neglecting to update policies or failing to report suspicious transactions. Non-compliance can lead to fines or license revocation. For example, missing a suspicious transaction report (STR) deadline can result in penalties up to EUR 500,000 or more.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Ignoring local presence: Slovakia requires a registered office and local compliance officer.
  • Choosing the wrong license class: Applying for a lower class than needed, leading to rejection.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Slovakia crypto license?

Under MiCA, the minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for exchange-only services, EUR 125,000 for custody or transfer, and EUR 150,000 for combined services including a trading platform. These must be maintained as own funds in a Slovak bank account.

How long does it take to get a Slovakia crypto license?

The typical timeline is 4-8 months from application to approval. This includes company setup, documentation, capital deposit, and NBS review. Delays often occur with bank account opening, so starting early is crucial.

Can I apply for a Slovakia crypto license remotely?

Yes, but you need a local registered office and a local compliance officer. NBS requires a physical presence for supervision. Consulting24 can coordinate with local partners to set up the necessary infrastructure.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

Ongoing requirements include annual audits, AML training for staff, transaction monitoring, and suspicious transaction reporting. NBS may also conduct periodic inspections. Non-compliance can lead to fines or license revocation.

Is a Slovakia crypto license valid across the EU?

Yes, under MiCA passporting, a Slovakia CASP license allows you to serve clients in all EU/EEA member states without additional licensing. You must notify NBS of your intent to passport.

What happens if I make a mistake in my application?

NBS may request corrections or reject the application. Common mistakes like incomplete documentation or wrong license class can delay approval by months. Consulting24 helps you avoid these errors.

Can I use a Panama license instead of Slovakia?

Yes, if your target market is non-EU. Panama offers a flat fee of EUR 6,000, no minimum capital, and faster setup. It is ideal for global operations without EU compliance burdens.

Do I need a local bank account in Slovakia?

Yes, you must open a corporate bank account in Slovakia to deposit the required capital and conduct business. This can be challenging for crypto firms; we recommend starting the process early.

What activities are not allowed under a Slovakia crypto license?

Derivative products (e.g., futures, options) require a MiFID license, not a CASP. Unregulated gambling and services outside the defined crypto activities are also prohibited.
