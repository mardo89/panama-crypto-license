Overview: What Is the Difference Between El Salvador and Panama for Crypto Companies?

El Salvador and Panama present two contrasting models for crypto businesses. El Salvador embraces Bitcoin as legal tender, offering a pro-crypto environment but lacking a dedicated licensing regime for exchanges and custodians. Panama, by contrast, has a clear licensing pathway under the Superintendencia del Mercado de Valores (SMV) for crypto asset service providers (CASPs). Panama also boasts a territorial tax system, meaning income earned outside Panama is tax-free, and there is no capital gains tax on crypto profits.

For founders, the choice often boils down to: do you want a pioneering but uncertain regulatory environment (El Salvador) or a stable, cost-effective, and well-defined framework (Panama)? Our experience shows that Panama's EUR 6,000 flat fee and straightforward process make it the preferred choice for most crypto companies, especially those targeting global markets.

To illustrate, consider a crypto exchange serving clients in Latin America and Europe. In Panama, the exchange can obtain a CASP license in 4-8 weeks, pay no tax on foreign income, and access international banking. In El Salvador, the same exchange would face legal uncertainty for non-Bitcoin assets, longer registration times, and potential tax complications. The operational simplicity of Panama is a decisive factor for many.

Who Needs a Crypto License in El Salvador or Panama?

Any business offering crypto-related services such as exchange, custody, payment processing, or token issuance may need a license or registration. In Panama, the SMV regulates CASPs under the Securities Law, requiring a license for activities like trading, custody, and advisory services. In El Salvador, while there is no specific crypto license, companies engaging in Bitcoin-related activities must register with the Central Reserve Bank (BCR) and comply with anti-money laundering (AML) regulations.

Typical clients include:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (centralized and decentralized)
  • Wallet providers and custodians
  • Payment processors using crypto
  • Token issuers and ICO/STO platforms
  • DeFi protocols seeking a legal entity

License Type and Regulator

Panama: The regulator is the Superintendencia del Mercado de Valores (SMV). The license is called a 'Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) License' under the Securities Law (Law 67 of 2011, as amended). It covers exchange, custody, and brokerage services. The license is issued for an indefinite period, subject to annual compliance.

El Salvador: There is no dedicated crypto license. The main regulator is the Central Reserve Bank (BCR) for Bitcoin-related activities under the Bitcoin Law (2021). Additionally, the Superintendencia del Sistema Financiero (SSF) oversees AML compliance. Companies must register as a 'Bitcoin Service Provider' (BSP) with the BCR, but this is not a full licensing regime.

For comparison, Panama's framework is more comprehensive and aligned with international standards, while El Salvador's approach is more experimental. The SMV in Panama actively engages with the industry and has issued guidance on custody, tokenization, and stablecoins. In contrast, El Salvador's BCR has limited resources and a narrow focus on Bitcoin.

Capital Requirements

Panama: There is no minimum capital requirement for a CASP license. However, the SMV expects the company to have sufficient financial resources to operate. In practice, a paid-up capital of $10,000-$50,000 is recommended to demonstrate substance.

El Salvador: No minimum capital is required under the Bitcoin Law. However, the BCR may require proof of financial stability during registration.

For both jurisdictions, capital requirements are lower than in EU member states under MiCA, which imposes tiers of EUR 50,000 / 125,000 / 150,000 depending on services. Panama's flexibility is a major advantage for startups. For example, a small exchange with limited initial capital can start in Panama without the burden of high capital thresholds, whereas in Lithuania or Estonia, the MiCA capital requirements would apply.

Tax Treatment

Panama: Territorial tax system: income earned outside Panama is tax-free. There is no capital gains tax on crypto profits. The only tax is on local-source income (e.g., services provided within Panama) at a flat 25% corporate rate. However, most crypto companies serve global clients and pay 0% tax. Panama also has no VAT on digital services.

El Salvador: No capital gains tax on Bitcoin (by law). However, other crypto assets may be subject to standard income tax (30% corporate rate). El Salvador also imposes a 13% VAT on goods and services, which may apply to crypto transactions. The tax treatment of non-Bitcoin crypto is unclear.

For tax efficiency, Panama is generally superior for international crypto businesses. Consider a company earning $1 million in trading fees from global clients: in Panama, tax is $0 on foreign income; in El Salvador, if the income is considered local, the tax could be $300,000 plus VAT. The difference is stark.

Allowed Activities Under the License

Panama (CASP License): Permitted activities include:

  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat or other crypto
  • Custody and wallet services
  • Brokerage and trading platforms
  • Payment processing using crypto
  • Token issuance (subject to SMV approval)

El Salvador (BSP Registration): The Bitcoin Law allows:

  • Bitcoin exchange and trading
  • Bitcoin payment processing
  • Bitcoin custody
  • Other crypto activities are not explicitly covered; legal uncertainty exists.

Panama's license covers a broader range of crypto assets, while El Salvador is limited to Bitcoin. If your business involves Ethereum, stablecoins, or DeFi tokens, Panama is the only viable choice. For example, a multi-asset exchange cannot operate legally in El Salvador for non-Bitcoin assets, whereas in Panama it can.

Banking and Payment Services

Panama: The banking sector is well-developed, with many banks accepting crypto company accounts. However, due to AML concerns, some banks may require the SMV license. Consulting24 can assist in opening accounts at banks like Banco General or Credicorp Bank. Payment processors like Stripe and PayPal are available, though some may have restrictions on crypto companies.

The Chivo wallet (government wallet) is available but limited to Bitcoin transactions. For global operations, Panama offers better banking access. Compliance and Trust: What You Need to Know

Both jurisdictions require strong AML/KYC programs. In Panama, the SMV conducts regular audits and expects compliance with FATF recommendations. El Salvador's BCR also enforces AML rules, but enforcement is less stringent.

As of 2026, Panama has been removed from the FATF grey list, enhancing its reputation. El Salvador remains under FATF scrutiny due to its Bitcoin adoption. For trust and credibility, Panama is the safer choice. We recommend all clients implement strong AML policies regardless of jurisdiction, as international partners will scrutinize them.

Frequently asked questions

Can I get a crypto license in El Salvador for non-Bitcoin assets?

No. El Salvador's Bitcoin Law only covers Bitcoin. Other crypto assets are not explicitly regulated, creating legal uncertainty. If your business involves Ethereum, stablecoins, or other tokens, Panama's CASP license is the appropriate choice as it covers all crypto assets.

What is the total cost to set up a crypto company in Panama?

The flat fee for the license is EUR 6,000, which includes company incorporation and application. Additional costs include annual compliance (~$2,000), legal fees if needed, and banking setup. Total first-year cost is typically EUR 8,000-10,000. Consulting24 provides a transparent breakdown.

How long does it take to get a Panama crypto license?

The process takes 4-8 weeks from application submission to license issuance. This depends on the completeness of your application and the SMV's workload. Consulting24 helps prepare all documents to avoid delays.

Is Panama tax-free for crypto companies?

Panama has a territorial tax system: income earned outside Panama is tax-free. There is no capital gains tax on crypto. Most crypto companies serve global clients and pay 0% tax. Only income from Panama-sourced services is taxed at 25%.

Do I need to travel to Panama to get the license?

No. Consulting24 handles the entire process remotely. You do not need to visit Panama. All documents can be submitted electronically, and the company can be managed from anywhere.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements for a Panama CASP license?

You must appoint a local compliance officer, conduct annual AML audits, file transaction reports, and renew the license annually. Consulting24 offers compliance packages starting at $2,000 per year.

Can I use Panama as a gateway to Latin American markets?

Yes. Panama's location, dollarized economy, and trade agreements make it an excellent hub for Latin America. The CASP license is recognized by regional partners, and banking access facilitates cross-border payments.

What happens if I operate without a license in Panama?

Operating without a CASP license in Panama is illegal and can result in fines, business closure, and criminal liability. The SMV actively monitors unlicensed activity. It is safer to obtain the license before launching.

Does Panama have a minimum capital requirement for crypto licenses?

No. There is no statutory minimum capital. However, the SMV expects sufficient financial resources. A recommended paid-up capital of $10,000-$50,000 demonstrates substance.

Can I convert my El Salvador BSP registration to a Panama license later?

You can, but it would require a new application in Panama. There is no reciprocity. If you plan to deal with multiple assets, starting in Panama is more efficient.

How does Panama compare to EU jurisdictions under MiCA?

Panama is cheaper and faster, with no capital requirements. EU jurisdictions require capital of EUR 50,000-150,000 and have higher ongoing costs. However, EU licenses offer passporting. Panama is better for non-EU focused businesses.
