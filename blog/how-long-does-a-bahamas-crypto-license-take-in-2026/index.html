What Is a Bahamas Crypto License?

The Bahamas crypto license is a regulatory authorization issued under the Digital Assets and Registered Exchanges (DARE) Act 2020, as amended. It allows businesses to operate digital asset services, including exchanges, custodians, and token issuers. The regulator is the Securities Commission of The Bahamas (SCB). The DARE Act provides a comprehensive framework that covers anti-money laundering (AML), consumer protection, and market integrity. In 2026, the Bahamas remains a popular choice for crypto firms seeking a balanced regulatory environment with a relatively fast licensing process compared to some European jurisdictions.

The DARE Act is well-regarded for its clarity and has attracted firms like FTX (prior to its collapse) and other digital asset businesses, though the regulator has since tightened requirements.

Who Needs a Bahamas Crypto License?

Any business that provides digital asset services in or from the Bahamas needs a DARE license. This includes:

  • Digital asset exchanges (centralized and decentralized)
  • Custodians and wallet providers
  • Token issuers and ICO platforms
  • Brokers and dealers in digital assets
  • Payment processors using digital assets
  • Digital asset investment funds

If you are targeting clients in the Caribbean or Latin America, or if you prefer a jurisdiction with a stable legal system and no capital gains tax, the Bahamas may be a good fit. However, for founders who want a faster and simpler process, Panama (with a flat fee of EUR 6,000 and no minimum capital) is often a better choice. License Type & Regulator

The Bahamas crypto license falls under the DARE Act, which creates two main categories: a Registration for digital asset businesses and a License for digital asset exchanges. Most applicants apply for a DARE registration, which covers a broad range of activities. The regulator is the Securities Commission of The Bahamas (SCB), which also oversees traditional securities. The SCB has a dedicated digital assets team that reviews applications.

Unlike some EU jurisdictions that now follow MiCA capital tiers (EUR 50,000 to 150,000), the Bahamas has its own capital requirements (see below). The SCB is known for being approachable and responsive, which contributes to the 4-6 month timeline. The SCB also requires a local physical office and at least one resident director or manager.

Capital Requirement for a Bahamas Crypto License

The Bahamas DARE Act does not prescribe a single minimum capital amount. Instead, the SCB assesses capital adequacy on a case-by-case basis, depending on the nature and scale of the business. Typically, applicants need to demonstrate at least EUR 100,000 in paid-up capital or equivalent. This is higher than Panama (no minimum) but lower than some EU MiCA tiers (EUR 125,000 for exchanges). Tax Treatment in the Bahamas

The Bahamas has no corporate income tax, no capital gains tax, no VAT, and no withholding tax. This makes it a tax-neutral jurisdiction for crypto businesses. However, there is a business license fee (annual) and stamp duties on certain transactions. The tax regime is a major draw for crypto founders, especially those from high-tax jurisdictions.

Panama also offers territorial taxation (no tax on foreign-source income) and no capital gains tax. Both jurisdictions are tax-friendly, but Panama has a simpler corporate structure and lower ongoing costs. Consulting24 can help you compare the tax implications for your specific business model. In the Bahamas, you must also pay an annual registration fee to the SCB, which ranges from EUR 5,000 to 10,000 depending on the license type.

Allowed Activities Under a Bahamas Crypto License

A Bahamas DARE registration or license permits the following activities:

  • Operating a digital asset exchange (order book, matching engine)
  • Providing custodial wallet services
  • Issuing tokens (security tokens, utility tokens, stablecoins)
  • Brokering digital asset transactions
  • Payment processing using digital assets
  • Asset management for digital assets
  • Digital asset advisory services

Note that the Bahamas does not allow unregistered securities offerings; all token issuances must comply with the DARE Act. Step-by-Step Process for a Bahamas Crypto License

The application process typically follows these steps:

  1. Pre-application preparation: Engage local legal counsel, prepare business plan, AML/KYC policies, and financial projections. Consulting24 can recommend trusted partners.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Bahamas company (International Business Company or ordinary company) with the Registrar General. This takes 1-2 weeks.
  3. Submit application to SCB: File the DARE application with all supporting documents. The SCB will acknowledge receipt within 5 business days.
  4. Review and queries: The SCB reviews the application and may request additional information. This stage takes 2-3 months.
  5. Approval and license issuance: Once satisfied, the SCB issues the license. Total timeline: 4-6 months.
