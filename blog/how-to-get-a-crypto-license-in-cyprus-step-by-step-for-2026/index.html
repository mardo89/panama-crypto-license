What is a Cyprus crypto license?

A Cyprus crypto license, officially a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) registration under the Cyprus Securities and Exchange Commission (CySEC), authorizes companies to offer crypto-related services such as exchange, custody, and transfer. Since MiCA came into full force in 2026, the Cyprus framework aligns with EU-wide standards, requiring CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) authorization. This license allows firms to passport services across all EU member states, making it a strategic gateway to the European market.

The regulator, CySEC, oversees compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorist financing (CTF) obligations. The license is mandatory for any entity providing crypto services to Cyprus residents or operating from Cyprus. It covers both fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto transactions, wallet custody, and other related activities.

Cyprus has been regulating crypto since 2018, giving it a mature framework compared to newer EU regimes. The MiCA harmonization means that a Cyprus CASP license is recognized across all 27 EU countries without additional registration. This is a major advantage for founders targeting multiple European markets.

Who needs a Cyprus crypto license?

Any business offering virtual asset services in or from Cyprus must obtain a license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralized and decentralized platforms)
  • Wallet providers (custodial and non-custodial)
  • Payment processors handling crypto
  • Brokers and dealers in virtual assets
  • Transfer services between crypto and fiat

If you plan to serve EU customers, a Cyprus license is one of the most efficient options due to its EU passporting rights. Founders from outside the EU often choose Cyprus for its relatively straightforward process and business-friendly environment. For example, a startup offering a crypto-to-fiat debit card for EU residents would need a CASP license in Cyprus (or another EU state). Similarly, a platform facilitating peer-to-peer crypto transfers for EU users must be licensed. Even non-custodial wallet providers may fall under MiCA if they offer additional services like exchange.

License type and regulator

The regulator is the Cyprus Securities and Exchange Commission (CySEC). Under MiCA, the license is classified as a Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) authorization. There is no separate 'class' of license; instead, the authorization covers specific services listed in the application. CySEC evaluates applications based on operational readiness, AML controls, and fit-and-proper criteria for management.

Key regulatory requirements include:

  • Registered office and physical presence in Cyprus
  • AML/CTF policies and procedures
  • Appointment of a compliance officer, MLRO, and auditor
  • Capital adequacy based on service type (see below)
  • Secure IT systems and data protection measures

CySEC is known for its rigorous but fair supervision, balancing innovation with investor protection. The regulator has published detailed guidelines on CASP applications, including templates for business plans and risk assessments. Capital requirement

Under MiCA, capital requirements for CASPs in Cyprus are tiered by service type:

  • EUR 50,000 for services like order execution, portfolio management, and advice
  • EUR 125,000 for exchange services (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto)
  • EUR 150,000 for custody and transfer services

These minimums must be maintained as own funds. If a company offers multiple services, the highest tier applies. For example, an exchange with custody would need EUR 150,000. Capital must be fully paid up and held in a Cyprus bank account. Consulting24 can assist with structuring capital efficiently.

It is important to note that these capital requirements are higher than in some other EU jurisdictions. For instance, Lithuania requires EUR 125,000 for exchanges, while Estonia requires EUR 100,000. Cyprus's tiered approach aligns with MiCA and reflects the risk profile of each service.

Tax treatment for crypto businesses in Cyprus

Cyprus offers one of the most attractive corporate tax regimes in the EU:

  • Corporate income tax rate: 12.5% (one of the lowest in the EU)
  • No withholding tax on dividends, interest, and royalties paid to non-residents
  • Participation exemption: Gains from the sale of shares or securities are tax-free
  • VAT: Crypto transactions are generally exempt from VAT
  • Notional interest deduction: On new equity capital, reducing effective tax rate

Cyprus has a double tax treaty network with over 60 countries, minimizing cross-border tax leakage. Crypto founders should structure operations through Cyprus to optimize tax efficiency. Consulting24 works with local tax advisors to ensure compliance.

For example, a Cyprus company holding crypto as an investment may benefit from the participation exemption on capital gains. Similarly, profits from trading activities are taxed at 12.5%, significantly lower than the average EU rate of 21%.

Allowed activities under the Cyprus crypto license

A Cyprus CASP license permits the following activities:

  • Exchange of virtual assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of virtual assets for other virtual assets
  • Operation of a trading platform (order book or automated)
  • Transfer of virtual assets on behalf of clients
  • Custody and administration of virtual assets
  • Participation in and provision of financial services related to ICOs/STOs
  • Advice on virtual assets (if registered separately)

Activities not covered include issuing crypto assets (unless as part of an ICO under prospectus rules) and mining. Any new service must be pre-approved by CySEC. The license allows passporting across the EU under MiCA, making it a powerful tool for pan-European operations.

Step-by-step process to get a Cyprus crypto license

  1. Pre-incorporation planning: Define business model, services, and structure. Engage legal and compliance advisors.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Cyprus limited company with a registered office and local directors (if required). Minimum share capital EUR 1,000.
  3. Prepare AML/CTF policies: Draft internal procedures, risk assessment, and appoint an AML compliance officer and MLRO.
  4. IT and security setup: Implement secure systems, data protection, and cybersecurity measures. Prepare business continuity plan.
  5. Capital deposit: Open a bank account in Cyprus and deposit the required minimum capital (EUR 50,000-150,000).
  6. Submit application to CySEC: Complete the application form, provide all supporting documents (business plan, policies, organizational chart, fit-and-proper forms).
  7. Review and response: CySEC reviews the application, may request clarifications or additional documents. This stage takes 3-6 months.
  8. On-site inspection (if required): CySEC may conduct an on-site visit to verify physical presence and operations.
  9. License approval: Once satisfied, CySEC issues the CASP authorization. You can then begin operations.
  10. Ongoing compliance: Submit periodic reports, maintain capital, and undergo annual audits.

Common mistakes when applying for a Cyprus crypto license

Avoid these pitfalls to speed up your application:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing business plans or AML policies cause delays
  • Underestimating capital requirements: Not having sufficient funds in the bank account before submission
  • Poor fit-and-proper checks: Directors with criminal records or insufficient experience may be rejected
  • Inadequate IT security: Lack of cybersecurity measures or data protection policies
  • Ignoring local presence: CySEC requires a physical office and local management

Frequently asked questions

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Cyprus?

The process typically takes 6 to 12 months from application submission to approval. Pre-submission preparation can add 2-4 months. Consulting24 can help streamline the process.

What is the minimum capital for a Cyprus crypto license?

Under MiCA, capital requirements are EUR 50,000 for advice/execution, EUR 125,000 for exchange, and EUR 150,000 for custody/transfer. The highest tier applies if multiple services are offered.

Can I passport a Cyprus crypto license to other EU countries?

Yes, under MiCA, a Cyprus CASP license allows you to provide services across all EU member states without additional licensing, subject to notification procedures.

What are the tax benefits of a Cyprus crypto license?

Cyprus offers a 12.5% corporate tax rate, no withholding tax on dividends, participation exemption on capital gains, and VAT exemption on crypto transactions.

Is a physical office required in Cyprus?

Yes, CySEC requires a registered office and physical presence in Cyprus. This can be a serviced office or a dedicated space. Consulting24 can help arrange this.

Can I apply for a Cyprus crypto license as a non-EU resident?

Yes, non-EU residents can apply. However, you must incorporate a Cyprus company and meet all regulatory requirements, including local directors and AML compliance.

What happens if my application is rejected?

How much does ongoing compliance cost?

What is the difference between a Cyprus VASP and CASP license?

VASP was the pre-MiCA term. Since 2026, all licenses are CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) under MiCA. The requirements are harmonized across the EU.

Can I operate a crypto exchange with a Cyprus license?

Yes, exchange services (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto) are permitted under the CASP license. You will need capital of EUR 125,000 and strong AML controls.
