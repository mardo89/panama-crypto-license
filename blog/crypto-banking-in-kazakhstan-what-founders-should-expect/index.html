What Is Crypto Banking in Kazakhstan?

Crypto banking in Kazakhstan refers to the provision of financial services that involve digital assets, such as custody, exchange, payment processing, and lending, under the regulatory oversight of the Astana International Financial Centre (AIFC). The AIFC is a special economic zone based in Nur-Sultan, modelled on the Dubai International Financial Centre, and operates under English common law. It offers a comprehensive legal framework for fintech and crypto businesses, including a dedicated regulatory body: the Astana Financial Services Authority (AFSA).

Unlike many other jurisdictions, Kazakhstan's crypto banking regime is not yet fully integrated with traditional banking, but the AIFC allows licensed entities to offer a range of services to both retail and institutional clients. The key advantage is the legal clarity provided by the AIFC's Digital Asset Framework, which covers asset tokenisation, exchange operations, and custody services.

For founders, this means you can establish a regulated crypto bank or a crypto-friendly banking service within a recognised international financial centre, with access to a growing market and potential connections to the wider Eurasian economic region.

Who Needs a Crypto Banking License in Kazakhstan?

A crypto banking license in Kazakhstan is required for any entity that intends to offer digital asset services to clients within or from the AIFC. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralised and decentralised)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Payment processors handling crypto-to-fiat conversions
  • Lending and borrowing platforms using digital assets
  • Asset management firms dealing with crypto portfolios
  • Token issuers and STO platforms

If your business model involves holding client funds or assets, or facilitating transactions between crypto and fiat currencies, you will likely need a license from AFSA. Startups and established firms alike can apply, but the process requires a substantive presence in the AIFC, including a local office and key personnel.

For those who prefer a lighter regulatory touch, Panama offers a company setup at a flat EUR 6,000 with no specific crypto license requirement for certain activities, though it lacks the same level of regulatory clarity.

License Type and Regulator

The primary regulator for crypto banking in Kazakhstan is the Astana Financial Services Authority (AFSA), operating under the AIFC. AFSA issues several types of licenses depending on the services offered:

Each license type has specific capital requirements, governance rules, and operational standards. The AIFC framework is aligned with international standards, including FATF recommendations, making it a credible jurisdiction for regulated crypto activities.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a crypto banking license in Kazakhstan depends on the specific activities you intend to carry out. Under the AIFC Digital Asset Framework, the capital tiers are:

  • EUR 50,000 for payment services and simple custody
  • EUR 125,000 for exchange and trading facilities
  • EUR 150,000 for lending and complex asset management

These amounts must be held in liquid assets, typically fiat currency, and maintained throughout the license period. The capital serves as a buffer against operational risks and demonstrates financial stability to the regulator. Unlike some jurisdictions, Kazakhstan does not require a security deposit or bond, but the capital must be fully paid up before licensing.

For comparison, Panama has no minimum capital requirement for a standard company setup, making it a lower-cost entry point for crypto businesses.

Tax Treatment

Tax treatment for crypto banking in Kazakhstan is favourable within the AIFC. The AIFC offers a tax incentive regime that includes:

  • 0% corporate income tax on income derived from AIFC-regulated activities for the first 10 years
  • 0% VAT on services provided within the AIFC
  • Exemption from withholding tax on dividends and interest paid to non-residents
  • No capital gains tax on disposal of digital assets within the AIFC

However, these benefits apply only to activities conducted through an AIFC-licensed entity. If your business also operates outside the AIFC, standard Kazakh tax rates may apply (20% corporate income tax, 12% VAT). It is essential to structure your operations to maximise the AIFC advantages. Always consult a tax professional with expertise in Kazakh and AIFC tax law.

Allowed Activities

Under an AIFC crypto banking license, you can engage in a wide range of activities, subject to the specific license type. Common permitted activities include:

  • Operating a digital asset exchange (order book, AMM, or hybrid)
  • Providing custodial services for digital assets
  • Processing crypto-to-fiat payments and withdrawals
  • Issuing stablecoins or tokenised assets
  • Lending and borrowing digital assets
  • Managing crypto investment portfolios
  • Offering staking and yield products

Activities that are explicitly prohibited include mixing/tumbling services, privacy coin operations, and any activities that violate AML/CFT regulations. The AIFC also restricts dealings with sanctioned entities and high-risk jurisdictions. It is important to review the AFSA rulebook for a complete list of allowed and prohibited activities.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The application process for a crypto banking license in Kazakhstan involves several stages. Here is a typical step-by-step outline:

  1. Pre-application consultation: Engage with AFSA to discuss your business model and determine the appropriate license type.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a legal entity within the AIFC. This requires a local registered address and a corporate structure compliant with AIFC regulations.
  3. Prepare documentation: Draft a detailed business plan, AML/KYC policies, risk management framework, governance structure, and financial projections.
  4. Submit application: File the application with AFSA, including all required documents and the application fee.
  5. Regulatory review: AFSA reviews the application, which may take 2-4 months. They may request additional information or clarifications.
  6. On-site inspection: AFSA may conduct an on-site visit to verify your office, systems, and personnel.
  7. License approval: Once satisfied, AFSA issues the license. You must then pay the license fee and meet any conditions.
  8. Post-licensing compliance: Ongoing reporting, audits, and AML/CFT obligations apply.

Banking and Payment Partners

One of the challenges of crypto banking in Kazakhstan is finding traditional banking partners. While the AIFC has its own banking infrastructure, including the AIFC Bank, many international banks are cautious about servicing crypto firms. However, several local and regional banks are open to working with licensed AIFC entities, especially if they have a clear regulatory status.

For payment processing, you can integrate with global payment gateways that support crypto-to-fiat conversions, such as those based in Lithuania or Estonia. These gateways often have existing relationships with EU banks. Alternatively, you can use the AIFC's own payment systems, which are still developing.

If banking access is a primary concern, Lithuania offers a more mature banking environment for crypto firms, with multiple banks willing to serve licensed entities.

Benefits of Crypto Banking in Kazakhstan

Kazakhstan offers several unique benefits for crypto banking founders:

  • Regulatory clarity under the AIFC Digital Asset Framework, which is aligned with international standards
  • Tax incentives, including 0% corporate tax for 10 years within the AIFC
  • Access to the Eurasian economic market, including Russia, Belarus, and other CIS countries
  • English common law legal system, familiar to international investors
  • Relatively low operational costs compared to Singapore or Dubai
  • Growing local talent pool in fintech and blockchain

For founders targeting the Central Asian and CIS markets, Kazakhstan provides a strategic base. However, the licensing process can be lengthy and costly, so it is important to weigh these benefits against alternatives like Panama or Estonia.

Compliance and Trust

Compliance is critical for maintaining your crypto banking license in Kazakhstan. AFSA requires strong AML/CFT measures, including customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting. You must also comply with the AIFC's Data Protection Regulations and ensure adequate cybersecurity measures.

Building trust with clients and partners requires transparency and regular audits. Many licensed firms publish their audited financial statements and compliance reports. It is also advisable to obtain ISO 27001 certification for information security.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make several mistakes when pursuing a crypto banking license in Kazakhstan. Here are the most common:

  • Underestimating capital requirements: Ensure you have sufficient liquid capital before applying.
  • Poor documentation: A weak business plan or AML policy can lead to rejection.
  • Ignoring local presence: AFSA requires a physical office and key personnel in the AIFC.
  • Overlooking ongoing costs: Annual compliance, audit, and office costs can be substantial.
  • Choosing the wrong license type: Applying for a license that does not match your activities can waste time and money.

Frequently asked questions

What is the regulatory body for crypto banking in Kazakhstan?

The Astana Financial Services Authority (AFSA) is the regulator for crypto banking within the Astana International Financial Centre (AIFC). AFSA issues licenses and oversees compliance with the Digital Asset Framework.

How much does a crypto banking license in Kazakhstan cost?

Total setup costs typically range from EUR 85,000 to EUR 240,000, including application fees, legal fees, and capital requirements. Exact costs depend on the license type and complexity of your business.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a crypto license in Kazakhstan?

Minimum capital ranges from EUR 50,000 for payment services to EUR 150,000 for lending and asset management, depending on the license type. Capital must be held in liquid assets.

Are there tax incentives for crypto businesses in Kazakhstan?

Yes, within the AIFC, licensed entities enjoy 0% corporate income tax for 10 years, 0% VAT, and no withholding tax on dividends to non-residents. These benefits apply only to AIFC-regulated activities.

How long does it take to get a crypto banking license in Kazakhstan?

The process typically takes 4 to 8 months, depending on the completeness of your application and the regulator's workload. Pre-application consultation can help streamline the process.

Can I use a Kazakhstan license to serve clients in the EU?

No, a Kazakhstan license does not provide EU passporting rights. For EU access, consider a license under MiCA in Lithuania or Estonia, which allows cross-border services within the EU.

What activities are allowed under a Kazakhstan crypto license?

Permitted activities include operating a crypto exchange, custody, payment services, lending, asset management, and token issuance. Activities must comply with AML/CFT regulations.

Do I need a physical office in Kazakhstan?

Yes, AFSA requires a physical office within the AIFC and at least one key person (e.g., compliance officer) based locally. Remote operations are not permitted for licensed entities.

Is Panama a better option than Kazakhstan for crypto banking?

Panama offers a much lower cost (EUR 6,000 flat) and faster setup (1-2 weeks), but lacks a dedicated crypto license and regulatory clarity. Kazakhstan provides a regulated environment with tax incentives but at higher cost.
