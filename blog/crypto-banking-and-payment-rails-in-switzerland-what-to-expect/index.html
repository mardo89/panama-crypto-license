What Is a Crypto Banking License in Switzerland?

A crypto banking license in Switzerland allows a company to accept deposits, provide custody of digital assets, and operate payment systems for third parties. The Swiss Financial Market Supervisory Authority (FINMA) regulates these activities under the Banking Act, the Financial Market Infrastructure Act (FMIA), and the DLT Act (effective since 2021). The DLT Act provides a legal basis for tokenized securities and crypto custody, making Switzerland a pioneer in blockchain regulation.

For crypto founders, this license enables integration with traditional finance, including SEPA transfers, SWIFT, and card issuing. Licensed banks like SEBA Bank and Sygnum offer regulated services for digital assets. However, obtaining a license is rigorous and requires substantial capital. For a more accessible alternative, consider Panama where company setup is a flat EUR 6,000 with no minimum capital. The Swiss model works best for established firms with significant funding.

In 2026, FINMA continues to refine its approach, especially for DeFi and stablecoins. The DLT Act allows for the issuance of uncertificated securities on blockchain, which reduces paperwork for tokenized assets. However, the high entry barrier means only well-capitalized firms can proceed. Startups often begin in lighter jurisdictions and later expand to Switzerland.

Who Needs a Crypto Banking License in Switzerland?

Any company that plans to accept deposits from the public, provide custody of crypto assets, or operate a payment system for third parties needs a license from FINMA. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges offering fiat on/off ramps
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Payment processors for crypto transactions
  • Banks offering digital asset services
  • Tokenization platforms issuing securities

If your business only deals with non-custodial wallets or pure peer-to-peer services, you may be exempt. However, most crypto startups require some form of authorization. Similarly, a payment processor handling fiat conversions must be licensed.

Capital Requirements

As of 2026, capital requirements for crypto banking in Switzerland are set by FINMA. For a FinTech license, minimum capital is CHF 1.5 million (approx. EUR 1.5 million). For a full banking license, it is CHF 10 million (approx. EUR 10 million). These must be fully paid in and maintained at all times. Capital can be held in fiat or high-quality liquid assets. Crypto assets are generally not accepted as regulatory capital due to volatility.

In contrast, the EU's MiCA regime (fully in force in 2026) requires capital tiers of EUR 50,000, 125,000, or 150,000 depending on service class. Switzerland is not an EU member, so its requirements are higher. For founders with limited capital, Panama offers a cost-effective alternative with no minimum capital requirement for crypto companies. Capital must be held in a Swiss bank account and cannot be used for operational expenses. FINMA may also require additional capital based on risk profile. For example, a company holding large amounts of client crypto may need extra capital for operational risk. We recommend discussing your specific situation with a Swiss advisor.

Tax Treatment

Switzerland has a favorable tax regime for crypto companies. Corporate income tax rates vary by canton but typically range from 12% to 21%. Capital gains from trading are tax-free for companies, and crypto-to-crypto transactions are generally exempt from VAT. However, mining income and staking rewards are taxable as ordinary income. Wealth tax applies to crypto holdings at the cantonal level.

For individuals, crypto held as private assets is exempt from capital gains tax if held for more than 6 months. Professional traders are subject to income tax. Switzerland also has double taxation treaties with many countries, reducing withholding taxes on dividends and interest. Consulting24 can help you structure your operations to optimize tax efficiency across jurisdictions. For example, a holding company in Panama may reduce overall tax burden.

Value-added tax (VAT) is generally not applied to crypto transactions, but services like custody may be subject to VAT. It is important to get a tax ruling from the cantonal tax authority to confirm your specific treatment. Consulting24 can connect you with tax advisors in Switzerland.

Allowed Activities Under a Swiss Crypto Banking License

A Swiss banking license permits a wide range of activities, including:

  • Accepting deposits from the public (fiat and crypto)
  • Providing custody services for digital assets
  • Offering payment accounts and card issuance
  • Executing fiat and crypto payments (SEPA, SWIFT, blockchain)
  • Lending and borrowing of crypto assets (full license only)
  • Operating a DLT exchange or trading platform
  • Tokenization of securities and other assets

FinTech license holders cannot lend or invest deposited funds. All activities must comply with anti-money laundering (AML) regulations, including KYC and transaction monitoring. FINMA also requires segregation of client assets and strong cybersecurity measures. For example, a licensed exchange can offer fiat on/off ramps, but must keep client funds in separate accounts. For a more flexible scope, consider Panama where activities are less restricted.

Additionally, the DLT Act allows for the creation of uncertificated securities on blockchain, which simplifies the issuance of tokenized assets. This is a unique advantage of Switzerland. Step-by-Step Application Process

The application process for a Swiss crypto banking license involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application: Engage with FINMA informally to discuss your business model and scope. Prepare a detailed business plan, risk management framework, and AML policies.
  2. Formal application: Submit all documents, including audited financial statements, organizational structure, and proof of capital. Pay the application fee (typically CHF 10,000-50,000).
  3. Review and feedback: FINMA reviews the application and may request additional information. This stage can take 3-6 months.
  4. On-site inspection: FINMA conducts an on-site audit of your operations, IT systems, and compliance procedures.
  5. Decision: FINMA grants or denies the license. If approved, you must maintain ongoing reporting and compliance.

Banking and Payment Integration

Once licensed, crypto companies in Switzerland can access traditional banking services. Major banks like UBS and Credit Suisse (now merged) offer accounts to regulated fintechs. Specialized crypto banks like SEBA and Sygnum provide integrated fiat and crypto accounts, payment rails, and custody. Payment integration includes SEPA Instant, SWIFT, and real-time blockchain transfers.

For payment processing, companies can use Swiss payment gateways or connect to the SIC (Swiss Interbank Clearing) system. Card issuing is possible through partnerships with Visa or Mastercard. Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make these mistakes when pursuing a Swiss crypto banking license:

  • Ignoring local presence: FINMA expects at least one board member to be resident in Switzerland. Some founders try to avoid this, leading to rejection.
  • Overlooking ongoing costs: Annual compliance costs can be EUR 100,000-300,000. Budget for these from the start.
  • Choosing the wrong license type: Applying for a full banking license when a FinTech license would suffice wastes time and money.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Swiss crypto banking license?

For a FinTech license, the minimum capital is CHF 1.5 million (approx. EUR 1.5 million). For a full banking license, it is CHF 10 million (approx. EUR 10 million). These must be fully paid in and maintained. Crypto assets are not accepted as capital.

How long does it take to get a Swiss crypto banking license?

The process typically takes 6-12 months for a FinTech license and 12-18 months for a full banking license. This includes pre-application, formal submission, review, and on-site inspection. Delays are common if documentation is incomplete.

Can I use a Swiss crypto banking license to operate in the EU?

No, Switzerland is not an EU member. A Swiss license does not automatically grant EU passporting rights. For EU operations, you need a license under MiCA in an EU member state, such as Lithuania or Estonia.

What is the difference between a FinTech license and a full banking license in Switzerland?

A FinTech license allows deposit-taking up to CHF 100 million but prohibits lending. A full banking license allows all banking activities including lending. Capital requirements are CHF 1.5 million vs CHF 10 million respectively.

Is Panama a good alternative to Switzerland for crypto banking?

Yes, Panama offers a much cheaper and faster option. Company setup is a flat EUR 6,000 with no minimum capital, and it can be completed in 2-4 weeks. However, Panama does not have a dedicated crypto banking license; you operate under general corporate law.

What are the ongoing compliance costs for a Swiss crypto banking license?

Ongoing compliance costs typically range from EUR 100,000 to 300,000 per year. This includes annual audits, AML training, regulatory reporting, and compliance officer salaries. Costs vary based on business complexity.

Can I hold crypto assets as regulatory capital in Switzerland?

No, FINMA does not accept crypto assets as regulatory capital due to volatility. Capital must be held in fiat or high-quality liquid assets. This is a key difference from some other jurisdictions.

Do I need a physical presence in Switzerland to get a license?

Yes, FINMA expects at least one board member to be resident in Switzerland. You also need a registered office and local management. Consulting24 can help you set up the required presence.

What activities are prohibited under a Swiss FinTech license?

FinTech license holders cannot lend or invest deposited funds. They can only accept deposits up to CHF 100 million and provide payment services. Lending requires a full banking license.

How does Swiss tax treatment compare to Panama for crypto companies?

Switzerland has corporate tax rates of 12-21% and taxes mining/staking income. Panama has territorial taxation, meaning income earned outside Panama is tax-free. Panama also has no capital gains tax. Consulting24 can help you structure for tax efficiency.
