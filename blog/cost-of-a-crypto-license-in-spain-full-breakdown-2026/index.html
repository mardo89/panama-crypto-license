What Is a Crypto License in Spain?

A crypto license in Spain, officially a registration as a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) with the CNMV, authorises businesses to offer services such as cryptocurrency exchange, custody of virtual assets, and operation of trading platforms. Under MiCA, which is fully implemented in 2026, all EU member states apply harmonised rules, but Spain has additional national requirements, including a mandatory registration with the Bank of Spain for anti-money laundering (AML) compliance. The license is essential for any crypto business targeting the Spanish market or EU-wide operations.

The cost of a crypto license in Spain includes initial application fees, legal and consultancy fees, capital requirements, and ongoing compliance costs. Consulting24 provides a full breakdown to help you budget accurately.

Who Needs a Crypto License in Spain?

Any entity providing crypto-asset services in or from Spain must obtain a license. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Platforms for initial coin offerings (ICOs) or security token offerings (STOs)
  • Brokers and dealers in crypto-assets
  • Payment services using crypto-assets

Foreign companies offering services to Spanish residents also need to register. The CNMV requires a physical presence or a legal representative in Spain. License Type & Regulator

The primary regulator for crypto-asset service providers in Spain is the Comisión Nacional del Mercado de Valores (CNMV). The license type is a registration as a VASP under MiCA, supplemented by Spanish AML regulations enforced by the Bank of Spain (Banco de España). The CNMV maintains a public register of all authorised CASPs.

Under MiCA, there are three capital tiers based on services offered: EUR 50,000 for certain services, EUR 125,000 for others, and EUR 150,000 for full-service providers. Spain applies these tiers strictly. Consulting24 advises on which tier applies to your business model.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for CASPs in Spain is determined by the type of services provided. The three tiers are:

  • Tier 1 (EUR 50,000): For services limited to order execution and transmission, or providing advice on crypto-assets.
  • Tier 2 (EUR 125,000): For services including custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients, or operating a trading platform.
  • Tier 3 (EUR 150,000): For full-service providers offering all services, including exchange, custody, and platform operation.

These funds must be held in a Spanish bank account or as a guarantee. Consulting24 can help you structure your capital allocation efficiently.

Tax Treatment

Spain has a specific tax regime for crypto-assets. Corporate income tax (CIT) is generally 25% (reduced to 15% for new companies in the first two years on qualifying profits). VAT is not applicable to crypto exchange transactions under EU law. However, capital gains from crypto trading are subject to CIT. Additionally, Spain imposes a wealth tax on crypto holdings above EUR 700,000 (threshold varies by region).

For individuals, crypto gains are taxed as savings income at rates up to 28%. Companies must report all crypto transactions to the Spanish tax agency (AEAT). Consulting24 advises on tax-efficient structuring, including the use of a Panama entity for non-EU operations.

Allowed Activities

With a Spanish crypto license, you can legally offer the following services within Spain and across the EU under MiCA passporting:

  • Exchange of fiat currency for crypto-assets
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for other crypto-assets
  • Custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • Operation of a trading platform for crypto-assets
  • Execution of orders for crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • Placement of crypto-assets (underwriting or private placement)
  • Advice on crypto-assets

Services not covered by MiCA, such as decentralised finance (DeFi) protocols or non-fungible tokens (NFTs) that are not classified as crypto-assets, may fall outside the license scope. Consulting24 can clarify which activities require authorisation.

Step-by-Step Process

The application process for a crypto license in Spain involves several stages. Consulting24 coordinates each step to ensure compliance.

  1. Pre-application assessment: We review your business model, services, and jurisdiction needs. This includes a gap analysis against MiCA and Spanish requirements.
  2. Company setup: Incorporate a Spanish company (Sociedad Limitada) with a registered office and appoint a legal representative. Minimum share capital is EUR 3,000 (separate from MiCA capital).
  3. Documentation preparation: Draft a business plan, AML/KYC policies, risk assessment, internal controls, and governance framework. All documents must be in Spanish.
  4. Capital deposit: Open a Spanish bank account and deposit the required minimum capital (EUR 50,000/125,000/150,000).
  5. Application submission: Submit the application to CNMV along with supporting documents and pay the fee. Simultaneously, register with the Bank of Spain for AML purposes.
  6. Review and interviews: CNMV may request additional information or conduct interviews with management. Timeline: 4-8 months.
  7. Approval and registration: Once approved, the company is listed on the CNMV register. You can then commence operations.

Compliance & Trust

Compliance is ongoing. License holders must:

  • Appoint an AML compliance officer based in Spain.
  • Conduct regular AML training for staff.
  • Report suspicious transactions to the Bank of Spain.
  • Undergo annual audits by an external auditor.
  • Maintain records of all transactions for at least five years.

Failure to comply can result in fines up to EUR 5 million or 5% of annual turnover. Consulting24 provides ongoing compliance support to ensure your operations remain within the regulatory framework. This is general guidance, not legal advice.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make these errors when applying for a Spanish crypto license:

  • Underestimating capital requirements: Ensure you have the correct tier funds available.
  • Incomplete documentation: CNMV requires detailed policies; generic templates are rejected.
  • Ignoring AML registration: The Bank of Spain registration is mandatory and separate from CNMV.
  • Choosing the wrong corporate structure: A Spanish SL is required; using a foreign entity delays the process.
  • Not budgeting for ongoing costs: Annual compliance can be EUR 10,000-25,000.
