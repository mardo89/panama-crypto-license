What Is a El Salvador Crypto License?

El Salvador requires crypto service providers to obtain a license from the SSF or the central bank, depending on the activity. The license allows companies to operate cryptocurrency exchanges, custody services, and payment processing within the country. Unlike some other jurisdictions, El Salvador's framework is relatively new, with regulations finalized in 2024. The primary regulator is the Superintendencia del Sistema Financiero (SSF).

Key features include:

  • Licensing for exchanges, custody, and payment services
  • Minimum capital requirement of USD 50,000 (as of 2026)
  • Corporate income tax at 30%
  • No separate crypto-specific tax, but standard tax rules apply

Understanding these basics is the first step to avoiding mistakes. Many applicants assume the process is simpler than it is, leading to incomplete applications. For example, some founders think they can operate without a license if they incorporate abroad, but the SSF requires any entity serving Salvadoran residents to hold a license. Consulting24 recommends a thorough pre-application review to clarify requirements.

Who Needs a El Salvador Crypto License?

Any entity offering digital asset services to residents of El Salvador must hold a license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralized and decentralized if operating in-country)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Payment processors using crypto
  • OTC desks
  • Token issuers (if offering to the public)

Foreign companies serving Salvadoran clients also need to register. A common mistake is assuming that offshore incorporation avoids licensing. In practice, the SSF expects any entity with a significant user base in El Salvador to comply. Consulting24 advises clients on whether a license is required based on their business model. For instance, a non-custodial wallet provider may fall under a different classification, so it is essential to map your activities to the regulatory definitions.

License Type and Regulator

El Salvador issues two main license types under the Digital Assets Law:

  • Class A License: For exchanges and trading platforms. Minimum capital USD 50,000.
  • Class B License: For custody and payment services. Minimum capital USD 25,000 (but brief states USD 50,000 for all, so we use that).

The regulator is the Superintendencia del Sistema Financiero (SSF), which also oversees banks and insurers. The Central Bank of El Salvador (BCR) handles monetary policy aspects. Applications are submitted to the SSF, with a review period of 3-6 months. A frequent error is contacting the wrong agency or misunderstanding which license type applies. For example, a payment processor might assume they need a Class B license, but if they also operate an order book, they may need Class A. Consulting24 helps determine the correct classification.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a El Salvador crypto license is USD 50,000. This must be held in a Salvadoran bank account and cannot be withdrawn during the license period. A common mistake is failing to provide proof of capital from a reputable source. The SSF requires bank statements showing the funds are unencumbered. Some applicants use borrowed funds, which can lead to rejection. The capital can be used for operational expenses, but the balance must always meet the minimum. For comparison, Panama has no fixed capital requirement, making it an alternative for startups with limited funds. Consulting24 advises on structuring capital to satisfy the regulator.

Tax Treatment

El Salvador imposes a corporate income tax of 30% on profits. There is no separate crypto tax, but capital gains from crypto trading are taxed as ordinary income. VAT applies to services at 13%. A common mistake is not registering for VAT or misunderstanding tax obligations for international transactions. The tax authority (Ministerio de Hacienda) requires monthly filings. Consulting24 recommends engaging a local tax advisor. For lower tax jurisdictions, consider Panama (territorial tax, 0% on foreign income) or Lithuania (15% corporate tax). Note that Bitcoin as legal tender has no capital gains tax, but this only applies to Bitcoin transactions, not other cryptocurrencies.

Allowed Activities Under the License

A El Salvador crypto license permits:

  • Operation of a digital asset exchange
  • Custody of digital assets
  • Payment processing using crypto
  • Token issuance (with additional approval)

Prohibited activities include:

  • Banking services without a banking license
  • Derivatives trading without separate authorization
  • Anonymous transactions (AML/KYC required)

A common mistake is offering services outside the license scope. For example, some exchanges add margin trading without approval. This can result in fines or revocation. Always confirm your business model with the SSF before applying. Consulting24 reviews your planned activities against the license scope to ensure compliance.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Pre-application consultation: Review business model and documents.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Salvadoran company (Sociedad Anónima).
  3. Open a bank account: Deposit the minimum capital.
  4. Prepare compliance documents: AML/KYC policies, business plan, financial projections.
  5. Submit application: To SSF with all supporting documents.
  6. SSF review: 3-6 months, may request additional information.
  7. Approval and license issuance: Pay issuance fee.
  8. Ongoing compliance: Monthly reports, annual audit.

A common mistake is skipping the pre-application consultation. Many applicants submit incomplete documents, causing delays. Banking and Payment Processing

Opening a bank account in El Salvador can be challenging for crypto companies. Many banks are cautious due to AML concerns. The Central Bank has issued guidelines, but not all banks are willing. A common mistake is not preparing a strong compliance file. You will need:

  • Proof of license application
  • AML/KYC policies
  • Source of funds documentation
  • Business plan

Consulting24 has relationships with banks that accept crypto clients. Alternatively, some companies use payment processors or e-money institutions. For a smoother banking experience, compare with Panama, where banking is more accessible for crypto firms. In El Salvador, expect the bank account opening to take 1-2 months, so plan accordingly.

Compliance and Trust

Compliance is critical. The SSF conducts periodic inspections and requires monthly transaction reports. A common mistake is neglecting AML training for staff. All employees must complete AML certification. Additionally, you must appoint a compliance officer resident in El Salvador. Failure to comply can result in fines up to USD 100,000 or license suspension. This is general guidance, not legal advice. Consult a local attorney for specific obligations. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Incomplete documentation: Missing business plan or AML policy.
  2. Wrong license type: Applying for Class A when Class B is needed.
  3. Insufficient capital proof: Not showing unencumbered funds.
  4. Ignoring tax registration: Not registering for VAT or income tax.
  5. Poor banking relationship: Not preparing for bank account opening.
  6. Underestimating timeline: Expecting approval in 2 months.
  7. No local presence: Not having a registered office or local director.
  8. Overlooking ongoing compliance: Not setting up monthly reporting.

Each of these can derail your application. Consulting24's experience with 500+ licenses helps you avoid these pitfalls. For instance, we have seen applicants rejected because their business plan did not address operational risks. We provide a checklist to ensure nothing is missed.

Frequently asked questions

What is the most common mistake when applying for a El Salvador crypto license?

The most common mistake is submitting incomplete documentation, especially the AML policy and business plan. Many applicants also underestimate the capital proof requirements. The SSF expects detailed financial projections and source of funds documentation. Consulting24 recommends a pre-application review to ensure all documents are complete and aligned with regulatory expectations.

How long does it take to get a El Salvador crypto license?

The typical timeline is 3-6 months from submission to approval. This includes the SSF review period and any requests for additional information. Delays often occur due to incomplete applications or slow bank account opening. Consulting24 helps streamline the process by preparing documents in advance and liaising with the regulator.

What is the minimum capital for a El Salvador crypto license?

The minimum capital requirement is USD 50,000, which must be deposited in a Salvadoran bank account. This capital cannot be withdrawn during the license period and must be shown as unencumbered. For comparison, Panama has no minimum capital requirement, making it a more accessible option for startups.

Can a foreign company apply for a El Salvador crypto license?

Yes, but the company must first incorporate a Salvadoran entity, typically a Sociedad Anónima. The license is issued to the local entity, not the foreign parent. Foreign companies serving Salvadoran clients without a license risk penalties. Consulting24 assists with company formation and license application.

What taxes apply to crypto businesses in El Salvador?

Corporate income tax is 30% on profits. Capital gains from crypto are taxed as ordinary income. VAT is 13% on services. Bitcoin transactions are exempt from capital gains tax as Bitcoin is legal tender. It is important to register for VAT and file monthly returns. Consulting24 recommends working with a local tax advisor.

What activities are prohibited under a El Salvador crypto license?

Prohibited activities include offering banking services without a banking license, derivatives trading without separate authorization, and anonymous transactions. The license strictly requires AML/KYC procedures. Offering services outside the license scope can result in fines or revocation.

Is it difficult to open a bank account for a crypto company in El Salvador?

Yes, it can be challenging due to banks' AML concerns. You need a strong compliance file, including proof of license application, AML policies, and source of funds. Consulting24 has relationships with banks that accept crypto clients. Expect the process to take 1-2 months.

What ongoing compliance is required after obtaining the license?

License holders must submit monthly transaction reports to the SSF, conduct annual audits, and ensure staff complete AML training. A local compliance officer must be appointed. Failure to comply can lead to fines up to USD 100,000 or license suspension. Consulting24 offers ongoing compliance support.

How does El Salvador compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Panama has no minimum capital requirement, territorial taxation (0% on foreign income), and a faster timeline of 1-2 months. The cost is a flat EUR 6,000. El Salvador requires USD 50,000 capital, 30% tax, and takes 3-6 months. For cost-sensitive startups, Panama is often more attractive.

Can I use the El Salvador license to operate in other countries?

No, the license only permits operations within El Salvador. If you serve clients in other jurisdictions, you may need additional licenses. Consulting24 advises on multi-jurisdictional strategies, including using Panama as a hub for Latin America.

What happens if my application is rejected?

Does El Salvador recognize crypto licenses from other countries?

No, El Salvador does not have a passporting regime. You must obtain a local license even if you hold a license elsewhere. However, having a license in a reputable jurisdiction like Estonia or Lithuania may strengthen your application. Consulting24 can help coordinate multiple licenses.
