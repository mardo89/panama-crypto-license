What Is a Crypto License in El Salvador?

A crypto license in El Salvador is an authorization granted by the CNAD to operate digital asset services legally within the country. The license covers activities such as operating a cryptocurrency exchange, providing custody services, issuing tokens, and facilitating peer-to-peer transactions. The regulatory framework is one of the most advanced in Latin America, offering legal certainty for both local and international businesses.

El Salvador's approach is unique because it treats Bitcoin as legal tender alongside the US dollar. This means that businesses dealing with Bitcoin are subject to specific obligations, such as accepting Bitcoin as a means of payment if they offer goods or services. The license ensures that your operations comply with AML, counter-terrorism financing (CTF), and consumer protection standards.

For founders, the key advantage is access to a growing crypto-friendly market with a clear legal framework. However, the process requires careful preparation, including a detailed business plan, compliance policies, and proof of adequate capital.

Who Needs a Crypto License in El Salvador?

Any entity that provides digital asset services to residents of El Salvador or operates within its territory must obtain a license. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (centralized and decentralized)
  • Digital wallet providers
  • Token issuers (security tokens, utility tokens, stablecoins)
  • Custodial service providers
  • Payment processors using digital assets
  • Brokerage and trading platforms

If your business involves any of these activities and you target Salvadoran users, you need a license. Even if your company is registered abroad, serving Salvadoran clients triggers the licensing requirement. The CNAD has extraterritorial reach for activities that affect the local market.

License Types and Regulatory Authority

The CNAD (Comisión Nacional de Activos Digitales) is the sole regulator for digital assets in El Salvador. Under the Digital Assets Issuance Law, there are two main license categories:

License TypeActivities Covered
Digital Asset Service Provider (DASP)Exchange, wallet, custody, payment processing, brokerage
Digital Asset Issuer LicenseToken issuance (security tokens, stablecoins, NFTs)

The DASP license is the most common for crypto exchanges and wallet providers. The issuer license is for companies planning to launch their own tokens. Both licenses require compliance with AML/CTF regulations, corporate governance standards, and reporting obligations.

The CNAD also oversees the registration of Bitcoin ATMs and other physical crypto service points. If you plan to operate kiosks or ATMs, you may need an additional permit.

Capital Requirements

El Salvador's capital requirements for crypto licenses are set by the CNAD and depend on the license type and the scope of activities. As of 2026, the minimum capital requirements are:

  • Digital Asset Service Provider (DASP): EUR 25,000
  • Digital Asset Issuer License: EUR 50,000
  • Combined license (DASP + Issuer): EUR 75,000

The capital must be deposited in a Salvadoran bank account and can be in US dollars or Bitcoin (at the current market value). The CNAD requires proof of capital before the license is issued. The funds must remain in the account as a reserve and cannot be used for operational expenses without prior approval.

Founders should note that these are minimums. The CNAD may require higher capital if your business model involves higher risk, such as large transaction volumes or complex token structures. Consulting24 can help you structure your capital plan to meet regulatory expectations.

Tax Treatment for Crypto Businesses in El Salvador

El Salvador offers a favorable tax regime for digital asset businesses. Key tax points include:

  • No capital gains tax on Bitcoin or other digital assets
  • Income tax rate of 30% on net profits (standard corporate rate)
  • VAT (13%) applies to services provided to Salvadoran residents, but crypto-to-crypto trades are generally exempt
  • No withholding tax on dividends paid to foreign shareholders

Bitcoin is treated as legal tender, so transactions in Bitcoin are considered as payments in foreign currency for tax purposes. This simplifies accounting for businesses that accept Bitcoin. However, companies must keep detailed records of all transactions for tax reporting.

El Salvador also has tax incentives for tech companies, including a 10-year exemption from municipal taxes for businesses located in the Bitcoin City development zone. Founders should consult a local tax advisor to optimize their tax structure.

Allowed Activities Under the License

Once you obtain a crypto license in El Salvador, you can engage in a wide range of activities, including:

  • Operating a cryptocurrency exchange (buying and selling digital assets)
  • Providing custodial wallet services
  • Issuing digital tokens (security tokens, stablecoins, utility tokens)
  • Facilitating peer-to-peer transactions
  • Running Bitcoin ATMs
  • Offering payment processing using digital assets
  • Providing crypto lending and borrowing services

The license does not cover activities that require a banking license, such as accepting deposits from the public or issuing credit. If your business model includes such activities, you may need a separate banking license from the Central Reserve Bank of El Salvador.

It is important to note that the CNAD has the authority to impose conditions on your license, such as transaction limits or reporting requirements. These conditions are typically outlined during the application process.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Here is the step-by-step process to get a crypto license in El Salvador:

  1. Pre-application preparation: Prepare your business plan, AML/CTF policies, corporate documents (articles of incorporation, shareholder register), and proof of capital. Engage a local lawyer to draft the application.
  2. Submit the application: File the application with the CNAD, including all required documents and the application fee. The CNAD will acknowledge receipt within 5 business days.
  3. Review and due diligence: The CNAD conducts a thorough review, including background checks on directors and shareholders, assessment of AML policies, and evaluation of the business model. This stage takes 2-4 months.
  4. Capital deposit: Once the CNAD approves the application in principle, you must deposit the required minimum capital in a Salvadoran bank account and provide proof of deposit.
  5. License issuance: After the capital is verified, the CNAD issues the license. You can then commence operations.
  6. Post-licensing compliance: Submit regular reports (monthly transaction reports, annual audit, AML updates). The CNAD may conduct on-site inspections.

These banks accept licensed crypto firms, but they may require additional documentation, such as the license certificate, AML policies, and a detailed business plan. Some banks may also require a minimum deposit of USD 10,000 to open a corporate account.

Compliance and Trust: Ongoing Obligations

After obtaining a crypto license in El Salvador, you must maintain ongoing compliance to retain the license. Key obligations include:

  • AML/CTF reporting: Submit suspicious transaction reports (STRs) to the Financial Intelligence Unit (UIF) within 24 hours of detection.
  • Monthly transaction reports: Provide the CNAD with a summary of all transactions, including volumes and counterparties.
  • Annual audit: Engage an independent auditor to review your financial statements and compliance program.
  • Corporate governance: Maintain a board of directors, hold annual general meetings, and keep updated shareholder registers.
  • Capital maintenance: Ensure that the minimum capital requirement is met at all times.
  • On-site inspections: The CNAD may conduct unannounced inspections of your premises and records.

Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. Consulting24 recommends partnering with a local compliance officer or outsourcing compliance to a specialized firm. Common Mistakes When Applying for a Crypto License in El Salvador

Founders often make these mistakes during the application process:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing or poorly prepared AML policies, business plans, or corporate documents can delay the application.
  • Underestimating capital requirements: The CNAD may require higher capital than the minimum if your business model is high-risk.
  • Ignoring banking requirements: Some founders assume they can open a bank account easily, but banks often require the license to be issued first, creating a chicken-and-egg problem.
  • Not engaging local legal counsel: The CNAD prefers applications prepared by Salvadoran lawyers who understand the local regulatory nuances.
  • Overlooking ongoing compliance: Many founders focus on obtaining the license but neglect the reporting and audit obligations, leading to penalties.

Frequently asked questions

What is the first step to get a crypto license in El Salvador?

The first step is to prepare a comprehensive business plan and AML/CTF policies. Engage a local Salvadoran lawyer to help draft the application and ensure all corporate documents are in order. Then submit the application to the CNAD with the required fee.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in El Salvador?

The process typically takes 3 to 6 months from submission to approval. Delays can occur if the application is incomplete or if the CNAD requires additional information. Expedited processing may be available for an extra fee.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a crypto license in El Salvador?

For a Digital Asset Service Provider (DASP), the minimum capital is EUR 25,000. For a Digital Asset Issuer License, it is EUR 50,000. A combined license requires EUR 75,000. The capital must be deposited in a Salvadoran bank account.

Can I operate a crypto exchange in El Salvador without a license?

No, operating a crypto exchange without a license is illegal. The CNAD requires all digital asset service providers to obtain a license before offering services to Salvadoran residents. Unlicensed operations can result in fines and criminal penalties.

What are the tax benefits for crypto businesses in El Salvador?

El Salvador imposes no capital gains tax on Bitcoin or other digital assets. Corporate income tax is 30% on net profits. VAT at 13% applies to services provided to residents, but crypto-to-crypto trades are generally exempt. There are also tax incentives for tech companies in special zones.

Do I need to be physically present in El Salvador to apply?

No, you do not need to be physically present. The application can be submitted through a local legal representative. However, you may need to travel for bank account opening or regulatory interviews if required by the CNAD.

Can I use Bitcoin to meet the capital requirement?

Yes, the CNAD accepts Bitcoin as capital, valued at the current market rate. The Bitcoin must be held in a Salvadoran bank account that supports digital assets. The exact amount in USD equivalent must meet the minimum requirement.

What happens if I fail to comply with ongoing obligations?

Non-compliance can lead to fines, suspension of your license, or revocation. The CNAD may also impose restrictions on your operations. It is crucial to maintain proper records, submit reports on time, and adhere to AML/CTF requirements.

Is El Salvador a good jurisdiction for crypto startups compared to Panama?

El Salvador offers unique benefits like Bitcoin legal tender and a crypto-friendly government, but the licensing process is more expensive and time-consuming.
