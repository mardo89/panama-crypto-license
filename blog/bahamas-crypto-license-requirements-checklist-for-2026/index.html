What Is the Bahamas Crypto License?

The Bahamas crypto license, officially called a Digital Asset Business (DAB) license, is issued under the DARE Act. It allows companies to conduct a range of digital asset activities legally within the Bahamas. The regulator is the Securities Commission of the Bahamas (SCB), which oversees compliance, anti-money laundering (AML), and investor protection.

The license covers services such as operating a digital asset exchange, providing custodial wallet services, trading digital assets, and issuing tokens. The framework is designed to be both innovative and compliant with international standards, including FATF recommendations. In 2026, the Bahamas remains a non-EU jurisdiction, meaning it is not subject to MiCA, but it has its own rigorous regime.

For founders, the appeal lies in the jurisdiction's stability, tax neutrality, and a regulator that understands digital assets. However, the process is thorough and requires a physical presence, local directors, and a detailed business plan. Who Needs a Bahamas Crypto License?

Any business that provides digital asset services to persons in or from the Bahamas must hold a DAB license. This includes:

  • Digital asset exchanges (centralized and decentralized, if operating from the Bahamas)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Digital asset trading platforms
  • Token issuers (if the token is considered a security or digital asset under Bahamian law)
  • Payment service providers using digital assets
  • Brokers and dealers in digital assets

If your target market includes the Bahamas or you plan to establish a physical presence there, you need this license. The SCB also requires that the ultimate beneficial owners (UBOs) and key personnel pass a fit-and-proper test. License Type and Regulator

The Bahamas crypto license is a single license type called the Digital Asset Business License. However, the SCB may impose conditions based on the specific activities. The regulator is the Securities Commission of the Bahamas (SCB), established under the Securities Industry Act. The SCB is responsible for licensing, supervision, and enforcement.

The DARE Act also distinguishes between different classes of digital assets, but the license itself is unified. Applicants must demonstrate that they have adequate systems, controls, and capital. The SCB has issued guidelines on the application process, which we detail below. For 2026, the SCB continues to refine its approach, with a focus on AML/CFT compliance and consumer protection.

Capital Requirement

The Bahamas DARE Act does not prescribe a fixed minimum capital requirement in the law itself. Instead, the SCB determines the required capital on a case-by-case basis, taking into account the nature, scale, and complexity of the business. As a general guideline, applicants should expect to demonstrate capital of at least EUR 100,000 to EUR 500,000, depending on the activities.

This capital must be maintained in liquid form, such as cash or highly liquid assets, and may be subject to regulatory restrictions on withdrawal. The SCB also expects that the company has sufficient operational funds for at least 12 months. For comparison, EU jurisdictions under MiCA have tiered capital requirements: EUR 50,000 for certain services, EUR 125,000 for exchanges, and EUR 150,000 for custody. Panama has no minimum capital requirement. Consulting24 advises on structuring your capital to meet the SCB's expectations.

Tax Treatment

The Bahamas is a tax-neutral jurisdiction. There is no corporate income tax, no capital gains tax, no VAT, and no withholding tax on dividends or interest. Instead, the government relies on other fees, such as business license fees and stamp duties. This makes the Bahamas highly attractive for crypto businesses that want to minimize tax leakage.

However, companies must still comply with international tax transparency standards, including the Common Reporting Standard (CRS) and FATCA. The Bahamas has also implemented economic substance requirements for certain entities, but digital asset businesses are generally not subject to substance requirements beyond having a physical office and local directors. Allowed Activities Under the License

The Bahamas DAB license permits a broad range of digital asset activities. Specifically, the DARE Act lists the following as regulated activities:

  • Operating a digital asset exchange (order book, matching engine)
  • Providing custodial wallet services
  • Digital asset trading (broker/dealer)
  • Digital asset issuance (including ICOs/STOs)
  • Payment services using digital assets
  • Digital asset management
  • Any other service prescribed by the SCB

Notably, the Bahamas does not license decentralized finance (DeFi) protocols as such, but if a DeFi platform has a central operator in the Bahamas, it may fall under the license. Mining and staking services are generally not regulated unless they involve custody of client assets. Consulting24 can map your business activities to the license scope and identify any gaps.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining a Bahamas crypto license involves several stages. Here is a step-by-step overview:

  1. Pre-application consultation - Engage with the SCB informally to discuss your business model. This is optional but recommended.
  2. Company incorporation - Register a Bahamian company with a registered office and local directors.
  3. Document preparation - Prepare a detailed business plan, AML/CFT policies, risk assessment, financial projections, and organizational structure.
  4. Fit and proper checks - Submit personal declarations, CVs, and police certificates for all directors, shareholders, and key personnel.
  5. Submit application - File the complete application with the SCB, including all supporting documents and the application fee.
  6. SCB review - The SCB reviews the application, may request additional information, and conducts background checks.
  7. Interview - Key personnel may be required to attend an interview with the SCB.
  8. Approval and license issuance - Once approved, the license is issued with any conditions. The company must pay the annual license fee.
  9. Ongoing compliance - Submit periodic reports, undergo audits, and maintain AML/CFT compliance.

Compliance and Trust Framework

The SCB requires all licensees to maintain strong AML/CFT controls in line with FATF standards. This includes appointing a Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO), conducting customer due diligence (CDD), and filing suspicious transaction reports (STRs). Licensees must also undergo annual audits by a SCB-approved auditor.

Additionally, the Bahamas has a mandatory data protection regime under the Data Protection Act, which applies to personal data of customers. Companies must register with the Data Protection Commissioner. The SCB also conducts periodic on-site inspections. Common Mistakes When Applying

Founders often make several avoidable errors when applying for a Bahamas crypto license. Here are the most common:

  • Lack of local substance - Not having a physical office or local directors can delay or derail the application.
