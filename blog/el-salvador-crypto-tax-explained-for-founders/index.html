What Is the El Salvador Crypto Tax Regime?

El Salvador does not impose a specific crypto tax law. Instead, crypto transactions are treated under existing tax legislation. The key points are:

  • No capital gains tax on Bitcoin (BTC) - Since Bitcoin is legal tender, gains from selling BTC are not taxed as capital gains. This applies to both individuals and businesses.
  • Income tax on business profits - If you are a company earning revenue in crypto, that income is subject to standard corporate income tax (30% for most entities).
  • VAT (IVA) - Standard 13% VAT applies to goods and services, including those paid in crypto. However, certain crypto-to-crypto trades may be exempt if considered currency exchange.
  • No withholding tax on dividends - Dividends paid to shareholders are not subject to withholding tax, which is attractive for founders.

The lack of a dedicated crypto tax framework creates both opportunities and ambiguities. Many founders use El Salvador as a residency or incorporation base, while operating their crypto business elsewhere.

Who Needs to Pay El Salvador Crypto Tax?

Any individual or legal entity that is tax resident in El Salvador must declare and pay taxes on worldwide income, including crypto income. Non-residents are only taxed on Salvadoran-source income.

You are considered tax resident if you spend more than 183 days in the country in a calendar year, or if your center of vital interests is in El Salvador. For companies, incorporation in El Salvador generally creates tax residency.

Founders who incorporate in El Salvador but live elsewhere may still have tax obligations in their home country. Consulting24 advises clients to check double tax treaties and plan accordingly. For those seeking a simple, low-cost jurisdiction, Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 company setup with territorial taxation.

License Type & Regulator

El Salvador does not have a dedicated crypto license or regulator for crypto asset service providers (CASPs). The country's approach is permissive: Bitcoin is legal tender, and businesses can operate without a specific license for crypto activities.

However, certain activities may require registration under the Law for the Issuance of Digital Assets (Ley de Emisión de Activos Digitales, or LEAD), passed in 2023. This law covers digital asset issuances (e.g., security tokens) and is regulated by the National Digital Assets Commission (CNAD). For most crypto exchanges, wallets, and payment processors, no license is needed, but they must comply with anti-money laundering (AML) regulations under the Financial Investigation Unit (UIF).

In contrast, EU member states under MiCA require a CASP license with capital tiers of EUR 50,000 to EUR 150,000. Consulting24 helps founders choose the right path, whether it is the open regime in El Salvador or a regulated license in Lithuania.

Capital Requirement

El Salvador does not impose a minimum capital requirement for standard companies (Sociedad Anónima de Capital Variable, or S.A. de C.V.). You can start with as little as USD 1 (approximately EUR 0.90). However, for digital asset issuances under LEAD, there may be capital requirements set by the CNAD, typically starting at USD 50,000.

This is a major advantage over EU MiCA regimes, where capital requirements range from EUR 50,000 to EUR 150,000 depending on services offered. For founders who want low upfront costs, El Salvador or Panama (with no minimum capital) are attractive.

Tax Treatment of Crypto Transactions

The tax treatment of crypto in El Salvador can be summarized as follows:

  • Bitcoin (BTC) as legal tender: No capital gains tax on BTC sales or exchanges. This is unique to El Salvador.
  • Other cryptocurrencies (altcoins): Not considered legal tender. Gains from trading altcoins may be subject to capital gains tax at standard income tax rates (up to 30%). However, the law is unclear and many founders treat them similarly to BTC.
  • Business revenue in crypto: Taxed as ordinary income at 30% corporate tax rate. Expenses paid in crypto are deductible if properly documented.
  • Mining and staking rewards: Considered income at the time of receipt, valued at market price. Subject to income tax.
  • VAT (IVA): 13% on goods and services. Crypto-to-crypto trades may be VAT-exempt if considered currency exchange, but this is not fully settled.

Allowed Activities for Crypto Businesses

El Salvador allows a wide range of crypto activities without a specific license:

  • Exchange operations - Buying and selling crypto for fiat or other crypto.
  • Wallet services - Custodial and non-custodial wallets.
  • Payment processing - Accepting crypto payments for goods and services.
  • Mining and staking - Both individual and industrial.
  • Token issuance - Under LEAD for security tokens; utility tokens may not require registration.
  • DeFi protocols - No specific restrictions, but AML compliance is required.

However, activities that involve securities (e.g., tokenized stocks) fall under CNAD regulation. For a fully regulated environment, many founders prefer Estonia or Lithuania, where Consulting24 obtains licenses directly.

Step-by-Step Process to Set Up a Crypto Business in El Salvador

  1. Choose your legal structure - Most founders use an S.A. de C.V. (variable capital corporation).
  2. Register the company - With the National Commerce Registry (CNR). You need a local legal representative if you are not resident.
  3. Obtain a tax ID (NIT) - From the Ministry of Finance.
  4. Register for VAT (IVA) - If you will provide taxable services.
  5. Open a bank account - This can be challenging for crypto businesses. Consulting24 can assist with introductions.
  6. Register with the UIF - For AML compliance, file a Beneficial Ownership Declaration.
  7. If issuing digital assets - Apply to the CNAD for authorization under LEAD.
  8. Set up accounting and tax filing - Hire a local accountant to handle monthly and annual filings.

The process takes 4-8 weeks. Consulting24 advises on the entire journey, from incorporation to ongoing compliance.

Banking & Payment Services

Banking in El Salvador for crypto businesses is improving but still limited. Local banks are cautious due to AML concerns. Options include:

  • Banco Agrícola - May open accounts for crypto businesses with proper documentation.
  • Banco Cuscatlán - Similar, but requires a local director.
  • Digital banks - Such as Chivo (government-backed Bitcoin wallet) and some neobanks.
  • International payment processors - Like Stripe or PayPal, but they may restrict crypto businesses.

Many founders use a multi-jurisdictional approach: incorporate in El Salvador for tax benefits, but bank in Panama or Lithuania. Consulting24 can help you set up accounts in Panama where banking is more straightforward.

Benefits of El Salvador for Crypto Founders

  • No capital gains tax on Bitcoin - A major advantage for BTC-heavy portfolios.
  • No withholding tax on dividends - Profits can be repatriated tax-free.
  • Low incorporation costs - No minimum capital and fast registration.
  • Bitcoin legal tender - Encourages adoption and innovation.
  • No specific crypto license - Low regulatory burden for most activities.
  • Strategic location - Close to the US and with a dollarized economy (USD is official currency).

Compliance & Trust Considerations

While El Salvador is permissive, compliance is still mandatory. Key requirements:

  • AML/CFT compliance - Register with the UIF, implement KYC procedures, and report suspicious transactions.
  • Beneficial ownership register - Must be filed annually.
  • Tax filings - Monthly VAT returns and annual income tax returns. Late filings incur penalties.
  • Transfer pricing - If you have related-party cross-border transactions, documentation is required.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Assuming all crypto is tax-free - Only Bitcoin as legal tender has a clear exemption. Altcoins and business income are taxable.
  2. Ignoring VAT obligations - Even if you transact in crypto, you may need to charge and remit 13% VAT.
  3. Not registering for AML - Operating without UIF registration can lead to fines or closure.
  4. Using personal accounts for business - This creates tax and legal risks. Always use a company account.
  5. Overlooking home country taxes - If you are tax resident elsewhere, you may still owe tax there. Double tax treaties may help.

Frequently asked questions

Is Bitcoin tax-free in El Salvador?

Yes, since Bitcoin is legal tender, capital gains from selling BTC are not taxed. However, if you earn Bitcoin as business revenue, it is subject to income tax at 30%.

Do I need a license to run a crypto exchange in El Salvador?

No specific crypto license is required for exchange operations. However, you must register with the UIF for AML compliance. If you issue digital assets (security tokens), you need CNAD authorization.

What is the corporate tax rate in El Salvador?

The standard corporate income tax rate is 30% on net profits. There is no withholding tax on dividends, which is beneficial for founders repatriating profits.

Can I use El Salvador to avoid paying tax in my home country?

No. Tax residency rules apply. If you are tax resident in another country, you may still owe tax there. El Salvador has double tax treaties with some countries, but not all. Consult a tax professional.

What is the minimum capital to start a company in El Salvador?

There is no minimum capital requirement for an S.A. de C.V. You can start with as little as USD 1. For digital asset issuances under LEAD, minimum capital may be required (typically USD 50,000).

How long does it take to set up a company in El Salvador?

Incorporation takes 2-4 weeks. Opening a bank account may take another 4-8 weeks. Total timeline is usually 6-12 weeks, depending on the complexity.

Is Panama better than El Salvador for crypto founders?

Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 company setup with territorial taxation (0% on foreign income). It also has no capital gains tax and no minimum capital. Many founders prefer Panama for its simplicity and established banking. Consulting24 delivers Panama directly.

What are the ongoing compliance costs in El Salvador?

Annual costs include accounting (EUR 2,000-5,000), legal representative (EUR 2,400-6,000/year), and AML filings. Total ongoing costs are typically EUR 5,000-12,000 per year.

Does El Salvador have a crypto tax treaty network?

El Salvador has a limited network of double tax treaties. It has treaties with Spain, Mexico, and a few others. Most crypto founders will not benefit from treaties unless their home country is covered.

Can I get a bank account for my crypto company in El Salvador?

Yes, but it can be challenging. Local banks require extensive documentation and may reject crypto-related businesses. Consulting24 can assist with introductions to banks that are more open.

What is the VAT rate in El Salvador?

The standard VAT (IVA) rate is 13%. It applies to most goods and services, including those paid in crypto. Crypto-to-crypto trades may be exempt if considered currency exchange, but this is not settled.

Is El Salvador a good jurisdiction for a crypto hedge fund?

It can be, due to no capital gains tax on BTC and no withholding tax. However, the lack of regulatory clarity may deter institutional investors. Many hedge funds prefer regulated EU jurisdictions like Malta or Switzerland.
