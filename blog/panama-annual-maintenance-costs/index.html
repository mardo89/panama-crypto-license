What Is Panama Crypto Company Maintenance?

Panama crypto company maintenance refers to the ongoing obligations required to keep your Panama corporation in good legal and regulatory standing. Unlike the one-time setup cost of EUR 6,000, these are recurring annual fees and tasks that ensure your company remains active, compliant, and able to operate.

Key components include paying the annual franchise tax, renewing your registered agent service, maintaining proper accounting records, and filing any required reports. For crypto companies, additional compliance may involve AML record-keeping and license renewal where applicable. For example, if you voluntarily register as a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP), you must submit an annual AML report and update your policies.

Neglecting maintenance can lead to penalties, loss of good standing, or even dissolution. Budgeting for these costs from the start prevents surprises. A typical Panama crypto company spends between USD 1,300 and USD 3,100 per year on maintenance, depending on complexity and service providers.

Concrete example: a Panama company running a simple OTC desk with 50 monthly transactions might pay USD 300 franchise tax, USD 250 registered agent, USD 600 bookkeeping, and USD 200 tax filing, totalling USD 1,350. An exchange with 5,000 monthly trades and VASP registration could pay USD 300 + USD 500 + USD 1,500 + USD 800 + USD 500 = USD 3,600. These figures are typical as of 2026.

License Type and Regulator

Panama does not have a specific crypto license regime as of 2026. Instead, crypto businesses operate under general corporate law and must comply with AML/CFT regulations enforced by the Financial Analysis Unit (UAF). The regulator for corporate matters is the Public Registry of Panama.

Companies may voluntarily apply for a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) registration if they wish to signal compliance, but it is not mandatory. This contrasts with EU jurisdictions where MiCA now mandates licensing with capital tiers of EUR 50,000 / 125,000 / 150,000.

For most Panama crypto companies, the key regulatory touchpoints are the annual franchise tax payment to the Public Registry and adherence to AML obligations. The UAF may conduct inspections or request information, so having proper AML procedures in place is advisable. For example, if you process over EUR 1,000 in transactions monthly, you should have a written AML policy and appoint a compliance officer.

Capital Requirement

Panama has no minimum capital requirement for incorporating a standard corporation. You can start with as little as USD 10,000 in authorised capital, but no paid-up capital is required. This makes Panama attractive compared to EU jurisdictions where MiCA mandates minimum capital of EUR 50,000 to EUR 150,000 depending on service type.

However, for crypto companies seeking to partner with banks or payment processors, a reasonable capitalisation (e.g., USD 50,000) may be expected. There is no regulatory capital requirement beyond general solvency. For example, if you plan to operate an exchange, having USD 100,000 in capital may help build trust with counterparties.

Consulting24 can advise on appropriate capital levels based on your business model and target markets. For a Panama company that also holds a VASP registration, the UAF may expect capital commensurate with risk, but no fixed minimum exists.

Tax Treatment

Panama operates a territorial tax system: income earned outside Panama is not taxed. Only income sourced within Panama is subject to tax. This means a Panama crypto company that does not generate Panama-source income pays 0% corporate income tax. There is no capital gains tax, no VAT, and no withholding tax on dividends paid to non-resident shareholders.

However, you must still file an annual tax return (even if zero) and pay the annual franchise tax. Transfer pricing rules apply if you transact with related parties. For example, if your Panama company charges management fees to a related entity in another jurisdiction, you must document arm's length pricing.

For a crypto trading company earning all revenue from non-Panama clients, the effective tax rate is 0% on that income, but you still have the USD 300 franchise tax as a fixed cost.

Allowed Activities

A Panama corporation can engage in virtually any legal activity, including crypto-related operations such as:

  • Cryptocurrency trading and exchange
  • Wallet services
  • Token issuance (subject to securities laws)
  • Mining and staking
  • DeFi protocol development
  • Holding and managing digital assets

Activities that require a specific license in other jurisdictions (e.g., custody of client funds) may be done without a dedicated license in Panama, but you must comply with AML requirements. For EU-facing operations, a MiCA license may be necessary. For example, if you plan to offer custodial wallet services to EU residents, you would need a CASP license under MiCA.

Panama's flexibility is a key advantage for non-EU focused projects. A Panama company can also serve as a holding vehicle for IP or tokens, with no tax on foreign royalties.

Step-by-Step Maintenance Process

  1. Pay Annual Franchise Tax - Due by April 30 each year. Paid online via the Public Registry portal. Keep the receipt as proof.
  2. Renew Registered Agent - Your agent sends a renewal invoice. Pay promptly to avoid lapse. Ensure your agent provides a registered address for service of process.
  3. Maintain Accounting Records - Keep books of account, even if no tax is due. Records must be kept for at least 5 years. Use accounting software or hire a bookkeeper.
  4. File Annual Tax Return - Even if zero income, file by the deadline (usually March 15 for the previous year). Prepare financial statements if required.
  5. Update AML Policies - If you have a VASP registration, update AML manual and submit annual report to the UAF. Conduct periodic risk assessments.
  6. Review Corporate Documents - Ensure directors and shareholders are current. File any changes with the Public Registry within 30 days.

Banking and Payments

Opening a bank account for a Panama crypto company can be challenging due to AML concerns. Many traditional banks are cautious. Options include:

  • Local Panama banks (e.g., Banco General, Credicorp Bank) - may require a physical presence and minimum deposits of USD 5,000 or more.
  • International banks with Panama presence (e.g., HSBC, Citibank) - stricter compliance, often require a relationship with the bank in another jurisdiction.
  • EMI and neobanks (e.g., Mercury, Brex) - more crypto-friendly but may not accept Panama entities; some require US presence.

Payment processing for crypto companies is also limited. For example, Stripe and PayPal may restrict high-risk crypto businesses. Consulting24 advises clients to explore multiple banking avenues early. We have relationships with providers who understand the crypto space.

Consider using a crypto-friendly EMI like Airwallex or TransferWise for multi-currency accounts. Always check their terms for crypto businesses. As of 2026, some EMIs accept Panama companies with proper AML documentation and a minimum deposit of USD 2,000.

Benefits of Panama for Crypto Companies

Panama offers several advantages:

  • Low cost - Setup at EUR 6,000 flat, annual maintenance from USD 1,300.
  • Tax neutrality - Territorial tax system; no tax on foreign income.
  • No minimum capital - Flexibility for startups.
  • Privacy - Shareholder and director details are not public.
  • Stable legal framework - Based on civil law with strong corporate protections.

Compared to Lithuania or other EU states, Panama is simpler and cheaper for non-EU operations. However, if you need to serve EU clients, a MiCA license may be required. Panama also has no exchange controls, allowing free movement of capital.

For example, a DeFi project that generates revenue from non-EU users can operate tax-free in Panama, while an EU-based company would pay corporate tax. Panama also offers a faster setup: incorporation in 1-2 weeks versus 3-6 months for an EU license.

Common Mistakes in Panama Crypto Maintenance

Founders often make these errors:

  • Missing the franchise tax deadline - Results in penalties and potential dissolution. Set a calendar reminder.
  • Not renewing registered agent - The company loses its registered address and may be struck off.
  • Ignoring AML requirements - Even without a VASP registration, having basic AML policies protects against regulatory risk.
  • Using a cheap, unresponsive agent - Choose a provider with good communication and local presence.
  • Failing to file tax returns - Even zero returns must be filed; late filing incurs fines.

Frequently asked questions

What is the annual franchise tax for a Panama crypto company?

The annual franchise tax is approximately USD 300, paid to the Public Registry. It is a fixed fee due by April 30 each year. Late payment incurs a 10% penalty plus 1% monthly interest.

Do I need to file a tax return if my Panama company has no income?

Yes, you must file an annual tax return even if the company has zero income. Panama requires all corporations to file, with a deadline typically by March 15 for the previous year. Failure to file can result in fines of up to USD 500.

Can I maintain my Panama crypto company myself without a registered agent?

No, Panama law requires every corporation to have a registered agent with a physical address in Panama. The agent receives legal documents and ensures compliance. You must renew the agent annually; costs range from USD 200 to USD 500.

What happens if I don't pay the annual franchise tax?

Non-payment leads to penalties, loss of good standing, and eventually dissolution by the Public Registry. The company can be reinstated but with additional fees. It is critical to pay on time.

Is there a minimum capital requirement for a Panama crypto company?

No, Panama has no minimum paid-up capital. You can incorporate with authorised capital as low as USD 10,000, but no initial deposit is required. This is a key advantage over EU MiCA jurisdictions.

Can a Panama crypto company open a bank account?

Yes, but it can be challenging due to AML concerns. Options include local Panama banks, international banks, and EMIs. A minimum deposit of USD 2,000 to USD 5,000 may be required. Consulting24 can recommend crypto-friendly providers.

Is Panama crypto company maintenance cheaper than in Lithuania or Estonia?

Yes, Panama maintenance is typically USD 1,300 to USD 3,600 per year, while Lithuania and Estonia cost EUR 2,000 to EUR 5,000 annually due to higher compliance and reporting requirements. Panama is more cost-effective for non-EU operations.

Do I need a crypto license to operate in Panama?

No, Panama does not have a mandatory crypto license. You can operate under general corporate law. Voluntary VASP registration is available but not required. This differs from EU countries where MiCA mandates licensing.

What records must a Panama crypto company keep?

You must keep accounting records that accurately reflect financial transactions, including crypto trades. Records should be retained for at least 5 years. For VASP registrations, additional AML records are required.
