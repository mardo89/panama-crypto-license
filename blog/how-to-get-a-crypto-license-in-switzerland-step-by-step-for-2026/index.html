What Is a Swiss Crypto License?

A Swiss crypto license, officially a FINMA license for financial intermediaries, is required for businesses that provide crypto-related financial services such as exchange, custody, or trading. Switzerland applies a risk-based approach: if your business model involves accepting client assets or operating a trading platform, you likely need a license. The key regulation is the Anti-Money Laundering Act (AMLA) and, for larger operations, the Financial Institutions Act (FinIA). In 2026, Switzerland is not an EU member, so MiCA does not apply. Instead, FINMA oversees licensing under Swiss law.

There are two main categories: a simple AML authorization for smaller firms (e.g., exchange services without custody) and a full FinIA license for banks or securities dealers. Most crypto startups fall under the AML authorization, which is less capital-intensive but still requires strong compliance. The AML authorization is obtained by joining a self-regulatory organization (SRO) such as VQF or PolyReg. These SROs are approved by FINMA and handle day-to-day supervision. The full FinIA license is reserved for firms that want to offer a broader range of services, including securities trading or lending.

Switzerland's approach is stable and predictable, but the process is not quick. Businesses must be prepared for a thorough review of their operations, including background checks on all beneficial owners and key personnel. Who Needs a Swiss Crypto License?

Any business that professionally trades, exchanges, or custodies cryptocurrencies for third parties needs a license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralized and decentralized if they hold client funds)
  • Wallet providers that manage private keys for clients
  • Brokers and OTC desks
  • Asset managers dealing in crypto assets
  • Issuers of stablecoins or tokens that are considered securities

If you only develop blockchain software or mine crypto, you do not need a license. But if you touch client money or facilitate transactions, you must apply. Panama has no capital requirements and a faster setup, making it ideal for startups that want to test the market before committing to a full Swiss license.

In Switzerland, even peer-to-peer platforms that match buyers and sellers but never hold funds may need an AML authorization if they facilitate the transfer of crypto assets. The threshold is low: any activity that could be used for money laundering triggers the obligation. If you are unsure, a compliance audit is recommended before applying.

The sole regulator is FINMA (Swiss Financial Market Supervisory Authority). The license type depends on your activities:

Most crypto startups obtain an AML authorization by joining a self-regulatory organization (SRO) like VQF or PolyReg. This is faster and cheaper but still requires a solid AML policy. For larger operations, a full FinIA license is mandatory. The choice depends on the scale of your business and the services you plan to offer. A full FinIA license allows you to operate as a securities firm, which includes the ability to trade securities and offer custody. This is necessary if you plan to list security tokens or provide margin trading.

FINMA also distinguishes between 'payment tokens' (like Bitcoin), 'utility tokens', and 'asset tokens' (securities). Each classification affects the licensing requirements. Capital Requirement

Switzerland does not have a fixed minimum capital for AML authorization. However, the company must have sufficient financial resources to cover operational and compliance costs. For a full FinIA license (securities firm), the minimum capital is CHF 1.5 million (around EUR 1.5 million). For a bank license, it is CHF 10 million. Most crypto startups opt for the AML route, which has no statutory minimum but requires adequate equity. As a rule of thumb, plan for at least CHF 50,000 to CHF 100,000 in capital to demonstrate viability. Compare this with other jurisdictions: Panama has no minimum capital, while EU MiCA licenses require EUR 50,000 to 150,000 depending on services.

In practice, FINMA expects the company to have enough capital to operate for at least 6 months without revenue. This includes salaries, rent, compliance costs, and legal fees. If you are applying for a full FinIA license, you must provide proof of capital, such as a bank statement or a letter from a Swiss bank. The capital must be unencumbered and available at all times. This is lower than the EU MiCA minimum of EUR 50,000 for basic exchange services. However, the total cost of setting up in Switzerland is still higher due to legal and compliance fees.

Tax Treatment

Switzerland offers a favorable tax environment for crypto businesses. Corporate income tax rates range from 11.9% to 21% depending on canton. Zug, the Crypto Valley, has one of the lowest rates at around 11.9%. Crypto assets are treated as assets for wealth tax purposes, but capital gains for private investors are tax-free. For businesses, profits from crypto trading are subject to corporate tax. VAT is not applied on crypto transactions (as of 2026), but advisory services may be VAT-exempt. Switzerland has no withholding tax on dividends, and double taxation treaties are in place. This makes it attractive compared to high-tax EU countries. However, the compliance burden is higher than in simpler jurisdictions like Panama, which has no corporate tax on offshore income.

One key advantage is that Switzerland does not impose a stamp duty on the issuance of tokens, unlike some EU countries. Additionally, the Swiss tax authorities have issued clear guidance on the treatment of staking rewards and airdrops, reducing uncertainty. For example, staking rewards are taxed as income at the time of receipt, while airdrops are treated as miscellaneous income. Allowed Activities Under the License

A Swiss crypto license (AML authorization) allows you to:

  • Exchange cryptocurrencies for fiat and vice versa
  • Provide custody of crypto assets for clients
  • Operate a trading platform (order matching)
  • Transfer crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Issue and redeem stablecoins (subject to additional rules)

If you plan to offer used trading or derivatives, a full FinIA license is required. The license does not cover securities brokerage unless specifically granted. Activities must be limited to those disclosed in your application. Any expansion requires prior approval. For example, if you initially apply for an exchange license and later want to offer custody, you must amend your license. This can take additional time and cost.

Switzerland also allows 'crypto banking' under a full banking license, but this is reserved for large institutions. Most startups find the AML authorization sufficient for their needs. However, if you plan to serve institutional clients, a full FinIA license may be necessary to comply with their due diligence requirements. Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Business structure: Incorporate a Swiss company (AG or GmbH) with a registered office in Switzerland. You can use our company setup service.
  2. Compliance framework: Draft AML/KYC policies, risk assessment, and internal controls. Engage a compliance consultant.
  3. Choose an SRO: Apply to a recognized self-regulatory organization (e.g., VQF, PolyReg).
  4. Submit application: File with FINMA (or via SRO for AML authorization). Include business plan, organizational chart, and financial projections.
  5. Background checks: All beneficial owners and key management must pass fit-and-proper tests.
  6. Audit and approval: FINMA reviews the application; an external audit may be required. Approval takes 3-6 months.
  7. Ongoing compliance: Submit regular reports to SRO and FINMA, conduct annual audits.

Compliance and Trust

Compliance is the backbone of a Swiss crypto license. You must implement strong AML/KYC procedures, screen clients against sanctions lists, and report suspicious transactions. FINMA conducts periodic inspections. Failure to comply can result in fines or license revocation. It is critical to hire a qualified compliance officer and conduct regular training. In Switzerland, the compliance officer must be resident in Switzerland and have relevant experience. The SRO will review the officer's qualifications during the application process. Additionally, you must implement a transaction monitoring system that can detect unusual patterns. For small startups, this can be outsourced to a compliance-as-a-service provider. Trust is a major asset in the crypto industry. A Swiss license signals to clients that you operate under a strict regulatory framework. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Underestimating costs: Budget for legal, compliance, and SRO fees. Many startups run out of funds midway.
  • Incomplete documentation: FINMA requires a detailed business plan and risk assessment. Generic templates are rejected.
  • Ignoring fit-and-proper requirements: All directors and shareholders must have clean backgrounds. Disclose everything.
  • Choosing the wrong SRO: Some SROs are more crypto-friendly. Research before joining.
  • Operating before license: You must not provide services until the license is granted. This can take months.

Avoid these pitfalls by working with experienced advisors. Consulting24 has guided many clients through the process. Another common mistake is failing to plan for ongoing compliance. After the license is granted, you must submit regular reports and undergo annual audits. If you are not prepared for this, you may face penalties. It is also important to keep your business plan updated; any material change in operations must be reported to FINMA.

Finally, do not underestimate the language barrier. While many Swiss regulators speak English, official documents may need to be in German, French, or Italian depending on the canton. Hiring a local lawyer who is fluent in the relevant language is essential.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between an AML authorization and a FinIA license in Switzerland?

An AML authorization is a lighter regime for businesses that provide exchange or transfer services without holding client assets. It is obtained by joining an SRO and costs less. A FinIA license is for full-service firms that want to offer custody, securities trading, or banking services. It requires higher capital (CHF 1.5M for securities firms) and more extensive compliance.

How long does it take to get a Swiss crypto license in 2026?

For an AML authorization, expect 3 to 6 months. For a full FinIA license, 6 to 12 months. The timeline depends on the complexity of your application and the completeness of your documentation. Working with experienced advisors can speed up the process.

Can I apply for a Swiss crypto license without a local office?

No, you must have a registered office in Switzerland. This can be a virtual office provided by a service provider, but it must be a physical address where official documents can be served. You also need a Swiss resident director or compliance officer.

What are the ongoing compliance costs for a Swiss crypto license?

Annual costs include SRO membership (CHF 1,000-5,000), audit fees (CHF 5,000-20,000), compliance officer salary (CHF 50,000-100,000 if full-time), and legal fees. Total ongoing costs can range from CHF 15,000 to over CHF 100,000 per year.

Is Switzerland part of the EU's MiCA regulation?

No, Switzerland is not an EU member. MiCA does not apply. Swiss crypto licensing is governed by FINMA under national law. This means different capital requirements and rules. Some businesses prefer Switzerland to avoid MiCA's higher capital tiers.

Can I operate a crypto exchange in Switzerland with just an AML authorization?

Yes, if you do not hold client funds or provide custody. For example, a peer-to-peer platform that matches buyers and sellers but does not take possession of crypto can operate under an AML authorization. If you hold private keys or offer margin trading, you need a full FinIA license.

What are the tax implications for a Swiss crypto license holder?

Corporate income tax is 11.9% to 21% depending on the canton. Crypto transactions are VAT-exempt. Capital gains for private investors are tax-free. Businesses pay tax on trading profits. Wealth tax applies to crypto assets held by the company. Switzerland has no withholding tax on dividends.

How does the Swiss crypto license compare to Panama?

Panama is much cheaper (EUR 6,000 flat), faster (4-6 weeks), and has no capital or tax requirements. Switzerland offers higher reputation and access to the European market but costs EUR 20,000-80,000 and takes 3-12 months. Choose based on your budget and target clients.

Do I need a Swiss bank account for the license?

Yes, for a full FinIA license you need a Swiss bank account. For AML authorization, some SROs may accept foreign accounts, but a Swiss account is recommended. Crypto-friendly banks like SEBA and Sygnum are available but require a license application.

What happens if I operate without a license in Switzerland?

Operating without a license is illegal and can result in criminal charges, fines, and imprisonment. FINMA actively monitors the market and can shut down unlicensed businesses. It is essential to obtain the license before offering any services.
