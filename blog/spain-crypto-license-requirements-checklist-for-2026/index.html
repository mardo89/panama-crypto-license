What Is the Spain Crypto License?

The Spain crypto license is the national authorisation required to provide crypto-asset services under MiCA. It replaces the previous fragmented registration system. The license is issued by the CNMV and allows firms to operate across the EU via passporting. Key services covered include custody, exchange, trading platform operation, and advisory services. The regime applies to both fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto transactions, as well as stablecoin issuance under certain conditions.

Spain's implementation aligns with MiCA's three-tier capital structure: EUR 50,000 for basic services (e.g., advisory), EUR 125,000 for exchange and custody, and EUR 150,000 for trading platforms. Additional requirements include AML/CFT policies, a registered office in Spain, and fit-and-proper management. The license is valid for the entire EU, making Spain an attractive hub for crypto businesses targeting the European market.

For example, a startup offering only custodial wallet services would need EUR 50,000 capital, while an exchange handling both fiat and crypto orders needs EUR 125,000. A full trading platform with order matching requires EUR 150,000. These tiers are non-negotiable under MiCA, and Spain does not add extra buffers. Who Needs a Spain Crypto License?

Any legal entity established in Spain that offers crypto-asset services to EU residents must obtain a CASP license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Trading platforms (order-book or AMM-based)
  • Brokers and dealers in crypto-assets
  • Advisory services on crypto investments
  • Issuers of asset-referenced tokens or e-money tokens

Foreign firms serving Spanish clients without a local branch may also need to register. However, under MiCA's passporting, a license in one EU member state covers the entire bloc. If you already hold a license in Lithuania or Estonia, you may not need a separate Spain license. License Type and Regulator

The license is formally called the Crypto-Asset Service Provider (CASP) authorisation. The regulator is the Comisión Nacional del Mercado de Valores (CNMV), which supervises compliance with MiCA and AML obligations. The Bank of Spain also plays a role in anti-money laundering oversight. The CNMV maintains a public register of authorised CASPs.

Spain's regime is fully MiCA-compliant, meaning no additional national requirements beyond the EU framework. However, the CNMV has published guidelines on advertising and marketing of crypto assets, which must be followed. The licensing process is expected to take 3-6 months for standard applications, though complex cases may extend to 9 months. The CNMV may conduct interviews with key management and request clarifications on business models, especially for innovative services like DeFi integration.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement depends on the services offered:

  • EUR 50,000 for custody and advisory services only
  • EUR 125,000 for exchange services (including fiat-to-crypto)
  • EUR 150,000 for operating a trading platform

These amounts must be held as paid-up share capital or a combination of capital and insurance. The capital must be maintained throughout the license period. For firms offering multiple services, the highest tier applies. Spain does not impose additional capital buffers beyond MiCA. Tax Treatment for Crypto Businesses in Spain

Spain's corporate tax rate is 25% (standard). Crypto-asset gains are treated as taxable income. VAT is not applied to crypto-to-crypto exchanges, but fiat-to-crypto transactions may be subject to VAT in certain cases. A 19% withholding tax applies to dividends distributed to non-residents. Crypto mining income is taxable as business income. Spain has no specific crypto tax incentives, but the general regime is predictable.

For individual investors, capital gains from crypto sales are taxed at progressive rates (19-26%). The CNMV requires all CASPs to report transactions to the tax authorities. Spain has a mandatory declaration of crypto holdings on the Modelo 720 form for residents holding over EUR 50,000 abroad. Consulting24 recommends consulting a Spanish tax advisor for your specific structure.

Compared to Panama, which has 0% tax on offshore income, Spain's tax burden is higher. However, the EU passporting benefit may outweigh the tax cost for businesses targeting multiple EU markets.

Allowed Activities Under the Spain Crypto License

The CASP license permits the following activities:

  • Provision of custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • Operation of a trading platform for crypto-assets
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for other crypto-assets
  • Execution of orders for crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • Placement of crypto-assets
  • Receipt and transmission of orders for crypto-assets
  • Advice on crypto-assets

Stablecoin issuance (asset-referenced tokens or e-money tokens) requires additional authorisation under MiCA. The license does not cover derivatives or securities tokens, which fall under MiFID II. If your business involves DeFi or NFTs, specific guidance from the CNMV is recommended. For example, a platform offering non-custodial DeFi services may not require a CASP license if it does not hold client assets, but the CNMV has indicated that certain DeFi activities may still fall under MiCA.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The Spain CASP application follows these steps:

  1. Company incorporation: Register a Spanish limited liability company (SL) with a registered office in Spain. This typically takes 2-4 weeks.
  2. Prepare documentation: Draft AML/CFT policies, business plan, risk assessment, and governance framework. These must be tailored to your specific services.
  3. Appoint management: Ensure directors and beneficial owners meet fit-and-proper criteria (no criminal record, relevant experience). Provide CVs and police certificates.
  4. Capital deposit: Deposit the required minimum capital in a Spanish bank account and obtain a certificate from the bank.
  5. Submit application: File with the CNMV via their online portal, including all supporting documents and the application fee. The portal requires digital signatures.
  6. Review and interview: The CNMV may request additional information or conduct interviews with management. This phase can take 1-3 months.
  7. Decision: The CNMV issues a decision within 3-6 months. If approved, the firm is added to the public register and can begin operations.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, CASPs must comply with ongoing requirements:

  • Annual AML/CFT audits and reporting to the CNMV
  • Transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting (SARs)
  • Maintenance of minimum capital at all times
  • Regular financial reporting (quarterly/annual)
  • Client asset segregation and custody rules
  • Marketing and advertising pre-approval for certain campaigns

Non-compliance can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. Consulting24 provides ongoing compliance support to ensure your firm meets CNMV expectations. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Insufficient capital: Failing to maintain the required capital can delay approval. Ensure the capital is fully paid up and documented.
  • Poor AML policies: The CNMV scrutinises AML frameworks; generic templates are rejected. Policies must be specific to your business model and risk profile.
  • Incomplete documentation: Missing or inconsistent information leads to requests for additional data, extending the timeline. Use a checklist.
  • Unfit management: Directors with criminal records or lack of crypto experience may be deemed unfit. Provide detailed CVs and references.
  • Ignoring marketing rules: Advertising crypto services without CNMV pre-approval can result in fines. Submit all marketing materials in advance.
