What Is the El Salvador Crypto License?

El Salvador's crypto license is an authorization granted by the Central Reserve Bank of El Salvador (BCR) and the Superintendencia del Sistema Financiero (SSF) under the Digital Assets Issuance Law (Ley de Emisión de Activos Digitales). This law, enacted in 2023, regulates all activities involving digital assets, including exchanges, custody, and issuance.

The license allows companies to legally offer crypto services within the country and to international clients. It is part of El Salvador's broader strategy to become a hub for digital innovation. Unlike many other jurisdictions, El Salvador offers a clear legal framework that recognizes Bitcoin and other digital assets as legal tender or regulated commodities.

Key features include a progressive tax regime, no capital gains tax on Bitcoin, and a streamlined application process through the National Digital Assets Commission (CNAD). For founders, this means a competitive environment with lower operational costs compared to European or North American alternatives.

Who Needs a Crypto License in El Salvador?

Any entity that provides digital asset services to residents or businesses in El Salvador must obtain a license. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (centralized and decentralized)
  • Wallet providers (custodial and non-custodial)
  • Payment processors using digital assets
  • Token issuers (including security token offerings)
  • Custodians and asset managers dealing in digital assets
  • Brokers and dealers in digital currencies

If your business involves any of these activities, you need to apply for a license. Operating without one can result in fines, suspension, or criminal penalties. The law applies equally to foreign companies if they target Salvadoran customers.

For founders who want to test the market without full licensing, El Salvador offers a sandbox regime for fintech startups. However, for full commercial operations, a license is mandatory.

License Type and Regulator

The primary regulator for digital assets in El Salvador is the National Digital Assets Commission (Comisión Nacional de Activos Digitales, CNAD), established under the Digital Assets Issuance Law. The CNAD works alongside the Central Reserve Bank (BCR) and the Superintendencia del Sistema Financiero (SSF) to oversee compliance.

There is one main license type for digital asset service providers (DASPs), which covers all regulated activities. However, the license may include specific authorizations depending on the services offered:

  • Class A: Full license for exchanges, custody, and payment services
  • Class B: Limited license for specific activities like token issuance or advisory

The CNAD evaluates applications based on the company's business plan, compliance framework, and financial stability. The license is valid indefinitely but subject to annual renewal fees and ongoing reporting.

Capital Requirement

El Salvador does not impose a statutory minimum capital requirement for digital asset service providers. However, the CNAD expects applicants to demonstrate sufficient financial resources to operate safely and cover potential liabilities. In practice, a minimum of $50,000 in paid-up capital is recommended to show seriousness.

This is significantly lower than the EU's MiCA capital tiers (EUR 50,000 to 150,000 depending on services) and makes El Salvador attractive for startups. The capital can be in fiat or digital assets, but must be held in a Salvadoran bank account or approved custodian.

Founders should also budget for operational expenses, including office space (physical presence is required) and local staff. Consulting24 advises clients on structuring their capital to meet regulatory expectations.

Tax Treatment in El Salvador

El Salvador offers one of the most favorable tax regimes for crypto businesses. Key points include:

  • No capital gains tax on Bitcoin or other digital assets (as per the Bitcoin Law)
  • Corporate income tax at a standard rate of 30% (but with exemptions for digital asset activities)
  • VAT of 13% on services, but digital asset transactions may be exempt
  • No withholding tax on dividends paid to non-residents
  • No wealth tax on digital assets

Additionally, the Digital Assets Issuance Law provides tax incentives for token issuers, including a 10-year exemption on income tax for certain approved projects. This makes El Salvador a tax-efficient jurisdiction for crypto operations.

Allowed Activities Under the License

The El Salvador crypto license permits a wide range of activities, including:

  • Exchange of digital assets for fiat currency or other digital assets
  • Operation of digital asset trading platforms (order books, AMMs)
  • Custody and safekeeping of digital assets
  • Issuance of digital assets (including security tokens and stablecoins)
  • Payment services using digital assets
  • Brokerage and dealing in digital assets
  • Advisory and portfolio management for digital assets

Activities that are not allowed without additional authorization include banking services (lending, deposits) and insurance. The license does not cover derivatives or margin trading unless explicitly approved.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining a crypto license in El Salvador involves several stages. Here is a typical step-by-step process:

  1. Pre-incorporation: Register a Salvadoran company with the National Registry (CNR). This requires a local director and registered address.
  2. Document preparation: Prepare a business plan, AML/KYC policies, risk management framework, and financial projections. All documents must be in Spanish.
  3. Submit application: File the application with the CNAD, including all supporting documents and the application fee.
  4. Review and due diligence: The CNAD conducts background checks on directors and shareholders. This may take 2-3 months.
  5. Interview: Founders may be required to present their business model to the CNAD.
  6. Approval and payment: Once approved, pay the issuance fee and receive the license certificate.
  7. Ongoing compliance: Submit quarterly reports on transactions, AML compliance, and financial statements.

Banking and Payment Integration

One of the challenges in El Salvador is banking. While the country is crypto-friendly, local banks are cautious about serving crypto businesses. However, the government has encouraged state-owned banks to open accounts for licensed entities.

Options include:

  • Banco de Desarrollo de El Salvador (BANDESAL) - state development bank
  • Banco Hipotecario - offers accounts for crypto companies
  • Private banks - some accept crypto clients with proper licensing

For international payments, many firms use fintech partners or stablecoin rails. El Salvador's Chivo wallet infrastructure can also be used for Bitcoin payments.

Compliance and Trust: Ongoing Obligations

Maintaining a crypto license in El Salvador requires ongoing compliance. Key obligations include:

  • AML/CFT programs: Implement and update policies to prevent money laundering and terrorist financing
  • Transaction monitoring: Report suspicious transactions to the Financial Intelligence Unit (UIF)
  • Audits: Annual financial audit by a licensed auditor
  • Reporting: Submit quarterly reports to the CNAD on volumes, customer base, and compliance
  • Data protection: Comply with El Salvador's data privacy laws

Common Mistakes When Applying for an El Salvador License

Founders often make these errors during the application process:

Frequently asked questions

What is the crypto license in El Salvador called?

It is called the Digital Asset Service Provider (DASP) license, issued under the Digital Assets Issuance Law. It is regulated by the National Digital Assets Commission (CNAD).

How much does an El Salvador crypto license cost?

Is there a minimum capital requirement for a crypto license in El Salvador?

No statutory minimum capital is set by law. However, the CNAD expects applicants to demonstrate sufficient funds. A recommended minimum of $50,000 in paid-up capital is advisable.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in El Salvador?

The process typically takes 3 to 6 months from application to approval. Timelines depend on document completeness and CNAD workload. Consulting24 can help expedite the process.

What activities are allowed under the El Salvador crypto license?

Do I need a physical office in El Salvador to get a license?

Yes, the CNAD requires a physical presence, including a registered office and a local director. Virtual offices are not accepted.

What are the tax benefits of a crypto license in El Salvador?

El Salvador offers no capital gains tax on Bitcoin, potential income tax exemptions for token issuers, and no withholding tax on dividends. Corporate income tax is 30% but may be reduced for digital asset activities.

What ongoing compliance is required after obtaining the license?
