What Is the Seychelles Crypto License?

The Seychelles crypto license, officially a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) licence, is issued by the FSA. It allows companies to legally offer services such as crypto exchange, custody, and transfer within and from Seychelles. The licence is part of a broader effort to attract fintech while maintaining AML/CFT compliance. Unlike some jurisdictions, Seychelles does not require a physical office presence; a registered agent and local director suffice. This makes it popular among remote-first teams.

The VASPA framework categorises licence types by service class, each with its own capital requirement. The FSA also issues a Class 1 (dealing in virtual assets for own account) and Class 2 (providing services to clients) licence. Most applicants apply for a Class 2 licence. The regime is not yet part of MiCA, so it offers more flexibility in capital and reporting compared to EU member states. As of 2026, the FSA has processed over 50 VASP applications, with a typical approval rate of 70% for well-prepared submissions. The licence is valid for one year and must be renewed annually, with a renewal fee of EUR 5,000.

Who Needs a Seychelles Crypto License?

Any entity that provides virtual asset services in or from Seychelles must hold a VASP licence. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (centralised and peer-to-peer)
  • Wallet providers (custodial and non-custodial with control over private keys)
  • OTC trading desks
  • Virtual asset transfer services
  • Issuers of stablecoins or other digital tokens
  • Brokers and dealers in virtual assets

If you are a crypto founder targeting international clients but want a reputable offshore base, Seychelles is a strong candidate. However, if your primary market is the EU, you may need a MiCA-compliant licence in an EU member state. For a comparison of Seychelles vs EU jurisdictions, see our vs Lithuania page. Additionally, founders who plan to offer services to EU residents should note that the EU's Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA) requires a CASP licence with capital tiers of EUR 50,000, 125,000, or 150,000 depending on service class, which is higher than Seychelles' requirements.

Licence Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Seychelles Financial Services Authority (FSA), which also oversees securities, insurance, and pensions. The VASP licence is divided into:

  • Class 1 Licence: For dealing in virtual assets for own account (e.g., proprietary trading). Minimum capital: EUR 50,000.
  • Class 2 Licence: For providing virtual asset services to clients (e.g., exchange, custody, transfer). Minimum capital: EUR 80,000.

Most applicants choose Class 2. The FSA requires a detailed business plan, AML/CFT policies, and fit-and-proper checks on directors and shareholders. The licence is valid for one year and must be renewed annually. Consulting24 advises on the appropriate class and prepares the application package. The FSA also conducts a compliance interview as part of the review process, which typically lasts 30-45 minutes and covers the applicant's understanding of AML obligations and business model.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirements are set by the FSA based on licence class:

  • Class 1 (own account): EUR 50,000
  • Class 2 (client services): EUR 80,000

Capital must be fully paid up before licence issuance and held in a Seychelles bank account or as a bank guarantee. The FSA may require higher capital if the business volume or risk profile is high, for example, if the projected transaction volume exceeds EUR 1 million per month. These figures are lower than EU MiCA thresholds (EUR 125,000 for exchanges), making Seychelles attractive for smaller firms. Consulting24 can help structure the capital proof, including guidance on acceptable forms of capital such as cash, bank guarantees, or liquid assets. It is important to note that the capital must be maintained throughout the licence period and cannot be withdrawn without FSA approval.

Tax Treatment

Seychelles offers a territorial tax system: income sourced outside Seychelles is generally exempt from tax. For VASPs, this means:

  • Corporate tax rate: 15% on Seychelles-source income only.
  • No capital gains tax, no withholding tax on dividends.
  • No VAT on virtual asset services (as of 2026).
  • International Business Company (IBC) status may offer further exemptions, but VASPs typically use a standard company.

Most crypto businesses structure their operations to minimise Seychelles-source income. For example, if the company's clients are all non-residents and transactions occur outside Seychelles, the income may be considered offshore and thus tax-exempt. However, tax advice should be obtained for your specific model, as the FSA may require proof of substance. For a jurisdiction with zero corporate tax, see Panama, which imposes no corporate income tax on offshore income.

Allowed Activities

Under a Class 2 VASP licence, you can perform the following activities:

  • Exchange between virtual assets and fiat currency
  • Exchange between one or more virtual assets
  • Transfer of virtual assets (on behalf of clients)
  • Custody and administration of virtual assets
  • Participation in and provision of financial services related to an issuer's offer or sale of virtual assets

Activities not covered include: operating a mining pool, developing software, or providing non-custodial wallet services (where you never hold private keys). The FSA may also restrict certain high-risk activities, such as those involving privacy coins or unregistered securities. It is advisable to define your service scope clearly in the application and to seek pre-approval for any novel activities. For a broader range of permitted activities, consider jurisdictions like Lithuania, which offers a more comprehensive list under its CASP regime.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a Seychelles crypto license involves several stages:

  1. Company Incorporation: Register a Seychelles International Business Company (IBC) or a standard company with a registered agent. This typically takes 1-2 weeks.
  2. Document Preparation: Draft business plan, AML/CFT policies, risk assessment, and compliance manual. Include personal details of directors and shareholders. The business plan must include financial projections for three years and a detailed description of the target market.
  3. Due Diligence: Submit fit-and-proper declarations and background checks for all key individuals. This includes criminal record checks from the country of residence and professional references.
  4. Application Submission: File the application with the FSA, including all documents and the application fee of EUR 2,500. The FSA will acknowledge receipt within 5 business days.
  5. FSA Review: The FSA reviews the application, may request clarifications, and conducts a compliance interview. The review period is typically 60-90 days.
  6. Licence Issuance: Upon approval, pay the annual licence fee of EUR 5,000 and provide proof of capital (e.g., bank statement showing the minimum capital).
  7. Ongoing Compliance: Submit quarterly reports, annual audited accounts, and maintain AML procedures. The FSA may conduct on-site inspections.

Banking and Payment Solutions

Opening a bank account in Seychelles for a VASP can be challenging due to global de-risking. Options include:

  • Seychelles-based banks (e.g., Nouvobanq, Seychelles Commercial Bank) - require physical presence and high minimum deposits, typically EUR 10,000-50,000.
  • International payment processors (e.g., EMI accounts in Lithuania or UK) - often more flexible, with lower minimum deposits and faster setup.
  • Crypto-friendly banks in other jurisdictions (e.g., in Malta or Switzerland) - may accept Seychelles-licensed VASPs but require additional due diligence.

Many founders opt for a multi-banking strategy: a Seychelles bank account for capital maintenance and an EMI for operational transactions. Benefits of the Seychelles Crypto License

Key advantages include:

  • Low capital requirements (EUR 50,000-80,000) compared to EU MiCA (EUR 125,000+).
  • No physical office requirement - only a registered agent.
  • Territorial tax - offshore income is tax-free.
  • Fast licensing (3-6 months) relative to other jurisdictions.
  • English-speaking legal system based on English common law.
  • No personal income tax for directors (if non-resident).
  • Flexibility in service offerings, including custody and exchange.

These benefits make Seychelles ideal for startups and mid-sized crypto businesses that want a regulated status without heavy overhead. For example, a small exchange with 10,000 users can operate profitably with the EUR 80,000 capital, whereas in Lithuania the same business would need EUR 125,000. However, for businesses targeting institutional clients, a more recognised jurisdiction like Switzerland may be preferable.

Compliance and Trust Considerations

While the Seychelles licence is legitimate, some counterparties may view it as a higher-risk jurisdiction due to past associations with offshore secrecy. To build trust:

  • Maintain strong AML/CFT procedures and conduct regular audits by a reputable firm.
  • Use a reputable registered agent and legal counsel with experience in crypto licensing.
  • Consider obtaining a secondary licence in a more recognised jurisdiction (e.g., Lithuania or Estonia) for credibility.
  • Publish your licence number and FSA registration on your website, along with your compliance policies.
  • Engage a third-party AML auditor to review your procedures annually.

Common Mistakes in the Application

Founders often make these errors:

  • Incomplete business plan: The FSA expects detailed projections, source of funds, and risk analysis. Generic plans are rejected.
  • Weak AML policies: Generic templates are rejected; policies must be tailored to your business and include specific procedures for transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting.
  • Ignoring fit-and-proper requirements: Directors with criminal records or adverse financial history will be rejected. Ensure all key individuals have clean backgrounds.
  • Underestimating timeline: Rushing leads to mistakes; plan for 6 months. Many applicants fail to account for the time needed to gather documents from multiple jurisdictions.
  • Not engaging local expertise: The FSA prefers dealing with local representatives. Without a local registered agent, the application may be delayed.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Seychelles crypto license?

The minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for a Class 1 licence (own account) and EUR 80,000 for a Class 2 licence (client services). Capital must be fully paid up before issuance and held in a Seychelles bank account or as a bank guarantee.

How long does it take to get a Seychelles crypto license?

The typical timeline is 3 to 6 months from submission to approval. The FSA aims to process complete applications within 90 days, but delays can occur if additional information is requested. Engaging a local registered agent can help expedite the process.

What is the tax rate for crypto businesses in Seychelles?

Seychelles has a territorial tax system. Corporate tax is 15% on Seychelles-source income only. Offshore income (from non-resident clients) is generally tax-exempt. There is no capital gains tax, no withholding tax on dividends, and no VAT on virtual asset services.

Do I need a physical office in Seychelles?

No, a physical office is not required. You only need a registered agent and a local director. This makes Seychelles attractive for remote-first teams. The registered agent provides a registered address and handles regulatory correspondence.

Can I use a Seychelles license to serve EU clients?

Yes, but you must comply with EU AML/CFT regulations if you actively market to EU residents. The Seychelles license does not provide MiCA equivalence, so you may need to register in each EU member state where you have clients. Consulting24 can advise on the best approach.

What activities are allowed under a Class 2 VASP license?

Class 2 allows exchange between virtual assets and fiat, exchange between virtual assets, transfer of virtual assets, custody and administration, and services related to token offerings. Activities like mining or non-custodial wallets are not covered.

How much does a Seychelles crypto license cost in total?

The total cost is typically EUR 9,500 to 19,000, including government fees (EUR 2,500 application + EUR 5,000 annual), registered agent (EUR 1,500-3,000), AML policy drafting (EUR 1,000-2,000), and legal support (EUR 3,000-5,000). Consulting24 can provide a detailed quote.

Is the Seychelles crypto license recognized internationally?

The license is issued by the FSA, which is a member of the International Organization of Securities Commissions (IOSCO). However, it may not be automatically recognized in all jurisdictions. Banks and partners may require additional due diligence. A secondary license in a major jurisdiction can enhance credibility.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

Licensees must submit quarterly reports on transactions and AML/CFT activities, annual audited financial statements, and maintain up-to-date AML policies. The FSA may conduct on-site inspections. Non-compliance can result in fines or license revocation.

Can I switch from a Class 1 to a Class 2 license later?

Yes, you can apply to upgrade your license. You will need to meet the higher capital requirement (EUR 80,000) and submit a new business plan. The FSA will review the application, which typically takes 2-3 months. Consulting24 can assist with the upgrade process.
