Overview: The Czech Republic Crypto License in 2026

The Czech Republic crypto license, officially a CASP license under MiCA, is issued by the Czech National Bank (Česká národní banka, CNB). Since MiCA came into full force in 2024, the CNB has been the sole regulator for crypto services. The license allows companies to offer services such as custody, exchange, and transfer of crypto-assets legally across the EU. The process involves a thorough review of the company's governance, anti-money laundering (AML) procedures, and financial standing.

As of 2026, the CNB aims to process complete applications within 3 to 6 months, but this can extend to 9 months or more if additional information is required. The timeline is influenced by the complexity of the business model and the quality of the application. Consulting24 provides expert coordination to minimize delays. For example, a simple exchange-only application with well-prepared AML policies can be approved in 3 months, while a full-scope custody and exchange service may take 8 months. The CNB typically requests one or two rounds of clarifications, so having a responsive compliance team is essential.

Compared to other EU jurisdictions, the Czech Republic offers a middle ground: faster than Malta (6-12 months) but slower than Lithuania (2-4 months). However, the Czech market provides access to a skilled workforce and a stable regulatory environment. For founders who prioritize speed and lower capital, Panama remains an attractive non-EU alternative with a flat fee of EUR 6,000 and no minimum capital.

Who Needs a Czech Republic Crypto License?

Any company that provides crypto-asset services to clients in the Czech Republic or elsewhere in the EU needs a CASP license. This includes exchanges, wallet providers, custodians, and brokers. If your business involves any of the following activities, you are likely required to obtain a license:

  • Operating a crypto exchange (fiat-to-crypto or crypto-to-crypto)
  • Providing custody or administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • Executing orders for crypto-assets
  • Placing crypto-assets
  • Transferring crypto-assets
  • Advising on crypto-assets

Even if your company is based outside the EU, if you target Czech or EU clients, you must comply with MiCA. For non-EU founders, the Czech Republic offers a straightforward path to EU market access. It is important to note that the license is required for any service that involves holding client funds or assets, including simple order execution. DeFi protocols that do not hold client assets may fall outside the scope, but most business models will require licensing. Consulting24 can assess your specific activities during a consultation.

License Type and Regulator

The license is a single CASP authorization under MiCA, covering all crypto-asset services. The regulator is the Czech National Bank (CNB), which oversees financial markets and implements EU regulations. The CNB has a reputation for being thorough but fair. There is no separate license for different service types; instead, the license scope is defined by the services listed in the application.

Key regulatory requirements include:

  • Registered office in the Czech Republic
  • Fit and proper management
  • Strong AML/CFT policies
  • Minimum capital requirements as per MiCA tiers
  • IT security and business continuity plans

The CNB also requires that at least one director is a Czech resident or an EU national with a local presence. The application must include a detailed business plan with financial projections for three years, a risk assessment, and a description of the internal control mechanisms. Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the Czech Republic applies the standard EU capital tiers. The minimum capital depends on the services offered:

  • EUR 50,000 for custody and administration, exchange, and transfer services (Tier 1)
  • EUR 125,000 for execution of orders, placing, and advisory services (Tier 2)
  • EUR 150,000 for full scope including all services (Tier 3)

This capital must be held in a bank account in the Czech Republic or another EU member state and cannot be used for operational expenses. It serves as a buffer for client protection. The capital must be maintained at all times, and the CNB may request proof of capital adequacy during periodic reviews. For example, if you offer only exchange services, you need EUR 50,000; if you add custody, you may need EUR 125,000. Consulting24 can help you structure the capital efficiently, for instance by using a combination of share capital and reserves. It is important to note that the capital cannot be borrowed or provided as a loan; it must be equity.

Tax Treatment

The Czech Republic has a favorable tax regime for crypto businesses. As of 2026, corporate income tax is a flat 19% on profits. Crypto-to-crypto transactions are generally exempt from VAT. However, capital gains from crypto trading are subject to corporate tax. Personal income tax for crypto gains is 15% for individuals, but businesses are taxed at the corporate rate.

It is essential to maintain proper accounting records. The CNB requires audited financial statements for license holders. Consulting24 advises on tax structuring to optimize your position. For example, holding crypto as a long-term investment may qualify for lower tax rates. The Czech Republic also has double tax treaties with many countries, which can reduce withholding taxes on dividends. However, tax laws are complex and subject to change. Step-by-Step Process

The application process involves several stages. Here is a typical timeline:

  1. Company setup (2-4 weeks): Incorporate a Czech company with a registered office. Consulting24 can assist via our company setup service. The company must have a physical office in the Czech Republic, not just a virtual address.
  2. Document preparation (4-8 weeks): Draft AML policies, business plan, risk management, IT security, and governance documents. This is the most time-consuming phase. The AML policy must include customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and reporting procedures.
  3. Application submission (1 day): Submit to CNB with fees. The application is submitted electronically through the CNB's portal.
  4. CNB review (3-6 months): The CNB may request additional information. Expect at least one round of questions. The review includes background checks on directors and beneficial owners.
  5. License issuance (1-2 weeks): Once approved, you receive the license. You must then register with the CNB's register of CASPs.

Common Mistakes

Founders often make these errors:

  • Underestimating the AML documentation: The CNB expects detailed policies covering customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting. A generic template will not pass.
  • Insufficient capital: Ensure you meet the tier for your services. If you plan to offer custody and exchange, you need EUR 50,000, but if you also offer order execution, you need EUR 125,000.
  • Poorly prepared business plan: Must include financial projections for 3 years, market analysis, and a clear description of your business model. The CNB will scrutinize revenue assumptions.
  • Ignoring IT security: A penetration test is often required. Your IT systems must meet standards such as ISO 27001 or equivalent.

Frequently asked questions

How long does a Czech Republic crypto license take in 2026?

The Czech National Bank typically processes complete applications in 3 to 6 months, but it can take up to 9 months if additional information is needed. Preparation of documents adds 4-8 weeks. Consulting24 can help streamline the process.

What is the minimum capital for a Czech crypto license?

Under MiCA, the minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for basic services like custody and exchange, EUR 125,000 for higher risk services, and EUR 150,000 for full scope. The capital must be held in an EU bank account.

Can I serve all EU countries with a Czech license?

Yes, a CASP license from the Czech Republic allows passporting to all EU member states under MiCA. You can offer services cross-border without additional licenses.

What are the main costs besides capital?

Government fees are EUR 1,000-2,000. Professional fees for legal and compliance setup start at EUR 15,000. Company incorporation and registered office add approximately EUR 2,000-5,000. Total costs excluding capital are around EUR 20,000-25,000.

Is a Czech crypto license valid for stablecoin issuance?

No, stablecoin issuance is not covered by the CASP license. It requires a separate authorization as an e-money institution or credit institution under EU regulations.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations?

Can I apply for a Czech license if my company is outside the EU?

Yes, but you must establish a subsidiary in the Czech Republic with a registered office and local management. The CNB will assess the parent company's reputation as well.
