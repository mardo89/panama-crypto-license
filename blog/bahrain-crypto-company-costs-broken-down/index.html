What Is a Bahrain Crypto License?

The Central Bank of Bahrain (CBB) introduced the Crypto-Asset Module (CRA) in 2019, making Bahrain one of the first MENA countries to regulate crypto assets. The license allows firms to operate a crypto exchange, provide custody services, or facilitate crypto payments. The CBB is the sole regulator, and licensing is mandatory for any crypto asset service provider operating in or from Bahrain.

Key features of the Bahrain regime include a clear regulatory framework, a tiered capital requirement, and a relatively fast approval process (typically 3-6 months). The CBB focuses on consumer protection, anti-money laundering (AML), and financial stability. As of 2026, the regime is fully operational and has attracted numerous global firms. The CRA module is detailed and covers everything from initial coin offerings to tokenisation, making it one of the most comprehensive frameworks in the region.

For firms looking to serve the Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) region, Bahrain offers a stable, well-regulated environment. However, the high capital requirement may be a barrier for smaller startups. In contrast, our Panama package requires no minimum capital and costs a flat EUR 6,000, making it a more accessible entry point.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Central Bank of Bahrain (CBB), specifically its Financial Institutions Supervision Directorate. The license is called a Crypto-Asset Module (CRA) License. There is one main license type, but firms must specify the services they intend to offer (e.g., exchange, custody, payment services). The CBB also offers a regulatory sandbox for innovative projects, but a full license is required for commercial operations.

Bahrain does not have separate licenses for different activities like the EU's MiCA tiers. Instead, the CBB assesses each application based on the proposed business model and risk profile. This means the application process is more bespoke but also requires thorough preparation. The CBB has published a detailed rulebook (CRA Module) that outlines all requirements, including governance, risk management, and disclosure obligations.

For a simpler licensing process, consider jurisdictions like Panama, where the company setup is straightforward and no specific crypto license is required for certain activities. Our company setup page provides more details.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a full Crypto-Asset Module license is BHD 200,000 (approx. EUR 490,000). This must be maintained as paid-up capital and cannot be withdrawn. For firms operating in the regulatory sandbox, the requirement may be lower (e.g., BHD 50,000). The CBB may also impose additional capital based on the risk profile and volume of transactions.

This is significantly higher than the EU MiCA capital tiers (EUR 50,000-150,000) and far above Panama's zero capital requirement. If you are a startup with limited capital, Panama or Lithuania may be more accessible. Check our requirements page for comparisons. The capital must be deposited in a Bahraini bank account and cannot be used for operational expenses; it serves as a buffer for consumer protection.

For firms that can meet the capital threshold, Bahrain offers a prestigious license that signals regulatory compliance to partners and customers. However, the high capital requirement is a common reason why many firms choose alternative jurisdictions like Panama, where our flat fee covers everything without capital demands.

Tax Treatment

Bahrain has no corporate income tax, no capital gains tax, and no VAT on most services. This makes it a tax-neutral jurisdiction for crypto companies. However, there is a 5% VAT on certain goods and services, and social insurance contributions for employees. The absence of corporate tax is a major advantage over EU jurisdictions (e.g., Estonia 20%, Lithuania 15%).

Note that tax residency and permanent establishment rules apply. If your company is managed from Bahrain, it will be considered a tax resident. Always seek local tax advice. Bahrain also has double tax treaties with several countries, which can reduce withholding taxes on cross-border payments. For a tax-free alternative, Panama also offers 0% corporate tax on offshore income, with no capital requirements. Our Panama page has more details.

Allowed Activities Under a Bahrain Crypto License

The CBB license permits the following activities (subject to approval):

  • Operating a crypto exchange (order book or broker)
  • Custody of crypto assets
  • Crypto payment services
  • Initial coin offerings (ICOs) - requires additional approval
  • Tokenisation of assets
  • Providing crypto-related advisory services

Activities not allowed include unlicensed lending, derivatives without a separate license, and any service involving anonymous cryptocurrencies (e.g., Monero). The CBB also prohibits certain high-risk activities. For a full list, consult the CRA module. The CBB may impose conditions on the license, such as transaction limits or reporting requirements, depending on the business model.

If your activities are limited to token issuance or advisory, a simpler jurisdiction like Panama may suffice. Our exchange license page covers other options.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Pre-application consultation: Engage with the CBB to discuss your business model and eligibility. This is highly recommended to avoid surprises.
  2. Prepare documentation: Business plan, AML/KYC policies, financial projections, governance structure, and capital proof. The CBB expects detailed policies aligned with FATF standards.
  3. Submit application: Pay the non-refundable BHD 2,000 fee and submit all documents via the CBB's online portal.
  4. Review and due diligence: The CBB assesses the application, including background checks on directors and shareholders. This can take 2-3 months.
  5. In-principle approval: If satisfied, the CBB grants in-principle approval, allowing you to set up the company and deposit capital.
  6. Company incorporation: Register a Bahraini company (typically a WLL) with the Ministry of Industry and Commerce. This requires a local registered address and a resident director.
  7. Final approval and license issuance: Once the company is registered and capital is deposited, the CBB issues the full license. Total time: 3-6 months.

Compliance and Trust Considerations

Bahrain has a strong AML/CFT regime aligned with FATF recommendations. Licensees must appoint a Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO), implement strong KYC procedures, and submit regular reports to the CBB. The CBB conducts on-site inspections and can impose fines for non-compliance. The compliance burden is significant, requiring dedicated staff and systems.

This is general guidance, not legal advice. Always engage a local compliance consultant to ensure full adherence. For a simpler compliance burden, Panama offers a lighter regulatory touch with no specific crypto license requirements for many activities. Our Panama page provides details. In Bahrain, non-compliance can result in license revocation or fines up to BHD 100,000, so it is critical to invest in compliance from day one.

Common Mistakes When Applying

  • Underestimating capital: The BHD 200,000 minimum is strict; ensure you have it in liquid assets. Some applicants try to use borrowed funds, which the CBB will detect.
  • Incomplete documentation: The CBB expects detailed business plans and AML policies. Generic templates are often rejected.
  • Ignoring local presence: You need a physical office in Bahrain and a resident director. Virtual offices are not accepted.
  • Choosing the wrong license category: Some activities require additional approvals; failing to specify them can delay the process.
  • Not engaging with the CBB early: Pre-application consultation is highly recommended to align expectations.
  • Overlooking ongoing costs: Annual compliance costs can be EUR 25,000-40,000, which many startups underestimate.

Frequently asked questions

What is the total cost to set up a crypto company in Bahrain?

The total first-year cost, including the minimum capital of BHD 200,000 (EUR 490,000), application fee, legal setup, and compliance, is approximately EUR 556,500. This excludes ongoing annual costs of EUR 25,000-40,000. For a cheaper alternative, Panama costs a flat EUR 6,000 with no capital requirement.

How long does it take to get a Bahrain crypto license?

The CBB typically processes applications within 3-6 months, provided all documents are complete and the capital is deposited. This is faster than many EU jurisdictions but slower than Panama, where company setup can be completed in days.

What is the minimum capital for a Bahrain crypto license?

The minimum capital is BHD 200,000 (approx. EUR 490,000) for a full license. For firms in the regulatory sandbox, it may be lower (e.g., BHD 50,000). This capital must be paid up and maintained in a Bahraini bank account.

Is Bahrain tax-free for crypto companies?

Yes, Bahrain has no corporate income tax, no capital gains tax, and no VAT on most crypto services. However, there is a 5% VAT on some goods and services, and social insurance contributions for employees. Panama also offers 0% corporate tax on offshore income.

Can I operate a crypto exchange with a Bahrain license?

Yes, the CBB license permits operating a crypto exchange (order book or broker model), custody, payment services, and token issuance. Additional approvals may be needed for ICOs. Activities like lending or derivatives require separate licenses.

Do I need a physical office in Bahrain?

Yes, the CBB requires a physical office in Bahrain and a resident director. Virtual offices are not accepted. This adds to the setup cost. Panama does not require a physical office for offshore companies.

What are the ongoing compliance costs for a Bahrain crypto license?

Annual compliance costs typically range from BHD 5,000 to 15,000 (EUR 12,250-36,750), covering an MLRO, audits, and regulatory reporting. These costs are higher than in Panama, where annual compliance is around EUR 2,000-5,000.

Is a Bahrain crypto license recognized internationally?

Yes, the CBB is a respected regulator and the license is recognized globally. It signals strong regulatory compliance, which can help with banking relationships and partnerships. However, it is not automatically passportable to other jurisdictions.

What are the main alternatives to a Bahrain crypto license?

The main alternatives are Panama (no capital, flat EUR 6,000), Lithuania (EUR 125,000 capital, 15% tax), and Estonia (EUR 100,000 capital, 20% tax). Dubai is another option but has high setup costs. Panama is the most cost-effective for startups.
