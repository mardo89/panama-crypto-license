What is the Panama AML/KYC Program?

The Panama AML/KYC program is a regulatory framework that requires crypto service providers to implement strong anti-money laundering (AML) and know-your-customer (KYC) procedures. It is enforced by the Financial Analysis Unit (UAF) of Panama, which oversees compliance with Law 23 of 2015 and subsequent amendments. The program mandates that companies verify customer identities, monitor transactions, report suspicious activities, and maintain records for at least five years.

For crypto firms, the program is part of the licensing process. Without a compliant AML/KYC program, you cannot obtain or maintain a Panama crypto license. The requirements are similar to those in other jurisdictions but are tailored to Panama's legal environment. Consulting24 advises clients on building a program that meets both local and international expectations. The program covers risk assessment, customer due diligence (CDD), enhanced due diligence (EDD) for high-risk clients, and ongoing transaction monitoring. It also requires the appointment of a compliance officer and regular training for staff. Panama's framework follows FATF recommendations, so it is recognized globally. License Type and Regulator

Panama does not have a specific 'crypto license' per se. Instead, crypto businesses operate under a general business license combined with a mandatory AML/KYC program registration. The regulator is the Financial Analysis Unit (UAF) of Panama, which supervises AML compliance. The Superintendency of Banks and other financial regulators may also have oversight depending on the activities.

As of 2026, Panama is not an EU member, so MiCA does not apply. However, the country has its own AML laws that align with FATF recommendations. Capital Requirement

Panama has no statutory minimum capital requirement for crypto license applicants. However, firms must demonstrate sufficient financial resources to operate and maintain compliance. Typically, a paid-up capital of around USD 10,000 (approximately EUR 9,200) is recommended to show substance. This is significantly lower than EU jurisdictions where MiCA imposes capital tiers of EUR 50,000 / 125,000 / 150,000 depending on service class. Tax Treatment

Panama operates a territorial tax system. Income earned outside Panama is not taxed. Crypto businesses that generate revenue from international clients typically pay no corporate income tax on that income. However, income sourced within Panama (e.g., from Panamanian residents) may be subject to local taxes. There is no VAT on crypto transactions, but a 7% ITBMS (transfer tax) may apply to certain services. Consulting24 recommends a tax review to ensure compliance. Panama also has no capital gains tax on crypto profits if the gains are from offshore sources.

For example, if your exchange serves customers worldwide but none in Panama, your revenue is tax-free. If you have a Panamanian client, only that portion is taxed. This is a powerful incentive for international crypto firms. Consulting24 works with local tax advisors to structure your operations optimally. Compare this to Lithuania where corporate tax is 15% on worldwide income, or Estonia where distributed profits are taxed at 20%. Panama's territorial system can save you significant amounts.

Step-by-Step Process to Obtain the License

Obtaining a Panama crypto license involves the following steps:

  1. Company incorporation: Register a Panama corporation (Sociedad Anónima) with a local registered agent. This takes 1-2 weeks.
  2. AML/KYC program development: Draft policies covering customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, record keeping, and suspicious activity reporting. Consulting24 provides templates.
  3. Appoint a compliance officer: Designate a local or remote compliance officer responsible for AML oversight. The officer must have relevant experience.
  4. Submit to UAF: Register the AML program with the Financial Analysis Unit. This includes filing the program documents and paying a registration fee (included in the flat fee).
  5. Obtain business license: Secure the municipal business license (Aviso de Operación) from the municipality where your registered office is located.
  6. Open a bank account: Establish a corporate bank account in Panama or abroad. Consulting24 assists with introductions to crypto-friendly banks.
  7. Ongoing compliance: File annual reports, renew the AML program, and submit suspicious activity reports as needed.
