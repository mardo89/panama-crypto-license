Overview: What Is the Czech Republic Crypto License?

The Czech Republic, as an EU member, fully implemented MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation) by 2026. Crypto asset service providers (CASPs) must obtain a license from the Czech National Bank (CNB). The regime is harmonized across the EU, meaning a Czech license allows passporting to other EU states. The license covers activities like exchange, custody, and transfer services. Capital requirements are tiered: EUR 50,000 for basic services, EUR 125,000 for custody, and EUR 150,000 for exchange services. The process involves a detailed application, AML policies, and proof of operational readiness. The CNB is known for thorough scrutiny, so expect a rigorous application process. For a deeper look at the application steps, see our application process guide.

Who Needs a Czech License vs Panama Company?

Founders targeting the European market need a Czech license (or another EU license) to comply with MiCA. It suits exchanges, wallet providers, and DeFi projects that want to serve EU clients legally. Panama, on the other hand, is ideal for non-EU focused businesses, startups wanting minimal upfront costs, or firms that need a holding company for international operations. Panama does not have a specific crypto license; instead, you register a standard corporation (Sociedad Anónima) and conduct crypto activities under general business law. If you need EU passporting, choose the Czech Republic. If you want speed and low cost, choose Panama. For a comparison with another EU option, see our vs Lithuania page.

License Type & Regulator

Czech Republic: The regulator is the Czech National Bank (CNB). The license is a single CASP authorization covering all crypto activities. It is a full compliance license with ongoing reporting requirements. The CNB is known for thorough scrutiny, so expect a rigorous application process. The license is valid indefinitely but subject to annual renewal and compliance checks.

Panama: No dedicated crypto license exists. Instead, you register a standard Panamanian corporation. The regulatory body is the Superintendencia de Bancos de Panamá (for banking) and the Registro Público for company registration. Crypto businesses operate under general commercial law, but must comply with AML regulations if they engage in financial activities. Panama is working on a crypto bill, but as of 2026, no license is required. This lack of specific regulation offers flexibility but also less legal certainty for some activities.

Capital Requirements

The Czech Republic imposes mandatory capital tiers under MiCA: EUR 50,000 for simple services (e.g., order execution), EUR 125,000 for custody, and EUR 150,000 for exchange services. This capital must be held in a Czech bank account and is subject to ongoing verification. The capital serves as a safeguard for clients and must be maintained throughout the license period. Panama has no minimum capital requirement for crypto companies. The standard authorized capital is USD 10,000, but it does not need to be paid up. This makes Panama much more accessible for bootstrapped startups. However, some banks may require a minimum deposit to open an account. For more on capital requirements across jurisdictions, see our requirements page.

Tax Treatment

Czech Republic: Corporate income tax is 19%. Crypto transactions are subject to VAT (21%) on fees, and capital gains tax applies. The tax authority has issued guidance treating crypto as intangible assets. Double taxation treaties are available with many countries. Tax compliance is rigorous, with quarterly filings and annual audits required.

Panama: Territorial taxation - only income sourced in Panama is taxed. Offshore income is exempt. Corporate tax is 25% on local income, but most crypto firms structure to earn income outside Panama, resulting in near-zero tax. No VAT on crypto services. Panama also has no capital gains tax. This is a major advantage for international trading firms. However, tax residents of other countries must still report global income to their home tax authority. Panama's tax regime is a key reason why many choose it for holding companies.

Allowed Activities

Czech Republic: Under the CASP license, you can operate crypto exchanges (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto), custodial wallets, transfer services, and order execution. DeFi and non-custodial services may require additional scrutiny. Activities must be registered with the CNB. The license also permits staking and lending if properly disclosed. Any expansion of services requires prior approval from the CNB.

Panama: No specific restrictions. You can run an exchange, wallet, payment processor, mining pool, or DeFi platform. However, if you touch fiat currency or banking, you need a license from the Superintendencia de Bancos. Most crypto firms operate without a financial license by using third-party payment processors. Panama is more flexible but less regulated. Activities like ICOs or token issuance may fall under securities laws if the tokens are deemed securities. Legal advice is recommended for complex structures.

Banking & Payment Partners

Banking is a challenge in both jurisdictions. In the Czech Republic, major banks (ČSOB, Komerční banka) are cautious with crypto clients, but some fintech-friendly banks accept CASP license holders. Expect strict AML checks, including source of funds verification. The process can take 1-3 months. Panama offers more banking options, including private banks that accept crypto companies, but due diligence is heavy. Some banks require a minimum deposit of USD 10,000. Consulting24 can introduce you to banking partners in both locations. For payment processing, Panama allows integration with international PSPs like Stripe or Coinbase Commerce, while Czech firms must use EU-compliant PSPs. Crypto-to-crypto businesses in Panama can often use offshore payment processors without a local bank account.

Benefits of Each Jurisdiction

Czech Republic: EU passporting rights, high credibility with partners and regulators, clear legal framework under MiCA, access to EU banking and payment infrastructure. Best for firms targeting European customers. The license also allows participation in EU-wide initiatives like the Digital Euro pilot.

Panama: Low cost (EUR 6,000 flat), no capital requirement, territorial tax (offshore income tax-free), quick setup (2-4 weeks), no crypto-specific license. Ideal for startups, trading firms, and holding companies. Panama also offers privacy and asset protection. The corporate structure can be used to hold intellectual property or investments. Compliance & Trust Considerations

Compliance is a key differentiator. The Czech Republic requires ongoing AML reporting, annual audits, and a local compliance officer. This builds trust with banks and partners but adds cost. Panama has lighter compliance - no specific crypto regulation, but you must follow general AML laws if you handle fiat. For pure crypto-to-crypto businesses, compliance is minimal. However, lack of regulation may deter some partners. Consulting24 provides compliance support for both jurisdictions. This is general guidance, not legal advice. Always consult a local attorney for your specific situation. For a broader view of compliance across jurisdictions, see our jurisdictions overview.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Czech Republic: Underestimating the timeline (4-8 months is realistic). Not preparing a thorough AML policy. Choosing a bank without crypto experience. Failing to deposit the full capital before application. Ignoring ongoing reporting obligations - annual reports must be filed on time to avoid fines.

Panama: Assuming no regulation means no compliance - if you touch fiat, you need a license. Not using a reputable registered agent. Ignoring tax reporting in your home country. Panama is not a tax haven for residents; territorial tax applies only to local income. Also, failing to maintain a physical address or registered agent can lead to company dissolution. Always keep your corporate records up to date.

Alternatives: Panama vs Lithuania and Estonia

If you are considering the Czech Republic, you might also look at Lithuania or Estonia, both EU states with established crypto licensing under MiCA. Lithuania offers a CASP license with capital requirements similar to the Czech Republic, but with a faster timeline (3-5 months). Estonia has a stricter regime with higher capital (EUR 100,000) and a more complex process. Panama remains the cheapest and fastest option overall. Frequently asked questions

Is a crypto license required in Panama?

No, Panama does not have a specific crypto license as of 2026. You can operate a crypto business using a standard Panamanian corporation. However, if you handle fiat currency or offer financial services, you may need a banking license from the Superintendencia de Bancos.

How long does it take to get a Czech crypto license?

The Czech National Bank typically takes 4-8 months to review and approve a CASP application. The timeline depends on the completeness of your application and the CNB's workload. Consulting24 can help prepare a thorough submission to avoid delays.

Can I passport my Czech license to other EU countries?

Yes, under MiCA, a Czech CASP license allows you to provide services across all EU member states without additional licensing. You must notify the CNB of your intent to passport and comply with local marketing rules.

What are the capital requirements for a Czech crypto license?

Under MiCA, capital requirements are tiered: EUR 50,000 for basic services (e.g., order execution), EUR 125,000 for custody, and EUR 150,000 for exchange services. This capital must be held in a Czech bank account.

Does Panama tax crypto profits?

Panama uses a territorial tax system. Only income sourced in Panama is taxed at 25%. Most crypto firms earn income outside Panama, resulting in near-zero tax. There is no capital gains tax. However, you must comply with tax laws in your country of residence.

Can I open a bank account for a Panama crypto company?

Yes, but it can be challenging. Some Panamanian banks accept crypto companies, but due diligence is strict. Consulting24 can introduce you to banking partners. Alternatively, you can use online payment processors or offshore banks.

What activities are allowed under a Czech CASP license?

The license covers exchange (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto), custodial wallet services, transfer services, and order execution. Staking and lending may be permitted with disclosure. DeFi and non-custodial services require additional scrutiny.

Is Panama a good jurisdiction for a crypto startup?

Yes, Panama is ideal for startups due to low cost (EUR 6,000 flat), no capital requirement, quick setup (2-4 weeks), and territorial tax. However, it lacks a dedicated crypto license, which may affect credibility with some partners.

What are the ongoing compliance costs in the Czech Republic?

Annual compliance costs range from EUR 5,000 to EUR 15,000, including audit, AML officer salary, and regulatory reporting. These costs are higher than in Panama but provide greater regulatory certainty.
