When Panama wins

Panama is the better choice in five specific scenarios:

  1. Capital-light early-stage operators. Lithuania's €125,000 minimum capital is locked working capital. Panama needs $0. For pre-revenue or bootstrapped operators, Panama frees up $135,000+ for actual business operations.
  2. LatAm, Asia, or HNW target market. If your customers are NOT EU retail, you're paying for an EU passport you won't use. Panama costs less and serves these markets just as well.
  3. Speed to market. 2-3 weeks vs 4-8 months. If launch timing matters more than EU access, Panama wins.
  4. Territorial-tax efficiency. Panama's 0% on foreign-source income is the cleanest in the Western hemisphere. For operators with non-Panamanian customer bases, ongoing tax is dramatically lower than Lithuania's 15%.
  5. Lower regulatory burden. Lithuania's MiCA framework is comprehensive; Panama's general financial-services framework is lighter-touch. For operators who want operational flexibility, Panama wins.

When Lithuania wins

Lithuania CASP is the better choice in five specific scenarios:

  1. EU retail customers are the target market. MiCA passport across 27 EU member states is unique to EU CASPs. Without it, you can't legally serve EU retail. Period.
  2. Public-facing crypto exchange brand. Bank of Lithuania CASP authorization on your homepage = institutional trust. Panama (no specific license) doesn't compare for brand-driven exchanges.
  3. Banking partnerships matter. Lithuania's EMI ecosystem (Paysera, Verse, ConnectPay, Genome) is the deepest crypto-friendly banking environment in the EU. Panama banking has tightened significantly since 2023.
  4. Investor / acquirer due diligence. EU CASP authorization is a "real" regulator badge that VCs and acquirers recognize. Panama's general corporate structure doesn't carry the same weight in due diligence.
  5. Long-term EU presence. If you're building toward a 5+ year EU operation, the Lithuania CASP investment pays back across the period via market access.

The combined approach (most institutional players)

Many serious crypto operators end up with both: a Lithuania CASP entity for EU retail + a Panama entity for offshore HNW / non-EU markets. This combo costs roughly $65,000-$165,000 setup + €125K capital, but unlocks both EU retail and offshore tax-efficient operations.

The combined approach is common for:

  • Crypto exchanges with global ambitions (one entity per region)
  • OTC desks serving both EU institutional and offshore HNW
  • Token issuers needing EU MiCA white-paper compliance + offshore treasury
  • Payment processors with split fiat-corridor strategy

FAQ - Panama vs Lithuania crypto license

Is Panama or Lithuania cheaper for a crypto license?

Panama is significantly cheaper: €6,000 fixed setup vs Lithuania CASP at $50,000-$120,000. Panama also has $0 minimum capital vs Lithuania's €50,000-€150,000 (depending on CASP class). The trade-off: Panama provides no EU passport; Lithuania CASP grants service across 27 EU member states under MiCA.

Does Panama have a crypto license like Lithuania?

No. Panama has no dedicated crypto/VASP license as of 2026. Companies operate as Sociedad Anónima or Foundation under existing financial-services frameworks. Lithuania has a fully formalized MiCA CASP framework supervised by Bank of Lithuania with three service classes (Class 1 €50K, Class 2 €125K, Class 3 €150K capital).

When should I pick Lithuania over Panama for crypto?

Pick Lithuania CASP if you target EU retail customers - MiCA passport is the killer feature. Pick Panama if you target Latin America, Asia, HNW, or offshore-first operations where 0% tax on foreign-source income matters more than EU access. Many founders use both: Panama for offshore operations, separate Lithuania CASP for EU retail.

Can a Panama company serve EU customers without Lithuania CASP?

Technically yes, but compliance with MiCA is required for any crypto service offered to EU customers. Without an EU CASP entity, you cannot legally market to EU retail or rely on EU CASP passporting. Most operators that serve EU customers seriously end up adding a Lithuania CASP entity.

Which has stronger banking access - Panama or Lithuania?

Lithuania. The Lithuanian EMI ecosystem (Paysera, Verse, ConnectPay, Genome) was built around fintech/crypto and offers deep crypto-friendly rails. Panama banking has tightened significantly since 2023 - expect 2-4 weeks for onboarding and budget for 1-2 bank rejections. Lithuania banking onboarding is 1-2 weeks for most CASP entities.

Is Lithuania CASP more credible than Panama for institutional clients?

Yes - significantly. Bank of Lithuania CASP authorization appears in the public Bank of Lithuania CASP register and ESMA's central CASP register. Institutional counterparties verify regulator status before transacting. Panama's general corporate structure doesn't appear in any specific crypto register.
