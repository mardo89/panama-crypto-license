What is a Crypto Gambling License in Hong Kong?

A crypto gambling license in Hong Kong allows operators to offer gambling services using cryptocurrencies such as Bitcoin, Ethereum, and stablecoins. The license is issued by the Hong Kong Police Force's Gambling Licensing Division, which oversees all gambling activities, while the Securities and Futures Commission (SFC) regulates crypto-related financial products. As of 2026, Hong Kong has implemented a dedicated licensing regime for virtual asset service providers (VASPs) that also covers gambling platforms accepting crypto.

The license is essential for any platform that facilitates crypto-based wagering, including casino games, sports betting, poker, and fantasy sports. It ensures that operators meet anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorist financing (CTF) standards, as well as player protection requirements. Without this license, operating a crypto gambling platform in Hong Kong is illegal and carries severe penalties.

Any business that offers gambling services to Hong Kong residents or operates from Hong Kong must obtain this license. This includes:

  • Online casinos accepting crypto deposits and payouts
  • Sports betting platforms using digital currencies
  • Poker rooms and skill game sites with real-money wagering
  • Fantasy sports platforms with cash prizes
  • White-label gambling solutions serving Hong Kong players

If your platform involves any form of betting or staking of crypto assets, you likely fall under the licensing requirement. License Type and Regulator

The primary regulator for gambling in Hong Kong is the Hong Kong Police Force's Gambling Licensing Division, under the Gambling Ordinance (Cap. 148). For crypto-related activities, the Securities and Futures Commission (SFC) also plays a role, especially if the platform offers tokens that qualify as securities. As of 2026, the SFC's VASP licensing regime applies to any platform trading or facilitating crypto assets, including gambling tokens.

There are two main license types: a Bookmaker License for sports betting and a Casino License for casino games. Both are issued by the Gambling Licensing Division. The SFC may require a separate license if the platform issues its own token or offers derivative products. Capital Requirement

Hong Kong does not impose a fixed minimum capital requirement for gambling licenses. However, operators must demonstrate sufficient financial resources to cover operational costs and player payouts. As a guideline, the Gambling Licensing Division expects a minimum working capital of approximately EUR 50,000 to EUR 100,000, depending on the scale of operations. This is not a statutory requirement but a practical expectation to ensure solvency.

Tax Treatment

Hong Kong's tax regime is one of its biggest attractions. Gambling operators are subject to a profits tax rate of 16.5% on net profits derived from Hong Kong. However, there is no withholding tax on dividends, no capital gains tax, and no VAT or GST. For crypto gambling platforms, this means only profits from Hong Kong-based players are taxed; offshore income is generally exempt.

Additionally, Hong Kong does not tax cryptocurrency transactions as income unless they are part of a trade or business. This favorable treatment makes Hong Kong a competitive jurisdiction for crypto gambling. Allowed Activities

A Hong Kong crypto gambling license permits the following activities:

  • Operating online casino games (slots, blackjack, roulette, etc.) with crypto
  • Sports betting on local and international events
  • Poker tournaments and cash games
  • Fantasy sports with real-money entry fees
  • Skill-based gaming with wagering
  • Issuing and managing in-game tokens (subject to SFC approval)

Activities that are not allowed include unlicensed lottery operations, betting on illegal events, and any form of gambling that violates Hong Kong's anti-money laundering laws. The license also requires operators to implement responsible gambling measures, such as deposit limits and self-exclusion tools.

Is crypto gambling legal in Hong Kong?

Yes, crypto gambling is legal in Hong Kong if the operator holds a valid gambling license from the Hong Kong Police Force's Gambling Licensing Division. The license covers both fiat and crypto-based gambling activities, provided the operator complies with AML and responsible gambling regulations.

Crypto gambling operators pay profits tax at 16.5% on net profits from Hong Kong-sourced income. Offshore income is generally tax-free. There is no VAT, capital gains tax, or withholding tax on dividends.
