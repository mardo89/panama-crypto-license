What is the Saint Lucia Crypto License?

The Saint Lucia crypto license is a regulatory authorisation issued by the Financial Services Regulatory Authority (FSRA) under the Virtual Asset Business Act (VABA) 2024. It allows companies to conduct virtual asset services, including exchange, transfer, and custody. The regime is designed to align with FATF recommendations and provide a clear legal framework for crypto businesses. As of 2026, Saint Lucia offers a streamlined application process with a focus on compliance and transparency.

Key features include a flat corporate tax rate, no capital gains tax, and a relatively low minimum capital requirement compared to EU MiCA regimes. The license is suitable for both retail and institutional services, with tiered capital based on activity type. The FSRA has published guidelines on virtual asset service provider obligations, and we ensure your application meets every requirement. Who Needs a Saint Lucia Crypto License?

A Saint Lucia crypto license is essential for any entity that intends to offer virtual asset services within or from Saint Lucia. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralised or peer-to-peer)
  • Wallet providers (custodial and non-custodial)
  • Payment processors using virtual assets
  • Token issuers (if the token is classified as a virtual asset)
  • OTC trading desks
  • Asset managers dealing in crypto

Founders from any jurisdiction can apply, but the company must be incorporated in Saint Lucia. The license is particularly attractive for businesses targeting the Caribbean market or seeking a cost-effective alternative to EU or US licensing. License Type and Regulator

The Saint Lucia crypto license is a single license type under the Virtual Asset Business Act (VABA), administered by the Financial Services Regulatory Authority (FSRA). The FSRA is the sole regulator for virtual asset service providers (VASPs) in Saint Lucia. There is no separate exchange or wallet license; the same license covers all permitted activities, subject to the scope defined in the application.

As of 2026, the FSRA requires all VASPs to register and obtain a license. The regime is not a sandbox; it is a full regulatory framework. The FSRA conducts background checks on directors and shareholders, and requires a local physical presence (registered office and at least one director resident in Saint Lucia). Consulting24 can assist with finding a local director and office space. The regulator has also issued guidance on cybersecurity and consumer protection, which we incorporate into your compliance documentation. For comparison, the Estonia crypto license is issued by the FIU under a different framework.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a Saint Lucia crypto license is set by the FSRA under the VABA. As of 2026, the requirement is tiered based on the type of services offered:

  • Class A (exchange and transfer services): Minimum capital of USD 50,000
  • Class B (custody and wallet services): Minimum capital of USD 25,000
  • Class C (combined services): Minimum capital of USD 75,000

These figures are subject to change and may be higher for complex operations. The capital must be maintained in a Saint Lucian bank account or in liquid assets. Consulting24 can advise on the exact requirement for your business model. Note that this is significantly lower than EU MiCA requirements (EUR 50,000 - 150,000). For example, a wallet-only startup can meet the USD 25,000 threshold, while a full exchange with custody needs USD 75,000. We help you structure your application to optimise capital allocation.

Tax Treatment

Saint Lucia offers a highly favourable tax environment for crypto businesses. Key tax features include:

  • Corporate income tax: Flat rate of 30% on profits, but international business companies (IBCs) can benefit from a reduced rate of 1-2% under certain conditions.
  • No capital gains tax: Gains from the sale of virtual assets are not taxed.
  • No withholding tax: On dividends, interest, or royalties paid to non-residents.
  • No VAT on crypto services: Virtual asset services are exempt from VAT.
  • Double taxation treaties: Saint Lucia has limited treaties, but no tax on foreign-sourced income for IBCs.

Most crypto license holders structure as an IBC to benefit from the low tax rate. Consulting24 can coordinate with local tax advisors to optimise your structure. Always seek professional tax advice. The effective tax rate can be as low as 1% for qualifying IBCs, making Saint Lucia one of the most tax-efficient crypto jurisdictions globally. Allowed Activities

Under the VABA, a Saint Lucia crypto license permits the following activities:

  • Exchange between virtual assets and fiat currency
  • Exchange between one or more forms of virtual assets
  • Transfer of virtual assets (on behalf of clients)
  • Custody and administration of virtual assets (including private keys)
  • Participation in and provision of financial services related to an issuer’s offer or sale of virtual assets
  • Operation of a virtual asset trading platform (exchange)

The license does not cover securities tokens (which fall under securities law) or derivatives. The FSRA may impose conditions on the license scope. Consulting24 can help define your activities in the application to ensure full compliance. If you plan to offer staking or lending, these may require additional approval. Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a Saint Lucia crypto license involves several stages. Consulting24 coordinates each step with local partners.

  1. Pre-incorporation planning: Define business model, select company type (IBC recommended), and prepare documentation.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Saint Lucia company with a registered office and local director. Cost: USD 2,000 - 5,000.
  3. Document preparation: Compile business plan, AML/KYC policies, risk assessment, financial projections, and background checks for directors and shareholders.
  4. Submission to FSRA: Submit application with government fee (USD 5,000 - 10,000). The FSRA reviews within 2-3 months.
  5. Due diligence and interview: FSRA may request additional information or interview key personnel.
  6. License issuance: Upon approval, pay issuance fee (USD 15,000 - 25,000) and receive license.
  7. Post-license compliance: Annual renewal (USD 10,000 - 20,000), ongoing AML reporting, and audits.

Total timeline: 3-6 months. Consulting24 provides end-to-end coordination. For comparison, see our guide on Lithuania crypto license which follows a different process under the Bank of Lithuania.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make these errors when applying for a Saint Lucia crypto license:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing business plans or AML policies cause delays. Prepare all documents before submission.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Saint Lucia crypto license?

The minimum capital is tiered: USD 25,000 for custody/wallet services, USD 50,000 for exchange/transfer, and USD 75,000 for combined services. These figures are as of 2026 and must be maintained in liquid assets.

The process typically takes 3 to 6 months from application to approval, depending on the completeness of documentation and the FSRA's workload. Consulting24 coordinates to expedite the process.

What taxes apply to a Saint Lucia crypto license?

International business companies (IBCs) benefit from a reduced corporate tax rate of 1-2%, no capital gains tax, no withholding tax, and no VAT on crypto services. Standard companies pay 30% but can structure as IBCs.

Can I apply for a Saint Lucia crypto license remotely?

Yes, you can apply remotely, but you must appoint a local director and maintain a registered office in Saint Lucia. Consulting24 can assist with finding both.

What activities are allowed under the Saint Lucia crypto license?

Allowed activities include exchange between crypto and fiat, crypto-to-crypto exchange, transfer, custody, and operation of a trading platform. Securities tokens and derivatives are excluded.

Is the Saint Lucia crypto license recognized internationally?

The license is FATF-compliant and recognised by many international banks and partners. However, it does not automatically grant access to the EU market under MiCA.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?
