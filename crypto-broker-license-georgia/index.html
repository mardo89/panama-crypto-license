What Is a Crypto Broker License in Georgia?

A crypto broker license in Georgia is a regulatory authorization that allows companies to operate as virtual asset service providers (VASPs). The license covers activities such as exchanging cryptocurrencies for fiat or other digital assets, operating a trading platform, and providing custody services. Georgia's framework aligns with international anti-money laundering (AML) standards, making it a credible option for crypto entrepreneurs.

The primary regulator is the National Bank of Georgia (NBG), which oversees the registration and supervision of VASPs. Unlike some jurisdictions that offer multiple license tiers, Georgia currently issues a single type of registration for crypto brokers. This registration is mandatory for any business offering crypto exchange or custody services to Georgian residents.

For a detailed comparison with other jurisdictions, see our vs Lithuania page. Georgia's approach is distinct from MiCA-regulated EU states: it does not impose a statutory minimum capital, but expects applicants to demonstrate financial substance. This makes it an appealing alternative for companies that want a credible license without the high capital demands of EU jurisdictions.

The NBG has been proactive in updating its rules. As of 2026, the registration process includes a thorough assessment of the applicant's AML controls, business model viability, and the beneficial ownership structure. The regulator also requires that all VASPs implement strong cybersecurity measures and maintain transaction monitoring systems.

Who Needs a Crypto Broker License in Georgia?

A crypto broker license Georgia is required for any entity that provides the following services to Georgian residents:

  • Exchange between virtual assets and fiat currencies
  • Exchange between one or more forms of virtual assets
  • Transfer of virtual assets
  • Custody and administration of virtual assets
  • Participation in and provision of financial services related to an issuer's offer or sale of a virtual asset

This includes startups, established exchanges, custody providers, and payment processors. If you plan to target the Georgian market or establish a regional hub, obtaining a crypto license Georgia is a prerequisite. Even if your primary market is outside Georgia, having a Georgian license can serve as a credible regulatory credential for partnerships and banking relationships.

Typical applicants include:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralized and decentralized)
  • OTC trading desks
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Payment processors handling crypto transactions
  • Token issuers and ICO platforms

The NBG does not distinguish between retail and institutional services under the current framework, so all VASPs must register. However, the regulator may impose additional conditions for high-volume or high-risk operations.

License Type and Regulator

The relevant license type is the Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) registration, overseen by the National Bank of Georgia (NBG). The NBG acts as the financial regulator and is responsible for AML/CFT supervision. There is no separate broker license; the VASP registration covers all broker-like activities.

Georgia's regulatory framework was established by amendments to the Law on Combating Money Laundering and the Financing of Terrorism, effective from 2023. As of 2026, the NBG continues to refine its requirements, including capital thresholds and reporting obligations. The NBG is a member of the Basel Committee on Banking Supervision and follows international standards.

Key regulatory features:

  • Single-tier registration for all VASP activities
  • Mandatory AML/CFT compliance program
  • Appointment of a local AML compliance officer
  • Annual reporting and audit requirements
  • On-site inspections by the NBG

The NBG maintains a public register of licensed VASPs, which enhances transparency and trust. For more details on regulatory expectations, see our requirements page.

Capital Requirement

As of 2026, Georgia does not have a statutory minimum capital requirement for VASP registration. However, the NBG expects applicants to demonstrate sufficient financial resources to cover operational costs and AML compliance. In practice, a minimum of EUR 10,000 - 20,000 in paid-up capital is recommended to show substance. This is lower than EU jurisdictions under MiCA, which require EUR 50,000 - 150,000 depending on services. For comparison, see our requirements page.

Capital must be held in a Georgian bank account and be freely available for business operations. The NBG may request proof of capital adequacy during the application review. The capital can be used for operational expenses such as salaries, office rent, and compliance tools. There is no requirement to maintain a specific amount after registration, but the NBG expects that the company remains solvent.

Examples of acceptable capital proof:

  • Bank statements showing the required balance
  • Audited financial statements of the parent company (if applicable)
  • Commitment letter from investors or shareholders

It is advisable to keep at least EUR 10,000 in the company's account throughout the licensing process to demonstrate financial substance.

Tax Treatment

Georgia offers a favorable tax regime for crypto businesses. Corporate income tax is 15%, but with a territorial system: income earned outside Georgia is generally exempt. Additionally, Georgia has a special tax regime for IT companies, which may apply to crypto brokers if they qualify. VAT is 18%, but crypto-to-crypto exchanges may be exempt. Personal income tax for employees is 20%.

Key tax advantages:

  • Territorial taxation: only Georgian-source income is taxed
  • No withholding tax on dividends paid to non-residents
  • No capital gains tax on crypto-to-crypto trades (subject to conditions)
  • IT company status can reduce corporate tax to 5% for qualifying activities

However, you must still file annual tax returns and maintain proper transfer pricing documentation if you transact with related parties.

Allowed Activities

Under the VASP registration, the following activities are permitted:

  • Exchange between virtual assets and fiat currencies
  • Exchange between virtual assets
  • Transfer of virtual assets
  • Custody and administration of virtual assets
  • Participation in and provision of financial services related to an issuer's offer or sale of a virtual asset

Notably, Georgia does not currently license derivatives or margin trading under the VASP framework. Such activities may require additional authorization. The NBG may also impose restrictions on certain high-risk activities, such as privacy coins or unregistered token offerings. If your business model includes these, you may need to modify your services or seek a license in another jurisdiction like Lithuania where such activities are regulated under MiCA.

The NBG has indicated that it may introduce separate licenses for specific activities in the future. As of 2026, the single VASP registration covers all listed activities, but you must describe your intended services in detail in the application. The NBG will assess whether each activity is adequately covered by your AML policies and operational controls.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining a crypto broker license Georgia involves the following steps:

  1. Company incorporation: Register a legal entity in Georgia (e.g., LLC) with a physical office address. This typically takes 1-2 weeks.
  2. Prepare documentation: Draft AML/KYC policies, business plan, risk assessment, and internal controls. This is the most time-consuming step and requires expert input.
  3. Appoint a local AML officer: Designate a resident responsible for AML compliance. The officer must have relevant experience and be approved by the NBG.
  4. Submit application: File with the NBG along with the application fee. The NBG will acknowledge receipt and begin its review.
  5. NBG review: The regulator assesses the application, which may take 2-4 months. They may request additional information or clarifications.
  6. Receive registration: Once approved, you are added to the VASP registry and can commence operations. You will receive a certificate of registration.

Benefits of a Georgia Crypto License

Georgia offers several advantages for crypto brokers:

  • Low capital requirements compared to EU jurisdictions
  • Favorable tax treatment with territorial exemption
  • Fast incorporation process (1-2 weeks)
  • Growing crypto-friendly ecosystem with a supportive regulator
  • Strategic location at the crossroads of Europe and Asia
  • No requirement for a local director (but a local AML officer is needed)

However, Georgia is not part of the EU, so a license does not provide passporting rights. For EU market access, consider a Lithuania crypto license or Estonia crypto license. Georgia is best suited for companies targeting the Caucasus, Central Asia, or the Middle East, or those seeking a cost-effective regulatory base.

Another benefit is the NBG's pragmatic approach. The regulator is open to dialogue and provides guidance during the application process, which reduces uncertainty. This is in contrast to some EU regulators that are more rigid.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Once registered, VASPs must comply with ongoing AML/CFT obligations, including:

  • Customer due diligence (CDD) and enhanced due diligence (EDD) for high-risk clients
  • Transaction monitoring and reporting of suspicious activities to the Financial Monitoring Service of Georgia
  • Annual audits and submission of financial statements to the NBG
  • Maintenance of records for at least 5 years
  • Notification of any changes in ownership, management, or business activities

Failure to comply can result in fines or revocation of the license. The NBG conducts periodic inspections and may request ad-hoc reports. Common Mistakes to Avoid

When applying for a crypto broker license Georgia, avoid these pitfalls:

  • Incomplete AML documentation: The NBG is strict on AML policies; ensure they are tailored to your business model and cover all risk areas.
  • Underestimating timeline: The process can take 2-4 months; plan accordingly and do not commit to launch dates before receiving the license.
  • Lack of local presence: The NBG expects a physical office and local staff. A virtual office may not be accepted.
  • Ignoring tax obligations: Even with territorial tax, you must file annual returns and pay social contributions for employees.
  • Insufficient capital: While there is no minimum, having less than EUR 10,000 may raise questions about financial stability.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a crypto broker license in Georgia?

Georgia does not have a statutory minimum capital requirement, but the NBG expects applicants to demonstrate financial substance. A recommended minimum of EUR 10,000-20,000 in paid-up capital is advisable. This is much lower than EU MiCA requirements of EUR 50,000-150,000.

Is a Georgia crypto license recognized in the EU?

No, Georgia is not an EU member state, so its license does not provide passporting rights under MiCA. If you need to serve EU clients, you will need a license from an EU jurisdiction like Lithuania or Estonia. However, Georgia can be a cost-effective base for non-EU markets.

What are the tax benefits of a Georgia crypto license?

Georgia offers a territorial tax system: corporate income tax is 15% but only on Georgian-source income. Income from foreign clients may be exempt. There is also a special IT company tax regime that can reduce the rate to 5% for qualifying activities. Crypto-to-crypto exchanges may be VAT-exempt.

Do I need a physical office in Georgia for the license?

Yes, the NBG expects a physical office address and local staff, including a designated AML compliance officer. A virtual office is not sufficient. The office must be a commercial space (not residential) and should have a sign with the company name.

What activities are not allowed under a Georgia VASP license?

Derivatives trading, margin trading, and offering securities tokens are not covered under the current VASP framework. These activities may require additional authorization or a separate license. The NBG also restricts services involving privacy coins or unregistered token offerings.

How does Georgia compare to Panama for crypto licensing?
