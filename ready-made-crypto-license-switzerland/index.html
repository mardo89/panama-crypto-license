What Is a Ready Made Switzerland Crypto License?

A ready made Switzerland crypto license refers to a company that has already been incorporated and has obtained the necessary FINMA authorisation to conduct crypto-related activities. This means the legal entity and the regulatory approval are in place, allowing you to start operations immediately after acquiring the company. The license covers activities such as operating a crypto exchange, wallet services, and token issuance under Swiss law.

Switzerland categorises crypto businesses as financial intermediaries, requiring a FINMA license under the Anti-Money Laundering Act (AMLA) or the Financial Market Infrastructure Act (FMIA). A ready made license eliminates the typical 6-12 month application process, giving you a compliant setup from day one. The company already has a track record with FINMA, which can simplify future expansions and partnerships. For example, a ready made entity may have existing banking relationships and a history of regulatory filings, reducing the burden on new owners.

The license is not a one-size-fits-all product. It comes with specific conditions tied to the original business plan. When you acquire a ready made company, you inherit its regulatory permissions, but you may need to update the business plan with FINMA if your activities differ. Consulting24 ensures that the license scope aligns with your intended operations before acquisition.

License Type and Regulator

The Swiss regulator is the Swiss Financial Market Supervisory Authority (FINMA). Depending on the activities, the license type can be a banking license (for deposit-taking), a securities dealer license, or a simpler FINMA AML authorization for custodial wallet providers and exchanges. Most crypto businesses apply for a FINMA AML license under the AMLA, which requires compliance with anti-money laundering regulations.

For more complex activities like operating a DLT trading facility, a license under the FMIA may be needed. The ready made license typically covers the most common crypto activities, but you should verify the scope with your advisor. FINMA is known for its principle-based regulation, which provides flexibility but also demands high standards of governance and risk management. As of 2026, FINMA continues to refine its approach to stablecoins and DeFi, so staying updated is essential.

Capital Requirement

Switzerland does not have a fixed minimum capital for a FINMA AML license, but FINMA expects the company to have sufficient financial resources for its business volume. For a banking license, the minimum capital is CHF 10 million (approx. EUR 10.5 million). For a securities dealer license, it is CHF 1.5 million (approx. EUR 1.6 million). For a simple AML license, no statutory minimum, but typically CHF 100,000 (approx. EUR 105,000) is considered adequate. The ready made license entity will already meet the capital requirements, which are part of the due diligence. Actual capital held may be higher depending on the company's history and business plan.

It is important to note that FINMA may require additional capital if the company's activities expand. For example, if you plan to handle large trading volumes, the regulator may expect a higher capital buffer. Consulting24 advises on capital planning to ensure ongoing compliance. In practice, most ready made AML license companies have a paid-up capital of CHF 100,000 to CHF 500,000. This capital is typically held in a Swiss bank account and cannot be used for operational expenses without FINMA approval.

Tax Treatment

Switzerland offers a favourable tax regime for crypto businesses. Corporate income tax rates vary by canton, typically between 12% and 21%. Crypto assets are treated as assets for tax purposes, and trading gains are subject to capital gains tax (exempt for private individuals but taxable for companies). VAT is not applicable on crypto transactions in most cases. The ready made company will be tax-resident in Switzerland, so you benefit from the Swiss tax treaties and low rates. Additionally, cantons like Zug and Zurich have specific incentives for blockchain companies, including potential tax holidays or reduced rates for qualifying activities.

For example, a company in Zug may pay an effective tax rate of around 11.5% after deductions. However, tax planning is complex and depends on the nature of your crypto activities. Mining income, staking rewards, and token sales are treated differently. Consulting24 can introduce you to Swiss tax advisors who specialise in crypto. We also recommend reviewing the requirements page for general compliance obligations. As a general rule, you should budget for annual tax advisory costs of EUR 5,000-15,000.

Alternatives and Comparison: Switzerland vs Panama and Others

Switzerland is a premium jurisdiction with high costs and strict regulation.
