What Is the Anjouan Crypto OTC Desk License?

The Anjouan crypto OTC desk license is a regulatory authorization issued by the Anjouan Financial Services Authority (AFSA) that permits a company to operate an over-the-counter trading desk for cryptocurrencies. OTC desks facilitate large trades directly between buyers and sellers, often with better pricing and privacy than exchange-based trading. The license covers the execution of crypto trades, custody of client assets, and related services.

Anjouan's framework is designed to be business-friendly: low setup costs, no capital requirement, and a straightforward application process. It is particularly attractive for startups and small to medium enterprises that want to enter the crypto market without the heavy compliance burden of EU MiCA or US regulations. License Type and Regulator

Regulator: Anjouan Financial Services Authority (AFSA)

The Anjouan Financial Services Authority (AFSA) is the regulatory body responsible for licensing and supervising financial services in Anjouan. AFSA issues the International Brokerage License, which covers OTC desk activities for crypto assets. The license is classified as a Class B license under the International Companies Act, tailored for forex, crypto, and derivatives brokerage.

AFSA's approach is principles-based, focusing on AML/CFT compliance rather than prescriptive capital rules. This makes it accessible for smaller firms. The regulator requires a physical office in Anjouan (which can be a virtual office service) and the appointment of a local compliance officer. Capital Requirement

Anjouan imposes no minimum capital requirement for the crypto OTC desk license. This is a significant advantage compared to EU jurisdictions where capital requirements range from EUR 50,000 to 150,000 under MiCA. The absence of a capital floor means you can allocate funds to business operations rather than locking them in a bank account.

However, AFSA expects the company to maintain sufficient financial resources to cover operational expenses and potential liabilities. As a best practice, we recommend having at least EUR 10,000 in working capital to demonstrate financial stability. Tax Treatment

Anjouan offers a zero corporate tax regime for offshore companies. There is no income tax, capital gains tax, or withholding tax on profits derived from international operations. This makes Anjouan a tax-neutral jurisdiction, ideal for crypto OTC desks that serve non-resident clients.

However, you must consider tax obligations in the countries where your clients are based. For example, if you provide services to EU residents, you may need to comply with VAT or other indirect taxes. Consulting24 recommends working with a tax advisor to ensure your structure remains compliant. We can introduce you to partner firms for tax planning. Note that even with zero tax in Anjouan, your management location (where key decisions are made) could create a taxable presence elsewhere. It is essential to maintain substance in Anjouan, such as a local director and office, to mitigate this risk.

Allowed Activities Under the License

The Anjouan crypto OTC desk license permits the following activities:

  • Execution of over-the-counter trades in cryptocurrencies (e.g., Bitcoin, Ethereum, stablecoins).
  • Provision of custodial services for client crypto assets.
  • Price quoting and market making for crypto pairs.
  • Facilitation of block trades and large liquidity transactions.
  • Brokerage services for institutional clients.

Activities not permitted include operating a retail-facing exchange, issuing tokens, or providing investment advice without an additional license. The license is specifically designed for wholesale, B2B operations. If you need to serve retail clients, consider a crypto exchange license in a different jurisdiction. Compliance and Trust Considerations

While Anjouan offers a light regulatory touch, you must still implement strong AML/KYC procedures. AFSA expects licensees to:

  • Conduct customer due diligence (CDD) on all clients.
  • Monitor transactions for suspicious activity.
  • Maintain records for at least 5 years.
  • Appoint a local compliance officer (can be outsourced).

Failure to comply can result in license revocation. Consulting24 provides templates for AML policies and ongoing compliance support. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Incomplete documentation: Submitting a generic business plan or AML policy leads to delays. Tailor documents to your operations.
  • Ignoring banking: Assuming you can easily open a bank account. Start the banking process early.
  • Overlooking tax residence: Even with zero tax in Anjouan, your management location may trigger tax liability elsewhere.
  • Mixing client funds: Segregate client assets from operational funds to avoid regulatory issues.
  • Not renewing on time: Late renewal can result in penalties or license cancellation. Set reminders well in advance.
  • Choosing the wrong license type: Ensure the OTC desk license covers your intended activities. If you plan to offer exchange services, you need a different license.
