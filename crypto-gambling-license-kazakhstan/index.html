What Is a Crypto Gambling License in Kazakhstan?

A crypto gambling license in Kazakhstan authorizes operators to offer gambling services using cryptocurrencies as a payment method. The license is issued under the Law on Gambling Business and related regulations, which have been updated to include digital assets. The primary regulator is the Ministry of Culture and Sports, which oversees gambling activities, while the Ministry of Digital Development, Innovation and Aerospace Industry handles crypto-related aspects.

The license permits online casinos, sports betting, and other gambling formats where bets and payouts can be made in crypto. Operators must comply with anti-money laundering (AML) and know-your-customer (KYC) requirements, similar to traditional gambling licenses. As of 2026, Kazakhstan has a clear legal framework that distinguishes crypto gambling from general crypto trading, providing a dedicated licensing track. The license is valid for 5 years and renewable, with ongoing compliance obligations including regular reporting and audits.

Kazakhstan's approach is unique in that it explicitly permits crypto for gambling, unlike many jurisdictions that treat crypto gambling under general gambling laws. This clarity reduces legal uncertainty for operators. However, the framework is still evolving, and operators should stay updated on regulatory changes. License Type and Regulator

The crypto gambling license in Kazakhstan is a specific category under the general gambling license. The main regulator is the Ministry of Culture and Sports, which issues licenses for gambling activities. For crypto-related aspects, the Ministry of Digital Development, Innovation and Aerospace Industry provides oversight on digital asset usage. The license type is typically an online gambling license that explicitly permits cryptocurrency transactions.

There is no separate 'crypto gambling license' per se; instead, the standard gambling license is amended to include crypto provisions. Operators must also register with the Financial Monitoring Agency for AML compliance. The license is valid for a fixed term, usually 5 years, and is renewable. The regulatory framework is based on the Law on Gambling Business (2019) and subsequent amendments that integrated digital assets.

Capital Requirement

Kazakhstan imposes a minimum capital requirement for gambling operators. For crypto gambling, the requirement is aligned with the general online gambling license. As of 2026, the minimum share capital is KZT 100,000,000 (approximately EUR 190,000). This amount must be fully paid up before license issuance. The capital can be held in fiat or cryptocurrency, subject to valuation rules set by the regulator.

Additionally, operators may need to maintain a reserve fund or bank guarantee, typically 10% of the capital, to cover player liabilities. These requirements ensure financial stability and player protection. The capital must be deposited in a Kazakh bank account and cannot be withdrawn without regulatory approval. This is a significant barrier for smaller operators, but it demonstrates financial solidity to players and partners.

In contrast, Panama has no minimum capital requirement for gaming licenses, making it more accessible for startups. However, Kazakhstan's higher capital can be a trust signal in the market.

Tax Treatment

Gambling operators in Kazakhstan are subject to a specific tax regime. The tax rate for online gambling is 10% of gross gaming revenue (GGR). For crypto gambling, the same rate applies, but the GGR is calculated based on the fiat equivalent of crypto bets at the time of transaction. Operators must file tax returns quarterly.

There is no VAT on gambling services. Corporate income tax applies at the standard rate of 20%, but gambling income is often treated separately. Withholding tax on dividends is 15% for non-residents. Tax incentives may be available for operators in special economic zones, such as the Astana International Financial Centre (AIFC), which offers a 0% corporate tax rate for 10 years for certain activities. However, gambling is not always eligible, so specific advice is needed.

Allowed Activities

The crypto gambling license permits the following activities:

  • Online casino games (slots, table games, live dealer) using crypto.
  • Sports betting (pre-match and live) with crypto deposits.
  • Poker and other card games.
  • Lotteries and bingo.
  • Fantasy sports.
  • Providing gambling software to licensed operators (B2B license).

Operators must not offer games that are prohibited under Kazakh law, such as certain types of high-risk derivatives. All games must be certified for fairness by an accredited testing lab, such as iTech Labs or GLI. The license does not cover physical casinos or betting shops unless specifically applied for. Crypto mining or exchange services are not permitted under this license; a separate crypto license is needed for those activities.

Operators can also offer fiat gambling alongside crypto, but must clearly segregate accounts and report fiat and crypto transactions separately. This flexibility allows for hybrid models that cater to both traditional and crypto users.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Applying for a crypto gambling license in Kazakhstan can be complex. Avoid these common pitfalls:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing or incorrect documents delay the process. Ensure all forms are notarised and translated into Kazakh or Russian. Use a professional translation service.
  • Underestimating capital requirements: The minimum capital of EUR 190,000 must be fully paid and proven. Some applicants fail to provide bank statements or proof of source of funds.
  • Ignoring AML obligations: A weak AML policy can lead to rejection. Use a local compliance expert to draft policies that meet Kazakh requirements.
  • Choosing the wrong legal structure: An LLP is standard, but other forms (e.g., JSC) may have tax implications. Consult a local lawyer.
  • Not planning for banking: Start the bank account application early, as it can take months. Some operators try to use personal accounts, which is not allowed.
  • Overlooking software certification: Games must be certified by an accredited lab. Using uncertified software can result in license suspension.

What is the minimum capital for a crypto gambling license in Kazakhstan?

The minimum share capital is KZT 100,000,000 (approximately EUR 190,000). This must be fully paid up before license issuance and can be held in fiat or crypto, subject to valuation rules. A reserve fund of 10% may also be required.
