What Is a Crypto NFT Marketplace License in Belize?

A crypto NFT marketplace license in Belize is a regulatory authorization issued by the IFSC that allows a company to operate a platform for trading, listing, and facilitating transactions of non-fungible tokens (NFTs) and other digital assets. The license falls under the IFSC's framework for digital asset businesses, which covers exchanges, wallet providers, and marketplace operators.

Belize offers a cost-effective and efficient licensing regime compared to many EU jurisdictions, with no requirement for a physical office presence locally. The license enables you to offer services to clients globally, subject to compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) regulations. It is important to note that the Belize regime is not a full MiCA-equivalent but is recognized for its pragmatic approach to digital assets.

For NFT marketplaces specifically, the license covers the operation of a platform where users can mint, buy, sell, and trade NFTs. The IFSC assesses the business model, source of funds, and fit-and-proper status of directors and shareholders. As of 2026, the licensing process typically takes 3 to 6 months from application to approval.

Who Needs a Belize Crypto License for an NFT Marketplace?

Any business that intends to operate a platform for NFT trading or digital asset exchange and targets clients from outside Belize (or within Belize, if local regulations apply) should consider a Belize crypto license. Typical applicants include:

  • Founders launching a new NFT marketplace for art, gaming, or collectibles.
  • Existing crypto exchanges expanding into NFT services.
  • White-label marketplace providers seeking a regulated entity.
  • Investment funds or DAOs that operate NFT trading platforms.

If your marketplace will handle customer funds, custody of NFTs, or facilitate peer-to-peer transactions, the Belize license is appropriate. For businesses that only provide informational services or non-custodial wallets, a simpler registration may suffice, but legal advice is recommended.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator for crypto and NFT marketplace licenses in Belize is the International Financial Services Commission (IFSC). The IFSC issues a Class I or Class II license depending on the scope of activities. For an NFT marketplace that holds client assets or operates an order book, a Class II license is typically required. For purely peer-to-peer or non-custodial platforms, a Class I license may be sufficient.

The license is officially called a Digital Asset Business License. It covers the operation of a digital asset exchange, which includes NFT marketplaces. The IFSC requires the licensee to have a physical presence in Belize, such as a registered office and a local director or manager, though this can often be arranged through service providers.

Capital Requirement for Belize Crypto License

The minimum capital requirement for a digital asset business license in Belize is $50,000 USD for a Class I license and $100,000 USD for a Class II license. This capital must be demonstrated as paid-up and available for the business operations. It can be held in a bank account in Belize or another jurisdiction approved by the IFSC.

For an NFT marketplace, the capital requirement is typically $100,000 USD if the platform holds customer assets. If the marketplace is purely non-custodial and does not handle client funds, the Class I threshold of $50,000 USD may apply. Tax Treatment in Belize for Crypto NFT Marketplaces

Belize offers a favorable tax regime for international business companies (IBCs) that hold a digital asset license. Key tax features include:

  • No corporate income tax on profits derived from offshore activities.
  • No capital gains tax on the sale of digital assets or NFTs.
  • No withholding tax on dividends or interest paid to non-residents.
  • No VAT or sales tax on digital services provided to non-residents.

However, if your marketplace serves clients within Belize, local taxes may apply. Most licensees operate on a pure offshore basis, so the tax burden is minimal. It is essential to maintain proper accounting and file annual returns with the IFSC.

Allowed Activities Under a Belize Crypto License

A Belize digital asset business license for an NFT marketplace permits the following activities:

  • Operating a platform for the listing and trading of NFTs.
  • Minting and issuing NFTs on behalf of creators.
  • Providing custodial wallet services for NFTs and digital assets.
  • Facilitating peer-to-peer transactions between buyers and sellers.
  • Offering staking or yield services related to NFTs (if applicable).

The license does not automatically cover fiat currency exchange or payment processing; separate authorization may be needed for those activities. Additionally, the IFSC prohibits activities that involve securities or derivatives without further licensing.

Compliance and Trust: Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, you must maintain compliance with Belize AML/CTF regulations. This includes:

  • Appointing a compliance officer (can be outsourced).
  • Implementing KYC/CDD procedures for all customers.
  • Reporting suspicious transactions to the Financial Intelligence Unit.
  • Submitting annual audited financial statements to the IFSC.
  • Paying annual license renewal fees (typically $5,000 - $10,000 USD).

Failure to comply can result in fines or license revocation. This is general guidance, not legal advice. Always consult with a local attorney for specific obligations.

What is the minimum capital for a Belize crypto NFT marketplace license?

Does Belize tax offshore crypto income?

No. Belize does not impose corporate income tax, capital gains tax, or withholding tax on profits derived from offshore activities. This makes it attractive for NFT marketplaces serving international clients.

Can I operate an NFT marketplace with a Belize license?

Yes. The Belize digital asset business license covers the operation of a platform for trading, listing, and facilitating NFT transactions. You can also offer custodial wallet services and minting.

Yes, you need a registered office and a local agent in Belize. These can be arranged through service providers. You do not need a physical office with employees.
