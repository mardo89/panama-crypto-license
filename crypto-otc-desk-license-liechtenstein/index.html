What Is a Crypto OTC Desk License in Liechtenstein?

A crypto OTC desk license in Liechtenstein is an authorization granted by the Financial Market Authority (FMA) under the Token and Trusted Technology Service Providers Act (TVTG). It allows a company to facilitate large-volume, off-exchange trades of cryptocurrencies between institutional or high-net-worth clients. OTC desks typically offer better liquidity, lower slippage, and privacy compared to public order books. For example, a hedge fund wanting to buy 1,000 BTC without moving the market would use an OTC desk.

The license classifies the OTC desk as a “Token Service Provider” and specifically covers the service of “exchange of tokens for fiat currency or other tokens”. As of 2026, the license is fully MiCA-compliant, meaning it qualifies as a CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) with passporting rights. The FMA regulates both the licensing and ongoing supervision, ensuring compliance with AML/CFT rules, capital adequacy, and client asset segregation.

Key activities permitted include: executing trades on behalf of clients, providing price quotes, and settling transactions. The license is ideal for firms that handle large trade sizes and need a reputable, EU-regulated environment. Unlike a full exchange license, an OTC desk does not operate a public order book, so the regulatory burden is lighter. For instance, the FMA does not require the same level of market surveillance as for a trading platform.

Who Needs a Crypto OTC Desk License?

Any business that operates an over-the-counter trading desk for crypto assets in Liechtenstein, or from Liechtenstein to clients in the EEA, must hold this license. Typical applicants include:

  • Institutional trading firms offering block trades to hedge funds, asset managers, or family offices.
  • Brokerages that match large buyers and sellers off-exchange.
  • Liquidity providers that quote two-way prices for major cryptocurrencies.
  • Companies that offer “best execution” services for high-volume clients.

If you are a startup or established firm looking to serve professional clients in Europe, the Liechtenstein license provides a clear regulatory framework and credibility. Even if you operate outside the EEA, having a licensed entity in Liechtenstein can open doors to European institutional capital. For example, a Swiss crypto prime broker might use a Liechtenstein license to serve EU clients without needing separate registrations in each member state. Additionally, firms that already have a license in another jurisdiction, such as Estonia, may consider Liechtenstein for its stronger reputation and passporting rights under MiCA.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Financial Market Authority (FMA) Liechtenstein. The license type is a “Token Service Provider” (TSP) license under the TVTG, which is now fully integrated with MiCA. Under MiCA, the service of “exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency or other crypto assets” is a CASP activity, requiring a minimum capital of EUR 50,000 for OTC desk services (since it does not involve custody of client assets).

The FMA is known for its thorough but efficient review process. The license is valid indefinitely, subject to ongoing compliance. As of 2026, Liechtenstein has not introduced a separate “OTC desk license” - it is simply a TSP license with the relevant service authorization. The FMA maintains a public register of all licensed entities, which enhances transparency and trust. For comparison, other EU regulators like the Bank of Lithuania (for Lithuania) also follow MiCA but may have different application timelines.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for a CASP that provides exchange services without custody is EUR 50,000. This applies to OTC desk operations where the firm does not hold client funds or crypto assets overnight. If the OTC desk also offers custody (e.g., holding client assets during settlement), the requirement rises to EUR 125,000. For more complex services like operating a trading platform, it is EUR 150,000.

Liechtenstein has adopted these tiers exactly. Most OTC desks will fall under the EUR 50,000 tier. The capital must be fully paid up and maintained as own funds. It can be in cash or highly liquid assets. The FMA may require additional capital based on the firm's risk profile, such as operational risk or concentration risk. For example, a desk handling very large trade sizes (e.g., >100 BTC per trade) may need to hold extra capital. This is general guidance, not legal advice. Tax Treatment

Liechtenstein offers a favorable tax regime for crypto businesses. The corporate income tax rate is a flat 12.5%, one of the lowest in Europe. Importantly, crypto-to-crypto trades are generally exempt from VAT (0% VAT), as per EU VAT rules. There is no capital gains tax for companies on crypto trading profits (profits are taxed as ordinary income at 12.5%).

Stamp duty may apply on certain securities transactions, but not on typical crypto OTC trades. The country has no net worth tax. For international firms, Liechtenstein has a wide network of double tax treaties. As always, tax advice should be sought from a qualified professional. This favorable treatment makes Liechtenstein attractive compared to jurisdictions like Lithuania, where corporate tax is 15%, or Poland, where the rate is 19% (see Poland crypto license).

Allowed Activities

Under the TVTG and MiCA, a crypto OTC desk license in Liechtenstein permits the following activities:

  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency (e.g., BTC to EUR).
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets (e.g., BTC to ETH).
  • Providing quotes and executing trades on behalf of clients.
  • Matching large orders off-exchange.
  • Offering settlement services (if not holding client assets).

Activities not allowed without additional authorization: operating a crypto exchange platform (order book matching), custody of client crypto assets, and providing staking or lending services. Each requires a separate license extension. For example, if you want to also offer custody, you would need to apply for a custody license under MiCA, which has a higher capital requirement of EUR 125,000. The FMA may also allow ancillary services like advisory, but only if explicitly included in the license scope.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Company formation: Incorporate a Liechtenstein entity (usually an Aktiengesellschaft or GmbH) with a registered office in the country.
  2. AML program: Develop a comprehensive AML/CFT policy, including KYC procedures, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting.
  3. Appoint a compliance officer: The officer must be resident in Liechtenstein or the EEA.
  4. Prepare application: Compile business plan, financial projections, organizational structure, and proof of capital.
  5. Submit to FMA: File the application with the required fee. The FMA may request additional information.
  6. Review and interview: The FMA will review the application and may conduct an interview with management.
  7. License issuance: Once approved, the company is added to the FMA's register. Ongoing reporting begins.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, the firm must comply with ongoing AML/CFT obligations, including transaction monitoring, regular reporting to the FMA, and annual audits. The FMA requires submission of annual financial statements and compliance reports. Capital must be maintained at all times.

Additionally, the firm must have a local compliance officer and a registered office in Liechtenstein. The FMA may conduct on-site inspections. Failure to comply can result in fines or license revocation. This is general guidance, not legal advice. Common Mistakes to Avoid

Applying for a crypto OTC desk license in Liechtenstein involves several pitfalls that can delay or derail the process. Common mistakes include:

  • Incomplete AML documentation: The FMA requires a detailed AML policy tailored to OTC operations, not a generic template. For example, you must describe how you handle large trades and verify counterparties.
  • Underestimating capital requirements: Some applicants assume EUR 50,000 is always enough, but if you plan to hold client assets temporarily, you may need EUR 125,000. Consult with us to confirm your tier.
  • Ignoring banking needs: Waiting until after license approval to open a bank account can cause delays. Start banking conversations early.
  • Poor choice of compliance officer: The officer must have relevant experience and be resident in the EEA. Hiring someone without crypto compliance experience can lead to rejection.
  • Not planning for ongoing costs: Annual compliance costs can be EUR 5,000-10,000, plus audit fees. Budget accordingly.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto OTC desk license in Liechtenstein?

Under MiCA, the minimum capital is EUR 50,000 if the OTC desk does not hold client assets (no custody). If you also offer custody, the requirement rises to EUR 125,000. The capital must be fully paid up and maintained as own funds.

How long does it take to get a Liechtenstein crypto OTC desk license?

The FMA typically processes applications in 3-6 months. Including company formation and AML setup, total time is 4-8 months. Pre-application preparation takes 1-2 months. Exact timelines depend on the completeness of your application.

Can I serve clients in other EU countries with a Liechtenstein license?

Yes, under MiCA, a Liechtenstein CASP license grants passporting rights across the EEA. You can serve clients in any EU/EEA member state without needing separate registrations, subject to notification to the host regulator.

What is the corporate tax rate in Liechtenstein for crypto OTC desks?

The corporate income tax rate is a flat 12.5%. Crypto-to-crypto trades are exempt from VAT (0% VAT). There is no capital gains tax for companies; profits are taxed as ordinary income.

Do I need a local compliance officer?

Yes, you must appoint a compliance officer who is resident in Liechtenstein or the EEA. The officer should have relevant experience in AML/CFT and crypto operations.

What activities are allowed under a Liechtenstein OTC desk license?

You can exchange crypto for fiat or other crypto on behalf of clients, provide quotes, and execute off-exchange trades. You cannot operate a public order book or offer custody without additional authorization.

Is it difficult to open a bank account for a Liechtenstein crypto OTC desk?

It is generally straightforward if you are licensed. Local banks like LLB and VP Bank accept licensed firms, but may require a minimum deposit (e.g., CHF 50,000). Start the process early to avoid delays.

How does Liechtenstein compare to Panama for a crypto OTC desk?

Panama is cheaper (flat EUR 6,000) and faster (1-2 months), but offers no EU passporting. Liechtenstein costs EUR 10,500-20,000 upfront and takes 4-8 months, but provides access to the EEA market. Choose based on your target clients.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations after obtaining the license?

You must submit annual financial statements and compliance reports to the FMA, maintain AML/CFT procedures, and undergo periodic audits. The FMA may conduct on-site inspections. Non-compliance can lead to fines or revocation.
