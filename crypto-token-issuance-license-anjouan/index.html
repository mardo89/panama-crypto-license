What Is the Crypto Token Issuance License in Anjouan?

The crypto token issuance license in Anjouan is a regulatory authorization granted by AFSA that permits a company to issue digital tokens to investors. It is not a full crypto exchange license but focuses on the initial creation and sale of tokens. The license covers both utility and security tokens, provided the issuer complies with disclosure and AML requirements.

Key features include:

  • Issuance of tokens via public or private sales
  • Ability to list tokens on partner exchanges (subject to exchange policies)
  • Exemption from local capital gains tax on token sales
  • No requirement for a physical office in Anjouan

This license is particularly attractive for projects that want to avoid the lengthy and costly processes of EU or US jurisdictions while maintaining a credible regulatory standing. For example, a DeFi project launching a governance token can use this license to legally sell tokens to global investors without the overhead of MiCA compliance.

License Type & Regulator

The license is issued by the Anjouan Financial Services Authority (AFSA), the primary regulator for financial services in the autonomous island of Anjouan. AFSA oversees company registration, licensing, and ongoing compliance for offshore entities.

The license type is a Class B (Crypto Token Issuance) License, which specifically authorizes token issuance activities. It does not cover exchange operations, custody, or trading services. For those activities, a separate license (e.g., Class A exchange license) would be required.

AFSA requires licensees to appoint a local registered agent (typically the licensing firm) and maintain a minimum level of compliance, including AML policies and annual reporting. The regulator conducts due diligence on directors and beneficial owners, including background checks and source of funds verification.

Capital Requirement

Anjouan does not impose a statutory minimum capital requirement for token issuance license applicants. However, the regulator expects the company to demonstrate sufficient financial resources to cover operational expenses and potential liabilities. In practice, a paid-up capital of EUR 10,000 to EUR 50,000 is recommended to show substance and to satisfy due diligence from banks or payment partners.

This is significantly lower than EU jurisdictions under MiCA, where capital requirements range from EUR 50,000 to EUR 150,000 depending on service class. Tax Treatment

Anjouan offers a highly favorable tax regime for offshore license holders:

  • Corporate income tax: 0% on income derived from token issuance and related activities
  • Capital gains tax: 0% on gains from token sales
  • Withholding tax: 0% on dividends and interest paid to non-residents
  • VAT: Not applicable for offshore entities
  • Personal income tax: No tax on crypto gains for individuals (though personal tax residency rules apply)

This tax-neutral environment makes Anjouan a competitive alternative to jurisdictions like Panama (which also offers 0% offshore tax) or the Cayman Islands. However, licensees must ensure they comply with tax reporting in their home country. For example, US citizens must still report global income to the IRS, even if no tax is due in Anjouan.

Allowed Activities

The crypto token issuance license Anjouan permits the following activities:

  • Issuance of utility tokens, security tokens, and governance tokens
  • Conducting token generation events (TGEs) and initial DEX offerings (IDOs)
  • Marketing and promotion of token sales to accredited and retail investors (subject to compliance)
  • Listing tokens on third-party exchanges (exchange approval required)
  • Staking and yield farming programs (if disclosed in whitepaper)

Activities explicitly not allowed include operating a crypto exchange, providing custody services, or engaging in proprietary trading. For those, a separate license is needed. Compliance & Trust Considerations

While Anjouan offers a light-touch regulatory environment, license holders must still adhere to international AML standards. AFSA requires:

  • Appointment of a compliance officer (can be outsourced)
  • Implementation of AML/KYC procedures for token purchasers
  • Annual filing of audited financial statements
  • Maintenance of records for at least 5 years

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Projects often stumble on these points:

  • Inadequate whitepaper: AFSA expects a clear description of the token's utility, rights, and risks. Vague or misleading whitepapers can delay approval.
  • Ignoring AML requirements: Even in a low-tax jurisdiction, you must screen investors. Skipping KYC can lead to regulatory issues.
  • Choosing the wrong license: Some projects apply for a token issuance license but later need exchange or custody services. Plan ahead.
  • Underestimating banking: Opening a bank account can take longer than the license itself. Start early.

Frequently asked questions

What is the cost of a crypto token issuance license in Anjouan?

The total upfront cost is EUR 6,000 flat, covering company incorporation, license application fee, and first-year registered agent services. Annual renewal is approximately EUR 2,500. There are no hidden fees.

How long does it take to get the license?

The typical timeline is 4 to 6 weeks from submission of complete documents. This includes company incorporation, AFSA due diligence, and license issuance. Expedited processing may be available for an additional fee.

Is there a minimum capital requirement?

No statutory minimum capital is required. However, a paid-up capital of EUR 10,000 to EUR 50,000 is recommended to demonstrate financial substance and facilitate banking relationships.

What taxes apply to Anjouan license holders?

Offshore license holders enjoy 0% corporate income tax, 0% capital gains tax, 0% withholding tax, and no VAT on token issuance activities. Personal income tax on crypto gains is also 0% for individuals, subject to home country tax laws.

Can I issue security tokens under this license?

Yes, the license covers both utility and security tokens, provided you comply with disclosure requirements and AML/KYC procedures. You must clearly describe the token's rights and risks in the whitepaper.

Do I need a physical office in Anjouan?

No, there is no requirement for a physical office. A local registered agent is sufficient. This makes Anjouan a cost-effective option for remote teams.

Can I use the license to operate a crypto exchange?

No, the token issuance license does not permit exchange operations, custody, or trading. For those activities, you would need a separate Class A exchange license or consider jurisdictions like Panama.

What documents are required for the application?

You need KYC documents (passports, proof of address for directors and shareholders), a business plan, token whitepaper, AML policy, financial projections, and a completed application form. Consulting24 provides templates for the AML policy.

Is the Anjouan license recognized internationally?

While Anjouan is not an EU member, its license is accepted by many crypto-friendly banks, payment processors, and exchanges. It provides a credible regulatory framework for token issuance, but you should verify acceptance with your partners.

Can I apply if my project is already live?

Yes, existing projects can apply for a license to retroactively comply. However, you must disclose prior token sales and demonstrate that they were conducted in compliance with applicable laws. Consulting24 can advise on the best approach.
