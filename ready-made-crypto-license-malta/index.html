What Is a Ready Made Malta Crypto License?

A ready made Malta crypto license refers to a company that has already been incorporated and has obtained (or is in the advanced stages of obtaining) a Class 3 VFA license from the MFSA. The company is typically a shelf company with no prior trading activity, and the license is either already issued or the application is far along, allowing a new owner to take over and start operations much faster than starting from scratch.

This is distinct from a 'shell' license; the company must still meet all ongoing compliance requirements. The buyer acquires the entire entity, including its license, and then updates the beneficial ownership and management with MFSA approval. The key advantage is time: a fresh application can take 12-18 months, whereas a ready made license can be transferred in 3-6 months. However, the buyer must pass the same fit and proper tests as a new applicant. The MFSA will scrutinise the new owners, directors, and MLRO. The ready made entity should have a clean compliance record, with no prior fines or enforcement actions. Consulting24 can help you verify this through a thorough due diligence process.

License Type & Regulator

The relevant license is a Class 3 VFA License under the Virtual Financial Assets Act (Chapter 590 of the Laws of Malta). The regulator is the Malta Financial Services Authority (MFSA). As of 2026, Malta has transposed MiCA into national law, so the license also qualifies as a CASP (Crypto-Asset Service Provider) registration for passporting.

The Class 3 license is the most comprehensive, covering all VFA services including operating a trading platform, execution of orders, and portfolio management. Lower classes (Class 1 and 2) exist but are less common for ready made offerings. Class 1 covers only advisory services, while Class 2 covers custody and transfer services. Class 3 is the only one that allows exchange operations. The MFSA is known for rigorous oversight, so expect thorough checks on your business plan, AML policies, and key personnel.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for a CASP depends on the services offered. For a full-scope Class 3 VFA license (covering all services), the capital requirement is EUR 150,000. This is the highest tier under MiCA (Article 67). If the license only covers certain services like custody or exchange without dealing on own account, the requirement may be EUR 125,000 or EUR 50,000. The ready made entity should already have this capital in place or you must inject it post-acquisition. The capital must be held in liquid assets and cannot be used for operational expenses. It serves as a buffer against insolvency. Consulting24 can advise on structuring the capital injection to meet MFSA requirements.

Tax Treatment

Malta offers a highly competitive corporate tax system. The standard corporate tax rate is 35%, but through a full imputation system and refunds, the effective tax rate can be as low as 5% for trading companies. Crypto transactions are treated as ordinary business income. No VAT applies to crypto-to-crypto trades, but fiat conversions may be subject to VAT. Capital gains from crypto are generally taxable, but specific exemptions may apply for long-term holdings. Malta also has a participation exemption for dividends from subsidiaries. Consulting24 recommends a local tax advisor for structuring. The tax benefits make Malta attractive for holding companies and trading firms.

Allowed Activities Under a Malta Crypto License

  • Operating a virtual asset exchange (order book or automated matching).
  • Custodial and non-custodial wallet services.
  • Execution of orders on behalf of clients.
  • Portfolio management of virtual assets.
  • Transfer services (sending/receiving crypto on behalf of clients).
  • Advising on virtual assets (if ancillary).
  • Underwriting and placing of virtual assets (STOs).

Note: The license does not cover derivatives or securities that fall under MiFID II. Those require a separate investment firm license. Also, activities like mining or node operation do not require a license. The MFSA may impose conditions on the license, such as restricting certain services until you demonstrate compliance. Always check the exact scope of the ready made license.

Step-by-Step Process to Acquire a Ready Made License

  1. Engage Consulting24 for advisory and coordination. We assess your needs and identify suitable ready made entities from our network of licensed partners.
  2. Due diligence on the target company: review its MFSA license status, compliance history, financials, and shareholder structure. Check for any pending investigations or tax liabilities.
  3. Negotiation and agreement: sign a share purchase agreement (SPA) with the seller, subject to MFSA approval. Include warranties and indemnities for regulatory risks.
  4. Application to MFSA for change of control. This requires submission of personal questionnaires, business plan, proof of funds, and AML policies. The new beneficial owners and directors must pass fit and proper tests.
  5. MFSA review (2-4 months). The regulator may request additional information or interviews. You may need to appoint a local MLRO and provide evidence of substance.
  6. Closing: once approved, shares are transferred, and you assume control. Update AML policies and appoint a local MLRO if needed. Notify the MFSA of any changes.
  7. Ongoing compliance: monthly reporting, annual audit, and AML obligations. Maintain a physical office in Malta and ensure adequate record-keeping.

For fiat on-ramps, you may need a payment institution license or partner with a third-party processor. Some EMIs offer dedicated crypto-friendly accounts with IBANs. Compliance & Trust: What You Must Maintain

Owning a ready made license does not exempt you from ongoing compliance. You must appoint a local MLRO, maintain a physical office in Malta (or a registered address with substance), conduct regular AML training, and submit annual audited financials. The MFSA conducts periodic inspections. Failure to comply can result in fines or license revocation. This is general guidance, not legal advice. Always engage a local compliance officer. You must also file suspicious transaction reports (STRs) and maintain transaction monitoring systems. The MFSA expects a risk-based approach. Consulting24 can help you set up compliance procedures and liaise with the regulator.

Common Mistakes When Buying a Ready Made License

  • Skipping due diligence: The entity may have hidden liabilities or past regulatory issues. Always request a compliance audit.
  • Underestimating ongoing costs: Annual compliance can be EUR 30,000+ including MLRO fees, audit, and legal retainer.
  • Ignoring substance requirements: You need a local director and office. The MFSA may require a minimum of two directors.
  • Assuming automatic passporting: You must notify MFSA of cross-border activities and comply with host state rules.
  • Not updating AML policies: The existing policies may not fit your business model. Tailor them to your specific services.
  • Overlooking capital maintenance: The capital must be maintained at all times; using it for operations can lead to breach.

Alternatives: Malta vs Panama vs Lithuania vs Estonia

Malta offers EU passporting and a strong reputation but higher costs and capital requirements. Panama is a flat EUR 6,000 for a license, no minimum capital, and no tax on offshore income, but it does not provide EU market access. Lithuania is a lower-cost EU option (around EUR 2,500 setup, EUR 125,000 capital for full CASP) but has a more bureaucratic process and longer timelines. Estonia is another EU alternative with a simpler VASP register (capital EUR 100,000+), but its regulator has tightened scrutiny. For a full comparison, see our jurisdictions page. Each jurisdiction has trade-offs; the best choice depends on your target market, budget, and timeline.

Frequently asked questions

What is a ready made Malta crypto license?

It is a pre-incorporated company that already holds (or is close to holding) a Class 3 VFA license from the MFSA. You acquire the entire entity, including the license, allowing you to start operations much faster than a new application. The company is typically a shelf company with no prior trading activity.

How long does it take to transfer a ready made license?

Typically 3 to 6 months, depending on the complexity of the change of control and MFSA review speed. This is significantly faster than a fresh application which can take 12-18 months. The timeline also depends on how quickly you can provide the required documentation.

What is the minimum capital for a Malta crypto license?

Under MiCA, the capital requirement for a full-scope CASP is EUR 150,000. For limited services, it may be EUR 125,000 or EUR 50,000. The ready made entity should already meet this. You must maintain the capital at all times.

Can I use a Malta license to serve clients in other EU countries?

Yes. Malta has transposed MiCA, so a Class 3 VFA license qualifies as a CASP registration. You can passport services across the EU/EEA by notifying the MFSA. You must comply with local marketing rules and may need to register with host state regulators.

What tax rate applies to crypto profits in Malta?

The standard corporate tax rate is 35%, but through refunds, the effective rate can be as low as 5% for trading companies. Crypto gains are treated as ordinary income. No VAT on crypto-to-crypto trades. Consult a local tax advisor for specifics.

Do I need a physical office in Malta?

Yes, the MFSA requires a registered office and substance in Malta. This can be a serviced office or co-working space. You also need a local director or MLRO. The office must be more than a mailbox.

Can I buy a ready made license if I am a non-EU resident?

Yes, non-EU residents can acquire a Malta license. However, the beneficial owners and directors must pass fit and proper tests. You may need to appoint a local director with crypto experience. The MFSA will scrutinise your background.

What are the ongoing compliance costs?

Annual compliance costs typically range from EUR 20,000 to 40,000. This includes MLRO fees, audit, AML software, and legal updates. The exact amount depends on the complexity of your operations and transaction volume.

Can I use a Malta license for DeFi operations?

Yes, but the MFSA expects the DeFi protocol to be operated by a regulated entity. You must have control over the smart contracts and comply with AML/KYC requirements. Some DeFi models may not fit the regulatory framework, so seek legal advice.
