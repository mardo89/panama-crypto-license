What Is the Crypto NFT Marketplace License in Indonesia?

The crypto NFT marketplace license in Indonesia is a regulatory approval from Bappebti that allows a platform to facilitate the trading of crypto assets, including NFTs that are classified as crypto assets. As of 2026, Indonesia does not have a separate license for NFT marketplaces; instead, platforms dealing with crypto assets must register as a Crypto Asset Physical Trader (PFAK) under Bappebti Regulation No. 8 of 2021. This registration is mandatory for any entity offering trading, exchange, or custody services for crypto assets. The license ensures compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) standards, as well as consumer protection measures. It is important to note that NFTs representing digital art or collectibles may fall under different regulations if they are not considered crypto assets, but most NFT marketplaces that accept cryptocurrencies for payment or trade NFTs on a blockchain will need this license.

Unlike some jurisdictions that have a dedicated NFT license, Indonesia treats NFTs as crypto assets when they are traded on a marketplace. This means that if your platform allows users to buy, sell, or exchange NFTs using cryptocurrencies, you fall under Bappebti's purview. The PFAK license is the same as for crypto exchanges, so you must comply with all capital, reporting, and security requirements. The license does not cover fiat-to-crypto exchange unless you also obtain a payment system license from Bank Indonesia. For platforms that only facilitate peer-to-peer NFT transfers without custody or trading, the requirement may be different, but legal advice is essential.

Who Needs This License?

Any business operating an online platform that facilitates the buying, selling, or exchange of crypto assets, including NFTs, must obtain the PFAK license from Bappebti. This includes:

  • NFT marketplaces that accept cryptocurrencies as payment
  • Platforms that allow users to trade NFTs for other crypto assets
  • Exchanges that list NFTs as tradable assets
  • Marketplaces that offer custodial services for NFTs
  • Secondary marketplaces where users resell NFTs for crypto

If your platform only deals with non-fungible tokens that are purely utility or non-financial (e.g., gaming items with no speculative value), you may not need this license, but legal advice is recommended. As of 2026, Bappebti has been actively enforcing registration, and unlicensed platforms face penalties including fines and shutdown orders. Even if your platform is based outside Indonesia, if you serve Indonesian users, you may be required to register. License Type and Regulator

The relevant license is the Crypto Asset Physical Trader (PFAK) registration, issued by the Commodity Futures Trading Regulatory Agency (Bappebti). Bappebti is the primary regulator for crypto asset trading in Indonesia, operating under the Ministry of Trade. The license is not a full 'crypto license' as seen in some jurisdictions but a registration that allows legal operation. As of 2026, there is no separate license for NFT marketplaces; the PFAK covers all crypto asset trading activities. The registration process involves meeting capital requirements, implementing AML/CTF procedures, and ensuring technological security. Bappebti also requires membership in the Indonesian Crypto Asset Exchange (ICX) for clearing and settlement.

Bappebti has been tightening regulations to align with global standards, including FATF recommendations. In 2025, they introduced new guidelines for NFT platforms, requiring additional disclosures for high-value NFTs. The regulator conducts periodic audits and can impose sanctions for non-compliance. Capital Requirement

As of 2026, Bappebti requires a minimum paid-up capital of IDR 1 trillion (approximately EUR 60,000) for crypto asset traders. This is a significant amount compared to other jurisdictions like Panama (EUR 6,000 fixed) or Lithuania (EUR 125,000 for CASP). The capital must be deposited in a bank account in Indonesia and maintained throughout the license period. This requirement ensures that the platform has sufficient financial resources to cover operational risks and customer liabilities. For startups, this can be a barrier, but joint ventures or partnerships with local entities can help meet the requirement. The capital must be in fiat currency (IDR) and cannot be in crypto. It must be shown as paid-up capital in the company's bank account before the license application is submitted. Bappebti may request proof of capital at any time. Failure to maintain the minimum capital can result in license suspension. Tax Treatment

Indonesia imposes a value-added tax (VAT) of 11% on crypto asset transactions, as well as a final income tax on gains from crypto trading. For NFT marketplaces, the tax treatment depends on the nature of the NFT. If the NFT is considered a crypto asset, the same tax rules apply. As of 2026, the government has not issued specific NFT tax guidelines, so general crypto tax rules are applied. Platforms are required to withhold taxes on transactions and report to the tax authority (DJP). Consulting24 recommends engaging a local tax advisor to ensure compliance. The tax regime in Indonesia is less favourable than in Panama, which has no capital gains tax on crypto, or Lithuania, which has a 15% corporate tax.

In addition to transaction taxes, NFT marketplaces must pay corporate income tax at a standard rate of 22% (reduced for small businesses). There are also withholding taxes on dividends, interest, and royalties. For platforms operating globally, Indonesia's tax treaties may reduce double taxation, but the compliance burden is high. Allowed Activities

With a PFAK license from Bappebti, a crypto NFT marketplace can engage in the following activities:

  • Facilitating the trading of crypto assets, including NFTs classified as crypto assets
  • Providing custody services for crypto assets
  • Operating an order book or matching engine for NFT trades
  • Accepting crypto deposits and withdrawals
  • Offering staking or lending services (if separately approved)
  • Listing and delisting NFTs based on internal policies

Activities that are not allowed include operating as a derivatives exchange without a separate license, offering unregistered securities, or facilitating transactions in assets deemed illegal by Bappebti. The license does not cover fiat-to-crypto exchanges unless the platform also obtains a payment system license from Bank Indonesia. Additionally, Bappebti prohibits trading of privacy coins and certain high-risk assets. NFT marketplaces must screen listed NFTs for compliance with local laws, including intellectual property rights and anti-fraud provisions.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain the crypto NFT marketplace license in Indonesia involves several stages:

  1. Company Incorporation: Establish a local entity (PT) in Indonesia with the required capital. This typically takes 1-2 months.
  2. Prepare Documentation: Draft AML/CTF policies, risk management framework, business plan, and technical specifications. Engage a local compliance officer.
  3. Engage Local Partners: Secure membership with the Indonesian Crypto Asset Exchange (ICX) and appoint a local compliance officer. ICX membership is mandatory and costs around EUR 5,000-10,000.
  4. Submit Application: File the PFAK registration with Bappebti, including all required documents and fees. The application is submitted online through Bappebti's system.
  5. Regulatory Review: Bappebti conducts a thorough review, which may take 4-8 months. They may request additional information or clarifications.
  6. Approval and Launch: Once approved, the platform can operate legally. Ongoing compliance includes regular reporting and audits.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make these errors when applying for the Indonesian crypto license:

  • Underestimating the capital requirement: IDR 1 trillion is mandatory, not optional. Some founders try to use crypto as capital, which is not accepted.
  • Neglecting local partnerships: Membership in ICX is required, and local compliance officers are essential. Attempting to go without them leads to rejection.
  • Incomplete AML documentation: Bappebti requires detailed policies; generic templates are rejected. The AML manual must be tailored to the Indonesian context.
  • Ignoring tax obligations: Failure to register for VAT and income tax can lead to penalties and license revocation.
  • Choosing the wrong jurisdiction: Some founders apply in Indonesia without considering faster, cheaper alternatives like Panama or Lithuania. Consulting24 can help you compare.
  • Overlooking language barriers: All documents must be in Bahasa Indonesia. Translations must be certified.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a separate license for an NFT marketplace in Indonesia?

No, as of 2026, Indonesia does not have a separate license for NFT marketplaces. If your platform trades crypto assets, including NFTs classified as crypto assets, you need a Crypto Asset Physical Trader (PFAK) license from Bappebti. If your NFTs are purely utility or non-financial, you may not need a license, but legal advice is recommended.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a crypto license in Indonesia?

Bappebti requires a minimum paid-up capital of IDR 1 trillion (approximately EUR 60,000) for crypto asset traders. This capital must be in fiat currency and deposited in an Indonesian bank account. It must be maintained throughout the license period.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Indonesia?

The application process typically takes 4-8 months from submission to approval. This includes company incorporation, document preparation, ICX membership, and Bappebti review. Delays can occur if additional information is requested.

Can a foreign company apply for an Indonesian crypto license?

Yes, but you must establish a local entity (PT) in Indonesia. Foreign ownership is allowed up to 100% for most sectors, but you need a local compliance officer and director. Consulting24 can help with company setup and local partnerships.

What are the tax implications for NFT marketplaces in Indonesia?

Indonesia imposes 11% VAT on crypto transactions and a final income tax on gains. NFT marketplaces must withhold taxes and report to the tax authority. Corporate income tax is 22% (reduced for small businesses). Tax treatment for NFTs is still evolving.

Is it mandatory to join the Indonesian Crypto Asset Exchange (ICX)?

Yes, Bappebti requires all licensed crypto asset traders to become members of ICX for clearing and settlement. ICX membership involves additional fees and compliance requirements. Consulting24 can assist with the membership process.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations after obtaining the license?

License holders must submit annual reports, undergo AML audits, maintain minimum capital, and comply with Bappebti inspections. They must also renew ICX membership annually. Failure to comply can result in fines or license revocation.

Can I operate an NFT marketplace in Indonesia without a license?

No, operating without a PFAK license is illegal and can lead to penalties, including fines and shutdown. Bappebti actively monitors unlicensed platforms. It is safer to obtain the license or choose a jurisdiction with lower barriers, such as Panama.

How does Indonesia compare to Panama for an NFT marketplace license?

Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 fee, no capital requirement, no capital gains tax, and a 2-3 month timeline. Indonesia has higher costs (EUR 10,000-30,000+), EUR 60,000 capital, and 4-8 months. For global NFT marketplaces, Panama is often more cost-effective.

What activities are not allowed under the Indonesian crypto license?

The license does not allow derivatives trading, offering unregistered securities, or facilitating transactions in illegal assets. Fiat-to-crypto exchange requires a separate payment system license from Bank Indonesia. Privacy coins and high-risk assets are also prohibited.
