What Is a Crypto OTC Desk License in the Philippines?

A crypto OTC desk license in the Philippines is not a standalone license but rather a registration as a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) with the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP). This registration authorises you to operate an over-the-counter (OTC) desk for buying and selling cryptocurrencies directly with clients, typically in large volumes. OTC desks are distinct from exchanges because they facilitate trades off-exchange, often with negotiated prices and higher privacy.

The BSP regulates VASPs under Circular No. 1108 (2021) and subsequent guidelines. As of 2026, the Philippines has a mature regulatory environment that requires all VASPs, including OTC desks, to register and comply with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) obligations. This makes the Philippines one of the most credible jurisdictions for crypto OTC operations in Southeast Asia.

Who Needs a Crypto OTC Desk License in the Philippines?

Any entity that facilitates the exchange of fiat currency for cryptocurrencies or vice versa, or the exchange of one cryptocurrency for another, on an OTC basis must register as a VASP with the BSP. This includes:

  • OTC desks serving institutional investors, family offices, or high-net-worth individuals.
  • Brokerages that match buyers and sellers off-exchange.
  • Liquidity providers that offer direct trades to clients.
  • Any business that holds client funds or crypto assets for settlement.

If you are a crypto exchange that also offers OTC services, you still need VASP registration. The BSP does not distinguish between exchange and OTC desk; all VASPs are treated under the same framework. However, the capital requirement may vary based on the volume of transactions (see capital section).

License Type and Regulator

The Philippines does not issue a specific 'OTC desk license'. Instead, all virtual asset service providers must register with the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP) as a VASP. The BSP is the central bank and primary financial regulator. Registration is a one-time process, but you must maintain compliance with ongoing reporting and AML obligations.

There are two main categories under BSP regulations:

  • VASP Registration - for entities that provide exchange, transfer, or safekeeping of virtual assets.
  • VASP with Expanded Services - if you also offer custody or other services, you may need additional approvals.

For OTC desks, standard VASP registration is sufficient. The BSP requires a minimum capital of PHP 50 million (approximately EUR 800,000 as of 2026) for VASPs, though this may be adjusted based on transaction volume. Consulting24 can confirm the exact capital requirement during a consultation.

Capital Requirement

The BSP requires VASPs to maintain a minimum capital of PHP 50 million (approximately EUR 800,000) for entities with high transaction volumes. For smaller OTC desks, the minimum may be lower, but as of 2026, the standard is PHP 50 million. This capital must be paid up before registration. Additionally, you must maintain a surety bond or deposit of PHP 100 million (approx. EUR 1.6 million) to cover potential liabilities.

Tax Treatment

The Philippines imposes a 12% value-added tax (VAT) on the sale of cryptocurrencies if the transaction is considered a sale of services. However, the Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) has not issued specific guidance on crypto OTC desks. Generally, income from OTC trading is subject to corporate income tax at 25% (or 20% for small enterprises).

Additionally, a 1% percentage tax on gross sales may apply. It is crucial to engage a local tax advisor to structure your operations efficiently. Allowed Activities

With a BSP VASP registration, you are allowed to:

  • Facilitate OTC trades of virtual assets for fiat currency (PHP or foreign currencies).
  • Exchange one virtual asset for another.
  • Provide custody services for virtual assets (if specifically approved).
  • Offer liquidity to clients through negotiated trades.

You are not allowed to operate a public exchange (order book matching) without additional approvals. OTC desks must only deal with pre-negotiated trades. Also, you cannot offer derivatives or margin trading without a separate license from the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC).

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a crypto OTC desk license in the Philippines involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step.

  1. Company Incorporation: Register a Philippine corporation with the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) with a minimum paid-up capital of PHP 5 million (for VASP purposes).
  2. Prepare Documentation: Draft AML/CTF policies, risk management framework, business plan, and organizational structure.
  3. Submit VASP Application: File the application with the BSP, including all required documents and the application fee.
  4. BSP Evaluation: The BSP reviews your application, conducts background checks on directors and shareholders, and may request additional information.
  5. In-Person Interview: Key officers may be required to attend an interview with the BSP.
  6. Approval and Registration: Once approved, you receive a Certificate of Registration as a VASP. You must then post the required surety bond.
  7. Ongoing Compliance: Submit quarterly reports, undergo annual audits, and comply with BSP circulars.

Can I operate an OTC desk without a license in the Philippines?

No. Operating a crypto OTC desk without BSP registration is illegal and can result in fines, closure, and criminal penalties. All VASPs must register with the BSP.

The BSP registration is a local license. However, it demonstrates compliance with international AML standards, which can facilitate partnerships with banks and counterparties globally.

No. The BSP requires a physical office in the Philippines with local staff. A virtual office is not acceptable.

In the Philippines, there is no separate exchange license. All VASPs, including OTC desks and exchanges, register under the same framework. However, OTC desks may have lower capital requirements if they do not operate a public order book.
