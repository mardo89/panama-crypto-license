What Is a Crypto OTC Desk License in Romania?

A crypto OTC desk license in Romania is an authorisation from the ASF that permits a company to offer over-the-counter trading of crypto assets. OTC desks handle large trades (often institutional) with negotiated prices, minimal slippage, and high privacy. The license is part of Romania's implementation of MiCA, which harmonises rules across the EU. It ensures your OTC desk meets anti-money laundering (AML), capital adequacy, and operational standards.

Key features of the Romanian OTC desk license include:

  • Legal recognition across the EU under the MiCA passport.
  • Supervision by the ASF, a reputable regulator.
  • Flexibility to offer additional services like custody or exchange if authorised.

Consulting24 advises on the exact scope of activities to include in your application. For example, if you plan to also offer custody, you need to meet a higher capital requirement. The license is not a one-size-fits-all; it is tailored to your specific service mix.

Who Needs This License?

Any entity that intends to operate a crypto OTC desk in Romania or serve Romanian clients needs this license. Typical applicants include:

  • Institutional trading firms offering block trades to hedge funds, family offices, or other financial institutions.
  • High-net-worth individuals providing personalised crypto trading services.
  • Exchanges that want to offer off-order-book large trades.
  • Market makers or liquidity providers that negotiate directly with clients.

If your business involves matching buyers and sellers of crypto assets outside a public order book, you likely require an OTC desk license. Consulting24 can help determine if your specific business model falls under this category. For instance, a firm that only executes small retail trades on a public exchange would not need this license, but a firm that arranges private block trades would.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator for crypto OTC desk licenses in Romania is the Financial Supervisory Authority (ASF). The license is a 'Crypto Asset Service Provider' (CASP) authorisation under MiCA. Romania does not have a separate 'OTC desk license' - instead, OTC trading is classified under 'execution of orders on behalf of clients' and 'dealing on own account' services. You apply for a CASP license covering these activities.

The ASF is the same body that regulates traditional financial markets, securities, and insurance. Its involvement ensures a high standard of compliance and investor protection. Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital for a CASP license in Romania depends on the services offered. For OTC desk operations (execution of orders and dealing on own account), the required capital is:

  • EUR 50,000 if you only provide execution of orders on behalf of clients (brokerage).
  • EUR 125,000 if you also deal on your own account (market making or proprietary trading).
  • EUR 150,000 if you additionally provide custody of client crypto assets.

Most OTC desks combine execution and dealing, so the EUR 125,000 tier is typical. The capital must be held in a bank account in Romania and be fully paid up before license issuance. Consulting24 can advise on structuring your capital to meet the requirement efficiently. For example, you can use a combination of share capital and a subordinated loan if needed.

Tax Treatment

Romania's tax regime for crypto businesses is competitive. Corporate income tax is 16% on profits. There is no specific crypto tax law - general corporate tax rules apply. VAT is not charged on crypto-to-crypto transactions, but fiat-to-crypto trades may be subject to VAT in certain cases (consult a tax advisor).

For OTC desks, income from trading spreads and fees is taxable as ordinary business income. Romania has double tax treaties with many countries, which can reduce withholding taxes on dividends or interest. Consulting24 can connect you with local tax experts for precise planning. Compared to Panama, which taxes only local-source income, Romania's 16% is moderate for EU access.

Allowed Activities

With a Romanian CASP license covering OTC desk operations, you are permitted to:

  • Execute orders for clients to buy or sell crypto assets (brokerage).
  • Deal on your own account (proprietary trading).
  • Provide custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients (if additional capital is met).
  • Offer crypto-to-crypto and fiat-to-crypto exchange services.
  • Provide transfer services for crypto assets.

The license does not automatically allow operating a public exchange or offering investment advice. You must specify the services in your application. Consulting24 helps define the scope to match your business model. For a full exchange license, see our exchange license page.

Step-by-Step Process

Obtaining a crypto OTC desk license in Romania involves these steps:

  1. Company setup: Incorporate a Romanian limited liability company (SRL) with a registered office and a director (can be foreign).
  2. Document preparation: Draft a business plan, AML policy, risk assessment, internal controls, and governance documents.
  3. Capital deposit: Open a bank account in Romania and deposit the required minimum capital.
  4. Application submission: Submit the CASP application to the ASF with all supporting documents.
  5. ASF review: The regulator assesses the application, may request clarifications, and conducts interviews with management.
  6. License issuance: Upon approval, you receive the license and can commence operations.

Compliance and Trust

Operating a licensed OTC desk in Romania requires ongoing compliance with AML/CFT rules, regular reporting to the ASF, and annual audits. Your AML policy must include customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting. The ASF conducts periodic inspections.

Trust is built through transparency and regulatory adherence. A Romanian license signals to counterparties that you operate legally and securely. Common Mistakes to Avoid

Applicants often make these errors:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing business plans or risk assessments delay the process.
  • Underestimating capital: Not having the full capital in the bank before application.
  • Ignoring beneficial ownership: Failing to disclose all ultimate beneficial owners (UBOs) can lead to rejection.
  • Choosing the wrong service scope: Applying for services you don't need or missing ones you do.
  • Poor AML controls: Weak policies are a red flag for the ASF.

What is the minimum capital for a crypto OTC desk license in Romania?

The minimum capital depends on services. For execution of orders only, EUR 50,000. For dealing on own account, EUR 125,000. If custody is included, EUR 150,000. Most OTC desks need EUR 125,000.

Can I passport the Romanian license to other EU countries?

Yes, under MiCA, a Romanian CASP license allows you to provide services across the EU without needing additional licenses in each member state. This is a key advantage.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

You must maintain AML/CFT procedures, report suspicious transactions, submit annual financial audits, and pay an annual supervision fee (EUR 1,000-3,000). The ASF may conduct inspections.

What is the corporate tax rate in Romania for crypto businesses?

The standard corporate income tax rate is 16% on profits. There is no specific crypto tax law; general rules apply. VAT is not charged on crypto-to-crypto trades.

Can I operate an OTC desk without a license in Romania?

No, as of 2026, all crypto asset service providers must be licensed by the ASF under MiCA. Operating without a license is illegal and can result in fines or criminal penalties.
