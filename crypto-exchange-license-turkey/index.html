What is the Turkey Crypto Exchange License?

The Turkey crypto exchange license is a regulatory authorization issued by the Capital Markets Board (CMB) that allows companies to operate a cryptocurrency exchange, provide custody services, or engage in other crypto asset activities within Turkey. The license is mandatory for any platform that facilitates the buying, selling, or storage of cryptocurrencies for Turkish residents.

Key features of the regime include:

  • Regulated by the CMB under the Capital Markets Law No. 6362.
  • Applies to exchanges, custody providers, and wallet services.
  • Requires a minimum capital of TRY 10 million (approximately EUR 300,000 as of 2026 exchange rates).
  • Subject to ongoing compliance, reporting, and audit requirements.
  • Mandatory membership in the Turkish Capital Markets Association (TCMA) for certain activities.

Who Needs a Turkey Crypto Exchange License?

Any legal entity that offers crypto asset trading, exchange, custody, or wallet services to customers in Turkey must obtain a CMB license. This includes:

  • Centralized exchanges (order book or market maker models)
  • Decentralized exchange operators with a Turkish presence
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Brokers facilitating crypto trades
  • Payment processors handling crypto-to-fiat conversions
  • OTC desks and institutional trading platforms

If your business involves holding customer funds or executing trades, you likely fall under the licensing requirement. Even foreign companies targeting Turkish users may need to establish a local entity and obtain a license. The CMB has also indicated that DeFi platforms with a Turkish nexus may be subject to licensing. Consulting24 can help determine your specific obligations.

Exemptions exist for non-custodial wallet providers that do not hold private keys, and for businesses that only provide blockchain analytics or advisory services. However, the line can be blurry, and a consultation is recommended.

License Type and Regulator

The Turkey crypto exchange license is a single license category for crypto asset service providers (CASPs). The regulator is the Capital Markets Board (CMB) (Sermaye Piyasası Kurulu). The CMB oversees all capital market activities, including crypto assets, under the Capital Markets Law No. 6362.

Key points:

  • License type: Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) license.
  • Regulator: Capital Markets Board (CMB).
  • Legal basis: Capital Markets Law No. 6362, amendments effective 2024-2025.
  • Scope: Exchange, custody, wallet, and related services.
  • Secondary oversight: The Financial Crimes Investigation Board (MASAK) for AML/CFT compliance.

The CMB sets capital requirements, operational standards, and compliance obligations. It also conducts on-site inspections and can impose administrative fines. Consulting24 coordinates with local partners to ensure your application meets all CMB expectations.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a Turkey crypto exchange license is TRY 10 million (approximately EUR 300,000 as of 2026 exchange rates). This capital must be fully paid-up and held in a bank account in Turkey. The capital serves as a buffer for operational risks and customer protection.

Additional capital may be required if the exchange plans to offer advanced services or has a large customer base. The CMB may also impose higher capital for specific risk profiles, such as if the exchange holds customer assets in hot wallets. The capital must be maintained throughout the license period and cannot be withdrawn without CMB approval.

Consulting24 advises on structuring your capital to meet regulatory expectations, including the use of escrow accounts or capital increase procedures. For a jurisdiction with no minimum capital, see Panama.

Tax Treatment

Turkey has a progressive corporate tax system. As of 2026, the standard corporate income tax rate is 25%, with a reduced rate of 23% for companies with certain qualifications (e.g., export-oriented or technology development zones). Cryptocurrency trading profits are generally subject to corporate income tax. Additionally, a 15% withholding tax may apply on dividends distributed to foreign shareholders, subject to double tax treaties.

Value Added Tax (VAT) is not applied to cryptocurrency transactions in Turkey, following the European model. However, service fees charged by exchanges (e.g., trading fees, withdrawal fees) may be subject to VAT at 18%. Exchanges must register for VAT and file periodic returns.

Personal income tax on crypto gains for individuals is still evolving; as of 2026, occasional trading by individuals is not taxed, but frequent trading may be considered a business activity. Consulting24 recommends working with a local tax advisor to optimize your tax structure. Panama offers a more favorable tax environment for crypto businesses, with 0% corporate tax on foreign-sourced income, which may be a better fit for some founders.

Allowed Activities Under the License

The Turkey crypto exchange license permits the following activities:

  • Operating a cryptocurrency exchange (buying/selling crypto for fiat or other crypto)
  • Custody of crypto assets on behalf of clients (including cold and hot storage)
  • Wallet services (non-custodial and custodial)
  • Brokerage services for crypto trades (matching buyers and sellers)
  • Staking and lending (if disclosed and approved by CMB, subject to additional conditions)
  • Conversion services (crypto-to-fiat and crypto-to-crypto)

Activities not allowed without separate licenses include: derivatives trading (requires a separate derivatives license from the CMB), banking services (requires a banking license from the Banking Regulation and Supervision Agency), and issuing securities tokens (subject to capital markets law and prospectus requirements).

Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining a Turkey crypto exchange license involves several stages. Consulting24 coordinates each step with local experts.

  1. Company Incorporation: Register a Turkish joint-stock company (Anonim Şirket) with the required capital. Minimum share capital is TRY 10 million, of which at least 25% must be paid up before registration. The company must have a registered address in Turkey and at least one director (resident or non-resident).
  2. Document Preparation: Prepare a comprehensive application package including business plan, AML/KYC policies (aligned with MASAK and FATF), risk management framework, IT security documentation (including penetration test results), organizational chart, and corporate governance structure. All documents must be in Turkish.
  3. CMB Application: Submit the application package to the CMB along with the application fee (currently estimated at TRY 50,000-100,000, subject to change). The CMB will issue an acknowledgment and begin the review.
  4. Review and Feedback: The CMB reviews the application and may request additional information or modifications. This stage typically takes 2-4 months. The CMB may also conduct an on-site inspection of the company's premises and systems.
  5. License Issuance: Once approved, the license is granted and published in the CMB bulletin. The company must then comply with ongoing reporting and audit requirements, including quarterly transaction reports and annual financial audits.

Banking and Payment Services

Turkish banks have become more open to crypto businesses since the regulatory framework was established. However, opening a corporate bank account for a crypto exchange still requires careful preparation. Banks typically require proof of license application or license, AML policies, and a solid business plan. Some banks may also require a minimum deposit or a relationship manager.

Payment services: Exchanges can integrate with Turkish payment providers for fiat deposits and withdrawals. Most exchanges use bank transfers (EFT/FAST) and credit/debit cards. The Central Bank of Turkey has also introduced a fast payment system (FAST) that can be used for real-time transfers. International wire transfers may be subject to capital controls, so exchanges should plan accordingly.

Compliance and Trust Considerations

Operating under a CMB license requires rigorous ongoing compliance. Key obligations include:

  • AML/CFT procedures aligned with FATF recommendations, including customer due diligence (CDD), transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting to MASAK.
  • Regular reporting to the CMB on transactions, customer data, and financial health (quarterly and annual).
  • Annual audit by a licensed independent auditor, with audit reports submitted to the CMB.
  • Cybersecurity standards and data protection (KVKK compliance, similar to GDPR).
  • Segregation of client assets (custodial funds must be held in separate accounts) and insurance requirements (minimum coverage of TRY 5 million for cyber risks).
  • Appointment of a compliance officer and a risk manager, both resident in Turkey.

Failure to comply can result in fines (up to TRY 10 million), suspension, or revocation of the license. The CMB conducts periodic inspections and can impose corrective measures. Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make these errors when applying for a Turkey crypto license:

  • Underestimating the capital requirement: TRY 10 million must be fully paid-up and available in a Turkish bank account before application. Some founders use loaned capital, which may be scrutinized by the CMB.
  • Incomplete AML documentation: The CMB scrutinizes AML policies heavily. Many applications are rejected due to insufficient CDD procedures or lack of transaction monitoring systems.
  • Ignoring local partnership requirements: While foreign ownership is allowed, the CMB may require that at least one board member or manager be a Turkish resident. Some activities may require a local partner.
  • Not planning for ongoing costs: Annual compliance and audit fees, plus CMB supervision fees, can add up to EUR 10,000-15,000 per year. Budget accordingly.
  • Choosing the wrong jurisdiction: Turkey may not be optimal for all business models. For example, if your target market is global rather than Turkey-specific, a jurisdiction like Panama or Lithuania may be more suitable.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Turkey crypto exchange license?

The minimum capital is TRY 10 million (approximately EUR 300,000 as of 2026 exchange rates). This must be fully paid-up and held in a Turkish bank account. The capital cannot be withdrawn without CMB approval.

How long does it take to get a Turkey crypto license?

The typical timeline is 3 to 6 months from engagement to license issuance. The CMB has a statutory review period of 90 days, but this can be extended if additional information is requested. Consulting24 streamlines the process to minimize delays.

Can a foreign company apply for a Turkey crypto license?

Yes, but the company must first incorporate a Turkish subsidiary (joint-stock company) with a registered address in Turkey. Foreign ownership is allowed, but the CMB may require at least one resident director or manager.

What activities are covered under the Turkey crypto license?

The license covers exchange, custody, wallet, brokerage, and conversion services. Staking and lending may be allowed with CMB approval. Derivatives and banking services require separate licenses.

What is the corporate tax rate for crypto exchanges in Turkey?

The standard corporate tax rate is 25% as of 2026. A reduced rate of 23% may apply for certain qualified companies. Cryptocurrency trading profits are subject to corporate income tax.

Is VAT applied to cryptocurrency transactions in Turkey?

No, cryptocurrency transactions themselves are exempt from VAT. However, service fees charged by exchanges (e.g., trading fees) may be subject to VAT at 18%.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

Ongoing requirements include quarterly transaction reporting to the CMB, annual financial audits, AML/CFT compliance with MASAK, cybersecurity standards, and segregation of client assets. A compliance officer and risk manager must be appointed.

Can I use the Turkey license to serve customers in the EU?

No, the Turkey license only authorizes services to Turkish residents. To serve EU customers, you need a license under MiCA in an EU member state, such as Lithuania or Estonia. Consulting24 delivers directly in those jurisdictions.

What happens if I don't comply with CMB regulations?

Non-compliance can result in fines up to TRY 10 million, suspension of operations, or revocation of the license. The CMB conducts periodic inspections and can impose corrective measures.

Is Turkey a good jurisdiction for a crypto exchange startup?

Turkey offers a large user base and clear regulation, but the high capital requirement (TRY 10 million) and 25% corporate tax may be challenging for startups. For a lower-cost alternative, consider Panama (EUR 6,000 flat, no capital, 0% tax).
