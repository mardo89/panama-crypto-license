What Is a Crypto License in the Cayman Islands and Czech Republic?

A crypto license authorizes a business to provide virtual asset services such as exchange, custody, or transfer. In the Cayman Islands, there is no standalone crypto license; instead, crypto activities fall under the Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) registration regime administered by the Cayman Islands Monetary Authority (CIMA). This registration is mandatory for any entity providing virtual asset services from or within the Cayman Islands. The regime is based on the Virtual Asset Service Provider Act (2020, amended 2024) and focuses on AML/CTF compliance rather than operational standards.

In the Czech Republic, crypto asset service providers (CASPs) must obtain a license from the Czech National Bank (CNB) under the EU's MiCA regulation, which came fully into force in 2024. The Czech license is a full-fledged authorization that allows passporting across the European Economic Area. It covers a broad range of services and imposes strict capital, governance, and consumer protection requirements. Both jurisdictions require compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorist financing (CTF) rules, but the level of scrutiny and capital requirements differ significantly. The Cayman Islands registration is lighter, while the Czech license is more comprehensive and aligned with EU standards.

Who Needs a Crypto License in These Jurisdictions?

Any business offering the following services to clients in the Cayman Islands or Czech Republic needs to register or obtain a license:

  • Virtual asset exchange (crypto-to-crypto, crypto-to-fiat)
  • Custodial wallet services
  • Virtual asset transfer services
  • Brokerage or dealing in virtual assets
  • Issuance of virtual assets (in some cases)

If your target market is the EU, the Czech Republic license allows you to serve clients across all 27 member states. If you are serving institutional or high-net-worth clients in a tax-neutral environment, the Cayman Islands may be more suitable. Capital Requirement

The Cayman Islands VASP registration does not impose a statutory minimum capital requirement. However, CIMA expects firms to maintain adequate financial resources proportional to their activities. In practice, most applicants maintain at least USD 50,000 in liquid assets, though this is not mandated by law. The lack of a fixed minimum makes the Cayman Islands attractive for startups with limited capital.

Under MiCA, the Czech Republic imposes tiered capital requirements based on the services offered:

  • EUR 50,000 for custody and transfer services
  • EUR 125,000 for exchange services (crypto-to-fiat and crypto-to-crypto)
  • EUR 150,000 for trading platforms and full-service CASPs

These capital requirements are strictly enforced and must be maintained at all times. Consulting24 can help you structure your capital to meet these thresholds. For a jurisdiction with no minimum capital, consider our Panama service.

Tax Treatment

Cayman Islands: No corporate income tax, no capital gains tax, no withholding tax, and no VAT. This makes it a highly attractive jurisdiction for crypto businesses, especially those dealing with institutional investors. However, you must still comply with economic substance requirements if your business is managed and controlled from the Cayman Islands. The absence of direct taxation can lead to significant savings for profitable firms.

Czech Republic: Corporate income tax is 19% (2026). Crypto transactions are subject to standard VAT rules (generally exempt for exchange services). Capital gains on crypto are taxed at 19% for corporations. The Czech Republic has a growing number of tax treaties that can reduce withholding taxes on dividends and interest. For tax-neutral structuring, the Cayman Islands is superior. For EU market access with moderate taxation, the Czech Republic is competitive. Step-by-Step Application Process

The application process for each jurisdiction involves several stages. Consulting24 coordinates with local legal partners to ensure a smooth process.

Cayman Islands VASP Registration

  1. Incorporate a Cayman Islands exempted company or limited liability company.
  2. Appoint a registered office and local secretary (if required).
  3. Prepare AML/CTF policies, risk assessment, and business plan.
  4. Submit application to CIMA with supporting documents.
  5. Undergo CIMA review and respond to queries.
  6. Receive registration certificate.

Czech Republic CASP License

  1. Incorporate a Czech company (e.g., s.r.o.) with registered office.
  2. Prepare detailed operational and compliance documentation (MiCA requirements).
  3. Appoint a local compliance officer and board members.
  4. Submit application to CNB via the official portal.
  5. CNB reviews and may request additional information (typically 3-6 months).
  6. Receive license and register with the CNB.

Banking and Payment Services

Access to banking is a critical factor for crypto businesses. In the Cayman Islands, many international banks are present, but they are often cautious about crypto clients. You may need to approach specialized fintech-friendly banks or use payment processors. The Cayman Islands does not have a central bank digital currency (CBDC) yet. Some banks require a minimum deposit of USD 100,000 to open an account for a crypto firm.

In the Czech Republic, the banking landscape is more open to crypto businesses, especially after MiCA implementation. Major banks like CSOB, Komercni banka, and Raiffeisenbank have started accepting CASP clients, though with enhanced due diligence. Payment institutions and EMI licenses are also available for fiat on-ramps. Consulting24 can introduce you to banking partners in both jurisdictions. Compliance and Trust

Compliance is non-negotiable in both jurisdictions. The Cayman Islands requires strong AML/CTF measures, including customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting. CIMA conducts periodic inspections. Failure to comply can result in fines or revocation of registration. The Cayman Islands also has economic substance requirements that may apply to certain entities.

The Czech Republic, under MiCA, imposes even stricter requirements: mandatory segregation of client assets, regular audits, governance standards, and incident reporting. The CNB has the power to impose penalties of up to 10% of annual turnover for serious breaches. CASPs must also comply with MiCA's marketing and disclosure rules. Common Mistakes to Avoid

Many applicants make avoidable errors that delay or derail their application. Common mistakes include:

  • Incomplete or poorly drafted AML policies: Both jurisdictions require detailed, jurisdiction-specific AML procedures. Generic templates are often rejected.
  • Underestimating timeline: The Czech Republic license can take 6-12 months; rushing the process leads to errors.
  • Insufficient capital: In the Czech Republic, failing to maintain the required capital can result in license suspension.
  • Ignoring economic substance: In the Cayman Islands, you must demonstrate real presence and decision-making locally.
  • Choosing the wrong jurisdiction for your business model: For example, a retail-focused exchange targeting EU clients may struggle with the Cayman Islands' lack of passporting.
  • Not engaging local experts: Both jurisdictions have specific legal nuances that require local knowledge.

Frequently asked questions

What is the main difference between a Cayman Islands VASP registration and a Czech Republic CASP license?

The Cayman Islands VASP registration is a lighter AML-focused registration with no minimum capital, no corporate tax, and no EU passporting. The Czech Republic CASP license is a full MiCA authorization with tiered capital requirements, 19% corporate tax, and the ability to serve clients across the entire European Economic Area.

Which jurisdiction is cheaper for a crypto startup?

The Cayman Islands typically costs USD 25,000-50,000, while the Czech Republic costs EUR 32,000-62,000. However, the cheapest option is Panama at a flat EUR 6,000, which we deliver directly. Your choice should also consider ongoing compliance costs and tax implications.

Can I serve EU clients with a Cayman Islands VASP registration?

No, the Cayman Islands registration does not provide EU passporting. To serve EU clients, you would need a license in an EU member state like the Czech Republic or Lithuania, which allows passporting under MiCA.

What are the capital requirements for a Czech Republic CASP license?

Under MiCA, capital requirements are tiered: EUR 50,000 for custody and transfer, EUR 125,000 for exchange, and EUR 150,000 for trading platforms. These must be maintained at all times. The Cayman Islands has no statutory minimum, but firms typically hold at least USD 50,000.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in the Czech Republic?

The Czech Republic CASP license typically takes 6-12 months from application to approval, including preparation time. The Cayman Islands VASP registration is faster at 3-6 months. Panama is the fastest at 2-3 months.

Is the Cayman Islands tax-free for crypto businesses?

Yes, the Cayman Islands has no corporate income tax, no capital gains tax, no withholding tax, and no VAT. However, economic substance requirements may apply if the business is managed and controlled from the islands.

What activities are allowed under a Czech Republic CASP license?

The license covers exchange (crypto-to-fiat and crypto-to-crypto), custodial wallet services, transfer services, brokerage, dealing on own account, and issuance of stablecoins under MiCA. All activities must comply with MiCA's operational and capital requirements.

Do I need a local office in the Cayman Islands or Czech Republic?

For the Cayman Islands, you need a registered office and may need a local secretary. For the Czech Republic, you need a registered office and a local compliance officer. Both jurisdictions require a physical presence for substance purposes.

What happens if I fail to meet compliance requirements?

In the Cayman Islands, CIMA can impose fines or revoke registration. In the Czech Republic, the CNB can impose penalties up to 10% of annual turnover. It is crucial to maintain ongoing compliance with AML, capital, and reporting obligations.
