What Is a Crypto Gambling License in Poland?

A crypto gambling license in Poland is a regulatory authorization issued by the Polish Ministry of Finance that permits operators to offer gambling services using cryptocurrencies. It is governed by the Polish Gambling Act of 19 November 2009, as amended, and aligns with EU anti-money laundering (AML) directives. The license covers online casino, sports betting, poker, and other gambling activities where digital assets like Bitcoin, Ethereum, or stablecoins are accepted as payment or wagering instruments.

Unlike general crypto licenses (e.g., VASP registration in other EU states), Poland treats crypto gambling as a specific subset of gambling regulation. Operators must obtain a separate gambling license and comply with both gambling laws and crypto-specific AML/CFT obligations. The license is valid for a fixed term, typically 6 years, and is renewable.

Who Needs a Crypto Gambling License in Poland?

Any entity that offers gambling services to Polish residents and accepts cryptocurrencies as payment or stake must obtain a Polish gambling license. This includes:

  • Online casinos accepting crypto deposits and withdrawals
  • Sports betting platforms that allow crypto wagering
  • Poker rooms and skill game operators using digital assets
  • White-label providers powering crypto gambling sites
  • Affiliates and B2B software providers if they are deemed to be offering gambling services

If you operate from outside Poland but target Polish players, you are still subject to Polish law. The Polish regulator actively blocks unlicensed sites, and penalties can include fines, imprisonment, and asset seizure. A valid license is the only legal route.

License Type and Regulator

The regulatory authority for crypto gambling in Poland is the Ministry of Finance (Ministerstwo Finansów), specifically the department responsible for gambling supervision. The license type is a casino gambling license (online) under the Gambling Act. There is no separate “crypto license” - the standard gambling license covers crypto activities.

Key features of the license:

  • Valid for 6 years, renewable
  • Non-transferable
  • Requires a physical presence in Poland (registered office and local management)
  • Subject to ongoing supervision and audits

Poland is an EU member state, so the license also complies with MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation) for crypto asset services, but gambling is exempt from MiCA. However, AML obligations under the Polish AML Act (implementing EU directives) apply fully.

Capital Requirement

The Polish Gambling Act mandates a minimum share capital of PLN 2,000,000 (approx. EUR 500,000) for online casino licenses. This capital must be fully paid in cash before submitting the application and must be maintained throughout the license term. The capital can be used for operational expenses but cannot fall below the minimum.

In addition, operators must provide a bank guarantee or equivalent financial security of at least EUR 200,000 to cover potential player winnings and deposits. This guarantee is released only after the license is surrendered or revoked and all player liabilities are settled.

These capital requirements are significantly higher than those for general crypto licenses in other EU states (e.g., Estonia or Lithuania), reflecting the higher risk profile of gambling.

Tax Treatment

Poland imposes a gross gaming revenue (GGR) tax on licensed gambling operators. As of 2026, the tax rate for online casino games is 12% of GGR. Sports betting is taxed at 12% as well. There is no separate tax on crypto transactions; the gambling tax applies to all bets, regardless of currency.

Corporate income tax (CIT) in Poland is a flat 19% (9% for small taxpayers with revenues under EUR 2 million). VAT is not applicable to gambling services, as they are exempt under EU law.

Operators should also consider withholding tax on dividends and interest, which is generally 19% unless reduced by a double tax treaty. Consulting24 recommends a tax structuring review to optimize the overall tax burden.

Allowed Activities

A Polish crypto gambling license permits the following activities:

  • Online casino games (slots, roulette, blackjack, etc.)
  • Sports betting (including live betting)
  • Poker (cash games and tournaments)
  • Bingo and keno
  • Lotteries and scratch cards
  • Any other game of chance offered via the internet

All games must use a certified random number generator (RNG) and be tested by an approved laboratory. Cryptocurrencies can be used for deposits, withdrawals, and wagering, but the operator must also support fiat currency (PLN or EUR) for compliance with anti-money laundering rules. The license does not cover physical casinos or slot machines in land-based venues.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining a Polish crypto gambling license involves several stages. Consulting24 assists at every step:

  1. Pre-application assessment: Review of business model, source of funds, and suitability of directors.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Polish limited liability company (sp. z o.o.) with minimum share capital of PLN 2,000,000.
  3. Bank guarantee: Arrange a bank guarantee or insurance for player protection.
  4. Technical certification: Submit games and systems for testing by an accredited lab (e.g., GLI, BMM).
  5. Application submission: File the license application with the Ministry of Finance, including all required documents (business plan, AML policies, financial statements, etc.).
  6. Regulatory review: The Ministry reviews the application, conducts background checks, and may request additional information. This stage takes 3-6 months.
  7. License issuance: Once approved, the license is granted for 6 years. The operator must pay the license fee and commence operations within 12 months.
  8. Ongoing compliance: Annual reporting, AML audits, and tax filings are required.

Banking and Payments

Polish licensed gambling operators must offer players the ability to deposit and withdraw using fiat currency (PLN) via bank transfer, credit/debit cards, and e-wallets. Cryptocurrency acceptance is optional but must comply with AML/KYC procedures. Operators need a Polish bank account to process fiat transactions and hold player funds.

Opening a bank account for a gambling company can be challenging due to perceived risk. Consulting24 can introduce clients to banks with experience in the gambling sector. Alternatively, some operators use electronic money institutions (EMIs) or payment service providers that specialize in high-risk industries.

For crypto transactions, the operator must register as a VASP (Virtual Asset Service Provider) under the Polish AML Act if they exchange crypto for fiat. This registration is separate from the gambling license and involves additional compliance obligations.

Benefits of a Polish Crypto Gambling License

Despite the high cost and strict requirements, a Polish license offers several advantages:

  • EU recognition: Poland is an EU member, so the license is recognized across the European Economic Area (EEA) under the freedom to provide services, though some countries may impose local restrictions.
  • Player trust: A regulated license signals legitimacy and attracts players who prefer licensed operators.
  • Access to Polish market: Poland has a population of 38 million and a growing online gambling market, with increasing crypto adoption.
  • Stable regulatory environment: The Polish framework is well-established and not subject to frequent changes.
  • Tax efficiency: The 12% GGR tax is competitive compared to some other EU jurisdictions (e.g., Germany 5.3% but with additional levies).

Compliance and Trust

Operating under a Polish license requires strong compliance with AML/CFT regulations, player protection rules, and technical standards. Key obligations include:

  • Appointing a local AML officer
  • Implementing a transaction monitoring system
  • Conducting customer due diligence (CDD) for all players
  • Reporting suspicious transactions to the General Inspector of Financial Information (GIIF)
  • Ensuring game fairness through regular audits

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Many applicants fail due to avoidable errors. Common pitfalls include:

  • Insufficient capital: Underestimating the share capital requirement or failing to maintain it.
  • Incomplete documentation: Missing business plans, financial projections, or AML policies.
  • Uncertified games: Using RNGs that have not been tested by an approved lab.
  • Ignoring local presence: Not having a registered office or local directors.
  • Poor source of funds: Inability to prove the legitimacy of investment capital.
  • Overlooking VASP registration: Failing to register as a VASP if exchanging crypto for fiat.
