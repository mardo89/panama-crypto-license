What Is the Crypto Fund License in Indonesia?

The crypto fund license in Indonesia is a regulatory authorization issued by Bappebti that allows firms to manage pooled investments in crypto assets. It is designed for fund managers, investment advisors, and asset managers who wish to offer crypto-focused funds to Indonesian investors or operate from Indonesia. The license is part of Indonesia's broader efforts to regulate the digital asset sector, which began with the recognition of crypto as a commodity in 2019 and has evolved to include specific rules for fund management.

Key features of the license include compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) regulations, capital adequacy requirements, and regular reporting to Bappebti. The license is distinct from a crypto exchange license, which covers trading platforms. Instead, it focuses on the management of crypto assets on behalf of clients, similar to a traditional fund management license but tailored for digital assets.

Who Needs This License?

Any entity that intends to manage a crypto investment fund in Indonesia must obtain this license. This includes:

  • Asset management firms launching crypto-focused mutual funds or hedge funds.
  • Investment advisors providing discretionary portfolio management in crypto assets.
  • Foreign fund managers looking to establish a presence in Indonesia.
  • Blockchain-based investment platforms offering pooled crypto investments.

Even if your fund is based overseas, if you target Indonesian investors or have a physical presence in Indonesia, you are likely required to hold a license from Bappebti. License Type and Regulator

The regulator for crypto fund management in Indonesia is Bappebti (Badan Pengawas Perdagangan Berjangka Komoditi), which oversees commodity futures trading, including crypto assets. The license type is often referred to as a Crypto Asset Fund Manager License, though officially it falls under Bappebti's regulations for crypto asset trading and fund management. As of 2026, Bappebti requires fund managers to register as a Crypto Asset Fund Manager (CAFM) and comply with specific operational standards.

Unlike some jurisdictions that have separate regimes for crypto funds, Indonesia integrates crypto fund management under the broader commodity futures framework. This means that firms must also adhere to general fund management rules issued by the Financial Services Authority (OJK) for certain aspects, but Bappebti remains the primary regulator for crypto-specific activities.

Capital Requirement

Bappebti requires a minimum capital for crypto fund managers, which as of 2026 is set at IDR 1 billion (approximately USD 64,000) for most fund types. However, for funds that involve more complex strategies or higher risk, the capital requirement may be higher, up to IDR 5 billion (USD 320,000). The capital must be maintained as paid-up share capital and cannot be withdrawn without regulatory approval.

This capital is intended to ensure that the fund manager has sufficient financial resources to cover operational costs and potential liabilities. It is separate from the fund's assets under management. Consulting24 can help you structure your capital contribution to meet Bappebti's requirements efficiently.

Tax Treatment

Indonesia's tax treatment for crypto fund managers is still evolving, but as of 2026, the following general principles apply:

  • Corporate income tax: Fund managers are subject to the standard corporate tax rate of 22% on their management fees and other income.
  • Capital gains tax: Gains from crypto asset trading by the fund are subject to a final tax of 0.1% on the transaction value, similar to stock trading. However, this is subject to change.
  • VAT: Management fees are subject to 11% VAT.
  • Withholding tax: Dividends and interest paid to foreign investors may be subject to withholding tax at rates depending on tax treaties.

It is crucial to consult with a tax advisor familiar with Indonesian crypto regulations, as the tax landscape can shift rapidly. Consulting24 can coordinate with local tax experts to ensure compliance.

Allowed Activities

With a crypto fund license in Indonesia, you are permitted to engage in the following activities:

  • Managing pooled crypto assets for investors.
  • Executing trades in crypto assets on behalf of the fund.
  • Providing discretionary portfolio management services for crypto assets.
  • Offering crypto asset investment products to Indonesian investors.
  • Staking and lending crypto assets within the fund, if explicitly allowed by Bappebti.

Activities that are not allowed include operating a crypto exchange, issuing new crypto tokens (unless separately licensed), and providing custody services to third parties outside the fund. The license is specifically for fund management, not for broader crypto services.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining a crypto fund license in Indonesia involves several stages. Here is a typical step-by-step process:

  1. Pre-incorporation: Determine the legal structure (e.g., PT or foreign representative office) and prepare the company's articles of association.
  2. Company registration: Register the company with the Ministry of Law and Human Rights and obtain a business identification number (NIB).
  3. Capital deposit: Deposit the required minimum capital in a bank account and obtain a proof of capital.
  4. Compliance setup: Implement AML/CFT policies, appoint a compliance officer, and set up internal controls.
  5. Application submission: Submit the license application to Bappebti, including all required documents such as business plan, fund prospectus, and background checks on directors.
  6. Review and interview: Bappebti reviews the application and may conduct interviews with key personnel.
  7. License issuance: If approved, the license is issued, and the fund can commence operations.
  8. Ongoing compliance: Submit regular reports to Bappebti and maintain capital adequacy.

Fund managers must also maintain accurate records and submit periodic reports to Bappebti.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

When applying for a crypto fund license in Indonesia, avoid these common pitfalls:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing or incorrect documents can delay the process by months.
  • Underestimating capital requirements: Ensure you have sufficient capital before applying.
  • Ignoring local partner requirements: Some structures may require a local director or shareholder.
  • Poor AML/CFT policies: Bappebti scrutinizes these closely; generic policies will be rejected.
  • Overlooking ongoing compliance: The license is not the end; regular reporting is mandatory.

Frequently asked questions

What is the crypto fund license in Indonesia?

It is a regulatory authorization from Bappebti allowing firms to manage pooled crypto investments. It covers fund management, discretionary portfolio management, and related activities for crypto assets.

Who regulates crypto fund managers in Indonesia?

Bappebti (Commodity Futures Trading Regulatory Agency) is the primary regulator. It oversees crypto asset trading and fund management under the commodity futures framework.

What is the minimum capital for a crypto fund license in Indonesia?

As of 2026, the minimum capital is IDR 1 billion (approx USD 64,000). For more complex funds, it can be up to IDR 5 billion (USD 320,000).

How long does it take to get a crypto fund license in Indonesia?

The typical timeline is 4-6 months, depending on the completeness of your application and the speed of local processes.

What are the tax implications for crypto fund managers in Indonesia?

Corporate income tax is 22% on management fees. Capital gains from crypto trades are subject to a final tax of 0.1% per transaction. VAT of 11% applies to management fees.

Can a foreign company apply for a crypto fund license in Indonesia?

Yes, but typically a local presence is required, such as a PT (limited liability company) or a foreign representative office. Local directors may also be needed.

What activities are allowed under the crypto fund license?

Allowed activities include managing pooled crypto assets, executing trades, discretionary portfolio management, and offering crypto investment products. Exchange operations are not permitted.

Is the Indonesian crypto fund license recognized internationally?

No, it is a domestic license. However, it can facilitate operations within Indonesia and may be used to attract local investors. For international recognition, consider EU licenses under MiCA.
