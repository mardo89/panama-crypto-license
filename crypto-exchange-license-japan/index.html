What Is the Crypto Exchange License Japan?

The crypto exchange license Japan is a registration with the Financial Services Agency (FSA) that allows a company to legally operate a cryptocurrency exchange in Japan. It is officially known as a Crypto Asset Exchange Service Provider (CAESP) registration. Japan was one of the first countries to introduce a comprehensive regulatory framework for digital assets, following the 2017 amendment to the Payment Services Act.

Key features of the license include:

  • Obligation to segregate client assets from company assets
  • Stringent cybersecurity and operational risk management requirements
  • Regular reporting to the FSA and annual audits
  • Membership in a self-regulatory organization (JCVDA or similar)

The license is mandatory for any entity offering exchange services, custody, or trading of crypto assets to Japanese residents. Operating without it is a criminal offense.

Who Needs a Crypto Exchange License Japan?

Any business that provides the following services in or to Japan must obtain a crypto exchange license Japan:

  • Buying and selling crypto assets (as a principal or agent)
  • Operating a crypto asset exchange platform (order book matching)
  • Providing custody or management of crypto assets for clients
  • Offering margin trading or used trading of crypto assets

Foreign companies targeting Japanese customers are also subject to these requirements. Even if your company is registered overseas, if you actively solicit Japanese residents, you must register with the FSA. This includes advertising in Japanese or offering customer support in Japanese. License Type and Regulator

The regulator for the crypto exchange license Japan is the Financial Services Agency (FSA). The FSA is a Japanese government agency responsible for overseeing banking, securities, and exchange, and insurance. For crypto assets, the FSA works in conjunction with the Japan Virtual Currency Exchange Association (JVCEA), a self-regulatory organization that sets industry standards.

The license is a registration, not a full license, but it carries similar weight. There is only one type of registration for crypto asset exchange service providers. However, depending on the services offered, additional permissions may be required under the Financial Instruments and Exchange Act (e.g., for security tokens).

As of 2026, the FSA has increased its scrutiny on compliance, particularly around anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF). The application process involves a thorough review of the company's business model, financial health, and governance.

Capital Requirement

The capital requirement for a crypto exchange license Japan is not explicitly set by law, but the FSA expects a minimum of JPY 10 million (approximately EUR 62,000) as a general guideline. However, in practice, the FSA may require significantly higher capital depending on the scale of operations and risk profile. For example, if you plan to offer margin trading, the capital requirement could be JPY 50 million or more.

As of 2026, the FSA has been considering a tiered capital system similar to MiCA, but no official change has been enacted. Therefore, we recommend maintaining at least JPY 20-30 million in liquid assets to demonstrate financial stability. Tax Treatment

Japan's tax treatment of crypto assets is unique and can be a burden. For corporations, profits from crypto trading are taxed as ordinary income at the corporate tax rate of approximately 30%. For individuals, crypto gains are classified as miscellaneous income and taxed at progressive rates up to 55% (including local taxes).

There is no special tax regime for crypto exchanges, but certain deductions are available. For example, losses from crypto trading can be offset against other crypto gains. Additionally, the FSA requires exchanges to report transaction details to tax authorities. Allowed Activities

With a crypto exchange license Japan, you are permitted to:

  • Operate an exchange platform for buying and selling crypto assets
  • Provide custody services for client crypto assets
  • Offer margin trading and used trading (with additional approvals)
  • Facilitate initial coin offerings (ICOs) and token listings (subject to FSA review)
  • Provide crypto asset derivatives (requires separate registration under Financial Instruments and Exchange Act)

Activities that are not allowed include unregistered securities offerings, lending of client assets without consent, and any form of gambling. The FSA strictly prohibits market manipulation and insider trading. Consulting24 helps clients define their service scope to ensure compliance from day one.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process for obtaining a crypto exchange license Japan involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step.

  1. Pre-application preparation: Incorporate a Japanese entity (e.g., Kabushiki Kaisha) with a registered office in Japan. Prepare a detailed business plan, AML/CTF policies, and system documentation.
  2. System development: Build or adapt your exchange platform to meet FSA cybersecurity standards. Engage a third-party auditor for a security assessment.
  3. Submit application: File the application with the FSA through the electronic system. Include all required documents, such as articles of incorporation, financial statements, and compliance manuals.
  4. FSA review: The FSA will conduct a thorough review, including interviews with management and on-site inspections. Expect requests for additional information.
  5. Approval and registration: Once approved, you will be registered as a CAESP. You must then join the JVCEA and comply with its rules.
  6. Post-licensing: Submit regular reports to the FSA and undergo annual audits. Maintain a strong compliance program.

Banking and Payment Integration

One of the biggest challenges for crypto exchanges in Japan is opening a corporate bank account. Major Japanese banks are often reluctant to work with crypto businesses due to perceived risks. However, some regional banks and newer digital banks are more open.

Common banking partners include:

  • Sumitomo Mitsui Banking Corporation (SMBC) - selective
  • Mizuho Bank - limited availability
  • Regional banks like Bank of Kyoto or Shizuoka Bank
  • Digital banks such as Sony Bank or PayPay Bank

Compliance and Trust

Compliance is the cornerstone of the crypto exchange license Japan. The FSA expects exchanges to have strong AML/CTF programs, including customer due diligence (CDD), transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting. Exchanges must also segregate client assets in trust accounts and conduct regular audits.

Trust is built through transparency. Exchanges are required to disclose fees, risks, and operational status. The FSA also publishes a list of registered exchanges, which helps customers verify legitimacy. Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make these mistakes when pursuing a crypto exchange license Japan:

  • Underestimating the timeline: The process can take over a year. Plan accordingly.
  • Inadequate AML/CTF policies: The FSA scrutinizes these heavily. Generic policies are not enough.
  • Ignoring local presence: You need a physical office in Japan and local staff, including a compliance officer.
  • Poor system security: Hacking incidents have led to license revocations. Invest in top-tier cybersecurity.
  • Not budgeting for ongoing costs: Annual compliance costs can be significant. Ensure you have the resources.

Frequently asked questions

What is the primary regulator for crypto exchanges in Japan?

The Financial Services Agency (FSA) is the primary regulator. It oversees all crypto asset exchange service providers (CAESPs) under the Payment Services Act. The FSA works with the Japan Virtual Currency Exchange Association (JVCEA) for self-regulation.

Is a crypto exchange license Japan required for foreign companies?

Yes, if you target Japanese residents. The FSA requires any entity offering crypto exchange services to Japanese customers to register, regardless of where the company is incorporated. This includes advertising in Japanese or having Japanese-language support.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a crypto exchange license Japan?

The FSA expects at least JPY 10 million (about EUR 62,000), but in practice, higher capital may be needed. For margin trading, the requirement can be JPY 50 million or more. Consulting24 can advise on the appropriate level for your business.

How long does it take to get a crypto exchange license Japan?

The typical timeline is 6 to 12 months from application submission, but it can extend to 18 months. Pre-application preparation (company setup, system development) takes an additional 3-6 months. Consulting24 helps streamline the process.

What are the tax implications for crypto exchanges in Japan?

Corporate profits from crypto trading are taxed at around 30% (effective rate). Individual gains are taxed as miscellaneous income at progressive rates up to 55%. There is no special tax regime for crypto, but deductions for losses are available.

Can I operate a crypto exchange in Japan without a license?

No, operating without a license is illegal and can result in criminal penalties. The FSA actively monitors and takes enforcement actions against unregistered entities. Always obtain the proper registration before offering services.

What activities are allowed under the crypto exchange license Japan?

Allowed activities include operating an exchange, custody, margin trading (with extra approval), and facilitating token listings. Derivatives require separate registration. Activities like unregistered securities offerings or market manipulation are prohibited.
