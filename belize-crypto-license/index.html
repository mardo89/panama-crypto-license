What Is a Belize Crypto License?

A Belize crypto license is a regulatory authorization issued by the IFSC that allows companies to legally offer digital asset services. It covers activities such as operating a cryptocurrency exchange, providing custody solutions, facilitating token trading, and processing crypto payments. The license is issued under the Digital Assets Act, which came into effect in 2023 and was updated in 2025 to align with international anti-money laundering (AML) standards.

Belize positions itself as a flexible jurisdiction: it does not require a physical office presence, and the licensing process can be completed remotely. The IFSC takes a risk-based approach, meaning the level of scrutiny depends on the services offered and the jurisdictions your business serves. This makes the Belize crypto license a popular choice for companies that want to avoid the heavy capital requirements of EU member states while still obtaining a recognized license.

Key features include a streamlined application process, no minimum capital for certain activities (though capital is recommended for credibility), and a corporate tax rate of 0% on income earned outside Belize. The license is valid for one year and must be renewed annually.

Who Needs a Belize Crypto License?

A Belize crypto license is suitable for a wide range of crypto businesses, including:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (centralized and peer-to-peer platforms)
  • Digital asset custodians and wallet providers
  • Payment processors handling crypto transactions
  • Token issuers and ICO/STO platforms
  • DeFi protocols that offer custodial services
  • OTC trading desks

If your business model involves holding customer funds, facilitating trades, or providing any form of digital asset service, you likely need a license in Belize. The IFSC also requires licensing for any company that markets crypto services to Belizean residents, though most licensees serve international clients. License Type and Regulator

The regulator for crypto activities in Belize is the International Financial Services Commission (IFSC). The IFSC issues a single type of license for digital asset service providers, known as the Digital Asset Service Provider (DASP) License. This license covers all regulated activities under the Digital Assets Act.

There are no sub-categories for different services; instead, the license scope is defined by the activities you apply for. For example, you can apply to operate an exchange, provide custody, or both. The IFSC reviews each application on its merits and may impose conditions based on the risk profile.

As of 2026, Belize is not part of the European Union, so MiCA does not apply. However, the IFSC has incorporated many FATF recommendations into its regulatory framework, ensuring that a Belize crypto license meets international compliance standards.

Capital Requirement

The IFSC does not impose a statutory minimum capital requirement for a Belize crypto license. However, the regulator expects applicants to demonstrate sufficient financial resources to cover operational costs and potential liabilities. In practice, most successful applicants maintain a capital base of at least EUR 50,000 (or equivalent in USD or BZD) to satisfy the IFSC's fit-and-proper test.

For higher-risk activities such as operating an exchange with high trading volumes, the IFSC may require a higher capital amount, often in the range of EUR 100,000 to EUR 250,000. These figures are not set in law but are based on regulatory expectations. Consulting24 advises on the appropriate capital level for your business to avoid undercapitalization.

Capital can be held in a bank account in Belize or abroad, as long as it is accessible and properly documented. The IFSC does not require the capital to be deposited in a Belizean bank, which adds flexibility.

Tax Treatment

One of the main attractions of a Belize crypto license is the favorable tax regime. Belize offers:

  • 0% corporate tax on income earned outside Belize (offshore income)
  • No capital gains tax
  • No withholding tax on dividends, interest, or royalties paid to non-residents
  • No VAT on digital asset services

However, if your Belize company earns income from Belizean sources (e.g., providing services to Belize residents), that income may be subject to Belize's standard corporate tax rate of 25%. Most crypto licensees structure their operations to serve international clients, thereby enjoying the 0% rate.

Belize does not have a double taxation treaty network, so you should consider the tax implications in your home country. Consulting24 recommends consulting with a tax advisor to ensure compliance with your local tax obligations.

Allowed Activities

Under the Digital Assets Act, a Belize crypto license permits the following activities:

  • Operating a digital asset exchange (order book, matching engine)
  • Providing custodial wallet services
  • Processing crypto-to-fiat and crypto-to-crypto transactions
  • Issuing and redeeming stablecoins
  • Running an OTC trading desk
  • Offering crypto payment processing to merchants
  • Managing digital asset portfolios (with additional conditions)

The license does not cover activities such as operating a crypto ATM network or mining, which are not regulated under the Digital Assets Act. If you plan to offer services that involve securities tokens, you may need additional licensing under Belize's Securities Act. Benefits of a Belize Crypto License

Choosing Belize for your crypto license offers several advantages:

  • Low cost: Compared to EU jurisdictions like Lithuania or Malta, Belize is significantly cheaper, with no minimum capital requirement and lower annual fees.
  • Tax efficiency: 0% corporate tax on offshore income, no capital gains tax, and no VAT on crypto services.
  • Fast setup: The licensing process can be completed in 3-6 months, faster than many other jurisdictions.
  • No physical office requirement: You can operate remotely, though a registered agent in Belize is required.
  • Flexible capital: No statutory minimum capital, though practical capital of EUR 50,000+ is recommended.
  • International recognition: The IFSC is a member of the International Association of Insurance Supervisors (IAIS) and follows FATF standards, giving the license credibility.

Compliance and Trust

Maintaining a Belize crypto license requires ongoing compliance. The IFSC expects licensees to:

  • Implement strong AML/KYC procedures
  • Submit annual audited financial statements
  • Report suspicious transactions
  • Maintain adequate capital and insurance
  • Undergo periodic inspections

Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. Common Mistakes to Avoid

Many applicants make avoidable errors when applying for a Belize crypto license. Common mistakes include:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing business plans, AML policies, or personal declarations can delay the process.
  • Underestimating capital needs: While no minimum is set, having too little capital can raise red flags with the IFSC.
  • Ignoring source of funds: The IFSC will scrutinize the source of funds for directors and shareholders. Be prepared to provide evidence.
  • Choosing the wrong corporate structure: An IBC is standard, but sometimes a local company is more appropriate.
  • Not engaging a local agent: The IFSC requires a registered agent in Belize; failing to appoint one can lead to rejection.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Belize crypto license?

The IFSC does not set a statutory minimum capital requirement. However, in practice, applicants should have at least EUR 50,000 in available capital to demonstrate financial stability. Higher-risk activities may require EUR 100,000-250,000.

How long does it take to get a Belize crypto license?

The typical timeline is 3 to 6 months from application submission to license issuance. Delays can occur if documentation is incomplete or if the IFSC requires additional due diligence.

What taxes do Belize crypto license holders pay?

Belize imposes 0% corporate tax on income earned outside Belize. There is no capital gains tax, no withholding tax on dividends, and no VAT on digital asset services. Income from Belizean sources is taxed at 25%.

Can I operate a crypto exchange with a Belize license?

Yes, a Belize crypto license covers operating a digital asset exchange, including order matching, trading, and settlement. You can also offer custody, payment processing, and OTC trading.

Does Belize require a physical office?

No, Belize does not require a physical office presence. However, you must appoint a registered agent in Belize to receive legal documents and regulatory correspondence.

Is a Belize crypto license recognized internationally?

The IFSC follows FATF standards, and the license is recognized by many international banks and partners. However, it does not provide EU passporting rights like a MiCA license.

Can I apply for a Belize crypto license remotely?

Yes, the entire application process can be conducted remotely. Consulting24 coordinates all steps, including document submission and communication with the IFSC.

What are the annual renewal fees for a Belize crypto license?

Annual renewal fees are typically EUR 3,000-6,000, depending on the license scope. Additional costs include compliance updates and audited financial statements.

How does Belize compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Panama offers a simpler and cheaper option with a flat fee of EUR 6,000 and no capital requirement. Belize has a more formal regulatory framework under the IFSC, which may be preferred for institutional credibility.

Can I use a Belize license to serve EU customers?

Yes, but you must comply with EU AML regulations and MiCA if you actively market to EU residents. A Belize license alone does not provide EU passporting rights.
