What Is the Belize Crypto Exchange License?

The Belize crypto exchange license is a regulatory authorization granted by the IFSC that allows a company to operate a digital assets exchange, including trading, custody, and related services. It is designed for businesses that facilitate the exchange of cryptocurrencies for fiat or other digital assets. The license is part of Belize's broader framework to attract fintech and crypto enterprises while maintaining compliance with international anti-money laundering (AML) standards.

Key features include a straightforward application process, low setup costs, and a favorable tax regime. Unlike some jurisdictions that require a physical presence or high minimum capital, Belize allows for remote operation with minimal ongoing obligations. The license is valid for one year and must be renewed annually.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator for the Belize crypto exchange license is the International Financial Services Commission (IFSC). The IFSC oversees all financial services in Belize, including digital assets. The license type is the Digital Assets Business License, which covers exchange, custody, and wallet services.

There is no distinction between different classes of licenses in Belize; one license covers all digital asset activities. This contrasts with jurisdictions like Lithuania, where separate licenses are needed for exchange and custody. The IFSC requires that the licensee be a company incorporated in Belize, typically as an International Business Company (IBC).

Capital Requirement

The Belize crypto exchange license does not have a statutory minimum capital requirement. However, the IFSC expects that the company maintains sufficient capital to cover operational expenses and potential liabilities. In practice, most applicants allocate between EUR 10,000 and EUR 50,000 as working capital. This is far lower than EU MiCA requirements, which range from EUR 50,000 to EUR 150,000 depending on the service class.

Consulting24 advises that you demonstrate adequate financial resources in your business plan. The IFSC may request proof of funds or a bank statement showing the capital. Since there is no fixed minimum, you have flexibility to set a capital level that matches your business scale. For a small startup, EUR 15,000 is often sufficient. For a larger operation, EUR 50,000 provides a comfortable buffer.

We have seen applications approved with as little as EUR 10,000 in capital, provided the business plan shows realistic projections. The key is to show that you can sustain operations for at least 6 months without revenue. Our team helps you prepare a convincing financial plan.

Compare this to the requirements of other jurisdictions, where capital can be a major barrier. Belize's flexibility is a major draw for early-stage companies.

Tax Treatment

Belize offers a territorial tax system, meaning that income earned outside Belize is generally exempt from local taxes. For a crypto exchange license holder, profits derived from international clients are not subject to Belize corporate income tax. There is no capital gains tax, no withholding tax on dividends, and no VAT on digital services. The only tax obligation is an annual license renewal fee and a small business tax (if applicable) for local income.

This tax regime makes Belize highly attractive for crypto businesses that serve a global clientele. However, you must comply with tax reporting requirements in your home country or where your clients are based. Consulting24 recommends consulting with a tax advisor to ensure full compliance with your local tax obligations.

For example, a Belize-licensed exchange serving customers in Asia and Africa would pay zero corporate tax in Belize. The only tax liability would be in the countries where the company is tax resident (if any) or where the customers are located. This can lead to significant savings compared to jurisdictions with worldwide taxation.

Allowed Activities

The Belize Digital Assets Business License permits the following activities:

  • Operating a digital assets exchange (order book, matching engine).
  • Custody and safekeeping of digital assets.
  • Wallet services (hot and cold wallets).
  • Brokerage and trading services.
  • Token issuance and initial exchange offerings (IEOs) with prior approval.
  • Staking services (if disclosed in the business plan).

Activities that are NOT allowed include derivatives trading (unless specifically authorized), gambling-related tokens, and services that violate AML/CFT regulations. The IFSC may also restrict the listing of privacy coins or tokens with anonymous features. Consulting24 helps you define your activity scope to align with IFSC guidelines.

For example, a client who wanted to offer margin trading had to apply for a separate authorization. Another client who planned to list Monero was advised against it due to AML concerns. We help you navigate these restrictions and find compliant alternatives.

If your business model includes activities not explicitly covered, we can discuss with the IFSC on a case-by-case basis. The license is flexible but requires transparency.

Banking and Payment Solutions

Opening a bank account for a Belize crypto exchange can be challenging due to the perceived risk. However, several international banks and payment processors accept Belize-licensed entities. Options include:

  • Traditional banks in Belize (e.g., Atlantic Bank, Belize Bank) for fiat accounts. These may require a local director or physical presence.
  • EMIs (electronic money institutions) in the EU or UK for multi-currency accounts. Examples include TransferWise (now Wise) and Revolut Business, though they may have restrictions on crypto.
  • Cryptocurrency-friendly banks in Switzerland or Singapore, such as SEBA Bank or Sygnum, but these have high minimum deposits.
  • Payment processors like MoonPay, Simplex, or Banxa for fiat on-ramp services. These integrate with your exchange platform.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Incomplete Documentation: Missing or poorly prepared AML policies are a top reason for delays. Use our templates to ensure completeness. We have seen applications rejected because the business plan lacked financial projections.
  • Ignoring Beneficial Ownership: The IFSC requires full disclosure of ultimate beneficial owners (UBOs). Failing to identify UBOs can lead to rejection. Ensure you have a clear ownership structure.
  • Underestimating Ongoing Costs: While the license fee is low, annual renewal, compliance, and legal costs add up. Budget at least EUR 5,000 per year. Some clients forget to factor in the cost of a local agent.
  • Overlooking Tax Reporting: Even if Belize taxes are low, you must report income to your home country. Consult a tax professional to avoid penalties.

No statutory minimum capital is required. However, the IFSC expects sufficient working capital to cover expenses and liabilities. Most applicants allocate between EUR 10,000 and EUR 50,000.
