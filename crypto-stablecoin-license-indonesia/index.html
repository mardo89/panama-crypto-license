Overview: What Is the Crypto Stablecoin License Indonesia?

The crypto stablecoin license Indonesia refers to the regulatory approval required to issue, trade, or manage stablecoins within the Indonesian jurisdiction. As of 2026, the primary regulator for crypto assets is Bappebti, but the OJK is transitioning to oversee digital financial assets under the UU P2SK. Stablecoins are treated as digital assets and must comply with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) regulations set by the Indonesian Financial Transaction Reports and Analysis Center (PPATK).

This license is not a single permit but a combination of registrations: a crypto asset trader license from Bappebti for exchanges, and potentially a separate approval for stablecoin issuance under OJK's forthcoming framework. Consulting24 advises clients on the exact requirements based on their business model and coordinates with local legal experts to ensure full compliance. The process typically involves multiple government agencies, and the timeline can be unpredictable without experienced guidance.

For example, a stablecoin issuer must first register as a crypto asset trader with Bappebti, then apply for a digital financial asset permit from OJK once the framework is finalized. This dual registration can take 6-12 months, depending on the completeness of documentation and regulatory backlog. Consulting24 helps streamline the process by preparing all required materials in advance and liaising with authorities.

Who Needs a Crypto Stablecoin License in Indonesia?

Any business entity involved in the creation, distribution, or trading of stablecoins in Indonesia must obtain the necessary licenses. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges listing stablecoins
  • Stablecoin issuers (e.g., fiat-backed, crypto-backed, or algorithmic)
  • Payment service providers integrating stablecoins
  • Asset managers offering stablecoin-based products

Foreign companies looking to operate in Indonesia must establish a local entity (PT) and comply with all regulatory requirements. Consulting24 helps clients determine if they need a full license or a simpler registration, depending on their activities. For example, a small fintech startup may only require a Bappebti registration, while a large stablecoin issuer will need both Bappebti and OJK approvals.

Additionally, any entity that facilitates stablecoin transfers or provides custody services must be licensed. Even if you are a foreign stablecoin issuer targeting Indonesian users, you may need to partner with a local licensed entity or establish a subsidiary. Consulting24 can advise on the most cost-effective structure, such as using a licensed exchange as a distribution partner rather than obtaining a full license yourself.

License Type & Regulator

The primary regulator for crypto assets in Indonesia is Bappebti (Commodity Futures Trading Regulatory Agency). For stablecoins specifically, the Financial Services Authority (OJK) is expected to assume regulatory authority under the UU P2SK law, which was enacted in 2023 and is being implemented gradually. As of 2026, OJK has issued draft regulations for digital financial assets, including stablecoins, but the final framework is still evolving.

The license type is a crypto asset trader registration for exchanges, and a digital financial asset issuer permit for stablecoin issuers. Consulting24 advises that clients monitor OJK's announcements closely and prepare for dual compliance with Bappebti and OJK during the transition period. This dual oversight can complicate the application process, but with proper planning, it is manageable.

For instance, a stablecoin issuer must first obtain a Bappebti registration to operate a crypto asset trading platform, then apply for an OJK permit to issue the stablecoin as a digital financial asset. The OJK permit may require additional capital, reserve audits, and consumer protection measures. Consulting24 helps you prepare for both sets of requirements simultaneously to avoid delays.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a crypto asset trader in Indonesia is set by Bappebti at IDR 1 trillion (approximately EUR 60,000) for a licensed exchange. For stablecoin issuers under OJK's upcoming framework, the capital requirement is expected to be higher, potentially IDR 5-10 trillion (EUR 300,000-600,000), depending on the type of stablecoin and the issuer's risk profile.

These figures are subject to change as OJK finalizes its regulations. Consulting24 advises clients to prepare a strong capital plan and engage with local legal counsel to ensure compliance. For smaller operators, a partnership with an existing licensed exchange may be a more viable option. Compare this to Panama, which has no minimum capital requirement, making it attractive for startups.

In practice, the capital must be deposited in a local bank account and maintained throughout the license period. Tax Treatment for Stablecoins in Indonesia

Indonesia imposes a value-added tax (VAT) of 11% on the sale of crypto assets, including stablecoins, and a 0.1% income tax on gains from crypto transactions. Stablecoin issuers may also be subject to corporate income tax at the standard rate of 22% (reduced to 20% for certain qualifying entities).

Tax treatment for stablecoins is still evolving, and the government may introduce specific regulations for stablecoins as digital payment instruments. Consulting24 recommends working with a local tax advisor to structure operations efficiently and ensure compliance with Indonesian tax laws. For example, holding stablecoins as reserves may have different tax implications than trading them.

Additionally, stablecoin issuers must report transactions to the tax authority (DJP) and may be subject to withholding tax on interest or fees. Consulting24 can coordinate with local tax experts to minimize your tax burden while staying compliant. For instance, using a special purpose vehicle in a lower-tax jurisdiction like Panama may reduce overall tax costs, but transfer pricing rules must be observed.

Allowed Activities Under the License

A crypto stablecoin license in Indonesia permits the following activities:

  • Issuance of fiat-backed, crypto-backed, or algorithmic stablecoins
  • Trading of stablecoins on a licensed exchange
  • Custody and management of stablecoin reserves
  • Cross-border transfers and payments using stablecoins
  • Integration with payment systems and e-money services

Activities that are explicitly prohibited include using stablecoins for gambling, money laundering, or any illegal transactions. Consulting24 ensures that your business model aligns with the allowed activities and advises on any restrictions. For instance, algorithmic stablecoins may face additional scrutiny due to their volatility risks, and you may need to provide extra disclosures to regulators.

Furthermore, stablecoin issuers are typically not allowed to lend out reserves without explicit permission, as this could undermine the peg. Consulting24 helps you design a compliant reserve management strategy, such as holding reserves in government bonds or cash equivalents, and reporting regularly to regulators.

Applying for a crypto stablecoin license in Indonesia can be fraught with pitfalls. Here are common mistakes and how to avoid them:
