What is the Crypto Stablecoin License in Portugal?

As of 2026, Portugal does not have a specific 'stablecoin license' separate from its general crypto-asset service provider (CASP) regime under MiCA. Instead, stablecoin issuers and service providers must obtain authorization as a CASP from the Bank of Portugal, or if issuing asset-referenced tokens (ARTs) or e-money tokens (EMTs), comply with additional requirements under MiCA Titles III and IV. The Bank of Portugal is the primary regulator for CASPs, while the CMVM oversees certain aspects. The license allows companies to offer services such as custody, exchange, and transfer of crypto-assets, including stablecoins. The process involves a thorough assessment of the applicant's governance, capital, and operational resilience. Portugal's framework is harmonized with EU standards, making it a credible jurisdiction for stablecoin projects seeking pan-European access. For example, a stablecoin issuer must submit a detailed whitepaper to the Bank of Portugal, outlining the token's design, reserve assets, and redemption rights. The regulator then reviews the whitepaper for compliance with MiCA's transparency and consumer protection rules. This process typically takes 2-3 months for approval, separate from the CASP authorization. Consulting24 helps clients prepare these documents to meet regulatory expectations.

Who Needs This License?

Any company that intends to issue stablecoins (ARTs or EMTs) or provide crypto-asset services involving stablecoins in Portugal needs to be authorized. This includes:

  • Stablecoin issuers, for example companies launching a fiat-backed stablecoin like a EUR-pegged token or a crypto-backed stablecoin such as a DAI-like asset.
  • Exchanges that list stablecoins, whether centralized or decentralized platforms.
  • Custodial wallet providers that hold stablecoins on behalf of clients.
  • Payment service providers integrating stablecoins for remittances or merchant payments.
  • DeFi platforms that offer stablecoin-related services, such as lending or staking pools.

If your project involves a stablecoin that is an ART or EMT, you must comply with MiCA's specific requirements, which may include a whitepaper approval, reserve management, and additional capital. For example, an ART issuer must maintain a reserve of assets equal to the value of tokens in circulation, with strict custody and audit requirements. The CMVM (Comissão do Mercado de Valores Mobiliários) may be involved for tokens that qualify as securities. The license is a single EU passport, allowing services across all 27 member states. Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for CASPs in Portugal is tiered by service class: EUR 50,000 for certain services (e.g., custody, exchange), EUR 125,000 for others (e.g., order execution), and EUR 150,000 for the highest tier (e.g., operating a trading platform). For stablecoin issuers (ARTs/EMTs), additional capital may be required based on the reserve size. Specifically, MiCA requires ART issuers to hold own funds of at least EUR 350,000 or 2% of the reserve assets, whichever is higher. EMT issuers must hold own funds of at least the higher of EUR 350,000 or 2% of the average e-money float. These figures are set by MiCA and are uniform across the EU. For example, a company offering both custody and a trading platform would need the highest tier of EUR 150,000. We advise on the most capital-efficient structure.

Tax Treatment

Portugal has a favorable tax regime for crypto. As of 2026, individuals holding crypto for more than one year are exempt from capital gains tax. For companies, profits from crypto activities are subject to the standard corporate income tax rate of 21% (plus municipal surcharges up to 1.5%). Stablecoin transactions may be subject to VAT if they involve goods or services. The tax treatment of stablecoin issuance (e.g., reserve income) is complex and requires specialized advice. For example, interest earned on reserve assets may be taxed as ordinary income. Additionally, Portugal has a participation exemption regime that may apply to dividends from subsidiaries. Allowed Activities

A Portuguese CASP license permits the following activities under MiCA:

  • Custody and administration of crypto-assets (including stablecoins).
  • Operation of a crypto-asset trading platform.
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for fiat currency or other crypto-assets.
  • Execution of orders on behalf of clients.
  • Placement of crypto-assets.
  • Receiving and transmitting orders.
  • Advising on crypto-assets.
  • Portfolio management.
  • Transfer services for crypto-assets.

Stablecoin issuers can also issue ARTs and EMTs, subject to additional requirements. The license is passportable across the EU. For example, a Portuguese CASP can offer custody services to clients in Germany without needing a separate license there. This is a key advantage for projects targeting the European market. Frequently asked questions

Does Portugal have a specific stablecoin license?

No, Portugal does not have a standalone stablecoin license. Stablecoin issuers and service providers must obtain a CASP authorization under MiCA from the Bank of Portugal. Issuers of ARTs or EMTs must also comply with additional requirements in MiCA Titles III and IV.

What is the minimum capital for a stablecoin issuer in Portugal?

For CASPs, capital ranges from EUR 50,000 to 150,000 depending on services. For ART issuers, own funds must be at least EUR 350,000 or 2% of reserve assets, whichever is higher. EMT issuers need at least EUR 350,000 or 2% of average e-money float.

How long does it take to get a CASP license in Portugal?

The process typically takes 6-12 months, depending on application completeness and regulatory workload. Consulting24 can help streamline the process with thorough documentation and ongoing liaison with the Bank of Portugal.

Can I passport my Portuguese CASP license to other EU countries?

Yes, the CASP license is passportable across all 27 EU member states under MiCA. This allows you to offer services throughout the EU without needing separate licenses in each country.

What are the tax implications for stablecoin companies in Portugal?

Corporate profits are taxed at 21% (plus up to 1.5% municipal surcharge). Individuals holding crypto for over one year are exempt from capital gains tax. Stablecoin issuance income may be subject to complex tax rules; professional advice is recommended.

Is Portugal cheaper than other EU jurisdictions for crypto licensing?

Portugal's total setup cost (EUR 80,000+) is higher than Lithuania (EUR 15,000-25,000) but lower than some others. However, Portugal offers a favorable tax regime for individuals and a pragmatic regulator. Compare with Panama (EUR 6,000 flat) for a lower-cost alternative.

What documents are needed for a CASP application in Portugal?

You need a detailed business plan, governance policies, AML/CFT procedures, risk management framework, technical documentation, and proof of capital. For stablecoin issuers, a whitepaper and reserve management plan are also required.
