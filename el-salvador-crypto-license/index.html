What Is the El Salvador Crypto License?

The El Salvador crypto license is a regulatory authorization issued by the CNAD (Comisión Nacional de Activos Digitales) that allows firms to provide digital asset services legally within the country. It comes in two main forms: a Digital Asset Service Provider (DASP) license under the Digital Assets Issuance Law (Ley de Emisión de Activos Digitales) and a Bitcoin Service Provider (BSP) license under the Bitcoin Law (Ley Bitcoin).

The DASP license covers a broad range of activities such as exchange, custody, brokerage, and token issuance. The BSP license is tailored for services specifically involving Bitcoin, taking advantage of its legal tender status. Both licenses are part of El Salvador's forward-looking framework that aims to attract crypto innovation while ensuring consumer protection and AML compliance.

Key features include:

  • Regulation by CNAD, a dedicated digital assets regulator.
  • Bitcoin as legal tender, enabling seamless integration with the economy.
  • Tax incentives for qualifying digital asset activities (e.g., exemption from capital gains tax on Bitcoin).
  • Clear licensing pathways for both digital assets and Bitcoin-specific services.

Who Needs an El Salvador Crypto License?

Any entity that provides digital asset services to residents of El Salvador or operates from within the country must obtain the appropriate license. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (centralized and decentralized platforms).
  • Custodial wallet providers holding private keys on behalf of clients.
  • Brokerage and trading platforms facilitating buy/sell orders.
  • Payment processors handling Bitcoin or other digital assets.
  • Token issuers conducting initial DASP offerings (IDOs) or security token offerings (STOs).
  • Bitcoin ATM operators and other Bitcoin-specific service providers.

License Types & Regulator

The primary regulator is the Comisión Nacional de Activos Digitales (CNAD), established under the Digital Assets Issuance Law. CNAD oversees both DASP and BSP licenses.

  • DASP License (Digital Asset Service Provider): Covers all digital assets except Bitcoin. Activities include exchange, custody, brokerage, and issuance. Requires compliance with AML/CFT rules and capital adequacy.
  • BSP License (Bitcoin Service Provider): Specifically for services involving Bitcoin, such as Bitcoin exchanges, Bitcoin wallets, and Bitcoin payment processing. Benefits from Bitcoin's legal tender status.

Both licenses require a local registered office, a compliance officer, and adherence to reporting obligations. The application is submitted to CNAD, which conducts a thorough due diligence review. Consulting24 advises on the best license type for your operations.

Capital Requirement

El Salvador does not impose a single minimum capital requirement for all license types. Instead, the capital requirement is determined on a case-by-case basis by CNAD, depending on the scope and risk profile of the services. Typically, a minimum of $50,000 is expected for basic services, with higher amounts for more complex activities like custody or exchange.

This is in contrast to EU MiCA regimes, where capital tiers are fixed (e.g., €50,000 for simple services, €125,000 for exchanges, €150,000 for custody). El Salvador’s flexible approach can be advantageous for smaller startups. Consulting24 helps you prepare a capital adequacy plan that meets CNAD’s expectations.

Tax Treatment

El Salvador offers attractive tax incentives for digital asset businesses:

  • No capital gains tax on Bitcoin transactions (as Bitcoin is legal tender).
  • Income tax exemption for qualifying digital asset activities for a period of up to 15 years (under the Digital Assets Issuance Law).
  • No VAT on digital asset transactions.
  • Low corporate income tax of 30% for non-exempt activities, but many crypto services qualify for exemptions.

These incentives make El Salvador one of the most tax-friendly jurisdictions for crypto businesses. However, tax treatment depends on the specific license and activities. Consulting24 advises on structuring your operations to maximize tax benefits.

Allowed Activities Under the License

An El Salvador crypto license permits a wide range of activities, depending on the license type:

  • Exchange: Trading digital assets for fiat or other digital assets.
  • Custody: Safekeeping of digital assets on behalf of clients.
  • Brokerage: Facilitating transactions between buyers and sellers.
  • Payment services: Processing payments using digital assets, especially Bitcoin.
  • Token issuance: Conducting initial DASP offerings (IDOs) or security token offerings (STOs).
  • Mining: Operating Bitcoin mining facilities (requires additional permits).
  • ATM operation: Running Bitcoin ATMs.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain an El Salvador crypto license involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application consultation: Discuss your business model with Consulting24 to determine the appropriate license type and requirements.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a local company in El Salvador (e.g., Sociedad Anónima) with a registered office.
  3. Document preparation: Compile necessary documents: business plan, AML/CFT policies, organizational structure, financial projections, background checks on directors.
  4. Submission to CNAD: File the application along with the required fees. CNAD will acknowledge receipt and begin review.
  5. Due diligence review: CNAD conducts background checks and may request additional information. This stage typically takes 2-4 months.
  6. License issuance: Upon approval, you receive the license certificate. You must then comply with ongoing reporting obligations.
  7. Post-license compliance: Submit periodic reports to CNAD, maintain AML program, and undergo annual audits.

Banking & Payment Services

Access to banking in El Salvador has improved significantly since the Bitcoin Law. Several local banks now accept crypto-related businesses, though they still conduct rigorous due diligence. Options include:

  • Digital payment providers like Chivo Wallet (government-backed) can be used for Bitcoin transactions.

Compliance & Trust: What License Holders Must Do

Maintaining an El Salvador crypto license requires ongoing compliance:

  • AML/CFT program: Implement robust policies including KYC, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting.
  • Annual audits: Submit audited financial statements to CNAD.
  • Regular reporting: File periodic reports on transactions, volumes, and compliance activities.
  • Compliance officer: Appoint a resident compliance officer responsible for regulatory adherence.
  • Record keeping: Maintain records of all transactions for at least five years.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a DASP and BSP license in El Salvador?

A DASP license covers digital assets other than Bitcoin, such as Ethereum or stablecoins, and includes activities like exchange, custody, and token issuance. A BSP license is specifically for Bitcoin-related services, taking advantage of Bitcoin’s legal tender status. Both are regulated by CNAD.

How long does it take to get an El Salvador crypto license?

The process typically takes 3 to 6 months from application submission to issuance. This includes document preparation, CNAD’s due diligence review (2-4 months), and any follow-up queries. Consulting24 coordinates to streamline the timeline.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a DASP license?

There is no fixed minimum; CNAD determines it case-by-case based on risk. Typically, $50,000 is expected for basic services, with higher amounts for custody or exchange. This is more flexible than EU MiCA’s fixed tiers.

Are there tax incentives for crypto businesses in El Salvador?

Yes. Bitcoin transactions are exempt from capital gains tax. Qualifying digital asset activities may receive income tax exemptions for up to 15 years. No VAT applies to digital asset transactions. Corporate income tax is 30% for non-exempt activities.

Can I operate a Bitcoin ATM with a BSP license?

Yes, Bitcoin ATM operation is covered under the BSP license, as it involves Bitcoin services. You must comply with AML/CFT requirements and register the ATM locations with CNAD.

Do I need a local company to apply for a license?

Yes, you must incorporate a local company in El Salvador (e.g., Sociedad Anónima) with a registered office and a local director. Consulting24 can assist with company setup.

Is banking accessible for crypto license holders in El Salvador?

Several commercial banks accept licensed crypto firms, but due diligence is rigorous. Options include Banco Agrícola and Banco Cuscatlán. Consulting24 can introduce you to banking partners.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations?

License holders must maintain an AML/CFT program, submit annual audited financial statements, file periodic reports to CNAD, and appoint a compliance officer. Record keeping is required for at least five years.

Can I apply for both DASP and BSP licenses simultaneously?

Yes, if your business involves both Bitcoin and other digital assets, you can apply for both licenses. CNAD will review each application separately, and you must meet the requirements for each.

How does El Salvador compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Panama has no dedicated crypto license; firms use general business licenses. El Salvador offers explicit regulation, tax incentives, and Bitcoin legal tender. Panama company setup is flat EUR 6,000, but lacks regulatory clarity. See our Panama page for details.
