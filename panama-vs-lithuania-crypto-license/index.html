What Is a Panama Crypto License?

Panama does not have a specific crypto license in the traditional sense. Instead, crypto businesses operate under Panama's general corporate and financial services framework, often using a Panamanian corporation (Sociedad Anónima) combined with a registration or declaration with the local financial regulator (Superintendencia de Bancos de Panamá or SBP) if engaging in regulated activities. As of 2026, Panama has been moving toward a more formalized crypto regulation, but it remains a lighter-touch jurisdiction compared to EU member states.

The Panama crypto license is effectively a company setup with a legal opinion and compliance package that allows you to offer crypto services such as exchange, wallet, and payment processing without a dedicated crypto-specific license. This makes it attractive for startups seeking speed and low cost. However, the lack of a dedicated regulatory framework means that businesses must rely on legal structuring and compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) laws.

For example, a typical Panama setup includes incorporation of a Panama corporation, drafting of a legal opinion confirming that crypto activities are permissible under Panamanian law, and registration with the SBP for AML purposes. The company can then open a corporate bank account and begin operations. The total cost is a flat EUR 6,000, making it one of the most affordable options globally.

What Is a Lithuania Crypto License?

Lithuania offers a fully regulated crypto license under the Law on Prevention of Money Laundering and Terrorist Financing (PMLTF) and the Law on Markets in Crypto-assets (MiCA) implementation. As an EU member state, Lithuania has transposed MiCA into national law, requiring crypto-asset service providers (CASPs) to obtain a license from the Bank of Lithuania. The license allows companies to legally operate across the EU under the single passport regime.

The Lithuania crypto license is a comprehensive authorization that covers exchange services, wallet custody, and other crypto activities. It requires a minimum capital of EUR 125,000 for most services (or EUR 150,000 for certain high-risk activities), a detailed business plan, AML policies, and a local presence. The process is rigorous but provides credibility and access to the European market.

For instance, a company offering crypto-to-fiat exchange and custody must hold EUR 150,000 in capital, appoint a local AML officer, and submit quarterly reports to the Bank of Lithuania. The application process takes 3-6 months, and ongoing compliance includes annual audits and AML training for staff. Despite the higher cost and time, the license is highly respected by banks and institutional partners.

Who Needs a Panama vs Lithuania Crypto License?

The choice between Panama and Lithuania depends on your target market, business model, and budget. Panama is ideal for:

  • Startups and small businesses looking for a low-cost entry point (EUR 6,000 flat fee for company setup).
  • Businesses targeting Latin American or global markets without a strong EU focus.
  • Projects that require fast incorporation (2-4 weeks) and minimal ongoing compliance.
  • Token issuers, DeFi protocols, and NFT platforms that do not require a formal license but need a legal entity.

Lithuania is better suited for:

  • Companies aiming to serve EU customers and benefit from the MiCA passport.
  • Businesses that need a regulated status to partner with banks, payment processors, or institutional investors.
  • Projects with higher budgets (EUR 125,000 minimum capital) and willingness to comply with strict AML/KYC requirements.
  • Exchanges and custodians seeking to build trust with regulators and clients.

For example, a crypto payment gateway targeting European merchants would benefit from Lithuania's license, while a Latin American remittance platform might prefer Panama's flexibility.

Capital Requirement

Panama has no statutory minimum capital for crypto businesses. You can start with as little as EUR 6,000 (the company setup fee). However, you must maintain sufficient funds to cover operational expenses and AML compliance costs. In practice, banks may require a minimum deposit of EUR 10,000-50,000 to open a corporate account.

Lithuania requires a minimum capital of EUR 125,000 for most crypto services (exchange, wallet custody). For certain services like crypto-to-fiat exchange, the requirement is EUR 150,000. This capital must be paid up and held in a Lithuanian bank account or EU credit institution. The capital serves as a buffer for operational risks and demonstrates financial stability to regulators.

For example, a Lithuania CASP offering exchange and custody must show EUR 125,000 in paid-up capital. This can be in cash or liquid assets. The capital must be maintained throughout the license period. Failure to meet capital requirements can result in license suspension.

Tax Treatment

Panama operates a territorial tax system: income earned outside Panama is generally tax-free. Crypto trading profits from non-Panamanian sources are not subject to local income tax. However, income sourced in Panama (e.g., from local customers) may be taxed at standard corporate rates (25%). There is no VAT on crypto services. Panama also offers tax exemptions for offshore companies that do not conduct business within Panama.

Lithuania taxes worldwide income for resident companies. Corporate income tax is 15% (or 0% for small companies under certain conditions). Crypto-to-crypto trades are subject to capital gains tax. VAT applies to crypto services at 21% unless exempted. Lithuania has a tax treaty network to avoid double taxation. For example, a Lithuania CASP earning profits from EU customers pays 15% corporate tax, but may claim credits for taxes paid in other EU states.

Allowed Activities

Panama: Exchange (crypto-to-crypto and crypto-to-fiat), wallet services, payment processing, token issuance (subject to legal structuring), and OTC trading. No specific restrictions on derivatives or DeFi, but must comply with AML laws. Panama allows a wide range of activities without prior approval, but certain activities like banking or securities may require additional licenses.

Lithuania: Exchange, wallet custody, crypto-to-fiat exchange, transfer services, and participation in ICOs/STOs. Under MiCA, activities are defined and regulated. Derivatives and certain DeFi activities may require additional authorization under MiFID II. Lithuania's license covers 10+ service types, each with specific capital and compliance requirements.

Benefits of Each Jurisdiction

Panama: Low cost (EUR 6,000 flat), fast setup (2-4 weeks), no capital requirement, territorial tax, and flexibility. Ideal for early-stage projects and non-EU markets. Panama also offers strong privacy protections and no exchange controls.

Lithuania: EU passport under MiCA, credibility with banks and partners, clear legal framework, and access to a skilled workforce. Suitable for businesses targeting Europe and seeking institutional trust. Lithuania also offers a supportive regulatory environment with a dedicated fintech sandbox.

For example, a startup with a limited budget can launch in Panama for under EUR 10,000, while a growth-stage exchange seeking EU customers would benefit from Lithuania's license despite the higher cost.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Choosing Panama without considering future EU expansion needs. If you plan to serve EU customers later, switching to a Lithuania license may be costly and time-consuming.
  • Underestimating Lithuania's capital requirement and ongoing costs. Ensure you have sufficient funds before starting the application.
  • Failing to implement proper AML/KYC from day one. Both jurisdictions require compliance from the start; non-compliance can lead to fines or license revocation.
  • Not verifying bank account availability before incorporation. Some banks refuse crypto businesses; confirm account opening feasibility first.
  • Ignoring tax obligations in your home country. Even if your company is in Panama or Lithuania, you may still owe taxes in your country of residence.
  • Using a generic legal opinion in Panama. Each business model requires a tailored legal opinion to ensure compliance.

Frequently asked questions

What is the main difference between a Panama and Lithuania crypto license?

Panama offers a company incorporation with a legal compliance package, no dedicated crypto license, low cost (EUR 6,000), and fast setup. Lithuania provides a formal CASP license under MiCA with a minimum capital of EUR 125,000, EU passport, and stricter compliance. Panama suits startups with limited budgets and non-EU focus, while Lithuania is for businesses targeting the European market and needing regulatory credibility.

Can I use a Panama company to serve EU customers?

Yes, but you must comply with EU AML laws and MiCA if you have a branch or actively market to EU residents. Without a local license, you may face restrictions. Many Panama companies serve global customers outside the EU, but for EU-focused business, a Lithuania license is safer.

How long does it take to get a Lithuania crypto license in 2026?

The process typically takes 3-6 months from application submission to approval. Preparation of documents (AML policies, business plan) adds 4-6 weeks. Consulting24 can expedite the process with pre-prepared templates and experienced lawyers.

Is there a minimum capital requirement for Panama?

No statutory minimum. You can start with the EUR 6,000 setup fee. However, banks may require a minimum deposit of USD 10,000-50,000 to open a corporate account. Operational costs are low, making Panama ideal for bootstrapped startups.

What are the ongoing costs for a Panama crypto company?

Annual costs include registered agent fees (approx. EUR 300), legal opinion renewal (EUR 500-1,000), AML compliance software (EUR 200-500), and bank account maintenance (EUR 100-300). Total ongoing costs are typically under EUR 2,000/year.

Does Lithuania require a physical office?

Can I convert a Panama company to a Lithuania license later?

No, you cannot convert. You would need to incorporate a new company in Lithuania and apply for a license separately. However, you can maintain both entities for different markets. Consulting24 can help with multi-jurisdiction structuring.

What activities are prohibited under Lithuania's license?

Activities not covered by MiCA, such as certain derivatives (unless under MiFID), gambling-related tokens, and unregistered securities. DeFi protocols may require additional authorization. Always check with the Bank of Lithuania before launching new services.

How does tax work for a Panama company trading crypto?

If your Panama company earns income from non-Panamanian sources (e.g., trading on international exchanges), it is generally tax-free in Panama. However, if you have a local presence or serve Panamanian customers, income may be taxed at 25%. No VAT on crypto services.

Can I use a Panama company for an ICO or token issuance?
