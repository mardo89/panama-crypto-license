What is a Crypto OTC Desk License Switzerland?

A crypto OTC desk license Switzerland is a regulatory authorization issued by FINMA that allows a company to operate an over-the-counter trading desk for cryptocurrencies. OTC desks facilitate large-volume trades directly between buyers and sellers, often with better pricing and privacy than exchange trading. In Switzerland, such activities fall under financial market regulation, requiring compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and know-your-customer (KYC) rules. The license is typically categorized as a financial intermediary under the AMLA, and depending on the scope, may also require a securities dealer license under the FinSA. FINMA classifies crypto OTC desks as part of the broader "crypto asset trading" sector, subject to specific capital and organizational requirements.

Who Needs a Crypto OTC Desk License in Switzerland?

Any entity that provides OTC trading services in cryptocurrencies on a professional basis must obtain a Swiss crypto license. This includes:

  • OTC desks that match buyers and sellers of digital assets
  • Firms offering block trades or large-volume crypto transactions
  • Brokers or dealers that act as principal in crypto trades
  • Platforms that facilitate peer-to-peer crypto trading with order book or matching engine

If your business handles client funds or assets, or provides custody alongside trading, additional licensing may be required. Even if you operate from abroad, serving Swiss clients may trigger licensing obligations. Consulting24 can help assess your specific situation and advise on the appropriate regulatory path.

License Type and Regulator

The primary regulator for crypto OTC desks in Switzerland is the Swiss Financial Market Supervisory Authority (FINMA). FINMA oversees licensing under the Anti-Money Laundering Act (AMLA) and the Financial Services Act (FinSA). The license type is typically a "financial intermediary" license, but firms that trade on their own account or provide custody may need a "securities dealer" license or a "bank" license, depending on the scale and nature of activities. For most OTC desks, a FINMA AML authorization combined with membership in a self-regulatory organization (SRO) is sufficient. FINMA's 2026 guidelines clarify that crypto OTC desks must comply with enhanced due diligence for high-risk customers and report suspicious transactions. The regulator also requires a physical presence in Switzerland, including a registered office and local management.

Capital Requirement

Switzerland does not have a fixed minimum capital requirement for all crypto OTC desks under the AMLA. However, FINMA expects firms to maintain sufficient capital to cover operational risks and potential losses. For securities dealers, the minimum capital is CHF 1.5 million (approx. EUR 1.5 million). For firms that provide custody, additional capital may be required. In practice, most OTC desks are advised to hold at least CHF 100,000 to CHF 500,000 in capital to demonstrate financial stability. The exact amount depends on the volume of transactions and risk profile. Consulting24 can help determine the appropriate capital level for your business model.

Tax Treatment

Switzerland offers a favorable tax environment for crypto businesses. Corporate income tax rates vary by canton, ranging from 11% to 21% on profits. Cryptocurrencies are treated as assets for tax purposes, and capital gains from trading are generally tax-exempt for private individuals. For corporate OTC desks, profits from trading are subject to corporate income tax. Value-added tax (VAT) is not applied to crypto trading activities, as they are considered exempt financial services. However, advisory fees and other services may be subject to VAT at 7.7%. Switzerland also has a withholding tax of 35% on dividends and interest, but this does not typically apply to crypto trading. It is essential to consult a Swiss tax advisor for your specific situation.

Allowed Activities Under the License

A crypto OTC desk license Switzerland permits the following activities:

  • Facilitating over-the-counter trades of cryptocurrencies (e.g., Bitcoin, Ethereum, stablecoins)
  • Acting as principal or agent in crypto transactions
  • Providing price quotes and liquidity for digital assets
  • Settling trades via on-chain transfers or custodial accounts
  • Offering crypto-to-fiat and fiat-to-crypto conversion services

Additional activities such as custody, staking, or lending may require separate licenses. FINMA strictly prohibits unlicensed activities like operating a crypto exchange or accepting deposits. The license does not cover securities trading unless the firm holds a securities dealer license. Consulting24 can advise on the exact scope of your license.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process for obtaining a crypto OTC desk license Switzerland involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application assessment: Review your business model, target clients, and regulatory obligations. Consulting24 provides a gap analysis.
  2. Company formation: Incorporate a Swiss company (AG or GmbH) with a registered office and local directors.
  3. AML/KYC policy development: Draft compliance manuals, risk assessments, and procedures aligned with FINMA requirements.
  4. SRO membership: Join a recognized self-regulatory organization (e.g., VQF, ARIF, or PolyReg) for AML supervision.
  5. Application submission: Submit the application to FINMA with supporting documents (business plan, financial projections, organizational chart, personal questionnaires for key personnel).
  6. Review and feedback: FINMA reviews the application and may request additional information. This stage takes 2-4 months.
  7. Approval and licensing: Upon approval, FINMA issues the license. The firm must then maintain ongoing compliance, including periodic audits and reporting.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, crypto OTC desks must comply with ongoing requirements:

  • Annual AML audit by an external auditor
  • Submission of periodic reports to FINMA and the SRO
  • Maintenance of AML/KYC records for at least 10 years
  • Immediate reporting of suspicious transactions to the Money Laundering Reporting Office (MROS)
  • Updates to compliance policies as regulations evolve

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Applying for a crypto OTC desk license Switzerland can be complex. Avoid these common pitfalls:

  • Underestimating the importance of a strong AML framework: FINMA scrutinizes AML policies heavily.
  • Choosing the wrong SRO: Not all SROs are suitable for crypto OTC desks; some have stricter requirements.
  • Insufficient capital: Even without a fixed minimum, having too little capital can lead to rejection.
  • Lack of local presence: FINMA requires a physical office and local management.
  • Delaying bank account setup: Without a bank account, the application cannot proceed smoothly.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a crypto OTC desk license Switzerland?

Switzerland does not impose a fixed minimum capital for OTC desks under the AMLA. However, FINMA expects firms to hold sufficient capital to cover operational risks. Typically, CHF 100,000 to CHF 500,000 is recommended. For securities dealers, the minimum is CHF 1.5 million. Exact requirements depend on your business model.

How long does it take to get a Swiss crypto license?

The process usually takes 3 to 6 months from application to approval. This includes company formation, AML policy development, SRO membership, and FINMA review. Complex cases may take longer. Consulting24 can help streamline the process.

Can I operate a crypto OTC desk in Switzerland without a license?

No. Operating a crypto OTC desk on a professional basis without a license is illegal in Switzerland. FINMA requires all financial intermediaries, including crypto OTC desks, to be licensed or registered. Unlicensed operations risk fines and criminal prosecution.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations after obtaining the license?

Licensed firms must conduct annual AML audits, submit reports to FINMA and the SRO, maintain records for 10 years, report suspicious transactions to MROS, and update compliance policies as needed. Non-compliance can lead to penalties or license revocation.

What activities are allowed under a Swiss crypto OTC desk license?

Do I need to have a physical office in Switzerland?

Yes. FINMA requires a registered office and local management in Switzerland. The office must be a physical location, not just a virtual address. This demonstrates a genuine presence and facilitates regulatory oversight.
