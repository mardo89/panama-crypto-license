What Is the Crypto Wallet Custody License in El Salvador?

The crypto wallet custody license El Salvador is a regulatory authorization issued by the Comisión Nacional de Activos Digitales (CNDA) that allows companies to hold, store, and manage digital assets on behalf of clients. It covers custodial wallet services, including private key management, transaction signing, and asset safekeeping. The license is part of the Digital Assets Law passed in 2023, which provides a legal framework for all digital asset service providers (DASPs).

Unlike some jurisdictions that treat custody as an ancillary service, El Salvador has a distinct license category. The CNDA requires applicants to demonstrate strong security protocols, adequate capital, and compliance with AML/CFT regulations. The license is valid for one year and renewable, with ongoing supervisory fees.

Key features of the license include:

  • Legal recognition of digital assets as property
  • Clear rules for custody, including segregation of client funds
  • Requirement for a local physical presence and compliance officer
  • Annual audits and reporting to the CNDA

Who Needs This License?

The crypto wallet custody license El Salvador is mandatory for any entity that offers custodial wallet services to residents or businesses in El Salvador. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges that hold client funds in wallets
  • Wallet providers (e.g., mobile wallets, hardware wallet services)
  • Asset managers offering custody as part of their service
  • Payment processors that store digital assets on behalf of merchants
  • Any DASP that controls private keys for clients

If you only provide non-custodial wallets (where the user controls the keys), you may not need this license, but you should still check with the CNDA. The license is also relevant for international companies targeting the Salvadoran market or using El Salvador as a base for Latin American operations.

License Type & Regulator

The regulator for the crypto wallet custody license El Salvador is the Comisión Nacional de Activos Digitales (CNDA), an autonomous government agency created under the Digital Assets Law. The CNDA is responsible for licensing, supervision, and enforcement for all digital asset service providers.

There is one main license type for wallet custody, but the CNDA may issue different classes based on the scope of services (e.g., retail vs. institutional). The license is non-transferable and requires annual renewal. The CNDA also maintains a public registry of licensed entities.

As of 2026, the CNDA has issued over 20 licenses to crypto firms, including exchanges and wallet providers. The regulator is known for being accessible and responsive, with a clear application process.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a crypto wallet custody license El Salvador is set by the CNDA based on the risk profile of the services offered. As of 2026, the typical minimum is $50,000 for basic custody services, but may increase to $200,000 for higher-risk activities (e.g., lending or margin trading). The capital must be held in a local bank account and cannot be withdrawn without CNDA approval.

The capital serves as a buffer against operational risks and is part of the CNDA's prudential requirements. It is separate from the application fee and must be maintained throughout the license period. Consulting24 can help you determine the exact amount needed for your specific business model.

Tax Treatment

El Salvador offers a favorable tax regime for crypto businesses. The corporate income tax rate is 30%, but there are exemptions for digital asset companies under certain conditions. For example, income from digital asset services may be taxed at a reduced rate of 0% for the first three years if the company is registered under the Digital Assets Law. After that, the standard rate applies.

Value-added tax (VAT) is 13%, but digital asset transactions are generally exempt from VAT. Additionally, there is no capital gains tax on crypto-to-crypto trades, and no withholding tax on dividends paid to foreign shareholders. However, these benefits are subject to compliance with the CNDA's regulations. Consulting24 advises on tax structuring to maximize these advantages.

Allowed Activities

With a crypto wallet custody license El Salvador, you are permitted to:

  • Hold and store digital assets on behalf of clients (custody)
  • Manage private keys and facilitate transactions
  • Offer wallet recovery and backup services
  • Integrate with third-party services (e.g., exchanges, payment gateways)
  • Provide staking services (if explicitly authorized)

Activities that require additional licensing include exchange trading, lending, and issuance of digital assets. The CNDA may impose conditions on the license, such as limiting the types of assets that can be custodied or requiring insurance for large holdings. Always check the latest scope of activities with the CNDA.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process for obtaining a crypto wallet custody license El Salvador involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application consultation: Discuss your business model with Consulting24 to determine eligibility and required documentation.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a local company (Sociedad Anónima) with the Salvadoran Commercial Registry. This typically takes 2-3 weeks.
  3. Document preparation: Prepare a business plan, AML/CFT policies, security protocols, audited financial statements, and background checks on directors.
  4. Submit application: File the application with the CNDA, including all supporting documents and the application fee.
  5. Review and due diligence: The CNDA reviews the application, conducts background checks, and may request additional information.
  6. Approval and license issuance: Once approved, you receive the license, which must be renewed annually.

For payment processing, you can integrate with local payment gateways or use Bitcoin Lightning Network for fast, low-cost transactions.
