What Is the Fastest Singapore Crypto License?

The fastest Singapore crypto license is the Major Payment Institution (MPI) licence under the Payment Services Act (PSA). It is issued by the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) and allows businesses to conduct payment services, including digital payment token (DPT) services, money transfer, and merchant acquisition. The MPI licence is designed for firms that exceed certain transaction thresholds, making it the most common route for crypto exchanges and wallet providers.

As of 2026, the MAS has fully implemented its regulatory framework, requiring all DPT service providers to hold an MPI licence. The application process is rigorous but predictable, with a typical timeline of 6 to 12 months for approval. This makes Singapore one of the fastest jurisdictions for obtaining a crypto licence in Asia, especially compared to neighbours like Hong Kong or Japan.

Key features of the MPI licence include a minimum capital requirement of SGD 250,000 (approximately EUR 170,000), a corporate tax rate of 17%, and a clear list of allowed activities. The MAS also requires strong anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CFT) controls, as well as a physical presence in Singapore. The MAS has published detailed guidelines on DPT services, which are updated regularly to address market developments. For example, in 2025, the MAS introduced enhanced requirements for custody of customer assets, requiring segregation and insurance. These rules are now fully enforced in 2026.

Who Needs This License?

Any business that provides digital payment token (DPT) services in Singapore must hold an MPI licence. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (centralised and decentralised)
  • Digital wallet providers
  • OTC trading desks
  • Payment gateway services involving crypto
  • Token issuance platforms (if they facilitate secondary trading)

Startups and established firms alike are required to register. Even if your company is based outside Singapore, if you offer services to Singapore residents, you may be deemed to be operating in Singapore and need a licence. The MAS has been proactive in enforcing this, with several enforcement actions in 2025-2026 against unlicensed entities. For example, in early 2026, the MAS issued a public warning against an unlicensed OTC desk operating from a co-working space in Raffles Place. The firm was fined SGD 100,000 and ordered to cease operations.

If you are considering other jurisdictions, note that Singapore's regime is particularly suited for firms targeting the Asian market or seeking a reputable, well-regulated base. However, the cost and compliance burden are higher than in some other jurisdictions like Panama, where a flat fee of EUR 6,000 applies. For firms with limited budgets, Panama offers a faster and cheaper alternative, though it lacks the regulatory depth of Singapore.

License Type and Regulator

The relevant regulator is the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS). The licence type is the Major Payment Institution (MPI) licence under the Payment Services Act (PSA). There is also a smaller Standard Payment Institution (SPI) licence for firms with lower transaction volumes, but most crypto businesses require the MPI licence due to the high volume of DPT transactions. The SPI licence has a lower capital requirement of SGD 100,000 but restricts monthly transaction limits to SGD 3 million for DPT services, which is often insufficient for exchanges.

The MAS is known for its rigorous but transparent regulatory approach. It requires applicants to demonstrate strong governance, AML/CFT capabilities, and a sustainable business model. The MAS also mandates that key personnel (directors, compliance officers) be fit and proper, with relevant experience. In 2026, the MAS introduced a new requirement for all MPI licensees to appoint a resident compliance officer who is a Singapore citizen or permanent resident. This has increased the demand for local compliance talent.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for an MPI licence is SGD 250,000 (approximately EUR 170,000). This must be maintained at all times as regulatory capital. The MAS may require higher capital depending on the scale and risk profile of the business. For example, if your exchange handles large transaction volumes or offers custody services, the MAS may impose a capital add-on of up to SGD 500,000. The capital must be held in liquid assets, such as cash or government bonds, and cannot be used for operational expenses.

This is significantly higher than the EUR 6,000 flat fee for a Panama company setup, but Singapore offers access to a sophisticated financial market and a strong reputation. For firms that cannot meet this capital, alternative jurisdictions like Panama or Lithuania may be more suitable. Panama requires no minimum capital, only a flat setup fee of EUR 6,000, making it ideal for startups testing the market.

Tax Treatment

Singapore offers a competitive corporate tax rate of 17%. There are no capital gains taxes, and specific incentives may apply for fintech companies. For example, the MAS's Financial Sector Technology and Innovation (FSTI) scheme provides grants for innovation projects. However, the Inland Revenue Authority of Singapore (IRAS) treats cryptocurrency transactions as subject to goods and services tax (GST) in certain cases, and income from trading may be taxable. As of 2026, IRAS has clarified that DPT exchange fees are exempt from GST, but trading gains are subject to corporate income tax.

For a detailed tax comparison, see our blog on crypto tax in Asia. Note that Singapore does not have a withholding tax on dividends, which is beneficial for repatriating profits. However, the 17% tax rate is higher than Panama's 0% on foreign-source income, making Panama more tax-efficient for international operations.

Allowed Activities

Under the MPI licence, the following activities are permitted:

  • Digital payment token (DPT) exchange services (buying/selling crypto)
  • Cross-border money transfer using DPTs
  • Merchant acquisition for crypto payments
  • E-money issuance (if combined with DPT services)
  • OTC trading of DPTs

Activities not allowed include derivatives trading (which requires a Capital Markets Services licence) and lending/borrowing of DPTs (unless separately licensed). The MAS strictly prohibits unlicensed activities, and enforcement has been active. In 2025, the MAS revoked the licence of a crypto exchange that was offering staking services without approval. The exchange was fined SGD 500,000 and its directors were banned from managing financial institutions for five years.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Pre-application preparation: Incorporate a Singapore company (private limited) with at least one director who is a Singapore resident. Engage a compliance consultant to draft AML/CFT policies, risk assessment, and business plan. The business plan must detail your target market, revenue model, and risk management framework. Consulting24 can assist with this phase.
  2. Submit application to MAS: File the MPI licence application via the MAS's online portal. Include all required documents: business model, financial projections, governance structure, and background checks on directors. The application fee of SGD 5,000 is paid at submission.
  3. Review and queries: MAS typically takes 3-6 months to review. They may request additional information or clarifications. Respond promptly to avoid delays. Common queries include requests for more detail on AML controls or the source of funds for capital.
  4. In-principle approval (IPA): Once satisfied, MAS issues an IPA, allowing you to set up operations under conditions. You must then fulfil any outstanding requirements (e.g., office lease, hiring compliance staff). The IPA is valid for 6 months, after which the licence may lapse if conditions are not met.
  5. Final approval: After meeting all conditions, MAS grants the full MPI licence. You can then commence regulated activities. The MAS may impose additional conditions, such as transaction limits or reporting frequency.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, you must maintain ongoing compliance:

  • Annual AML/CFT audit and submission of reports to MAS within 5 months of year-end.
  • Regular financial reporting and capital maintenance. The MAS requires quarterly reports on transaction volumes and customer funds.
  • Transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting. All transactions above SGD 10,000 must be reported to the Suspicious Transaction Reporting Office (STRO).
  • Periodic fit and proper checks on key personnel. Directors and compliance officers must undergo background checks every two years.

Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the licence. In 2026, the MAS fined a licensed exchange SGD 200,000 for failing to maintain adequate AML controls. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Incomplete application: Missing documents or unclear business model cause delays. For example, many applicants fail to provide a detailed risk assessment or financial projections. Consulting24 can help you prepare a complete package.
  • Underestimating capital: Ensure you have SGD 250,000 in liquid capital. Some firms try to use borrowed funds, which the MAS will detect and reject.
  • Ignoring local presence: You need a physical office and resident director. Virtual offices are not accepted; the MAS requires a lease agreement for a physical space.
  • Poor AML/CFT policies: MAS is strict on compliance; generic policies will be rejected. Your policies must be tailored to your business model and include specific procedures for transaction monitoring and customer due diligence.
  • Overpromising timeline: Even the fastest Singapore crypto license takes 6 months; plan accordingly. Some consultants promise 3-month approval, which is unrealistic. The MAS's average processing time in 2025 was 8 months.
