What Is a Crypto Exchange License in Bermuda?

A crypto exchange license in Bermuda is a regulatory authorization issued by the Bermuda Monetary Authority under the Digital Asset Business Act 2018 (DABA). It allows companies to operate a digital asset exchange, provide custodial services, and engage in other digital asset activities. Bermuda's regime is known for its high standards of compliance, anti-money laundering (AML) controls, and investor protection, making it a trusted hub for institutional crypto businesses.

The license is classified into three tiers based on the scope and risk of activities. The BMA conducts a thorough assessment of the applicant's business model, governance, and financial soundness. Once licensed, firms must adhere to ongoing reporting and auditing requirements. For example, a Class F license holder must submit quarterly financial statements and undergo an annual audit by a BMA-approved auditor. The BMA also expects a strong risk management framework, including cybersecurity protocols and business continuity planning. Who Needs a Bermuda Crypto Exchange License?

Any entity that wishes to operate a digital asset exchange, facilitate trading of cryptocurrencies, or provide custodial wallet services in or from Bermuda must obtain a Digital Asset Business License. This includes:

  • Centralized and decentralized exchanges offering trading pairs
  • Over-the-counter (OTC) trading desks
  • Digital asset custodians and wallet providers
  • Brokerage services for digital assets
  • Issuers of digital assets (if engaging in exchange activities)

If your business model involves any of these activities, you should consider Bermuda as a jurisdiction. Consulting24 can help you assess whether Bermuda is the right fit for your crypto exchange. For example, a startup focusing on a niche token trading pair might benefit from Bermuda's regulatory clarity, while a larger exchange targeting institutional clients may find the tax advantages compelling.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator for crypto exchange licenses in Bermuda is the Bermuda Monetary Authority (BMA). The license type is the Digital Asset Business License, which is divided into three classes:

ClassActivities PermittedCapital Requirement
Class FFull scope: exchange, custody, and other digital asset servicesHigher capital (exact amount determined by BMA)
Class MMedium scope: limited services (e.g., exchange only)Moderate capital
Class RRestricted scope: small-scale or pilot activitiesLower capital

The BMA assesses each application on a case-by-case basis, so the exact capital requirement is negotiated. Consulting24 advises clients on the appropriate class and capital structure. For instance, a startup with a simple exchange model may apply for Class M, while a full-service platform offering custody and staking would need Class F.

Capital Requirements

Bermuda does not prescribe a fixed minimum capital for all digital asset businesses. Instead, the BMA determines the capital requirement based on the risk profile and scope of activities. For a full-scope exchange (Class F), capital is typically in the range of $100,000 to $500,000, but this can vary. The capital must be maintained as liquid assets and may be in the form of cash or highly liquid securities.

Consulting24 advises clients on structuring their capital to meet BMA expectations. We recommend budgeting for at least $200,000 in initial capital for a standard exchange operation. For example, a Class M exchange might require $100,000, while a Class F exchange with custody services might need $400,000. The capital serves as a buffer against operational risks and must be held in a Bermuda bank account.

Tax Treatment in Bermuda

Bermuda offers a highly favorable tax environment for crypto businesses. There is no corporate income tax, no capital gains tax, no withholding tax, and no value-added tax (VAT). The only taxes are payroll tax (on employee salaries) and social insurance contributions. This makes Bermuda a tax-neutral jurisdiction for crypto exchanges, allowing them to retain more of their profits.

However, companies must still comply with international tax reporting standards, such as Common Reporting Standard (CRS) and FATCA, if applicable. Consulting24 can connect you with tax advisors familiar with Bermuda's regime. For example, a Bermuda-based exchange earning $10 million in trading fees would pay zero corporate tax, whereas in the EU, it might face 15-20% corporate tax under MiCA jurisdictions.

Allowed Activities Under the License

A Digital Asset Business License in Bermuda permits the following activities:

  • Operating a digital asset exchange (matching buyers and sellers)
  • Providing custodial services for digital assets
  • Executing orders on behalf of clients
  • Facilitating the transfer of digital assets
  • Providing digital asset wallet services
  • Engaging in initial exchange offerings (IEOs) with BMA approval

Activities that are explicitly not allowed include providing financial advice without a separate license, and dealing in securities without a securities license. The BMA may impose conditions on the license to limit certain activities. For instance, a Class R license may restrict the number of users or transaction volume. Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining a crypto exchange license in Bermuda involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step:

  1. Pre-application consultation: Discuss your business model with the BMA (optional but recommended).
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Bermuda company with the Registrar of Companies.
  3. Prepare documentation: Draft business plan, AML/KYC policies, risk management framework, and governance structure.
  4. Submit application: File the Digital Asset Business License application with the BMA, including all supporting documents and fees.
  5. BMA review: The BMA assesses the application, conducts background checks, and may request additional information.
  6. Approval and issuance: If approved, the BMA issues the license, often with conditions.
  7. Post-licensing compliance: Submit periodic reports, undergo audits, and maintain ongoing compliance.

The entire process typically takes 3-5 months. Consulting24 provides project management and local coordination to ensure a smooth application. Once licensed, crypto exchanges in Bermuda must comply with ongoing obligations:

  • Quarterly and annual financial reporting

  • Maintaining minimum capital requirements
  • Notification of any material changes to the business

Common Mistakes to Avoid

When applying for a Bermuda crypto exchange license, avoid these pitfalls:

  • Incomplete documentation: The BMA requires detailed business plans and policies. Missing information delays the process.
  • Underestimating capital: Ensure you have sufficient capital to cover the BMA's expectations.
  • Ignoring beneficial ownership: All ultimate beneficial owners must be disclosed and pass background checks.
  • Lack of local presence: While not mandatory, having a local director or office can strengthen the application.
  • Rushing the process: The BMA values thoroughness. Allow at least 3-5 months.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital required for a Bermuda crypto exchange license?

Bermuda does not have a fixed minimum capital. The BMA determines capital on a case-by-case basis, typically ranging from $100,000 to $500,000 for a full-scope exchange. Consulting24 can help you estimate the appropriate capital for your business model.

How long does it take to get a Bermuda crypto exchange license?

The typical timeline is 3 to 5 months from application submission to approval. This includes BMA review, background checks, and document verification. A well-prepared application can be processed faster.

What are the tax benefits of a Bermuda crypto license?

Bermuda has no corporate income tax, no capital gains tax, no withholding tax, and no VAT. The only taxes are payroll tax and social insurance. This makes it a tax-neutral jurisdiction for crypto exchanges.

Can I operate a decentralized exchange (DEX) under a Bermuda license?

Yes, the Digital Asset Business License covers both centralized and decentralized exchanges. However, the BMA will assess the control and governance of the DEX. You must demonstrate adequate AML/KYC measures even for a DEX.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements after obtaining the license?

Ongoing obligations include annual audits, quarterly financial reporting, AML/CFT compliance, maintaining minimum capital, and notifying the BMA of material changes. Non-compliance can lead to sanctions.

Is it necessary to have a physical office in Bermuda?

While not explicitly required, having a local presence (e.g., a registered office and local director) strengthens the application. Many applicants use virtual office services. Consulting24 can assist with local arrangements.

Can I apply for a Bermuda license if I am a non-resident?

Yes, non-residents can apply. However, all directors and beneficial owners must pass background checks. You may need to appoint a local director or authorized representative.

How does Bermuda compare to Panama for crypto exchanges?

Panama offers a faster and cheaper company setup (EUR 6,000) with no specific crypto license, but less regulatory clarity. Bermuda provides a regulated framework, tax neutrality, and institutional credibility, but at higher cost and longer timeline.

What activities are not allowed under a Bermuda crypto license?

Activities such as providing financial advice without a separate license or dealing in securities without a securities license are not permitted. The BMA may also impose conditions restricting certain activities.
