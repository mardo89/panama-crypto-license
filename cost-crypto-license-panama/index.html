What Is the Panama Crypto License?

There is no Panama crypto licence today; crypto activity runs through a Panama company with an AML/CTF programme (a dedicated VASP framework, Anteproyecto 314, is pending). It allows companies to offer crypto-related services such as exchange, custody, and payment processing from Panama. The regime is based on Law 23 of 2015, which was updated in 2021 to include virtual assets. Panama does not have a specific crypto law (unlike El Salvador); crypto activity runs through a company that must meet Panama's anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) obligations.

Key features include no minimum capital requirement, 0% tax on foreign-source income, and a relatively fast setup timeline. The cost Panama crypto license is a flat EUR 6,000 for company formation and registration, making it one of the cheapest options globally. However, ongoing compliance costs include annual AML officer fees and accounting, typically EUR 2,000-4,000 per year. For example, a small exchange processing EUR 1 million monthly would pay around EUR 3,500 annually for compliance, while a larger OTC desk might budget EUR 5,000.

License Type & Regulator

There is no VASP registration in Panama today; the applicable setup is a Panama company with an AML/CTF programme. The UAF (Unidad de Análisis Financiero) is Panama's financial intelligence unit and AML authority. Unlike some jurisdictions that issue separate licenses for exchange, custody, etc., Panama uses a single registration covering multiple services. The registration is not a banking or securities license; it is an AML/CTF compliance registration.

For comparison, in the EU under MiCA, a CASP license is required, with capital tiers of EUR 50,000 (receiving/transmitting orders), EUR 125,000 (exchange/custody), and EUR 150,000 (trading platform). Panama has no such capital tiers, which significantly reduces the cost Panama crypto license for startups. There is no crypto-specific regulator conducting inspections today; standard AML record-keeping and company annual filings apply.

Capital Requirement

Panama imposes no minimum capital requirement for VASP registration. This is a major advantage compared to EU jurisdictions under MiCA, where capital requirements range from EUR 50,000 to EUR 150,000 depending on services. Panama also does not require capital to be held in a bank account or as a bond. However, the company must have sufficient operational funds to cover expenses. The absence of a capital requirement is a key factor in the low cost Panama crypto license.

For example, a startup exchange can begin operations with just the EUR 6,000 fee and a few thousand dollars for compliance, whereas in Lithuania the same business would need to deposit EUR 125,000 in a bank account. This makes Panama particularly attractive for bootstrapped projects.

Tax Treatment

Panama operates a territorial tax system: only income sourced within Panama is taxed. Foreign-source income, including most crypto trading and exchange fees from international clients, is tax-free. There is no capital gains tax, no VAT on crypto services, and no withholding tax on dividends paid to non-resident shareholders. Corporate income tax on local-source income is 25%, but this rarely applies to crypto businesses serving global clients.

Panama has also signed tax information exchange agreements (TIEAs) but does not automatically exchange financial account information under CRS for non-resident entities. This makes it a tax-neutral jurisdiction for crypto operations. However, founders must ensure compliance with their home country tax laws. The cost Panama crypto license includes tax registration and obtaining a tax ID. For example, a US-based founder would still owe US tax on worldwide income, but Panama itself does not tax foreign earnings.

Allowed Activities Under the Panama Crypto License

The Panama VASP registration permits the following activities:

  • Exchange between virtual assets and fiat currencies
  • Exchange between one or more forms of virtual assets
  • Transfer of virtual assets (on behalf of clients)
  • Custody and administration of virtual assets
  • Participation in and provision of financial services related to an issuer's offer or sale of virtual assets
  • Payment services using virtual assets

Activities that are not allowed include operating a securities exchange (unless registered with the SMV), issuing tokenized securities without a license, and providing banking services. The cost Panama crypto license covers all these permitted activities under a single registration. For instance, a company can simultaneously run an exchange and a custody service without needing separate licenses.

Common Mistakes When Applying for a Panama Crypto License

Avoid these pitfalls:

  • Underestimating compliance: Many founders think the low cost Panama crypto license means low compliance burden. Banks and compliance reviews expect proper AML policies. We have seen applications rejected due to vague risk assessments.
  • Ignoring tax residency: If you are tax resident in a high-tax country, Panama's territorial tax may not protect you from home country taxation. Always consult a tax advisor.
  • Using a non-compliant resident agent: Some cheap agents do not provide proper AML support. We ensure full compliance by using vetted local partners.
  • Delaying bank account opening: Start the banking process early to avoid operational delays. Some founders wait until after license approval, which can cause cash flow issues.
  • Not preparing a detailed business plan: A sound AML programme includes a clear description of services, target markets, and risk controls. A generic plan may lead to requests for additional information.
