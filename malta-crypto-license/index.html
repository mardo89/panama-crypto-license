Malta Crypto License 2026: MiCA CASP on the ‘Blockchain Island’

Malta was one of the first movers in crypto regulation, branding itself the “Blockchain Island” with its Virtual Financial Assets (VFA) framework. Under MiCA, the MFSA now issues CASP authorizations — backed by a mature regulator, an English-speaking professional ecosystem and full EU passporting.

From VFA to MiCA CASP

Malta's VFA regime gave it a head start and a deep bench of crypto-literate lawyers, auditors and service providers. MiCA harmonises the rules EU-wide, so Malta now grants CASP authorizations under the MFSA. The upside: a credible, established jurisdiction with an English-language legal system and EEA passporting.

Why founders pick Malta

  • Established, reputable regulator with crypto track record
  • English-speaking legal and professional ecosystem
  • EU passporting from a recognised brand jurisdiction
  • Deep pool of compliance and audit expertise

The application process

  1. Scope your CASP services and capital tier.
  2. Incorporate a Maltese company and fund capital.
  3. Build the file — business plan, governance, AML/CFT, IT/custody, key persons.
  4. Apply to the MFSA and complete its review.
  5. Authorization and ongoing supervision.

Common mistakes

  • Assuming the old VFA rules still apply unchanged — MiCA is now the framework.
  • Underestimating the MFSA's thorough, methodical review.
  • Thin substance or AML staffing.

Malta vs Panama: Malta offers a reputable EU CASP with passporting; Panama offers speed and low cost (€6,000, 2–3 weeks, 0% foreign-income tax). Compare with Cyprus and the jurisdictions hub.

Who Malta suits

Malta fits founders who want a recognised, English-language EU jurisdiction and can support MiCA capital, substance and a thorough review. For a fast, low-cost launch serving a global audience, start with Panama.
