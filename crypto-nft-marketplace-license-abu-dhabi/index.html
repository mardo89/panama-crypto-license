What Is a Crypto NFT Marketplace License in Abu Dhabi?

A crypto NFT marketplace license is a regulatory authorisation granted by the FSRA under the ADGM framework. It permits a company to operate a multilateral trading facility (MTF) or a broker-dealer platform for virtual assets, including NFTs. The license covers activities such as custody, exchange, and facilitation of NFT transactions. As of 2026, the ADGM has fully implemented its Digital Assets Framework, which aligns with international standards and provides legal certainty for businesses.

Key features include a clear taxonomy for virtual assets, strong anti-money laundering (AML) requirements, and a tiered capital regime. The license is essential for any platform that wants to offer NFT trading to institutional and retail investors in a compliant manner. Consulting24 advises clients on structuring their business to meet FSRA expectations. For example, a marketplace focusing on fractional NFT ownership must clearly define token rights and custody arrangements in its application.

Who Needs This License?

Any entity that operates an online marketplace for buying, selling, or minting NFTs in or from Abu Dhabi must hold this license. This includes:

  • NFT marketplaces connecting creators and collectors
  • Platforms offering fractional ownership of NFTs
  • Exchanges that support NFT trading pairs
  • Custodians storing NFTs on behalf of clients
  • Brokers facilitating NFT transactions

If your business involves any of these activities, you are likely required to be regulated by the FSRA. Even if you are based outside the UAE, targeting Abu Dhabi residents may trigger licensing obligations. Consulting24 can help you assess whether you need a license and which category applies. For instance, a gaming platform that sells in-game NFT assets may fall under the FSRA's scope if the assets are tradeable on a secondary market.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Financial Services Regulatory Authority (FSRA) of the Abu Dhabi Global Market (ADGM). The license type depends on the activities you intend to perform:

  • Operating a Multilateral Trading Facility (MTF) - for platforms that bring together multiple buyers and sellers
  • Broker-Dealer - for firms that execute trades on behalf of clients
  • Custody - for holding virtual assets for others

Each activity requires a separate license category, though a single entity can hold multiple licenses. The FSRA applies a risk-based approach, and applicants must demonstrate strong governance and compliance frameworks. As of 2026, the ADGM has also introduced a specific regime for NFTs under its Digital Assets Framework. This regime clarifies that NFTs are treated as virtual assets unless they represent securities, in which case additional rules apply. Capital Requirement

The FSRA imposes minimum capital requirements based on the license category and risk profile. For an NFT marketplace operating an MTF, the minimum capital is typically in the range of USD 500,000 to USD 1,000,000. For broker-dealers, the requirement is lower, around USD 200,000 to USD 500,000. Custody activities may require additional capital for safeguarding client assets.

These figures are subject to change and depend on the specific business model. Consulting24 can provide a precise estimate after reviewing your business plan. Capital must be held in liquid assets and maintained at all times. For example, if your platform also offers staking services, the FSRA may require higher capital to cover potential losses from slashing events.

Tax Treatment

Abu Dhabi offers a highly favourable tax environment for crypto businesses. The ADGM is a zero-tax jurisdiction for corporate income, capital gains, and withholding taxes. There is no VAT on digital asset transactions, and no personal income tax on salaries or investment income. However, businesses must register for VAT if their taxable supplies exceed the mandatory threshold (AED 375,000).

As of 2026, the UAE has implemented a corporate tax of 9% on profits exceeding AED 375,000, but this does not apply to qualifying ADGM entities that meet certain conditions. Consulting24 can help you structure your company to optimise tax benefits. For instance, by ensuring your revenue is derived from regulated activities within ADGM, you may qualify for the tax exemption.

Allowed Activities

With a crypto NFT marketplace license from ADGM, you can engage in the following regulated activities:

  • Operating a platform for primary and secondary NFT sales
  • Minting and issuing NFTs on behalf of creators
  • Providing custody services for NFTs and other virtual assets
  • Facilitating peer-to-peer trading of NFTs
  • Offering staking or yield-generating services for NFTs
  • Managing a marketplace for fractional NFT ownership

All activities must comply with FSRA rules on conduct of business, market abuse, and client asset protection. NFTs that represent securities or derivatives may fall under additional regulatory requirements. For example, an NFT that gives the holder a share in future profits of a project would likely be classified as a security, requiring a separate prospectus and compliance with securities laws.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a crypto NFT marketplace license in Abu Dhabi involves several stages. Consulting24 advises clients through each step:

  1. Pre-application assessment - Review your business model, identify the appropriate license category, and prepare a business plan.
  2. Company incorporation - Set up a company in ADGM, including registering a legal entity, obtaining a commercial license, and opening a bank account.
  3. Prepare application pack - Compile required documents: AML/CTF policies, governance framework, financial projections, and personal questionnaires for key personnel.
  4. Submit to FSRA - File the application along with the fee. The FSRA will conduct a preliminary review.
  5. Respond to queries - Address any questions or requests for additional information from the regulator.
  6. On-site inspection - The FSRA may visit your premises to verify operational readiness.
  7. Approval and licensing - Once satisfied, the FSRA issues the license, subject to ongoing compliance conditions.

The entire process typically takes 4-8 months. Consulting24 can expedite by ensuring all documents are in order. For example, having a detailed AML policy tailored to NFTs (e.g., how to verify ownership of digital art) can speed up the review.

Compliance and Trust

Operating under an FSRA license requires ongoing compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorist financing (CTF) regulations. You must appoint a Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO), conduct regular audits, and submit periodic reports to the regulator. The FSRA also expects strong cybersecurity measures and client asset segregation.

Building trust with users is critical for an NFT marketplace. Displaying your regulatory status, publishing a transparent fee schedule, and implementing strong KYC procedures can help. Common Mistakes to Avoid

Many applicants face delays or rejections due to avoidable errors. Common pitfalls include:

  • Inadequate business plan - The FSRA expects a detailed plan covering revenue model, target market, and risk management.
  • Poor AML/CTF policies - Templates are not enough; your policies must be tailored to your business.
  • Underestimating capital requirements - Ensure you have sufficient liquid capital before applying.
  • Lack of local presence - You must have a physical office in ADGM and a local manager.
  • Ignoring ongoing costs - Budget for annual license fees, compliance staff, and audit expenses.
