What Is the Crypto Broker License Singapore?

The crypto broker license Singapore refers to the Major Payment Institution (MPI) license issued by the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS). Under the Payment Services Act (PSA), any business that provides digital payment token (DPT) services - including buying, selling, or facilitating the exchange of cryptocurrencies - must hold this license. The MPI license allows firms to conduct multiple payment services, including DPT services, with higher transaction limits than the Standard Payment Institution (SPI) license.

As of 2026, the MPI license is the only route for crypto brokers in Singapore. The regime is fully operational and strictly enforced. MAS requires applicants to meet strong anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-financing of terrorism (CFT) standards, as well as technology risk management guidelines. Who Needs a Crypto Broker License in Singapore?

Any entity that offers the following services in Singapore or to Singapore residents must apply for the crypto broker license Singapore:

  • Buying or selling digital payment tokens (e.g., Bitcoin, Ethereum, stablecoins)
  • Facilitating the exchange of DPTs for fiat currency or other DPTs
  • Operating a digital payment token exchange or brokerage platform
  • Providing custodial wallet services for DPTs

This applies to both local startups and international firms expanding into Asia. Even if your company is headquartered elsewhere, if you target Singapore customers, you fall under MAS jurisdiction. The regulator has been active in enforcement, issuing warnings and taking action against unlicensed entities. License Type and Regulator

The regulator for the crypto broker license Singapore is the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS). MAS is the central bank and integrated financial regulator, known for its rigorous but transparent licensing regime. The specific license type is the Major Payment Institution (MPI) license under the Payment Services Act (PSA).

The MPI license is required for any payment service provider that exceeds certain thresholds: monthly transaction volume above SGD 3 million for one payment service, or SGD 6 million for two or more services. However, for DPT services, MAS has effectively mandated the MPI license for all crypto brokers, as the Standard Payment Institution (SPI) license has lower limits that are impractical for most commercial operations. As of 2026, no new SPI licenses are being granted for DPT services.

MAS also requires that the applicant be a Singapore-incorporated company (or a foreign company registered with ACRA). The company must have a permanent place of business in Singapore, with at least one executive director who is a Singapore resident (citizen, permanent resident, or Employment Pass holder).

Capital Requirement for the Crypto Broker License Singapore

For the Major Payment Institution (MPI) license covering digital payment token services, MAS requires a minimum base capital of SGD 250,000 (approximately EUR 170,000 as of 2026). This is a regulatory capital requirement that must be maintained at all times. The capital can be in the form of paid-up share capital, retained earnings, or other forms acceptable to MAS.

In addition to base capital, MAS may impose an additional capital requirement based on the volume of transactions. The formula is complex, but generally, the higher your transaction volume, the more capital you need. For most crypto brokers, the base capital of SGD 250,000 is sufficient, but firms with very high volumes may need up to SGD 1 million or more. Tax Treatment for Crypto Brokers in Singapore

Singapore offers a favourable tax environment for crypto brokers. The corporate income tax rate is a flat 17%. However, there are specific rules for digital payment tokens:

  • GST (Goods and Services Tax): As of 2026, the exchange of digital payment tokens for fiat currency is exempt from GST. However, fees charged by brokers for their services are subject to GST at the standard rate of 9% (if the broker is GST-registered).
  • Capital gains: Singapore does not tax capital gains. Therefore, profits from the sale of cryptocurrencies held as investments are generally not taxable. However, if you are a broker trading on your own account, the income may be considered trading income and subject to tax.
  • Withholding tax: Payments to non-residents for services rendered in Singapore may be subject to withholding tax. MAS guidance clarifies that certain crypto-related payments may fall under this regime.

Allowed Activities Under the Crypto Broker License Singapore

The MPI license for DPT services allows the following activities:

  • Buying and selling digital payment tokens (e.g., Bitcoin, Ethereum, USDT) for fiat currency (SGD, USD, etc.)
  • Exchanging one DPT for another (e.g., BTC to ETH)
  • Operating a digital payment token exchange (order book or peer-to-peer)
  • Providing custodial wallet services for DPTs
  • Facilitating the transfer of DPTs (e.g., remittance services using crypto)

MAS prohibits certain activities without additional licensing, such as dealing in derivatives of DPTs (which fall under the Securities and Futures Act) or offering interest-bearing accounts on crypto deposits (which may be considered a deposit-taking business). Step-by-Step Process to Obtain the Crypto Broker License Singapore

Obtaining the crypto broker license Singapore involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step:

  1. Pre-application assessment: We review your business model, target market, and existing compliance framework to determine readiness.
  2. Company incorporation: You must incorporate a Singapore private limited company with ACRA. The company must have a registered address and at least one local director.
  3. Appointment of key personnel: MAS requires at least one executive director who is a Singapore resident. Compliance officer, AML/CFT officer, and technology risk officer must also be appointed.
  4. Documentation preparation: Prepare a detailed business plan, AML/CFT policies, risk assessment, technology risk management framework, and audit reports. Consulting24 provides templates and guidance.
  5. Submission to MAS: Submit the application via MAS's online portal, along with the application fee. MAS will acknowledge receipt and may request additional information.
  6. Review and interview: MAS reviews the application and may conduct interviews with key personnel. This stage typically takes 4-8 months.
  7. In-principle approval (IPA): If MAS is satisfied, it issues an IPA, allowing you to commence operations under certain conditions (e.g., transaction limits).
  8. Final approval and license issuance: After meeting all conditions (e.g., operational readiness, capital injection), MAS grants the full MPI license.

Banking and Payment Services for Crypto Brokers in Singapore

Access to banking is a critical challenge for crypto brokers in Singapore. Most major banks (DBS, OCBC, UOB) are cautious about onboarding crypto firms, though some have dedicated teams for regulated entities. As of 2026, the following options exist:

  • DBS offers corporate accounts for MAS-licensed MPI holders, but with strict due diligence and high minimum deposits.
  • OCBC and UOB also consider applications on a case-by-case basis.
  • Digital banks like Trust Bank (a joint venture) may be more open to crypto firms.
  • Payment service providers such as Xfers (StraitsX) provide fiat on-ramp/off-ramp solutions.

Frequently asked questions

What is the crypto broker license Singapore?

It is the Major Payment Institution (MPI) license issued by the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) under the Payment Services Act. It allows firms to provide digital payment token services, including crypto brokerage, exchange, and custodial services.

Who needs a crypto broker license in Singapore?

Any business that buys, sells, or facilitates the exchange of digital payment tokens for fiat or other DPTs, or provides custodial wallet services, must hold an MPI license if they serve Singapore residents or operate in Singapore.

What is the minimum capital requirement for the crypto broker license Singapore?

The minimum base capital is SGD 250,000 (approximately EUR 170,000). This must be maintained at all times. Higher volumes may require additional capital.

How long does it take to get a crypto broker license in Singapore?

Typically 6 to 12 months from application to in-principle approval (IPA). Full license issuance may take longer depending on conditions set by MAS.

What are the costs involved in obtaining the license?

Application fees are SGD 10,000-15,000, annual license fees SGD 10,000-40,000, and compliance setup costs SGD 50,000-150,000. Total first-year cost is estimated SGD 100,000-250,000, excluding advisory fees.

Can a foreign company apply for the crypto broker license Singapore?

Yes, but the company must be incorporated in Singapore (or registered as a foreign company with ACRA) and have a local director who is a Singapore resident.

What activities are allowed under the MPI license?

Buying/selling DPTs, exchanging DPTs, operating a DPT exchange, providing custodial wallet services, and facilitating DPT transfers. Derivatives and deposit-taking require additional licenses.

Is Singapore tax-friendly for crypto brokers?

Yes. Corporate tax is 17%, and GST is exempt on DPT-fiat exchanges. There is no capital gains tax. However, brokerage fees are subject to GST.

How does Singapore compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 company setup but no dedicated crypto license, making it cheaper but less regulated. Singapore provides a strong regulatory framework suitable for serious operations.
