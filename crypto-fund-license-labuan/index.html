What Is a Crypto Fund License Labuan?

A crypto fund license Labuan is a regulatory authorisation issued by the Labuan FSA that permits a fund manager to establish and operate a collective investment scheme focused on digital assets. This includes funds that invest in cryptocurrencies, tokens, blockchain projects, and other digital asset classes. The licence is part of Labuan's broader framework for fund management, which has been updated to accommodate the unique risks and operational needs of crypto assets.

The licence is typically structured as a Labuan Limited Partnership or a Labuan Company, with the fund manager holding a Capital Markets Services Licence (CMSL) for fund management. As of 2026, the Labuan FSA requires fund managers to demonstrate adequate systems for custody, valuation, and risk management of digital assets. The regulator also expects compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) requirements aligned with FATF recommendations.

Key features of the Labuan crypto fund license include:

  • Regulated by the Labuan FSA under the Labuan Financial Services and Securities Act 2010 and the Labuan Limited Partnerships and Limited Liability Partnerships Act 2010.
  • Allows management of both traditional and digital asset funds.
  • Tax exemption on trading profits and a low corporate tax rate (typically 3% of net audited profits, or an alternative fixed rate).
  • No capital gains tax, withholding tax, or stamp duty on fund transactions.
  • Access to a network of double taxation agreements.

License Type and Regulator

The crypto fund license in Labuan is not a standalone licence but a combination of a fund management licence (Capital Markets Services Licence) and the registration of the fund vehicle. The regulator is the Labuan Financial Services Authority (Labuan FSA), which oversees all financial services in Labuan, including fund management, banking, insurance, and securities.

The licence type is typically a Labuan Fund Management Licence under the Labuan Financial Services and Securities Act 2010. The fund itself is registered as a Labuan Limited Partnership (LLP) or a Labuan Company (Sdn Bhd). For crypto funds, the Labuan FSA requires additional disclosures on custody arrangements, valuation methodologies, and risk management specific to digital assets.

As of 2026, the Labuan FSA has not introduced a separate 'crypto fund' category; instead, it applies the existing fund management framework with enhanced due diligence for digital asset exposures. The regulator expects fund managers to have at least two years of relevant experience and a clean compliance record.

Capital Requirement

The Labuan FSA imposes a minimum capital requirement for fund managers, which as of 2026 is typically USD 100,000 for a fund management licence. This capital must be maintained as operational capital and cannot be used for investment. For larger funds or those with higher risk profiles, the regulator may require additional capital up to USD 500,000.

The capital must be held in a Labuan bank account or a licensed financial institution. It can be in the form of cash or liquid assets. The requirement is lower than in many other jurisdictions (e.g., Singapore requires SGD 500,000 for a fund management licence), making Labuan a cost-effective option.

Note that the capital requirement is for the fund manager, not the fund itself. The fund's own capital is determined by the investment strategy and investor commitments. Consulting24 advises on structuring to meet the capital requirement efficiently.

Tax Treatment

Labuan offers a highly favourable tax regime for fund managers and funds. The key benefits include:

  • Tax exemption on trading profits: Labuan entities engaged in trading activities (including crypto trading) are exempt from income tax if they elect to be taxed under the Labuan Business Activity Tax Act 1990.
  • Low corporate tax rate: Alternatively, a Labuan entity can opt to pay tax at 3% of net audited profits, which is often lower than the standard Malaysian rate of 24%.
  • No capital gains tax: Profits from the sale of digital assets are not subject to capital gains tax.
  • No withholding tax: Dividends, interest, and royalties paid to non-residents are not subject to withholding tax.
  • No stamp duty: On instruments relating to the fund's business.
  • Double taxation agreements: Labuan entities can access Malaysia's extensive network of DTAs to reduce tax leakage on cross-border investments.

This tax structure makes Labuan one of the most tax-efficient jurisdictions for crypto fund operations. However, careful structuring is required to ensure compliance with the substance requirements (e.g., having a local office and directors).

Allowed Activities

A Labuan crypto fund license permits a wide range of activities related to digital asset fund management:

  • Establishing and managing collective investment schemes that invest in cryptocurrencies, tokens, and digital assets.
  • Providing fund administration services (valuation, transfer agency, custody oversight) for the fund.
  • Engaging in proprietary trading of digital assets for the fund's account.
  • Offering fund management services to accredited and institutional investors.
  • Structuring tokenised funds where fund units are represented by digital tokens.
  • Investing in initial coin offerings (ICOs), security token offerings (STOs), and decentralised finance (DeFi) protocols.

The licence does not permit direct retail solicitation or trading on behalf of unaccredited investors. The fund must comply with Labuan's securities laws, including restrictions on marketing to the Malaysian public. The Labuan FSA also expects strong AML/CFT controls, including know-your-customer (KYC) and transaction monitoring for all investors.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

When applying for a Labuan crypto fund license, avoid these pitfalls:

  • Insufficient substance: The Labuan FSA expects a local office, directors, and compliance officer. Remote operations without real presence may be rejected.
  • Poor AML/CTF documentation: Vague or generic policies are a red flag. Tailor them to your fund's specific crypto activities.
  • Underestimating timeline: The process can take 6-8 months, especially if the regulator has queries. Plan accordingly.
  • Choosing the wrong fund vehicle: An LLP may be more tax-efficient than a company, but the choice depends on investor preferences.
  • Ignoring tax residency: Ensure the fund's management and control are in Labuan to benefit from the tax regime.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Labuan crypto fund license?

The Labuan FSA requires a minimum operational capital of USD 100,000 for the fund manager. This must be maintained in a Labuan bank account. Larger funds may need additional capital up to USD 500,000.

How long does it take to get a Labuan crypto fund license?

The typical timeline is 4 to 8 months from application to approval. This includes incorporation, document preparation, and regulatory review. Delays can occur if the regulator requests additional information.

What is the tax rate for a Labuan crypto fund?

Labuan offers a low tax regime. A fund manager can elect to pay 3% of net audited profits or be exempt from tax on trading profits. There is no capital gains tax, withholding tax, or stamp duty.

Can I manage a crypto fund from Labuan without being physically present?

The Labuan FSA requires a local presence, including a registered office and at least one local director or compliance officer. However, day-to-day management can be delegated to a local service provider.

What types of funds can be set up in Labuan?

Labuan allows various fund structures, including limited partnerships, companies, and unit trusts. Crypto funds can be structured as closed-end or open-end funds, depending on the investment strategy.

Is a Labuan crypto fund license recognised internationally?

Labuan is a respected international financial centre. Its licence is recognised by many institutional investors and banks, though some may require additional due diligence for crypto-focused funds.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

Annual audit, filing of returns, maintaining AML/CTF policies, and reporting material changes to the Labuan FSA. A local compliance officer must be appointed.

Can I convert an existing fund to a Labuan structure?

Yes, it is possible to redomicile an existing fund to Labuan or establish a parallel fund. This involves legal restructuring and approval from the Labuan FSA.

How does Labuan compare to Panama for crypto funds?

Panama offers faster setup (2-4 weeks) and lower cost (EUR 6,000 flat fee) but lacks a dedicated regulatory framework for crypto funds. Labuan provides a regulated environment with tax benefits, which may be more attractive to institutional investors.
