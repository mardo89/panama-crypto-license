What Is the Crypto Stablecoin License in Turkey?

The crypto stablecoin license in Turkey is a regulatory authorization issued by the Capital Markets Board of Turkey (SPK) for entities that wish to issue, manage, or distribute stablecoins pegged to fiat currencies or other assets. As of 2026, Turkey has implemented a comprehensive licensing regime under the Law on Capital Markets, requiring stablecoin issuers to register and comply with ongoing obligations.

This license is distinct from a general crypto service provider license. It specifically covers the issuance and redemption of stablecoins, including reserve management and transparency requirements. The SPK oversees the licensing process, ensuring that stablecoin projects maintain adequate reserves, provide clear terms to users, and follow anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) rules.

For founders, this license provides a clear legal pathway to operate in Turkey's growing crypto economy. It also signals credibility to investors and users who demand regulatory oversight. Without it, issuing a stablecoin in Turkey is illegal and can lead to severe penalties.

Who Needs a Crypto Stablecoin License in Turkey?

Any entity planning to issue a stablecoin in Turkey or offer stablecoin-related services to Turkish residents must obtain the license. This includes:

  • Startups launching a new stablecoin pegged to TRY, USD, EUR, or other assets.
  • Existing crypto exchanges that want to offer their own stablecoin.
  • Fintech companies integrating stablecoin payments or remittance services.
  • International stablecoin projects seeking to enter the Turkish market.

License Type and Regulator

The regulatory authority for the crypto stablecoin license in Turkey is the Capital Markets Board of Turkey (SPK). The license is formally a 'Capital Markets Activity License' with a specific scope for stablecoin issuance. This is not a simple registration but a full licensing process that includes a review of the issuer's business plan, reserve management policy, technology infrastructure, and AML/CTF compliance.

The SPK has set specific requirements for stablecoin issuers, including the need to hold reserves in liquid assets, conduct regular audits, and provide transparent reporting. The license is valid indefinitely but subject to annual renewal and ongoing supervision. Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license.

Capital Requirement for Stablecoin Issuers

The SPK imposes a minimum capital requirement for stablecoin license applicants. As of 2026, the minimum paid-in capital is TRY 10 million (approximately EUR 300,000). This capital must be fully paid in cash and held in a Turkish bank account. The capital serves as a buffer to cover operational risks and potential losses.

In addition to the initial capital, issuers must maintain a reserve fund equal to at least 100% of the value of stablecoins in circulation. These reserves must be held in liquid assets such as Turkish government bonds or bank deposits. The SPK may require higher reserves for stablecoins pegged to volatile assets or algorithmic stablecoins.

Tax Treatment for Stablecoin Operations in Turkey

Turkey's tax framework for stablecoin activities is still evolving, but as of 2026, the following general principles apply:

  • Corporate income tax: 20% on profits from stablecoin issuance and related services.
  • Value-added tax (VAT): Stablecoin transactions may be exempt from VAT, but advisory and management fees are subject to 18% VAT.
  • Withholding tax: Payments to foreign entities for services may be subject to 15% withholding tax, unless reduced by a tax treaty.
  • Stamp duty: Certain documents and agreements may incur stamp duty at 0.948% of the value.

Tax treatment can vary based on the specific structure of your stablecoin project. Consulting24 recommends engaging a local tax advisor to optimize your tax position. Allowed Activities Under the Stablecoin License

With a crypto stablecoin license from the SPK, you are permitted to:

  • Issue stablecoins pegged to fiat currencies, commodities, or other assets.
  • Manage the redemption of stablecoins for the underlying asset.
  • Hold and manage reserve assets in accordance with SPK rules.
  • Distribute stablecoins through exchanges, wallets, or direct sales.
  • Offer stablecoin-related services such as staking, lending, or payment processing, subject to additional approvals.

Activities that are not allowed without further licensing include operating a crypto exchange, providing custody services, or engaging in proprietary trading. The stablecoin license is narrowly scoped to issuance and redemption. If you plan to offer a broader range of services, you may need multiple licenses. Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining a crypto stablecoin license in Turkey involves several stages. Here is a typical process:

  1. Pre-application consultation: Assess your project's eligibility and prepare a business plan, reserve policy, and compliance framework.
  2. Company incorporation: If you do not already have a Turkish entity, incorporate a joint-stock company (Anonim Şirket) with the required capital.
  3. Document preparation: Gather all required documents, including audited financial statements, AML/CTF policies, technology security reports, and board resolutions.
  4. Application submission: Submit the application to the SPK along with the application fee. The SPK will review the completeness within 30 days.
  5. Review and due diligence: The SPK conducts a thorough review of your application, including background checks on shareholders and directors. This stage can take 3-6 months.
  6. Approval and licensing: If approved, you will receive the license and must pay the remaining fees. You can then commence stablecoin issuance.
  7. Ongoing compliance: Submit regular reports, maintain reserves, and undergo annual audits. The SPK may conduct periodic inspections.

Compliance and Trust: Ongoing Obligations

Holding a stablecoin license in Turkey comes with continuous compliance duties. The SPK requires licensees to:

  • Submit monthly reports on stablecoin issuance, redemption, and reserve levels.
  • Conduct annual external audits of financial statements and reserve holdings.
  • Implement strong AML/CTF procedures, including customer due diligence (CDD) and transaction monitoring.
  • Maintain a minimum capital level at all times.
  • Notify the SPK of any material changes in ownership, management, or business model.

Common Mistakes When Applying for a Turkish Stablecoin License

Many applicants face delays or rejections due to avoidable errors. Common pitfalls include:

  • Incomplete or inconsistent documentation: Ensure all forms are filled correctly and supporting documents are up to date.
  • Insufficient capital: The minimum capital must be fully paid and verifiable.
  • Poor AML/CTF policies: The SPK scrutinizes compliance frameworks. Generic policies are often rejected.
  • Unclear reserve management: You must detail how reserves are held, valued, and audited.
  • Ignoring local presence requirements: The licensed entity must have a physical office and management in Turkey.

What is the minimum capital for a crypto stablecoin license in Turkey?

What activities are allowed under the stablecoin license?
