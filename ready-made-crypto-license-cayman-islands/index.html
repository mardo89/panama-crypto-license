What Is a Ready Made Cayman Islands Crypto License?

A ready made Cayman Islands crypto license refers to a pre-registered company that has already submitted or obtained a VASP license from CIMA. The entity is typically a Cayman Islands exempted company with a corporate services provider in place. The buyer acquires the company and the associated license application or approval, bypassing the initial incorporation and application drafting phases.

This approach is attractive for founders who need to launch quickly or who want to avoid the administrative burden of setting up a new entity. However, CIMA still requires the ultimate beneficial owners (UBOs) and directors to undergo background checks. The ready made structure does not exempt you from compliance obligations; it simply accelerates the timeline.

License Type and Regulator

The Cayman Islands Monetary Authority (CIMA) is the sole regulator for virtual asset service providers. Under the Virtual Asset (Service Providers) Act, 2020 (as amended), any business that provides virtual asset exchange, transfer, custody, or administration services must hold a VASP license. There is no tiered license system; all VASPs are subject to the same core requirements, though the scope of activities may affect the application depth.

CIMA conducts a thorough assessment of the applicant's business model, AML/CFT policies, financial resources, and the fitness of directors and shareholders. The regulator also requires a local physical presence, including a registered office and at least one director or officer resident in the Cayman Islands. A ready made license typically includes these local arrangements.

For comparison, other jurisdictions like Estonia and Lithuania have different regimes. Estonia now requires a full license with capital requirements, while Lithuania is transitioning to MiCA. The Cayman Islands remains a non-EU option with its own strong framework.

Capital Requirement

The Cayman Islands VASP regime does not prescribe a fixed minimum capital requirement in the legislation. Instead, CIMA expects applicants to maintain adequate financial resources proportionate to the nature, scale, and complexity of their business. In practice, most successful applicants demonstrate at least $100,000 to $500,000 in liquid capital, though this is not a statutory minimum.

For ready made licenses, the existing entity may already have a capitalisation in place. Consulting24 advises on the appropriate level based on your business plan. We recommend holding sufficient funds to cover operational expenses for at least six months. Unlike EU MiCA regimes (which require EUR 50,000 to 150,000 depending on service type), the Cayman Islands offers flexibility but requires justification.

Tax Treatment

The Cayman Islands imposes no direct taxes on corporate profits, capital gains, or income. There is no VAT, GST, or withholding tax. The main ongoing costs are annual government fees, registered office fees, and compliance costs. This tax-neutral environment is a major draw for crypto businesses seeking to maximise after-tax returns.

However, founders must consider tax obligations in their home country. The Cayman Islands does not have double tax treaties with most jurisdictions, so profits may be taxed where the business is effectively managed. Consulting24 recommends obtaining professional tax advice in your jurisdiction of residence. The Panama jurisdiction also offers territorial taxation, which can be compared during your evaluation.

Allowed Activities

A Cayman Islands VASP license permits the following activities:

  • Virtual asset exchange (trading platform, order book matching)
  • Virtual asset transfer services (sending/receiving on behalf of clients)
  • Virtual asset custody (safekeeping of private keys)
  • Virtual asset administration (voting, staking, governance)
  • Issuance of virtual assets (if conducted as a service)

Activities not covered include fiat currency exchange (unless separately licensed) and derivatives trading, which may fall under securities regulation. If your business involves multiple service types, you must apply for a single VASP license covering all activities. Consulting24 helps you define your scope accurately to avoid regulatory gaps.

Common Mistakes

Avoid these pitfalls:

  • Ignoring beneficial ownership rules: CIMA requires full disclosure of UBOs. Attempting to hide ownership will lead to rejection.
  • Underestimating local presence: You need a physical office and a local director or officer. Virtual offices are not sufficient.
  • Incomplete AML policies: CIMA expects detailed, risk-based procedures. Generic templates are often rejected.
  • Choosing an unvetted ready made entity: Some entities have hidden liabilities or past compliance issues. Always conduct due diligence.
  • Delaying bank account opening: Start the banking process early to avoid operational delays.
