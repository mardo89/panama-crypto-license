What is the Crypto Payment Institution License Anjouan?

The Crypto Payment Institution License Anjouan is a regulatory authorization that allows companies to provide payment services involving cryptocurrencies. It is issued by the Anjouan Financial Services Authority (AFSA), the island's financial regulator. This license is designed for businesses that want to offer crypto-to-fiat exchange, custodial wallet services, and payment processing. Unlike some jurisdictions that require a physical presence, Anjouan permits remote operations, making it ideal for digital-first companies.

The license is part of Anjouan's broader effort to attract fintech and crypto businesses by offering a straightforward regulatory environment. It is not a full-fledged banking license but focuses on payment services. The key features include:

  • Low setup cost: EUR 6,000 flat fee.
  • Minimum capital: EUR 12,000.
  • Processing time: 4-6 weeks.
  • No corporate tax on offshore income.
  • Remote application and operation possible.

License Type and Regulator

The Crypto Payment Institution License Anjouan is a specific license type issued by the Anjouan Financial Services Authority (AFSA). AFSA is the sole regulatory body for financial services in Anjouan, overseeing banking, insurance, and now crypto-asset services. The license falls under the category of 'Payment Institution' and is distinct from a full banking license or a simple registration.

Key regulatory details:

  • Regulator: Anjouan Financial Services Authority (AFSA).
  • License class: Payment Institution (Crypto).
  • Legal basis: Anjouan Financial Services Act (as amended for crypto).
  • Supervision: Ongoing compliance with AML/CFT rules.

AFSA requires licensees to implement strong AML policies, appoint a compliance officer, and submit periodic reports. The regulator conducts background checks on directors and shareholders. While Anjouan is not part of the EU, its regime is designed to align with FATF recommendations. For comparison, other jurisdictions like Poland crypto license or Czech Republic crypto license have different regulatory frameworks under MiCA.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for the Crypto Payment Institution License Anjouan is EUR 12,000. This amount must be paid up and maintained as working capital. Unlike some jurisdictions that require higher capital for specific activities (e.g., EUR 125,000 under MiCA for certain services), Anjouan's requirement is uniform and low.

Capital must be held in a bank account in the company's name, and proof of deposit is required during application. The funds can be used for operational expenses but must not fall below the minimum. If you plan to offer services that involve holding client funds, additional capital may be required as a buffer. Tax Treatment

Anjouan offers a favorable tax regime for licensed companies. There is no corporate tax on income derived from offshore activities, meaning profits from crypto services to non-residents are tax-free. Additionally, there are no withholding taxes on dividends, interest, or royalties paid to non-residents. However, a small annual license renewal fee applies (typically EUR 2,000-3,000).

Value-added tax (VAT) is not applicable to most crypto services in Anjouan. However, if your company has a physical presence or serves local clients, local taxes may apply. Allowed Activities

The Crypto Payment Institution License Anjouan permits a range of activities related to digital assets. The exact scope is defined in the license, but typical allowed activities include:

  • Exchange of cryptocurrencies for fiat money and vice versa.
  • Exchange of cryptocurrencies for other cryptocurrencies.
  • Operation of custodial wallets for clients.
  • Payment processing using cryptocurrencies (merchant services).
  • Remittance services involving crypto.
  • Issuance of crypto-backed payment instruments (e.g., prepaid cards).

Activities not covered include: operating a crypto exchange (order book matching), which may require a separate exchange license; and investment services (e.g., asset management).
