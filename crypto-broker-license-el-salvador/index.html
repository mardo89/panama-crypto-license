What Is the Crypto Broker License in El Salvador?

The crypto broker license in El Salvador is a regulatory authorization issued by the National Digital Assets Commission (Comisión Nacional de Activos Digitales, CNAD). It allows companies to operate as digital asset service providers, including brokerage, exchange, custody, and related services. The license is governed by the Digital Assets Issuance Law (Ley de Emisión de Activos Digitales), which came into effect in 2023 and has been refined through 2026.

Unlike some jurisdictions that treat crypto brokers under general financial services laws, El Salvador has a dedicated framework. The license is mandatory for any entity offering digital asset services to Salvadoran residents or operating from within the country. It covers both Bitcoin and other digital assets, reflecting the country's dual approach: Bitcoin as legal tender and other digital assets as securities or commodities.

Key features of the license include:

  • Regulated by the CNAD, an independent government body.
  • Valid for an initial period of 5 years, renewable.
  • Allows activities such as order execution, portfolio management, custody, and advisory services.
  • Requires a minimum paid-up capital of USD 200,000 (as of 2026).
  • Subject to ongoing compliance, including AML/CFT obligations and regular reporting.

The license is designed to attract legitimate crypto businesses while ensuring consumer protection and financial stability. It is not a simple registration but a full licensing process with substantive requirements.

Who Needs a Crypto Broker License in El Salvador?

Any legal entity that provides digital asset services in or from El Salvador must obtain the crypto broker license. This includes:

  • Brokerage firms that buy/sell digital assets on behalf of clients.
  • Exchanges operating order books or matching platforms.
  • Custodians holding digital assets for third parties.
  • Asset managers offering portfolio management in digital assets.
  • Advisors providing investment advice on digital assets.
  • Payment processors facilitating crypto-to-fiat conversions.

Foreign companies that target Salvadoran customers or have a physical presence in the country also need the license. The CNAD has extraterritorial reach for services directed at residents. If you are a crypto founder considering expansion into Latin America, El Salvador's license can serve as a gateway to the region, especially given its Bitcoin-friendly stance.

Note that pure software developers or miners who do not handle client funds may be exempt, but it is advisable to seek legal confirmation. Consulting24 can help determine your specific obligations.

License Type and Regulator

The relevant regulator for the crypto broker license in El Salvador is the National Digital Assets Commission (CNAD). The CNAD is an autonomous government agency established under the Digital Assets Issuance Law. It is responsible for licensing, supervision, and enforcement for all digital asset service providers.

The license itself is a single category called Digital Asset Service Provider (DASP) license. However, depending on the services offered, the CNAD may impose specific conditions. For example, a broker-dealer license may have additional requirements compared to a pure custody license. The CNAD has the authority to classify activities and set tailored capital and operational requirements.

As of 2026, the CNAD has issued over 50 licenses, with a growing pipeline. The regulator is known for being approachable but thorough, with a focus on AML/CFT compliance and consumer protection. Unlike some other jurisdictions, the CNAD does not have a tiered capital system; instead, it sets a single minimum capital of USD 200,000 for all DASP licenses, though higher amounts may be required based on business volume.

For comparison, in Panama, the crypto license is issued by the Superintendencia de Bancos de Panama (SBP) under a different framework, with a flat fee of EUR 6,000 and no minimum capital requirement as of 2026. In contrast, El Salvador's capital requirement is higher but offers a more established regulatory environment.

Capital Requirement

The minimum paid-up capital for a crypto broker license in El Salvador is USD 200,000 as of 2026. This capital must be maintained throughout the license period and cannot be withdrawn without CNAD approval. The capital can be in the form of cash or liquid assets, and must be deposited in a bank account in El Salvador.

The CNAD may require additional capital based on the scale of operations, number of clients, or types of services. For example, if the broker intends to offer custody services, the regulator may impose a higher capital buffer to cover potential losses. The capital requirement is designed to ensure financial stability and protect clients.

It is important to note that the capital is not a fee but a requirement that remains on the company's balance sheet. It can be used for operational expenses as long as the minimum is maintained. This is different from jurisdictions like Panama, which has no minimum capital requirement, or EU member states under MiCA, which have tiered capital of EUR 50,000 / 125,000 / 150,000 depending on services.

Tax Treatment

El Salvador offers a favorable tax regime for crypto businesses. The key points are:

  • No capital gains tax on Bitcoin: As per the Bitcoin Law, Bitcoin is considered legal tender, and gains from its sale are exempt from capital gains tax.
  • Income tax: Corporate income tax is standard at 30% on net profits. However, crypto-specific income may be subject to special rules; consult a tax advisor.
  • VAT: Standard VAT rate is 13%. Crypto transactions may be exempt if they qualify as financial services, but this is not automatic.
  • Withholding taxes: Dividends paid to foreign shareholders may be subject to 5% withholding tax if a tax treaty applies.
  • No wealth tax: There is no annual wealth tax on digital assets held.

Compared to Panama, which has territorial taxation (no tax on foreign-source income) and no capital gains tax, El Salvador's tax regime is less favorable for pure offshore structures. However, for businesses with local operations, the exemptions on Bitcoin gains can be a significant advantage. Always obtain professional tax advice tailored to your specific situation.

Allowed Activities Under the License

The crypto broker license in El Salvador permits a wide range of digital asset services. The CNAD authorizes activities on a case-by-case basis, but generally includes:

  • Brokerage: Buying and selling digital assets on behalf of clients.
  • Exchange: Operating a platform for trading digital assets, including order matching.
  • Custody: Safekeeping of digital assets and private keys.
  • Portfolio management: Managing digital asset portfolios for clients.
  • Advisory services: Providing investment advice on digital assets.
  • Transfer services: Facilitating transfers of digital assets between addresses.
  • Issuance: Assisting in the issuance of digital assets (e.g., security tokens).

Note that the license does not cover banking activities (e.g., taking deposits) or securities trading unless specifically authorized. The CNAD may impose restrictions on use or derivatives trading. For a full list of permitted activities, a consultation with Consulting24 is recommended.

In contrast, Panama allows similar activities under its crypto license, but with less regulatory oversight. The choice between jurisdictions often depends on the level of regulatory certainty desired.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process for obtaining a crypto broker license in El Salvador involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step to ensure compliance.

  1. Pre-incorporation: Register a company in El Salvador (Sociedad Anónima or similar). The company must have a registered address and a local director.
  2. Document preparation: Gather required documents including business plan, AML/CFT policies, risk management framework, organizational structure, and financial projections. All documents must be in Spanish.
  3. Capital deposit: Open a bank account in El Salvador and deposit the minimum capital of USD 200,000. Provide proof of deposit to the CNAD.
  4. Application submission: Submit the application to the CNAD along with the government fee. The CNAD will acknowledge receipt and begin review.
  5. Review and queries: The CNAD may request additional information or clarifications. This stage can take 2-4 months.
  6. Approval and license issuance: Once satisfied, the CNAD issues the license. The company must then pay the annual supervision fee (typically USD 5,000-10,000).
  7. Post-licensing: Implement ongoing compliance, including regular reporting, AML training, and audits.

The timeline is typically 4-8 months from start to finish. Consulting24 can help streamline the process by ensuring documentation meets CNAD standards.

Compliance and Trust Considerations

Maintaining compliance is essential to keep the license active. Key obligations include:

  • AML/CFT program: Implement policies for customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting.
  • Annual audit: Submit audited financial statements to the CNAD.
  • Regular reporting: Provide quarterly reports on operations, including transaction volumes and client numbers.
  • Data protection: Comply with El Salvador's data privacy laws.
  • Local presence: Maintain a registered office and a local compliance officer.

Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. The CNAD conducts periodic inspections. Consulting24 advises on setting up strong compliance frameworks. This is general guidance, not legal advice. Always engage local counsel for specific obligations.

For founders who prioritize a lighter compliance burden, Panama may be more attractive, as its regulatory requirements are less stringent. However, Panama's license may not carry the same level of international trust.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

When applying for a crypto broker license in El Salvador, avoid these common pitfalls:

  • Incomplete documentation: The CNAD requires detailed policies; missing sections can delay approval.
  • Underestimating capital: Ensure the full USD 200,000 is available and properly documented.
  • Ignoring local language requirements: All documents must be in Spanish; translations must be certified.
  • Choosing the wrong legal structure: Use a local company; foreign entities may not be eligible.
  • Neglecting AML compliance: The CNAD scrutinizes AML programs; a weak plan can lead to rejection.
  • Overlooking ongoing costs: Budget for annual compliance fees and audits.
