What Is the Labuan Crypto License?

The Labuan crypto license is a regulatory authorisation issued by the Labuan Financial Services Authority (Labuan FSA) for businesses engaged in digital asset activities. It covers two main categories: the Labuan Digital Asset Fund (DAF) for managing collective investment schemes in digital assets, and the Labuan Digital Asset Custodian (DAC) for safekeeping and administration of digital assets. Unlike some jurisdictions that offer a single 'crypto license', Labuan distinguishes between fund management and custody, allowing firms to apply for the type that matches their business model.

Labuan operates under a territorial tax system, meaning income derived from outside Malaysia is generally tax-exempt. This makes it particularly attractive for international crypto businesses. The license is part of Labuan's broader strategy to position itself as a fintech-friendly hub while maintaining strong AML/CFT standards. For 2026, the regime is fully operational, with clear guidelines and a track record of approvals. The Labuan FSA has processed dozens of applications since the digital asset framework was introduced, and the regime continues to evolve to keep pace with industry developments.

Compared to other jurisdictions, Labuan offers a unique combination of low tax, regulatory clarity, and proximity to Asian markets. It is not a full member of the EU, so it is not subject to MiCA, but it aligns with FATF recommendations. This makes it a viable option for firms that want a credible license without the higher capital requirements of EU regimes.

Who Needs a Labuan Crypto License?

Any entity that wishes to provide digital asset services from Labuan must obtain the appropriate license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (order-book, peer-to-peer, or automated market maker)
  • Digital asset custodians (storing private keys or assets on behalf of clients)
  • Fund managers operating digital asset funds (e.g., crypto hedge funds, venture capital funds)
  • Token issuers conducting security token offerings (STOs) or utility token sales
  • Brokers or dealers in digital assets
  • Advisory firms providing investment advice on digital assets

If your business involves holding client funds or assets, or managing investment schemes, you fall under Labuan FSA's purview. Even if you are based elsewhere but target Asian markets, a Labuan license can serve as a credible regulatory stamp. For example, a crypto exchange based in Singapore might establish a Labuan entity to serve clients in Malaysia and the wider ASEAN region, benefiting from the territorial tax exemption. License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Labuan Financial Services Authority (Labuan FSA), established under the Labuan Financial Services Authority Act 1996. It oversees all financial services in Labuan, including digital assets. The two main license types are:

License TypeActivityCapital Requirement (MYR)
Labuan Digital Asset Fund (DAF)Managing collective investment schemes that invest in digital assets500,000
Labuan Digital Asset Custodian (DAC)Safekeeping, administration, and custody of digital assets for clients1,000,000

There is no separate 'exchange' license; exchanges typically require a DAC license if they hold client assets, or a DAF if they operate a fund. The Labuan FSA also recognises digital asset derivatives and structured products under the Labuan Securities Act. All licensees must comply with the Labuan Anti-Money Laundering and Counter Financing of Terrorism (AML/CFT) Guidelines, which align with FATF recommendations. The regulator conducts regular inspections and requires annual audits by a Labuan-approved auditor.

Capital Requirement

The minimum paid-up capital for a Labuan crypto license is set by the Labuan FSA. For 2026, the requirements are:

  • Labuan Digital Asset Fund (DAF): Minimum paid-up capital of MYR 500,000 (approximately EUR 100,000)
  • Labuan Digital Asset Custodian (DAC): Minimum paid-up capital of MYR 1,000,000 (approximately EUR 200,000)

These figures are subject to change and may be higher if the firm engages in additional regulated activities. Capital must be maintained in a Labuan bank account and cannot be withdrawn without regulatory approval. Consulting24 advises on structuring capital to meet regulatory expectations, including whether to use share capital or a combination of share capital and retained earnings. It is important to note that the capital must be fully paid up before the license is granted, and it must be held in a Labuan-licensed bank.

Tax Treatment

Labuan offers a highly favourable tax regime for licensed entities. The key features are:

  • Corporate tax rate of 3% on audited net profits (or a flat rate of MYR 20,000 if profits are below a threshold)
  • Exemption from withholding tax on dividends, interest, and royalties paid to non-residents
  • No capital gains tax, no sales tax, and no value-added tax (VAT)
  • Territorial taxation: income derived from outside Malaysia is generally tax-exempt

This makes Labuan one of the most tax-efficient jurisdictions for crypto businesses. However, firms must ensure they comply with transfer pricing rules and substance requirements (e.g., having a physical office and local directors). For example, a crypto fund that generates trading profits from global markets may pay only 3% tax on those profits, whereas in an EU jurisdiction the rate could be 12-25%. Allowed Activities

Under the Labuan crypto license, the following activities are permitted:

  • Operating a digital asset exchange (order book, P2P, or AMM)
  • Providing custodial services for digital assets (hot and cold storage)
  • Managing digital asset funds (e.g., crypto index funds, hedge funds)
  • Issuing and trading security tokens (subject to Labuan Securities Act)
  • Broking and dealing in digital assets
  • Providing digital asset advisory services

Activities not permitted include retail forex trading (unless separately licensed), gambling-related tokens, and any activity that violates Malaysian law. The Labuan FSA also prohibits activities that could undermine financial stability or facilitate money laundering. For example, a platform that offers used trading in crypto derivatives would need to ensure it does not fall foul of the Labuan Securities Act. Step-by-Step Application Process

The application process for a Labuan crypto license involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application consultation: Discuss your business model with Consulting24 to determine the appropriate license type and structure.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Labuan company (LLC or PLC) with a registered office and local secretary.
  3. Document preparation: Prepare a business plan, AML/CFT policies, risk management framework, and financial projections.
  4. Submission: Submit the application to Labuan FSA along with the fee and supporting documents (e.g., passports, CVs, bank references).
  5. Due diligence: Labuan FSA conducts background checks on directors, shareholders, and beneficial owners.
  6. Interview: Key personnel may be required to attend an interview with the regulator.
  7. Approval and licensing: Upon approval, pay the annual license fee and receive the license certificate.
  8. Post-licensing compliance: File annual returns, maintain capital, and submit audit reports.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a Labuan DAF and DAC license?

The DAF (Digital Asset Fund) license is for managing collective investment schemes that invest in digital assets, such as crypto hedge funds. The DAC (Digital Asset Custodian) license is for safekeeping and administration of digital assets for clients, like a crypto exchange that holds client funds. The capital requirement for DAC is higher (MYR 1,000,000 vs MYR 500,000 for DAF).

Can a Labuan crypto license be used to serve clients outside Malaysia?

Yes, Labuan's territorial tax system means income from outside Malaysia is generally tax-exempt. The license is designed for international business, and many licensees serve clients across Asia and globally. However, you must ensure you comply with the laws of the jurisdictions where your clients are based.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements for a Labuan crypto license?

Licensees must file annual returns with the Labuan FSA, maintain a minimum capital, appoint a compliance officer and internal auditor, and submit audited financial statements annually. They must also conduct ongoing AML/CFT monitoring, including transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting. Failure to comply can result in fines or license revocation.

How long does it take to get a Labuan crypto license?

The typical timeline is 4 to 8 months from submission to approval. This includes company incorporation, document preparation, due diligence by the Labuan FSA, and possibly an interview. Is there a physical presence requirement in Labuan?

Can I apply for a Labuan crypto license if my company is already incorporated elsewhere?

What activities are not allowed under a Labuan crypto license?
