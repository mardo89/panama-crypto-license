What Is a Crypto Gambling License in Dubai?

In Dubai, a 'crypto gambling license' is not a single permit. It typically requires approval from the Dubai Gaming Regulatory Authority (DGRA) for any gambling activity, plus a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) license from the Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA) for handling crypto. As of 2026, the DGRA has not yet issued any licenses for online crypto gambling, and the framework is still under development. Operators often base themselves in free zones like Dubai Multi Commodities Centre (DMCC) or Dubai World Trade Centre (DWTC) to access crypto-friendly regulations.

Given the uncertainty, many founders choose established jurisdictions like Panama, which offers a clear licensing path for crypto gambling at a fixed EUR 6,000. Consulting24 helps you compare these routes and set up where we operate directly.

For example, a typical setup might involve a DMCC free zone company holding a VARA VASP license, while the gambling component remains in a grey area. This lack of clarity can delay launches and increase legal costs. In contrast, Panama's gambling license explicitly covers crypto activities under a single regulatory body, the Gaming Control Board, with no separate VASP requirement.

Who Needs a Crypto Gambling License in Dubai?

Any business that offers online casino games, sports betting, or poker using cryptocurrencies and targets customers in Dubai or the UAE needs this license. This includes:

  • Online casinos accepting Bitcoin, Ethereum, or stablecoins
  • Sports betting platforms with crypto deposits
  • Poker rooms using digital assets
  • Fantasy sports operators with crypto payouts
  • Blockchain-based gaming platforms with tokenized rewards

Operating without a license in Dubai carries severe penalties, including fines and imprisonment. License Type & Regulator

The primary regulator for gambling in Dubai is the Dubai Gaming Regulatory Authority (DGRA), established in 2023. For crypto activities, the Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA) oversees VASPs. As of 2026, there is no combined 'crypto gambling license'. Instead, you may need:

  • A DGRA gambling license (if and when issued for online operations)
  • A VARA VASP license for crypto custody, exchange, or transfer services
  • A free zone company license (e.g., from DMCC)

This multi-layered approach increases complexity and cost. In contrast, Panama offers a single gambling license that covers crypto, with no separate VASP requirement. Consulting24 can guide you through the Dubai process, but our direct delivery focuses on Panama, Estonia, and Lithuania.

The DGRA's mandate currently covers land-based casinos and lotteries, with online gambling expected to be included in future regulations. VARA's rulebook, on the other hand, is fully operational and requires VASPs to comply with AML, KYC, and cybersecurity standards. Operators must navigate both regimes, often with conflicting requirements.

Tax Treatment

Dubai offers a 0% corporate tax rate for most businesses, including gambling and crypto operations, as long as they are set up in a free zone. There is no VAT or withholding tax. This is a major advantage over EU jurisdictions where gambling taxes can reach 20-30%. However, operators must comply with UAE economic substance requirements and anti-money laundering (AML) regulations. Panama also offers territorial taxation (no tax on foreign-source income), making it a strong alternative. Consulting24 advises on tax structuring for both Dubai and Panama.

It is important to note that while Dubai has no corporate tax, the UAE introduced a 9% federal corporate tax in 2023 for businesses with profits above AED 375,000, but free zone entities may still qualify for 0% if they meet certain conditions. Panama's territorial system means only income sourced in Panama is taxed, which is ideal for offshore gambling operations.
