What Is a Cryptocurrency License Crypto License?

A cryptocurrency license crypto license is a formal permission granted by a financial regulator to conduct activities involving virtual assets. This includes exchanging cryptocurrencies for fiat or other digital assets, operating trading platforms, providing custody services, and facilitating payments. In the EU, the term is now synonymous with the CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) authorisation under MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation). Outside the EU, jurisdictions like Panama offer a lighter-touch regime for non-EU operations.

The licence ensures that the business meets minimum standards for anti-money laundering (AML), counter-terrorist financing (CTF), cybersecurity, and consumer protection. Without it, operating a crypto business in regulated markets is illegal and can lead to severe penalties. As of 2026, over 30 countries have introduced dedicated crypto licensing frameworks, making compliance a global necessity.

Who Needs a Cryptocurrency License?

Any business that provides one or more of the following services must hold a cryptocurrency license crypto license:

  • Exchange between virtual assets and fiat currencies
  • Exchange between one or more forms of virtual assets
  • Transfer of virtual assets (on behalf of clients)
  • Custody and administration of virtual assets
  • Participation in and provision of financial services related to an issuer's offer or sale of virtual assets
  • Operation of a trading platform for virtual assets
  • Advising on virtual assets or managing portfolios

This applies to startups, established fintech firms, and traditional financial institutions entering the crypto space. Even decentralised finance (DeFi) projects that have a central point of control or offer custodial services may fall under the scope. If you are unsure whether your business model requires a licence, consult with an expert.

License Type & Regulator

Under MiCA, the primary licence type is the CASP authorisation. Each EU member state designates a national competent authority (NCA) to issue and supervise these licences. For example, in Lithuania, the Bank of Lithuania is the regulator; in Estonia, it is the Financial Supervision Authority (FSA). The licence is passportable across the entire EU once granted by one member state.

Outside the EU, Panama offers a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) licence under the supervision of the Superintendencia de Bancos de Panamá (SBP). This licence is attractive for businesses targeting Latin American markets or seeking a cost-effective, non-EU alternative. Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for a CASP licence depends on the services offered:

  • €50,000 for services that do not involve custody of client assets (e.g., exchange-only, advice)
  • €125,000 for services involving custody of client virtual assets or fiat
  • €150,000 for operating a trading platform

These capital thresholds must be met in cash or high-liquidity equivalents and maintained at all times. In Panama, the VASP licence has no statutory minimum capital requirement, though the regulator expects sufficient financial resources to cover operational risks. Tax Treatment

Tax treatment of crypto activities varies by jurisdiction and is a key factor in choosing your licensing location. Under EU tax rules, crypto transactions are generally subject to VAT and corporate income tax (CIT) depending on the nature of the service. For example, in Lithuania, crypto-to-fiat exchanges are VAT-exempt, while crypto-to-crypto trades are taxable as barter transactions. Corporate income tax is 15% on profits.

Panama offers a territorial tax system: only income sourced within Panama is taxed. Crypto businesses that operate outside Panama (e.g., serving global clients) may benefit from 0% local tax on foreign-source income. However, transfer pricing and substance requirements apply. Always obtain professional tax advice tailored to your specific structure.

Allowed Activities Under the License

A cryptocurrency license crypto license permits a defined set of activities. Under MiCA CASP, the allowed activities include:

  • Exchange of virtual assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of virtual assets for other virtual assets
  • Transfer of virtual assets on behalf of clients
  • Custody and administration of virtual assets
  • Operation of a trading platform
  • Placement of virtual assets (underwriting)
  • Receiving and transmitting orders for virtual assets
  • Advising on virtual assets
  • Managing portfolios of virtual assets

Panama's VASP licence covers similar activities but is more flexible for non-EU operations. It is important to note that certain activities, such as issuing stablecoins or operating a decentralised exchange with a central administrator, may require additional authorisation or fall under separate regulations. Alternatives and Comparison: Panama vs Other Jurisdictions

Panama's VASP licence is one of the most cost-effective options globally, with a flat fee of EUR 6,000 and no minimum capital requirement. Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a CASP licence and a VASP licence?

A CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) licence is the EU-wide authorisation under MiCA, allowing passporting across all member states. A VASP (Virtual Asset Service Provider) licence is a national licence, like Panama's, which does not offer EU passporting but may have lower costs and capital requirements.

How long does it take to get a cryptocurrency license crypto license in Panama?

The Panama VASP licence typically takes 2-3 months from application to approval, assuming all documents are in order. Consulting24's flat fee of EUR 6,000 covers company incorporation and the licence application.

Can I use a Panama licence to serve EU clients?

No, a Panama VASP licence does not provide EU passporting rights. To serve EU clients, you need a CASP licence from an EU member state. However, you can combine a Panama licence with an EU licence for global coverage.

What are the minimum capital requirements for a CASP licence?

Under MiCA, the minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for non-custodial services, EUR 125,000 for custodial services, and EUR 150,000 for operating a trading platform. These must be maintained at all times.

Does Panama have a minimum capital requirement for its VASP licence?

No, Panama does not impose a statutory minimum capital for the VASP licence. However, the regulator expects the business to have sufficient financial resources to cover operational risks. Exact requirements are assessed on a case-by-case basis.

What activities are allowed under a Panama VASP licence?

The Panama VASP licence permits exchange of virtual assets for fiat or other virtual assets, custody, transfer, operation of a trading platform, and related services. It does not cover stablecoin issuance or activities regulated by securities laws.

How much does it cost to maintain a crypto licence annually?

Annual costs vary by jurisdiction. For Panama, expect around EUR 2,000-5,000 for compliance, audit, and renewal fees. EU licences typically cost EUR 5,000-15,000 per year. Consulting24 provides detailed cost breakdowns during consultation.

Can I apply for a crypto licence if my business is based outside the jurisdiction?

Yes, but most regulators require a local registered office and local directors or compliance officers. In Panama, you can incorporate a company with local nominee directors if needed. Consulting24 can arrange all local requirements.

What happens if I operate without a licence?

Operating without a required licence is illegal and can result in fines, criminal charges, asset seizure, and reputational damage. Regulators are increasingly active in enforcement, especially under MiCA. Always obtain proper authorisation.

Do I need a separate licence for each country I operate in?

Within the EU, a single CASP licence from one member state allows you to passport services across all EU countries. Outside the EU, you generally need a licence in each jurisdiction where you have a physical presence or target clients. Consulting24 can advise on multi-jurisdiction strategies.
