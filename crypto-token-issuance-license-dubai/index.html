What is a Crypto Token Issuance License in Dubai?

A crypto token issuance license in Dubai is a regulatory approval granted by VARA that permits a company to create and distribute digital tokens. This license is essential for any business looking to launch a token in or from Dubai, ensuring compliance with local anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) laws. The license covers various token types, including utility tokens, security tokens, and stablecoins, depending on the specific activities approved.

Dubai's approach is unique: it offers a dedicated free zone for virtual assets, the Dubai Multi Commodities Centre (DMCC) Crypto Centre, but the ultimate regulator is VARA. The license is part of Dubai's broader strategy to become a global crypto hub, attracting entrepreneurs and investors with its clear rules and business-friendly environment.

As of 2026, VARA has fully implemented its regulatory framework, which includes specific requirements for token issuance. This includes mandatory disclosure documents, token classification, and ongoing reporting obligations.

Who Needs a Crypto Token Issuance License in Dubai?

Any entity that intends to issue tokens in or from Dubai requires this license. This includes:

  • Startups planning an Initial Coin Offering (ICO) or Initial DEX Offering (IDO).

  • Projects issuing stablecoins or asset-backed tokens.
  • Decentralized finance (DeFi) protocols that issue governance tokens.
  • Non-fungible token (NFT) platforms that issue fungible tokens alongside NFTs.

If you are a crypto founder looking to raise capital or create a token-based economy, this license is your gateway. License Type & Regulator

The regulator for crypto token issuance in Dubai is the Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA). VARA was established in 2022 and has since developed a comprehensive rulebook for virtual asset service providers (VASPs). The license type is a 'Token Issuance License' under VARA's framework, which is distinct from other VASP licenses like exchange or custody.

VARA categorizes token issuance as a regulated activity. The license is mandatory for any token offering, whether public or private, if it involves marketing or sale to UAE residents. The license application requires a detailed token whitepaper, legal review, and compliance with VARA's marketing and disclosure rules.

Capital Requirement

Dubai does not impose a specific minimum capital requirement for token issuance licenses under VARA as of 2026. However, VARA requires that the applicant demonstrates sufficient financial resources to operate the business and meet regulatory obligations. This typically means having a bank balance or capital commitment that covers at least 6 months of operational expenses.

In practice, most successful applicants show capital of at least $50,000 to $100,000. This is not a fixed rule but a guideline from VARA's assessments. Consulting24 can help you structure your capital plan to meet VARA's expectations.

Compare this to Lithuania, which has a minimum capital of EUR 125,000 for certain crypto activities, or Panama with no minimum capital requirement.

Tax Treatment for Token Issuance in Dubai

Dubai offers a highly favorable tax environment for crypto businesses. As of 2026, there is no corporate income tax on most business activities in Dubai free zones, including token issuance. The UAE has a federal corporate tax of 9% on profits exceeding AED 375,000, but free zone companies that meet certain conditions (e.g., having substantial activities in the UAE) can continue to benefit from 0% tax.

Additionally, there is no capital gains tax, no withholding tax, and no value-added tax (VAT) on crypto transactions in most cases. This makes Dubai an attractive jurisdiction for token issuers. However, token issuers must comply with transfer pricing rules and economic substance requirements.

Consulting24 advises clients on structuring their token issuance to maximize tax efficiency. For a simpler tax regime, Panama also offers territorial taxation with no tax on foreign-source income.

Allowed Activities Under the License

A crypto token issuance license in Dubai permits the following activities:

  • Creation and issuance of fungible tokens (e.g., ERC-20, BEP-20).
  • Marketing and sale of tokens to qualified investors and the public (subject to VARA's marketing rules).
  • Listing tokens on approved exchanges within Dubai.
  • Managing token sales (e.g., private sale, public sale, airdrops).
  • Issuing stablecoins or asset-backed tokens (requires additional approval).

Activities not covered include operating a crypto exchange, custody services, or providing crypto lending. These require separate VARA licenses. Consulting24 can help you determine which additional licenses you may need if your business model extends beyond token issuance.
