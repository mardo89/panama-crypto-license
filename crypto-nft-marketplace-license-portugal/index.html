What is a Crypto NFT Marketplace License in Portugal?

A crypto NFT marketplace license in Portugal is a VASP registration granted by the Bank of Portugal under MiCA. It allows companies to operate a platform where users can create, buy, sell, and trade non-fungible tokens (NFTs) using cryptocurrencies. The license is mandatory for any business providing exchange services between crypto assets and fiat currencies, or between crypto assets themselves, as well as custody and transfer services.

Under MiCA, NFT marketplaces fall under the classification of 'crypto-asset service providers' (CASPs). The Portuguese transposition of MiCA requires all CASPs to be registered with the Bank of Portugal and comply with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) obligations. This includes conducting customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and reporting suspicious activities.

The license is not a one-time approval; it requires ongoing compliance, including regular audits, capital maintenance, and reporting. Consulting24 advises clients on the full lifecycle of the license, from initial setup to ongoing compliance. For example, a typical NFT marketplace must implement transaction monitoring systems that flag suspicious patterns, such as rapid buying and selling of newly minted tokens, and report these to the regulator within 24 hours.

Who Needs This License?

Any business that operates a platform facilitating the exchange of NFTs for crypto or fiat currency needs this license. This includes:

  • NFT marketplaces (e.g., OpenSea-style platforms)
  • NFT launchpads and minting platforms
  • Secondary marketplaces for NFTs
  • Platforms offering fractionalised NFTs
  • Any service that allows users to buy, sell, or trade NFTs using crypto

If your platform also offers custody of user funds or private keys, additional licensing may apply. Consulting24 can help you determine the exact scope of your license based on your business model. For instance, a marketplace that holds user crypto in a pooled wallet would need custody authorization, which increases capital requirements and compliance obligations.

License Type & Regulator

The relevant regulator in Portugal is the Bank of Portugal (Banco de Portugal), which oversees the registration and supervision of VASPs. The license type is a VASP registration under MiCA, classified as a Crypto-Asset Service Provider (CASP).

Under MiCA, there are several service categories, each with its own capital requirement. For NFT marketplaces, the most relevant categories are:

  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets
  • Transfer services for crypto assets
  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients

The Bank of Portugal maintains a public register of all licensed VASPs. Compliance with AML/CTF rules is strict, and the regulator conducts periodic inspections. As of 2026, the Bank of Portugal has increased its supervisory staff to handle the growing number of applications, but thoroughness remains a priority. Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for CASPs in Portugal depends on the services offered. For NFT marketplaces, typical capital tiers are:

  • EUR 50,000 for exchange services only (no custody)
  • EUR 125,000 for exchange plus custody
  • EUR 150,000 for exchange, custody, and transfer services

These amounts must be maintained as own funds, either in cash or liquid assets. The capital must be held in a Portuguese bank account and is subject to verification by the regulator. Consulting24 advises on the exact requirement based on your service scope. For instance, if your marketplace also offers a wallet service, you will need the EUR 125,000 tier. The capital must be unencumbered and cannot be used for operational expenses.

Tax Treatment

Portugal offers a favourable tax regime for crypto businesses. As of 2026:

  • Corporate income tax (IRC) at a standard rate of 21%, with potential reductions for small and medium enterprises.
  • No VAT on crypto-to-crypto transactions (under EU VAT rules).
  • Capital gains from crypto trading may be exempt for individuals if held for more than one year, but corporate gains are taxed as regular income.
  • NFT transactions are treated as digital asset sales, subject to IRC.

Consulting24 recommends working with a local tax advisor to optimise your structure, as tax laws can change. We can connect you with trusted partners. For example, a marketplace that charges a 2% fee on each sale would pay 21% corporate tax on that fee income, but no VAT on the underlying crypto transaction.

Allowed Activities

With a Portuguese VASP license, your NFT marketplace can engage in the following activities:

  • Operating a platform for buying and selling NFTs using crypto or fiat
  • Providing custody of NFTs and related private keys
  • Facilitating peer-to-peer transfers of NFTs
  • Offering minting services for new NFT collections
  • Running auctions and fixed-price sales
  • Integrating with third-party wallets and payment providers

Activities that are not allowed include operating a centralised exchange without proper segregation of client funds, or offering derivatives without additional licensing. Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto NFT marketplace license in Portugal?

The minimum capital depends on your services: EUR 50,000 for exchange only, EUR 125,000 for exchange plus custody, and EUR 150,000 for exchange, custody, and transfer services. These amounts must be maintained as own funds.

How long does it take to get a Portugal crypto license?

The timeline is typically 3 to 6 months from application submission to approval, depending on the complexity of your business model and the completeness of your documentation.

Can I operate across the EU with a Portuguese crypto license?

Yes, under MiCA, a Portuguese VASP license allows you to passport your services across all EU member states without additional licensing, subject to notification requirements.

What are the tax implications for an NFT marketplace in Portugal?

Corporate income tax is 21% (with potential SME reductions). Crypto-to-crypto transactions are exempt from VAT. NFT sales are treated as digital asset sales and subject to corporate tax.

Do I need a physical office in Portugal?

No, a virtual office is acceptable for the application phase. However, you must have a registered address in Portugal and a local director or representative.
