What Is an Offshore El Salvador Crypto License?

An offshore El Salvador crypto license is a regulatory authorization issued by the Central Reserve Bank of El Salvador (BCR) and the National Digital Assets Commission (CNAD). It allows companies to operate cryptocurrency services such as exchange, custody, and payment processing from within El Salvador, benefiting from its favorable tax regime and legal recognition of Bitcoin.

Unlike onshore licenses in the EU or US, the offshore variant is designed for non-resident entities targeting international markets. It is not a MiCA license but a standalone framework under El Salvador's Digital Assets Law (Ley de Activos Digitales), enacted in 2023 and fully operational by 2026. The license is ideal for businesses that want a regulated status without the high costs and compliance burdens of European jurisdictions.

Key features include territorial taxation, low capital requirements, and a progressive regulator that actively supports innovation. The license covers multiple service classes, allowing companies to tailor their authorization to their specific business model.

Who Needs This License?

The offshore El Salvador crypto license suits a range of businesses looking for a regulated offshore base. Typical applicants include:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (centralized and decentralized) serving global clients
  • Digital asset custodians and wallet providers requiring a formal license
  • Payment processors handling crypto-to-fiat conversions for merchants
  • Token issuers and blockchain startups launching security or utility tokens
  • Hedge funds and investment firms dealing in digital assets
  • Brokerage platforms offering crypto trading services

It is particularly attractive for founders seeking a jurisdictions with low operational costs, minimal bureaucracy, and a progressive regulatory stance. If your target market is Latin America or global, El Salvador offers a stable legal environment with Bitcoin as legal tender, reducing currency risk. The license is also suitable for companies that want to avoid the high capital requirements of MiCA while still obtaining a recognized regulatory status.

License Type & Regulator

The primary regulator is the National Digital Assets Commission (CNAD), an independent body under the Central Reserve Bank of El Salvador. The license is called a Digital Asset Service Provider (DASP) authorization. It covers multiple service classes, each with specific requirements and permitted activities.

Capital Requirement

El Salvador's Digital Assets Law imposes a minimum capital requirement based on the service class. For Class A (exchange), the minimum is USD 50,000. For Class B (custody), it is USD 25,000. Class C and D require USD 10,000. These amounts must be deposited in a local bank account and maintained throughout the license period.

Compared to EU jurisdictions under MiCA (which require EUR 50,000-150,000), El Salvador's capital requirements are lower, making it an attractive offshore option. However, exact figures should be confirmed during a consultation, as they may be adjusted by the regulator. The capital must be fully paid up before license issuance and cannot be withdrawn without CNAD approval. This requirement ensures that the company has sufficient financial resources to operate and meet liabilities.

Tax Treatment

El Salvador offers a territorial tax system: income earned outside the country is generally exempt from local taxation. For offshore license holders, this means no corporate income tax on foreign-sourced revenue. There is no VAT on digital asset services, and no capital gains tax on cryptocurrency transactions.

This tax regime is a major draw for crypto businesses seeking to minimize tax liabilities. However, companies must still comply with international tax reporting standards (CRS) and anti-money laundering (AML) obligations. Consulting24 recommends consulting a tax advisor for your specific situation. Additionally, El Salvador has double tax treaties with a few countries, but they are limited; most offshore income remains untaxed locally.

Allowed Activities

The offshore El Salvador crypto license permits a wide range of activities under the Digital Assets Law:

  • Operating a cryptocurrency exchange (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto)
  • Providing custodial wallet services
  • Processing payments using digital assets
  • Issuing tokens (security or utility) subject to CNAD approval
  • Offering staking and lending services
  • Acting as a digital asset broker or dealer
  • Running an automated market maker (AMM) or DeFi platform

Activities that are explicitly prohibited include gambling-related services and any operations that violate international sanctions. The license does not cover derivatives or securities trading unless separately authorized. The CNAD has issued guidance on what constitutes a digital asset, providing clarity for token issuers.

Step-by-Step Process

Obtaining an offshore El Salvador crypto license involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step:

  1. Pre-application assessment: Determine the appropriate service class and prepare a business plan. This includes a detailed description of operations, target market, and risk management.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Salvadoran company (Sociedad Anónima) with a local registered address. The process takes 1-2 weeks.
  3. Documentation: Gather KYC/AML policies, financial statements, and background checks for directors and beneficial owners. All documents must be notarized and apostilled.
  4. Submit application: File with the CNAD, including the application fee. The application form requires detailed information on the business model and compliance framework.
  5. Due diligence review: The CNAD conducts a 60-90 day review, possibly requesting additional information. They may interview directors.
  6. Approval and license issuance: Once approved, pay the remaining fee and receive the license certificate. The license is valid for one year and renewable.
  7. Post-licensing compliance: Submit periodic reports (quarterly or annually) and maintain the minimum capital. Appoint a local compliance officer.

The entire process can be completed remotely with the help of local representatives. Consulting24 provides end-to-end coordination.

Benefits of an Offshore El Salvador Crypto License

El Salvador offers unique advantages for crypto entrepreneurs:

  • Low capital requirements: Starting at USD 10,000, much lower than EU MiCA thresholds.
  • Territorial tax: No tax on foreign income, no VAT, no capital gains tax.
  • Bitcoin legal tender: Full legal recognition of Bitcoin, reducing regulatory uncertainty.
  • Fast processing: 3-6 months compared to 6-12 months in some jurisdictions.
  • Progressive regulator: CNAD is open to innovation and provides clear guidance.
  • No mandatory fiat reserves: Unlike some jurisdictions, El Salvador does not require segregation of client funds in fiat, though best practices apply.

Compared to Panama, which offers a flat EUR 6,000 company setup but no dedicated crypto license, El Salvador provides a formal regulatory framework. For businesses needing a regulated status, El Salvador is a strong choice. The license also enhances credibility with partners and customers.

Compliance & Trust

Operating under an offshore El Salvador crypto license requires ongoing compliance with AML/CFT regulations. License holders must appoint a local compliance officer, conduct customer due diligence (CDD), and report suspicious transactions to the Financial Investigation Unit (UIF). Annual audits by a licensed auditor are mandatory.

This is general guidance, not legal advice. Each business should engage local legal counsel to ensure full compliance. Consulting24 works with reputable Salvadoran law firms to help you meet these obligations. The compliance regime includes regular training for staff, transaction monitoring systems, and record-keeping for at least five years. Failure to comply can result in fines or license suspension.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make these errors when applying for an offshore El Salvador crypto license:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing AML policies or financial statements cause delays. Ensure all documents are properly notarized and translated.
  • Underestimating capital: Failing to maintain the minimum capital can lead to license revocation. The capital must be deposited in a local bank and cannot be used for operations.
  • Ignoring tax reporting: Even with territorial tax, CRS and FATCA obligations apply. Non-compliance can trigger audits.
  • Choosing the wrong service class: Applying for a class that doesn't match your business model. For example, a pure exchange should apply for Class A, not Class C.
  • Neglecting local presence: A local director and office are required; virtual offices may not suffice. The CNAD may conduct physical inspections.
  • Overlooking beneficial ownership disclosure: All ultimate beneficial owners must be disclosed; hidden ownership can lead to rejection.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between an offshore and onshore El Salvador crypto license?

An offshore license is designed for non-resident companies serving international clients, with territorial tax benefits. An onshore license would be for domestic operations. El Salvador's Digital Assets Law does not formally distinguish between offshore and onshore, but the license is typically used for offshore activities due to the tax regime.

Can I apply for multiple service classes under one license?

Yes, you can apply for multiple classes (e.g., Class A and B) in a single application. The capital requirement will be the highest among the classes applied for. The CNAD encourages bundling to cover all intended activities.

Is the El Salvador crypto license recognized internationally?

Recognition depends on the counterparty. The license is issued by a sovereign regulator and complies with FATF standards, so it is generally accepted by banks and partners. However, it does not provide EU passporting like MiCA licenses.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations after obtaining the license?

License holders must submit quarterly transaction reports, annual audited financial statements, and maintain AML/CFT programs. A local compliance officer must be appointed. The CNAD may conduct on-site inspections.

Do I need to travel to El Salvador to apply?

No, the entire process can be handled remotely through local representatives. However, some banks may require in-person meetings for account opening. Consulting24 coordinates with local partners to minimize travel.

How long does the license remain valid?

The license is valid for one year and must be renewed annually. Renewal requires payment of a fee and demonstration of ongoing compliance. There is no maximum term; licenses can be renewed indefinitely.

Can I use the license to operate in other countries?

The license authorizes operations from El Salvador. If you serve clients in other countries, you must comply with their local laws. The license does not provide cross-border recognition, but it can help with banking and partnerships.

What happens if I fail to maintain the minimum capital?

The CNAD may suspend or revoke the license. You are required to maintain the capital at all times. If you need to use the funds, you must apply for a reduction in capital, which is rarely granted.

Is there a physical office requirement?

Yes, you must have a registered office in El Salvador. A virtual office may not be sufficient; the CNAD expects a physical presence with staff. Consulting24 can help arrange suitable office space.

How does the tax treatment work for a US-based founder?

US citizens are subject to worldwide taxation by the IRS. The territorial tax in El Salvador means no local tax on foreign income, but you must still report and pay US taxes. Consult a tax advisor for your specific situation.

Can I convert my Panama company to an El Salvador license?

You cannot directly convert, but you can set up a new Salvadoran company and transfer operations. Panama company setup is quick and cheap, so many founders maintain both structures for different purposes.

What is the success rate of applications?

The CNAD does not publish statistics. With proper documentation and a solid business plan, most applications succeed. Common reasons for rejection include incomplete KYC, unclear business models, or links to high-risk activities.
