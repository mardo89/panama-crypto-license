What Is the Hong Kong Crypto Token Issuance License?

The Hong Kong crypto token issuance license refers to the regulatory approval required to issue digital tokens to investors in Hong Kong. Depending on the token's characteristics, it may be classified as a security or a virtual asset. The SFC regulates token issuance under the Securities and Futures Ordinance (SFO) and the Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorist Financing Ordinance (AMLO). For security tokens, firms need a Type 1 (dealing in securities) and Type 7 (automated trading) license. For non-security tokens, a VASP license under AMLO may be required. The regime aims to protect investors while fostering innovation.

Key features include mandatory disclosure, investor suitability assessments, and ongoing reporting. The SFC also requires issuers to have a physical office in Hong Kong and employ competent staff. Consulting24 helps firms understand the classification of their token and the corresponding license requirements. As of 2026, the SFC has published detailed guidelines on token classification, including factors such as the token's rights, transferability, and marketing. For example, a token that entitles holders to a share of profits is almost certainly a security, while a pure utility token used for accessing a platform may not be. However, even utility tokens can be deemed securities if they are marketed as investment opportunities. Consulting24's pre-assessment service can help you avoid misclassification, which is a common cause of application delays.

Who Needs This License?

Any entity that intends to issue digital tokens to the public in Hong Kong or to Hong Kong residents likely needs a license. This includes:

  • Startups conducting security token offerings (STOs)
  • Platforms issuing utility tokens that may be deemed securities
  • Decentralized finance (DeFi) projects with token sales
  • Real-world asset tokenization projects
  • Funds issuing tokenized shares

If your token grants rights to profits, voting, or dividends, it is likely a security. Even utility tokens can fall under securities laws if they are marketed as investments. For example, a project that issues a token representing ownership in a real estate property would need a Type 1 license. Conversely, a token that simply provides access to a software platform may not require a license if it is not marketed as an investment. However, the SFC has broad discretion, so it is prudent to seek expert advice. Consulting24 can conduct a preliminary analysis based on your tokenomics and business model.

License Type & Regulator

The primary regulator is the Securities and Futures Commission (SFC) of Hong Kong. Depending on the token type, the applicable license may be:

  • Type 1 (Dealing in Securities) and Type 7 (Automated Trading): For platforms that issue and trade security tokens.
  • VASP License: Under AMLO, for virtual asset service providers dealing with non-security tokens. As of 2026, the VASP regime is fully implemented, requiring all centralized crypto exchanges and issuers of non-security tokens to obtain a license from the SFC.

Both regimes require compliance with capital, AML, and conduct rules. The SFC also publishes a list of licensed entities. Consulting24 can help determine the correct license based on your token's structure. For example, if your token is a security, you will need to apply for Type 1 and 7 licenses, which involve a more rigorous process including responsible officer requirements. If your token is a non-security virtual asset, the VASP license is applicable, which has slightly different capital and reporting requirements. The SFC also offers a streamlined process for firms that already hold a Type 1 license and wish to add token issuance services. Consulting24 can guide you through the nuances of each license type.

Capital Requirement

For Type 1 and Type 7 licenses, the minimum paid-up capital is HKD 5 million (approximately USD 640,000). For VASP licenses, the capital requirement is HKD 5 million as well. Additionally, firms must maintain liquid capital of at least HKD 3 million. These figures are subject to change; Consulting24 provides up-to-date information during consultations. Capital must be held in a licensed bank in Hong Kong. The SFC may require higher capital for firms with larger trading volumes or higher risk profiles. For example, a platform that intends to issue tokens to retail investors may need to demonstrate additional financial resources to cover potential investor losses. Consulting24 can help you structure your capital to meet regulatory requirements while optimizing for your business model. It is important to note that the capital must be fully paid up before the license is granted, and it cannot be withdrawn without SFC approval.

Tax Treatment

Hong Kong operates a territorial tax system, meaning only profits sourced in Hong Kong are taxable. The profits tax rate is 16.5% for corporations. Crypto token issuance income may be considered taxable if the issuance activities are conducted in Hong Kong. However, capital gains on tokens held as investments may be exempt. The Inland Revenue Department (IRD) has issued guidelines on digital asset taxation. Proper structuring, such as using a Hong Kong holding company, can optimize tax. Consulting24 advises on tax planning in coordination with local tax experts. For example, if your token issuance is structured as a sale of a security, the proceeds may be treated as trading income. Conversely, if the tokens are held for long-term investment, any gains may be capital in nature and potentially exempt. The IRD has a practice note on digital assets that provides some clarity, but each case is assessed on its facts. Consulting24 can introduce you to tax advisors who specialize in crypto. In comparison, Panama offers territorial taxation with no capital gains tax on crypto, and a flat EUR 6,000 license fee, making it attractive for tax-efficient structuring.

Allowed Activities

A licensed entity can engage in the following activities:

  • Issuance of security tokens and non-security tokens (subject to classification)
  • Marketing and distribution of tokens to professional and retail investors (with suitability checks)
  • Operating a trading platform for the issued tokens
  • Custody of client assets (if licensed as a VASP)
  • Providing token-related advisory services

Activities not allowed include unlicensed derivatives trading, lending of client assets without consent, and activities that contravene AML laws. The SFC may impose additional conditions on a case-by-case basis. For example, a VASP license may restrict the types of virtual assets that can be offered, or require that retail investors only trade certain tokens. The SFC also prohibits the use of use or margin trading for retail clients. Consulting24 can help you understand the scope of your license and any conditions that may apply. It is important to note that the SFC has the power to vary or revoke a license if the licensee engages in unauthorized activities.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between Type 1/7 and VASP license for token issuance?

Type 1 and 7 licenses are for security tokens (securities under SFO), while VASP license is for non-security virtual assets under AMLO. The choice depends on token classification. Both require HKD 5 million capital. The SFC has published a flowchart to help determine classification, but expert advice is recommended.

How long does it take to get a Hong Kong crypto token issuance license?

Typically 6 to 12 months, depending on application completeness and SFC review. Complex cases may take longer. Consulting24 advises on expediting the process by preparing thorough documentation and pre-qualifying responsible officers.

What are the capital requirements for a Hong Kong token issuance license?

Minimum paid-up capital is HKD 5 million for both Type 1/7 and VASP licenses. Additionally, firms must maintain liquid capital of at least HKD 3 million. Capital must be held in a licensed Hong Kong bank.

Is a Hong Kong crypto token issuance license recognized in other countries?

No, it is specific to Hong Kong. However, it may facilitate business relationships with Asian partners and investors. For global coverage, firms often combine it with licenses in other jurisdictions like Panama or Lithuania.

Can I issue a token without a license in Hong Kong?

If the token is not a security and you are not offering it to the Hong Kong public, you may not need a license. However, the SFC has broad discretion, and any marketing to Hong Kong residents could trigger licensing requirements. It is safer to seek a legal opinion.

What are the ongoing costs after obtaining the license?

Ongoing costs include annual SFC fees (approximately HKD 50,000-100,000), compliance officer salary, audit fees, and AML software. Total annual costs can range from HKD 500,000 to 1,000,000. Consulting24 can provide a detailed estimate.

Do I need a physical office in Hong Kong?

Yes, the SFC requires licensed entities to have a physical office in Hong Kong. The office must be suitable for business operations and meet regulatory standards. Consulting24 can help with office setup guidance.

What happens if my token is classified differently after licensing?

If the SFC reclassifies your token, you may need to apply for a different license or amend your existing one. This can be costly and time-consuming. Consulting24's pre-assessment helps minimize this risk.

How does Hong Kong compare to Panama for token issuance?

Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 fee, no capital requirement, and territorial tax. Hong Kong has higher costs (HKD 1M+) and capital requirements but provides access to Asian markets and institutional credibility. Consulting24 can help you choose based on your goals.
