What Is the Cyprus Crypto Fund License?

The Cyprus crypto fund license, officially the CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) authorisation under MiCA, is a regulatory approval granted by CySEC. It allows firms to provide crypto-related services legally within the European Economic Area. The license covers a range of activities, including custody, exchange, and platform operation. Cyprus was an early adopter of crypto regulation, and its current framework is aligned with the EU's MiCA regulation, which came into full force in 2025. This means that any firm holding a Cyprus CASP can passport its services across all EU member states.

The license is often referred to as a 'crypto fund license' because it is commonly used by investment funds that deal in crypto assets. However, the term is broader: it applies to any entity offering crypto asset services. The regulatory regime is strong, requiring firms to meet capital, governance, and AML requirements. Who Needs a Cyprus Crypto License?

The Cyprus crypto license is essential for any business that intends to offer crypto asset services in or from Cyprus. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Operators of crypto trading platforms
  • Asset managers running crypto investment funds
  • Brokers dealing in crypto derivatives
  • Payment service providers using crypto

If your business falls into any of these categories and you plan to serve EU clients, you need a CASP license from an EU member state. Cyprus is a popular choice due to its pragmatic regulator, relatively low costs, and efficient processing times. Compared to other jurisdictions, Cyprus offers a balanced approach between regulatory rigour and operational flexibility.

License Type and Regulator

The Cyprus crypto license is a CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) authorisation under the Investment Services and Activities and Regulated Markets Law of 2017, as amended to implement MiCA. The regulator is the Cyprus Securities and Exchange Commission (CySEC), based in Nicosia. CySEC is a well-respected EU regulator with extensive experience in financial services regulation, including crypto assets.

The license is categorised by the types of services offered. There are three main classes, each with a different minimum capital requirement:

Service ClassDescriptionMinimum Capital (EUR)
Class 1Services not involving client money or crypto (e.g., advice, marketing)50,000
Class 2Services involving custody or exchange of crypto125,000
Class 3Operating a trading platform or multilateral trading facility150,000

These capital tiers are mandated by MiCA and apply uniformly across all EU member states. Cyprus has not added any additional local capital requirements. Consulting24 can help you determine which class applies to your business.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a Cyprus CASP is determined by the service class, as shown in the table above. The capital must be held in a bank account in Cyprus or another EU member state and must be fully paid up before the license is granted. The capital can be used for operational expenses, but it must always remain above the minimum threshold. CySEC may require additional capital based on the firm's risk profile, but the standard tiers are EUR 50,000, 125,000, and 150,000.

For crypto fund managers, the capital requirement is typically the Class 2 or Class 3 level, depending on whether they operate a platform. Consulting24 can help structure the capital to meet regulatory requirements while optimising liquidity.

Tax Treatment

Cyprus offers a very favourable tax regime for crypto businesses. The corporate income tax rate is 12.5%, one of the lowest in the EU. Additionally, Cyprus has no withholding tax on dividends and no capital gains tax on the disposal of securities (including crypto assets, under certain conditions). For crypto funds, profits from trading are generally subject to corporate tax, but capital gains from long-term holdings may be exempt. VAT is not applicable on crypto transactions, and there is no stamp duty on crypto trades.

It is important to note that tax treatment depends on the specific activities and structure of the fund. Consulting24 advises clients to engage a local tax advisor to ensure compliance. The Cyprus tax authorities have issued guidelines on crypto taxation, but the environment is evolving. As of 2026, the regime remains highly competitive compared to other EU hubs.

Allowed Activities

A Cyprus CASP license permits the following activities, as defined under MiCA:

  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Operation of a trading platform for crypto assets
  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets
  • Execution of orders for crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Placing of crypto assets
  • Receiving and transmitting orders for crypto assets
  • Providing advice on crypto assets
  • Portfolio management of crypto assets

Each activity may require a specific authorisation within the license. The scope can be tailored to the business model. Cyprus also allows the operation of crypto ATMs and the issuance of asset-referenced tokens (ARTs) and e-money tokens (EMTs) under separate regimes. Firms must also appoint a compliance officer and an AML officer, both resident in Cyprus. Annual audits and periodic reporting to CySEC are mandatory.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Many applicants make avoidable errors that delay or derail their license application. Common mistakes include:

  • Incomplete documentation: Failing to provide a detailed business plan or AML policy.
  • Underestimating capital: Not having the full capital available at the time of application.
  • Ignoring substance requirements: CySEC expects a physical office and local management in Cyprus.
  • Poor background checks: Key personnel with criminal records or regulatory sanctions will be rejected.
  • Overlooking ongoing compliance: After approval, many firms neglect reporting obligations.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Cyprus crypto fund license?

The minimum capital depends on the service class: EUR 50,000 for Class 1, EUR 125,000 for Class 2, and EUR 150,000 for Class 3. These are set by MiCA and apply to all CASP licenses in Cyprus.

How long does it take to get a Cyprus crypto license?

The typical timeline is 6 to 9 months from application to approval. This can vary based on the completeness of your documentation and CySEC's workload.

Can I passport a Cyprus crypto license to other EU countries?

Yes, a Cyprus CASP license allows you to provide services across all EU member states under the MiCA passporting regime, without needing additional licenses.

What is the tax rate for crypto businesses in Cyprus?

The corporate income tax rate is 12.5%. There is no withholding tax on dividends and no capital gains tax on securities under certain conditions. Crypto transactions are VAT-exempt.

Does Cyprus require a physical office for a crypto license?

Yes, CySEC expects licensees to have a physical office in Cyprus with local management and staff. This is part of the substance requirements.

What activities are allowed under a Cyprus crypto license?

Allowed activities include custody, exchange (fiat and crypto), operation of trading platforms, order execution, advice, and portfolio management of crypto assets, among others.

Can I operate a crypto fund under a Cyprus CASP license?

Yes, a crypto fund can operate under a CASP license if it provides investment services. For fund-specific structures, an AIF (Alternative Investment Fund) license may also be needed.

Is the Cyprus crypto license recognised globally?

The license is primarily recognised within the EU. Outside the EU, acceptance depends on the counterparty's policies. However, a CySEC license is well-regarded internationally.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

Ongoing requirements include annual audits, AML/CFT reporting, transaction monitoring, and maintaining the minimum capital. CySEC conducts periodic inspections.

How does the Cyprus license compare to Panama?

Panama is cheaper (EUR 6,000 flat) and faster (2-3 months) but does not offer EU passporting. Cyprus is more expensive but provides access to the EU market. Consulting24 can help you decide.
