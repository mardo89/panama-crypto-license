What is the Crypto Broker License Indonesia?

The crypto broker license Indonesia, officially known as a Crypto Asset Physical Trader (CPFAK) license, is a regulatory authorization issued by Bappebti (Badan Pengawas Perdagangan Berjangka Komoditi). This license allows companies to operate as crypto asset brokers, facilitating the buying and selling of digital assets for customers. The license is part of Indonesia's broader regulatory framework for crypto assets, which classifies them as commodities under the Commodity Futures Trading Law.

Key characteristics of the license include:

  • Issued by Bappebti, under the Ministry of Trade.
  • Required for any entity offering crypto trading services to Indonesian residents.
  • Includes compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) regulations.
  • Requires partnership with a recognized clearing house and a licensed crypto exchange.
  • Mandatory technology audit by an approved auditor before application.

The CPFAK license is distinct from a securities license. Crypto assets are treated as commodities, so the regulatory approach differs from jurisdictions like Malta or Switzerland. As of 2026, Bappebti has licensed over 30 crypto asset traders, and the list of approved crypto assets available for trading is updated regularly. The license is valid indefinitely but requires annual renewal of certain compliance filings.

Who Needs a Crypto Broker License in Indonesia?

Any business that intends to offer crypto asset trading services to customers in Indonesia must obtain a CPFAK license. This includes:

  • Crypto broker platforms that match buyers and sellers.
  • Over-the-counter (OTC) desks facilitating large trades.
  • Any entity acting as an intermediary for crypto transactions.
  • Mobile apps or web platforms that allow users to trade crypto.

If you are a foreign company looking to enter the Indonesian market, you will typically need to establish a local entity (PT) and then apply for the license. Foreign ownership of a PT is allowed, but there are restrictions on certain business classifications. License Type and Regulator

The regulator for crypto broker licenses in Indonesia is Bappebti (Commodity Futures Trading Regulatory Agency). The license type is a Crypto Asset Physical Trader (CPFAK) license. This is not a securities license but a commodity trading license, as crypto assets are treated as commodities in Indonesia.

Bappebti has issued regulations, most notably Bappebti Regulation No. 8 of 2021 (and subsequent amendments), which set out the requirements for crypto asset traders. The regulatory framework is evolving, and as of 2026, Bappebti continues to refine its approach, including potential coordination with the Financial Services Authority (OJK) for broader financial oversight. In 2025, Bappebti introduced stricter AML requirements and mandatory reporting of suspicious transactions to the Indonesian Financial Transaction Reports and Analysis Center (PPATK).

Bappebti also maintains a list of approved crypto assets that can be traded. As of 2026, there are over 500 approved assets, including Bitcoin, Ethereum, and various altcoins. New assets must be submitted for approval before listing.

Capital Requirement

As of 2026, Bappebti requires a minimum paid-up capital for crypto asset traders. The exact amount is subject to change, but based on current regulations, the minimum capital requirement is approximately IDR 1.5 billion (around EUR 90,000). This capital must be deposited in a bank account in Indonesia and cannot be withdrawn without Bappebti's approval. The capital requirement may be higher for entities offering additional services or with larger trading volumes.

It is important to note that the capital must be maintained throughout the license period. If the company incurs losses that reduce capital below the minimum, Bappebti may suspend or revoke the license. Tax Treatment

Indonesia has specific tax rules for crypto transactions. As of 2026, crypto asset trading is subject to:

  • Value Added Tax (VAT): 0.1% of the transaction value for crypto asset sales (PPN).
  • Income Tax (PPh): 0.1% of the transaction value for the seller (final tax).
  • Corporate Income Tax: Standard rate of 22% for the company's net profit.
  • Withholding Tax: Brokers must withhold and remit taxes on behalf of customers.

Tax obligations apply to both the broker and the customers. Brokers are responsible for withholding and remitting taxes on behalf of customers. It is essential to work with a local tax advisor to ensure compliance. The tax regime is relatively simple compared to some jurisdictions, but penalties for non-compliance can be severe. Allowed Activities

With a crypto broker license Indonesia, you are permitted to:

  • Facilitate the trading of crypto assets listed on Bappebti-approved exchanges.
  • Offer custody services for customer assets (subject to additional requirements).
  • Provide market data and analysis to customers.
  • Engage in OTC trading with institutional clients.
  • Offer staking services for approved assets (with Bappebti approval).

However, the license does not allow:

  • Operating a crypto exchange (requires a separate exchange license).
  • Offering derivative products without additional approval.
  • Providing lending or margin trading unless explicitly permitted.
  • Listing unapproved crypto assets.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The application process for a crypto broker license in Indonesia involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step with local partners.

  1. Company Incorporation: Establish a PT (Perseroan Terbatas) with a suitable business classification for crypto trading. This typically takes 4-6 weeks.
  2. Prepare Documentation: Draft internal policies (AML, CTF, risk management), business plan, and technical specifications. Policies must be in Indonesian language.
  3. Technology Audit: Engage an approved auditor to review your trading platform and security systems. The auditor will check for compliance with Bappebti's technical standards, including data protection and system resilience.
  4. Submit Application: File the application with Bappebti, including all required documents and proof of capital. The application fee is paid at this stage.
  5. Bappebti Review: The regulator reviews the application, which may include interviews and additional requests. This phase can take 2-4 months.
  6. Obtain License: Upon approval, you receive the CPFAK license and can commence operations. You must then complete registration with the clearing house and exchange.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make these errors when applying for the Indonesia crypto license:

  • Incomplete Documentation: Missing policies or insufficient detail can delay the process. Ensure all documents are in Indonesian and properly notarized.
  • Underestimating Capital: Failing to maintain the minimum capital after license issuance can lead to suspension. Plan for ongoing capital maintenance.
  • Ignoring Local Partnerships: Not securing a partnership with a Bappebti-approved exchange beforehand can block the application. Start these discussions early.
  • Poor Technology Security: Submitting a platform that does not meet security standards will result in rejection. Invest in a thorough technology audit.
  • Assuming Fast Approval: The timeline can be longer than expected; plan accordingly. Build in buffer time for unexpected delays.
  • Neglecting Tax Registration: Failing to register for VAT and income tax before operations can lead to penalties.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a crypto broker license and an exchange license in Indonesia?

A crypto broker license (CPFAK) allows you to facilitate trading for customers, matching buy and sell orders. An exchange license allows you to operate a platform where multiple brokers and traders interact. Brokers typically partner with an exchange to execute trades. Both licenses are issued by Bappebti but have different requirements.

Can a foreign company apply for a crypto broker license in Indonesia?

Yes, but you must first establish a local PT (limited liability company) with Indonesian directors and shareholders. Foreign ownership is allowed up to 100% for certain business classifications, but you need to comply with the Investment Coordinating Board (BKPM) regulations. Consulting24 can help with the incorporation process.

How long does the technology audit take?

The technology audit typically takes 4-6 weeks, depending on the complexity of your platform and the auditor's schedule. The auditor will review your system architecture, security measures, data protection, and disaster recovery plans. You should prepare your platform thoroughly before engaging an auditor.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements after obtaining the license?

You must submit quarterly reports to Bappebti on trading volumes, customer complaints, and suspicious transactions. Annual audits are required. You also need to update your AML/CTF policies regularly and conduct staff training. Failure to comply can result in fines or license revocation.

Is there a minimum capital requirement for maintaining the license?

Yes, you must maintain the minimum paid-up capital of IDR 1.5 billion (around EUR 90,000) throughout the license period. If capital falls below this threshold due to losses, you must top it up within a specified period. Bappebti monitors capital levels through periodic reports.

Can I trade all cryptocurrencies with a broker license?

No, you can only trade crypto assets that are approved by Bappebti. As of 2026, over 500 assets are approved, including major ones like Bitcoin and Ethereum. To list a new asset, you must submit an application to Bappebti for approval. Trading unapproved assets is illegal.

What taxes apply to crypto brokers in Indonesia?

Brokers must pay corporate income tax at 22% on net profit. Additionally, they must withhold and remit VAT (0.1% of transaction value) and income tax (0.1% for sellers) on behalf of customers. Brokers are also subject to withholding tax on certain payments. Consulting with a local tax advisor is essential.

How does the Indonesia license compare to Panama for crypto brokers?

Panama offers a faster and cheaper option with no specific crypto license required and no capital requirement. However, Panama does not provide direct access to the Indonesian market. Indonesia offers a regulated environment with a large local user base but higher costs and longer timeline. Your choice depends on your target market.

What happens if I operate without a license in Indonesia?

Operating without a license is illegal and can result in criminal penalties, including imprisonment and fines. Bappebti actively monitors the market and can shut down unlicensed platforms. Additionally, banks and payment gateways will not work with unlicensed entities. Always obtain the license before offering services.
