Overview: What Is the El Salvador vs Switzerland Crypto License?

The El Salvador vs Switzerland crypto license comparison centers on two fundamentally different regulatory philosophies. El Salvador, under its Bitcoin Law (2021) and the Comisión Nacional de Activos Digitales (CNDA), issues a Digital Asset Service Provider (DASP) license. It is a pioneering jurisdiction that embraces Bitcoin as legal tender, offering a light-touch regulatory approach with no capital gains tax on Bitcoin. Switzerland, regulated by the Swiss Financial Market Supervisory Authority (FINMA), provides a comprehensive framework under the Financial Market Infrastructure Act (FMIA) and the Anti-Money Laundering Act (AMLA). Swiss licenses are categorised into fintech licenses, securities firms, or traditional banking, depending on the services offered.

Both jurisdictions are attractive but serve different business profiles. El Salvador targets innovative startups and Bitcoin-focused enterprises, while Switzerland appeals to established firms seeking a reputed, stable regulatory environment. The choice depends on your target market, risk appetite, and operational scale. Consulting24 helps you evaluate which jurisdiction aligns with your long-term goals.

Who Needs an El Salvador or Switzerland Crypto License?

Businesses that require a crypto license in either jurisdiction include:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (centralized and peer-to-peer)
  • Wallet providers (custodial and non-custodial)
  • Payment processors handling digital assets
  • Asset managers offering crypto investment products
  • Token issuers and DeFi platforms interacting with fiat
  • Bitcoin ATMs and other fiat-to-crypto gateways

If your primary focus is on Bitcoin adoption and you want to benefit from a tax-friendly regime, El Salvador is compelling. If you need to serve European clients and require a high-trust license for banking relationships, Switzerland is often the better fit. Capital Requirement

El Salvador does not impose a fixed minimum capital requirement for the DASP license. Instead, the CNDA assesses capital adequacy on a case-by-case basis, typically expecting a minimum of USD 50,000 to USD 200,000 depending on the risk profile. Switzerland, under FINMA, requires a minimum capital of CHF 1.5 million for a securities firm license, while the fintech license requires CHF 500,000 (approximately EUR 500,000). For full banking, the requirement is CHF 10 million. These figures are as of 2026 and are subject to change. Consulting24 helps you structure the capital efficiently. For instance, a Swiss fintech license applicant must demonstrate that the capital is fully paid up and held in a blocked account.

Tax Treatment

Tax is a major differentiator in the El Salvador vs Switzerland crypto license comparison. El Salvador offers a unique advantage: no capital gains tax on Bitcoin or other digital assets (as per the Bitcoin Law). Corporate income tax is 30%, but no VAT on digital asset transactions. Switzerland, on the other hand, has a cantonal tax system with corporate tax rates ranging from 11.9% to 21.6%. Profits from crypto trading are generally subject to corporate income tax, but private investors are exempt from capital gains tax. Switzerland also has no withholding tax on dividends from qualifying holdings. Both jurisdictions have double taxation treaties, but El Salvador's network is limited. Consulting24 recommends a tax review with a local advisor. For example, a company based in Zug (Switzerland) might pay around 14% effective corporate tax, while an El Salvador entity pays 30% but saves on capital gains.

Allowed Activities Under Each License

El Salvador's DASP license permits the following activities:

  • Exchange of digital assets for fiat or other digital assets
  • Custody and wallet services
  • Payment services using digital assets
  • Issuance of digital assets (subject to additional approval)
  • Bitcoin ATM operations

Switzerland's fintech license allows deposit-taking up to CHF 100 million (no lending), payment services, and limited securities activities. A securities firm license permits trading, underwriting, and custody of securities (including crypto assets). Full banking license covers all activities. Switzerland does not allow unlicensed issuance of tokens that qualify as securities. Consulting24 clarifies which activities fit your business plan. For instance, a DeFi platform offering staking may need a Swiss banking license if it takes deposits, whereas in El Salvador it could operate under a DASP license.

Compliance & Ongoing Obligations

Both El Salvador and Switzerland require ongoing compliance: AML/CFT programs, transaction monitoring, regular reporting, and annual audits. El Salvador's CNDA mandates quarterly reports on transaction volumes and customer due diligence. Switzerland requires FINMA to be notified of material changes, and an annual audit by a licensed auditor. Non-compliance can result in fines or license revocation. Frequently asked questions

What is the main difference between El Salvador and Switzerland crypto licenses?

El Salvador offers a DASP license with no capital gains tax on Bitcoin, lower costs, and faster processing (3-6 months). Switzerland provides FINMA-regulated licenses (fintech, securities, or banking) with higher capital requirements, longer timelines (6-12 months), but stronger banking infrastructure and regulatory reputation.

Which jurisdiction is cheaper for a crypto license?

El Salvador is cheaper, with estimated costs of EUR 10,000-25,000. Switzerland costs EUR 50,000-150,000 depending on license type. However, consider ongoing compliance costs: El Salvador requires quarterly reports, while Switzerland requires annual audits.

Do I need a physical office in El Salvador or Switzerland?

Both jurisdictions typically require a local registered address and a local director or manager. El Salvador may accept a virtual office, while Switzerland often expects a physical presence. Consulting24 can help arrange compliant office solutions.

Can I use an El Salvador license to serve European clients?

No, El Salvador is not part of the EU. For European clients, a Swiss license (though not EU) offers better access due to bilateral agreements, or consider an EU license like Lithuania's CASP under MiCA.

What are the capital requirements for a Swiss fintech license?

The minimum capital for a Swiss fintech license is CHF 500,000 (approx. EUR 500,000). This must be fully paid up and held in a blocked account. Higher capital may be required based on business volume.

Is there a minimum capital for El Salvador's DASP license?

El Salvador does not have a fixed minimum capital. The CNDA assesses capital adequacy case-by-case, typically expecting USD 50,000-200,000. Consulting24 can help you prepare a capital plan that meets regulatory expectations.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Switzerland?

The process takes 6-12 months from application to approval. Pre-application preparation (business plan, AML policies) can take 2-3 months. Consulting24 streamlines the process to minimize delays.

What activities are allowed under El Salvador's DASP license?

The DASP license covers exchange, custody, payment services, token issuance (with additional approval), and Bitcoin ATM operations. It does not allow deposit-taking or lending without a separate banking license.

Can I convert a Swiss fintech license to a full banking license later?

Yes, but you must meet higher capital requirements (CHF 10 million) and additional regulatory standards. Many crypto firms start with a fintech license and upgrade as they grow.

Does Switzerland tax crypto gains for corporations?

Yes, corporate profits from crypto trading are subject to cantonal and federal income tax (11.9%-21.6%). Private investors are exempt from capital gains tax. El Salvador has no capital gains tax on Bitcoin but corporate tax is 30%.
