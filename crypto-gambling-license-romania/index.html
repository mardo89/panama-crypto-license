What Is a Crypto Gambling License Romania?

A crypto gambling license Romania is a permit issued by the National Gambling Office (ONJN) that allows operators to offer gambling services using cryptocurrencies. The license covers both online and land-based operations, but the focus is on digital platforms. Romania's framework is one of the few in the EU that explicitly addresses crypto gambling, making it attractive for operators seeking a regulated environment.

The license is part of Romania's gambling law (OG 77/2009) and subsequent amendments. It requires operators to comply with anti-money laundering (AML) and know-your-customer (KYC) rules, as well as technical standards for gaming platforms. The ONJN is the sole regulator, responsible for issuing licenses, monitoring compliance, and enforcing penalties. As of 2026, Romania has updated its technical standards to include specific requirements for blockchain-based games, such as provably fair algorithms and smart contract audits. This makes the license particularly suitable for operators using decentralized platforms.

Romania's approach contrasts with many EU countries that have not yet regulated crypto gambling explicitly. By obtaining this license, operators gain a first-mover advantage in a market that is both regulated and growing. The license also provides a pathway to the broader European market, as Romania is an EU member state.

Who Needs a Crypto Gambling License Romania?

Any entity that offers gambling services to Romanian residents using cryptocurrencies must obtain a license. This includes:

  • Online casinos accepting Bitcoin, Ethereum, or other digital assets
  • Sports betting platforms that settle bets in crypto
  • Poker rooms and other card games using crypto
  • Lottery and bingo operators with crypto payment options
  • Fantasy sports platforms that use crypto for entry fees and payouts

If you are targeting the Romanian market or operating from Romania, you need this license. Even if your company is based abroad, offering services to Romanian players triggers the licensing requirement. The ONJN actively monitors cross-border activities and has agreements with other EU regulators to share data. For example, if a Malta-licensed operator accepts Romanian players without a Romanian license, they risk enforcement actions including fines and blacklisting.

Startups and established operators alike must comply. The license is also required for B2B providers of gambling software if they offer their platform to Romanian-licensed operators. Consulting24 can help determine whether your business model falls under the licensing requirement.

License Type & Regulator

The regulator is the National Gambling Office (Oficiul Național pentru Jocuri de Noroc - ONJN). The license type is a Class 1 or Class 2 license depending on the activities:

  • Class 1 license: For operators who own and operate the gambling platform directly. This is the most common license for crypto casinos and betting sites.
  • Class 2 license: For providers of gambling software or platforms to Class 1 licensees. This includes game developers, platform providers, and aggregators.

Most crypto gambling operators need a Class 1 license. The license is valid for 10 years and renewable. The ONJN also issues a Class 3 license for land-based gambling, but this is less relevant for crypto operators. The regulatory framework is detailed in OG 77/2009, which has been amended multiple times to include digital assets. As of 2026, the ONJN requires all Class 1 licensees to implement a responsible gambling tool that allows players to set deposit limits in both fiat and crypto.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a crypto gambling license Romania is typically €50,000 for Class 1 licenses. This amount must be paid up and held in a Romanian bank account. The capital requirement may be higher for certain types of gambling (e.g., sports betting) or if you plan to offer multiple games. For example, if you offer both casino and sports betting, the ONJN may require €100,000. The capital serves as a guarantee for player funds and operational solvency. It must be maintained throughout the license period; if it drops below the minimum, you must top it up within 30 days.

The capital can be in the form of cash or bank guarantees. It must be deposited in a segregated account that is not used for daily operations. The ONJN may request proof of capital at any time. Consulting24 advises clients on structuring their capital to meet these requirements efficiently.

Tax Treatment

Gambling operators in Romania are subject to a turnover tax rather than profit tax. The tax rate is 16% of gross gaming revenue (GGR) for online gambling. There is no VAT on gambling services. Additionally, operators must pay a 2% tax on player winnings (withheld at source). Corporate income tax applies at 16% on profits, but the gambling tax is deductible. For crypto gambling, the tax treatment is the same as for fiat gambling. However, the ONJN requires that all transactions be recorded in fiat for tax purposes, meaning crypto amounts must be converted to EUR or RON at the time of the transaction.

Operators must submit monthly tax returns and annual audited financial statements. The tax authority (ANAF) may audit crypto transactions to ensure proper conversion rates are used. Consulting24 can recommend local accountants experienced in crypto gambling taxation.

Allowed Activities

A crypto gambling license Romania allows you to offer:

  • Casino games (slots, table games, live dealer)
  • Sports betting (pre-match and live)
  • Poker (cash games and tournaments)
  • Lottery and bingo
  • Fantasy sports
  • Virtual sports

You can accept cryptocurrencies as payment and settle bets in crypto. However, you must also offer at least one fiat currency option (EUR or RON). The platform must be hosted on servers located in Romania or in an EU/EEA country with a data protection agreement. The ONJN also requires that all games have a theoretical return-to-player (RTP) of at least 75% for slots and 80% for table games. For crypto-specific games like crash games, the RNG must be certified by an approved lab.

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Company registration: Incorporate a Romanian company (SRL) with a minimum share capital of €50,000. The company must have a registered office in Romania.
  2. Bank account: Open a corporate bank account in Romania to hold the capital. This can be challenging; Consulting24 can introduce you to banks that accept gambling companies.
  3. Technical compliance: Ensure your gaming platform meets ONJN technical standards. This includes RNG certification by an approved lab (GLI, BMM, or iTech Labs), responsible gambling tools, and data security measures.
  4. Documentation: Prepare AML/KYC policies, responsible gambling policy, business plan, financial projections, and a description of your platform's technical architecture.
  5. Application submission: Submit the application to ONJN with all supporting documents and the application fee of €15,000.
  6. Review and inspection: ONJN reviews the application and may conduct an on-site inspection of your platform. This can take 2-3 months.
  7. License issuance: If approved, you receive the license valid for 10 years. You must then pay the annual fee and begin operations.

Operators must submit regular reports on financials, game fairness, and responsible gambling measures. Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. The ONJN conducts periodic audits, both announced and unannounced. As of 2026, the ONJN has increased its enforcement activities, issuing fines of up to €50,000 for non-compliance with AML rules.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Underestimating capital: The €50,000 minimum must be paid up and maintained; failing to do so can delay the license or lead to revocation.
  • Ignoring technical standards: Your platform must be certified by an approved testing lab (e.g., GLI, BMM). Using uncertified RNG is a common reason for rejection.
  • Incomplete documentation: Missing AML policies or business plans are common reasons for rejection. Ensure all documents are translated into Romanian.
  • Not using a local representative: The ONJN requires a local contact person for correspondence. This can be a lawyer or a consulting firm.
  • Assuming crypto is exempt from tax: All crypto transactions must be reported in fiat for tax purposes. Failure to do so can result in penalties.
  • Neglecting responsible gambling: The ONJN requires a responsible gambling policy and tools. Operators without these may be fined.

Alternatives: Romania vs Other Jurisdictions

Romania is one of the few EU countries with a dedicated crypto gambling license. Alternatives include:

  • Panama: No specific crypto gambling license, but a general gambling license allows crypto. Lower cost (€6,000 flat) but no EU passporting. Panama is suitable for operators targeting Latin America or global markets without EU focus.
  • Malta: MGA license covers crypto gambling but is more expensive (€25,000+ application fee) and has stricter capital requirements (€100,000+). Malta also has a 5% tax on GGR for remote gaming, but additional compliance costs are higher.
  • Curacao: Popular for crypto gambling, but the regulatory framework is less stringent and not EU-recognized. Curacao licenses are cheaper (around €15,000 total) but may not be accepted by some payment processors.

Romania offers a balance of EU recognition, clear crypto rules, and moderate costs. For operators seeking a European base, Romania is often the best choice.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto gambling license Romania?

The minimum capital is €50,000 for a Class 1 license. This must be paid up and held in a Romanian bank account. Higher capital may be required for certain activities like sports betting or multiple game types.

Can I use the Romanian license to operate in other EU countries?

Yes, under EU freedom of services, a Romanian license allows you to offer services cross-border. However, you must comply with local laws in each country, which may require additional registration or notification.

What taxes apply to crypto gambling in Romania?

The main tax is 16% of gross gaming revenue (GGR). Additionally, a 2% tax on player winnings is withheld. Corporate income tax of 16% applies on profits, but the gambling tax is deductible.

Is it mandatory to have a physical office in Romania?

Yes, you need a registered office in Romania for your company. The ONJN also requires a local representative for correspondence. Consulting24 can help with office setup and local representation.

Can I accept both fiat and crypto payments?

Yes, you must offer at least one fiat currency option alongside crypto. All transactions must be recorded in fiat for tax purposes. Crypto payment processors can be integrated, but they must comply with Romanian regulations.

What happens if I operate without a license in Romania?

Operating without a license is illegal and can result in fines of up to €100,000, closure of your platform, and criminal charges. The ONJN actively monitors and enforces against unlicensed operators.

Do I need to certify my gaming platform?

Yes, your platform must be certified by an ONJN-approved testing lab (e.g., GLI, BMM, iTech Labs). Certification includes RNG testing, game fairness, and security audits.
