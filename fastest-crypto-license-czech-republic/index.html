What Is the Czech Republic Crypto License?

The Czech Republic crypto license is not a traditional license but a registration with the Czech National Bank (CNB) under the Act on Anti-Money Laundering (AML Act). Since 2023, crypto asset service providers must register with the CNB to operate legally. This registration covers activities such as crypto exchange, custody, and wallet services. The process is known for being one of the fastest in the EU, with a typical timeline of 2-3 months for approval.

Unlike some EU member states that require a full authorization under MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation), the Czech Republic has implemented a transitional regime that allows existing and new providers to register under the current AML framework until the MiCA national implementation is complete. This makes it an attractive option for companies that want to start operating quickly while preparing for future MiCA compliance. The registration is valid indefinitely as long as the company maintains compliance and pays annual supervisory fees.

For example, a crypto exchange startup can begin onboarding clients within three months of starting the process, whereas in some other EU countries, the same process might take six months or more. This speed is a key differentiator for founders who need to move fast.

Who Needs a Czech Republic Crypto License?

Any company that provides crypto asset services to Czech residents or operates from the Czech Republic must register with the CNB. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Brokers and trading platforms
  • Payment processors using crypto
  • ATMs operators
  • Token issuers (if the token qualifies as a crypto asset under Czech law)

License Type & Regulator

The Czech Republic does not issue a 'license' per se; instead, it requires a registration with the Czech National Bank (CNB) under the AML Act. The CNB is the competent authority for supervising crypto asset service providers. The registration is a mandatory requirement for legal operation, and it is not a full authorization like a banking license. It focuses on AML/CFT compliance, fit and proper requirements for management, and operational transparency.

As of 2026, the Czech Republic is in the process of implementing MiCA, but the current registration regime remains in effect. This means that new applicants can still benefit from the relatively fast and low-capital process. Consulting24 monitors regulatory changes closely and advises clients on the transition to MiCA when applicable. The CNB also requires that the company have a physical office in the Czech Republic and a local AML officer. This is a practical requirement that ensures the regulator can effectively supervise the entity.

Tax Treatment

The Czech Republic offers a favorable tax environment for crypto businesses. As of 2026, capital gains from crypto assets held for more than three years are tax-exempt for individuals. For corporate entities, crypto transactions are subject to corporate income tax at a flat rate of 19%. VAT is not applied to crypto-to-fiat exchanges, but other services may be subject to standard VAT. For example, if you charge fees for wallet custody, those fees may be VAT-exempt if they qualify as financial services.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain the Czech crypto registration involves several stages:

  1. Company incorporation: Set up a Czech company (typically a s.r.o.) with a registered office and a designated AML officer. You will need a local director or a representative.
  2. Document preparation: Draft AML policies, risk assessment, business plan, and organizational structure. These documents must be in Czech or officially translated.
  3. Application submission: Submit the registration application to the CNB via the official portal, including all required documents. The CNB will issue an acknowledgment of receipt.
  4. CNB review: The CNB reviews the application, may request additional information, and conducts a fit and proper test on management. This typically takes 4-6 weeks.
  5. Approval: Once satisfied, the CNB issues a registration certificate. The company is then added to the official list of registered crypto asset service providers. You can then open a bank account and start operations.

Alternatives & Comparison: Czech Republic vs. Panama and Other Jurisdictions

When considering a crypto license, founders often compare multiple jurisdictions. The Czech Republic offers speed and low capital, but it is not the only option.

Czech Republic vs. Panama

Panama offers a flat-fee license at EUR 6,000 with no capital requirement, but it is not an EU member. Panama's license is ideal for non-EU focused businesses or those seeking a low-cost, fast setup. However, Panama does not provide access to the European single market. The Czech Republic, on the other hand, allows you to passport services across the EU, which is crucial if you target European clients. Panama also has a different regulatory framework, with the license issued by the Superintendencia de Bancos de Panama (SBP) for certain activities, but for pure crypto services, the regime is less defined.

Czech Republic vs. Estonia

Estonia has a similar registration-based system but with a higher share capital requirement (EUR 12,000) and a longer timeline (3-6 months). Estonia also requires a local AML officer and a physical presence. The Czech Republic is generally faster and more cost-effective for startups.

Czech Republic vs. Lithuania

Lithuania requires a minimum capital of EUR 125,000 for exchange and custody services under MiCA, making it significantly more expensive. The timeline is also longer (4-6 months). The Czech Republic is better for early-stage companies with limited capital.

How long does it take to get a Czech Republic crypto license?

The typical timeline is 2-3 months from application to approval. This makes it one of the fastest EU jurisdictions. Factors like document completeness and CNB workload can affect the timeline. Consulting24's proactive management helps ensure the fastest possible process.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a Czech crypto registration?

There is no statutory minimum capital requirement under the current AML Act. However, you must demonstrate sufficient financial resources to cover operational costs and AML compliance. In practice, we recommend at least EUR 10,000 in starting capital.

Can I serve clients from other EU countries with a Czech crypto license?

Yes, as an EU member, the Czech registration allows you to provide services across the EU under the freedom of services, subject to the transitional MiCA regime. You may need to notify the CNB of your cross-border activities. Full MiCA implementation may require additional compliance.

What activities are covered under the Czech crypto registration?

The registration covers exchange (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto), custody, wallet services, brokerage, ATM operation, and transfer services. Activities involving security tokens or derivatives require separate licenses.

Do I need a physical office in the Czech Republic?

Yes, you must have a registered office in the Czech Republic. This can be a virtual office or a co-working space, but it must be a physical address where the CNB can send official correspondence. Consulting24 can help you arrange this.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

You must maintain AML/CFT policies, conduct regular staff training, file annual reports with the CNB, and appoint an AML officer. The CNB may conduct on-site inspections. Non-compliance can result in fines or revocation of registration.
