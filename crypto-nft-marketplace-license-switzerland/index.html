What Is a Crypto NFT Marketplace License in Switzerland?

A crypto NFT marketplace license in Switzerland refers to the regulatory authorization required to operate a platform where users can buy, sell, or trade non-fungible tokens (NFTs) using cryptocurrencies or fiat currency. Depending on the services offered, the license may fall under FINMA's supervision as a financial intermediary (for AML purposes) or as a securities firm / trading venue if the NFTs qualify as securities under Swiss law. FINMA has issued guidance on the treatment of NFTs: generally, NFTs representing unique digital art or collectibles are not considered securities, but fractionalized NFTs or those with investment characteristics may be. The license ensures compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) obligations, client asset segregation, and operational resilience.

For most NFT marketplaces that act as an intermediary between buyers and sellers and hold custody of crypto assets or fiat, a FinIA license as a securities firm or a VASP registration under AMLA is required. Consulting24 advises clients on the correct classification and assists with the application process. The Swiss regime is known for its principle-based approach, allowing flexibility for innovative business models while maintaining high standards of investor protection.

Who Needs This License?

Any entity that operates an NFT marketplace in or from Switzerland, or that provides services to Swiss residents, may need a license. Specifically, the following activities trigger licensing requirements:

  • Operating a platform that facilitates the exchange of NFTs for crypto or fiat currency (trading venue).
  • Providing custody services for private keys or crypto assets on behalf of users (custodian wallet provider).
  • Executing orders for the purchase or sale of NFTs (brokerage).
  • Accepting fiat or crypto payments from users and settling transactions (payment services).
  • Issuing NFTs that represent securities (e.g., tokenized shares or bonds) and offering them to the public.

If your marketplace only connects buyers and sellers without holding funds or assets (peer-to-peer with no intermediation), you may fall outside the licensing scope, but AML registration may still be required. Capital Requirement

Switzerland does not have a statutory minimum capital for VASP registration under AMLA. However, for a FinIA license as a securities firm, the minimum capital is CHF 100,000 (or equivalent in EUR/USD). This capital must be fully paid in and maintained at all times. For a bank license (if you take deposits), the minimum is much higher (CHF 10 million). Most NFT marketplaces will not need a bank license.

The capital requirement serves as a buffer for operational risks and client asset protection. In practice, FINMA expects the applicant to have sufficient financial resources to cover setup costs and at least 6-12 months of operating expenses. Tax Treatment

Switzerland offers a favorable tax environment for crypto businesses. Corporate income tax rates vary by canton, typically between 11.9% and 21% (effective). VAT is not applied on crypto-to-crypto trades, but fiat-to-crypto exchanges may be subject to VAT. For NFT marketplaces, the tax treatment depends on the nature of the NFT (e.g., art vs. security).

  • Income tax: Profits from trading fees, listing fees, and platform commissions are taxable as ordinary business income.
  • Capital gains: For private individuals, capital gains from NFT trading are generally tax-free. For businesses, they are taxable.
  • VAT: Commission fees for facilitating NFT sales are subject to VAT at the standard rate (8.1% as of 2026).
  • Withholding tax: Dividends paid to shareholders may be subject to 35% withholding tax, but reduced under double tax treaties.

Consulting24 recommends engaging a Swiss tax advisor for precise structuring. In Panama, the territorial tax system means no tax on foreign-sourced income, which can be advantageous for global NFT marketplaces.

Allowed Activities Under the License

A Swiss crypto NFT marketplace license permits the following activities, subject to the specific license scope:

  • Operating an electronic trading platform for NFTs (primary and secondary markets).
  • Providing custody of NFTs and associated private keys for clients.
  • Executing buy/sell orders on behalf of users (brokerage).
  • Exchanging cryptocurrencies for fiat or other crypto assets (if licensed as a VASP).
  • Issuing NFTs that represent securities (requires prospectus and securities firm license).
  • Providing staking or lending services for NFTs (if not prohibited by license conditions).

Activities that are not allowed without additional licensing include: taking deposits (banking), issuing e-money, and operating a derivatives exchange. Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, you must adhere to ongoing compliance requirements. These include:

  • AML/CFT: Implement and maintain AML policies, conduct customer due diligence (CDD), monitor transactions, and report suspicious activities to the Money Laundering Reporting Office (MROS).
  • Annual audit: For FinIA licensees, an annual audit by a licensed auditor is mandatory. VASP registrants may also need an audit depending on size.
  • Regulatory reporting: Submit periodic reports to FINMA on financial health, business volume, and material changes.
  • Client asset segregation: If you hold client funds or crypto, they must be segregated from your own assets.
  • Data protection: Comply with Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection (FADP).

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Applying for a Swiss crypto license can be complex. Avoid these common pitfalls:

  • Misclassifying your NFT: Assuming all NFTs are non-securities can lead to wrong license type. Get a legal assessment.
  • Insufficient AML documentation: FINMA requires detailed AML policies tailored to your business. Generic templates are rejected.
  • Underestimating capital needs: Even without a statutory minimum, you need enough capital to cover operational costs for at least 6 months.
  • Ignoring local presence requirements: You need a Swiss resident director or compliance officer. Outsourcing to a provider is common.
  • Not preparing for the timeline: The process can take 6 months or more. Plan accordingly.
  • Choosing the wrong jurisdiction: Switzerland may be overkill for a small marketplace. Consider Panama or Lithuania as alternatives.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a license to operate an NFT marketplace in Switzerland?

Yes, if you provide services such as custody of crypto assets, order execution, or exchange of NFTs for fiat/crypto. Even if you only connect buyers and sellers, you may need to register as a VASP under AMLA. Consulting24 can assess your specific activities.

What is the minimum capital for a Swiss NFT marketplace license?

For VASP registration, there is no statutory minimum capital. For a FinIA license as a securities firm, the minimum is CHF 100,000. In practice, FINMA expects sufficient capital to cover operational expenses for at least 6-12 months.

How long does it take to get a Swiss crypto license?

VASP registration typically takes 2-3 months after submission. A FinIA license can take 3-6 months. Pre-submission preparation adds 1-2 months. Total timeline from start to approval is usually 4-8 months.

Can I apply for a Swiss license if my company is based outside Switzerland?

Yes, but you need a Swiss registered entity (AG or GmbH) and a local director or compliance officer. Consulting24 can help with incorporation and finding local representatives.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations?

Ongoing obligations include AML/CFT compliance, annual audits (for FinIA licensees), regulatory reporting to FINMA, client asset segregation, and data protection compliance. Consulting24 offers ongoing support.

Is Switzerland cheaper than other jurisdictions for NFT licensing?

No, Switzerland is one of the more expensive options. Total costs can exceed CHF 50,000. In contrast, Panama offers a flat fee of EUR 6,000, and Lithuania under MiCA costs around EUR 15,000-30,000. Switzerland is chosen for its reputation and regulatory clarity.

Does FINMA consider NFTs as securities?

FINMA assesses NFTs on a case-by-case basis. Unique digital art or collectibles are generally not securities. However, fractionalized NFTs or those with investment characteristics may be classified as securities, triggering additional requirements.

Can I operate an NFT marketplace with just a VASP registration?

Yes, if you do not hold client assets or operate an order book. VASP registration covers exchange and custody services. If you provide trading platform services with order matching, a FinIA license may be required.

What taxes apply to NFT marketplace revenue in Switzerland?

Corporate income tax (11.9-21% depending on canton) applies to profits. Commission fees are subject to VAT at 8.1%. Capital gains from NFT trading are tax-free for individuals but taxable for businesses. Withholding tax on dividends is 35% (reduced under treaties).
