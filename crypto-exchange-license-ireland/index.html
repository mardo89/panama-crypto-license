What is the Crypto Exchange License in Ireland?

The crypto exchange license in Ireland refers to the authorization required to operate a virtual asset exchange platform. Under MiCA, which is fully applicable from 2026, crypto asset service providers (CASPs) must be registered with the Central Bank of Ireland. This includes exchanges, wallet providers, and custodian services. The regime replaces the previous VASP registration, introducing higher standards for capital, governance, and consumer protection.

Key points:

  • MiCA harmonizes rules across the EU, making an Irish license valid throughout the European Economic Area (EEA).
  • The Central Bank of Ireland is the competent authority for licensing and supervision.
  • Licensed firms must comply with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) obligations.
  • Firms must also adhere to operational resilience, cybersecurity, and client asset segregation requirements.

For example, a crypto exchange that offers both fiat-to-crypto trading and custody services must meet the higher capital tier of EUR 125,000 and implement strong custody procedures, including cold storage and multi-signature wallets.

Who Needs a Crypto Exchange License in Ireland?

Any business that provides crypto asset services in or from Ireland must obtain a license. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto).
  • Platforms offering custody or wallet services.
  • Brokers dealing in crypto assets.
  • Automated trading platforms (e.g., robo-advisors).
  • Market makers and liquidity providers.
  • Initial exchange offerings (IEOs) platforms.

If you plan to serve Irish or EU customers, an Irish license under MiCA is essential. Non-compliance can result in fines, reputational damage, and legal action. Even if your business is based outside Ireland, if you actively target Irish residents, you may need to register. For instance, a UK-based exchange that advertises to Irish users would fall under the CBI's jurisdiction.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Central Bank of Ireland (CBI). The license type is a CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) authorization under MiCA. This replaces the earlier VASP registration. The CBI oversees compliance with MiCA, AML/CTF rules, and consumer protection.

For smaller firms, a simplified registration may apply, but most exchanges require full authorization. The CBI is known for its rigorous assessment, so thorough preparation is key. The CBI also conducts regular on-site inspections and off-site monitoring. As of 2026, the CBI has published detailed guidance on its expectations for CASP applicants, including fit and proper tests for directors and beneficial owners.

Capital Requirements

Under MiCA, capital requirements are tiered based on the services offered:

  • EUR 50,000 for simple exchange services (no custody).
  • EUR 125,000 for exchange with custody or wallet services.
  • EUR 150,000 for full-service exchanges including trading, custody, and advisory.

Additional capital may be required for operational risks. These figures are minimums; the CBI may require higher capital based on risk assessment. For example, if your exchange handles high volumes or offers derivatives, the CBI might demand EUR 250,000 or more. Capital must be held in liquid assets and cannot be used for operational expenses.

Tax Treatment

Ireland offers a competitive corporate tax rate of 12.5% on trading profits. However, crypto transactions may be subject to capital gains tax (33%) or income tax depending on the activity. VAT treatment is also evolving. As of 2026, crypto-to-crypto exchanges are generally VAT-exempt, but fiat-to-crypto trades may incur VAT. Professional advice is essential.

For example, if your exchange earns fees from trading pairs, those fees are subject to corporate tax. If you hold crypto as an investment, any gains on disposal are subject to capital gains tax. Ireland also has a stamp duty on certain transfers. Consulting24 recommends engaging a tax advisor familiar with crypto to optimize your structure.

Allowed Activities Under the License

The Irish CASP license permits the following activities:

  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency.
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets.
  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients.
  • Operation of a trading platform.
  • Execution of orders for crypto assets.
  • Placing of crypto assets.
  • Receiving and transmitting orders for crypto assets.

Each activity requires specific compliance measures. For instance, custody services must implement strict segregation of client assets and regular audits. Trading platforms must have transparent order book and trade execution policies. The CBI may impose additional conditions on a case-by-case basis.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application: Develop a business plan, AML/CTF policies, and risk assessment. This includes drafting a compliance manual, appointing a money laundering reporting officer (MLRO), and preparing a capital adequacy plan.
  2. Submit application: File with the CBI including all required documents: business plan, AML/CTF policies, risk assessment, financial projections, organisational structure, and personal questionnaires for directors and beneficial owners.
  3. Review: The CBI assesses the application, which may take 3-6 months. They may request additional information or clarifications.
  4. Interview: Key personnel may be interviewed by the CBI to assess their fitness and propriety.
  5. Decision: Approval or rejection. If approved, you must maintain ongoing compliance. The CBI may impose conditions, such as limiting the volume of transactions or requiring external audits.

Banking and Payment Solutions

Opening a bank account for a crypto exchange in Ireland can be challenging due to risk aversion. However, several banks now offer services to licensed CASPs. Options include:

  • Traditional banks like AIB and Bank of Ireland (with strict due diligence). Typically require a minimum deposit of EUR 10,000 and a track record of compliance.
  • EMI (Electronic Money Institution) accounts, such as Revolut Business or N26, which may have lower thresholds but still require a license.
  • Payment processors specializing in crypto, like Coinbase Commerce or BitPay, which can handle fiat on-ramps.

We help you identify suitable banking partners. It is advisable to approach multiple institutions simultaneously, as the process can take 2-4 months. Some banks may also require a personal guarantee from directors.

Benefits of an Irish Crypto License

Key advantages:

  • Passporting rights across the EU under MiCA, allowing you to operate in all 27 member states without additional licenses.
  • Strong reputation and regulatory clarity, which can attract institutional investors and partners.
  • Access to a skilled workforce and tech ecosystem, particularly in Dublin's Silicon Docks.
  • Competitive tax regime, with a 12.5% corporate tax rate and a network of double tax treaties.

However, the process is rigorous and costly compared to some other jurisdictions. For example, Panama offers a simpler, cheaper setup (EUR 6,000 flat) with no specific crypto license, but it lacks EU passporting.

Compliance and Trust

Compliance is ongoing. You must:

  • Conduct regular AML/CTF audits, at least annually.
  • Report suspicious transactions to the CBI and the Financial Intelligence Unit.
  • Maintain capital adequacy at all times, with quarterly reporting.
  • Submit periodic reports to the CBI, including transaction volumes, client complaints, and financial statements.
  • Ensure all staff undergo AML training annually.

Failure to comply can lead to license revocation, fines of up to EUR 1 million or 10% of annual turnover, and personal liability for directors. This is general guidance, not legal advice.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common pitfalls include:

  • Underestimating the time and cost. Many applicants assume 6 months but the process often takes 9-12 months.
  • Incomplete AML/CTF policies. The CBI expects detailed, practical procedures, not generic templates.
  • Lack of experienced management. The CBI requires at least one director with crypto compliance experience.
  • Ignoring ongoing compliance. Some firms focus only on the application and neglect post-license obligations.
  • Choosing the wrong jurisdiction. Ireland is not suitable for every business; consider alternatives like Lithuania or Panama.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between VASP and CASP in Ireland?

VASP (Virtual Asset Service Provider) was the previous registration under AML laws. From 2026, MiCA introduces CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) authorization, which is a full license with higher capital and operational requirements. Existing VASPs must transition to CASP by 2027 or risk losing their authorization.

How long does it take to get a crypto exchange license in Ireland?

The process typically takes 6 to 12 months, depending on the complexity of your application and the CBI's workload. Pre-application preparation can take additional 2-4 months. Consulting24 recommends starting early and allowing for delays.

Can I passport an Irish crypto license to other EU countries?

Yes, under MiCA, a CASP license from Ireland allows you to provide services across the EEA without needing separate licenses in each country. You must notify the CBI of your intention to passport, and they will inform the host regulator.

What are the ongoing compliance costs?

Annual compliance costs include AML audits, legal fees, and reporting. Expect EUR 10,000 to 30,000 per year, depending on the size of your operation. Larger exchanges with high transaction volumes may incur higher costs due to more frequent audits.

Is there a minimum capital requirement for crypto exchanges in Ireland?

Yes, under MiCA, the minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for simple exchange services, EUR 125,000 if custody is involved, and EUR 150,000 for full-service exchanges. The CBI may require higher capital based on risk assessment.

Do I need to have a physical office in Ireland?

Yes, you must have a registered office and a physical presence in Ireland, including a local director or compliance officer. Virtual offices are not accepted. The CBI expects a substantive presence with staff and operational capabilities.

Consulting24 advises and coordinates the process but does not directly file. We work with local legal partners to ensure your application meets CBI standards. For direct filing, we recommend our services in Estonia, Lithuania, or Panama.

What happens if I operate without a license?

Operating without a license is illegal and can result in fines, criminal charges, and shutdown of your business. The CBI actively monitors for unlicensed activity and can impose penalties of up to EUR 1 million or 10% of annual turnover.

How does Ireland compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Panama offers a simpler, cheaper setup (EUR 6,000 flat) with no specific crypto license, but it lacks EU passporting. Ireland is more expensive and time-consuming but provides regulatory clarity and access to the EU market. Choose Panama if you target non-EU markets; choose Ireland for EU expansion.

What taxes apply to crypto exchanges in Ireland?

Corporate tax is 12.5% on trading profits. Capital gains tax (33%) may apply to certain transactions. VAT treatment varies; professional advice is recommended. Ireland also has a stamp duty on share transfers, which may apply to crypto securities.

Can I use an EMI instead of a traditional bank for my exchange?

Yes, many licensed exchanges use EMI accounts for fiat operations. EMIs like Revolut or N26 offer faster onboarding but may have lower transaction limits. However, the CBI expects you to have a relationship with a regulated credit institution for client fund segregation.

What is the role of the MLRO in an Irish exchange?

The Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO) is responsible for AML/CTF compliance, including suspicious transaction reporting and staff training. The MLRO must be a senior employee, resident in Ireland, and approved by the CBI as fit and proper.
