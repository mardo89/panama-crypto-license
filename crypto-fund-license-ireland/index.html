What Is a Crypto Fund License in Ireland?

An Irish crypto fund license is not a single permit but a regulatory approval for an Alternative Investment Fund (AIF) that invests in crypto assets. The Central Bank of Ireland authorises AIFs under the European Alternative Investment Fund Managers Directive (AIFMD). The two main structures are:

  • QIAIF (Qualifying Investor AIF): For professional and qualifying investors (min. EUR 100,000 initial investment). No retail access. Faster approval (approx. 4-6 months).
  • RIAIF (Retail Investor AIF): Open to retail investors. Stricter rules, higher capital requirements, longer timeline (6-12 months).

Both structures can hold crypto assets directly or through derivatives. The fund must appoint an AIFM (Alternative Investment Fund Manager) authorised by the CBI or another EU regulator. As of 2026, the CBI applies enhanced due diligence for crypto strategies, focusing on custody, valuation, and anti-money laundering (AML) controls.

This license is ideal for fund managers targeting EU institutional and professional investors who demand a regulated, MiFID-compliant framework. Consulting24 advises clients on choosing the right structure and coordinating with Irish legal counsel.

Capital Requirement

For Irish AIFs, the capital requirement depends on the structure:

  • QIAIF: No statutory minimum fund capital, but the AIFM must have initial capital of at least EUR 125,000 (if external) or EUR 50,000 (if internal and not holding client assets). Additionally, the AIFM must maintain professional indemnity insurance or own funds of 0.01% of assets under management (up to EUR 10 million).
  • RIAIF: The fund itself must have a minimum capital of EUR 300,000 (or 1% of NAV if higher). The AIFM capital requirements are similar to QIAIF.

In practice, the CBI expects a crypto fund to have sufficient capital to cover operational risks, especially cybersecurity and custody risks. Most fund promoters target at least EUR 500,000 in initial seed capital to demonstrate commitment. Consulting24 advises on capital structuring to meet CBI expectations.

Tax Treatment

Ireland offers a favourable tax regime for investment funds:

  • Corporate tax: Irish regulated funds are generally exempt from Irish corporation tax on income and gains, provided they are resident in Ireland and meet certain conditions (Section 110 of Taxes Consolidation Act 1997 for qualifying companies).
  • Withholding tax: No withholding tax on distributions to non-Irish resident investors.
  • Stamp duty: Exempt for fund shares/units.
  • VAT: Fund management services are exempt from Irish VAT.

However, crypto-specific transactions (e.g., staking rewards, DeFi lending) may have different tax implications. Fund managers should obtain Irish tax advice. Allowed Activities

An Irish crypto fund can engage in a wide range of activities, subject to its investment policy approved by the CBI:

  • Direct investment in cryptocurrencies (Bitcoin, Ethereum, etc.)
  • Investment in tokenised securities and security tokens
  • Participation in DeFi protocols (liquidity mining, staking) - with risk disclosure
  • Derivatives on crypto assets (futures, options, swaps)
  • Venture capital investments in crypto startups (equity or tokens)
  • Lending of crypto assets to generate yield

Restrictions: The fund cannot engage in proprietary trading outside its stated strategy, nor can it provide custody services to third parties. All activities must align with the fund's prospectus and risk management framework. The CBI prohibits retail funds (RIAIF) from investing in certain high-risk crypto strategies unless clearly disclosed.

Compliance & Trust

The CBI expects strong compliance in areas critical to crypto funds:

  • Custody of crypto assets: Must use a qualified custodian or a regulated third party with multi-signature controls. Self-custody is generally not permitted.
  • Valuation: Crypto assets must be valued at fair market value using reliable price sources (e.g., CoinMarketCap, exchange feeds). Policies must address volatility.
  • AML/KYC: Full investor due diligence, including source of funds checks. The fund must appoint a designated AML officer.
  • Risk management: Regular stress testing for liquidity, market, and operational risks.
  • Reporting: Annual audited financial statements, periodic investor reports, and CBI regulatory filings.

This is general guidance, not legal advice. Consulting24 coordinates with compliance specialists to build a framework that meets CBI standards. For a lighter compliance burden, consider Panama, where fund regulation is less prescriptive.

Common Mistakes

  1. Underestimating timeline: Expecting approval in 3 months - realistic QIAIF timeline is 4-6 months minimum.
  2. Insufficient capital: Starting with too little seed capital; CBI expects at least EUR 500k to show commitment.
  3. Poor custody arrangement: Not having a clear, regulated custody solution for crypto assets.
  4. Ignoring tax advice: Assuming all crypto income is tax-free - staking, lending may have different treatment.
  5. Choosing wrong structure: Selecting RIAIF when QIAIF would suffice, leading to higher costs and longer timeline.
  6. Lack of local presence: Not having a local director or AIFM - CBI prefers substance in Ireland.
  7. Overlooking AML: Weak AML procedures can delay or derail the application.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto fund in Ireland?

For a QIAIF, there is no statutory minimum fund capital, but the AIFM must have initial capital of EUR 125,000 (external) or EUR 50,000 (internal). In practice, the CBI expects at least EUR 500,000 in seed capital to demonstrate commitment. For RIAIF, the fund must have minimum EUR 300,000.

How long does it take to get a crypto fund license in Ireland?

A QIAIF typically takes 4-6 months from submission to approval. RIAIF takes 6-12 months. Pre-submission preparation adds 2-4 months. Consulting24 coordinates the process to avoid delays.

Can I market my Irish crypto fund to EU investors?

Yes, an Irish AIF can be passported across the EEA under AIFMD. This allows you to market to professional investors in all EU member states without additional authorisation.

What are the tax benefits of an Irish crypto fund?

Irish regulated funds are generally exempt from Irish corporation tax on income and gains. No withholding tax on distributions to non-resident investors. Fund management services are VAT-exempt.

Do I need a local director in Ireland?

Yes, the CBI expects the fund and/or AIFM to have a local presence, including at least one director resident in Ireland or the EEA. Consulting24 can help find suitable directors.

Can I use a third-party AIFM?

Yes, you can appoint an external AIFM authorised by the CBI or another EU regulator. This is common for smaller fund promoters. The AIFM takes on regulatory responsibility.

What custody options exist for crypto assets?

The CBI requires a qualified custodian or a regulated third party with multi-signature controls. Options include Coinbase Custody, BitGo, or Fidelity Digital Assets. Self-custody is generally not permitted.

How does Ireland compare to Panama for crypto funds?

Panama offers a faster (1-2 months) and cheaper (EUR 6,000) setup with territorial tax (0% on foreign income). Ireland provides EU passporting and regulatory credibility. Panama is better for non-EU investors; Ireland for EU institutional capital.

What is the difference between QIAIF and RIAIF?

QIAIF is for professional investors (min. EUR 100,000 investment) with faster approval and lighter regulation. RIAIF is open to retail investors but has stricter rules, higher capital, and longer timeline.

Can I invest in DeFi through an Irish fund?

Yes, but the CBI requires clear risk disclosure and strong risk management. Activities like liquidity mining and staking are allowed if outlined in the fund's prospectus and policies.
