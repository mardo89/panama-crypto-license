What Is a Crypto Staking License in Poland?

A crypto staking license in Poland is a regulatory approval that allows a company to offer staking services to customers. Staking involves locking up cryptocurrencies (e.g., proof-of-stake coins like Ethereum, Solana, or Cardano) to support network operations and earn rewards. Under MiCA, staking is considered a crypto-asset service, and Polish law requires VASPs to be registered with the KNF. The registration covers activities like custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients, which includes staking pools. The license ensures that the company complies with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) rules, as well as operational security standards.

Poland's approach is practical: it does not issue a separate 'staking license' but includes staking under the broader VASP registration. However, because staking involves client funds and potential yield generation, the KNF scrutinizes the business model, risk disclosures, and safeguarding measures. For example, if your platform offers variable staking rewards, you must clearly explain the risks of slashing or market volatility. Who Needs a Crypto Staking License in Poland?

Any entity that provides staking services to clients in Poland or from a Polish entity must register as a VASP. This includes:

  • Staking-as-a-service platforms that pool client tokens and stake them on their behalf, like Lido or Rocket Pool but with a centralized model.
  • Exchanges that offer staking rewards to users holding certain coins, such as Binance or Kraken staking products.
  • Wallet providers that facilitate staking through their custodial wallets, like Exodus or Trust Wallet with a Polish entity.
  • DeFi protocols with a Polish legal entity that manage staking pools, if they have custody of client assets.

If you are a non-EU company targeting Polish customers, you may need to establish a Polish subsidiary and obtain registration. The KNF also expects that the beneficial owners and management are fit and proper, meaning no criminal record in financial crimes and relevant experience. License Type and Regulator

The regulator for crypto activities in Poland is the Polish Financial Supervision Authority (KNF). The license type is a VASP registration (Virtual Asset Service Provider). Under MiCA, which is fully in force in 2026, staking services fall under the category of 'custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients' or 'execution of orders on behalf of clients', depending on the structure. The KNF maintains a public register of VASPs. The registration is mandatory and must be renewed periodically (typically annually with a fee of around EUR 500-1,000).

Poland does not have a separate staking license; staking is covered under the VASP regime. However, the KNF may require additional disclosures for staking products, such as risk warnings about slashing or market volatility. For example, if you offer a 'guaranteed return' on staking, the KNF may classify it as a security. Capital Requirement for Staking Services in Poland

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for VASPs offering staking services is EUR 125,000 (for custody and administration of client assets). This is a tier 2 capital requirement. If your staking service also involves execution of orders (e.g., automatically staking client funds), the requirement may be EUR 150,000. Poland has adopted these MiCA tiers as of 2026. The capital must be maintained as own funds (e.g., share capital or reserves) and cannot be borrowed. You must provide proof of capital at the time of application.

For comparison, Panama has no minimum capital requirement for its crypto license (fixed fee of EUR 6,000), while Lithuania requires EUR 125,000 for custody services (see our Lithuania crypto license page). Tax Treatment of Staking Rewards in Poland

In Poland, staking rewards are generally treated as income from capital gains (taxed at 19% flat rate) for individuals, or as corporate income tax (19% standard rate) for companies. The tax point is when the rewards are received (credited to the wallet). VAT is not applicable on staking rewards as they are considered a return on investment. However, fees charged to clients for staking services are subject to VAT at 23% (standard rate). For example, if your company charges a 10% fee on staking rewards, that fee is subject to VAT.

Poland has a favorable tax regime for crypto businesses compared to some EU countries. For example, Estonia taxes staking rewards as business income at 20% (corporate income tax) but only when distributed. Lithuania taxes staking rewards as capital gains at 15%. Poland's 19% rate is competitive, but you should also consider social security contributions if you are a director. Allowed Activities Under the Polish Crypto Staking License

The Polish VASP registration allows the following activities related to staking:

  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients (including staking pools).
  • Execution of staking orders (automated staking) where the VASP decides which validators to delegate to.
  • Provision of staking rewards distribution to clients.
  • Management of staking nodes (if operated by the VASP) as part of custody services.

It does NOT allow: operating a crypto exchange (requires a separate license under MiCA), issuing stablecoins (requires an e-money license), or providing lending services (unless separately licensed as a credit institution). The KNF may impose conditions, such as segregating client funds from operational funds and maintaining a minimum liquidity ratio. Step-by-Step Application Process for a Polish Staking License

  1. Company Setup: Incorporate a Polish limited liability company (Sp. z o.o.) with a registered office in Poland. Minimum share capital is EUR 1,000 (approx. 5,000 PLN).
  2. AML/CTF Compliance: Appoint an AML officer (can be an employee or external), draft internal policies (KYC, transaction monitoring, risk assessment, and reporting suspicious transactions).
  3. Document Preparation: Compile a detailed business plan (including staking model, target market, revenue projections), financial projections for 3 years, security measures (IT security, cold storage), and beneficial ownership details (all ultimate owners).
  4. Submit Application: File the VASP registration with the KNF via their online portal, including all supporting documents and the registration fee (approx. EUR 2,000).
  5. Review and Interview: The KNF reviews the application within 3 months. They may request additional information or conduct an interview with management (e.g., to explain the staking technology).
  6. Approval and Registration: Upon approval, your company is added to the KNF's VASP register. You can then start offering staking services. You must notify the KNF of any changes in management or ownership.

The KNF conducts periodic inspections, which may include on-site visits. Non-compliance can result in fines up to 5% of annual turnover or revocation of the license. Common Mistakes When Applying for a Polish Staking License

  • Incomplete AML Policies: The KNF expects detailed, operational AML procedures, not generic templates. You must tailor them to your staking model, including how you monitor for suspicious staking activity (e.g., large deposits from high-risk wallets).
  • Underestimating Capital: Ensure you have the required EUR 125,000 in own funds from day one. Some applicants try to use borrowed funds, which the KNF rejects. The capital must be fully paid up and available.
  • Poor Business Plan: The KNF reviews the viability of your staking model; include realistic financial projections (e.g., expected number of users, staking volume, fee income) and a clear risk management strategy.
  • Ignoring Fit and Proper Requirements: Management and beneficial owners must have clean criminal records and relevant experience. A CEO with a prior fraud conviction will be rejected.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a Polish VASP registration and a staking license?

Poland does not issue a separate staking license. Staking services are covered under the VASP registration for custody and administration of crypto assets. The KNF includes staking as a permitted activity under the VASP regime. You must register as a VASP before offering staking to clients.

Do I need a physical office in Poland to get a staking license?

Yes, you need a registered office in Poland. This can be a virtual office or a physical space. The KNF may inspect the office during the application process. Consulting24 can help you set up a compliant office address.

Can I stake client assets without a license if I use non-custodial wallets?

If you do not have custody of client assets (e.g., clients stake directly from their own wallets using your software), you may not need a VASP registration. However, if you provide any management or delegation services, the KNF may still require registration. Consulting24 can assess your specific model.

How long does the KNF take to approve a staking license?

The KNF has up to 3 months to review a complete application. In practice, approval takes 4-8 months from start to finish, depending on the complexity of your business model and the completeness of your documentation. Consulting24 helps streamline the process.

What are the ongoing compliance costs for a Polish VASP?

Ongoing costs include annual AML audits (EUR 2,000-5,000), renewal fees (EUR 500-1,000), and potential compliance officer salary (EUR 1,000-3,000 per month). You also need to maintain the capital requirement and file annual reports. Consulting24 offers compliance monitoring packages.

Is staking considered a security under Polish law?

Generally, staking rewards are not considered securities if they are purely a return from network validation. However, if you offer a guaranteed return or pool staking with profit-sharing, the KNF may classify it as a security. Consulting24 can help you structure your offering to avoid this.

Can I use the Polish license to serve clients in other EU countries?

Yes, as a MiCA-compliant VASP, you can passport your services to other EU member states without additional registration. You must notify the KNF of your intention to operate cross-border. This is a key advantage over non-EU licenses like Panama.

What happens if I do not meet the capital requirement?

The KNF will reject your application if you cannot prove the required capital of EUR 125,000. After approval, if your capital falls below the requirement, you must notify the KNF and may face sanctions. You can top up capital through retained earnings or additional share capital.

Are there any restrictions on which cryptocurrencies I can stake?

The KNF does not restrict specific cryptocurrencies, but you must ensure that the staked assets are not used for illegal activities. You should conduct due diligence on the blockchain and avoid privacy coins if they pose AML risks. Consulting24 can advise on acceptable assets.

How does Poland's tax treatment compare to Panama for staking?

Poland taxes staking rewards at 19% (corporate or capital gains), while Panama has no tax on offshore income but may have local taxes if you operate from Panama.
