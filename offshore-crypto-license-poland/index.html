What Is an Offshore Poland Crypto License?

An offshore Poland crypto license is a regulatory authorization that allows a company registered in Poland to provide crypto asset services to clients globally, often with a focus on non-Polish markets. Under the MiCA framework, this license is formally a CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) authorization granted by the Polish Financial Supervision Authority (KNF). It enables activities such as crypto exchange, custody, and wallet services.

Key characteristics of the offshore Poland route include:

  • Full EU passporting rights under MiCA, allowing service across all EU/EEA member states.
  • A relatively straightforward corporate setup in Poland, with a standard corporate tax rate of 19% (or 9% for small taxpayers).
  • No requirement for a physical office in Poland, though a registered address and local director may be needed.
  • Access to a skilled workforce and a growing crypto ecosystem in Warsaw and Krakow.

For founders seeking an EU-compliant base with lower operational costs than Germany or France, Poland presents a compelling option.

Who Needs an Offshore Poland Crypto License?

This license is ideal for crypto businesses that want to serve EU clients without establishing a physical presence in more expensive jurisdictions. Typical applicants include:

  • Crypto exchanges (spot and derivatives) targeting European retail and institutional investors.
  • Custodial wallet providers offering storage and management of digital assets.
  • OTC trading desks and liquidity providers serving EU counterparties.
  • Token issuers and STO platforms that require a regulated entity for compliance.
  • DeFi projects transitioning to a regulated model to attract institutional capital.

If your business model involves any of these activities and you want to operate legally within the EU, the offshore Poland crypto license is a strong candidate. License Type & Regulator

The offshore Poland crypto license is a CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) license under the EU's Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) regulation. The competent authority is the Polish Financial Supervision Authority (Komisja Nadzoru Finansowego, KNF). The KNF is responsible for granting, supervising, and revoking CASP licenses in Poland.

Key regulatory details:

  • Regime: MiCA (fully in force since 2026).
  • License class: CASP - covers all crypto asset services as defined in MiCA.
  • Passporting: Yes, full EU passport under MiCA.
  • Capital requirements: EUR 50,000 for simple custody/wallet services; EUR 125,000 for exchange services; EUR 150,000 for combined services including trading platform.
  • Reporting: Periodic AML/CFT reports, transaction monitoring, and annual audits.

Poland has a mature regulatory environment, and the KNF is known for its thorough but fair assessment process. Consulting24 advises clients on preparing the required documentation and liaising with KNF-approved auditors.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for a CASP in Poland is tiered based on the services provided:

  • EUR 50,000: For custodial wallet services and simple crypto transfer services.
  • EUR 125,000: For crypto exchange services (fiat-to-crypto or crypto-to-crypto).
  • EUR 150,000: For operating a trading platform or providing multiple services that include exchange and custody.

This capital must be maintained as a permanent endowment and cannot be used for operational expenses. It can be held in a bank account or as liquid assets. Poland does not require a separate security deposit; the capital is simply shown on the balance sheet. Tax Treatment

Poland offers a competitive tax environment for crypto businesses. Key points:

  • Corporate income tax (CIT): 19% standard rate, with a reduced 9% rate for small taxpayers (revenue under EUR 2 million).
  • VAT: Crypto-to-fiat exchanges are generally exempt from VAT. Crypto-to-crypto trades are also VAT-exempt under EU law.
  • Withholding tax: Dividends paid to EU parent companies are exempt under the Parent-Subsidiary Directive.
  • Transfer pricing: Transactions with related parties must be at arm's length and documented.
  • Tax treaties: Poland has an extensive network, minimizing double taxation.

Importantly, Poland does not impose a specific crypto tax regime; normal corporate tax rules apply. Capital gains from crypto trading are treated as business income. Consulting24 recommends working with a local tax advisor to optimize your structure.

Allowed Activities

An offshore Poland crypto license permits the following activities under MiCA:

  • Providing custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients.
  • Operating a crypto trading platform (order book or AMM).
  • Exchanging crypto assets for fiat currency or other crypto assets.
  • Executing orders for crypto assets on behalf of clients.
  • Placing crypto assets (underwriting).
  • Receiving and transmitting orders for crypto assets.
  • Providing advice on crypto assets.
  • Managing crypto assets on a discretionary basis.
  • Transferring crypto assets on behalf of clients.

Activities not covered include issuing asset-referenced tokens (ARTs) or e-money tokens, which require separate licenses under MiCA. The license does not cover derivatives or securities tokens that qualify as financial instruments under MiFID II. Consulting24 can help define your service scope to match the license class.

The process to obtain an offshore Poland crypto license involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step:

  2. Company incorporation: Register a Polish limited liability company (sp. z o.o.) with a registered address. This typically takes 1-2 weeks.
  3. Documentation preparation: Draft AML/CFT policies, business plan, risk assessment, internal procedures, and governance framework. This is the most time-intensive part.
  4. Capital deposit: Open a corporate bank account and deposit the required minimum capital (EUR 50k-150k).
  5. Application submission: Submit the complete application to the KNF, including all supporting documents and the application fee.
  6. KNF review: The KNF examines the application, may request clarifications, and conducts background checks on directors and shareholders.
  7. Approval & license issuance: If satisfied, the KNF grants the CASP license. You can then commence operations.
  8. Ongoing compliance: Submit periodic reports, maintain capital, and undergo annual audits.

  • EMI accounts: Electronic money institutions like Revolut Business or Payoneer often accept licensed crypto firms.

Compliance & Trust

Maintaining compliance is critical for the longevity of your license. Key ongoing obligations include:

  • AML/CFT program: Implement transaction monitoring, KYC, and suspicious activity reporting.
  • Audit: Annual financial audit by a KNF-approved auditor.
  • Reporting: Submit quarterly and annual reports to the KNF on transaction volumes, client assets, and capital adequacy.
  • Corporate governance: Maintain a board of directors with at least one Polish resident (if required).

Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. Consulting24 provides ongoing compliance advisory to help you stay on track. General guidance, not legal advice.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

When applying for an offshore Poland crypto license, avoid these pitfalls:

  • Insufficient documentation: The KNF requires detailed AML policies and business plans. Generic templates are often rejected.
  • Underestimating capital: You must maintain the minimum capital at all times; using it for expenses is a violation.
  • Ignoring tax residency: Ensure your company's management and control are in Poland to avoid tax issues.
  • Choosing the wrong service scope: Applying for a broader scope than needed increases capital and complexity.
  • Delaying bank account opening: Start the banking process early, as it can take weeks.
  • Not engaging local experts: The KNF expects local representation; working with a Polish legal advisor is essential.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for an offshore Poland crypto license?

The minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for simple custody services, EUR 125,000 for exchange services, and EUR 150,000 for operating a trading platform or combined services. This capital must be maintained throughout the license period.

The typical timeline is 3 to 6 months from company incorporation to license approval. The KNF aims to decide within 3 months of receiving a complete application.

Can I operate across the EU with a Poland crypto license?

Yes, under MiCA, a Polish CASP license allows passporting to all EU/EEA member states without additional licensing, subject to notification requirements.

The standard CIT rate is 19%. Small taxpayers (revenue under EUR 2 million) can benefit from a reduced 9% rate. Crypto-to-fiat exchanges are VAT-exempt.
