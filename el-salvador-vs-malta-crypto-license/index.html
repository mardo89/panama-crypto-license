What Is an El Salvador Crypto License and a Malta Crypto License?

An El Salvador crypto license refers to the authorisation granted under the Digital Assets Law (Ley de Activos Digitales) and the Bitcoin Law. It allows businesses to operate as a Digital Assets Service Provider (DASP) within El Salvador, covering activities such as exchange, custody, and token issuance. The regulator is the National Commission of Digital Assets (CNAD). Since 2023, the CNAD has issued over 50 licences, and the regime is designed to be agile, with a focus on fostering innovation in Bitcoin and digital assets.

A Malta crypto license, officially a Virtual Financial Assets (VFA) licence, is issued by the Malta Financial Services Authority (MFSA) under the VFAA. It permits the provision of VFA services, including exchange, wallet, and investment services. Since 2025, Malta has fully implemented MiCA, meaning VFA licence holders also comply with EU-wide standards for CASPs. The MFSA has a reputation for thorough oversight, with over 30 VFA licence holders as of 2026.

The core difference lies in regulatory maturity and market access: El Salvador offers a nimble, low-capital regime with a focus on Bitcoin adoption, while Malta provides a strong EU-compliant framework with higher capital thresholds but broader recognition across Europe. Who Needs an El Salvador or Malta Crypto License?

Businesses that should consider an El Salvador crypto license include startups and SMEs looking for a cost-effective, fast licensing process with minimal capital. It is ideal for companies focused on Bitcoin-related services, remittances, or serving the Latin American market. Also, firms that want to use El Salvador's tax incentives (no capital gains tax on Bitcoin) find it attractive. For example, a remittance platform targeting the US-El Salvador corridor could benefit from the low cost and Bitcoin-friendly environment.

A Malta crypto license suits established crypto businesses aiming for EU-wide passporting rights under MiCA. It is appropriate for exchanges, custodians, and asset managers that require regulatory credibility and access to European investors. Malta is also preferred by companies that need a well-defined legal framework for token offerings (VFA offerings). For instance, a DeFi protocol seeking to offer custody and exchange services to EU clients would find Malta's regime ideal.

License Type and Regulator

El Salvador

  • License type: Digital Assets Service Provider (DASP) authorisation
  • Regulator: National Commission of Digital Assets (Comisión Nacional de Activos Digitales, CNAD)
  • Legal basis: Digital Assets Law (2023), Bitcoin Law (2021)
  • Regulatory approach: Innovation-friendly, risk-based supervision, no sandbox requirement

Malta

  • License type: Virtual Financial Assets (VFA) licence (Class 1, 2, 3, or 4 depending on services)
  • Regulator: Malta Financial Services Authority (MFSA)
  • Legal basis: Virtual Financial Assets Act (VFAA, 2018) as amended, MiCA (2025)
  • Regulatory approach: Principle-based, with a mandatory sandbox phase (the VFA Test) and ongoing supervision

Both regulators require fit and proper assessments of directors and shareholders, but the MFSA's due diligence is more extensive and time-consuming, often requiring background checks and interviews. The CNAD process is more streamlined, with a focus on business viability and AML compliance.

Capital Requirement

El Salvador: The Digital Assets Law does not prescribe a fixed minimum capital for DASPs. However, in practice, the CNAD expects a minimum of USD 50,000 in paid-up capital, adjusted for the risk profile of the services offered. Higher capital may be required for custody or exchange services, sometimes up to USD 100,000. The capital must be maintained as liquid assets and can be used for operational expenses.

Malta: Under the VFAA and MiCA, capital requirements depend on the licence class. For Class 3 (dealing on own account, underwriting) and Class 4 (operating a VFA exchange), the minimum is EUR 125,000 and EUR 150,000 respectively. Lower classes (Class 1, 2) have lower thresholds (EUR 50,000-75,000). Capital must be held in liquid assets and is subject to ongoing solvency requirements. For example, a Class 4 exchange must maintain capital equal to at least 25% of its fixed overheads.

Tax Treatment

El Salvador: No capital gains tax on Bitcoin or other digital assets. Corporate income tax is 30% on net profits, but there are no withholding taxes on dividends paid to non-residents. VAT is 13% but digital asset transactions may be exempt. The country offers a 15-year tax holiday for tech companies under the Innovation and Technology Manufacturing Incentives Law, which can reduce corporate tax to 0% for qualifying firms. This makes El Salvador highly attractive for crypto startups.

Malta: Corporate tax rate is 35%, but effective rates can be as low as 5% through the full imputation system and tax refunds (e.g., 6/7 refund on trading income). No VAT on VFA services under the VFAA. Capital gains on VFAs may be exempt under certain conditions. Malta has an extensive double tax treaty network with over 70 countries, which can reduce withholding taxes on cross-border payments.

Both jurisdictions allow a broad range of activities, but Malta's classification is more granular and aligned with MiFID II categories. El Salvador's regime is more flexible, allowing new services with CNAD notification. For example, a DASP can add staking services without a full licence amendment, while a VFA licence holder may need a variation of licence.

Step-by-Step Application Process

El Salvador

  1. Pre-application: Engage Consulting24 to prepare corporate structure and documentation. This includes drafting a business plan, AML/KYC policies, and risk assessment.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Salvadoran company (Sociedad Anónima) with a minimum of one director and shareholder. The process takes 1-2 weeks.
  3. Submit application: File with CNAD including business plan, AML/KYC policies, financial statements, and background checks on directors. The CNAD charges an application fee of ~USD 2,000-5,000.
  4. Review and interview: CNAD reviews and may request additional information; a meeting with the board may be required. This phase takes 1-2 months.
  5. Approval: Licence granted within 2-4 months. Post-licence, ongoing reporting is required, including annual audits and transaction monitoring reports.

Malta

  1. Pre-application: Consulting24 assists with legal structuring and drafting of policies. A VFA Agent must be appointed (mandatory).
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Maltese company (usually a private limited liability company). This takes 1-2 weeks.
  3. VFA Agent: Appoint a licensed VFA Agent (mandatory) to guide the application. The agent conducts initial due diligence and liaises with MFSA.
  4. Submit application: File with MFSA including business plan, risk assessment, governance arrangements, and VFA Agent's report. Application fee is ~EUR 4,500-10,000.
  5. Assessment: MFSA conducts a four-phase assessment: pre-licence, sandbox (VFA Test), testing, and full licence. The sandbox phase typically lasts 3-6 months.
  6. Approval: Full licence typically granted in 6-12 months. Ongoing compliance with MiCA and MFSA rules, including regular reporting and audits.

Compliance and Trust Considerations

Both jurisdictions require strong AML/CFT compliance. El Salvador's CNAD mandates a risk-based approach, transaction monitoring, and annual audits. The CNAD has published guidelines on AML for DASPs, including requirements for customer due diligence and suspicious transaction reporting. Non-compliance can result in fines or licence revocation.

Malta's MFSA enforces strict AML rules under the Prevention of Money Laundering Act and EU directives, including the 5AMLD. Malta also requires a VFA Agent to oversee compliance. The MFSA conducts regular on-site inspections and requires annual AML audits. Trust is built through transparency and regulatory adherence. Common Mistakes When Choosing Between El Salvador and Malta

  • Ignoring capital requirements: Underestimating the capital needed for Malta can delay the application. For example, a Class 4 exchange needs EUR 150,000, not EUR 50,000.
  • Overlooking tax implications: El Salvador's 30% corporate tax can be offset by the tech holiday, but not all businesses qualify. Malta's effective 5% rate requires careful structuring.
  • Choosing based on cost alone: El Salvador is cheaper but lacks EU passporting. If your target market is Europe, Malta may be worth the higher cost.
  • Neglecting banking relationships: Both jurisdictions require local bank accounts. In El Salvador, banking is still developing; in Malta, due diligence is intense. Start early.
  • Failing to appoint a VFA Agent (Malta): This is mandatory and often overlooked. The agent must be licensed and experienced.
  • Assuming MiCA compliance is optional: Malta's regime is fully MiCA-aligned. Businesses must comply with EU standards, including capital tiers and reporting.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for an El Salvador crypto license?

The Digital Assets Law does not set a fixed minimum, but the CNAD expects at least USD 50,000 in paid-up capital. Higher amounts may be required for riskier services like custody or exchange. Consulting24 can advise on the appropriate level for your business.

What is the minimum capital for a Malta crypto license?

Under MiCA and the VFAA, capital depends on the licence class: Class 1 and 2 require EUR 50,000-75,000, Class 3 requires EUR 125,000, and Class 4 requires EUR 150,000. Capital must be held in liquid assets and is subject to ongoing solvency requirements.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in El Salvador?

The typical timeline is 2-4 months from application to approval. This is faster than Malta due to a less bureaucratic process. However, the timeline can vary based on the complexity of the application and CNAD workload.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Malta?

The full process takes 6-12 months, including a mandatory sandbox phase (VFA Test) that lasts 3-6 months. The MFSA conducts thorough due diligence, which extends the timeline compared to El Salvador.

Can I passport my Malta crypto license to other EU countries?

Yes, under MiCA, a Malta VFA licence allows passporting to all EU/EEA member states. This means you can provide services across the EU without additional licences, subject to notification requirements.

Does El Salvador offer tax incentives for crypto businesses?

Yes, El Salvador offers no capital gains tax on Bitcoin and a 15-year tax holiday for tech companies under the Innovation and Technology Manufacturing Incentives Law. Corporate income tax is 30% but can be reduced to 0% for qualifying firms.

What is the effective tax rate for a Malta crypto license?

The statutory corporate tax rate is 35%, but through the full imputation system and tax refunds, the effective rate can be as low as 5% on trading income. For example, a company can claim a 6/7 refund on tax paid on trading profits.

Do I need a VFA Agent in Malta?

Yes, it is mandatory to appoint a licensed VFA Agent to guide your application and ongoing compliance. The agent conducts initial due diligence and liaises with the MFSA. Consulting24 can recommend experienced agents.

Can I use El Salvador's Bitcoin legal tender status for my business?

Yes, El Salvador recognises Bitcoin as legal tender, and DASPs can offer Bitcoin payment processing and remittance services. This can be a unique selling point for businesses targeting the Salvadoran market or Bitcoin enthusiasts.

What are the common reasons for rejection of a crypto license application?

Common reasons include inadequate AML policies, insufficient capital, poor fit and proper assessments of directors, and incomplete documentation. Consulting24 helps prepare a strong application to minimise rejection risk.
