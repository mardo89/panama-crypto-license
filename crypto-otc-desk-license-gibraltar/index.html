What Is a Crypto OTC Desk License in Gibraltar?

A crypto OTC desk license in Gibraltar is a regulatory authorization issued by the GFSC under the Distributed Ledger Technology (DLT) Provider framework. It allows a company to operate an OTC desk that facilitates the buying and selling of cryptocurrencies directly between counterparties, often for large volumes. Unlike exchange-based trading, OTC desks provide price discretion and reduced slippage.

The license is part of Gibraltar's DLT regulatory regime, which was among the first in the world to create a bespoke framework for blockchain businesses. The GFSC requires licensees to comply with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorist financing (CTF) obligations, as well as capital adequacy and operational resilience standards. As of 2026, the regime remains fully in force and is aligned with international standards.

License Type and Regulator

The relevant regulator is the Gibraltar Financial Services Commission (GFSC). The license type is a DLT Provider License, which covers a range of activities including operating an OTC desk. The GFSC does not issue separate licenses for OTC desks; instead, the DLT license is activity-based and must be tailored to your business model.

The DLT framework includes two tiers: a full license for firms that hold client assets or operate a trading venue, and a lighter registration for firms that do not. For OTC desks that hold client funds or execute trades on behalf of clients, a full DLT license is typically required. The GFSC assesses each application on a case-by-case basis, focusing on the firm's governance, capital adequacy, and AML controls.

Tax Treatment in Gibraltar

Gibraltar offers a highly favorable tax regime for crypto businesses. The standard corporate tax rate is 10% on profits, but there is no capital gains tax, no VAT on crypto transactions, and no withholding tax on dividends. For OTC desks, this means that trading profits are taxed at 10%, and capital gains from crypto holdings are tax-free.

Gibraltar also has a tax exemption for passive income from overseas sources, which can benefit firms with international clients. However, firms must demonstrate substance in Gibraltar, including a physical office, local directors, and compliance staff. Allowed Activities Under a Gibraltar DLT License

A Gibraltar DLT license for an OTC desk permits the following activities:

  • Facilitating the purchase and sale of cryptocurrencies directly between counterparties.
  • Acting as a principal in crypto trades (buying and selling from own inventory).
  • Providing price quotes and executing trades for institutional clients.
  • Safekeeping and administration of client crypto assets (if licensed for custody).
  • Operating a multi-currency wallet for settlement purposes.

Activities that require additional authorization include operating a public exchange, offering derivatives, or providing lending services. Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a Gibraltar crypto OTC desk license involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application consultation with GFSC to discuss your business model and licensing requirements.
  2. Company incorporation in Gibraltar with a registered office and local directors.
  3. Preparation of application documents: business plan, compliance manual, AML policies, financial projections, and capital proof.
  4. Submission of application to GFSC along with the fee.
  5. GFSC review including background checks on directors and shareholders.
  6. On-site inspection (if required) to verify substance and controls.
  7. License issuance subject to conditions, followed by ongoing supervision.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Gibraltar crypto OTC desk license?

The GFSC requires minimum capital typically between EUR 100,000 and EUR 200,000, depending on the volume and nature of OTC activities. This capital must be held in liquid assets. The exact amount is determined during the application process.

How long does it take to get a Gibraltar crypto license?

The process usually takes 4 to 8 months from application to issuance. This timeline depends on the completeness of your application and the GFSC's workload. Consulting24 helps prepare a thorough application to minimize delays.

Can I operate an OTC desk without a license in Gibraltar?

No. Any firm providing DLT services in or from Gibraltar must be licensed by the GFSC. Operating without a license can result in penalties and legal action. Exemptions are limited and typically apply to non-commercial activities.

What are the tax benefits of a Gibraltar crypto license?

Gibraltar offers a 10% corporate tax rate on profits, no capital gains tax, no VAT on crypto transactions, and no withholding tax on dividends. This makes it attractive for OTC desks, though substance requirements must be met.

Do I need a physical office in Gibraltar?

Yes. The GFSC requires licensees to have a physical presence in Gibraltar, including a registered office and local directors. This is part of the substance requirements to demonstrate genuine operations.

Can Consulting24 guarantee approval of my Gibraltar license?

No. No firm can guarantee regulatory approval. Consulting24 advises and coordinates the application process to maximize your chances, but the final decision rests with the GFSC. We provide general guidance, not legal advice.

What is the difference between a Gibraltar DLT license and a Panama crypto license?

Gibraltar requires higher capital (EUR 100k-200k) and has a 10% corporate tax, while Panama has no capital requirement and zero tax on offshore income. Panama's license is a flat EUR 6,000 and faster (2-4 months). Gibraltar offers regulatory prestige but at a higher cost.

Can I use a Gibraltar license to serve EU clients?

Gibraltar is not in the EU, so it does not benefit from MiCA passporting. However, it has a strong reputation and may be accepted by some EU counterparties. For EU market access, consider a Lithuanian or Estonian license under MiCA.

What ongoing costs are there for a Gibraltar crypto license?

Annual costs include GFSC supervision fees (EUR 5,000-15,000), compliance officer salaries, audit fees, and office rent. Total ongoing costs typically range from EUR 20,000 to 50,000 per year.

Is Gibraltar a good jurisdiction for a crypto OTC desk startup?

Gibraltar is suitable for well-funded startups with a clear business plan and strong compliance framework. However, the high capital requirement and long timeline may be challenging for early-stage firms. Panama or Lithuania may be more accessible alternatives.
