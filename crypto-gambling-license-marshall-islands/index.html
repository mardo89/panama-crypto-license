What is a Crypto Gambling License in the Marshall Islands?

A crypto gambling license from the Marshall Islands is a regulatory permit that authorises a company to operate online gambling services using cryptocurrencies. The license is issued under the RMI Business Licensing Act and is administered by the Business Registrar. It covers a wide range of gambling activities, including casino games, sports betting, poker, and lottery, all conducted with digital assets.

The Marshall Islands does not have a separate crypto-specific regulator; instead, the Business Registrar handles licensing for all business types, including crypto gambling. This centralised approach simplifies the application process. Unlike some jurisdictions that require a physical office or local directors, the Marshall Islands allows remote operation, making it attractive for international operators.

Key features of the license include:

  • No minimum capital requirement (though a reasonable capitalisation is expected)
  • Zero corporate tax on foreign-sourced income
  • Fast processing: typically 4-6 weeks
  • No mandatory local bank account or office
  • Full anonymity of beneficial owners is possible

This license is ideal for startups and established operators who want a quick, low-cost entry into the regulated crypto gambling market.

License Type and Regulator

The Marshall Islands issues a single type of gambling license under the Business Licensing Act. There is no separate classification for crypto gambling; the license covers all forms of online gambling, including those using cryptocurrencies. The regulator is the RMI Business Registrar, which is part of the Ministry of Finance.

The Business Registrar is responsible for reviewing applications, issuing licenses, and ensuring ongoing compliance. Unlike some jurisdictions that have dedicated gambling commissions, the Marshall Islands keeps the process within the general business licensing framework. This reduces bureaucracy and speeds up approval times.

Key regulatory features:

  • The license is valid for one year and must be renewed annually.
  • There is no requirement for a local physical presence or resident director.
  • The regulator does not conduct technical audits of gaming software, but operators must ensure their systems are fair and secure.
  • Anti-money laundering (AML) policies are required but are less stringent than in the EU.

For comparison, Estonia and Lithuania have more rigorous AML and capital requirements, while Panama offers a similar low-regulation environment but with a longer timeline.

Capital Requirement

The Marshall Islands does not impose a statutory minimum capital requirement for a crypto gambling license. However, the Business Registrar expects the company to have sufficient capital to operate responsibly. In practice, a reasonable capitalisation of at least $10,000 to $50,000 is recommended to demonstrate financial stability.

This is a significant advantage over EU jurisdictions, which require minimum capital of €50,000 to €150,000 under MiCA. For example, Lithuania requires €125,000 for certain services, while Estonia has a €250,000 requirement for crypto exchanges.

The lack of a fixed capital requirement allows startups to enter the market with less upfront investment. However, operators must still maintain adequate funds to cover player liabilities and operational costs.

Tax Treatment

The Marshall Islands offers a tax-neutral environment for offshore companies. There is no corporate income tax, no capital gains tax, no withholding tax, and no value-added tax (VAT) on income derived from outside the Marshall Islands. This makes it an extremely attractive jurisdiction for crypto gambling operators.

Key tax benefits:

  • 0% corporate tax on foreign-sourced income
  • No tax on gambling revenues
  • No stamp duty or annual filing fees (beyond the license renewal)
  • No exchange control restrictions

Operators are responsible for tax compliance in the countries where their customers are located. The Marshall Islands does not have a double taxation treaty network, so you cannot rely on treaty benefits to reduce withholding taxes. However, the absence of local taxes is a major cost saving.

For comparison, Panama also offers territorial taxation (no tax on foreign income), while EU jurisdictions like Malta impose corporate tax (though refundable) and VAT.

Allowed Activities

The Marshall Islands crypto gambling license permits a wide range of activities. The license covers all forms of online gambling, including but not limited to:

  • Casino games (slots, table games, live dealer)
  • Sports betting (pre-match and in-play)
  • Poker and card games
  • Lottery and bingo
  • Fantasy sports
  • eSports betting

There is no restriction on the type of cryptocurrency that can be accepted. Operators can accept Bitcoin, Ethereum, Litecoin, stablecoins, and other digital assets. The license does not require a specific fiat currency conversion, allowing operators to run a fully crypto-based business.

However, the license does not permit physical gambling establishments or activities that are illegal under Marshall Islands law, such as unlicensed lotteries. Operators must also comply with international AML standards, though the requirements are less onerous than in the EU.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining a crypto gambling license in the Marshall Islands involves several steps. Consulting24 advises and coordinates the entire process to ensure a smooth application.

  1. Company Incorporation: First, you need to incorporate a company in the Marshall Islands. This can be done remotely. The company must have a registered agent and a physical address in the Marshall Islands (provided by the agent).
  2. Document Preparation: Prepare the required documents, including a business plan, AML policy, personal information of directors and shareholders, and a description of the gambling platform.
  3. Submit Application: File the application with the RMI Business Registrar along with the government fee. The application is reviewed for completeness and compliance with the Business Licensing Act.
  4. Background Check: The Registrar may conduct a basic background check on the directors and shareholders. Criminal records or financial irregularities can delay or deny the application.
  5. License Issuance: Once approved, the license is issued. It is valid for one year and must be renewed annually. The entire process typically takes 4-6 weeks.
  6. Post-License Compliance: Maintain records of transactions, implement AML procedures, and file annual returns. The regulator may request information on request.

One of the challenges for crypto gambling operators is finding banking partners. The Marshall Islands does not require a local bank account, and many operators use international payment processors or crypto-friendly banks.

Options for payment processing:

Compliance and Trust Considerations

While the Marshall Islands license is relatively easy to obtain, operators must still maintain compliance to avoid revocation. Key compliance requirements include:

  • Implementing an AML policy that meets international standards (FATF recommendations).
  • Keeping records of customer identification, transactions, and gambling activities for at least five years.
  • Reporting suspicious transactions to the regulator.
  • Ensuring the gambling platform is fair and secure (though no technical audit is mandated).

Trust is crucial in the gambling industry. Players may be wary of licenses from small jurisdictions. To build trust, operators can:

  • Display the license number on their website.
  • Use third-party auditors for game fairness (e.g., iTech Labs, GLI).
  • Offer transparent terms and conditions.
  • Provide responsive customer support.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Applying for a Marshall Islands crypto gambling license is straightforward, but mistakes can delay the process or lead to rejection. Avoid these common pitfalls:

  • Incomplete documentation: Ensure all required documents are submitted, including a clear business plan and AML policy.
  • Poor AML compliance: The regulator expects a basic AML framework. Do not submit a generic policy; tailor it to your business.
  • Ignoring beneficial ownership: Even though ownership is not public, the regulator requires disclosure. Attempting to hide ownership can result in denial.
  • Using unlicensed software: Ensure your gaming platform is legally sourced and does not infringe on intellectual property.
  • Targeting prohibited markets: Some countries explicitly prohibit unlicensed gambling. Check the legality in your target markets.
