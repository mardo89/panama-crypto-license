What Is the Lithuania Crypto License?

Lithuania's crypto license, officially a CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) authorization under MiCA, allows companies to offer exchange, custody, and other virtual asset services legally within the EU. The Bank of Lithuania is the primary regulator, enforcing strict AML/CFT rules and capital adequacy requirements. Since MiCA's full application in 2025, the license is a passportable EU authorization, replacing the earlier registration regime.

Key features include a minimum capital of EUR 125,000 for most services, a 6-9 month application process, and a corporate income tax rate of 15%. The license permits activities such as fiat-to-crypto exchange, crypto-to-crypto exchange, custody, and transfer services. Compared to other EU jurisdictions, Lithuania offers a balanced cost and regulatory environment, though the Panama alternative at a flat EUR 6,000 is significantly cheaper for non-EU focused operations.

Under MiCA, the license is valid across all EU member states without additional registration, making it attractive for businesses targeting the European single market. However, the application process is rigorous, requiring detailed business plans, AML policies, and proof of capital. The Bank of Lithuania has a reputation for thorough due diligence, often requesting supplementary information. License Type & Regulator

The license is a Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) authorization under the Law on Markets in Crypto Assets (MiCA). The regulator is the Bank of Lithuania, which oversees compliance, AML, and prudential requirements.

There is no separate 'exchange license' or 'custody license'; the CASP covers multiple service classes. Each class requires a specific minimum capital tier: EUR 50,000 for certain limited services, EUR 125,000 for exchange and custody, and EUR 150,000 for more complex activities. The Bank of Lithuania has a reputation for thorough due diligence, so applicants must demonstrate strong internal controls and a fit-and-proper management team.

The regulator also expects applicants to have a local presence, including a registered office and a local AML officer. The Bank of Lithuania may conduct on-site inspections before granting the license, particularly for higher-risk activities. Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital for a Lithuania CASP is EUR 125,000 for most service classes (exchange, custody). This capital must be fully paid up and held in a regulated Lithuanian bank or credit institution. It serves as a prudential buffer and is not a fee; it remains on the company's balance sheet.

For limited services (e.g., only advisory or transfer services), the minimum may be EUR 50,000. For complex activities including proprietary trading, EUR 150,000 applies. The capital must be maintained throughout the license period. Consulting24 advises on structuring capital efficiently, including the option of a company setup in Lithuania with a local bank account.

Applicants must demonstrate the source of funds for the capital, and the Bank of Lithuania may require proof that the funds are not derived from criminal activity. Allowed Activities Under the License

The Lithuania CASP license permits the following activities:

  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets
  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Transfer services for crypto assets
  • Execution of orders on behalf of clients
  • Placement of crypto assets (underwriting)
  • Receiving and transmitting orders

Activities not covered include issuing crypto assets (requires a separate prospectus) and operating a trading platform without a license. The Bank of Lithuania may impose additional conditions based on the service scope. For example, if you offer margin trading, the regulator may require higher capital or additional risk disclosures.

It is important to define your service classes precisely in the application, as adding services later requires a license amendment. Step-by-Step Application Process

Applying for a Lithuania crypto license involves several stages. Consulting24 coordinates the process for clients. Here is a typical timeline:

  1. Pre-application (1-2 months): Company incorporation, bank account opening, AML policy drafting, appointment of local AML officer.

The typical timeline is 6 to 9 months from application submission to license grant. This includes a 3-month review period by the Bank of Lithuania, plus additional time for document preparation and any requests for further information.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a Lithuania CASP license?

For most service classes (exchange and custody), the minimum capital is EUR 125,000. For limited services, it is EUR 50,000, and for complex activities, EUR 150,000. The capital must be held in a Lithuanian bank account.

Can I use the Lithuania license to serve clients across the EU?

Yes, under MiCA, a Lithuania CASP license is passportable across all EU member states. You can serve clients in any EU country without needing additional licenses, though you may need to notify local regulators.

Allowed activities include exchange of crypto for fiat or other crypto, custody, transfer services, order execution, and placement. Issuing crypto assets requires a separate prospectus. The license covers multiple service classes under MiCA.
