What Is a Crypto Staking License in Dubai?

A crypto staking license in Dubai is a regulatory authorization issued by VARA that allows a company to offer staking services to clients. Staking involves locking up cryptocurrencies to support blockchain network operations, earning rewards in return. Under Dubai's 2026 framework, staking is classified as a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) activity, requiring a license for any entity that provides staking on behalf of others.

The license ensures that the staking provider meets capital adequacy, custody, and risk disclosure standards. It is part of Dubai's comprehensive regime for virtual assets, which includes rules for anti-money laundering (AML) and consumer protection. Without this license, offering staking services in or from Dubai is illegal.

For founders, this license provides legitimacy and access to Dubai's growing crypto ecosystem, including banking and institutional partnerships. The license also signals to clients that your operation is compliant and trustworthy, which is essential for attracting retail and institutional investors.

VARA's rulebook for staking services specifies requirements for token due diligence, reward distribution transparency, and segregation of client assets. For example, you must clearly communicate the risks of slashing and lock-up periods. The license covers staking of proof-of-stake tokens like Ethereum, Solana, and Cardano, but VARA may approve other tokens on a case-by-case basis.

Who Needs a Crypto Staking License in Dubai?

Any business that provides staking services to clients, whether retail or institutional, must obtain a VARA license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges offering staking rewards to users
  • Wallet providers that stake tokens on behalf of customers
  • Asset managers operating staking pools
  • DeFi protocols that facilitate staking for a fee
  • Brokerage firms that include staking in their product suite

If your business model involves earning staking rewards for clients and distributing them, you fall under VARA's scope. Even if you are based outside Dubai, if you target Dubai residents or operate a physical presence in the emirate, you need this license. For example, a European exchange that offers staking to UAE users must either obtain a Dubai license or restrict access to UAE residents.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator for crypto staking in Dubai is the Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA), established under Dubai Law No. 4 of 2022. VARA is the sole regulator for virtual assets in Dubai (excluding the Dubai International Financial Centre, which has its own regime).

Staking is generally licensed under the VASP License category, specifically for "Staking Services". The license is activity-based, meaning you must specify staking as a service. VARA also licenses other activities like exchange, custody, and transfer. The license is valid for one year and must be renewed annually.

VARA's framework is aligned with international standards, including FATF recommendations. As of 2026, the regime is fully operational, with clear rulebooks for each activity. VARA also publishes enforcement actions, so compliance is strictly monitored. Consulting24 stays current with VARA updates to guide applicants through the process.

It is important to note that VARA requires a physical presence in Dubai mainland (not free zone) for staking licenses. This means you must set up a mainland company with a trade license that includes virtual asset activities. Capital Requirement

VARA requires a minimum paid-up capital for staking services. As of 2026, the requirement is typically AED 1,000,000 (approximately EUR 250,000). This capital must be maintained in a bank account in Dubai and cannot be used for operational expenses. It serves as a buffer for client protection.

The capital requirement may be higher if you also offer other VASP activities. For example, combining staking with custody or exchange may trigger a higher threshold. Consulting24 advises clients on structuring their application to meet capital requirements efficiently.

It is important to note that the capital must be in fiat (AED) and held in a Dubai-based bank account. Crypto assets cannot be used to satisfy the capital requirement. The funds must be available from day one of the application, as VARA may request proof of capital during the review process. Tax Treatment

Dubai offers a highly tax-friendly environment for crypto businesses. There is no corporate income tax on most business activities, including staking services, as of 2026. However, a 9% corporate tax applies to certain large businesses (profit over AED 375,000), but most crypto startups fall below this threshold.

Value Added Tax (VAT) at 5% applies to services provided to UAE residents. Staking rewards may be subject to VAT treatment depending on the structure. For example, if you charge a management fee for staking, that fee is subject to VAT. The staking rewards themselves may be considered a return on investment and not subject to VAT, but this depends on the specific arrangement. There is no capital gains tax on crypto holdings.

Allowed Activities

Under a Dubai crypto staking license, you are permitted to:

  • Accept client crypto assets for staking
  • Stake tokens on supported blockchains (e.g., Ethereum, Solana, Cardano)
  • Distribute staking rewards to clients
  • Charge fees (management, performance, or spread)
  • Offer staking pools or delegated staking
  • Provide staking-as-a-service to institutional clients

You must not commingle client funds with your own, and you must provide clear risk disclosures. Staking of unregistered securities or tokens not approved by VARA is prohibited. The license does not cover lending or margin trading unless separately licensed.

VARA also requires that you conduct due diligence on the tokens you stake. For example, you must assess the token's smart contract risk, slashing conditions, and lock-up periods. You must also have a process for handling hard forks or network upgrades that affect staked assets. Consulting24 can help you draft the required token due diligence policies.

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Pre-consultation: Discuss your business model with Consulting24 to determine suitability and identify any red flags.
  2. Company Setup: Incorporate a mainland Dubai company with a trade license covering virtual assets. This includes registering with the Department of Economic Development (DED) and obtaining initial approval.
  3. Documentation: Prepare AML/KYC policies, business plan, financial projections, risk management framework, and token due diligence reports. These must meet VARA's standards.
  4. VARA Application: Submit the application via VARA's portal with all required documents. Pay the application fee.
  5. Review & Audit: VARA reviews the application, may request clarifications, and conducts a compliance audit. This stage often takes 2-4 months.
  6. Approval: Once approved, pay the license fee and receive the VASP license. You can then commence staking operations.
  7. Post-licensing: Maintain compliance, submit periodic reports (e.g., transaction monitoring, audit reports), and renew annually.

Banking and Payments

Opening a corporate bank account in Dubai for a crypto business is challenging but possible. Banks like Mashreq, RAK Bank, and some international banks accept VARA-licensed entities. You will need to provide the license, proof of capital, and AML policies. Some banks may require a minimum deposit of AED 100,000 or more.

Payment processing for fiat on-ramps/off-ramps can be arranged through licensed exchanges or payment service providers. For example, you can partner with a local exchange that has a VARA license to handle fiat conversions. Alternatively, you can use a payment gateway that supports crypto-to-fiat settlement.

Compliance and Trust

Compliance is non-negotiable. VARA requires strong AML/CFT policies, regular audits, and transparent reporting. You must appoint a compliance officer and maintain records for at least five years. Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license.

Key compliance requirements include:

  • Customer due diligence (CDD) for all clients, including beneficial ownership checks
  • Transaction monitoring for suspicious activity
  • Regular independent audits of financial statements and AML controls
  • Reporting of suspicious transactions to the UAE Financial Intelligence Unit
  • Annual renewal of the VARA license with updated documentation

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Underestimating capital: Ensure you have the full AED 1,000,000 capital ready before applying. Delays in proving capital can stall the application.
  • Poor documentation: Incomplete AML policies or business plans cause delays. VARA expects detailed, professional documents.
  • Choosing the wrong activity: Staking is specific; don't apply for a generic VASP license without specifying staking. This can lead to rejection or additional requirements.
  • Ignoring ongoing compliance: The license requires annual renewal and continuous reporting. Many firms neglect post-licensing obligations and face penalties.
  • Operating without a license: Offering staking before approval is illegal and risks penalties, including fines and potential criminal charges.
  • Not engaging local experts: VARA applications often require local legal representation. Trying to do it alone can lead to mistakes.
