What Is the Crypto Stablecoin License Kazakhstan?

The crypto stablecoin license in Kazakhstan is a regulatory authorization issued by the Astana Financial Services Authority (AFSA) for entities operating within the Astana International Financial Centre (AIFC). It allows companies to issue, manage, and distribute stablecoins-digital assets pegged to a stable asset like fiat currency or commodities.

This license is part of the broader digital asset regulatory framework under the AIFC's FinTech Lab and permanent licensing regime. It covers activities such as token issuance, custody, exchange, and payment services involving stablecoins. The regime is designed to attract fintech and crypto innovators while ensuring compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and consumer protection standards.

As of 2026, Kazakhstan has positioned itself as a forward-thinking jurisdiction for stablecoin projects, with clear rules on reserve assets, redemption rights, and transparency. Consulting24 helps clients navigate the application, from entity setup to license approval.

For example, a fiat-backed stablecoin issuer must maintain a 1:1 reserve in a segregated account and undergo monthly attestations. The AIFC also requires that stablecoin terms are clearly disclosed to users, including the redemption process and any fees. This level of detail is similar to the requirements under MiCA in the EU, but with a lower capital threshold.

  • Digital asset exchanges listing stablecoins
  • Custodians holding stablecoin reserves
  • Payment service providers using stablecoins for remittances or merchant settlement
  • DeFi protocols that issue or manage stablecoins

Even if you only provide custody for stablecoins, you fall under the DASP regime.

License Type and Regulator

The primary regulator for crypto stablecoin activities in Kazakhstan is the Astana Financial Services Authority (AFSA), operating under the AIFC. AFSA issues a 'Digital Asset Service Provider' (DASP) license, which covers stablecoin issuance and related services. For activities involving the national currency (KZT) or significant monetary policy implications, the National Bank of Kazakhstan (NBK) may also have oversight.

There are two main license tiers:

TierActivities CoveredCapital Requirement
Category AIssuance of stablecoins, operating a trading platform, custodyEUR 50,000 (minimum)
Category BBrokerage, advisory, investment managementEUR 25,000 (minimum)

Category A is the relevant one for most stablecoin projects. The exact capital may be higher based on the scale of operations. Consulting24 advises on the appropriate tier and helps prepare the application.

AFSA also requires that key personnel (directors, compliance officers) meet fit and proper criteria. They must have relevant experience and no criminal record. The regulator conducts background checks and may interview applicants. This process is similar to other jurisdictions like the Estonia crypto license but with a more streamlined approach within the AIFC.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a Category A DASP license (stablecoin issuance) is EUR 50,000. This must be held in liquid assets and maintained throughout the license period. For Category B, the minimum is EUR 25,000.

In practice, AFSA may require higher capital if the stablecoin issuance volume is large or if the business involves significant risk. The capital must be deposited in a bank account in Kazakhstan or an AIFC-recognized institution. Consulting24 helps structure the capital requirement to meet regulatory expectations.

For example, if you plan to issue a stablecoin with a market cap of USD 10 million, AFSA might ask for EUR 200,000 in capital. This is a sliding scale, and we have seen cases where the regulator required up to 5% of the stablecoin's market cap. Tax Treatment

Kazakhstan offers a favorable tax regime within the AIFC. Corporate income tax is 0% for the first 10 years for companies registered in the AIFC. After that, a standard rate of 20% applies, but many activities may qualify for exemptions.

Value-added tax (VAT) is not applicable to most financial services within the AIFC. Stablecoin transactions may be treated as exempt from VAT. Personal income tax for employees is a flat 10%. Consulting24 recommends engaging a local tax advisor to confirm the treatment for your specific stablecoin operations.

It is worth noting that the zero-tax period starts from the date of incorporation, not license approval. So you can incorporate early and benefit from the tax holiday while preparing your application. Also, dividends paid to foreign shareholders are generally exempt from withholding tax under AIFC rules, which is a significant advantage for international founders.

Allowed Activities

Under the Kazakhstan DASP license, the following activities are permitted:

  • Issuance of stablecoins (fiat-backed, crypto-backed, commodity-backed)
  • Operation of a digital asset trading platform (including stablecoin pairs)
  • Custody and safekeeping of stablecoins and their reserves
  • Exchange services between stablecoins and fiat or other digital assets
  • Payment services using stablecoins (e.g., merchant acquisition, remittances)
  • Investment management of stablecoin-based funds

Activities not covered include derivatives trading without additional licensing and services related to algorithmic stablecoins that may be classified as securities. Consulting24 can review your business model for compliance.

For example, if you want to offer a stablecoin that pays interest to holders, that might be considered a security and require a separate license. Similarly, if your stablecoin uses an algorithm that adjusts supply based on demand, AFSA may treat it as an unregulated asset or require additional disclosures. Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a stablecoin license in Kazakhstan?

The minimum capital for a Category A DASP license (stablecoin issuance) is EUR 50,000. For Category B (brokerage, advisory) it is EUR 25,000. AFSA may require higher capital based on the scale of operations. The capital must be held in liquid assets and deposited in an AIFC-approved bank account.

How long does it take to get a Kazakhstan stablecoin license?

The typical timeline is 3 to 6 months from application to approval. This depends on the completeness of your documentation and AFSA's workload. Engaging a local partner like Consulting24 can help expedite the process by ensuring all requirements are met upfront.

What is the tax rate for stablecoin companies in Kazakhstan?

Companies registered in the AIFC enjoy 0% corporate income tax for the first 10 years. After that, the standard rate is 20%, but many activities may qualify for exemptions. VAT is not applicable to most financial services within the AIFC.

Can I issue an algorithmic stablecoin under this license?

Yes, but AFSA may treat algorithmic stablecoins differently depending on their structure. If the algorithm involves discretionary management or promises a return, it could be classified as a security. Consulting24 can help you assess whether your specific model is permissible.

Do I need to have a physical office in Kazakhstan?

Yes, the AIFC requires that licensed companies have a registered office within the AIFC. This can be a virtual office provided by a registered agent. You do not need a large physical presence, but you must have a local address for service of process.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

License holders must submit annual AML/CFT audits, regular reports on stablecoin reserves and transaction volumes, and maintain KYC procedures for all customers. They must also have cybersecurity measures in place. Consulting24 offers compliance monitoring support.

Can I use the Kazakhstan license to serve customers in the EU?

No, the Kazakhstan license is only valid for activities within the AIFC and with residents of Kazakhstan. To serve EU customers, you would need a license under MiCA in an EU member state, such as Lithuania. Consulting24 can advise on multi-jurisdictional strategies.

What happens if I don't maintain the minimum capital?

AFSA may suspend or revoke your license if the capital falls below the required minimum. You must maintain the capital throughout the license period. We recommend keeping a buffer to avoid any shortfall.

Is there a fast-track option for the license?

There is no official fast-track, but AFSA may prioritize applications that are complete and involve low-risk activities. Consulting24 can help prepare a comprehensive application to minimize delays.

Can I apply for the license remotely?

Yes, the entire application process can be conducted remotely. You do not need to travel to Kazakhstan. However, you will need to appoint a local director or compliance officer. Consulting24 can assist with finding suitable candidates.
