What Is the Seychelles Crypto License?

The Seychelles crypto license is a regulatory registration under the Virtual Asset Service Providers Act 2024, administered by the Financial Services Authority (FSA). It allows companies to legally offer virtual asset services, including exchange, wallet custody, and brokerage, from within Seychelles. The regime is designed to be business-friendly: low capital requirements, fast processing, and a tax-neutral environment for offshore income.

Key features include:

  • Regulator: Financial Services Authority (FSA)
  • License type: VASP registration
  • Timeline: 2-4 months
  • Corporate tax: 0% on foreign-source income
  • Capital requirement: Low (exact figure confirmed in consultation)

The Seychelles VASP regime is not a full 'license' in the traditional sense but a registration that authorises specific virtual asset activities. It is distinct from the EU MiCA framework, which imposes higher capital thresholds (EUR 50,000-150,000) and more extensive reporting obligations. Seychelles positions itself as a lighter-touch alternative, ideal for startups and mid-size operators who want regulatory credibility without the overhead of European compliance.

License Type & Regulator

The Seychelles crypto license is a VASP registration under the Virtual Asset Service Providers Act 2024. The regulator is the Financial Services Authority (FSA), which oversees all virtual asset activities. The FSA requires applicants to demonstrate fit-and-proper management, strong AML/CFT policies, and adequate operational controls.

License categories cover:

  • Exchange of virtual assets for fiat or other virtual assets
  • Transfer of virtual assets
  • Custody and administration of virtual assets
  • Participation in ICOs

There is no tiered capital structure like MiCA; the FSA sets a single, low capital requirement. For a detailed breakdown of capital and costs, see the next section.

The FSA also expects applicants to have a physical presence in Seychelles, including a registered office and a local company secretary. This is a common requirement for offshore financial centres and ensures the regulator can exercise effective oversight. The Seychelles VASP regime does not impose a high minimum capital. The exact amount is set by the FSA on a case-by-case basis, typically in the range of EUR 10,000 - 50,000 depending on the services offered. This is significantly lower than EU MiCA tiers (EUR 50,000 / 125,000 / 150,000) and makes Seychelles accessible for startups.

Tax Treatment

Seychelles offers a territorial tax system: 0% corporate tax on foreign-source income. Only income derived from within Seychelles is taxable at the standard rate of 15-30%. For most crypto businesses serving international clients, this means effectively zero tax. There is also no capital gains tax, no withholding tax on dividends, and no VAT on digital services.

This tax neutrality, combined with low licensing costs, makes Seychelles a top choice for crypto operators. However, founders must ensure they comply with tax obligations in their home country. For a comparison with Lithuania's tax regime, read our vs Lithuania guide.

It is important to note that Seychelles is not on the EU's list of non-cooperative jurisdictions for tax purposes, which adds to its credibility. The country has also signed tax information exchange agreements with many nations, facilitating transparency.

Allowed Activities Under the VASP License

The Seychelles VASP license permits a wide range of virtual asset activities, including:

  • Exchange between virtual assets and fiat currencies
  • Exchange between one or more forms of virtual assets
  • Transfer of virtual assets (on behalf of clients)
  • Custody and administration of virtual assets
  • Participation in and provision of financial services related to an issuer’s offer or sale of a virtual asset

Activities not explicitly covered (e.g., decentralized finance protocols) may require additional guidance from the FSA. Consulting24 helps define your business model to ensure it falls within the allowed scope.

The FSA may also impose conditions on specific activities, such as requiring additional capital for custody services. It is essential to clearly describe your intended activities in the application to avoid scope limitations.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a Seychelles crypto license typically takes 2-4 months and involves the following steps:

  1. Company incorporation - Register an International Business Company (IBC) or Seychelles company with a registered office and local secretary.
  2. Prepare documentation - Business plan, AML/CFT policies, risk assessment, fit-and-proper declarations for directors and shareholders.
  3. Submit VASP application - File with the FSA along with the application fee.
  4. FSA review - The regulator assesses compliance, background checks, and operational readiness.
  5. Approval & license issuance - Once satisfied, the FSA issues the VASP registration.
