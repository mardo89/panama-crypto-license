What Is the Indonesia Crypto Exchange License?

The Indonesia crypto exchange license, formally known as the Physical Crypto Asset Trader (PFAK) license, is a mandatory registration issued by Bappebti (the Commodity Futures Trading Regulatory Agency). It allows companies to operate as a crypto exchange, offering trading of digital assets such as Bitcoin, Ethereum, and other approved cryptocurrencies.

This license is part of Indonesia's broader regulatory framework under Law No. 10 of 2011 on Commodity Futures Trading and subsequent Bappebti regulations. The regime is distinct from securities-based licensing in other jurisdictions, treating crypto as a commodity. Key features include:

  • Legal recognition to operate a crypto exchange in Indonesia.
  • Compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) obligations under Indonesian law.
  • Ongoing reporting requirements to Bappebti, including transaction reports and financial statements.
  • Mandatory partnership with a clearing house (e.g., Indonesia Clearing House for Digital Assets, ICHDA).

This license is not a passport to other ASEAN markets; each country has its own regime. However, it positions your exchange to serve the world's fourth most populous nation, with over 20 million crypto investors as of 2026. Who Needs a Crypto Exchange License in Indonesia?

Any entity that facilitates the trading, buying, selling, or exchange of crypto assets for Indonesian customers must hold a PFAK license. This includes:

  • Centralized crypto exchanges (CEX) that operate order books and match trades.
  • Decentralized exchanges (DEX) that have a physical presence or target Indonesian users through marketing or local offices.
  • Brokerage platforms offering crypto trading services, including automated trading bots.
  • Over-the-counter (OTC) desks operating within Indonesia, even if they only facilitate large trades.

Foreign companies without a local entity cannot directly apply. You must incorporate an Indonesian company (PT) with at least one local director and a majority of local shareholders. This requirement is non-negotiable and often requires finding a trusted local partner. License Type and Regulator

The regulator is Bappebti (Badan Pengawas Perdagangan Berjangka Komoditi), under the Ministry of Trade. The license type is a Physical Crypto Asset Trader (PFAK) registration. As of 2026, this is the only recognized license for crypto exchanges in Indonesia.

Bappebti also oversees the Indonesia Commodity and Derivatives Exchange (ICDX) and the Indonesia Clearing House for Digital Assets (ICHDA), which serve as the clearing and settlement infrastructure for crypto trades. All licensed exchanges must become members of ICHDA to clear trades.

There is no separate license for crypto wallets or custody services; these are typically covered under the PFAK scope if offered by the exchange. However, pure wallet providers may need to register separately under other regulations, such as those from Bank Indonesia for payment systems.

Capital Requirement

As of 2026, Bappebti requires a minimum paid-up capital of IDR 1 billion (approximately USD 65,000) for a crypto exchange license. This capital must be deposited in a local bank account and cannot be withdrawn without regulatory approval. Some sources suggest higher thresholds for larger exchanges, but the official minimum remains IDR 1 billion.

This capital requirement is relatively low compared to EU jurisdictions under MiCA, where capital tiers range from EUR 50,000 to EUR 150,000 depending on services. For example, Lithuania requires EUR 125,000 for exchange services. Indonesia's capital requirement is competitive for startups targeting the Southeast Asian market.

Tax Treatment

Indonesia has a specific tax regime for crypto assets. As of 2026, crypto transactions are subject to:

  • Value Added Tax (VAT): 0.1% of the transaction value for crypto asset sales on exchanges.
  • Income Tax (PPh): 0.1% of the transaction value for the seller (final tax).
  • Corporate Income Tax: Standard rate of 22% on exchange profits (trading fees, spreads, etc.).

These rates are defined under Government Regulation No. 9 of 2021 and subsequent updates. Exchanges are responsible for withholding and remitting taxes on behalf of users. This creates an administrative burden but also ensures compliance.

Allowed Activities Under the License

The PFAK license permits the following activities:

  • Operating a crypto exchange for trading approved digital assets (list of approved coins published by Bappebti).
  • Providing custody services for customer crypto assets, including cold storage.
  • Offering fiat on-ramp and off-ramp services (IDR deposits/withdrawals) through partner banks.
  • Facilitating peer-to-peer trading between users.
  • Listing new crypto assets after obtaining Bappebti approval for each coin.

Activities not allowed include:

  • Offering derivatives or futures products (requires separate license from Bappebti).
  • Lending or staking services (not explicitly regulated, but may be considered securities under other laws).
  • Operating a decentralized exchange without a local entity and PFAK license.

Step-by-Step Process to Get Your License

  1. Company Incorporation: Register a PT (Perseroan Terbatas) with the Ministry of Law and Human Rights. You need at least one local director and two shareholders. The process takes 1-2 months.
  2. Open a Local Bank Account: Deposit the minimum capital (IDR 1 billion) and obtain a certificate of deposit. This step requires a notarized deed.
  3. Prepare Documentation: Draft AML/CTF policies, business plan, risk management framework, and IT security procedures. These must be in Indonesian.
  4. Apply to Bappebti: Submit the application with all required documents, including company deeds, capital proof, and compliance manuals. The application fee is paid at this stage.
  5. Clearing House Membership: Register with ICHDA (Indonesia Clearing House for Digital Assets) to clear trades. This involves an annual fee and compliance with their rules.
  6. Bappebti Review: The regulator reviews your application, may request additional information, and conducts an on-site inspection. This can take 3-6 months.
  7. License Issuance: Upon approval, you receive the PFAK license and can commence operations. You must then register with Bank Indonesia for payment system oversight.

Banking and Payment Integration

Integrating with Indonesian banks is a key challenge. Most major banks (BCA, Mandiri, BNI, BRI) are open to working with licensed exchanges, but they require proof of the PFAK license and strong AML compliance. Without a license, bank accounts are nearly impossible to open.

Payment gateways like GoPay, OVO, and DANA are popular for fiat on-ramps, but they also require the exchange license. As of 2026, Bank Indonesia has issued guidelines for digital asset service providers, making it easier for licensed exchanges to partner with banks. However, banks still conduct thorough due diligence, including background checks on directors and shareholders.

Compliance and Trust

Compliance is a major focus for Bappebti. Licensed exchanges must adhere to strict AML/CTF requirements, including customer due diligence (CDD), transaction monitoring, and suspicious transaction reporting. As of 2026, Bappebti conducts regular audits and may impose sanctions for non-compliance, including license revocation.

Trust is built through transparency. Exchanges must publish their list of approved coins, maintain segregated customer funds, and undergo annual audits by a registered public accountant. Additionally, the mandatory clearing house membership ensures that trades are settled securely.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make these mistakes when applying for the Indonesia crypto license:

  • Incomplete Documentation: Submitting incomplete or poorly translated documents can delay the process. All documents must be in Indonesian.
  • Underestimating Timeline: The 6-12 month timeline is realistic; rushing may lead to rejection.
  • Ignoring Local Partner Requirements: The requirement for a local director and majority local shareholders is strict. Failing to find a reliable partner can derail the application.
  • Neglecting AML/CTF Policies: Bappebti scrutinizes compliance frameworks. Generic policies are not enough; they must be tailored to your business model.
  • Not Planning for Banking: Opening a bank account after license approval can take months. Start early.

Frequently asked questions

What is the Indonesia crypto exchange license called?

It is called the Physical Crypto Asset Trader (PFAK) license, issued by Bappebti. It is mandatory for any entity offering crypto trading services to Indonesian residents.

Who regulates crypto exchanges in Indonesia?

The regulator is Bappebti (Commodity Futures Trading Regulatory Agency) under the Ministry of Trade. They oversee all crypto asset trading activities.

What is the minimum capital for a crypto exchange license in Indonesia?

The minimum paid-up capital is IDR 1 billion (approximately USD 65,000). This must be deposited in a local bank account and cannot be withdrawn without approval.

How long does it take to get the Indonesia crypto license?

The process typically takes 6-12 months from company incorporation to license issuance. This includes document preparation, Bappebti review, and on-site inspection.

Can a foreign company apply for the Indonesia crypto license?

No, a foreign company must first incorporate a local PT (Perseroan Terbatas) with at least one local director and majority local shareholders. The license is issued to the local entity.

What taxes apply to crypto exchanges in Indonesia?

Exchanges must withhold 0.1% VAT and 0.1% income tax on transactions. Corporate income tax of 22% applies to exchange profits.

Is staking or lending allowed under the PFAK license?

Staking and lending are not explicitly allowed. They may be considered securities under other regulations. You should consult with Bappebti or a local lawyer before offering such services.

Do I need a clearing house membership?

Yes, all licensed exchanges must become members of the Indonesia Clearing House for Digital Assets (ICHDA) to clear and settle trades.

Can I use the Indonesia license to operate in other ASEAN countries?

No, the Indonesia license is not a passport to other ASEAN markets. Each country has its own regulatory regime. You would need separate licenses for other jurisdictions.

How does Indonesia compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 cost, no capital gains tax, and a 2-3 month timeline, but no large domestic market. Indonesia has a larger market but higher costs (IDR 1 billion capital) and longer timeline.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

Exchanges must submit regular transaction reports, undergo annual audits, maintain AML/CTF programs, and report any changes in management or ownership to Bappebti.
