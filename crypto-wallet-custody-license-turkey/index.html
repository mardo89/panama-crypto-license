What is the Crypto Wallet Custody License Turkey?

The crypto wallet custody license Turkey is a regulatory authorization issued by the Capital Markets Board of Turkey (CMB) that permits companies to safekeep and manage private keys on behalf of clients. This license is part of Turkey's broader regulatory framework for crypto asset service providers, which came into full effect following amendments to the Capital Markets Law in 2024.

Under the Turkish regime, any platform that holds customer crypto assets-whether for trading, staking, or simple storage-must hold this license. The license covers both hot and cold wallet custody, and imposes strict operational and security requirements. Without it, providing custody services to Turkish residents is illegal.

Who Needs This License?

The crypto wallet custody license Turkey is mandatory for any legal entity that:

  • Offers crypto wallet services to Turkish residents, including custodial wallets for individuals or institutions.
  • Manages private keys on behalf of clients, whether for exchange platforms, payment processors, or dedicated custody providers.
  • Provides staking or lending services where the provider holds the customer's crypto assets.

Foreign companies offering such services to Turkish users must also obtain the license or partner with a licensed local entity. The CMB has extraterritorial reach: if you target Turkish customers, you fall under their jurisdiction.

If your business model does not involve holding customer assets (e.g., non-custodial wallets), you may not need this license, but you should still seek legal advice. Consulting24 can help you assess your obligations. For a broader view of licensing options across jurisdictions, explore our guides.

License Type and Regulator

The crypto wallet custody license Turkey is a single license type under the Capital Markets Law, administered by the Capital Markets Board of Turkey (CMB). The CMB is the primary financial regulator in Turkey, responsible for overseeing capital markets, including crypto asset service providers.

Unlike some jurisdictions that have multiple tiers (e.g., MiCA's Class 1-3), Turkey's regime is unified: one license covers custody, exchange, and transfer services. However, the CMB may impose additional conditions depending on the scope of activities. The license is non-transferable and must be renewed periodically.

For comparison, the Estonia crypto license is issued by the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) and has different capital requirements. Consulting24 can advise on which jurisdiction best fits your business model.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for the crypto wallet custody license Turkey is set by the CMB. As of 2026, the required share capital is TRY 10 million (approximately EUR 300,000 at current exchange rates, but subject to fluctuation). This capital must be fully paid up and maintained throughout the license period.

This is significantly higher than many other jurisdictions. For example, under MiCA in the EU, the minimum capital for custody services is EUR 125,000 (Class 2), while Panama has no minimum capital requirement. Consulting24 can help you structure your capital efficiently, whether you choose Turkey or another jurisdiction.

Note that the capital must be held in a Turkish bank account and cannot be used for operational expenses without CMB approval. We advise clients to budget for this requirement early in the planning process.

Tax Treatment

Turkey's tax treatment of crypto custody services is evolving. As of 2026, income from custody fees is subject to corporate income tax at the standard rate of 20% (reduced from 22% in 2025). Value-added tax (VAT) at 18% applies to custody services provided to Turkish clients, unless an exemption is available.

Capital gains from crypto assets held by the custodian for its own account are also taxable. However, transactions on behalf of clients are not taxable to the custodian. The CMB and Turkish Revenue Administration have issued guidance clarifying that custody providers must maintain detailed records for tax purposes.

International tax treaties may reduce withholding tax on cross-border payments. Consulting24 recommends engaging a local tax advisor to optimize your structure. For a jurisdiction with no corporate tax on crypto activities, explore the Panama option.

Allowed Activities

With the crypto wallet custody license Turkey, you are permitted to:

  • Safekeep and administer crypto assets on behalf of clients (custody).
  • Facilitate transfers of crypto assets between wallets.
  • Provide staking and lending services where the custodian holds the assets.
  • Offer wallet management and recovery services.

Activities that require additional authorization include operating a crypto exchange (trading platform) and issuing stablecoins. The CMB may also restrict certain activities, such as offering unbacked crypto assets to retail investors. If you plan to combine custody with exchange services, you may need a separate exchange license or an expanded scope.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, you must comply with ongoing obligations set by the CMB. These include:

  • AML/KYC: Implement strong anti-money laundering procedures, including customer due diligence and transaction monitoring.
  • Reporting: Submit periodic reports to the CMB on custody assets, financial statements, and suspicious transactions.
  • Audit: Annual external audit by a CMB-approved auditor.
  • Capital maintenance: Maintain the minimum capital at all times.
  • Cybersecurity: Adhere to technical standards for key management, data protection, and incident response.

What is the crypto wallet custody license Turkey?

It is a license issued by the Capital Markets Board of Turkey (CMB) that allows companies to safekeep and manage crypto assets on behalf of clients. It became mandatory in 2024 under the Capital Markets Law.

Who needs this license?

Any company offering custodial wallet services to Turkish residents, including exchanges, custody providers, and platforms that hold customer private keys. Non-custodial services may not require it.

What is the minimum capital for the Turkish custody license?

The minimum capital is TRY 10 million (approximately EUR 300,000 as of 2026). This must be fully paid and maintained throughout the license period.

How long does it take to get the license?

Can a foreign company apply for this license?
