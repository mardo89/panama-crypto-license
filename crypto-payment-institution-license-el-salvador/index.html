What Is the Crypto Payment Institution License in El Salvador?

The crypto payment institution license is a regulatory authorization issued by the Central Reserve Bank of El Salvador (BCR) under the Digital Assets Issuance Law (Ley de Emisión de Activos Digitales). It allows companies to provide crypto-related payment services, including custody, exchange, and transfer of digital assets. This license is distinct from a Bitcoin Service Provider (BSP) registration, which is simpler and limited to Bitcoin-only services.

Key features of the license include:

  • Authorization to offer multi-currency crypto services (not just Bitcoin)
  • Compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) regulations
  • Ongoing reporting obligations to the BCR and the Financial Investigation Unit (FIU)
  • Minimum capital requirement of approximately USD 500,000 (exact amount confirmed in consultation)

The license is designed for institutions that handle customer funds and facilitate digital asset payments. It is not a simple registration; it requires a strong operational framework, including a physical presence in El Salvador, a local compliance officer, and audited financial statements. The BCR expects applicants to demonstrate technical capacity, such as secure custody solutions and transaction monitoring systems. For example, an exchange that wants to offer both Bitcoin and Ethereum trading must hold this license, whereas a Bitcoin-only ATM operator might only need a BSP registration. Who Needs This License?

Any business that intends to provide crypto payment services to residents or businesses in El Salvador must obtain this license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (buying/selling digital assets for fiat or other crypto)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Payment processors that handle crypto transactions
  • Remittance services using digital assets
  • Issuers of crypto-backed payment instruments (e.g., prepaid cards)

If your business model involves holding customer funds or facilitating crypto payments, you likely fall under the scope. The license is also relevant for international companies targeting the Salvadoran market. For instance, a European payment processor that wants to offer crypto remittances to El Salvador would need this license to operate legally. Even if your company is based abroad, if you serve Salvadoran customers, you must comply. License Type and Regulator

The crypto payment institution license is a specific category under the Digital Assets Issuance Law. The primary regulator is the Central Reserve Bank of El Salvador (BCR), which oversees payment systems and digital asset service providers. The BCR works in coordination with the Financial Investigation Unit (FIU) for AML/CTF compliance and the National Commission of Digital Assets (CNAD) for broader digital asset regulation.

Unlike some jurisdictions that have multiple license tiers, El Salvador currently offers a single license type for crypto payment institutions. However, the scope of activities must be clearly defined in the application. The regulator expects applicants to demonstrate operational readiness, including strong AML policies and adequate capital. The BCR conducts a thorough due diligence process, including background checks on shareholders and directors. In practice, the regulator may ask for additional information or clarifications, which can extend the timeline. Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a crypto payment institution license in El Salvador is approximately USD 500,000. This figure is based on the Digital Assets Issuance Law and market practice as of 2026. The capital must be maintained at all times and can be in fiat or approved digital assets, subject to regulatory approval.

This capital serves as a buffer to ensure the institution can meet its obligations and cover operational risks. It is higher than the capital required for a Bitcoin Service Provider (which is around USD 50,000) because the license covers a broader range of activities. The capital must be deposited in a Salvadoran bank account and cannot be withdrawn without regulatory permission. In practice, the BCR may require the capital to be held in a specific form, such as a time deposit or government bonds. Consulting24 can advise on structuring the capital and acceptable forms. Tax Treatment

El Salvador does not impose capital gains tax on Bitcoin or other digital assets. However, crypto payment institutions are subject to standard corporate income tax at a rate of 30% on profits from their services. Value-added tax (VAT) at 13% applies to fees charged for services.

There are no specific tax incentives for crypto payment institutions, though the government has signaled a pro-business approach. Companies must maintain proper accounting records and file tax returns with the Ministry of Finance. For example, if your company earns fees from crypto exchange services, those fees are subject to corporate tax. But if you hold Bitcoin as an investment and sell it at a profit, that gain is not taxed. This makes El Salvador attractive for crypto-heavy business models. However, you must still comply with transfer pricing rules if you transact with related parties. Consulting24 recommends working with a local tax advisor to ensure compliance. Allowed Activities

Holders of the crypto payment institution license can engage in the following activities:

  • Custody and safekeeping of digital assets
  • Exchange of digital assets for fiat currency and vice versa
  • Exchange of one digital asset for another
  • Transfer of digital assets on behalf of clients
  • Payment processing using digital assets
  • Issuance of payment instruments (e.g., crypto debit cards)

The license does not permit the issuance of unbacked digital assets (i.e., creating new tokens) or operating a digital asset exchange that lists securities. Those activities require separate authorization under the Digital Assets Issuance Law. For instance, if you want to launch a new token, you would need a separate issuer license. Similarly, if your exchange lists tokens that are considered securities, you may need a securities exchange license. The BCR provides guidance on what constitutes a security, but the determination is case-by-case. Consulting24 can help you define your service scope to avoid regulatory overlap.

  • Insufficient capital: Underestimating the capital requirement can lead to application rejection. Ensure you have at least USD 500,000 available.

  • Incomplete application: Missing documents or inaccurate information delays the process. Double-check all submissions.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a Bitcoin Service Provider (BSP) and a crypto payment institution license in El Salvador?

A BSP registration is simpler and limited to Bitcoin-only services, with a lower capital requirement (around USD 50,000). The crypto payment institution license covers multi-currency services (e.g., Ethereum, stablecoins) and requires USD 500,000 capital. If you plan to offer services beyond Bitcoin, you need the full license.

Can a foreign company apply for the crypto payment institution license in El Salvador?

Yes, but you must incorporate a Salvadoran subsidiary or branch. The license is issued to the local entity, which must have a physical presence in El Salvador, including a registered office and a local compliance officer. Consulting24 can help with incorporation.

How long does the application process take?

Typically 6 to 12 months from submission. The timeline depends on the completeness of your application and the BCR's workload. Engaging an experienced advisor like Consulting24 can help avoid delays caused by missing documents or regulatory queries.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements after obtaining the license?

You must submit monthly transaction reports, conduct annual audits, maintain an AML compliance officer, and file suspicious activity reports. The BCR may conduct on-site inspections. Non-compliance can lead to fines or license revocation.

Is there a minimum capital requirement for the crypto payment institution license?

Yes, approximately USD 500,000. This capital must be deposited in a Salvadoran bank account and maintained at all times. It can be in fiat or approved digital assets, subject to regulatory approval.

What taxes apply to crypto payment institutions in El Salvador?

Corporate income tax is 30% on profits from services. VAT at 13% applies to fees. However, there is no capital gains tax on digital assets. You must file tax returns with the Ministry of Finance.

Can I operate a crypto exchange in El Salvador with this license?

Yes, the license allows exchange of digital assets for fiat or other crypto, custody, and payment processing. However, listing securities or issuing new tokens requires separate authorization.

How does the El Salvador license compare to Panama's crypto license?

Panama offers a flat fee of EUR 6,000 with no minimum capital and faster processing (2-4 months), but less regulatory clarity. El Salvador requires USD 500,000 capital and takes 6-12 months, but provides a stronger legal framework and no capital gains tax on crypto.

Do I need to have a physical office in El Salvador?
