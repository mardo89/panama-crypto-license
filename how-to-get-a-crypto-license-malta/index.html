What Is a Malta Crypto License?

A Malta crypto license, officially a VFA Service Provider licence, authorises firms to conduct activities such as exchange, custody, and investment services involving virtual financial assets. The regime is governed by the VFAA and the Virtual Financial Assets Regulations, with the MFSA as the sole regulator. Malta's approach is principles-based but thorough, requiring a detailed business plan, strong governance, and fit-and-proper management.

Unlike some EU member states that offer simpler registration, Malta demands a full authorisation process similar to a MiFID investment firm. This makes the licence highly reputable but also more demanding. For firms targeting institutional clients or seeking to passport services across the EU, a Malta VFA licence is a strong choice. However, the timeline and cost are higher than jurisdictions like Lithuania or Panama.

The MFSA classifies virtual financial assets separately from e-money and utility tokens. If your business deals with VFAs as defined in the VFAA, you fall under this regime. The licence is non-transferable and must be renewed annually. As of 2026, Malta has issued approximately 30 VFA licences, reflecting the high bar set by the regulator.

Who Needs a Malta Crypto License?

Any entity providing VFA services in or from Malta must hold a licence. This includes:

  • VFA exchanges (order-matching platforms)
  • VFA wallet providers (custodial and non-custodial)
  • VFA investment firms (portfolio management, advice)
  • VFA brokers (executing orders on behalf of clients)
  • VFA asset managers

If your business involves the transfer, exchange, or safekeeping of virtual financial assets, and you are based in Malta or target Maltese clients, you need this licence. For non-custodial services or utility tokens, a different classification may apply. Consulting24 can advise on whether your specific business model falls under the VFA regime or the less stringent 'Innovative Technology Arrangement' (ITA) sandbox. The ITA sandbox is for start-ups testing innovative products, but it does not allow full commercial operations. Most serious businesses go straight for the VFA licence.

License Type & Regulator

The regulator is the Malta Financial Services Authority (MFSA). Under the VFAA, there are three classes of VFA licences, each with increasing scope and capital requirements:

ClassPermitted ActivitiesMinimum Capital
Class 1Receiving and transmitting orders, execution of orders on behalf of clients€50,000
Class 2Class 1 + dealing on own account, portfolio management, investment advice€125,000
Class 3Class 2 + operating a multilateral trading facility (MTF), underwriting, and full exchange services€730,000

Most crypto exchanges and custodians require a Class 3 licence. The MFSA also offers a 'VFA Agent' role - a mandatory intermediary that assists with the application and ongoing compliance. The VFA Agent must be an approved person or firm registered with the MFSA. As of 2026, there are about 15 approved VFA Agents. Choosing a competent agent is critical, as they are your primary liaison with the regulator.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital for a Class 3 VFA licence is €730,000. This must be held in liquid assets (cash or equivalents) and maintained at all times. For Class 2, the requirement is €125,000, and for Class 1, €50,000. Capital can be used for operational expenses but must not fall below the minimum. The MFSA may require additional capital based on the risk profile and volume of transactions. As of 2026, MiCA's capital tiers (€50k/€125k/€150k) are also relevant for EU passporting, but Malta's Class 3 requirement exceeds MiCA's top tier for CASPs.

Capital must be evidenced by a bank confirmation letter or audited financial statements. The MFSA does not accept cryptocurrencies as capital. For firms that cannot tie up €730k, alternatives like Panama (no minimum capital) or Lithuania (€125k under MiCA) may be more accessible. Tax Treatment

Malta offers a competitive corporate tax regime. The standard corporate tax rate is 35%, but through a full imputation system and tax refunds, the effective rate can be as low as 5% for non-resident shareholders. Crypto gains are treated as ordinary income unless held as capital assets. VAT is not applicable on VFA transactions (exempt without credit). There is no capital gains tax on crypto for non-trading entities. However, specific tax rulings should be obtained for each business model. Consulting24 can connect you with Maltese tax advisors for a tailored opinion.

Malta has a wide network of double taxation treaties, which can reduce withholding taxes on dividends and interest. For companies distributing profits to non-resident shareholders, the tax refund system effectively lowers the tax burden. This makes Malta attractive for holding companies and investment firms.

Allowed Activities

A Class 3 VFA licence permits the following activities:

  • Operating a VFA exchange (order book or automated matching)
  • Custody and administration of VFAs on behalf of clients
  • Execution of orders for VFAs
  • Portfolio management of VFAs
  • Investment advice on VFAs
  • Underwriting and placing of VFAs
  • Operating a multilateral trading facility (MTF) for VFAs

Note: The licence does not cover fiat currency services (e-money or payment services) - those require separate licences. Also, derivatives on VFAs fall under MiFID II, not VFAA. The MFSA strictly prohibits unlicensed activities and imposes heavy penalties. For example, operating an exchange without a licence can result in fines up to €5 million or imprisonment. Always confirm your activity scope with a legal expert.

Step-by-Step Process

Obtaining a Malta crypto licence involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application: Engage a VFA Agent (mandatory). Prepare business plan, compliance manual, risk management policies, and governance structure.
  2. Application submission: The VFA Agent submits the application to the MFSA, including all required documents (e.g., shareholder details, source of funds, financial projections).
  3. MFSA review: The MFSA assesses the application, may request clarifications, and conducts interviews with key personnel. This stage takes 4-6 months.
  4. In-principle approval: If satisfied, the MFSA grants an in-principle approval, allowing the company to set up operational infrastructure (office, systems, staff).
  5. Final approval: After a final review and payment of the licence fee, the MFSA issues the licence. The entire process typically takes 6-12 months.

Banking & Payments

Opening a bank account in Malta for a VFA company can be challenging due to de-risking. Many traditional banks are reluctant to serve crypto firms. However, a few Maltese banks and payment institutions accept VFA-licensed entities, especially after licence issuance. Alternatively, firms can use EU-based payment service providers (PSPs) or electronic money institutions (EMIs). The MFSA requires that client funds be segregated and held in a trust account. Benefits of a Malta Crypto License

Despite the higher cost and longer timeline, a Malta VFA licence offers significant advantages:

  • EU passporting: Once licensed, you can provide services across the EU under MiCA equivalence.
  • High reputation: The MFSA is a respected regulator, enhancing trust with partners and clients.
  • Tax efficiency: Effective tax rate as low as 5% for non-residents.
  • Stable legal environment: English-based common law, with clear crypto-specific legislation.
  • Access to EU talent pool: Malta has a growing fintech ecosystem.

For firms that prioritise credibility and EU market access, Malta is a top-tier jurisdiction. However, for those seeking speed and lower cost, alternatives like Panama (EUR 6,000 flat, 2-3 months) or Lithuania may be more suitable. Compliance & Trust

Ongoing compliance is rigorous. Licensees must submit periodic reports (audited financial statements, transaction reports, AML/CFT returns) and maintain a local presence (office, directors, compliance officer). The MFSA conducts on-site inspections and can impose fines or revoke licences for non-compliance. Key requirements include:

  • Appointment of a MLRO (Money Laundering Reporting Officer)
  • Annual independent audit
  • Client asset segregation
  • Transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting

Common Mistakes

Applicants often make these errors:

  • Underestimating capital: The €730k minimum for Class 3 is strict; failing to maintain it can delay approval.
  • Poor business plan: The MFSA expects detailed financial projections and a clear value proposition.
  • Ignoring the VFA Agent role: The agent is not optional; choose one with experience in crypto.
  • Incomplete source of funds documentation: The MFSA scrutinises shareholder backgrounds.
  • Rushing the process: Expect 6-12 months; shortcuts lead to rejection.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Malta crypto license?

For a Class 3 VFA licence, the minimum capital is €730,000. Class 2 requires €125,000 and Class 1 requires €50,000. This capital must be held in liquid assets and maintained at all times.

How long does it take to get a Malta crypto license?

The typical timeline is 6 to 12 months from application to issuance. This includes the MFSA review period (4-6 months) and the operational setup phase after in-principle approval.

What activities are covered under a Malta VFA license?

A Class 3 licence covers operating an exchange, custody, execution, portfolio management, investment advice, underwriting, and operating an MTF for VFAs. It does not cover fiat services or derivatives.

Is a VFA Agent mandatory for Malta crypto license applications?

Yes, a VFA Agent is mandatory. The agent must be approved by the MFSA and assists with the application, compliance, and ongoing reporting. You cannot apply directly without one.

Can I passport a Malta VFA license to other EU countries?

Yes, under MiCA equivalence, a Malta VFA licence allows you to provide services across the EU via passporting. However, you must notify the MFSA and comply with host state requirements.

What are the tax benefits of a Malta crypto license?

Malta's corporate tax rate is 35%, but through tax refunds, the effective rate can be as low as 5% for non-resident shareholders. VFA transactions are VAT-exempt, and there is no capital gains tax on crypto for non-trading entities.

How does Malta compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Malta offers EU passporting and high reputation but costs €60k-€150k plus €730k capital and takes 6-12 months. Panama costs a flat €6,000, no minimum capital, and takes 2-3 months, but does not provide EU access.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements for a Malta VFA license?

Licensees must submit annual audited financial statements, AML/CFT returns, transaction reports, and maintain a local presence. The MFSA conducts on-site inspections and requires a MLRO.

Can I get a Malta crypto license if my company is already registered elsewhere?

Yes, you can apply as a foreign company by setting up a Maltese subsidiary. The subsidiary must have substance in Malta, including a registered office, directors, and compliance staff.

What happens if I fail to maintain the minimum capital?

The MFSA may suspend or revoke your licence if capital falls below the minimum. You must notify the regulator immediately and restore capital within a specified period, typically 30 days.
