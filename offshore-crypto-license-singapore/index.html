What is the Offshore Singapore Crypto License?

The offshore Singapore crypto license refers to the Major Payment Institution (MPI) license under the Payment Services Act (PSA), which regulates digital payment token (DPT) services. This license allows companies to offer crypto exchange, transfer, and custodial services from Singapore to clients worldwide, subject to MAS oversight. Unlike some jurisdictions, Singapore does not have a separate 'crypto license' per se; instead, crypto activities fall under the PSA's definition of payment services.

Key features of the MPI license include:

  • Regulated by the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS)
  • Applies to companies providing DPT services (e.g., buying, selling, exchanging, transferring, or safeguarding crypto assets)
  • Requires a minimum capital of SGD 250,000 (approximately USD 185,000) for MPI license
  • Subject to AML/CFT, cybersecurity, and risk management requirements
  • Allows operations across multiple payment services, including money transfer and e-money issuance

The license is considered one of the most reputable in the crypto space, offering access to Singapore's strong banking infrastructure and global connectivity. However, the application process is demanding, often taking 6-12 months or more.

Who Needs an Offshore Singapore Crypto License?

Any business that intends to provide digital payment token services from Singapore or to Singapore residents must obtain the MPI license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralized and decentralized platforms)
  • Over-the-counter (OTC) trading desks
  • Wallet providers and custodians
  • Payment processors that handle crypto transfers
  • Brokerage firms facilitating crypto trades

If your target market includes institutional investors or high-net-worth individuals seeking a regulated environment, Singapore is an attractive option. However, due to the high cost and complexity, it is often more suitable for well-funded companies with a strong compliance background. Startups may find the Panama crypto license (flat EUR 6,000) or Lithuania's regime more accessible, but Singapore offers unparalleled credibility.

License Type and Regulator

The relevant regulator is the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS). Under the Payment Services Act, there are two types of licenses:

  • Standard Payment Institution (SPI): For businesses with lower transaction volumes (monthly average below SGD 3 million for e-money, SGD 6 million for other services). Minimum capital: SGD 100,000.
  • Major Payment Institution (MPI): For larger businesses exceeding SPI thresholds or those offering multiple payment services. Minimum capital: SGD 250,000. This is the typical license for crypto firms.

The MAS is known for its rigorous oversight, requiring detailed business plans, AML policies, and a local presence (e.g., a registered office and compliance officer). The license is not transferable and requires ongoing reporting.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for an MPI license is SGD 250,000 (approximately USD 185,000). This must be paid up and maintained in a Singapore bank account. The MAS may require higher capital based on the scale and risk profile of the business. For SPI licenses, the minimum is SGD 100,000. Capital must be held in liquid assets and cannot be used for operational expenses.

In comparison, the Panama crypto license requires no paid-up capital beyond the EUR 6,000 flat fee, making it significantly cheaper. However, Singapore's capital requirement reflects its higher regulatory standards and market credibility.

Tax Treatment

Singapore offers a territorial tax system, meaning income sourced within Singapore is taxed, while foreign-sourced income may be exempt if remitted. The corporate tax rate is a flat 17%. For crypto businesses:

  • Capital gains from crypto trading are generally not taxed (as Singapore does not impose capital gains tax).
  • Revenue from crypto services (e.g., trading fees, spreads) is subject to 17% corporate tax.
  • GST (Goods and Services Tax) at 9% applies to certain crypto services, but the MAS has provided exemptions for digital payment tokens.

Singapore has no withholding tax on dividends or interest, and there are numerous double tax treaties. However, tax treatment can vary based on the specific activities. Consulting24 recommends engaging a local tax advisor for personalized advice.

Allowed Activities

Under the MPI license, companies can perform the following activities:

  • Digital payment token exchange (buying, selling, swapping)
  • Cross-border money transfer (including crypto)
  • Domestic money transfer
  • Merchant acquisition (accepting crypto payments)
  • E-money issuance (if combined with DPT services)
  • Custodial services for digital assets

Note that the license does not cover securities tokens (which fall under the Securities and Futures Act) or derivatives. Companies must ensure they do not offer products that constitute securities without additional licensing. The MAS also restricts certain activities, such as lending crypto to retail investors.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Pre-application preparation: Engage local legal and compliance advisors. Draft AML/CFT policies, business plan, risk assessment, and governance framework.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Singapore company with a local director (Singapore resident) and a registered address. Minimum paid-up capital of SGD 1 (but at least SGD 250,000 for MPI).
  3. Submit application to MAS: File the license application via the MAS online portal, including all required documents (e.g., shareholder details, source of funds, compliance manual). Pay the application fee.
  4. MAS review: The MAS will conduct a preliminary assessment (4-6 weeks) and then a detailed review (6-12 months). They may request additional information or interviews.
  5. In-principle approval (IPA): If successful, you receive an IPA, which allows you to set up operations while final conditions are met (e.g., appointing a compliance officer, establishing a local office).
  6. Final approval and launch: Once all conditions are satisfied, the MAS issues the full license. You can commence regulated activities.

Banking and Payments

Singapore has a well-developed banking sector, but crypto firms often face challenges opening bank accounts due to perceived risks. Major banks like DBS, OCBC, and UOB have begun accepting crypto clients, but they require thorough due diligence. Alternative solutions include:

  • Digital banks (e.g., DBS Digital Exchange, though limited)
  • Payment service providers (e.g., Stripe, PayPal) for fiat on/off ramps
  • Third-party payment processors specializing in crypto

Benefits of the Singapore Crypto License

  • Regulatory credibility: The MAS is a gold standard regulator, enhancing trust with partners and clients.
  • Access to Asian markets: Singapore is a gateway to Southeast Asia and beyond.
  • Stable business environment: Strong rule of law, low corruption, and political stability.
  • Tax efficiency: No capital gains tax, territorial taxation, and extensive treaty network.
  • Banking infrastructure: World-class financial services, though crypto-friendly banking is still evolving.

However, these benefits come at a high cost and complexity. For many businesses, a more cost-effective alternative like the Panama crypto license (EUR 6,000 flat) or Estonia's regime may be more suitable.

Compliance and Trust

Singapore's regulatory framework is built on strict compliance. Key requirements include:

  • Appointment of a local compliance officer and AML officer
  • Regular audits and submission of financial statements
  • Transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting
  • Cybersecurity measures (e.g., ISO 27001 certification)
  • Annual AML/CFT training for staff

Failure to comply can result in fines, license suspension, or revocation. The MAS conducts regular inspections. Consulting24 advises on building a compliance framework that meets MAS expectations. General guidance, not legal advice.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Underestimating costs: Many applicants budget only for the minimum capital, ignoring legal and compliance setup fees.
  • Incomplete documentation: The MAS requires detailed policies; generic templates are often rejected.
  • Ignoring local presence: You need a Singapore resident director and office; using a nominee director service may raise red flags.
  • Overpromising in business plan: Unrealistic projections can lead to rejection.
  • Not engaging local advisors: The MAS prefers dealing with local representatives who understand the regulatory landscape.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between an SPI and MPI license in Singapore?

The Standard Payment Institution (SPI) license is for smaller businesses with transaction volumes below SGD 3 million per month for e-money or SGD 6 million for other services, requiring SGD 100,000 capital. The Major Payment Institution (MPI) license is for larger firms or those offering multiple services, with SGD 250,000 capital. Most crypto exchanges apply for the MPI license.

Can a foreign company apply for a Singapore crypto license?

Yes, but the company must be incorporated in Singapore as a private limited company. Foreign shareholders are allowed, but at least one director must be a Singapore resident (citizen, permanent resident, or Employment Pass holder). The MAS also requires a local compliance officer.

How long does the MAS take to process a license application?

The process typically takes 6 to 12 months from submission to final approval. This includes a preliminary assessment (4-6 weeks) and a detailed review. Delays can occur if the MAS requests additional information or if the application is incomplete.

Is the Singapore crypto license recognized in other countries?

The license is issued under Singapore law and is not automatically recognized elsewhere. However, Singapore's regulatory framework is highly respected, and holding an MAS license can facilitate licensing in other jurisdictions due to its strong AML/CFT standards.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements after obtaining the license?

Licensees must submit annual audited financial statements, AML/CFT reports, and notify the MAS of any material changes (e.g., new shareholders, business expansion). They must also maintain a compliance officer, conduct regular audits, and undergo periodic inspections by the MAS.

Can I operate a crypto exchange without a license in Singapore?

No. As of 2026, all entities providing digital payment token services in or from Singapore must hold a valid license from the MAS. Operating without a license is illegal and can result in fines, imprisonment, or both.

What is the tax rate for crypto businesses in Singapore?

The corporate tax rate is 17% on profits derived from Singapore. Capital gains from crypto trading are generally not taxed. GST at 9% applies to certain services, but digital payment tokens are exempt. Consult a tax advisor for your specific situation.

How does the Singapore license compare to Panama?

Panama offers a much cheaper and faster option: a flat EUR 6,000 fee, no minimum capital, and a 2-3 month timeline. However, Panama lacks the regulatory prestige and banking infrastructure of Singapore. Choose Singapore if you need high credibility; choose Panama for cost efficiency.

What happens if my application is rejected?

The MAS may reject applications if they fail to meet regulatory standards. Common reasons include insufficient AML policies, unclear source of funds, or lack of local presence.
