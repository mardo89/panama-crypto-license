What is the Crypto Payment Institution License in Dubai?

The Crypto Payment Institution License in Dubai is a regulatory authorization issued by VARA that allows companies to provide payment services using virtual assets. This includes processing crypto payments, issuing e-money in virtual assets, and operating payment systems for digital currencies. The license is part of Dubai's broader Virtual Assets Regulatory Framework, which came into full effect in 2023 and has been refined through 2026.

Unlike a general VASP license, the payment institution license is tailored for businesses whose primary activity is facilitating crypto payments. It requires a dedicated compliance framework, specific capital allocation, and adherence to VARA's Payment Services Rulebook. The license is essential for any firm that wants to offer crypto payment services to customers in Dubai or from Dubai to the world.

Who Needs This License?

Any business that intends to provide the following services in or from Dubai must obtain the Crypto Payment Institution License:

  • Processing crypto payments for merchants or individuals
  • Issuing stablecoins or other virtual asset-based payment instruments
  • Operating a crypto payment gateway or wallet service
  • Facilitating cross-border crypto transfers as a payment service
  • Providing virtual asset exchange services integrated with payment processing

This license is ideal for fintech startups, payment processors, and established financial institutions looking to expand into crypto. It is also relevant for e-commerce platforms that want to accept crypto payments directly. If your business model involves any form of payment initiation, execution, or settlement using virtual assets, you likely fall under VARA's purview.

License Type & Regulator

The regulator for the Crypto Payment Institution License in Dubai is the Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA), established under Dubai Law No. 4 of 2022. VARA is the sole regulator for virtual assets in Dubai (excluding the Dubai International Financial Centre, which has its own framework).

The license type is a 'Category 3' license under VARA's classification, specifically for 'Payment Services' involving virtual assets. This is distinct from the 'Category 1' (Advisory) and 'Category 2' (Exchange) licenses. The license is issued for a specific set of payment services as defined in VARA's rulebook.

VARA requires all licensees to maintain a physical presence in Dubai, including a registered office and a local compliance officer. The regulatory framework is principles-based but detailed, with strict requirements on anti-money laundering (AML), counter-terrorist financing (CTF), and consumer protection. Capital Requirement

VARA mandates a minimum capital requirement for the Crypto Payment Institution License, which as of 2026 is set at AED 500,000 (approximately EUR 125,000). This capital must be maintained as liquid assets and cannot be used for operational expenses. The requirement is based on the risk profile of the payment services offered.

For businesses offering higher-risk services, such as cross-border transfers or stablecoin issuance, VARA may require additional capital up to AED 1,000,000 (EUR 250,000). The capital must be held in a UAE bank account and is subject to annual audit.

Tax Treatment

Dubai offers a favorable tax environment for crypto payment institutions. As of 2026, the UAE has a 9% corporate tax rate for taxable profits exceeding AED 375,000 (approximately EUR 95,000). However, free zone companies in Dubai, including those licensed by VARA, may qualify for a 0% corporate tax rate if they meet certain conditions (e.g., no onshore business).

There is no value-added tax (VAT) on crypto transactions in the UAE, as virtual assets are generally treated as exempt supplies. However, advisory fees and other services may be subject to 5% VAT. Personal income tax is zero, and there are no capital gains taxes on crypto holdings for individuals.

Allowed Activities

Under the Crypto Payment Institution License, VARA permits the following activities:

  • Issuance of payment instruments in virtual assets (e.g., stablecoins)
  • Execution of payment transactions using virtual assets
  • Money remittance services using virtual assets
  • Payment initiation services (third-party payment initiation)
  • Account information services (aggregation of crypto accounts)
  • Acquiring of virtual asset payments for merchants

The license does not permit proprietary trading, custody of client assets (unless specifically allowed), or issuance of securities tokens. Each activity must be pre-approved by VARA and is subject to operational limits. Consulting24 helps you define your activity scope and ensures your application covers all intended services.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The application process for the Crypto Payment Institution License in Dubai involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application consultation: Engage with VARA or a consulting firm like Consulting24 to assess eligibility and scope.
  2. Company incorporation: Set up a legal entity in Dubai (mainland or free zone) with a physical office.
  3. Document preparation: Prepare business plan, AML/CTF policies, risk assessment, and governance framework.
  4. Submit application: File with VARA along with the application fee and supporting documents.
  5. Review and feedback: VARA reviews the application and may request additional information or clarifications.
  6. On-site inspection: VARA may conduct an on-site visit to verify your office and compliance infrastructure.
  7. Approval and licensing: Upon approval, pay the licensing fee and receive your license.
  8. Ongoing compliance: Submit regular reports, maintain capital, and undergo annual audits.

Compliance & Trust

Compliance is at the core of VARA's regulatory framework. Licensees must implement strong AML/CTF programs, including customer due diligence (CDD), transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting. VARA requires the appointment of a local compliance officer and a money laundering reporting officer (MLRO).

Additionally, licensees must undergo an annual audit by a VARA-approved auditor and submit periodic reports on transactions, capital adequacy, and risk management. Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a VARA license and a DIFC license?

VARA regulates virtual assets in Dubai mainland and free zones (excluding DIFC). DIFC has its own regulator, the Dubai Financial Services Authority (DFSA), which also offers a crypto license. VARA is more focused on virtual asset services, while DFSA covers a broader range of financial services. The choice depends on your target market and business structure.

Can I apply for the license if my company is based outside the UAE?

Yes, but you must incorporate a local entity in Dubai (mainland or free zone) as part of the application. VARA requires a physical presence in Dubai, including a registered office and a local compliance officer. Consulting24 can assist with the incorporation process.

How long does the VARA license application take?

The typical timeline is 4 to 6 months from submission to approval. This can vary based on the complexity of your application and VARA's workload. Factors like incomplete documentation or additional requests can extend the timeline. Consulting24 helps streamline the process to minimize delays.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements after obtaining the license?

Licensees must submit quarterly and annual reports to VARA, maintain AML/CTF programs, conduct regular audits, and ensure capital adequacy. You must also appoint a local compliance officer and MLRO. VARA may conduct periodic inspections. Consulting24 offers ongoing compliance support.

Is there a minimum capital requirement for the license?

Yes, the minimum capital requirement is AED 500,000 (approx. EUR 125,000) for most payment services. For higher-risk activities, VARA may require up to AED 1,000,000. The capital must be held in a UAE bank account and cannot be used for operations.

Can I offer services to customers outside the UAE with this license?

Yes, the license allows you to serve customers globally, but you must comply with the laws of the customer's jurisdiction. VARA does not restrict cross-border services, but you may need additional registrations in other countries. Consulting24 can advise on multi-jurisdictional compliance.

What happens if I don't maintain the required capital?

Failure to maintain the minimum capital can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. VARA monitors capital adequacy through periodic reports. Consulting24 helps you set up processes to ensure ongoing compliance.

How does the Dubai license compare to the Panama crypto license?

Panama offers a much lower cost (EUR 6,000 flat) and no minimum capital for most activities. The timeline is 2-3 weeks, and there is no tax on foreign-sourced income. However, Panama lacks the regulatory prestige and market access of Dubai. The choice depends on your budget and target market.
