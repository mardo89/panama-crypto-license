What is a Crypto Wallet Custody License in Saint Lucia?

A crypto wallet custody license in Saint Lucia authorizes a company to hold, store, and manage private keys or digital assets on behalf of clients. This is a specific category under the Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) regime, regulated by the Financial Intelligence Authority (FIU). The license covers custodial wallet services, including safekeeping of cryptocurrencies, transaction execution, and recovery services.

Saint Lucia's framework aligns with FATF recommendations, requiring strong AML/CFT controls. Unlike some jurisdictions, Saint Lucia does not impose a minimum capital requirement for this license, making it accessible for smaller operators. The license is valid for one year and must be renewed annually. The FIU conducts periodic audits to ensure ongoing compliance. For example, a startup offering a multi-signature wallet for institutional clients would need this license to legally hold client assets. The license also permits ancillary services such as transaction monitoring and reporting, provided they are directly related to custody.

Who Needs This License?

Any business that intends to offer custodial wallet services to third parties in or from Saint Lucia must obtain this license. Typical applicants include:

  • Crypto wallet providers (hot, cold, or multi-signature wallets)
  • Digital asset custodians serving institutional clients
  • Exchanges that offer integrated wallet services
  • Fintech firms adding crypto storage features

If your business model involves holding client private keys or assets, you likely fall under this licensing requirement. Exemptions apply only for non-custodial wallets where the user retains full control of keys. For instance, a software wallet that never touches user funds does not require a license. However, if you offer recovery services or manage keys on behalf of clients, you need the license. Consulting24 can help determine whether your specific business model requires licensing.

License Type & Regulator

The crypto wallet custody license in Saint Lucia is a VASP license under the Virtual Asset Business Act (VABA) 2021. The regulator is the Financial Intelligence Authority (FIU), which also oversees AML/CFT compliance. The license is specific to custodial wallet services and is distinct from other VASP categories like exchange or transfer services.

The FIU requires applicants to demonstrate strong internal controls, a fit-and-proper management team, and a clear business plan. The license does not have a minimum capital requirement, but the applicant must show sufficient financial resources to operate. The FIU also requires a local registered agent and a physical office in Saint Lucia. The regulator conducts background checks on directors and shareholders, including criminal record checks and financial history reviews. Capital Requirement

Saint Lucia does not impose a statutory minimum capital requirement for the crypto wallet custody license. However, applicants must demonstrate that they have sufficient financial resources to operate the business and meet ongoing compliance obligations. In practice, a minimum of EUR 10,000 to 20,000 in working capital is advisable to cover initial setup and operational costs. The FIU may request proof of financial stability during the application review, such as bank statements or audited accounts. For example, a company with EUR 15,000 in liquid assets would likely satisfy this requirement. Compared to Lithuania, which requires EUR 125,000 for custody services under MiCA, Saint Lucia offers a lower barrier to entry.

Tax Treatment

Saint Lucia offers a highly favorable tax regime for international businesses. Companies holding a crypto wallet custody license that derive income from non-resident sources are subject to 0% corporate income tax. There is no capital gains tax, no withholding tax on dividends, and no VAT on digital asset services. The only ongoing tax obligation is an annual license fee to the FIU and a small business tax (if applicable). This makes Saint Lucia one of the most tax-efficient jurisdictions for crypto custody operations. However, licensees must still file annual tax returns to maintain good standing. For a similar tax-free environment, Panama also offers 0% corporate tax on offshore income with a flat licensing fee of EUR 6,000.

Allowed Activities

Under the crypto wallet custody license, the following activities are permitted:

  • Safekeeping and administration of virtual assets on behalf of clients
  • Management of private keys and recovery phrases
  • Execution of transactions on behalf of clients (from custodial wallets)
  • Provision of wallet-as-a-service (WaaS) to other businesses
  • Integration with third-party protocols for staking or lending (subject to separate approval)

The license does not permit proprietary trading, exchange operations, or issuance of tokens without additional authorization. For example, a custodian cannot run a trading desk under this license. If you need to offer exchange services, you would require a separate VASP license. The FIU may also allow limited ancillary services such as transaction monitoring and reporting, provided they are directly related to custody.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Company incorporation - Register a Saint Lucia International Business Company (IBC) with a local registered agent. This typically takes 1-2 weeks.
  2. Document preparation - Compile business plan, AML/CFT policies, risk assessment, and personal declarations for directors and shareholders. The AML policy must include customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting procedures.
  3. Submit application - File the VASP license application with the FIU, including all supporting documents and the application fee. The FIU may request additional information within 30 days.
  4. FIU review - The regulator conducts due diligence on the company and its beneficial owners. This may take 2-4 months, during which the FIU may interview directors or request clarifications.
  5. Approval & payment - Upon approval, pay the annual license fee and receive the license certificate. The license is valid for one year from the date of issue.
  6. Ongoing compliance - Submit periodic reports (e.g., quarterly transaction reports, annual AML audit) and renew the license annually. The renewal process is simpler but still requires updated documentation.
