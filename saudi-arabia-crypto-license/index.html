What Is the Saudi Arabia Crypto License?

The Saudi Arabia crypto license is not a single permit but a set of regulatory approvals required to operate crypto-related services in the Kingdom. As of 2026, the regulatory landscape is divided: SAMA supervises payment token services (including stablecoins and crypto payments) under the Payment Services Provider (PSP) regime, while the CMA oversees crypto assets that qualify as securities or investment instruments. For other activities, such as exchange platforms or custody, the Saudi government is actively developing a comprehensive framework. Currently, no unified “crypto license” exists; businesses must obtain appropriate authorizations based on their specific activities.

The process is rigorous but offers a clear path to operate legally in one of the Gulf’s largest economies.

Who Needs a Saudi Arabia Crypto License?

Any entity offering crypto-related services to residents of Saudi Arabia or targeting the Saudi market should consider obtaining the relevant authorization. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (spot trading, fiat-to-crypto, crypto-to-crypto)
  • Payment token issuers (stablecoins, e-money tokens)
  • Wallet providers (custodial and non-custodial)
  • Crypto custody services
  • Token issuers conducting ICOs or STOs that fall under CMA jurisdiction
  • DeFi platforms offering regulated activities

If your business involves any of these activities, you must engage with SAMA or CMA. Operating without authorization can lead to penalties, account freezes, or criminal liability. Consulting24 helps you determine the exact requirements based on your service offering.

Capital Requirement

The Saudi Arabia crypto license capital requirement is not publicly fixed in a single regulation. Based on SAMA’s PSP framework, the minimum capital for payment services is typically SAR 500,000 (approx. USD 133,000) for small-scale operations, but may be higher for larger volumes. For CMA securities licenses, capital requirements align with CMA’s general securities rules: SAR 10 million (approx. USD 2.67 million) for dealing and advising. However, these figures are subject to change and depend on the specific activity. Tax Treatment

Saudi Arabia does not impose a personal income tax or corporate income tax on most businesses, but there are specific levies relevant to crypto companies:

  • Zakat: 2.5% on net assets for Saudi-owned entities (or a portion based on ownership). Foreign-owned companies may be subject to income tax at 20% on profits.
  • VAT: 15% on taxable supplies, including crypto services if they are considered financial services (exemptions may apply).
  • Withholding tax: 5-20% on certain payments to non-residents.

Cryptocurrency transactions may be subject to VAT if they involve the exchange of goods or services. However, pure crypto-to-crypto trades are often treated as barter transactions and may be VAT-exempt. Consulting24 recommends engaging a local tax advisor to structure your operations efficiently.

Allowed Activities

Under the Saudi Arabia crypto license framework, the following activities are permitted with appropriate authorization:

  • Issuance and redemption of payment tokens (stablecoins) under SAMA
  • Processing crypto payments for merchants
  • Custody of crypto assets (if classified as securities, under CMA)
  • Operating a crypto exchange for security tokens (CMA)
  • Tokenized securities offerings (STOs) under CMA
  • Participation in SAMA’s regulatory sandbox for innovative crypto services

Activities not yet explicitly regulated include spot trading of non-security cryptocurrencies (e.g., Bitcoin, Ethereum) and decentralized finance (DeFi) protocols. The government is expected to issue a comprehensive crypto law by 2027. Until then, businesses often rely on the sandbox or operate from free zones. Obtaining a Saudi Arabia crypto license involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates with local partners throughout the process:

Banking & Payment Partners

Opening a corporate bank account for a crypto business in Saudi Arabia is challenging but possible. Most traditional banks are cautious. Options include:

  • Local Saudi banks: Riyad Bank, Al Rajhi Bank, Saudi National Bank - require a physical presence and a local license.
  • Digital banks: STC Pay (digital wallet) may offer limited services for crypto companies.
  • International banks: Some global banks with Saudi branches (e.g., HSBC, Standard Chartered) may accept crypto clients with strong compliance.
  • Payment processors: Local PSPs like PayTabs or HyperPay can facilitate fiat on/off ramps.

Compliance is critical for maintaining a Saudi Arabia crypto license. Key requirements include:
