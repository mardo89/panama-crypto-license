What Is a Crypto Gambling License in Slovakia?

A crypto gambling license in Slovakia is a combined authorization that allows operators to offer online casino, sports betting, and other gambling services using cryptocurrencies. It consists of two components: a gambling license issued by the Ministry of Finance under the Gambling Act, and a CASP registration with the NBS under MiCA. The license is mandatory for any entity targeting Slovak players or operating from Slovak territory. As of 2026, Slovakia fully implements MiCA, meaning crypto gambling operators must meet capital requirements of at least EUR 50,000 for simple custody and transfer services, up to EUR 150,000 for more complex services like trading platforms. The gambling license itself requires a minimum capital of EUR 100,000 for online casino and EUR 50,000 for betting. Combined, the total capital requirement is typically EUR 100,000-150,000, depending on the scope of services.

Who Needs a Crypto Gambling License in Slovakia?

Any business that offers online gambling services accepting cryptocurrencies and targets Slovak residents must obtain this license. This includes:

  • Online casinos accepting Bitcoin, Ethereum, or other cryptos
  • Sports betting platforms with crypto deposits and withdrawals
  • Poker rooms and other skill-based gambling sites using digital assets
  • White-label gambling operators serving Slovak players

Even if your company is based outside Slovakia, if you actively market to Slovak users or process transactions from Slovak bank accounts, you need a license. The regulator takes a strict territorial approach. Operators without a license face fines up to EUR 1 million or criminal liability. Capital Requirement

Slovakia imposes a minimum capital requirement of EUR 100,000 for online casino licenses and EUR 50,000 for betting-only licenses. For crypto gambling operators combining both, the higher amount applies. Additionally, under MiCA, CASP registration requires capital based on the services offered: EUR 50,000 for custody and transfer, EUR 125,000 for exchange services, and EUR 150,000 for trading platforms. Most crypto gambling operators fall into the EUR 125,000-150,000 tier. The total capital required is the higher of the gambling and CASP amounts, typically EUR 150,000. This capital must be fully paid up and held in a Slovak bank account before license issuance. It can be used for operational expenses but must be maintained as a buffer. Tax Treatment

Slovakia applies a gambling tax of 20% on gross gaming revenue (GGR) for online casino and betting. This is a flat rate, with no VAT on gambling services. For crypto transactions, capital gains tax may apply if the operator holds crypto as an investment, but the gambling tax is the primary levy. Corporate income tax is 21% on net profits, standard for Slovak companies. Operators can deduct legitimate business expenses, including license fees, marketing, and salaries. There is no specific crypto gambling tax regime; the general rules apply. Slovakia has double tax treaties with many countries, which can reduce withholding taxes on dividends or interest. Allowed Activities

A crypto gambling license in Slovakia permits the following activities:

  • Online casino games (slots, table games, live dealer)
  • Sports betting (pre-match and in-play)
  • Poker tournaments and cash games
  • Lotteries and bingo (if offered online)
  • Fantasy sports (if considered gambling under Slovak law)

Crypto-specific allowed activities under CASP registration include:

  • Accepting cryptocurrencies for deposits and withdrawals
  • Converting crypto to fiat and vice versa (exchange service)
  • Custody of player crypto wallets
  • Transferring crypto between players (if applicable)

Operators cannot offer unlicensed games, such as binary options or unregulated financial betting. The license does not cover physical gambling venues. All games must be certified for fairness by an approved testing lab (e.g., GLI, BMM).

Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining a crypto gambling license in Slovakia involves these steps:

  1. Company incorporation: Register a Slovak limited liability company (s.r.o.) with a registered office in Slovakia. Minimum share capital EUR 5,000.
  2. Prepare documentation: Business plan, AML/CTF policies, responsible gambling policy, technical system description, financial projections, and personal background checks on directors.
  3. Apply for gambling license: Submit to Ministry of Finance with application fee. Include proof of capital deposit, lease agreement for office, and software certifications.
  4. Apply for CASP registration: Simultaneously file with NBS. Provide AML program, internal controls, and information on crypto services offered.
  5. Regulatory review: Both regulators assess compliance. They may request additional information or interviews with management.
  6. License issuance: Once approved, pay the license fee and receive the gambling license. CASP registration is confirmed.
  7. Go live: After license in hand, you can launch operations. Ongoing reporting required quarterly.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, you must adhere to ongoing compliance requirements:

  • AML/CTF: Implement risk-based AML policies, conduct player due diligence, and report suspicious transactions to the Slovak Financial Intelligence Unit.
  • Responsible gambling: Offer self-exclusion tools, deposit limits, and reality checks. Publish responsible gambling information.
  • Reporting: Submit quarterly reports to the Ministry of Finance on GGR, player numbers, and tax payments. Annual CASP reports to NBS.
  • Audits: Annual financial audit and periodic technical audits of gaming systems.
  • Data protection: Comply with GDPR for player data.

Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

Many applicants face delays or rejection due to these pitfalls:

  • Incomplete AML documentation: Ensure your AML policy covers crypto-specific risks, such as anonymous transactions and mixing services.
  • Underestimating capital requirements: The combined capital of EUR 150,000 must be fully paid and maintained; using it prematurely can cause issues.
  • Ignoring technical certifications: All games must be certified by an approved lab before submission. Start this early.
  • Poor background checks: Directors and beneficial owners must have clean criminal records and financial stability. Disclose all relevant information.
  • Choosing the wrong legal structure: An s.r.o. is standard, but a joint-stock company (a.s.) may be required for larger operations. Consult a lawyer.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto gambling license in Slovakia?

The combined minimum capital is typically EUR 100,000-150,000, depending on the gambling services (casino vs betting) and crypto services offered. The gambling license requires EUR 100,000 for casino and EUR 50,000 for betting; CASP registration adds EUR 50,000-150,000. The higher amount applies.

How long does it take to get a crypto gambling license in Slovakia?

The process takes 4-8 months. The gambling license from the Ministry of Finance takes 2-4 months, and CASP registration from the National Bank of Slovakia takes 2-4 months. Parallel processing can reduce overall time.

Can I use a Slovak license to operate in other EU countries?

The gambling license is valid only in Slovakia, but under EU freedom of services, you can apply for cross-border recognition in other EU member states. However, each country may impose additional requirements. CASP registration under MiCA is passportable across the EU for crypto services.

What taxes apply to crypto gambling in Slovakia?

A gambling tax of 20% on gross gaming revenue (GGR) applies. Corporate income tax is 21% on net profits. No VAT on gambling. Crypto capital gains tax may apply if the operator holds crypto as an investment.

Do I need a physical office in Slovakia?

Yes, you need a registered office in Slovakia. The Ministry of Finance requires a physical presence, which can be a serviced office. The office must have staff (at least a director or compliance officer) during business hours.

Can I accept cryptocurrencies directly from players?

Yes, but you must be registered as a CASP with the National Bank of Slovakia. You can accept crypto deposits and withdrawals, but you must implement KYC/AML checks and convert crypto to fiat through a registered exchange if needed.

What are the ongoing compliance costs?

Annual compliance costs range from EUR 10,000 to EUR 20,000, including AML officer salary, audits, reporting fees, and legal retainer. The exact amount depends on the complexity of your operations.

Is a Slovak license recognized by payment processors?

Yes, a Slovak license is well-regarded by European payment processors and banks. However, some banks may still be cautious about crypto. Consulting24 can introduce you to crypto-friendly banking partners.

Can I apply for a Slovak license if my company is registered in another country?

No, you must incorporate a Slovak company (s.r.o. or a.s.) to apply. The license is issued to the Slovak entity. You can structure a holding company abroad, but the operating entity must be Slovak.

What happens if I operate without a license?

Operating without a license in Slovakia is illegal and can result in fines up to EUR 1 million, criminal prosecution, and seizure of assets. The regulator actively monitors online gambling and blocks unlicensed sites.
