What Is the Lithuania Crypto License?

The Lithuania crypto license, officially a Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) authorization under the Law on Markets in Crypto Assets (MiCA), is a regulatory permit required to offer crypto-related services in Lithuania and across the EU. It replaced the previous registration-only regime in 2025. The license is issued by the Bank of Lithuania and covers activities such as crypto exchange, custody, transfer services, and more. Holding a Lithuanian CASP license allows you to passport services to other EU member states under MiCA's single passport rule.

Key features of the license include compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) directives, strong governance requirements, and a minimum capital threshold based on the services offered. The Bank of Lithuania is known for its pragmatic approach and clear guidelines, making it one of the more accessible EU regulators for crypto firms.

Who Needs a Lithuania Crypto License?

Any business that provides crypto asset services to clients in Lithuania or the EU must obtain a CASP license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (trading platforms)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Crypto-to-fiat exchange services
  • Transfer services for crypto assets
  • Brokerage and dealing services
  • Advisory services on crypto assets

If your company is based outside the EU but targets EU clients, you will likely need a CASP license in an EU member state like Lithuania. The license is also required for decentralised finance (DeFi) platforms that exercise control over user funds or provide custody.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Bank of Lithuania (Lietuvos bankas), which oversees the licensing and supervision of CASPs under MiCA. The license type is a single CASP authorization, but the specific permissions depend on the services you intend to offer. There is no separate license for exchanges versus custodians; instead, you apply for a CASP license and list the services you will provide.

The Bank of Lithuania requires applicants to have a physical presence in Lithuania, including a registered office and local management. The application process involves a thorough assessment of the business plan, AML policies, IT security, and fit-and-proper checks on directors and beneficial owners.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital for a CASP license in Lithuania is tiered based on the services provided:

  • EUR 50,000 for simple services like advisory or transfer services without custody
  • EUR 125,000 for exchange services (including crypto-to-crypto and crypto-to-fiat)
  • EUR 150,000 for custodial wallet services and exchange combined

This capital must be paid in full before the license is granted and maintained throughout the license period. It can be in the form of cash or liquid assets held in a Lithuanian bank account. The capital serves as a buffer to cover operational risks and potential liabilities.

Tax Treatment

Lithuania offers a competitive tax environment for crypto businesses. The corporate income tax rate is 15%, with a reduced rate of 0% for small companies (up to EUR 300,000 revenue) that meet certain criteria. Crypto-to-crypto transactions are generally exempt from VAT, and profits from crypto trading are treated as capital gains, taxed at the standard corporate rate.

For individuals, crypto gains are subject to a 15% personal income tax. Lithuania does not impose a specific crypto tax regime; instead, it applies general tax rules. The country has a wide network of double taxation treaties. Allowed Activities Under the License

A Lithuania CASP license permits the following activities, as defined by MiCA:

  • Providing custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Operating a trading platform for crypto assets
  • Exchanging crypto assets for fiat currency or other crypto assets
  • Executing orders for crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Placing crypto assets (underwriting or placing)
  • Transfer services for crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Providing advice on crypto assets
  • Managing portfolios of crypto assets

You must specify which services you will offer in your application. The license does not cover crypto mining or non-fungible tokens (NFTs) unless they qualify as crypto assets under MiCA.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining a Lithuania crypto license involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step to ensure a smooth process.

  1. Pre-application preparation: Define your business model, services, and target market. Prepare a detailed business plan, AML/KYC policies, risk management framework, and IT security documentation.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a legal entity in Lithuania (e.g., UAB) with a registered office and local directors. The minimum share capital is EUR 2,500 for a private limited company.
  3. Capital deposit: Open a corporate bank account in Lithuania and deposit the required minimum capital (EUR 50,000-150,000, depending on services).
  4. Submit application: File the CASP application with the Bank of Lithuania, including all supporting documents. Pay the application fee.
  5. Regulatory review: The Bank of Lithuania reviews the application, conducts fit-and-proper checks, and may request additional information. This stage takes 2-4 months.
  6. On-site inspection (if required): The regulator may visit your premises to verify operational readiness.
  7. License issuance: Once approved, you receive the CASP license and can start operations. You must comply with ongoing reporting and AML obligations.

Benefits of a Lithuania Crypto License

Lithuania offers several advantages for crypto businesses:

  • EU passporting: Once licensed, you can operate across all 27 EU member states without additional licenses.
  • Clear regulation: The Bank of Lithuania provides transparent guidelines and a predictable application process.
  • Competitive tax: 15% corporate tax (0% for small companies) and no VAT on crypto-to-crypto trades.
  • Fast processing: Compared to other EU jurisdictions like Malta or France, Lithuania's timeline of 3-6 months is relatively quick.
  • Skilled workforce: Lithuania has a strong fintech talent pool and a supportive government ecosystem.
  • Cost-effective: Setup and ongoing costs are lower than in many Western European countries.

These benefits make Lithuania a top choice for crypto entrepreneurs seeking a reputable EU license.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Holding a Lithuania CASP license comes with ongoing compliance duties. These include:

  • Annual AML/CFT audits and reporting to the Bank of Lithuania
  • Submission of financial statements and capital adequacy reports
  • Transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting
  • Regular updates to AML policies and procedures
  • Fit-and-proper re-assessments of management every few years

Non-compliance can result in fines, license suspension, or revocation. Common Mistakes to Avoid

Many applicants face delays or rejections due to avoidable errors. Common mistakes include:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing business plans, AML policies, or risk assessments.
  • Insufficient capital: Not depositing the full minimum capital before application.
  • Unclear business model: Vague descriptions of services or target clients.
  • Poor IT security: Lack of proper cybersecurity measures or data protection policies.
  • Ignoring local presence: Not having a physical office or local management in Lithuania.
  • Underestimating timeline: Starting the process too late or expecting immediate approval.

Frequently asked questions

How long does it take to get a Lithuania crypto license in 2026?

The typical timeline is 3 to 6 months from application submission to approval. Pre-application preparation can add 1-2 months. The Bank of Lithuania reviews applications thoroughly, so ensure all documents are complete.

What is the minimum capital for a Lithuania crypto license?

The minimum capital depends on the services: EUR 50,000 for advisory/transfer services, EUR 125,000 for exchange services, and EUR 150,000 for custodial services. This must be paid in full before the license is granted.

Can I passport my Lithuania license to other EU countries?

Yes, under MiCA, a CASP license issued by the Bank of Lithuania allows you to provide services across all EU member states without additional licenses, subject to notification procedures.

What are the tax rates for crypto businesses in Lithuania?

Corporate income tax is 15% (0% for small companies with revenue under EUR 300,000). Crypto-to-crypto transactions are VAT-exempt. Personal income tax on crypto gains is 15%.

Do I need a physical office in Lithuania?

Yes, you must have a registered office and local management in Lithuania. The Bank of Lithuania requires a physical presence for supervision and compliance purposes.

What documents are required for the application?

You need a business plan, AML/KYC policies, risk management framework, IT security documentation, financial projections, and fit-and-proper declarations for directors and beneficial owners. The exact list is provided by the Bank of Lithuania.

Can I apply for a Lithuania crypto license if my company is outside the EU?

Yes, but you must incorporate a subsidiary in Lithuania (e.g., a UAB) that will hold the license. The parent company can be based anywhere, but the licensed entity must be Lithuanian.

How much does the entire process cost?

Excluding the minimum capital, costs typically range from EUR 10,000 to EUR 30,000, including government fees, legal setup, and compliance drafting. Exact costs depend on your service scope and complexity. Contact us for a tailored quote.

What happens if I don't comply with ongoing obligations?

Non-compliance can lead to fines, license suspension, or revocation. You must submit annual reports, conduct AML audits, and maintain capital adequacy. Consulting24 offers ongoing compliance support to help you stay compliant.

Is a Lithuania crypto license valid for DeFi projects?

It depends. If your DeFi project involves custody, exchange, or transfer of crypto assets and you control user funds, you likely need a license. Pure protocol-based DeFi without intermediary services may fall outside the scope. Consult a legal expert.
