Luxembourg has established itself as a leading hub for investment funds in Europe, and its framework for crypto funds is no exception. The crypto fund license in Luxembourg, often referred to as a specialized investment fund for digital assets, is regulated by the Commission de Surveillance du Secteur Financier (CSSF). This license allows fund managers to operate a collective investment scheme that invests primarily in crypto assets, such as Bitcoin, Ethereum, and other tokens.

For crypto founders looking to access European investors and benefit from a stable, regulated environment, Luxembourg offers a sophisticated yet accessible route. What is the Luxembourg Crypto Fund License?

The Luxembourg crypto fund license is not a single license but a regulatory approval for a fund structure that invests in crypto assets. Typically, it falls under Part II of the Luxembourg law of 17 December 2010 relating to undertakings for collective investment (UCI), or as a specialized investment fund (SIF) under the law of 13 February 2007. The fund must be managed by a regulated management company (AIFM) or be self-managed.

Key characteristics include:

  • Regulated by the CSSF (Commission de Surveillance du Secteur Financier)
  • Available as a SIF (Specialised Investment Fund) or Part II UCI
  • Minimum capital requirements vary: €50,000 for a SIF, €125,000 for Part II UCI
  • Tax efficient: exempt from corporate income tax, net wealth tax, and subscription tax (0.01% for SIF)
  • Timeline: 3-6 months for approval

This license is ideal for professional investors and offers a high degree of flexibility in asset allocation, including 100% crypto exposure. The fund can invest directly in cryptocurrencies, tokens, or crypto-related derivatives, provided the investment policy is clearly defined in the prospectus.

Who Needs a Luxembourg Crypto Fund License?

This license is designed for fund managers and investment professionals who want to launch a regulated collective investment scheme focused on digital assets. Typical applicants include:

  • Asset managers launching a crypto hedge fund or venture capital fund
  • Family offices seeking a regulated vehicle for crypto investments
  • Fintech companies creating tokenized investment products
  • Traditional fund managers expanding into digital assets

If you plan to raise capital from institutional or well-informed investors in the EU, a Luxembourg fund structure provides the regulatory credibility and passporting rights under AIFMD. For example, a fund manager based in Germany can market the fund to German professional investors without additional licensing. For founders targeting a simpler structure, the Lithuania crypto license may be an alternative, but it does not offer fund passporting.

License Type & Regulator

The competent authority for crypto funds in Luxembourg is the Commission de Surveillance du Secteur Financier (CSSF). The fund itself is typically structured as one of the following:

Fund TypeRegulatory FrameworkInvestor Type
Specialised Investment Fund (SIF)Law of 13 February 2007Well-informed investors (minimum €125,000 investment)
Part II UCILaw of 17 December 2010Retail and institutional investors

The management company (AIFM) must be authorized under the AIFM Directive (2011/61/EU) or be a regulated entity. The CSSF requires a detailed business plan, risk management policies, and a depositary for safekeeping of assets. Crypto assets are typically held with a qualified custodian or through a regulated crypto custodian. The CSSF has issued specific guidance on crypto asset custody, requiring segregation of assets and regular audits.

Capital Requirement

Minimum capital requirements depend on the fund type:

  • SIF (Specialised Investment Fund): Minimum capital of €50,000, to be reached within 12 months of launch.
  • Part II UCI: Minimum capital of €125,000, to be reached within 6 months.

Additionally, the management company (AIFM) must have initial capital of at least €125,000 if it is an external AIFM. For self-managed funds, the fund itself must meet the higher capital threshold. These figures are set by Luxembourg law and are not negotiable. The capital must be fully paid up in cash or liquid assets. Crypto assets cannot be used to meet the capital requirement.

Tax Treatment

Luxembourg offers a highly attractive tax regime for investment funds:

  • Corporate income tax (CIT): Funds are generally exempt from CIT on income and gains.
  • Net wealth tax (NWT): Exempt for SIF and Part II UCI.
  • Subscription tax (taxe d'abonnement): 0.01% per annum on net assets for SIF (reduced to 0.00% for money market funds and certain ESG funds). Part II UCI: 0.05%.
  • VAT: Management services are exempt from VAT.

No withholding tax on distributions to non-resident investors. This tax efficiency is a key reason why Luxembourg is a top choice for fund domiciliation. However, the fund must file annual tax returns and comply with transfer pricing rules if related parties are involved.

Allowed Activities

A Luxembourg crypto fund can engage in a wide range of activities related to digital assets, including:

  • Direct investment in cryptocurrencies (e.g., Bitcoin, Ethereum)
  • Investment in crypto tokens (utility, security, or payment tokens)
  • Staking and lending of crypto assets (subject to risk management)
  • Participation in DeFi protocols (with appropriate due diligence)
  • Investment in crypto-related equities and derivatives

The fund must have a clear investment policy in its prospectus and adhere to risk diversification rules. For SIFs, the principle of risk spreading applies, while Part II UCI has more stringent diversification requirements. For example, a SIF can invest up to 100% in a single crypto asset if disclosed, but a Part II UCI cannot invest more than 10% in any single issuer.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a crypto fund license in Luxembourg involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application consultation: Define fund structure, investment strategy, and target investors. Consulting24 advises on the optimal vehicle.
  2. Drafting legal documents: Prospectus, management regulations, and depositary agreement. Must comply with CSSF circulars.
  3. Appointment of service providers: Depositary bank, auditor, and (if needed) external AIFM.
  4. Submission to CSSF: File application with all supporting documents, including business plan, AML/CFT policies, and risk management framework.
  5. CSSF review: Typically 3-6 months, with possible requests for additional information.
  6. Approval and launch: Once approved, the fund can start operations and accept investors.

Banking & Payments

Opening a bank account for a Luxembourg fund is straightforward but requires a regulated entity. The fund must appoint a depositary bank, which holds the fund's assets (including crypto) in custody. Major Luxembourg banks like Banque Internationale à Luxembourg (BIL), BNP Paribas, and Société Générale offer depositary services for crypto funds.

For fiat banking, the fund will need a current account for operational expenses. Some banks may require a minimum deposit of €50,000. Crypto assets are typically held with a specialized crypto custodian that is CSSF-approved or regulated under EU law. For example, BitGo or Coinbase Custody may be acceptable if they meet CSSF standards. Consulting24 can introduce you to suitable banking partners.

Benefits of a Luxembourg Crypto Fund License

Luxembourg offers several advantages for crypto fund managers:

  • EU passporting: Under AIFMD, the fund can be marketed to professional investors across the EU.
  • Tax neutrality: No corporate tax, net wealth tax, or withholding tax.
  • Regulatory credibility: CSSF supervision enhances investor trust.
  • Experienced ecosystem: Deep pool of service providers (lawyers, auditors, depositaries) familiar with crypto.
  • Flexible structures: SIF and Part II UCI allow for various investment strategies.

Compared to other jurisdictions, Luxembourg is particularly strong for institutional-grade fund structures. For smaller funds, a Panama private interest foundation may be a cost-effective alternative, but it lacks the regulatory seal and EU market access.

Compliance & Trust

Compliance is critical for a Luxembourg crypto fund. The CSSF requires strong AML/CFT procedures, including customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting. The fund must also appoint a compliance officer and an AML responsible person.

Risk management must cover market, liquidity, operational, and custody risks specific to crypto assets. The depositary is responsible for safekeeping and oversight. Common Mistakes to Avoid

When applying for a Luxembourg crypto fund license, avoid these pitfalls:

  • Inadequate business plan: The CSSF expects a detailed plan with realistic projections.
  • Poor choice of depositary: Not all banks accept crypto funds; engage early.
  • Overlooking AML requirements: Crypto funds are high-risk; strong policies are mandatory.
  • Underestimating timeline: 3-6 months is typical; delays can occur if documents are incomplete.
  • Ignoring tax reporting: Even though tax-exempt, annual filings are required.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a SIF and Part II UCI for crypto funds?

A SIF (Specialised Investment Fund) is designed for well-informed investors with a minimum investment of €125,000, offering more flexibility and lower capital requirements (€50,000). Part II UCI is for retail investors and has stricter diversification rules, higher capital (€125,000), and a higher subscription tax (0.05% vs 0.01%).

Can a Luxembourg crypto fund invest in DeFi protocols?

Yes, but the fund must conduct thorough due diligence on the protocol's risks, including smart contract risk, liquidity risk, and regulatory compliance. The CSSF expects the fund's risk management policy to address these specific risks. Consulting24 can help draft appropriate policies.

How long does the CSSF application process take?

Typically 3-6 months from submission to approval. Delays can occur if the business plan is incomplete or if the depositary is not pre-approved. Consulting24 coordinates the process to minimize delays.

What are the ongoing compliance costs for a Luxembourg crypto fund?

Ongoing costs include depositary fees (€10,000-€30,000/year), audit fees (€5,000-€15,000/year), CSSF annual fees (around €2,000), and compliance officer costs. Total annual costs typically range from €20,000 to €50,000.

Is a Luxembourg crypto fund exempt from corporate tax?

Yes, both SIF and Part II UCI are exempt from corporate income tax and net wealth tax. They only pay a subscription tax (0.01% for SIF, 0.05% for Part II UCI) on net assets. This tax neutrality is a key advantage.

Can I use a crypto custodian outside Luxembourg?

The depositary must be established in Luxembourg, but it can delegate custody of crypto assets to a qualified sub-custodian (e.g., Coinbase Custody) provided the CSSF approves. The depositary retains oversight responsibility.

What happens if the fund fails to meet the minimum capital within the deadline?

If the SIF does not reach €50,000 within 12 months (or Part II UCI €125,000 within 6 months), the CSSF may revoke the license or impose sanctions. It is crucial to have a realistic capital raising plan.

Can a non-EU manager set up a Luxembourg crypto fund?

Yes, but the fund must have a local management company (AIFM) or be self-managed with a local board. The CSSF requires substance in Luxembourg, such as a local office and directors. Consulting24 can assist with substance arrangements.

How does the Luxembourg crypto fund compare to a Panama foundation?

A Panama foundation is simpler and cheaper (€6,000 flat) but lacks regulatory oversight and EU passporting. Luxembourg offers institutional credibility and access to EU investors, but at a higher cost and complexity.

What are the AML requirements for a Luxembourg crypto fund?

The fund must implement customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting. It must appoint an AML responsible person and comply with CSSF circulars. Crypto transactions require enhanced due diligence.
