What is a Crypto OTC Desk License in Ireland?

A crypto OTC desk license in Ireland refers to the authorisation required to operate an over-the-counter trading platform for crypto assets. Under MiCA, this falls under the category of 'execution of orders on behalf of clients' or 'placing of crypto assets'. The Central Bank of Ireland (CBI) is the competent authority responsible for licensing and supervision. The license is formally a 'Crypto Asset Service Provider' (CASP) authorisation under the European Union (Markets in Crypto-Assets) Regulations 2025.

OTC desks typically facilitate large trades between institutional clients, requiring strong compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorist financing (CTF) rules. The CBI expects applicants to have a physical presence in Ireland, including a registered office and key management functions. Unlike a standard exchange license, an OTC desk license focuses on off-exchange, bilateral trades. The CBI has published guidelines on what constitutes an OTC desk versus an exchange, and misclassification can lead to rejection. Who Needs an Irish Crypto OTC Desk License?

Any entity that provides crypto OTC desk services to clients in Ireland or across the EU must hold a CASP authorisation. This includes:

  • Firms executing large block trades of crypto assets for institutional or high-net-worth clients.
  • Brokerages that match buyers and sellers off-exchange.
  • Market makers providing liquidity to OTC desks.
  • Any entity that holds client funds or crypto assets during the trade process.

If your firm is based outside Ireland but offers services to Irish residents, you may need to establish a branch or subsidiary in Ireland. The CBI has a strict 'passporting' regime for non-EU firms. For example, a UK-based OTC desk serving Irish clients must either set up an Irish entity or rely on the UK's temporary permissions regime, which is limited. License Type and Regulator

The license is a Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) authorisation under MiCA. The regulator is the Central Bank of Ireland (CBI). The CBI has been proactive in regulating crypto assets since 2021 and now fully implements MiCA. The license is required for specific services, including:

  • Execution of orders on behalf of clients.
  • Placing of crypto assets.
  • Reception and transmission of orders.
  • Portfolio management (if applicable to OTC desks).

There is no separate 'OTC desk license' - it is covered under the CASP framework. The CBI publishes a list of authorised CASPs on its website. Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for CASPs depends on the services provided. For an OTC desk that executes orders and holds client funds, the capital requirement is EUR 125,000. If the desk also provides custody of crypto assets, the requirement rises to EUR 150,000. For pure order execution without custody, it is EUR 50,000. These figures are set by MiCA and are non-negotiable. Capital must be held in liquid assets, such as cash or government bonds.

It is important to note that the capital must be maintained at all times. The CBI may require proof of capital adequacy quarterly. For firms with higher trading volumes, additional capital may be required based on operational risk. Consulting24 helps you calculate the exact capital needed and structure it appropriately. Tax Treatment for Crypto OTC Desks in Ireland

Ireland has a favourable corporate tax rate of 12.5% for trading income. Crypto OTC desks are treated as financial service providers, and their profits are subject to this rate. Capital gains from crypto assets held for investment may be taxed at 33%, but for trading desks, revenue is typically classified as trading income. VAT is not applicable on crypto-to-crypto trades, but fiat conversions may incur VAT. Ireland has no withholding tax on dividends paid to EU residents, making it attractive for international firms.

However, tax treatment can be complex. For example, if the OTC desk holds crypto as inventory, unrealised gains may be taxed. Ireland also offers R&D tax credits for developing proprietary trading algorithms. Consulting24 advises on tax structuring as part of the licensing process, working with Irish tax advisors. For a zero-tax alternative, Panama offers 0% tax on offshore income, but lacks double tax treaties with many countries. Always consult a tax professional for your specific situation.

Allowed Activities Under the License

The CASP authorisation permits the following activities for an OTC desk:

  • Executing OTC trades for clients in crypto assets (e.g., Bitcoin, Ethereum, stablecoins).
  • Placing crypto assets on behalf of clients.
  • Receiving and transmitting orders for crypto assets.
  • Providing custody of crypto assets (if the license includes this service).
  • Offering portfolio management for crypto assets (requires additional permissions).

Prohibited activities: operating a centralised exchange (order book matching) without a separate license, offering derivatives without a MiFID license, and providing unregulated lending. The CBI strictly enforces these boundaries. For example, if your OTC desk also operates an order book for retail clients, you would need an exchange license under MiCA. Step-by-Step Application Process

The application process with the Central Bank of Ireland involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application: Engage with the CBI through a 'pre-application' meeting. This is informal but recommended. The CBI provides feedback on your business model and readiness.
  2. Company setup: Incorporate an Irish company (e.g., a private limited company) with a registered office and directors. Minimum two directors, one of whom must be resident in the EEA.
  3. Documentation: Prepare a detailed business plan, AML/CTF policies, risk management framework, and governance structure. Include financial projections and capital adequacy evidence. The business plan must cover at least three years.
  4. Submit application: File the application with the CBI along with the fee. The CBI has 3 months to assess completeness. Incomplete applications are rejected without refund.
  5. Review and interviews: The CBI may request additional information and conduct interviews with key personnel. This can take 3-6 months.
  6. Decision: Approval or refusal. If approved, the license is granted with conditions. Conditions may include limits on client types or asset classes.
  7. Ongoing supervision: Annual reporting, audits, and compliance reviews. The CBI conducts periodic inspections.

Compliance and Trust Considerations

Operating a crypto OTC desk in Ireland requires ongoing compliance with MiCA and local regulations. Key obligations include:

  • Annual AML/CTF audits by an external auditor.
  • Transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting to the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU).
  • Capital maintenance at all times, with quarterly reporting to the CBI.
  • Client asset segregation (if holding client funds) in a separate bank account.
  • Regular reporting to the CBI on financial health and trading volumes.

Failure to comply can result in fines up to EUR 5 million or 10% of annual turnover, suspension, or revocation of the license. The CBI conducts thematic reviews on AML controls and has recently increased scrutiny on OTC desks. This is general guidance, not legal advice. Common Mistakes to Avoid

Many applicants face delays or rejection due to avoidable errors:

  • Incomplete business plan: The CBI expects detailed operational and financial projections for at least three years. Many applicants underestimate the level of detail required.
  • Weak AML controls: Outsourcing AML to a third party without oversight is a red flag. The CBI wants to see in-house expertise.
  • Lack of local presence: The CBI requires a physical office and key management in Ireland. A virtual office is not sufficient.
  • Underestimating capital: Ensure the required capital is fully funded and documented. Some applicants fail to demonstrate that capital is unencumbered.
  • Ignoring tax obligations: Plan for Irish corporation tax and VAT from day one. Late filing can damage your application.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto OTC desk license in Ireland?

Under MiCA, the minimum capital is EUR 125,000 if you execute orders and hold client funds. If you also provide custody, it is EUR 150,000. For pure order execution without custody, it is EUR 50,000. Capital must be held in liquid assets.

How long does it take to get an Irish crypto OTC desk license?

The timeline is 6 to 12 months from application to approval. The CBI takes 3 months for initial assessment, followed by a detailed review. Pre-application meetings can help streamline the process.

Can a non-EU company apply for an Irish crypto OTC desk license?

Yes, but you must establish a subsidiary in Ireland with a physical office and at least one director resident in the EEA. The CBI requires local presence and key management functions.

What activities are allowed under the Irish CASP license for OTC desks?

Allowed activities include execution of orders, placing of crypto assets, reception and transmission of orders, and custody if included. Prohibited activities include operating an exchange or offering derivatives without additional licenses.
