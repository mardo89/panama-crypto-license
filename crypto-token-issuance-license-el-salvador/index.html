What Is the Crypto Token Issuance License in El Salvador?

The crypto token issuance license, officially known as the Digital Asset Issuance Authorization, is a regulatory approval granted by the CNAD. It allows legal entities to issue digital assets (tokens) to the public or qualified investors. The license is mandatory for any token offering that is not explicitly exempt, such as small-scale offerings below EUR 1 million or those limited to accredited investors. The law came into effect in 2023 and is fully operational in 2026, providing a stable legal framework.

Key features of the license include:

  • Regulatory oversight by the CNAD, ensuring compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and know-your-customer (KYC) standards.
  • Legal recognition of token issuances, providing investor confidence and market access.
  • Flexibility to issue various token types, including payment tokens, utility tokens, and asset-referenced tokens.

This license is distinct from a general crypto exchange or wallet license, focusing specifically on the issuance side of digital assets. The CNAD publishes a public register of authorized issuers, adding transparency. Who Needs This License?

Any entity planning to issue digital tokens in El Salvador or targeting Salvadoran investors must obtain this license. This includes:

  • Startups launching initial coin offerings (ICOs) or security token offerings (STOs).
  • Established companies tokenizing assets such as real estate, commodities, or intellectual property.
  • Protocols or foundations issuing governance or utility tokens.

Exemptions may apply for offerings below EUR 1 million or those limited to professional investors. However, even exempt issuers often seek the license to enhance credibility and facilitate secondary market trading. For example, a real estate tokenization project raising EUR 2 million would need the license, while a small community token under EUR 500,000 might be exempt. License Type and Regulator

The license is issued by the Comisión Nacional de Activos Digitales (CNAD), the sole regulator for digital assets in El Salvador. The CNAD operates under the Digital Assets Issuance Law (Ley de Emisión de Activos Digitales), enacted in 2023 and fully operational by 2026. The regulator is independent and oversees both issuance and secondary market activities.

There is one main license category for token issuance, but the CNAD may impose conditions based on the token's characteristics (e.g., security vs. utility). For instance, a security token may require a more detailed prospectus and ongoing reporting. The license is non-transferable and must be renewed annually. Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a crypto token issuance license in El Salvador is EUR 50,000 (or equivalent in USD). This capital must be fully paid up and maintained throughout the license period. It serves as a financial buffer to cover operational costs and potential liabilities. Unlike some EU jurisdictions that have tiered capital based on service types (e.g., EUR 125,000 for custody services under MiCA), El Salvador's requirement is a flat amount for all token issuers. However, the CNAD may require additional capital for issuers handling significant volumes or complex tokens. For example, an issuer planning to raise EUR 10 million may need to demonstrate higher reserves. Consulting24 can provide exact figures based on your business model. If capital is a concern, consider Panama where no minimum capital is required for a general business license.

Tax Treatment

El Salvador offers a favorable tax regime for digital asset issuers. Key points:

  • Corporate income tax: Standard rate of 30%, but token issuers may qualify for exemptions under the Digital Assets Law for the first three years. This exemption is subject to CNAD approval and requires meeting certain criteria.
  • Value-added tax (VAT): Token issuances are generally exempt from VAT, as they are treated as financial services. This reduces the tax burden on issuance fees.
  • Capital gains tax: Gains from token sales are taxed at 10% for residents; non-residents may be exempt if they do not have a permanent establishment in El Salvador.

It is essential to obtain a tax ruling from the Ministry of Finance to confirm your specific obligations. Consulting24 coordinates with local tax advisors to ensure compliance. For projects targeting EU markets, compare with Estonia crypto license where corporate tax is 0% on retained profits.

Allowed Activities

Under the Digital Assets Issuance License, you can:

  • Issue digital tokens to the public or qualified investors.
  • List tokens on approved exchanges within El Salvador.
  • Conduct secondary market trading of issued tokens, subject to additional approvals.
  • Offer token-related services such as staking or voting rights, if disclosed in the whitepaper.

Activities not allowed include operating a crypto exchange (requires a separate license) or providing custody services for third-party tokens. The license is strictly for issuance; other activities require additional authorization. For example, if you want to also operate a trading platform, you would need an exchange license. Compliance and Trust

Maintaining the license requires ongoing compliance with AML/KYC regulations. The CNAD conducts periodic audits and may request additional information. Key obligations include:

  • Annual renewal and fee payment. Late renewal can result in suspension.
  • Submission of audited financial statements within six months of year-end.
  • Reporting of any material changes to the token or business structure, such as a change in tokenomics or management.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Incomplete whitepaper: The CNAD requires detailed tokenomics, risk factors, and use of proceeds. Vague documents lead to rejection. Example: a whitepaper that does not explain the token's utility or the team's background will be returned.
  • Ignoring AML requirements: Even token issuers must have strong AML/KYC procedures. Failure can result in fines or license revocation. Ensure you have a compliance officer.
  • Underestimating capital: The EUR 50,000 minimum must be maintained; dipping below can trigger suspension. Plan for operational costs on top of capital.
  • Choosing the wrong jurisdiction: Compare El Salvador with Panama (flat EUR 6,000) or Czech Republic for different cost structures. El Salvador is best for dedicated token issuers, while Panama suits general crypto businesses.

Frequently asked questions

What is the cost of a crypto token issuance license in El Salvador?

The total cost ranges from EUR 12,000 to 25,000, including application fees (EUR 2,000-5,000) and legal/consulting fees (EUR 10,000-20,000). Annual renewal is EUR 1,000-3,000. Additionally, you must maintain a minimum capital of EUR 50,000. Consulting24 provides a detailed cost breakdown during the initial consultation.

How long does it take to get a token issuance license in El Salvador?

The CNAD aims to process applications within 90 days, but the total timeline is typically 3 to 6 months. This includes document preparation, submission, and due diligence. Complex cases may take longer. Consulting24 helps expedite the process by ensuring all documents are complete and accurate.

What are the capital requirements for the El Salvador token issuance license?

The minimum capital requirement is EUR 50,000 (or equivalent in USD). This must be fully paid up and maintained throughout the license period. The CNAD may require additional capital for larger issuances. Unlike MiCA, there are no tiers based on service type.

Can I issue both security tokens and utility tokens under this license?

Yes, the license covers various token types, including payment tokens, utility tokens, and asset-referenced tokens. However, security tokens may require additional disclosures and a more detailed prospectus. The CNAD assesses each application based on the token's characteristics.

Is El Salvador's token issuance license recognized in the EU?

No, El Salvador is not an EU member, so the license does not provide passporting rights under MiCA. If you need to target EU investors, consider a CASP license in Lithuania or Estonia. However, El Salvador's license is valid for local issuance and can be used for global offerings outside the EU.

What are the tax benefits of issuing tokens in El Salvador?

Token issuers may qualify for a corporate income tax exemption for the first three years. Token issuances are generally exempt from VAT. Capital gains tax is 10% for residents, and non-residents may be exempt. A tax ruling from the Ministry of Finance is recommended.

Do I need a physical office in El Salvador to get the license?

Yes, you need a registered office in El Salvador. This can be a virtual office or a physical space. The CNAD requires a local address for correspondence. Consulting24 can assist with finding a suitable office provider.

What happens if I don't maintain the minimum capital?

Falling below the EUR 50,000 minimum capital can result in license suspension or revocation. The CNAD conducts periodic audits. It is important to maintain the capital throughout the license period. Consulting24 provides compliance monitoring to help you stay compliant.

How does El Salvador compare to Panama for token issuance?

Panama has no dedicated token issuance license; a general business license costs EUR 6,000 flat and can be set up in 2-3 weeks. However, token issuers face legal uncertainty. El Salvador offers a regulated framework with higher costs (EUR 12,000-25,000) but provides legal clarity and tax incentives.
