Overview: What Is a Crypto Broker License in Vietnam?

In Vietnam, there is no standalone crypto broker license. Instead, companies providing crypto brokerage services must register as a business with the Department of Planning and Investment and comply with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) regulations under the State Bank of Vietnam. The government is actively developing a legal framework for digital assets, with a pilot program for crypto exchanges expected. As of 2026, the most common approach is to establish a local company with a business license that covers technology or trading services, and then apply for relevant approvals.

This hybrid regime means that your company must meet general corporate requirements while also satisfying AML obligations. The lack of a specific license creates both flexibility and uncertainty. For example, you may need to interpret how existing laws on securities, payments, and technology apply to your operations. Who Needs a Crypto Broker License in Vietnam?

Any entity that facilitates the buying, selling, or exchange of cryptocurrencies for clients likely needs to register in Vietnam. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges operating in the Vietnamese market
  • Over-the-counter (OTC) trading desks
  • Brokerage platforms offering crypto trading to Vietnamese residents
  • Payment service providers that integrate crypto transactions
  • Any foreign company marketing crypto services to Vietnamese users

Foreign companies targeting Vietnamese customers must also comply with local laws. Even if your company is based abroad, if you have a Vietnamese user base, you may be subject to local registration and AML rules. License Type & Regulator

The primary regulators for crypto activities in Vietnam are:

  • State Bank of Vietnam (SBV): Oversees AML/CTF compliance and payment services.
  • Ministry of Finance (MOF): Regulates securities and may oversee digital asset offerings.
  • Ministry of Information and Communications (MIC): Manages technology and cybersecurity aspects.

Currently, crypto brokers operate under an enterprise registration certificate (business license) with additional approvals from the SBV for AML compliance. A dedicated crypto license is expected but not yet enacted. The National Assembly has discussed a Digital Asset Law, but its passage timeline remains uncertain. Capital Requirement

Vietnam does not have a statutory minimum capital requirement specifically for crypto brokers. However, the company must have charter capital adequate for its operations. Typically, authorities expect at least EUR 10,000 to EUR 50,000 (VND 250 million to 1.25 billion) depending on the scale. For brokerage activities, a higher capital may be required to demonstrate financial stability. Some local practitioners recommend a minimum of EUR 20,000 to avoid scrutiny. Tax Treatment

Vietnam imposes corporate income tax (CIT) at a standard rate of 20%. Crypto transactions may be subject to value-added tax (VAT) at 10% if considered a service. Personal income tax applies to crypto gains for individuals, but specific guidance is still evolving. There are no special tax incentives for crypto brokers as of 2026. However, the government is considering tax breaks for technology companies in certain zones. Consulting24 recommends working with a local tax advisor to ensure compliance and optimize your tax structure.   • Provide OTC brokerage services
  • Facilitate crypto-to-fiat conversions (subject to SBV approval)
  • Offer custody services for digital assets
  • Develop and operate blockchain-based trading platforms
  • Provide crypto payment processing for merchants

Activities such as derivatives trading or security token offerings may require additional licenses from the Ministry of Finance. Step-by-Step Process

Obtaining a crypto broker license in Vietnam involves these steps:

  1. Company Incorporation: Register a limited liability company (LLC) with the Department of Planning and Investment. Choose a business line that covers technology services, such as "software development" or "IT consulting".
  2. Business License: Apply for an enterprise registration certificate. This may include a technology license if offering software platforms.
  3. AML Compliance: Develop internal AML/CTF policies, appoint a compliance officer, and register with the State Bank of Vietnam's AML department. This includes implementing KYC procedures and transaction monitoring systems.
  4. Bank Account: Open a corporate bank account in Vietnam. This can be challenging for crypto businesses; Consulting24 assists with introductions to banks that are more open to fintech clients.
  5. Ongoing Reporting: Submit periodic reports to the SBV and tax authorities. This includes suspicious transaction reports (STRs) and annual AML audits.

Banking & Payments

Opening a bank account for a crypto brokerage in Vietnam is difficult due to risk-averse local banks. Most crypto firms use international payment processors or partner with banks that have a digital asset focus. Some options include:

  • Local banks with fintech-friendly policies (e.g., TPBank, OCB)
  • Digital banks like Timo or Viet Capital Bank
  • International neobanks (e.g., Mercury, Brex) for USD accounts
  • Payment processors like Stripe or Payoneer for crypto-to-fiat conversions

Compliance & Trust

Compliance is critical in Vietnam. The government has increased scrutiny of crypto activities to prevent money laundering. Key requirements include:

  • Customer due diligence (CDD) and know-your-customer (KYC) procedures
  • Transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting
  • Record keeping for at least five years
  • Annual AML audits by an external auditor
  • Appointment of a local compliance officer

Common Mistakes

Avoid these pitfalls when applying for a crypto broker license in Vietnam:

  • Ignoring AML requirements: The SBV can impose heavy fines for non-compliance, including suspension of operations.
  • Using a generic business license: Ensure your business scope explicitly covers crypto activities; otherwise, you risk license revocation.
  • Underestimating timeline: Delays are common; plan for 6 months or more. Some applications take up to a year.
  • Neglecting tax obligations: Crypto profits must be declared; penalties apply for evasion, including back taxes and interest.
  • Choosing the wrong bank: Some banks close accounts without notice for crypto-related businesses. Verify the bank's policy beforehand.
