What Is a Ready Made Lithuania Crypto License?

A ready made Lithuania crypto license refers to a pre-registered company that already holds or is immediately eligible to apply for a Crypto-Asset Service Provider (CASP) registration under Lithuania's transposition of MiCA. The company is fully incorporated, with a registered address, nominee directors (if needed), and a bank account in progress. You acquire the company and then complete the licensing process with the Bank of Lithuania (Lietuvos bankas) as the regulator.

This approach saves 2-4 months compared to starting from scratch, as the corporate setup and initial documentation are already prepared. However, you still must pass the fit-and-proper checks, submit a detailed business plan, and meet capital requirements. Consulting24 advises on selecting the right shelf company and guides you through the licensing steps. For example, a shelf company typically has a clean history with no previous trading, reducing due diligence risks. The ready-made structure is particularly useful for founders who need to open a corporate bank account quickly or who want to start the licensing process without waiting for incorporation.

License Type and Regulator

The relevant license is the Crypto-Asset Service Provider (CASP) registration under the Law on Markets in Crypto-Assets, which implements MiCA. The regulator is the Bank of Lithuania (Lietuvos bankas), responsible for supervision and enforcement.

Under MiCA, CASPs are categorized by service class, each with specific capital requirements (see next section). The Bank of Lithuania assesses applications within 3-6 months after submission of a complete dossier. The license allows passporting to other EU member states without additional registration. This means a Lithuania-licensed CASP can offer services in Germany, France, or Spain simply by notifying the local regulator, a major advantage over national licenses. The Bank of Lithuania also publishes a public register of licensed CASPs, enhancing transparency and trust.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital for CASPs depends on the services offered:

  • Class 1 (e.g., order execution, placement): EUR 50,000
  • Class 2 (e.g., custody, transfer services): EUR 125,000
  • Class 3 (e.g., exchange, dealing on own account): EUR 150,000

These amounts must be maintained as own funds (equity). The Bank of Lithuania may require higher capital based on risk assessment. For a ready-made company, the share capital is typically paid in at incorporation, and you may need to top up to meet the class requirement. For instance, if the shelf company has a share capital of EUR 2,500, you must inject additional funds to reach the required threshold. The capital must be held in a bank account and cannot be used for operational expenses without regulatory approval. Consulting24 assists in structuring the capital injection and verifying compliance.

Tax Treatment

Lithuania offers a competitive corporate income tax rate of 15% (standard) or 0% for small companies (revenue under EUR 300,000 and fewer than 10 employees) for the first year, then 5% for the next two years. Crypto-to-crypto transactions are generally exempt from VAT. However, crypto-to-fiat conversions are subject to standard VAT rules.

Capital gains from crypto trading are treated as business income and taxed at the corporate rate. Lithuania has no withholding tax on dividends paid to EU residents. Transfer pricing rules apply to related-party transactions. Consulting24 recommends engaging a local tax advisor for your specific situation. For example, if your company earns revenue from trading fees and spreads, the effective tax rate after the small company relief can be as low as 5% for the first three years, making Lithuania one of the most tax-efficient EU jurisdictions for crypto businesses.

Allowed Activities

A Lithuania CASP license permits the following activities under MiCA:

  • Providing custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • Operating a trading platform for crypto-assets
  • Exchanging crypto-assets for fiat currency or other crypto-assets
  • Executing orders for crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • Placing crypto-assets (including ICOs)
  • Receiving and transmitting orders for crypto-assets
  • Providing advice on crypto-assets
  • Managing portfolios of crypto-assets

Activities not covered (e.g., DeFi protocol operation without intermediation) may fall outside CASP scope. The Bank of Lithuania provides guidance on a case-by-case basis. For instance, if you operate a non-custodial wallet that does not hold private keys, you may not need a CASP license, but you should seek legal confirmation. Compliance and Trust

Operating under a Lithuania CASP license requires ongoing compliance with AML/CFT regulations, including customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and reporting suspicious transactions to the Financial Crime Investigation Service (FNTT). Annual audits by an independent auditor are mandatory. The Bank of Lithuania conducts periodic inspections.

Consulting24 provides compliance advisory services, including policy drafting, training, and audit support. We emphasize that this is general guidance, not legal advice. Always consult a qualified lawyer for your specific obligations. For example, your AML policy must include a risk assessment that covers all products, customers, and geographic regions. The Bank of Lithuania expects policies to be updated at least annually. Non-compliance can result in fines of up to 5% of annual turnover or EUR 5 million, whichever is higher.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Underestimating capital requirements - ensure you have the full amount in liquid assets.
  • Incomplete AML documentation - the Bank of Lithuania is strict; missing policies cause delays.
  • Ignoring fit-and-proper checks - criminal records or poor credit history can disqualify directors.
  • Choosing the wrong shelf company - some ready-made companies have hidden liabilities; vet carefully.
  • Neglecting ongoing compliance - failure to submit reports can lead to license revocation.
