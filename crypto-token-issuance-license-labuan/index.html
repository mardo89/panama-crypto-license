What is the Labuan Crypto Token Issuance License?

The Labuan crypto token issuance license is a regulatory approval that permits a company incorporated in Labuan to issue digital tokens to investors. This license is essential for businesses planning to launch an ICO, STO, or any token-based fundraising within the Labuan IBFC. The Labuan FSA regulates token issuance under the Labuan Financial Services and Securities Act 2010, treating tokens as securities if they represent an investment contract.

Key features of the license include:

  • Legal certainty for token issuers under a recognised regulatory framework.
  • Access to a tax-efficient jurisdiction with a flat 3% corporate tax rate.
  • Ability to issue tokens to both retail and institutional investors, subject to compliance with Labuan's securities laws.
  • Exemption from withholding tax on dividends, interest, and royalties.
  • No stamp duty on instruments related to token issuance.

The license is ideal for projects that want a credible, regulated environment for token sales while maintaining operational flexibility. Labuan's regulatory approach is principles-based, allowing for innovation while ensuring investor protection.

Who Needs a Labuan Crypto Token Issuance License?

Any entity that plans to issue digital tokens to the public or to a group of investors within Labuan or targeting Labuan-based investors should obtain this license. This includes:

  • Startups conducting an initial coin offering (ICO) to raise capital.
  • Established companies issuing security tokens to represent equity, debt, or real-world assets.
  • Decentralised autonomous organisations (DAOs) that issue governance tokens.
  • Real estate or asset-backed tokenisation projects.
  • Fund managers issuing tokenised fund shares.

If your token issuance involves any form of investment contract where investors expect profits from the efforts of others, the Labuan FSA will likely classify the token as a security. In such cases, a token issuance license is mandatory. Even utility tokens may fall under the regulator's purview if they are marketed as investment opportunities.

License Type and Regulator

The Labuan crypto token issuance license is a specific authorisation under the Labuan Financial Services and Securities Act 2010 (LFSSA). The regulator is the Labuan Financial Services Authority (Labuan FSA), which oversees all financial services activities in the Labuan IBFC.

Under the LFSSA, token issuance is treated as a securities activity. The license allows the holder to:

  • Issue, offer, or market digital tokens to investors.
  • Operate a token issuance platform (if combined with a market operator license).
  • Provide token custody services (requires separate approval).

The Labuan FSA distinguishes between two main categories of tokens: securities tokens (which fall under the LFSSA) and non-securities tokens (utility tokens that do not represent an investment). For non-securities tokens, the Labuan FSA may still require a license if the token is offered to the public. Consulting24 advises on the correct classification for your token.

Capital Requirement

The Labuan FSA mandates a minimum paid-up capital of USD 50,000 for a token issuance license. This capital must be maintained in a Labuan bank account and cannot be withdrawn without regulatory approval. For more complex token structures or if the issuer plans to also provide custody or exchange services, the capital requirement may increase to USD 100,000 or more.

The capital serves as a financial buffer to ensure the issuer can meet its obligations to token holders. It must be fully paid up before the license is granted. Consulting24 advises on the exact capital amount required for your specific token issuance model.

Tax Treatment

Labuan offers a highly favourable tax regime for token issuers. The key tax benefits include:

  • Corporate tax rate: 3% of net audited profits (or a fixed rate of MYR 20,000 if profits are below MYR 3 million).
  • No withholding tax on dividends, interest, royalties, or technical fees paid to non-residents.
  • No capital gains tax on the sale of tokens or digital assets.
  • No stamp duty on instruments related to token issuance.
  • Exemption from Goods and Services Tax (GST) for most financial services.

Token issuers must maintain proper accounting records and file annual tax returns. The Labuan tax regime is designed to be transparent and compliant with international standards, including the OECD's Base Erosion and Profit Shifting (BEPS) framework. Allowed Activities

With a Labuan crypto token issuance license, you are permitted to:

  • Issue digital tokens, including security tokens, utility tokens, and asset-backed tokens.
  • Conduct initial coin offerings (ICOs) and security token offerings (STOs).
  • Market and promote token sales to investors, subject to compliance with Labuan's securities laws.
  • Manage the proceeds of token sales in accordance with the approved business plan.
  • Provide token redemption or buyback services (if specified in the token terms).
