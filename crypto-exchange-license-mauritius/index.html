What is the Mauritius Crypto Exchange License?

The Mauritius crypto exchange license is a regulatory authorization granted by the Financial Services Commission (FSC) under the VAITOS Act. It allows companies to operate a virtual asset exchange, provide custody services, and facilitate token offerings. The license is part of Mauritius's strategy to attract fintech innovation while ensuring anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CFT) compliance.

Key features include:

  • Full compliance with FATF recommendations for virtual assets.
  • Access to a well-regulated financial ecosystem with a growing pool of banking partners.
  • No capital gains tax on crypto trading profits for companies.
  • Flexible corporate structures with low minimum capital.

Compared to other jurisdictions, Mauritius offers a middle ground between strict EU regimes like Lithuania and more flexible offshore hubs like Panama. The FSC requires a physical presence, including a registered office and a local resident director, but the capital requirement is significantly lower than the EU's MiCA thresholds (EUR 50,000-150,000). For example, a startup exchange can launch with EUR 6,000 capital in Mauritius versus EUR 125,000 in Lithuania. The FSC also allows a wider range of activities under a single license, including custody and OTC services.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Financial Services Commission (FSC) of Mauritius. The license type is a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) license under the VAITOS Act. The FSC also regulates securities and insurance, providing a mature oversight framework.

There are two main categories:

  • Category 1 - Exchange and Custody: For operating a virtual asset exchange, providing custody, or facilitating trading.
  • Category 2 - Token Issuance: For conducting initial token offerings.

Most crypto exchange operators apply under Category 1. The FSC requires a detailed business plan, AML policies, and proof of adequate capital. The license is not a passport to other jurisdictions, but Mauritius has double taxation treaties with over 40 countries. The FSC conducts thorough due diligence on directors and shareholders, including background checks and source of funds verification. Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a Mauritius crypto exchange license is approximately EUR 6,000 (or equivalent in USD). This is significantly lower than EU MiCA requirements (EUR 50,000-150,000) and comparable to Panama (also EUR 6,000 flat). The capital must be maintained in a bank account in Mauritius and can be used for operational expenses.

There is no requirement to hold capital in crypto assets. The FSC may ask for higher capital depending on the business volume and risk profile. For example, if your projected trading volume exceeds EUR 10 million monthly, the FSC might require EUR 50,000 or more. Consulting24 advises on structuring your capital to meet FSC expectations. Tax Treatment

Mauritius offers a favorable tax regime for crypto businesses:

  • Corporate tax rate: 15% on net profits.
  • Partial exemption: Up to 80% exemption on certain types of income (e.g., foreign source income, trading profits) subject to conditions, effectively reducing the rate to 3%.
  • No capital gains tax on crypto trading profits for companies.
  • No withholding tax on dividends paid to non-residents.
  • VAT: 15% standard rate, but crypto transactions may be exempt depending on structure.

Mauritius has a network of double taxation treaties with over 40 countries, helping avoid double taxation. For instance, a company resident in Mauritius but earning income from clients in India may benefit from the India-Mauritius treaty. Allowed Activities Under the License

The Mauritius VASP license permits the following activities:

  • Operating a virtual asset exchange (order book, market making).
  • Providing custody services for virtual assets.
  • Facilitating the exchange of virtual assets for fiat or other virtual assets.
  • Conducting initial token offerings (ITOs) under Category 2.
  • Providing advisory services related to virtual assets.

Activities that are not allowed without additional licensing include securities trading (covered by the Securities Act) and banking services. The FSC prohibits anonymous transactions and requires KYC/AML procedures. For example, if your platform offers margin trading with use, the FSC may classify that as a securities activity. Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make these errors when applying for a Mauritius crypto license:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing AML policies or business plan details can delay the application. Ensure you include a detailed risk assessment.
  • Underestimating timeline: The 4-6 month process requires patience; rushing leads to mistakes. Start early and plan for contingencies.

  • Inadequate capital: While minimum is EUR 6,000, the FSC may require more based on business volume. Prepare a realistic budget.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Mauritius crypto exchange license?

The minimum capital is approximately EUR 6,000 (or equivalent in USD). This is significantly lower than EU MiCA requirements. The FSC may require higher capital based on business volume, but the base is accessible for startups.

How long does it take to get a Mauritius crypto license?

The process typically takes 4 to 6 months from application submission to approval. This includes document preparation, FSC due diligence, and license issuance. Consulting24 helps expedite the process by ensuring all documents are complete.

What taxes apply to crypto businesses in Mauritius?

Corporate tax is 15% with potential partial exemption up to 80%, effectively reducing the rate to 3%. There is no capital gains tax on crypto trading profits and no withholding tax on dividends. VAT is 15% but crypto transactions may be exempt.

Can I use the Mauritius license to serve clients in the EU?

No, the Mauritius license does not provide EU passporting. To serve EU clients, you need a license under MiCA in an EU member state. However, Mauritius is suitable for serving clients in Africa, Asia, and other regions.

What activities are allowed under the Mauritius VASP license?

The license allows operating a virtual asset exchange, custody services, OTC trading, and token issuance (under Category 2). It does not cover securities trading or banking services. All activities must comply with AML/CFT requirements.

Do I need a local director in Mauritius?

Yes, the FSC requires at least one resident director. This can be a nominee director provided by a service provider. Consulting24 can help arrange a compliant nominee director.

Is it difficult to open a bank account for a crypto exchange in Mauritius?

It is moderately difficult. Major banks accept licensed crypto businesses but require thorough due diligence. Consulting24 assists with documentation and bank introductions to improve success rates.

How does Mauritius compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Both have low capital requirements (EUR 6,000). Panama is faster (2-3 months) and has no corporate tax on foreign income, but lacks a specific crypto law. Mauritius offers more regulatory clarity and a stronger reputation. The choice depends on your need for regulation versus speed.

Can I convert my Mauritius license to an EU license later?

No direct conversion. You would need to apply for a new license in an EU jurisdiction. However, having a Mauritius license can demonstrate regulatory compliance, which may facilitate the EU application process.

What ongoing compliance is required?

Annual AML/CFT reports, audited financial statements, transaction monitoring, and appointment of a compliance officer. The FSC may conduct inspections. Consulting24 offers ongoing compliance support to ensure you meet all requirements.
