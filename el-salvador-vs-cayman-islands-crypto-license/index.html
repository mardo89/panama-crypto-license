What Is the El Salvador vs Cayman Islands Crypto License Comparison?

This comparison examines two distinct regulatory environments for crypto businesses. El Salvador, through the Comision Nacional de Activos Digitales (CNAD), issues a Digital Asset Service Provider (DASP) license. The Cayman Islands, under the Cayman Islands Monetary Authority (CIMA), requires a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) registration. Both are designed to bring crypto businesses under regulatory oversight, but the requirements, costs, and operational freedoms vary. Understanding these differences is essential for founders seeking a jurisdiction that aligns with their business model, budget, and risk tolerance.

El Salvador's DASP license was introduced under the Digital Assets Issuance Law (Ley de Emision de Activos Digitales) in 2023, making it one of the first comprehensive digital asset frameworks globally. The Cayman Islands' VASP regime, effective since 2020, is part of the Virtual Asset (Service Providers) Act and aligns with FATF recommendations. Both regulators require strong AML/CFT programs, but the scope of regulated activities differs. El Salvador's license covers a broader spectrum, including token issuance and DeFi services, while the Cayman Islands focuses on exchange, custody, and transfer services. Capital Requirement

Capital requirements differ significantly between the two jurisdictions. El Salvador does not impose a statutory minimum capital for DASP licenses, but the CNAD expects applicants to demonstrate sufficient financial resources to operate. In practice, a minimum of USD 50,000 is often recommended to cover operational costs and show financial stability. The Cayman Islands requires a minimum capital of USD 100,000 for VASP registration, which must be maintained at all times. This capital can be in the form of cash or liquid assets, and proof of source of funds is required.

It is important to note that capital requirements are subject to change and may vary based on the specific activities proposed. For example, a custody service may require higher capital due to greater risk. Consulting24 advises clients on the appropriate capital structure for their application. Tax Treatment

Tax is a major consideration for crypto businesses. El Salvador offers a favourable tax regime: no capital gains tax on Bitcoin (as legal tender), and a 0% tax on digital asset transactions for non-residents. Corporate income tax is 25% for local companies, but exemptions may apply for certain activities, such as those related to Bitcoin mining or token issuance. The Cayman Islands imposes no direct taxes - no corporate income tax, no capital gains tax, no withholding tax - making it a pure tax-neutral jurisdiction. However, the Cayman Islands does have annual registration fees (typically USD 3,000 to 5,000) and a stamp duty on certain documents.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Both El Salvador and the Cayman Islands require ongoing compliance with AML/CFT regulations. This includes:

  • Appointment of a local AML compliance officer (can be outsourced in El Salvador, must be local in Cayman Islands)
  • Submission of periodic reports to the regulator (e.g., quarterly for El Salvador, annually for Cayman Islands)
  • Maintenance of transaction records for at least five years
  • Conducting regular audits (annual audit for Cayman Islands, biennial for El Salvador)
  • Notification of any material changes to the business, such as change of directors or shareholding
  • Filing of annual returns and payment of annual license fees (typically USD 5,000 for El Salvador, USD 10,000 for Cayman Islands)
