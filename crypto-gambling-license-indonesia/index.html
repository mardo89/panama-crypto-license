Overview: What Is a Crypto Gambling License for Indonesia?

A crypto gambling license for Indonesia is not a single permit issued by Indonesian authorities. Instead, it refers to an offshore license (typically from jurisdictions like Panama, Curacao, or Estonia) that allows a company to legally operate a crypto casino or sportsbook targeting Indonesian users. Indonesian law prohibits all forms of online gambling, including crypto gambling, under the Electronic Information and Transactions Law (UU ITE). However, foreign-licensed operators can legally offer services to Indonesian players as long as they do not have a physical presence in Indonesia and comply with their licensing jurisdiction's rules.

Consulting24 specializes in helping clients obtain the Panama crypto license, which is a popular choice due to its low cost (EUR 6,000 flat), no minimum capital requirement, and favorable tax regime. Other options include licenses from Curacao, Malta, or Estonia, but each has different costs and compliance burdens. For example, Estonia requires a physical office and a minimum capital of EUR 50,000, making it less accessible for startups. Panama, on the other hand, allows you to operate remotely with minimal overhead.

License Type & Regulator

There is no single "crypto gambling license" in Indonesia. Instead, businesses use offshore licenses. The most common regulators for crypto gambling include:

  • Panama: Regulated by the Ministry of Commerce and Industries (MICI). License type: Gaming License (Class A or B). No specific crypto regulation, but crypto-friendly. The corporate license (Sociedad Anónima) suffices for gambling activities if the articles of association permit it.
  • Curacao: Regulated by the Curacao Gaming Control Board (GCB). License type: Master License or Sub-License. Widely accepted, but undergoing reforms that may increase costs in 2026.
  • Estonia: Regulated by the Estonian Tax and Customs Board (EMTA) under the Gambling Act. License type: Online Gambling License. Requires physical presence and a local director.

For most clients, Consulting24 recommends Panama due to its simplicity and low cost. The Panama license is a general corporate license that covers gambling activities, including crypto, as long as the company's articles of association allow it. No separate gambling license application is needed; the company incorporation itself grants the right to operate.

Capital Requirement

Capital requirements differ by jurisdiction:

  • Panama: No minimum capital requirement. You can start with as little as EUR 10,000 in paid-up capital, but the law does not mandate a specific amount. Most operators use a nominal capital of USD 10,000.
  • Curacao: No statutory minimum capital, but operators typically show at least EUR 50,000 in liquid assets to satisfy the GCB. This can be in the form of a bank statement or a letter of credit.
  • Estonia: Minimum share capital of EUR 50,000 for an online gambling license, fully paid up before operation. This amount must be deposited in an Estonian bank account.

Panama's lack of capital requirement makes it ideal for startups. Consulting24 can help you structure the company to meet any practical requirements, such as showing sufficient funds for operational expenses.

Tax Treatment

Tax treatment is a key factor in choosing a jurisdiction:

  • Panama: Territorial taxation - only income sourced in Panama is taxed. Offshore gambling income is tax-free. No corporate income tax on foreign-source revenue. Annual franchise tax of USD 300.
  • Curacao: Corporate tax rate of 22%, but gambling companies often qualify for reduced rates (2-5%) under certain conditions, such as being an e-zone company.
  • Estonia: Corporate income tax of 20% on distributed profits (not on retained earnings). Gambling license holders also pay a gambling tax of 5% on gross gaming revenue (GGR). This can be a significant ongoing cost.

Panama offers the most favorable tax regime for crypto gambling operations targeting Indonesia, as you pay zero tax on profits if the revenue is sourced outside Panama. This means your entire gross gaming revenue (minus operational costs) can be retained without tax leakage.
