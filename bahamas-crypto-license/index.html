What Is a Bahamas Crypto License?

A Bahamas crypto license, officially a Digital Asset Business License under the DARE Act 2024, authorises companies to conduct activities such as operating a digital asset exchange, providing custodial wallet services, trading digital assets, and issuing tokens. The Securities Commission of The Bahamas is the sole regulator, ensuring compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorist financing (CTF) standards.

The DARE Act replaced earlier frameworks to align with international standards, including FATF recommendations. It distinguishes between different classes of license depending on the scope of activities. The Bahamas is not part of the EU, so MiCA does not apply, but the jurisdiction maintains strong oversight to attract serious operators.

Key features include a clear application process, fit and proper requirements for directors and shareholders, and ongoing reporting obligations. The license is valid for one year and must be renewed annually.

Who Needs a Bahamas Crypto License?

Any business that provides digital asset services to customers in or from the Bahamas must hold a license. This includes:

  • Digital asset exchanges (centralised and decentralised)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Digital asset trading platforms
  • Token issuers (including ICOs and STOs)
  • Payment processors using digital assets
  • Digital asset fund managers

If your target market includes high-net-worth individuals in the Caribbean, Latin America, or globally, the Bahamas offers a reputable, tax-efficient base. The license is also suitable for startups and established firms seeking a second license to complement an EU or Asian registration.

License Type and Regulator

The Securities Commission of The Bahamas is the regulatory authority. Under the DARE Act, there is a single license category - the Digital Asset Business License - but the Commission may impose conditions based on the applicant's business model. There is no formal tier system like in Lithuania or Estonia, but the scope of activities determines the level of scrutiny.

The regulator expects applicants to demonstrate strong governance, a clear business plan, adequate capital, and AML/CTF controls. The Commission also requires that at least one director be a resident of the Bahamas, or the company appoint a local registered agent and maintain a physical office.

Capital Requirement

The DARE Act does not prescribe a fixed minimum capital requirement in the legislation. Instead, the Securities Commission determines capital adequacy on a case-by-case basis, considering the volume of transactions, number of customers, and risk profile of the business. In practice, applicants are expected to maintain sufficient capital to cover operational expenses and potential liabilities.

Typical capital expectations range from USD 100,000 to USD 500,000, but this is not a statutory minimum. The Commission may require a higher amount for exchange platforms handling significant trading volumes. Tax Treatment in the Bahamas

The Bahamas is a zero-tax jurisdiction. There is no corporate income tax, no capital gains tax, no withholding tax, and no VAT (Value Added Tax) on digital asset services. The government generates revenue through license fees and other charges, not through income taxation.

This tax neutrality makes the Bahamas highly attractive for crypto businesses that want to minimise their global tax burden. However, founders must consider their home country tax obligations and any controlled foreign corporation (CFC) rules. The Bahamas does not have tax treaties with most countries, so double taxation relief is limited. Consulting24 recommends consulting with a tax advisor in your home jurisdiction.

Allowed Activities Under the License

A Bahamas digital asset business license permits the following activities:

  • Operating a digital asset exchange (order book, matching engine, OTC desk)
  • Providing custodial wallet services for digital assets
  • Digital asset trading as principal or agent
  • Issuing digital assets (tokens, stablecoins, security tokens)
  • Digital asset payment services (merchant processing, remittance)
  • Digital asset fund management (if structured as a fund)

Activities not covered include derivatives trading (unless separately licensed) and banking services. The Commission may impose restrictions on the types of digital assets that can be listed, requiring due diligence on each token.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

After receiving the license, the company must comply with ongoing requirements:

  • Annual renewal of the license (fee payable each year).
  • Submission of audited financial statements within 9 months of year-end.
  • AML/CTF reporting, including suspicious transaction reports (STRs).
  • Maintenance of a physical office in the Bahamas (or a registered agent with a local presence).
  • Appointment of a compliance officer (may be outsourced).
  • Regular reporting to the Securities Commission on business activities.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make errors that delay or derail their application. Here are the most common pitfalls:

  • Inadequate business plan: The Commission expects a detailed plan with financial projections and risk analysis.
  • Weak AML/CTF policies: Generic templates are not accepted; policies must be tailored to the business.
  • Unclear source of funds: The regulator will scrutinise the background of shareholders and directors.
  • Underestimating capital needs: Arriving with insufficient capital can lead to rejection.
  • Ignoring local presence requirements: A registered office alone may not suffice; a physical office or director residency may be required.

Frequently asked questions

What is the DARE Act 2024?

The Digital Assets and Registered Exchanges (DARE) Act 2024 is the primary legislation governing digital asset businesses in the Bahamas. It replaced earlier regulations to create a comprehensive framework for licensing, supervision, and consumer protection, aligning with FATF standards.

Is a Bahamas crypto license recognized internationally?

The license is issued by the Securities Commission of The Bahamas, a member of the International Organization of Securities Commissions (IOSCO). While not automatically recognised in other countries, it demonstrates compliance with international AML/CTF standards, which helps with bank relationships and partner due diligence.

Can I operate globally with a Bahamas license?

The license permits services to customers in the Bahamas and, depending on the regulator's conditions, to international clients. However, you must comply with the laws of each country where your customers reside. Many firms use the Bahamas license as a base for global operations, supplementing with local registrations where needed.

What are the director requirements?

The Securities Commission requires that at least one director be a resident of the Bahamas, or the company must appoint a local registered agent and maintain a physical office. All directors must pass fit and proper checks. Consulting24 can help you find a qualified local director.

How long does the application process take?

The typical timeline is 3 to 6 months from submission to approval. The Commission aims to process complete applications within 90 days, but complex cases or requests for additional information can extend the timeline. Consulting24 streamlines the process to minimise delays.

What is the minimum capital required?

The DARE Act does not set a fixed minimum capital. The Commission determines capital adequacy based on the business model and risk profile. In practice, applicants should expect to demonstrate capital of at least USD 100,000, though higher amounts may be required for larger operations.

Can I apply for a Bahamas crypto license if I already have one in another country?

Yes, many firms hold multiple licenses to expand their geographic reach. The Bahamas is often used as a second license for tax optimisation and regulatory diversification. Consulting24 can help you manage multi-jurisdictional licensing strategies.

What ongoing costs should I expect?

Annual license fees range from USD 10,000 to USD 25,000, plus costs for compliance officer, audit, legal, and registered office services. Total annual compliance costs typically range from USD 20,000 to USD 50,000, depending on the complexity of the business.

Does the Bahamas have a tax treaty network?

The Bahamas has limited tax treaties. It has signed a few bilateral agreements for exchange of information but no comprehensive double taxation treaties. This means you may not get foreign tax credits, so consult a tax advisor for your specific situation.

What happens if I don't comply with ongoing obligations?

Non-compliance can result in fines, suspension of the license, or revocation. The Securities Commission takes enforcement seriously. Consulting24 offers ongoing compliance support to help you meet all reporting and regulatory requirements.
