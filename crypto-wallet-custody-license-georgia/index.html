What Is the Crypto Wallet Custody License in Georgia?

The crypto wallet custody license in Georgia is a regulatory authorization issued by the National Bank of Georgia (NBG) under the Law on Combating Money Laundering and Terrorism Financing. It classifies crypto wallet custodians as Virtual Asset Service Providers (VASPs). This license permits companies to offer custody services for cryptographic assets, including holding private keys, storing digital assets on behalf of clients, and providing wallet management.

Georgia's framework is designed to foster innovation while ensuring compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) obligations. Unlike some jurisdictions that only register entities, Georgia requires a full licensing process with ongoing supervision. The license is essential for any business that wants to legally operate a custodial wallet service in or from Georgia.

Key features of the license include:

  • Regulated by the NBG, the central bank.
  • Valid for an indefinite period, subject to annual compliance reviews.
  • Covers both hot and cold wallet custody.
  • Requires a minimum capital of 350,000 GEL (approximately EUR 110,000 as of 2026).
  • Corporate income tax of 15% on distributed profits only.

Who Needs This License?

Any business entity that intends to provide custodial wallet services for cryptocurrencies in Georgia must obtain this license. This includes startups, established fintech firms, and international companies looking to set up a regulated presence in the Caucasus region. Typical applicants include:

  • Crypto wallet providers (mobile, web, hardware).
  • Exchanges that offer integrated custody.
  • Asset managers holding client funds.
  • White-label custody solution providers.
  • Banks or financial institutions expanding into digital assets.

If your business involves holding private keys or controlling crypto assets on behalf of others, you likely fall under the NBG's definition of a custodian. Even if you only provide non-custodial wallets, you may still need to register if you facilitate transfers or exchange services. License Type and Regulator

The license is categorized as a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) registration, specifically for custodial wallet services. The regulator is the National Bank of Georgia (NBG), which oversees all VASPs in the country. The NBG sets the rules for AML/CTF compliance, capital adequacy, and operational standards.

Unlike some European jurisdictions that have separate licenses for exchanges and custodians, Georgia combines them under the VASP umbrella. However, the NBG distinguishes between different service types, and a custody-only license may have slightly different requirements than a full exchange license. The minimum capital for custody is 350,000 GEL (around EUR 110,000), while exchange services may require higher capital.

For comparison, Lithuania's crypto license (under the Financial Crime Investigation Service) requires EUR 125,000 for custody, and Panama has no specific crypto license but uses a general business license. Georgia's capital requirement is moderate, making it attractive for mid-sized operations.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a crypto wallet custody license in Georgia is 350,000 GEL (approximately EUR 110,000 as of 2026). This capital must be paid up before the license is issued and maintained throughout the operation. The NBG may require higher capital if the business handles large volumes or offers additional services like exchange or transfer.

This capital can be in the form of cash or liquid assets. It serves as a buffer to cover operational risks and client protection. For comparison, Lithuania requires EUR 125,000 for custody, while Estonia's old regime had no minimum (but new MiCA rules now apply). Panama has no capital requirement for crypto businesses. Tax Treatment

Georgia offers a favorable tax regime for crypto businesses. The corporate income tax is 15%, but only on distributed profits (i.e., dividends). Retained earnings are not taxed until they are distributed. This makes Georgia a tax-efficient jurisdiction for reinvesting profits.

Value-added tax (VAT) is 18%, but crypto custody services are often exempt from VAT as financial services. Personal income tax is a flat 20%. There are no specific crypto taxes beyond the general corporate framework. However, the NBG requires that all transactions be recorded and reported for AML purposes.

Compared to Panama (0% corporate tax on offshore income, but no specific crypto license), Georgia provides a regulated environment with a clear tax treatment. Lithuania taxes corporate profits at 15% (standard rate) and applies VAT to crypto services. Consulting24 can provide detailed tax planning during your consultation.

Allowed Activities

Under the crypto wallet custody license in Georgia, the following activities are permitted:

  • Safekeeping and administration of cryptographic assets on behalf of clients.
  • Generation and management of private keys.
  • Provision of custodial wallet services (hot and cold storage).
  • Execution of client instructions for transfers between wallets.
  • Integration with third-party platforms for trading or staking (with additional approvals).

Activities not covered include operating a crypto exchange (requires separate VASP registration), issuing stablecoins, or providing investment advice. However, the NBG allows bundled services under a single VASP license if all activities are disclosed. For pure custody, this license is sufficient. If you plan to offer exchange services as well, you may need a combined license with higher capital.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a crypto wallet custody license in Georgia involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step:

  1. Company incorporation: Register a limited liability company (LLC) in Georgia with a registered address and director. This takes 1-2 weeks.
  2. AML/KYC policy preparation: Develop internal policies, risk assessments, and procedures aligned with NBG requirements.
  3. Appoint a compliance officer: Designate a local or remote compliance officer with relevant experience.
  4. Capital deposit: Deposit the minimum capital of 350,000 GEL into a Georgian bank account.
  5. Submit application: File the application with the NBG, including business plan, policies, and supporting documents.
  6. NBG review: The NBG reviews the application, which may include interviews and requests for additional information. This takes 2-4 months.
  7. License issuance: Upon approval, the license is issued. The company must then maintain ongoing compliance.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto wallet custody license in Georgia?

The minimum capital is 350,000 GEL (approximately EUR 110,000 as of 2026). This must be paid up before license issuance and maintained throughout operations.

How long does it take to get a Georgia crypto custody license?

The typical timeline is 3 to 6 months from company incorporation to license approval. This includes document preparation, NBG review, and any follow-up requests.

What activities are allowed under the Georgia custody license?

The license permits safekeeping and administration of crypto assets, private key management, custodial wallet services, and execution of client transfer instructions. Exchange services require separate registration.

What is the tax rate for crypto custody businesses in Georgia?

Corporate income tax is 15% on distributed profits only. Retained earnings are not taxed. VAT is 18% but custody services are often exempt as financial services.

Can a foreign company apply for a Georgia crypto license?

Yes, but you must first incorporate a Georgian LLC. There is no residency requirement for directors, but a local registered address is needed. Consulting24 can assist with company setup.

Does Georgia accept crypto as payment for license fees?

No, all fees to the NBG and government must be paid in GEL or major fiat currencies. Crypto payments are not accepted for regulatory fees.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations?

Licensees must conduct annual AML audits, submit suspicious activity reports, maintain transaction records, and undergo NBG inspections. Non-compliance can lead to fines or revocation.

How does Georgia compare to Panama for crypto custody?

Panama has no specific crypto license and no capital requirement, with a flat EUR 6,000 company setup. Georgia offers a regulated framework with legal certainty, but at a higher cost (EUR 8,500-15,500) and capital requirement.

Can I offer staking or lending under the Georgia custody license?

Staking and lending are not automatically covered. You may need additional approvals or a separate license. The NBG evaluates each service on a case-by-case basis.
