What is the Italy Crypto License?

The Italy crypto license under MiCA is a mandatory CASP registration for businesses that exchange, store, transfer, or provide custody of crypto assets. It replaces the previous VASP regime and aligns with EU-wide standards. The license is issued by the OAM, which also oversees anti-money laundering compliance. Firms must be established in Italy or have a branch there. The license covers multiple service categories, each with specific capital requirements.

Key features include a maximum of 12 months for application processing, a minimum capital of EUR 50,000 for basic services, and a flat corporate tax rate of 26% on profits. The regime also requires compliance with the Travel Regulation (TRF) and strict AML/KYC procedures. Italy's approach under MiCA is considered thorough, with the OAM conducting background checks on all beneficial owners and senior management. This ensures only well-structured firms enter the market.

For founders considering multiple EU markets, the Italy license allows passporting across the EU, but the application process is more demanding than in some smaller jurisdictions. Who Needs an Italy Crypto License?

Any business offering the following services to Italian residents must hold a CASP license:

  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets
  • Operation of a trading platform
  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Transfer of crypto assets
  • Provision of financial advice related to crypto assets
  • Underwriting and placing of crypto assets

Foreign firms can serve Italian clients only if they have a branch in Italy or are registered under MiCA's passporting rules. Non-EU firms must establish a physical presence in Italy. This includes having a registered office and appointing local directors or managers. The OAM expects at least one senior manager to be based in Italy.

Startups and established exchanges alike must comply. Even if you only offer peer-to-peer services or a non-custodial wallet, you may fall under the definition of a CASP if you facilitate transactions. License Type and Regulator

The license is a single CASP registration under MiCA, supervised by the Organismo Agenti e Mediatori (OAM). The OAM is the Italian authority for financial intermediaries and crypto service providers. It handles registration, ongoing compliance, and enforcement. The Bank of Italy and CONSOB also have oversight roles for prudential and market conduct matters.

There is no separate license for different services; instead, the registration covers all permitted activities, but capital requirements vary by service class. The license is valid across the EU under MiCA passporting, allowing Italian CASPs to serve clients in other member states without additional registration. However, you must notify the OAM of your intention to passport and comply with host state rules.

The OAM maintains a public register of licensed CASPs. As of 2026, the register includes both Italian firms and EU branches. Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital for Italian CASPs depends on the types of services provided:

  • Class 1: Basic services (e.g., exchange, transfer) - EUR 50,000
  • Class 2: Custody and administration - EUR 125,000
  • Class 3: Trading platform or combined services - EUR 150,000

Capital must be held in liquid assets and maintained at all times. The OAM may require higher capital based on risk assessment. This is a key difference from pre-MiCA regimes where no minimum capital was required. The capital can be in the form of cash or cash equivalents, and must be deposited in a blocked account until the license is granted.

For firms offering multiple service classes, the highest applicable capital applies. Tax Treatment

Corporate income tax (IRES) is 24%, plus regional tax (IRAP) of about 3.9%, making the effective rate around 26-28%. Capital gains on crypto assets held by businesses are taxed as ordinary income. Individuals may be subject to a 26% capital gains tax on crypto profits exceeding EUR 2,000 per year. VAT is not applicable on crypto transactions under current rules.

Italy has no specific crypto tax incentives, but losses can offset gains. Transfer pricing rules apply to related-party transactions. Consulting24 can coordinate with Italian tax advisors for precise structuring. For example, if your firm holds crypto as inventory, gains are taxed at the corporate rate. If you trade on your own account, the tax treatment may differ.

Allowed Activities

An Italian CASP license permits the following activities under MiCA:

  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat or other crypto assets
  • Operation of a trading platform (order matching)
  • Custody and administration of crypto assets
  • Transfer of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Execution of orders on behalf of clients
  • Placement of crypto assets
  • Receiving and transmitting orders
  • Providing advice on crypto assets
  • Portfolio management of crypto assets

Stablecoin issuance and decentralized finance (DeFi) services may require additional authorization under MiCA's asset-referenced token (ART) or e-money token (EMT) regimes. The OAM provides guidance on scope. For instance, if you plan to issue a stablecoin pegged to the euro, you need an EMT license from the Bank of Italy.

Activities not covered include mining, non-custodial wallet software (if no control over funds), and pure education. Consulting24 can help you map your business model to the correct license category. Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Pre-assessment: Consulting24 reviews your business model and determines the required capital and documentation.
  2. Company setup: Incorporate an Italian company (SRL or SPA) with registered office in Italy. Minimum share capital of EUR 10,000 for SRL.
  3. Documentation: Prepare AML/KYC policies, business plan, risk assessment, governance structure, and IT security measures. These must be detailed and tailored to your operations.
  4. Capital deposit: Deposit the required minimum capital in a blocked bank account. Provide proof to OAM.
  5. Submission: File the application with OAM via the online portal, including all documents and fees. The portal requires digital signatures.
  6. Review: OAM reviews the application, may request additional information, and conducts interviews with management. Expect at least one round of questions.
  7. Approval: License granted within 6-12 months. You can start operations immediately after registration.
  8. Ongoing compliance: Submit periodic reports, maintain AML procedures, and pay annual supervision fees. OAM may conduct on-site inspections.
