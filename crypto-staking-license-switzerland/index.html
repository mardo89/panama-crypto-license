What is a Crypto Staking License in Switzerland?

A crypto staking license in Switzerland refers to the authorisation required to operate a staking service for clients. In practice, FINMA regulates staking under existing financial market laws. If you offer staking as a service where you pool client tokens and stake them on their behalf, you are likely conducting custody business (under the Banking Act or FinIA) and possibly asset management. The key is whether you have discretion over the staked assets. If you do, you need a licence as a securities firm or a bank (for larger operations) or a simpler registration as a financial intermediary under the AMLA if you only provide custody without discretion.

As of 2026, FINMA has clarified that staking of crypto assets is considered a custody activity if the staker retains ownership. The licensed entity must hold client assets in segregated accounts and comply with capital adequacy rules. The regime is principles-based, allowing for innovation while ensuring investor protection. Who Needs a Crypto Staking License in Switzerland?

Any company that offers staking services to third parties in or from Switzerland needs to assess whether it requires a licence. The following scenarios typically trigger licensing:

  • You accept client tokens for staking and have discretion over which validators to use or when to stake.
  • You pool client assets with your own or other clients' assets for staking.
  • You offer staking as part of a broader custody or asset management service.
  • You hold client private keys and execute staking transactions on their behalf.

If you merely provide a non-custodial staking interface (e.g., a wallet where users stake directly without your involvement), you may not need a licence. However, any form of intermediation or discretion brings you under FINMA's oversight. Consulting24 recommends a preliminary assessment to determine your exact licensing obligations.

License Type and Regulator

The primary regulator is the Swiss Financial Market Supervisory Authority (FINMA). Depending on the nature and scale of your staking business, you may need one of the following:

ActivityLicence Type
Non-discretionary custody of client staking assetsRegistration as a financial intermediary under AMLA (no prudential supervision)
Discretionary staking management (pooling, choosing validators)Securities firm licence (FinIA) or bank licence (Banking Act) depending on volume
Operating a staking pool as a collective investment schemeCollective investment scheme licence (CISA)

FINMA assesses each application on a case-by-case basis. The most common route for staking startups is the FinIA securities firm licence, which requires a minimum capital of CHF 100,000 (approximately EUR 100,000) and a simple organisational structure. For larger operations, a bank licence may be needed with capital of at least CHF 10 million.

Capital Requirement

For a securities firm licence under FinIA, the minimum capital is CHF 100,000 (approximately EUR 100,000). This capital must be fully paid up and maintained at all times. For a bank licence, the requirement is at least CHF 10 million. The capital must be held in liquid assets and cannot be used for operational expenses. FINMA also requires that the capital is sufficient to cover operational risks, including custody and staking risks. Tax Treatment of Staking Rewards

Switzerland has a clear tax framework for crypto staking. Staking rewards are treated as income at the time they are received (i.e., when the tokens are credited to your wallet). For individuals, this is taxed as ordinary income. For companies, staking rewards are part of corporate income and subject to corporate income tax (effective rates vary by canton, typically 12-20%). Capital gains from selling staked assets are generally tax-free for individuals, but for businesses, they are part of ordinary income. VAT is not applicable on staking services as they are considered financial services. Consulting24 advises on tax structuring to optimise your position.

Allowed Activities Under a Swiss Staking License

A Swiss staking licence (securities firm) permits the following activities:

  • Accepting client crypto assets for custody and staking.
  • Executing staking transactions on proof-of-stake blockchains (e.g., Ethereum, Solana, Polkadot).
  • Pooling client assets for staking (with appropriate disclosure).
  • Distributing staking rewards to clients (minus fees).
  • Providing related services such as reporting and tax documentation.

Activities not allowed without additional licences include operating a crypto exchange, lending staked assets, or offering staking as part of a collective investment scheme. FINMA strictly separates custody/management from proprietary trading. Consulting24 can help define your activity scope precisely.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a crypto staking license in Switzerland involves several stages:

  1. Pre-assessment: Consulting24 evaluates your business model to determine the appropriate licence type.
  2. Company setup: Incorporate a Swiss company (AG or GmbH) with a registered office and local directors.
  3. Documentation: Prepare a business plan, risk management policies, AML/CFT procedures, and organisational regulations.
  4. Capital deposit: Deposit the minimum capital in a Swiss bank account (blocked until licence approval).
  5. Submission: Submit the application to FINMA with all required documents and pay the fee.
  6. Review: FINMA reviews the application, may request additional information, and conducts a fit-and-proper test on key individuals.
  7. Approval: Once approved, you receive the licence and can start operations. Ongoing reporting to FINMA is required.

Benefits of a Swiss Staking License

Switzerland offers several advantages for staking businesses:

  • Legal certainty: FINMA's clear guidance on staking reduces regulatory risk.
  • Tax clarity: Staking rewards are taxed only upon receipt, and capital gains are tax-free for individuals.
  • Reputation: A Swiss licence signals trustworthiness to clients and partners globally.
  • Access to talent: Switzerland has a deep pool of blockchain and finance professionals.

  • No EU MiCA overlay: Switzerland is not an EU member, so MiCA rules do not apply, offering flexibility.

These benefits make Switzerland a top choice for staking operators, despite higher costs compared to jurisdictions like Panama or Lithuania.

Compliance and Trust Considerations

Operating a staking service in Switzerland requires strong compliance. Key areas include:

  • AML/CFT: Implement customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and reporting to the Money Laundering Reporting Office (MROS).
  • Data protection: Comply with the Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection (FADP).
  • Segregation of assets: Client staked assets must be held separately from the firm's own assets.
  • Risk management: Have policies for slashing risks, validator failure, and cybersecurity.
  • Reporting: Submit annual audited financial statements and periodic reports to FINMA.

Common Mistakes in the Application Process

Many applicants encounter pitfalls that delay or derail their licence. Common mistakes include:

  • Underestimating the fit-and-proper test: FINMA scrutinises the background of directors and shareholders.
  • Insufficient business plan: The plan must detail staking operations, risk management, and financial projections.
  • Ignoring AML requirements: Even for small operations, AML policies are mandatory.
  • Choosing the wrong licence type: Applying for a bank licence when a securities firm licence suffices wastes time and capital.
  • Incomplete documentation: Missing organisational regulations or capital proof leads to rejection.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are answers to common questions about crypto staking licenses in Switzerland.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a licence to stake my own crypto assets in Switzerland?

No. Staking your own assets does not require a licence. Licensing is only triggered when you offer staking services to third parties, especially if you have discretion over their assets.

What is the minimum capital for a Swiss staking licence?

For a securities firm licence under FinIA, the minimum capital is CHF 100,000. For a bank licence, it is CHF 10 million. The exact amount depends on your business model and risk profile.

How long does it take to get a staking licence in Switzerland?

The process typically takes 6 to 12 months from preparation to approval. A lighter AMLA registration can be obtained in 2 to 4 months. Consulting24 can help expedite the process.

Can a non-Swiss company obtain a Swiss staking licence?

Yes, but you must incorporate a Swiss subsidiary (AG or GmbH) with a local registered office and directors. FINMA requires the licensed entity to be based in Switzerland.

What are the ongoing compliance costs after obtaining the licence?

Annual costs include FINMA supervision fees, audit fees, and compliance officer salaries. These typically range from CHF 15,000 to CHF 40,000 per year, depending on the complexity.

Is staking income taxed in Switzerland?

Yes. Staking rewards are treated as income at the time of receipt and are subject to corporate or individual income tax. Capital gains from selling staked assets are generally tax-free for individuals.

Can I offer staking services to EU clients with a Swiss licence?

Yes, but you must comply with EU regulations if you actively market to EU residents. MiCA may require a separate licence or passporting. Consulting24 can advise on cross-border compliance.

What happens if I operate without a licence?

Operating without a required licence is a criminal offence in Switzerland. FINMA can impose fines, order cessation of business, and refer for prosecution. Always seek legal advice before starting.

Does Switzerland recognise staking as a financial service under MiCA?

Switzerland is not an EU member, so MiCA does not apply directly. However, FINMA's framework is aligned with international standards, and a Swiss licence may facilitate recognition in other jurisdictions.

Can I stake assets for clients without taking custody?

If you do not hold client private keys or assets, you may not need a licence. However, if you provide any form of direction or pooling, FINMA may still require registration. A preliminary assessment is recommended.
