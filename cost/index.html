Why Panama costs so much less than other jurisdictions

Three structural reasons:

  1. No specific crypto license to apply for. Panama crypto operations run under existing corporate framework (Sociedad Anónima), so there's no government license fee like Lithuania's €5K-€10K Bank of Lithuania fee or Dubai's AED 100K-500K VARA fee. You pay only for company formation + notarization + agent.
  2. 0 minimum capital. Lithuania requires €125,000 paid-up capital. Singapore PSA requires S$250K-$5M+. Dubai VARA requires AED 1.5M-7M. Panama: €0.
  3. No mandatory local substance. Lithuania expects local MLRO + Lithuanian board member ($15K-$80K/year). Estonia mandates local director + office. Panama needs only the resident agent (Panama lawyer, ~$1,500/year) - no local director, no office, no MLRO required.

FAQ - Panama crypto cost

What is the total cost of Panama crypto company registration?

€6,000 fixed fee. Covers Panama Sociedad Anónima registration, notarization for 1 person (passport + utility bill), 2 directors, resident agent, government fees, RUC tax registration, and 7 crypto-friendly payment provider onboarding introductions. No hidden fees, no surprise add-ons.

How much does each additional person cost?

€250 per additional person added to the structure. Panama law requires 3 directors minimum; the €6,000 base fee includes 2 directors. So a typical setup with 3 directors and 1 UBO would be €6,000 + (€250 × 2 extra people) = €6,500.

What's the annual maintenance cost after year 1?

~$600-$2,000/year ongoing. Includes ~$300 government franchise tax per year (due July 15) plus resident agent renewal (~$300-$1,500 depending on agent). Optional add-ons quoted separately.

Is the €6,000 really all-in, or are there hidden fees?

It's really all-in for the registration itself. The only things outside the €6,000 are: (1) annual maintenance from year 2 onwards, (2) optional add-ons like additional banking, nominee directors, custom legal opinions. Government fees, registration costs, notarization, agent fees, payment-provider intros - all included.

Why is Panama so much cheaper than Lithuania or Dubai?

Three reasons: (1) no government license fee (Panama has no dedicated crypto license, so no regulator fee), (2) €0 minimum capital (vs Lithuania €125K, Dubai $400K-$1.9M, Singapore S$250K-$5M), (3) no mandatory local substance (no in-country director or office required).
