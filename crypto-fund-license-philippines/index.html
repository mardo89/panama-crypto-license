What Is the Crypto Fund License Philippines?

The crypto fund license Philippines refers to the regulatory authorisation required to operate a fund that invests in or deals with cryptocurrencies and digital assets. In the Philippines, this typically involves registration with the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) as a fund manager or investment company, and potentially a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) license from the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP) if the fund engages in exchange, transfer, or custody of virtual assets. The BSP VASP license is mandatory for any entity that offers services involving virtual assets, including fund managers that directly handle client crypto assets.

As of 2026, the Philippines has a dual regulatory approach: the SEC oversees fund registration and securities offerings, while the BSP regulates VASPs. For a crypto fund, you typically need both: SEC registration for the fund vehicle and BSP VASP registration if the fund itself provides custody or exchange services. However, some fund structures may outsource these functions to a licensed VASP, simplifying the licensing burden. Consulting24 can help you assess which structure fits your model.

Who Needs a Crypto Fund License in the Philippines?

Any entity that operates as a collective investment scheme investing primarily in cryptocurrencies or digital assets, and offers its shares or units to the public, needs SEC registration. Additionally, if the fund provides services such as buying, selling, exchanging, or safekeeping virtual assets on behalf of investors, a BSP VASP license is required. The following types of businesses typically need the license:

  • Crypto hedge funds and venture capital funds investing in digital assets
  • Fund-of-funds that allocate capital to other crypto funds
  • Asset managers offering discretionary management of crypto portfolios
  • Platforms that pool investor funds to invest in crypto assets

License Type & Regulator

The primary regulator for crypto fund licenses in the Philippines is the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) for fund registration, and the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP) for VASP licensing. As of 2026, the BSP requires all VASPs to register under the Virtual Asset Service Provider framework (Circular No. 1108). The SEC requires fund managers to register as Investment Company or as a Fund Manager under the Investment Company Act.

There is no single 'crypto fund license' per se; rather, it is a combination of registrations. The typical structure is a Philippine corporation (domestic or foreign-owned) that is registered as an investment company with the SEC, and also holds a BSP VASP license if the fund directly handles virtual assets. For funds that outsource custody, only SEC registration may suffice. Consulting24 coordinates with local legal partners to ensure all requirements are met.

Capital Requirement

The Philippines does not prescribe a specific minimum capital for crypto funds under SEC rules, but the BSP requires a minimum capital of PHP 50 million (approximately USD 900,000) for VASP license applicants as of 2026. For SEC-registered investment companies, the minimum paid-up capital is typically PHP 5 million (approx. USD 90,000) for domestic funds, though higher amounts may be required for public offerings. Consulting24 recommends budgeting at least PHP 50 million if a BSP VASP license is needed, or PHP 5 million if only SEC registration is required. These figures are subject to change and should be verified with a local advisor.

Tax Treatment

Crypto funds in the Philippines are subject to corporate income tax at a rate of 25% (or 20% for small enterprises with net taxable income below PHP 5 million). Capital gains from trading cryptocurrencies are generally treated as ordinary income. The Philippines does not have a specific crypto tax regime; instead, existing tax laws apply. Additionally, a 12% VAT may apply on management fees. For foreign investors, remittance of profits may be subject to withholding tax. Consulting24 advises clients to engage a local tax specialist to optimise the structure. Compared to Panama, which has territorial taxation (no tax on foreign-source income), the Philippines is less tax-efficient for funds that primarily invest abroad.

Allowed Activities

A crypto fund license in the Philippines permits the following activities:

  • Pooling investor funds to invest in cryptocurrencies and digital assets
  • Buying, selling, and exchanging virtual assets (if BSP VASP licensed)
  • Providing custody of virtual assets for fund investors
  • Offering fund units to qualified or retail investors (subject to SEC rules)
  • Engaging in staking, lending, or yield farming as part of fund strategy

Activities that are not allowed include operating a crypto exchange open to the public (requires a separate VASP license) and offering derivatives without SEC approval. The fund must comply with anti-money laundering (AML) regulations under the Anti-Money Laundering Act.

Step-by-Step Process

Obtaining a crypto fund license in the Philippines involves several steps. Consulting24 coordinates with local partners to streamline the process for international founders.

  1. Incorporate a Philippine corporation with the SEC (minimum PHP 5 million capital).
  2. Apply for SEC registration as an Investment Company or Fund Manager, submitting a prospectus, fund rules, and due diligence.
  3. If required, apply for BSP VASP license: submit business plan, AML policy, IT security audit, and proof of capital (PHP 50 million).
  4. Obtain AML registration with the Anti-Money Laundering Council (AMLC).
  5. Set up local bank accounts and engage a local auditor.
  6. Receive SEC approval and/or BSP license, then commence operations.

Typical timeline: 6-12 months for full licensing. Consulting24 can assist with document preparation and liaising with regulators.

Some digital banks and fintechs are more open. Consulting24 recommends preparing a strong compliance package, including AML policies, source of funds documentation, and a clear business plan. For international fund managers, it may be easier to use a Philippine payment gateway or e-money issuer for fiat transactions. Frequently asked questions

What is a crypto fund license Philippines?

It is the regulatory approval required to operate a fund that invests in cryptocurrencies. Typically, it involves SEC registration as an investment company and, if the fund handles virtual assets directly, a BSP VASP license.

Who regulates crypto funds in the Philippines?

The Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) regulates fund registration, while the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP) regulates virtual asset service providers (VASPs). Both may be involved depending on the fund's activities.

What is the minimum capital for a crypto fund license Philippines?

For SEC registration, minimum paid-up capital is PHP 5 million (approx. USD 90,000). If a BSP VASP license is needed, the minimum is PHP 50 million (approx. USD 900,000).

How long does it take to get a crypto fund license in the Philippines?

The timeline ranges from 6 to 12 months, depending on whether a BSP VASP license is required. SEC registration alone may take 2-4 months.

How much does a crypto fund license Philippines cost?

Total costs range from USD 17,000 to USD 40,000, including SEC and BSP fees, legal setup, and compliance. Exact pricing is confirmed during a consultation.

Can a foreigner apply for a crypto fund license in the Philippines?

Yes, foreigners can own up to 100% of a Philippine corporation for fund management, but a local resident director and corporate secretary are required. Consulting24 can coordinate with local partners.

What activities are allowed under a crypto fund license Philippines?

Allowed activities include pooling investor funds, buying/selling virtual assets (if VASP licensed), custody, staking, and lending. Operating a public exchange is not allowed without a separate VASP license.

What are the tax implications for a crypto fund in the Philippines?

Corporate income tax is 25% (20% for small enterprises). Capital gains are taxed as ordinary income. Management fees may be subject to 12% VAT. Foreign-source income may be exempt if the fund is structured properly.

Is a BSP VASP license always required for a crypto fund?

No. If the fund outsources custody and exchange services to a licensed VASP, it may only need SEC registration. However, if the fund directly handles client virtual assets, a BSP VASP license is mandatory.

How does the Philippines compare to Panama for crypto fund licensing?

Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 license with no capital requirement and territorial tax, making it faster and cheaper. The Philippines is more expensive and slower but provides access to the Asian market.
