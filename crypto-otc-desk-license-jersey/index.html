What Is a Crypto OTC Desk License in Jersey?

A crypto OTC desk license in Jersey is a regulatory registration that allows a company to facilitate over-the-counter trades of virtual assets directly between buyers and sellers. Unlike exchange platforms, OTC desks handle large block trades privately, often with institutional clients. The JFSC requires any business providing virtual asset exchange services, including OTC trading, to register under the AML/CFT regime. This is not a full securities license but a registration that imposes strict due diligence, record-keeping, and reporting obligations.

Jersey's approach is principles-based, focusing on the substance and control of the business. The license is issued to the legal entity, and key personnel must be fit and proper. The registration is valid indefinitely but subject to ongoing supervision. OTC desks must maintain a physical presence in Jersey, including an office and at least one local director. The JFSC expects the business to have a clear understanding of its client base and the source of funds for large transactions. For example, a typical OTC desk handling trades over EUR 100,000 must conduct enhanced due diligence on each counterparty, including verifying the origin of the crypto assets. The registration does not permit the operation of a public order book or matching engine for retail clients; it is strictly for bilateral trades.

Who Needs This License?

Any company that offers OTC trading services in or from Jersey must register with the JFSC. This includes:

  • OTC desks matching buyers and sellers of cryptocurrencies
  • Brokers or intermediaries facilitating large block trades
  • Market makers providing liquidity via OTC channels
  • Any entity that holds customer funds or crypto for settlement

If your business only trades proprietary capital (no third-party funds), you may still need registration if you act as a counterparty. Exemptions exist for incidental activities, but most commercial OTC desks must register. For instance, a proprietary trading firm that occasionally fills a client order without holding client assets may be exempt, but this is rare. Consulting24 can assess your specific model. The JFSC can impose penalties for operating without registration.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Jersey Financial Services Commission (JFSC). The registration is under the Virtual Asset Exchange Provider category, which covers exchange services including OTC trading. There is no separate 'OTC desk license' - it falls under the broader virtual asset service provider (VASP) regime. The JFSC does not issue a 'license' per se but a registration. However, the term 'license' is commonly used. The registration requires compliance with the JFSC's AML/CFT Handbook and the Money Laundering (Jersey) Order 2008.

The JFSC also oversees fit and proper assessments of all directors and beneficial owners. The regulator expects the compliance officer to be locally based and have relevant experience. As of 2026, the JFSC has increased scrutiny on VASP applications, particularly around source of wealth and business model sustainability. They may request a detailed business plan showing projected transaction volumes, counterparty types, and risk management procedures. Capital Requirement

Jersey does not impose a statutory minimum capital for VASP registration. However, the JFSC expects the company to have adequate financial resources to operate. In practice, most applicants maintain at least EUR 50,000 in paid-up capital to demonstrate substance. For OTC desks handling large volumes, higher capital may be required to cover operational risks. For instance, if you plan to hold client crypto assets for settlement, the JFSC may expect capital equivalent to a percentage of assets under custody.

Consulting24 recommends a minimum of EUR 100,000 to show financial standing, but this is not a fixed rule. The JFSC assesses capital adequacy on a case-by-case basis. They will review your business plan and projected liabilities. If your OTC desk expects to settle trades worth millions daily, you should have capital to cover potential losses from failed trades or fraud. Some applicants use a combination of paid-up capital and a parent company guarantee.

Tax Treatment

Jersey offers a 0% standard corporate income tax rate for most companies. However, a 10% rate applies to certain regulated financial services (e.g., banking, insurance). Virtual asset service providers are generally taxed at 0%, provided they do not fall into the 10% category. There is no capital gains tax, no VAT (GST), and no withholding tax on dividends. This makes Jersey very attractive for crypto OTC desks. However, you must have substance (office, staff, board meetings in Jersey) to benefit. Tax rulings are available from Revenue Jersey to confirm your tax status.

For example, a Jersey OTC desk earning EUR 2 million in trading fees would pay zero corporate tax, while a similar business in Lithuania would pay 15% (EUR 300,000). The savings can offset the higher setup costs. Note that if your OTC desk also provides services to Jersey residents, you may need to charge GST (5%) on fees, but this is rare for institutional clients. Allowed Activities Under the License

With a Jersey VASP registration, you can:

  • Facilitate OTC trades of virtual assets (e.g., Bitcoin, Ethereum, stablecoins)
  • Act as a counterparty in principal trading
  • Provide custody of virtual assets for settlement
  • Offer price negotiation and execution services
  • Serve institutional and high-net-worth clients

You cannot operate a public order-book exchange without additional authorization. The OTC desk must not accept retail clients without appropriate safeguards. All activities must be conducted in line with the JFSC's AML/CFT requirements. For instance, you cannot offer margin trading or derivatives without a separate license. The JFSC may also restrict the types of virtual assets you can trade; for example, privacy coins like Monero may be prohibited. Your AML policies must address how you handle assets with anonymity features.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process for obtaining a Jersey crypto OTC desk registration involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application: Engage a local compliance advisor (Consulting24 can coordinate). Prepare business plan, AML/CFT policies, risk assessment, and financial projections.
  2. Company formation: Incorporate a Jersey company (private limited company) with physical office and local directors (or at least one).
  3. Fit and proper checks: All directors, beneficial owners, and key personnel must submit personal questionnaires and undergo background checks.
  4. Application submission: Submit the application to the JFSC with supporting documents. The JFSC reviews for completeness.
  5. Review and interview: The JFSC may request additional information or an interview with the compliance officer.
  6. Approval and registration: Once satisfied, the JFSC issues a registration certificate. The company is then listed on the JFSC's register.

Jersey offers several advantages for OTC desks:

  • Low tax: 0% corporate tax, no capital gains, no VAT
  • Regulatory clarity: JFSC has a clear framework for VASPs
  • International reputation: Jersey is a well-regulated finance centre

Compared to Panama (EUR 6,000 flat fee, no substance requirement), Jersey offers more regulatory credibility but higher costs. Jersey's registration signals to counterparties that you are subject to strong AML oversight. This can be a competitive advantage when negotiating with large funds or family offices.

Compliance and Trust

Compliance is critical for Jersey VASPs. The JFSC expects:

  • Written AML/CFT policies tailored to OTC operations
  • Customer due diligence (CDD) for all clients, including enhanced due diligence for PEPs
  • Transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting
  • Annual audit and submission of financial statements
  • Appointment of a local money laundering reporting officer (MLRO)

Frequently asked questions

What is the primary regulator for crypto OTC desks in Jersey?

The Jersey Financial Services Commission (JFSC) regulates virtual asset service providers, including OTC desks, under the Money Laundering (Jersey) Order 2008.

Is there a separate license for OTC desks in Jersey?

No. OTC desks fall under the Virtual Asset Exchange Provider registration. There is no separate 'OTC desk license' category.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a Jersey OTC desk?

There is no statutory minimum, but the JFSC expects adequate financial resources. Most applicants maintain at least EUR 50,000, and Consulting24 recommends EUR 100,000 to demonstrate substance.

How long does it take to get a Jersey crypto OTC desk registration?

Typically 4 to 8 months, depending on the completeness of your application and the JFSC's workload. Pre-application preparation can take 1-2 months.

Can I operate an OTC desk from Jersey without a physical office?

No. The JFSC requires a physical office in Jersey and at least one local director. Substance is a key requirement for registration.

What taxes will my Jersey OTC desk pay?

Jersey has a 0% standard corporate income tax rate for most companies, including VASPs. There is no capital gains tax, no VAT, and no withholding tax on dividends.

Can I use a Jersey OTC desk license to serve EU clients?

Jersey is not in the EU, so you cannot passport into EU markets. For EU clients, a license under MiCA (e.g., Lithuania) may be more suitable. However, you can serve EU clients on a reverse solicitation basis.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations for a Jersey VASP?

You must maintain AML/CFT policies, conduct CDD, monitor transactions, report suspicious activities, submit annual financial statements, and appoint a local MLRO. The JFSC may conduct on-site inspections.

Is Jersey cheaper than Panama for an OTC desk?

No. Panama is cheaper (EUR 6,000 flat fee vs. EUR 15,000-30,000 for Jersey) and faster (2-3 weeks vs. 4-8 months). However, Jersey offers more regulatory credibility and a 0% tax rate on all income, not just foreign-source.

Can Consulting24 guarantee approval of my Jersey application?

No. No advisor can guarantee approval. Consulting24 maximises your chances by preparing a thorough application, but the JFSC makes the final decision. Our success rate is high due to our experience.

What activities are prohibited under a Jersey OTC desk registration?

You cannot operate a public order-book exchange, offer margin trading or derivatives, or serve retail clients without additional safeguards. Privacy coins may also be restricted.
