What Is a Crypto Wallet Custody License in Singapore?

A crypto wallet custody license in Singapore is a regulatory authorization issued by MAS under the Payment Services Act (PSA). It allows a company to provide digital payment token (DPT) services, specifically the custody of crypto assets on behalf of clients. This includes storing private keys, managing wallets, and facilitating transfers. The license is part of Singapore's broader effort to regulate virtual asset service providers (VASPs) and align with Financial Action Task Force (FATF) standards.

As of 2026, MAS has tightened requirements to address risks such as money laundering, terrorist financing, and consumer protection. The license is mandatory for any business that holds or controls customers' crypto assets. Failure to obtain the license can result in penalties up to SGD 100,000 and imprisonment. The license covers both hot and cold wallet storage, multi-signature arrangements, and recovery services. MAS also requires segregation of client assets from the firm's own assets, typically through trust accounts or insurance coverage. This protects clients in case of insolvency. As of 2026, MAS has proposed a new Digital Asset Custody Framework that will further specify custody standards, including requirements for private key generation, storage, and backup procedures. Firms must also conduct regular penetration testing and vulnerability assessments.

Who Needs a Crypto Wallet Custody License in Singapore?

Any entity that provides custodial wallet services for digital payment tokens (DPTs) must hold a license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges that offer wallet storage for customers
  • Independent custody providers (e.g., BitGo, Fireblocks-type services)
  • Wealth managers and family offices that hold crypto for clients
  • Decentralized finance (DeFi) platforms that offer non-custodial wallets may also require a license if they have control over private keys

Exemptions exist for businesses that only provide software wallets where the user retains full control of private keys. However, any form of custodianship triggers licensing. License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS). Under the PSA, there are three main license tiers:

License TypeScope
Standard Payment Institution (SPI)Up to SGD 3 million monthly transaction volume; lower capital requirement
Major Payment Institution (MPI)Exceeds SPI thresholds; higher capital and compliance obligations
Money-Changing LicenseLimited to currency exchange; not relevant for custody

For crypto wallet custody, most providers apply for an MPI license due to transaction volumes. MPI licensees must also comply with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) requirements, including regular audits and reporting. MAS publishes a list of licensed entities on its website. Consulting24 advises and coordinates with Singapore counsel to determine the appropriate license type.

As of 2026, MAS has also introduced a new class of license for digital asset custodians under the proposed Digital Asset Custody Framework. This may eventually replace the DPT custody component of the MPI license. The new framework will impose specific custody standards, such as requiring at least two-person approval for any movement of client assets, and mandatory insurance coverage for custodial losses. Capital Requirement

MAS requires a minimum base capital for MPI license holders. As of 2026, the requirement is SGD 250,000 (approximately EUR 170,000) in paid-up capital. This must be maintained at all times. For SPI license holders, the minimum is SGD 100,000. The capital must be held in liquid assets such as cash or government bonds. Tax Treatment

Singapore offers a territorial tax system. Corporate income tax is 17% on profits derived from or remitted to Singapore. However, there are specific incentives for digital asset businesses:

  • Capital gains tax: Not applicable, so gains from crypto trading may be tax-free if considered capital in nature.
  • GST (Goods and Services Tax): As of 2026, the supply of digital payment tokens is exempt from GST if it qualifies as a DPT under MAS guidelines.
  • Pioneer tax incentives: MAS may grant tax holidays for qualifying fintech firms, subject to application.

Allowed Activities Under the License

An MPI license for DPT services permits the following activities:

  • Custody of digital payment tokens (private key management)
  • Facilitating the transfer of DPTs between wallets
  • Exchange of DPTs for fiat currency (if additional license obtained)
  • Cross-border money transfer involving DPTs

Prohibited activities include: offering interest on crypto deposits (unless a banking license is held), providing unregulated lending, and dealing in securities tokens without a capital markets license. MAS strictly enforces the scope. Consulting24 advises and coordinates with Singapore lawyers to draft your business scope correctly.

Additionally, MAS has clarified that staking services may be allowed if the custodian does not take ownership of the staked assets and the client retains full beneficial ownership. However, any form of pooling or lending of client assets for yield generation is prohibited without a separate license. Firms must clearly disclose the risks of staking to clients. Step-by-Step Application Process

The application process involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application preparation: Engage a local compliance consultant. Draft AML/CTF policies, risk assessments, and business plan.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Singapore company with ACRA. Minimum SGD 250,000 paid-up capital for MPI.
  3. Submit application to MAS: Complete Form 1 (for MPI) and provide supporting documents: audited financials, shareholder details, organizational structure.
  4. MAS review: MAS will assess the application, conduct background checks on directors and shareholders, and may request additional information.
  5. In-principle approval (IPA): Once satisfied, MAS issues an IPA. The applicant must then operationalize the business within 6 months.
  6. Final approval: After meeting all conditions, MAS grants the full license.

During the operationalization phase, the firm must set up its custody infrastructure, hire key personnel (e.g., AML compliance officer, IT security head), and undergo a pre-operational audit. MAS may also conduct an on-site inspection. The entire process from start to full license can take up to 18 months if delays occur.
