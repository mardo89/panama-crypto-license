What Is a Crypto NFT Marketplace License in Saudi Arabia?

In Saudi Arabia, there is no single license titled "crypto NFT marketplace license." Instead, NFT marketplace operators must comply with regulations from multiple authorities. The Capital Market Authority (CMA) oversees securities-like tokens, while the Saudi Central Bank (SAMA) regulates payment tokens and virtual asset activities under its Regulatory Sandbox. The Saudi Arabian Monetary Authority (SAMA) also issues licenses for financial technology (Fintech) activities, which may cover crypto-related services.

For NFT marketplaces, the key requirement is obtaining approval from the CMA if the NFTs are considered securities (e.g., fractionalized or offering profit-sharing). Non-security NFTs (e.g., digital art) may fall under general e-commerce regulations but still require registration with the Ministry of Commerce. As of 2026, the regulatory environment is still developing, and the government has signaled that a comprehensive crypto asset framework is being drafted. Until then, operators typically apply for a Fintech sandbox license from SAMA or a securities license from CMA, depending on the nature of the NFTs.

Who Needs This License?

Any entity operating a platform that facilitates the creation, listing, buying, or selling of NFTs in or from Saudi Arabia likely needs some form of regulatory approval. This includes:

  • NFT marketplaces that allow Saudi residents to trade digital art, collectibles, or virtual real estate.
  • Platforms that fractionalize NFTs into tradeable shares, which may be classified as securities.
  • Exchanges that offer NFT derivatives or staking services.
  • Businesses that mint NFTs for third parties and charge fees.

Foreign companies targeting Saudi users must also comply, even if their servers are outside the kingdom. The CMA and SAMA have extraterritorial reach if services are offered to Saudi residents. License Type & Regulator

The primary regulators for NFT marketplace activities in Saudi Arabia are:

  • Capital Market Authority (CMA): Regulates securities, including tokenized assets and fractional NFTs. If your NFTs represent ownership in real-world assets or offer dividends, you need a CMA license for securities business.
  • Saudi Central Bank (SAMA): Oversees payment systems and virtual assets. SAMA's Regulatory Sandbox allows fintech firms to test crypto services under a temporary license. For NFT marketplaces that involve payment tokens or stablecoins, SAMA approval is required.
  • Ministry of Commerce (MC): General e-commerce registration is needed for any online marketplace, including NFT platforms.

As of 2026, there is no single "crypto license" in Saudi Arabia. The closest is the SAMA sandbox license, which is typically valid for 12-24 months and can lead to a full license after successful testing. The CMA also issues licenses for securities activities, which can cover security tokens. Consulting24 advises clients on which regulator to approach based on their NFT classification.

Capital Requirement

Saudi Arabia does not have a publicly stated minimum capital requirement for NFT marketplace licenses under the SAMA sandbox. However, applicants must demonstrate sufficient financial resources to operate and cover potential liabilities. Typically, regulators expect at least $50,000 in paid-up capital for sandbox participants, though this is assessed on a case-by-case basis.

For CMA securities licenses, the capital requirement is higher, often starting at $200,000 for a Category 1 license (dealing in securities). Fractionalized NFTs that are deemed securities would fall under this category. Consulting24 advises clients to prepare a detailed business plan and financial projections to satisfy the regulator's capital adequacy assessment.

In contrast, Panama requires no minimum capital for its crypto license, only the flat EUR 6,000 fee. This makes Panama an attractive option for NFT startups with limited funding.

Tax Treatment

Saudi Arabia does not impose personal income tax or corporate income tax on most businesses, but there is a 15% VAT on goods and services. For NFT marketplaces, VAT may apply on platform fees and commissions. Additionally, if the NFTs are classified as securities, capital gains tax may apply under the Zakat and Tax Authority (ZATCA) regulations. As of 2026, there is no specific crypto tax law, but general tax principles apply.

Foreign companies operating in Saudi Arabia may be subject to withholding taxes on certain payments. Consulting24 recommends engaging a tax advisor to structure your NFT marketplace tax-efficiently. For comparison, Panama offers territorial taxation (only income sourced in Panama is taxed) and no VAT on digital services, making it a tax-friendly jurisdiction for NFT businesses.

Allowed Activities

Under the current Saudi regulatory framework, an NFT marketplace license (via SAMA sandbox or CMA license) typically permits the following activities:

  • Listing and trading of NFTs that are not classified as securities (e.g., digital art, music, collectibles).
  • Minting NFTs for users (as a service provider).
  • Secondary market trading of non-security NFTs.
  • Custody of private keys for users (with appropriate security measures).

Activities that are generally not allowed without additional licensing include:

  • Fractionalizing NFTs into tradeable shares (requires CMA securities license).
  • Offering NFT derivatives or margin trading (requires CMA derivatives license).
  • Using payment tokens or stablecoins without SAMA approval.

Banking & Payments

Opening a bank account for a crypto-related business in Saudi Arabia is challenging due to the cautious stance of local banks. Most banks require a regulatory license or at least a sandbox approval before opening an account. Even with approval, banks may impose restrictions on transactions involving crypto.

For NFT marketplaces, payment processing is another hurdle. Saudi Arabia's payment systems (e.g., Mada, SADAD) are not designed for crypto transactions. Many operators use third-party payment gateways that support fiat-to-crypto conversions, but these must comply with SAMA's payment regulations.

Consulting24 recommends considering Panama as an alternative, where banking is more accessible for crypto businesses. Panama's international banking sector offers accounts for licensed crypto firms, and the flat EUR 6,000 license includes support for banking introductions.

Common Mistakes

Founders often make these errors when seeking a Saudi NFT marketplace license:

  • Misclassifying NFTs: Assuming all NFTs are non-securities. If your NFTs offer profit-sharing or fractional ownership, you need a CMA license.
  • Ignoring local presence: Regulators require a physical office and local management. Remote operations are not accepted.
  • Underestimating timelines: The process can take 12-18 months. Many startups run out of runway before approval.
  • Overlooking banking: Failing to secure banking early can delay operations post-license.
