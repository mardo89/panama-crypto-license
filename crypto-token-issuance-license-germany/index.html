What Is the Crypto Token Issuance License Germany?

The crypto token issuance license Germany is a regulatory authorisation required under the German Banking Act (KWG) and the EU Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA). Since MiCA's full application in 2026, any person or entity that offers crypto tokens to the public or applies for their admission to trading on a trading platform must obtain a licence from BaFin. This licence is classified as a CASP licence with the specific service 'offering crypto assets to the public' or 'admission to trading of crypto assets'. The regime covers all types of tokens, including utility tokens, asset-referenced tokens, and e-money tokens, unless exempted (e.g., fully decentralised or non-fungible tokens that are truly unique).

BaFin's approach is principles-based but demanding. Applicants must demonstrate strong governance, AML/CFT controls, capital adequacy, and operational resilience. The licence is not a simple registration; it is a full authorisation process that typically takes 6-12 months. Who Needs a Crypto Token Issuance License in Germany?

Any legal entity (usually a GmbH or AG) that intends to issue tokens to the German or EU market needs this licence. This includes:

  • Startups launching utility tokens for decentralised applications or platforms.
  • Companies issuing security tokens representing shares, debt, or other financial instruments.
  • Stablecoin issuers offering asset-referenced or e-money tokens.
  • Projects conducting initial DEX offerings (IDOs) or initial exchange offerings (IEOs) where the token is offered to the public.

Exemptions exist for tokens offered only to qualified investors, tokens with a total consideration under EUR 1 million over 12 months, or tokens that are truly non-fungible and not part of a series. However, most commercial token issuances will require a licence. License Type and Regulator

The relevant regulator is the Federal Financial Supervisory Authority (BaFin), based in Bonn and Frankfurt. The licence type is a CASP licence with authorisation for 'offering crypto assets to the public' under Section 1(1a) of the KWG in conjunction with MiCA. This is a single licence that covers both the issuance and, if desired, other CASP services such as custody or exchange. However, you must specify the services you intend to offer in your application.

BaFin is known for its thorough scrutiny. Applicants must submit a detailed business plan, AML policies, risk management framework, organisational chart, and evidence of fit and proper management. The process involves a pre-application phase, formal submission, and a review period that can extend to 12 months. Capital Requirement for the German Crypto Token Issuance License

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for offering crypto assets to the public is EUR 50,000. This is the lowest tier under the three-tier capital structure (EUR 50,000 / 125,000 / 150,000 depending on the services offered). However, if your token issuance also involves custody of client funds or other services, higher capital may be required. BaFin may also impose additional capital based on the risk profile of your business, including operational risk and the volume of issuance.

The capital must be held in liquid assets, typically in a bank account in Germany or another EU member state. It must be maintained at all times and cannot be used for operational expenses. Tax Treatment of Token Issuance in Germany

Germany has a well-defined tax framework for crypto tokens. Issuance of tokens is generally treated as follows:

  • Utility tokens: The issuance proceeds are considered taxable income (corporate income tax at ~30% plus trade tax of 7-17%). VAT is not applicable if the token provides a right to receive a service (VAT exemption).
  • Security tokens: Treated as financial instruments; issuance may be subject to securities transfer tax (0.2% on secondary market) but not VAT. Income from issuance is corporate taxable.
  • Stablecoins (asset-referenced): Issuance may be treated as a debt instrument; interest or redemption gains are taxable.
  • E-money tokens: Subject to strict regulations; issuance is not subject to VAT but income is taxable.

Private investors holding tokens for more than one year benefit from tax-free capital gains on disposal. However, for issuers, the tax treatment is complex and depends on the token's classification. Allowed Activities Under the German Crypto Token Issuance License

With a German crypto token issuance licence, you are authorised to:

  • Offer crypto tokens to the public in Germany and across the EU (under MiCA passporting).
  • Apply for admission of your tokens to trading on a trading platform (e.g., a regulated exchange).
  • Market and advertise your token issuance to retail and professional investors.
  • Provide ancillary services such as token creation, smart contract deployment, and initial distribution.

The licence does not automatically permit other CASP services like custody, exchange, or transfer services. If you wish to offer those, you must apply for additional authorisations under the same CASP licence. BaFin expects a clear separation of functions and strong conflict of interest policies. Benefits of the German Crypto Token Issuance License

Obtaining a German licence offers several strategic advantages:

  • EU passporting: Once licensed, you can offer tokens across all EU member states without additional authorisations, thanks to MiCA's single licence regime.
  • Regulatory credibility: BaFin's rigorous standards are recognised globally, enhancing trust among investors, partners, and exchanges.
  • Access to German capital markets: Germany has a large investor base and a mature financial ecosystem.
  • Clear legal framework: Germany's token classification and tax rules provide legal certainty for long-term planning.
  • Strong infrastructure: Access to legal, accounting, and tech talent in Berlin, Munich, and Frankfurt.

For projects that aim for a European or global presence, the German licence is a gold standard. However, the cost and complexity are higher than in some other jurisdictions. Consulting24 helps you weigh these benefits against alternatives like Panama or Estonia.

Compliance and Trust: What BaFin Expects

BaFin expects licence holders to maintain ongoing compliance. Key obligations include:

  • Annual AML audit and submission of reports.
  • Maintenance of minimum capital at all times.
  • Regular reporting on token issuance volumes, investor details, and suspicious transactions.
  • Implementation of a strong AML/CFT programme, including customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and record keeping.
  • Fit and proper management: any changes in key personnel must be notified to BaFin.

Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the licence. Consulting24 provides ongoing advisory support to ensure your operations remain compliant. This is general guidance, not legal advice. Always consult a qualified lawyer for your specific situation.

Common Mistakes When Applying for the German Crypto Token Issuance License

Many applications are delayed or rejected due to avoidable errors. Common mistakes include:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing business plan details or insufficient AML policies.
  • Underestimating capital requirements: Not holding enough liquid capital or failing to demonstrate its source.
  • Poor token classification: Misclassifying a token as a utility token when it has security-like features.
  • Unrealistic business projections: BaFin expects a credible, conservative financial plan.
  • Ignoring tax implications: Not structuring the token to minimise tax liabilities.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a German crypto token issuance license?

The minimum capital is EUR 50,000 under MiCA's lowest tier. However, if your services include custody or other activities, higher capital may be required. BaFin may also impose additional capital based on risk.

How long does it take to get a crypto token issuance license in Germany?

Typically 6 to 12 months from start to licence issuance. Pre-application preparation takes 2-4 months, followed by BaFin's review of 4-8 months. Consulting24's advisory can help streamline the process.

Can I issue tokens across the EU with a German license?

Yes. Under MiCA, a German CASP licence allows you to passport your services to all EU member states without additional authorisations.

What types of tokens can I issue with a German license?

Utility tokens, security tokens, asset-referenced tokens, and e-money tokens are all covered. However, each type has specific regulatory and tax implications. Consulting24 can help you classify your token correctly.

Is a German crypto token issuance license required for NFTs?

It depends. Truly unique non-fungible tokens that are not part of a series are exempt. However, fractionalised NFTs or NFTs representing financial rights may require a licence. BaFin assesses on a case-by-case basis.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations after obtaining the license?

You must maintain minimum capital, conduct annual AML audits, report suspicious transactions, and notify BaFin of any changes in management or business model. Regular reporting on issuance volumes is also required.

Can Consulting24 guarantee approval of my German license application?

How does the German license compare to Panama for token issuance?

Panama offers a flat fee of EUR 6,000, no capital requirement, and 0% tax on foreign income. Germany is more expensive and complex but provides EU passporting and higher regulatory credibility. Your choice depends on your target market and budget.

What is the tax rate for token issuance in Germany?

Corporate income tax is approximately 30% (including solidarity surcharge) plus trade tax of 7-17%. VAT is generally not applicable. Security token issuance may incur securities transfer tax. Consulting24 works with tax advisors to optimise your structure.
