What Is the Crypto Exchange License Portugal?

The crypto exchange license Portugal, officially the Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) registration, is a mandatory authorization for businesses offering crypto exchange, custody, and related services. Regulated by the Bank of Portugal (Banco de Portugal) and the Portuguese Securities Market Commission (CMVM) for certain activities, it aligns with the EU's MiCA framework fully in force since 2025. Unlike a full license, it is a registration with ongoing compliance obligations, including AML/KYC procedures, reporting, and capital requirements.

Portugal's regime is known for its relatively straightforward process compared to some other EU member states, but it still requires a substantive application. The registration allows companies to passport services across the European Economic Area (EEA) under MiCA, making it a gateway to the European market. Who Needs a Crypto License in Portugal?

Any entity providing the following services in or from Portugal must obtain VASP registration:

  • Exchange between virtual assets and fiat currencies (crypto-to-fiat exchange)
  • Exchange between one or more virtual assets (crypto-to-crypto exchange)
  • Transfer of virtual assets
  • Custody and administration of virtual assets on behalf of clients
  • Participation in and provision of financial services related to an issuer's offer of virtual assets

This includes centralized exchanges, decentralized finance (DeFi) platforms if they have a central operator, and wallet providers. If you plan to operate a crypto exchange or offer related services to Portuguese residents, you must register. Even if your company is based elsewhere, targeting Portuguese clients may trigger the requirement. Consulting24 can help you assess whether your business model falls under the scope.

License Type and Regulator

The relevant regulator is the Bank of Portugal (Banco de Portugal), which oversees VASP registration and AML compliance. For services involving securities tokens or investment products, the CMVM (Comissão do Mercado de Valores Mobiliários) may also have oversight. The registration is not a full license but a mandatory authorization under Portuguese law (Law No. 83/2017, as amended). Under MiCA, Portugal applies the EU-wide classification of CASPs (Crypto Asset Service Providers) with capital tiers.

Key details:

  • Regulator: Bank of Portugal (Banco de Portugal) - www.bportugal.pt
  • Type: VASP registration / CASP authorization under MiCA
  • Capital: Minimum EUR 50,000 for exchange services; higher tiers for custody (EUR 125,000) and other services (EUR 150,000)
  • Timeline: Typically 3-6 months from submission

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, Portugal applies capital tiers based on the type of crypto services offered:

  • Exchange services (crypto-to-fiat and crypto-to-crypto): EUR 50,000 minimum own funds
  • Custody and administration of virtual assets: EUR 125,000
  • Other services (e.g., transfer, advice): EUR 150,000

These amounts must be held as own funds (equity) and cannot be borrowed. The capital must be maintained throughout the license period. If you offer multiple services, the highest applicable tier applies. For example, an exchange offering custody needs EUR 125,000. Portugal does not require a separate security deposit; the capital is part of the company's equity. Consulting24 can advise on structuring your capital to meet requirements efficiently.

Tax Treatment for Crypto in Portugal

Portugal has a favorable tax regime for crypto, though it has evolved. As of 2026, individual crypto gains are taxed as capital gains at a flat rate of 28% if held for less than one year. Gains from crypto held over one year are tax-exempt for individuals (subject to conditions). For businesses, corporate income tax (CIT) applies at a standard rate of 21% (plus municipal surcharges up to 1.5%). Crypto-to-crypto exchanges are generally not taxable events for individuals, but for businesses, they are part of taxable income.

VAT is not applicable on crypto exchange services in Portugal, as the EU Court of Justice ruled that crypto transactions are exempt from VAT. However, advisory and management fees may be subject to VAT. Portugal also does not levy wealth tax or stamp duty on crypto holdings. This tax environment makes Portugal attractive for crypto exchanges, but professional tax advice is essential. Consulting24 coordinates with local tax experts to ensure compliance.

Allowed Activities Under the Portuguese Crypto License

The VASP registration allows the following activities, as defined by Portuguese law and MiCA:

  • Exchange of virtual assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of virtual assets for other virtual assets
  • Transfer of virtual assets (on behalf of clients)
  • Custody and administration of virtual assets (including private keys)
  • Operation of a trading platform (order book, matching engine)
  • Receipt and transmission of orders for virtual assets
  • Placement of virtual assets (underwriting)
  • Advice on virtual assets

Activities such as mining, non-custodial wallet development, and pure DeFi protocols without a central operator are generally not regulated. However, if you provide any of the above services as a business, registration is mandatory. The license does not cover securities tokens (which fall under CMVM) or e-money services (which require a separate license). Consulting24 can help define your service scope precisely.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Pre-assessment: Consulting24 evaluates your business model, identifies the required service classes, and confirms eligibility.
  2. Company incorporation: Set up a Portuguese company (or branch) with a registered office in Portugal. Minimum share capital for a limited company is EUR 5,000 (but higher for VASP).
  3. Document preparation: Prepare AML/KYC policies, business plan, risk assessment, governance structure, and capital proof. These must be in Portuguese or certified translation.
  4. Submit application: File the application with the Bank of Portugal via the online portal. Pay the application fee.
  5. Regulator review: The Bank of Portugal reviews the application, may request additional information, and conducts background checks on directors and beneficial owners.
  6. Approval and registration: Upon approval, you are added to the VASP register. You can then commence operations.
  7. Ongoing compliance: Submit periodic reports, maintain AML procedures, and undergo annual audits.

The entire process typically takes 3-6 months. Consulting24 coordinates with local lawyers and compliance experts to streamline the application.

Several Portuguese banks accept VASP-registered companies, including Caixa Geral de Depósitos, Millennium BCP, and Santander Portugal. However, they may require a minimum deposit, proof of registration, and a detailed business plan. Some banks also offer dedicated crypto-friendly accounts.

For payment processing, you can integrate with European PSPs like Stripe, Adyen, or local providers. Portugal is part of SEPA, enabling fast euro transfers. Compliance and Trust

Compliance is the backbone of your crypto license. Portugal requires a strong AML framework, including:

  • Appointment of a local AML compliance officer
  • Customer due diligence (CDD) and enhanced due diligence (EDD) for high-risk clients
  • Transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting
  • Record keeping for at least 5 years
  • Annual independent audit

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Incomplete application: Missing documents or incorrect translations can delay the process. Use a professional service.
  2. Underestimating capital: Ensure you have the required own funds before applying. Some applicants fail because capital is not fully paid up.
  3. Ignoring local presence: You need a registered office and a local director or representative. Remote-only setups are not accepted.
  4. Poor AML policies: Generic templates are rejected. Your policies must be tailored to your business model.
  5. Overlooking tax: Even if crypto is tax-friendly, corporate tax obligations apply. Get professional tax advice.
  6. Choosing the wrong jurisdiction: Portugal may not suit everyone. Compare with Lithuania or Panama for different benefits.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a VASP registration and a full license in Portugal?

Portugal uses a VASP registration system, which is a mandatory authorization but not a full license like a banking license. Under MiCA, it is classified as a CASP authorization. It allows you to provide crypto services in Portugal and passport across the EU. The requirements are substantial but less onerous than a full credit institution license.

How much does a crypto exchange license in Portugal cost in total?

Total setup costs (excluding capital) typically range from EUR 15,000 to EUR 45,000, including application fees, legal fees, and compliance setup. The exact cost depends on your service scope and complexity. Consulting24 provides a detailed quote during a consultation.

Can I apply for a Portuguese crypto license remotely?

Yes, you can apply remotely, but you must have a registered office in Portugal and a local representative (director or AML officer). Consulting24 can assist with finding a registered address and local compliance support.

What is the minimum capital for a crypto exchange in Portugal?

For exchange services (crypto-to-fiat and crypto-to-crypto), the minimum capital is EUR 50,000. If you also offer custody, it is EUR 125,000. For other services, EUR 150,000. These are own funds and must be maintained.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Portugal?

The typical timeline is 3 to 6 months from submission of a complete application. Delays can occur if additional information is requested. Consulting24 helps ensure your application is complete to minimize delays.

Is Portugal tax-friendly for crypto businesses?

Yes, Portugal has a favorable tax regime. Corporate income tax is 21%, and there is no VAT on crypto transactions. Individual long-term gains (over one year) are tax-exempt. However, business income is taxable, so professional advice is recommended.

Can I passport my Portuguese crypto license to other EU countries?

Yes, under MiCA, a CASP authorization in Portugal allows you to provide services across the EEA without additional licenses. You must notify the Bank of Portugal of your intention to passport.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements after obtaining the license?

You must maintain AML/KYC procedures, appoint a local compliance officer, submit periodic reports to the Bank of Portugal, conduct annual audits, and keep records for at least 5 years. Non-compliance can lead to penalties.

Does Portugal regulate DeFi platforms?

DeFi platforms without a central operator are generally not regulated. However, if you have a central entity that provides exchange, custody, or other services, you likely need registration. Each case is assessed individually.

Can I switch from a Portuguese VASP to a full MiCA license later?

Portugal's VASP registration is already aligned with MiCA as a CASP authorization. There is no separate upgrade; you are already compliant with MiCA. However, if you expand services, you may need to update your registration.
