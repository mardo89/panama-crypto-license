What is the Crypto Wallet Custody License in Bulgaria?

The crypto wallet custody license in Bulgaria is a regulatory authorization issued by the Financial Supervision Commission (FSC) under MiCA. It allows companies to safekeep and administer crypto assets on behalf of clients, including private keys. This license is essential for any business offering custodial wallet services to Bulgarian or EU residents.

Key features of the license include:

  • Full compliance with MiCA's harmonized standards across the EU, enabling passporting to all member states.
  • Requirement for a minimum initial capital of EUR 125,000 for custody services, as per MiCA's tiered capital requirements (EUR 50,000 for certain services, EUR 125,000 for custody, EUR 150,000 for exchanges dealing with both fiat and crypto).
  • Mandatory AML/KYC procedures aligned with the EU's 5th and 6th Anti-Money Laundering Directives, including transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting.
  • Ongoing reporting obligations to the FSC, including annual financial statements, AML audits, and changes in management or ownership.

Bulgaria's regulatory framework is designed to foster innovation while ensuring consumer protection and financial stability. The FSC has been proactive in implementing MiCA, providing clear guidelines for applicants.

Who Needs a Crypto Wallet Custody License in Bulgaria?

Any legal entity that intends to provide custodial wallet services to clients in Bulgaria or across the EU must obtain this license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges offering wallet services as part of their platform (e.g., Binance-style custodial wallets).
  • Independent wallet providers like hardware wallet companies that also offer custodial services.
  • Fintech companies integrating crypto custody into their payment or investment platforms.
  • Asset managers holding crypto for clients, including hedge funds or pension funds.
  • Any business that holds private keys or controls crypto assets on behalf of others.

If your business involves holding private keys or controlling crypto assets on behalf of others, you fall under the definition of a CASP. Operating without a license can result in fines up to 5% of annual turnover, legal action, and reputational damage. Even if you are based outside Bulgaria, if you target Bulgarian or EU residents, you need a license in an EU member state under MiCA.

License Type and Regulator

The relevant regulator for crypto wallet custody in Bulgaria is the Financial Supervision Commission (FSC). Under MiCA, the license is categorized as a CASP license for the service of 'custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients'. This is a specific authorization that does not automatically cover other CASP services like exchange or investment advice.

Bulgaria has opted for a full licensing regime, not a simple registration. This means applicants must undergo a thorough assessment of their business plan, governance, capital adequacy, and AML controls. The FSC coordinates with the Bulgarian National Bank and other authorities for certain checks, such as background checks on beneficial owners and management.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for crypto wallet custody services is EUR 125,000. This must be maintained as own funds and can be in the form of share capital, reserves, or subordinated loans. The capital must be fully paid up before the license is granted, and evidence of the deposit must be provided to the FSC.

Additionally, the FSC may require higher capital based on the volume of assets under custody or the risk profile of the business. For example, if you plan to hold large amounts of client assets, the regulator may ask for a capital buffer. It is advisable to have a buffer above the minimum to demonstrate financial stability and cover initial operational costs. The capital can be held in a bank account in Bulgaria or another EU member state, but the FSC prefers local accounts.

Tax Treatment for Crypto Custody in Bulgaria

Bulgaria has a flat corporate income tax rate of 10%, one of the lowest in the EU. For crypto custody services, the tax treatment follows standard corporate tax rules:

  • Income from custody fees is taxable at 10%.
  • Capital gains from the company's own crypto holdings are also taxed at 10%.
  • VAT: Crypto custody services are generally exempt from VAT under EU rules, but advisory services may be subject to 20% VAT. You should confirm with a tax advisor.
  • Withholding tax on dividends: 5% for EU residents, 10% for non-EU (subject to tax treaties).

Allowed Activities Under the License

The Bulgarian crypto wallet custody license permits the following activities:

  • Safekeeping and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients, including holding private keys.
  • Management of private cryptographic keys, including key generation, storage, and recovery.
  • Facilitating the transfer of crypto-assets between wallets, including transaction signing.
  • Providing reporting and transaction history to clients, such as balance statements and transaction logs.

The license does not automatically cover other CASP services like exchange, trading, or investment advice. Those require separate authorizations or extensions under MiCA. For example, if you want to offer exchange services, you need an additional license with a capital requirement of EUR 150,000. You can apply for multiple services under one application, but each service has its own capital requirement.

Banking and Payments for Crypto Custody Firms

Opening a corporate bank account for a crypto custody firm in Bulgaria can be challenging due to perceived risks. However, several banks are open to working with licensed entities. Key considerations:

  • Choose a bank with experience in crypto: e.g., DSK Bank, UniCredit Bulbank, or Postbank. These banks have dedicated teams for fintech clients.
  • Provide the FSC license (or proof of application) and a clear business plan to satisfy compliance. Banks will conduct enhanced due diligence.
  • Expect higher account maintenance fees, typically EUR 50-150 per month, and possibly minimum balance requirements.
  • Some banks may require a personal guarantee from directors or a security deposit.

Compliance and Trust Considerations

Maintaining compliance is an ongoing obligation. Key requirements include:

  • Annual AML/CFT audits by an external auditor.
  • Regular reporting to the FSC on financial health, suspicious transactions, and changes in management or shareholding.
  • Appointment of a compliance officer and a money laundering reporting officer (MLRO), who must be resident in Bulgaria.
  • Secure custody infrastructure, including cold wallet storage for the majority of assets, multi-signature protocols, and insurance coverage for hot wallets.

Common Mistakes When Applying for a Bulgarian Crypto License

  1. Insufficient capital: Failing to have the full EUR 125,000 paid up before application. Some applicants try to use loans or unpaid capital, which is not accepted.
  2. Weak AML/KYC policies: Not tailoring procedures to the specific risks of custody services. Generic policies are often rejected by the FSC.
  3. Incomplete documentation: Missing business plan details, financial projections, or risk assessments. The FSC expects a comprehensive submission.
  4. Ignoring local presence: The FSC requires a physical office and a local director or manager. Virtual offices are not acceptable.
  5. Underestimating timeline: Rushing the application leads to errors and delays. Plan for at least 4-6 months from start to license issuance.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto wallet custody license in Bulgaria?

Under MiCA, the minimum capital is EUR 125,000. This must be paid up in cash and held as own funds. The FSC may require more based on your business volume.

How long does it take to get a Bulgarian crypto custody license?

Typically 3-6 months from application submission. The FSC review takes 3-4 months, but preparation can add 1-2 months. Consulting24 helps streamline the process.

Can I passport my Bulgarian license to other EU countries?

Yes, under MiCA, a Bulgarian CASP license allows you to provide services across the EU without additional licenses, subject to notification to the host regulator.

What are the tax benefits of Bulgaria for crypto custody?

Bulgaria has a 10% corporate tax rate, one of the lowest in the EU. Crypto custody fees are taxed at 10%, and capital gains on the company's own crypto are also 10%.

Do I need a physical office in Bulgaria?

Yes, the FSC requires a registered office and a physical presence in Bulgaria, including a local director or manager. Virtual offices are not accepted.

Can I apply for multiple CASP services under one license?

Yes, you can apply for multiple services, but each has a capital requirement. For custody (EUR 125,000) and exchange (EUR 150,000), you would need EUR 275,000 total.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

Annual AML audits, regular reporting to the FSC, appointment of a compliance officer and MLRO, and secure custody infrastructure. Non-compliance can lead to fines.

Is Bulgaria cheaper than Lithuania for a crypto license?

Bulgaria has a lower corporate tax (10% vs 15%) and similar setup costs. However, Lithuania may have a faster timeline for some applicants. Consulting24 can compare both.

Can I use a Bulgarian license to serve non-EU clients?

Yes, but the license is primarily for EU clients. For global operations, consider Panama, which has no capital requirement and a flat fee of EUR 6,000.

What happens if my application is rejected?
