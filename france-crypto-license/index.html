What Is the France Crypto License?

The France crypto license is a regulatory authorisation issued by the AMF under the French Monetary and Financial Code (transposing MiCA). It allows companies to operate as a crypto asset service provider (PSAN - Prestataire de Services sur Actifs Numériques) legally in France and across the EU under the MiCA passport. The regime covers a broad range of activities, from custody and exchange to advisory services.

France was an early mover in crypto regulation, introducing a voluntary regime in 2019 that became mandatory in 2024 with MiCA. As of 2026, all CASPs must hold a full license - not just registration - to serve French clients. The AMF is the primary regulator, with the ACPR (Prudential Supervision and Resolution Authority) handling anti-money laundering (AML) aspects. The license is valid indefinitely, subject to ongoing compliance. The AMF maintains a public register of all licensed PSANs, which builds trust with clients and partners.

For example, a crypto exchange that previously operated under a simple registration must now upgrade to a full license, meeting higher capital and governance standards. This ensures that only well-capitalised and compliant firms can offer services to French residents. Consulting24 advises on the transition from registration to full licensing if needed.

Who Needs a France Crypto License?

Any company providing crypto asset services to clients in France or from a French establishment must obtain the license. This includes:

  • Exchanges (crypto-to-fiat and crypto-to-crypto)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Brokers and market makers
  • Platforms for initial coin offerings (ICOs) or token issuance
  • Advisory firms specialising in crypto assets
  • Portfolio managers of crypto assets

Foreign firms targeting French users also need the license if they have no other EU MiCA passport. Even if your company is based elsewhere, offering services to French residents triggers the requirement. For instance, a UK-based exchange that actively markets to French users must either obtain a French license or rely on a MiCA passport from another EU member state. License Type & Regulator

The France crypto license is a full CASP authorisation under MiCA, supervised by the AMF. The AMF is responsible for ensuring market integrity, investor protection, and compliance with operational requirements. The ACPR handles AML/CFT controls, including the mandatory registration with the ACPR before the AMF can issue the license.

There is no separate “optional” regime anymore - all CASPs must hold the full license. The license is valid indefinitely, subject to ongoing compliance. The AMF maintains a public register of all licensed PSANs, which builds trust with clients and partners.

The AMF also coordinates with the European Securities and Markets Authority (ESMA) for cross-border supervision. This means that a French license allows you to passport services to other EU countries without additional local licensing, but you must notify the AMF of your intent. Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, France applies the standard capital tiers based on the service classes offered. For 2026, the minimum capital requirements are:

  • EUR 50,000 - for simple services like advisory or non-custodial wallet provision
  • EUR 125,000 - for exchange services (crypto-to-fiat or crypto-to-crypto) without custody
  • EUR 150,000 - for custodial wallet services, exchange with custody, or full-service platforms

These are minimums; the AMF may require higher capital based on risk assessment. Capital must be held in liquid assets and maintained at all times. For example, if you offer both exchange and custody, you need at least EUR 150,000. If you also offer advisory, the highest tier applies. Tax Treatment

France has a clear tax framework for crypto assets. Corporate income tax applies to profits from crypto activities at the standard rate of 25% (reduced for small businesses under certain conditions). VAT is not applicable on crypto-to-crypto exchanges, but fiat transactions may be subject to VAT. Capital gains tax for individuals is 30% (flat tax), but corporate holders follow standard rules.

For licensed CASPs, specific rules apply to transaction reporting and withholding. The French tax authority (DGFiP) requires detailed reporting of crypto transactions, including annual declarations of crypto asset accounts held abroad. Failure to report can result in penalties of up to 5% of the unreported amount per year.

Transfer pricing rules apply if you have related parties in other jurisdictions. Allowed Activities

A France crypto license permits the following activities under MiCA:

  • Operation of a trading platform (exchange)
  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets
  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Placing of crypto assets (underwriting)
  • Reception and transmission of orders for crypto assets
  • Advice on crypto assets
  • Portfolio management of crypto assets

Each activity requires separate authorisation within the license. The AMF assesses the firm's operational capability for each service. Activities not listed (e.g., derivatives on crypto) may fall under other regulations, such as MiFID II. For instance, offering crypto futures would require a MiFID license in addition to the PSAN license.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process for obtaining a France crypto license involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application preparation: Define your business model, services, and target market. Prepare a detailed business plan, AML/CFT policies, and risk management framework. This typically takes 2-3 months.
  2. ACPR registration: Submit an AML/CFT compliance file to the ACPR. This includes KYC procedures, transaction monitoring, and appointment of a compliance officer. The ACPR may request clarifications.
  3. AMF application: Once ACPR registration is obtained (or in parallel), file the full license application with the AMF. Provide governance documents, capital proof, IT security audits, and organisational chart.
  4. Review and interviews: The AMF reviews the application and may request additional information. Key personnel may be interviewed. The AMF aims to decide within 6 months of a complete application.
  5. Decision: The AMF issues a decision. If approved, the company is added to the PSAN register. If rejected, reasons are given and you can reapply after addressing deficiencies.
  6. Post-licensing compliance: Ongoing reporting, audits, and regulatory updates. Consulting24 advises on maintaining compliance, including annual AML audits and capital maintenance.

Compliance & Trust

Compliance is at the heart of the France crypto license. The AMF expects strong AML/CFT controls, including customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting. Firms must appoint a compliance officer and conduct regular audits.

Data protection (GDPR) and IT security are also key. The AMF requires a security audit by an approved provider. Ongoing reporting includes annual financial statements, compliance reports, and any material changes to the business. For example, if you change your IT system or key personnel, you must notify the AMF within 30 days.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make these errors when applying for a France crypto license:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing or poorly prepared AML policies, business plans, or governance documents cause delays. For instance, a business plan that does not detail revenue projections or operational risks is often rejected.
  • Underestimating capital: Failing to maintain the required capital or not having it in liquid form. Some firms use illiquid assets like crypto, which the AMF does not accept.
  • Ignoring ACPR registration: Some firms apply to the AMF without first clearing the ACPR, leading to rejection. The ACPR registration is a prerequisite.
  • Overpromising services: Listing services you cannot yet deliver - the AMF checks operational capability. For example, claiming to offer custody without having the technical infrastructure.
  • Neglecting local presence: While not mandatory, having a physical office in France strengthens the application. The AMF may ask for a local registered address and a local compliance officer.
  • Poor IT security: Failing to conduct a proper security audit or not addressing vulnerabilities can lead to rejection.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between the France crypto license and a simple registration?

As of 2026, all CASPs in France must hold a full license under MiCA. The previous optional registration regime is no longer available. A full license requires higher capital, more comprehensive AML controls, and ongoing compliance. Simple registration was phased out in 2024.

Can I passport a France crypto license to other EU countries?

Yes, under MiCA, a French license allows you to provide services across the EU without additional licensing. You must notify the AMF of your intent to passport and comply with host state requirements, such as language and consumer protection rules.

What are the minimum capital requirements for a France crypto license?

Capital requirements depend on services offered: EUR 50,000 for advisory, EUR 125,000 for exchange without custody, and EUR 150,000 for custody or full-service platforms. The AMF may require higher capital based on risk.
