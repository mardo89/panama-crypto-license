What Is a Crypto Stablecoin License?

A crypto stablecoin license is a regulatory permit that authorizes a legal entity to issue, redeem, or facilitate transactions involving stablecoins. Stablecoins are cryptocurrencies designed to maintain a stable value relative to a reference asset, typically a fiat currency like the US dollar or euro. Under MiCA, stablecoins are classified as either e-money tokens (EMTs) or asset-referenced tokens (ARTs), each subject to specific rules. The license ensures the issuer complies with reserve requirements, transparency obligations, and consumer protection standards.

In Panama, the crypto license covers stablecoin activities under a general virtual asset service provider (VASP) framework, with no separate stablecoin-specific license. This makes Panama a flexible jurisdiction for stablecoin projects, as the same license can cover multiple crypto services. Consulting24 advises clients on the best jurisdiction based on their stablecoin model, target market, and operational needs. For instance, a startup issuing a USD-pegged stablecoin for cross-border payments might choose Panama for its tax efficiency, while a project targeting EU customers would need a MiCA-compliant CASP license in Lithuania or Estonia.

Who Needs a Crypto Stablecoin License?

Any entity that issues stablecoins, operates a stablecoin exchange, or provides custodial services for stablecoins likely needs a license. This includes:

  • Startups launching a new fiat-backed stablecoin (e.g., USD-pegged token)
  • Fintech companies integrating stablecoin payments
  • Exchanges listing stablecoins or offering stablecoin trading pairs
  • Asset managers using stablecoins for settlement
  • DeFi protocols that issue algorithmic stablecoins (subject to additional scrutiny)

If your business involves creating, distributing, or managing stablecoins in any capacity, you should seek regulatory advice. License Type and Regulator

Under MiCA, stablecoin issuers must obtain authorization as an e-money institution (for EMTs) or as an asset-referenced token issuer (for ARTs). The relevant regulator in each EU member state is the national competent authority (NCA), such as the Bank of Lithuania or the Estonian Financial Supervision Authority. For CASPs dealing with stablecoins, a CASP license is required, with capital tiers of EUR 50,000, 125,000, or 150,000 depending on services offered.

In Panama, the regulator is the Superintendencia de Bancos de Panamá (SBP) for banking-related activities, but crypto licensing falls under Law 23 of 2015 on digital assets, overseen by the Ministry of Commerce and Industries (MICI). The license is a single VASP authorization covering all crypto activities, including stablecoin issuance and exchange. This unified approach simplifies compliance for stablecoin projects. Capital Requirement

Panama has no minimum capital requirement for a crypto license, making it highly accessible. In contrast, EU MiCA imposes capital tiers: EUR 50,000 for simple custody and exchange services, EUR 125,000 for more complex services, and EUR 150,000 for full CASP authorization including stablecoin issuance. For stablecoin issuers specifically, additional reserve capital may be required, typically 2-3% of the stablecoin's market value. For instance, a stablecoin with a market cap of USD 10 million would need reserves of USD 200,000-300,000. Consulting24 advises on structuring your capital to meet the chosen jurisdiction's rules. In Panama, the lack of a capital requirement allows founders to allocate funds directly to business development rather than regulatory compliance.

Tax Treatment

Panama operates a territorial tax system: income generated outside Panama is tax-exempt. For stablecoin operations, if the issuer conducts business primarily with non-Panamanian clients, the tax liability is minimal. There is no VAT on crypto transactions. In the EU, stablecoin activities are subject to corporate income tax (typically 15-25%) and VAT may apply to certain services. Some EU countries offer tax incentives for crypto startups. Consulting24 recommends Panama for tax-efficient stablecoin issuance, but always advise clients to seek local tax counsel. For example, a Panama-based stablecoin issuer earning revenue from transaction fees on global transfers would pay 0% Panamanian tax, whereas a Lithuanian issuer would pay 15% corporate tax on profits.
