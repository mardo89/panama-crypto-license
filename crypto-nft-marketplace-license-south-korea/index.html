What Is a Crypto NFT Marketplace License in South Korea?

A crypto NFT marketplace license in South Korea refers to the regulatory approval required to operate a platform where users can buy, sell, and trade non-fungible tokens (NFTs) using cryptocurrencies. As of 2026, the Financial Services Commission (FSC) and the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) oversee digital asset service providers (VASPs), which include NFT marketplaces that facilitate crypto transactions.

Unlike some jurisdictions that treat NFTs as simple digital collectibles, South Korea applies its anti-money laundering (AML) and user protection rules to any platform handling virtual assets. This means you must register as a VASP with the FIU and comply with strict reporting obligations. The license is not a single document but a registration process that includes a real-name account system and information security management system (ISMS) certification.

For example, if your marketplace allows users to mint and trade NFTs using Bitcoin or Ethereum, you are handling virtual assets and must register. Even platforms that only use fiat currency for NFT purchases may still fall under VASP rules if they offer crypto wallets or custody. The FIU has broad discretion, so it is safer to assume you need registration if any crypto is involved.

Who Needs This License?

Any business operating a marketplace that allows users to trade NFTs for cryptocurrency or fiat currency must register as a VASP in South Korea. This includes platforms that support minting, listing, bidding, and secondary sales of NFTs where payment is made in virtual assets.

Specifically, you need this license if you:

  • Operate an NFT marketplace that accepts crypto payments
  • Provide wallet services integrated with the marketplace
  • Facilitate peer-to-peer trading of NFTs
  • Offer custody of NFTs or related crypto assets

Foreign companies targeting South Korean users are also subject to registration. For instance, a Singapore-based NFT platform that markets to Korean users and accepts Korean won must register. License Type & Regulator

The primary regulator is the Financial Services Commission (FSC) and its subordinate Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU). The license type is a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) registration. As of 2026, all VASPs must register under the Specific Financial Information Act.

Key regulatory bodies include:

  • Financial Services Commission (FSC): Sets policy and oversees the financial system
  • Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU): Handles registration, AML compliance, and reporting
  • Korea Internet & Security Agency (KISA): Manages ISMS certification

Unlike some EU jurisdictions under MiCA, South Korea does not have a tiered capital system for VASPs. Instead, it focuses on operational requirements such as real-name accounts and ISMS. The FIU also requires that VASPs report all transactions above a certain threshold and maintain records for five years. Failure to comply can result in fines up to KRW 50 million.

Capital Requirement

South Korea does not impose a fixed minimum capital requirement for VASP registration. However, the FIU expects that the company has sufficient capital to operate and meet its obligations. In practice, authorities may look for at least KRW 500 million (approximately EUR 350,000) in paid-up capital as a sign of financial soundness, but this is not a published rule.

For comparison, under the EU's MiCA framework, capital requirements range from EUR 50,000 to 150,000 depending on service type. Tax Treatment

South Korea taxes cryptocurrency gains as other income. As of 2026, a 20% tax (plus local surtax) applies to annual crypto gains exceeding KRW 2.5 million (approximately EUR 1,750). NFT marketplace operators must also pay corporate income tax at standard rates (9-24% for domestic companies).

Value-added tax (VAT) of 10% may apply to marketplace fees. For example, if you charge a 2% commission on NFT sales, that fee is subject to VAT. Foreign companies without a permanent establishment may be subject to withholding tax on certain income, such as royalties from NFT smart contracts. Tax residency and treaty benefits should be reviewed with a local specialist. Allowed Activities

With a VASP registration, you can operate an NFT marketplace that supports the following activities:

  • Minting and listing of NFTs
  • Secondary market trading of NFTs
  • Crypto-to-crypto and crypto-to-fiat transactions
  • Wallet services for NFTs and virtual assets
  • Custody of digital assets (if registered accordingly)

Note that the registration does not automatically permit offering securities tokens or derivatives; those require separate licenses under the Capital Markets Act. You must also implement a real-name account system for fiat transactions and obtain ISMS certification. The FIU may also restrict certain activities, such as use trading, unless specifically authorised.

Step-by-Step Process

The application process involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step:

  1. Pre-assessment: Review your business model against FIU requirements
  2. Company incorporation: Set up a South Korean entity (e.g., stock company) with a minimum of two directors
  3. ISMS certification: Obtain from KISA (takes 3-6 months) - includes security audits and policy development
  4. Bank partnership: Secure a real-name account with a local bank like Kookmin or Shinhan
  5. Document preparation: AML policy, business plan, risk assessment, organisational chart
  6. Submit application: File with the FIU via the electronic system
  7. Review & approval: FIU reviews within 3-6 months, may request additional information
  8. Post-registration: Ongoing reporting, annual audit, and compliance maintenance

Each stage requires careful attention. We help you avoid common pitfalls and streamline the process. For example, we recommend starting ISMS certification before incorporation to save time.

Banking & Payments

One of the most challenging aspects of obtaining a South Korean VASP registration is securing a real-name account from a local bank. Major banks like Kookmin, Shinhan, and Woori have strict due diligence processes. The bank will review your AML policies, ISMS certification, and business model. They may also require a minimum deposit or collateral.

Without a real-name account, you cannot process fiat deposits and withdrawals for users, which is mandatory for compliant operations. Some banks have refused to work with crypto companies altogether. For payment processing, you may also integrate crypto-to-fiat gateways like those offered by local payment processors.

Compliance & Trust

Compliance is non-negotiable in South Korea. The FIU conducts regular inspections and imposes fines or suspension for non-compliance. Key obligations include:

  • Transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting (STR) within 3 days
  • Customer due diligence (CDD) and KYC for all users
  • Record keeping for 5 years, including transaction logs and identification data
  • Annual external audit by a licensed accounting firm
  • ISMS maintenance and periodic security updates

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a license to run an NFT marketplace in South Korea?

Yes, if your marketplace facilitates the trading of NFTs using cryptocurrencies or fiat currency, you must register as a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) with the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU). This includes platforms that allow minting, listing, or secondary sales of NFTs involving virtual assets.

What is the cost of a South Korean crypto NFT marketplace license?

The total cost typically ranges from EUR 67,000 to 147,000, including legal advisory, ISMS certification, bank account setup, and application fees. Exact pricing depends on your business complexity and is confirmed in a consultation with Consulting24.

How long does it take to get a VASP registration in South Korea?

The process usually takes 6 to 12 months. Key time-consuming steps include ISMS certification (3-6 months) and securing a real-name bank account (2-4 months). The FIU review itself takes 3-6 months.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a VASP in South Korea?

There is no official minimum capital requirement, but the FIU expects sufficient capital to operate. In practice, authorities may look for at least KRW 500 million (approximately EUR 350,000) as a sign of financial soundness.

Can a foreign company apply for a South Korean VASP license?

Yes, foreign companies can apply, but they must incorporate a local entity in South Korea and comply with all local requirements, including ISMS certification and a real-name bank account. Consulting24 advises international firms on this process.

What taxes apply to NFT marketplace operators in South Korea?

Corporate income tax rates range from 9% to 24%. A 20% tax (plus surtax) applies to crypto gains exceeding KRW 2.5 million. VAT of 10% applies to marketplace fees. Foreign companies may face withholding tax on certain income.

What is ISMS certification and why is it required?

ISMS (Information Security Management System) certification is a mandatory security standard issued by KISA. It requires implementing security controls, policies, and audits to protect user data and assets. It typically takes 3-6 months to obtain.

What are the main challenges in the South Korean licensing process?

The biggest challenges are securing a real-name bank account (many banks refuse crypto firms) and obtaining ISMS certification. Incomplete documentation and lack of local legal counsel are common mistakes that cause delays.

How does South Korea compare to Panama for NFT marketplace licensing?

Panama is much faster (2-3 months) and cheaper (EUR 6,000 flat) with no capital requirement and 0% offshore tax. South Korea offers access to a large local market but is more expensive and time-consuming. Consulting24 delivers directly in Panama.

Can I operate an NFT marketplace in South Korea without a license?

No, operating without a VASP registration is illegal and can result in fines, criminal penalties, and platform shutdown. The FIU actively monitors and penalises unregistered platforms, including foreign ones targeting Korean users.

What ongoing compliance is required after registration?

You must conduct transaction monitoring, file suspicious activity reports, perform KYC, maintain records for 5 years, undergo annual external audits, and keep ISMS certification current. Failure to comply can lead to fines or license revocation.
