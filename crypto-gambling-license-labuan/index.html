What is a Labuan Crypto Gambling License?

The Labuan crypto gambling license is a regulatory approval issued by the Labuan Financial Services Authority (LFSA) that permits companies to operate gambling platforms where bets are placed and settled using cryptocurrencies. It combines elements of a traditional gambling license with crypto-specific provisions, ensuring anti-money laundering (AML) compliance and consumer protection.

Labuan is a federal territory of Malaysia with its own legal system, offering a business-friendly environment. The license is available to both domestic and international operators, but the company must be incorporated in Labuan. The LFSA regulates the license under the Labuan Financial Services and Securities Act 2010 and the Labuan Business Activity Tax Act 1990.

Key features include a low corporate tax rate of 3% on net profits, no withholding tax, and no capital gains tax. The license is valid for one year and renewable annually. As of 2026, the LFSA has updated its guidelines to address crypto-specific risks, including wallet security and responsible gambling measures.

License Type and Regulator

The Labuan crypto gambling license is issued by the Labuan Financial Services Authority (LFSA), the sole regulator for financial services in Labuan. The license type is a "Labuan Digital Gambling License" under the Labuan Financial Services and Securities Act 2010. It is not a traditional gambling license but a hybrid that covers both financial services (crypto exchange) and gambling operations.

The LFSA requires the licensee to hold a Labuan company with a registered office in Labuan. The company must appoint at least two directors (individuals or corporate) and a company secretary. The license is divided into two classes:

  • Class A: For operators offering multiple gambling products (casino, sportsbook, poker).
  • Class B: For single-product operators or smaller platforms.

The LFSA conducts due diligence on shareholders, directors, and key personnel. The regulator also requires a local compliance officer and a physical office in Labuan (shared office space is acceptable). As of 2026, the LFSA has increased scrutiny on source of funds and AML policies, aligning with FATF recommendations.

Capital Requirement

The Labuan crypto gambling license has a minimum paid-up capital requirement. As of 2026, the LFSA mandates a minimum of EUR 100,000 (or equivalent in MYR) for Class A licenses and EUR 50,000 for Class B. This capital must be deposited in a Labuan bank account and maintained throughout the license period.

The capital serves as a financial guarantee for player funds and operational stability. It can be used for business expenses but must not fall below the minimum. The LFSA may require additional capital if the operator handles high transaction volumes or offers high-risk games. Consulting24 recommends capitalizing the company with at least EUR 150,000 to cover initial setup and operational costs.

Tax Treatment

Labuan offers one of the most attractive tax regimes for crypto gambling operators. The corporate tax rate is a flat 3% on net audited profits. There is no withholding tax on dividends, no capital gains tax, and no stamp duty. This compares favorably to other jurisdictions like Malta (5% effective) or the UK (19%).

However, the 3% rate applies only if the company is a Labuan entity and does not trade in Malaysian Ringgit. Operators must file annual audited accounts with the LFSA. The tax year is the calendar year, and returns are due within 7 months of year-end. Consulting24 advises on tax structuring to optimize your global tax position.

Allowed Activities

Under the Labuan crypto gambling license, operators can offer the following activities:

  • Online casino games (slots, table games, live dealer) using crypto.
  • Sports betting (pre-match and in-play) with crypto deposits.
  • Poker rooms and peer-to-peer gaming.
  • Lottery and bingo platforms.
  • Esports betting.
  • Fantasy sports.

The license does not permit: gambling on financial markets (binary options), gambling targeting Malaysian residents, or offering fiat currency gambling without a separate license. Crypto-to-crypto transactions are allowed, but the operator must implement AML/KYC procedures and verify player identities. The LFSA requires that all games use provably fair algorithms and that player funds are held in segregated wallets.

Founders often make these errors when applying for a Labuan crypto gambling license:
