What Is the Crypto Payment Institution License Georgia?

The crypto payment institution license Georgia is a regulatory authorization issued by the National Bank of Georgia (NBG). It allows licensed entities to offer virtual asset services such as buying, selling, exchanging, transferring, and safeguarding cryptocurrencies. This license is part of Georgia's efforts to create a transparent and secure environment for digital finance, attracting both local and international businesses.

Georgia's regulatory framework for crypto assets is based on the Law on the National Bank of Georgia and subsequent regulations. The NBG classifies virtual asset service providers (VASPs) as payment institutions, subjecting them to anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorist financing (CFT) obligations, capital requirements, and regular reporting.

Key features of the license include:

  • Regulated by the NBG, a reputable central bank.
  • Allows a wide range of crypto activities (see allowed activities section).
  • Compliance with FATF recommendations.
  • Access to the Georgian market and potential passporting benefits within the region.

Who Needs This License?

The crypto payment institution license Georgia is essential for any business that intends to provide virtual asset services to Georgian residents or operate from Georgia. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralized and peer-to-peer platforms).
  • Wallet providers offering custodial services.
  • Payment processors facilitating crypto-to-fiat conversions.
  • OTC trading desks.
  • Token issuers (if the tokens are considered virtual assets under NBG rules).

If your business model involves holding customer funds or executing transactions in crypto, you likely need this license. Operating without it can result in fines, suspension, or criminal liability. Even if your company is based abroad, providing services to Georgian clients may trigger licensing requirements.

License Type and Regulator

The relevant regulator for the crypto payment institution license Georgia is the National Bank of Georgia (NBG). The NBG oversees the financial system, including payment institutions and virtual asset service providers. The license is formally called a "Virtual Asset Service Provider License" or "Payment Institution License for Virtual Assets." It is a single license that covers multiple activities.

Georgia does not have a separate licensing regime for crypto assets; instead, it integrated VASPs into the existing payment institution framework. This means applicants must meet the same standards as traditional payment institutions, with additional requirements specific to virtual assets.

The NBG requires applicants to be incorporated as a legal entity in Georgia, have a physical office, and appoint a local compliance officer. The license is non-transferable and must be renewed periodically. Consulting24 advises clients on the full scope of regulatory expectations and helps prepare the necessary documentation.

Capital Requirement

The National Bank of Georgia imposes a minimum capital requirement for crypto payment institution license applicants. As of 2026, the minimum initial capital is GEL 500,000 (approximately EUR 160,000). This capital must be fully paid up before the license is granted and maintained throughout the license period.

The capital can be in the form of cash or liquid assets acceptable to the NBG. It serves as a buffer to cover operational risks and ensure the institution's financial stability. For businesses offering additional services (e.g., custody of client assets), the NBG may require higher capital based on risk assessment.

Tax Treatment

Georgia offers a favorable tax regime for crypto businesses. Corporate income tax is 15%, but with a special regime: profits are taxed only when distributed (e.g., as dividends). This means reinvested profits are tax-deferred, which is attractive for growing companies.

Value-added tax (VAT) is 18%, but crypto transactions may be exempt depending on their nature. The Georgian Revenue Service has issued guidance that certain crypto-to-crypto trades are outside the scope of VAT. However, fiat-to-crypto transactions and fees for services may be subject to VAT.

Personal income tax for employees is a flat 20%. There are also tax treaties with many countries to avoid double taxation. Consulting24 recommends working with a local tax advisor to optimize your structure. We can coordinate with our network of tax professionals in Georgia.

Allowed Activities

Under the crypto payment institution license Georgia, the following activities are permitted:

  • Exchange between virtual assets and fiat currencies.
  • Exchange between one or more forms of virtual assets.
  • Transfer of virtual assets (on behalf of clients).
  • Safekeeping and administration of virtual assets (custody).
  • Participation in and provision of financial services related to an issuer's offer and sale of virtual assets.

The NBG may also authorize ancillary services such as staking, lending, and margin trading, subject to additional requirements. The license does not cover activities that qualify as securities under Georgian law; for those, a separate securities license may be needed.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto payment institution license in Georgia?

The National Bank of Georgia requires a minimum initial capital of GEL 500,000 (approximately EUR 160,000) for a crypto payment institution license. This must be fully paid up before the license is granted.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Georgia?

The typical processing time is 3 to 6 months from submission of a complete application. Pre-application preparation can add 1-2 months. Consulting24 can help streamline the process.

Can a foreign company apply for a Georgia crypto license?

Yes, but the applicant must incorporate a legal entity in Georgia (usually an LLC) and have a physical office and local compliance officer. The license is issued to the Georgian entity.

What activities are allowed under the Georgia crypto license?

The license permits exchange between virtual assets and fiat, exchange between virtual assets, transfer of virtual assets, safekeeping/custody, and participation in token offerings. Ancillary services may be allowed with additional approval.

Is the Georgia crypto license recognized in the EU?

No, the Georgia license does not provide EU passporting rights. If you need to operate across the EU, consider a license under MiCA in Lithuania or Estonia. Consulting24 can advise on both options.

What are the tax implications for a crypto business in Georgia?

Do I need a local office for the Georgia crypto license?

Yes, the NBG requires a physical office in Georgia. A virtual office is not sufficient. You also need at least one local resident as a director or compliance officer.

What happens if I operate without a license in Georgia?
