What Is a Stablecoin License Crypto License?

A stablecoin license crypto license, under the EU MiCA regulation, is a CASP authorisation that permits the issuance, offering, and provision of services related to stablecoins. Stablecoins are crypto-assets designed to maintain a stable value by referencing a fiat currency (e-money tokens) or a basket of assets (asset-referenced tokens). The licence is issued by the national competent authority of an EU member state, such as the Bank of Lithuania or the Estonian Financial Supervision Authority. It replaces the previous VASP registration and imposes stricter capital, governance, and reserve requirements.

The licence covers all CASP services, including custody, exchange, and transfer of stablecoins. It also mandates that issuers of asset-referenced tokens must have a registered office in the EU and maintain a reserve of assets. The key advantage is the EU-wide passport: once licensed in one member state, you can operate across the entire European Economic Area without additional national licences. Who Needs a Stablecoin License Crypto License?

Any business that intends to issue, offer, or provide services related to stablecoins within the EU needs this licence. This includes:

  • Stablecoin issuers (e-money tokens and asset-referenced tokens)
  • Crypto exchanges listing stablecoins
  • Custodial wallet providers holding stablecoins
  • Payment service providers integrating stablecoin transfers
  • Decentralised finance (DeFi) platforms that intermediate stablecoin services

If you are a startup or an established company from outside the EU, you must set up a legal entity in an EU member state and obtain a CASP licence. License Type and Regulator

The stablecoin license crypto license is a CASP authorisation under MiCA. The regulator is the national competent authority (NCA) of the EU member state where you apply. For example:

  • In Lithuania: Bank of Lithuania
  • In Estonia: Financial Supervision Authority (Finantsinspektsioon)
  • In Latvia: Financial and Capital Market Commission (FCMC)

The licence is a single category covering all CASP services, but capital requirements vary by the services offered. MiCA sets three capital tiers: EUR 50,000 for basic services like custody, EUR 125,000 for exchange services, and EUR 150,000 for full services including trading and issuance. The regulator assesses your AML/CTF policies, governance, IT security, and reserve management. Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for a CASP licence depends on the services you provide:

  • EUR 50,000 for custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • EUR 125,000 for exchange of crypto-assets for fiat currency or other crypto-assets
  • EUR 150,000 for trading, execution of orders, and placement of crypto-assets

These amounts are permanent capital that must be held as equity or equivalent. Additionally, stablecoin issuers must maintain a reserve of assets equal to the value of tokens in circulation, with at least 30% held in highly liquid instruments. The capital must be fully paid up before licence issuance. Consulting24 helps you structure your capital allocation to meet these requirements efficiently. Note that some member states may impose higher capital for specific activities, but the MiCA tiers are the minimum.

Tax Treatment

Tax treatment of stablecoin activities varies by EU member state. Generally, stablecoins are treated as digital assets for tax purposes. Issuance may be subject to VAT in some jurisdictions, while trading and custody are often treated as financial services, exempt from VAT. Corporate income tax applies to profits from stablecoin operations, typically at standard rates (e.g., 15% in Lithuania, 20% in Estonia).

Some countries offer incentives: Estonia has a 0% corporate income tax on retained earnings, while Lithuania has a 15% rate for small companies. Consulting24 advises on tax-efficient structures, including using Panama as a holding company (0% territorial tax) for non-EU operations. However, for EU passporting, the operating entity must be tax resident in the EU. Always consult a tax advisor for your specific situation.

Allowed Activities

A CASP licence allows the following activities related to stablecoins:

  • Issuance of asset-referenced tokens and e-money tokens
  • Custody and administration of stablecoins on behalf of clients
  • Operation of a trading platform for stablecoins
  • Exchange of stablecoins for fiat currency or other crypto-assets
  • Execution of orders for stablecoins
  • Placement of stablecoins
  • Transfer services for stablecoins
  • Advice on stablecoin investments

Stablecoin issuers must also comply with additional requirements: publish a white paper, maintain a reserve, and report to the regulator. The licence does not cover activities that are not listed, such as lending or staking, which may require separate authorisation under MiCA or other EU regulations.
