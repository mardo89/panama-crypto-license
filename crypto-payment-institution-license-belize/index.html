What Is the Crypto Payment Institution License Belize?

The crypto payment institution license Belize is a regulatory authorization granted by the IFSC that permits companies to operate as a payment institution for digital assets. It covers services such as exchanging cryptocurrencies for fiat, custody of digital assets, and facilitating payments using crypto. The license is designed to bring crypto businesses under a formal regulatory umbrella, ensuring compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) standards.

Belize positions itself as a flexible jurisdiction, often compared to other offshore centers. The license is particularly attractive for firms that want a credible regulatory status without the heavy capital requirements of some EU member states. However, as of 2026, Belize has updated its framework to align with international standards, including the FATF recommendations.

Key features include a relatively fast application process (typically 3-6 months) and a corporate tax rate of 0% on offshore income. The license is valid for one year and must be renewed annually. The IFSC has published detailed guidelines for VASPs, which include requirements for cybersecurity, customer due diligence, and transaction monitoring. For example, licensees must implement systems to detect suspicious transactions and report them to the Financial Intelligence Unit.

One concrete example: a crypto exchange based in Belize can offer trading pairs between BTC, ETH, and USD, with the license covering both the exchange and custody of customer assets. The exchange must hold customer funds in segregated accounts and maintain a minimum capital of USD 50,000. The IFSC conducts annual audits to verify compliance.

Who Needs a Crypto Payment Institution License Belize?

This license is suitable for a range of businesses involved in digital asset services. Typical applicants include:

  • Crypto exchanges (both centralized and peer-to-peer platforms)
  • Digital wallet providers
  • Payment processors that handle crypto transactions
  • Custodians of digital assets
  • Firms offering crypto-to-fiat conversion services

If your business model involves holding customer funds or facilitating transfers of digital assets, you likely need this license. Belize also allows companies to serve international clients, making it a hub for cross-border crypto services.

Founders who choose Belize often do so because they want a reputable license without the high capital requirements of jurisdictions like Lithuania or Estonia. Compared to Panama, Belize offers a more structured regulatory framework, while still maintaining tax advantages. For example, a startup offering a crypto payment gateway for e-commerce merchants would find Belize's license appropriate, as it covers payment processing and conversion.

However, if your business involves trading derivatives or securities tokens, you may need additional licensing. The IFSC distinguishes between payment services and securities activities, so it is important to define your scope clearly.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator for the crypto payment institution license Belize is the International Financial Services Commission (IFSC). The IFSC oversees all financial services in Belize, including digital asset businesses. The license is categorized as a "Payment Institution License" under the Financial Services Act, with specific provisions for virtual asset service providers (VASPs).

Belize does not have a separate "crypto license" per se; instead, it integrates crypto activities under the existing payment institution framework. This means applicants must comply with the same AML/CFT requirements as traditional payment institutions, plus additional guidelines for digital assets.

As of 2026, Belize has not adopted the EU's Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) regulation, so its rules remain independent. This can be an advantage for firms that want to avoid the higher capital tiers of MiCA (EUR 50,000-150,000). However, Belize still requires a minimum capital of approximately USD 50,000 for a basic license, with higher amounts for more complex services.

The IFSC has a dedicated VASP unit that reviews applications. They require detailed information about the beneficial owners, source of funds, and business plan. The regulator also expects applicants to have a physical presence in Belize, such as a registered office and a local director or manager.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a crypto payment institution license Belize depends on the scope of services. For basic payment services, the required capital is approximately USD 50,000. For more complex activities such as custody or exchange, the requirement may be higher, typically up to USD 100,000.

This capital must be maintained as liquid funds and cannot be used for operational expenses. It serves as a buffer to protect customer assets. Belize does not have the tiered capital system of MiCA (EUR 50,000/125,000/150,000), so the exact amount is negotiated with the IFSC based on the business plan.

Consulting24 advises clients on structuring the capital to meet regulatory expectations. For precise figures, we recommend a consultation. The capital can be held in a Belize bank account or a recognized financial institution. Proof of capital is required at the time of application.

Example: a company offering exchange and custody services would likely need USD 100,000 in capital. This amount ensures the firm can cover potential losses and operational risks. The IFSC may also require a surety bond or insurance for certain activities.

Tax Treatment

Belize offers a highly favorable tax regime for offshore companies. The corporate tax rate on income derived from outside Belize is 0%. There is no capital gains tax, no withholding tax on dividends, and no VAT on services provided to non-residents.

However, if the company generates income from Belizean sources, that income is subject to the standard corporate tax rate of 25%. Most crypto businesses operate internationally, so they benefit from the 0% rate.

It is important to maintain proper substance and economic activity in Belize to avoid being considered tax resident elsewhere. Consulting24 can help structure your operations to maximize tax efficiency while remaining compliant. For example, having a local office, employing local staff, and holding board meetings in Belize can demonstrate substance.

Belize also has double taxation agreements with a few countries, but these are limited. Most crypto businesses do not rely on treaties. The tax advantages make Belize attractive for holding companies and payment processors.

Allowed Activities

Under the crypto payment institution license Belize, the following activities are typically permitted:

  • Exchange of cryptocurrencies for fiat currency and vice versa
  • Exchange of one cryptocurrency for another
  • Operation of a digital wallet (custodial and non-custodial)
  • Payment processing using digital assets
  • Custody of digital assets on behalf of clients
  • Facilitating token offerings (subject to additional approval)

Activities that are not allowed without further licensing include: operating a securities exchange, offering derivatives, or engaging in banking activities. The IFSC reviews each application to ensure the proposed activities fall within the license scope.

For a full list of permitted activities, consult the IFSC guidelines or speak with our team. The license does not cover decentralized finance (DeFi) protocols directly, but if you operate a centralized interface for DeFi services, you may need to apply for a separate license.

Example: a company that wants to offer a crypto debit card would need both the payment institution license and possibly a card issuing partnership. The IFSC allows such activities if properly structured.

Compliance and Trust

  • Annual AML/CFT training for staff
  • Appointment of a Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO)
  • Regular audits by an approved auditor
  • Submission of annual returns and financial statements

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Applying for a crypto payment institution license Belize can be straightforward if you avoid these pitfalls:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing business plans or AML policies cause delays. Ensure all documents are professionally prepared.
  • Underestimating capital: The IFSC may require more than the minimum if your business plan shows higher risk. Budget accordingly.
  • Ignoring substance requirements: Belize expects a physical presence. A mailbox company will not suffice. You need a registered office and possibly a local director.
  • Poor banking relationships: Some banks reject crypto firms. Start the banking process early and have a backup.
  • Not renewing on time: The license is annual. Late renewal can lead to penalties or suspension.

Consulting24 helps you avoid these mistakes by providing a checklist and timeline. For example, we recommend starting the bank account application at the same time as the license application, as it can take 4-8 weeks.

Another common mistake is not understanding the scope of allowed activities. If you plan to offer services beyond the license, you risk regulatory action. Always confirm with the IFSC before expanding.

Panama offers a simpler company setup (flat EUR 6,000) but lacks a dedicated crypto license. Crypto businesses in Panama operate under general business laws, which may not satisfy some partners or banks. Belize provides a formal license with regulatory oversight, which builds trust.

Belize vs. Lithuania

Lithuania has a crypto license under MiCA with capital requirements of EUR 125,000 for exchange and custody. The process takes 4-6 months. Belize is cheaper but not EU-recognized. If you need to serve EU customers, Lithuania is better. See our comparison page.

Belize vs. Estonia

Estonia also has a crypto license under MiCA, with similar capital requirements. Estonia's e-residency program makes it easy to incorporate remotely. However, Belize's tax regime (0% offshore) is more favorable than Estonia's 20% corporate tax. For tax efficiency, Belize wins.
