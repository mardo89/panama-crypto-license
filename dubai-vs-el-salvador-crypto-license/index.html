What Is a Dubai Crypto License?

The Dubai crypto license is issued by the Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA), established under Law No. 4 of 2022. VARA regulates all virtual asset service providers (VASPs) operating in the Emirate of Dubai (excluding the Dubai International Financial Centre). The license covers activities such as exchange, custody, transfer, and advisory services. As of 2026, VARA has a tiered licensing structure based on the type and scale of services offered.

Key features of the Dubai regime include a mandatory presence requirement (physical office in Dubai), strong AML/CFT compliance, and a focus on investor protection. The regulatory framework is aligned with FATF recommendations and is considered one of the most comprehensive in the Middle East. For example, a full exchange license requires a minimum capital of EUR 500,000, while a smaller advisory license may require EUR 50,000. The timeline is typically 6-12 months, and total costs including legal setup and office rent can range from EUR 80,000 to EUR 150,000.

What Is an El Salvador Crypto License?

El Salvador's crypto license is governed by the Bitcoin Law (2021) and the Digital Assets Issuance Law (2023). The regulator is the National Commission of Digital Assets (CNAD). The license allows businesses to operate as digital asset service providers, including exchanges, custodians, and payment processors. El Salvador offers a unique advantage: Bitcoin is legal tender, and there is zero capital gains tax on Bitcoin transactions.

The licensing process is relatively straightforward, with no minimum capital requirement for most activities. However, companies must demonstrate strong AML/CFT procedures. The timeline is typically 2-3 months, making it one of the faster options in the Americas. Total costs are low, ranging from EUR 5,000 to EUR 15,000, but local representation is required. For founders on a tight budget, Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 fee with no capital requirement and a 4-6 week timeline.

Who Needs These Licenses?

Both licenses are suitable for crypto businesses targeting specific markets. The Dubai license is ideal for companies seeking access to the Middle East and Asian markets, with a strong regulatory framework that enhances credibility. It is particularly suited for exchanges, custodians, and DeFi platforms that require a strong legal environment. For example, a crypto exchange wanting to serve UAE residents must have a VARA license.

The El Salvador license appeals to Bitcoin-focused businesses, payment processors, and startups looking for a tax-efficient jurisdiction with a pro-crypto government. It is also a good option for companies that want to avoid high capital requirements. For founders needing a quick and affordable entry, Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 license with no capital requirement and a 4-6 week timeline.

Capital Requirement

Dubai: VARA imposes a minimum capital requirement that varies by license category. For example, a full exchange license may require EUR 500,000 or more, while a smaller advisory license may require EUR 50,000. As of 2026, these figures are subject to change based on VARA's latest regulations. Capital must be maintained in a local bank account.

El Salvador: There is no statutory minimum capital requirement for most digital asset service providers. However, the CNAD may require proof of sufficient financial resources to cover operational costs and liabilities. This makes El Salvador attractive for startups.

Panama has no capital requirement for its crypto license, further reducing the barrier to entry. For more details, see our requirements page.

Tax Treatment

Dubai: 0% corporate tax for most businesses, no VAT on crypto transactions, and no capital gains tax on virtual assets. However, as of 2026, a 9% corporate tax applies to profits exceeding AED 375,000 (approx. EUR 95,000) for certain businesses. Crypto companies should confirm their tax status with a local advisor.

El Salvador: 0% capital gains tax on Bitcoin, 0% corporate tax on Bitcoin-related income, and no VAT on digital asset transactions. Other cryptocurrencies may be subject to standard tax rates (30% corporate income tax). This makes El Salvador extremely tax-efficient for Bitcoin-focused businesses.

Panama: No corporate tax on offshore income, no capital gains tax, and no VAT on crypto transactions. For a detailed comparison, see our vs Lithuania page.

Allowed Activities

Dubai: VARA licenses cover exchange (spot and derivatives), custody, transfer, advisory, and issuance of virtual assets. DeFi platforms and NFT marketplaces may also be licensed under specific categories. Activities must be conducted from a physical office in Dubai. For example, a licensed exchange can offer spot trading, margin trading, and staking services.

El Salvador: The license covers exchange, custody, payment processing, and issuance of digital assets (including security tokens). Bitcoin-related activities are explicitly permitted. The regime is less prescriptive than Dubai's, allowing more flexibility. For instance, a payment processor can integrate Bitcoin Lightning Network without additional approvals.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Dubai: Pre-application consultation with VARA -> Submit application with business plan, AML policies, and financials -> VARA initial review (2-3 months) -> In-principle approval -> Set up legal entity and physical office -> Final approval and license issuance (total 6-12 months).
  2. El Salvador: Register a local company -> Prepare AML/CFT manual -> Submit application to CNAD -> CNAD review (1-2 months) -> License issuance (total 2-3 months).

Banking and Payment Processing

Dubai: Banking is relatively accessible once licensed. Major banks like Emirates NBD and Mashreq offer accounts to VARA-licensed firms, though compliance is strict. Payment processors like Checkout.com and Stripe are available. However, banks may require a minimum balance and charge monthly fees.

El Salvador: Banking is more challenging due to the country's limited international banking network. However, the government's pro-Bitcoin stance has led to the emergence of crypto-friendly banks like Bancoagrícola. Payment processing via Bitcoin Lightning Network is widely supported. Compliance and Trust Considerations

Dubai's VARA is known for rigorous enforcement. Licensees must submit regular financial reports, undergo annual audits, and maintain a compliance officer. Failure to comply can result in fines or license revocation. This high standard enhances trust among partners and customers. For example, a licensed exchange must implement transaction monitoring and report suspicious activities.

El Salvador's CNAD is less stringent but still requires AML/CFT compliance. The country's reputation as a Bitcoin pioneer can be a marketing advantage, but some investors may perceive regulatory risk. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Underestimating costs: Dubai's total cost can exceed EUR 150,000 when including office rent and legal fees. Budget accordingly.
  • Ignoring local presence: Dubai requires a physical office; El Salvador requires a local director. Failure to comply can delay licensing.
  • Overlooking tax obligations: Even in tax-free zones, corporate tax may apply above certain thresholds. Consult a tax advisor.
  • Choosing based solely on cost: A cheap license may not provide the credibility or market access you need. Consider your target market.

Frequently asked questions

Which is cheaper: Dubai or El Salvador crypto license?

El Salvador is significantly cheaper, with total costs ranging from EUR 5,000 to EUR 15,000, compared to Dubai's EUR 80,000 to EUR 150,000. However, Dubai offers a stronger regulatory framework and access to Middle East markets. For the lowest cost, Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 license with no capital requirement.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a Dubai crypto license?

The minimum capital requirement varies by license category. For a full exchange license, it is typically EUR 500,000 or more. For smaller advisory licenses, it may be as low as EUR 50,000. These figures are subject to change and should be confirmed with VARA.

Does El Salvador require a physical office for a crypto license?

No, El Salvador does not require a physical office. However, a local director or legal representative is mandatory. This reduces setup costs compared to Dubai, which requires a physical office.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Dubai?

The timeline for a Dubai crypto license is typically 6-12 months. This includes pre-application consultation, VARA review, in-principle approval, company setup, and final approval. Delays can occur if documentation is incomplete.

Can I use a Panama license to serve customers in Dubai or El Salvador?

No, a Panama license only authorizes operations in Panama. To serve customers in Dubai or El Salvador, you must obtain a local license from the respective regulator. However, Panama can be used as a holding company or for non-regulated activities.

What taxes apply to crypto businesses in El Salvador?

El Salvador imposes zero capital gains tax on Bitcoin and zero corporate tax on Bitcoin-related income. Other cryptocurrencies are subject to standard corporate income tax of 30%. There is no VAT on digital asset transactions.

Is a Dubai crypto license recognized internationally?

A Dubai VARA license is recognized within the UAE and is respected globally due to VARA's rigorous standards. However, it does not automatically allow operations in other countries. International recognition depends on bilateral agreements and FATF compliance.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements for an El Salvador crypto license?

Licensees must submit annual reports, maintain AML/CFT procedures, and appoint a compliance officer. The CNAD may conduct periodic audits. Failure to comply can result in fines or license revocation.

Can I apply for a Dubai crypto license remotely?

No, Dubai requires a physical presence. You must establish a legal entity and lease a physical office in Dubai. VARA also requires in-person meetings during the application process. Consulting24 can assist with local setup.

Which license is better for a Bitcoin ATM business?

El Salvador is more suitable due to its Bitcoin-friendly regulations and zero capital gains tax. Dubai's VARA license also covers ATM operations, but the higher cost and capital requirement may be prohibitive for smaller operators.

What is the fastest way to get a crypto license in 2026?

Panama offers the fastest timeline at 4-6 weeks with a flat EUR 6,000 fee. El Salvador takes 2-3 months, and Dubai takes 6-12 months. For speed and cost, Panama is the best option.
