What Is a Cyprus Crypto License?

A Cyprus crypto license, officially a registration as a Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) under CySEC, allows companies to legally offer crypto-related services within Cyprus and across the EU under the MiCA passport. MiCA came into full effect in 2025, and by 2026 all EU member states, including Cyprus, have fully implemented the regime. The license covers services such as exchange between crypto and fiat, exchange between crypto assets, custody of crypto assets, and operation of trading platforms.

Cyprus was an early adopter of crypto regulation, having introduced a registration framework in 2021. With MiCA now fully in force, the Cypriot regime aligns with the EU-wide standards, providing a harmonised approach. This means a CASP registered in Cyprus can serve clients across the EU without needing separate licences in each country, subject to notification procedures.

Who Needs a Cyprus Crypto License?

Any business that provides crypto asset services in or from Cyprus must obtain a CASP registration. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (centralised and decentralised if they have a legal entity in Cyprus)
  • Brokers and dealers in crypto assets
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Operators of crypto ATMs
  • Firms offering crypto payment services
  • Issuers of asset-referenced tokens or e-money tokens (subject to additional rules)

Even if your company is based outside Cyprus but targets Cypriot clients, you may need to register. The MiCA passport allows EU-licensed firms to operate across borders, but non-EU firms must establish a presence in an EU member state. License Type and Regulator

The regulator for crypto licenses in Cyprus is the Cyprus Securities and Exchange Commission (CySEC). Under MiCA, the Cypriot regime categorises CASPs based on the services they offer. There is no separate 'crypto license' per se; instead, firms apply for a CASP registration. The registration covers one or more of the following service categories:

  • Class 1: Exchange between fiat and crypto
  • Class 2: Exchange between crypto assets
  • Class 3: Custody of crypto assets
  • Class 4: Operation of a trading platform
  • Class 5: Other services (e.g., transfer services, advice)

Each class has specific capital requirements (see below). CySEC is known for its thorough but fair oversight, and the application process is rigorous, requiring detailed business plans, AML procedures, and fit-and-proper checks on directors and shareholders.

Capital Requirements

Under MiCA, capital requirements for CASPs are tiered based on the services provided. For Cyprus, the minimum capital must be held as own funds (e.g., share capital plus reserves). The tiers are:

  • EUR 50,000 for Class 1 (fiat-crypto exchange) and Class 2 (crypto-crypto exchange) only
  • EUR 125,000 for Class 3 (custody) or Class 4 (trading platform) alone
  • EUR 150,000 for any combination of services that includes custody or platform operation

These are the minimums; CySEC may require higher capital based on the scale and risk of operations. Capital must be maintained at all times and can be in the form of cash or highly liquid assets. Consulting24 can help structure your capital base.

Tax Treatment for Crypto Businesses in Cyprus

Cyprus offers a competitive corporate tax regime. The standard corporate income tax rate is 12.5%, one of the lowest in the EU. Crypto asset gains are generally treated as capital gains or trading income depending on the business model. Specifically:

  • Capital gains from crypto assets held as investments are tax-free in Cyprus (no capital gains tax for companies).
  • Profits from crypto trading or exchange services are subject to corporate tax at 12.5%.
  • VAT: Crypto-to-fiat exchanges are exempt from VAT; crypto-to-crypto may be subject to VAT depending on the service.
  • Withholding tax on dividends paid to non-residents is 0% (subject to conditions).

Cyprus also has an extensive network of double tax treaties. For personalised tax planning, consult a Cypriot tax advisor.

Allowed Activities Under a Cyprus Crypto License

A Cyprus CASP registration permits the following activities, as defined under MiCA:

  • Providing custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Operating a trading platform for crypto assets
  • Exchanging crypto assets for fiat currency
  • Exchanging crypto assets for other crypto assets
  • Executing orders for crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Placing crypto assets
  • Receiving and transmitting orders for crypto assets
  • Providing advice on crypto assets
  • Managing crypto assets on a discretionary basis

Note that issuing asset-referenced tokens or e-money tokens requires additional authorisation under separate EU regulations. The license does not cover gambling or unlicensed financial services.

Step-by-Step Process to Obtain a Cyprus Crypto License

Here is the typical process for obtaining a Cyprus crypto license. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step.

  1. Company Incorporation: Register a Cyprus limited company with a physical office in Cyprus. The company must have at least two directors (one may be a corporate entity) and a secretary.
  2. Prepare Documentation: Draft a business plan, AML policy, risk assessment, organisational structure, and financial projections. Engage a local AML compliance officer.
  3. Appoint Local Partners: You need a Cyprus-based AML compliance officer, a lawyer, and an auditor. Consulting24 can introduce trusted partners.
  4. Submit Application to CySEC: File the CASP registration application along with all supporting documents and the application fee.
  5. CySEC Review: CySEC will review the application, conduct background checks on directors and shareholders, and may request additional information. This stage takes 3-6 months.
  6. Approval and Ongoing Compliance: Once approved, you must maintain compliance with MiCA, submit periodic reports, and undergo annual audits.

Banking and Payment Services for Crypto Firms

Opening a bank account for a crypto business in Cyprus can be challenging due to perceived risks. However, several banks in Cyprus now accept crypto firms that are CySEC-registered. Options include:

  • Traditional banks: Bank of Cyprus, Hellenic Bank (with compliance checks)
  • EMI (Electronic Money Institutions) and payment processors: e.g., Revolut Business, Monese, PayrNet
  • Correspondent banking through specialised providers

You will need to provide your CASP registration, AML policies, and proof of source of funds. Consulting24 can advise on banking relationships and help prepare your application. Also consider requirements for banking.

Benefits of a Cyprus Crypto License

Cyprus offers several advantages for crypto businesses:

  • EU Passport: Under MiCA, a Cyprus CASP can serve clients across all EU member states without additional licences.
  • Low Corporate Tax: 12.5% corporate tax rate, with no capital gains tax on crypto investments.
  • Established Regulatory Framework: CySEC is a respected regulator with a clear application process.
  • Strategic Location: Cyprus is a gateway between Europe, the Middle East, and Asia.
  • English-Speaking Workforce: Business is conducted in English, and there is a pool of experienced compliance professionals.
  • No VAT on Crypto Exchanges: Crypto-to-fiat exchanges are VAT-exempt.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, you must adhere to ongoing compliance requirements under MiCA and Cypriot law. Key obligations include:

  • AML/CFT: Implement strong AML procedures, conduct customer due diligence (CDD), and report suspicious transactions to the Cypriot Financial Intelligence Unit.
  • Capital Maintenance: Maintain the required minimum capital at all times.
  • Reporting: Submit annual audited financial statements, quarterly reports on transactions, and any material changes to CySEC.
  • Fit and Proper: Directors and shareholders must remain fit and proper; notify CySEC of any changes.
  • Data Protection: Comply with GDPR for handling personal data.

Common Mistakes to Avoid When Applying

Many applicants face delays or rejection due to avoidable errors. Common mistakes include:

  • Incomplete Documentation: Missing business plans, insufficient AML policies, or lack of local presence.
  • Unrealistic Financial Projections: CySEC expects conservative and realistic forecasts.
  • Ignoring Local Requirements: Not appointing a local AML officer or auditor early enough.
  • Poorly Prepared Directors: Directors must demonstrate experience and good character; background checks are thorough.
  • Underestimating Timeline: Expect 6-12 months; rushing leads to errors.

Frequently asked questions

How long does it take to get a Cyprus crypto license?

The typical timeline is 6 to 12 months from company incorporation to CySEC approval. This includes document preparation, submission, and regulatory review. Delays can occur if the application is incomplete or if CySEC requests additional information.

What is the minimum capital for a Cyprus crypto license?

Under MiCA, the minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for simple exchange services, EUR 125,000 for custody or trading platform services, and EUR 150,000 for combined services that include custody or a platform. These amounts must be held as own funds.

Can I operate in the whole EU with a Cyprus crypto license?

Yes, under the MiCA passport, a Cyprus-registered CASP can provide services across all EU member states without needing additional licenses, subject to notification to the host country's regulator.

What is the corporate tax rate for crypto businesses in Cyprus?

The standard corporate tax rate is 12.5%. Capital gains from crypto investments are generally tax-free. Crypto trading income is subject to corporate tax. No VAT is charged on crypto-to-fiat exchanges.

Do I need a physical office in Cyprus?

Yes, you must have a registered office and a physical presence in Cyprus. This can be a serviced office or a leased space. You also need local directors or a local compliance officer.

What documents are required for the application?

You need a detailed business plan, AML policy, risk assessment, organisational chart, financial projections, personal questionnaires for directors and shareholders, and proof of capital. All documents must be in English or officially translated.

Can Consulting24 guarantee approval of my Cyprus crypto license?

No, we cannot guarantee approval. CySEC makes the final decision. However, our advisory services help ensure your application is complete and compliant, reducing the risk of rejection. We have a high success rate.

How much does it cost to apply for a Cyprus crypto license?

Total costs typically range from EUR 28,000 to EUR 58,000, excluding capital. This includes CySEC fees, legal and compliance setup, AML software, and first-year audit. Exact costs depend on the complexity of your business.

Is Cyprus better than Lithuania for a crypto license?

Cyprus offers a lower corporate tax rate (12.5% vs 15%) and a more established regulatory environment. Lithuania has a faster timeline (3-6 months) and lower capital requirements for some services. The choice depends on your priorities. See our comparison page.

What happens if I don't maintain the minimum capital?

Failing to maintain the required capital can result in sanctions, including fines, suspension, or revocation of your license. CySEC monitors capital adequacy through periodic reporting.
