What Is the Bahamas Crypto NFT Marketplace License?

The Bahamas crypto NFT marketplace license is a regulatory authorization granted by the Securities Commission of the Bahamas (SCB) under the DARE Act. It allows businesses to operate a digital asset exchange or marketplace, including platforms that facilitate the trading of non-fungible tokens (NFTs). The license is part of the Bahamas' comprehensive framework for digital assets, which includes registration, ongoing compliance, and investor protection measures.

Key features of the license include:

  • Regulation by the SCB, a well-respected financial regulator.
  • Applicable to various digital asset activities, including NFT marketplaces.
  • Requires a minimum capital of $100,000 (or equivalent in other currencies).
  • No capital gains tax, making it attractive for crypto businesses.
  • Renewal is required annually with ongoing compliance obligations.

The license is designed to provide legal certainty and credibility to NFT marketplaces, helping them attract users and partners. It is important to note that the Bahamas is not a European Union member, so MiCA rules do not apply here.

Who Needs a Bahamas Crypto License for an NFT Marketplace?

Any business that operates a platform for buying, selling, or trading NFTs and other digital assets in or from the Bahamas likely needs to register with the SCB. This includes:

  • NFT marketplaces that facilitate peer-to-peer trading of NFTs.
  • Platforms that issue or list NFTs for sale.
  • Exchanges that allow trading of crypto assets alongside NFTs.
  • Businesses that provide custodial services for digital assets related to NFTs.

If your platform handles customer funds or assets, or if you are based in the Bahamas or serve Bahamian residents, you should consider the DARE registration. License Type and Regulator

The regulator for digital asset businesses in the Bahamas is the Securities Commission of the Bahamas (SCB). The SCB oversees the DARE Act, which establishes a registration regime for digital asset businesses. Unlike a full license, the DARE registration is a mandatory registration for any entity conducting digital asset business in or from the Bahamas.

The registration covers several activities, including operating a digital asset exchange, providing custodial wallet services, and acting as a digital asset broker or dealer. For NFT marketplaces, the relevant category is typically "digital asset exchange" or "digital asset trading platform." The SCB has the authority to impose conditions and conduct inspections.

Capital Requirement

The Bahamas DARE Act requires a minimum capital of $100,000 for digital asset businesses. This capital must be maintained at all times and can be in the form of cash or liquid assets. The SCB may require a higher capital amount depending on the scale and risk profile of the business.

It is important to note that this capital is not a fee but a regulatory requirement to ensure the business has sufficient financial resources. Tax Treatment

The Bahamas is known for its tax-friendly environment. There is no corporate income tax, no capital gains tax, no VAT, and no withholding tax on dividends or interest. This makes it an attractive jurisdiction for crypto and NFT businesses.

However, businesses must pay an annual business license fee (typically a percentage of gross revenue, capped at a certain amount) and may be subject to stamp duties on certain transactions. The tax regime is straightforward, but professional advice is recommended to ensure compliance with local laws.

For comparison, Panama also offers territorial taxation (no tax on foreign-source income) and no capital gains tax on crypto, with a flat company setup fee of EUR 6,000. Panama's tax system is similarly attractive for international crypto businesses.

Allowed Activities Under the Bahamas Crypto License

The Bahamas DARE registration allows a wide range of digital asset activities, including:

  • Operating a digital asset exchange (including NFT marketplace).
  • Providing custodial wallet services.
  • Acting as a digital asset broker or dealer.
  • Issuing digital assets (subject to additional requirements).
  • Providing payment services using digital assets.

NFT marketplaces specifically are allowed to list, trade, and facilitate the transfer of NFTs. The SCB expects platforms to implement strong AML/KYC procedures and to safeguard customer assets. Activities that are not permitted include operating as an unregistered securities exchange or engaging in fraudulent practices.

  • Implementing and updating AML/KYC procedures.
  • Submitting periodic financial reports and audit reports.
  • Notifying the SCB of any material changes to the business.
