What Is the Crypto Gambling License Abu Dhabi?

The crypto gambling license Abu Dhabi is a financial services permission issued by the ADGM FSRA under its virtual asset regime. It allows firms to operate a multilateral trading facility (MTF) or provide custody and other services for virtual assets used in gambling. The license is not a traditional gambling permit but a financial services license tailored for crypto-based betting and gaming activities.

Key features include:

  • Regulated by ADGM FSRA, a respected international regulator.
  • Full compliance with FATF recommendations and UAE anti-money laundering laws.
  • Access to a 0% corporate tax environment and no personal income tax.
  • Ability to serve both retail and institutional clients.

This license is ideal for operators who want a high-trust, transparent jurisdiction that appeals to serious investors and partners. Who Needs This License?

The crypto gambling license Abu Dhabi is designed for businesses that facilitate gambling transactions using cryptocurrencies. Typical applicants include:

If your business involves accepting crypto bets, paying out winnings in crypto, or operating a tokenised gambling ecosystem, you need this license. The ADGM does not regulate traditional fiat gambling; only virtual asset activities fall under its scope. License Type & Regulator

The relevant regulator is the Abu Dhabi Global Market Financial Services Regulatory Authority (ADGM FSRA). The license is a Financial Services Permission (FSP) for Virtual Asset activities, specifically:

  • Operating a Multilateral Trading Facility (MTF) for virtual assets used in gambling.
  • Providing custody of virtual assets for clients.
  • Arranging deals in virtual assets (brokerage).
  • Managing virtual assets on behalf of clients.

The FSRA has published a detailed regulatory framework for virtual assets, including rules on market conduct, client asset protection, and technology governance. As of 2026, the regime is fully operational and has licensed several crypto exchanges and custodians. Gambling-specific activities are permitted if the operator complies with all FSRA rules and obtains the appropriate licence category. The FSRA also requires a physical presence in Abu Dhabi, including a registered office and a local director or agent. Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a crypto gambling license in Abu Dhabi is $150,000 (or equivalent in crypto). This must be held in liquid assets and maintained throughout the license period. The capital serves as a buffer for operational risks and client protection.

The FSRA may require higher capital depending on the scale of operations, number of clients, and risk profile. For example, firms holding client assets (custody) may need up to $500,000. Consulting24 helps structure your capital allocation to meet regulatory expectations. In contrast, Panama has no minimum capital requirement, making it a lower-entry option for startups. Additionally, the capital must be held in a UAE bank account or a recognised financial institution, and evidence of the funds must be provided during the application. Tax Treatment

Abu Dhabi offers a 0% corporate tax on profits from licensed activities. There is no personal income tax, capital gains tax, or withholding tax. This makes the ADGM one of the most tax-efficient jurisdictions for crypto gambling operators.

However, operators must comply with UAE VAT rules (5% on taxable supplies) if applicable. Gambling services may be exempt from VAT in some cases. Consulting24 recommends a tax review to optimise your structure. Compare with Lithuania (15% corporate tax) or Estonia (20% distributed profits tax). The 0% tax rate in Abu Dhabi is a major draw, but operators should also consider the cost of living and business setup expenses, which are higher than in Panama or Lithuania.

Allowed Activities

Under the ADGM crypto gambling license, permitted activities include:

  • Operating a crypto betting platform (sports, esports, casino games).
  • Issuing and managing gambling tokens (subject to prospectus rules).
  • Providing crypto custody for player funds.
  • Running a peer-to-peer betting exchange using virtual assets.
  • Facilitating crypto-to-crypto gambling transactions.

The FSRA does not permit activities that involve fiat currency gambling or unlicensed lottery schemes. All virtual asset activities must comply with the FSRA's rules on market abuse, client disclosure, and technology risk.
