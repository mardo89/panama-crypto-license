What Is the Crypto Fund License in Turkey?

The crypto fund license in Turkey is a regulatory authorization issued by the Capital Markets Board (SPK) that allows a fund to invest primarily in crypto assets. As of 2026, Turkey has implemented specific regulations under the Capital Markets Law, requiring any fund that allocates more than a defined threshold (typically 50%) of its portfolio to crypto assets to obtain a special license. This license is distinct from a general investment fund license and imposes additional compliance, custody, and disclosure requirements.

Key features include: the fund must be established as a venture capital fund or a mutual fund, with a focus on crypto assets. The SPK requires a minimum capital of TRY 10 million (approximately EUR 300,000 as of 2026 exchange rates) for the fund manager, and the fund itself must have a minimum initial size of TRY 5 million. The license is not a passport to the EU but allows operation within Turkey and with Turkish investors. Consulting24 advises on structuring your fund to meet SPK standards.

Who Needs a Crypto Fund License in Turkey?

Any entity that manages a collective investment scheme focused on crypto assets and offers shares to Turkish investors (retail or professional) must hold this license. This includes:

  • Venture capital funds investing in crypto startups or tokens.
  • Hedge funds trading digital assets.
  • Index funds tracking crypto benchmarks.
  • Funds of funds with crypto exposure.

If you are a foreign fund manager looking to raise capital from Turkish investors, you will likely need a local SPK-licensed entity. Exemptions exist for private placements to qualified investors only, but even then, registration is often required. License Type and Regulator: SPK

The regulator is the Capital Markets Board of Turkey (Sermaye Piyasası Kurulu, SPK). The license type is a "Crypto Asset Fund Manager License" (Kripto Varlık Fon Yöneticisi Lisansı) under the Communiqué on Principles Regarding Crypto Asset Funds (III-52.5). This is a specific license category introduced in 2024 and fully operational in 2026. It applies to both domestic and foreign fund managers establishing a fund in Turkey.

Key regulatory documents include the SPK's guidelines on custody, valuation, and risk management. The SPK requires the fund's depositary to be a Turkish bank or an authorized custodian. Additionally, the fund must appoint a portfolio management company that holds a separate license. Capital Requirements for a Crypto Fund in Turkey

The SPK imposes two capital thresholds: one for the fund manager and one for the fund itself. As of 2026, the fund manager (the entity that establishes and manages the fund) must have a minimum paid-in capital of TRY 10 million, which is approximately EUR 300,000. This capital must be maintained throughout the fund's life. The fund itself must have a minimum initial size of TRY 5 million (EUR 150,000), and this amount must be raised from investors within 12 months of approval.

These capital requirements are higher than many EU jurisdictions but reflect Turkey's desire to ensure fund stability. The capital can be held in Turkish Lira or foreign currency, but the SPK prefers Lira for regulatory reporting. Consulting24 can help you structure the capital infusion, including options for using crypto assets as part of the capital (subject to SPK approval). Note that capital requirements are subject to change; always verify with the SPK.

Tax Treatment for Crypto Funds in Turkey

Turkey has a specific tax regime for investment funds, including crypto funds. As of 2026, crypto funds are generally treated as investment funds under the Corporate Tax Law. The fund itself is exempt from corporate tax on its investment income, including gains from crypto asset trading. However, the fund manager pays corporate tax at the standard rate of 20% on its management fees and other income.

Investors (fund participants) are taxed on their share of the fund's income when they redeem their units. For individual Turkish residents, capital gains from fund units are subject to a 10% withholding tax if held for less than one year, and 0% if held longer. Non-resident investors may be exempt from Turkish tax on fund gains, depending on double tax treaties. Consulting24 recommends consulting a Turkish tax advisor for your specific situation, as tax laws can change. The SPK also requires the fund to disclose tax treatment in its prospectus.

Allowed Activities Under the Turkey Crypto Fund License

The license permits a wide range of activities focused on crypto assets. Specifically, the fund can:

  • Invest directly in cryptocurrencies, tokens, and digital assets listed on SPK-approved exchanges.
  • Participate in initial coin offerings (ICOs) and token sales, subject to SPK approval of the offering.
  • Engage in staking, lending, and yield farming, provided the fund's prospectus discloses these strategies.
  • Use derivatives (futures, options) on crypto assets for hedging or investment purposes.
  • Hold crypto assets in custody with a licensed Turkish bank or authorized custodian.

The fund cannot engage in proprietary trading outside the fund's stated strategy, nor can it provide direct lending to third parties without a separate license. The SPK also restricts use to 2x for crypto funds. Consulting24 can help draft a prospectus that clearly defines your allowed activities to avoid compliance issues. For comparison, other jurisdictions like Panama offer more flexibility but less regulatory clarity.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining the crypto fund license in Turkey involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step with local legal partners.

  1. Pre-application preparation: Define fund strategy, structure, and prepare a draft prospectus. Engage a Turkish custodian bank and portfolio manager.
  2. Company setup: Incorporate the fund manager as a joint stock company (Anonim Şirket) with minimum capital of TRY 10 million. Register with the Trade Registry.
  3. Capital deposit: Deposit the fund manager's capital in a blocked account and obtain a certificate from the bank.
  4. Application submission: Submit the application to the SPK, including prospectus, capital evidence, custodian agreement, and management team CVs. Pay the application fee.
  5. SPK review: The SPK reviews the application within 3 months, may request amendments. Respond promptly.
  6. License issuance: Upon approval, the SPK issues the license. The fund must launch within 6 months and raise the minimum fund size within 12 months.
  7. Post-license compliance: File periodic reports, maintain capital, and comply with ongoing disclosure requirements.

Banking and Payment Solutions for Crypto Funds in Turkey

Opening a bank account for a crypto fund in Turkey can be challenging due to banks' cautious stance on crypto. However, as of 2026, several Turkish banks accept crypto fund accounts, including İşbank, Garanti BBVA, and Akbank, provided the fund is SPK-licensed. The account is used for fiat operations (investor subscriptions, redemptions, fee payments). The fund must also maintain a custody account with a licensed custodian for crypto assets.

For international transfers, the fund can use SWIFT or local payment systems. Consulting24 can introduce you to banks that are crypto-friendly and assist with the account opening process. Note that banks may require a minimum deposit and proof of license. For founders seeking faster fiat on/off ramps, consider integrating with Turkish crypto exchanges that have banking partnerships. However, the SPK requires that all fund transactions be conducted through the licensed custodian.

Compliance and Trust: Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, the fund must adhere to ongoing compliance requirements. These include:

  • Periodic reporting: Submit quarterly and annual reports to the SPK, including portfolio composition, NAV, and risk metrics.
  • Audit: Annual audit by an SPK-approved independent auditor.
  • Custody: All crypto assets must be held with a licensed custodian, with regular reconciliation.
  • Anti-money laundering (AML): Implement AML/KYC procedures for investors and report suspicious transactions to MASAK (Turkey's financial intelligence unit).
  • Disclosure: Publish a prospectus and key investor information document (KIID) updated annually.

Non-compliance can result in fines or license revocation. Consulting24 offers ongoing compliance support, including assistance with reporting and liaising with the SPK. This is general guidance, not legal advice. Always consult a Turkish attorney for specific obligations.

Common Mistakes When Applying for a Turkey Crypto Fund License

Founders often make these errors, which can delay or derail the application:

  • Insufficient capital: Not having the full TRY 10 million for the fund manager paid upfront. The SPK requires proof of capital before application.
  • Poor prospectus: A vague or incomplete prospectus leads to requests for amendments. Include detailed investment strategy, risk factors, and fee structure.
  • Ignoring custodian requirements: The SPK mandates a licensed Turkish custodian. Engaging a foreign custodian will not be accepted.
  • Underestimating timeline: Expect 3-6 months; rushing the process increases error risk.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto fund manager in Turkey?

The fund manager must have a minimum paid-in capital of TRY 10 million (approximately EUR 300,000 as of 2026). This capital must be deposited before applying to the SPK.

How long does it take to get a crypto fund license in Turkey?

The typical timeline is 3 to 6 months from application submission to license approval. Delays can occur if the SPK requests additional information or amendments to the prospectus.

Is the crypto fund license in Turkey recognized in the EU?

No. The Turkey license is not a passport under MiCA. It allows operation within Turkey and with Turkish investors only. For EU access, consider a license in Lithuania or Estonia.

What taxes apply to a crypto fund in Turkey?

The fund itself is exempt from corporate tax on investment income. The fund manager pays 20% corporate tax on fees. Investors pay 10% withholding tax on gains if units are held less than one year, 0% if longer.

Can a foreign entity apply for a Turkey crypto fund license?

Yes, but the fund manager must be incorporated in Turkey as a joint stock company. Foreign shareholders are permitted. Consulting24 can assist with company setup.

What are the allowed activities under the license?

The fund can invest in cryptocurrencies, tokens, ICOs, staking, lending, and crypto derivatives, as long as these are disclosed in the prospectus and comply with SPK rules.

Do I need a Turkish bank account for the fund?

Yes. The fund must open a fiat account with a Turkish bank and a custody account with an SPK-licensed custodian for crypto assets. Several major banks accept licensed funds.

What happens if the fund fails to raise the minimum size within 12 months?

The SPK may revoke the license or impose sanctions. It is important to have a realistic fundraising plan. Consulting24 can help you structure the fund to meet the requirement.

Can I use the Turkey license to manage a fund for non-Turkish investors?

Yes, but the fund must comply with Turkish regulations. Non-resident investors may benefit from tax exemptions under double tax treaties. However, the fund's primary market is Turkey.

Is there a specific license for crypto hedge funds vs venture capital funds?

The SPK license covers both, but the fund must be classified as either a venture capital fund or a mutual fund. The prospectus must clearly define the strategy. Consulting24 advises on the appropriate structure.
