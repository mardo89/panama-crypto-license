What Is the Slovakia Crypto License?

The Slovakia crypto license, officially a MiCA authorization for crypto-asset service providers, is a regulatory approval issued by the National Bank of Slovakia (NBS). It allows companies to offer crypto services legally within Slovakia and, via passporting, across the entire European Union and EEA. The license covers a range of activities defined under MiCA, including custody, exchange, and transfer services.

MiCA came into full force in 2025, and Slovakia transposed the regulation into national law. As of 2026, all crypto service providers targeting EU residents must hold a MiCA license from an EU member state. Slovakia is one of several EU countries where you can obtain this authorization. The NBS is known for its thorough but pragmatic approach, making Slovakia a credible option for serious crypto businesses.

Who Needs a Slovakia Crypto License?

Any business that provides crypto-asset services to clients in Slovakia or the EU generally needs a MiCA license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralized and decentralized platforms that match orders)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Brokers and dealers in crypto-assets
  • Payment services using crypto (e.g., crypto-to-fiat on-ramps)
  • Token issuance platforms (if not exempt under MiCA)
  • Investment firms offering crypto products

If your company is based outside the EU but wants to serve EU customers, you must establish a legal entity in an EU member state like Slovakia and obtain a MiCA license. Non-EU firms cannot rely on reverse solicitation alone for ongoing, systematic services.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator for the Slovakia crypto license is the National Bank of Slovakia (Národná banka Slovenska, NBS). The NBS is the central bank and financial market supervisor. Under MiCA, it is the competent authority for authorizing and supervising CASPs.

The license type is a MiCA CASP authorization. There is no separate national crypto license; MiCA harmonizes the regime across the EU. The NBS issues a single authorization that covers all the services you apply for, subject to capital requirements per service class.

MiCA defines three tiers of capital:

  • EUR 50,000 for certain services like order reception and transmission
  • EUR 125,000 for custody and transfer services
  • EUR 150,000 for exchange services (trading platforms)

These are minimums; the NBS may require higher capital based on risk assessment. The license is valid indefinitely, subject to ongoing compliance.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirements are based on the type of services provided:

  • EUR 50,000 for services such as reception and transmission of orders, advice, and portfolio management.
  • EUR 125,000 for custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients, and transfer services.
  • EUR 150,000 for operating a trading platform (exchange).

If you apply for multiple services, the highest capital requirement applies. The capital must be held in liquid assets and is subject to ongoing prudential supervision. The NBS may also impose additional capital based on the size and risk of your operations.

For comparison, Panama has no minimum capital requirement for its crypto license, making it a lower-cost alternative for non-EU markets. However, Panama does not provide EU passporting.

Tax Treatment

Slovakia's tax treatment of crypto-assets is relatively favorable compared to some EU peers. As of 2026:

  • Corporate income tax: Standard rate is 21% (15% for small businesses with turnover under EUR 100,000).
  • Capital gains on crypto: For individuals, gains from crypto held for more than one year are tax-exempt (subject to conditions). For companies, gains are part of ordinary income.
  • VAT: Crypto-asset services are generally exempt from VAT under the EU VAT Directive, but careful analysis is needed for specific activities.
  • Withholding tax: No withholding tax on dividends paid to EU residents; 35% to non-EU residents unless a tax treaty applies.

Allowed Activities

The Slovakia crypto license allows the following activities under MiCA (Annex I):

  • Providing custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • Operation of a trading platform for crypto-assets
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for other crypto-assets
  • Execution of orders on behalf of clients
  • Placing of crypto-assets
  • Reception and transmission of orders on behalf of clients
  • Providing advice on crypto-assets
  • Providing portfolio management on crypto-assets
  • Providing transfer services for crypto-assets on behalf of clients

You must specify which services you intend to offer in your application. The NBS may impose conditions or restrictions. Activities not listed may require additional authorization under other EU financial services directives.

Step-by-Step Process

Obtaining a Slovakia crypto license involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step.

  2. Company incorporation: Establish a Slovak entity (typically a limited liability company, s.r.o.) with a registered office in Slovakia. Minimum share capital is EUR 5,000.
  3. Documentation preparation: Draft the required policies and procedures: AML/CFT policy, risk assessment, business continuity plan, governance framework, and IT security measures.
  4. Capital deposit: Pay up the minimum capital in a Slovak bank account and obtain confirmation.
  5. Application submission: Submit the application to the NBS via the official portal, including all supporting documents and the application fee.
  6. NBS review: The NBS assesses the application, may request additional information, and conducts interviews with key personnel.
  7. Approval and licensing: If satisfied, the NBS grants the MiCA authorization. You then become a registered CASP.
  8. Ongoing compliance: Maintain AML/CFT procedures, submit regular reports, and undergo audits.

Banking and Payments

Opening a bank account for a crypto business in Slovakia can be challenging, as many traditional banks are cautious about crypto clients. However, there are options:

  • Slovak banks: Some banks like Tatra Banka, Slovenská Sporiteľňa, and ČSOB may open accounts for licensed CASPs, but due diligence is rigorous.
  • EMIs and payment institutions: Electronic money institutions (e.g., Revolut Business, Paysera) often accept crypto companies with a license.
  • International banks: Some EU-based banks specialize in fintech and crypto, such as Solarisbank or ClearBank.

You will need a bank account to hold client funds (if applicable) and to meet capital requirements. Consulting24 can introduce you to banking partners with experience in crypto licensing.

Benefits of a Slovakia Crypto License

Choosing Slovakia for your MiCA authorization offers several advantages:

  • EU passporting: Serve all 27 EU member states and EEA countries from a single license.
  • Credible regulator: The NBS is a respected central bank with growing expertise in crypto.
  • Competitive tax regime: Low corporate tax (21% standard, 15% for small firms) and tax exemption for long-term crypto gains for individuals.
  • Central European location: Access to talent and a growing fintech ecosystem.
  • Relatively fast process: Compared to some other EU states, the NBS aims for a 6-month review period.

However, the capital requirement (EUR 50k-150k) is higher than some non-EU jurisdictions like Panama (EUR 6,000 flat fee, no capital minimum). For businesses focused solely on non-EU markets, Panama may be more cost-effective.

Compliance and Trust

Holding a Slovakia crypto license demonstrates a high level of regulatory compliance, which builds trust with clients, partners, and banks. Key compliance obligations include:

  • AML/CFT program: Implement strong KYC, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting.
  • Prudential requirements: Maintain minimum capital, adequate insurance (e.g., professional indemnity), and sound risk management.
  • Governance: Appoint a board with at least two members, a compliance officer, and an AML officer (can be outsourced).
  • Reporting: Submit periodic reports to the NBS on financials, transactions, and AML.
  • Audits: Annual external audit of financial statements and compliance.

Non-compliance can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. Consulting24 provides ongoing compliance advisory to help you stay on track. This is general guidance, not legal advice.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make these errors when applying for a Slovakia crypto license:

  • Incomplete documentation: Submitting policies that are generic or not tailored to your business model. The NBS expects detailed, specific procedures.
  • Underestimating capital: Trying to meet the minimum but not having enough for operational expenses. The NBS may require more.
  • Ignoring substance requirements: The NBS expects real management presence in Slovakia, not just a mailbox. You need at least one director resident in the EU.
  • Poor AML/CFT framework: A weak AML program is a common reason for rejection. Invest in a strong system.
  • Not engaging experts: Attempting to DIY the application can lead to delays. Professional advisory is strongly recommended.

Frequently asked questions

What is the Slovakia crypto license?

The Slovakia crypto license is a MiCA authorization for crypto-asset service providers, issued by the National Bank of Slovakia. It allows companies to offer crypto services legally in Slovakia and across the EU through passporting.

Who regulates crypto in Slovakia?

The National Bank of Slovakia (NBS) is the competent authority for MiCA licensing and supervision of crypto-asset service providers in Slovakia.

What are the capital requirements for a Slovakia crypto license?

Minimum capital ranges from EUR 50,000 to EUR 150,000 depending on the services offered. For example, custody services require EUR 125,000, while exchange services require EUR 150,000.

How long does it take to get a Slovakia crypto license?

The process typically takes 6 to 12 months from application to approval. This includes preparation, submission, and the NBS review period of up to 9 months.

Can I passport my Slovakia license to other EU countries?

Yes, a MiCA license from Slovakia allows you to provide services in all EU and EEA member states without needing additional licenses, subject to notification procedures.

What is the corporate tax rate in Slovakia for crypto companies?

The standard corporate income tax rate is 21%. Small businesses with turnover under EUR 100,000 may qualify for a reduced rate of 15%.

Do I need a physical office in Slovakia?

Yes, you need a registered office in Slovakia. The NBS expects substance, including local management or at least a director resident in the EU.

What activities are allowed under the Slovakia crypto license?

Allowed activities include custody, exchange, transfer services, order execution, advice, portfolio management, and operation of a trading platform, as defined under MiCA.

How does Slovakia compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Panama offers a lower-cost option (EUR 6,000 flat fee, no capital minimum) but no EU passporting. Slovakia requires higher capital and longer timeline but provides access to the EU market.
