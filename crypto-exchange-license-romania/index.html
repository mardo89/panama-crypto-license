What Is the Crypto Exchange License Romania?

The crypto exchange license Romania is a regulatory authorisation issued by the ASF under the Romanian transposition of MiCA. It allows companies to operate as a crypto-asset service provider (CASP) within Romania and across the EU via passporting. The license covers services such as exchanging crypto for fiat, exchanging crypto for crypto, operating a trading platform, and custody of crypto assets.

Romania's framework is fully aligned with MiCA, which came into full force in 2024. This means that any entity offering crypto services in Romania must hold a CASP license from the ASF. The license is mandatory for both domestic and foreign companies targeting Romanian clients. Who Needs a Crypto Exchange License in Romania?

Any business that provides one or more of the following crypto-asset services to Romanian residents or within Romania must obtain a CASP license from the ASF:

  • Exchange of crypto-assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for other crypto-assets
  • Operation of a crypto-asset trading platform
  • Custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • Placement of crypto-assets (underwriting or placing)
  • Reception and transmission of orders for crypto-assets
  • Advice on crypto-assets

If your business model involves any of these activities, you need a license. Even if you are a non-EU company, you must establish a local presence or partner with a licensed Romanian CASP. Capital Requirement for a Romanian Crypto License

Under MiCA, the capital requirements for CASPs are tiered based on the type of services provided. For a crypto exchange license in Romania, the minimum capital is as follows:

  • For exchange services (fiat-crypto and crypto-crypto): EUR 125,000
  • For operating a trading platform: EUR 150,000
  • For custody services: EUR 125,000
  • For other services (e.g., order reception, advice): EUR 50,000

If your company offers multiple services, the highest capital requirement applies. The capital must be fully paid up and held in a bank account in Romania. Consulting24 assists in structuring your capital and ensuring compliance with ASF's solvency requirements. The capital can be in the form of cash or liquid assets, and it must be maintained throughout the license period.

For example, if you plan to operate both an exchange and a trading platform, you need EUR 150,000. If you only offer custody and advice, EUR 125,000 applies. Consulting24 helps you determine the exact amount based on your service list.

Tax Treatment for Crypto Exchanges in Romania

Romania offers a competitive tax environment for crypto businesses. Corporate income tax is 16% on profits. Additionally, there is a 1% tax on turnover for certain micro-enterprises (if revenue is under EUR 500,000 and the company has fewer than 9 employees). VAT is not applicable to crypto-asset transactions under MiCA, but standard VAT rules apply to other services.

For founders, dividend tax is 8% (plus health insurance contributions). Romania also has a network of double taxation treaties. For a zero-tax alternative, explore Panama, which offers territorial taxation (no tax on foreign-source income).

It is important to note that crypto-to-crypto trades are generally exempt from VAT, but fiat-to-crypto trades may be subject to VAT in some cases. Step-by-Step Process to Obtain a Romanian Crypto License

Obtaining a crypto exchange license in Romania involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step with local legal partners.

  1. Company Setup: Incorporate a Romanian company (typically SRL) with a registered office and local director. This takes 1-2 weeks.
  2. Document Preparation: Draft a business plan, AML/KYC policies, risk management framework, and governance documents. This is the most time-consuming step (4-8 weeks).
  3. Capital Deposit: Open a Romanian bank account and deposit the required minimum capital. This can take 2-4 weeks due to bank due diligence.
  4. Application Submission: Submit the application to the ASF with all supporting documents. The ASF will acknowledge receipt within 10 business days.
  5. ASF Review: The ASF reviews the application, which may take 3-6 months. They may request additional information or clarifications.
  6. Approval and Licensing: Once approved, you receive the CASP license and can start operations. The license is valid indefinitely, subject to ongoing compliance.

Common Mistakes When Applying for a Romanian Crypto License

Founders often make these errors during the application process:
