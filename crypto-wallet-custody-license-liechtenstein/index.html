What Is the Crypto Wallet Custody License in Liechtenstein?

The crypto wallet custody license in Liechtenstein is a regulatory authorization under the TVTG (Token and Trusted Technologies Service Provider Act) that permits a company to hold, store, and safeguard cryptographic keys and digital assets on behalf of clients. This license is specifically designed for wallet service providers, custodians, and any entity that exercises control over private keys.

Liechtenstein was one of the first countries to create a dedicated legal framework for blockchain-based services, and the TVTG has been fully operational since 2020. In 2026, the regime remains aligned with EU AML/CFT standards and is recognized as a gold standard for crypto custody. The license is issued by the Financial Market Authority (FMA) Liechtenstein.

Key features of the license include:

  • Legal certainty for custody of digital assets
  • Comprehensive consumer protection requirements
  • Ongoing compliance and reporting obligations
  • Passporting rights within the European Economic Area (EEA) under certain conditions

For example, a wallet provider holding private keys for 10,000 clients must implement multi-signature security, insurance coverage, and regular audits. The TVTG mandates that custodians segregate client assets from corporate assets, ensuring that client funds are protected even in insolvency. This level of protection is a major reason why institutional investors prefer licensed custodians in Liechtenstein.

Any business that offers custodial wallet services to customers in Liechtenstein or the EEA may need this license. Typical applicants include:

Even if you are based outside Liechtenstein, offering services to Liechtenstein residents may trigger licensing requirements. For instance, a Swiss-based fintech that provides custodial wallets to Liechtenstein users must obtain a license from the FMA or rely on a passport under MiCA. License Type and Regulator

The relevant license under the TVTG is the "Custodian Wallet Provider" license, which falls under the broader category of Token Service Providers. The regulator is the Financial Market Authority (FMA) Liechtenstein, which oversees all financial services, including crypto asset services.

The FMA requires applicants to meet strict criteria regarding governance, capital, risk management, and AML compliance. The license is non-expiring but subject to ongoing supervision. Liechtenstein is part of the EEA, so a license here can facilitate cross-border operations within the EEA under the EU's Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) regulation, which is fully in force in 2026. Under MiCA, a Liechtenstein license allows passporting to other EEA states without additional local licensing, significantly reducing expansion costs.

For comparison, other jurisdictions like Lithuania and Estonia offer different regimes, but Liechtenstein is often preferred for its legal clarity and reputation. Unlike some EU countries that only register VASPs, Liechtenstein issues a full license with rigorous oversight, which enhances trust among partners and clients.

Capital Requirement

Under the TVTG, the minimum capital requirement for a custodian wallet provider is EUR 50,000. This is the same as the base capital requirement for most token service providers. However, if your business model involves additional services (e.g., exchange or proprietary trading), higher capital tiers may apply under MiCA, which is fully in force in 2026. For example, operating a crypto exchange requires EUR 150,000 capital under MiCA, while order execution services require EUR 125,000.

The capital must be fully paid up in cash or highly liquid assets and held in a regulated bank account. The FMA may require additional capital based on the volume of assets under custody and operational risk. For instance, a custodian holding over EUR 10 million in client assets might need to maintain capital of EUR 100,000 or more, depending on the risk assessment. Tax Treatment

Liechtenstein offers a favorable tax environment for crypto businesses. The corporate income tax rate is a flat 12.5%, one of the lowest in Europe. There is no capital gains tax on the sale of crypto assets held by the company, and no withholding tax on dividends paid to non-resident shareholders.

Value-added tax (VAT) treatment of crypto services is generally exempt, following the European Court of Justice's ruling on Bitcoin. However, specific activities may be subject to VAT. For example, if a custodian charges separate fees for reporting or advisory services, those may be VAT-taxable. Liechtenstein has a double taxation treaty network that can help avoid double taxation for international operations.

Allowed Activities

A Liechtenstein crypto wallet custody license permits the following activities:

  • Safe custody of cryptographic keys and digital assets
  • Generation and management of private keys
  • Recovery of lost keys (subject to security protocols)
  • Transfer of digital assets on behalf of clients
  • Integration with third-party services (e.g., exchanges, payment processors)

Additional activities, such as operating a crypto exchange or providing staking services, may require separate licenses or extensions. The TVTG allows for a modular licensing approach, so you can add services as your business grows. For instance, a wallet custodian could later apply for an exchange license extension under the same entity, provided they meet the higher capital requirement. Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a Liechtenstein crypto wallet custody license and a VASP registration in other EU countries?

Liechtenstein issues a full license under the TVTG, which includes comprehensive oversight by the FMA. In contrast, some EU countries only register VASPs with minimal capital requirements. Under MiCA (2026), all EU CASPs must meet capital tiers of EUR 50,000/125,000/150,000, but Liechtenstein's regime is more established and offers greater legal certainty.

Can I passport a Liechtenstein crypto wallet custody license to other EEA countries?

Yes, under MiCA, which is fully in force in 2026, a Liechtenstein license can be passported to other EEA states. This means you can offer services across the EEA without obtaining separate licenses in each country, saving time and cost.

What are the ongoing costs of maintaining a Liechtenstein crypto wallet custody license?

Ongoing costs include annual FMA supervision fees (typically EUR 2,000-5,000), compliance officer salary, AML audit costs (EUR 5,000-15,000 per year), and legal/accounting fees. Total annual costs can range from EUR 15,000 to 50,000 depending on business complexity.

How long does the FMA take to review a license application?

The FMA's review process typically takes 3-6 months after submission of a complete application. Delays can occur if documentation is incomplete or if background checks raise issues. Consulting24 helps ensure your application is complete to minimize delays.

Is it possible to obtain a Liechtenstein crypto wallet custody license without a local office?

Yes, but you must have a registered office in Liechtenstein and a local compliance officer or representative. The FMA requires a physical presence for supervision purposes. Consulting24 can help you set up a registered office and find a local compliance officer.

What happens if my application is rejected by the FMA?

If your application is rejected, the FMA will provide reasons. You can address the issues and resubmit. Common reasons include insufficient AML procedures or unqualified management. Consulting24 reviews your application before submission to reduce the risk of rejection.

Can I offer staking services with a Liechtenstein crypto wallet custody license?

Staking services may require an additional license or extension under the TVTG, as they involve generating returns from client assets. The FMA treats staking as a separate activity. Consulting24 can advise on the scope of your license and whether staking is permitted.

What are the AML requirements for a Liechtenstein crypto wallet custody license?

You must implement strong AML/KYC procedures, appoint a money laundering reporting officer, conduct customer due diligence, and report suspicious transactions to the FMA. Annual AML audits are required. The TVTG aligns with EU AML directives.

How does Liechtenstein's tax treatment compare to Panama for crypto custody?

Panama offers 0% corporate tax on offshore income, while Liechtenstein has a 12.5% corporate tax. However, Liechtenstein provides EEA passporting and a more established regulatory framework, which may be worth the tax cost for businesses targeting European clients.

Can I use a Liechtenstein crypto wallet custody license to serve clients outside the EEA?

Yes, the license allows you to serve clients globally, but you must comply with local laws in the client's jurisdiction. For example, serving US clients may require SEC registration. Consulting24 can help you assess cross-border compliance requirements.
