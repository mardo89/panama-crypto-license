What Is a Crypto Gambling License in Seychelles?

A crypto gambling license in Seychelles is a regulatory authorization issued by the Seychelles Gaming Authority (SGA) that permits operators to offer online gambling services, including those using cryptocurrencies. Seychelles has positioned itself as a hub for iGaming, with a straightforward licensing process and favorable tax regime. The license covers casino games, sports betting, poker, and other gambling activities, and it explicitly allows the use of digital assets for deposits, withdrawals, and wagering.

Unlike some jurisdictions that separate gambling and crypto licensing, Seychelles integrates both under one framework. The SGA oversees compliance, ensuring operators meet anti-money laundering (AML) and responsible gambling standards. The license is valid for one year and renewable annually. As of 2026, the Seychelles regime remains attractive for startups and mid-sized operators due to its relatively low costs and quick timeline. For example, a typical operator can expect to pay between EUR 27,000 and EUR 60,000 in the first year, including company incorporation, legal fees, and the license fee. This is significantly cheaper than a Malta gaming license, which can cost over EUR 100,000 annually.

The SGA requires all operators to use a licensed agent for the application process. This agent acts as a liaison between the applicant and the regulator, ensuring all documentation is complete and compliant. License Type & Regulator

The regulator is the Seychelles Gaming Authority (SGA), established under the Gambling Act 2014. The SGA issues two main license types for online gambling:

  • Bookmaker License - for sports betting and race betting
  • Casino License - for casino games, poker, and other table games

Operators can apply for a combined license covering both. The license is classified as Class 1 (bookmaker) or Class 2 (casino) depending on the activity. As of 2026, the SGA does not have a separate "crypto" license; rather, it permits cryptocurrency use under the standard gambling license. This means you do not need a separate crypto asset service provider license. The application is submitted through a licensed agent or directly by the company, though most applicants use a local representative due to the SGA's preference for local contact.

The SGA conducts thorough due diligence on all beneficial owners, including background checks and source of funds verification. The regulator also requires operators to implement responsible gambling measures, such as self-exclusion tools and deposit limits. Capital Requirement

The Seychelles Gaming Authority does not prescribe a fixed minimum capital requirement for gambling license applicants. However, operators must demonstrate sufficient financial resources to cover operational costs and player payouts. Typically, a minimum paid-up capital of EUR 50,000 is expected by the SGA, though this is not a statutory figure. Some agents recommend EUR 100,000 or more for credibility, especially if you plan to offer high-value jackpots or large sports betting limits.

For example, a startup offering only slots and table games might get by with EUR 50,000, while a sportsbook with significant liability might need EUR 200,000. The SGA will review your business plan and financial projections to assess adequacy. Consulting24 advises clients to prepare a detailed business plan showing funding sources and projected player liabilities. For comparison, Panama has no minimum capital requirement, while EU jurisdictions under MiCA require EUR 50,000 to 150,000 depending on service class. Tax Treatment

Seychelles offers a highly favorable tax regime for gambling operators:

  • Corporate tax: 0% on offshore income (gambling income is considered offshore if the operator is a Seychelles company but serves non-residents)
  • Gambling tax: No specific gambling tax or turnover tax; instead, a flat annual license fee (as above)
  • VAT: No VAT on gambling services
  • Withholding tax: None on dividends or interest paid to non-residents

This makes Seychelles one of the most tax-efficient jurisdictions for crypto gambling. However, operators must ensure they comply with tax laws in the players' home countries. For example, if you target UK players, you may need to register with HMRC and pay UK gambling tax. Consulting24 recommends seeking local tax advice in your target markets. The tax advantages are similar to those in Panama, which also has 0% corporate tax on offshore income. However, Panama does not require an annual license fee; instead, it charges a fixed EUR 6,000 for the license and company setup.

One nuance: Seychelles has a Goods and Services Tax (GST) of 15%, but it does not apply to gambling services provided to non-residents. The SGA also requires that all games offered are fair and transparent, and operators must provide players with clear terms and conditions.

If you plan to offer both casino and sports betting, you should apply for a combined license to avoid separate applications. Step-by-Step Process

  1. Company incorporation - Register a Seychelles International Business Company (IBC) or a Special License Company (CSL) if required. This typically takes 1-2 weeks and costs EUR 2,000-5,000.
  2. Prepare documentation - Business plan, financial projections, AML/KYC policies, source of funds, and personal background checks for directors. The AML policy must be tailored to your business, not a generic template.
  3. Engage a local agent - The SGA requires a licensed agent to submit the application (Consulting24 can coordinate this). Agent fees are typically EUR 3,000-8,000.
  4. Submit application - Pay the application fee (EUR 5,000-10,000) and provide all documents to the SGA. The agent will handle the submission.
  5. SGA review - The authority conducts due diligence on the company and its beneficial owners (8-12 weeks). During this time, the SGA may request additional information or clarifications.
  6. License issuance - Once approved, pay the annual license fee (EUR 15,000-30,000) and receive the license certificate. The license is valid for one year from the date of issuance.
  7. Post-licensing compliance - Implement AML controls, submit annual reports, and renew the license yearly. You must also maintain a physical presence in Seychelles (a registered office and a local director or manager).

Banking & Payments

One of the challenges in Seychelles is opening a bank account for a gambling company. Many international banks are cautious due to perceived risks. However, Seychelles-based banks and some offshore banks accept gambling operators. Alternatives include:

  • EMIs (Electronic Money Institutions) in the EU or UK, such as MisterTango or PayDo
  • Cryptocurrency payment processors (e.g., BitPay, Coinbase Commerce, CoinsPaid)
  • Multi-currency IBAN accounts from fintech providers like TransferWise (now Wise) or Revolut Business

For crypto gambling, most transactions are in digital assets, reducing reliance on fiat banking. However, you may still need a fiat account for paying suppliers or taxes. Compliance & Trust

Operating under a Seychelles license requires adherence to the SGA's AML and responsible gambling rules. Key compliance obligations include:

  • Implementing AML/KYC procedures for all players, including identity verification and transaction monitoring
  • Reporting suspicious transactions to the Seychelles Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) within 24 hours
  • Maintaining player fund segregation (if holding fiat) in a separate trust account
  • Annual audit of financial statements by a Seychelles-approved auditor
  • Renewal of license each year with updated documentation, including a compliance report

While Seychelles is not a high-risk jurisdiction, some payment processors and banks may still view it as such due to its offshore status. To enhance trust, operators often obtain additional certifications like eCOGRA or GLI for game fairness. The SGA also requires that all marketing materials are truthful and not misleading. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Insufficient due diligence - Failing to provide clear source of funds or background checks can delay or reject the application. Prepare bank statements, tax returns, and professional references.
  2. Ignoring AML requirements - The SGA expects strong AML policies; a generic template may not suffice. Tailor your AML program to your business model and risk profile.
  3. Underestimating timeline - While 2-4 months is typical, complex cases can take longer. Factor in potential delays for document collection and SGA queries.
  4. Choosing the wrong license type - Ensure you apply for the correct class (bookmaker vs casino) to avoid reapplication. If you plan to offer both, apply for a combined license from the start.
  5. Neglecting tax obligations elsewhere - Seychelles low tax does not exempt you from taxes in players' countries. For example, if you target German players, you may need to pay German gambling tax.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a Class 1 and Class 2 license in Seychelles?

Class 1 is for bookmaking (sports betting), while Class 2 is for casino games. You can apply for a combined license covering both. The application process and fees are similar, but the scope of activities differs. Choose based on your business model.

Can I use cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin for all gambling activities under a Seychelles license?

Yes, the Seychelles Gaming Authority explicitly allows the use of cryptocurrencies for deposits, withdrawals, and wagering. There is no separate crypto license needed. However, you must comply with AML requirements, including tracking crypto transactions.

How long does it take to get a Seychelles gambling license?

The typical timeline is 2 to 4 months from application submission to license issuance. This includes 1-2 weeks for company incorporation, 8-12 weeks for SGA review, and 1-2 weeks for license issuance after approval. Rushed applications may be possible for an additional fee.

Is there a minimum capital requirement for a Seychelles gambling license?

There is no statutory minimum capital requirement. However, the SGA expects operators to demonstrate sufficient financial resources, typically around EUR 50,000 in paid-up capital. Some agents recommend EUR 100,000 or more for credibility. The exact amount depends on your business plan.

What are the ongoing costs after obtaining the license?

Ongoing costs include annual license renewal (EUR 15,000-30,000), compliance reporting fees, audit costs (EUR 2,000-5,000), and agent fees if you continue using a local representative. You should budget at least EUR 10,000 per year for compliance and legal support.

Do I need to have a physical presence in Seychelles?

Yes, you must have a registered office in Seychelles and a local director or manager. The SGA requires a local contact for compliance purposes. Your licensed agent can often provide a registered office service. Consulting24 can help you arrange this.

Can I target players from the EU with a Seychelles license?

A Seychelles license does not provide EU passporting rights. You must comply with each EU member state's gambling laws separately. Many EU countries require a local license. If you want to target EU players, consider a license from Lithuania or Estonia under MiCA.

How does Seychelles compare to Panama for crypto gambling?

Panama offers a lower cost (EUR 6,000 flat via Consulting24) and a similar timeline (2-3 months). Both have 0% corporate tax on offshore income. Panama has a more established banking environment, while Seychelles has a slightly lower license fee range. Panama does not require a local agent, making it simpler.

What documents are required for the application?

You need a business plan, financial projections, AML/KYC policies, source of funds documentation, personal background checks for directors, and company incorporation documents. All documents must be in English or translated. Consulting24 can help you prepare a complete application package.

Is the Seychelles gambling license recognized globally?

While the license is issued by a reputable regulator, it does not automatically grant access to all markets. Each country has its own gambling laws. The license is best for targeting markets that accept offshore licenses, such as Asia, Africa, and Latin America. For US or EU markets, additional licensing may be required.

Can I switch from a Curacao license to Seychelles?

Yes, you can apply for a Seychelles license even if you already hold a Curacao license. The SGA will consider your existing compliance history. However, you must still meet all Seychelles requirements, including company incorporation and local agent. Consulting24 can assist with the transition.

What happens if my application is rejected?

If the SGA rejects your application, you may appeal the decision or reapply after addressing the deficiencies. Common reasons for rejection include incomplete documentation, insufficient funds, or adverse background checks. Consulting24 can help you identify and fix issues before submission.
