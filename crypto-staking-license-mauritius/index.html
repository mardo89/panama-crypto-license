What is a Crypto Staking License in Mauritius?

A crypto staking license in Mauritius is a regulatory authorisation issued by the Financial Services Commission (FSC) under the Virtual Asset and Initial Token Offering Services Act 2021 (VAITOS Act). It allows licensed entities to offer staking services to clients, where they pool virtual assets and earn rewards on behalf of investors. The license is part of Mauritius's broader framework for virtual asset service providers (VASPs), which also covers exchange, custody, and advisory services.

The FSC regulates staking as a category of virtual asset service. Licensed firms must comply with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) obligations, maintain adequate capital, and ensure client asset segregation. The regime is designed to support innovation while protecting investors and maintaining market integrity.

Who Needs a Crypto Staking License in Mauritius?

Any business that offers staking services to clients in or from Mauritius must hold a valid license from the FSC. This includes:

  • Platforms that pool client tokens and stake them on proof-of-stake networks (e.g., Ethereum, Solana, Cardano).
  • Companies that provide delegated staking or staking-as-a-service products.
  • Entities that manage staking pools and distribute rewards to participants.
  • Intermediaries that facilitate staking for third parties.

If your business merely holds staked tokens for its own account (not for clients), you may not need a license, but professional advice is recommended. The FSC's definition of virtual asset service is broad, and any activity that involves safeguarding or administering virtual assets on behalf of others likely triggers licensing.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Financial Services Commission (FSC) of Mauritius. The relevant license is the Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) license under the VAITOS Act. Staking is classified as a virtual asset service, and the license covers staking alongside other services like exchange, custody, and advisory. There is no separate 'staking license' per se; rather, staking is an activity permitted under the VASP license.

The FSC issues different categories of VASP licenses depending on the services offered. For staking, the standard VASP license applies. The FSC also regulates initial token offerings (ITOs) and digital asset marketplaces. All licensed entities must be incorporated in Mauritius and have a physical office there.

Capital Requirement for a Mauritius Crypto Staking License

The FSC imposes a minimum capital requirement based on the type of services offered. For staking services, the capital requirement is aligned with the EU's MiCA framework, which Mauritius has adopted as a benchmark. As of 2026, the minimum capital is:

  • EUR 50,000 for staking-only services (no custody of client assets).
  • EUR 125,000 for staking combined with custody or exchange services.
  • EUR 150,000 for full-service VASPs offering multiple services including staking, custody, and trading.

Capital must be maintained in liquid form (cash or cash equivalents) and held in a licensed bank in Mauritius. The FSC may require higher capital if the business volume is large. These figures are based on current regulatory guidance; exact requirements are confirmed during the application process.

Tax Treatment for Staking Income in Mauritius

Mauritius offers a highly favourable tax regime for VASPs. The corporate tax rate is 15%, but licensed VASPs can benefit from a partial exemption that reduces the effective rate to 3% on certain income. Specifically, staking rewards and fees from staking services may qualify for the 80% partial exemption on foreign-source income, provided the company meets substance requirements (e.g., having a physical office, local staff, and board meetings in Mauritius).

There is no capital gains tax in Mauritius, which is beneficial for staking rewards that are treated as capital appreciation. VAT is not applicable on virtual asset services. The tax treatment is a key advantage for staking businesses, as it allows them to retain more of their earnings. However, tax advice should be obtained from a qualified professional.

Allowed Activities Under a Mauritius Crypto Staking License

A VASP license in Mauritius permits the following activities related to staking:

  • Operating a staking platform where clients deposit tokens to earn rewards.
  • Providing delegated staking services (staking on behalf of clients).
  • Managing staking pools and distributing rewards.
  • Offering staking-as-a-service to institutional and retail clients.
  • Combining staking with custody services (if licensed for custody).

The license does not automatically permit exchange or trading services; those require separate authorisation under the same VASP license. The FSC may impose conditions on the license, such as limits on the types of tokens that can be staked or requirements for risk disclosures.

Step-by-Step Application Process for a Mauritius Crypto Staking License

The application process involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application consultation: Discuss your business model with the FSC or a licensed advisory firm like Consulting24 to confirm licensing requirements.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a company in Mauritius with a physical office and local directors (at least one).
  3. Document preparation: Prepare a business plan, AML/CTF policies, risk assessment, financial projections, and background checks on key personnel.
  4. Submit application: File the application with the FSC, including all required documents and the application fee.
  5. FSC review: The FSC conducts due diligence, which may take 3-6 months. They may request additional information or clarifications.
  6. Approval and licensing: Once approved, pay the annual license fee and receive the license certificate.
  7. Ongoing compliance: Submit regular reports (AML, financial, operational) and maintain substance requirements.

Compliance and Trust: Maintaining Your License

Once licensed, VASPs must comply with ongoing obligations:

  • Appoint a compliance officer and MLRO (Money Laundering Reporting Officer).
  • Conduct regular AML/CTF training.
  • File annual returns and audited financial statements.
  • Submit quarterly AML reports to the FSC.
  • Maintain substance: physical office, local staff, and board meetings in Mauritius.
  • Ensure client asset segregation and adequate insurance.

Common Mistakes When Applying for a Mauritius Crypto Staking License

Avoid these pitfalls to ensure a smooth application:

  • Inadequate business plan: The FSC expects a detailed plan with realistic financial projections and risk analysis.
  • Insufficient capital: Ensure you meet the minimum capital requirement and can demonstrate source of funds.
  • Poor AML/CTF policies: The FSC scrutinises AML frameworks; they must be tailored to your business model.
  • Lack of substance: Having a registered agent is not enough; you need a physical office and local staff.
  • Ignoring tax obligations: Even with low effective rates, proper tax filings are mandatory.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto staking license in Mauritius?

As of 2026, the minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for staking-only services, EUR 125,000 if combined with custody, and EUR 150,000 for full-service VASPs. Capital must be held in a licensed bank in Mauritius.

How long does it take to get a Mauritius crypto staking license?

The typical timeline is 3 to 6 months from application to approval, assuming all documents are complete and the FSC's due diligence is satisfied. Pre-application preparation can add 1-2 months.

What is the tax rate for staking income in Mauritius?

Licensed VASPs can benefit from an effective tax rate as low as 3% on eligible foreign-source income, thanks to an 80% partial exemption. There is no capital gains tax.

Can I stake any cryptocurrency under a Mauritius license?

The FSC does not prescribe specific tokens, but you must have policies to assess the risks of each token. Staking of privacy coins or tokens associated with illicit activities may be restricted.

Do I need a physical office in Mauritius?

Yes, the FSC requires licensed VASPs to have a physical office in Mauritius, with local staff and board meetings held there. A registered agent alone is insufficient.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

You must appoint a compliance officer and MLRO, file quarterly AML reports, annual returns, and audited financial statements, and maintain substance requirements. The FSC may conduct inspections.

Can I apply for a staking license if I am not based in Mauritius?

Yes, but you must incorporate a company in Mauritius and have local directors. The FSC does not require shareholders to be residents, but substance requirements apply.

How does Mauritius compare to Panama for staking licensing?

Panama offers a faster (2-4 weeks) and cheaper (EUR 6,000 flat) option with no capital requirement and zero tax on offshore income. However, Mauritius provides a regulated framework that may be necessary for institutional partners.

Is the Mauritius crypto staking license recognized in the EU?

No, the Mauritius license does not provide EU passporting rights. For EU market access, consider Lithuania or Estonia under MiCA. Mauritius is best for non-EU operations.

What happens if I operate without a license in Mauritius?

Operating without a license is illegal and can result in fines, imprisonment, and seizure of assets. The FSC actively monitors unlicensed activity.
