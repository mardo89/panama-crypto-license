What Is the Crypto NFT Marketplace License in Jersey?

The Jersey crypto license for NFT marketplaces is a regulatory authorization issued by the JFSC. It allows businesses to operate a platform where non-fungible tokens (NFTs) are created, listed, bought, sold, and traded, using cryptocurrency or fiat currency. The license ensures compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorist financing (CTF) regulations, aligning with international standards set by the Financial Action Task Force (FATF).

Jersey does not have a separate 'NFT license' but regulates digital asset activities under its existing financial services framework. Depending on the specific activities, an NFT marketplace may require a 'Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP)' registration or a full 'Investment Business' license. In 2026, the JFSC applies a risk-based approach, and most NFT platforms fall under VASP registration.

Key features of the license include mandatory AML/CTF policies, regular reporting, and a fit and proper test for directors and beneficial owners. The license is valid indefinitely but subject to annual renewal and ongoing supervision.

Who Needs a Jersey Crypto License for an NFT Marketplace?

Any business that facilitates the exchange, transfer, or custody of digital assets (including NFTs) for or on behalf of users likely needs a Jersey crypto license. This includes:

  • NFT marketplaces that allow users to buy, sell, or trade NFTs using cryptocurrency.
  • Platforms that offer custodial wallet services for NFTs or associated cryptocurrencies.
  • Businesses that provide exchange services between NFTs and fiat currency or other digital assets.
  • Launchpads or primary issuance platforms for NFT collections.

If your platform merely hosts NFT content without handling funds or assets (e.g., a gallery that only displays NFTs), you may not need a license. However, the JFSC recommends seeking legal advice if unsure. Consulting24 can help you determine whether your specific business model requires licensing.

License Type and Regulator

The primary regulator for crypto activities in Jersey is the Jersey Financial Services Commission (JFSC). For NFT marketplaces, the relevant license type is typically a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) registration under the Proceeds of Crime (Jersey) Law 1999. This registration is mandatory for any business that provides virtual asset services in or from Jersey.

In some cases, if the marketplace also offers investment advice or manages client assets, an Investment Business license under the Financial Services (Jersey) Law 1998 may be required. However, for most NFT platforms, VASP registration is sufficient. The JFSC also requires registration under the Money Laundering (Jersey) Order 2008.

The JFSC assesses applications based on the business's AML/CTF controls, the fitness and propriety of key individuals, and the overall risk profile. As of 2026, the JFSC has not introduced a separate 'NFT license' and treats NFTs as virtual assets for regulatory purposes.

Capital Requirement

Jersey does not impose a fixed minimum capital requirement for VASP registration. However, the JFSC expects the business to maintain adequate financial resources to operate effectively and meet its liabilities. As a guideline, most applicants demonstrate at least EUR 50,000 in paid-up capital or equivalent liquid assets.

For businesses that also require an Investment Business license, the minimum capital requirement is typically EUR 125,000 (or equivalent in GBP). This higher threshold applies if the marketplace holds client funds or provides investment advice.

Capital must be held in a Jersey bank account or a reputable financial institution. The JFSC may also require a surety bond or professional indemnity insurance, depending on the risk profile. Consulting24 can advise on the appropriate capital level for your specific business model.

Tax Treatment for NFT Marketplaces in Jersey

Jersey offers a favorable tax regime for crypto businesses. The standard corporate income tax rate is 0% for most companies, including those operating NFT marketplaces. However, a 10% rate applies to certain regulated financial services activities, such as banking and insurance. For VASP-registered entities, the 0% rate generally applies.

There is no capital gains tax, withholding tax, or value-added tax (VAT) in Jersey. This makes it an attractive jurisdiction for NFT platforms seeking tax efficiency. However, businesses must still comply with economic substance requirements, including having a physical presence, board meetings, and core income-generating activities in Jersey.

Consulting24 recommends consulting with a tax advisor to confirm your specific tax obligations, as international tax treaties and cross-border activities may affect your liability.

Allowed Activities Under the Jersey Crypto License

With a Jersey VASP registration, an NFT marketplace can conduct the following activities:

  • Operating a platform for the creation, listing, and trading of NFTs.
  • Exchanging NFTs for fiat currency or other virtual assets.
  • Providing custodial wallet services for NFTs and associated cryptocurrencies.
  • Facilitating primary sales (minting) and secondary market transactions.
  • Offering auction services for NFTs.

Activities that are not covered without additional licensing include: providing investment advice, managing collective investment schemes, or issuing securities tokens. If your platform offers staking, lending, or derivatives, you may need a separate license under Jersey's regulatory framework.

  • Transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting.
  • Annual AML/CTF audit and submission of a compliance report to the JFSC.
  • Maintaining records for at least five years.
  • Appointing a Jersey-based MLRO and deputy MLRO.

Common Mistakes When Applying for a Jersey Crypto License

Many applicants face delays or rejections due to avoidable errors. Common mistakes include:

  • Incomplete application: Missing documents or insufficient detail in the business plan.
  • Weak AML/CTF policies: The JFSC expects strong, tailored policies, not generic templates.
  • Unfit directors: Individuals with a history of regulatory breaches or financial crime are likely to be rejected.
  • Underestimating capital: While no fixed minimum, the JFSC expects adequate financial resources. Many applicants fail to demonstrate sufficient liquidity.
  • Ignoring substance requirements: Jersey requires a physical presence, including a registered office and local directors or agents.
