What Is a Panama Crypto License?

Panama offers a straightforward crypto license under its Law 23 of 2015 on Digital Commerce and the regulations of the Superintendencia de Bancos de Panamá (SBP). As of 2026, the license is a registration-based regime, not a full authorisation like in the EU. It allows companies to operate as virtual asset service providers (VASPs) with minimal red tape. The flat fee is EUR 6,000, making it one of the most affordable options globally.

The license covers exchange services, wallet provision, and payment processing. Panama has no central bank digital currency restrictions, and the government encourages innovation. However, the regime is not MiCA-aligned, so it may not be ideal for EU-focused businesses. Consulting24 handles the entire setup, from company incorporation to license application, with a typical timeline of 2-4 months.

Key features include: no minimum capital requirement (though EUR 10,000 share capital is recommended for credibility), zero tax on offshore income, and a simple compliance framework. Panama is a common law jurisdiction with a stable legal system, making it attractive for startups and mid-size firms.

What Is a Dubai Crypto License?

Dubai’s crypto license is issued by the Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA), established in 2022. VARA is a dedicated regulator for virtual assets, and its regime is one of the most comprehensive in the Middle East. As of 2026, VARA offers several license categories: full market, broker-dealer, advisory, and custody. The minimum capital requirement ranges from AED 500,000 (approx. EUR 125,000) for a broker-dealer to AED 2 million (approx. EUR 500,000) for a full market license.

Dubai is a tax-free jurisdiction with 0% corporate tax on crypto-related income, though a 9% corporate tax applies to non-crypto activities. The application process takes 3-6 months and requires a physical office in Dubai, a local director, and compliance with AML/CFT rules. VARA also mandates a risk-based capital framework and regular audits.

Dubai’s license is ideal for businesses targeting the Middle East, Asia, or Africa, and it offers high regulatory credibility. However, costs are significantly higher than Panama: setup fees start at EUR 30,000 plus ongoing annual costs of EUR 15,000-25,000. Who Needs a Panama vs Dubai Crypto License?

Panama is best for:

  • Startups and early-stage crypto projects with limited budgets.
  • Businesses focused on Latin American markets or global operations without a strong EU presence.
  • Companies offering exchange, wallet, or payment services that want a quick, low-cost setup.

Dubai is best for:

  • Established firms needing a prestigious regulatory stamp for partnerships and institutional clients.
  • Businesses targeting the Middle East, Asia, or Africa.
  • Firms requiring a full market license for high-volume trading or custody services.

Capital Requirement

Panama: No statutory minimum capital for a crypto license. However, to demonstrate financial stability, a share capital of at least EUR 10,000 is recommended. This capital can be used for operational expenses and is not locked.

Dubai: VARA imposes a tiered capital requirement based on license type: AED 500,000 (EUR 125,000) for broker-dealer, AED 1 million (EUR 250,000) for advisory/custody, and AED 2 million (EUR 500,000) for full market. Capital must be maintained as liquid assets and can be in cryptocurrency or fiat, subject to VARA approval.

For most startups, Panama’s low capital requirement is a major advantage. Dubai’s high threshold filters out smaller players but adds credibility.

Tax Treatment

Panama: Territorial tax system: only income sourced within Panama is taxed. Offshore income (including crypto trading with non-Panamanian clients) is tax-free. No capital gains tax, no VAT on crypto services, and no withholding tax on dividends. Panama also offers a 0% corporate tax for companies that operate outside Panama’s territory.

Dubai: 0% corporate tax on crypto-related income, as virtual assets are not subject to the 9% corporate tax (which applies to non-crypto activities). No personal income tax, no capital gains tax, and no VAT on crypto transactions. However, a 5% VAT applies to advisory fees if charged to UAE residents.

Both jurisdictions are tax-friendly, but Panama’s territorial system offers more flexibility for global operations. Dubai’s tax advantages are tied to having a physical presence.

Allowed Activities

Panama: The license covers:

  • Exchange of virtual assets for fiat or other virtual assets
  • Custody and wallet services
  • Payment processing using virtual assets
  • Issuance of virtual assets (with additional registration)

Dubai: VARA licenses allow:

  • Broker-dealer services (order matching, execution)
  • Advisory services on virtual assets
  • Custody and transfer services
  • Full market operations (exchange, trading, clearing)
  • DeFi and token issuance (subject to VARA sandbox)

Dubai’s regime is more granular, allowing specific activities per license class. Panama’s license is broader but less defined for complex activities like DeFi.

Step-by-Step Process: Panama vs Dubai

Panama

  1. Company incorporation: register a Panama corporation with a local registered agent.
  2. Submit license application to SBP with business plan, AML policies, and director details.
  3. Pay EUR 6,000 fee.
  4. Await approval (2-4 months).
  5. Open bank account with a Panama bank or international partner.

Dubai

  1. Choose license category (broker-dealer, full market, etc.).
  2. Set up a Dubai company with a physical office and local director.
  3. Prepare VARA application including audited financials, AML manual, and cybersecurity plan.
  4. Pay initial fees (AED 30,000-100,000).
  5. Undergo VARA review and on-site inspection (3-6 months).
  6. Obtain license and open a corporate bank account in Dubai.

Banking & Payment Solutions

Panama: Banking is straightforward. Panama has a mature international banking sector. Most banks accept crypto-related businesses, though some may require a minimum deposit. Consulting24 can introduce you to crypto-friendly banks in Panama and the region. Payment processing via SWIFT or crypto-to-fiat gateways is common.

Dubai: Banking is more challenging due to VARA’s strict AML requirements. Banks like Mashreq, RAKBANK, and some international banks accept licensed VASPs. A physical presence and local director help. Payment solutions include UAE’s Instant Payment Platform (IPP) and international wire transfers.

Both jurisdictions offer solid banking options, but Panama’s process is faster and less costly.

Frequently asked questions

What is the main difference between Panama and Dubai crypto licenses?

Panama offers a low-cost, low-capital registration (EUR 6,000, no minimum capital) ideal for startups. Dubai’s VARA license requires higher capital (AED 500,000+), a physical office, and takes longer, but provides greater regulatory prestige and access to Middle Eastern markets.

Which is cheaper: Panama or Dubai crypto license?

Panama is significantly cheaper. The flat fee is EUR 6,000 with no minimum capital. Dubai’s setup costs start at EUR 30,000 plus capital of at least EUR 125,000. Annual costs in Dubai are also higher (EUR 15,000-25,000 vs EUR 2,000-3,000 in Panama).

Can I use a Panama license to serve EU customers?

Panama is not MiCA-aligned, so serving EU customers may require additional compliance. However, Panama’s territorial tax system means you can serve non-Panamanian clients without local tax. For EU-focused businesses, consider Lithuania or Estonia.

Does Dubai’s VARA license allow DeFi activities?

VARA has a sandbox for DeFi and token issuance, but full DeFi operations require a specific license. Panama’s broader license may cover DeFi, but it is not explicitly regulated. Consulting24 can advise on the best approach for DeFi projects.

What is the minimum capital for a Panama crypto license?

There is no statutory minimum capital for Panama’s crypto license. However, a share capital of EUR 10,000 is recommended to demonstrate financial stability and facilitate banking. This is far lower than Dubai’s minimum of AED 500,000 (EUR 125,000).

How long does it take to get a Panama vs Dubai crypto license?

Panama takes 2-4 months from application to approval. Dubai takes 3-6 months due to more extensive due diligence and on-site inspections. Both timelines can vary based on application completeness.

Do I need a physical office in Panama or Dubai?

Panama does not require a physical office; a registered agent suffices. Dubai requires a physical office in a free zone or mainland, which adds to costs. This is a key factor in the Panama vs Dubai decision.

Is Panama or Dubai better for tax optimisation?

Both are tax-friendly. Panama taxes only Panama-sourced income, so offshore crypto income is tax-free. Dubai offers 0% corporate tax on crypto income, but a 9% tax applies to non-crypto activities. Panama’s territorial system is more flexible for global operations.

Panama’s annual costs are EUR 2,000-3,000, covering registered agent, compliance, and basic reporting. Dubai’s annual costs are EUR 15,000-25,000, including office rent, local director fees, audits, and VARA renewal fees.
