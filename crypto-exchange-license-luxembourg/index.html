What Is the Luxembourg Crypto Exchange License?

The Luxembourg crypto exchange license is an authorisation granted by the CSSF to operate a crypto-asset service provider (CASP) under MiCA. It allows firms to offer services such as exchange of crypto-assets for fiat currency, exchange of crypto-assets for other crypto-assets, and custody of crypto-assets on behalf of clients. Luxembourg's regulatory framework is known for its high standards and alignment with EU financial regulations, making it a reputable jurisdiction for crypto businesses.

Unlike some other EU member states that previously operated lighter-touch registration regimes, Luxembourg has fully implemented MiCA since its entry into force in 2025. This means that as of 2026, all CASPs must hold a full licence with minimum capital requirements, governance standards, and ongoing compliance obligations. The CSSF is a well-respected regulator, and obtaining its approval signals credibility to clients, partners, and financial institutions.

Who Needs a Luxembourg Crypto License?

Any entity that provides crypto-asset services in or from Luxembourg must obtain a CASP licence from the CSSF. This includes:

  • Exchanges offering crypto-to-fiat or crypto-to-crypto trading
  • Platforms providing custody of crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • Brokers that facilitate crypto transactions
  • Market makers and liquidity providers in crypto-assets
  • Any other service defined under MiCA as a crypto-asset service

If you plan to target Luxembourg clients or establish a physical presence in Luxembourg, you must apply. The licence is also recognised across the EU under the MiCA passport, allowing you to serve clients in other member states without additional authorisation. Licence Type and Regulator

The relevant regulator is the Commission de Surveillance du Secteur Financier (CSSF), which supervises all financial services in Luxembourg, including crypto-asset service providers. The licence type is a CASP (Crypto-Asset Service Provider) authorisation under MiCA. This is a full licence, not a simple registration, and it requires compliance with MiCA's operational, prudential, and conduct-of-business requirements.

The CSSF assesses applications based on the fitness and propriety of management, the adequacy of internal controls, capitalisation, and the strongness of the business plan. The application process is rigorous and typically takes 6 to 12 months, depending on the complexity of the business and the completeness of the application. Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, capital requirements for CASPs are tiered based on the types of services provided. As of 2026, the minimum capital is:

  • EUR 50,000 for custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • EUR 125,000 for exchange of crypto-assets for fiat currency
  • EUR 150,000 for exchange of crypto-assets for other crypto-assets and for operating a trading platform

If your business offers multiple services, the highest applicable capital requirement applies. The capital must be held in liquid assets and maintained throughout the licence period. Consulting24 can help you structure your capital and ensure compliance with prudential requirements.

Tax Treatment

Luxembourg has a favourable tax regime for crypto businesses. Corporate income tax is standard at 24.94% (including the solidarity surcharge), but certain income from crypto-assets may be exempt under the Luxembourg participation exemption if certain conditions are met. VAT treatment of crypto transactions is generally exempt, following the European Court of Justice's ruling on Bitcoin. However, specific tax advice is essential, as crypto activities can trigger VAT on fees or other services.

Luxembourg also offers a special regime for investment funds that may apply to certain crypto funds. Allowed Activities Under the Luxembourg Crypto License

The CASP licence covers the following services as defined under MiCA:

  • Exchange of crypto-assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for other crypto-assets
  • Operation of a trading platform for crypto-assets
  • Custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • Placement of crypto-assets
  • Reception and transmission of orders for crypto-assets
  • Execution of orders for crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • Providing advice on crypto-assets
  • Portfolio management of crypto-assets

Each activity requires specific operational procedures and compliance measures. The licence does not cover activities related to stablecoins or asset-referenced tokens (ARTs) unless separately authorised under MiCA's specific regimes for those tokens. Step-by-Step Application Process

The application process for a Luxembourg CASP licence involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step:

  1. Pre-application assessment: We review your business model, target services, and suitability. This includes a gap analysis against CSSF requirements.
  2. Documentation preparation: You will need a detailed business plan, governance framework, AML/CFT policies, risk management procedures, and financial projections. The CSSF requires extensive documentation.
  3. Capital deposit: The minimum capital must be deposited in a Luxembourg bank account and verified.
  4. Submission of application: The formal application is submitted to the CSSF, along with the application fee.
  5. Review and queries: The CSSF reviews the application and may request additional information or clarifications. This phase can take several months.
  6. On-site visit (if required): The CSSF may conduct an on-site inspection of your premises and systems.
  7. Decision: The CSSF issues a decision. If approved, you receive the CASP licence and can begin operations.

Benefits of a Luxembourg Crypto License

Obtaining a Luxembourg CASP licence offers several advantages:

  • EU passport: Once licensed, you can serve clients across all EU member states without additional authorisation.
  • Regulatory credibility: The CSSF is a highly respected regulator, enhancing your reputation with partners, investors, and customers.
  • Access to banking: Licensed firms find it easier to open bank accounts and obtain payment services.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, CASPs must comply with ongoing requirements under MiCA and CSSF supervision. These include:

  • AML/CFT compliance: Implementing strong anti-money laundering policies, conducting customer due diligence, and reporting suspicious transactions.
  • Prudential requirements: Maintaining the minimum capital and holding adequate insurance or professional indemnity cover.
  • Reporting: Submitting periodic reports to the CSSF on financial condition, transaction volumes, and compliance matters.
  • Governance: Ensuring fit and proper management, independent board members, and clear organisational structure.
  • Audit: Annual financial audit by an approved auditor.

Non-compliance can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the licence. Common Mistakes When Applying for a Luxembourg Crypto License

Many applicants face delays or rejection due to avoidable errors. Common mistakes include:

  • Incomplete documentation: The CSSF requires a comprehensive application. Missing documents or insufficient detail can lead to requests for information and extended timelines.
  • Unrealistic business plans: Overly optimistic projections or vague descriptions of operations are red flags.
  • Poor AML/CFT framework: The CSSF scrutinises AML policies. A weak framework can result in rejection.
  • Inadequate capital: Failing to demonstrate that the minimum capital is held and will be maintained.
  • Choosing the wrong service scope: Applying for activities that do not match the business model or that require higher capital than necessary.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a CASP licence and a simple registration in Luxembourg?

A CASP licence under MiCA is a full authorisation with capital requirements, governance standards, and ongoing supervision by the CSSF. A simple registration (like the old VASP regime in other EU countries) no longer exists. Luxembourg only issues full licences.

Can I passport my Luxembourg licence to other EU countries?

Yes, under MiCA, once you obtain a CASP licence in Luxembourg, you can provide services across the EU without additional authorisation, subject to notification procedures. This is a major advantage of the Luxembourg licence.

How long does it take to get a Luxembourg crypto license?

The typical timeline is 6 to 12 months from submission to decision. The CSSF reviews applications thoroughly, and delays can occur if documentation is incomplete. Consulting24 helps streamline the process.

What is the minimum capital for a crypto exchange in Luxembourg?

Under MiCA, the minimum capital depends on the services offered. For exchange of crypto for fiat, it is EUR 125,000. For exchange of crypto for crypto, it is EUR 150,000. Custody requires EUR 50,000. The highest applicable tier applies.

Is Luxembourg tax-friendly for crypto businesses?

Luxembourg has a standard corporate tax rate of 24.94%, but with proper structuring, certain income may be exempt. VAT on crypto transactions is generally exempt. We recommend obtaining a tax ruling.

Can I apply for the licence if my company is not based in Luxembourg?

Yes, you can set up a Luxembourg subsidiary and apply for the licence. The entity must have a physical presence in Luxembourg, including a registered office and local management.

What are the ongoing compliance costs for a Luxembourg CASP?

Annual costs typically range from EUR 15,000 to EUR 40,000, including CSSF fees, audit, compliance officer, and AML reporting. These costs can be higher depending on the complexity of your business.

Does the Luxembourg licence cover DeFi operations?

MiCA generally applies to centralised services. Fully decentralised protocols that do not involve an intermediary may fall outside the scope. However, the CSSF assesses each case individually. Consulting24 can advise on this.

What happens if I operate without a licence in Luxembourg?

Operating without a licence is illegal and can result in fines, criminal penalties, and forced cessation of activities. The CSSF actively monitors the market and takes enforcement action.
