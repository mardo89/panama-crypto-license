What is the Crypto Payment Institution License Ireland?

The Crypto Payment Institution License Ireland is a regulatory authorization granted by the Central Bank of Ireland (CBI). It allows companies to provide crypto payment services, including exchange, wallet custody, and payment processing, in compliance with MiCA. This license is part of Ireland's transposition of the EU's MiCA regulation, which harmonizes crypto asset rules across member states.

Unlike a simple VASP registration, this license requires substantial capital, strong governance, and ongoing compliance. It is designed for businesses that handle customer funds and execute payment transactions using crypto assets. The license is recognized across the EU, enabling passporting into other member states. For example, a company licensed in Ireland can offer services in Germany or France without separate local licenses, provided they notify the CBI.

The CBI has been proactive in regulating crypto, and the MiCA framework ensures a consistent standard across the EU. This license is ideal for firms that want to build trust with customers and partners by demonstrating regulatory compliance.

Who Needs This License?

This license is essential for any business that offers crypto payment services to EU residents. Typical applicants include:

  • Crypto exchanges offering fiat on/off ramps
  • Wallet providers that hold customer private keys
  • Payment processors facilitating crypto transactions
  • Merchant acquirers accepting crypto payments
  • Firms issuing crypto-backed payment cards

If your business involves holding customer funds or executing payment transactions in crypto, you likely need this license. Smaller operations may consider alternatives like the Lithuania crypto license for lower capital requirements, but that does not offer the same EU passporting rights. For businesses that plan to scale across Europe, the Irish license is a strong choice.

Even if you are a startup, if you intend to handle payments, you must comply with MiCA. The CBI expects applicants to have a clear business plan and sufficient capital from day one.

License Type & Regulator

The regulator is the Central Bank of Ireland (CBI). The license type is a Crypto Payment Institution License under MiCA. This is distinct from the older VASP registration, which no longer suffices for payment services. The CBI classifies services into three tiers with corresponding capital requirements:

Service ClassMinimum Capital
Class 1: Exchange & custodyEUR 50,000
Class 2: Payment services (incl. transfers)EUR 125,000
Class 3: Full payment institution (all services)EUR 150,000

The CBI is known for thorough vetting, including fit-and-proper tests for directors and beneficial owners. They also require a detailed business plan, risk management framework, and AML policies. The CBI may impose additional conditions based on the applicant's risk profile.

Compared to other EU regulators, the CBI is considered rigorous but fair. They expect applicants to have substance in Ireland, including a physical office and local management.

Capital Requirement

Minimum capital is determined by the service class you apply for. As per MiCA: EUR 50,000 for exchange and custody, EUR 125,000 for payment services, and EUR 150,000 for full payment institution. This capital must be held as own funds (e.g., share capital or reserves) and cannot be borrowed. The CBI may require higher amounts based on business volume and risk profile.

For example, if you plan to process large transaction volumes, the CBI might ask for EUR 250,000 or more. It is important to have the capital readily available in a bank account and to provide evidence of source of funds.

For comparison, the Lithuania crypto license has a minimum capital of EUR 12,000 for VASP registration, but that does not cover payment services under MiCA. The Estonia crypto license requires EUR 12,000 for VASP, but again, no payment services.

Tax Treatment

Ireland's corporate tax rate is 12.5% for trading income, applicable to crypto payment services. Capital gains on crypto held as investments are taxed at 33%. VAT treatment: crypto payment services are generally exempt from VAT, but advisory fees are subject to 23% VAT. Ireland has a favorable tax regime for startups, including R&D tax credits.

For example, if your company earns EUR 1 million in profit from crypto payment services, the corporate tax would be EUR 125,000. Capital gains from selling crypto held for investment would be taxed at 33% on the gain.

Allowed Activities

Under the license, you can offer the following services:

  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets
  • Custody and administration of crypto assets
  • Execution of payment transactions in crypto
  • Transfer services for crypto assets
  • Receipt and transmission of crypto orders

You cannot engage in proprietary trading or investment advice without additional authorizations. For example, if you want to offer crypto derivatives or margin trading, you may need a separate investment firm license under MiFID II.

The license covers both retail and institutional clients. You must clearly disclose your services and risks to customers. The CBI expects you to have strong systems to prevent market abuse and money laundering.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application: Determine service class, prepare business plan, governance documents, and capital proof. This stage typically takes 1-2 months.
  2. Submit application: File with CBI including AML policies, risk management, and fit-and-proper declarations. The CBI charges an application fee of EUR 5,000-10,000.
  3. Review: CBI assesses completeness and may request additional information. This can take 3-6 months. They often ask for clarifications on business model or source of funds.
  4. Decision: If approved, you receive a license with conditions. Timeline: 6-12 months total. The CBI may impose conditions such as transaction limits or enhanced reporting.

Compliance & Ongoing Obligations

After licensing, you must maintain:

  • AML/CFT program with transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting
  • Annual audits and financial reporting to the CBI
  • Capital adequacy at all times, with monthly reporting
  • Periodic CBI reporting on transactions, customer complaints, and suspicious activity
  • Regular fit-and-proper reviews of directors and beneficial owners

Non-compliance can result in fines, suspension, or license revocation. For example, failure to report suspicious transactions can lead to penalties of up to EUR 1 million or more. Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a VASP registration and a Crypto Payment Institution License in Ireland?

VASP registration is for basic exchange and custody services under older Irish law. The Crypto Payment Institution License under MiCA is required for payment services, has higher capital requirements, and allows EU passporting. The VASP route is no longer sufficient for payment activities.

How long does it take to get the license?

The Central Bank of Ireland typically processes applications within 6-12 months. The timeline depends on application completeness and complexity. Consulting24 advises on preparing a comprehensive submission to avoid delays.

What is the minimum capital for a crypto payment institution in Ireland?

Minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for exchange and custody, EUR 125,000 for payment services, and EUR 150,000 for full payment institution. These figures are set by MiCA and are subject to adjustment based on business risk.

Can I passport the Irish license to other EU countries?

Yes, the license allows passporting to all EU member states under MiCA. You must notify the Central Bank of Ireland of your intention to operate in other countries, and they will coordinate with local regulators.

What are the ongoing compliance costs?

Annual compliance costs typically range from EUR 15,000 to EUR 30,000, covering AML audits, reporting, and legal fees. Exact costs depend on transaction volume and service complexity.

Is Ireland's corporate tax rate applicable to crypto income?

Yes, trading income from crypto payment services is taxed at 12.5%. Capital gains on crypto investments are taxed at 33%. VAT exemption applies to crypto payment services.

Do I need a physical office in Ireland?

Yes, you must have a registered office and a physical presence in Ireland, including a local director or management. The CBI expects substance and effective management in the country.

What happens if I provide incorrect information in the application?

How does the Irish license compare to the Panama license?

The Irish license offers EU passporting and high credibility but requires higher capital and longer timeline. The Panama license costs a flat EUR 6,000 and is faster, but does not provide EU access. Your choice depends on your target market.

What are the key AML requirements for the license?

You need a written AML policy, customer due diligence procedures, transaction monitoring systems, and suspicious activity reporting. The CBI expects these to be tailored to crypto payment services, including blockchain analysis tools.
