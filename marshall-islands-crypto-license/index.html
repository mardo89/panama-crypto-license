What Is the Marshall Islands Crypto License?

The Marshall Islands crypto license is a regulatory authorization issued by the government of the Republic of the Marshall Islands (RMI) for businesses offering virtual asset services. It is designed to bring legal clarity and consumer protection to the crypto sector while fostering innovation. The license allows companies to operate legally within the RMI and, in many cases, serve clients globally, subject to compliance with international standards.

The framework was established under the Non-Bank Financial Institutions (NBFI) Act and subsequent regulations specific to digital assets. As of 2026, the regime is fully operational and has attracted a range of businesses from startups to established exchanges. The license is known for its relatively low cost and efficient timeline compared to some European or Asian jurisdictions. However, because the Marshall Islands is a small island nation, its regulatory infrastructure is less developed than that of major financial centres. This means applicants must work closely with local agents and legal experts to ensure compliance. The license is particularly suitable for businesses that do not require a strong regulatory brand but want a cost-effective, legitimate home base.

One key aspect is that the Marshall Islands does not have a specific digital asset law; instead, VASPs are regulated under the broader NBFI framework. This can create some uncertainty, but the Banking Commission has issued guidance and is generally responsive to inquiries. For founders who prioritise regulatory clarity, jurisdictions like Lithuania or Estonia may offer more detailed rules. License Type & Regulator

The Marshall Islands crypto license falls under the oversight of the Marshall Islands Banking Commission (also referred to as the Banking Commissioner or the Financial Institutions Commission). This regulator is responsible for licensing and supervising non-bank financial institutions, including VASPs. The license is technically a “Virtual Asset Business License” or “Digital Asset Business License” depending on the specific activity.

There is typically one main license category that covers multiple services, but the regulator may impose conditions based on the business model. The regime is principles-based, focusing on anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-financing of terrorism (CFT) compliance, consumer protection, and operational resilience. Unlike some jurisdictions that have multiple license tiers (e.g., MiCA's Class 1, 2, 3), the Marshall Islands uses a single license with activity-specific endorsements. This simplifies the application process but may require more detailed descriptions of your business operations.

The Banking Commission is a small regulator with limited resources, so communication can sometimes be slow. However, they are generally cooperative and willing to work with applicants who demonstrate a clear understanding of compliance requirements. Consulting24 coordinates directly with the regulator on behalf of clients, ensuring that applications are complete and responsive to any queries. For a more hands-on regulatory experience, some founders prefer jurisdictions with larger regulators, such as the Switzerland FINMA or Malta MFSA, but these come with higher costs and longer timelines.

Capital Requirement

The Marshall Islands does not impose a statutory minimum capital requirement for crypto license applicants. However, the regulator expects the company to have sufficient financial resources to operate and meet its obligations. In practice, applicants should demonstrate at least $50,000 - $100,000 in available capital, though this is not a fixed rule. The exact amount is assessed on a case-by-case basis.

This flexible approach contrasts with EU MiCA regimes where capital tiers are EUR 50,000 / 125,000 / 150,000 depending on service class. For businesses that prefer a clear minimum, jurisdictions like Czech Republic or Poland may be more suitable. The lack of a fixed minimum can be both an advantage and a disadvantage: it allows startups with limited capital to apply, but it also creates uncertainty about what the regulator will accept. Consulting24 advises clients to prepare a detailed financial plan showing how the company will sustain operations for at least the first year, including projected revenues, expenses, and capital reserves.

In some cases, the Banking Commission may ask for a security deposit or a surety bond, especially if the business involves holding client funds. This is not common but can occur. We recommend that applicants have access to at least $100,000 in liquid assets to cover potential requirements. For comparison, the requirements for a Panama crypto license are more straightforward, with no minimum capital and a fixed fee structure.

Tax Treatment

The Marshall Islands offers a tax-neutral environment: there is no corporate income tax, no capital gains tax, no withholding tax, and no VAT/GST. The only taxes are import duties and a small annual business license fee. This makes the jurisdiction extremely attractive for crypto businesses seeking to minimize tax burdens.

However, companies must be mindful of tax obligations in the countries where their clients or operations are based. The Marshall Islands does not have double tax treaties, so proper transfer pricing and substance planning are essential. Consulting24 advises clients on structuring to ensure compliance with international tax standards. For example, if your company is managed from a high-tax country, the tax authorities there may consider it tax resident and impose taxes on worldwide income. To mitigate this, we recommend having a local director, maintaining a physical office, and holding board meetings in the Marshall Islands.

Another consideration is the OECD's Base Erosion and Profit Shifting (BEPS) project. The Marshall Islands is not a signatory to the BEPS Multilateral Instrument, but it is on the EU's list of non-cooperative jurisdictions for tax purposes. This may affect the perception of your business by European partners. For EU-focused operations, a license in Lithuania or Estonia may be more tax-efficient despite higher corporate tax rates, because those jurisdictions have extensive treaty networks and are fully compliant with EU tax standards.

Allowed Activities

A Marshall Islands crypto license permits a wide range of virtual asset activities, including but not limited to:

  • Operating a cryptocurrency exchange (buying/selling/trading)
  • Providing custodial and non-custodial wallet services
  • Issuing and managing digital tokens (including stablecoins)
  • Processing payments using virtual assets
  • OTC trading and brokerage services
  • Asset management involving crypto

Activities that involve securities (e.g., security tokens) may require additional licensing under securities laws. The regulator reviews each application to ensure the proposed activities are within scope. The license does not automatically cover derivatives or margin trading; those may require a separate license under the Marshall Islands Securities Act. Consulting24 helps clients define their activity scope precisely to avoid scope creep and regulatory breaches.

It is also worth noting that the Marshall Islands has a separate framework for digital asset custody, which may require additional approval if you plan to hold client assets. We always include a detailed activity description in the application to ensure full coverage. For a broader scope, some founders consider Dubai VARA or Cyprus, but those come with higher costs and more prescriptive rules.

  • Local banks in the Marshall Islands (limited services)
  • International banks in jurisdictions like Singapore, Hong Kong, or the UAE (with proper compliance)
  • EMI (Electronic Money Institution) accounts and payment processors

Compliance & Trust

The Marshall Islands Banking Commission expects licensees to maintain strong AML/CFT programs, including customer due diligence (CDD), transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting. The regime is aligned with FATF recommendations. Licensees must also appoint a local compliance officer and maintain records for at least five years.

While the Marshall Islands is not a member of the Financial Action Task Force (FATF), it has committed to implementing international standards. Companies should be aware that some counterparties may view the jurisdiction as higher risk due to its small size and limited regulatory history. This is general guidance, not legal advice. Always consult with a qualified lawyer for your specific situation.

To build trust, we recommend that licensees obtain a legal opinion on the regulatory status of their license and undergo regular external audits. Some clients also choose to register with the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) for additional credibility. Consulting24 can assist with these steps. For a more established regulatory environment, consider Switzerland or El Salvador, but note the higher costs and more stringent requirements.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Insufficient substance - The regulator expects real operations, not just a mailbox. Ensure you have a local director, office, or agent.
  • Poor AML/CFT documentation - A generic compliance manual will be rejected. Tailor your program to your business model.
  • Ignoring tax obligations elsewhere - The Marshall Islands tax neutrality does not exempt you from taxes in your home country or client countries.
  • Underestimating timeline - While fast, delays can occur if documents are incomplete. Start early.
  • Choosing the wrong jurisdiction - The Marshall Islands is not ideal for EU-focused businesses due to MiCA. Compare with Lithuania vs other jurisdictions.

Another common mistake is failing to budget for ongoing compliance costs. Many founders focus on the initial license fee but overlook the annual costs of local agents, compliance officers, and audits. Consulting24 provides a full cost breakdown during the consultation. We also see applicants who submit incomplete background checks, leading to delays. Ensure that all directors and shareholders provide clean criminal records and financial references. Finally, do not assume that a Marshall Islands license will automatically allow you to serve clients in other countries. You must comply with local laws in each jurisdiction where your clients reside, which may require additional registrations.

Frequently asked questions

What is the cost of a Marshall Islands crypto license?

The total first-year cost typically ranges from USD 16,000 to USD 38,000, including application fees, license fees, legal setup, and compliance costs. Exact pricing depends on the complexity of your business model and should be confirmed in a consultation. Annual renewal fees are lower.

Is there a minimum capital requirement for the Marshall Islands crypto license?

No statutory minimum capital is required, but the regulator expects sufficient financial resources. Practically, applicants should demonstrate at least USD 50,000 to USD 100,000 in available capital. This is assessed case by case.

How long does it take to get a Marshall Islands crypto license?

The typical timeline is 3 to 6 months from application to approval. Delays can occur if documents are incomplete or if the regulator requests additional information. Pre-prepared applications can be processed faster.

What activities are allowed under a Marshall Islands crypto license?

The license covers a wide range of virtual asset services, including exchange operation, wallet provision, token issuance, payment processing, OTC trading, and asset management. Security token activities may require additional licensing.

Does the Marshall Islands have corporate tax?

No, the Marshall Islands has no corporate income tax, capital gains tax, withholding tax, or VAT. Only import duties and a small annual business license fee apply. However, companies must comply with tax laws in other jurisdictions where they operate.

Can I serve EU clients with a Marshall Islands crypto license?

Serving EU clients directly may be challenging because the Marshall Islands license does not provide EU passporting under MiCA. EU clients may require the company to register in each member state or obtain a local license. A Lithuania or Estonia license is better for EU focus.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?
