What Is a Ready Made Panama Crypto License?

A ready made Panama crypto license refers to a pre-registered Panama corporation that already holds the necessary business license and AML registration to operate a crypto-related business. The company is shelf-ready, meaning you can take over ownership immediately and start commercial activities. Panama does not have a dedicated crypto licensing regime; instead, crypto exchanges, wallet providers, and payment processors operate under a standard commercial license with an added AML compliance obligation.

This structure is ideal for founders who want to avoid the lengthy application processes common in other jurisdictions. The ready made aspect means the company is already incorporated, with a clean history and no prior trading. You simply purchase the shelf company, update the directors and shareholders, and begin operations. The entire process can be completed in 2 to 4 weeks, depending on the speed of document preparation and due diligence.

License Type and Regulator

In Panama, crypto businesses are not licensed by a single financial regulator. Instead, the key authorities are:

  • Ministry of Commerce and Industries (MICI) - issues the commercial license (Aviso de Operación) required for any business activity
  • Superintendency of Banks of Panama (SBP) - oversees AML/CFT compliance for all financial and non-financial entities, including crypto businesses
  • Panama Financial Analysis Unit (UAF) - the financial intelligence unit that monitors suspicious transactions

The registration type is a general business license with AML registration. There is no specific crypto license law as of 2026, though a bill (Project 697) has been discussed. Currently, the framework is light-touch, focusing on AML obligations rather than prudential supervision. This makes Panama one of the most flexible jurisdictions for crypto startups.

Capital Requirement

Panama imposes no minimum capital requirement for crypto businesses. You can register a company with a nominal capital of USD 10,000 (often split into 100 shares of USD 100 each) without needing to deposit the funds. This is a major advantage over EU jurisdictions like Lithuania (EUR 125,000 for exchange services) or Malta (EUR 50,000+).

However, you must demonstrate that the company has sufficient working capital to cover operational expenses for at least 12 months. This is part of the AML due diligence process. In practice, a bank statement showing a few thousand euros in personal or company funds is usually sufficient. There is no requirement to maintain a fixed amount in the company account.

Tax Treatment

Panama operates a territorial tax system. This means income earned outside Panama is not taxed. For crypto businesses that serve international clients (non-Panamanian residents), the effective tax rate on foreign-source income is 0%. There is no capital gains tax, no withholding tax on dividends, and no VAT on services provided to non-residents.

The only tax obligations are:

  • Annual license fee (Aviso de Operación) - approximately USD 300 per year
  • ITBM (transfer tax) - 7% on services provided to Panamanian residents (not applicable for B2B international)
  • Dividend tax - 5% on dividends paid from Panama-source income (if any)

Most crypto founders structure their Panama company to earn foreign-source income, resulting in a near-zero tax burden. This is one of the most favorable tax regimes for digital assets globally.

There are no restrictions on the types of cryptocurrencies you can support, provided you comply with AML/KYC requirements. However, you cannot engage in activities that require a banking license (e.g., taking deposits) without additional authorization. For most crypto business models, the Panama license is sufficient.

Compliance and Trust

Panama's AML framework requires all crypto businesses to implement:

  • Customer due diligence (CDD) and enhanced due diligence (EDD) for high-risk clients
  • Ongoing transaction monitoring
  • Suspicious transaction reporting (STR) to the UAF
  • Record keeping for at least 5 years
  • Appointment of a compliance officer
  • Annual AML training for staff

We provide template policies and procedures as part of our setup service. However, you must tailor these to your business and ensure ongoing compliance. Failure to comply can result in fines or license revocation. Panama is a member of the FATF and follows its recommendations.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Ignoring AML compliance - Even though the license is easy to obtain, regulators expect active compliance. Many founders neglect ongoing reporting and risk penalties.
  • Using a shelf company with prior activity - Always use a clean ready made company. We only provide companies with no trading history.
  • Underestimating banking difficulty - Bank account opening is the most common bottleneck. Prepare thorough documentation and be patient.
  • Assuming Panama is a passport to the EU - Panama does not provide MiCA equivalence. If you need to serve EU clients, consider combining with a Lithuania crypto license or another EU jurisdiction.
  • Not seeking professional advice - Tax and legal structures vary by individual circumstances. Always consult with experts before committing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Frequently asked questions

Is a ready made Panama crypto license legal?

Yes, it is legal. Panama allows the transfer of ownership of a pre-registered company. The company must hold a valid business license (Aviso de Operación) and comply with AML obligations. We ensure all companies are clean and have no prior activity.

Can I use a Panama crypto license to serve EU customers?

Panama is not MiCA-compliant. If you target EU residents, you may need a license from an EU member state. However, Panama can be used for non-EU clients or as a holding company. Consider combining it with a Lithuania crypto license for EU access.

How long does it take to get a ready made Panama crypto license?

The entire process, from company purchase to operational readiness, takes 3 to 6 weeks. This includes company transfer, business license application, AML registration, and bank account opening.

What are the ongoing costs after setup?

Annual costs include the business license renewal (~USD 300), registered agent fees (~USD 1,000-2,000), compliance officer fees (if outsourced, ~USD 500-1,000), and bank account maintenance. Total annual costs typically range from EUR 2,500 to 4,000.

Do I need to travel to Panama to set up the license?

No, you do not need to travel. All documents can be submitted digitally. We handle all government filings and correspondence with local agents. Bank account opening may require a video call or notarized documents, but physical presence is not required.

What cryptocurrencies can I trade with a Panama license?

There are no restrictions on the types of cryptocurrencies. You can trade Bitcoin, Ethereum, stablecoins, altcoins, and even launch your own token. However, you must comply with AML/KYC requirements for all transactions.

Can I convert crypto to fiat with a Panama license?

Yes, you can offer crypto-to-fiat conversion services. You will need a bank account that accepts such transactions. We help you find crypto-friendly banks that allow fiat on/off ramps.

Is Panama a tax haven? Will I face issues with my home country?

Panama has a territorial tax system, not a zero-tax regime. It is not considered a tax haven by the OECD (it is on the white list). However, you must declare your Panama company in your home country and comply with local tax laws. We recommend consulting a tax advisor.

What if I already have a Panama company? Can I convert it to a crypto license?

Yes, if your existing company has a business license, we can add the AML registration and compliance framework. However, if the company has prior activity, due diligence may be more complex. We recommend using a clean ready made company for simplicity.

Can I get a Panama crypto license if I am a non-resident?

Yes, Panama does not require residency. You can be a director and shareholder from any country. The company can be managed remotely. Nominee services are available if you prefer privacy.
