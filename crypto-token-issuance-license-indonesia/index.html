Overview: What is the Crypto Token Issuance License in Indonesia?

The crypto token issuance license in Indonesia is a regulatory approval issued by the Commodity Futures Trading Regulatory Agency (Bappebti) and the Ministry of Trade. It allows entities to legally offer digital tokens to Indonesian residents. The license is part of Indonesia's broader framework for crypto assets, which treats certain tokens as commodities.

As of 2026, token issuers must register with Bappebti and comply with anti-money laundering (AML) and know-your-customer (KYC) requirements. The process involves submitting a detailed whitepaper, legal documents, and proof of technical capability. Consulting24 can guide you through each step, ensuring your offering meets local standards.

Indonesia's regulatory approach is designed to protect investors while fostering innovation. The license covers both utility tokens and, in some cases, security tokens, though the latter may also require approval from the Financial Services Authority (OJK). The framework is updated periodically, so working with experienced advisors is essential.

Who Needs This License?

Any entity that intends to issue tokens to Indonesian residents must obtain the crypto token issuance license. This includes:

  • Startups conducting an ICO or initial exchange offering (IEO)
  • Established companies launching utility or security tokens
  • Foreign entities targeting Indonesian investors
  • Exchanges that facilitate token listings for Indonesian projects

Exemptions may apply for very small offerings or tokens not offered to the public, but most commercial token issuances require registration. Consulting24 can help determine if your project falls under the licensing requirement. For example, a company issuing tokens solely to employees or a private placement to accredited investors might not need a full license, but the rules are strict.

If you are unsure, it is safer to assume the license is needed. The regulator has the authority to impose penalties on unregistered issuers, including fines and criminal charges.

License Type & Regulator

The primary regulator for crypto token issuance in Indonesia is Bappebti (Badan Pengawas Perdagangan Berjangka Komoditi), under the Ministry of Trade. Bappebti oversees all crypto asset trading, including token offerings. The license is officially called a "Crypto Asset Issuer Registration" or similar, depending on the token type.

For tokens classified as securities, the Financial Services Authority (OJK) may also have oversight. However, most utility tokens fall under Bappebti. The licensing process is separate from the exchange license required for trading platforms. Consulting24 coordinates with local legal experts to navigate the dual regulatory landscape.

Bappebti has published guidelines on token classification, which consider factors like the token's utility, the rights it confers, and its economic purpose. Understanding these criteria is crucial for a successful application.

Capital Requirement

Indonesia does not impose a fixed minimum capital requirement specifically for token issuance. However, issuers must demonstrate sufficient financial resources to cover operational costs and investor protection measures. Typically, regulators expect a minimum paid-up capital of around IDR 1 billion (approximately $65,000) for the issuing entity, though this can vary.

For foreign entities, a local presence (e.g., a subsidiary) is often required, which may have its own capital requirements. Consulting24 advises on structuring your entity to meet these expectations while minimizing upfront costs. The capital should be in the form of cash or liquid assets, and the company must maintain it throughout the license period.

Some projects opt for a lower capital base and supplement it with a bank guarantee or insurance. This approach requires prior approval from Bappebti.

Tax Treatment

Indonesia has a specific tax regime for crypto assets. As of 2026, income from token issuance may be subject to:

  • Corporate income tax at 22% (standard rate) for the issuing company
  • Value-added tax (VAT) at 11% on certain crypto transactions
  • Withholding tax on dividends or interest paid to token holders

Capital gains from token trading are taxed as income. The tax treatment can be complex, especially for hybrid tokens. Consulting24 works with Indonesian tax advisors to ensure your token issuance is tax-efficient and compliant. For example, if tokens are classified as commodities, VAT may apply on the initial sale, but secondary trading might be exempt.

Token holders also have tax obligations, such as reporting gains from selling tokens. The issuer is not responsible for withholding tax on investor gains unless the token pays dividends or interest.

Allowed Activities

With a crypto token issuance license, you are permitted to:

  • Offer and sell tokens to Indonesian residents through a public sale or private placement
  • List tokens on licensed Indonesian exchanges
  • Market and advertise the token offering (subject to promotional guidelines)
  • Manage the token smart contract and distribution

Activities not allowed include offering unregistered securities, using deceptive marketing, or issuing tokens that violate sharia principles (if applicable). The license does not cover operating a crypto exchange or wallet service. If you plan to run a trading platform, you need a separate exchange license from Bappebti.

Additionally, token issuers cannot guarantee returns or promise profits, as this would classify the token as a security under Indonesian law.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The application process for a crypto token issuance license in Indonesia involves the following steps:

  1. Pre-consultation: Engage with Consulting24 to assess your project and determine the appropriate license type.
  2. Entity setup: Establish a local company (PT) with the required capital.
  3. Document preparation: Draft a whitepaper, legal opinion, AML/KYC policies, and technical specifications.
  4. Submission: File the application with Bappebti, including all supporting documents and fees.
  5. Review & approval: Bappebti reviews the application, which may take 2-4 months. They may request amendments.
  6. Post-approval: Fulfill ongoing reporting obligations and conduct the token sale.

Banking & Payment Integration

Opening a corporate bank account in Indonesia for a crypto-related business can be challenging. Most traditional banks are cautious. However, several local banks and payment gateways support crypto businesses, such as Bank Mandiri, BCA, and fintech platforms like OVO and GoPay.

Issuers must also set up a payment mechanism for investors to purchase tokens using Indonesian rupiah (IDR). Consulting24 can introduce you to banking partners and payment processors that accept crypto firms. Some banks require a minimum deposit or a proven track record before opening an account.

For international investors, cross-border payments may be subject to foreign exchange controls. It is advisable to use a licensed money transfer service or a crypto-friendly bank that can handle USD-IDR conversions.

Obtaining a crypto token issuance license in Indonesia offers several advantages:

  • Regulatory clarity reduces legal risks
  • Ability to list on local exchanges, increasing liquidity

Compliance & Trust: Ongoing Obligations

After obtaining the license, issuers must comply with ongoing requirements:

  • Submit periodic reports to Bappebti (e.g., financial statements, token holder updates)
  • Maintain AML/KYC procedures and report suspicious transactions
  • Ensure token smart contracts are audited and secure
  • Adhere to advertising and marketing restrictions

Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. Consulting24 offers compliance monitoring services to help you stay on track. This is general guidance, not legal advice. For example, issuers must report any material changes to the token's functionality or the issuer's ownership structure within 14 days.

Annual audits by an independent third party are also required. The audit report must be submitted to Bappebti within 90 days of the fiscal year end.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

When applying for a crypto token issuance license in Indonesia, avoid these pitfalls:

  • Submitting an incomplete whitepaper lacking tokenomics or risk disclosures
  • Using unlicensed exchanges for token distribution
  • Overlooking the need for a local entity
  • Ignoring tax obligations for token holders
  • Failing to implement proper KYC procedures

Consulting24 helps you avoid these errors by providing a checklist and review process. Another common mistake is underestimating the time required for entity setup. Registering a PT (Perseroan Terbatas) can take 4-6 weeks, and the capital must be deposited in a local bank account before the company is fully operational.

Some applicants also forget to include a legal opinion on the token's classification. This document is mandatory and must address whether the token is a commodity or a security.
