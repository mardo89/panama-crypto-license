What is the Labuan Crypto License for NFT Marketplaces?

The Labuan crypto license, officially a Digital Asset Business License under the Labuan FSA, authorizes companies to operate digital asset exchanges, including NFT marketplaces. It covers the facilitation of trading, custody, and related services for digital assets. Labuan's framework is designed to align with international standards such as FATF recommendations, ensuring strong anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CFT) measures.

Key features include a clear regulatory environment, tax incentives, and a relatively fast setup timeline compared to other jurisdictions. The license is suitable for both primary issuance and secondary trading of NFTs, as well as cryptocurrency exchange services. Labuan distinguishes itself by allowing a broad range of digital asset activities under a single license, reducing the need for multiple authorizations.

For example, an NFT marketplace that also offers crypto-to-crypto trading and custodial wallets can operate under one license. This is unlike some jurisdictions that require separate licenses for each activity. The Labuan FSA also provides a regulatory sandbox for innovative products, allowing testing before full licensing.

Who Needs a Labuan Crypto License?

Any business planning to operate a digital asset exchange or NFT marketplace in or from Labuan must obtain this license. This includes:

  • Platforms for trading NFTs (both fungible and non-fungible tokens)
  • Cryptocurrency exchanges offering spot trading, margin trading, or staking
  • Custodial wallet providers for digital assets
  • Brokerage services for digital assets
  • Initial exchange offerings (IEOs) or token launchpads

If your business model involves facilitating peer-to-peer transactions of digital assets, you likely fall under the Labuan FSA's scope. Even if your target market is outside Malaysia, operating through a Labuan entity requires licensing. Consulting24 can help determine if your business model requires a license.

For instance, a company that only develops NFT smart contracts but does not operate a marketplace may not need a license. However, if you also run a platform where users buy and sell those NFTs, licensing is mandatory. The Labuan FSA takes a substance-over-form approach, so even if you outsource operations, the licensed entity retains responsibility.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Labuan Financial Services Authority (Labuan FSA). The license type is a Digital Asset Business License issued under the Labuan Financial Services and Securities Act 2010 (LFSSA) and the Labuan Digital Asset Guidelines. There are two main categories:

  • Operator License: For operating a digital asset exchange (including NFT marketplace).
  • Broker License: For brokering digital asset transactions (less common for marketplaces).

Most NFT marketplaces require the Operator License. The Labuan FSA applies a risk-based approach, and the license is subject to ongoing compliance obligations, including regular reporting and AML/CFT audits. The regulator also requires at least one local director and a registered office in Labuan. The license is valid for one year and must be renewed annually.

Capital Requirement

The Labuan FSA imposes a minimum paid-up capital requirement for digital asset businesses. As of 2026, the requirement is USD 500,000 for operators of digital asset exchanges. This capital must be maintained throughout the license period and can be in the form of cash or liquid assets. The capital requirement is designed to ensure financial stability and operational resilience. For NFT marketplaces with lower trading volumes, the regulator may consider a reduced amount on a case-by-case basis, but the standard is USD 500,000.

This capital must be deposited in a Labuan bank account and cannot be withdrawn without regulatory approval. It serves as a buffer against operational risks. Compared to Lithuania's EUR 50,000-150,000 under MiCA, Labuan's requirement is significantly higher, which may be a barrier for smaller startups but provides greater credibility.

Tax Treatment

Labuan offers a favorable tax regime for licensed entities. Under the Labuan Business Activity Tax Act 1990, a Labuan entity that is licensed and conducts qualifying activities (including digital asset services) can elect to pay a flat tax rate of 3% on net profits. Alternatively, if the entity is not engaged in trading with Malaysian residents, it may opt for a tax exemption under the Labuan International Business and Financial Centre (IBFC) framework. This makes Labuan highly attractive for international NFT marketplaces. However, careful structuring is required to avoid unintended tax liabilities. Consulting24 recommends consulting with a tax advisor.

For example, an NFT marketplace that only serves non-Malaysian customers may qualify for the exemption. The election must be made annually and documented. Additionally, Labuan does not impose withholding tax on dividends or interest paid to non-residents, and there is no capital gains tax. This can result in significant savings compared to jurisdictions like Lithuania, where corporate tax is 15%.

Allowed Activities

A Labuan Digital Asset Business License permits a wide range of activities, including:

  • Operating a digital asset exchange (crypto-to-crypto, fiat-to-crypto, and NFT trading)
  • Custody and safekeeping of digital assets
  • Brokerage and dealing in digital assets
  • Facilitating primary issuance of tokens (including NFTs)
  • Staking and lending services (subject to additional approval)
  • Providing digital asset wallet services

Activities involving derivatives or securities tokens may require additional licensing under the LFSSA. The Labuan FSA has a sandbox framework for innovative products. For instance, if your NFT marketplace also offers fractionalized NFTs that may be considered securities, you may need a separate license. Labuan FSA requires licensees to implement strong AML/CFT measures, including customer due diligence (CDD), transaction monitoring, and suspicious transaction reporting. Regular audits and annual compliance reports are mandatory. The regulator conducts on-site inspections periodically. Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. What is the minimum capital for a Labuan crypto license?

The minimum paid-up capital is USD 500,000 for digital asset exchange operators. This must be maintained throughout the license period. The regulator may consider a lower amount for NFT marketplaces with limited trading volumes, but the standard is USD 500,000.

Labuan offers a flat tax rate of 3% on net profits, or a full exemption if the entity does not trade with Malaysian residents. There is no capital gains tax, no withholding tax on dividends, and no stamp duty. This makes Labuan highly tax-efficient for international NFT marketplaces.

Can I operate an NFT marketplace with a Labuan crypto license?

Yes, the Digital Asset Business License covers operating a digital asset exchange, including NFT marketplaces. It allows both primary issuance and secondary trading of NFTs, as well as related services like custody and brokerage.
