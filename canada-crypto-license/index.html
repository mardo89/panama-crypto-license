What Is the Canada Crypto License?

The Canada crypto license is a registration with FINTRAC as a Money Services Business (MSB) dealing in virtual currency. It is not a discretionary license but a mandatory registration for any entity that engages in the exchange, transfer, or custody of virtual currencies on behalf of clients. The registration is governed by the Proceeds of Crime (Money Laundering) and Terrorist Financing Act (PCMLTFA).

Key features include:

  • Regulatory body: FINTRAC, Canada's financial intelligence unit.
  • Registration vs. license: It is a registration, meaning compliance with AML/CFT obligations is the primary requirement, rather than a discretionary approval.
  • Provincial considerations: While FINTRAC handles federal AML registration, provincial securities regulators may impose additional requirements if your business deals with tokens that qualify as securities.
  • Scope: Covers virtual currency exchange services, remittance, and payment processing.

This framework is designed to be efficient: applications are processed within weeks to a few months, provided all documentation is in order. For businesses seeking a quick entry into a regulated North American market, Canada is a top choice.

Who Needs a Canada Crypto License?

Any business that provides virtual currency services in Canada or to Canadian residents must register with FINTRAC. This includes:

  • Virtual currency exchanges (centralized and decentralized platforms facilitating trades between fiat and crypto).
  • Virtual currency transfer services (e.g., sending crypto on behalf of clients).
  • Payment processors that accept virtual currency and convert to fiat.
  • Crypto ATMs operators (each machine may require separate registration).
  • Wallet providers that hold private keys on behalf of users (custodial wallets).

Foreign entities offering services to Canadians also need to register. Even if your business is based outside Canada, if you have Canadian clients, FINTRAC registration is mandatory. Exemptions exist for non-profit activities or businesses that only use crypto for investment purposes (not as a service). License Type & Regulator

The Canada crypto license is a Money Services Business (MSB) registration under FINTRAC. FINTRAC is Canada's financial intelligence unit, responsible for enforcing the PCMLTFA. The registration is not a discretionary license-it is a compliance-based registration. This means that if you meet the AML/CFT requirements and submit a complete application, you are entitled to registration.

However, there is a second layer: provincial securities regulation. If your crypto business involves tokens that are considered securities (e.g., ICOs, security tokens, or stablecoins), you may need to register with provincial securities commissions (e.g., the Ontario Securities Commission). This is separate from FINTRAC and can be more complex. Consulting24 advises on how to navigate both federal and provincial requirements.

For most crypto exchanges dealing in major coins like Bitcoin and Ethereum, FINTRAC registration is sufficient. But if you plan to offer tokenized assets or conduct an ICO, seek legal advice. Our team can coordinate with Canadian legal partners to ensure full compliance.

Capital Requirement

Canada does not impose a statutory minimum capital requirement for FINTRAC MSB registration. However, you must demonstrate financial stability and have sufficient capital to operate your business. In practice, most applicants maintain a minimum of CAD 50,000-100,000 in liquid assets to cover operational costs and compliance obligations.

If provincial securities registration is required, capital requirements may be higher (e.g., CAD 100,000-500,000 depending on the type of securities dealer registration). Tax Treatment

Canada treats cryptocurrency as a commodity for income tax purposes. The Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) considers crypto transactions as barter transactions, meaning each disposal (sale, trade, or use) is a taxable event. Capital gains or business income rules apply depending on whether you are a trader or investor.

Key tax points:

  • Business income: If you operate a crypto exchange or provide services, income is taxed as business income at corporate rates (15% federal + provincial, typically 25-30% combined).
  • Capital gains: For investors, 50% of capital gains are included in income.
  • GST/HST: Crypto transactions are generally exempt from GST/HST, but exchange fees may be subject to tax.
  • Reporting: All transactions over CAD 10,000 must be reported to FINTRAC.

Canada does not have a specific crypto tax regime, but the CRA has issued guidance. Consulting24 recommends working with a Canadian tax advisor to optimize your structure. For businesses seeking tax efficiency, Panama offers a territorial tax system with 0% tax on foreign-source income.

Allowed Activities

Under the FINTRAC MSB registration, the following activities are allowed:

  • Virtual currency exchange: Buying, selling, or trading virtual currencies for fiat or other virtual currencies.
  • Virtual currency transfer: Sending virtual currency on behalf of a client (e.g., remittance services).
  • Payment processing: Facilitating payments using virtual currency.
  • Custodial wallet services: Holding private keys for clients.
  • Crypto ATM operation: Each ATM must be registered separately.

Activities that are not allowed without additional licensing include:

  • Dealing in securities (e.g., tokenized assets, ICOs).
  • Operating as a bank or taking deposits.
  • Offering derivatives or margin trading (requires IIROC registration).

Step-by-Step Process

Obtaining a Canada crypto license involves the following steps:

  1. Business structure: Incorporate a Canadian entity (federal or provincial). Most choose a federal corporation for flexibility.
  2. AML/CFT program: Develop a comprehensive program including policies, risk assessment, and procedures for KYC, transaction monitoring, and reporting.
  3. Appoint a compliance officer: Designate a senior person responsible for AML compliance (can be a director or employee).
  4. Register with FINTRAC: Submit the MSB registration application online via FINTRAC's portal. Provide details of the business, compliance program, and officers.
  5. Submit supporting documents: Include corporate documents, AML program, and proof of identity for officers.
  6. Await review: FINTRAC reviews the application and may request additional information. Typical timeline: 30-90 days.
  7. Receive registration number: Once approved, you receive an MSB registration number. You can then start operations.
  8. Ongoing compliance: File annual reports, maintain records, and report suspicious transactions.

Banking & Payments

Opening a bank account for a crypto business in Canada can be challenging due to risk-averse policies. Major banks like RBC, TD, and Scotiabank have historically been cautious. However, some credit unions and fintech-friendly banks accept crypto MSBs. Key options include:

  • Credit unions: Many credit unions (e.g., Coast Capital, Vancity) are more open to crypto businesses.
  • Neobanks: Platforms like Wise or Payoneer may offer accounts, but they require FINTRAC registration.
  • Payment processors: Services like Stripe or Square may integrate with crypto platforms.

Benefits of a Canada Crypto License

Canada offers several advantages for crypto businesses:

  • Fast registration: No discretionary licensing means approval in weeks to months, not years.
  • Low cost: No government fees, making it one of the cheapest regulated regimes.
  • No minimum capital: Flexible capitalization requirements.
  • Strong reputation: Canada is a G7 country with a stable legal system, enhancing trust with partners and clients.
  • Access to North American market: Being in Canada allows you to serve Canadian clients and potentially expand to the US.
  • Clear AML framework: FINTRAC's rules are well-defined, reducing regulatory uncertainty.

Compared to the EU's MiCA (which requires EUR 50,000-150,000 capital), Canada is more accessible for startups. However, for businesses seeking zero corporate tax, Panama remains a strong alternative.

Compliance & Trust

Maintaining compliance is crucial for a Canada crypto license. Key obligations include:

  • AML/CFT program: Must be updated annually and include risk assessment, KYC, transaction monitoring, and record-keeping.
  • Reporting: Report suspicious transactions to FINTRAC within 30 days. Report large cash transactions (over CAD 10,000) and virtual currency transactions over CAD 10,000.
  • Record keeping: Maintain records for at least 5 years.
  • Compliance officer: Must be a senior officer resident in Canada (or with a strong presence).
  • Audits: Annual independent audit of AML program may be required.

Common Mistakes

Avoid these pitfalls when applying for a Canada crypto license:

  • Incomplete application: Missing documents or incomplete AML program delays processing.
  • Ignoring provincial securities laws: Offering unregistered securities can lead to enforcement actions.
  • Poor AML program: A generic or poorly drafted program will be rejected.
  • Not appointing a compliance officer: FINTRAC requires a designated officer.
  • Operating before registration: You must register before providing services to Canadians.
  • Underestimating banking challenges: Start the bank account process early.

Frequently asked questions

What is the Canada crypto license?

It is a FINTRAC Money Services Business (MSB) registration for dealing in virtual currency. It is mandatory for any business offering crypto exchange, transfer, or payment services to Canadians.

How long does it take to get a Canada crypto license?

Typically 2 to 6 months. FINTRAC aims to process complete applications within 30 days, but additional information requests can extend the timeline.

Is there a minimum capital requirement?

No statutory minimum capital. However, you must demonstrate financial stability. Most applicants maintain at least CAD 50,000-100,000 in liquid assets.

What are the costs involved?

FINTRAC registration has no fee. Total costs including legal and compliance setup range from CAD 5,000 to 15,000. Annual compliance costs are CAD 5,000-15,000.

Can a foreign company get a Canada crypto license?

Yes, foreign companies offering services to Canadians must register with FINTRAC. You may need to incorporate a Canadian subsidiary or have a physical presence.

Do I need a provincial securities license?

If your business deals with tokens that are securities (e.g., ICOs, security tokens), you may need registration with provincial securities commissions. Consulting24 can advise.

What is the tax rate for crypto businesses in Canada?

Corporate income tax is around 25-30% combined federal and provincial. Crypto transactions are taxable as business income or capital gains. GST/HST generally does not apply to crypto transactions.

Can I operate a crypto ATM with a Canada crypto license?

Yes, but each ATM must be registered separately with FINTRAC. You also need to comply with provincial regulations.

What happens if I operate without registration?

Operating without FINTRAC registration is illegal and can result in fines up to CAD 1 million or imprisonment. You must register before offering services.

How does Canada compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Canada offers a fast, low-cost registration with no capital requirement but higher taxes. Panama has a flat EUR 6,000 fee, zero tax on foreign income, and a simple process. Both are attractive depending on your target market.
