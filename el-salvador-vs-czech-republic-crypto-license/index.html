Overview: What Is the El Salvador vs Czech Republic Crypto License?

The El Salvador crypto license is a regulatory authorization issued by the Central Bank of El Salvador (Banco Central de Reserva) for digital asset service providers. It is based on the Digital Assets Issuance Law (Ley de Emisión de Activos Digitales) enacted in 2023. The Czech Republic crypto license is a registration under the Act on Selected Information Society Services, overseen by the Czech National Bank (CNB). Both licenses allow companies to offer crypto services legally, but the regulatory frameworks and operational requirements differ.

El Salvador's regime is relatively new and designed to attract crypto innovation, with a fast-track process and low capital requirements. The Czech Republic, as an EU member, has a more established regulatory framework that now incorporates MiCA, requiring higher capital and more strong compliance. Understanding these differences is essential for founders choosing between a nimble, low-cost jurisdiction and a stable, EU-compliant one.

For example, a startup launching a simple exchange might prefer El Salvador for speed and low cost, while a firm targeting EU clients with custody services would benefit from the Czech Republic's MiCA alignment. Both jurisdictions have specific requirements for AML/KYC, corporate governance, and reporting.

Who Needs a Crypto License in El Salvador or the Czech Republic?

Any business offering crypto-related services in or from these jurisdictions typically needs a license or registration. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralized and decentralized)
  • Wallet providers (custodial and non-custodial)
  • Payment processors using crypto
  • Token issuers and ICOs/STOs
  • Brokerage and trading platforms
  • DeFi protocols (if providing services to residents)

In El Salvador, the license is mandatory for any digital asset service provider operating within the country, including foreign entities targeting Salvadoran clients. In the Czech Republic, registration is required for entities providing services to Czech residents, even if the company is based elsewhere. Both jurisdictions have extraterritorial reach, so it is important to assess your customer base. For instance, if your platform serves Czech users, you must register with the CNB regardless of your incorporation location.

Exemptions may apply for businesses that only provide services to non-residents or operate purely as software developers without handling customer funds. License Type & Regulator

El Salvador

The regulator is the Central Bank of El Salvador (BCR), responsible for authorizing digital asset service providers. The license is a single category covering exchange, custody, and payment services. The application is submitted via the BCR's online portal. There is no distinction between service classes, which simplifies the process but may lack granularity for specialized activities.

Czech Republic

The regulator is the Czech National Bank (CNB). The license is a registration under the Act on Selected Information Society Services, now harmonized with MiCA. There are different classes of authorization depending on services offered: Class 1 (exchange and custody) requires EUR 50,000 capital; Class 2 (higher-risk services like proprietary trading) requires EUR 125,000; Class 3 (full-service including investment advice) requires EUR 150,000. The CNB also requires a local presence and a designated AML officer.

Both regulators require fit and proper tests for management and shareholders, anti-money laundering (AML) policies, and operational safeguards. The Czech regime imposes more detailed ongoing reporting, including quarterly transaction reports and annual audits.

Capital Requirement

Capital requirements are a major differentiator. El Salvador does not impose a statutory minimum capital for digital asset service providers, though the BCR may require evidence of sufficient financial resources. In practice, a modest capital of around EUR 10,000-50,000 is often sufficient. This low barrier makes El Salvador attractive for startups.

The Czech Republic, under MiCA, has tiered capital requirements: EUR 50,000 for basic services (exchange and custody), EUR 125,000 for more complex services, and EUR 150,000 for full-service providers. This capital must be maintained as own funds and cannot be used for operational expenses. The higher capital reflects the EU's emphasis on consumer protection and financial stability. For example, a Czech Class 1 license requires EUR 50,000 in paid-up capital, which must be held in a bank account and cannot be withdrawn without CNB approval.

These capital requirements are strictly enforced, and failure to maintain them can result in license suspension. Consulting24 advises on capital structuring and proof of funds documentation.

Tax Treatment

Tax treatment is a critical factor for crypto businesses. El Salvador offers a highly favorable tax regime: no capital gains tax on Bitcoin (as legal tender), and corporate income tax is 30% with potential exemptions for digital asset companies. The government has also introduced a tax holiday for new tech companies in certain zones. However, specific crypto-related tax incentives require confirmation with local authorities. For example, companies operating in the International Services Free Zone may enjoy 15-year exemptions on income tax.

The Czech Republic has a standard corporate income tax rate of 19%. Crypto transactions are subject to VAT and capital gains tax, though certain exemptions apply for long-term holdings (e.g., gains on assets held over 3 years may be exempt). The tax treatment is less favorable than El Salvador but offers legal certainty within the EU framework. Double taxation treaties may apply for international operations. For instance, a Czech company with US clients may benefit from the US-Czech tax treaty to avoid double taxation.

Allowed Activities

Both jurisdictions allow a broad range of crypto activities, but with different scopes. In El Salvador, the license covers: exchange between crypto and fiat, custody of digital assets, payment services using crypto, token issuance, and operation of trading platforms. The law also permits Bitcoin as legal tender for all transactions, which can be a unique selling point.

In the Czech Republic, the registration allows: exchange services, custody, transfer services, and advisory services (depending on class). Under MiCA, stablecoin issuance and decentralized finance services are also regulated. The Czech regime is more comprehensive but also more restrictive, with detailed reporting and compliance obligations. For example, offering crypto lending or staking services may require a Class 2 license with higher capital.

Certain activities, such as operating a crypto ATM or providing non-custodial wallet software, may have different regulatory treatments. Consulting24 can map your specific services to the appropriate license category.

Step-by-Step Process for Obtaining the License

El Salvador

  1. Company incorporation in El Salvador (required).
  2. Prepare AML/KYC policies, business plan, and financial projections.
  3. Submit application to BCR via online portal.
  4. BCR reviews and may request additional information.
  5. Approval and issuance of license (2-4 months).

Czech Republic

  1. Company incorporation in the Czech Republic (required).
  2. Appoint a local AML officer and set up compliance framework.
  3. Prepare documentation including AML manual, risk assessment, and organizational structure.
  4. Submit registration to CNB.
  5. CNB review and potential on-site inspection.
  6. Registration granted (4-8 months).

Banking & Payments

Access to banking is a common challenge for crypto businesses. El Salvador has a developing banking sector, but several local banks are open to crypto companies, especially those licensed by the BCR. International payment gateways are also available. However, correspondent banking relationships may be limited, and some banks require a minimum deposit of EUR 50,000 to open an account. For example, Banco Agricola and Banco Cuscatlan have been known to serve licensed crypto firms.

The Czech Republic has a mature banking system with several banks willing to serve licensed crypto firms, including major institutions like ČSOB and UniCredit. Payment processing is straightforward, and SEPA transfers are standard. The Czech Republic also offers access to EU-wide payment infrastructure. Some banks may require a minimum turnover or a compliance interview. Consulting24 can assist with bank introductions and prepare the necessary documentation.

For both jurisdictions, we recommend starting the banking process early, as account opening can take 4-8 weeks.

Benefits of Each Jurisdiction

El Salvador offers low cost, fast setup, no minimum capital, and a crypto-friendly tax environment. It is ideal for startups and small to medium enterprises looking to minimize initial investment. The country's Bitcoin adoption also provides unique marketing opportunities. For example, you can accept Bitcoin payments directly without conversion, appealing to crypto-native customers.

The Czech Republic provides EU market access, regulatory clarity under MiCA, and a stable legal environment. It is better suited for larger, compliance-focused firms that need to serve EU clients and seek long-term credibility. The Czech Republic also has a skilled workforce and strong infrastructure. For instance, Prague has a growing tech talent pool and competitive office rents.

Compliance & Trust

Both jurisdictions require strong AML/KYC compliance. El Salvador's regime is newer and less tested, but the BCR enforces strict reporting. The Czech Republic's regime is mature and aligned with EU standards, offering higher trust among partners and investors. However, compliance costs are higher. For example, a Czech firm must conduct regular transaction monitoring and file suspicious activity reports, which may require dedicated compliance staff.

Frequently asked questions

What is the main difference between El Salvador and Czech Republic crypto licenses?

El Salvador offers a faster, cheaper license with no minimum capital, ideal for startups. The Czech Republic requires higher capital (EUR 50,000-150,000) and longer timelines but provides EU market access and regulatory clarity under MiCA.

How much does a crypto license cost in El Salvador?

Total costs typically range from EUR 6,000 to 18,000, including government fees (EUR 1,000-3,000) and advisory fees (EUR 5,000-15,000). Exact pricing depends on your business model and complexity.

What is the minimum capital for a Czech Republic crypto license?

Under MiCA, the minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for basic services (Class 1), EUR 125,000 for Class 2, and EUR 150,000 for full-service Class 3. This capital must be maintained as own funds.

Which jurisdiction has better tax treatment for crypto?

El Salvador offers more favorable tax treatment, including no capital gains tax on Bitcoin and potential tax holidays. The Czech Republic has a 19% corporate tax rate and standard capital gains tax, but offers legal certainty within the EU.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in El Salvador?

The process typically takes 2-4 months, depending on the completeness of your application and BCR workload. Consulting24 can help streamline the process.

Can I serve EU clients with a Czech Republic crypto license?

Yes, a Czech Republic license allows you to serve clients across the EU under MiCA passporting rules. This is a key advantage over non-EU jurisdictions.

Is banking easier in El Salvador or the Czech Republic?

Banking is generally easier in the Czech Republic due to a mature banking system with several crypto-friendly banks. El Salvador has fewer options, but licensed firms can open accounts with local banks.

What ongoing compliance is required for a Czech Republic license?

Ongoing compliance includes quarterly transaction reporting, annual audits, AML/KYC updates, and maintaining a local AML officer. Non-compliance can lead to fines or license revocation.
