What Is the Crypto Token Issuance License Switzerland?

The crypto token issuance license in Switzerland refers to the regulatory approval required to publicly offer tokens to investors. Unlike some jurisdictions that have a single license for all crypto activities, Switzerland applies a principles-based approach. Depending on the token's economic function, FINMA classifies tokens into three categories: payment tokens (cryptocurrencies), utility tokens (access to a service), and asset tokens (representing assets like equity or debt). Each category triggers different regulatory obligations under Swiss financial market laws.

Token issuers must comply with anti-money laundering (AML) regulations, prospectus requirements, and, in some cases, banking or securities laws. The key regulatory framework includes the Swiss Financial Market Infrastructure Act (FMIA) and the Swiss Code of Obligations. For asset tokens, a prospectus approved by FINMA is typically required. Utility tokens that do not have an investment character may fall outside securities regulation but still require AML compliance.

Who Needs a Crypto Token Issuance License in Switzerland?

Any entity planning to issue tokens to the public in or from Switzerland may need regulatory approval. This includes:

  • Startups conducting an initial coin offering (ICO) or security token offering (STO)
  • Established companies launching utility tokens for platform access
  • Asset managers tokenizing real-world assets (real estate, art, commodities)
  • Decentralized finance (DeFi) projects issuing governance tokens
  • Non-Swiss entities targeting Swiss investors

Even if the token is classified as a utility token, AML obligations apply. For asset tokens, a prospectus and possibly a securities dealer license are required. Consulting24 can help you determine whether your token issuance falls under FINMA's regulatory perimeter and what steps are needed.

Capital Requirements

Switzerland does not impose a fixed minimum capital requirement for token issuers per se. However, if the token issuance triggers a licensing requirement as a securities dealer or bank (e.g., for asset tokens that are considered deposits), minimum capital applies:

  • Securities dealer: Minimum capital of CHF 1.5 million (approximately EUR 1.5 million)
  • Bank: Minimum capital of CHF 10 million (approximately EUR 10 million)
  • For pure AML authorization via SRO: No minimum capital, but the entity must have sufficient financial resources for operations

Most token issuances do not require a banking license. Consulting24 helps structure the issuance to avoid unnecessary capital burdens while remaining compliant.

Tax Treatment of Token Issuance in Switzerland

Switzerland offers a favorable tax environment for token issuers. Key points:

  • Income tax: Proceeds from token sales are generally treated as taxable income at the corporate level. However, for utility tokens, the income may be deferred until the service is provided.
  • VAT: Token issuance is typically exempt from Swiss VAT (value-added tax) if classified as a financial service. Utility tokens may be subject to VAT if they represent a prepayment for taxable services.
  • Wealth tax: Tokens held as assets are subject to cantonal wealth tax for individuals. For companies, tokens are part of the taxable equity.
  • Stamp duty: Issuance of asset tokens may trigger stamp duty if they qualify as securities. Exemptions apply for certain structures.

Allowed Activities Under the License

Depending on the regulatory pathway, the following activities are permitted:

  • Issuance of payment, utility, or asset tokens to the public
  • Marketing and distribution of tokens to Swiss and international investors (subject to cross-border rules)
  • Operation of a secondary market platform (requires a separate license as a trading venue)
  • Provision of custody services for issued tokens (requires a custodian license under the DLT Act)
  • Staking and yield generation from issued tokens (if disclosed in the prospectus)

Activities not covered: banking (taking deposits), collective investment schemes, and insurance. Consulting24 ensures your business model aligns with the permitted scope.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a license to issue a utility token in Switzerland?

It depends. If the utility token does not have an investment character and is not considered a security, you may not need a prospectus. However, if you act as a financial intermediary (e.g., by accepting fiat or crypto in exchange for tokens), you must comply with AML regulations and join an SRO. Consulting24 can help determine your obligations.

What is the cost of a Switzerland crypto token issuance license?

The total cost typically ranges from EUR 31,000 to EUR 68,000, including advisory fees, legal fees, SRO membership, and FINMA application fees. Exact pricing depends on the complexity of your token and the regulatory pathway. Contact Consulting24 for a tailored quote.

How long does it take to get a token issuance license in Switzerland?

The timeline is usually 3 to 6 months. Simple utility token projects may be faster (2-4 months), while asset token offerings requiring a FINMA-approved prospectus can take 6-9 months. The speed depends on the completeness of your documentation and the regulator's workload.

Is there a minimum capital requirement for token issuers in Switzerland?

No fixed minimum capital for most token issuers. However, if your issuance triggers a securities dealer license (e.g., for asset tokens), you need CHF 1.5 million. For pure AML compliance via an SRO, no minimum capital is required, but you must have sufficient financial resources to operate.

What taxes apply to token issuance in Switzerland?

Corporate income tax applies to proceeds (cantonal rates 12-18%). VAT is generally exempt for token issuance if classified as a financial service. Wealth tax and stamp duty may apply in certain cases. Consult a Swiss tax advisor for your specific situation.

Can I issue tokens to investors outside Switzerland with a Swiss license?

Yes, but you must comply with the securities laws of the investors' home countries. A Swiss license does not automatically grant access to other markets. You may need additional registrations or exemptions. Consulting24 can advise on cross-border compliance.

What is the role of an SRO in Switzerland?

Self-Regulatory Organizations (SROs) are private bodies authorized by FINMA to supervise AML compliance. Most token issuers join an SRO (e.g., VQF, PolyReg) instead of being directly supervised by FINMA. The SRO reviews your AML policies and conducts periodic audits.

Does Switzerland have a specific DLT law for token issuance?

Yes, Switzerland enacted the DLT Act (Distributed Ledger Technology Act) that came into force in 2021. It provides a legal framework for tokenized securities and introduces a new license category for DLT trading facilities. Token issuers benefit from legal certainty for asset tokens.

Can I use a Swiss foundation for token issuance?

Yes, many token issuers use a Swiss foundation structure, especially for utility tokens or charitable projects. Foundations are not subject to corporate income tax if they pursue non-profit purposes. However, AML obligations still apply. Consulting24 can advise on the optimal legal structure.

What happens if I issue tokens without a license in Switzerland?

Issuing tokens without the required regulatory approvals can lead to enforcement actions by FINMA, including fines, cease-and-desist orders, and criminal prosecution. Investors may also have claims for damages. Compliance is essential to avoid legal and reputational risks.
