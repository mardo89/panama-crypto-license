What Is a Crypto Fund License in Belize?

A crypto fund license in Belize is a regulatory authorization granted by the IFSC that allows an entity to operate an investment fund whose underlying assets include cryptocurrencies, digital tokens, or other crypto assets. The license is issued under the Investment Funds Act (IFA) and the International Business Companies Act (IBCA). Belize does not have a dedicated 'crypto law' but regulates crypto funds under its existing investment fund framework, which is considered flexible and business-friendly.

The license is typically structured as an International Business Company (IBC) that is licensed as a 'Private Fund' or 'Public Fund' depending on the investor base. Most crypto funds in Belize are set up as private funds, which are exempt from certain public disclosure requirements. The IFSC supervises the fund's compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorist financing (CTF) obligations, as well as fit and proper requirements for directors and managers.

Key features include: no minimum capital requirement for private funds (though practical capital is expected), zero corporate tax on offshore income, and a streamlined application process that can be completed in 2-4 months. Belize is not a member of the EU or OECD, so it is not subject to MiCA or other European directives, making it an attractive alternative for fund managers who want to avoid EU-level regulation.

  • Hedge funds that trade cryptocurrencies, derivatives, or digital assets.

It is also a popular choice for startups and smaller fund managers who cannot afford the high capital requirements of jurisdictions like Luxembourg, Ireland, or the Cayman Islands.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator for crypto fund licenses in Belize is the International Financial Services Commission (IFSC). The IFSC oversees all investment funds, including those dealing with crypto assets. Belize does not have a separate 'crypto fund license' category; instead, funds are licensed under the Investment Funds Act as either:

  • Private Fund: For up to 50 investors, no public offering. No minimum capital requirement, but the fund must have sufficient assets to cover operational costs. Most crypto funds use this structure.
  • Public Fund: For unlimited investors, can be publicly offered. Subject to higher disclosure and capital requirements (typically USD 100,000 or more). Rare for crypto funds.

The IFSC also requires the fund to appoint a licensed fund administrator (often a local Belize company) and a compliance officer. The fund's directors must be fit and proper, and at least one director must be a Belize resident (or the fund must have a local registered agent). The IFSC does not impose a specific minimum capital for private funds, but in practice, regulators expect at least USD 50,000-100,000 in assets under management to demonstrate substance.

For comparison, other jurisdictions like the Cayman Islands require a minimum capital of USD 100,000 for mutual funds, while Malta requires EUR 125,000-150,000 under MiCA. Belize offers a lighter touch.

Capital Requirement for Belize Crypto Fund

Belize does not impose a statutory minimum capital requirement for private funds under the Investment Funds Act. However, the IFSC expects the fund to have sufficient capital to cover its operational expenses and to demonstrate that it is a going concern. In practice, most fund managers maintain at least USD 50,000 to USD 100,000 in assets under management or capital reserves.

For public funds, the IFSC may require a minimum capital of USD 100,000 or more, depending on the fund's investment strategy and risk profile. Additionally, the fund must maintain a minimum net asset value (NAV) to remain licensed. If the NAV falls below a certain threshold (e.g., USD 50,000), the IFSC may revoke the license.

It is important to note that Belize does not have a specific crypto-asset capital regime like the EU's MiCA (which requires EUR 50,000 for simple custody, EUR 125,000 for exchange, and EUR 150,000 for dealing on own account). Belize's flexibility is a key advantage for smaller funds, but it also means the regulator may assess capital on a case-by-case basis. Tax Treatment for Belize Crypto Funds

Belize offers a zero-tax regime for offshore funds. An International Business Company (IBC) that is licensed as a fund is exempt from all Belizean taxes, including corporate income tax, capital gains tax, and withholding tax on dividends and interest. There is no VAT on fund management services. The only ongoing costs are government fees (annual license renewal, registered agent fees) and compliance costs.

However, fund managers must consider tax obligations in the jurisdictions where their investors are resident. Belize does not have double tax treaties with most countries, so investors may be subject to withholding taxes on distributions depending on their home country's laws. The fund itself is not taxed in Belize, but the manager's income may be taxable where the manager is located.

For US investors, Belize funds are typically treated as passive foreign investment companies (PFICs) unless structured as a partnership. EU investors may face anti-tax avoidance rules. Consulting24 advises clients to obtain tax advice from a qualified international tax advisor. Belize's tax neutrality is a strong selling point for fund managers targeting non-EU, non-US investors.

Allowed Activities Under a Belize Crypto Fund License

A Belize crypto fund license permits a wide range of activities related to digital assets, as long as they are conducted within the fund's investment mandate. Typical allowed activities include:

  • Buying, selling, and holding cryptocurrencies (Bitcoin, Ethereum, etc.)
  • Investing in initial coin offerings (ICOs) and security token offerings (STOs)
  • Staking and lending crypto assets to generate yield
  • Derivatives trading (futures, options, swaps) on crypto exchanges
  • Investing in blockchain-based venture capital deals
  • Operating a tokenized fund where shares are represented by digital tokens
  • Providing liquidity to decentralized finance (DeFi) protocols

Activities that are not allowed without additional licensing include: operating a crypto exchange open to the public, providing custody services to third parties (beyond the fund's own assets), or offering investment advice to non-investors. If the fund wants to offer custody to external clients, a separate custody license would be needed. Belize does not have a specific crypto custody license, but the IFSC may consider it under the Banking Act or a special purpose license.

The fund must also comply with AML/CFT regulations, including conducting due diligence on all investors and reporting suspicious transactions to the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU). The fund's administrator is typically responsible for these obligations.

Step-by-Step Process to Get a Crypto Fund License in Belize

Obtaining a crypto fund license in Belize involves several steps. Consulting24 advises and coordinates the process, but the legal work is typically done by a Belize-licensed law firm or corporate services provider.

  1. Incorporate the Fund Entity: Register an International Business Company (IBC) with the Belize Companies Registry. The IBC will be the legal vehicle for the fund. Choose a name, appoint directors and shareholders, and issue shares.
  2. Prepare Fund Documentation: Draft the fund's offering memorandum, trust deed or articles of association, investment management agreement, and subscription agreement. These must comply with the Investment Funds Act and IFSC guidelines.
  3. Appoint Service Providers: Engage a licensed fund administrator (often a Belize trust company), a compliance officer, and a registered agent. The administrator handles NAV calculation, investor onboarding, and AML compliance.
  4. Submit License Application: File the application with the IFSC, including the fund documents, business plan, financial projections, and details of directors and service providers. Pay the application fee.
  5. IFSC Review & Approval: The IFSC reviews the application, which may take 4-6 weeks. They may request additional information or clarifications. Once satisfied, they issue the fund license.
  6. Post-Licensing Compliance: After receiving the license, the fund must file annual returns, audited financial statements, and maintain AML records. The IFSC may conduct periodic inspections.

The entire process typically takes 2-4 months. Consulting24 can provide a detailed timeline and checklist during a consultation.

Banking and Payment Services for Belize Crypto Funds

Opening a bank account for a Belize crypto fund can be challenging due to the perceived risk of crypto-related businesses. Belize has a small banking sector, and most local banks are conservative. Options include:

  • Local Belize banks: Atlantic Bank, Belize Bank, Heritage Bank. They may open accounts for licensed funds, but require extensive due diligence and may reject crypto-related accounts.
  • International banks: Some offshore banks in jurisdictions like Panama, the Cayman Islands, or Puerto Rico accept Belize IBCs. Consulting24 can introduce you to banking partners.
  • EMI and payment processors: Electronic money institutions (EMIs) like TransferWise (now Wise), Revolut, or specialized crypto-friendly EMIs can provide multi-currency accounts. However, they often have restrictions on crypto inflows/outflows.
  • Crypto-friendly banks: A few banks in Switzerland, Liechtenstein, or the Baltics (e.g., Estonia) are open to crypto funds, but require the fund to be licensed in a reputable jurisdiction. Belize's IFSC license may be accepted, but expect higher fees and compliance requirements.

Compliance and Trust Considerations

While Belize offers a light regulatory touch, fund managers must still meet international compliance standards. The IFSC requires all licensed funds to implement AML/CFT policies consistent with the Financial Action Task Force (FATF) recommendations. This includes:

  • Customer due diligence (CDD) on all investors, including beneficial ownership identification.
  • Ongoing transaction monitoring.
  • Reporting suspicious transactions to the Belize Financial Intelligence Unit.
  • Appointing a compliance officer (can be outsourced).
  • Annual audits by a licensed auditor.

Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. Additionally, Belize is on the FATF grey list (as of 2026) for deficiencies in AML enforcement, which may affect the fund's ability to open bank accounts or attract institutional investors. The government is working to address these issues, but fund managers should be aware of the reputational risk.

Consulting24 advises clients to implement strong compliance measures beyond the minimum requirements. This includes using third-party AML screening tools, maintaining detailed records, and conducting regular internal audits. For investors who require a higher level of trust, consider pairing the Belize fund with a licensed fund administrator in a more regulated jurisdiction like Switzerland or Cyprus.

Common Mistakes When Applying for a Belize Crypto Fund License

Based on our experience advising clients, here are common pitfalls to avoid:

  • Inadequate documentation: Submitting a generic offering memorandum or business plan. The IFSC expects detailed, fund-specific documents that clearly outline the investment strategy, risk management, and fee structure.
  • Ignoring substance requirements: Belize requires the fund to have a local registered agent and administrator. Some managers try to use a shell structure without local substance, which can lead to rejection.
  • Underestimating AML compliance: The IFSC takes AML seriously. Failing to have proper policies in place can delay the application or result in post-licensing sanctions.
  • Choosing the wrong fund structure: Some managers opt for a public fund when a private fund would suffice, incurring higher costs and disclosure requirements.
  • Not planning for banking: As noted, banking is a major hurdle. Starting the banking process after the license is issued can cause delays.
  • Overlooking tax implications for investors: Belize's tax neutrality does not shield investors from their home country taxes. Failing to inform investors can lead to legal issues.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto fund license in Belize?

Belize does not have a statutory minimum capital for private funds. However, the IFSC expects the fund to have sufficient assets to cover operational costs. In practice, most funds maintain at least USD 50,000-100,000 in assets or capital reserves. For public funds, the IFSC may require USD 100,000 or more.

How long does it take to get a crypto fund license in Belize?

The typical timeline is 2-4 months from incorporation to license issuance. The IFSC review takes 4-6 weeks, but preparation of documents and appointment of service providers can take additional time. Consulting24 advises budgeting for 3 months.

What taxes does a Belize crypto fund pay?

Belize offshore funds are exempt from corporate income tax, capital gains tax, and withholding tax. There is no VAT on fund services. The only ongoing costs are government fees and compliance expenses. However, investors may be subject to taxes in their home countries.

Can a Belize crypto fund hold investor funds in fiat currency?

Yes, a Belize fund can hold fiat currency as part of its portfolio. The fund can open bank accounts in Belize or abroad to hold fiat. However, banking options are limited, and many funds use multi-currency accounts with EMIs or international banks.

Is a Belize crypto fund license recognized by the EU?

No, Belize is not an EU member, so its license does not provide EU passporting rights. Investors in the EU may face additional regulatory requirements. The fund cannot market itself to EU investors without complying with local laws (e.g., AIFMD).

Do I need to be a resident of Belize to set up a crypto fund?

No, you do not need to be a resident. However, the fund must have a local registered agent and at least one director who is a Belize resident (or the fund must appoint a local corporate director). Many service providers offer nominee director services.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements for a Belize crypto fund?

The fund must file annual returns with the IFSC, submit audited financial statements, maintain AML/CFT records, and appoint a compliance officer. The IFSC may conduct inspections. Annual costs for administration and compliance typically range from USD 5,000-15,000.

Can I convert an existing company into a Belize crypto fund?

Yes, an existing IBC can apply for a fund license if it meets the requirements. However, the company must be in good standing and the conversion may require amendments to its articles. Consulting24 can advise on the process.

What is the difference between a private fund and a public fund in Belize?

A private fund is limited to 50 investors and cannot be publicly offered. It has fewer disclosure requirements and no minimum capital. A public fund can have unlimited investors and is subject to higher capital and reporting standards. Most crypto funds are private.

Does Belize have a specific crypto regulatory framework?

No, Belize regulates crypto funds under the Investment Funds Act and the IBC Act. There is no separate crypto law. The IFSC treats crypto assets as investments, allowing funds to invest in them as part of their strategy. This flexibility is both an advantage and a risk.
