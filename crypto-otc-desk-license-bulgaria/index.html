What Is a Crypto OTC Desk License in Bulgaria?

A crypto OTC desk license in Bulgaria is a regulatory authorisation issued by the Financial Supervision Commission (FSC) under the EU MiCA framework. It allows a company to provide crypto asset services, specifically for OTC trading - meaning large-volume trades executed directly between two parties, not on a public exchange. The license covers the service of 'exchange of crypto assets for funds' and 'exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets' (MiCA Article 3(1)(8)-(9)).

Bulgaria implemented MiCA through amendments to the Markets in Financial Instruments Act and the Anti-Money Laundering Act. As of 2026, all CASPs must be authorised by the FSC and comply with capital, governance, and AML/CFT rules. The license is mandatory for any entity that operates an OTC desk for crypto, whether as a principal (dealing on own account) or as an agent (matching buyers and sellers).

Who Needs a Crypto OTC Desk License in Bulgaria?

Any legal entity that provides OTC crypto trading services to clients in the EU must obtain a Bulgarian crypto OTC desk license. This includes:

  • OTC desks that execute large-block trades for institutional investors (e.g., family offices, hedge funds, pension funds).
  • Market makers that provide liquidity to other platforms.
  • Brokerage firms that arrange OTC trades for clients.
  • Proprietary trading firms dealing on own account in crypto assets.

If your firm is based outside the EU but wants to serve EU clients, obtaining a license in an EU member state like Bulgaria is the most straightforward route under MiCA’s passporting regime. Once licensed in Bulgaria, you can operate across all 27 EU countries without additional authorisation.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator for crypto asset service providers in Bulgaria is the Financial Supervision Commission (FSC), located in Sofia. The license type is a 'Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) authorisation' under the MiCA regulation. The FSC is responsible for supervising all CASPs, including those offering OTC desk services.

Under MiCA, there are three capital tiers based on the services provided:

  • EUR 50,000 - for services that do not involve custody of client assets (e.g., only order matching).
  • EUR 125,000 - for custody of client crypto assets and/or exchange services (most OTC desks fall here).
  • EUR 150,000 - if the firm also deals on own account or provides transfer services.

For a typical OTC desk that holds client assets temporarily and executes trades, the EUR 125,000 tier applies. If the desk also engages in proprietary trading, the EUR 150,000 tier is required.

Capital Requirement

As noted, the minimum capital for a Bulgarian crypto OTC desk license is EUR 125,000 (if the service includes custody and exchange) or EUR 150,000 (if dealing on own account). This capital must be held in a bank account in the company's name and be fully paid up before the license is granted. The FSC requires proof of capital through audited financial statements or a bank letter.

Capital can be in fiat (EUR) or in highly liquid crypto assets, subject to FSC approval. However, most firms prefer fiat to avoid volatility risks. The capital must be maintained throughout the license period; if it falls below the threshold, the firm must top it up or risk suspension.

Tax Treatment

Bulgaria has a flat corporate income tax rate of 10%, one of the lowest in the EU. For crypto OTC desk operations, profits from trading are subject to this rate. VAT is not applicable on crypto trading (exempt under EU VAT rules). However, if the desk charges fees for services (e.g., commission), those fees may be subject to VAT at 20% unless the service qualifies as a financial service exemption.

Consulting24 recommends engaging a local tax advisor to structure the entity optimally. Bulgaria also has a favourable tax treaty network, which can reduce withholding taxes on dividends and interest for foreign shareholders.

Allowed Activities Under the License

A Bulgarian CASP license for an OTC desk permits the following activities:

  • Exchange of crypto assets for funds (fiat on-ramp/off-ramp).
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets.
  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients (if included in the application).
  • Execution of orders for clients (as agent or principal).
  • Placement of crypto assets (underwriting).
  • Transfer services for crypto assets (if applied for).

Activities not covered by the standard OTC desk license include operating a trading platform (exchange) - that requires a separate authorisation under MiCA. If your OTC desk also offers margin trading or derivatives, those may fall under MiFID II rules, requiring a different license.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process for obtaining a crypto OTC desk license in Bulgaria involves several stages:

  1. Company incorporation: Register a Bulgarian limited liability company (EOOD or AD) with a registered office in Bulgaria. Minimum share capital is EUR 1 (but you will need the higher CASP capital).
  2. Prepare documentation: Draft a business plan, AML/KYC policies, risk management procedures, and a governance framework. These must align with MiCA requirements.
  3. Engage local partners: Appoint a local AML officer, a board of directors (at least one Bulgarian resident recommended), and an external auditor.
  4. Submit application to FSC: File the application with the required documents and pay the fee. The FSC has up to 3 months to review, but can request additional information.
  5. Capital verification: Provide proof that the minimum capital is held in a Bulgarian bank account.
  6. FSC inspection: The regulator may conduct an on-site visit or interview key personnel.
  7. License issuance: Once approved, the firm is added to the FSC's register of CASPs and can start operations.

Banking and Payment Services

Opening a bank account for a crypto OTC desk in Bulgaria can be challenging due to the risk-averse nature of traditional banks. However, several Bulgarian banks accept CASP clients, including UniCredit Bulbank, DSK Bank, and First Investment Bank (FIB). Some may require a minimum deposit of EUR 10,000-50,000 and proof of the license application.

For fiat on-ramp/off-ramp, many OTC desks use payment service providers (PSPs) like SatchelPay, Modulr, or Railsr, which integrate with the Bulgarian banking system. Consulting24 can introduce you to banking partners who understand the crypto sector.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, the firm must comply with ongoing AML/CFT obligations under MiCA and Bulgarian law. This includes:

  • Appointing a local AML compliance officer.
  • Conducting regular AML/KYC checks on all clients.
  • Reporting suspicious transactions to the Bulgarian Financial Intelligence Directorate.
  • Maintaining capital at the required level.
  • Submitting annual audited financial statements to the FSC.
  • Notifying the FSC of any changes in management, ownership, or business model.

Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. Consulting24 provides ongoing compliance support to help you stay on track. This is general guidance, not legal advice.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Many applicants make avoidable errors. Here are the most common:

  • Underestimating capital requirements: Ensure you have the full EUR 125,000 or 150,000 in liquid assets before applying.
  • Incomplete AML documentation: The FSC expects detailed policies, not generic templates.
  • Ignoring local presence: The regulator prefers a physical office and local director.
  • Overlooking tax structuring: Proper tax planning can save significant costs.
  • Not engaging experienced advisors: DIY applications often face delays or rejection.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto OTC desk license in Bulgaria?

The minimum capital is EUR 125,000 if the service includes custody and exchange, or EUR 150,000 if the firm also deals on own account. This must be paid up and held in a Bulgarian bank account.

How long does it take to get a Bulgarian crypto OTC desk license?

The typical timeline is 4 to 8 months from company incorporation to license approval, depending on the completeness of the application and the FSC's workload.

Can I passport the Bulgarian license to other EU countries?

Yes. Once licensed by the Bulgarian FSC, you can provide services across all EU member states under the MiCA passporting regime without additional authorisation.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations?

Ongoing obligations include maintaining the minimum capital, appointing a local AML officer, conducting regular KYC, reporting suspicious transactions, and submitting annual audited financial statements to the FSC.

What is the corporate tax rate in Bulgaria for crypto OTC desks?

The corporate income tax rate is a flat 10% on profits. VAT is generally not applicable on crypto trading, but service fees may be subject to VAT.

Does Consulting24 file the Bulgarian license application?

No, Consulting24 advises and coordinates the process. We do not file the application ourselves, but we guide you through the steps and connect you with local experts.

What documents are required for the license application?

Required documents include a business plan, AML/KYC policies, risk management procedures, governance framework, proof of capital, and details of management and shareholders.

Can I use a Bulgarian license to operate an OTC desk outside the EU?

Yes, but the license primarily covers EU clients. For non-EU clients, you may need additional licenses or a different structure, such as a Panama license.

Are there any restrictions on the types of crypto assets I can trade?

Under MiCA, you can trade any crypto asset that is not classified as a security or a stablecoin (which have separate rules). The FSC follows the EU's classification guidelines.

What happens if I fail to maintain the minimum capital?

If capital falls below the required threshold, you must top it up immediately. Failure to do so can lead to fines, suspension, or revocation of the license by the FSC.
