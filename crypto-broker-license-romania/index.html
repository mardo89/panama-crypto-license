What Is the Crypto Broker License in Romania?

The crypto broker license in Romania, officially a CASP authorization under MiCA, allows firms to operate as a broker in crypto assets. This includes executing orders on behalf of clients, placing orders, and providing portfolio management. The license is issued by the Autoritatea de Supraveghere Financiară (ASF), Romania's financial regulator. Unlike some EU member states that previously operated a simple registration regime, Romania now enforces MiCA's full requirements, including minimum capital, governance standards, and AML/CFT obligations.

The license is essential for any entity that wants to legally offer crypto brokerage services to Romanian or EU clients. It covers activities such as receiving and transmitting orders, executing orders, and providing investment advice in crypto assets. The ASF aims to ensure market integrity and investor protection, aligning with EU standards. For firms already operating in other EU jurisdictions, Romania offers a stable and well-regulated environment.

License Type & Regulator

The crypto broker license in Romania is a CASP authorization under MiCA, specifically for brokerage services. The regulator is the Autoritatea de Supraveghere Financiară (ASF), which oversees the entire financial market, including crypto assets. The ASF has been designated as the competent authority for MiCA implementation in Romania.

The license is classified under service types: (1) execution of orders on behalf of clients, (2) placing of orders, (3) reception and transmission of orders, and (4) portfolio management. Firms may apply for one or multiple services. The ASF requires a detailed business plan, risk management policies, and proof of capital. The license is valid across the EU via passporting, making Romania an attractive entry point. For a full list of allowed activities, see the section below. The ASF also coordinates with the European Securities and Markets Authority (ESMA) to ensure consistent application of MiCA across member states.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, Romania applies tiered capital requirements based on the services provided. For a crypto broker license, the minimum capital is EUR 125,000 if the firm provides custody of client funds or EUR 50,000 if no custody. If the firm also offers portfolio management, the requirement is EUR 150,000. These figures are mandatory and must be held as own funds, typically in a bank account or liquid assets.

The ASF may require additional capital based on risk assessment. The capital must be maintained at all times and reported annually. Tax Treatment of Crypto Brokerage in Romania

Romania's tax regime for crypto brokers is defined by the Fiscal Code. As of 2026, corporate income tax is 16% on profits. Crypto brokerage income is treated as ordinary business income. VAT is not applicable on crypto transactions, but brokerage fees may be subject to VAT if considered financial services. The ASF and tax authority (ANAF) have issued guidance clarifying that crypto asset services are exempt from VAT under EU law.

Personal income tax for crypto gains by individuals is 10%, but for corporate entities, the standard rate applies. Romania also has a 5% withholding tax on dividends. There are no specific crypto-only taxes, but transfer pricing rules apply for related-party transactions. Consulting24 coordinates with tax advisors to ensure compliance. Allowed Activities Under the License

The crypto broker license in Romania permits the following activities as per MiCA:

  • Reception and transmission of orders for crypto assets
  • Execution of orders on behalf of clients
  • Placing of crypto assets
  • Portfolio management of crypto assets
  • Providing investment advice on crypto assets

Activities not covered include operating a trading platform (exchange) or custody services, which require separate authorizations. However, a broker can combine with an exchange license if both are applied for. The ASF allows firms to offer ancillary services like research and analysis. Step-by-Step Process for Obtaining the License

The application process for a crypto broker license in Romania involves several stages:

  1. Pre-incorporation planning: Define business model, services, and capital. Consulting24 advises on structure.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Romanian limited liability company (SRL) with a local registered address and at least one local director or representative.
  3. Document preparation: Draft business plan, risk management policies, AML/CFT procedures, and internal controls. These must comply with ASF requirements.
  4. Capital deposit: Open a bank account and deposit the required minimum capital (EUR 50,000-150,000).
  5. Submission: Submit the application to ASF with all supporting documents. The ASF has 90 working days to review, but can request additional information.
  6. Approval and ongoing compliance: Once approved, the firm must submit regular reports, maintain capital, and undergo audits.
