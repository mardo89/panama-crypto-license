Overview: Singapore vs Czech Republic Crypto License

In Singapore, crypto service providers must hold a Major Payment Institution (MPI) license from the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) under the Payment Services Act (PSA). This license covers digital payment token (DPT) services, including exchange, wallet, and transfer services. The regime is known for its rigorous anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CFT) standards, and it is one of the most respected in Asia.

The Czech Republic, as an EU member state, implements the Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA) fully in force since 2025. Crypto asset service providers (CASPs) must register with the Czech National Bank (CNB) and comply with MiCA's harmonised rules. The Czech regime is often seen as more accessible for startups due to lower capital requirements and faster processing times.

Both jurisdictions offer clear legal frameworks, but they differ significantly in cost, timeline, and operational obligations. The table below summarises the key differences.

Who Needs a Crypto License in Singapore or the Czech Republic?

Any business that provides the following services in or from these jurisdictions needs a license or registration:

  • Cryptocurrency exchange (fiat-to-crypto or crypto-to-crypto)
  • Digital wallet services (custodial or non-custodial)
  • Cross-border money transfer involving digital payment tokens
  • Initial exchange offerings (IEOs) or token issuance platforms
  • Brokerage or dealing in crypto assets

In Singapore, the MPI license is required if you exceed certain thresholds (e.g., monthly transaction volume above SGD 3 million for payment services). Smaller operations may fall under a smaller license, but most crypto exchanges aim for the MPI. In the Czech Republic, any entity providing crypto services to EU residents must register as a CASP, regardless of size, under MiCA.

If you are a startup or an established exchange expanding into Asia, Singapore offers prestige and access to a wealthy market. If you prefer a European gateway with lower upfront costs, the Czech Republic is a strong contender.

License Type & Regulator

Singapore: The regulator is the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS). The relevant license is the Major Payment Institution (MPI) license under the Payment Services Act (PSA). MAS also issues a Standard Payment Institution license for smaller operators, but most crypto businesses opt for the MPI due to higher transaction limits.

Czech Republic: The regulator is the Czech National Bank (CNB). Under MiCA, crypto asset service providers must obtain authorisation as a CASP. The CNB oversees compliance with MiCA's operational, capital, and conduct requirements. Unlike Singapore, there is no tiered license; all CASPs follow the same rules.

Both regulators are well-regarded, but MAS is known for its stringent enforcement and high compliance costs, while the CNB is more streamlined for EU-focused businesses.

Capital Requirement

Singapore: The MAS requires a minimum base capital of SGD 250,000 (approx. EUR 160,000) for an MPI license. This capital must be maintained at all times and can be in the form of cash or liquid assets. Higher capital may be required if you handle larger transaction volumes.

Czech Republic: Under MiCA, the capital requirement is tiered based on the type of service: EUR 50,000 for exchange and wallet services, EUR 125,000 for custody of client funds, and EUR 150,000 for dealing on own account. These are lower than Singapore's, making the Czech Republic more accessible for startups.

Both jurisdictions require the capital to be held in a bank account and not used for operational expenses. Consulting24 can help structure your capital to meet local requirements.

Tax Treatment

Singapore: Singapore does not impose capital gains tax. Corporate income tax is at a flat rate of 17%, with tax exemptions for startups (up to SGD 100,000 exempt for the first three years). Goods and Services Tax (GST) is not applied to digital payment tokens, making it a tax-efficient jurisdiction for crypto trading.

Czech Republic: Corporate income tax is 19%. Cryptocurrency is treated as an intangible asset for tax purposes, and gains are subject to corporate tax. VAT is not applied to crypto transactions under EU rules. The Czech Republic offers a tax exemption for long-term holdings (over 3 years) for individuals, but for corporate entities, standard rates apply.

Both jurisdictions are relatively tax-friendly compared to other EU or Asian countries. For detailed tax planning, consult a local advisor.

Allowed Activities

Singapore (MPI License): You may operate a digital payment token exchange (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto), provide custodial wallet services, facilitate cross-border money transfers using DPTs, and offer merchant acquisition services. You cannot conduct securities-like token offerings without a separate capital markets license.

Czech Republic (CASP): Under MiCA, you may provide exchange services (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto), custody and administration of crypto assets, operation of a trading platform, transfer services, and advice on crypto assets. MiCA also allows for the issuance of asset-referenced tokens and e-money tokens, subject to additional rules.

Both jurisdictions prohibit unlicensed activities such as unregistered securities offerings or money laundering. The Czech Republic's scope is slightly broader due to MiCA's comprehensive coverage.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Singapore:

  1. Incorporate a company in Singapore (e.g., a private limited company).
  2. Appoint a director who is a Singapore resident (or obtain a local nominee).
  3. Prepare detailed AML/CFT policies, business plan, and risk assessment.
  4. Engage a local compliance consultant (Consulting24 can coordinate).
  5. Submit the MPI application via MAS's online portal, including all supporting documents.
  6. Undergo MAS's review and respond to queries (typically 6-12 months).
  7. Upon approval, pay the license fee and commence operations under MAS supervision.

Czech Republic:

  1. Register a company in the Czech Republic (e.g., s.r.o. - limited liability company).
  2. Appoint a local director or representative (Czech resident).
  3. Prepare a compliance program, including AML policies and a business continuity plan.
  4. Submit the CASP application to the Czech National Bank with required documentation.
  5. The CNB reviews the application within 3 months (extendable).
  6. Once approved, register with the CNB and begin operations.

Banking & Payment Services

Singapore: Opening a corporate bank account is challenging but possible with major banks like DBS, OCBC, or UOB, especially if you have a strong compliance framework. Some digital banks (e.g., Revolut, Wise) also accept crypto businesses. MAS requires segregation of client funds and regular audits.

Czech Republic: Banking is more accessible for crypto firms. Local banks such as CSOB, Komercni Banka, and Moneta Money Bank offer accounts to licensed CASPs, though they may require a compliance review. EU payment institutions (e.g., Paysera, TransferWise) are also options. Under MiCA, client funds must be held in a separate account.

In both jurisdictions, having a local bank account is crucial for fiat on/off ramps. Consulting24 can introduce you to banking partners.

Benefits of Each Jurisdiction

Singapore Benefits:

  • Strong reputation and trust among Asian investors
  • No capital gains tax and low corporate tax (17%)
  • Stable political and legal environment
  • Access to a wealthy, tech-savvy market

Czech Republic Benefits:

  • Lower capital requirements and faster approval (3-6 months)
  • EU passporting under MiCA (serve all EU/EEA markets)
  • Lower professional fees and ongoing costs
  • Central location in Europe with good infrastructure

Your choice depends on your target market and budget. If you need a global hub with prestige, choose Singapore. If you want a cost-effective EU gateway, choose the Czech Republic.

Compliance & Trust

Both jurisdictions demand high compliance standards. Singapore's MAS is known for its rigorous enforcement, including on-site inspections and hefty fines for non-compliance. You must appoint a compliance officer, conduct regular AML training, and submit periodic reports. The trust associated with an MAS license is significant, but the cost of compliance is high.

The Czech Republic, under MiCA, requires similar AML/CFT measures, but the regulatory burden is lighter. The CNB focuses on consumer protection and market integrity. Compliance costs are lower, but the license may carry less prestige than Singapore's.

Common Mistakes When Choosing Between Singapore and Czech Republic

Founders often make these errors:

  • Underestimating costs: Singapore's total cost can exceed EUR 200,000 in the first year, while the Czech Republic is around EUR 50,000-80,000. Budget accordingly.
  • Ignoring timeline: Singapore's 9+ month process can delay market entry. If you need speed, the Czech Republic is better.
  • Overlooking tax implications: While both are tax-friendly, the Czech Republic's 19% corporate tax is slightly higher than Singapore's 17%, and Singapore has no capital gains tax.
  • Neglecting local presence: Both require a local director or representative. Plan for this early.
  • Failing to prepare AML documentation: Both regulators reject incomplete applications. Use a compliance specialist.

Frequently asked questions

What is the main difference between a Singapore MPI license and a Czech CASP registration?

The Singapore MPI license is regulated by MAS under the Payment Services Act, with a capital requirement of SGD 250,000 and a 6-12 month timeline. The Czech CASP is regulated by the CNB under MiCA, with capital from EUR 50,000 and a 3-6 month timeline. Singapore is more prestigious but costlier.

Can I serve EU clients with a Czech crypto license?

Yes, under MiCA, a Czech CASP can passport its services across all EU/EEA member states without additional licenses. This is a major advantage of the Czech Republic over Singapore, which only covers Singapore.

Which jurisdiction is cheaper for a startup: Singapore or Czech Republic?

The Czech Republic is significantly cheaper. Total first-year costs (including professional fees) are around EUR 50,000-80,000, compared to EUR 150,000-250,000 for Singapore. The Czech Republic also has lower ongoing compliance costs.

Do I need to be physically present in Singapore or the Czech Republic to get a license?

Both require a local director or representative. In Singapore, at least one director must be a Singapore resident. In the Czech Republic, you need a local representative or director. Consulting24 can help you find a nominee service if needed.

What are the tax rates for crypto businesses in Singapore vs Czech Republic?

Singapore has no capital gains tax and a corporate tax of 17%, with startup exemptions. The Czech Republic has a 19% corporate tax and treats crypto as intangible assets. Both are tax-friendly, but Singapore is slightly better for capital gains.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Singapore?

The process typically takes 6-12 months from application to approval. MAS is thorough and often requests additional information, which can extend the timeline. Professional preparation can help speed things up.

Is the Czech Republic a good alternative if I cannot afford Singapore?

Absolutely. The Czech Republic offers a lower capital requirement (EUR 50,000), faster approval (3-6 months), and lower overall costs. It is an excellent gateway to the EU market. For an even cheaper option, consider Panama at EUR 6,000 flat.

What happens if I operate without a license in Singapore or Czech Republic?

Operating without a license is illegal and can result in fines, imprisonment, or business closure. In Singapore, penalties include fines up to SGD 125,000 and/or imprisonment. In the Czech Republic, the CNB can impose fines up to EUR 5 million or 10% of annual turnover under MiCA.

Which license is better for a crypto exchange targeting both Asia and Europe?

If you have the budget, obtaining both licenses is possible. However, for a single jurisdiction, the Czech Republic offers EU-wide access, while Singapore is limited to Asia. Many businesses start with a Czech license and later add Singapore for Asian expansion.

Do I need a local bank account for the license application?
