What Is the Romania Crypto License?

The Romania crypto license is a regulatory authorization issued by the National Authority for Financial Supervision (ASF) that permits companies to offer crypto asset services within Romania and across the EU under the MiCA passport. It is a mandatory registration for any entity that provides virtual currency exchange, custody, transfer, or other CASP activities to Romanian residents or from a Romanian establishment.

Under MiCA, the license is classified as a CASP registration, with capital requirements tied to the type of services offered. The regime replaced earlier, less formal registration systems and aligns Romania with other EU member states. Key features include:

  • Full compliance with MiCA's operational and prudential standards.
  • Passporting rights to serve clients in other EU countries without additional local licenses.
  • Supervision by ASF, which also oversees traditional financial markets.

Who Needs a Romania Crypto License?

Any legal entity that provides one or more of the following services in Romania or to Romanian residents must obtain a crypto license from ASF:

  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency or other crypto assets.
  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients.
  • Operation of a trading platform for crypto assets.
  • Transfer of crypto assets on behalf of clients.
  • Receipt and transmission of orders for crypto assets.
  • Placement of crypto assets (underwriting or placing).

This applies to both startups and established companies expanding into Romania. If you are a foreign entity, you must incorporate a local subsidiary (typically an SRL) before applying. The license is also required for decentralized finance (DeFi) projects that involve intermediation or custody, though pure software development may be exempt.

License Type and Regulator

The Romania crypto license is a Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) registration under MiCA. The competent authority is the National Authority for Financial Supervision (ASF), which is also responsible for supervising securities markets, insurance, and private pensions. ASF has published guidelines on the application process and ongoing compliance requirements.

There is no separate license for different service types; instead, the license covers all CASP activities, but the capital requirement varies. The license is valid indefinitely as long as the company remains compliant with MiCA and local regulations. ASF conducts regular inspections and requires annual reporting.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the capital requirement for a CASP in Romania is tiered based on the services provided. As of 2026, the minimum capital is:

  • EUR 50,000 for services such as custody and administration of crypto assets, or exchange of crypto for fiat.
  • EUR 125,000 for services that include the operation of a trading platform or the exchange of crypto for crypto.
  • EUR 150,000 for services involving the placement of crypto assets or the reception and transmission of orders.

These amounts must be fully paid up in cash and held in a bank account in Romania. The capital serves as a buffer to cover potential liabilities and operational risks. ASF may require additional capital if the company's business model involves higher risk.

Tax Treatment for Crypto Businesses in Romania

Romania's tax regime for crypto businesses is relatively favorable. Corporate income tax is 16% on profits, which is standard for all companies. VAT is not applicable to crypto asset transactions under current law, as they are treated as exempt financial services. However, advisory and management fees may be subject to VAT.

For individual investors, capital gains from crypto are taxed at 10% if held for more than one year, or as regular income (up to 10% plus social contributions) if traded frequently. Companies must comply with transfer pricing rules for related-party transactions.

Allowed Activities Under the Romania Crypto License

The Romania crypto license permits the following activities, as defined by MiCA:

  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency.
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets.
  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients.
  • Operation of a crypto asset trading platform.
  • Transfer of crypto assets on behalf of clients.
  • Reception and transmission of orders for crypto assets.
  • Placement of crypto assets (underwriting or placing with a firm commitment).
  • Providing advice on crypto assets.

Stablecoin issuance and other activities involving asset-referenced tokens may require a separate license under MiCA. ASF has not yet issued specific guidance on DeFi, but intermediaries are expected to register.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a Romania crypto license involves several stages. Consulting24 coordinates each step with local legal partners.

  1. Pre-application assessment: We review your business model, identify the required capital tier, and prepare a roadmap.
  2. Company incorporation: You must incorporate a Romanian SRL (limited liability company) with a registered office and a local director. This takes 2-4 weeks.
  3. Document preparation: Draft a detailed business plan, AML/KYC policies, risk management procedures, and governance framework. These must comply with MiCA and ASF guidelines.
  4. Capital deposit: Open a corporate bank account in Romania and deposit the required minimum capital. This can be challenging due to bank reluctance; Consulting24 assists with introductions.
  5. Application submission: File the application with ASF, including all supporting documents and the fee. ASF has 3 months to review, with possible requests for additional information.
  6. Approval and ongoing compliance: Once approved, you must submit annual reports, maintain capital, and undergo periodic audits.

Operating under a Romania crypto license requires strong compliance with AML/CFT regulations. You must appoint a local AML officer, implement transaction monitoring systems, and conduct customer due diligence. ASF conducts on-site inspections and can impose fines for non-compliance.

Common Mistakes When Applying for a Romania Crypto License

Avoid these pitfalls to ensure a smooth application:

  • Incomplete documentation: ASF requires detailed policies. Submitting generic templates leads to rejection or delays.
  • Underestimating capital requirements: Ensure you have the correct tier for your services and that funds are available in cash.
  • Choosing the wrong legal structure: An SRL is standard, but some businesses benefit from a branch or partnership.
  • Ignoring banking challenges: Start the bank account opening process early; it can take months.

Frequently asked questions

What is the Romania crypto license?

What are the capital requirements for a Romania crypto license?

Minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for basic services, EUR 125,000 for trading platforms, and EUR 150,000 for placement services, as per MiCA tiers.
