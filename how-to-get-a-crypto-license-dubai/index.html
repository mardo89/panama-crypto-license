What Is a Dubai Crypto License?

A Dubai crypto license is a regulatory authorization issued by the Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA) that allows businesses to provide virtual asset services within or from Dubai (excluding the Dubai International Financial Centre, which has its own regulator). The license covers a range of activities including exchange, custody, payment services, and more. VARA was established in 2022 and has since developed a comprehensive rulebook that aligns with international standards, including FATF recommendations.

Dubai's approach is to support innovation while ensuring investor protection and market integrity. The license is mandatory for any entity offering virtual asset services in Dubai. Without it, operations are illegal. The framework is divided into four main categories: Advisory, Broker-Dealer, Exchange, and Custody, each with specific requirements. For example, an exchange license requires a higher minimum capital and more stringent cybersecurity measures compared to an advisory license. VARA also requires a physical office in Dubai and the appointment of a local compliance officer. This regulatory clarity makes Dubai attractive for serious crypto entrepreneurs who want to operate in a well-defined legal environment.

Who Needs a Dubai Crypto License?

Any business that provides virtual asset services in Dubai must obtain a license from VARA. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (spot and derivatives)
  • Broker-dealers and trading platforms
  • Custody and wallet providers
  • Payment and remittance services using virtual assets
  • Advisory and portfolio management services related to virtual assets
  • Token issuers and DeFi protocols (depending on structure)

If your business touches any of these activities and is based in Dubai, you need a license. Even if you are a foreign entity targeting Dubai clients, you may need a local presence and license. For instance, a crypto exchange based in Singapore that actively markets to Dubai residents would likely fall under VARA's jurisdiction. License Type and Regulator

The regulator for Dubai's virtual asset sector is the Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA). VARA is the sole authority for regulating virtual assets in Dubai (mainland and free zones except DIFC). It operates under Dubai Law No. 4 of 2022.

VARA offers several license categories:

  • Advisory Services - providing advice on virtual assets
  • Broker-Dealer Services - arranging deals in virtual assets
  • Exchange Services - operating a virtual asset exchange
  • Custody Services - safekeeping of virtual assets
  • Payment Services - facilitating payments using virtual assets

Each category has its own capital and compliance requirements. Most applicants start with a single category and expand later. VARA also requires a physical office in Dubai and the appointment of a local compliance officer. For example, a broker-dealer license requires a minimum capital of AED 500,000 and a detailed AML policy. The regulatory framework is designed to be modular, allowing businesses to add services as they grow. Consulting24 can help you choose the right category and prepare the necessary documentation.

Capital Requirement

VARA imposes minimum capital requirements based on the license category. As of 2026, the typical ranges are:

  • Advisory Services: AED 100,000 - 200,000 (approx. EUR 25,000 - 50,000)
  • Broker-Dealer Services: AED 500,000 - 1,000,000 (approx. EUR 125,000 - 250,000)
  • Exchange Services: AED 1,000,000 - 2,000,000 (approx. EUR 250,000 - 500,000)
  • Custody Services: AED 1,000,000 - 2,000,000 (approx. EUR 250,000 - 500,000)

Capital must be held in a bank account in Dubai and be fully paid up before license issuance. These figures are subject to change; exact amounts are confirmed during the application process. Tax Treatment for Dubai Crypto Licenses

Dubai offers a highly favorable tax environment. There is no corporate income tax for most businesses (except oil and foreign banks). However, a 9% corporate tax was introduced in 2023 for taxable profits exceeding AED 375,000 (approx. EUR 95,000). For crypto businesses, this applies to profits from trading, fees, and other revenue. There is no VAT on virtual asset transactions, and no capital gains tax on crypto holdings. Personal income tax is zero. Double taxation treaties are available with many countries.

In contrast, Panama also has territorial taxation (no tax on foreign-source income), making it another tax-efficient choice. However, Dubai's tax regime is more established for crypto-specific activities. Allowed Activities Under a Dubai Crypto License

A VARA license permits a range of activities, depending on the category. Common allowed activities include:

  • Operating a virtual asset exchange (spot and derivatives)
  • Broker-dealer services (matching buyers and sellers)
  • Custody of virtual assets
  • Virtual asset payment services
  • Advisory and portfolio management
  • Token issuance (subject to additional approval)
  • Staking and lending (if disclosed and approved)

Prohibited activities include unlicensed gambling-related tokens, privacy coins (if not compliant with AML), and activities that violate Islamic finance principles (if operating in a sharia-compliant manner). VARA requires all activities to be clearly defined in the license application. For instance, if you plan to offer margin trading, you must specify the use limits and risk management protocols. Step-by-Step Process to Get a Dubai Crypto License

The process involves several stages. Consulting24 covers Dubai for comparison only - we do not file VARA applications and deliver licenses directly in Panama, Estonia, and Lithuania.

  1. Pre-application assessment - Determine the appropriate license category and prepare a business plan.
  2. Company incorporation - Register a legal entity in Dubai mainland or a free zone (e.g., DMCC, ADGM).
  3. Office setup - Secure physical office space and meet VARA's premises requirements.
  4. Documentation - Prepare AML/KYC policies, risk assessment, governance framework, and financial projections.
  5. Submit application - File with VARA along with the application fee.
  6. VARA review - VARA assesses the application, may request additional information, and conducts interviews.
  7. Capital deposit - Deposit the required minimum capital in a Dubai bank account.
  8. License issuance - Upon approval, pay the license fee and receive the license.
  9. Ongoing compliance - Submit periodic reports, undergo audits, and maintain AML/CFT controls.

Frequently asked questions

What is a Dubai crypto license?

A Dubai crypto license is a regulatory authorization from VARA allowing businesses to provide virtual asset services in Dubai (excluding DIFC). It covers exchanges, custody, payments, advisory, and more. The license is mandatory for any crypto business operating in Dubai and ensures compliance with AML/CFT standards.

How much does a Dubai crypto license cost?

The total first-year cost typically ranges from EUR 67,000 to EUR 145,000, including application fees, license issuance, compliance setup, office rent, and ongoing costs. Exact pricing depends on the license category and complexity. For a cheaper alternative, Panama costs a flat EUR 6,000.

How long does it take to get a Dubai crypto license?

The process takes 3 to 6 months from application to license issuance. This depends on the completeness of your documentation and VARA's review workload. For a faster option, Panama can be done in 4-6 weeks.

What are the capital requirements for a Dubai crypto license?

Minimum capital varies by category: Advisory (AED 100,000-200,000), Broker-Dealer (AED 500,000-1,000,000), Exchange and Custody (AED 1,000,000-2,000,000). Capital must be deposited in a Dubai bank account before license issuance.

Do I need a physical office in Dubai for a crypto license?

Yes, VARA requires a physical office in Dubai. A virtual office is not acceptable. The office must meet certain standards, such as having a reception area and meeting rooms. Free zones like DMCC offer suitable office spaces.

What taxes apply to a Dubai crypto license holder?

Dubai has no personal income tax and no VAT on virtual asset transactions. Corporate tax is 9% on profits exceeding AED 375,000 (approx. EUR 95,000). There is no capital gains tax on crypto holdings. Double taxation treaties are available.

Can I operate a crypto exchange with a Dubai license?

Yes, a VARA exchange license allows you to operate a spot or derivatives exchange. You must meet higher capital requirements (AED 1,000,000+) and implement strong cybersecurity and AML measures. Additional approvals may be needed for derivatives.

Is a Dubai crypto license recognized internationally?

While not automatically passportable like an EU MiCA license, a VARA license is highly respected and signals regulatory compliance. It can facilitate partnerships with banks and institutional investors globally. For EU passporting, consider Lithuania or Estonia.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations for a Dubai crypto license?

Obligations include annual AML audits, quarterly financial reporting, transaction monitoring, maintaining a local compliance officer, and periodic VARA inspections. Custodians need insurance of at least AED 1 million. Non-compliance can lead to fines or license revocation.
