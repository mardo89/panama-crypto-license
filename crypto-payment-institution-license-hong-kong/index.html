What Is the Crypto Payment Institution License in Hong Kong?

The crypto payment institution license in Hong Kong is a regulatory authorization that allows companies to provide crypto-related payment services, such as crypto-to-fiat conversion, digital wallet services, and merchant payment processing. It is part of Hong Kong's updated 2026 regulatory framework, which treats crypto payment services as a subset of stored value facilities and payment systems. The license is issued by the HKMA, with additional oversight from the SFC for activities involving securities-like tokens.

This license is distinct from the traditional money service operator license and is tailored for businesses that handle crypto assets. It requires a minimum capital of HKD 5 million (approximately EUR 600,000) and mandates strong anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorist financing (CTF) controls. The application process typically takes 6 to 9 months, with ongoing compliance obligations including regular audits and reporting.

Who Needs This License?

Any company intending to offer crypto payment services to Hong Kong residents or businesses must obtain this license. Key entities that require it include:

  • Crypto exchanges that offer payment or wallet services
  • Digital wallet providers that support crypto transactions
  • Payment processors facilitating crypto-to-fiat conversions
  • Merchant service providers that accept crypto payments
  • Remittance services using crypto assets

If your business involves holding, transferring, or converting crypto assets as part of a payment service, this license is mandatory. Exemptions apply only for very limited use cases, such as closed-loop systems or services offered solely to professional investors. License Type & Regulator

The crypto payment institution license in Hong Kong is regulated by the Hong Kong Monetary Authority (HKMA) as the primary regulator for payment systems and stored value facilities. The Securities and Futures Commission (SFC) also plays a role if the crypto assets involved are classified as securities. The license is formally a type of Stored Value Facility (SVF) license, adapted for crypto assets under the Payment Systems and Stored Value Facilities Ordinance (PSSVFO).

In 2026, the HKMA and SFC have issued joint guidelines clarifying the scope of crypto payment services. The license covers activities such as issuing crypto-backed stored value, facilitating crypto payments, and providing crypto wallet services. It does not cover crypto trading platforms (which require a separate SFC license) or asset management. Consulting24 advises clients on the correct license type based on their specific activities.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a crypto payment institution license in Hong Kong is HKD 5 million (approximately EUR 600,000). This capital must be maintained at all times and can be in the form of cash or highly liquid assets. The HKMA may require higher capital based on the volume of transactions or risk profile of the business.

This capital serves as a buffer against operational risks and ensures the institution can meet its obligations to customers. It must be held in a licensed bank in Hong Kong. Tax Treatment

Hong Kong operates a territorial tax system, meaning only profits sourced in Hong Kong are subject to tax. The corporate tax rate is a flat 16.5% on assessable profits. For crypto payment institutions, income from crypto payment services (e.g., transaction fees, spread income) is generally considered Hong Kong-sourced if the services are provided to Hong Kong customers.

Capital gains from crypto assets are not taxed, but trading gains may be subject to tax if considered part of the business. There is no VAT or GST on crypto services. Hong Kong has no withholding tax on dividends or interest. Consulting24 recommends engaging a local tax advisor to optimize your tax structure. Allowed Activities

Under the crypto payment institution license, the following activities are permitted:

  • Issuance of crypto-backed stored value (e.g., stablecoins)
  • Facilitation of crypto payments between merchants and customers
  • Digital wallet services for storing and transferring crypto assets
  • Conversion of crypto to fiat and vice versa (as part of payment service)
  • Remittance services using crypto assets

Activities that are not allowed include operating a crypto exchange (requires SFC license), asset management, and lending or staking services. The license is strictly for payment-related services. Consulting24 can help you define your business scope to ensure it aligns with the license.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The application process for a Hong Kong crypto payment institution license involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application preparation: Define your business model, prepare compliance policies (AML/CTF, risk management), and engage a local auditor. Consulting24 coordinates this phase.
  2. Submission: Submit the application to HKMA with all required documents, including business plan, financial projections, and ownership structure.
  3. Initial review: HKMA reviews the application for completeness and may request additional information within 1-2 months.
  4. Evaluation: HKMA conducts a thorough assessment, including interviews with key personnel and background checks. This takes 3-4 months.
  5. Approval: If approved, the license is issued with conditions. The institution must then operationalize its systems and undergo a final audit.
  6. Post-licensing: Ongoing compliance includes monthly reporting, annual audits, and regular AML training.
