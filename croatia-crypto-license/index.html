What Is the Croatia Crypto License?

The Croatia crypto license is the regulatory authorisation required to operate as a crypto-asset service provider (CASP) in Croatia. As of 2026, it is fully governed by the EU Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA), replacing any previous national regimes. The license is issued by the Croatian Financial Supervisory Authority (HANFA) and allows companies to offer services such as crypto exchange, custody, and transfer within the EU single market.

Key features of the croatia crypto license include:

  • Full EU passporting rights to serve clients across all 27 member states
  • Compliance with MiCA's harmonised rules on capital, governance, and consumer protection
  • Registration with HANFA as the competent authority
  • Ongoing reporting and AML obligations

For businesses seeking a foothold in the EU crypto market, the croatia crypto license offers a credible and regulated pathway. Consulting24 advises on the suitability of this license for your specific business model, comparing it with options in other jurisdictions.

Who Needs a Croatia Crypto License?

Any legal entity intending to provide crypto-asset services in or from Croatia must obtain a croatia crypto license. This includes both domestic startups and foreign companies looking to establish an EU presence. Typical candidates include:

  • Crypto exchanges (spot and derivatives)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Brokerage platforms
  • Payment services involving crypto assets
  • Token issuers (if offering services like transfer or exchange)

If your business involves any of the activities listed under MiCA, you must be licensed. Operating without a license can lead to fines, reputational damage, and legal action. License Type & Regulator

The croatia crypto license is a CASP registration under MiCA, supervised by HANFA (Hrvatska agencija za nadzor financijskih usluga). HANFA is the single regulator for financial services in Croatia, including insurance, securities, and now crypto assets. The license is not a simple registration; it involves a thorough assessment of the applicant's governance, AML procedures, IT security, and financial soundness.

Under MiCA, there are several service classes, each with specific capital requirements. The croatia crypto license covers all CASP activities, and applicants must specify which services they intend to offer. HANFA expects a detailed business plan, risk management framework, and evidence of fit and proper management.

For more details on the regulatory framework, you can visit the official HANFA website (see authority links below). Consulting24 coordinates the entire application process, ensuring all documentation meets HANFA's standards.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the croatia crypto license requires minimum capital based on the type of services offered. As of 2026, the capital tiers are:

  • EUR 50,000 for simple services like custody and transfer
  • EUR 125,000 for exchange services (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto)
  • EUR 150,000 for full-service platforms including trading and exchange

These amounts must be maintained as own funds, and HANFA may require additional capital based on operational risk. The capital must be fully paid up before authorisation. Tax Treatment for Crypto Businesses in Croatia

Croatia's tax regime for crypto businesses is relatively favourable. Corporate income tax is a flat 10% for companies with annual revenue up to EUR 1 million, and 12% above that. VAT is generally not applicable to crypto-to-crypto transactions, though fiat conversions may be subject to standard VAT (25%). Capital gains on crypto assets held by the company are taxed at the corporate rate.

For individual investors, crypto gains are subject to a 12% capital gains tax if assets are sold within two years of acquisition. Holding beyond two years is tax-exempt. However, for licensed CASPs, the tax treatment of trading income is treated as business income. Allowed Activities Under the Croatia Crypto License

The croatia crypto license permits all CASP activities defined under MiCA, including:

  • Providing custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Operating a trading platform for crypto assets
  • Exchanging crypto assets for fiat currency or other crypto assets
  • Executing orders on behalf of clients
  • Placing crypto assets
  • Receiving and transmitting orders for crypto assets
  • Providing advice on crypto assets
  • Managing a crypto-asset portfolio

Additionally, licensed CASPs can offer ancillary services such as staking, lending, and yield products, provided they comply with MiCA and HANFA's guidelines. The license does not cover security tokens or e-money tokens, which fall under other EU regulations. Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining a croatia crypto license involves several stages. Here is the typical process:

  1. Pre-application preparation: Define your business model, select the CASP services, and prepare a detailed business plan. Engage legal counsel and compliance experts.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a legal entity in Croatia (usually a limited liability company, d.o.o.). The minimum share capital is EUR 2,500, but higher capital may be needed for the license.
  3. Documentation: Prepare AML/KYC policies, risk management framework, IT security policy, business continuity plan, and governance documents. All must be in Croatian or certified translation.
  4. Submission: Submit the application to HANFA with all required documents and the application fee. HANFA will review and may request additional information.
  5. Assessment: HANFA evaluates the application, including fit and proper checks on management and shareholders. This stage may take 3-6 months.
  6. Approval: If successful, HANFA issues the CASP registration. You can then begin operations.

Common Mistakes in the Application Process

Many applicants face delays or rejection due to common errors. Avoid these pitfalls:

  • Underestimating IT security: A security audit is often required; ensure you have appropriate measures in place.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a Croatia crypto license and a simple registration?

Under MiCA, the Croatia crypto license is a full CASP authorisation, not a simple registration. It requires meeting capital, governance, and AML standards, and allows EU-wide passporting. A simple registration would not provide the same legal certainty or market access.

Can I apply for a Croatia crypto license as a non-EU company?

Yes, non-EU companies can apply by incorporating a subsidiary in Croatia. The subsidiary must have a physical office and local management. Consulting24 assists with company setup and the application process.

How long does the Croatia crypto license application take?

The typical timeline is 3 to 6 months from submission to approval. Preparation can take an additional 1-2 months. Delays can occur if documents are incomplete or if HANFA requests further information.

What are the capital requirements for a Croatia crypto license?

Minimum capital depends on services: EUR 50,000 for custody/transfer, EUR 125,000 for exchange, and EUR 150,000 for full-service platforms. These amounts must be maintained as own funds.

Is Croatia a tax-friendly jurisdiction for crypto businesses?

Yes, Croatia offers a corporate tax rate of 10% for companies with revenue up to EUR 1 million, and 12% above that. Crypto-to-crypto transactions are generally VAT-exempt. Individual investors benefit from a 12% capital gains tax on short-term holdings.

Can I passport my Croatia crypto license to other EU countries?

Yes, the Croatia crypto license allows passporting across the entire EU single market. You can serve clients in any member state without additional licensing, subject to notification to the host regulator.

What activities are NOT allowed under a Croatia crypto license?

The license does not cover security tokens, e-money tokens, or asset-referenced tokens, which fall under other EU regulations. Additionally, services like crypto lending may be restricted unless explicitly included in your license scope.

Do I need a local office in Croatia?

Yes, you need a registered office in Croatia. HANFA expects a physical presence, including a local compliance officer and management. Virtual offices are typically not accepted.

How much does the Croatia crypto license cost in total?

Total costs range from approximately EUR 14,500 to EUR 31,000, including government fees, legal advisory, AML setup, and IT audit. Ongoing annual compliance costs are EUR 5,000-10,000. Exact figures depend on your business complexity.

What happens if I operate without a Croatia crypto license?

Operating without a license is illegal and can result in fines, criminal charges, and forced cessation of activities. HANFA has enforcement powers under MiCA. Always ensure you are licensed before offering services.
