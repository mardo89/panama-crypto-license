What Is a Crypto NFT Marketplace License Romania?

A crypto NFT marketplace license Romania is a regulatory authorization that allows a company to operate a platform where users can buy, sell, and trade NFTs using cryptocurrencies. The license is issued by the Romanian Financial Supervisory Authority (ASF) under the transposition of the EU's 5th Anti-Money Laundering Directive (5AMLD) and the Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA), which is fully in force in 2026.

Key features of the license:

  • It classifies NFT marketplaces as Virtual Asset Service Providers (VASPs).
  • The license covers the exchange between crypto assets and fiat currency, exchange between crypto assets, and transfer of crypto assets.
  • It requires strong AML/KYC procedures and a local presence in Romania.
  • The license is valid indefinitely, subject to ongoing compliance and annual reporting.

Compared to Panama, which offers a flat-fee company setup without ongoing licensing, Romania provides a regulated environment that may increase trust among users and partners. However, Panama is faster and cheaper if you do not need EU passporting.

Who Needs This License?

Any business that operates an NFT marketplace where users can trade NFTs using cryptocurrencies needs the Romanian crypto license. This includes:

  • NFT marketplaces that accept Bitcoin, Ethereum, or other crypto as payment for NFTs.
  • Platforms that offer NFT minting and trading with crypto settlement.
  • Marketplaces that allow peer-to-peer NFT trades using crypto.
  • Businesses that provide custodial wallet services for NFTs and crypto.
  • Platforms that facilitate crypto-to-crypto exchanges as part of the NFT trading process.

If your platform only deals with fiat currency and does not involve crypto transactions, you may not need this license. However, if you plan to integrate crypto payments or allow crypto withdrawals, the license is mandatory. Consulting24 can help you determine if your business model falls under the VASP definition. For example, a marketplace that only lists NFTs for fiat payment and does not handle crypto may be exempt, but most NFT platforms today incorporate crypto payments.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator for crypto NFT marketplace licenses in Romania is the Financial Supervisory Authority (ASF). The license type is a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) registration. Under MiCA, which is fully in force in 2026, VASPs are subject to harmonized rules across the EU, including capital requirements and conduct of business standards.

Romania has transposed MiCA into national law, and the ASF is the competent authority for granting licenses. The license is valid for an indefinite period, subject to ongoing compliance. The ASF has published guidelines on the application process, which includes submitting a detailed business plan, AML/KYC policies, and proof of capital.

For comparison, Estonia and Lithuania also offer VASP licenses, but Romania is often chosen for its lower operational costs and efficient processing. Estonia has a higher capital requirement (EUR 100,000) and Lithuania requires EUR 125,000 for most services, while Romania starts at EUR 50,000 for simple exchange services.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the capital requirement for VASPs in Romania depends on the services offered. For NFT marketplaces that only provide exchange between crypto assets (no fiat), the minimum capital is EUR 50,000. If the marketplace also offers fiat-to-crypto exchange or custodial wallet services, the requirement may increase to EUR 125,000 or EUR 150,000 respectively.

These figures are subject to confirmation by the ASF based on your specific business model. The capital must be maintained as share capital or a combination of share capital and reserves. It must be fully paid up before the license is granted. Consulting24 can advise on the exact capital needed for your NFT marketplace and help structure the share capital appropriately. For example, a marketplace that only allows crypto-to-crypto NFT trades would likely fall under the EUR 50,000 tier, while one that also offers fiat on-ramp would need EUR 125,000.

Tax Treatment

Romania offers a favorable tax regime for crypto businesses. The corporate income tax rate is 16% on profits. However, there are specific rules for crypto transactions:

  • Income from crypto trading is treated as taxable income and subject to corporate tax.
  • VAT is generally not applicable on crypto-to-crypto transactions, but may apply to services like platform fees.
  • NFT sales may be subject to VAT if considered digital services; the standard VAT rate in Romania is 19%.
  • Capital gains from crypto are taxed as corporate income.

It is essential to work with a local tax advisor to ensure compliance. Consulting24 can introduce you to trusted partners for tax planning. Compared to Panama, which has 0% tax on offshore income, Romania's 16% rate is higher but offers access to the EU market and double tax treaties.

Allowed Activities

With a Romanian crypto NFT marketplace license, you can:

  • Operate an NFT marketplace accepting cryptocurrencies as payment.
  • Exchange crypto assets for fiat currency (if authorized under your license tier).
  • Exchange one crypto asset for another (crypto-to-crypto).
  • Transfer crypto assets on behalf of clients (e.g., from marketplace wallets to user wallets).
  • Provide custodial wallet services for NFTs and crypto.
  • Offer NFT minting services where users pay in crypto.

Activities that are not allowed include operating as a bank, offering investment advice, or dealing in securities without separate licenses. The license strictly covers VASP activities as defined by MiCA. If you plan to offer staking or lending services related to NFTs, you may need additional authorization. Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a crypto NFT marketplace license Romania involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step.

  1. Company Formation: Register a Romanian limited liability company (SRL) with a minimum share capital of EUR 200 (or higher if required by capital requirements). This typically takes 1-2 weeks.
  2. Document Preparation: Prepare a detailed business plan, AML/KYC policies, risk assessment, organizational structure, and compliance manual. This stage takes 2-4 weeks.
  3. Submit Application: File the application with the ASF, including all required documents and payment of the government fee. The ASF will acknowledge receipt and begin review.
  4. ASF Review: The regulator reviews the application, which may take 2-4 months. They may request additional information or clarifications. Consulting24 handles all communication with the ASF.
  5. License Issuance: Once approved, you receive the VASP license and can start operations. You must maintain a local registered office and compliance officer.
  6. Ongoing Compliance: Submit regular reports (e.g., transaction reports, AML audits), conduct employee training, and maintain records. Consulting24 offers ongoing compliance support.

Compliance and Trust

Operating under a regulated license builds trust with users and partners. The Romanian ASF requires strict AML/KYC procedures, which include:

  • Customer due diligence (CDD) for all users, including identity verification and beneficial ownership checks.
  • Transaction monitoring and reporting of suspicious activities to the National Office for Prevention and Control of Money Laundering.
  • Appointment of a compliance officer who is a Romanian resident.
  • Regular audits and reporting to the ASF, including annual financial statements and AML reports.
  • Maintenance of records for at least 5 years.

This is general guidance, not legal advice. Consulting24 can help you implement a compliant framework tailored to your NFT marketplace, including drafting policies and training staff. Non-compliance can result in fines or license revocation, so it is crucial to maintain high standards.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Many applicants face delays or rejections due to common errors. Avoid these pitfalls:

  • Incomplete or inaccurate documentation: Ensure all forms are filled correctly and supporting documents are translated into Romanian if required.
  • Insufficient AML/KYC policies: The ASF expects detailed procedures that go beyond generic templates.
  • Underestimating capital requirements: Make sure you have the required capital fully paid up before application.
  • Choosing a business model that falls outside VASP definition: If your activities are not clearly covered, the ASF may reject the application.
  • Not engaging a local advisor familiar with ASF expectations: The regulator prefers dealing with local representatives.
  • Ignoring ongoing compliance: Even after license issuance, you must submit regular reports and maintain records.
