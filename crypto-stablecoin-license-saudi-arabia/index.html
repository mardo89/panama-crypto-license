What Is the Saudi Arabia Crypto Stablecoin License?

The Saudi Arabia crypto stablecoin license is a regulatory authorization from the Saudi Central Bank (SAMA) that permits the issuance and management of stablecoins-digital assets pegged to a stable reserve, typically the Saudi Riyal or a fiat currency. This license is part of SAMA's Sandbox and Regulatory Framework for Digital Payment Tokens, introduced to foster innovation while ensuring financial stability and consumer protection.

Key features include:

  • Regulated by SAMA under the Payment Services Provider (PSP) regime, with specific provisions for stablecoins.
  • Requires a minimum capital of SAR 500,000 (approx. USD 133,000) for sandbox entry, with higher thresholds for full licensing.
  • Zero corporate tax on stablecoin operations, but subject to Zakat (2.5% for Saudi-owned entities).
  • Timeline: 6-12 months for sandbox approval, then 12-18 months for full license.

This license is distinct from a general crypto license in Panama or other jurisdictions, as it is tailored to stablecoin activities and aligns with Saudi Vision 2030. The framework is designed to position Saudi Arabia as a regional fintech hub, attracting both local and international players. Who Needs the Saudi Arabia Crypto Stablecoin License?

This license is designed for businesses that intend to issue, redeem, or distribute stablecoins within Saudi Arabia or to Saudi residents. Typical applicants include:

  • Fintech startups launching a Saudi Riyal-pegged stablecoin for remittances or payments.
  • Established payment companies expanding into digital assets to offer faster settlement.
  • Banks and financial institutions exploring blockchain-based settlement tokens for interbank transfers.
  • International stablecoin issuers seeking regulatory compliance in the Gulf region to serve Saudi customers.

If your business model involves any of the following, you likely need this license: issuing stablecoins, operating a stablecoin wallet, facilitating stablecoin transfers, or providing custody for stablecoin reserves. License Type & Regulator

The Saudi Arabia crypto stablecoin license falls under the Payment Services Provider (PSP) license category, administered by the Saudi Central Bank (SAMA). SAMA is the sole regulator for payment and digital token services in the Kingdom, operating under the Payment Systems Law (Royal Decree M/69).

There are two stages:

  1. Sandbox License: A temporary permit to test stablecoin operations with limited customers. Capital requirement: SAR 500,000. This stage typically lasts 6-12 months and allows up to 5,000 users.
  2. Full License: After successful sandbox completion, firms can apply for a full PSP license with stablecoin authorization. Capital requirement: SAR 5 million (approx. USD 1.33 million). The full license is valid for 5 years and renewable.

For comparison, the Estonia crypto license has no minimum capital for virtual currency service providers, while Panama offers a flat fee of EUR 6,000 for a crypto license with no capital requirement. Saudi Arabia's approach is more stringent, reflecting its focus on financial stability and consumer protection. Capital Requirement

The minimum capital for the Saudi Arabia crypto stablecoin license is:

  • Sandbox: SAR 500,000 (approx. USD 133,000) - must be held in a Saudi bank account and be fully paid up.
  • Full license: SAR 5 million (approx. USD 1.33 million) - to be maintained throughout the license period, with quarterly attestation.

Capital must be fully paid up and can be in cash or liquid assets. This requirement ensures that stablecoin issuers have sufficient reserves to back their tokens and cover operational risks. In contrast, jurisdictions like Lithuania require EUR 125,000 for a CASP license under MiCA, while Panama has no capital requirement at all. The Saudi capital requirement is among the highest in the region, but it also signals regulatory seriousness. Consulting24 can help you structure your capital to meet SAMA's expectations.

Tax Treatment

Saudi Arabia offers a favorable tax environment for stablecoin operations:

  • Corporate Income Tax: 0% for stablecoin-related activities, as they are considered payment services under SAMA regulation.
  • Zakat: 2.5% of net assets for Saudi-owned entities (or Saudi share of ownership). Foreign investors may be exempt if they have no Saudi partners.
  • VAT: 15% on service fees, but stablecoin issuance may be exempt if classified as a financial service. This requires a ruling from the Zakat, Tax and Customs Authority (ZATCA).

This tax regime makes Saudi Arabia competitive compared to the EU, where MiCA imposes capital requirements and standard corporate tax rates. However, the Zakat obligation can be a consideration for foreign investors. Consulting24 advises on structuring your entity to optimize tax outcomes, for example by using a holding company in a zero-tax jurisdiction like Panama.

Allowed Activities

Under the Saudi Arabia crypto stablecoin license, the following activities are permitted:

  • Issuance of stablecoins pegged to fiat currency (e.g., SAR, USD).
  • Redemption of stablecoins for fiat at par value.
  • Transfer of stablecoins between users, including cross-border.
  • Custody of stablecoin reserves (must be held in segregated accounts with a licensed Saudi bank).
  • Wallet services for stablecoins, including multi-signature and smart contract wallets.

Activities not allowed include: issuing unbacked cryptocurrencies, operating an exchange for volatile crypto assets, and providing lending/borrowing services without additional licensing. If your business involves multiple crypto activities, you may need a separate crypto exchange license from the CMA. The stablecoin license is narrowly focused on payment tokens, so ensure your business plan aligns with these permitted activities.

Compliance & Trust Requirements

License holders must comply with SAMA's AML/CFT regulations, including customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting. Stablecoin reserves must be audited quarterly by an approved auditor. Additionally, issuers must maintain a 1:1 reserve ratio at all times, with monthly attestation.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Underestimating capital: Many applicants fail to maintain the SAR 5 million capital for the full license, leading to revocation.
  • Incomplete documentation: SAMA requires detailed technical and financial documents; missing information delays approval by months.
  • Ignoring Zakat: Non-Saudi owners may not be subject, but Saudi partners are, which affects profit-sharing and entity structure.
  • Choosing the wrong jurisdiction: Some businesses apply for a Saudi license when a simpler license like Panama would suffice, wasting time and money.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for the Saudi Arabia crypto stablecoin license?

The sandbox requires SAR 500,000 (approx. USD 133,000), and the full license requires SAR 5 million (approx. USD 1.33 million). Capital must be fully paid up and held in a Saudi bank account.

How long does it take to get the Saudi Arabia crypto stablecoin license?

The total timeline is 12-24 months: 3-6 months for sandbox approval, 6-12 months of sandbox operation, and 6-12 months for full license approval after sandbox completion.

What taxes apply to stablecoin operations in Saudi Arabia?

Corporate income tax is 0% for stablecoin activities. Zakat of 2.5% applies to Saudi-owned entities. VAT of 15% may apply to service fees, but stablecoin issuance may be exempt if classified as a financial service.

Can foreign companies apply for the Saudi Arabia crypto stablecoin license?

Yes, foreign companies can apply, but they must establish a legal presence in Saudi Arabia, typically a branch or subsidiary. Consulting24 can advise on the best structure.

What activities are allowed under the Saudi Arabia crypto stablecoin license?

Issuance, redemption, transfer, and custody of fiat-pegged stablecoins, as well as wallet services. Activities like exchange of volatile cryptocurrencies or lending are not permitted under this license.

Is the Saudi Arabia crypto stablecoin license recognized internationally?

The license is specific to Saudi Arabia and does not provide passporting rights. However, it demonstrates regulatory compliance that may facilitate partnerships with international banks and exchanges.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements for the Saudi Arabia crypto stablecoin license?

License holders must conduct AML/CFT checks, quarterly audits of reserves, monthly reporting to SAMA, and maintain a 1:1 reserve ratio. Non-compliance can result in fines or license revocation.

How does the Saudi Arabia crypto stablecoin license compare to Panama?

Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 license with no capital requirement and a 2-4 week timeline, but no specific stablecoin framework. Saudi Arabia provides regulatory clarity for stablecoins but at higher cost and time.

Can I use the Saudi Arabia crypto stablecoin license to issue a USD-pegged stablecoin?

Yes, the license allows issuance of stablecoins pegged to any fiat currency, including USD. However, the reserve must be held in a Saudi bank account, which may require conversion to SAR.

What happens if I fail the sandbox phase?

If SAMA determines that the sandbox test is unsuccessful, the license may be revoked or the firm may be required to modify its operations. It is important to have a strong compliance and risk management framework.
