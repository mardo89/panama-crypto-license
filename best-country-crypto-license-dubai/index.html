What Is a Dubai Crypto License?

A Dubai crypto license, issued by the Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA), authorizes companies to provide virtual asset services within the Dubai Multi Commodities Centre (DMCC) or other designated free zones. VARA was established in 2022 and has been fully operational since 2024, with a comprehensive rulebook covering exchange, custody, and advisory services. The license is mandatory for any entity offering crypto services in Dubai, and it aligns with international AML/CFT standards.

Dubai's approach is proactive: VARA licenses are tiered by service type and capital, with minimum capital requirements ranging from AED 100,000 to AED 1,000,000 depending on the activity. The regulator conducts thorough background checks and requires a physical presence in Dubai, including office space and a local director. This makes it a credible option for founders seeking regulatory clarity and access to the UAE's growing crypto ecosystem.

Who Needs a Dubai Crypto License?

Any business offering virtual asset services in or from Dubai must hold a VARA license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (spot, derivatives, or OTC)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Token issuers (including STOs and utility tokens)
  • DeFi platforms that operate as a business
  • Crypto payment processors
  • Advisory and brokerage firms

If your target market is the UAE or the broader Middle East, a Dubai license is essential for regulatory compliance. For projects that want to avoid a physical presence or high setup costs, Panama offers a simpler alternative with no specific crypto license requirements. License Type and Regulator

The regulator for Dubai crypto licenses is the Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA). VARA operates under Dubai Law No. 4 of 2022 and is the sole authority for virtual asset regulation in Dubai (excluding the Dubai International Financial Centre, which has its own framework).

License types under VARA include:

  • Category 1: Virtual Asset Exchange (matching orders, order book trading)
  • Category 2: Virtual Asset Broker-Dealer (agency or principal trading)
  • Category 3: Virtual Asset Custody and Transfer
  • Category 4: Virtual Asset Advisory and Management

Each category has specific capital and operational requirements. For Panama, there is no dedicated crypto regulator; businesses simply register a standard corporation with the Public Registry and comply with general AML laws. Panama's lack of a crypto-specific license is both a pro (low cost) and a con (less regulatory certainty).

Capital Requirement

Dubai's VARA mandates minimum paid-up capital based on the license category:

  • Category 1 (Exchange): AED 1,000,000 (EUR 250,000)
  • Category 2 (Broker-Dealer): AED 500,000 (EUR 125,000)
  • Category 3 (Custody/Transfer): AED 200,000 (EUR 50,000)
  • Category 4 (Advisory): AED 100,000 (EUR 25,000)

These amounts must be deposited in a local bank account and cannot be withdrawn without VARA approval. Panama has no statutory minimum capital for a standard corporation; founders can start with as little as USD 10,000 in authorized capital (no paid-up requirement). This makes Panama much more accessible for bootstrapped startups.

Tax Treatment

Dubai offers a 0% corporate tax for most free zone companies, including those licensed by VARA, provided they do not trade with the mainland. However, as of 2026, the UAE has introduced a 9% corporate tax on profits exceeding AED 375,000, applicable to all businesses (including free zone entities that conduct business with mainland). Crypto companies should confirm their tax status with a local advisor.

Panama has a territorial tax system: income sourced outside Panama is tax-exempt. For crypto businesses that serve international clients (non-Panama), this means zero corporate tax. Panama also has no VAT, no capital gains tax, and no withholding tax on dividends. This makes Panama a highly tax-efficient jurisdiction for global crypto operations.

Allowed Activities

Under a VARA license, allowed activities include:

  • Operating a crypto exchange (order book, OTC, P2P)
  • Custody of virtual assets
  • Transfer services (sending/receiving crypto)
  • Token issuance (subject to additional approval)
  • Advisory and portfolio management

Panama does not explicitly regulate crypto, so any crypto activity not prohibited by law is allowed: exchange, custody, mining, DeFi, token issuance, and payment processing. However, Panama's AML law requires registration with the Financial Analysis Unit if the business involves money transmission or exchange services. Step-by-Step Application Process

Dubai (VARA):

  1. Incorporate a company in a free zone (e.g., DMCC, ADGM) with a physical office lease.
  2. Submit a pre-application to VARA with business plan, AML policy, and financial projections.
  3. Pay initial fees and undergo a fit-and-proper test for directors and shareholders.
  4. Receive in-principle approval (IPA) within 60 days.
  5. Complete technical setup (IT systems, compliance software) and submit final documentation.
  6. VARA conducts a site visit and grants the license. Total timeline: 4-8 months.

Panama:

  1. Engage a licensed agent (e.g., Consulting24) to incorporate a Panama corporation.
  2. Provide passport copies and proof of address for directors and shareholders.
  3. Pay the flat fee of EUR 6,000.
  4. Receive incorporation documents within 2-4 weeks.
  5. Optionally register with the Financial Analysis Unit for AML compliance (if offering exchange services).

Banking and Payment Services

Dubai offers relatively accessible banking for licensed crypto companies. VARA-licensed entities can open accounts with UAE-based banks (e.g., Mashreq, RAKBank) and international banks with a Dubai presence. However, banks still conduct enhanced due diligence, and some may reject crypto-related businesses. Consulting24 can introduce you to crypto-friendly banks in Dubai.

Panama has a well-established banking sector with many private banks. However, most Panamanian banks are cautious about crypto and may require a physical presence or high minimum deposits. As an alternative, Panama companies can use EMI accounts (e.g., in Lithuania or the UK) for fiat on/off ramps. Consulting24 assists with banking introductions in both jurisdictions.

Compliance and Trust Considerations

Dubai's VARA license signals to partners and customers that your business meets international AML/CFT standards. This can be a competitive advantage when onboarding institutional clients or listing on exchanges. However, compliance costs are high: you need a local AML officer, regular audits, and transaction monitoring systems.

Panama, while not a regulated crypto hub, still requires AML compliance if you handle fiat or crypto transfers. The Financial Analysis Unit (UAF) mandates registration and reporting for money service businesses. Many founders choose Panama for its simplicity, but you must implement your own compliance program. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Choosing jurisdiction based solely on cost: A cheap license with no banking access can stall your business. Consider the full ecosystem.
  • Ignoring tax residency: Even if Panama has territorial tax, your personal tax residency may expose you to taxes elsewhere. Plan accordingly.
  • Underestimating Dubai's compliance burden: VARA requires ongoing reporting and audits. Budget for a compliance officer and legal fees.
  • Not checking banking compatibility: Some banks refuse crypto. Verify banking options before committing to a license.
  • Overlooking substance requirements: Dubai mandates physical office and local staff. Panama does not, but substance may be needed for tax benefits.

Alternatives and Comparisons

  • Switzerland: FINMA-regulated, high credibility but high cost (CHF 100,000+). Timeline: 4-8 months.

Is a Dubai crypto license recognized internationally?

Yes, VARA is a reputable regulator and its license is recognized by many international banks and partners. However, it does not automatically grant passporting rights outside the UAE. For EU access, consider a MiCA license in Lithuania or Estonia.

Panama does not have a specific crypto license, but you must comply with AML laws if you offer exchange services. This includes registering with the Financial Analysis Unit (UAF) and implementing KYC/AML procedures. Consulting24 can assist with compliance setup.

Minimum capital ranges from AED 100,000 (EUR 25,000) for advisory services to AED 1,000,000 (EUR 250,000) for exchange services. The capital must be deposited in a local bank account and cannot be withdrawn without VARA approval.
