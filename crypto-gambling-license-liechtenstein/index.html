What Is a Crypto Gambling License in Liechtenstein?

A crypto gambling license in Liechtenstein is not a single licence but a combination of two regulatory approvals: a licence as a trusted technology service provider (TTSP) under the TVTG and a gambling licence under the Gambling Act. The TVTG covers crypto custody, exchange, and transfer services, while the Gambling Act governs online casinos, sports betting, and other gambling activities that accept crypto. The Financial Market Authority (FMA) Liechtenstein oversees the TTSP licence, and the Gambling Authority (Amt für Volkswirtschaft) handles gambling permits. This dual regime means any crypto gambling operator must comply with both sets of rules, including anti-money laundering (AML) and responsible gambling obligations. The TVTG was one of the first comprehensive blockchain laws globally, giving Liechtenstein a first-mover advantage in legal clarity.

Who Needs a Crypto Gambling License in Liechtenstein?

Any company that offers gambling services to Liechtenstein residents or operates from Liechtenstein while accepting cryptocurrencies needs this licence. This includes online casinos, sports betting platforms, poker rooms, and other games of chance where bets are placed using crypto. Even if your target market is international, if your business is registered in Liechtenstein, you must hold both the TTSP and gambling licences. Operators based elsewhere but serving Liechtenstein residents also need a local licence. License Type & Regulator

The crypto gambling license in Liechtenstein involves two distinct authorisations:

  • Trusted Technology Service Provider (TTSP) Licence: Issued by the Financial Market Authority (FMA) Liechtenstein under the TVTG. This covers crypto custody, exchange, and transfer services. Minimum capital requirement is CHF 50,000 (approximately EUR 50,000) for basic services, but higher tiers apply for more complex activities.
  • Gambling Licence: Issued by the Gambling Authority (Amt für Volkswirtschaft) under the Gambling Act. This permits online casino, sports betting, and other gambling activities. Capital requirements and fees are set by the authority and typically range from EUR 100,000 to EUR 500,000 depending on the type and scale of gambling.

Both licences require a physical presence in Liechtenstein, including a registered office and local management. The FMA is the primary regulator for crypto activities, while the Gambling Authority ensures compliance with gambling laws. Capital Requirement

Liechtenstein imposes minimum capital requirements for both licences. For the TTSP licence, the minimum capital is CHF 50,000 (approx. EUR 50,000) for basic services like crypto custody. If you also offer exchange or transfer services, the requirement may increase to CHF 100,000 (approx. EUR 100,000). For the gambling licence, the minimum capital is set by the Gambling Authority and typically starts at EUR 100,000 for online casinos and EUR 200,000 for sports betting platforms. Higher amounts may be required based on the volume of bets and risk profile. This capital must be fully paid up and maintained throughout the licence period. Tax Treatment

Liechtenstein offers a highly attractive tax regime for crypto gambling operators. The corporate income tax rate is a flat 12.5% on profits, one of the lowest in Europe. There is no withholding tax on dividends paid to non-resident shareholders. Gambling winnings are tax-free for players, which is a significant marketing advantage. VAT is not applicable on gambling services, as they are exempt. However, the TTSP services (e.g., custody) may be subject to VAT at 7.7% if provided separately. Overall, the tax burden is low compared to other European jurisdictions. Consulting24 advises and coordinates tax planning as part of the licensing process. For example, a company earning EUR 1 million in profit would pay only EUR 125,000 in corporate tax, leaving more capital for growth.

Allowed Activities

With a crypto gambling license in Liechtenstein, you can operate the following activities:

  • Sports betting and e-sports betting with crypto deposits
  • Poker rooms and other card games

  • Crypto custody and wallet services for gambling funds
  • Exchange of crypto to fiat for withdrawal purposes

All activities must comply with AML and responsible gambling regulations. The licence does not cover traditional fiat gambling; if you also accept fiat, you need a separate fiat gambling licence. Step-by-Step Process

Obtaining a crypto gambling license in Liechtenstein involves the following steps:

  2. Company incorporation: Register a Liechtenstein entity (Aktiengesellschaft or Gesellschaft mit beschränkter Haftung) with a local registered office and management.
  3. TTSP licence application: Prepare and submit the application to the FMA, including AML policies, business plan, and capital proof.
  4. Gambling licence application: Submit to the Gambling Authority with detailed game rules, responsible gambling measures, and technical system descriptions.

  6. Approval and licence issuance: Once approved, you receive both licences and can commence operations.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a TTSP licence and a gambling licence in Liechtenstein?

The TTSP licence, issued by the FMA under the TVTG, covers crypto services like custody, exchange, and transfer. The gambling licence, issued by the Gambling Authority, permits casino games, sports betting, and other gambling activities. A crypto gambling operator needs both licences to legally offer gambling services using cryptocurrencies.

How long does it take to get a crypto gambling license in Liechtenstein?

The combined process typically takes 6 to 12 months. The TTSP licence takes 3-6 months, and the gambling licence an additional 3-6 months. Delays can occur if applications are incomplete or if regulatory reviews require additional information.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a crypto gambling license in Liechtenstein?

For the TTSP licence, minimum capital is CHF 50,000 (approx. EUR 50,000) for basic services, increasing to CHF 100,000 for more complex services. For the gambling licence, capital starts at EUR 100,000 for online casinos and can go up to EUR 500,000 depending on the scale.

Can I use a Liechtenstein licence to operate in other EU countries?

Liechtenstein is part of the EEA, which allows passporting of certain financial services. However, gambling services are not fully harmonised, so you may need additional licences in each target country. The TTSP licence can be passported for crypto services under the TVTG, but gambling remains a national competence.

Is a physical office required in Liechtenstein?

Yes, both licences require a physical presence, including a registered office and local management. You must have a director or manager based in Liechtenstein or the EEA. Consulting24 can help arrange a registered office and local representation.

What are the tax benefits of a Liechtenstein crypto gambling license?

Corporate income tax is a flat 12.5%, one of the lowest in Europe. There is no withholding tax on dividends to non-residents, and player winnings are tax-free. VAT is exempt on gambling services. This makes Liechtenstein very attractive for profitable operators.

Can I accept both crypto and fiat with a Liechtenstein licence?

The crypto gambling licence covers crypto-only activities. If you also want to accept fiat currency, you need a separate fiat gambling licence. Consulting24 can advise on how to structure your business to handle both.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations after obtaining the licence?

You must conduct AML checks, monitor transactions, file suspicious activity reports, and undergo regular audits. You also need to pay annual supervision fees and maintain capital requirements. Consulting24 can help set up your compliance framework.

How does Liechtenstein compare to Panama for crypto gambling?

Panama offers a much lower cost (EUR 6,000 flat company setup) and faster setup (2-4 weeks), but no specific crypto licence exists, so regulatory credibility is lower. Liechtenstein provides a clear legal framework and EEA access but at a higher cost and longer timeline.

Does Liechtenstein require a gambling licence for crypto betting?

Yes, any form of gambling, including crypto betting, requires a gambling licence from the Gambling Authority. The TTSP licence alone does not permit gambling activities. Both licences are mandatory.
