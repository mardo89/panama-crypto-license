Overview: Lithuania vs Cayman Islands Crypto License

Lithuania's crypto license, officially a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) authorization under the Law on Prevention of Money Laundering and Terrorist Financing, is now fully aligned with MiCA. As of 2026, the Bank of Lithuania (Lietuvos Bankas) is the competent authority. The Cayman Islands, on the other hand, regulates virtual asset service providers under the Virtual Asset (Service Providers) Act, 2020, administered by the Cayman Islands Monetary Authority (CIMA). Both jurisdictions require licensing for exchange, custody, and transfer services, but the scope and requirements differ significantly.

Lithuania offers a straightforward path to operate across the European Economic Area (EEA) via passporting, while the Cayman Islands provides a neutral, tax-neutral base with strong privacy protections but limited automatic market access to the EU. Your choice depends on whether you prioritize EU market access or a lighter regulatory touch.

Who Needs a Crypto License in Lithuania or the Cayman Islands?

Any business that provides virtual asset services - such as exchanging fiat for crypto, crypto-to-crypto trading, custody, transfer, or wallet services - must obtain a license in both jurisdictions. Specifically:

  • Lithuania: Suitable for startups and established firms targeting the EU market. The license is mandatory for any entity offering services to Lithuanian residents or passporting to other EU states. Under MiCA, firms can passport across all 27 EU member states.
  • Cayman Islands: Ideal for funds, trading platforms, and custodians that want a reputable offshore jurisdiction with no direct taxes. Often used by institutional players and high-net-worth clients. The Cayman Islands is also a hub for crypto hedge funds and venture capital.

If your primary market is the EU, Lithuania is the natural choice. If you need a tax-efficient, stable regulatory environment with a global client base, the Cayman Islands may be better. Consulting24 can help you assess your specific needs through a personalized consultation.

License Type & Regulator

Lithuania: The license is a VASP authorization under the Bank of Lithuania. Under MiCA, it is a CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) license. The regulator is the Bank of Lithuania (Lietuvos Bankas). The license covers all major services: exchange, custody, transfer, and wallet provision. The regulatory framework is detailed and prescriptive, with clear guidelines on governance, risk management, and reporting.

Cayman Islands: The license is a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) license under CIMA. There are three categories: A (full license), B (restricted), and C (sandbox). Most commercial operators apply for a Category A license. CIMA is the regulator. The Cayman regime is principle-based, offering flexibility but requiring strong internal controls.

Both regulators enforce AML/CFT compliance, but Lithuania's regime is more prescriptive due to EU directives and MiCA. CIMA follows FATF recommendations with some flexibility. For more on regulatory specifics, see our requirements page.

Capital Requirement

Lithuania: Under MiCA, capital requirements are tiered by service class. For 2026: EUR 50,000 for basic services (e.g., exchange-only), EUR 125,000 for custody, and EUR 150,000 for combined services. These are minimum own funds that must be maintained at all times. The capital can be in the form of cash or certain liquid assets.

Cayman Islands: There is no statutory minimum capital requirement for VASP licenses. However, CIMA expects applicants to demonstrate adequate financial resources relative to the nature and scale of operations. In practice, a minimum of USD 100,000 - 250,000 is often expected for Category A licenses, but this can vary. The flexibility allows startups to start with lower capital, but they must show a solid business plan.

Lithuania has a clear, lower threshold, while the Cayman Islands offers flexibility but may require higher actual capital depending on business plan. For more details, visit our requirements page.

Tax Treatment

Lithuania: Corporate income tax is 15% (5% for small companies with revenues under EUR 300,000 and fewer than 10 employees). VAT is 21% on crypto services if deemed taxable (subject to interpretation). No specific crypto tax regime; profits from crypto trading are taxed as ordinary income. Lithuania has tax treaties with many countries to avoid double taxation.

Cayman Islands: No corporate income tax, no VAT, no capital gains tax, no withholding tax. The jurisdiction is tax-neutral. Annual fees include a flat registration fee (approx. USD 1,000 - 2,000) and licensing fees. This makes the Cayman Islands highly attractive for tax optimization. However, be aware of economic substance requirements: companies must demonstrate adequate physical presence and activity in the Cayman Islands.

For tax efficiency, the Cayman Islands is superior. However, Lithuania offers access to the EU market, which may offset the tax cost. Consulting24 can help you model the tax implications for your specific business.

Allowed Activities

Lithuania: The VASP/CASP license allows: exchange between fiat and crypto, exchange between crypto assets, custody and administration of crypto assets, transfer of crypto assets, and wallet services. Also allowed: crypto-to-crypto trading, fiat on/off ramps, and staking as a service (subject to MiCA). The license does not cover token issuance or ICOs separately; those may require additional regulatory approvals.

Cayman Islands: The VASP license permits: exchange, custody, transfer, and wallet services. Additionally, the Cayman Islands is a hub for crypto funds and structured products. Token issuance and DeFi platforms may also be licensed under certain conditions. The Cayman Islands is particularly friendly to fund structures, allowing for the establishment of mutual funds and hedge funds that invest in crypto assets.

Both jurisdictions cover core activities, but the Cayman Islands offers more flexibility for fund structures and investment vehicles. For a detailed list, see our exchange license page.

Step-by-Step Process

Lithuania:

  1. Company incorporation in Lithuania (or registration of a branch).
  2. Appoint a local AML officer and board members (if required).
  3. Prepare AML/KYC policies, business plan, and risk assessment.
  4. Submit application to the Bank of Lithuania with required documents.
  5. Pay application fee and undergo regulatory review.
  6. Receive license (or refusal) within 3-6 months.

Cayman Islands:

  1. Incorporate an exempted company or limited liability company in Cayman.
  2. Engage a local legal counsel and compliance advisor.
  3. Prepare a detailed business plan, AML policies, and governance framework.
  4. Submit VASP application to CIMA with supporting documents.
  5. Undergo due diligence and possible interview.
  6. Receive license (typically 4-8 months).

Banking & Payments

Lithuania: Banking relationships are available but have tightened due to AML concerns. Major banks like Swedbank and SEB may open accounts for licensed VASPs, but due diligence is rigorous. Payment institutions and EMI licenses are alternatives. Lithuania is part of SEPA, enabling easy euro transfers. Some crypto-friendly banks in the Baltics, such as Revolut and Paysera, offer services to licensed firms.

Cayman Islands: Banking is more challenging for crypto firms due to perceived risk. Many use correspondent banking or specialized crypto-friendly banks like Bank of America or local banks that accept crypto clients. The Cayman Islands dollar is pegged to USD. Offshore banking is common, but expect higher fees and longer onboarding. Some firms use payment processors or stablecoin solutions to bypass traditional banking.

Lithuania offers better banking access for EU-focused businesses. The Cayman Islands may require more effort to secure banking partners. Consulting24 can provide introductions to banking partners.

Cayman Islands:

  • Flexible regulatory approach with no fixed capital minimum.
  • Strong privacy and confidentiality laws.
  • Ideal for institutional investors and funds.
  • Stable, common-law legal system with a well-established financial services industry.

Compliance & Trust

Both jurisdictions require strong AML/CFT compliance. Lithuania follows the EU's 5th AML Directive and MiCA, with strict reporting obligations to the Bank of Lithuania and the Financial Crime Investigation Service. The Cayman Islands adheres to FATF recommendations and has a strong reputation for regulatory compliance, but enforcement is less prescriptive. In Lithuania, you must appoint a local AML officer and maintain a register of beneficial owners. In the Cayman Islands, you must file annual returns and maintain a physical presence (economic substance).

Lithuania's regime is more transparent and predictable, which can build trust with EU partners and banks. The Cayman Islands offers a more discreet environment but may face scrutiny from EU regulators. Regardless, a licensed entity in either jurisdiction signals credibility to clients and counterparties. Common Mistakes When Choosing a Jurisdiction

Entrepreneurs often make these errors:

  • Ignoring market access: Choosing a tax haven without considering where your customers are based can limit growth.
  • Underestimating compliance costs: Ongoing AML, reporting, and audit costs can be significant in both jurisdictions.
  • Overlooking banking: A license is useless without a bank account. Ensure you have a banking plan.
  • Assuming no capital requirement: Even in Cayman, CIMA expects adequate capital. Prepare to show financial strength.
  • Not consulting experts: Each jurisdiction has nuances. Professional advice is essential.

Frequently asked questions

What is the main difference between Lithuania and Cayman Islands crypto licenses?

The main difference is market access and tax. Lithuania offers EU passporting under MiCA, allowing you to serve clients across the EEA, but has a 15% corporate tax. The Cayman Islands is tax-neutral but does not provide automatic EU market access. Your choice depends on whether you prioritize EU customers or tax efficiency.

How much does a crypto license cost in Lithuania vs Cayman Islands?

In Lithuania, total costs range from EUR 20,000 to 30,000 including professional fees. In the Cayman Islands, costs are higher, typically USD 30,000 to 60,000. Exact figures depend on your business complexity. Consulting24 provides a detailed cost breakdown during consultation.

What are the capital requirements for a crypto license in Lithuania?

Under MiCA, capital requirements are tiered: EUR 50,000 for basic exchange services, EUR 125,000 for custody, and EUR 150,000 for combined services. These are minimum own funds that must be maintained. The Cayman Islands has no statutory minimum but expects adequate capital based on business scale.

Can I passport my Lithuania crypto license to other EU countries?

Yes, under MiCA, a Lithuania CASP license allows passporting to all 27 EU member states. You can provide services across the EEA without needing separate licenses in each country, saving time and cost.

Is the Cayman Islands tax-free for crypto businesses?

Yes, the Cayman Islands imposes no corporate income tax, capital gains tax, VAT, or withholding tax. However, you must comply with economic substance requirements, which may involve having a physical office and local staff. Annual fees are minimal.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Lithuania vs Cayman Islands?

Lithuania typically takes 3-6 months from application to approval. The Cayman Islands takes 4-8 months, depending on the complexity of your application and CIMA's workload. Both timelines can be expedited with proper preparation.

Do I need a local office or staff for a crypto license?

In Lithuania, you need a registered office and a local AML officer. In the Cayman Islands, economic substance rules require a physical office and local staff for most license categories. Consulting24 can help you set up the required presence.

Can I use a Lithuania license to serve clients outside the EU?

Yes, a Lithuania license does not restrict you to EU clients. You can serve global clients, but you must comply with local laws in the client's jurisdiction. Passporting rights only apply within the EEA.

What activities are allowed under a Cayman Islands VASP license?

The license allows exchange, custody, transfer, and wallet services. It is also suitable for crypto funds, token issuance, and DeFi platforms. The Cayman Islands is particularly known for its flexible fund structures.

Which jurisdiction is better for a startup with limited budget?

Lithuania is generally more cost-effective for startups, with lower total costs and faster timelines. The Cayman Islands may be better if you prioritize tax neutrality and have a higher budget. Consulting24 can advise based on your specific situation.
