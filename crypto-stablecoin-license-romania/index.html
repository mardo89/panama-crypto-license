What Is the Crypto Stablecoin License Romania?

The crypto stablecoin license Romania is a regulatory authorization issued by the Romanian Financial Supervisory Authority (ASF) for companies that issue stablecoins (asset-referenced tokens or electronic money tokens) under the EU's Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA), which is fully in force in 2026. This license is mandatory for any entity that wants to offer stablecoins to the public in Romania or the broader EU market.

Key features:

  • Regulated under MiCA as a CASP (Crypto-Asset Service Provider) for issuance of ARTs and EMTs.
  • Requires a legal entity in Romania with a registered office and local management.
  • Subject to capital requirements, governance standards, and ongoing compliance.
  • Passportable across all EU member states, giving access to 450 million consumers.

The license provides legal certainty and access to the EU single market, making it a strategic choice for stablecoin projects. Unlike pre-MiCA regimes, Romania now enforces a harmonized framework with strict reserve management rules. For example, EMT issuers must hold reserves in a separate account with a credit institution, and ARTs require a detailed white paper approved by the ASF.

Who Needs a Crypto Stablecoin License in Romania?

Any company that plans to issue stablecoins (tokens pegged to a fiat currency, commodity, or other assets) to the public in Romania or the EU must obtain this license. This includes:

  • Startups launching a new stablecoin, such as a EUR-backed payment token.
  • Existing crypto exchanges that want to issue their own stablecoin for trading pairs.
  • Fintech firms offering payment services backed by stablecoins, like remittance platforms.
  • Traditional financial institutions expanding into digital assets, including banks issuing a digital euro equivalent.

License Type and Regulator

The crypto stablecoin license in Romania is issued by the Financial Supervisory Authority (ASF) under the MiCA framework. The license covers two main categories:

  • Asset-Referenced Tokens (ARTs): Stablecoins backed by a basket of assets (e.g., multiple currencies or commodities). These require a white paper with detailed reserve composition and redemption rights.
  • Electronic Money Tokens (EMTs): Stablecoins pegged to a single fiat currency (e.g., EUR, USD). These are treated similarly to e-money and must be redeemable at par at any time.

The ASF is the competent authority for authorizing and supervising stablecoin issuers. For cross-border operations, the license allows passporting across all EU member states. The application process is rigorous, requiring detailed documentation on the issuer's governance, reserve management, and compliance systems. The ASF also coordinates with the European Securities and Markets Authority (ESMA) for significant tokens classified under higher thresholds.

Capital Requirements

Under MiCA, capital requirements for stablecoin issuers are tiered based on the type of token and the volume of issuance:

  • For EMTs (electronic money tokens): Minimum capital of EUR 50,000, plus ongoing capital equal to 2% of the average amount of outstanding tokens.
  • For ARTs (asset-referenced tokens): Minimum capital of EUR 150,000, plus ongoing capital equal to 3% of the average amount of outstanding tokens.
  • For significant tokens (classified by ESMA): Higher capital requirements up to EUR 2 million, with additional oversight.

Capital must be held in liquid assets such as cash or government bonds and cannot be used for other purposes. The ASF may require additional capital based on risk assessment. These figures are as of 2026 and are subject to change; always verify with the regulator. For example, a startup issuing EUR 10 million in EMTs would need EUR 50,000 initial capital plus EUR 200,000 ongoing (2% of 10 million).

Tax Treatment

Romania offers a favorable tax regime for crypto businesses. As of 2026:

  • Corporate income tax: 16% on profits, standard rate.
  • VAT: Exempt for crypto transactions (including stablecoin issuance) under EU VAT rules.
  • Withholding tax: 5% on dividends paid to EU residents, 10% to non-EU (subject to tax treaties).
  • Stablecoin-specific: Gains from issuance and redemption are treated as ordinary income. No special tax on token appreciation.
  • Reserve income: Interest earned on reserve assets is taxable as ordinary income.

Allowed Activities

With a crypto stablecoin license Romania, you can:

  • Issue asset-referenced tokens (ARTs) and electronic money tokens (EMTs).
  • Offer custody and administration of stablecoins on behalf of clients.
  • Operate a trading platform for stablecoins (requires additional CASP authorization).
  • Provide exchange services between stablecoins and fiat or other crypto.
  • Execute orders and place stablecoin products.

The license does not cover general crypto exchange services (e.g., spot trading of volatile assets) unless combined with a CASP license. For a full exchange setup, see exchange license options. Additionally, stablecoin issuers can offer redemption services directly to holders, but must ensure 24/7 redeemability for EMTs.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Pre-application assessment: Determine the appropriate license type and prepare a business plan. Consulting24 conducts a gap analysis of your project.
  2. Company setup: Incorporate a Romanian legal entity (e.g., SRL) with a registered office and local directors. This takes 2-4 weeks.
  3. Documentation: Prepare a white paper, risk management policies, AML/CFT procedures, governance framework, and reserve management plan. Expect 2-3 months.
  4. Capital deposit: Deposit the minimum capital in a Romanian bank account (proof required). Start banking early as onboarding can take 4-8 weeks.
  5. Submission: Submit the application to ASF with all supporting documents and the application fee. ASF has 3 months to review, extendable by 3 months.
  6. Review and interview: ASF reviews the application, may request additional information, and conducts interviews with key personnel.
  7. Approval: If compliant, the license is granted. Post-approval, you must register with the Romanian tax authority and submit periodic reports.

Compliance and Trust

Compliance is the backbone of a successful stablecoin operation. The ASF requires:

  • Strong AML/CFT policies, including customer due diligence and transaction monitoring. This includes screening against EU sanctions lists.
  • Regular audits of reserves and financial statements by an independent auditor. For EMTs, audits must be quarterly.
  • Appointment of a compliance officer and AML officer, both resident in Romania or the EU.
  • Incident reporting (within 24 hours for major incidents) and business continuity plans.
  • Public disclosure of reserve composition and valuation on a monthly basis for ARTs.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Insufficient capital: Underestimating the capital needed for the chosen token type. For ARTs, EUR 150,000 is the minimum, but higher volumes require more.
  • Poor documentation: Submitting an incomplete or vague white paper or risk management plan. The ASF expects detailed reserve custody arrangements.
  • Ignoring local presence: Not having a physical office or local director can lead to rejection. A virtual office is not sufficient.
  • Delaying bank account: Banks may take months to onboard; start early. Some applicants lose time by starting banking after submission.
  • Overlooking ongoing costs: Budget for annual compliance and audit fees of EUR 10,000-25,000. Also factor in ASF supervision fees (0.1% of outstanding tokens).
  • Misclassifying token type: Ensure your stablecoin is correctly categorized as ART or EMT; misclassification can require a new application.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a stablecoin license in Romania?

For EMTs, the minimum capital is EUR 50,000. For ARTs, it is EUR 150,000. Significant tokens classified by ESMA may require up to EUR 2 million. Capital must be held in liquid assets and cannot be used for operational expenses.

How long does it take to get a crypto stablecoin license in Romania?

The typical timeline is 6 to 12 months from application to approval. This includes a 3-month review period by the ASF, extendable by 3 months. Pre-application company setup and documentation can add 2-4 months.

What are the ongoing compliance costs for a Romanian stablecoin issuer?

Annual costs typically range from EUR 10,000 to EUR 25,000. This includes the compliance officer salary, external audit fees, ASF supervision fees (0.1% of outstanding tokens), and AML/CFT reporting costs.

Can I passport the Romanian stablecoin license to other EU countries?

Yes, under MiCA, a license from the ASF allows you to operate in all 27 EU member states through passporting. You must notify the ASF of your intention to provide services cross-border.

What is the difference between an ART and an EMT license?

ARTs are backed by a basket of assets (e.g., multiple currencies or commodities) and require a detailed white paper. EMTs are pegged to a single fiat currency and have stricter redemption requirements. Capital requirements differ: EUR 150,000 for ARTs vs EUR 50,000 for EMTs.

Do I need a physical office in Romania for the license?

Yes, the ASF requires a registered office and local management. A virtual office is not sufficient. You must have a physical presence where records are kept and compliance staff operate.

What tax rate applies to stablecoin issuance in Romania?

Corporate income tax is 16% on profits. VAT is exempt for crypto transactions. Dividends to EU residents are subject to 5% withholding tax, and to non-EU residents 10% (subject to tax treaties).

Can I issue a stablecoin without a license in Romania?

No, issuing stablecoins to the public in Romania or the EU requires a license under MiCA. Unlicensed issuance can result in fines and legal action. Only licensed entities can offer ARTs or EMTs.

What banks work with stablecoin issuers in Romania?

Banca Transilvania and BRD have dedicated fintech units that accept crypto clients. International banks like Revolut Business and N26 also work with licensed issuers. Banking setup can take 2-3 months.

How does Romania compare to Panama for stablecoin licensing?

Panama has no specific stablecoin license and costs EUR 6,000 flat with no capital requirement, but offers no EU passporting. Romania provides EU market access with higher costs and capital requirements. Consult our Panama page for details.

What happens if my stablecoin becomes 'significant' under MiCA?

If your token exceeds thresholds (e.g., 10 million holders or EUR 5 billion market cap), ESMA classifies it as significant. This triggers higher capital requirements (up to EUR 2 million) and additional oversight from the European Banking Authority.
