What Is the Crypto NFT Marketplace License in Slovakia?

The crypto NFT marketplace license in Slovakia is the authorization required to operate a platform where users can buy, sell, or trade non-fungible tokens (NFTs) using crypto assets. Under MiCA, which is fully in force in 2026, any platform that facilitates the exchange of crypto assets for fiat or other crypto assets, or provides custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients, must be registered as a CASP. The Slovak National Bank (Národná banka Slovenska, NBS) is the competent authority responsible for licensing and supervision.

This license covers activities such as operating an NFT marketplace, providing crypto custody for NFTs, and executing orders for clients. It is important to note that NFTs that are unique and non-fungible may fall outside MiCA if they are truly one-of-a-kind, but most NFT marketplaces that deal with fractionalized or semi-fungible tokens will be within scope. The exact classification depends on the nature of the NFTs and the services offered.

Who Needs a Crypto NFT Marketplace License in Slovakia?

Any legal entity that intends to offer crypto-asset services related to NFTs in Slovakia needs this license. Specifically, you need a license if your business:

  • Operates a platform for trading NFTs against fiat currency or other crypto assets.
  • Provides custody or administration of NFTs on behalf of clients.
  • Executes orders for clients to buy or sell NFTs.
  • Offers exchange services between NFTs and other crypto assets.

If you are a startup or an established exchange looking to expand into NFTs, you must register with the NBS. Even if your platform is decentralized, the operator (the entity that controls the platform) is likely subject to licensing. For example, a marketplace that charges fees in crypto and holds user funds will require a license. License Type and Regulator

The relevant license is the Crypto-Asset Service Provider (CASP) license under MiCA, transposed into Slovak law. The regulator is the National Bank of Slovakia (Národná banka Slovenska, NBS). The NBS is responsible for authorizing, supervising, and enforcing compliance for all CASPs operating in Slovakia. As of 2026, Slovakia has fully adopted MiCA, so the licensing regime is harmonized with other EU member states.

The license covers multiple service classes, each with its own capital requirement. For an NFT marketplace, the typical services include exchange of crypto assets for fiat (class 3) and execution of orders (class 4), among others. The NBS expects applicants to have strong AML/KYC procedures, operational resilience, and adequate governance.

Capital Requirement for a Slovak CASP License

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement depends on the types of services you offer. For an NFT marketplace, the relevant capital tiers are:

  • EUR 50,000 for providing custody and administration of crypto assets (if you hold NFTs for clients).
  • EUR 125,000 for operating a trading platform (exchange of crypto assets for fiat or other crypto).
  • EUR 150,000 for executing orders on behalf of clients.

If your marketplace offers multiple services, the highest capital requirement applies. Most NFT marketplaces will need at least EUR 125,000. This capital must be held in liquid assets and maintained throughout the license period. Consulting24 can advise on the exact calculation for your business model.

Tax Treatment for Crypto NFT Marketplaces in Slovakia

Slovakia applies a corporate income tax rate of 15% for companies with turnover up to EUR 49,790, and 21% for larger companies. Crypto transactions are treated as barter transactions for tax purposes, meaning that each trade may trigger a taxable event. VAT is generally not applicable to crypto-to-crypto trades, but fees charged by the marketplace may be subject to VAT. For NFT sales, the tax treatment can be complex: if the NFT represents a digital artwork, it may be subject to VAT in some cases. It is essential to engage a local tax advisor. Allowed Activities Under the Slovak CASP License

With a Slovak CASP license, you can legally offer the following services in relation to NFTs and crypto assets:

  • Operation of an NFT marketplace (exchange of NFTs for fiat or crypto).
  • Custody and administration of NFTs on behalf of clients.
  • Execution of orders for clients to buy or sell NFTs.
  • Reception and transmission of orders for NFTs.
  • Placement of crypto assets (including NFTs) without a firm commitment.

You cannot offer services that are not covered by your license. For example, if you only have a custody license, you cannot operate a trading platform. The NBS will specify which service classes you are authorized for. Consulting24 can help you define the scope of your application.

The minimum capital depends on the services offered. For operating a trading platform, it is EUR 125,000. If you also execute orders, it may be EUR 150,000. Custody alone requires EUR 50,000.
