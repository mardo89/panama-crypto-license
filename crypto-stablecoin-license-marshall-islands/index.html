What Is the Crypto Stablecoin License Marshall Islands?

The crypto stablecoin license Marshall Islands is a regulatory approval granted by the Marshall Islands Financial Services Authority (MIFSA) under the Digital Asset Business Act (DABA) and related stablecoin-specific regulations. It allows entities to issue, redeem, and manage stablecoins pegged to fiat currencies, commodities, or other assets. The RMI has created a bespoke framework that recognises stablecoins as a distinct asset class, separate from general cryptocurrencies or securities.

Key features of the licence include:

  • Legal recognition of stablecoins as a form of digital asset with defined rights and obligations.
  • Regulatory oversight focused on reserve management, transparency, and consumer protection.
  • Flexibility for both fiat-backed and algorithmic stablecoin models, subject to specific reserve requirements.
  • Access to the RMI's blockchain-friendly environment, including the possibility of using the country's digital identity system.

The licence is suitable for startups and established firms that want to operate a stablecoin in a regulated manner, especially those targeting Pacific Rim markets or seeking a cost-effective alternative to more expensive regimes like the EU under MiCA.

Who Needs This Licence?

The crypto stablecoin license Marshall Islands is designed for a range of stablecoin-related activities. You likely need this licence if you:

  • Issue a stablecoin (fiat-backed, crypto-backed, or algorithmic) to the public.
  • Operate a stablecoin redemption service or manage reserve assets.
  • Provide custody or wallet services specifically for stablecoins.
  • Act as a stablecoin exchange or trading platform that lists stablecoins as a primary pair.
  • Develop a stablecoin protocol or smart contract that involves issuance or governance of stablecoins.

The licence is also relevant for fintech companies that want to integrate stablecoins into payment systems, remittance services, or decentralised finance (DeFi) applications. If your project involves creating a new stablecoin or bringing an existing one to the RMI market, this licence is mandatory. Licence Type and Regulator

The regulator for the crypto stablecoin license Marshall Islands is the Marshall Islands Financial Services Authority (MIFSA). MIFSA is the integrated financial regulator responsible for banking, insurance, and digital assets. Under the Digital Asset Business Act (DABA) as amended in 2025, stablecoin issuance is classified as a separate licence category within the digital asset framework.

The licence type is a Stablecoin Issuer Licence (or Class S licence), distinct from the general Digital Asset Business Licence. This classification reflects the specific risks and requirements of stablecoins, particularly around reserve management and redemption rights. As of 2026, MIFSA has issued a limited number of such licences, making it a niche but credible option.

MIFSA requires applicants to demonstrate sound governance, adequate capital, and strong anti-money laundering (AML) controls. The regulator is known for being responsive and open to innovation, but it also enforces strict compliance with international standards, including FATF recommendations. Capital Requirement

The capital requirement for the crypto stablecoin license Marshall Islands is set by MIFSA and depends on the type and volume of stablecoin issuance. As of 2026, the minimum paid-up capital is:

  • $50,000 for stablecoin issuers with a total issuance value below $1 million.
  • $100,000 for issuers with issuance between $1 million and $10 million.
  • $150,000 for issuers with issuance above $10 million or for algorithmic stablecoins.

These figures are comparable to the EU MiCA capital tiers (EUR 50,000 / 125,000 / 150,000) but are in USD. The capital must be held in a trust account or as a bank guarantee, and it must be unencumbered. MIFSA may require additional capital if the stablecoin is pegged to a volatile asset or if the issuer engages in other digital asset activities. Tax Treatment

The Marshall Islands offers a tax-neutral environment for stablecoin issuers. There is no corporate income tax, no capital gains tax, no withholding tax, and no VAT/GST on digital asset transactions. However, there is a licence fee and an annual registration fee that are effectively taxes on the business. The RMI also has a gross revenue tax of 3% on certain business activities, but stablecoin issuance may be exempt if structured properly.

It is important to note that the Marshall Islands does not have a double tax treaty network, so issuers should consider their global tax position. Many stablecoin projects incorporate in the RMI but operate from other jurisdictions, which can create tax liabilities elsewhere. Allowed Activities

Under the crypto stablecoin license Marshall Islands, the following activities are permitted:

  • Issuance of stablecoins pegged to fiat currencies (e.g., USD, EUR), commodities (e.g., gold), or other digital assets.
  • Redemption of stablecoins at par value, subject to reserve requirements.
  • Management of reserve assets, including custody and investment in permitted instruments.
  • Provision of stablecoin wallet services, including multi-signature and smart contract wallets.
  • Operation of a stablecoin exchange or trading platform, provided the primary pair is a stablecoin.
  • Integration of stablecoins into payment systems, remittance services, and merchant solutions.

Activities that are not allowed without additional licensing include: general cryptocurrency exchange (non-stablecoin), securities token issuance, and banking services. Compliance and Trust

Maintaining a crypto stablecoin license Marshall Islands requires ongoing compliance with MIFSA regulations. Key obligations include:

  • Reserve management: You must hold reserves equal to at least 100% of the stablecoin in circulation, in liquid assets (cash, government bonds, or approved stablecoins).
  • Audits: Annual financial audits by a licensed auditor, with results submitted to MIFSA.
  • AML/CFT: Implement a risk-based AML program, including KYC, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting.
  • Reporting: Quarterly reports on stablecoin issuance, redemption, and reserve composition.
  • Consumer protection: Maintain a clear redemption policy and handle complaints promptly.
