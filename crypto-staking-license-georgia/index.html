What Is a Crypto Staking License in Georgia?

A crypto staking license in Georgia is a regulatory authorization that allows a business to offer staking services to clients. Staking involves locking up cryptocurrency to support the operations of a proof-of-stake blockchain network, earning rewards in return. Under Georgian law, this activity falls under the definition of a virtual asset service provider (VASP), requiring registration with the National Bank of Georgia (NBG).

The license is not a standalone permit but part of the broader Georgian crypto license regime. It covers the safekeeping and administration of virtual assets on behalf of clients, which includes staking. Businesses must comply with AML/CTF obligations, including customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and reporting suspicious activities.

Key features of the Georgia crypto staking license include:

  • Regulated by the National Bank of Georgia (NBG).
  • Requires a minimum capital of GEL 350,000 (approximately EUR 110,000 as of 2026).
  • Corporate income tax at 15% (with incentives for reinvested profits).
  • Application timeline typically 3-6 months.
  • No specific staking license; staking is covered under VASP registration.

For example, a staking-as-a-service platform that pools client ETH and stakes it on Ethereum 2.0 would need this registration. The NBG expects the business to have clear policies on reward distribution, slashing risk management, and client communication. Who Needs a Crypto Staking License in Georgia?

Any legal entity offering commercial staking services to clients in or from Georgia requires a crypto staking license. This includes:

  • Staking-as-a-service providers.
  • Cryptocurrency exchanges that offer staking pools.
  • Wallet providers that facilitate staking on behalf of users.
  • DeFi platforms that manage staking for customers.

If your business merely stakes its own treasury assets without offering the service to third parties, you may not need a license. However, if you hold client funds or operate a staking pool for profit, registration is mandatory. For instance, a company that stakes its own BTC to earn yield does not need a license, but a platform that stakes client deposits in a pooled fund does.

License Type and Regulator

The crypto staking license in Georgia is part of the virtual asset service provider (VASP) registration overseen by the National Bank of Georgia (NBG). The NBG is the central bank and primary financial regulator, responsible for licensing and supervising all VASPs under the Law on Combating Money Laundering and the Financing of Terrorism.

There is no separate staking license; staking is considered a VASP activity under the category of “safekeeping and administration of virtual assets on behalf of clients.” The NBG requires all VASPs to register before commencing operations. The registration process involves submitting detailed documentation, including business plans, AML policies, and information on beneficial owners.

As of 2026, Georgia is not part of the EU, so MiCA rules do not apply. However, the NBG aligns with FATF recommendations. Consulting24 can guide you through the NBG requirements and coordinate with local legal partners.

Capital Requirement for a Crypto Staking License in Georgia

The minimum capital requirement for a VASP in Georgia is GEL 350,000, which is approximately EUR 110,000 as of 2026. This capital must be paid up before registration and maintained throughout the business operations. The capital can be in the form of cash or liquid assets and must be held in a Georgian bank account.

This requirement is relatively high compared to some other jurisdictions. For example, Panama has no minimum capital for a crypto company, and Estonia requires EUR 12,000 for a license. However, Georgia's capital requirement signals a serious commitment to compliance and financial stability. The NBG may also require proof that the capital is unencumbered and available for operational needs.

Tax Treatment for Crypto Staking in Georgia

Georgia offers a favorable tax regime for businesses, including crypto staking operators. The corporate income tax rate is 15%, but there is a unique feature: retained earnings are not taxed until they are distributed as dividends. This means that if you reinvest profits, you defer tax liability indefinitely.

For individuals, capital gains from cryptocurrency trading are generally not taxed, but staking rewards may be subject to personal income tax at a flat rate of 20%. However, if staking is conducted through a licensed VASP, the business entity is taxed, not the individual client. This can be advantageous for structuring.

Value-added tax (VAT) at 18% applies to most goods and services, but crypto transactions may be exempt. Consulting24 recommends consulting with a local tax advisor to optimize your structure. For a comparison, see our vs Lithuania page.

Allowed Activities Under a Georgia Crypto License

A Georgia crypto license (VASP registration) permits the following activities:

  • Exchange between virtual assets and fiat currencies.
  • Exchange between one or more virtual assets.
  • Transfer of virtual assets.
  • Safekeeping and administration of virtual assets (including staking).
  • Participation in and provision of financial services related to an issuer's offer of virtual assets.

Staking is explicitly allowed under the safekeeping and administration category. However, activities like operating a crypto exchange without a license or offering unregistered securities are prohibited. The NBG may impose additional conditions depending on the business model. For example, if your staking service involves lending client assets, it may require separate authorization.

Step-by-Step Process to Obtain a Crypto Staking License in Georgia

The application process for a Georgia crypto license involves several steps. Consulting24 coordinates with local partners to streamline the process.

  1. Pre-incorporation: Choose a company name and prepare incorporation documents. A local registered address is required.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a legal entity (LLC or JSC) with the National Agency of Public Registry. This typically takes 1-2 weeks.
  3. Capital deposit: Open a corporate bank account in Georgia and deposit the minimum capital of GEL 350,000.
  4. Document preparation: Draft AML/KYC policies, business plan, risk assessment, and internal controls.
  5. Submit application: File the VASP registration application with the NBG, including all supporting documents.
  6. Review and approval: The NBG reviews the application within 3-6 months. They may request additional information.
  7. Post-license compliance: Submit regular reports and maintain AML procedures.

Compliance and Trust Considerations
