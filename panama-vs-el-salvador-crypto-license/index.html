What Is a Panama Crypto License?

A Panama crypto license is not a dedicated regulatory license but a corporate registration that permits a Panama company to conduct crypto-related activities. Panama does not have a specific crypto licensing law; instead, it relies on its general corporate and securities framework. The process is fast and flexible, making it a popular choice for startups and small to medium businesses.

Key features include a flat setup fee of EUR 6,000, no minimum capital requirement, and zero tax on foreign-source income. Panama offers strong privacy protections and a stable legal environment based on civil law. However, because it is not a formal regulatory license, some banking partners and counterparties may require additional due diligence. The license is effectively a corporate registration with a business purpose that covers exchange, custody, payment processing, mining, staking, and DeFi operations.

Panama's territorial tax system means only income sourced in Panama is taxed. For crypto businesses serving clients outside Panama, income is tax-free. There is no capital gains tax, no VAT on crypto services, and no withholding tax on dividends. This makes Panama one of the most tax-efficient jurisdictions for crypto operations.

What Is an El Salvador Crypto License?

El Salvador offers a formal crypto license under the Digital Assets Law (Ley de Activos Digitales), enacted in 2023 and fully operational by 2026. This law provides a comprehensive regulatory framework for digital asset service providers, including exchanges, custodians, and token issuers. The regulator is the National Commission of Digital Assets (CNAD), which oversees licensing and supervision.

The license requires a minimum capital of USD 250,000 (or equivalent in Bitcoin), a detailed business plan, and compliance with AML/KYC regulations. The timeline is typically 3-6 months. El Salvador also offers tax incentives: income from digital assets is exempt from income tax for a period of 10 years (until 2033), and there is no capital gains tax on Bitcoin transactions. This makes it attractive for businesses focused on Bitcoin and blockchain innovation.

El Salvador's Bitcoin Law, which made Bitcoin legal tender, further supports crypto businesses. The government-backed Chivo wallet and local banks facilitate Bitcoin transactions. However, the high capital requirement and longer timeline make it more suitable for larger, well-funded operations seeking institutional credibility.

Who Needs a Crypto License in Panama or El Salvador?

Both jurisdictions cater to different types of crypto businesses:

  • Panama: Ideal for startups, small to medium exchanges, payment processors, and crypto funds that want a low-cost, quick setup with minimal regulatory oversight. Best for businesses that do not require a formal license for banking relationships.
  • El Salvador: Suitable for larger exchanges, Bitcoin-focused businesses, token issuers, and companies that need a regulated environment to attract institutional investors. Also for businesses that want to use El Salvador's Bitcoin-friendly policies and tax exemptions.

Consulting24 advises on which jurisdiction aligns with your business model, target market, and compliance needs. For example, a small DeFi startup with limited capital would benefit from Panama's low barrier, while a Bitcoin ATM network seeking regulatory approval would find El Salvador's formal license more appropriate.

License Type and Regulator

Panama: There is no specific crypto license. The license is a corporate registration with the Public Registry of Panama, allowing a company to engage in crypto activities as part of its business purpose. The regulator for financial activities is the Superintendency of Banks of Panama (SBP), but crypto is not directly regulated unless it involves securities.

El Salvador: The license is a Digital Asset Service Provider (DASP) license under the Digital Assets Law, regulated by the National Commission of Digital Assets (CNAD). This is a formal licensing regime with specific requirements for capital, governance, and reporting. The CNAD conducts thorough due diligence, including background checks on directors and shareholders.

For comparison, EU member states like Estonia and Lithuania now operate under MiCA, with capital tiers of EUR 50,000, 125,000, or 150,000 depending on service class. Panama's approach is lighter, while El Salvador's is more formal but still less complex than MiCA.

Capital Requirement

Panama: No minimum capital requirement. You can start with as little as EUR 10,000 in share capital, but there is no regulatory minimum. This makes Panama highly accessible for bootstrapped startups.

El Salvador: Minimum capital of USD 250,000 (or equivalent in Bitcoin) is required. This must be maintained as liquid capital. The high threshold reflects the formal regulatory nature and is designed to ensure solvency. For comparison, under MiCA, EU member states require EUR 50,000 for simple exchange services, EUR 125,000 for custody, and EUR 150,000 for trading platforms.

If your business cannot meet the USD 250,000 requirement, Panama or an EU jurisdiction with lower capital may be more suitable. Consulting24 can help you structure your capital efficiently.

Tax Treatment

Panama: Territorial taxation: only income sourced in Panama is taxed. Offshore income (including crypto trading with non-Panamanian clients) is tax-free. No capital gains tax, no VAT on crypto services, and no withholding tax on dividends. Corporate income tax is 25% on local-source income only.

El Salvador: Income from digital assets is exempt from income tax for 10 years (until 2033). Bitcoin transactions are exempt from capital gains tax. There is no VAT on crypto services. After the exemption period, standard corporate tax rates apply (30%).

Both jurisdictions offer significant tax advantages. Panama is better for businesses with mostly offshore clients, while El Salvador's tax holiday is attractive for those targeting the local market or Bitcoin-focused operations.

Allowed Activities

Panama: Any crypto activity not explicitly prohibited is allowed, including exchange (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto), custody, payment processing, mining, staking, and DeFi operations. However, securities-related activities (e.g., tokenized securities) may require additional licensing.

El Salvador: Activities covered under the Digital Assets Law include exchange, custody, transfer, and issuance of digital assets (including security tokens). Bitcoin mining and Lightning Network services are also permitted. The law explicitly recognizes Bitcoin as legal tender, so Bitcoin-related services are fully supported.

Both jurisdictions allow a broad range of activities. Panama is more permissive for DeFi and mining, while El Salvador offers a clear framework for token issuance.

Benefits of Panama vs El Salvador

  • Panama: Low cost, fast setup, no capital requirement, tax-free offshore income, privacy, and stability. Best for small to medium businesses that need a quick, low-cost entry.
  • El Salvador: Formal regulatory license, Bitcoin legal tender, tax exemptions for 10 years, institutional credibility, and access to a Bitcoin-friendly ecosystem. Best for larger, regulated operations.

Panama's main advantage is speed and cost. El Salvador's is regulatory recognition. Your choice depends on your business goals and budget.

Compliance and Trust Considerations

Panama: While Panama has AML laws, the crypto sector is lightly regulated. This can be a double-edged sword: low compliance burden but may be seen as risky by partners. Consulting24 recommends implementing strong internal AML/KYC procedures to build trust. For example, using third-party KYC providers and transaction monitoring software can enhance credibility.

El Salvador: The CNAD enforces strict AML/KYC compliance, including transaction monitoring and reporting. This enhances credibility with banks and institutional investors. However, the regulatory burden is higher. You will need to appoint a compliance officer and file regular reports.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Panama: Assuming no compliance needed. Even without a license, you must comply with AML laws. Also, avoid using Panama for high-risk activities without proper legal structure. Another mistake is not planning for banking: some banks close accounts of crypto companies without notice.
  • El Salvador: Underestimating the capital requirement. USD 250,000 is a significant barrier. Also, failing to prepare a thorough business plan can delay approval. Some applicants overlook the need for a local representative or office.
  • Both: Not considering banking access upfront. Ensure you have a bank or payment processor lined up before starting. Also, ignoring ongoing compliance costs can strain your budget.
