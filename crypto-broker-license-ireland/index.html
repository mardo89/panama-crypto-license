What is the Crypto Broker License in Ireland?

The crypto broker license in Ireland is a regulatory authorisation issued by the Central Bank of Ireland (CBI) under MiCA. It allows firms to provide crypto-asset services such as custody, exchange, and broker services to clients within the EU. The license is part of the EU-wide CASP regime, which replaced national VASP registers. As of 2026, all crypto service providers must be licensed under MiCA to operate legally in Ireland and passport services across the EU.

Key features of the Irish regime include a strong fit-and-proper test, AML/CFT compliance, and operational resilience requirements. The CBI is known for its thorough review process, making professional guidance essential. The license covers activities like order execution, advice, and portfolio management. Firms must maintain a physical presence in Ireland, including a registered office and local management.

Compared to other jurisdictions, Ireland offers a stable regulatory environment but with higher upfront costs and longer timelines. For example, a Panama license costs a flat EUR 6,000 and takes 4-6 weeks, but does not provide EU passporting.

Who Needs a Crypto Broker License in Ireland?

Any entity that provides crypto-asset services to Irish or EU residents must hold a license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto)
  • Brokerage platforms
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • OTC desks
  • Market makers

If you plan to offer any of these services from Ireland or target EU clients, you need a CASP license. The CBI also requires a physical presence in Ireland, including a registered office and local management. Even if you are a startup, you must meet the same requirements as larger firms. For example, a small brokerage handling under EUR 1 million in monthly volume must still hold EUR 125,000 in capital and submit a full application.

License Type & Regulator

The regulator is the Central Bank of Ireland (CBI). The license type is a Class 1 or Class 2 CASP license under MiCA, depending on the services offered. Class 1 covers custody and exchange services, while Class 2 includes broker and advisory services. The CBI also oversees AML compliance and prudential supervision.

For a crypto broker license, you typically need a Class 2 authorisation. The CBI requires detailed documentation, including a business plan, risk assessment, governance structure, and AML policies. The CBI has a reputation for rigorous scrutiny, so incomplete applications often face delays. As of 2026, the CBI has dedicated a team to review CASP applications, but processing times remain 6-12 months.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for a CASP in Ireland depends on the services provided. For a crypto broker license (Class 2), the capital requirement is EUR 125,000 as of 2026. This must be held as own funds and maintained at all times. Additional capital may be required based on the volume of transactions or risk profile.

For comparison, a Class 1 license (custody/exchange) requires EUR 50,000, and a full-service license (including proprietary trading) requires EUR 150,000. The capital must be fully paid up before authorisation. The CBI may also require a higher amount if your business model involves high transaction volumes or complex risks. For example, a broker handling over EUR 10 million monthly may need EUR 200,000 or more.

Tax Treatment for Crypto Businesses in Ireland

Ireland offers a competitive corporate tax rate of 12.5% on trading income. Crypto transactions are generally treated as trading income, subject to corporation tax. VAT on crypto services is typically exempt under EU VAT rules. However, capital gains tax may apply to certain transactions. It is advisable to structure your operations with Irish tax advisors to optimise liability.

Ireland also has a wide network of double taxation treaties, making it attractive for international operations. For example, a crypto broker based in Ireland can serve clients in Germany, France, and Spain without facing withholding taxes on cross-border payments, provided the treaties are applied correctly. However, the tax treatment of crypto-assets can be complex, especially for staking, lending, and DeFi activities. Professional tax advice is essential.

Allowed Activities Under the License

A crypto broker license in Ireland permits the following activities under MiCA:

  • Execution of orders for crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • Placing of crypto-assets
  • Reception and transmission of orders
  • Provision of advice on crypto-assets
  • Portfolio management
  • Custody and administration of crypto-assets (if included)

You may also apply for a broader license covering exchange and custody services. All activities are subject to ongoing compliance and reporting. For example, a broker that also offers custodial wallets needs a Class 1 license or a combined authorisation. The CBI expects clear segregation of client assets and strong cybersecurity measures.

If your business model is limited to peer-to-peer trading or non-custodial services, a full license may not be required. Consulting24 can assess your specific activities during a consultation.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process for obtaining a crypto broker license in Ireland involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application: Engage advisors, prepare business plan, governance documents, AML policies, and capital proof.
  2. Company setup: Incorporate an Irish company (typically a private limited company) with a registered office and local directors.
  3. Submit application: File with the CBI, including all required documentation and application fee.
  4. CBI review: The CBI assesses fit-and-proper, business model, and compliance. They may request additional information. Expect at least one round of queries.
  5. Decision: If approved, you receive a conditional or full license. Conditions may include ongoing reporting and capital maintenance.
  6. Post-license: Implement ongoing compliance, annual audits, and regulatory reporting.

Compliance & Trust: Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, you must maintain ongoing compliance with MiCA and CBI requirements. This includes:

  • Annual AML/CFT audits
  • Regular reporting to the CBI on transactions, financial health, and risk
  • Fit-and-proper reassessments of management
  • Capital maintenance (minimum EUR 125,000)
  • Client asset segregation and custody rules

Common Mistakes When Applying for an Ireland Crypto License

Many applicants face delays or rejection due to common errors:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing business plans or risk assessments.
  • Weak AML policies: Not meeting CBI standards. For example, failing to include transaction monitoring procedures.
  • Unclear source of funds: The CBI scrutinises capital sources. If funds come from crypto trading, you need a clear audit trail.
  • Lack of local presence: No registered office or local directors. The CBI requires at least one director resident in Ireland or the EEA.
  • Underestimating timeline: Expect 6-12 months, not 3. Many applicants start the process too late.
