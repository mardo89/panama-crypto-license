What Is the Crypto NFT Marketplace License in Thailand?

The crypto NFT marketplace license in Thailand is a regulatory authorization issued by the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) under the Digital Asset Business Decree B.E. 2561 (2018). It allows operators to facilitate trading of digital assets, including cryptocurrencies and non-fungible tokens (NFTs), on a centralised platform. The license is required for any business that provides exchange, brokerage, dealer, or fund management services related to digital assets.

Thailand's SEC classifies digital asset businesses into four types: exchange, broker, dealer, and fund manager. For an NFT marketplace, the most relevant category is the digital asset exchange license, which permits matching buyers and sellers of NFTs and other tokens. The regulatory framework is designed to protect investors, prevent market abuse, and ensure operational integrity. As of 2026, the SEC has updated guidelines to address NFTs specifically, including requirements for token classification and anti-money laundering (AML) measures.

Key features of the license include mandatory membership in the Digital Asset Operators Association, compliance with SEC notification rules, and periodic reporting. The license is valid for one year and must be renewed annually. Consulting24 advises and coordinates the entire process, from preparing the application to liaising with the SEC.

License Type and Regulator

The primary regulator for digital asset businesses in Thailand is the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) Thailand. The SEC operates under the Ministry of Finance and enforces the Digital Asset Business Decree. The license type for an NFT marketplace is typically a Digital Asset Exchange License, which allows the operator to facilitate trading of digital assets. There are also licenses for brokers, dealers, and fund managers, but the exchange license is most common for marketplaces.

The SEC has issued detailed regulations covering capital requirements, custody of assets, technology standards, and AML/CFT obligations. As of 2026, the SEC requires exchanges to have a minimum registered capital of THB 50 million (approximately EUR 1.3 million) and to maintain a net capital ratio of at least 7% of total liabilities. The regulator also mandates that at least one director and one manager be based in Thailand.

For NFT-specific activities, the SEC classifies NFTs as digital assets if they have utility or investment characteristics. Pure collectibles or art NFTs may be exempt, but if your marketplace involves fractionalisation or trading, the license is required. Capital Requirement

The minimum registered capital for a digital asset exchange in Thailand is THB 50 million (approximately EUR 1.3 million). This capital must be fully paid-up at the time of application. The SEC also requires the company to maintain a net capital ratio of at least 7% of total liabilities, meaning the company must have sufficient liquid assets to cover its obligations.

For other license types (broker, dealer, fund manager), the capital requirement is lower: THB 25 million for brokers and dealers, and THB 10 million for fund managers. However, for an NFT marketplace that operates as an exchange, the THB 50 million threshold applies. The capital must be held in a Thai bank account and cannot be used for operational expenses without SEC approval.

Tax Treatment for Crypto NFT Marketplaces in Thailand

Thailand has a specific tax regime for digital assets. As of 2026, the Revenue Department taxes profits from digital asset trading as capital gains at the personal income tax rate (up to 35%) for individuals, and at the corporate income tax rate of 20% for companies. For NFT marketplaces, the platform's revenue from transaction fees is subject to corporate income tax, as well as 7% VAT (value-added tax) on the service fee.

Additionally, the SEC requires exchanges to deduct 15% withholding tax on payments to foreign entities for services. For NFT creators, income from primary sales may be taxed as ordinary income, while secondary sales may attract capital gains tax. The tax treatment of NFTs is still evolving, and the SEC and Revenue Department have issued joint guidance. Step-by-Step Process to Obtain the License

The process to obtain a crypto NFT marketplace license in Thailand involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step:

  1. Pre-application consultation: Assess your business model, determine the license type, and prepare a roadmap.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Thai company with the Ministry of Commerce, with a minimum registered capital of THB 50 million and at least one Thai director.
  3. Prepare documentation: Draft business plan, AML/CFT policy, risk management framework, IT security plan, and financial projections.
  4. Submit application: File with the SEC along with the application fee (THB 100,000). The SEC will review within 90 days.
  5. SEC interview and due diligence: The SEC may request additional information or conduct interviews with directors.
  6. License issuance: Once approved, pay the annual fee (THB 200,000) and receive the license.
  7. Post-licensing compliance: Implement ongoing reporting, AML monitoring, and annual audits.

Banking and Payment Integration

Operating an NFT marketplace in Thailand requires integration with the local banking system for fiat on-ramps and off-ramps. Thai banks are generally cautious about working with crypto businesses, but licensed exchanges can open corporate accounts. The Bank of Thailand has issued guidelines for banks to serve digital asset operators, provided they conduct enhanced due diligence.

Common payment methods include bank transfers, PromptPay, and credit/debit cards via third-party processors. For crypto-to-fiat conversion, exchanges typically partner with licensed crypto-to-fiat gateways. The SEC requires that customer fiat funds be held in segregated accounts with a Thai bank.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, your NFT marketplace must adhere to ongoing compliance requirements. These include:

  • AML/CFT: Implement customer due diligence (CDD), transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting.
  • Reporting: Submit monthly and annual reports to the SEC on trading volumes, financial statements, and material changes.
  • Audit: Engage an SEC-approved auditor for annual financial and compliance audits.
  • Technology: Maintain cybersecurity standards, including penetration testing and incident response plans.
  • Asset custody: Ensure customer assets are held in cold storage with insurance coverage.
