What is a Crypto Gambling License Philippines Crypto License?

A crypto gambling license Philippines crypto license is a regulatory authorization issued by PAGCOR, allowing operators to conduct online gambling activities using digital currencies. PAGCOR is the sole government agency that regulates and licenses all gambling activities in the Philippines, including those involving cryptocurrencies.

The license covers various forms of online gaming, such as casino games, sports betting, poker, and lottery, all transacted in crypto. Unlike traditional fiat-based licenses, this license specifically accommodates blockchain-based operations, including smart contract settlements and tokenized rewards.

As of 2026, PAGCOR has updated its regulations to align with international anti-money laundering (AML) standards, requiring strong KYC procedures and transaction monitoring. The license is valid for two years and renewable, with a focus on ensuring fair play and player protection. Operators must also comply with the Data Privacy Act of 2012, enforced by the National Privacy Commission (NPC).

For example, a crypto casino accepting Bitcoin deposits must implement a wallet system that records all transactions on a private ledger, auditable by PAGCOR. The platform must also use provably fair algorithms to ensure game outcomes are verifiable by players. This transparency builds trust and reduces disputes.

License Type & Regulator

The regulator is the Philippine Amusement and Gaming Corporation (PAGCOR), a government-owned corporation under the Office of the President. PAGCOR issues several license types, but for crypto gambling, the relevant ones are:

  • Internet Gaming License (IGL) - For online casino and sports betting operators. This is the most common license for crypto gambling.
  • Special Class License - For high-volume or specialized crypto operations, such as those using proprietary blockchain technology or offering unique game formats.
  • Service Provider License - For platform providers and software developers that supply gaming systems to licensed operators. This license does not allow direct offering to players.

Each license type has specific requirements, but all mandate compliance with PAGCOR's AML and data privacy rules. The license is non-transferable and requires annual renewal. PAGCOR also conducts regular audits and may revoke licenses for non-compliance.

For example, a company developing a crypto-based slot machine platform would need a Service Provider License, while the operator using that platform would need an IGL. Consulting24 helps determine the correct license type based on your business model.

Capital Requirement

PAGCOR does not impose a minimum paid-up capital requirement for crypto gambling license applicants. However, operators must demonstrate sufficient financial capacity to cover operational costs and player payouts. Typically, a capital of at least $100,000 is expected to show financial stability, though this is not a formal requirement.

Tax Treatment

The tax regime for crypto gambling in the Philippines is favorable. The key taxes are:

  • Gross Gaming Revenue (GGR) Tax: 5% on monthly GGR
  • Corporate Income Tax: 25% on net income (standard rate)
  • Withholding Tax: 15% on dividends to non-residents
  • Value Added Tax (VAT): Exempt for gambling services

No VAT is applied to gambling services. The 5% GGR tax is one of the lowest in Asia, making the Philippines a cost-effective jurisdiction. For example, if your platform generates $1 million in GGR per month, you pay $50,000 in GGR tax. Compare this to Malta where the GGR tax is also 5% but with higher minimum capital requirements.

Corporate income tax applies to net profits after expenses. Operators should maintain proper accounting to maximize deductions. Consulting24 can connect you with local tax advisors.

For example, a live dealer blackjack game using crypto bets must have a certified random number generator (RNG) and be streamed from a licensed studio. PAGCOR may require on-site inspections of the studio.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The application process for a crypto gambling license in the Philippines involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step:

  1. Pre-application consultation - Determine the appropriate license type and prepare documentation. This includes a business plan, AML policy, and technical specifications.
  2. Company incorporation - Register a Philippine corporation with the SEC. Minimum of 2 directors, at least one of whom must be a Philippine resident. Paid-up capital of at least PHP 1 million (approx. $20,000) is recommended.
  3. Document submission - Provide business plan, AML policies, financial statements, and background checks for all directors and shareholders. Documents must be notarized and translated to English if needed.
  4. Due diligence - PAGCOR conducts background checks on directors and shareholders. This includes criminal record checks and financial probity. Expect interviews or requests for additional information.
  5. Technical review - Platform and game fairness testing may be required. Submit your gaming platform for certification by an approved testing lab. This can take 4-8 weeks.
  6. License issuance - Pay the license fee and compliance deposit. Receive the license certificate, valid for 2 years.
  7. Post-licensing compliance - Submit monthly GGR reports, annual audited financials, and undergo regular audits. Maintain AML records for at least 5 years.
