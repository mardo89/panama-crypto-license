What Is the Crypto NFT Marketplace License in Liechtenstein?

The crypto NFT marketplace license in Liechtenstein is a regulatory authorization issued under the TVTG (Token and Trusted Technology Service Provider Act). It allows companies to operate a platform where users can create, buy, sell, and trade non-fungible tokens (NFTs) and other digital assets. The license is part of Liechtenstein's comprehensive blockchain regulation, which covers all types of token-based services, including exchange, custody, and transfer.

Unlike some jurisdictions that treat NFTs as unregulated collectibles, Liechtenstein explicitly includes NFTs within its legal framework, providing clarity for marketplace operators. The license is issued by the Financial Market Authority (FMA) Liechtenstein, which supervises compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and investor protection rules. This makes Liechtenstein one of the most reputable and forward-thinking jurisdictions for NFT businesses in Europe. The TVTG was enacted in 2020 and has since been updated to cover emerging token types, including NFTs, security tokens, and utility tokens.

Who Needs This License?

Any company that operates a platform facilitating the trading of NFTs or other tokenized assets in or from Liechtenstein requires a license under the TVTG. This includes:

  • NFT marketplaces where users list and sell digital art, collectibles, music, or virtual real estate.
  • Platforms that offer primary issuance of NFTs (minting) and secondary trading.
  • Businesses that provide custody or transfer services for NFTs on behalf of clients.
  • Marketplaces that accept cryptocurrencies or fiat currency as payment for NFTs.

If your business model involves any of these activities, you must obtain a license from the FMA. Even if your company is based outside Liechtenstein, you may need a license if you target Liechtenstein residents or offer services in the EEA. Consulting24 can assess your specific case and advise on the necessity of licensing. For example, a startup minting NFT art for European collectors would likely need a license, whereas a purely informational platform without transactions may not.

License Type and Regulator

The relevant license for NFT marketplace operators in Liechtenstein is the Token Service Provider License under the TVTG. The regulator is the Financial Market Authority (FMA) Liechtenstein, which oversees all financial services, including crypto-asset services. The FMA is known for its pragmatic approach and thorough but fair review process.

Under the TVTG, there are several sub-categories of service providers, including token issuers, token custodians, and token transfer service providers. An NFT marketplace typically requires a combination of these authorizations, depending on whether the platform holds client assets or merely facilitates peer-to-peer trading. Consulting24 helps determine the exact scope of your license based on your business model. The FMA also requires that at least one director or manager be a resident of Liechtenstein or the EEA, which can be arranged through our network.

Capital Requirement

Liechtenstein imposes a minimum capital requirement for token service providers. As of 2026, the minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for most license types, including NFT marketplaces. This capital must be fully paid up before the license is granted and maintained throughout the life of the license. The capital can be held as cash or highly liquid assets and must be deposited in a Liechtenstein bank account.

Higher capital may be required if the marketplace handles large volumes of transactions or holds client assets. The FMA assesses capital adequacy on a case-by-case basis. Consulting24 advises on the exact amount needed for your specific business plan and helps structure the capital in compliance with regulatory expectations. For comparison, EU MiCA regimes require EUR 50,000-150,000 depending on service class, so Liechtenstein's requirement is competitive.

Tax Treatment

Liechtenstein offers a favorable tax regime for crypto businesses. The corporate income tax rate is a flat 12.5%, one of the lowest in Europe. Additionally, there is no VAT on cryptocurrency transactions, including NFT sales, under current legislation. However, VAT may apply to platform fees charged to users.

Liechtenstein does not impose a capital gains tax on the sale of crypto assets held by companies, making it attractive for NFT marketplaces that hold tokens as part of their treasury. Stamp duty and other transaction taxes are minimal. Consulting24 works with local tax advisors to ensure your company's tax structure is optimized and fully compliant with Liechtenstein and international tax laws. For example, if your marketplace charges a 2% fee on NFT sales, that fee is subject to corporate tax at 12.5%, but the underlying NFT sale itself is VAT-free.

Allowed Activities

With a Liechtenstein crypto license, your NFT marketplace can engage in the following activities:

  • Operating a platform for the issuance (minting) and trading of NFTs.
  • Custody of NFTs and other tokens on behalf of clients.
  • Transfer of NFTs between users (peer-to-peer).
  • Exchange of NFTs for fiat currency or other cryptocurrencies.
  • Providing wallet services for NFTs.
  • Marketing and promoting NFT projects to Liechtenstein and EEA residents.

Activities that are not permitted without additional licensing include offering derivatives or securities tokens (which may fall under traditional financial regulation). The FMA can provide guidance on the boundaries of your license. Consulting24 ensures your business model aligns with the permitted scope. For instance, if your platform also offers fractionalized NFTs that resemble securities, a separate MiFID license may be needed.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Many applicants make errors that delay or derail their license application. Common mistakes include:

  • Incomplete business plan: The FMA requires a detailed plan covering revenue model, target market, and risk management. Avoid generic templates.
  • Inadequate AML policies: Your policies must be tailored to NFT-specific risks, such as wash trading and money laundering through art. Generic policies are rejected.
  • Missing local director: At least one director must be a resident of Liechtenstein or the EEA. Consulting24 can provide a nominee director if needed.
  • Underestimating capital: Ensure the EUR 50,000 minimum is deposited and maintained in a Liechtenstein bank account before submission.
  • Ignoring tax obligations: Even though crypto transactions are VAT-free, platform fees are taxable. Engage a tax advisor early.
