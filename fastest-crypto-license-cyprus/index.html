What Is the Cyprus Crypto License?

The Cyprus crypto license, officially a registration as a Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) under the Investment Services and Activities and Regulated Markets Law of 2017 (as amended), is the mandatory authorization for offering crypto-related services in or from Cyprus. Since MiCA came fully into force in 2025, Cyprus has transposed the EU regulation into national law, aligning its capital requirements and operational standards with the pan-European framework.

CySEC is the competent authority responsible for licensing and supervision. The license covers services such as exchange between crypto and fiat, exchange between crypto assets, custody and administration, and operation of trading platforms. Cyprus offers a streamlined application process, often cited as one of the fastest in the EU, with decisions typically within 3-6 months from submission of a complete application. The country has a well-established legal system based on English common law, making it familiar to international investors. As of 2026, over 50 CASPs are registered in Cyprus, reflecting its popularity.

Who Needs a Cyprus Crypto License?

Any entity that provides crypto asset services to clients in Cyprus or from a Cyprus-based establishment must hold a CASP license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Brokers and dealers in crypto assets
  • Operators of crypto trading platforms
  • Advisory firms offering crypto investment advice

If your business targets EU clients, a Cyprus license can serve as your passport under MiCA, allowing you to operate across all EU member states. For non-EU firms, setting up a Cyprus subsidiary is a common route to access the European market. Consulting24 can help you determine if Cyprus is the right fit for your business model. For example, a US-based exchange wanting to serve EU customers would incorporate a Cyprus company and apply for the CASP license, avoiding the need for separate licenses in each EU country.

License Type and Regulator

The Cyprus crypto license is a single CASP authorization issued by the Cyprus Securities and Exchange Commission (CySEC). Unlike some jurisdictions that have multiple tiers, Cyprus offers one license type that covers all permitted activities, subject to the capital requirement applicable to the specific services offered.

CySEC is known for its pragmatic approach and efficient processing. The regulator requires a detailed business plan, AML policies, and fit-and-proper checks on key personnel. As of 2026, the application fee is approximately EUR 5,000, and the annual supervision fee is around EUR 3,000. These figures are indicative; exact amounts are confirmed during consultation. CySEC also requires that at least one director is resident in Cyprus, though this can be a local service provider. The regulator conducts background checks on all beneficial owners and senior management.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for CASPs in Cyprus depends on the type of services provided. The tiers are:

  • EUR 50,000 for services limited to exchange between crypto and fiat, and/or exchange between crypto assets (without custody)
  • EUR 125,000 for services including custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • EUR 150,000 for operating a trading platform

These amounts must be maintained as own funds, fully paid up. Cyprus does not require a higher national minimum; the EU tiers apply directly. Consulting24 can advise on the exact capital needed for your service mix. For example, if you plan to offer both exchange and custody, you need EUR 125,000. The capital must be held in a bank account in Cyprus and cannot be used for operational expenses without CySEC approval.

Tax Treatment

Cyprus offers one of the most attractive tax regimes in the EU for crypto businesses. The corporate income tax rate is a flat 12.5% on net profits. Additionally, capital gains from trading crypto assets are generally exempt from tax if the assets are held as investments (subject to conditions). VAT is not applicable on crypto-to-fiat exchanges (as per EU VAT directive).

Cyprus also has a wide network of double tax treaties, reducing withholding taxes on dividends and interest. There is no withholding tax on dividends paid to non-residents. For crypto founders, this can mean significant tax savings compared to other EU jurisdictions. However, tax planning should be done with a qualified advisor. For instance, a Cyprus CASP earning EUR 1 million in profit would pay only EUR 125,000 in corporate tax, leaving more capital for reinvestment.

Allowed Activities

A Cyprus CASP license permits the following activities, as defined under MiCA:

  • Providing custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Operation of a crypto asset trading platform
  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets
  • Execution of orders for crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Placing of crypto assets
  • Receiving and transmitting orders for crypto assets
  • Providing advice on crypto assets
  • Portfolio management of crypto assets

Staking and lending services may require additional authorization or fall under other regulations. CySEC provides guidance on the scope of each activity. For example, if you want to offer staking as a service, you may need to ensure it does not constitute a collective investment scheme. Step-by-Step Process

Obtaining the fastest Cyprus crypto license involves these steps:

  1. Company formation: Incorporate a Cyprus limited liability company with a local registered address and directors. This typically takes 1-2 weeks.
  2. Prepare documentation: Draft a business plan, AML/CFT policies, risk assessment, and organizational structure. Engage a local compliance consultant.
  3. Submit application: File the application with CySEC along with the fee. Include all required documents and proof of capital.
  4. Review and interview: CySEC reviews the application and may request additional information. Key personnel may be interviewed.
  5. Approval: If satisfied, CySEC grants the license. You then become a regulated CASP.
  6. Ongoing compliance: Submit periodic reports, maintain AML procedures, and pay annual fees.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make these errors when applying for a Cyprus crypto license:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing AML policies or a weak business plan can delay the process.
  • Underestimating capital: Failing to maintain the required own funds throughout the application.
  • Ignoring local presence: CySEC requires a physical office and local directors (or at least a local representative).
  • Choosing the wrong service scope: Selecting services that require higher capital without proper planning.

Frequently asked questions

How long does it take to get a Cyprus crypto license?

The fastest Cyprus crypto license typically takes 3-6 months from submission of a complete application. CySEC aims for 3 months, but complex cases may take longer. Pre-submission preparation adds 1-2 months.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a Cyprus CASP?

Under MiCA, the minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for exchange-only services, EUR 125,000 if custody is included, and EUR 150,000 for operating a trading platform. These amounts must be maintained as own funds.

Can I passport my Cyprus license to other EU countries?

Yes, under MiCA, a Cyprus CASP license allows you to provide services across all EU member states without additional licenses. You must notify CySEC of your intent to passport.

What are the tax benefits of a Cyprus crypto license?

Cyprus has a 12.5% corporate tax rate, no capital gains tax on crypto trading (subject to conditions), no withholding tax on dividends, and a wide double tax treaty network. This makes it one of the most tax-efficient EU jurisdictions.

Do I need a physical office in Cyprus?

Yes, CySEC requires a registered office in Cyprus. This can be a serviced office or a virtual office with a physical address. You also need at least one local director or a local representative.

Can I apply for a Cyprus crypto license remotely?

Yes, the application process can be handled remotely with the help of local advisors. However, you must have a local registered address and local directors (or representatives). Consulting24 coordinates everything remotely.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

Ongoing requirements include appointing a local AML compliance officer, submitting quarterly transaction reports, annual audits, and paying annual supervision fees (approx. EUR 3,000). CySEC may conduct inspections.

Is a Cyprus crypto license recognized outside the EU?

While the license is EU-specific, it is recognized by many international partners as a mark of regulatory compliance. It can facilitate banking relationships and partnerships globally.

How does Cyprus compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Panama does not require a crypto license and offers 0% tax on foreign income, making it simpler and cheaper for non-EU businesses. However, Cyprus provides EU market access and a regulated status that builds trust with clients and banks.

What activities are not covered by the Cyprus CASP license?

Staking, lending, and derivatives may require additional authorization. CySEC provides guidance; for example, staking as a service may be considered a collective investment scheme and need a separate license.
