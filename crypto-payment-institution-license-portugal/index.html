Crypto Payment Institution License Portugal Crypto License: Crypto Payment Institution License Portugal: Complete 2026 Guide

The crypto payment institution license Portugal is a regulated authorisation for firms to issue e-money, process payments, and offer crypto-fiat exchange services under Portuguese law. As of 2026, Portugal operates under the European Union's Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) framework, which fully harmonises crypto-asset service provider (CASP) licensing across member states. This means the Portuguese license is a MiCA passportable license, allowing you to serve clients across the entire EU/EEA from a single authorisation.

The primary regulator is the Banco de Portugal (BdP), which also supervises payment institutions and e-money institutions. The license type is a crypto payment institution license, distinct from a pure VASP registration under the old regime. Minimum capital requirements are set at EUR 125,000 for the core service of crypto-fiat exchange and custody, with higher tiers for more complex services. Corporate income tax is a flat 21% (reduced to 17% for the first EUR 50,000 of taxable income for small and medium enterprises).

What Is the Crypto Payment Institution License Portugal?

The crypto payment institution license Portugal is a regulatory authorisation granted by the Banco de Portugal under the Payment Services Law (transposing the EU Payment Services Directive 2, PSD2) and the new MiCA regulation. It allows a company to provide payment services involving crypto-assets, such as:

  • Execution of payment transactions where crypto-assets are used as a medium of exchange.
  • Issuance of electronic money backed by fiat currency.
  • Conversion services between fiat and crypto-assets (crypto-fiat exchange).
  • Safekeeping and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients.

This license is a passportable MiCA license, meaning once obtained, you can operate across all 27 EU member states and the EEA without needing separate national registrations. The license is distinct from the old “VASP register” which had no minimum capital; as of 2026, all EU crypto service providers must hold a MiCA CASP license with capital tiers of EUR 50,000, 125,000, or 150,000 depending on service classes.

Who Needs This License?

Any business that intends to offer crypto payment services to clients in Portugal or across the EU should apply for the crypto payment institution license Portugal. Typical candidates include:

  • Crypto exchanges that want to offer fiat on-ramps and off-ramps.
  • Payment processors that handle crypto transactions for merchants.
  • E-money issuers that want to integrate crypto wallets.
  • Fintech startups building crypto payment apps or cards.
  • Established payment institutions expanding into crypto services.

If your business model involves holding client funds, executing payments, or providing custodial wallets, this license is mandatory. For pure peer-to-peer platforms or non-custodial services, a lighter registration may suffice, but the crypto payment institution license offers the broadest scope and full passporting rights.

License Type and Regulator

The license is formally a crypto payment institution license under the Portuguese legal framework, regulated by the Banco de Portugal (BdP). The BdP is the central bank and the competent authority for payment services and e-money. For crypto-asset services specifically, the BdP works in coordination with the Portuguese Securities Market Commission (CMVM) for certain aspects, but the primary regulator for payment-related crypto services is the BdP.

The license is issued under the MiCA regulation (Regulation (EU) 2023/1114), which came fully into force in 2026. This replaces the transitional regime that allowed existing VASPs to operate under national law until 1 July 2026. After that date, all CASPs must hold a MiCA license. The crypto payment institution license Portugal is therefore a MiCA license, not a legacy VASP registration.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for a crypto payment institution license Portugal is determined by the type of services offered. For the most common service of crypto-fiat exchange and custody, the capital requirement is EUR 125,000. If you also offer execution of payment transactions or issuance of e-money, the requirement may increase to EUR 150,000. For pure advisory or non-custodial services, a lower tier of EUR 50,000 applies.

This capital must be held in liquid assets (cash or equivalents) and maintained at all times. It serves as a buffer to protect client funds and ensure operational continuity. The capital can be in the form of share capital, reserves, or subordinated loans, subject to regulatory approval. Consulting24 can help you structure the capital injection to meet BdP requirements.

Tax Treatment

Portugal offers a favourable corporate tax regime for crypto businesses. The standard corporate income tax rate is 21%, but for small and medium enterprises (SMEs) with taxable income up to EUR 50,000, the rate is reduced to 17% on that first tranche. Additionally, Portugal does not impose a specific crypto-asset tax; crypto transactions are treated as ordinary business income or capital gains, depending on the activity.

For individuals, Portugal has a flat 28% tax on capital gains from crypto, but for companies, the corporate rate applies. There is no VAT on crypto-to-fiat conversions, and e-money issuance is exempt from VAT. Portugal also has a participation exemption regime that can make dividend distributions tax-efficient. Always consult a local tax advisor for your specific situation.

Allowed Activities

With a crypto payment institution license Portugal, you can conduct the following activities under MiCA:

  • Providing custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients.
  • Operation of a trading platform for crypto-assets.
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for fiat currency.
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for other crypto-assets.
  • Execution of orders for crypto-assets on behalf of clients.
  • Placing of crypto-assets.
  • Receiving and transmitting orders for crypto-assets.
  • Providing advice on crypto-assets.
  • Providing portfolio management of crypto-assets.
  • Providing transfer services for crypto-assets.

Note that issuance of e-money and payment services are also covered if combined with crypto. The license does not cover decentralised finance (DeFi) protocols unless you operate a centralised interface. For pure DeFi, no license may be required, but legal advice is essential.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Company Setup: Incorporate a Portuguese limited liability company (Sociedade por Quotas or Sociedade Anónima) with a registered office in Portugal. The company must have at least two directors (one may be a legal entity).
  2. Document Preparation: Prepare a business plan, compliance manual, AML/CFT policies, risk assessment, governance structure, and financial projections. All documents must be in Portuguese or English with certified translations.
  3. Capital Deposit: Deposit the minimum capital of EUR 125,000 in a Portuguese bank account and obtain a certificate from the bank.
  4. Application Submission: Submit the application to the Banco de Portugal through the official online portal. Pay the application fee.
  5. Regulatory Review: The BdP reviews the application, conducts fit and proper checks on directors and shareholders, and may request additional information. This takes 3-6 months.
  6. On-Site Inspection: The BdP may conduct an on-site inspection of your premises and systems.
  7. License Grant: If approved, the BdP issues the license. You must then notify the BdP of any subsequent changes.
  8. Ongoing Compliance: Submit regular reports (AML, financial, operational) and pay annual supervisory fees.

Banking and Payment Services

Opening a bank account in Portugal for a crypto payment institution can be challenging due to the perceived risk. However, several banks in Portugal accept crypto-related businesses, especially those with a MiCA license. Options include:

  • Banco Português de Investimento (BPI) - Accepts licensed crypto firms but requires a minimum deposit and a relationship manager.
  • Millennium BCP - Has a dedicated fintech desk for regulated entities.
  • Novo Banco - More restrictive but possible with a strong compliance record.
  • Neobanks - Platforms like Revolut Business or N26 may offer accounts, but they often have lower transaction limits.

For payment processing, you can integrate with SEPA (Single Euro Payments Area) for fiat transfers. For crypto payments, you can use third-party processors like Coinbase Commerce or BitPay, but ensure they comply with your license scope. Compliance and Trust

Maintaining the license requires ongoing compliance with MiCA and Portuguese AML laws. Key obligations include:

  • AML/CFT Program: Implement risk-based policies, customer due diligence (CDD), transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting.
  • Governance: Appoint a compliance officer, a money laundering reporting officer (MLRO), and ensure board oversight.
  • Capital Maintenance: Keep the minimum capital at all times and notify the BdP if it falls below.
  • Reporting: Submit annual financial statements, quarterly AML reports, and any material changes to the business.
  • Audit: Engage an external auditor approved by the BdP to audit your financials and compliance annually.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Underestimating the Capital Requirement: Many applicants think EUR 50,000 is enough, but for most payment services, EUR 125,000 is required. Check the exact tier for your services.
  • Incomplete Documentation: The BdP requires detailed business plans and compliance manuals. Generic templates are often rejected.
  • Ignoring Fit and Proper Checks: Directors and shareholders must have a clean criminal record and relevant experience. Disclose any issues upfront.
  • Choosing the Wrong Corporate Structure: A simple limited company may not suffice if you have complex ownership or multiple shareholders.
  • Not Planning for Banking: Secure a bank account before applying, as the BdP may ask for proof of a banking relationship.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a crypto payment institution license and a VASP registration in Portugal?

The crypto payment institution license is a MiCA license with full passporting rights and higher capital requirements (EUR 125,000). The old VASP registration was a simple notification regime with no minimum capital, but it is no longer available after 1 July 2026. All new applicants must apply for the MiCA license.

Can I apply for the license with a company already registered in another EU country?

Yes, but you must establish a branch or subsidiary in Portugal with a registered office and local management. The license is issued to the Portuguese entity, which then can passport services to other EU states. Consulting24 can assist with the company setup.

How long does the license remain valid?

The license is valid indefinitely, subject to ongoing compliance. You must renew your registration annually by paying the supervisory fee and submitting reports. If you fail to comply, the BdP may suspend or revoke the license.

What are the ongoing costs after obtaining the license?

Annual supervisory fees to the BdP (EUR 1,000-3,000), compliance officer salary (EUR 30,000-60,000 per year), external audit costs (EUR 5,000-10,000), and AML software subscriptions. Total ongoing costs can range from EUR 50,000 to 100,000 per year for a small operation.

Is it possible to offer services in other EU countries without additional licenses?

Yes, the MiCA license allows passporting. You simply notify the BdP of your intention to operate in another member state, and the BdP informs the host regulator. You do not need a separate license in each country.

What happens if my application is rejected?

You can appeal the decision within 30 days. Common reasons for rejection include incomplete documentation, unfit directors, insufficient capital, or a weak business plan. Consulting24 helps you prepare a strong application to minimise the risk of rejection.

Can I use the license to issue a stablecoin?

Issuing a stablecoin (e-money token) requires an e-money institution license, which is a separate authorisation under PSD2. However, the crypto payment institution license allows you to offer custody and exchange of stablecoins issued by others. For issuance, you need additional capital and compliance.

Does Portugal require a physical office for the license?

Yes, you must have a registered office in Portugal. The BdP may require a physical presence with staff (compliance, management). Virtual offices are generally not accepted. Consulting24 can help you find suitable office space.

How does the Portuguese license compare to the Lithuanian one?

Both are MiCA licenses with similar capital requirements (EUR 125,000). Lithuania has a faster timeline (3-6 months) and lower professional fees, but Portugal offers a lower corporate tax rate (21% vs 15% in Lithuania, but Lithuania has a 0% rate on retained profits). Choose based on your tax strategy and operational needs.
