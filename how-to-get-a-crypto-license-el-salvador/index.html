What Is the El Salvador Crypto License?

The El Salvador crypto license is a registration for Digital Asset Service Providers (DASPs). It is required for any company offering services such as crypto exchange, custody, payment processing, or token issuance within or from El Salvador. The regime was established under the Digital Assets Issuance Law (Ley de Emisión de Activos Digitales) enacted in 2023 and fully operational by 2026.

The license is issued by the National Commission for Digital Assets (CNAD), an independent agency under the Central Reserve Bank. Unlike some jurisdictions, El Salvador does not have a separate sandbox; the DASP registration is the standard operating license. The law provides legal certainty for digital asset activities, including the recognition of Bitcoin as legal tender and the ability to issue tokens through a regulated process.

Key features include a clear legal framework, a pro-innovation stance, and a requirement for physical presence in the country, including a registered office and a local compliance officer. The CNAD maintains a public register of all licensed DASPs, which enhances transparency and trust. As of 2026, the regulator has licensed over 50 entities, indicating an active and growing ecosystem.

Who Needs a El Salvador Crypto License?

Any business that provides digital asset services to residents of El Salvador or operates from Salvadoran territory must obtain the DASP registration. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralized and decentralized)
  • Wallet providers (custodial and non-custodial)
  • Payment processors handling crypto
  • Token issuers (including STOs and utility tokens)
  • Brokers and dealers in digital assets
  • Custodians and asset managers

Foreign companies targeting the Salvadoran market also need to register. For example, a European exchange offering services to Salvadoran users without a local license would be in violation. Even non-custodial wallet providers may need to register if they facilitate transactions. License Type & Regulator

The license is officially called the Digital Asset Service Provider (DASP) Registration. It is regulated by the National Commission for Digital Assets (CNAD), which operates under the Central Reserve Bank of El Salvador (BCR). The CNAD was established specifically to oversee digital assets and has a dedicated team for licensing and supervision.

The CNAD is responsible for licensing, supervision, and enforcement. It publishes a public register of all licensed DASPs, which is updated quarterly. The regulator has the authority to impose fines up to $500,000, suspend licenses, or revoke them for non-compliance. It also conducts on-site inspections and can request additional information at any time.

There is no separate license for different activities; the DASP registration covers all permitted services, but the capital requirement varies by the scope of operations. For instance, a simple payment processor may need the base $100,000, while a full exchange with custody may require up to $250,000. The CNAD assesses each application on its merits and may impose conditions.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a DASP registration in El Salvador is $100,000. This must be paid up in cash and held in a Salvadoran bank account. For companies offering exchange services with high trading volumes, the regulator may require up to $250,000 depending on risk assessment. The capital serves as a buffer to cover operational risks and must be maintained at all times.

It cannot be withdrawn without prior approval from CNAD. The capital can be used for operational expenses, but the regulator expects it to remain as a reserve. Tax Treatment

El Salvador offers a favorable tax regime for crypto businesses:

  • Corporate income tax: 30% on net profits (standard rate)
  • Capital gains tax: 0% on gains from Bitcoin and other digital assets (as per the Bitcoin Law)
  • VAT: 13% on services, but crypto-to-crypto trades may be exempt
  • Withholding tax: 10% on dividends paid to non-residents (may be reduced under tax treaties)

There is no specific crypto tax law beyond the general framework. The lack of capital gains tax on crypto is a major advantage, especially for trading and investment firms. However, companies must file regular tax returns and maintain proper accounting records. For example, if a company earns revenue from trading fees, it pays 30% on net profit, but any gains from holding Bitcoin are tax-free. Consulting24 recommends working with a local tax advisor to optimize your structure and ensure compliance with transfer pricing rules if you have related parties.

Allowed Activities

Under the DASP registration, the following activities are permitted:

  • Exchange of digital assets for fiat or other digital assets
  • Custody and wallet services
  • Payment processing using digital assets
  • Token issuance (including security tokens, subject to securities law)
  • Brokerage and dealing
  • Portfolio management and advisory services

Activities not allowed include operating a crypto ATM network without a separate license, and offering derivatives or margin trading without a banking license. The CNAD may impose additional conditions on a case-by-case basis, such as restricting the types of tokens that can be listed. For example, privacy coins may be prohibited. Banking & Payments

Opening a bank account in El Salvador for a crypto company can be challenging but is possible. Major banks like Banco Agrícola, Banco Cuscatlán, and Banco Davivienda accept crypto businesses, provided they have the DASP registration. Requirements include the license certificate, company documents, and a local director. Some banks require a minimum deposit of $10,000 to $25,000 to open an account.

El Salvador uses the US dollar as its official currency. Bitcoin is also legal tender, and businesses can accept Bitcoin payments without triggering a taxable event. The government provides a Bitcoin wallet (Chivo) for citizens, but businesses can use any wallet. For international payments, SWIFT transfers are standard. Compliance & Trust

Compliance is critical for maintaining your DASP registration. The CNAD requires:

  • AML/CFT policies aligned with FATF recommendations, including customer due diligence and transaction monitoring
  • KYC procedures for all customers, with enhanced due diligence for high-risk clients
  • Suspicious activity reporting to the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) within 30 days
  • Annual audits by an approved auditor, with results submitted to CNAD
  • Submission of quarterly financial reports and semi-annual compliance reports

Failure to comply can result in fines up to $500,000 or license revocation. The CNAD conducts periodic inspections and may request on-site visits. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing business plans or AML policies cause delays. Ensure all documents are comprehensive and translated into Spanish if required.
  • Underestimating capital: The $100,000 minimum must be fully paid up; using loans is not allowed. The regulator may request proof of source of funds.
  • Ignoring local presence: You need a registered office and a local compliance officer; virtual offices may not be accepted. We can help you find a physical office and recruit a local officer.
  • Poor banking relationships: Some banks reject crypto firms; start the account opening process early. We have a network of banking partners.
  • Assuming no tax: While capital gains on crypto are zero, corporate income tax at 30% still applies. Plan for tax filings from day one.
  • Not preparing for background checks: Directors and beneficial owners must pass background checks. Disclose any past regulatory issues upfront.
