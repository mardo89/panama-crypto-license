What Is a Dubai Crypto License and a Cayman Islands Crypto License?

A Dubai crypto license is issued by the Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA), which regulates virtual asset service providers operating in or from the Emirate of Dubai (excluding the Dubai International Financial Centre). VARA categorizes licenses into different service types, including exchange, custody, advisory, and more. The regime is mandatory for any entity offering virtual asset services in Dubai. As of 2026, VARA has a detailed rulebook covering market conduct, custody standards, and token listing requirements, making it one of the most prescriptive regimes globally.

A Cayman Islands crypto license falls under the Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) regime administered by the Cayman Islands Monetary Authority (CIMA). The Cayman Islands has a more flexible, risk-based approach, with lighter initial requirements but ongoing compliance obligations. Both jurisdictions require a local presence, but the Cayman Islands is often chosen for its tax-neutral environment and established financial services infrastructure. CIMA's approach is principles-based, meaning it focuses on outcomes rather than prescriptive rules, which can be advantageous for innovative business models.

For founders seeking a simpler and lower-cost alternative, Panama provides a foundation-based structure at EUR 6,000 flat, without a dedicated crypto license regime. This can be suitable for certain business models, though it lacks the regulatory clarity of Dubai or Cayman.

Who Needs These Licenses?

Any business providing virtual asset services to clients in Dubai or the Cayman Islands needs the respective license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralized and decentralized if offering services to residents)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • OTC trading desks
  • Payment processors using virtual assets
  • Advisory and portfolio management services for virtual assets
  • Token issuers (if offering secondary market services)

Founders targeting the Middle East and North Africa (MENA) region often prefer Dubai, while those seeking a neutral offshore hub for global operations may choose the Cayman Islands. License Type and Regulator

Dubai: Regulated by the Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA). License types include:

  • Category 1: Exchange services (spot, margin, derivatives with approval)
  • Category 2: Custody and transfer services
  • Category 3: Advisory and management services
  • Category 4: Full suite (multiple activities)

Each category has specific capital and operational requirements. VARA also issues a 'sandbox' license for innovative projects.

Cayman Islands: Regulated by the Cayman Islands Monetary Authority (CIMA). The VASP regime has two tiers:

  • Class A: For entities providing VASP services to the public (full license)
  • Class B: For entities providing services to professional investors only (restricted license)

Both regulators require AML/CFT compliance, but VARA is more prescriptive with detailed rulebooks, while CIMA follows a principles-based approach. CIMA also requires a local physical office and at least one local director.

Capital Requirement

Dubai: VARA requires a minimum capital of AED 500,000 (approx EUR 125,000) for most license categories. Higher capital may be required for full-suite licenses or if offering custody services. For example, a Category 4 license may require AED 1,000,000 (approx EUR 250,000).

Cayman Islands: CIMA does not prescribe a fixed minimum capital for VASPs. Instead, it requires “adequate financial resources” proportionate to the nature and scale of the business. In practice, this often means a minimum of CI$ 100,000 - 200,000 (approx EUR 110,000 - 220,000) for Class A licenses, but it is assessed on a case-by-case basis. Class B licenses may have lower requirements.

Both jurisdictions require the capital to be maintained in liquid assets. Consulting24 can help you structure the capital efficiently. For a lower capital option, consider Lithuania under MiCA, where minimum capital starts at EUR 50,000 for certain services.

Tax Treatment

Dubai: No corporate income tax for most businesses (0% corporate tax rate as of 2026, with a 9% rate for taxable profits exceeding AED 375,000). No VAT on virtual asset services (though standard VAT applies to other goods/services). No capital gains tax. However, businesses must register for VAT if their taxable supplies exceed AED 375,000.

Cayman Islands: No corporate income tax, no capital gains tax, no VAT, and no withholding taxes. The Cayman Islands is a tax-neutral jurisdiction, meaning entities can operate without direct taxation. However, entities must pay annual registration fees and comply with economic substance requirements if they are not pure offshore. For example, a VASP with local employees and office would need to demonstrate economic substance.

Both jurisdictions offer significant tax advantages. For a tax-neutral alternative with a flat fee, explore Panama.

Allowed Activities

Dubai (VARA): Allowed activities include:

  • Exchange services (spot, margin, derivatives with approval)
  • Custody and transfer
  • Advisory and portfolio management
  • Token issuance (subject to additional approval)
  • Staking and lending (restricted)

VARA prohibits certain activities like algorithmic trading without approval and requires token listing approval.

Cayman Islands (CIMA): Allowed activities include:

  • Exchange of virtual assets for fiat or other virtual assets
  • Transfer of virtual assets
  • Custody and administration
  • Participation in ICOs/STOs (as issuer or advisor)
  • DeFi protocols (if not offering services to retail investors)

Both jurisdictions prohibit unlicensed activities and require AML/CFT compliance. Dubai is more restrictive on derivatives and lending, while Cayman offers more flexibility for institutional-focused services. For a less restrictive environment, Panama may be suitable, though it lacks a specific license.

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Pre-application: Business plan, AML/CFT policies, and corporate structure. Consulting24 helps prepare documentation, including risk assessments and governance frameworks.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a local entity (e.g., in Dubai Mainland or Cayman Islands). This involves selecting a local sponsor (Dubai) or registered office (Cayman).
  3. Submit application: File with VARA or CIMA, including fit-and-proper checks for directors and shareholders. Background checks are thorough; expect to provide police certificates and financial references.
  4. Regulatory review: Regulator assesses application, may request additional information or clarifications. In Dubai, VARA may conduct an on-site inspection.
  5. Approval and licensing: Once approved, pay license fee and receive license. Conditions may apply, such as reporting requirements.
  6. Post-licensing: Ongoing compliance, reporting, and audits. Both jurisdictions require annual AML audits and periodic regulatory returns.

Timelines vary: Dubai 4-8 months, Cayman 3-6 months. Consulting24 coordinates the entire process, liaising with local agents and regulators. For a faster and simpler process, consider Panama (2-4 weeks) or Lithuania (3-6 months under MiCA).

Banking and Payments

Opening a bank account is a common challenge for crypto businesses. In Dubai, several banks accept VARA-licensed entities, including Emirates NBD, Mashreq, and some international banks. However, due diligence is strict, and a local presence (office, director) is required. Banks may also require a minimum deposit and proof of licensed status.

In the Cayman Islands, banking is more straightforward for licensed VASPs, with local banks like Butterfield and Cayman National Bank offering services. The Cayman Islands also has a strong network of international banks with crypto-friendly policies. However, expect thorough KYC and source-of-funds checks.

Consulting24 can introduce you to banking partners in both jurisdictions. For alternative payment solutions, consider Panama or Lithuania, where banking is often easier for crypto firms. In Panama, banks are generally more open to crypto-related businesses if properly structured.

Compliance and Trust

Both jurisdictions require strong AML/CFT programs, including customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and reporting. Dubai’s VARA has detailed rulebooks and conducts regular inspections. Cayman’s CIMA follows FATF recommendations and expects equivalent standards. Non-compliance can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license.

Compliance costs are ongoing: expect EUR 20,000 - 50,000 annually for AML officer, audit, and legal fees. This includes hiring a local AML officer (often required to be resident), conducting annual audits, and filing regulatory returns. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Underestimating timeline: Both jurisdictions take months; plan accordingly. Start the process early and have backup plans.
  • Incomplete documentation: Missing AML policies or business plans cause delays. Use templates but tailor them to your business.
  • Ignoring economic substance: In Cayman, you need local directors and physical presence. In Dubai, you need a local office and possibly a local service agent.
  • Choosing based on cost alone: Consider tax, market access, and regulatory burden. A cheaper license may not suit your business model.
  • Not engaging local experts: Regulators prefer dealing with established local representatives. Consulting24 provides that local coordination.
  • Overlooking ongoing costs: Annual fees, compliance, and audit costs can add up. Budget for EUR 30,000+ per year.

Frequently asked questions

What is the main difference between a Dubai and Cayman Islands crypto license?

Dubai's VARA regime is prescriptive with detailed rulebooks, while Cayman's CIMA is principles-based and flexible. Dubai requires higher minimum capital (AED 500,000) and has a more structured licensing process. Cayman offers tax neutrality and lower initial costs but requires economic substance. The choice depends on your target market and business model.

How much does a Dubai crypto license cost in total?

Total initial setup costs for a Dubai crypto license range from EUR 50,000 to 100,000, including application fees, legal fees, and advisory services. Annual license fees are EUR 10,000-25,000. Exact costs depend on the license category and complexity. Consulting24 provides a detailed breakdown during consultation.

Is there a minimum capital requirement for a Cayman Islands VASP license?

CIMA does not prescribe a fixed minimum capital but requires 'adequate financial resources' proportionate to the business. In practice, Class A licenses often require CI$ 100,000-200,000 (approx EUR 110,000-220,000). Class B licenses may have lower requirements. The amount is assessed case-by-case.

Can I use a Dubai crypto license to serve clients in other countries?

A Dubai VARA license allows you to serve clients in Dubai and the UAE. For clients outside, you may need additional licenses or rely on reverse solicitation. VARA expects you to have appropriate controls for cross-border services. Consult a lawyer for specific cross-border implications.
