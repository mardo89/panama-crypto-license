What is the Crypto Token Issuance License in South Korea?

South Korea does not have a single "crypto token issuance license." Instead, token issuers must register as a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) with KoFIU under the Act on Reporting and Use of Specific Financial Transaction Information. This registration applies to businesses that exchange, transfer, issue, or manage virtual assets. For token issuance specifically, the issuer must also comply with the Electronic Financial Transactions Act and the Capital Markets Act if the token qualifies as a security token.

The VASP registration is mandatory for any entity that issues tokens to Korean residents, whether through an initial exchange offering (IEO) or direct sale. The FSC oversees compliance, and failure to register can result in criminal penalties including imprisonment for up to five years. As of 2026, the regulatory environment is fully mature, with clear guidelines on anti-money laundering (AML), customer due diligence, and reporting obligations. The FSC has also issued specific guidance on token classification, distinguishing utility tokens from security tokens based on the Howey test adapted for Korean law.

For example, a token that grants voting rights or profit-sharing is likely a security token, requiring dual registration under both VASP and FSCMA. Consulting24 can help you classify your token and determine the exact licensing pathway.

Who Needs a South Korean Crypto Token Issuance License?

Any company or individual that issues tokens to South Korean residents or conducts token sales targeting the Korean market needs a VASP registration. This includes:

  • Blockchain startups launching utility tokens or governance tokens
  • Security token offerings (STOs) under the Capital Markets Act
  • Foreign entities that market token sales to Korean investors
  • Decentralized finance (DeFi) protocols issuing native tokens
  • Non-fungible token (NFT) platforms if the NFTs have investment characteristics

The FSC has extraterritorial reach, so any marketing or sales activity directed at Korea triggers the requirement. For instance, if your website is in Korean or you accept Korean won, you likely need registration. Even airdrops to Korean wallets can be considered issuance. However, if your project uses a whitelist to exclude Korean residents, you may avoid the requirement. License Type and Regulator

The primary regulator for crypto token issuance in South Korea is the Financial Services Commission (FSC), with operational oversight by the Korea Financial Intelligence Unit (KoFIU). The relevant license is the Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) registration. Additionally, if the token is classified as a security, the issuer must also comply with the Financial Investment Services and Capital Markets Act (FSCMA) and may need a separate license from the FSC.

For utility tokens, VASP registration suffices. For security tokens, the issuer must also register as a financial investment business entity, which has higher capital requirements (minimum KRW 3 billion, approximately EUR 2.1 million) and stricter governance rules. The FSC has published guidelines for security token offerings (STOs) that came into full effect in 2024. As of 2026, the dual regulatory track is well established, with a clear distinction between utility and security tokens based on the Token Classification Guide issued by the FSC in 2025.

Capital Requirement

South Korea imposes a minimum capital requirement for VASP registration. As of 2026, the requirement is KRW 1 billion (approximately EUR 700,000) for crypto exchanges. For token issuers that do not operate an exchange, the capital requirement is typically lower, around KRW 500 million (EUR 350,000). However, if the issuer also holds customer assets or provides custody services, the higher threshold applies. These figures are subject to change, and exact requirements depend on the business model.

For security token issuers under FSCMA, the minimum capital is KRW 3 billion (EUR 2.1 million) for full brokerage or dealing licenses. The capital must be deposited in a Korean bank and maintained throughout the license period. Consulting24 can provide a tailored assessment of your capital needs and help you structure your funding.

Tax Treatment

South Korea taxes virtual asset income as miscellaneous income. As of 2026, a 20% tax (plus 2% local income tax) applies to gains from virtual asset transactions exceeding KRW 2.5 million (approximately EUR 1,750) per year. For token issuers, the sale of tokens is treated as revenue and subject to corporate income tax (9-24% for corporations). Additionally, value-added tax (VAT) at 10% may apply to certain services, such as transaction fees charged by the issuer.

For example, if your company issues tokens and sells them for KRW 100 million, the profit is taxed at the corporate rate. Investors who later sell the tokens on exchanges pay capital gains tax. The tax regime is evolving, and token issuers should engage a local tax advisor. Consulting24 can recommend tax experts familiar with crypto taxation.

Allowed Activities

With a VASP registration, a token issuer can:

  • Issue utility tokens to Korean residents
  • Conduct initial exchange offerings (IEOs) through registered exchanges
  • Operate a token sale platform (subject to additional approval)
  • Provide custody services for the issued tokens
  • Market and advertise token sales within Korea

Security token issuers can also conduct STOs but must comply with the FSCMA, including disclosure requirements and investor protection rules. Activities not allowed include operating an unregistered exchange or issuing tokens that are deemed illegal securities. The FSC maintains a whitelist of approved tokens for trading on Korean exchanges, and issuers must apply for listing. Step-by-Step Application Process

The process for obtaining a crypto token issuance license in South Korea involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application assessment: Determine if your token is a utility or security token. Engage a local legal advisor.
  2. Company setup: Incorporate a South Korean entity with a physical office in Korea. Minimum capital must be deposited in a Korean bank.
  3. AML/CFT system implementation: Install transaction monitoring, customer due diligence, and reporting systems. Obtain a real-name bank account for customer deposits.
  4. Document preparation: Prepare business plan, AML policy, risk management framework, and financial statements.
  5. Submit VASP application: File with KoFIU via the FSC's electronic system. Pay the application fee.
  6. Review and inspection: KoFIU reviews the application and may conduct an on-site inspection. This takes 3-6 months.
  7. Approval and registration: If approved, you receive a VASP registration certificate. You must then report to the FSC annually.

Banking and Payment Services

One of the biggest challenges for token issuers in South Korea is obtaining a real-name bank account. Korean banks require VASP registration before opening an account, but registration requires a bank account. This chicken-and-egg problem is often solved by using a local partner bank or a virtual asset-friendly bank. As of 2026, major banks like Kookmin, Shinhan, and Woori offer accounts to registered VASPs, but they perform rigorous due diligence.

For token issuers without an exchange, some fintech firms provide payment processing solutions, such as virtual accounts or escrow services. Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a VASP registration and a security token license in South Korea?

VASP registration is for utility token issuers and covers exchange, transfer, and issuance of virtual assets. A security token license under FSCMA is required if the token is classified as a security, involving higher capital (KRW 3 billion) and stricter disclosure rules. The FSC classifies tokens based on economic function and investor expectations.

Can a foreign company apply for a South Korean crypto token issuance license?

Yes, but the company must incorporate a South Korean subsidiary with a physical office and meet all capital and AML requirements. Foreign entities cannot directly register; they need a local legal entity. Consulting24 can advise on the incorporation process.

How long does it take to get a VASP registration for token issuance?

Typically 6 to 12 months from application to approval, depending on document completeness and regulator workload. For security tokens, it can take 12 to 18 months. Pre-application preparation (company setup, AML systems) adds 2 to 4 months.

What are the tax implications for token issuers in South Korea?

Token sales are treated as revenue and taxed at corporate income tax rates (9-24%). Investors pay 20% capital gains tax on profits exceeding KRW 2.5 million annually. VAT at 10% may apply to services. Consulting24 recommends engaging a local tax advisor.

Is it possible to issue tokens without a license if I exclude Korean residents?

Yes, if you implement effective geoblocking and do not market to Korea, you may avoid the licensing requirement. However, the FSC has extraterritorial reach, so any accidental exposure can lead to penalties. Consulting24 can help assess your risk.

What happens if I operate without a VASP registration in South Korea?

Operating without registration is a criminal offense punishable by imprisonment up to five years and fines up to KRW 50 million. The FSC actively monitors and prosecutes unregistered activities. Compliance is essential.

Can I use a Panama company to issue tokens to Korean residents?

No, if you target Korean residents, you must register in South Korea regardless of your company's jurisdiction. Panama is suitable for projects that do not target Korea. Consulting24 can help you set up in Panama for other markets.

What are the ongoing compliance costs for a South Korean VASP?

Annual costs include AML audit fees (EUR 5,000-15,000), legal retainer (EUR 10,000-20,000), and reporting system maintenance (EUR 2,000-5,000). Total ongoing costs are typically EUR 20,000-40,000 per year.

Does South Korea recognize MiCA licenses from EU member states?

No, South Korea does not have mutual recognition with MiCA. A separate VASP registration is required for any activity targeting Korean residents. However, a MiCA license (e.g., from Lithuania) can facilitate operations in the EU. Consulting24 advises on multi-jurisdiction strategies.
