For founders seeking a straightforward entry point into the Eurasian market without the heavy capital requirements of EU MiCA regimes, the Georgia crypto license is a compelling option.

What Is the Georgia Crypto License?

The Georgia crypto license is a mandatory registration for Virtual Asset Service Providers (VASPs) operating in Georgia. Established by the National Bank of Georgia (NBG) in 2023, the regime requires any business offering crypto exchange, wallet custody, or transfer services to register with the NBG. Unlike the older EU VASP registers that lacked minimum capital, Georgia's regime is modern but light-touch: it does not impose a high capital barrier, making it one of the most accessible licenses in the region.

The license is not a full authorisation like a banking licence; it is a registration that confirms your business meets anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) standards. Once registered, you can legally offer crypto services to Georgian residents and beyond, subject to the permitted activities list. The NBG also supervises ongoing compliance, including regular reporting and audits.

For international founders, Georgia offers a stable legal environment, a growing fintech ecosystem, and access to free industrial zones where corporate taxes can be as low as 0% on certain activities. This makes the Georgia crypto license a strategic choice for startups and established firms alike.

Who Needs a Georgia Crypto License?

Any legal entity that provides virtual asset services in or from Georgia must register with the NBG. Specifically, the following businesses need a Georgia crypto license:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralised or peer-to-peer platforms)
  • Wallet providers (custodial and non-custodial if they hold private keys)
  • Virtual asset transfer services (including OTC desks)
  • Token issuers and ICO platforms (if acting as an intermediary)
  • ATMs and kiosks that exchange crypto for fiat

If your business is incorporated in Georgia or has a physical presence there, you fall under NBG jurisdiction. Even if your target market is international, operating from Georgia requires registration. The NBG also expects foreign VASPs that actively solicit Georgian clients to register, though enforcement varies.

Founders who want a low-cost, fast license to test the Eurasian market often choose Georgia. It is also ideal for companies that do not need the high capital requirements of EU licenses but still want a regulated status for bank relationships and partner trust.

License Type and Regulator

The Georgia crypto license is a single VASP registration regime administered by the National Bank of Georgia (NBG). There is no tiered licensing system like in some EU countries; instead, the NBG assesses each applicant's business model and risk profile. The registration covers multiple service types under one umbrella, though you must specify which activities you intend to perform.

The NBG is the central bank and financial regulator of Georgia. It oversees banking, insurance, and capital markets, and since 2023, virtual asset services. The regulator is known for its pragmatic approach, focusing on AML/CTF compliance rather than imposing prescriptive capital or governance rules. This makes the Georgia crypto license relatively straightforward to obtain if you have solid compliance procedures.

Capital Requirement

Georgia does not impose a minimum capital requirement for VASP registration. This is a key difference from the EU MiCA regime, which requires EUR 50,000 to 150,000 depending on service class, and from many other jurisdictions that demand substantial paid-up capital. The NBG focuses on operational capability and AML compliance rather than financial buffers.

However, you must demonstrate that your company has sufficient funds to operate responsibly. This typically means showing a bank account with enough balance to cover at least six months of operating expenses. The NBG may ask for proof of funds, but there is no fixed number. For most applicants, a modest capital of EUR 10,000-20,000 is sufficient.

If you are comparing Georgia to Panama, note that Panama also has no minimum capital, but its company setup is a flat EUR 6,000. Georgia's overall cost is similar or lower, but you must factor in advisory fees.

Tax Treatment

Georgia offers a highly favourable tax regime for crypto businesses. Corporate income tax is 15% on distributed profits only - meaning if you reinvest profits, you pay 0% tax until distribution. This is a major advantage over most EU countries where corporate tax applies annually on all profits.

Additionally, Georgia has free industrial zones (FIZ) where companies can enjoy 0% corporate tax, 0% VAT, and 0% customs duties on exports. While not all crypto activities qualify for FIZ status, some token issuers and blockchain service providers have successfully established operations there. The standard VAT rate is 18%, but crypto-to-crypto transactions are generally exempt.

Personal income tax on dividends is 5% (if distributed from retained earnings). There is no capital gains tax on crypto held as an investment for individuals, though businesses must account for gains as ordinary income. Allowed Activities

Under the NBG regime, registered VASPs can perform the following activities:

  • Exchange between virtual assets and fiat currencies
  • Exchange between one or more forms of virtual assets
  • Transfer of virtual assets (on behalf of clients)
  • Custody and administration of virtual assets (holding private keys)
  • Participation in and provision of financial services related to an issuer's offer and sale of virtual assets

The NBG does not currently license crypto derivatives or margin trading; those may fall under securities regulation. If your business involves token issuance (STO), you may need additional approval from the Georgian Securities Commission. Most standard exchange and wallet services are covered.

Compared to Lithuania, which allows similar activities but under a more detailed AML framework, Georgia's list is broad but less prescriptive. Always confirm your specific activities with the NBG during the application.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Applying for a Georgia crypto license involves several stages. Consulting24 coordinates each step with local legal partners.

  1. Pre-consultation: We assess your business model and determine eligibility. You provide details on services, target market, and ownership structure.
  2. Company incorporation: If you do not have a Georgian entity, we help set up a limited liability company (LLC) with a registered address. This takes 1-2 weeks.
  3. Documentation: Prepare AML/KYC policies, a business plan, risk assessment, and internal controls. These must be in Georgian or English with a certified translation.
  4. Submission: Submit the application to the NBG along with the registration fee. The NBG reviews within 30-60 days, but may request additional information.
  5. Interview (possible): The NBG may invite key personnel for a meeting to discuss the business model and compliance measures.
  6. Registration: Once approved, you receive a VASP registration certificate. You must then open a bank account and begin operations within 6 months.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Once registered, VASPs must maintain ongoing compliance with NBG requirements. This includes:

  • Annual AML/CTF audit by an independent auditor
  • Transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting
  • Regular reporting to the NBG on volumes, client numbers, and risk indicators
  • Maintenance of records for at least 5 years
  • Appointment of a compliance officer resident in Georgia (or accessible)

The NBG conducts periodic inspections and may request additional information. Non-compliance can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of registration. Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make errors that delay or derail their Georgia crypto license application. Here are the most common pitfalls:

  • Incomplete documentation: Failing to provide a comprehensive AML policy or business plan. The NBG expects detailed procedures.
  • Ignoring local language requirements: Some documents must be in Georgian or accompanied by a certified translation.
  • Underestimating timeline: While the process is fast, delays can occur if the NBG requests clarifications. Build in buffer time.
  • Not having a local presence: The NBG prefers applicants with a physical office or at least a registered agent in Georgia.
  • Overlooking ongoing costs: Annual audit and compliance fees can add EUR 2,000-5,000 per year.

Frequently asked questions

What is the Georgia crypto license?

The Georgia crypto license is a mandatory VASP registration with the National Bank of Georgia. It allows businesses to legally offer crypto exchange, wallet, and transfer services. The regime is light-touch, with no minimum capital, and processing takes 1-3 months.

Who needs to register as a VASP in Georgia?

Any legal entity providing virtual asset services in or from Georgia must register. This includes exchanges, wallet providers, OTC desks, and crypto ATMs. Even if your target market is international, operating from Georgia requires NBG registration.

What is the cost of a Georgia crypto license?

Total costs typically range from EUR 5,000 to 11,500, including government fees, company incorporation, AML documentation, and advisory fees. The government registration fee is around EUR 500-1,000. Exact figures depend on your business model.

How long does it take to get a Georgia crypto license?

The process usually takes 1-3 months from application to approval. This includes company incorporation (1-2 weeks), documentation (2-4 weeks), and NBG review (30-60 days). Delays can occur if additional information is requested.

Is there a minimum capital requirement?

No, Georgia does not impose a minimum capital requirement for VASP registration. However, you must demonstrate sufficient funds to operate responsibly, typically EUR 10,000-20,000 in the bank account.

What taxes apply to crypto businesses in Georgia?

Corporate income tax is 15% on distributed profits only. If you reinvest profits, you pay 0% tax until distribution. Free industrial zones offer 0% corporate tax for qualifying activities. VAT is 18% but crypto-to-crypto transactions are generally exempt.

What activities are allowed under the Georgia crypto license?

Registered VASPs can exchange crypto for fiat, exchange one crypto for another, transfer virtual assets, provide custody services, and facilitate token offerings. Derivatives and margin trading are not covered and may require separate securities regulation.

Can I open a bank account for my crypto business in Georgia?

Yes, several Georgian banks accept VASP clients after NBG registration, including TBC Bank and Bank of Georgia. Having the registration significantly improves your chances of account approval.

How does Georgia compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Panama offers a faster (2-4 weeks) and cheaper (EUR 6,000 flat) company setup with no dedicated crypto regulator. Georgia provides a regulated status under the NBG, which may be preferred for bank relationships and partner trust, at a similar cost.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements after registration?

VASPs must conduct annual AML audits, monitor transactions, report to the NBG, and maintain records for 5 years. A compliance officer should be appointed. Non-compliance can lead to fines or revocation.

Can I use the Georgia license to serve EU clients?

No, the Georgia license does not provide EU passporting. To serve EU clients, you need a MiCA-compliant license from an EU member state. Georgia is best for targeting Eurasian markets or as a cost-effective base for non-EU operations.
