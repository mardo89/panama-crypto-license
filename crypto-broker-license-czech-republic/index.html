What Is a Crypto Broker License in the Czech Republic?

A crypto broker license in the Czech Republic is a regulatory authorization issued by the Czech National Bank (CNB) under the Act on the Digital Economy (effective from 2023, updated for MiCA). It allows companies to provide crypto asset services such as exchange, custody, and brokerage to clients. As of 2026, this license is fully aligned with the EU's Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) regulation, meaning it is a CASP license with harmonized standards across the EU.

Key features of the Czech crypto broker license include:

  • Regulated by the Czech National Bank (CNB)
  • Minimum capital requirement: EUR 125,000 for custody and exchange services (MiCA Tier 2)
  • Full passporting rights across the EU/EEA
  • Corporate income tax rate of 19% (standard), with potential exemptions for long-term holdings
  • Processing timeline: 6-12 months (typical, may vary)

Who Needs a Czech Crypto Broker License?

This license is essential for any business that intends to offer crypto asset services to clients in the Czech Republic or across the EU. Specifically, you need a Czech crypto broker license if you plan to:

  • Operate a crypto exchange (fiat-to-crypto or crypto-to-crypto)
  • Provide crypto custody services for clients
  • Act as a crypto broker matching buyers and sellers
  • Offer crypto payment services or wallet services
  • Provide crypto advisory or portfolio management

The license is also suitable for companies that already hold a license in another EU member state and want to establish a presence in Central Europe, or for non-EU firms seeking EU market access via a Czech entity.

License Type and Regulator

The Czech crypto broker license is a single CASP authorization under MiCA, supervised by the Czech National Bank (CNB). There is no separate 'broker' license; instead, the license covers all crypto asset services as defined in MiCA. The CNB is responsible for licensing, ongoing supervision, and enforcement.

Key regulatory details:

  • Regulator: Czech National Bank (CNB) - cnb.cz
  • Legal basis: Act on the Digital Economy (No. 370/2023 Coll.) as amended for MiCA
  • License type: CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) - single license for all services
  • Passporting: Yes, full EU/EEA passporting available

For comparison, other jurisdictions like Lithuania and Estonia also offer CASP licenses under MiCA, but with different capital thresholds and tax regimes.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for a CASP license depends on the services offered. For the Czech crypto broker license, the capital requirement is EUR 125,000 if you provide both custody and exchange services (Tier 2). If you only offer exchange services without custody, the requirement is EUR 50,000 (Tier 1). For services involving trading platforms or advice, the requirement may be EUR 150,000 (Tier 3).

Capital must be held in a bank account in the Czech Republic or another EU member state and must be freely available. Proof of capital is required at the time of application. The capital can be used for operational expenses, but must always remain above the minimum threshold.

Tax Treatment for Crypto Brokers in the Czech Republic

The Czech Republic offers a relatively favorable tax environment for crypto businesses. Corporate income tax is a flat 19% on profits. Importantly, as of 2025, the Czech Republic introduced an exemption from capital gains tax for crypto assets held for more than 3 years (similar to the holding period for securities). This applies to both individuals and companies.

Other tax considerations:

  • VAT: Crypto exchange services are generally exempt from VAT (subject to conditions)
  • Withholding tax: Dividends paid to non-residents are subject to 15% withholding tax (reduced under tax treaties)
  • Transfer pricing: Related-party transactions must be at arm's length

Tax residency: A company is tax resident if it is incorporated in the Czech Republic or has its place of effective management there. We recommend consulting a local tax advisor for your specific situation.

Allowed Activities Under the Czech Crypto Broker License

The Czech crypto broker license (CASP) permits the following activities under MiCA:

  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets
  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Operation of a trading platform for crypto assets
  • Execution of orders for crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Placement of crypto assets (underwriting or placing)
  • Receiving and transmitting orders for crypto assets
  • Providing advice on crypto assets
  • Portfolio management of crypto assets

Note that the license does not cover services related to non-fungible tokens (NFTs) unless they qualify as crypto assets under MiCA. Stablecoins and asset-referenced tokens are also covered, subject to additional requirements.

Step-by-Step Process to Obtain the License

The application process for a Czech crypto broker license involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step:

  1. Pre-application assessment: We review your business model, identify applicable services, and determine capital requirements.
  2. Company incorporation: Establish a Czech s.r.o. (limited liability company) with a registered address and nominee directors if needed.
  3. Documentation preparation: Draft AML/KYC policies, risk assessment, business plan, organizational structure, and internal controls.
  4. Capital deposit: Open a corporate bank account in the Czech Republic and deposit the required minimum capital.
  5. Application submission: Submit the complete application package to the Czech National Bank, including all supporting documents and the application fee.
  6. CNB review: The CNB reviews the application, may request additional information, and conducts interviews with management.
  7. License issuance: Upon approval, the CNB issues the CASP license, and your company is registered in the public register.
  8. Ongoing compliance: Submit regular reports (AML, financial, transaction monitoring) and maintain capital requirements.

Compliance and Trust Considerations

Operating a crypto broker in the Czech Republic requires strong compliance with AML/CFT regulations. Key obligations include:

  • Customer due diligence (CDD) and enhanced due diligence (EDD) for high-risk clients
  • Transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting to the Financial Analytical Office (FAO)
  • Appointment of a compliance officer and AML officer
  • Regular audits and reporting to the CNB
  • Data protection under GDPR

Common Mistakes When Applying for a Czech Crypto License

Many applicants face delays or rejections due to avoidable errors. Common mistakes include:

  • Lack of local substance: Not having a physical office or local management (the CNB expects substance).

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto broker license in the Czech Republic?

The minimum capital is EUR 125,000 if you provide both custody and exchange services (Tier 2 under MiCA). For exchange-only services, it is EUR 50,000. For trading platforms or advice, it may be EUR 150,000.

How long does it take to get a Czech crypto broker license?

The typical timeline is 6 to 12 months from submission of a complete application. Delays can occur if the CNB requests additional information. Consulting24 coordinates the process to minimize waiting times.

Can I passport my Czech license to other EU countries?

Yes, as a MiCA-compliant CASP license, the Czech crypto broker license allows you to provide services across the entire EU/EEA under the passporting regime, without needing separate licenses.

What is the corporate tax rate for crypto brokers in the Czech Republic?

The standard corporate income tax rate is 19%. Additionally, gains from crypto assets held for more than 3 years may be exempt from tax, subject to conditions.

Is a physical office required for the Czech crypto license?

Yes, the CNB expects a registered office in the Czech Republic and may require a physical presence (e.g., a local director or employee). Substance requirements are part of the application review.

What services can I offer with a Czech crypto broker license?

You can offer exchange (fiat/crypto), custody, trading platform operation, order execution, advisory, portfolio management, and placement of crypto assets, as defined under MiCA.

Can a non-EU company apply for a Czech crypto license?

Yes, non-EU companies can establish a Czech subsidiary (s.r.o.) and apply for the license. There is no citizenship requirement for shareholders or directors, though local substance is needed.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations after licensing?

You must conduct AML/KYC checks, monitor transactions, report suspicious activities to the Financial Analytical Office, submit regular reports to the CNB, and maintain capital requirements.
