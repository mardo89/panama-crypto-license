What Is the Crypto Payment Institution License Romania?

The crypto payment institution license Romania is a regulatory authorization issued by the National Bank of Romania (BNR) under the transposition of MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation). It allows companies to provide crypto-asset services such as exchange between crypto and fiat, custody of crypto assets, and execution of crypto payment transactions. This license is part of the EU's harmonized regime, meaning it can be passporte to other EU member states.

Unlike the old VASP register model, MiCA imposes minimum capital requirements, strict governance, and ongoing reporting. The license is designed for businesses that want to operate as a regulated payment institution with crypto capabilities. It is distinct from a simple VASP registration and offers higher credibility with banks and partners.

For example, a company offering both crypto exchange and custody must hold EUR 125,000 in capital under MiCA. The license also requires a detailed program of operations, AML policies, and a local compliance officer. Romania's implementation aligns with the EU's goal of consumer protection and market integrity.

Who Needs This License?

Any company that intends to offer crypto-asset services in Romania or across the EU on a professional basis must obtain this license. Typical applicants include:

  • Crypto exchanges (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Crypto payment processors
  • Brokers and OTC desks
  • Fintech firms integrating crypto payments

If you are a startup or an established business looking to expand into the EU, this license is your gateway. For those exploring other options, our jurisdictions page compares Romania with Estonia, Lithuania, and Panama. The license is mandatory for any entity that holds client funds or crypto assets, executes orders, or provides transfer services. Even non-custodial services may fall under MiCA if they involve order reception or transmission.

License Type & Regulator

The regulatory authority for the crypto payment institution license Romania is the National Bank of Romania (BNR). The license is classified under MiCA as a CASP (Crypto-Asset Service Provider) with payment institution status. This means the company must comply with both MiCA and the Payment Services Directive (PSD2) where applicable.

The BNR oversees the application process, ongoing supervision, and enforcement. As of 2026, Romania has fully implemented MiCA, so there is no transitional regime. All new applicants must meet the full MiCA requirements, including capital, governance, and AML/CFT obligations. The BNR also coordinates with the European Securities and Markets Authority (ESMA) for cross-border supervision.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for a crypto payment institution depends on the type of services offered. For Romania, the tiers are aligned with the EU regulation:

  • EUR 50,000 for simple exchange services (crypto-to-fiat)
  • EUR 125,000 for custodial wallet services
  • EUR 150,000 for combined services including payment initiation

These funds must be fully paid up and maintained throughout the license period. The capital can be in cash or liquid assets. It is important to note that these are minimums; the BNR may require higher capital based on risk assessment. For example, if your business handles large transaction volumes, the regulator may demand additional capital to cover operational risks. The capital must be deposited in a Romanian bank account and evidenced by a certificate.

Tax Treatment

Romania applies a corporate income tax rate of 16% on profits. Crypto transactions are treated as income from services, subject to standard corporate tax. VAT is not applicable on crypto-to-fiat exchanges, but may apply to other services. Additionally, Romania has a specific tax regime for crypto gains for individuals (10% tax), but for licensed entities, normal corporate rules apply.

Tax planning is crucial. Our team can connect you with Romanian tax advisors to optimize your structure. For a comparison with other jurisdictions, see our vs Lithuania page. Note that Romania also has a micro-enterprise tax regime for small companies, which may reduce the effective rate to 1-3% on revenue under certain conditions. However, licensed entities typically exceed the thresholds for this regime.

Allowed Activities

With a crypto payment institution license Romania, you can perform the following activities under MiCA:

  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets
  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Execution of payment transactions involving crypto assets
  • Transfer services for crypto assets
  • Reception and transmission of orders for crypto assets
  • Placement of crypto assets

Note that operating a crypto ATM or issuing stablecoins may require additional authorization. Consult with our experts to confirm your specific service scope. For example, if you plan to offer crypto-backed loans, this may fall under a different regulatory category. The license does not cover investment advice or portfolio management, which require separate MiFID authorization.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a crypto payment institution license and a VASP registration in Romania?

Under MiCA, the crypto payment institution license is a full CASP authorization with higher capital requirements and stricter oversight. The old VASP registration no longer exists in Romania as of 2026. The new license allows passporting across the EU and includes payment institution status under PSD2.

Can I passport the Romanian license to other EU countries?

Yes, once you obtain the crypto payment institution license from BNR, you can passport your services to any EU member state using the MiCA passporting mechanism. This involves notifying the BNR and the host country's regulator. No additional license is needed.

What are the ongoing costs after obtaining the license?

Ongoing costs include annual supervision fees (EUR 2,000-5,000), compliance officer salary, audit fees (EUR 5,000-10,000), and AML software costs. Additionally, you must maintain the minimum capital and pay for legal updates. Total annual costs typically range from EUR 20,000 to 50,000.

Is it mandatory to have a physical office in Romania?

Yes, you need a registered office in Romania for the company. The BNR may also require a local compliance officer or director. However, a virtual office may be acceptable if it includes physical presence for inspections. We recommend a physical office to demonstrate commitment.

How long does the BNR take to process the application?

The BNR typically takes 6 to 12 months to process a complete application. The timeline depends on the complexity of your business model and the quality of documentation. Incomplete applications can cause delays. Our coordination helps ensure timely submission.

Can I use the Romanian license for crypto-to-crypto exchanges only?

Yes, the license covers both crypto-to-fiat and crypto-to-crypto exchange services. However, if you only offer crypto-to-crypto exchange, the capital requirement may be lower (EUR 50,000). You must still comply with AML obligations for all transactions.

What are the tax implications for a licensed crypto payment institution in Romania?

Corporate income tax is 16% on profits. Crypto transactions are treated as service income. VAT is not applicable on exchanges. You may also be subject to local business tax (0.5-1% on revenue). Dividend withholding tax is 5% for EU residents. Consult a tax advisor.

Is the Romanian license recognized outside the EU?

While the license is EU-specific, it enhances credibility with international banks and partners. Some non-EU regulators may recognize the license for simplified entry. However, for operations outside the EU, separate licenses may be required. For global coverage, consider Panama as a base.

What happens if I fail to maintain the minimum capital?

If the capital falls below the minimum, the BNR may impose fines, suspend operations, or revoke the license. You must restore the capital immediately. Continuous monitoring is essential. We recommend setting aside reserves to cover operational risks.

Can I apply for the license as a non-EU resident?

Yes, non-EU residents can apply, but you must incorporate a Romanian company and appoint a local director or representative. The BNR will assess the beneficial owners' background. Our team can assist with company formation and compliance.
