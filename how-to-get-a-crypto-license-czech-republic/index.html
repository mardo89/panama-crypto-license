What Is the Czech Republic Crypto License?

The Czech Republic crypto license is a mandatory authorization for any entity providing crypto-asset services within the country. Under MiCA, which is fully in force since 2026, the Czech National Bank (CNB) acts as the competent authority for licensing and supervision. The license covers activities such as operating a crypto exchange, custodian wallet provider, and other CASP (Crypto-Asset Service Provider) functions.

Key features of the license include:

  • Full passporting rights across the European Economic Area (EEA).
  • Compliance with MiCA's harmonized rules on capital, governance, and consumer protection.
  • Ongoing reporting and audit requirements.

Unlike pre-MiCA regimes, the Czech license is not a simple registration - it is a comprehensive licensing process that ensures a high standard of investor protection and market integrity. For founders, this means a more rigorous application but also greater credibility and access to the entire EU market. The CNB expects applicants to demonstrate strong operational frameworks, including detailed risk management policies and a clear business plan. Who Needs a Czech Republic Crypto License?

Any legal entity that offers one or more of the following services in the Czech Republic must obtain a crypto license from the CNB:

  • Exchange of crypto-assets for fiat currency or other crypto-assets.
  • Operation of a crypto-asset trading platform (order book, DEX, etc.).
  • Custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients.
  • Transfer services for crypto-assets.
  • Advice on crypto-assets or portfolio management.
  • Underwriting or placing of crypto-assets.

This includes both Czech-based companies and foreign entities that actively market or provide services to Czech residents. If you are a startup, a growing exchange, or a traditional financial institution expanding into digital assets, you likely fall under this requirement. License Type and Regulator

The Czech Republic issues a single crypto-asset service provider (CASP) license under the authority of the Czech National Bank (CNB). The CNB is the central bank and financial market supervisor, responsible for licensing, supervision, and enforcement. The license is based on MiCA Regulation (EU) 2023/1114, which sets uniform rules across the EU.

Key regulatory details:

  • Regulator: Czech National Bank (Česká národní banka - ČNB).
  • Legal basis: Act on the Digital Economy (implementing MiCA) and directly applicable MiCA.
  • License type: CASP license - covers all crypto-asset services.
  • Supervision: Ongoing prudential and conduct supervision by CNB.

The CNB has a reputation for thorough review. Applications are assessed on governance, financial soundness, and compliance with AML/KYC rules. Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the Czech Republic applies tiered capital requirements based on the types of crypto-asset services provided. As of 2026, the minimum capital thresholds are:

  • EUR 50,000 - for services such as execution of orders, advice, and portfolio management (without custody).
  • EUR 125,000 - for exchange services, trading platforms, and transfer services.
  • EUR 150,000 - for custody and administration of crypto-assets.

These amounts must be held as own funds, fully paid up, and maintained at all times. The capital can be in the form of common equity, reserves, or certain subordinated loans. For example, if your business offers both exchange and custody services, you need the highest applicable capital (EUR 150,000). Tax Treatment

The Czech Republic offers a relatively favourable tax environment for crypto businesses. Corporate income tax is a flat 19% on profits. VAT is generally not applicable to crypto-to-fiat exchanges (exempt), but other services may be subject to standard VAT (21%). Personal income tax on crypto gains for individuals is 15% (or 23% above a threshold).

Key points:

  • Capital gains from crypto are treated as income, not subject to special crypto tax.
  • No withholding tax on dividends paid to EU residents (subject to conditions).
  • Double tax treaties available with many countries.

Allowed Activities

With a Czech Republic crypto license, you are permitted to offer the following services under MiCA:

  • Exchange of crypto-assets for fiat currency.
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for other crypto-assets.
  • Operation of a trading platform (including order book and automated matching).
  • Custody and administration of crypto-assets.
  • Transfer services (sending/receiving on behalf of clients).
  • Execution of orders on behalf of clients.
  • Placing of crypto-assets (underwriting).
  • Receiving and transmitting orders.
  • Advice on crypto-assets.
  • Portfolio management.

You may also provide ancillary services such as staking, lending, or DeFi integrations, provided they comply with MiCA and CNB guidelines. The license does not automatically permit investment services in traditional securities - a separate license would be needed for that. Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a Czech Republic crypto license involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step.

  1. Company incorporation: Set up a Czech limited liability company (s.r.o.) with a registered office and local director (or EU resident). Minimum share capital is CZK 1 (but higher for license purposes). We handle the entire incorporation process.
  2. Prepare documentation: Draft a business plan, risk management policy, AML/KYC procedures, governance framework, and financial projections. These must meet CNB standards. We provide templates and review.
  3. Capital deposit: Ensure the required minimum capital (EUR 50k-150k) is paid up and held in a Czech bank account. We assist with bank account opening.
  4. Submit application: File the complete application with the CNB, including all supporting documents and the application fee. We compile and submit on your behalf.
  5. CNB review: The CNB has up to 3 months to review, with possible requests for additional information. This can extend the timeline. We handle all communication with the regulator.
  6. Approval and ongoing compliance: Once approved, you must maintain capital, submit periodic reports, and undergo audits. We provide ongoing compliance support.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make several mistakes when applying for a Czech crypto license. Avoid these:

  • Insufficient capital: Underestimating the required capital or not having it fully paid up before submission.
  • Incomplete documentation: Submitting a business plan that lacks detail on risk management or AML procedures.
  • Ignoring local director requirements: The CNB expects a local director (or EU resident) with relevant experience.
  • Poor banking relationships: Not securing a bank account early, causing delays in capital deposit.
  • Misclassifying services: Applying for the wrong capital tier due to incorrect service categorization.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Czech crypto license?

The minimum capital depends on services offered: EUR 50,000 for advice/portfolio management, EUR 125,000 for exchange/transfer services, and EUR 150,000 for custody. These are under MiCA, fully in force in 2026.

How long does it take to get a Czech crypto license?

The total timeline is typically 4 to 8 months, including company setup (2-4 weeks), document preparation (4-8 weeks), and CNB review (up to 3 months). Delays can occur if additional information is requested.

Can I passport my Czech license to other EU countries?

Yes, under MiCA, a Czech CASP license allows you to provide services across all EU/EEA member states without additional licenses. You must notify the CNB of your intent to passport.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

You must maintain minimum capital, conduct annual audits, submit periodic reports to the CNB, and have AML/CFT procedures in place. A compliance officer and MLRO must be appointed.

Is the Czech Republic tax-friendly for crypto businesses?

Yes, corporate income tax is 19%, and crypto-to-fiat exchanges are VAT-exempt. Capital gains are treated as ordinary income. No special crypto tax applies.

Do I need a local director for the Czech company?

Yes, the CNB expects at least one director who is a resident of the EU. This person should have relevant experience in financial services or crypto.
