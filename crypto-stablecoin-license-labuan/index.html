What is the Labuan Crypto Stablecoin License?

The Labuan crypto stablecoin license is a regulatory authorization issued by the Labuan Financial Services Authority (Labuan FSA) for businesses that issue, manage, or trade stablecoins within or from Labuan International Business and Financial Centre (IBFC). It is part of the broader Labuan Digital Currency and Digital Token framework, which governs digital assets including stablecoins, utility tokens, and security tokens.

This license allows holders to operate legally in a well-regulated environment, offering credibility to investors and partners. The framework is designed to align with international standards such as FATF recommendations, ensuring strong anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CFT) controls.

Key features include a clear classification of stablecoins as digital currencies, a risk-based approach to regulation, and a streamlined application process for qualified applicants. Unlike some jurisdictions that treat stablecoins as e-money or securities, Labuan provides a dedicated digital currency category, reducing legal uncertainty. For example, a stablecoin pegged to USD is treated as a digital currency, not a security, avoiding dual regulation.

Who Needs a Labuan Stablecoin License?

Any entity that intends to issue, manage, or facilitate the trading of stablecoins in or from Labuan requires this license. This includes:

  • Stablecoin issuers (fiat-backed, crypto-backed, or algorithmic)
  • Digital asset exchanges listing stablecoins
  • Wallet providers offering stablecoin custody
  • Payment service providers using stablecoins for remittances or merchant services
  • Tokenization platforms issuing stablecoins for asset-backed tokens

If your business involves creating or distributing a stablecoin that is pegged to a fiat currency like USD, EUR, or a basket of assets, you fall under this regulatory scope. Even if your operations are overseas, if you target Labuan IBFC clients or use Labuan as a base, the license is mandatory. For example, a company issuing a USD-pegged stablecoin for cross-border payments in Southeast Asia would need this license if it establishes a presence in Labuan. Similarly, a crypto exchange that lists multiple stablecoins and offers trading pairs must be licensed if it operates from Labuan.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Labuan Financial Services Authority (Labuan FSA). The license type is a Digital Currency and Digital Token (DCDT) license, specifically for stablecoin activities. Labuan FSA categorizes digital assets into two main classes:

  • Digital Currency: A digital representation of value that functions as a medium of exchange, unit of account, or store of value (includes stablecoins).
  • Digital Token: A digital representation of an asset or right (utility or security tokens).

Stablecoins typically fall under Digital Currency. The license is issued under the Labuan Financial Services and Securities Act 2010 and the Labuan Digital Currency and Digital Token Guidelines. Labuan FSA is a well-respected regulator with a risk-based approach, meaning higher-risk activities may face additional scrutiny. For instance, an algorithmic stablecoin may require more detailed risk disclosures than a fully fiat-backed one.

Capital Requirement

Labuan FSA requires a minimum paid-up capital for stablecoin license holders. As of 2026, the requirement is typically USD 100,000 for digital currency licensees. This amount must be maintained as operating capital and may be in the form of cash, liquid assets, or a bank guarantee.

Higher capital may be required if the business involves higher risk activities, such as dealing with multiple fiat currencies or high transaction volumes. The capital must be deposited in a Labuan licensed bank and cannot be withdrawn without regulatory approval. For example, a stablecoin issuer handling over USD 10 million in monthly transactions might need to demonstrate additional capital reserves to cover operational risks. In contrast, Lithuania requires EUR 125,000 for CASP under MiCA, but that covers broader services.

Tax Treatment

Labuan offers a highly attractive tax regime for stablecoin license holders. The key benefits include:

  • Corporate tax rate: 3% of net audited profits (or a fixed rate of MYR 20,000, about USD 4,300, whichever is higher) for qualifying activities.
  • No withholding tax on dividends, interest, and royalties paid to non-residents.
  • No capital gains tax on disposal of digital assets.
  • No sales tax or VAT on digital currency transactions.

This makes Labuan one of the most tax-efficient jurisdictions for stablecoin operations globally. However, the low tax rate applies only to income from licensed activities; other income may be taxed at standard Malaysian rates. Professional tax advice is recommended. In contrast, jurisdictions like Lithuania have a 15% corporate tax rate but offer access to the EU market. For projects targeting Asian markets, Labuan's tax advantage is significant.

Allowed Activities

Under the Labuan stablecoin license, you can conduct the following activities:

  • Issuance of stablecoins (fiat-backed, crypto-backed, or algorithmic)
  • Redemption and buyback of stablecoins
  • Exchange of stablecoins for fiat currency or other digital assets
  • Custody and safekeeping of stablecoins
  • Payment processing using stablecoins
  • Remittance services via stablecoins
  • Tokenization of assets (if stablecoin is used as a settlement layer)

Activities that are not allowed include unlicensed securities trading, gambling-related services, and any activity that violates Malaysian law or international sanctions. For example, a stablecoin issuer cannot simultaneously operate a casino without a separate license. Also, if your stablecoin is deemed a security by Labuan FSA, you may need a separate securities license. The scope is broad enough for most legitimate stablecoin business models.

Compliance and Trust

Maintaining a Labuan stablecoin license requires ongoing compliance with Labuan FSA regulations. Key obligations include:

  • Annual audit by a Labuan-approved auditor
  • Submission of annual returns and financial statements
  • AML/CFT compliance including customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting
  • Maintaining minimum capital requirements
  • Notifying Labuan FSA of any material changes in business operations

Non-compliance can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. For example, failure to file annual returns on time may incur penalties. Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Labuan stablecoin license?

The minimum paid-up capital is typically USD 100,000. This must be maintained as operating capital and deposited in a Labuan licensed bank. Higher amounts may be required for higher-risk activities.

How long does it take to get a Labuan stablecoin license?

The process usually takes 4 to 8 months from application to approval. Straightforward cases may be processed in 4-6 months. Delays can occur if documents are incomplete or if the business model is complex.

What is the corporate tax rate for licensed stablecoin businesses in Labuan?

The tax rate is 3% of net audited profits, or a fixed amount of MYR 20,000 (about USD 4,300) whichever is higher. There is no capital gains tax, no withholding tax on dividends, and no VAT on digital currency transactions.

Can I issue an algorithmic stablecoin under this license?

Yes, algorithmic stablecoins are allowed under the Digital Currency category. However, you may need to provide additional risk disclosures and demonstrate strong mechanisms to maintain the peg. Labuan FSA will assess the risks on a case-by-case basis.

Do I need a local director or office in Labuan?

Yes, Labuan FSA requires a local presence, including a registered office address and at least one local director or a local manager. This can be arranged through a service provider. Consulting24 can help with this.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements after obtaining the license?

You must conduct annual audits by a Labuan-approved auditor, submit annual returns and financial statements, maintain AML/CFT procedures, and notify Labuan FSA of any material changes. Non-compliance can lead to penalties or license revocation.

Is Labuan a good jurisdiction for stablecoin projects targeting the EU market?

Labuan is more suited for Asian and global markets. For EU market access, a license under MiCA in an EU member state like Lithuania or Estonia would be more appropriate, though tax rates are higher. Labuan offers better tax efficiency but no EU passporting.

Can I use the Labuan license to operate in other countries?

The license authorizes operations within Labuan IBFC and with clients based there. If you target clients in other jurisdictions, you may need additional licenses or compliance with local laws. Always seek legal advice for cross-border activities.

What is the difference between a Labuan stablecoin license and a Panama crypto license?

Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 license with no capital requirement and 0% offshore tax, but its regulatory framework is not specific to stablecoins. Labuan has a higher cost and capital requirement but provides a dedicated stablecoin regime with clear guidelines and lower tax (3%).
