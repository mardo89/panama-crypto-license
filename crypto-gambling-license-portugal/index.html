What Is a Crypto Gambling License in Portugal?

A crypto gambling license in Portugal is not a standalone license but rather an authorization granted by the SRIJ to operate online gambling services that accept cryptocurrencies. The SRIJ is the regulatory body responsible for licensing and supervising all gambling activities in Portugal, including online casinos, sports betting, poker, and other games of chance. Crypto gambling operators must apply for the same license categories as traditional operators, but they must also demonstrate compliance with crypto-specific AML and KYC requirements.

The license allows operators to offer services to Portuguese residents and, depending on the license type, to international customers. The regulatory framework is based on Decree-Law No. 66/2015, which established the legal regime for online gambling and betting, and subsequent amendments that have not yet specifically addressed cryptocurrencies but apply by extension.

Who Needs a Crypto Gambling License in Portugal?

Any entity that offers online gambling services to Portuguese residents and accepts cryptocurrencies as a payment method must obtain a license from the SRIJ. This includes:

  • Online casinos offering slot games, table games, and live dealer games
  • Sports betting platforms (including eSports)
  • Poker rooms and bingo halls
  • Lottery and scratch card providers
  • Any other gambling service that uses crypto for deposits, withdrawals, or wagering

Operators based outside Portugal but targeting Portuguese players also need a Portuguese license. The SRIJ actively blocks unlicensed sites. If you are a crypto gambling startup or an established operator looking to enter the Portuguese market, you need this license.

License Type & Regulator

The SRIJ, part of the Portuguese Tourism and Services Directorate, is the sole regulator for all gambling activities in Portugal. It issues licenses for online gambling and betting, and oversees compliance with the law.

License Types

There are two main categories:

  • Online Gambling License (Jogos de Fortuna ou Azar): For casino games, poker, and other games of chance. This license is required for operators offering slots, roulette, blackjack, etc.
  • Online Betting License (Apostas Desportivas): For sports betting, including fixed-odds and exchange betting. This license can be combined with the gambling license.

Both licenses are issued for a renewable term of 3 years. The SRIJ also requires operators to use certified gaming software and adhere to strict technical standards.

Capital Requirement

Portugal imposes a minimum share capital requirement for gambling license applicants. The exact amount depends on the license type and the scope of operations:

  • For online gambling licenses: minimum share capital of EUR 50,000
  • For online betting licenses: minimum share capital of EUR 50,000
  • If applying for both, the combined minimum is typically EUR 100,000

This capital must be fully paid up and maintained throughout the license period. Additionally, operators may need to provide a bank guarantee or security deposit, often around EUR 50,000, to cover potential player liabilities.

Tax Treatment

Portugal's gambling tax regime is based on gross gaming revenue (GGR). The rates are as follows:

  • Online casino games: 8% to 16% of GGR, depending on the game type and operator's revenue
  • Sports betting: 8% to 16% of GGR
  • Poker: 15% of commission (rake)

In addition, operators are subject to the standard corporate income tax rate of 21% (plus possible municipal surcharges). VAT does not apply to gambling services. Cryptocurrency transactions are treated as any other payment method for tax purposes, but operators must ensure proper accounting and reporting of crypto gains/losses.

Portugal has a favorable tax regime for crypto investors (no capital gains tax on crypto held for over 365 days), but this does not extend to gambling operators. Consulting24 can connect you with tax advisors for detailed planning.

Allowed Activities

A Portuguese gambling license permits the following activities, provided they comply with SRIJ regulations:

  • Online casino games (slots, roulette, blackjack, baccarat, etc.)
  • Live dealer games
  • Sports betting (pre-match and live)
  • eSports betting
  • Poker (cash games and tournaments)
  • Bingo and keno
  • Virtual sports
  • Lottery and instant win games

All games must use certified random number generators (RNGs) and be tested by an accredited laboratory. Operators can accept cryptocurrencies as a payment method, but must implement strong AML/KYC procedures. The license does not permit offering games that are not explicitly approved by the SRIJ.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a crypto gambling license in Portugal involves several stages. Consulting24 coordinates with local partners to manage each step:

  1. Pre-application preparation: Define business model, prepare business plan, and gather required documents (company incorporation, shareholder details, financial statements, AML policies).
  2. Legal entity setup: Incorporate a Portuguese company (or register a branch) with the required minimum capital.
  3. Technical compliance: Ensure gaming platform and RNG are certified by an SRIJ-approved testing lab (e.g., GLI, BMM Testlabs).
  4. Submit application: File the application with the SRIJ, including all supporting documents and payment of the application fee.
  5. Review and due diligence: The SRIJ conducts background checks on key personnel and reviews technical and financial documentation.
  6. License issuance: If approved, the license is granted for 3 years, subject to annual renewal and ongoing compliance.
  7. Post-licensing: Implement operational procedures, connect to SRIJ monitoring systems, and commence operations.

Banking & Payment Processing

One of the biggest challenges for crypto gambling operators is finding banking partners. Portuguese banks are generally cautious about gambling and crypto-related businesses. For crypto payments, operators typically use third-party payment processors that specialize in crypto-to-fiat conversion. The SRIJ requires that all player funds be held in segregated accounts and that operators have adequate liquidity to cover withdrawals.

Compliance & Trust

Operating a crypto gambling platform in Portugal requires strict adherence to AML and KYC regulations. The SRIJ expects operators to have strong systems to prevent money laundering and terrorist financing, including transaction monitoring, suspicious activity reporting, and customer due diligence.

Operators must also comply with Portugal's data protection laws (GDPR) and ensure that player data is secure. The SRIJ conducts regular audits and can impose fines or revoke licenses for non-compliance.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Many applicants face delays or rejections due to common errors. Avoid these pitfalls:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing or incorrect documents can stall the application. Ensure all forms are filled accurately and translated into Portuguese if required.
  • Poor AML/KYC procedures: The SRIJ scrutinizes AML policies. Have a detailed AML manual and appoint a local AML officer.
  • Uncertified software: Using an uncertified RNG or platform will lead to rejection. Get certification from an approved lab before applying.
  • Underestimating capital requirements: Ensure the minimum capital is fully paid up and maintained.
  • Ignoring local presence: The SRIJ expects a physical office in Portugal and local management. Consider setting up a subsidiary.

Alternatives: Panama and Other Jurisdictions

Portugal is not the only option for crypto gambling licensing. Depending on your target market and budget, other jurisdictions may be more suitable:

  • Panama: Offers a flat fee of EUR 6,000 for a crypto license, with no minimum capital and a faster process (2-3 months). Panama is a popular choice for crypto gambling operators due to its flexible regulation and tax-free status on offshore income. However, it does not provide EU market access.
  • Estonia: Known for its e-residency program and crypto-friendly environment. The cost is around EUR 3,000-5,000 for a license, but the regulator has become stricter. Minimum capital is EUR 12,000.
  • Malta: A well-established gambling hub with a dedicated regulator (MGA). Costs are higher (EUR 25,000+), but the license is respected globally. Minimum capital is EUR 40,000.

Can I use cryptocurrencies as a payment method in Portugal?

Yes, operators can accept cryptocurrencies for deposits and withdrawals. However, they must implement strong AML/KYC procedures and ensure all crypto transactions are traceable. The SRIJ treats crypto payments like any other payment method.

Is there a minimum capital requirement for a crypto gambling license in Portugal?

Yes, the minimum share capital is EUR 50,000 for a single license (gambling or betting) and EUR 100,000 for combined licenses. This capital must be fully paid up and maintained.

Operators pay a gambling tax of 8-16% on gross gaming revenue, plus corporate income tax at 21%. There is no VAT on gambling services. Crypto transactions are taxed as normal business income.
