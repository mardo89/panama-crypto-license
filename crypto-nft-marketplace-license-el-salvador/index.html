What Is the El Salvador Crypto License for NFT Marketplaces?

The El Salvador crypto license, officially a registration as a Bitcoin Service Provider (BSP), is required for any entity offering services related to Bitcoin or other digital assets, including NFT marketplaces that use Bitcoin for transactions. The license is issued by the Banco Central de Reserva (BCR) under the Bitcoin Law (2021) and subsequent regulations. It allows legal operation within the country, subject to compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and know-your-customer (KYC) requirements.

Key features of the license include:

  • No minimum capital requirement (as of 2026).
  • 0% capital gains tax on Bitcoin profits.
  • Full legal recognition of Bitcoin as legal tender.
  • Ongoing reporting obligations to the BCR.

This license is distinct from the traditional financial services license and is tailored specifically for digital asset businesses. The BCR has published detailed guidelines on how BSPs must operate, including requirements for transaction monitoring and record keeping. For example, all transactions above a certain threshold must be reported to the Financial Investigation Unit. The license does not cap the number of users or transaction volume, making it scalable for growing NFT platforms. However, the BCR may impose additional conditions on a case-by-case basis, such as enhanced reporting for high-volume operators. Who Needs This License?

Any business that operates an NFT marketplace where users can buy, sell, or trade NFTs using Bitcoin or other digital assets must obtain the El Salvador crypto license. This includes platforms that facilitate peer-to-peer transactions, auction-style sales, or fixed-price listings. The requirement applies to both local and foreign companies that target Salvadoran users or process transactions in Bitcoin.

Typical applicants include:

  • NFT marketplace startups.
  • Existing crypto exchanges expanding into NFTs.
  • Art and collectible platforms integrating crypto payments.
  • Gaming platforms that trade in-game NFTs.
  • Music and entertainment platforms tokenizing digital content.

If your platform does not handle Bitcoin or other digital assets directly (e.g., only fiat currency transactions), you may not need this license. However, most NFT marketplaces involve crypto transactions, so the license is generally required. For example, a platform that lists NFTs but settles in US dollars via credit card would not need the BSP registration, but if it also allows Bitcoin payments, it must register. License Type and Regulator

The license is a registration as a Bitcoin Service Provider (BSP) under the supervision of the Banco Central de Reserva (BCR). The BCR is the central bank of El Salvador and is responsible for implementing the Bitcoin Law. The registration process involves submitting an application with detailed information about the business, its beneficial owners, and its AML/KYC policies.

There is no specific sub-category for NFT marketplaces; the license covers all types of Bitcoin service providers, including exchanges, custodians, and payment processors. The BCR evaluates each application on a case-by-case basis. The regulator has the authority to request additional information, conduct on-site inspections, and impose sanctions for non-compliance. As of 2026, the BCR has issued over 100 BSP registrations, with a growing number of NFT-focused platforms. Capital Requirement

As of 2026, El Salvador imposes no minimum capital requirement for Bitcoin Service Provider registration. This makes it one of the most accessible jurisdictions for startups. However, the BCR expects the business to have sufficient financial resources to operate and meet its obligations. In practice, you should demonstrate a reasonable operational budget to cover initial setup and compliance costs.

This contrasts with EU jurisdictions under MiCA, where capital requirements range from EUR 50,000 to EUR 150,000 depending on service class. For example, a custody service requires EUR 125,000, while an exchange needs EUR 150,000. El Salvador's zero capital requirement is a significant advantage for early-stage NFT marketplaces. However, the BCR may ask for a business plan showing projected revenues and expenses to ensure the entity is viable. Tax Treatment

El Salvador offers a highly favorable tax regime for digital asset businesses. Key points include:

  • 0% capital gains tax on profits from Bitcoin and other digital assets.
  • No VAT on Bitcoin transactions (as per the Bitcoin Law).
  • Corporate income tax applies to other business income at the standard rate of 30% (but gains from crypto are exempt).
  • No withholding tax on dividends paid to foreign shareholders.
  • No wealth tax or net worth tax.

This tax treatment is a major advantage for NFT marketplaces that generate revenue from transaction fees or trading profits. For example, if your marketplace charges a 2.5% fee on each NFT sale, that fee income is subject to corporate income tax, but any gains from holding Bitcoin are tax-free. However, you should consult a tax advisor to ensure compliance with your home country's tax laws, as many countries tax worldwide income. El Salvador does not have a tax treaty network, so double taxation may occur without proper planning. Consulting24 can connect you with international tax specialists.

Allowed Activities

With an El Salvador crypto license, your NFT marketplace can engage in the following activities:

  • Operation of a platform for buying, selling, and trading NFTs.
  • Facilitation of peer-to-peer transactions using Bitcoin or other digital assets.
  • Custody of digital assets (if applicable).
  • Conversion between Bitcoin and US Dollars (the other legal tender).
  • Integration with Bitcoin Lightning Network for faster payments.
  • Issuance of NFTs (if the platform also creates NFTs).

Activities that are not allowed include offering derivatives, margin trading, or lending without separate authorization. The license is specifically for spot transactions and custody. For a broader scope, consider a license from a jurisdiction like Lithuania under MiCA, which allows a wider range of services including transfer services, exchange between crypto and fiat, and custody of client assets. Banking and Payments

Opening a bank account in El Salvador for a crypto business can be challenging due to perceived risks. However, the Bitcoin Law provides legal clarity, and some local banks accept Bitcoin service providers. Options include:

  • Banco Agrícola, Banco Cuscatlán, and others may open accounts with proper documentation.
  • International payment processors like Stripe or PayPal may not support crypto businesses.
  • Bitcoin Lightning Network can be used for instant, low-cost payments.
  • Some banks require a minimum deposit of USD 1,000-5,000.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

When applying for the El Salvador crypto license, avoid these common pitfalls:

  • Incomplete documentation: Ensure all forms are fully filled and supporting documents are translated into Spanish.
  • Underestimating AML requirements: The BCR expects a detailed AML policy. Generic templates may be rejected.
  • Ignoring beneficial ownership disclosure: All ultimate beneficial owners must be identified.
  • Operating before approval: Starting operations without a license can lead to fines of up to USD 10,000.
  • Neglecting ongoing reporting: Failure to submit reports can result in license suspension.
  • Choosing the wrong legal structure: A limited liability company (Sociedad de Responsabilidad Limitada) is commonly used.
