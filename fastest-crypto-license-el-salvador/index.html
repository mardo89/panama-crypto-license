What Is the El Salvador Crypto License?

The El Salvador crypto license, officially the Digital Asset Service Provider (DASP) registration, is a regulatory authorization issued by the Comisión Nacional de Activos Digitales (CNAD). It allows companies to legally offer services such as crypto exchange, custody, and payment processing within El Salvador. Established under the Digital Assets Issuance Law (Ley de Emisión de Activos Digitales) in 2023, the framework is designed to attract crypto businesses with speed and clarity.

Key features include a fast approval timeline of around 30 days, no minimum capital requirement, and a 0% capital gains tax on crypto transactions. The license is valid for one year and renewable annually. It is recognized as one of the fastest El Salvador crypto license options globally, especially for companies targeting Latin American markets.

Unlike the EU's MiCA regime, which imposes tiered capital requirements (EUR 50,000 to 150,000), El Salvador's DASP has zero statutory capital. This makes it particularly attractive for startups and small to medium enterprises that want to avoid locking up significant funds. The CNAD also accepts Bitcoin as part of the company's balance sheet, reflecting the country's pro-Bitcoin stance.

Who Needs a DASP License in El Salvador?

The DASP license is mandatory for any entity providing digital asset services to residents of El Salvador or operating from within the country. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralized and decentralized)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Payment processors handling digital assets
  • Brokers and dealers in digital assets
  • Token issuers (subject to additional requirements)

If you plan to offer services like trading, custody, or fiat-to-crypto conversion, you need this license. Even foreign companies targeting Salvadoran customers must register. The fastest El Salvador crypto license is ideal for startups seeking quick market entry.

For example, a peer-to-peer exchange that matches buyers and sellers of Bitcoin would need a DASP if it holds any customer funds or facilitates trades. Similarly, a company offering crypto-backed loans would fall under the definition. The CNAD has clarified that even non-custodial services may require registration if they involve active solicitation of Salvadoran clients.

License Type and Regulator

The relevant regulator is the Comisión Nacional de Activos Digitales (CNAD), an independent government agency overseeing digital asset activities. The license type is a Digital Asset Service Provider (DASP) registration. Unlike some jurisdictions, El Salvador does not have multiple license tiers; one license covers all permitted activities.

The CNAD focuses on anti-money laundering (AML) compliance, consumer protection, and financial stability. Applicants must demonstrate strong AML policies, a fit-and-proper management team, and adequate operational infrastructure. The regulator aims to process applications within 30 business days, making it the fastest El Salvador crypto license process.

The CNAD operates with a relatively small team, which means applications that are complete and well-prepared are processed quickly. Incomplete submissions can cause delays, so working with experienced advisors is recommended. The regulator also conducts periodic on-site inspections to ensure ongoing compliance.

Capital Requirement

El Salvador imposes no minimum capital requirement for a DASP license. This is a major advantage compared to jurisdictions like Lithuania (EUR 125,000 for exchanges) or Panama (EUR 6,000 fixed). The absence of a capital barrier makes the fastest El Salvador crypto license accessible to startups and small businesses.

However, you must demonstrate sufficient operational funds to cover initial setup and at least six months of operating expenses. The CNAD may request proof of financial stability, but there is no statutory minimum. This flexibility is a key reason why entrepreneurs choose El Salvador.

For example, a startup with a lean team of three can budget around $20,000 for the first year (including license fees, legal, rent, and salaries). This is far lower than the $150,000+ needed for a MiCA-compliant license in Europe. The CNAD also accepts Bitcoin as part of the financial statement, which can reduce fiat capital needs.

Tax Treatment for Crypto Businesses

El Salvador offers a highly favorable tax regime for digital asset companies:

  • 0% capital gains tax on profits from crypto transactions
  • No withholding tax on dividends or interest from digital assets
  • Standard corporate income tax of 30% on other business income (e.g., fiat-based fees)
  • VAT of 13% applies to goods and services, but crypto-to-crypto transactions may be exempt

This tax structure makes El Salvador one of the most tax-efficient jurisdictions for crypto operations. Combined with the fastest El Salvador crypto license, it is a powerful combination for international businesses.

It is important to note that the 0% capital gains tax applies only to digital asset transactions, not to traditional business income. For example, if your exchange charges a 1% fee on trades, that fee is subject to corporate income tax. However, the appreciation of your own crypto holdings is tax-free. The CNAD and tax authority (DGII) have issued joint guidance clarifying this distinction.

Allowed Activities Under the DASP License

The DASP license authorizes a broad range of activities, including:

  • Exchange of digital assets for fiat currency or other digital assets
  • Custody and administration of digital assets on behalf of clients
  • Operation of a digital asset trading platform
  • Payment services using digital assets
  • Initial offerings of digital assets (subject to additional approval)

Notably, the license does not cover banking activities or securities issuance unless separately authorized. The scope is similar to a crypto exchange license in other jurisdictions, but with faster approval.

If you plan to offer staking services, the CNAD treats this as a custody activity and requires additional disclosure. Lending of crypto assets is also permitted if done on a non-banking basis. The regulator has published a list of prohibited activities, which includes accepting deposits repayable to the depositor (banking) and issuing securities without a prospectus.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining the fastest El Salvador crypto license involves these steps:

  1. Pre-incorporation: Register a company in El Salvador (typically a Sociedad Anónima or LLC).
  2. Document preparation: Gather AML policies, business plan, financial projections, and personal documents of directors.
  3. Submit application: File with CNAD, including the application form and fee.
  4. Review and interview: CNAD reviews the application and may request additional information or an interview.
  5. Approval: Upon approval, pay the issuance fee and receive the DASP certificate.
  6. Post-licensing: Implement ongoing compliance, including annual reporting and AML training.

Compliance and Trust Considerations

While the fastest El Salvador crypto license is easy to obtain, compliance is taken seriously. The CNAD requires:

  • AML/CFT policies aligned with FATF recommendations
  • Know Your Customer (KYC) procedures
  • Transaction monitoring and reporting
  • Annual audits by an external firm
  • Appointment of a compliance officer

Failure to comply can result in fines or license revocation. We recommend working with a compliance specialist. This is general guidance, not legal advice. For your specific situation, consult a qualified lawyer.

The CNAD has the power to conduct unannounced inspections and request transaction data. In 2025, they fined two licensees for inadequate KYC procedures. To avoid this, implement a strong transaction monitoring system that flags suspicious activity. Many firms use third-party tools like Chainalysis or Elliptic. The annual audit must be submitted within 90 days of the fiscal year end.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Applying for the fastest El Salvador crypto license? Avoid these pitfalls:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing AML policies or business plans cause delays.
  • Ignoring beneficial ownership rules: All ultimate beneficial owners must be disclosed.
  • Underestimating compliance costs: Ongoing AML training and audits add up.
  • Assuming bank account is automatic: Start banking discussions early.
  • Not renewing on time: License expires annually; late renewal can lead to suspension.

Frequently asked questions

How long does it take to get the El Salvador crypto license?

The CNAD aims to process applications within 30 business days. In practice, most complete applications are approved in 4-6 weeks. Delays may occur if documents are incomplete or if the regulator requests additional information.

Is there a minimum capital requirement for the DASP license?

No, El Salvador does not impose a minimum capital requirement. However, you must demonstrate sufficient funds to cover operating expenses for at least six months. This is a major advantage over EU jurisdictions that require EUR 50,000 to 150,000.

What taxes do crypto businesses pay in El Salvador?

Capital gains on crypto transactions are taxed at 0%. Other business income (e.g., fees) is subject to 30% corporate income tax. VAT of 13% applies to goods and services, but crypto-to-crypto trades may be exempt. No withholding tax on dividends from digital assets.

Can I use the El Salvador license to serve customers in other countries?

The DASP license allows you to serve customers in El Salvador. If you target customers in other jurisdictions, you may need additional licenses. However, the license is recognized as a credible regulatory status that can facilitate partnerships and banking relationships internationally.

Do I need to incorporate a company in El Salvador to apply?

Yes, you must register a local company (typically a Sociedad Anónima) before applying. The company must have a registered address in El Salvador and at least one director who is a resident or has a local representative.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

Licensees must conduct annual AML audits, submit transaction reports to the CNAD, appoint a compliance officer, and renew the license annually. The renewal fee is $2,000. Failure to comply can result in fines of up to $50,000 or license revocation.

Can I obtain a bank account for my crypto business in El Salvador?

Yes, several banks accept licensed DASPs, including Banco Agrícola and Banco Cuscatlán. However, the process can take 2-4 weeks and may require a minimum deposit. Consulting24 can assist with introductions.

Is the El Salvador DASP license recognized in other countries?

While not automatically passportable like EU licenses, the DASP is respected by many international partners and banks. It demonstrates compliance with FATF standards. Some countries may accept it as part of a reciprocal arrangement, but this is case-by-case.

What happens if my application is rejected?

The CNAD will provide reasons for rejection. You can address the issues and resubmit. Common reasons include incomplete documentation, insufficient AML policies, or concerns about the management team's fitness. Consulting24 can help you prepare a strong application.

Can I apply for the license remotely?

Yes, the entire process can be handled remotely with the help of local legal representatives. You do not need to travel to El Salvador. However, some banks may require an in-person meeting for account opening.
