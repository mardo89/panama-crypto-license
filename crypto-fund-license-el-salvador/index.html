What is the El Salvador Crypto Fund License?

The El Salvador crypto fund license is a regulatory authorization granted by the CNAD that permits a legal entity to operate a collective investment scheme focused on digital assets. This license covers the management of crypto funds, including the issuance of tokens representing fund shares, the custody of assets, and the administration of investor subscriptions and redemptions. It is designed to provide legal certainty and investor protection while fostering innovation in the digital asset space.

The license is part of El Salvador's broader Digital Assets Law, which came into effect in 2023 and has been refined through 2026. It applies to both open-ended and closed-ended funds, and can accommodate various investment strategies, from passive index funds to actively managed portfolios of cryptocurrencies and tokenized securities. The CNAD oversees compliance, anti-money laundering (AML) measures, and ongoing reporting.

Who Needs This License?

The crypto fund license is mandatory for any entity that wishes to offer fund management services for digital assets to third-party investors within or from El Salvador. This includes:

  • Asset managers launching a crypto-focused fund
  • Family offices setting up a pooled investment vehicle for digital assets
  • Tokenization platforms that create fund tokens representing ownership
  • Venture capital firms raising capital in crypto for blockchain startups
  • Hedge funds trading cryptocurrencies and digital assets

If you are a fund promoter, investment manager, or issuer of tokenized fund shares, you likely need this license. Exemptions may apply for fully private placements with a limited number of sophisticated investors, but professional advice is recommended to confirm eligibility.

License Type & Regulator

The regulator for crypto fund licenses in El Salvador is the National Digital Assets Commission (CNAD), an autonomous body established under the Digital Assets Issuance Law. The license itself is often referred to as a Digital Asset Fund Manager License or simply the crypto fund license. There is no separate classification for different fund types under the current framework; instead, the license covers all activities related to the management and operation of digital asset funds.

The CNAD also oversees the registration of digital asset issuers, service providers, and exchanges. For fund operators, the commission requires a detailed business plan, compliance policies, and evidence of adequate capital and expertise. The license is non-transferable and must be renewed periodically, typically every two years.

Capital Requirement

As of 2026, the minimum capital requirement for a crypto fund license in El Salvador is $100,000 in paid-up capital. This amount must be deposited in a local bank account and maintained throughout the license period. The capital can be in fiat (USD) or in digital assets, subject to CNAD approval. Higher capital may be required depending on the fund's size and risk profile, but the baseline is $100,000. This is lower than many EU jurisdictions (which start at €50,000 for certain services) and makes El Salvador attractive for smaller fund managers.

Tax Treatment

El Salvador offers a favorable tax regime for digital asset funds. Key points include:

  • No capital gains tax on profits from digital asset trading or investment for funds that are domiciled in El Salvador and managed by a licensed operator.
  • Corporate income tax at a standard rate of 30% applies to net income, but specific exemptions may apply for funds that reinvest profits or meet certain criteria under the Digital Assets Law.
  • No withholding tax on distributions to foreign investors, provided the fund is properly structured.
  • VAT is generally not applicable to fund management fees if the service is considered financial in nature.

It is important to obtain a tax ruling from the Salvadoran tax authority (Ministerio de Hacienda) to confirm the exact treatment for your fund structure. Consulting24 can coordinate this process with local tax advisors.

Allowed Activities

Under the crypto fund license, the following activities are permitted:

  • Establishment and management of collective investment schemes investing in digital assets
  • Issuance of digital tokens representing fund shares
  • Custody and safekeeping of digital assets on behalf of the fund
  • Administration of subscriptions, redemptions, and transfers of fund tokens
  • Portfolio management and trading of cryptocurrencies, tokens, and other digital assets
  • Provision of investment advice related to digital assets (as part of fund management)

Activities not covered include operating a crypto exchange (requires a separate exchange license), providing custodial services to third parties outside the fund, or issuing stablecoins without additional authorization.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The application process for a crypto fund license in El Salvador involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step with local legal partners.

  1. Pre-application consultation: Assess your fund's structure, strategy, and eligibility. Prepare a business plan, AML/KYC policies, and risk management framework.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Salvadoran company (Sociedad Anónima or similar) with the National Commerce Registry. The company must have a local registered address and a legal representative.
  3. Capital deposit: Open a corporate bank account in El Salvador and deposit the minimum capital of $100,000. Obtain a certificate of deposit.
  4. Submission to CNAD: File the application form, along with supporting documents: business plan, policies, financial projections, background checks on directors and shareholders, and proof of capital.
  5. Review and due diligence: The CNAD conducts a thorough review, including background checks and interviews with key personnel. Respond to any queries promptly.
  6. Approval and license issuance: Once approved, pay the remaining license fee and receive the official license certificate. The fund can then commence operations.

Post-license, ongoing obligations include annual reporting, AML audits, and notification of material changes. Consulting24 can assist with compliance monitoring.

Banking & Payment Services

One of the challenges in El Salvador is opening a corporate bank account for a crypto fund. While some local banks accept crypto-related businesses, many are cautious. Options include:

  • Banco Agrícola (part of Grupo Bancolombia) - has a policy of accepting licensed crypto firms, subject to due diligence.
  • Banco Cuscatlán - more open to fintech and crypto, but requires a strong compliance framework.
  • Banco de América Central (BAC) - accepts crypto fund licenses with a thorough AML review.
  • Digital banks like Chivo Wallet (government-backed) can be used for Bitcoin transactions, but are not suitable for corporate accounts.

Compliance & Trust Considerations

Operating a crypto fund in El Salvador requires a strong compliance framework. The CNAD mandates adherence to AML/CFT regulations, including customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting. Funds must appoint a compliance officer and conduct regular audits. Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license.

Trust is built through transparency. Fund operators should publish periodic reports, maintain accurate records, and engage reputable auditors. The CNAD also conducts on-site inspections. Common Mistakes to Avoid

Many applicants encounter delays or rejections due to avoidable errors. Common pitfalls include:

  • Incomplete business plan: The CNAD expects a detailed plan covering investment strategy, risk management, and operational procedures.
  • Insufficient capital proof: Ensure the capital is deposited in a Salvadoran bank and provide clear evidence.
  • Poor AML/KYC policies: Submit policies that meet international standards (FATF recommendations). Generic templates are not accepted.
  • Lack of local presence: The fund must have a registered office and a local legal representative. Using a virtual office may not suffice.
  • Ignoring tax registration: Register with the tax authority (Ministerio de Hacienda) before or immediately after license approval.

Alternatives: El Salvador vs Panama and Other Jurisdictions

When choosing a jurisdiction for your crypto fund, consider the following comparisons:

  • Panama: Panama offers a crypto fund license at a flat fee of EUR 6,000 (approx. $6,500) with no minimum capital requirement and no capital gains tax. However, Panama's regulatory framework is less mature than El Salvador's, and the licensing process is faster (2-3 months). Panama is ideal for smaller funds seeking a cost-effective solution.
  • Lithuania: As an EU member, Lithuania requires a CASP license under MiCA with capital tiers of €50,000 to €150,000. The process takes 4-6 months. Tax is 15% corporate income tax. Lithuania offers EU passporting rights, which may be valuable for funds targeting European investors. Compare El Salvador vs Lithuania.
  • Malta: Malta has a comprehensive Virtual Financial Assets (VFA) framework. Capital requirements start at €125,000 for fund managers. The process is rigorous and can take 6-9 months. Corporate tax is 35% but can be reduced through refunds. Malta is suited for larger, institutional funds.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto fund license in El Salvador?

The minimum paid-up capital is $100,000. This can be in USD or digital assets, subject to CNAD approval. Higher capital may be required for larger funds.

How long does it take to get a crypto fund license in El Salvador?

The typical timeline is 3 to 6 months from application to approval. The CNAD aims to process within 90 business days, but delays can occur if additional information is needed.

What taxes apply to crypto funds in El Salvador?

No capital gains tax on digital asset profits. Corporate income tax is 30%, but exemptions may apply. No withholding tax on distributions to foreign investors. A tax ruling is recommended.

Can I manage a fund that invests in Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies?

Yes, the license covers funds investing in any digital asset, including Bitcoin, altcoins, and tokenized securities. The fund must comply with CNAD rules on asset custody and valuation.

Do I need a physical office in El Salvador?

Yes, the fund must have a registered office in El Salvador. A virtual office may not be accepted; a physical presence with a local legal representative is required.

Is the El Salvador crypto fund license recognized internationally?

The license is valid in El Salvador. For international investors, the fund must comply with their local regulations. Some jurisdictions may require additional registration or exemptions.

Can I convert an existing fund to an El Salvador license?

Yes, you can redomicile an existing fund to El Salvador, subject to CNAD approval. The process involves transferring the fund's legal seat and obtaining the license.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

Annual financial audits, AML/CFT reporting, notification of material changes, and renewal every two years. The CNAD also conducts periodic inspections.

How does El Salvador compare to Panama for crypto funds?

Panama offers a lower cost (EUR 6,000 flat) and no minimum capital, but its regulatory framework is less developed. El Salvador has a dedicated digital assets law and higher capital requirement ($100,000). Choose based on your fund's size and target market.
