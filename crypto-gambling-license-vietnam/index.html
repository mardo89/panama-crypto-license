Overview: What Is a Crypto Gambling License in Vietnam?

Vietnam currently lacks a specific regulatory framework for crypto gambling. The government has taken a cautious stance on cryptocurrencies, with the State Bank of Vietnam banning crypto as a means of payment. However, blockchain technology and crypto-related businesses operate in a grey area. For gambling, the only legal forms are state-run lotteries and a few licensed casinos for foreigners. There is no separate license for crypto gambling.

This means that a crypto gambling business targeting Vietnamese customers or operating from Vietnam must consider alternative jurisdictions. License Type and Regulator

In Vietnam, there is no single regulator for crypto gambling. The Ministry of Finance oversees traditional gambling, while the State Bank of Vietnam regulates payment activities. For crypto, the Ministry of Information and Communications and the Ministry of Public Security have issued warnings about risks. However, there is no licensing pathway.

Given the lack of a dedicated license, businesses must look abroad. For example, Panama offers a flat-fee license for EUR 6,000 that covers online gambling and crypto payments. The regulator is the Panama Gaming Control Board (Junta de Control de Juegos). Estonia provides a crypto license under the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) for virtual asset service providers (VASPs), which can be combined with a gambling license from the Tax and Customs Board. Lithuania, under MiCA, offers a CASP license from the Bank of Lithuania, but gambling is separate. Capital Requirement

Vietnam does not specify capital requirements for crypto gambling because it does not license such activity. For alternative jurisdictions, capital requirements vary significantly:

  • Panama: No minimum capital for the license, but a paid-up capital of at least USD 10,000 is recommended for the company to demonstrate substance.
  • Estonia: Minimum share capital of EUR 12,000 for a public limited company, but no specific capital for the VASP license. However, the regulator expects sufficient financial resources to cover operational costs.
  • Lithuania: For a CASP license, minimum capital of EUR 125,000 for exchange services and EUR 350,000 for custodian wallet services (per MiCA). These are strict requirements.
  • Curacao: No formal minimum capital, but the regulator expects at least USD 100,000 in available funds.

Tax Treatment

Vietnam imposes corporate income tax at 20% on profits from gambling activities, but since crypto gambling is not explicitly legal, tax treatment is uncertain. For businesses operating from a licensed jurisdiction, tax rates are typically lower and more predictable:

  • Panama: Territorial tax system - only income sourced in Panama is taxed. Offshore profits are tax-free. No capital gains tax. This makes Panama highly attractive for online gambling operators serving global customers.
  • Estonia: 20% corporate income tax on distributed profits (i.e., dividends). Retained earnings are tax-free, allowing reinvestment without tax.

  • Curacao: Profit tax of 2% for offshore companies, but recent changes may increase this.
