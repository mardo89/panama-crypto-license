What is a Crypto Gambling License in Mauritius?

A crypto gambling license in Mauritius is a regulatory authorisation issued by the FSC that permits a company to operate gambling services where bets or wagers are placed using virtual assets (cryptocurrencies). It is not a generic gambling licence but a specific category under the VAITOS regime, tailored for businesses that issue, hold, transfer, or facilitate the exchange of virtual assets in connection with gambling.

Key features of the Mauritius regime include:

  • Regulated by the Financial Services Commission (FSC).
  • Legal basis: Virtual Asset and Initial Token Offering Services Act 2021 (VAITOS) and Financial Services Act 2007.
  • Licence type: Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) with a gambling endorsement.
  • Minimum capital: Typically between EUR 50,000 and EUR 150,000 depending on the scope of services.
  • Corporate tax rate: 15% (with potential partial exemptions).
  • Processing time: 4 to 6 months for a complete application.

Mauritius is a common law jurisdiction with a stable political environment and a growing fintech ecosystem. The FSC has published guidelines specifically addressing virtual asset gambling, making it one of the few jurisdictions with a dedicated framework.

License Type and Regulator

The relevant regulator is the Financial Services Commission (FSC) of Mauritius. The licence type is a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) licence under the VAITOS Act, with a specific endorsement for gambling services. The FSC does not issue a separate 'gambling licence' but rather a VASP licence that authorises the holder to conduct virtual asset activities, including those related to gambling.

Key regulatory features:

  • The FSC requires the licensee to implement strong anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-financing of terrorism (CFT) controls.
  • Licence holders must appoint a resident director (or a local agent) and maintain a physical office in Mauritius.
  • Annual audited financial statements must be submitted.
  • The FSC conducts ongoing supervision, including on-site inspections.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a VASP licence in Mauritius depends on the scope of services. Under VAITOS, the FSC has set the following tiers (as of 2026):

  • Class 1 (custody and transfer services): EUR 50,000 minimum.
  • Class 2 (exchange and trading platform): EUR 125,000 minimum.
  • Class 3 (full-service including gambling): EUR 150,000 minimum.

For a crypto gambling operation, you will typically need Class 3, thus EUR 150,000. This capital must be maintained at all times and can be in the form of cash or liquid assets. The FSC may require proof of capital source and that the funds are unencumbered.

Note: These figures are based on the FSC's published guidelines. They are subject to change and should be verified during the application process. Consulting24 can advise on capital structuring.

Tax Treatment

Mauritius offers a favourable corporate tax regime. The standard corporate income tax rate is 15%. However, companies that are tax resident in Mauritius may benefit from a partial exemption of up to 80% on certain types of income, including foreign-source income and income from financial services, subject to meeting substance requirements. This can effectively reduce the tax rate to 3% on qualifying income.

Key tax points:

  • No capital gains tax.
  • No withholding tax on dividends paid to non-residents.
  • Double taxation treaties with over 40 countries.
  • VAT (15%) applies to local services; exports of services are zero-rated.

For crypto gambling operators, the tax treatment of gambling revenue may be subject to specific rules. It is essential to obtain professional tax advice. The FSC also requires licensees to maintain proper accounting records and file annual returns.

Allowed Activities

Under the VASP licence with a gambling endorsement, the following activities are permitted:

  • Operating a virtual asset casino (including slots, table games, live dealer).
  • Running a sportsbook that accepts cryptocurrency bets.
  • Providing peer-to-peer poker platforms using digital assets.
  • Issuing and redeeming virtual assets for gambling purposes.
  • Exchanging virtual assets for fiat currency or other virtual assets within the platform.
  • Custody of customer virtual assets.

Activities that are NOT allowed without additional licensing:

  • Traditional fiat-only gambling (requires a separate gambling licence under the Gambling Regulatory Authority).
  • Banking or deposit-taking.
  • Securities or derivatives trading.

The FSC may impose conditions on the licence, such as limits on use, maximum bet sizes, or reporting requirements. Always review the licence terms carefully.

Banking and Payment Processing

One of the biggest challenges for crypto gambling operators is obtaining banking services. Mauritius has a developed banking sector, but many banks are cautious about virtual asset businesses. However, the FSC licence provides credibility that can help open corporate bank accounts.

Options include:

Requirements for bank account opening typically include: certified copies of the licence, company documents, proof of office, and personal identification of directors. Some banks may require a minimum deposit or turnover thresholds.

Compliance and Trust

Operating a crypto gambling platform requires rigorous compliance to maintain your licence and avoid penalties. The FSC expects licensees to adhere to the highest standards of AML/CFT, data protection, and responsible gambling.

Key compliance obligations:

  • Appoint a Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO).
  • Conduct customer due diligence (CDD) and enhanced due diligence (EDD) for high-risk customers.
  • Implement transaction monitoring systems.
  • Submit suspicious transaction reports (STRs) to the Financial Intelligence Unit.
  • Maintain records for at least 5 years.
  • Conduct annual independent audits.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make several mistakes when applying for a crypto gambling license in Mauritius. Avoid these pitfalls:

  • Insufficient capital: Underestimating the minimum capital requirement (EUR 150,000 for full-service). The FSC will verify the source of funds.
  • Poor AML/CFT policies: Submitting generic or incomplete AML manuals. The FSC expects detailed, tailored procedures.
  • Lack of substance: Not having a physical office or local director. The FSC requires genuine presence.
  • Incomplete documentation: Missing business plans, financial projections, or personal declarations.
  • Choosing the wrong jurisdiction: Mauritius may not be suitable for all business models. Compare with Lithuania or Panama.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto gambling license in Mauritius?

For a full-service VASP licence covering gambling activities, the minimum capital is EUR 150,000. Lower tiers (EUR 50,000 or EUR 125,000) apply for limited services. The capital must be maintained and proven from legitimate sources.

How long does it take to get a Mauritius crypto gambling license?

The FSC typically processes applications within 4 to 6 months, assuming all documents are complete and no additional information is requested. Delays can occur if due diligence raises questions.

What is the tax rate for crypto gambling companies in Mauritius?

The standard corporate tax rate is 15%, but an 80% partial exemption on certain income can reduce the effective rate to as low as 3%. No capital gains tax or withholding tax on dividends.

Can I operate a crypto casino with a Mauritius license?

Yes, the VASP licence with a gambling endorsement allows you to operate a crypto casino, sportsbook, poker site, and other gambling services using virtual assets.

Do I need a physical office in Mauritius?

Yes, the FSC requires a registered office in Mauritius and a resident director (or local agent). This is part of the substance requirements.

Is the Mauritius license recognized in the EU?

No, the Mauritius license does not provide EU passporting. For EU-wide operations, consider a MiCA-compliant license from Lithuania or another EU member state.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations?

Licensees must submit annual audited financial statements, maintain AML/CFT policies, appoint an MLRO, conduct transaction monitoring, and report suspicious transactions. Annual licence fees also apply.

Can I use the Mauritius license to obtain a bank account?

Yes, the licence helps with bank account opening, but banks still conduct their own due diligence. Some local banks (MCB, SBM) work with licensed VASPs, though requirements vary.

What happens if I don't meet the capital requirement?

The FSC may reject the application or impose conditions. If capital falls below the minimum after licensing, the FSC can suspend or revoke the licence. Maintaining adequate capital is critical.
