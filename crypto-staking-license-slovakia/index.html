What is the Crypto Staking License in Slovakia?

The Slovak crypto staking license is a regulatory authorization issued by the National Bank of Slovakia (NBS) under the Act on Virtual Assets (Act No. 213/2022 Coll.), harmonized with MiCA since 2024. It allows companies to offer staking services as a virtual asset service provider (VASP). Staking is treated as a form of custody and/or exchange of virtual assets, depending on the business model.

Key features of the license include:

  • Full EU passporting rights under MiCA
  • Compliance with AML/CFT obligations
  • Minimum capital requirements aligned with MiCA tiers (EUR 50,000 / 125,000 / 150,000)
  • Annual reporting and audit requirements

For staking-only operations, the license typically falls under the custody and transfer service categories. Consulting24 advises on the correct classification for your specific offering. The license does not cover issuance of virtual assets or operation of a trading platform without additional authorization. Staking rewards are considered income from virtual asset services and must be reported accordingly. For example, a staking platform that also offers a swap feature may need to apply for the exchange class, increasing the capital requirement.

Who Needs a Slovak Crypto Staking License?

Any legal entity that provides staking services to clients in Slovakia or across the EU (via passporting) must obtain this license. This includes:

  • Staking-as-a-service platforms

  • Custodians that stake client assets
  • Delegated staking operators

Even if your company is based outside Slovakia, if you actively target Slovak residents, you may need to register. MiCA's 'passporting' regime means a Slovak license can cover the entire EU, making it attractive for pan-European staking businesses. License Type & Regulator

The regulator is the National Bank of Slovakia (NBS), which has been the competent authority for virtual asset service providers since 2023. The license is a single VASP authorization that covers multiple services, including staking. There is no separate 'staking license' - staking is included under the VASP scope if it involves custody or transfer.

Under MiCA, staking services are classified as 'custody and administration of virtual assets on behalf of clients' (Class 3) and/or 'exchange of virtual assets for funds or other virtual assets' (Class 2). The capital requirement depends on the classes applied for. The NBS also requires that the company's management body is fit and proper, and that the ultimate beneficial owners are disclosed. The NBS publishes a register of licensed VASPs, which enhances transparency and trust for clients. Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, capital requirements are tiered by service class. For staking, typical classes are:

  • Class 2 (Exchange): EUR 50,000 minimum
  • Class 3 (Custody): EUR 125,000 minimum
  • Combined (both): EUR 150,000 minimum

If your staking service involves both exchange and custody, the higher tier applies. The capital must be held in a Slovak bank account and maintained throughout the license period. Consulting24 advises on the exact requirement based on your business model and assists with bank account setup. For instance, a pure staking-as-a-service platform that only stakes client assets (custody) would need EUR 125,000, while a platform that also allows exchange of staking rewards would need EUR 150,000. Note that the capital can be in the form of cash or liquid assets, but must be unencumbered and available at all times. The NBS may require proof of capital maintenance in annual reports.

Tax Treatment of Staking in Slovakia

Slovakia applies a corporate income tax rate of 21% (15% for small businesses under EUR 100,000 revenue). Staking rewards are generally treated as taxable income at the time of receipt, valued at market price. Capital gains from sale of staked assets are also taxable.

VAT: Staking services are likely exempt from VAT under the EU VAT Directive, but professional advice is recommended. Slovakia has no specific crypto tax regime; general tax rules apply. Consulting24 can connect you with local tax advisors for detailed planning. For example, if a staking platform earns 100 ETH in rewards in a year, that amount is added to taxable income at the EUR value on the day of receipt. If the platform later sells the ETH at a higher price, the gain is subject to capital gains tax. Proper record-keeping is essential to support tax filings. Allowed Activities Under the License

The Slovak VASP license permits the following activities relevant to staking:

  • Custody and administration of virtual assets on behalf of clients
  • Exchange of virtual assets for funds or other virtual assets
  • Transfer of virtual assets
  • Receiving and transmitting orders for virtual assets

Staking itself is not explicitly listed but falls under custody and administration. The license does not cover issuance of virtual assets (e.g., ICOs) or operation of a trading platform without additional authorization. Consulting24 clarifies the exact scope for your business. For example, if your platform also offers a secondary market for staked tokens, that may require a separate license class. Similarly, if you offer a DeFi staking pool where users retain control of their private keys, the activity may not be considered custody and thus may not require a license - but this is a grey area that requires legal analysis. Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto staking license in Slovakia?

Under MiCA, the capital requirement depends on the service classes. For staking, if only custody (Class 3), EUR 125,000; if exchange (Class 2), EUR 50,000; if both, EUR 150,000. The exact amount depends on your business model. Consulting24 can help you determine the correct classification.

How long does it take to get a Slovak crypto staking license?

The typical timeline is 4-8 months from application submission. This includes 2-3 months for documentation preparation, 2-4 weeks for bank account opening, and up to 3 months for NBS review. Consulting24 coordinates the process to minimize delays.

Can I passport a Slovak staking license to other EU countries?

Yes, under MiCA, a Slovak VASP license allows you to provide services across all EU/EEA member states via passporting. You need to notify the NBS of your intent to operate in other countries. Consulting24 can assist with the notification process.

What are the tax implications for staking rewards in Slovakia?

Staking rewards are treated as taxable income at fair market value on the date of receipt. Corporate income tax is 21% (15% for small businesses). Capital gains from selling staked assets are also taxable. VAT is likely exempt. Professional tax advice is recommended.

Do I need a physical office in Slovakia for the license?

Yes, you need a registered office in Slovakia. This can be a virtual office or a shared workspace. Consulting24 can provide a registered address service. The NBS may require a physical presence for compliance purposes.

Can I apply for the license if my company is already registered in another country?

Yes, you can incorporate a Slovak subsidiary and apply for the license. The parent company must be disclosed in the application. Consulting24 helps with the incorporation and ensures compliance with Slovak corporate law.

What happens if I don't maintain the minimum capital?

The NBS may impose fines or revoke the license. You must maintain the capital at all times. If capital falls below the requirement, you must top it up immediately. Consulting24 provides ongoing compliance monitoring to help you avoid this.

Is staking considered a regulated activity under MiCA?
