What Is the Crypto Fund License Germany?

The crypto fund license Germany refers to the authorisation required by BaFin for managing alternative investment funds (AIFs) that invest in crypto assets. Under the German Capital Investment Code (KAGB), fund managers must obtain a license as a Kapitalverwaltungsgesellschaft (KVG) to operate an AIF. Crypto assets are treated as financial instruments, and funds investing in them are classified as special AIFs or retail AIFs depending on the investor base.

As of 2026, the EU's Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA) is fully in force, and Germany has integrated MiCA's requirements into its national framework. This means crypto fund managers must also comply with MiCA's capital tiers: EUR 50,000 for simple custody, EUR 125,000 for exchange services, and EUR 150,000 for more complex activities. For fund management specifically, the KAGB sets separate capital requirements (see below).

The license is mandatory for any entity that manages a fund investing in crypto assets, including venture capital funds, hedge funds, and real estate funds that hold crypto. It ensures investor protection, transparency, and operational integrity. The license is not required for purely proprietary trading or for funds that are not offered to third-party investors.

For example, a fund that invests in Bitcoin and Ethereum for accredited investors would need a KVG license. A venture capital fund that acquires tokens as part of its investment strategy would also fall under BaFin's scope. Who Needs This License?

The crypto fund license Germany is required by:

  • Real estate funds that accept crypto-denominated investments or hold crypto as a treasury asset.
  • Family offices or wealth managers offering pooled crypto investment vehicles to third-party investors.
  • Hedge funds that trade crypto assets or derivatives.

Exemptions may apply for funds with fewer than 20 investors or those that are purely private and do not solicit investments. However, most commercial fund structures require a license. License Type and Regulator

The relevant regulator is the Federal Financial Supervisory Authority (BaFin) in Bonn, Germany. The license type is a Kapitalverwaltungsgesellschaft (KVG) license under the KAGB. For crypto-focused funds, BaFin applies additional requirements under the German Banking Act (KWG) and MiCA, depending on the services provided.

There are two main categories: internal KVG (the fund itself is the manager) and external KVG (a separate legal entity manages the fund). Most applicants opt for an external KVG. The license covers the management of AIFs that invest in crypto assets, including portfolio management, risk management, and administration.

BaFin is known for its thorough review process, requiring detailed business plans, compliance manuals, and proof of capital. The regulator also conducts fit-and-proper tests on all managers and beneficial owners. Consulting24 advises and coordinates the preparation of these documents, working with German legal partners to ensure compliance with BaFin's expectations.

Compared to other EU regulators, BaFin is one of the most stringent, but this also means the license carries significant weight with investors and counterparties.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a KVG in Germany is EUR 50,000 for simple fund management activities. However, if the fund manager also provides custody or exchange services, MiCA's capital tiers apply: EUR 125,000 for exchange services and EUR 150,000 for more complex activities. For funds that are open to retail investors, BaFin may require higher capital, up to EUR 300,000.

Capital must be held in liquid assets and can be in the form of cash or bank guarantees. The capital must be fully paid up before the application is submitted. Consulting24 advises and coordinates the structuring of capital to meet BaFin's requirements. It is important to note that the capital requirement is not just a one-time cost; it must be maintained throughout the life of the fund.

For example, a fund that only manages a portfolio of crypto assets (no custody) would need EUR 50,000. A fund that also offers custody services would need EUR 125,000. A fund targeting retail investors might need EUR 300,000. Consulting24 can help you determine the exact amount based on your business model.

Tax Treatment

Germany has a favourable tax regime for investment funds. Under the German Investment Tax Act (InvStG), investment funds are generally tax-transparent, meaning income is taxed at the investor level. However, crypto funds may be subject to special rules. As of 2026, crypto assets held by a fund are treated as other assets, and capital gains from the sale of crypto are subject to the fund's corporate tax rate (15% plus solidarity surcharge and trade tax, effective rate around 30%).

Investors in the fund pay personal income tax on distributions and capital gains at their individual rate (up to 45%). For foreign investors, withholding tax may apply unless reduced by a double tax treaty. Consulting24 advises and coordinates tax structuring to optimise the fund's tax position. For example, structuring the fund as a special AIF for institutional investors can reduce tax leakage.

It is important to consult a German tax advisor for specific tax planning. The tax treatment can significantly impact net returns, especially for high-frequency trading funds. Consulting24 works with tax experts to ensure compliance and efficiency.

Allowed Activities

A licensed KVG in Germany can engage in the following activities:

  • Portfolio management of AIFs investing in crypto assets
  • Risk management for the fund
  • Administration and valuation of fund assets
  • Marketing of fund shares to professional and retail investors (subject to additional requirements)
  • Custody of crypto assets (if licensed under MiCA)
  • Execution of transactions in crypto derivatives

Activities not covered by the license include proprietary trading, lending, or issuing stablecoins. These require separate authorisation. Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto fund license in Germany?

The minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for simple fund management. If you also offer custody or exchange services, MiCA requires EUR 125,000 or EUR 150,000 respectively. For retail funds, BaFin may require up to EUR 300,000. Capital must be held in liquid assets and fully paid up before application.

How long does it take to get a German crypto fund license?

The process typically takes 6 to 12 months from engagement to license issuance. This includes document preparation, submission, and BaFin's review. The timeline can vary based on application completeness and BaFin's workload. Consulting24 advises and coordinates to expedite the process.

Can I manage a crypto fund without a license in Germany?

No, managing a crypto fund for third-party investors without a license is illegal in Germany. Exemptions exist for funds with fewer than 20 investors that do not solicit investments, but most commercial structures require a KVG license. Consulting24 can help determine if your fund qualifies for an exemption.

What are the tax implications for a German crypto fund?

German investment funds are generally tax-transparent, meaning income is taxed at the investor level. The fund itself pays corporate tax on capital gains (around 30% effective). Investors pay personal income tax on distributions and gains. Foreign investors may be subject to withholding tax unless a double tax treaty applies.

Does the German crypto fund license allow marketing across the EU?

Yes, the license allows marketing to professional investors across the EU under the AIFMD passport. For retail investors, additional requirements apply, such as a prospectus. This passport is a major advantage of the German license over non-EU jurisdictions like Panama.

Can I outsource custody of crypto assets for my German fund?

Yes, you can outsource custody to a third-party service provider, but BaFin must approve the arrangement. The custodian must be licensed under MiCA or equivalent. Consulting24 can help you select and contract with a suitable custodian.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations for a German crypto fund?

Ongoing obligations include annual audits, regular reporting to BaFin, AML/KYC procedures, and maintaining minimum capital. The fund must appoint a compliance officer and conduct periodic risk assessments. Non-compliance can lead to fines or license revocation.

How does the German crypto fund license compare to Panama?

Germany offers higher regulatory credibility and an EU passport but is more expensive (EUR 95,000-270,000) and slower (6-12 months). Panama costs a flat EUR 6,000 and takes 2-3 months, but lacks an EU passport and has a less established regulatory framework. Choose based on your target investor base and budget.

Is a physical office required in Germany for the license?

Can I apply for the license myself without a consultant?
