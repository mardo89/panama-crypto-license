What Is the Crypto Payment Institution License Germany?

The crypto payment institution license Germany is a regulatory authorisation issued by BaFin under the German Banking Act (KWG) and the Payment Services Supervision Act (ZAG), now fully integrated with the EU’s Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA). It allows companies to provide crypto asset services such as custody, exchange, transfer, and fiat payment initiation alongside crypto operations. This license is essential for any business that wants to offer crypto payment services to German residents or operate from Germany.

Key features of the license include mandatory membership in a compensation scheme, compliance with BaFin’s extensive AML obligations, and adherence to capital requirements that vary by service class. As of 2026, the minimum capital for a crypto payment institution ranges from EUR 50,000 to EUR 150,000 depending on the specific services offered. The license is not a simple registration; it is a full prudential authorisation. BaFin expects applicants to have a strong operational framework, including IT security, business continuity planning, and internal audit functions. The application process involves a detailed review of the company’s governance, risk management, and compliance policies. Who Needs This License?

Any company that intends to provide crypto asset services in Germany must obtain the crypto payment institution license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges offering fiat-to-crypto trading pairs
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Payment processors that handle both fiat and crypto transactions
  • Brokers and OTC desks dealing in digital assets
  • Issuers of crypto payment cards

If your business involves holding client funds or crypto assets, or initiating payment transactions in fiat or crypto, you fall under BaFin’s supervision. Consulting24 coordinates the application process for clients across all these business models, ensuring compliance with German and EU law.

Even if your target market is outside Germany, operating from Germany requires this license. For example, a crypto exchange serving French clients but based in Berlin must hold a BaFin license. Conversely, if your company is already licensed in another EU member state under MiCA, you can passport into Germany without a separate German license. License Type and Regulator

The regulator for the crypto payment institution license Germany is the Federal Financial Supervisory Authority (BaFin). BaFin is the primary financial regulator in Germany, responsible for overseeing banks, payment institutions, and now crypto asset service providers under MiCA. The license type is a “Crypto Payment Institution” (Kryptozahlungsinstitut) under the ZAG, which also covers CASP activities.

BaFin requires applicants to demonstrate fit and proper management, strong AML controls, adequate capital, and operational resilience. The application process involves a detailed business plan, organisational charts, AML policies, and proof of capital. Consulting24 advises on preparing these documents and coordinates with BaFin throughout the review period.

BaFin is known for its thoroughness. The regulator expects applicants to have a local presence, including a physical office in Germany and at least one managing director who resides in Germany. The management team must have relevant experience in financial services or crypto. Capital Requirement

The minimum capital for a crypto payment institution license Germany is determined by the services you offer. Under MiCA, the capital tiers are:

  • EUR 50,000 for custody and transfer services only
  • EUR 125,000 for exchange services (fiat-to-crypto or crypto-to-crypto)
  • EUR 150,000 for services that include payment initiation or account information

These amounts must be fully paid up in cash and held in a dedicated account. BaFin may require additional capital if your business volume or risk profile is high. Consulting24 advises clients on the exact capital needed and helps structure the capital deposit to satisfy BaFin.

The capital must be maintained at all times. If the company incurs losses that reduce capital below the minimum, BaFin may require a capital injection or restrict activities. Consulting24 provides ongoing compliance monitoring to ensure capital adequacy is maintained.

Tax Treatment

Germany has a favourable tax treatment for crypto assets. Private investors are exempt from capital gains tax on crypto held for more than one year. For businesses, crypto transactions are subject to corporate income tax (approx. 15% plus trade tax) and VAT is generally not applicable on crypto-to-fiat exchanges. However, mining and staking income may be treated as business income. Consulting24 coordinates with German tax advisors to ensure your company’s tax structure is optimised. As of 2026, the tax landscape remains stable, but professional advice is essential.

Businesses should also consider trade tax, which varies by municipality (typically 7-17%). The effective corporate tax rate in Berlin, for example, is around 30%. Consulting24 can introduce you to tax advisors who specialise in crypto taxation in Germany.

Allowed Activities

With a crypto payment institution license Germany, you can perform the following activities under BaFin supervision:

  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency and vice versa
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets
  • Execution of payment transactions involving crypto assets
  • Transfer of crypto assets between addresses
  • Provision of crypto payment services (e.g., crypto cards)
  • Reception and transmission of orders for crypto assets

These activities are fully aligned with the MiCA CASP definitions. Consulting24 helps clients define their service scope and ensures the license covers all intended activities. If you plan to offer additional services later, you may need to apply for a license extension.

Common Mistakes in the Application

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing AML policies or a weak business plan are the top reasons for delays.
  • Underestimating capital: Some applicants try to minimise capital, but BaFin expects the full amount.
  • Lack of local presence: BaFin requires a physical office and at least one local manager.
  • Ignoring tax implications: Failure to structure tax properly can lead to unexpected liabilities.
  • Choosing the wrong service scope: Overly broad or narrow scope can cause rework.

Alternatives and Comparisons
