What Is the Crypto Token Issuance License Thailand?

The crypto token issuance license Thailand, officially known as the ICO Portal and ICO Issuer license, is a regulatory approval from the SEC of Thailand. It permits a company to issue digital tokens to investors, either as investment tokens (which give rights such as profit sharing or voting) or utility tokens (which provide access to a product or service). The license is part of Thailand's comprehensive Digital Asset Business regulation, which also covers exchanges, brokers, dealers, and fund managers.

Key features include:

  • Issuers must be a public company limited (PLC) or a limited company with a minimum paid-up capital of THB 5 million (approximately EUR 130,000 as of 2026).
  • Token offerings must be conducted through an approved ICO Portal, which acts as a gatekeeper and due diligence agent.
  • The SEC reviews the token whitepaper, business plan, and compliance measures before approving the issuance.
  • Ongoing reporting obligations include annual financial statements and material change disclosures.

This license is distinct from a crypto exchange license or other digital asset business licenses. It is specifically for companies that want to create and sell their own tokens.

Who Needs This License?

Any legal entity that intends to offer digital tokens to the public in Thailand or to Thai residents must obtain the crypto token issuance license. This includes:

  • Startups conducting an ICO to raise capital for a blockchain project.
  • Established companies tokenizing assets (real estate, art, commodities) for fractional ownership.
  • Businesses issuing utility tokens for access to a platform or service.
  • Foreign companies targeting Thai investors - they must either set up a Thai subsidiary or apply through a local ICO Portal.

Entities that do not need this license include those issuing tokens in a private placement to institutional investors (under certain exemptions) or tokens that are clearly not securities (e.g., simple reward points not transferable). However, the SEC has broad discretion, so legal advice is essential. Consulting24 advises clients on whether their token qualifies as a digital asset under Thai law and whether an exemption applies.

License Type & Regulator

The regulator is the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) of Thailand, specifically its Digital Asset Division. The license type is an ICO Issuer License, which is part of the Digital Asset Business Operator license categories. There are two main roles:

  • ICO Portal - An intermediary that facilitates token issuance, performs due diligence, and ensures compliance. ICO Portals themselves are licensed by the SEC.
  • ICO Issuer - The company that issues the tokens. The issuer must engage an ICO Portal to submit the application and oversee the offering.

The SEC has published detailed rules under the Emergency Decree on Digital Asset Businesses B.E. 2561 (2018) and subsequent notifications. The regulatory framework is mature and actively enforced. For comparison, other jurisdictions like Lithuania and Estonia have different models, but Thailand's is one of the most specific for token issuance.

Capital Requirement

The minimum paid-up capital for a company applying for a crypto token issuance license in Thailand is THB 5 million (approximately EUR 130,000 as of 2026). This capital must be maintained throughout the license period. The company must be a public company limited (PLC) or a limited company with this capital. In practice, the SEC may require higher capital depending on the size and risk of the token offering. For example, if the issuance is large (over THB 100 million), the SEC might ask for additional capital buffers. This requirement is separate from the costs of the application itself. Compare this to Panama, where no specific capital requirement exists for token issuance, or Lithuania where the EU's MiCA capital tiers apply (EUR 50,000 to 150,000).

Tax Treatment

Thailand's tax treatment for crypto token issuance is evolving. As of 2026, the following applies:

  • Corporate Income Tax (CIT): Proceeds from token issuance are generally considered taxable income. However, the SEC and Revenue Department have provided guidance that certain token issuances may be treated as capital rather than revenue, subject to conditions. Typically, if the tokens represent equity-like rights, the proceeds may be treated as share capital (non-taxable). For utility tokens, proceeds are often recognized as deferred revenue and taxed when the service is delivered.
  • Value Added Tax (VAT): The sale of digital tokens is exempt from VAT under the Revenue Code, as confirmed by the Revenue Department.
  • Withholding Tax: Payments to token holders (e.g., dividends from investment tokens) may be subject to 10% withholding tax for individuals and 15% for corporations, depending on the nature.
  • Personal Income Tax: Investors who sell tokens on secondary markets are subject to 15% withholding tax on gains (for individuals) or corporate tax (for companies).

Tax treatment can be complex and depends on the token's classification. Consulting24 advises clients to engage a Thai tax specialist for definitive advice.

Allowed Activities

With a crypto token issuance license, a company can:

  • Issue investment tokens and utility tokens to the public through an ICO Portal.
  • Market the token offering to Thai residents and international investors (subject to foreign securities laws).
  • Receive funds (THB or cryptocurrencies) from investors in exchange for tokens.
  • List the tokens on approved digital asset exchanges in Thailand after the issuance.
  • Conduct follow-on offerings or token buybacks with SEC approval.

Activities NOT allowed:

  • Operating a trading platform (exchange) - requires a separate exchange license.
  • Providing custody services for third-party assets - requires a custodian license.
  • Issuing tokens that are clearly securities without full compliance with the Securities and Exchange Act.

The license is specific to issuance; after the ICO, the company must comply with ongoing reporting and may need additional licenses for secondary market activities.

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Feasibility Study & Legal Structuring: Determine token type, rights, and legal structure. Engage a Thai law firm with SEC experience.
  2. Company Setup: Incorporate a Thai limited company or PLC with minimum paid-up capital of THB 5 million. Open a corporate bank account.
  3. Engage an ICO Portal: Select an SEC-approved ICO Portal. The Portal will conduct due diligence on the project and assist with the application.
  4. Draft Whitepaper & Documentation: Prepare a detailed whitepaper, business plan, risk disclosures, and token terms. The Portal reviews and submits to the SEC.
  5. Submit Application to SEC: The ICO Portal files the application on behalf of the issuer. The SEC reviews within 60 days (extendable).
  6. SEC Approval & Token Issuance: Upon approval, the issuer can launch the ICO through the Portal. Tokens are distributed to investors.
  7. Post-Issuance Compliance: File annual reports, disclose material changes, and maintain capital. Tokens can be listed on exchanges.

Consulting24 advises on each step, particularly company setup and coordination with local partners. For a faster route, consider Panama where the process is simpler and cheaper (EUR 6,000 flat).

Banking & Payments

Thailand's banking system is open to crypto businesses, but compliance is strict. To open a corporate bank account for a token issuance company, you typically need:

  • SEC license or proof of application.
  • Business plan and source of funds.
  • Physical presence in Thailand (directors may need to appear).

Major banks like Kasikornbank, Bangkok Bank, and Siam Commercial Bank have digital asset divisions. However, they conduct enhanced due diligence. Payment processing for ICOs is usually handled through the ICO Portal, which manages fiat and crypto collection. Investors can pay in THB or cryptocurrencies like USDT. After the ICO, the issuer receives funds through the Portal. For ongoing operations, a standard business bank account suffices. Benefits of a Thailand Crypto Token Issuance License

  • Legal Clarity: Thailand has one of the most specific regulatory frameworks for token issuance in Asia, reducing legal uncertainty.
  • Access to Thai Investors: The license allows you to tap into a growing retail and institutional investor base in Thailand.
  • Tax Exemptions: VAT exemption on token sales and potential capital treatment for investment tokens.
  • Gateway to ASEAN: Thailand's central location and trade agreements provide access to Southeast Asian markets.
  • Progressive Regulation: The SEC actively engages with industry, and the framework is updated regularly (e.g., recent rules on stablecoins and security tokens).

Compared to Dubai (VARA) or Switzerland, Thailand offers a lower cost of entry and easier access to a large retail investor market.

Compliance & Trust

Maintaining the license requires ongoing compliance. Key obligations include:

  • Annual financial statements audited by a Thai auditor.
  • Disclosure of any material changes (e.g., change of directors, business model).
  • Anti-money laundering (AML) and know-your-customer (KYC) procedures for token sales (usually handled by the ICO Portal).
  • Capital maintenance (minimum THB 5 million).
  • Reporting to the SEC on token holder statistics and secondary market trading.

Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. The SEC conducts periodic inspections. Consulting24 advises on establishing a compliance framework. Please note that this is general guidance, not legal advice. You should engage a Thai lawyer for specific compliance matters.

Common Mistakes

  • Incorrect Token Classification: Misclassifying a security token as a utility token can lead to rejection or enforcement. Get a legal opinion early.
  • Insufficient Capital: The THB 5 million capital must be fully paid up and maintained. Some companies try to reduce capital post-approval, which is not allowed.
  • Choosing the Wrong ICO Portal: Not all portals are equal. Some have slow processes or poor due diligence. Verify their SEC approval and track record.
  • Ignoring Tax: Proceeds from token sales may be taxable. Plan for tax liabilities from the start.
  • Overlooking Ongoing Reporting: Many issuers focus on the ICO and neglect post-issuance obligations, risking license revocation.

Frequently asked questions

What is the crypto token issuance license Thailand?

It is a license from the SEC of Thailand that allows a company to issue digital tokens (investment or utility) to the public through an ICO Portal. It is required for any public token sale in Thailand.

How much does the Thailand token issuance license cost?

Total costs range from approximately EUR 47,000 to EUR 156,000, including SEC fees, ICO Portal service, legal advisory, and documentation. Exact pricing depends on project complexity.

What is the minimum capital requirement?

The minimum paid-up capital is THB 5 million (about EUR 130,000). This must be maintained throughout the license period.

How long does it take to get the license?

The SEC review takes 3-6 months after a complete submission. Including preparation, total timeline is typically 6-10 months.

What taxes apply to token issuance in Thailand?

VAT is exempt on token sales. Corporate income tax treatment varies: investment tokens may be treated as capital (non-taxable), while utility tokens are deferred revenue. Withholding tax applies to distributions.

Can a foreign company apply for the license?

Yes, but the issuing entity must be a Thai company (PLC or limited company) with local directors. Foreign companies can set up a subsidiary and then apply.

What is an ICO Portal?

An ICO Portal is an SEC-licensed intermediary that conducts due diligence on token issuers, submits applications, and facilitates the token sale. Issuers must engage a Portal.

Is the Thailand license recognized in other countries?

No, it is specific to Thailand. For international offerings, you may need additional licenses in target jurisdictions. However, the SEC license adds credibility.

Can I issue tokens without a license?

Only in very limited exemptions (e.g., private placement to institutional investors). Otherwise, unlicensed public issuance is illegal and can result in criminal penalties.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

Annual audited financial statements, disclosure of material changes, AML/KYC procedures, and capital maintenance. The SEC may conduct inspections.

How does Thailand compare to Panama for token issuance?

Panama is cheaper (EUR 6,000 flat), faster (2-4 weeks), and has no capital requirement or tax on foreign income. Thailand offers a regulated market with access to local investors but is more expensive and slower.
