What Is the Singapore Crypto License?

The Singapore crypto license is not a single license but a registration under the Payment Services Act (PSA). MAS requires any business providing digital payment token (DPT) services - such as exchanges, custodians, or OTC desks - to hold a Major Payment Institution (MPI) license or be a Standard Payment Institution (SPI). The MPI license is the most common for crypto firms due to higher transaction limits.

Key features of the regime:

  • Regulator: Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS)
  • Legislation: Payment Services Act (PSA) and Payment Services Regulations
  • License type: Major Payment Institution (MPI) for DPT services
  • Application fee: S$100,000 (non-refundable) for MPI
  • Annual variable capital requirement: Minimum S$250,000 base, plus additional based on payment volume

The PSA was amended in 2021 to bring DPT services under MAS oversight. As of 2026, MAS has granted over 20 MPI licenses to crypto firms, with many more applications pending. The regime is considered one of the most stringent in Asia, requiring detailed AML/CFT policies, regular audits, and a physical presence in Singapore. Who Needs a Singapore Crypto License?

Any entity that provides DPT services in Singapore or to Singapore residents must apply for an MPI license. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (centralized and decentralized)
  • Digital asset custodians
  • OTC trading desks
  • Payment facilitators using DPTs
  • Issuers of stablecoins (subject to additional stablecoin framework)

Exemptions exist for small-scale operations under the SPI threshold, but most crypto businesses will need an MPI license. For example, a crypto exchange handling more than S$3 million monthly in DPT transactions must apply for an MPI license. Even if you operate outside Singapore but serve Singapore residents, the requirement applies. The MAS has extraterritorial reach for marketing and solicitation.

Capital Requirement

The base capital for an MPI license is S$250,000. However, MAS imposes an annual variable capital requirement based on the average monthly transaction volume of DPT services. The formula is:

  • First S$6 million of monthly volume: 0% additional
  • S$6 million to S$30 million: 1% of the excess
  • Above S$30 million: 2% of the excess

This means a firm with S$50 million monthly volume needs base S$250,000 + (S$24 million x 1%) + (S$20 million x 2%) = S$250,000 + S$240,000 + S$400,000 = S$890,000. Capital must be held in liquid assets in Singapore. For firms with lower volume, the variable capital may be minimal. For example, a startup with S$10 million monthly volume would need S$250,000 + (S$4 million x 1%) = S$290,000. The capital must be maintained at all times and reported annually.

Tax Treatment

Singapore offers a territorial tax system. Corporate income tax is 17% on profits. Capital gains are not taxed, and there is no capital gains tax on crypto. However, trading profits are taxable as income. MAS and IRAS treat DPT transactions as subject to GST (9%) only when provided to Singapore residents (B2C). For B2B, GST is zero-rated. Specific tax incentives for financial institutions may apply. Consulting24 recommends engaging a Singapore tax advisor for your structure. For example, the Financial Sector Incentive (FSI) scheme can reduce tax rate to 10% for qualifying income. However, crypto firms must ensure their activities qualify. Additionally, withholding tax on payments to non-residents may apply. Proper tax planning is essential.

Allowed Activities

An MPI license permits the following DPT services:

  • Buying and selling of digital payment tokens (exchange)
  • Facilitating the exchange of DPTs
  • Providing custodial services for DPTs
  • Cross-border money transfer using DPTs
  • Merchant acquisition services for DPTs

Activities not covered: issuing DPTs (except under stablecoin framework), derivatives, or securities tokens (require Capital Markets Services license). MAS prohibits lending of customer assets without specific approval. For example, a crypto exchange that offers margin trading may need additional licensing under the Securities and Futures Act. Similarly, if you offer staking services, MAS may consider it a regulated activity. Consulting24 can help you map your business model to the regulatory framework.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Pre-application: Determine if your activities require an MPI license. Consulting24 offers a gap analysis to identify missing requirements.
  2. Prepare documentation: Business plan, AML/CFT policies, risk assessment, financial projections, organizational structure, and compliance manual. The AML manual must be detailed, covering customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious transaction reporting.
  3. Appoint key personnel: At least one resident director, compliance officer, AML officer, and CEO. All must be fit and proper. MAS requires the compliance officer to be based in Singapore.
  4. Submit application: Via MAS’s online portal with S$100,000 fee. MAS will acknowledge and assign a case officer. You must also submit a notification of your intention to apply at least 30 days before submission.
  5. Review and queries: MAS may request additional information or meetings. Average 6-12 months. During this period, you may be required to submit periodic updates.
  6. Approval: If granted, you must operationalize within 6 months. Ongoing supervision includes annual audits and reporting. MAS may conduct on-site inspections.

Banking & Payments

Opening a corporate bank account in Singapore for a crypto firm is challenging but possible. Major banks like DBS, OCBC, and UOB have strict compliance. Alternatives include digital banks (e.g., Revolut, Wise) or specialized payment service providers. Consulting24 can recommend banking partners that accept crypto-licensed entities. For fiat on/off ramps, consider partnerships with local payment gateways. Some banks require a minimum deposit of S$50,000 and may take 2-3 months to open an account. It is advisable to start the banking process early, even before license approval, to show operational readiness. MAS also requires a settlement account with a Singapore bank for DPT transactions.

Benefits of a Singapore Crypto License

  • Regulatory clarity: MAS is a respected, clear regulator with a well-defined framework.
  • Tax efficiency: No capital gains tax, territorial system, and potential incentives.

Compliance & Trust

MAS requires ongoing compliance: annual AML/CFT audits, transaction monitoring, and reporting. Firms must maintain a compliance officer and MLRO. Failure to comply can result in fines up to S$1 million or license revocation. Consulting24 provides compliance advisory to ensure you meet MAS standards. Note: This is general guidance, not legal advice. Always consult a Singapore-qualified lawyer. For example, MAS expects transaction monitoring systems to flag suspicious activity in real time. Firms must also conduct regular training for staff. The annual audit must be conducted by an external auditor approved by MAS. Non-compliance can lead to public reprimands, which damage reputation.

Common Mistakes

  • Underestimating capital: Variable capital can be much higher than base. For example, a firm with S$100 million monthly volume needs over S$1.5 million in capital.
  • Incomplete documentation: MAS rejects applications with weak AML policies. A common mistake is using generic templates without customization.
  • Ignoring fit and proper criteria: Directors with criminal records will be rejected. Even past regulatory issues in other jurisdictions can be a problem.
  • Not planning for ongoing costs: Annual supervisory fees and audit costs add up. Budget at least S$100,000 per year for compliance.
  • Delaying banking: Open bank accounts early to show operational readiness. Some firms wait until license approval, which delays launch.
  • Assuming SPI is enough: Most crypto firms exceed the SPI transaction limit quickly, so applying for MPI directly is better.

Frequently asked questions

What is the total cost of a Singapore crypto license in 2026?

The total first-year cost for an MPI license ranges from S$413,000 to S$1,355,000, including the S$100,000 application fee, base capital of S$250,000, variable capital, supervisory fees, and compliance costs. Exact figures depend on transaction volume and business complexity.

How long does it take to get a Singapore crypto license?

MAS typically takes 6 to 12 months to process an MPI application. The timeline can be longer if documents are incomplete or if MAS requests additional information. Consulting24 advises on thorough preparation to minimize delays.

What is the minimum capital for a Singapore crypto license?

The base capital for an MPI license is S$250,000. However, an annual variable capital requirement applies based on transaction volume, which can increase the total capital needed to S$500,000 or more.

Is the Singapore crypto license tax-free?

No, but Singapore has no capital gains tax. Corporate income tax is 17% on profits. GST (9%) applies to DPT services provided to Singapore residents. B2B services are zero-rated. Tax incentives may be available.

Can I apply for a Singapore crypto license from abroad?

Yes, but you must incorporate a Singapore company and appoint at least one resident director. MAS requires the entity to have a physical presence in Singapore. Consulting24 can assist with company setup.

What activities are allowed under the Singapore MPI license?

Allowed activities include operating a cryptocurrency exchange, custodial services, OTC trading, and facilitating DPT transfers. Issuing DPTs or dealing in derivatives requires additional licenses.

How does the Singapore license compare to Panama?

The Panama license costs EUR 6,000 flat with no minimum capital, while Singapore costs S$400,000+. Panama is faster (2-3 months) but has less regulatory depth. Choose based on your target market and budget.

What are the ongoing compliance costs for a Singapore crypto license?

Ongoing costs include annual supervisory fees (S$10,000-S$50,000), external audit (S$20,000-S$50,000), compliance officer salary (S$80,000-S$150,000), and transaction monitoring tools. Budget at least S$100,000 per year.

What happens if MAS rejects my application?

Do I need a physical office in Singapore for the license?

Yes, MAS requires a physical presence, including a registered office and a compliance officer based in Singapore. You can use a serviced office initially.
