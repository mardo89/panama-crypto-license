What Is the BVI Crypto Payment Institution License?

The BVI crypto payment institution license is a regulatory authorization issued by the BVI Financial Services Commission (FSC) that allows a company to provide virtual asset payment services, including the exchange, transfer, and custody of cryptocurrencies. The license falls under the VASP regime, which was introduced to align with FATF recommendations and to regulate anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorist financing (CTF) compliance. Unlike some jurisdictions that treat crypto as a commodity, the BVI classifies virtual assets as property, and payment institutions must adhere to the SIBA framework.

Key features of the license include:

  • Regulated by the BVI FSC under the Securities and Investment Business Act (SIBA) and the VASP Code.
  • Allows both fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto payment services.
  • Requires a physical presence in the BVI, including a registered office and a local director or manager.
  • Subject to ongoing AML/CTF obligations, including transaction monitoring and reporting.

Who Needs a BVI Crypto Payment Institution License?

This license is designed for businesses that offer virtual asset payment services to clients, including:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (both centralised and peer-to-peer platforms).
  • Payment processors that facilitate crypto payments for merchants.
  • Wallet providers that offer custodial or non-custodial wallet services.
  • Companies that issue stablecoins or other virtual asset tokens.
  • Any entity that transfers virtual assets on behalf of clients.

If your business model involves holding client funds or facilitating the transfer of virtual assets, you likely need this license. The BVI FSC requires that any person carrying on virtual asset business in or from the BVI must be licensed. This includes entities that are incorporated in the BVI but operate internationally. License Type and Regulator

The BVI crypto payment institution license is a specific category under the VASP regime, regulated by the BVI Financial Services Commission (FSC). The FSC is the sole regulatory authority for financial services in the BVI, including banking, insurance, and securities. For virtual assets, the FSC has issued the Virtual Asset Service Provider Code, 2022, which sets out the licensing conditions, capital requirements, and compliance obligations.

There are two main types of VASP licenses:

  • Class A (Full License): Allows the holder to conduct all virtual asset activities, including holding client funds and operating a trading platform. This is the typical license for payment institutions.
  • Class B (Restricted License): For smaller operators with limited activities, such as only providing wallet services without holding client funds.

The BVI does not have a separate "payment institution" license for crypto; instead, the VASP license covers payment services. The FSC requires that applicants have a physical office in the BVI and at least one director who is a resident of the BVI.

Capital Requirement

The BVI FSC imposes minimum capital requirements for VASP license holders. As of 2026, the capital requirement is based on the type of license and the volume of transactions. For a Class A license (full payment institution), the minimum capital is USD 50,000. This capital must be maintained as liquid assets and cannot be used for operational expenses. For Class B licenses, the minimum capital is lower, typically around USD 20,000.

In addition to the initial capital, the FSC requires that the company maintain a minimum net worth at all times, which is calculated based on the company's liabilities and risk exposure. The capital requirement is subject to adjustment based on the volume of virtual asset transactions. For high-volume operators, the FSC may require additional capital to cover operational risks. It is important to note that these figures are subject to change, and we recommend confirming the exact requirement with the FSC or a local advisor. Tax Treatment

One of the main advantages of the BVI is its tax-neutral regime. The BVI does not impose corporate income tax, capital gains tax, or withholding tax on companies that are tax resident in the BVI but do not conduct business locally. This means that a BVI crypto payment institution license holder can operate globally without paying BVI corporate tax on its profits. However, the company may be subject to tax in the countries where it has a physical presence or where its clients are located.

The BVI also has no value-added tax (VAT) or goods and services tax (GST). The only annual government fees are the license renewal fee and the registered agent fee, which are relatively low. It is important to note that the BVI has implemented economic substance requirements, which require companies to have a physical presence and management in the BVI if they are conducting relevant activities. For crypto payment institutions, the FSC expects that the company has a substantive presence, including a local office and at least one local director. Failure to meet substance requirements can result in penalties or license revocation.

Allowed Activities

A BVI crypto payment institution license allows the holder to engage in a range of virtual asset activities, as defined by the VASP Code. The permitted activities include:

  • Exchange between virtual assets and fiat currencies.
  • Exchange between one or more forms of virtual assets.
  • Transfer of virtual assets on behalf of clients.
  • Custodial and non-custodial wallet services.
  • Participation in and provision of financial services related to an issuer's offer or sale of a virtual asset.

The license does not automatically allow the issuance of virtual assets (e.g., launching a new cryptocurrency) unless that activity is specifically approved. Additionally, the FSC prohibits certain activities, such as operating a gambling platform that uses virtual assets or engaging in activities that facilitate money laundering. The license is subject to ongoing compliance with AML/CTF regulations, including customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and reporting suspicious transactions to the BVI Financial Intelligence Agency.

Compliance and Trust

Maintaining a BVI crypto payment institution license requires ongoing compliance with the FSC's regulations. This includes:

  • Annual AML/CTF audits by an external auditor.
  • Submission of annual returns and financial statements to the FSC.
  • Maintaining a physical office and local director in the BVI.
  • Reporting suspicious transactions to the BVI Financial Intelligence Agency.
  • Ensuring that the company's capital remains above the minimum requirement.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

When applying for a BVI crypto payment institution license, founders often make the following mistakes:

  • Insufficient documentation: The FSC requires a detailed business plan, AML policies, and financial projections. Incomplete applications are rejected or delayed.
  • Underestimating capital requirements: The minimum capital of USD 50,000 must be maintained at all times, and some businesses need more based on transaction volume.
  • Lack of physical presence: The FSC requires a registered office and a local director. Using a virtual office without a real presence can lead to rejection.
  • Ignoring substance requirements: The BVI has economic substance rules, and companies must demonstrate that they are managed and controlled from the BVI.
  • Choosing the wrong license type: Applying for a Class A license when a Class B would suffice (or vice versa) can waste time and money.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a BVI crypto payment institution license?

The minimum capital for a Class A license is USD 50,000, which must be maintained as liquid assets. For Class B licenses, the minimum is USD 20,000.

How long does it take to get a BVI crypto payment institution license?

The application process typically takes 3 to 6 months, depending on the completeness of the application and the FSC's review time.

Does the BVI have corporate tax for crypto payment institutions?

No, the BVI does not impose corporate income tax, capital gains tax, or VAT. However, economic substance requirements apply.

Can I operate a crypto exchange with a BVI payment institution license?

Yes, the license allows exchange between virtual assets and fiat currencies, as well as between virtual assets, subject to FSC approval.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements for a BVI crypto license?

Ongoing requirements include annual AML audits, submission of financial statements, maintaining a physical office, and reporting suspicious transactions.

Is a physical presence required in the BVI?

Yes, the FSC requires a registered office and at least one local director or manager. A virtual office alone is not sufficient.

Can Consulting24 help with the BVI crypto license application?

Yes, Consulting24 advises and coordinates the application process, including company incorporation, document preparation, and liaison with the FSC.

How does the BVI license compare to Panama?

Panama offers a lower flat fee of EUR 6,000 and no capital requirement, but the BVI provides a more established regulatory framework and zero tax on global income.

What activities are prohibited under the BVI crypto license?

The license does not allow gambling services using virtual assets, nor any activity that facilitates money laundering or terrorist financing.

Can I use a BVI license to serve clients in the EU?

Yes, but you must comply with local regulations in the EU, including MiCA. The BVI license alone does not grant EU passporting rights.
