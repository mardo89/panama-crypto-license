What Is a Crypto Staking License in Thailand?

A crypto staking license in Thailand is a regulatory authorization issued by the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) Thailand under the Digital Asset Business Decree B.E. 2561 (2018). It allows licensed operators to offer staking services, where customers lock their digital assets to support blockchain network operations and earn rewards. The license falls under the broader category of digital asset exchange or broker/dealer licenses, depending on the business model.

Thailand's regulatory framework is one of the most advanced in Asia, with clear rules on custody, disclosure, and investor protection. Staking is treated as a regulated activity, meaning providers must meet capital, operational, and compliance standards. The SEC actively supervises licensed entities to ensure market integrity and consumer safety.

Who Needs a Crypto Staking License in Thailand?

Any business offering staking services to Thai residents or operating from Thailand must obtain the appropriate license. This includes:

  • Centralized exchanges that allow users to stake supported assets
  • Staking-as-a-service platforms
  • DeFi protocols that facilitate staking for Thai users (if deemed to be operating a digital asset business)
  • Brokers or dealers who promote staking products

Foreign companies targeting Thai customers are also subject to licensing requirements. The SEC has extraterritorial reach if services are directed at Thai residents. License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) Thailand. The license type is a Digital Asset Business License, specifically for digital asset exchange or digital asset broker/dealer. Staking is considered a service that may be offered under an exchange license or as part of a broader digital asset business. The SEC categorizes staking as a regulated activity requiring separate approval if it involves custody or management of customer assets.

Thailand does not have a standalone "staking license" but includes staking within the scope of licensed digital asset operators. The SEC has issued guidelines on staking services, requiring operators to disclose risks, reward mechanisms, and lock-up periods. As of 2026, the regime is fully enforced, with periodic audits and reporting obligations.

Capital Requirements

The SEC Thailand requires digital asset businesses to maintain minimum registered capital. For exchange licenses, the minimum is THB 25 million (~EUR 640,000). For broker/dealer licenses, the minimum is THB 5 million (~EUR 128,000). Staking services typically fall under the exchange category if they involve custody, so the higher capital threshold applies. Capital must be paid up and maintained throughout the license period.

In addition to registered capital, operators must hold a security deposit or guarantee bond, often 5-10% of capital. These requirements ensure financial stability and investor protection. Tax Treatment for Staking in Thailand

Thailand imposes corporate income tax at 20% on net profits for licensed digital asset operators. Staking rewards earned by the platform (e.g., fees or commissions) are taxable as revenue. For customers, staking rewards are considered assessable income under the Revenue Code, subject to personal income tax (0-35% progressive rates). The SEC and Revenue Department have issued guidance clarifying that staking rewards are taxable at the point of receipt.

Value Added Tax (VAT) at 7% applies to digital asset transaction fees but may be exempt for certain services. Operators must register for VAT if annual turnover exceeds THB 1.8 million. Allowed Activities Under a Thailand Crypto Staking License

A licensed digital asset operator in Thailand may offer the following staking-related activities:

  • Facilitating staking of supported cryptocurrencies (e.g., ETH, ADA, DOT)
  • Operating staking pools where customers delegate assets
  • Providing staking-as-a-service with automated reward distribution
  • Offering staking as part of exchange services (e.g., exchange staking)
  • Managing validator nodes on proof-of-stake networks

Activities not allowed include unregistered lending of staked assets, offering guaranteed returns, or engaging in activities that resemble unlicensed fund management. Step-by-Step Application Process

The application process for a crypto staking license in Thailand involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step.

  1. Pre-application assessment: Determine the appropriate license type (exchange or broker/dealer) based on your business model.
  2. Company registration: Incorporate a Thai company with the Ministry of Commerce. Minimum two shareholders, one director.
  3. Capital injection: Deposit the required registered capital in a Thai bank account.
  4. Document preparation: Prepare business plan, AML/CFT policies, IT security framework, financial projections, and governance structure.
  5. Submission: File the application with the SEC along with the application fee.
  6. Review and interview: The SEC reviews the application and may request additional information or an interview with key personnel.
  7. Approval and licensing: Upon approval, pay the annual license fee and receive the license.
  8. Post-licensing compliance: Submit regular reports, undergo audits, and maintain capital.
