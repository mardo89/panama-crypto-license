What Is a Ready Made Czech Republic Crypto License?

A ready made Czech Republic crypto license refers to a pre-registered company that has already obtained the required authorization from the Czech National Bank to provide crypto asset services. Instead of going through the entire application process from scratch, you acquire an existing entity that is already licensed, subject to regulatory approval of the change of beneficial ownership.

This option is particularly attractive for founders who want to enter the EU market quickly. The Czech Republic offers a relatively straightforward regulatory environment compared to some other EU member states, and the ready made route can cut setup time by several months.

Key features include:

  • Pre-registered company with a valid CNB license
  • Compliance with Czech AML Act and EU MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets) regulation
  • Ability to start operations faster than a new application
  • Requires due diligence and regulatory approval for ownership transfer

License Type and Regulator

The relevant regulatory authority is the Czech National Bank (CNB). Under the Czech Act on Anti-Money Laundering (AML Act) and EU MiCA regulation (fully in force as of 2026), crypto asset service providers must register with the CNB and comply with AML/CFT obligations.

The license type is a registration for virtual currency service providers. It covers activities such as exchange between crypto and fiat, exchange between different crypto assets, crypto custody, and wallet services. The CNB oversees compliance and can impose sanctions for non-compliance, including fines and license revocation.

As of 2026, MiCA introduces EU-wide standards, but the Czech Republic has implemented national provisions that align with MiCA. The ready made license ensures the entity is already compliant with these requirements. The CNB also conducts periodic inspections and requires annual reporting.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA (in force 2026), the Czech Republic applies capital requirements based on the type of services offered. For most crypto asset service providers, the minimum capital is:

  • EUR 50,000 for exchange services (crypto-to-fiat and crypto-to-crypto)
  • EUR 125,000 for custody and wallet services
  • EUR 150,000 for combined services or those involving higher risk

These figures are in line with MiCA's tiered approach. The ready made entity should already meet these capital requirements, but you must verify that the capital is fully paid up and available. The capital must be held in a Czech bank account or as specified by the CNB.

Tax Treatment

The Czech Republic offers a competitive tax environment for crypto businesses. Corporate income tax is a flat 19% (as of 2026). Capital gains from crypto trading are generally subject to corporate tax, but there are exemptions for long-term holdings (held for more than 3 years) under certain conditions.

Value Added Tax (VAT) is not applicable to crypto-to-fiat exchange transactions, as they are considered exempt financial services. However, other services like advisory or custody may be subject to VAT at the standard rate of 21%.

Personal income tax for individuals is progressive, but for corporate structures, the flat rate applies. There are no specific crypto taxes beyond the general corporate tax. However, transfer pricing rules apply if you transact with related parties.

Allowed Activities Under the Czech Crypto License

A registered crypto asset service provider in the Czech Republic can engage in the following activities under the CNB registration and MiCA:

  • Exchange between virtual currencies and fiat currencies
  • Exchange between virtual currencies
  • Transfer of virtual currencies
  • Custody and administration of virtual currencies
  • Wallet services (safekeeping of private keys)
  • Participation in initial coin offerings (ICOs) and security token offerings (STOs) (subject to additional securities regulation)

Activities not covered include derivatives or securities trading unless separately licensed. The ready made license should specify the exact scope of permitted activities. Banking and Payment Solutions

Access to banking is critical for crypto businesses. In the Czech Republic, several banks accept crypto companies, but they require proof of CNB registration and strong AML procedures. Options include:

  • Traditional banks (e.g., CSOB, Komercni Banka) - require a local presence and compliance review; account opening can take 1-3 months.
  • Neobanks and EMI accounts (e.g., Revolut, Wise) - may offer faster setup but with transaction limits and higher fees.
  • Crypto-friendly payment processors (e.g., Coinbase Commerce, BitPay) for fiat on/off ramps, but these are not bank accounts.

Banks typically require a physical presence in the Czech Republic, a local director, and a business plan. Some may ask for a minimum deposit or turnover. Compliance and Trust

Compliance is non-negotiable. The CNB expects all crypto service providers to implement strong AML/CFT measures, including:

  • Customer due diligence (CDD) and enhanced due diligence (EDD) for high-risk clients
  • Transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting
  • Appointment of a local AML officer (must be a Czech resident)
  • Regular audits and reporting to the CNB (annual AML report, financial statements)

Additionally, under MiCA, you must have a complaints handling procedure, a conflict of interest policy, and business continuity arrangements. The CNB can conduct on-site inspections and impose fines up to EUR 5 million or 10% of annual turnover for serious breaches.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Skipping Due Diligence: Not all ready made entities are clean. Verify the company's history, regulatory standing, and any hidden liabilities. For example, the entity might have unpaid taxes or prior AML breaches.
  • Ignoring Capital Requirements: Ensure the entity meets MiCA capital thresholds and that funds are not locked. If the capital is insufficient, you may need to inject additional funds.
  • Underestimating Banking Setup: Banking can take time; start the process early. Some banks require a minimum period of operation before opening an account.
  • Neglecting Local Presence: A registered office and local AML officer are mandatory. The registered office cannot be a virtual office; it must be a physical address.
  • Assuming Passporting is Automatic: Notify the host member state and comply with local rules. Passporting requires filing a notice with the CNB and the host regulator, and you may need to appoint a local agent.

What is a ready made Czech Republic crypto license?

A ready made Czech Republic crypto license is a pre-registered company that already holds a CNB authorization for crypto services. You acquire the entity (subject to regulatory approval) instead of applying from scratch, which can reduce setup time to 2-4 months.

What capital is required for a Czech crypto license?

Under MiCA, minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for exchange services, EUR 125,000 for custody, and EUR 150,000 for combined services. The ready made entity should already meet these requirements.

Yes, under MiCA, a Czech license allows you to provide services across the EU by notifying the host member state. You must comply with local AML rules and may need to appoint a local agent.
