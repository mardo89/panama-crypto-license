What is the Crypto Payment Institution License in Kazakhstan?

The Crypto Payment Institution License is a regulatory authorization issued by the Astana Financial Services Authority (AFSA) under the AIFC. It allows companies to offer crypto payment services, including processing payments, exchanging fiat for crypto, and safeguarding client digital assets. The license is part of the AIFC's Digital Asset Framework, which was updated in 2025 to align with FATF recommendations and global best practices.

Unlike some jurisdictions that offer a generic VASP registration, Kazakhstan's license is specifically tailored for payment institutions. It requires a legal entity registered in the AIFC, which benefits from a common law legal system based on English law. The license is valid for one year and renewable, with ongoing supervision by AFSA. The framework distinguishes between payment services and other crypto activities, meaning you cannot operate a full exchange under this license without additional authorization.

For example, a company that wants to process merchant payments in crypto and convert them to fiat would need this license. A pure crypto exchange that does not handle fiat would require a separate exchange license. The AIFC also offers a sandbox environment for innovative projects, which can be a stepping stone to a full license.

Who Needs This License?

This license is designed for businesses that intend to provide crypto payment services to customers in Kazakhstan or from the AIFC. Typical applicants include:

  • Payment processors that handle crypto-to-fiat conversions for merchants
  • Digital wallet providers offering crypto storage and transfers with fiat on-ramp/off-ramp
  • Exchanges that facilitate peer-to-peer or merchant payments in crypto
  • Remittance services using blockchain technology for cross-border transfers
  • Issuers of crypto-backed payment cards or prepaid instruments

If your business model involves holding client funds or executing payment transactions in crypto, you likely need this license. It is not required for pure mining operations, software development without custody of assets, or providing blockchain analytics. For instance, a company offering only non-custodial wallet software may not need a license, but adding fiat conversion would trigger the requirement.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Astana Financial Services Authority (AFSA), the independent financial regulator of the AIFC. The license type is a “Crypto Payment Institution License” under the AIFC Digital Asset Framework. AFSA oversees compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) rules, client asset protection, and operational standards.

Key features of the license include:

  • Authorized to provide payment services in crypto assets
  • Must maintain a physical presence in the AIFC, including a registered office and local management
  • Subject to ongoing reporting and audits, including annual financial statements and AML reports
  • Compliance with FATF Travel Rule for transactions above a threshold (typically EUR 1,000)
  • Fit and proper test for directors and significant shareholders

AFSA has the power to impose conditions on the license, such as limiting the volume of transactions or requiring additional capital. The regulator also conducts on-site inspections and can revoke the license for non-compliance. Consulting24 can help you prepare for these requirements.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a Crypto Payment Institution License in Kazakhstan is typically set at the equivalent of EUR 50,000, though the exact amount may vary based on the scope of services. This capital must be held in a bank account in the AIFC and be fully paid up before licensing. The capital serves as a buffer for operational risks and client protection. For firms offering additional services like custody, the requirement may be higher, up to EUR 125,000.

The capital must be in liquid form, such as cash or cash equivalents. It cannot be in crypto assets. AFSA may require proof of source of funds. Consulting24 can confirm the precise figure for your specific service class during a consultation. It is important to note that the capital requirement is separate from the application fees and must be maintained throughout the license period.

Tax Treatment

The AIFC offers a favorable tax regime. Companies licensed under the AIFC are exempt from corporate income tax for the first 10 years (until 2036). There is no VAT on financial services, including crypto transactions. However, income from mining or trading outside the AIFC may be subject to standard Kazakhstan corporate tax (20%). The AIFC also has double taxation treaties with many countries, including the UK, UAE, and Singapore.

For example, if your company earns fees from payment processing within the AIFC, those are tax-free. But if you also mine crypto using local energy, that mining income may be taxed at 20%. It is advisable to structure your operations to maximize tax benefits. Consulting24 can coordinate with local tax experts for tailored advice. The tax holiday is a major advantage over EU jurisdictions where corporate tax rates are 15-20%.

Allowed Activities

Under the Crypto Payment Institution License, you may conduct the following activities:

  • Processing crypto payment transactions for merchants and individuals
  • Exchanging crypto assets for fiat currency and vice versa
  • Providing crypto wallet services (custodial and non-custodial)
  • Facilitating remittances using crypto
  • Issuing crypto-backed payment instruments (e.g., prepaid cards)

Activities not permitted include operating a crypto exchange without a separate exchange license, or offering derivatives without a securities license. The license is strictly for payment services. If you need a full exchange license, consider other jurisdictions like Lithuania or Estonia, which offer MiCA-compliant CASP licenses. For example, if you want to run a spot exchange with order books, you would need an exchange license, not a payment institution license.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Company Incorporation: Register a legal entity in the AIFC. This requires a local registered office and a director (can be foreign). The company must have a physical office in Astana.
  2. Document Preparation: Prepare a business plan, AML/KYC policies, risk management framework, financial projections, and organizational structure. Engage a local compliance consultant.
  3. Application Submission: Submit the application to AFSA along with the fee. AFSA will conduct a fit and proper test on directors and shareholders, including background checks.
  4. Review and Interview: AFSA may request additional information or conduct an interview with key personnel. This step can take 1-2 months.
  5. License Issuance: Upon approval, the license is granted for one year. Annual renewal requires ongoing compliance, including submission of audited financial statements and AML reports.

Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step, liaising with local lawyers and AFSA to ensure a smooth process. The entire timeline typically takes 3 to 6 months. A common pitfall is underestimating the time needed for document preparation; start early.

Compliance and Trust

Maintaining the license requires ongoing compliance with AML/CFT regulations, including customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting. AFSA conducts periodic inspections and requires annual audits. Failure to comply can result in fines or revocation. The AIFC also requires adherence to the FATF Travel Rule for transactions above EUR 1,000, meaning you must share originator and beneficiary information with counterparties.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing business plans or AML policies can delay the process by months. Use templates from Consulting24 to ensure completeness.
  • Underestimating capital: Ensure you have the full capital requirement in liquid funds, not crypto. Some applicants fail because they cannot prove source of funds.
  • Ignoring local presence: The AIFC requires a physical office and at least one local director (or a representative). Renting a virtual office is not sufficient.
  • Overlooking renewal: The license must be renewed annually; start the process 3 months before expiry. Late renewal can result in penalties.
  • Not budgeting for ongoing costs: Annual compliance costs (audit, AML officer, reporting) can be EUR 10,000-20,000. Plan accordingly.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto payment institution license in Kazakhstan?

The minimum capital is typically EUR 50,000, though the exact amount may vary based on the services offered. This must be held in an AIFC bank account. Consulting24 can confirm the precise figure for your case.

How long does it take to get the license?

The process usually takes 3 to 6 months from company incorporation to license issuance. Delays can occur if documentation is incomplete or if AFSA requests additional information.

Can I operate a crypto exchange under this license?

No, this license is for payment services only. To operate a crypto exchange, you need a separate exchange license from AFSA. However, you can offer exchange as part of payment processing if it is incidental.

Is the AIFC tax holiday really 10 years?

Yes, as of 2026, companies licensed under the AIFC are exempt from corporate income tax for 10 years (until 2036). This applies to income generated within the AIFC. Income from outside the AIFC may be taxed at standard rates.

Do I need a local director in Kazakhstan?

Yes, the AIFC requires at least one local director or a representative. This person must be physically present in Astana. Consulting24 can help you find a suitable candidate.

Can I use the license to serve customers outside Kazakhstan?

Yes, the license allows you to serve customers globally, but you must comply with the laws of the customer's jurisdiction. The AIFC framework is designed for international business.

What are the ongoing compliance costs?

Annual compliance costs include audit fees (EUR 5,000-10,000), AML officer salary (EUR 20,000-40,000), and reporting software (EUR 2,000-5,000). Total ongoing costs are typically EUR 10,000-20,000 per year.

Is the license renewable?

Yes, the license is valid for one year and must be renewed annually. Renewal requires submission of audited financial statements, AML reports, and payment of a renewal fee (typically EUR 2,000-3,000).

What happens if I fail to comply with AML rules?

Non-compliance can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. AFSA may also impose restrictions on your activities. It is crucial to maintain a strong AML framework.

How does Kazakhstan compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Panama offers a flat fee of EUR 6,000 with no specific crypto license, making it faster and cheaper for offshore operations. Kazakhstan provides a regulated environment with a 10-year tax holiday but higher costs and longer timeline. Choose based on your need for regulatory credibility and market access.
