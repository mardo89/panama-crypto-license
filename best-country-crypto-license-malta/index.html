What Is the Malta Crypto License?

The Malta crypto license, officially the VFA (Virtual Financial Assets) licence, is a regulatory authorisation issued by the MFSA. It allows businesses to operate cryptocurrency exchanges, wallet providers, and other VFA services legally in Malta and across the EU under MiCA passporting. The regime is based on the VFAA and subsidiary legislation, requiring compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and conduct-of-business rules.

Malta's framework is one of the first comprehensive crypto regulatory systems globally, designed to foster innovation while protecting consumers. As of 2026, the MFSA continues to refine its approach, aligning with MiCA's full implementation. The licence covers a range of activities, from order execution to portfolio management, each with specific capital and governance requirements.

For businesses targeting EU clients, a Malta VFA licence is a strategic choice. However, the process is rigorous and requires significant preparation. Consulting24 advises and coordinates every step, from initial assessment to post-licence compliance.

Who Needs a Malta Crypto License?

Any business offering virtual financial asset services in or from Malta must obtain a VFA licence. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralised and decentralised platforms)
  • Wallet providers (custodial and non-custodial)
  • Brokerage services for crypto assets
  • Asset management firms dealing in VFAs
  • Initial VFA offerings (IVFAOs, similar to ICOs)

If you plan to serve EU clients from Malta, a VFA licence is mandatory. Even if your operations are outside Malta, offering services to Maltese residents triggers the licensing requirement. Consulting24 advises and coordinates applications for a wide range of business models, including startups and established firms.

Exemptions exist for certain activities, such as software development or advisory services that do not involve custody or trading. However, most commercial crypto businesses fall under the VFA regime.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Malta Financial Services Authority (MFSA). The licence type is the VFA Licence, categorised into four classes based on the services provided:

ClassServicesMinimum Capital
Class 1Receiving and transmitting orders, placing ordersEUR 50,000
Class 2Dealing on own account, underwriting, placingEUR 125,000
Class 3Portfolio management, investment adviceEUR 150,000
Class 4Operating a VFA exchange (MTF)EUR 150,000

These capital requirements align with MiCA tiers. Note that the MFSA may impose additional capital based on risk profile. For example, a high-volume exchange may need EUR 200,000 or more. The licence class determines the scope of permitted activities and the level of regulatory scrutiny.

Capital Requirement

Minimum capital requirements are set by the VFAA and range from EUR 50,000 to EUR 150,000 depending on the class of licence (see table above). This capital must be maintained at all times and can be in cash or liquid assets. The MFSA may require higher capital based on the business's risk assessment, operational scale, and client base.

For example, a Class 4 exchange with high transaction volumes may need EUR 250,000 or more. Capital must be held in a Maltese bank account or equivalent. The MFSA also requires that capital be unencumbered and available to cover operational risks.

For a lower-cost option, Panama has no minimum capital requirement, making it attractive for startups. Consulting24 can advise on capital planning for both jurisdictions.

Tax Treatment

Malta offers a favourable tax regime for crypto businesses. The standard corporate tax rate is 35%, but effective rates can be as low as 5% through Malta's full imputation system and tax refunds on distributed profits. Crypto transactions are treated as trading income or capital gains depending on the business model. VAT treatment: supplies of VFAs are exempt from VAT under current rules.

For example, if your company earns EUR 100,000 in profit and distributes dividends, shareholders can claim a refund of 6/7 of the tax paid, reducing the effective rate to 5%. This makes Malta one of the most tax-efficient jurisdictions in Europe for crypto businesses.

Consulting24 recommends a detailed tax review with a Maltese tax advisor to optimise your structure. For comparison, Panama taxes only locally sourced income, offering zero tax on foreign earnings.

Allowed Activities

A Malta VFA licence permits the following activities:

  • Operating a VFA exchange (order-matching platform)
  • Providing VFA wallet services (custodial and non-custodial)
  • Brokerage and dealing in VFAs
  • Portfolio management of VFAs
  • Investment advice on VFAs
  • Underwriting and placing of VFAs
  • Receiving and transmitting VFA orders

Activities must be conducted in compliance with AML/CFT obligations and MiCA requirements. The MFSA prohibits certain activities like unlicensed derivatives or gambling-related tokens. For instance, offering crypto derivatives requires a separate MiFID licence.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a Malta VFA licence involves the following steps:

  1. Pre-application: Engage a compliance consultant (e.g., Consulting24) to assess your business model and prepare documentation.
  2. Business plan and policies: Draft a detailed business plan, AML/CFT policies, risk assessment, and governance framework.
  3. Submit application: File the application with the MFSA, including all required documents and the application fee.
  4. MFSA review: The MFSA reviews the application, may request additional information, and conducts a fit-and-proper assessment of key persons.
  5. Interview: Key persons may be interviewed by the MFSA to assess their competence and integrity.
  6. Licence issuance: If approved, the licence is granted, subject to ongoing supervision.
  7. Post-licence: Maintain compliance, submit regular reports, and pay annual fees.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Many applicants face delays or rejection due to avoidable errors:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing business plans or AML policies.
  • Unfit key persons: Lack of relevant experience or criminal records.
  • Underestimating capital: Not maintaining the minimum capital requirement.
  • Ignoring local substance: Not having a physical office and local directors.
  • Poor AML framework: Weak policies that do not meet MFSA standards.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Malta crypto license?

The minimum capital ranges from EUR 50,000 to EUR 150,000 depending on the class of licence. Class 1 requires EUR 50,000, while Classes 2-4 require EUR 125,000-150,000. The MFSA may require additional capital based on risk. For example, a high-volume exchange may need EUR 200,000.

How long does it take to get a Malta crypto license?

The MFSA typically processes applications within 6 months, but complex cases can take up to 12 months. The timeline depends on the completeness of your application and the regulator's workload. Consulting24 helps streamline the process to avoid delays.

Can a Malta crypto license be used to serve clients across the EU?

Yes, under MiCA, a Malta VFA licence allows passporting to other EU member states, meaning you can provide services across the EU without additional licences. This is a key advantage over non-EU jurisdictions like Panama.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements for a Malta VFA licence?

Ongoing requirements include AML/CFT compliance, regular reporting to the MFSA, annual audits, and maintaining minimum capital. You must also appoint a local MLRO and conduct periodic risk assessments. Non-compliance can lead to fines or licence revocation.

Is Malta tax-friendly for crypto businesses?

Yes, Malta offers effective tax rates as low as 5% through its imputation system and refunds on distributed profits. Crypto transactions are treated as trading income or capital gains. VAT on VFA supplies is exempt. A tax advisor can help optimise your structure.

What activities are not allowed under a Malta VFA licence?

Activities such as unlicensed derivatives, gambling-related tokens, or services that do not meet AML standards are prohibited. For example, offering crypto futures requires a MiFID licence. The MFSA provides a list of excluded activities in its rulebook.

Can I apply for a Malta crypto license remotely?

Yes, but you must have local substance, including a physical office and at least one director resident in Malta. Consulting24 can help you set up the necessary infrastructure, including registered office and local representation.

How does Malta compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Malta offers EU passporting and a strong reputation but costs EUR 40,000-100,000+ and requires minimum capital of EUR 50,000-150,000. Panama costs a flat EUR 6,000 with no minimum capital and no tax on foreign income, but no EU access. Choose based on your target market.

What documents are needed for a Malta VFA application?

Key documents include a business plan, AML/CFT policies, risk assessment, governance framework, financial projections, and fit-and-proper declarations for key persons. Consulting24 prepares these documents to MFSA standards.

Does Consulting24 guarantee approval for a Malta crypto license?
