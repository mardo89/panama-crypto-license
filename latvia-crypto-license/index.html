What is the Latvia Crypto License?

The Latvia crypto license, officially a Crypto-Asset Service Provider (CASP) registration under MiCA, is issued by the Bank of Latvia. It allows companies to legally offer crypto services such as exchange, custody, and transfer within Latvia and across the EU under the single passport. Unlike the pre-MiCA era where Latvia had a simple registration with no minimum capital, the 2026 regime imposes strict AML/CFT obligations, governance requirements, and capital thresholds based on service types.

Key features of the Latvia CASP license include:

  • Full compliance with MiCA, enabling EU-wide operations via passporting.
  • Supervision by the Bank of Latvia and the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) for AML.
  • Capital requirements starting at EUR 50,000 for basic services, up to EUR 150,000 for complex activities.
  • Ongoing reporting, audit, and compliance obligations.

Who Needs a Latvia Crypto License?

The Latvia crypto license is essential for any business that intends to provide crypto-asset services to residents of Latvia or other EU member states. This includes startups, fintech companies, and established financial institutions offering one or more of the following services:

  • Exchange of crypto-assets for fiat currency or other crypto-assets.
  • Operation of a trading platform for crypto-assets.
  • Custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients.
  • Transfer services for crypto-assets (e.g., payment processing).
  • Placement of crypto-assets (underwriting or selling).
  • Advice on crypto-assets (investment advice).

If your business model involves any of these activities and you target EU clients, you need a CASP license in an EU member state. Latvia is an attractive choice due to its efficient regulatory process, competitive costs, and favorable tax treatment. Even if you already have a license in another EU country, you may need to register a branch or obtain a top-up authorization in Latvia if you plan to establish a physical presence there.

License Type and Regulator

The Latvia crypto license is a single license type: the Crypto-Asset Service Provider (CASP) authorization under MiCA. The primary regulator is the Bank of Latvia (Latvijas Banka), which oversees the licensing and ongoing supervision of CASPs. The Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) of Latvia handles AML/CFT compliance and reporting.

Unlike some jurisdictions that segregate licenses by service class (e.g., exchange vs. custody), Latvia's CASP license covers all services, but the capital requirement varies by the scope of activities. The license is non-transferable and requires a physical office in Latvia (or a registered branch if the company is foreign).

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for a Latvia crypto license depends on the type of services offered. As of 2026, the tiers are:

  • EUR 50,000 for basic services: exchange between crypto and fiat, exchange between crypto-assets, and transfer services.
  • EUR 125,000 for services including custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients.
  • EUR 150,000 for full-service CASPs offering all services, including operation of a trading platform and placement.

The capital must be held in a bank account in Latvia or a credit institution in the EU, and it must be fully paid up before license issuance. The capital can be used for operational expenses but must maintain the minimum at all times. Tax Treatment

Latvia offers a competitive tax regime for crypto businesses. Key points:

  • Corporate income tax (CIT): Latvia applies a 20% CIT rate on distributed profits (i.e., when dividends are paid). Reinvested profits are tax-deferred, which is beneficial for growing companies.
  • VAT: Crypto-asset services (exchange, transfer, custody) are generally exempt from VAT under EU law, as confirmed by the European Court of Justice. Latvia follows this exemption, meaning 0% VAT on crypto services.
  • Personal income tax: Crypto gains for individuals are taxed at 20% (if held over 12 months) or 23% (if held less). For corporate entities, gains are part of corporate profit.
  • Withholding tax: No withholding tax on dividends paid to EU residents; 10% for non-EU residents (subject to tax treaties).

Allowed Activities Under the Latvia Crypto License

The Latvia CASP license permits the following activities, as defined by MiCA:

  • Exchange of crypto-assets for fiat currency.
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for other crypto-assets.
  • Operation of a crypto-asset trading platform (order book, AMM, etc.).
  • Custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients (including private key management).
  • Transfer of crypto-assets on behalf of clients (including payment processing).
  • Placement of crypto-assets (underwriting or selling new tokens).
  • Receiving and transmitting orders for crypto-assets.
  • Providing advice on crypto-assets.
  • Portfolio management of crypto-assets.

Activities not covered include issuing stablecoins (which require a separate e-money license) and decentralized finance (DeFi) protocols that are fully non-custodial. The license does not permit banking activities or lending without additional authorization.

Compliance and Trust

Maintaining a Latvia crypto license requires ongoing compliance. Key obligations include:

  • AML/CFT: Implement transaction monitoring, customer due diligence (CDD), and suspicious transaction reporting to the FIU.
  • Governance: Appoint a compliance officer, a money laundering reporting officer (MLRO), and a board with relevant experience.
  • Reporting: Submit annual audited financial statements, compliance reports, and capital maintenance reports to the Bank of Latvia.
  • Audit: Engage an external auditor approved by the regulator.
  • Insurance: Professional indemnity insurance may be required, depending on services.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Latvia crypto license?

The minimum capital depends on the services: EUR 50,000 for basic exchange and transfer services, EUR 125,000 for custody services, and EUR 150,000 for full-service CASPs. These figures are set by MiCA and are current as of 2026.

How long does it take to get a Latvia crypto license?

The Bank of Latvia has a statutory review period of 3 months. In practice, the entire process from application to approval takes 3 to 6 months, depending on the completeness of documentation and complexity of the case.

Can I use a Latvia crypto license to operate in other EU countries?

Yes, under MiCA's passporting regime, a Latvia CASP license allows you to provide services across all EU member states without additional licensing, subject to notification requirements.

What are the tax benefits of a Latvia crypto license?

Latvia offers a 20% corporate income tax on distributed profits only; reinvested profits are tax-deferred. Crypto services are VAT-exempt. Personal crypto gains are taxed at 20-23% depending on holding period.

Do I need a physical office in Latvia?

Yes, the Bank of Latvia requires a registered office and a physical presence in Latvia. This can be a rented office space, not a virtual office. Consulting24 can help you find suitable premises.

What documents are required for the application?

Key documents include: business plan, AML/CFT policy, risk assessment, governance framework, financial projections, capital proof, and personal documents of directors and shareholders. All must be in Latvian or English with certified translations.

Can I apply for a Latvia crypto license if my company is already registered in another EU country?

Yes, you can set up a branch in Latvia or incorporate a new subsidiary. The process is similar, but you may need to provide additional documentation about the parent company. Consulting24 can advise on the best structure.

Is the Latvia crypto license recognized by banks?

Yes, licensed CASPs in Latvia can open bank accounts with major banks like Swedbank, SEB, and Citadele. However, banks conduct their own due diligence, so a clean compliance record is essential.

What happens if I don't maintain the minimum capital?

The Bank of Latvia requires the capital to be maintained at all times. Failure to do so can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. Regular reporting ensures compliance.

How does Latvia compare to Lithuania for crypto licensing?

Both are MiCA-compliant with similar capital requirements. Latvia has a slightly higher corporate tax rate (20% vs 15% in Lithuania) but offers tax deferral on reinvested profits. Lithuania has a larger fintech ecosystem. Consulting24 can help you compare.
