What Is a Crypto OTC Desk License in Bahrain?

A crypto OTC desk license in Bahrain is a regulatory authorisation issued by the Central Bank of Bahrain (CBB) under the Crypto Asset Module (CRA). It permits a company to operate an over-the-counter trading desk for crypto assets, facilitating direct trades between buyers and sellers without using a public order book. OTC desks typically handle large volumes and are used by institutional investors, high-net-worth individuals, and other licensed entities.

The license is part of Bahrain's comprehensive crypto asset regime, which also covers exchange, custody, and advisory services. OTC desks are considered a type of crypto asset service provider (CASP). The CBB requires firms to meet strict capital, governance, and AML/CFT standards. As of 2026, the regime is fully operational and aligned with international best practices.

Key features include: a defined regulatory framework, a clear licensing process, and ongoing supervision. The CBB also publishes a public register of licensed firms, enhancing transparency. For founders, the license provides credibility and access to the Bahraini market and the wider GCC region.

Who Needs a Crypto OTC Desk License in Bahrain?

Any entity that wishes to provide OTC trading services for crypto assets in or from Bahrain must hold a CBB license. This includes:

  • Dedicated OTC desks that match buyers and sellers off-exchange
  • Brokerage firms offering block trading of crypto assets
  • Market makers providing liquidity via OTC channels
  • Family offices or asset managers executing large trades for clients

The license is mandatory for both Bahrain-based companies and foreign firms targeting Bahraini clients. Even if your desk operates globally, serving Bahrain residents triggers the licensing requirement. Exemptions may apply for very limited activity, but most commercial OTC operations need authorisation.

License Type and Regulator

The crypto OTC desk license in Bahrain falls under the Crypto Asset Module (CRA) of the Central Bank of Bahrain (CBB). The CBB is the sole regulator for crypto asset services in Bahrain. The license type is a Category 1 or Category 2 CASP license, depending on the scope of activities. For OTC desks, a Category 1 license is typically required, as it covers dealing in crypto assets as principal or agent.

The CBB's regulatory framework includes:

  • Capital adequacy requirements
  • Client asset protection rules
  • Market conduct standards
  • Anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CFT) obligations
  • Reporting and audit requirements

As of 2026, the CBB has not yet implemented MiCA-style tiered capital, but the capital requirement for OTC desks is generally around EUR 125,000 (or equivalent in BHD) as a minimum. Capital Requirement

The Central Bank of Bahrain requires a minimum capital for crypto OTC desk license holders. As of 2026, the typical minimum is EUR 125,000 (or equivalent in Bahraini Dinars). This capital must be maintained at all times and can be in cash or liquid assets. The exact amount may vary based on the scale of operations and risk profile.

Capital must be deposited in a Bahraini bank account and is subject to verification by the CBB. The funds cannot be used for operational expenses; they serve as a buffer for client protection and operational risk. Higher capital may be required if the firm plans to hold client assets or engage in proprietary trading.

Tax Treatment for OTC Desks in Bahrain

Bahrain offers a highly favourable tax environment. There is no corporate income tax for most businesses, including crypto OTC desks. No capital gains tax, no VAT, and no withholding tax on dividends or interest. The only tax is a 1% social insurance contribution for Bahraini employees. This makes Bahrain one of the most tax-efficient jurisdictions for crypto operations.

However, firms must comply with economic substance requirements. The CBB expects licensed entities to have a physical presence, including an office and local staff. Transfer pricing rules apply if the entity transacts with related parties. Consulting24 advises on substance planning and tax compliance. Allowed Activities Under the License

A crypto OTC desk license in Bahrain permits the following activities:

  • Matching and executing OTC trades of crypto assets between clients
  • Providing price quotes and acting as a principal in trades
  • Settlement of trades (delivery versus payment or free delivery)
  • Client onboarding and KYC/AML checks
  • Holding client assets (if custodial services are separately licensed)

Additional activities such as operating a crypto exchange, providing custody, or offering advisory services require separate authorisation or a broader license. The CBB may restrict the types of crypto assets that can be traded (e.g., only Bitcoin and Ethereum initially). Stablecoins and utility tokens may require additional approval.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Pre-application consultation: Discuss your business model and determine the appropriate license category with Consulting24.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Bahraini company (e.g., WLL or SPC) with the Ministry of Industry and Commerce. Obtain a commercial registration (CR).
  3. Prepare documentation: Draft a business plan, AML/CFT policy, risk management framework, and governance structure. Engage a local AML officer.
  4. Submit application: File the application with the CBB along with all supporting documents and the application fee.
  5. Regulatory review: The CBB reviews the application, conducts background checks on directors and shareholders, and may request additional information.
  6. Capital deposit: Deposit the minimum capital in a Bahraini bank account and provide proof to the CBB.
  7. On-site inspection (if required): The CBB may visit your office to verify physical presence and systems.
  8. License issuance: Upon approval, the CBB issues the license. You can then commence OTC operations.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Underestimating capital requirements: Ensure you have the full EUR 125,000 available and can maintain it.
  • Incomplete documentation: The CBB requires detailed business plans and policies. Incomplete submissions cause delays.
  • Ignoring substance requirements: A physical office and local staff are mandatory. Virtual offices are not acceptable.
  • Choosing the wrong license category: OTC desks need Category 1; applying for a lower category may limit activities.
  • Neglecting ongoing compliance: After licensing, firms must submit regular reports and undergo audits.
