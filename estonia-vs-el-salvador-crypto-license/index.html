What Is the Estonia vs El Salvador Crypto License Comparison?

This comparison examines the key differences between obtaining a crypto license in Estonia versus El Salvador in 2026. Estonia's crypto license is a full authorization under the EU's Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) regulation, requiring compliance with strict capital, AML, and governance standards. El Salvador's crypto license, issued by the National Digital Assets Commission (CNAD), is part of a more permissive legal framework that encourages innovation but lacks the harmonization of EU law.

Both jurisdictions allow a range of crypto activities, but the costs, timelines, and ongoing obligations differ significantly. Understanding these differences is essential for founders seeking a compliant and cost-effective licensing route. For example, Estonia's license provides passporting rights across the European Economic Area, while El Salvador's license is limited to its domestic market. This fundamental distinction influences everything from capital requirements to tax treatment.

Who Needs a Crypto License in Estonia or El Salvador?

Any business offering crypto services such as exchange, wallet custody, trading, or payment processing needs a license in both jurisdictions. In Estonia, the license is mandatory for all virtual asset service providers (VASPs) under MiCA. In El Salvador, the Digital Assets Law requires a license for similar activities, including Bitcoin-related services.

Typical applicants include:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralized and decentralized)
  • Wallet providers
  • Payment processors using crypto
  • Token issuers and ICO platforms
  • DeFi protocols (if offering custody or exchange services)

Both jurisdictions welcome international founders, but Estonia requires a local legal entity and physical presence, while El Salvador allows remote setup with local representation. For startups with limited budgets, Panama offers a flat fee of EUR 6,000 with no capital requirement, making it an attractive alternative.

License Type & Regulator

Estonia: The regulator is the Financial Supervision Authority (Finantsinspektsioon). Under MiCA, the license is a 'CASP authorization' (Crypto Asset Service Provider). It covers multiple service classes with corresponding capital requirements.

El Salvador: The regulator is the National Digital Assets Commission (Comisión Nacional de Activos Digitales, CNAD). The license is a 'Digital Asset Service Provider' (DASP) authorization, governed by the Digital Assets Law (Ley de Activos Digitales).

Both regulators enforce AML/CFT compliance, but El Salvador's framework is newer and less prescriptive, offering more flexibility in some areas. For example, El Salvador does not mandate a specific AML officer certification, whereas Estonia requires a certified compliance officer.

Capital Requirement

Estonia (MiCA): Minimum capital is tiered by service class: EUR 50,000 for custody/wallet services, EUR 125,000 for exchange services, and EUR 150,000 for trading platforms. These funds must be maintained in a local bank account and cannot be borrowed or used.

El Salvador: The Digital Assets Law does not specify a fixed minimum capital. However, the CNAD assesses each applicant's financial standing. In practice, a minimum of USD 50,000 - 100,000 is expected, but this can be lower for smaller operations. No mandatory bank account in El Salvador is required; funds can be held abroad. This flexibility can reduce initial costs for startups.

If capital is a constraint, consider Panama, which has no capital requirement. For more on capital rules, visit our requirements page.

Tax Treatment

Estonia: Corporate income tax is 0% on retained profits; tax is only due when profits are distributed (dividends). This makes Estonia attractive for reinvesting profits. VAT is 20% on crypto services, but exemptions may apply for certain activities (e.g., exchange of fiat to crypto). The tax system is simple and transparent.

El Salvador: Corporate income tax is 30% (standard rate). However, crypto-related income may qualify for exemptions under the Digital Assets Law, especially for Bitcoin-related services. No VAT on crypto transactions. El Salvador offers a 15-year tax holiday for new digital asset businesses in certain zones, subject to approval. This can significantly reduce the effective tax rate for qualifying companies.

For tax-efficient structures, many founders also evaluate Lithuania or Panama.

Allowed Activities

Estonia (MiCA): Licensed activities include: custody and administration of crypto assets, operation of a trading platform, exchange of crypto for fiat or other crypto, execution of orders, placement of crypto assets, and transfer services. DeFi and NFT platforms may require additional authorization. The license covers all crypto assets defined under MiCA, including stablecoins and utility tokens.

El Salvador: The DASP license covers: exchange, custody, payment processing, token issuance, and operation of digital asset platforms. Bitcoin services are explicitly allowed. The law also permits mining and staking services. El Salvador is more permissive regarding decentralized services, but AML obligations still apply. For example, DeFi protocols can operate without a license if they do not hold customer funds.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Estonia:

  1. Incorporate a legal entity (e.g., OÜ) in Estonia.
  2. Prepare compliance documentation: AML policy, business plan, risk assessment, internal controls.
  3. Appoint a local AML officer and board members.
  4. Open a bank account in Estonia (or EU) with sufficient capital.
  5. Submit application to Finantsinspektsioon via the e-service portal.
  6. Undergo background checks and interviews (if required).
  7. Receive authorization (typically 3-6 months).

El Salvador:

  1. Register a local company (Sociedad Anónima) or use a branch.
  2. Prepare application: business plan, AML program, financial projections, background of directors.
  3. Submit to CNAD with application fee.
  4. Undergo a review process (including potential site visit).
  5. Receive license (2-4 months).
  6. Register with the Central Bank for reporting.

Banking & Payment Partners

Estonia: Banking is challenging but possible. Major banks like Swedbank and SEB accept licensed crypto firms, but due diligence is strict. Payment processors like Stripe and PayPal are available. El Salvador: Banking is more accessible. Local banks (e.g., Banco Agrícola, Banco Cuscatlán) work with crypto firms. The government's Bitcoin Office facilitates banking relationships. International payment partners like Bitrefill and Paxful are common. However, international wire transfers may be slower due to limited correspondent banking.

For a low-cost alternative, Panama offers easy banking with no capital requirement.

Benefits of Each Jurisdiction

Estonia:

  • EU passporting under MiCA (access to 27 markets)
  • 0% tax on retained profits
  • Established regulatory framework (since 2017)
  • E-residency for easy remote setup
  • High reputation and trust

El Salvador:

  • Fast and flexible licensing (2-4 months)
  • No minimum capital requirement
  • Tax incentives (15-year holiday for new businesses)
  • Bitcoin-friendly environment
  • Lower setup costs

Each jurisdiction has trade-offs. Estonia offers market access and stability; El Salvador offers speed and cost savings. For a balanced option, consider Panama with its flat EUR 6,000 fee.

Compliance & Trust Considerations

Both jurisdictions require strong AML/CFT programs. Estonia's MiCA regime is harmonized with EU standards, making it easier to operate across Europe. El Salvador's framework is newer, and international banks may view it as higher risk. However, El Salvador's proactive approach to Bitcoin regulation has built trust among crypto-native firms.

Key compliance requirements include:

  • AML policy and procedures
  • Customer due diligence (CDD) and KYC
  • Transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting
  • Record keeping for at least 5 years
  • Appointment of a compliance officer (certified in Estonia)

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Underestimating capital requirements: In Estonia, ensure you meet the tiered capital. In El Salvador, have sufficient funds to demonstrate financial stability.
  • Ignoring local presence: Estonia requires a physical office and local management. El Salvador requires a local legal representative.
  • Poor AML documentation: Both regulators reject applications with weak AML policies. Hire a compliance expert.
  • Choosing based on cost alone: Consider long-term compliance, tax, and market access.
  • Delaying bank account opening: Banking is a bottleneck; start early.
  • Overlooking ongoing obligations: Both jurisdictions require annual audits, reporting, and AML officer maintenance.

Frequently asked questions

What is the main difference between Estonia and El Salvador crypto licenses?

Estonia offers a MiCA-compliant CASP license with EU passporting, tiered capital requirements (EUR 50k-150k), and 0% tax on retained profits. El Salvador offers a DASP license with no fixed capital, faster processing (2-4 months), and potential tax holidays. Estonia is better for EU market access; El Salvador for speed and lower costs.

Which jurisdiction has lower capital requirements?

El Salvador has no specified minimum capital, though USD 50k-100k is typical. Estonia requires EUR 50k for custody, EUR 125k for exchange, and EUR 150k for trading. For the lowest capital, consider Panama with no capital requirement.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Estonia vs El Salvador?

Estonia takes 3-6 months; El Salvador takes 2-4 months. Panama is faster at 1-2 months. Timelines depend on application completeness and regulator workload.

Can I use the Estonian license to operate in other EU countries?

Yes, under MiCA passporting, an Estonian CASP license allows you to provide services across the European Economic Area without additional licenses. El Salvador's license is limited to its territory.

What are the tax benefits of Estonia for crypto businesses?

Estonia taxes corporate profits only when distributed (0% on retained earnings). This allows reinvestment without tax. VAT of 20% applies to crypto services, but exemptions exist for fiat-to-crypto exchanges.

Does El Salvador offer tax incentives for crypto companies?

Yes, El Salvador offers a 15-year tax holiday for new digital asset businesses in designated zones. Corporate income tax is normally 30%, but exemptions may apply for Bitcoin-related income. No VAT on crypto transactions.

Which jurisdiction is better for a startup with limited budget?

El Salvador has lower setup costs (USD 5k-15k) and no fixed capital. Panama is even cheaper at EUR 6,000 flat with no capital. Estonia has higher costs but offers EU market access and tax advantages.

Do I need a physical office in Estonia or El Salvador?

Estonia requires a physical office and local management. El Salvador requires a local legal representative but not necessarily a physical office. Panama allows fully remote setup.

What activities are allowed under the El Salvador DASP license?

Exchange, custody, payment processing, token issuance, platform operation, mining, and staking. Bitcoin services are explicitly permitted. DeFi protocols may operate without a license if they do not hold customer funds.

Recognition varies. El Salvador's license is not passportable like Estonia's MiCA license. Some international banks may view it as higher risk. However, it is respected in crypto-friendly circles and by the Bitcoin community.
