What Is the Crypto Token Issuance License Romania?

The crypto token issuance license Romania is a regulatory authorisation granted by the ASF under the EU's Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA), fully applicable since 2026. It allows a legal entity to issue and offer crypto tokens - including utility tokens, asset-referenced tokens (ARTs), and e-money tokens (EMTs) - to the public within Romania and across the European Economic Area (EEA).

Key characteristics:

  • Licence type: CASP (Crypto-Asset Service Provider) with issuance scope.
  • Regulator: Autoritatea de Supraveghere Financiară (ASF).
  • Legal basis: MiCA (Regulation (EU) 2023/1114) implemented via Romanian national law.
  • Passporting: Yes, full EEA passport for authorised services.
  • Duration: Indefinite, subject to ongoing compliance.

This licence replaces the previous optional registration regime and is mandatory for any token issuance exceeding EUR 1 million in a 12-month period (with some exemptions for small offerings). The ASF maintains a public register of all authorised entities, ensuring transparency for investors and counterparties.

Who Needs a Crypto Token Issuance License in Romania?

Any entity that plans to offer crypto tokens to the public in Romania - or to residents of other EU member states - must obtain this licence. Specifically, you need it if you:

  • Issue utility tokens that grant access to a product or service.
  • Launch asset-referenced tokens (ARTs) backed by multiple assets.
  • Issue e-money tokens (EMTs) pegged to a single fiat currency.
  • Conduct an initial exchange offering (IEO) or token sale.
  • Act as a project owner or issuer of a decentralised finance (DeFi) protocol that issues governance tokens.

Exemptions apply for fully decentralised projects with no identifiable issuer, and for small offerings below EUR 1 million (though a simplified white paper is still required). Non-EU issuers targeting Romanian investors must also obtain the licence or appoint a local authorised representative. Even if your project is based outside the EU, if you market tokens to Romanian residents, the ASF expects compliance.

License Type and Regulator

The licence falls under the CASP category, specifically for “offering and trading of crypto-assets” and “exchange of crypto-assets for funds or other crypto-assets”. The regulator is the Autoritatea de Supraveghere Financiară (ASF), which also oversees securities, insurance, and private pensions.

Applications are submitted via the ASF's online portal, and the process includes a fit-and-proper test for management, a business plan, and a white paper compliant with MiCA requirements. The ASF also coordinates with ESMA for cross-border oversight.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for CASPs depends on the services offered. For token issuance (offering crypto-assets to the public), the applicable tier is:

  • EUR 150,000 for issuers of asset-referenced tokens (ARTs) and e-money tokens (EMTs).
  • EUR 50,000 for issuers of utility tokens only (non-ART/EMT).

These amounts must be held as own funds (Tier 1 capital) and maintained at all times. The ASF may require a higher amount based on the risk profile and volume of issuance. For entities also providing other CASP services (e.g., custody, exchange), higher capital tiers apply (EUR 125,000 or 150,000).

Capital can be in the form of cash, bank guarantees, or certain liquid assets. Proof of capital must be submitted with the application. Tax Treatment of Crypto Token Issuance in Romania

Romania has a relatively favourable tax regime for crypto activities. As of 2026:

  • Corporate income tax: Standard rate of 16% on profits. Income from token issuance is generally treated as ordinary business income.
  • VAT: Exempt for the issuance and transfer of crypto-assets (following EU VAT rules).
  • Withholding tax: 10% on dividends and certain payments to non-residents, subject to double tax treaties.
  • Personal income tax: 10% on capital gains from crypto for individuals, with a tax-free threshold of RON 200 (approx. EUR 40) per transaction.

Token issuers should structure their operations to optimise tax efficiency. Consulting24 advises on tax planning as part of the licensing process. For a jurisdiction with zero corporate tax on foreign-source income, consider Panama. Romania also offers tax incentives for research and development activities, which may benefit blockchain projects.

Allowed Activities Under the License

With a crypto token issuance license in Romania, you can perform the following regulated activities:

  • Offer and sell crypto tokens to the public (including via IEOs).
  • List tokens on trading platforms (subject to platform rules).
  • Exchange tokens for fiat currency or other crypto-assets (if also licensed for exchange).
  • Custody and administration of tokens on behalf of clients (if scope includes custody).
  • Provide transfer services for tokens.

If you plan to operate a trading platform or exchange, you need a separate CASP authorisation for that activity. The issuance licence alone does not cover market-making or order-matching. However, you can apply for a combined licence covering multiple services. The ASF encourages modular licensing to match your business model precisely.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Pre-application consultation: Assess your project’s suitability and define the scope of services.
  2. Corporate setup: Incorporate a Romanian limited liability company (SRL) or joint-stock company (SA) with minimum share capital (EUR 200 for SRL).
  3. Prepare documentation: Draft a MiCA-compliant white paper, business plan, risk management policy, and AML/CFT procedures.
  4. Appoint management: Ensure directors and key personnel pass the ASF’s fit-and-proper test.
  5. Submit application: File with the ASF via the online portal, including all required documents and payment of the fee.
  6. ASF review: The regulator assesses the application within 3-6 months, may request clarifications.
  7. Approval and registration: Once approved, you are added to the ASF register and can commence token issuance.
  8. Ongoing compliance: Submit periodic reports, maintain capital, and update the white paper if material changes occur.

Compliance and Trust: Meeting ASF Standards

Maintaining your licence requires ongoing compliance with MiCA and Romanian law. Key obligations include:

  • White paper updates: Any material change to the token or project must be reflected in an updated white paper filed with the ASF.
  • AML/CFT program: Implement strong anti-money laundering and counter-terrorism financing measures, including customer due diligence and transaction monitoring.
  • Capital maintenance: Ensure own funds never fall below the required minimum.
  • Reporting: Submit annual reports, audited financial statements, and periodic regulatory returns.
  • Fit and proper: Notify the ASF of any changes in management or ownership.

Common Mistakes When Applying for a Romanian Crypto License

Founders often make these errors during the application process:

  • Incomplete white paper: The white paper must include all mandatory disclosures under MiCA, such as risks, rights, and the underlying technology.
  • Underestimating capital requirements: Some applicants try to minimise capital, but the ASF may demand higher amounts based on risk.
  • Poor fit-and-proper documentation: Management must provide detailed CVs, police certificates, and financial history. Missing documents cause delays.
  • Ignoring AML/CFT: A generic AML policy won't pass scrutiny. It must be tailored to your business model.
  • Not engaging early with banks: Banking is a bottleneck; start the process before the licence is granted.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto token issuance license in Romania?

For utility tokens only, the minimum capital is EUR 50,000. For asset-referenced tokens (ARTs) or e-money tokens (EMTs), it is EUR 150,000. These amounts must be held as own funds at all times.

How long does it take to get a Romanian crypto token issuance license?

The ASF aims to process complete applications within 3 to 6 months. Delays can occur if additional information is requested. Pre-application preparation typically takes 1-2 months.

Can I passport my Romanian license to other EU countries?

Yes, the CASP license under MiCA allows full passporting across all 27 EU member states and the EEA. You can offer tokens to residents of any EU country without additional licensing.

What are the tax implications for token issuers in Romania?

Corporate income tax is 16% on profits. VAT is exempt for crypto issuance. Personal capital gains tax is 10% for individuals. Withholding tax on dividends is 10%, subject to double tax treaties.

Do I need a physical office in Romania to get the license?

Yes, you need a registered office in Romania. It can be a virtual office or a shared workspace, but the ASF requires a physical address for correspondence and inspections.

Can I apply for the license if my company is already registered in another EU country?

Yes, but you must establish a Romanian branch or subsidiary. The license is issued to the Romanian legal entity. Non-EU companies must set up a Romanian subsidiary to apply.

What happens if I issue tokens without a license?

Issuing tokens without authorisation is illegal and can result in fines, criminal penalties, and liability to investors. The ASF actively monitors the market and can halt unlicensed offerings.

Is the Romanian license suitable for stablecoin issuers?

Yes, but stablecoin issuers (ARTs and EMTs) face higher capital requirements (EUR 150,000) and additional prudential rules under MiCA. The white paper must include detailed reserve and redemption policies.
