What is the Crypto Payment Institution License Philippines?

The crypto payment institution license in the Philippines, officially the Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) license, is issued by the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP). It authorises entities to facilitate the exchange, transfer, and custody of virtual assets such as Bitcoin, Ethereum, and other cryptocurrencies. The BSP regulates VASPs under its authority to ensure consumer protection, anti-money laundering (AML) compliance, and financial stability.

This license is essential for any business that wants to operate a crypto exchange, wallet service, or payment processing involving digital assets within the Philippines. The regulatory framework is based on BSP Circular No. 1108 (2021) and subsequent amendments, which align with Financial Action Task Force (FATF) recommendations.

As of 2026, the BSP has a strong licensing process that includes rigorous due diligence, capital requirements, and ongoing reporting obligations. Consulting24 advises clients on structuring their application to meet these standards.

Who Needs a Philippines Crypto License?

Any entity that provides virtual asset services to residents of the Philippines or operates within its territory must obtain a VASP license from the BSP. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (centralised and decentralised platforms)
  • Digital wallet providers that custody virtual assets
  • Payment processors that settle transactions in crypto
  • Over-the-counter (OTC) trading desks
  • Peer-to-peer (P2P) platforms facilitating crypto transfers
  • Any business that converts fiat to virtual assets or vice versa

Exemptions may apply for businesses that only provide ancillary services (e.g., blockchain analytics) but do not handle customer funds or execute transactions. However, the BSP takes a broad view, so it is safer to assume you need a license unless specifically advised otherwise.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator for the crypto payment institution license Philippines is the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP), specifically its Financial Supervision Sector. The license type is a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) license, which is a single category covering all virtual asset activities. There are no sub-tiers for different services; all VASPs are subject to the same core requirements.

The BSP also recognises the role of the Anti-Money Laundering Council (AMLC) in overseeing AML compliance for VASPs. Additionally, the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) may have oversight if the virtual assets qualify as securities under Philippine law.

Key regulatory references:

  • BSP Circular No. 1108 (2021) - Guidelines for Virtual Asset Service Providers
  • BSP Circular No. 1150 (2022) - Amendments to VASP regulations
  • Republic Act No. 9160 (Anti-Money Laundering Act) as amended

Capital Requirement

The BSP mandates a minimum paid-up capital of PHP 50 million (approximately EUR 850,000) for a VASP license. This capital must be maintained at all times and cannot be withdrawn as dividends or loans. The capital requirement is designed to ensure the financial stability of the VASP and to cover operational risks.

Additional capital may be required if the VASP plans to offer services beyond basic exchange and custody, such as lending or derivatives. The BSP assesses capital adequacy on a case-by-case basis, considering the volume of transactions and risk profile.

Funds must be deposited in a Philippine bank account and be freely available for use by the licensed entity. The BSP requires proof of capitalisation before issuing the license.

Tax Treatment for Crypto Businesses in the Philippines

Cryptocurrency transactions in the Philippines are subject to taxation under the National Internal Revenue Code. The Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) has issued guidance classifying virtual assets as property for tax purposes. Key tax points:

  • Corporate Income Tax: Standard rate of 25% (or 20% for small businesses with net taxable income below PHP 5 million and total assets below PHP 100 million).
  • Value-Added Tax (VAT): 12% on the sale of goods and services, but the BIR has not yet clarified whether crypto trading is subject to VAT. As of 2026, many VASPs treat exchange fees as VAT-exempt, but this is not definitive.
  • Withholding Tax: 1% on gross payments to local suppliers; 15-30% on dividends and interest paid to non-residents.
  • Capital Gains Tax: 15% on gains from sale of shares of stock not traded in the stock exchange; for crypto, the BIR treats gains as ordinary income subject to corporate tax.

Allowed Activities Under the Philippines Crypto License

A VASP license from the BSP permits the following activities:

  • Exchange between virtual assets and fiat currency
  • Exchange between one or more forms of virtual assets
  • Transfer of virtual assets (including payment processing)
  • Safekeeping and administration of virtual assets (custody)
  • Participation in and provision of financial services related to an issuer's offer or sale of a virtual asset

Activities that are explicitly prohibited include offering unregistered securities, engaging in money laundering, and providing services to sanctioned entities. The BSP also restricts VASPs from offering interest-bearing accounts on virtual assets unless specifically approved.

If your business model includes activities not listed, you may need a separate license from the SEC (e.g., for security tokens) or the Insurance Commission (e.g., for crypto insurance products). Consulting24 can advise on the full scope of permissible activities under your license.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining a crypto payment institution license Philippines involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step:

  1. Pre-application preparation: Incorporate a Philippine company (e.g., a domestic corporation) with the SEC. The company must have a minimum of five incorporators and a paid-up capital of at least PHP 50 million.
  2. Draft policies and procedures: Develop an AML/CFT program, risk management framework, customer due diligence (CDD) procedures, and a business continuity plan. These must align with BSP standards.
  3. Technology and security audit: Engage an independent auditor to assess your IT systems, cybersecurity measures, and data protection protocols.
  4. Submit application to BSP: File the VASP application with the BSP Financial Supervision Sector, including all required documents: business plan, AML program, audited financial statements, proof of capital, and organisational structure.
  5. BSP review and due diligence: The BSP conducts a thorough review, including background checks on directors and shareholders. This stage may take 4-8 months.
  6. In-principle approval: If the BSP is satisfied, it issues an in-principle approval, allowing you to set up operations but not yet transact.
  7. Pre-operational requirements: Complete remaining requirements such as hiring a compliance officer, registering with the AMLC, and establishing a local bank account.
  8. Final approval and license issuance: After a final inspection, the BSP issues the VASP license. You can then commence operations.

The entire process typically takes 6-12 months. Consulting24 can expedite by ensuring your documentation is complete and compliant.

Banking and Payment Services for Crypto Firms

One of the biggest challenges for crypto businesses in the Philippines is securing a banking relationship. Most traditional banks are cautious about dealing with VASPs due to perceived risks. However, some banks, such as UnionBank and BDO, have opened accounts for licensed VASPs under strict compliance conditions.

To open a corporate bank account, you will need:

  • BSP VASP license (or in-principle approval)
  • Proof of capitalisation
  • AML program and compliance officer details
  • Business plan and source of funds declaration

Payment gateways like PayMaya and GCash also support crypto transactions, but they require integration with licensed VASPs. For international payments, SWIFT and local payment networks are available.

Benefits of the Philippines Crypto License

The Philippines offers several advantages for crypto businesses:

  • Clear regulatory framework: The BSP's VASP regime is well-defined, providing legal certainty for operators.
  • Growing crypto adoption: The Philippines has one of the highest rates of crypto ownership in Southeast Asia, driven by remittances and a young tech-savvy population.
  • Strategic location: Access to the ASEAN market and a strong English-speaking workforce.
  • No foreign equity restriction: Foreigners can own 100% of a VASP company, unlike some other sectors.

Compliance and Trust: Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, VASPs must adhere to ongoing compliance requirements to maintain their license. These include:

  • AML/CFT reporting: Submit regular reports to the AMLC, including suspicious transaction reports (STRs) and threshold reports (PHP 500,000 or more).
  • Audit: Annual external audit of financial statements and AML program effectiveness.
  • BSP reporting: Quarterly and annual reports on transaction volumes, customer base, and financial health.
  • Compliance officer: A full-time, resident compliance officer must be appointed and approved by the BSP.
  • Record keeping: Maintain records of all transactions for at least five years.

Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. Consulting24 provides ongoing advisory support to ensure your compliance remains up to date. This is general guidance, not legal advice. You should engage local counsel for specific obligations.

Common Mistakes When Applying for a Philippines Crypto License

Many applicants make errors that delay or derail their application. Common pitfalls include:

  • Inadequate AML program: The BSP expects a detailed, risk-based AML program. Generic templates are not accepted.
  • Insufficient capital: Failing to show that the PHP 50 million is fully paid and unencumbered.
  • Poorly structured corporate governance: Directors and shareholders must have clean backgrounds and relevant experience.
  • Missing local presence: The BSP requires a physical office in the Philippines and a resident compliance officer.
  • Underestimating timeline: Expecting approval in less than 6 months is unrealistic.

What is the minimum capital for a crypto payment institution license in the Philippines?

Can a foreign company apply for a Philippines VASP license?

Yes, foreigners can own 100% of a VASP company. However, the company must be incorporated in the Philippines and have a local compliance officer and physical office.

What activities are allowed under the Philippines crypto license?

Allowed activities include exchange between virtual assets and fiat, exchange between virtual assets, transfer of virtual assets, custody, and participation in offers or sales of virtual assets.
