What Is a Bermuda Crypto License?

A Bermuda crypto license, officially a Digital Asset Business License under the DABA, authorises companies to conduct digital asset activities such as exchange, custody, payment services, and issuance of digital assets. The regime is administered by the Bermuda Monetary Authority (BMA), which applies a risk-based approach to supervision. Bermuda is known for its high compliance standards, making it a preferred jurisdiction for institutional-grade crypto businesses.

Key features include a clear legal framework, strong investor protections, and a favourable tax environment with no capital gains tax. The license is suitable for both startups and established firms looking to operate in a reputable offshore centre. The BMA requires all licensees to maintain a physical presence in Bermuda, including a registered office and at least one local director or officer. This ensures that the business is subject to effective supervision and can be held accountable.

Bermuda's regulatory approach has attracted major players like Circle (issuer of USDC) and other fintech firms. The jurisdiction also offers a sandbox for innovative projects, allowing limited-scale testing before full licensing. For founders seeking a gold-standard regulatory stamp, Bermuda is a top contender.

Who Needs a Bermuda Crypto License?

Any entity that carries on digital asset business in or from Bermuda must hold a license. This includes:

  • Digital asset exchanges (centralised and decentralised) that match buyers and sellers
  • Custodial wallet providers that hold private keys on behalf of clients
  • Digital asset payment processors that facilitate merchant acceptance
  • Issuers of digital assets, including initial coin offerings (ICOs) and security token offerings (STOs)
  • Digital asset investment platforms that offer trading or advisory services

If you are a founder targeting institutional clients or seeking a jurisdiction with a strong regulatory reputation, Bermuda is an excellent choice. The BMA requires all licensed entities to be incorporated in Bermuda and maintain a physical presence, including a registered office and local directors or officers. This commitment to substance ensures that Bermuda remains a credible, non-haven jurisdiction.

Startups that are pre-revenue or have limited capital may find Bermuda's costs prohibitive. In such cases, Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 license with no capital requirement, though with a less rigorous regulatory framework. License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Bermuda Monetary Authority (BMA). The license falls under the Digital Asset Business Act 2018, as amended (effective from 2024, fully in force by 2026). There is a single license class for digital asset businesses, but the BMA may impose conditions based on the specific activities and risk profile. The regime is principle-based, focusing on anti-money laundering (AML), counter-terrorist financing (CTF), cybersecurity, and consumer protection.

Bermuda also has a separate framework for digital asset custody and a sandbox environment for innovative projects. The BMA is a member of the International Organization of Securities Commissions (IOSCO) and follows global standards. The BMA has a reputation for being thorough but fair, with a willingness to engage with applicants during the pre-application phase.

For businesses that also hold licenses in other jurisdictions, the BMA may consider reciprocal recognition or streamlined processes, though this is handled case by case. The BMA publishes a list of licensed entities on its website, adding transparency and credibility.

Capital Requirement

Bermuda does not prescribe a fixed minimum capital requirement under the DABA. Instead, the BMA applies a risk-based capital adequacy framework. In practice, applicants should demonstrate sufficient capital to cover operational expenses, potential losses, and compliance costs for at least 6-12 months. For a typical exchange or custody business, a minimum of USD 100,000 - 250,000 is often expected, but this is not statutory and can vary.

Capital must be held in liquid assets, and the BMA may require a surety bond or insurance for certain activities. Consulting24 advises clients on structuring capital to meet BMA expectations. For example, a startup with a simple wallet service may need less capital than a full exchange offering margin trading.

By comparison, Lithuania under MiCA requires EUR 125,000 for exchange and custody services, while Panama has no capital requirement at all. These differences should factor into your decision.

Tax Treatment

Bermuda has no corporate income tax, no capital gains tax, and no withholding tax. This makes it a highly attractive jurisdiction for crypto businesses. There is no VAT or sales tax. The primary tax is a payroll tax (around 5-10%) on employee salaries, and a small business tax for certain entities. However, digital asset businesses may be exempt from payroll tax for a period under the Economic Development Act.

Bermuda also has no exchange controls, allowing free movement of capital. This tax-neutral environment is a key advantage over many other jurisdictions. For example, in the EU under MiCA, corporate tax rates of 15-25% apply, and capital gains may be taxed. Panama also has no capital gains tax but charges a 4.5% corporate tax on net income. Bermuda's tax regime is among the most favourable globally.

Founders should still seek professional tax advice, as tax treaties and substance requirements may affect their overall tax position. Consulting24 can coordinate with local tax advisors to ensure compliance.

Allowed Activities

A Bermuda crypto license permits the following activities:

  • Operating a digital asset exchange (order book, matching engine)
  • Providing custodial wallet services for digital assets
  • Digital asset payment services (merchant acquisition, payment processing)
  • Issuing digital assets (including security tokens and utility tokens)
  • Digital asset investment and advisory services
  • Mining and staking services (subject to conditions)

The BMA prohibits certain activities unless specifically authorised, such as offering derivatives or margin trading. Activities must be conducted in compliance with AML/CFT regulations and consumer protection rules. The BMA may also restrict the use of certain tokens deemed high-risk.

If your business model includes activities not listed, you should discuss with the BMA during the pre-application phase. The sandbox can be used to test novel products before full licensing.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a Bermuda crypto license involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application consultation - Discuss your business model with the BMA (optional but recommended). This helps identify potential issues early.
  2. Incorporation - Register a Bermuda exempted company with a registered office and local directors. This typically takes 1-2 weeks.
  3. Prepare documentation - Business plan, AML/CFT policies, risk assessment, financial projections, and governance framework. These must be detailed and tailored to your specific activities.
  4. Submit application - File with the BMA, including all supporting documents and the application fee. The BMA will acknowledge receipt within 5 business days.
  5. BMA review - The BMA conducts due diligence on directors, shareholders, and the business model. This may take 2-4 months. The BMA may request additional information or clarifications.
  6. License issuance - If approved, the BMA issues a license with specific conditions. The company must then maintain ongoing compliance, including annual audits and reporting.

Banking & Payment Services

Bermuda has a well-developed banking sector, with several local and international banks willing to work with licensed digital asset businesses. However, banking can still be challenging due to AML concerns. Licensed entities often find it easier to open accounts. Options include:

  • Local Bermuda banks (e.g., HSBC Bermuda, Butterfield Bank) - these are familiar with crypto businesses and offer standard corporate accounts.
  • International banks with a Bermuda presence - may have stricter compliance but offer global connectivity.
  • Payment service providers and fintechs offering crypto-friendly accounts (e.g., Mercury, Brex) - these can be used for operational accounts but may not replace a local bank for fiat settlement.

We recommend starting the banking process early, as due diligence can take several weeks. Consulting24 can assist in identifying suitable banking partners and preparing the necessary documentation. Some banks require the license to be issued before opening an account, creating a chicken-and-egg problem. In such cases, a pre-license consultation with the bank can help.

For payment processing, licensed entities can integrate with major crypto payment gateways like BitPay or Coinbase Commerce, but must ensure these comply with BMA regulations.

Additionally, Bermuda has a strong legal system based on English common law, which provides comfort to investors and counterparties. The jurisdiction also offers a digital nomad visa, making it easier to relocate talent.

Compliance & Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, businesses must comply with ongoing requirements:

  • Annual audited financial statements submitted to the BMA within 6 months of year-end
  • Annual AML/CFT audit and reporting, including a review of policies and procedures
  • Cyber security and business continuity plans, tested annually
  • Regular reporting to the BMA on transactions, suspicious activity, and changes in control
  • Maintenance of a physical presence in Bermuda (office, staff)

Non-compliance can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. The BMA conducts periodic inspections and may require remedial actions. Consulting24 provides ongoing compliance support to help you meet these obligations. This is general guidance, not legal advice.

For example, a licensed exchange must report any breach of its cybersecurity policy within 24 hours. Failure to do so could lead to a penalty. It is essential to have a dedicated compliance officer familiar with BMA expectations.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make these errors when applying for a Bermuda crypto license:

  • Underestimating the importance of a strong AML/CFT framework - the BMA expects detailed policies tailored to your business, not generic templates.
  • Insufficient capitalisation - ensure you have enough funds to cover at least 12 months of operations, including legal and compliance costs.
  • Lack of local presence - the BMA requires a registered office and local directors or officers. Using a virtual office is not acceptable.
  • Incomplete application - missing documents or inconsistent information can delay the process by months. Common omissions include a detailed business plan and financial projections.
  • Ignoring tax planning - while Bermuda is tax-neutral, payroll tax and other obligations still apply. Plan for these costs.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital requirement for a Bermuda crypto license?

Bermuda does not have a statutory minimum capital requirement. Instead, the BMA applies a risk-based approach. In practice, you should demonstrate sufficient capital to cover at least 6-12 months of operating expenses. For a typical exchange, USD 100,000-250,000 is often expected, but this can vary based on your business model.

How long does it take to get a Bermuda crypto license?

The timeline is typically 3-6 months from application submission to approval, depending on the completeness of your application and the BMA's workload. Pre-application consultation can help speed up the process. Consulting24 advises preparing all documents thoroughly to avoid delays.

Can I apply for a Bermuda crypto license if I am not based in Bermuda?

Yes, but you must incorporate a Bermuda exempted company and maintain a physical presence in Bermuda, including a registered office and at least one local director or officer. The BMA expects substance, so a virtual office alone is not sufficient.

What activities are allowed under a Bermuda crypto license?

Allowed activities include operating a digital asset exchange, custodial wallet services, payment processing, issuing digital assets (ICOs/STOs), and investment advisory services. Derivatives and margin trading are generally prohibited unless specifically authorised. Mining and staking may be permitted subject to conditions.

Is Bermuda tax-free for crypto businesses?

Bermuda has no corporate income tax, no capital gains tax, and no withholding tax. There is no VAT. However, a payroll tax of 5-10% applies to employee salaries, and a small business tax may apply. Digital asset businesses may qualify for payroll tax exemptions under the Economic Development Act.

How does Bermuda compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 license with no capital requirement and a 2-4 week timeline, making it ideal for startups. Bermuda is more expensive (USD 25,000-90,000) and takes longer (3-6 months), but provides a stronger regulatory reputation and tax neutrality. Choose based on your budget and target market.

Do I need to travel to Bermuda for the application?

You do not necessarily need to travel, but you must have a local presence (registered office and local directors). The BMA may request interviews with key personnel, which can be conducted remotely. Consulting24 can help coordinate local requirements.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations after obtaining a Bermuda crypto license?

Ongoing obligations include annual audited financial statements, AML/CFT audits, cybersecurity testing, regular reporting to the BMA, and maintaining a physical presence. Non-compliance can lead to fines or license revocation. Consulting24 offers ongoing compliance support.

Can I use a Bermuda license to serve clients in the EU?

A Bermuda license does not automatically grant EU passporting rights under MiCA. To serve EU clients, you may need a separate license in an EU member state or ensure your Bermuda entity complies with local regulations. Consulting24 can advise on multi-jurisdictional strategies.

What is the success rate for Bermuda crypto license applications?

The success rate is high for well-prepared applications. The BMA engages with applicants during the pre-application phase, which helps identify issues early.
