What Is the Bulgarian Crypto Exchange License?

The Bulgarian crypto exchange license is a CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) authorization granted by the Financial Supervision Commission (FSC). Under MiCA, it allows companies to legally offer crypto exchange services (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto), custody, and other virtual asset services to Bulgarian and EU clients. The license is mandatory for any business operating a crypto exchange, wallet provider, or trading platform in Bulgaria.

Bulgaria transposed MiCA into national law in 2025, replacing the previous registration-only regime. The license is valid across all EU member states under the single passport, making it a powerful tool for pan-European operations. For example, a Bulgarian-licensed exchange can serve clients in Germany, France, or Italy without additional national licenses, provided it complies with local marketing rules. The FSC maintains a public register of all licensed CASPs, which enhances trust and transparency for clients and partners.

Key features include a minimum capital of EUR 50,000 for basic exchange services, a flat corporate tax rate of 10%, and a clear regulatory framework under MiCA. Unlike some other EU jurisdictions, Bulgaria does not impose additional local top-ups beyond MiCA requirements, making it a straightforward option for licensing.

Who Needs a Bulgarian Crypto License?

Any legal entity that provides one or more of the following services in or from Bulgaria must obtain a CASP license:

  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets
  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Operation of a trading platform for crypto assets
  • Transfer services for crypto assets
  • Advice on crypto assets

This includes startups, established exchanges, and fintech companies expanding into the EU. Even if your company is registered outside Bulgaria, if you target Bulgarian clients, you need a Bulgarian license or a passport from another EU CASP license. For example, a company based in the UK that wants to offer crypto services to Bulgarian residents must either establish a Bulgarian subsidiary and obtain a license or rely on an EU-passported license from another member state.

Additionally, any business that markets crypto services to Bulgarian residents, even without a physical presence, may trigger licensing requirements. This includes online advertising, social media campaigns, or partnerships with local influencers. The FSC has been active in monitoring cross-border marketing and has issued warnings to unlicensed entities.

License Type and Regulator

The license is a Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) authorization under MiCA. The regulator is the Financial Supervision Commission (FSC) of Bulgaria, specifically its department for financial innovations and digital assets. The FSC is responsible for licensing, supervision, and enforcement.

Unlike the old Bulgarian VASP register (which had no minimum capital), the MiCA regime imposes strict capital, governance, and AML requirements. The license is issued for an indefinite term but subject to ongoing supervision. The FSC has the authority to conduct on-site inspections, request documentation, and impose sanctions for non-compliance. It also coordinates with the European Banking Authority (EBA) and other EU regulators to ensure consistent application of MiCA.

The FSC publishes guidelines and FAQs on its website to help applicants understand the requirements. However, the application process is detailed and requires professional assistance to avoid delays. Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital for a CASP license in Bulgaria is EUR 50,000 for basic exchange and custody services. If you plan to offer additional services (e.g., trading platform, advice), the capital may increase to EUR 125,000 or EUR 150,000 depending on the service classes. The capital must be fully paid up before licensing and maintained throughout the license period.

This capital is significantly higher than the old Bulgarian VASP regime (which had no minimum), reflecting the enhanced consumer protection and operational standards under MiCA. The capital can be held as cash or highly liquid assets, and it must be deposited in a Bulgarian bank account. The FSC requires evidence of the capital deposit, such as a bank statement or a letter from the bank.

For example, if your exchange also offers custody services, the capital requirement may be EUR 125,000. If you operate a trading platform, it could be EUR 150,000. It is important to correctly classify your services to avoid undercapitalization, which can lead to rejection. Consulting24 helps you determine the appropriate capital based on your business model.

Tax Treatment for Crypto Businesses in Bulgaria

Bulgaria has a flat corporate income tax rate of 10%, one of the lowest in the EU. Crypto asset transactions are treated similarly to traditional financial instruments for tax purposes. VAT is generally not applicable on crypto exchange fees (subject to specific conditions). However, the tax treatment can vary based on the specific activities (e.g., trading vs. mining). It is essential to consult a local tax advisor to structure your operations optimally.

For example, if your exchange also engages in proprietary trading, the tax implications may differ, and proper accounting segregation is recommended. Bulgaria also offers a 5% withholding tax on dividends, which is favorable for repatriating profits. Additionally, there are no capital gains taxes on crypto-to-crypto trades for companies, though this should be confirmed with a tax advisor.

Bulgaria has double tax treaties with many countries, which can reduce withholding taxes on cross-border payments. For instance, dividends paid to a US parent company may be subject to a reduced rate of 5% or 10% under the US-Bulgaria treaty. Proper tax planning can significantly reduce your overall tax burden.

Allowed Activities Under the Bulgarian Crypto License

The Bulgarian CASP license permits the following activities:

  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets
  • Custody and administration of crypto assets
  • Operation of a crypto trading platform
  • Transfer of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Advice on crypto assets
  • Portfolio management of crypto assets (with additional capital)

All activities must be conducted in compliance with MiCA's transparency, security, and anti-abuse rules. The license does not cover derivatives or securities tokens (which fall under MiFID II). If you plan to offer staking or lending services, these may require additional authorization or fall under a separate regulatory framework. For example, staking as a service may be considered a custody activity, while lending may be regulated under the EU's crowdfunding regulation or require a credit institution license.

The FSC has issued guidance clarifying that certain activities, such as non-custodial wallet provision or decentralized exchange operation, may be exempt from licensing if they do not involve possession of client assets. However, these exemptions are narrow and require careful analysis. Consulting24 can help you determine which activities are covered and whether any additional licenses are needed.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Pre-consultation: We assess your business model and determine the appropriate service classes and capital.
  2. Company incorporation: You need a Bulgarian legal entity (e.g., EOOD or AD) with a registered office in Bulgaria. Consulting24 can coordinate company setup.
  3. Document preparation: Prepare business plan, AML/KYC policies, risk management framework, governance structure, and financial projections.
  4. Capital deposit: Deposit the minimum capital (EUR 50k+) in a Bulgarian bank account and obtain a certificate.
  5. Application submission: Submit the complete application to the FSC, including all supporting documents.
  6. Review and interview: The FSC reviews the application, may request additional information, and conducts interviews with key personnel.
  7. License issuance: If approved, you receive the CASP license and are added to the FSC's public register.
  8. Post-licensing: Ongoing compliance, reporting, and annual audits.

The entire process typically takes 6-12 months. For a faster route, consider Lithuania (4-6 months) or Panama (2-3 months). The FSC may also require a physical presence in Bulgaria, such as a registered office and local management. Some applicants choose to appoint a local compliance officer to satisfy this requirement.

Common Mistakes in the Bulgarian Crypto Licensing Process

  1. Underestimating the capital requirement: Many applicants assume the old EUR 0 capital still applies. Under MiCA, you need at least EUR 50,000.
  2. Incomplete AML documentation: The FSC scrutinizes AML policies heavily. Generic templates often get rejected.
  3. Lack of local presence: You need a physical office and local management (or at least a registered agent).
  4. Ignoring tax planning: Not structuring your operations to take advantage of Bulgaria's 10% tax can lead to higher effective rates.
  5. Choosing the wrong service class: Over- or under-scoping your license can delay approval or limit your business.
