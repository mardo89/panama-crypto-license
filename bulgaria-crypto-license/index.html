What Is the Bulgaria Crypto License?

The Bulgaria crypto license is an authorization required for companies offering crypto asset services in or from Bulgaria. Under MiCA, which is fully in force in 2026, the license is formally a CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) registration. The primary regulator is the Bulgarian National Bank (BNB) for payment-related crypto services, and the Financial Supervision Commission (FSC) for other crypto activities like exchange and custody. The license allows firms to operate across the EU under the single passport regime.

Bulgaria has a relatively mature crypto regulatory environment, having introduced early legislation in 2018. However, the full MiCA implementation in 2025-2026 has replaced older, lighter regimes. The current framework requires substantial compliance, including AML/KYC procedures, capital requirements, and operational safeguards. Who Needs a Bulgaria Crypto License?

Any legal entity that intends to provide crypto asset services to Bulgarian residents or operate from Bulgaria needs this license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto)
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Brokerage services for crypto assets
  • Platforms for initial coin offerings (ICOs) or security token offerings (STOs)
  • Automated trading services (bots) if they hold client funds
  • Payment services using crypto assets (e.g., crypto debit cards)

If your business model involves any of these activities, you must obtain the appropriate license. Even if you are based outside Bulgaria, offering services to Bulgarian residents may trigger licensing obligations. Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, capital requirements for CASPs are tiered based on the type of services provided. For Bulgaria, the minimum capital requirements as of 2026 are:

  • EUR 50,000 for simple exchange or brokerage services (no custody)
  • EUR 125,000 for custody services or combined exchange and custody
  • EUR 150,000 for services involving trading platforms or complex activities

These figures are in line with the MiCA regulation. Additionally, the regulator may require higher capital if the business volume is large. The capital must be paid up and held in a Bulgarian bank account. Tax Treatment

Bulgaria has a flat corporate income tax rate of 10%, one of the lowest in the EU. Crypto asset transactions are treated similarly to traditional financial instruments for tax purposes. As of 2026, capital gains on crypto are subject to the standard 10% corporate tax. VAT is not applicable on crypto exchange services, but may apply to advisory or other services. Personal income tax for crypto gains is also 10% (flat).

Important: Bulgaria does not have a specific crypto tax law; instead, general tax principles apply. This can create some uncertainty, but the low tax rate is attractive. Transfer pricing rules apply for related-party transactions. Consulting24 can connect you with local tax advisors for detailed planning. Allowed Activities

Under the Bulgaria crypto license, the following activities are permitted:

  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets
  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Brokerage services (placing orders for clients)
  • Operation of a trading platform (order book matching)
  • Reception and transmission of orders for crypto assets
  • Advice on crypto assets (if incidental to other services)

Certain activities like crypto mining or pure software development do not require a license. However, if you offer any of the above services to Bulgarian residents, you need the license. The license is valid across the EU under the MiCA passport. For a broader comparison of allowed activities across jurisdictions, see our jurisdictions page.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make these mistakes when applying for a Bulgaria crypto license:

  • Underestimating documentation: The AML policy and business plan must be detailed and specific to your operations.
  • Choosing the wrong regulator: Applying to the BNB when your services fall under the FSC (or vice versa) causes delays.
  • Insufficient capital: Not having the full capital deposited before application leads to rejection.
  • Ignoring beneficial ownership: All ultimate beneficial owners must be disclosed and meet fit-and-proper criteria.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Bulgaria crypto license?

Under MiCA, the minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for simple exchange or brokerage, EUR 125,000 for custody services, and EUR 150,000 for trading platforms. These amounts must be paid up and held in a Bulgarian bank account.

How long does it take to get a Bulgaria crypto license?

The total process typically takes 4-8 months from start to license issuance. This includes company registration (if needed), documentation preparation, and regulatory review by the BNB or FSC.

What is the tax rate for crypto businesses in Bulgaria?

Bulgaria has a flat corporate income tax rate of 10% on profits, including capital gains from crypto. Personal income tax is also 10%. There is no specific crypto tax law; general tax principles apply.

Can I use my Bulgaria crypto license to operate in other EU countries?

Yes, under the MiCA passporting regime, a Bulgaria CASP license allows you to provide services across all EU member states without needing additional licenses. You must notify the regulator of your intent.

What activities are allowed under the Bulgaria crypto license?

Allowed activities include exchange (fiat-crypto and crypto-crypto), custody, brokerage, operation of a trading platform, order reception and transmission, and advisory services incidental to these activities.

Is it difficult to open a bank account for a crypto company in Bulgaria?

It can be challenging as many banks are cautious. With a license, your chances improve. Recommended banks include UniCredit Bulbank, DSK Bank, and Postbank. Consulting24 can assist with introductions.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements after obtaining the license?

You must conduct annual AML audits, submit quarterly financial reports, maintain minimum capital, pay an annual renewal fee, and notify the regulator of any changes in management or ownership.

How does the Bulgaria crypto license compare to Panama?

Bulgaria offers an EU passport and low tax (10%) but costs EUR 15,000-35,000 and takes 4-8 months. Panama costs a flat EUR 6,000, takes 2-3 months, and has territorial tax (0% on foreign income) but no EU passport.

Do I need a local office or director in Bulgaria?

Yes, you need a registered office in Bulgaria and at least one director (can be foreign). The company must have a physical presence, though remote work is possible with a local registered address.

No, we cannot guarantee approval as it depends on the regulator's assessment. We advise and coordinate to maximize your chances, but final approval is at the discretion of the BNB or FSC.
