What Is the Crypto Wallet Custody License Indonesia?

The crypto wallet custody license Indonesia is a regulatory approval from Bappebti that allows a company to offer custodial wallet services for crypto assets. This includes holding private keys, managing user funds, and providing secure storage. The license is part of Indonesia's efforts to regulate the growing digital asset sector and protect consumers.

Unlike some jurisdictions that separate custody from trading, Indonesia currently treats custody as an activity that can be performed by a licensed Crypto Asset Physical Trader (PFAK) or a dedicated custody provider. The license requires strong security measures, anti-money laundering (AML) compliance, and capital adequacy. As of 2026, Bappebti has not yet introduced a standalone custody license, but the PFAK license covers custody activities. The regulator is expected to issue specific custody guidelines in the near future, which may affect existing license holders.

Who Needs This License?

Any business that intends to offer crypto wallet custody services to Indonesian residents or clients must obtain this license. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges that offer built-in wallets
  • Dedicated custody service providers
  • Financial institutions entering the digital asset space
  • Blockchain startups building wallet applications
  • Over-the-counter (OTC) desks that hold client funds

If your business holds private keys or has control over client funds, you are likely required to be licensed. Even foreign companies targeting Indonesian users may need a local presence and license. The definition of custody under Indonesian law is broad, covering any service where the provider has control over the crypto assets. License Type and Regulator

The primary regulator for crypto wallet custody in Indonesia is the Commodity Futures Trading Regulatory Agency (Bappebti), under the Ministry of Trade. The license is typically a Crypto Asset Physical Trader (PFAK) license, which covers both trading and custody activities. As of 2026, there is no separate 'custody-only' license, but Bappebti is expected to introduce a dedicated custody license in the future. The PFAK license is the only option currently available for custody providers.

Key requirements for the PFAK license include:

  • Minimum paid-up capital of IDR 1 trillion (approximately EUR 60,000) as of 2026, but this may vary by service scope.
  • Implementation of AML/CFT procedures aligned with FATF recommendations.
  • Secure custody infrastructure with cold wallet storage for the majority of assets.
  • Regular reporting to Bappebti on transaction volumes and asset holdings.
  • Appointment of a compliance officer and a local director.

Capital Requirement

As of 2026, the minimum paid-up capital for a Crypto Asset Physical Trader (PFAK) license in Indonesia is IDR 1 trillion (approximately EUR 60,000). This amount must be deposited in a local bank account and cannot be withdrawn without regulatory approval. Additional capital may be required based on the volume of assets under custody. For example, if you plan to hold more than IDR 10 trillion in client assets, Bappebti may require a higher capital buffer.

Tax Treatment

Indonesia imposes a value-added tax (VAT) of 11% on crypto asset trading services, including custody fees. Additionally, income tax applies to profits generated from custody services at the corporate income tax rate of 22% (2026). Crypto transactions may also be subject to a final tax of 0.1% on the transaction value for traders. Custody fees are considered taxable services, and you must issue tax invoices to clients.

Allowed Activities

With the crypto wallet custody license Indonesia, you are permitted to:

  • Store and safeguard crypto assets for clients
  • Manage private keys and provide wallet infrastructure
  • Facilitate deposits and withdrawals of crypto assets
  • Offer staking services if integrated with custody
  • Provide multi-signature wallet solutions
  • Generate wallet addresses for clients

Activities not allowed include unlicensed trading, lending of client assets without explicit permission, and offering derivatives. The license strictly covers custody and related services. For a broader scope, exchange licenses in other jurisdictions may be more suitable.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Pre-incorporation: Register a local company (PT) with the Ministry of Law and Human Rights. This typically takes 2-4 weeks.
  2. Prepare documentation: Business plan, AML policy, security audit, and financial statements. The AML policy must include customer due diligence procedures and transaction monitoring.
  3. Submit application: File with Bappebti along with the application fee. The fee is non-refundable.
  4. Regulatory review: Bappebti assesses the application, may request additional information. This stage can take 3-6 months.
  5. Capital deposit: Deposit minimum paid-up capital in a local bank and provide proof to Bappebti.
  6. Infrastructure setup: Implement custody technology, cold storage, and security protocols. You may need to engage an external security auditor.
  7. On-site inspection: Bappebti may conduct a physical inspection of your office and systems. Ensure your office is fully operational.
  8. License issuance: Upon approval, you receive the PFAK license. You can then begin operations.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Underestimating the time required for regulatory approval (6-12 months). Many applicants expect 3 months and face delays.
  • Insufficient AML documentation - Bappebti is strict on this and may reject applications with weak policies.
  • Choosing the wrong company structure - a local PT is mandatory, and a foreign-owned PT may have additional restrictions.
  • Ignoring tax obligations - both VAT and income tax apply, and late filing can result in penalties.
  • Not having a local director or office - Bappebti requires a physical presence and a director who is a resident.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a PFAK license and a custody license in Indonesia?

As of 2026, Indonesia does not have a standalone custody license. The PFAK (Crypto Asset Physical Trader) license covers both trading and custody activities. Bappebti is expected to introduce a separate custody license in the future, but currently, custody providers must obtain a PFAK license.

Can a foreign company apply for the crypto wallet custody license Indonesia?

Yes, but you must first incorporate a local PT (limited liability company) in Indonesia. The PT must have at least one local director and a registered office. Foreign ownership is allowed but may require additional approvals from the Investment Coordinating Board (BKPM).

What are the ongoing compliance requirements after obtaining the license?

You must submit regular reports to Bappebti, including transaction volumes, asset holdings, and financial statements. AML/CFT procedures must be updated annually. Bappebti may conduct on-site inspections. You also need to maintain the minimum capital and ensure proper segregation of client assets.

How long does the application process typically take?

The process takes 6 to 12 months from company incorporation to license issuance. Delays can occur if documentation is incomplete or if Bappebti requests additional information. Consulting24 helps streamline the process to minimize delays.

Is there a minimum capital requirement for custody services?

Yes, the minimum paid-up capital is IDR 1 trillion (approximately EUR 60,000) as of 2026. This amount may increase if you plan to hold large volumes of client assets. The capital must be deposited in a local bank account and cannot be withdrawn without regulatory approval.

What taxes apply to crypto custody services in Indonesia?

Custody fees are subject to 11% VAT. Corporate income tax is 22% on profits. Crypto transactions may incur a final tax of 0.1% on the transaction value. You must issue tax invoices and file regular returns with the tax authority.

Can I use the Indonesian license to serve clients in other countries?

The license is primarily for serving Indonesian residents. If you want to serve clients in other countries, you may need additional licenses in those jurisdictions. However, the Indonesian license can be a starting point for regional expansion in Southeast Asia.

What happens if I operate without a license?

Operating without a license is illegal and can result in criminal penalties, including fines and imprisonment. Bappebti actively monitors the market and can shut down unlicensed operators. Clients may also face legal risks.

What are the advantages of Panama over Indonesia for custody services?

Panama offers a flat fee of EUR 6,000, no minimum capital, no specific crypto license, and a 2-4 week setup time. It also has no capital gains tax on crypto. However, Panama may not provide the same level of regulatory clarity as Indonesia for custody services.
