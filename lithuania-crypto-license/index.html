Lithuania Crypto License 2026: CASP under MiCA

Lithuania is one of the most popular EU entry points for crypto businesses — a credible Bank of Lithuania regulator, no director residency requirement, and full EU passporting once you hold a MiCA Crypto-Asset Service Provider (CASP) authorization. Here's the real picture on cost, capital, timeline and who it suits in 2026.

From VASP to MiCA CASP — what changed

Until recently, Lithuania ran a light-touch VASP registration that founders could obtain quickly. That era is over. With the EU's Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA) now in force, crypto firms operating from Lithuania must hold a CASP authorization from the Bank of Lithuania. The bar is higher — real substance, a qualified AML/compliance officer, fit-and-proper management, capital, and documented policies — but the payoff is a genuine EU licence that passports across all 30 EEA countries.

Capital requirements

MiCA sets minimum own-funds requirements by service class:

ClassServices (examples)Minimum capital
Class 1Reception/transmission of orders, advice, placement, execution€50,000
Class 2Custody, exchange crypto-to-fiat & crypto-to-crypto€125,000
Class 3Operating a trading platform (exchange)€150,000

Most exchange and custody businesses fall into Class 2 or 3. Capital can usually be met with paid-in share capital or qualifying insurance, subject to the regulator's assessment.

What the application involves

  • A Lithuanian company (UAB) with adequate share capital
  • A detailed business plan, governance structure and risk framework
  • AML/CFT program, transaction-monitoring tooling, and a qualified MLRO/compliance officer
  • Fit-and-proper documentation for directors and beneficial owners
  • Local operational substance appropriate to the activity

Cost & timeline

Realistic total project cost runs from roughly €20,000 to €50,000+ (company setup, compliance framework, personnel, and the authorization process), on top of the regulatory capital. Expect roughly 4–8 months end-to-end depending on the completeness of your file and regulator workload.

Lithuania vs Panama: Lithuania gives you an EU passport and regulated status — ideal if your users are European. Panama is far faster and cheaper (€6,000, 2–3 weeks, 0% foreign-income tax, no minimum capital) and suits global/non-EU-facing crypto businesses. See the full Panama vs Lithuania comparison.

Allowed activities under a Lithuanian CASP

A MiCA CASP authorization in Lithuania can cover the full range of regulated crypto-asset services, and you scope your licence to the activities you actually perform:

  • Operating a trading platform (centralised exchange)
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for funds (fiat) and for other crypto-assets
  • Custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • Execution, reception and transmission of orders
  • Placement of crypto-assets and advice / portfolio management
  • Transfer services for crypto-assets

Scoping matters: each added service can raise your capital class and the depth of policies the Bank of Lithuania expects, so most founders licence precisely what their roadmap needs for the first 12–18 months.

Banking and payment rails

One of the practical reasons founders pick an EU CASP is banking. A regulated Lithuanian entity is far easier to onboard with EU EMIs, IBAN providers and, increasingly, traditional banks, because the counterparty sees a supervised licence rather than an unregulated company. Lithuania's own fintech ecosystem (it hosts a large share of EU EMIs) means local payment partners understand crypto businesses. Expect enhanced due diligence regardless — a clean source-of-funds story, a credible business plan, and a working AML program are prerequisites.

The application process, step by step

  1. Structure & scope — choose your service classes, capital tier and ownership chart.
  2. Incorporate the Lithuanian UAB and inject the required share capital.
  3. Build the file — business plan, governance, risk and AML/CFT policies, IT and custody arrangements, and key-person documentation.
  4. Appoint fit-and-proper management and a qualified AML officer; arrange local substance.
  5. Submit the CASP application to the Bank of Lithuania and respond to its review questions.
  6. Authorization, then ongoing supervision and reporting.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Under-scoping capital: assuming €50k when your model (exchange/custody) needs €125k–€150k.
  • Treating it like the old VASP: the light-touch registration era is over; MiCA expects real substance.
  • Weak AML staffing: a nominal compliance officer with no genuine authority is a fast route to rejection.
  • No banking plan: securing the licence but never lining up payment rails in parallel.

Who Lithuania suits

Choose Lithuania if you need an EU-regulated CASP to serve European customers, plan to scale with banking partners who expect regulated status, and can support the capital and substance. If speed, low cost and tax efficiency for a global audience matter more, a Panama company is usually the better first move — and many founders start in Panama and add an EU licence later.
