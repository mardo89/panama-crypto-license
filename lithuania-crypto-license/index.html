What Is the Lithuania Crypto License?

The Lithuania crypto license is a MiCAR CASP authorisation granted by the Bank of Lithuania. It allows firms to provide crypto-asset services legally within Lithuania and across the EEA under the EU passport. The license covers a wide range of activities, from custody and exchange to operating a trading platform.

Key features:

  • Regulated by the Bank of Lithuania (BoL) – a reputable EU central bank.
  • Full MiCAR compliance – no need for separate national regimes.
  • Passporting rights to 30+ EEA countries.
  • Clear capital requirements based on activity type.
  • Local substance required: a local director and compliance officer.

Compared to other EU jurisdictions, Lithuania offers a balanced regulatory environment with relatively fast processing times and a pragmatic approach. For more details on other options, see our jurisdictions overview.

Who Needs a Lithuania CASP License?

Any business that provides crypto-asset services to clients in the EU must hold a CASP license. Under MiCAR, the following activities require authorisation:

  • Custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients.
  • Operation of a crypto-asset trading platform.
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for fiat currency or other crypto-assets.
  • Execution of orders for crypto-assets on behalf of clients.
  • Placing of crypto-assets.
  • Reception and transmission of orders for crypto-assets.
  • Advice on crypto-assets.
  • Portfolio management of crypto-assets.
  • Transfer services for crypto-assets on behalf of clients.

If your business model involves any of these, you need a CASP license. The Lithuania route is ideal for startups and scale-ups that want a credible EU regulatory home without the high costs of some other jurisdictions. For a comparison, see our comparison page.

License Type & Regulator

The license is a MiCAR CASP authorisation, issued by the Bank of Lithuania (BoL). The BoL is the central bank of Lithuania and the primary financial regulator. It oversees all financial institutions, including crypto-asset service providers.

Key regulatory details:

  • Regulator: Bank of Lithuania (Lietuvos bankas).
  • License type: CASP (Crypto-Asset Service Provider) under MiCAR.
  • Legal basis: EU Regulation 2023/1114 (MiCAR) and national implementing laws.
  • Passporting: Yes, full EEA passport.
  • Supervision: Ongoing compliance with MiCAR, AML/CTF, and prudential requirements.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCAR, the minimum capital requirement depends on the type of crypto-asset services provided. The Bank of Lithuania applies the following tiers:

  • EUR 50,000 – for advice on crypto-assets, portfolio management, placing/listing, transfer, execution of orders, and reception/transmission of orders.
  • EUR 125,000 – for custody & administration of crypto-assets, and exchanging crypto-assets for funds or other crypto-assets.
  • EUR 150,000 – for operating a trading platform.

If your firm provides multiple services, the highest capital requirement applies. The capital must be held as own funds and maintained at all times. This is a significant improvement over the old pre-MiCAR regime, which had no minimum capital for some activities. For more details on requirements, see our requirements page.

Tax Treatment for Crypto Businesses in Lithuania

Lithuania offers a competitive tax environment for crypto businesses. The standard corporate income tax rate is 15%, but a reduced rate of 5% applies to small companies (up to EUR 300,000 annual revenue) under certain conditions. Crypto-asset transactions are generally treated as financial instruments for tax purposes.

Key tax points:

  • Corporate income tax: 15% (or 5% for small companies).
  • VAT: Crypto-asset services are generally exempt from VAT in the EU.
  • Withholding tax: 15% on dividends (may be reduced under tax treaties).
  • Capital gains: Subject to corporate income tax.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Lithuania crypto license?

The minimum capital depends on services offered: EUR 50,000 for advice, portfolio management, placing, transfer, execution, and order reception/transmission; EUR 125,000 for custody and exchange; EUR 150,000 for operating a trading platform. If multiple services, the highest applies.

How long does it take to get a Lithuania crypto license?

The company adjustment and documentation phase takes about 7–8 weeks. Then the Bank of Lithuania authorisation takes 2–4 months. Total timeline is typically 4–6 months from start to license issuance.

What are the ongoing costs for a Lithuania CASP licensee?

Ongoing costs include staff salaries (director ~EUR 5,000/month, compliance officer ~EUR 4,000/month incl. social insurance), annual external audit (~EUR 10,000), IT security costs, and office rent. Total monthly staff costs are around EUR 6,000–6,500.

Do I need a local director and compliance officer in Lithuania?

Yes. The local director must have a degree in Law, Economics, or MBA/BBA with 1–2 years senior experience in financial organisations. The compliance officer/MLRO needs a degree in Economics or MBA/BBA, AML/CTF certification, and 1+ year compliance experience.

Can I passport my Lithuania license to other EU countries?

Yes, the MiCAR CASP license allows full passporting across the European Economic Area (EEA). You can provide services in any EU/EEA member state without needing a separate license.

What documents are required for the application?

You need a Program of Operations, Business Plan, Governance policies, Business Continuity Framework, Risk Management Framework, AML/CTF Policies, Information Security Framework, Compliance Handling Procedure, Accounting policy, and per-activity policies (e.g., custody rules, trading platform rules).

Is a physical office required in Lithuania?

Yes, you must have a physical office/HQ in Lithuania. The BoL requires a registered address and a local presence for supervision purposes.

What is the tax rate for crypto businesses in Lithuania?

The standard corporate income tax rate is 15%. Small companies (revenue under EUR 300,000) may qualify for a reduced 5% rate. Crypto services are generally VAT-exempt. Consult a tax advisor for specifics.

Can I apply for a Lithuania crypto license if I am a non-EU resident?

Yes, non-EU residents can apply. You will need to incorporate a Lithuanian company (UAB) and meet local substance requirements. There are no nationality restrictions for shareholders or directors.

What happens if I provide services without a license?

Providing crypto-asset services without a CASP license in the EU is illegal and can result in fines, criminal penalties, and reputational damage. It is essential to obtain proper authorisation.
