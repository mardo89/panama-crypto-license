What Is the Lithuania Crypto License (MiCA CASP)?

The Lithuania crypto license, officially a MiCA CASP authorization, is a regulatory permit issued by the Bank of Lithuania that allows companies to provide crypto-asset services legally within Lithuania and across the European Economic Area (EEA) under passporting rights. MiCA, effective from 2026, establishes a harmonized framework for crypto-asset service providers, replacing previous national regimes. The license covers a range of activities, from custody and exchange to advisory and portfolio management.

Key features include:

  • EU Passporting: Once licensed in Lithuania, you can serve clients in any EU/EEA member state without additional licensing in each country.
  • Regulator: The Bank of Lithuania oversees compliance with MiCA, anti-money laundering (AML) rules, and consumer protection standards.
  • No Director Residency Requirement: Unlike some EU jurisdictions, Lithuania does not mandate that directors or shareholders reside in the country.
  • Capital Tiers: Minimum capital requirements are EUR 50,000, EUR 125,000, or EUR 150,000 depending on the service class.

This license is ideal for businesses that want a credible, regulated presence in Europe with a relatively efficient timeline of 4–8 months.

Who Needs a Lithuania Crypto License?

The Lithuania crypto license is designed for a wide range of crypto businesses aiming to operate legally in the EU. You likely need this license if you:

  • Operate a crypto exchange (centralized or decentralized) matching buyers and sellers of crypto assets.
  • Provide custody services holding clients’ private keys or crypto assets on their behalf.
  • Offer crypto-to-fiat conversion or payment services involving virtual currencies.
  • Run a crypto ATM network in the EU.
  • Provide portfolio management or advisory services related to crypto assets.
  • Issue or offer crypto assets (e.g., stablecoins or utility tokens) that fall under MiCA.

If your business model involves any of the above and you target EU clients, a Lithuania CASP is a strategic choice. It is also suitable for companies from outside the EU that want to establish a European hub without relocating management. For simpler setups, Panama offers a faster, lower-cost alternative, but without EU passporting.

License Type & Regulator

The license is a MiCA Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) authorization. It is issued by the Bank of Lithuania, which acts as the national competent authority (NCA) under MiCA. The Bank of Lithuania is responsible for supervising compliance with MiCA regulations, AML/CFT obligations, and ongoing reporting.

Under MiCA, CASPs are categorized by service classes, each with specific capital requirements. The license is valid indefinitely, subject to annual fees and continuous compliance. The Bank of Lithuania conducts thorough background checks on beneficial owners, management, and business plans. It also requires a local registered office and a physical presence (e.g., a registered address) in Lithuania, but does not mandate local directors.

For comparison, Estonia’s crypto license is also MiCA-compliant but has different capital thresholds and a longer track record. Lithuania’s advantage is its modern regulatory approach and faster processing times.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for a Lithuania CASP depends on the services offered. The capital must be held as own funds (e.g., paid-up share capital or reserves) and is calculated as follows:

  • EUR 50,000 – for services such as execution of orders on behalf of clients, placing of crypto-assets, and transfer services for crypto-assets.
  • EUR 125,000 – for custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients, or operation of a trading platform.
  • EUR 150,000 – for services that combine custody with other activities, or for more complex operations.

These figures are fixed by MiCA and are non-negotiable. The capital must be fully paid up before license issuance and maintained throughout the license’s life. Additional prudential requirements may apply, such as holding a percentage of overheads as capital. Consulting24 can help structure your capital to meet the exact tier for your service mix.

Tax Treatment

Lithuania offers a competitive tax environment for crypto businesses. Corporate income tax (CIT) is a flat 15% on profits, with a reduced 0% rate for small companies (revenue under EUR 300,000 and fewer than 10 employees) for the first year, then 5% on the first EUR 300,000 profit. VAT treatment of crypto transactions is generally exempt under EU VAT rules, though advisory services may be subject to VAT.

Key points:

  • No withholding tax on dividends paid to EU/EEA residents (subject to conditions).
  • No capital gains tax on crypto-to-crypto trades for businesses (treated as taxable income).
  • Transfer pricing rules apply for related-party transactions.

Lithuania also has an extensive double taxation treaty network. Tax planning is essential, and we recommend consulting a local tax advisor. For a zero-tax jurisdiction, Panama offers a territorial tax system.

Allowed Activities Under the License

The Lithuania MiCA CASP authorizes the following activities (as per MiCA Article 3(1)):

  • Custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients.
  • Operation of a trading platform for crypto-assets.
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for funds (fiat).
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for other crypto-assets.
  • Execution of orders for crypto-assets on behalf of clients.
  • Placing of crypto-assets.
  • Reception and transmission of orders for crypto-assets on behalf of clients.
  • Providing advice on crypto-assets.
  • Portfolio management of crypto-assets.
  • Transfer services for crypto-assets on behalf of clients.

You can apply for one or multiple service classes. Each class may have additional operational requirements, such as safeguarding client assets, segregation of funds, and disclosure rules. The license does not cover crypto-asset issuance (e.g., ICOs) which falls under separate MiCA rules for asset-referenced tokens (ARTs) and e-money tokens (EMTs).

Compliance & Trust: Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, you must maintain compliance with MiCA and Lithuanian law. Key ongoing obligations include:

  • AML/CFT: Implement risk-based customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting to the Financial Crime Investigation Service (FCIS).
  • Reporting: Submit annual financial statements, regulatory reports, and audit results to the Bank of Lithuania.
  • Capital Maintenance: Ensure own funds never fall below the minimum capital requirement.
  • Client Asset Protection: Segregate client crypto assets from company assets and maintain insurance or a guarantee.
  • Record Keeping: Retain records for at least 5 years.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Many applicants stumble on avoidable pitfalls. Here are common mistakes:

  • Incomplete Documentation: Submitting a generic business plan or AML policy without tailoring to your specific services.
  • Underestimating Capital: Not having the full capital amount in liquid funds before application.
  • Ignoring Beneficial Ownership: Failing to disclose all ultimate beneficial owners (UBOs) with 25%+ ownership.
  • Lack of Local Presence: Not securing a registered office or local compliance officer.
  • Not Preparing for Interviews: Management must be able to explain the business model and compliance framework convincingly.
  • Choosing the Wrong Service Class: Applying for too many or too few services, leading to unnecessary capital or limited scope.

What is the minimum capital for a Lithuania crypto license?

Can I passport my Lithuania license to other EU countries?

Yes. Under MiCA, a Lithuania CASP authorization allows you to provide services across the entire European Economic Area (EEA) without additional licenses in each country. You must notify the Bank of Lithuania of your intent to passport.

Is there a director residency requirement in Lithuania?

No. Lithuania does not require directors or shareholders to reside in the country. However, you must have a local registered office and a compliance officer (can be outsourced).
