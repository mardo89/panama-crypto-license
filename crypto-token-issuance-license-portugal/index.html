What Is the Crypto Token Issuance License Portugal?

The crypto token issuance license Portugal is a regulatory authorization required for entities that wish to issue crypto tokens to the public or provide crypto-asset services in Portugal. Under MiCA, token issuance is classified as an “offer to the public” of crypto-assets, requiring a white paper and, in many cases, prior approval from the competent authority. In Portugal, the Bank of Portugal is the designated national competent authority (NCA) for MiCA, responsible for supervising crypto-asset service providers (CASPs) and token issuers.

This license covers various token types, including utility tokens, asset-referenced tokens (ARTs), and e-money tokens (EMTs). However, security tokens fall under the Portuguese Securities Market Commission (CMVM) and are not covered by this license. The regime is designed to protect investors, ensure market integrity, and foster innovation.

Who Needs This License?

Any legal entity established in Portugal or seeking to passport services into Portugal that intends to:

  • Issue crypto tokens (including initial coin offerings or token sales) to the public.
  • Provide custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients.
  • Operate a trading platform for crypto-assets.
  • Exchange crypto-assets for fiat currency or other crypto-assets.
  • Execute orders for crypto-assets on behalf of clients.

If your project involves token issuance and you plan to target Portuguese investors, you must either obtain a license in Portugal or rely on a passport from another EU member state where you are already licensed. License Type and Regulator

The primary regulator for crypto token issuance in Portugal is the Bank of Portugal (Banco de Portugal). Under MiCA, the Bank of Portugal is responsible for authorizing and supervising crypto-asset service providers (CASPs) and token issuers. The license is officially a “Crypto-Asset Service Provider (CASP) Authorization” under MiCA, which includes permission to issue tokens.

For token issuance specifically, the issuer must also comply with the white paper requirements set out in MiCA. The white paper must be notified to the Bank of Portugal, and for certain tokens (e.g., ARTs or EMTs), prior approval may be required. The CMVM (Comissão do Mercado de Valores Mobiliários) retains oversight for security tokens that qualify as financial instruments. Consulting24 can help you classify your token correctly.

Capital Requirements

Under MiCA, the capital requirements for crypto-asset service providers (CASPs) are tiered based on the types of services offered. For token issuance alone (without additional CASP services), the minimum capital requirement is typically EUR 50,000. If the entity also provides custody, exchange, or other services, the requirement increases:

  • EUR 125,000 for custody and administration of crypto-assets.
  • EUR 150,000 for operating a trading platform or exchanging crypto-assets for fiat currency.

These figures are set by MiCA and are uniformly applied across EU member states, including Portugal. The capital must be held in liquid assets and maintained at all times. Consulting24 can help you structure your capital allocation to meet these requirements.

Tax Treatment for Crypto Token Issuance in Portugal

Portugal offers a favorable tax environment for crypto activities, but token issuance has specific implications. As of 2026, the Portuguese tax authority (Autoridade Tributária) treats crypto-asset transactions as follows:

  • Capital gains: Gains from the sale of crypto-assets held for less than one year are taxed at a flat rate of 28%. Gains from holdings over one year are exempt for individuals.
  • Token issuance: Proceeds from token issuance may be considered taxable income for the issuer, subject to corporate income tax (CIT) at a standard rate of 21% (plus municipal surcharges up to 1.5%).
  • VAT: Crypto-asset transactions, including issuance, are generally exempt from VAT under EU law.
  • White paper costs: Deductible as business expenses.

Allowed Activities Under the License

The crypto token issuance license Portugal, under MiCA, permits the following activities:

  • Offering crypto tokens to the public (including via ICOs, STOs, or direct sales).
  • Providing custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients.
  • Operating a crypto-asset trading platform.
  • Exchanging crypto-assets for fiat currency or other crypto-assets.
  • Executing orders for crypto-assets on behalf of clients.
  • Placing crypto-assets.
  • Receiving and transmitting orders for crypto-assets.

Note that the license does not cover services related to security tokens (which fall under CMVM) or derivatives. If your token qualifies as a financial instrument, a different authorization is required. Consulting24 can help you determine the correct classification.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining a crypto token issuance license in Portugal involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates with local legal partners to guide you through each step.

  1. Pre-application assessment: Determine your token classification and the scope of services. Prepare a business plan, compliance policies, and governance framework.
  2. White paper preparation: Draft a MiCA-compliant white paper with all required disclosures (risks, rights, technology, etc.). This must be notified to the Bank of Portugal.
  3. Capital deposit: Ensure the minimum capital is held in a Portuguese bank account or equivalent liquid assets.
  4. Submission: File the application with the Bank of Portugal, including all supporting documents (e.g., AML/CFT policies, organizational chart, financial projections).
  5. Review and approval: The Bank of Portugal has up to 6 months to review. They may request additional information. Once approved, you receive the CASP authorization.
  6. Post-licensing compliance: Ongoing reporting, audits, and maintenance of capital requirements.

Frequently asked questions

What is the crypto token issuance license Portugal?

It is a regulatory authorization under MiCA, supervised by the Bank of Portugal, required for issuing crypto tokens to the public or providing crypto-asset services in Portugal.

Who is the regulator for crypto token issuance in Portugal?

The Bank of Portugal (Banco de Portugal) is the primary regulator for MiCA-compliant crypto-asset service providers and token issuers. The CMVM oversees security tokens.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a token issuance license in Portugal?

For token issuance alone, the minimum capital is EUR 50,000. Higher tiers apply if additional services are offered: EUR 125,000 for custody and EUR 150,000 for exchange platforms.

How long does it take to get a crypto token issuance license in Portugal?

Typically 4 to 8 months, including preparation of documentation, white paper notification, and regulator review. The Bank of Portugal has up to 6 months to decide.

What are the tax implications of token issuance in Portugal?

Corporate income tax of 21% applies to issuance proceeds. Individual capital gains on crypto held over one year are exempt. VAT is generally exempt.

Can I passport a Portuguese crypto license to other EU countries?

Yes, under MiCA, a Portuguese CASP authorization allows you to provide services across the EU via passporting, subject to notification to host member states.

What documents are required for the application?

You need a business plan, white paper, AML/CFT policies, governance framework, financial projections, and proof of capital. The exact list is provided by the Bank of Portugal.

Is a white paper mandatory for token issuance in Portugal?

Yes, under MiCA, any offer of crypto tokens to the public requires a white paper that must be notified to the Bank of Portugal. Exemptions exist for small offerings or tokens already admitted to trading.

Can I issue security tokens under the Portugal crypto license?
