What Is the Jersey Crypto Stablecoin License?

The Jersey crypto stablecoin license is a regulatory authorization issued by the JFSC that allows a company to issue, manage, and redeem stablecoins (crypto assets pegged to a stable asset like fiat currency) within or from Jersey. It falls under the Island's broader digital asset framework, which was updated in 2025 to align with international standards and the EU's MiCA regulation.

This license is designed for businesses that want to operate a stablecoin project with credibility and regulatory oversight. It covers both fiat-collateralized and algorithmic stablecoins, provided the issuer meets specific reserve and governance requirements. The JFSC's approach is risk-based, meaning the level of scrutiny depends on the scale and nature of the stablecoin's use.

License Type and Regulator

The relevant regulator is the Jersey Financial Services Commission (JFSC). The license type is the Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) License with a specific endorsement for stablecoin issuance. As of 2026, the JFSC has fully implemented the updated Digital Asset Framework (DAF), which includes specific provisions for stablecoins.

There are two sub-categories:

  • Class 1 - Issuance only: For entities that issue stablecoins but do not provide other VASP services (e.g., exchange, custody).
  • Class 2 - Issuance plus other services: For entities that issue stablecoins and also offer exchange, custody, or payment services.

The JFSC conducts a thorough fit-and-proper test for all directors and shareholders. Consulting24 advises on structuring your corporate governance to meet these standards.

Capital Requirement

The Jersey JFSC imposes a minimum capital requirement based on the type of stablecoin activity. As of 2026, the rules align with international standards:

  • Class 1 (Issuance only): Minimum capital of €50,000 (or equivalent in GBP).
  • Class 2 (Issuance plus other services): Minimum capital of €125,000.

Higher capital may be required if the stablecoin is deemed systemically important or if the issuer holds customer funds. The capital must be held in a regulated bank account in Jersey or a recognised jurisdiction. Tax Treatment

Jersey offers a favourable tax regime for stablecoin issuers. Corporate income tax is generally 0% for most businesses, except for certain financial services companies which pay 10% or 20%. For VASP license holders, the standard rate is 0% on profits from stablecoin issuance, provided the company does not conduct regulated banking or insurance activities.

There is no capital gains tax, no VAT on digital asset transactions, and no withholding tax on dividends paid to non-resident shareholders. However, stamp duty may apply on share transfers. Allowed Activities

Under the Jersey stablecoin license, the following activities are permitted:

  • Issuance of stablecoins (fiat-collateralised, crypto-collateralised, or algorithmic).
  • Redemption of stablecoins for fiat or other assets.
  • Management of reserve assets backing the stablecoin.
  • Provision of custody services for the stablecoin's underlying assets (Class 2 only).
  • Exchange services between stablecoins and other digital assets (Class 2 only).
  • Payment services using the stablecoin (Class 2 only).

Activities not covered include lending, staking, or offering derivatives, which may require separate licensing. Consulting24 can help you define your business model to ensure it fits within the license scope.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The application process for a Jersey stablecoin license involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step.

  1. Pre-application consultation: We assess your project and prepare a gap analysis against JFSC requirements.
  2. Company incorporation: Establish a Jersey company with a local registered office and directors (at least two, one of whom must be a Jersey resident).
  3. Documentation preparation: Draft a business plan, risk management framework, AML/CFT policies, financial projections, and governance structure.
  4. Submission: Submit the application to the JFSC along with the fee. The JFSC may request additional information within 30 days.
  5. Review and interview: The JFSC conducts a fit-and-proper test on directors and shareholders. A face-to-face interview may be required.
  6. Approval and licensing: Once satisfied, the JFSC issues the license. Conditions may apply (e.g., reporting obligations).
  7. Post-licensing compliance: Ongoing reporting, audits, and compliance with reserve requirements.

The entire process typically takes 4-8 months. Consulting24 provides a dedicated project manager to keep your application on track.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, stablecoin issuers must comply with ongoing obligations to maintain their license. These include:

  • Annual AML/CFT audit and reporting.
  • Monthly or quarterly reserve attestations (depending on the stablecoin's scale).
  • Submission of annual financial statements to the JFSC.
  • Notification of any changes in directors, shareholders, or business model.
  • Maintenance of minimum capital at all times.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make errors that delay or derail their application. Here are the most common pitfalls:

  • Insufficient capital: Underestimating the capital needed for both the license and the reserve backing.

  • Incomplete documentation: Submitting a business plan that lacks detail on risk management or exit strategies.
  • Ignoring tax implications: Assuming all activities are tax-free without proper structuring.
  • Choosing the wrong license class: Applying for Class 2 when Class 1 suffices, increasing costs and complexity.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Jersey stablecoin license?

The minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for Class 1 (issuance only) and EUR 125,000 for Class 2 (issuance plus other services). Higher capital may be required for systemically important stablecoins.

How long does it take to get a Jersey stablecoin license?

The typical timeline is 4 to 8 months from application submission to approval. This depends on the complexity of your application and the JFSC's workload.

What is the corporate tax rate for stablecoin issuers in Jersey?

Jersey applies a 0% corporate tax rate on profits from stablecoin issuance for most VASP license holders. There is no capital gains tax or VAT on digital asset transactions.

Can I issue an algorithmic stablecoin under the Jersey license?

Yes, the JFSC framework covers algorithmic stablecoins, but the issuer must demonstrate strong risk management and reserve mechanisms. The regulator may impose additional conditions.

Does Jersey require a local director?
