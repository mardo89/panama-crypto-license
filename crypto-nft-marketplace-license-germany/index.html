What Is the Crypto NFT Marketplace License Germany?

The crypto NFT marketplace license Germany is a BaFin authorisation required to operate a platform where users can buy, sell, or trade crypto assets - including NFTs that qualify as crypto assets under German law. Since MiCA came into full force in 2026, the license is issued under the German Banking Act (KWG) and aligns with the EU-wide CASP regime. The license allows you to offer custody, exchange, and brokerage services for crypto assets, including NFTs, provided they meet the legal definition of a crypto asset.

Key features of the license:

  • Regulated by BaFin (Bundesanstalt für Finanzdienstleistungsaufsicht)
  • Requires a minimum capital of EUR 50,000 to EUR 150,000 depending on service scope
  • Full AML compliance with a local AML officer
  • Ongoing reporting and audit obligations
  • Valid across the EU under MiCA passporting

License Type and Regulator

The relevant regulator for the crypto NFT marketplace license Germany is the Federal Financial Supervisory Authority (BaFin). The license type is a “Crypto Asset Service Provider” (CASP) authorisation under Section 2 of the German Banking Act (KWG), as amended by the EU MiCAR (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation).

Key points about the regulator:

  • BaFin is known for its thorough and lengthy application process
  • It requires a local physical office in Germany (or at least a registered branch)
  • Applicants must have at least two managing directors who are “fit and proper”
  • An external auditor is required for the application audit report

MiCA has harmonised requirements across the EU, but Germany applies its own national options, including higher capital tiers for certain services. Consulting24 coordinates with local partners to ensure your application meets all BaFin expectations.

Capital Requirement for the German Crypto License

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for a CASP in Germany is tiered based on the services offered. For an NFT marketplace that includes custody and exchange, the capital requirement is typically EUR 125,000. If you only provide exchange services without custody, it may be EUR 50,000. If you also offer brokerage or placement, it can rise to EUR 150,000.

Important points:

  • Capital must be held as equity or eligible capital instruments
  • BaFin may require additional capital based on the volume of transactions
  • The capital must be maintained at all times as part of ongoing solvency requirements

Tax Treatment for Crypto NFT Marketplaces in Germany

Germany has a clear tax framework for crypto assets. For NFT marketplace operators, the key taxes are:

  • Corporate income tax (Körperschaftsteuer): 15% plus solidarity surcharge (approx. 15.8%)
  • Trade tax (Gewerbesteuer): varies by municipality, typically 7-17% (effective combined rate ~30%)
  • VAT: 19% on service fees (commission, listing fees, etc.) - but NFT sales may be VAT-exempt if treated as digital assets
  • Personal income tax for individual traders: 0% if held >1 year (but not applicable to corporate operators)

NFTs are generally treated as “other assets” for tax purposes. If your marketplace charges fees, those are subject to VAT. Capital gains from your own trading are subject to corporate tax. Consulting24 recommends engaging a German tax advisor for your specific situation.

  • Insufficient capital or proof of capital (capital must be freely available)

Frequently asked questions

What is the cost of a crypto NFT marketplace license in Germany?

The total cost typically ranges from EUR 60,000 to 170,000, including BaFin fees, legal and consulting fees, external audit, and company setup. Exact figures depend on your business model and are confirmed during a consultation.

How long does it take to get a German crypto license?

The process usually takes 6 to 12 months. Preparation takes 2-4 months, and BaFin review takes 3-6 months. The timeline can vary based on the complexity of your application and BaFin's workload.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a German crypto license?

Under MiCA, the minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for simple exchange services, EUR 125,000 for custody and exchange, and EUR 150,000 for additional services like brokerage. Capital must be maintained at all times.

Do I need a physical office in Germany for the license?

Yes, BaFin expects a registered office in Germany with real substance. A virtual office is not sufficient. You need a physical presence where management and compliance functions are located.

Can I operate an NFT marketplace with a German license across the EU?

Yes, under MiCA passporting, a German license allows you to provide services in any EU member state without additional licenses. You must notify BaFin of your intent to passport.

What are the tax rates for a crypto NFT marketplace in Germany?

Corporate income tax is about 15.8% (including solidarity surcharge), plus trade tax of 7-17% depending on location, resulting in an effective rate of around 30%. VAT of 19% applies to service fees.

Is it possible to get a German crypto license without an external auditor?

No, an external auditor is mandatory. The auditor reviews your AML manual, business plan, and organisational structure, and issues a report that must be submitted to BaFin.

What happens if I operate without a license in Germany?

Operating without a license is a criminal offence. BaFin can impose fines, shut down your platform, and refer the matter to prosecutors. Individuals may face imprisonment.

How does Germany compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Panama is much cheaper (EUR 6,000 flat) and faster (1-2 months), with no capital requirement and 0% tax on foreign income. However, it does not offer EU passporting. Germany is better for EU market access and credibility.
