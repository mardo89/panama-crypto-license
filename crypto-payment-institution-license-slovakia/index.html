What Is the Crypto Payment Institution License Slovakia?

The crypto payment institution license in Slovakia is a regulatory authorization issued under the national implementation of the Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA). It allows firms to operate as a CASP (Crypto-Asset Service Provider) and offer services such as custody, exchange, transfer, and payment services using crypto-assets. The license is recognized across all EU member states via the single passport.

Slovakia's National Bank of Slovakia (Národná banka Slovenska, NBS) is the competent authority responsible for licensing and supervision. The license is designed for businesses that want to integrate crypto-asset services into traditional payment systems, bridging fiat and digital currencies.

Key features include a regulated environment, mandatory compliance with AML/CFT rules, and the ability to serve clients throughout the EU. Consulting24 advises and coordinates the entire application process, from company setup to regulatory submission.

License Type & Regulator

The license is formally called the "Crypto-Asset Service Provider" (CASP) license under MiCA, implemented in Slovakia by an amendment to the Payment Services Act. The regulator is the National Bank of Slovakia (NBS), which oversees licensing, ongoing supervision, and enforcement.

There is no separate "crypto payment institution" license distinct from the CASP license; the term reflects the payment-service nature of many crypto activities. The NBS applies a risk-based approach and requires applicants to demonstrate strong governance, capital adequacy, and AML controls.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for CASPs is tiered based on the services offered:

  • EUR 50,000 for services limited to custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients.
  • EUR 125,000 for exchange services (fiat-to-crypto or crypto-to-crypto) and transfer services.
  • EUR 150,000 for services involving the placement of crypto-assets (underwriting) or operating a trading platform.

These amounts must be fully paid up and maintained as own funds. Slovakia does not impose additional national top-ups. Consulting24 advises on the exact tier applicable to your service mix and helps structure your capital accordingly.

Tax Treatment in Slovakia

Slovakia applies a standard corporate income tax rate of 21% (15% for small businesses with turnover below EUR 100,000). Crypto-asset transactions are generally treated as barter transactions for VAT purposes, meaning they are exempt from VAT. However, income from crypto trading or mining is subject to corporate tax.

There is no specific crypto tax regime; general tax rules apply. Capital gains on crypto-assets held as investments are taxed at the same corporate rate. Slovakia has a wide network of double tax treaties that can reduce withholding taxes on cross-border payments.

Allowed Activities

The license permits the following activities under MiCA:

  • Custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients.
  • Operation of a crypto-asset trading platform.
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for fiat currency or other crypto-assets.
  • Execution of orders for crypto-assets on behalf of clients.
  • Placing of crypto-assets (underwriting).
  • Receiving and transmitting orders for crypto-assets.
  • Providing advice on crypto-assets.
  • Portfolio management of crypto-assets.
  • Transfer services for crypto-assets on behalf of clients.

Payment services in fiat currency require a separate Payment Institution license under PSD2. However, the crypto payment institution license can be combined with a payment license for a fully integrated offering. Consulting24 can advise on the optimal licensing structure.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Pre-application preparation: Define your business model, service types, and target markets. Prepare a detailed business plan, AML/CFT policies, risk management framework, and governance structure.
  2. Company incorporation: Set up a Slovak limited liability company (s.r.o.) with registered office and local management. Minimum share capital is EUR 5,000.
  3. Capital deposit: Deposit the required own funds in a Slovak bank account and obtain a confirmation from the bank.
  4. Submit application to NBS: File the complete application package including all required documents. The NBS has up to 3 months to review and may request additional information.
  5. On-site inspection (if required): NBS may conduct an on-site review of your premises and systems.
  6. License decision: If approved, you receive the license and are added to the NBS register. You can then begin operations and passport to other EU states.

Compliance & Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, you must comply with ongoing requirements:

  • AML/CFT: Implement and maintain strong KYC/AML procedures, report suspicious transactions, and conduct regular audits.
  • Capital maintenance: Always hold the minimum own funds. Additional capital may be required if your business grows.
  • Reporting: Submit periodic reports to NBS on financial health, transaction volumes, and compliance incidents.
  • Governance: Ensure fit and proper management, independent board members, and clear internal policies.
  • Consumer protection: Maintain transparent terms, complaint handling procedures, and segregation of client assets.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make these errors during the Slovak licensing process:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing or poorly prepared AML policies, business plans, or risk assessments cause delays.
  • Underestimating capital: Failing to hold the correct tier of own funds from the start.
  • Ignoring local presence: NBS requires a physical office and local management. Virtual offices may not suffice.
  • Overlooking passporting: Not notifying NBS when you plan to operate in other EU countries.
  • Choosing the wrong license type: Some firms apply for a payment license when they need a CASP license, or vice versa.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a crypto payment institution license and a CASP license in Slovakia?

In Slovakia, the term 'crypto payment institution license' is commonly used to refer to the CASP (Crypto-Asset Service Provider) license under MiCA. There is no separate license; the name reflects the payment-oriented nature of many crypto services. The license covers all CASP activities, including custody, exchange, and transfer services.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Slovakia?

The typical timeline is 6 to 12 months from preparation to issuance. The National Bank of Slovakia has up to 3 months to review a complete application, but preparation of documentation and company setup can take additional months. Consulting24 can provide a more precise estimate based on your specific case.

What are the capital requirements for a crypto license in Slovakia?

Capital requirements are tiered under MiCA: EUR 50,000 for custody-only services, EUR 125,000 for exchange and transfer services, and EUR 150,000 for platform operation or underwriting. These amounts must be maintained as own funds at all times.

Can I passport the Slovak crypto license to other EU countries?

Yes, the Slovak CASP license allows you to provide services across all EU member states via the single passport. You must notify the National Bank of Slovakia of your intention to operate in other countries, but no additional license is needed.

What is the corporate tax rate for crypto companies in Slovakia?

The standard corporate income tax rate is 21%. Small businesses with turnover below EUR 100,000 pay 15%. Crypto transactions are generally VAT-exempt. Capital gains on crypto are taxed as ordinary income. Consulting24 recommends consulting a local tax advisor for your specific situation.

Do I need a physical office in Slovakia to get the license?

Yes, the National Bank of Slovakia requires a registered office and a physical presence in Slovakia. A virtual office may not be sufficient. You also need at least one local director or manager. Consulting24 can help you set up a compliant office arrangement.

What are the main challenges in obtaining a Slovak crypto license?

Common challenges include meeting the capital requirement, preparing comprehensive AML policies, and demonstrating a strong business plan. The NBS also scrutinizes the fit and proper status of management. Working with experienced consultants like Consulting24 can mitigate these challenges.

How does Slovakia compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Panama offers a low-cost, fast setup (EUR 6,000 flat fee, 2-4 months) but no EU passport. Slovakia requires higher capital (EUR 50k-150k) and takes longer, but provides access to the entire EU market. Your choice depends on whether you need EU passporting or prefer a simpler, cheaper offshore structure.

Can I apply for a crypto license in Slovakia if my company is already registered in another EU country?

Yes, you can establish a Slovak subsidiary and apply for the license. Alternatively, if your home country has implemented MiCA, you may be able to passport your existing license to Slovakia. Consulting24 can advise on the most efficient approach.

What ongoing reporting obligations does the Slovak crypto license require?

Licensees must submit periodic reports to the NBS, including financial statements, transaction volumes, AML compliance reports, and any material changes to the business. Annual supervisory fees also apply. Consulting24 offers ongoing compliance support to help you meet these obligations.
