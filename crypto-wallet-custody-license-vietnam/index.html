Overview: What Is a Crypto Wallet Custody License in Vietnam?

A crypto wallet custody license in Vietnam refers to the regulatory approval required to offer custodial wallet services for digital assets. Currently, Vietnam has not enacted a specific law for crypto licensing, but the government is piloting a regulatory sandbox for blockchain and cryptocurrency businesses. The sandbox, expected to be fully operational by 2026, will allow companies to apply for a temporary license to provide custody services under the supervision of the SBV and Ministry of Finance.

Until the sandbox is finalised, crypto custody services operate in a legal grey area. However, the government has issued warnings against using crypto for payments, and banks are prohibited from facilitating crypto transactions. For businesses serious about compliance, obtaining a license through the sandbox or a future legal framework is critical. License Type and Regulator

The primary regulator for crypto activities in Vietnam is the State Bank of Vietnam (SBV), which oversees payment-related crypto services. The Ministry of Finance also plays a role in regulating securities token offerings and investment funds. As of 2026, the specific license type for custody is not finalised, but it is expected to be a “Crypto Custody Service Provider License” under the sandbox framework.

Key features of the expected license:

  • Valid for 2 years initially, renewable
  • Requires a local legal entity (e.g., limited liability company)
  • Must comply with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) laws
  • Subject to ongoing reporting to SBV

Tax Treatment for Crypto Custody in Vietnam

Vietnam's tax treatment of crypto activities is still developing. As of 2026, the following applies:

  • Capital Gains: Gains from crypto trading by individuals are taxed at 0.1% of transaction value (a provisional measure).

Step-by-Step Process to Obtain a Vietnam Crypto License

Obtaining a crypto wallet custody license in Vietnam involves several stages. Here is a typical process:

  1. Company Incorporation: Register a local legal entity (e.g., LLC) with the Department of Planning and Investment.
  2. Prepare Documentation: Draft business plan, AML/CTF policies, risk management framework, and technical specifications.
  3. Engage Local Counsel: Work with a Vietnamese law firm to submit the sandbox application to SBV.
  4. Submit Application: File the sandbox proposal along with supporting documents. The SBV reviews within 60 days.
  5. Background Checks: Ultimate beneficial owners (UBOs) and key management undergo fit and proper tests.
  6. License Issuance: If approved, a sandbox license is granted for 2 years, subject to conditions.
  7. Ongoing Compliance: Submit periodic reports, maintain AML procedures, and prepare for full licensing post-sandbox.
