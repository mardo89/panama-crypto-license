What Is a Crypto OTC Desk License in Portugal?

A crypto OTC desk license in Portugal is a regulatory authorization that permits a company to provide virtual asset services, specifically for off-exchange trading. OTC desks match buyers and sellers directly, often handling large transactions that would impact public order books. In Portugal, this activity falls under the scope of virtual asset service providers (VASPs) regulated by the Bank of Portugal (BdP) and the CMVM.

Key aspects of the license include:

  • Legal authorization to operate as a VASP in Portugal.
  • Compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorist financing (CTF) obligations.
  • Registration with the BdP and oversight by the CMVM for securities-related activities.

Portugal's regulatory framework is aligned with the EU's Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) regulation, which is fully in force as of 2026. This means higher capital requirements and stricter compliance, but also greater credibility and access to the European market. For example, an OTC desk handling trades over EUR 1 million per transaction must demonstrate strong risk management and liquidity controls.

Who Needs a Crypto OTC Desk License in Portugal?

Any entity that facilitates the exchange of virtual assets for fiat currency or other virtual assets on behalf of clients, especially in large volumes, requires a license. This includes:

  • OTC trading firms that execute large trades off-exchange.
  • Brokerage services that match buyers and sellers.
  • Market makers providing liquidity in digital assets.
  • Institutional investors offering trading services to clients.

If your business involves handling client funds or assets, you need a license to operate legally in Portugal. Operating without one can lead to fines up to EUR 5 million or 10% of annual turnover, plus legal action and reputational damage. License Type and Regulator

The crypto OTC desk license in Portugal is a registration as a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP). The primary regulator is the Bank of Portugal (Banco de Portugal), which oversees AML/CTF compliance. For activities that involve securities or financial instruments, the Portuguese Securities Market Commission (CMVM) also has authority.

Under MiCA, VASPs are subject to harmonized rules across the EU. Portugal has implemented MiCA through national legislation, requiring VASPs to obtain authorization from the BdP. The license is valid for the entire EU, allowing passporting of services.

For OTC desks specifically, the license covers the service of exchanging virtual assets for fiat currency or other virtual assets. If you also offer custody or transfer services, additional permissions may be needed. The regulator conducts fit-and-proper tests on all directors and beneficial owners, including background checks and financial soundness.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, capital requirements for VASPs in Portugal depend on the services offered. For OTC desk services (exchange of virtual assets), the minimum capital is EUR 125,000. This is a tier 2 capital requirement under MiCA, applicable to services that involve holding client assets or executing trades.

If your OTC desk also provides custody of virtual assets, the capital requirement may increase to EUR 150,000. For simple exchange services without custody, EUR 50,000 may suffice, but OTC desks typically require the higher tier due to the volume and risk.

Capital must be fully paid up and maintained as own funds. It can be in the form of cash or liquid assets. Consulting24 advises on the exact requirement based on your business model. For example, if your OTC desk plans to hold client crypto assets for settlement, you will need the EUR 150,000 tier.

Tax Treatment

Portugal offers a favorable tax environment for crypto businesses. As of 2026, capital gains from crypto assets held for more than one year are tax-exempt for individuals. For corporate entities, profits from crypto trading are subject to the standard corporate income tax rate of 21% (plus municipal surcharges up to 1.5%).

OTC desks must register for VAT, but crypto-to-fiat transactions are generally exempt from VAT under EU law. However, fees charged for OTC services may be subject to VAT at the standard rate of 23%. Transfer pricing rules apply if transactions involve related parties.

It is essential to maintain proper accounting records and file annual tax returns. Consulting24 can coordinate with local tax advisors to ensure compliance. For instance, if your OTC desk charges a commission of 0.5% per trade, that commission may be subject to VAT.

Allowed Activities

A crypto OTC desk license in Portugal permits the following activities:

  • Exchange of virtual assets for fiat currency (e.g., EUR, USD).
  • Exchange of virtual assets for other virtual assets.
  • Execution of orders on behalf of clients (OTC trading).
  • Provision of investment advice related to virtual assets (if registered separately).

Activities not allowed without additional licensing include:

  • Operating a public exchange or trading platform.
  • Providing custody of virtual assets (requires separate authorization).
  • Issuing stablecoins or other crypto assets.

If you plan to offer custody services, you need a separate custody license under MiCA. Consulting24 advises on the scope of your license and can help expand it later. For example, if you want to offer both OTC trading and custody, you would need two separate authorizations.

Step-by-Step Process

The process to obtain a crypto OTC desk license in Portugal involves several stages:

  1. Company incorporation: Register a company in Portugal with a minimum share capital of EUR 5,000. The company must have a registered office in Portugal.
  2. AML/CTF compliance: Develop internal policies, appoint a compliance officer, and register with the Bank of Portugal's AML register.
  3. Application submission: Submit the VASP registration application to the BdP, including business plan, financial projections, and governance structure.
  4. Background checks: Provide criminal records and proof of good repute for directors and beneficial owners.
  5. Review and approval: The BdP reviews the application within 3-6 months. If approved, the company is added to the VASP register.
  6. Ongoing compliance: Submit regular AML reports, undergo audits, and maintain capital requirements.

  • Transaction monitoring and reporting suspicious activities to the BdP.
  • Record keeping for at least 5 years.
  • Annual AML audit by an external auditor.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Many applicants make errors that delay or derail their license. Avoid these common pitfalls:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing business plans or financial projections. Ensure all required documents are submitted.
  • Underestimating capital: Not having sufficient paid-up capital. The EUR 125,000 minimum must be in the company's account.
  • Poor AML policies: Generic or copied policies. Tailor them to your OTC desk operations.
  • Ignoring beneficial ownership: All ultimate beneficial owners must be disclosed and pass fit-and-proper tests.
  • Not engaging local experts: Attempting to do it alone often leads to rejection. Consulting24 advises and coordinates to avoid these errors.

Another common mistake is underestimating the time needed for banking. Start the account opening process as soon as you receive your VASP registration.
