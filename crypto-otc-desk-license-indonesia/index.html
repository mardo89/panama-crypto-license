What Is a Crypto OTC Desk License Indonesia?

A Crypto OTC Desk License Indonesia is a regulatory approval from Bappebti that allows a company to operate an over-the-counter (OTC) crypto trading desk. Unlike exchange-based trading, OTC desks facilitate large-volume trades directly between counterparties, often with negotiated pricing. This license is essential for businesses that want to offer institutional-grade crypto trading services in Indonesia.

The license is part of Indonesia's broader crypto asset trading framework, which includes Physical Crypto Asset Traders (PFAK) and Crypto Asset Exchanges. An OTC desk license specifically permits the matching and execution of large trades without a public order book. As of 2026, Bappebti has refined its rules to ensure OTC desks comply with anti-money laundering (AML) and know-your-customer (KYC) standards.

Key features of the license include: permission to trade crypto assets with institutional clients, ability to hold client funds in segregated accounts, and obligation to report all transactions to Bappebti. The license is valid for a fixed period and must be renewed.

Who Needs a Crypto OTC Desk License Indonesia?

The Crypto OTC Desk License Indonesia is required for any legal entity that intends to operate an OTC crypto trading desk within Indonesia. This includes:

  • Crypto brokers offering direct trades to high-net-worth individuals or institutions.
  • Market makers providing liquidity for large crypto transactions.
  • Family offices or hedge funds that execute block trades off-exchange.
  • International crypto firms expanding into the Indonesian market.

If your business model involves matching buyers and sellers of crypto assets in a non-exchange setting, you need this license. Operating without it can lead to fines, suspension, or criminal charges. Even if you already hold a different crypto license (e.g., exchange license), an OTC desk requires separate authorization.

License Type and Regulator

The Crypto OTC Desk License Indonesia is issued by the Commodity Futures Trading Regulatory Agency (Bappebti), which is the primary regulator for crypto assets in Indonesia. Bappebti operates under the Ministry of Trade and has been overseeing crypto asset trading since 2019. The license is classified as a 'Crypto Asset OTC Desk Operator' permit, distinct from exchange or custodian licenses.

As of 2026, Bappebti requires all OTC desks to be registered as a Physical Crypto Asset Trader (PFAK) with an additional OTC endorsement. The regulatory framework is based on Bappebti Regulation No. 8 of 2021 and subsequent amendments. Key requirements include: a minimum paid-up capital, a secure trading platform, AML/KYC procedures, and regular reporting.

For comparison, other jurisdictions like Lithuania and Estonia have different regulatory bodies and capital requirements. Indonesia's approach is more prescriptive, with clear capital tiers and operational rules.

Capital Requirement

Bappebti sets a minimum paid-up capital requirement for Crypto OTC Desk License Indonesia. As of 2026, the minimum capital is IDR 1,000,000,000 (approximately EUR 60,000). This capital must be deposited in a bank account in Indonesia and cannot be withdrawn without Bappebti's approval. The capital serves as a financial guarantee for client protection and operational stability.

Additional capital may be required based on the volume of transactions or the number of clients. For high-volume OTC desks, Bappebti may ask for up to IDR 5,000,000,000 (EUR 300,000). The capital must be maintained throughout the license period. Failure to maintain the minimum capital can result in license suspension.

Compare this with Panama, where the company setup fee is a flat EUR 6,000 with no specific crypto capital requirement, or with Lithuania where capital tiers range from EUR 50,000 to 150,000 under MiCA. Indonesia's requirement is moderate but must be held locally.

Tax Treatment

Indonesia imposes specific taxes on crypto asset transactions. For OTC desks, the tax treatment includes:

  • Value Added Tax (VAT): 0.11% of the transaction value for crypto asset sales (as of 2026). This is a final tax.
  • Income Tax (PPh): 0.1% of the transaction value for the seller, and 0.2% for the buyer (if the buyer is a tax subject).
  • Corporate Income Tax: Standard rate of 22% on net profits from OTC desk operations.

OTC desks are responsible for withholding and remitting these taxes to the Indonesian tax authority (DJP). Proper tax compliance is essential to avoid penalties. International firms should also consider double taxation agreements between Indonesia and their home country.

Allowed Activities

With a Crypto OTC Desk License Indonesia, you are permitted to engage in the following activities:

  • Facilitating direct crypto asset trades between institutional clients.
  • Providing price quotes and executing trades at negotiated rates.
  • Holding client crypto assets in segregated wallets.
  • Offering custody services for large holdings (if additional approval obtained).
  • Reporting trades to Bappebti and ensuring AML/KYC compliance.

Prohibited activities include: operating a public exchange, offering margin trading or derivatives without separate licenses, and providing services to retail clients without proper safeguards. The license is strictly for OTC activities. If you plan to offer exchange services, you need a separate Crypto Asset Exchange license from Bappebti.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining a Crypto OTC Desk License Indonesia involves several steps. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each stage:

  1. Pre-incorporation: Determine the legal structure (PT company) and prepare draft articles of association.
  2. Company registration: Incorporate a PT with the Ministry of Law and Human Rights, with a minimum capital of IDR 1,000,000,000.
  3. Document preparation: Compile business plan, AML/KYC policies, IT security documentation, and financial statements.
  4. Application submission: Submit the application to Bappebti along with supporting documents and payment of the application fee.
  5. Review and interview: Bappebti reviews the application, may request clarifications, and conducts an interview with key personnel.
  6. Approval and licensing: If approved, Bappebti issues the OTC desk license. The company must then set up operations and start reporting.

The entire process typically takes 3-6 months. Delays often occur due to incomplete documentation or failure to meet capital requirements. Our advisory service helps avoid common pitfalls.

Banking and Payment Integration

Operating a crypto OTC desk in Indonesia requires a local bank account for fiat transactions. Most Indonesian banks are cautious about crypto-related accounts, but with a Bappebti license, opening an account becomes easier. Recommended banks include Bank Mandiri, Bank Central Asia (BCA), and Bank Negara Indonesia (BNI).

For payment integration, OTC desks typically use local payment gateways like Midtrans or Xendit for fiat settlements. Crypto settlements are done via blockchain. Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, OTC desks must comply with ongoing obligations set by Bappebti. These include:

  • Reporting: Submit monthly transaction reports and annual audited financial statements.
  • AML/KYC: Implement and maintain strong AML/KYC procedures, including suspicious transaction reporting.
  • Capital maintenance: Keep the minimum paid-up capital at all times.
  • Audits: Undergo periodic audits by Bappebti or appointed auditors.
  • Renewal: License renewal every 3 years, subject to compliance review.

Common Mistakes in the Application Process

Many applicants face delays or rejections due to common errors. Avoid these pitfalls:

  • Insufficient capital: Not meeting the minimum paid-up capital requirement.
  • Incomplete documentation: Missing business plans, AML policies, or IT security assessments.
  • Unclear ownership structure: Bappebti requires transparency on beneficial owners.
  • Poor AML/KYC procedures: Using generic templates that don't meet Indonesian standards.
  • Ignoring local partnerships: Bappebti may require a local director or shareholder.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a crypto OTC desk license and a crypto exchange license in Indonesia?

An OTC desk license allows direct, negotiated trades between parties, typically for large volumes, without a public order book. An exchange license permits a platform where multiple buyers and sellers trade on a central order book. Both are issued by Bappebti but have different requirements and operational rules.

Can a foreign company apply for a Crypto OTC Desk License Indonesia?

Yes, but the company must be incorporated as a PT (Perseroan Terbatas) in Indonesia. Foreign ownership is allowed up to 100% for crypto asset traders, but a local director is often required. Consulting24 advises on the optimal corporate structure.

What are the ongoing costs after obtaining the license?

Ongoing costs include annual compliance fees, audit costs, AML/KYC system maintenance, and staff salaries. Expect to budget around IDR 50-100 million per year for compliance and reporting. Exact figures depend on transaction volume and complexity.

Is there a physical office requirement?

Yes, Bappebti requires a physical office in Indonesia with a registered address. The office must have adequate facilities for operations and be accessible for inspections. Virtual offices are not accepted.

How long does the license remain valid?

The Crypto OTC Desk License Indonesia is valid for 3 years, after which it must be renewed. Renewal requires a compliance review and payment of a renewal fee. Failure to renew results in automatic revocation.

Can I offer crypto derivatives through an OTC desk license?

No, derivatives trading requires a separate license from Bappebti or the Financial Services Authority (OJK). The OTC desk license only covers spot crypto asset trades. Offering derivatives without proper authorization is illegal.

What happens if I operate without a license?

Operating an unlicensed OTC desk in Indonesia is a criminal offense. Penalties include fines up to IDR 10 billion, imprisonment up to 5 years, and confiscation of assets. Bappebti actively monitors and enforces compliance.

What is the typical timeline for the application?

Can I use the license to serve retail clients?

The OTC desk license is primarily for institutional clients. Serving retail clients may require additional registration or a different license type. Bappebti has specific rules for retail trading, including transaction limits and investor protection measures.
