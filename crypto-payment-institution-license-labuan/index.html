What Is the Crypto Payment Institution License Labuan?

The Crypto Payment Institution License Labuan is a regulatory authorization that allows companies to provide crypto payment services legally within and from Labuan. It covers activities such as exchanging digital currencies for fiat, facilitating crypto payments, and operating crypto wallets. The license is issued under the Labuan Financial Services and Securities Act 2010, as amended, and is supervised by the LFSA.

This license is part of Labuan's broader initiative to attract fintech and digital asset businesses. It offers a stable regulatory environment, low operational costs, and access to the ASEAN market. Unlike some jurisdictions that have imposed heavy restrictions, Labuan maintains a balanced approach, encouraging innovation while ensuring compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) standards.

Labuan's regulatory framework is designed to be flexible. For example, the LFSA has issued specific guidelines for digital assets, including a sandbox environment for testing new products. This makes Labuan particularly attractive for startups and established firms alike. The license is not a general VASP registration but a specific category for payment institutions, which means it has tailored requirements for capital, reporting, and risk management.

License Type and Regulator

The license is issued by the Labuan Financial Services Authority (LFSA), the sole regulator for financial services in Labuan. The LFSA operates under the Labuan Financial Services Authority Act 1996 and oversees all licensed entities, including banks, insurance companies, and digital asset service providers.

The Crypto Payment Institution License is a distinct category under the Labuan Financial Services and Securities Act 2010. It is not a general VASP registration but a specific license for payment institutions. The LFSA requires applicants to demonstrate strong AML/CTF policies, adequate capital, and fit-and-proper management. The regulator conducts thorough background checks on directors and shareholders, including criminal record checks and financial history reviews.

Labuan's regulatory approach is principles-based, meaning the LFSA expects firms to adhere to the spirit of the law rather than just ticking boxes. This can be an advantage for innovative business models, but it also means applicants must provide detailed explanations of their operations and risk controls. Capital Requirement

The LFSA mandates a minimum paid-up capital of MYR 500,000 (approximately EUR 100,000) for a Crypto Payment Institution License. This capital must be maintained at all times and can be in the form of cash or liquid assets. The requirement ensures the institution has sufficient financial resources to operate and meet obligations.

Compared to EU jurisdictions where capital requirements range from EUR 50,000 to EUR 150,000 under MiCA, Labuan's requirement is competitive. However, the actual capital needed may be higher depending on the scale of operations and risk profile. Consulting24 can help structure your capital efficiently, for example by using a combination of share capital and retained earnings.

It is important to note that the capital must be deposited in a Labuan bank account and cannot be used for operational expenses without LFSA approval. This ensures that the capital remains as a buffer against losses. We advise clients to budget for this requirement early in the planning process.

Tax Treatment

Labuan offers a highly attractive tax regime for licensed entities. The standard corporate tax rate is 3% of net audited profits, or a flat rate of MYR 20,000 (around EUR 4,000) per year, whichever is lower. This makes Labuan one of the most tax-efficient jurisdictions for crypto businesses.

Additionally, there is no withholding tax on dividends, no capital gains tax, and no sales tax. Labuan entities are also exempt from import duties on certain equipment. However, companies must comply with Labuan's substance requirements, including having a physical office and local directors or employees. Consulting24 advises on tax planning and substance compliance.

For comparison, Panama offers 0% tax on foreign income, which can be even more advantageous for companies that do not generate income from within Panama. Labuan's 3% rate is still very low relative to most jurisdictions. We help clients model their expected tax liability under different scenarios.

Allowed Activities

Under the Crypto Payment Institution License, the following activities are permitted:

  • Exchange of digital currencies for fiat money and vice versa
  • Facilitation of crypto payments for merchants
  • Operation of crypto wallets (custodial and non-custodial)
  • Remittance services using crypto
  • Issuance of payment instruments (e.g., prepaid cards) backed by crypto

Activities not covered include proprietary trading, investment advice, or issuance of security tokens. The LFSA strictly prohibits activities that fall outside the license scope. Consulting24 ensures your business model aligns with permitted activities before application. For example, if your business also plans to offer crypto lending, you may need a separate license or a variation of the existing one.

The LFSA has published a list of prohibited activities, which includes dealing with privacy coins and engaging in transactions with sanctioned entities. We help clients screen their business model against these restrictions.

  • Choosing the wrong license category: Some activities require a different license (e.g., securities license).

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a Labuan Crypto Payment Institution License and a VASP license in other jurisdictions?

The Labuan license is specifically for payment services, whereas VASP licenses (e.g., in Lithuania or Estonia) cover a broader range of activities including exchange and wallet services. Labuan's license is narrower but offers lower tax and faster processing. Consulting24 can help you decide which is best for your business.

Can I use the Labuan license to operate globally?

The license allows you to operate from Labuan and serve clients worldwide, but you must comply with local laws in the jurisdictions where your customers are based. For example, if you target EU customers, you may need to register under MiCA. Consulting24 advises on cross-border compliance.

What are the substance requirements for a Labuan license?

You must have a physical office in Labuan, at least one local director or employee, and maintain records locally. The LFSA expects the company to be managed and controlled from Labuan. Consulting24 can help you set up the necessary infrastructure.

How long does the Labuan license application take?

Typically 4 to 6 months from submission to approval. The timeline depends on the completeness of the application and the LFSA's workload. Consulting24 ensures your application is thorough to avoid delays.

What is the minimum capital for a Labuan Crypto Payment Institution License?

The minimum paid-up capital is MYR 500,000 (approximately EUR 100,000). This must be maintained at all times and can be in cash or liquid assets. Consulting24 can help structure the capital efficiently.

Is the Labuan license recognized by other regulators?

Can I convert an existing company to a Labuan licensed entity?

What happens if I fail to maintain compliance?

How does Labuan compare to Panama for crypto licensing?
