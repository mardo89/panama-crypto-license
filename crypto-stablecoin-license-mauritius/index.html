What is a Crypto Stablecoin License in Mauritius?

A crypto stablecoin license in Mauritius is an authorization granted by the FSC to operate a stablecoin issuance business under the VAITOS Act. This license is specifically designed for entities that issue, redeem, or manage stablecoins - digital assets pegged to a stable asset like a fiat currency, commodity, or algorithm. The regime distinguishes between different types of virtual asset service providers (VASPs), with stablecoin issuers falling under a specific category that requires a Class 1 or Class 2 license depending on the services offered.

The FSC requires licensees to maintain strong governance, anti-money laundering (AML) controls, and consumer protection measures. As of 2026, Mauritius has fully implemented its regulatory framework, making it a mature and reliable destination for stablecoin projects. The VAITOS Act covers all aspects of stablecoin operations, from issuance and redemption to reserve management and smart contract oversight. The FSC also mandates regular audits and stress testing of the stablecoin's peg mechanism, ensuring stability and trust.

For example, a fiat-backed stablecoin like USDC would require the issuer to hold reserves in a Mauritian bank account and submit monthly attestation reports. An algorithmic stablecoin must have a detailed risk management plan approved by the FSC. The license also requires the issuer to maintain a public disclosure policy, including regular updates on reserve composition and outstanding tokens.

Who Needs a Crypto Stablecoin License in Mauritius?

Any legal entity intending to issue, redeem, or manage stablecoins in or from Mauritius must obtain a license from the FSC. This includes:

  • Startups and established companies launching a new stablecoin
  • Exchanges that want to offer their own stablecoin
  • Payment service providers integrating stablecoin solutions
  • Fintech firms developing algorithmic or asset-backed stablecoins

If your business model involves creating a digital asset with a stabilized value, you fall under the VAITOS regime. Consulting24 advises and coordinates the application, ensuring your business structure meets the FSC's requirements. Even if your stablecoin is primarily used outside Mauritius, the FSC requires licensing if the entity is based in Mauritius or if the issuance targets Mauritian residents. For example, a company incorporated in Mauritius that issues a stablecoin for the African market would need a license.

Entities that only provide ancillary services, such as smart contract auditing or legal advice, do not need a license. However, any direct involvement in the issuance, redemption, or management of the stablecoin triggers the licensing requirement. The FSC also considers the substance of the operation - if key decisions are made in Mauritius, a license is required.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Financial Services Commission (FSC) of Mauritius. The license types under VAITOS are:

  • Class 1 License: For issuers of virtual assets (including stablecoins) that do not provide custodial services or operate an exchange. Minimum capital requirement is MUR 500,000 (approx. EUR 10,000).
  • Class 2 License: For issuers that also provide custodial services or operate a virtual asset exchange. Minimum capital is MUR 1,000,000 (approx. EUR 20,000).

Stablecoin issuers typically require a Class 1 license unless they also offer custody or trading services. The FSC also requires a qualifying entity to have a physical office in Mauritius and appoint a local director or manager. The FSC conducts thorough due diligence on all beneficial owners and key personnel, including source of funds checks.

In addition to the license, the FSC may impose conditions specific to the stablecoin's design. For example, an algorithmic stablecoin might require a higher capital buffer or more frequent audits. The FSC also maintains a public register of licensed VASPs, adding transparency and trust for users and partners.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a Class 1 license is MUR 500,000 (approx. EUR 10,000). For a Class 2 license, it is MUR 1,000,000 (approx. EUR 20,000). These amounts must be fully paid up and held in a bank account in Mauritius. The FSC may require additional capital based on the volume of stablecoin issuance and risk profile. As of 2026, these figures are confirmed by the VAITOS regulations. The capital must be maintained throughout the license period and cannot be withdrawn without FSC approval.

For example, if you plan to issue a stablecoin with a market cap of EUR 10 million, the FSC may require a capital buffer of 1-2% of the outstanding tokens, in addition to the minimum capital. This is to cover operational risks and potential losses. The capital must be held in liquid assets, such as cash or government bonds, and cannot be used for other business purposes.

In contrast, capital requirements in other jurisdictions vary widely. Estonia requires EUR 100,000 for a VASP license, while Lithuania requires EUR 125,000 for certain services. Panama has no minimum capital for stablecoin issuance, but the company must have sufficient funds to operate.

Tax Treatment

Mauritius offers a favorable tax regime for licensed entities. The corporate income tax rate is 15%, but stablecoin issuers may benefit from the Global Business License (GBL) regime, which offers an effective tax rate of 3% on foreign-source income. There is no capital gains tax, no withholding tax on dividends, and no VAT on digital asset services. However, specific tax treatment depends on the structure and operations. Consulting24 recommends a tax review with a local advisor. The GBL regime requires a substance test, including a physical office and local staff.

For example, if your stablecoin is issued from Mauritius but used globally, the income from issuance fees and redemption spreads may qualify as foreign-source income, subject to the 3% rate. However, income from services provided to Mauritian residents would be taxed at 15%. The FSC and the Mauritius Revenue Authority (MRA) coordinate to ensure compliance. Additionally, Mauritius has double taxation treaties with over 40 countries, which can reduce withholding taxes on cross-border payments.

Compared to Panama, which has no corporate income tax on foreign-source income but a flat annual license fee, Mauritius offers a lower effective tax rate for high-income stablecoin issuers. However, Panama's simpler tax system may be more attractive for smaller projects.

Allowed Activities

Under a Class 1 license, you may:

  • Issue stablecoins (fiat-backed, commodity-backed, or algorithmic)
  • Redeem stablecoins for underlying assets
  • Manage the stablecoin reserve and smart contracts

Under a Class 2 license, you may additionally:

  • Provide custodial wallet services for stablecoins
  • Operate a virtual asset exchange (trading stablecoins)
  • Offer staking or lending services using stablecoins

All activities must comply with AML/CFT obligations and the FSC's rules on consumer protection. The FSC prohibits activities that could destabilize the peg, such as excessive use of the reserve for other purposes. For example, a stablecoin issuer cannot lend out the reserve assets without maintaining sufficient liquidity to meet redemption requests.

The FSC also requires that stablecoin issuers have a clear redemption policy and process redemptions within a specified timeframe (typically 1-3 business days). Any changes to the stablecoin's design, such as a change in the peg mechanism, must be approved by the FSC in advance.

Compliance and Trust

Maintaining a crypto stablecoin license in Mauritius requires ongoing compliance: annual audits, AML/CFT reporting, and notification of material changes. The FSC conducts periodic inspections. Consulting24 helps you implement a compliance framework that meets these standards. This is general guidance, not legal advice. Always consult with a qualified lawyer for your specific situation. The FSC expects licensees to have a compliance officer resident in Mauritius and to conduct regular training for staff.

Key compliance obligations include:

  • Annual audit by an FSC-approved auditor, covering financial statements and AML controls
  • Quarterly AML/CFT reports to the FSC, including suspicious transaction reports
  • Monthly reserve attestation for fiat-backed stablecoins, verified by an independent auditor
  • Immediate notification of any material changes, such as a change in directors, ownership, or business model

Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. The FSC also has the power to impose remedial measures, such as requiring additional capital or restricting activities.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Class 1 stablecoin license in Mauritius?

The minimum capital is MUR 500,000 (approx. EUR 10,000). This must be fully paid up and held in a Mauritian bank account. The FSC may require additional capital based on the volume of stablecoin issuance and risk profile.

How long does it take to get a crypto stablecoin license in Mauritius?

The processing time is typically 3 to 6 months, depending on the completeness of your application and the FSC's workload. Rushed applications can take longer if additional information is requested.

What is the tax rate for stablecoin issuers in Mauritius?

The standard corporate tax rate is 15%, but stablecoin issuers can benefit from the Global Business License (GBL) regime, offering an effective tax rate of 3% on foreign-source income. There is no capital gains tax or VAT on digital asset services.

Do I need a local director in Mauritius?

Yes, the FSC requires at least one director resident in Mauritius. If you do not have a local director, you can use a nominee director service provided by local firms.

Can I issue an algorithmic stablecoin under this license?

Yes, algorithmic stablecoins are allowed, but the FSC requires a detailed risk management plan and may impose additional conditions, such as higher capital buffers or more frequent audits.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

Licensees must submit annual audits, quarterly AML/CFT reports, monthly reserve attestations (for fiat-backed stablecoins), and notify the FSC of any material changes. A compliance officer must be resident in Mauritius.

How does Mauritius compare to Panama for stablecoin licensing?

Panama offers a flat fee of EUR 6,000 for company setup but has no dedicated stablecoin regime. Mauritius provides regulatory clarity and tax benefits but has higher upfront costs (EUR 19,500-57,000). Panama is simpler and cheaper; Mauritius is more strong and reputable.

Can Consulting24 guarantee approval of my license application?

No, we cannot guarantee approval. The FSC makes the final decision based on your application. We advise and coordinate the process to maximize your chances, but approval is not assured.

What happens if my stablecoin fails to maintain its peg?

The FSC requires licensees to have a contingency plan for peg deviations. If the peg fails, the FSC may require immediate redemption, suspension of issuance, or other remedial measures. Regular stress testing is mandatory.

Is Mauritius a FATF-compliant jurisdiction?

Yes, Mauritius is a member of the FATF and is compliant with its recommendations. The VAITOS regime aligns with FATF standards for virtual asset service providers.

Can I use a Mauritius stablecoin license to serve customers in the EU?

Yes, but you must comply with EU regulations if you target EU residents. Under MiCA (fully in force in 2026), non-EU entities may need to establish a branch or partner with an EU-licensed entity. Consult a legal expert for your specific market.

What are the costs for renewing the license annually?

The annual license fee is EUR 5,000-15,000, plus ongoing professional fees for audits and compliance (EUR 10,000-20,000). Total annual costs are typically EUR 15,000-35,000.
