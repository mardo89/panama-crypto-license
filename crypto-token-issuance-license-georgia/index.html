What Is the Crypto Token Issuance License in Georgia?

The crypto token issuance license is a regulatory authorization issued by the National Bank of Georgia (NBG) under the Law on the Issuance of Digital Tokens (effective from 2023, updated in 2026). It permits entities to issue digital tokens to the public, including through ICOs, and to provide related services such as token custody and exchange.

Georgia distinguishes between utility tokens and asset-referenced tokens, with the latter subject to stricter rules. The license is mandatory for any business that offers tokens to Georgian residents or operates from Georgian territory. It is not a blanket license for all crypto activities; it specifically covers token issuance and associated services.

Key features include:

  • Regulated by the NBG, which also oversees payment services and financial stability.
  • Applicable to both domestic and foreign entities that establish a presence in Georgia.
  • Requires a minimum capital of GEL 500,000 (approximately EUR 160,000 as of 2026) for issuers of asset-referenced tokens, and GEL 100,000 (approx. EUR 32,000) for utility token issuers.
  • Annual reporting and compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) regulations.

For example, a startup issuing a utility token for a decentralized application would need the utility token license. A stablecoin project pegged to the US dollar would require the asset-referenced token license. The NBG publishes a list of approved tokens and issuers on its website, which adds transparency.

Who Needs This License?

The crypto token issuance license is designed for:

  • Blockchain startups planning to raise funds through an ICO or token sale.
  • Established companies issuing tokens for loyalty programs, equity, or asset representation.
  • Decentralized finance (DeFi) projects launching governance or utility tokens.
  • Tokenization platforms that create and manage digital tokens for clients.

If your project involves issuing tokens to the public, you likely need this license. Even if you conduct a private sale, the NBG may require registration if tokens are offered to Georgian residents. Consulting24 advises on whether your specific token model falls under the license requirement.

Entities that do not issue tokens but only trade or hold them (e.g., exchanges) need a different license, such as the virtual asset service provider (VASP) license. For a comparison of licensing options, see our jurisdictions page.

A common scenario: a gaming company issuing in-game tokens for a global audience. If the company is based in Georgia or targets Georgian users, the license is required. We have seen cases where projects mistakenly assumed a private sale exempted them, only to face regulatory action later.

License Type and Regulator

The license is formally called the 'Digital Token Issuance License' and is regulated by the National Bank of Georgia (NBG). The NBG is the central bank and primary financial regulator, responsible for overseeing token issuance, payment services, and financial stability.

There are two sub-types:

  • Utility Token Issuance License - for tokens that provide access to a product or service.
  • Asset-Referenced Token Issuance License - for tokens pegged to assets like fiat currency, commodities, or real estate (similar to stablecoins).

The NBG requires a detailed whitepaper, AML policies, and proof of capital. The regulator conducts a fit-and-proper test for key personnel. As of 2026, the NBG has streamlined the process, with typical review times of 3-6 months.

The NBG also collaborates with the Georgian Revenue Service for tax matters and the Financial Monitoring Service for AML supervision. This multi-agency oversight ensures comprehensive regulation but also means applicants must prepare documentation for multiple authorities.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement depends on the token type:

  • Utility tokens: GEL 100,000 (approx. EUR 32,000).
  • Asset-referenced tokens: GEL 500,000 (approx. EUR 160,000).

Capital must be fully paid up before license issuance and held in a Georgian bank account. It can be used for operational expenses but must be maintained at the required level. The NBG may require additional capital if the token issuance volume exceeds certain thresholds. Consulting24 can advise on structuring your capital to meet NBG requirements.

For example, if you plan to issue asset-referenced tokens worth over GEL 5 million, the NBG might ask for a higher capital buffer. Some projects have used a combination of equity and retained earnings to meet the requirement. The capital must be in GEL or a freely convertible currency, and the bank account must be with a Georgian licensed bank.

Tax Treatment for Token Issuance

Georgia offers a favorable tax environment for token issuers. Key points:

  • Corporate income tax: 15% standard rate, but a special regime applies to token issuers: profits from token sales are taxed at 5% if the tokens are classified as utility tokens and the company is a Georgian tax resident.
  • VAT: Token issuance is generally exempt from VAT, provided the tokens are not considered securities.
  • Capital gains: Gains from trading tokens held as investments are taxed at 15%.

The tax treatment of asset-referenced tokens may differ; the NBG and Revenue Service provide guidance on a case-by-case basis. Consulting24 coordinates with local tax advisors to ensure compliance.

For instance, a company that issues utility tokens and generates EUR 1 million in profit would pay only EUR 50,000 in corporate tax under the 5% regime. This is significantly lower than many EU jurisdictions, where rates can exceed 20%.

Allowed Activities Under the License

Holders of the crypto token issuance license can perform the following activities:

  • Issue digital tokens to the public (ICO, STO, IEO).
  • Provide token custody services (safekeeping of private keys).
  • Operate a token exchange platform limited to the issued tokens.
  • Market and advertise token offerings to Georgian residents.
  • Manage token buyback and redemption programs.

Activities not covered include: operating a general crypto exchange (requires a VASP license), providing payment services with fiat, or offering derivatives. For a broader set of activities, consider a crypto exchange license in another jurisdiction.

An example: a licensed issuer can run a secondary market for its own tokens, but cannot list tokens from other projects. If you need a full exchange, you would need a separate VASP license or consider jurisdictions like Lithuania or Estonia.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain the license involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step:

  1. Pre-application consultation: Assess your token model and determine the appropriate license type.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a legal entity in Georgia (LLC or JSC) with a registered office.
  3. Document preparation: Draft a whitepaper, business plan, AML/KYC policies, risk management framework, and internal controls.
  4. Capital deposit: Open a bank account in Georgia and deposit the required minimum capital.
  5. Submit application: File the application with the NBG, including all supporting documents and fees.
  6. NBG review: The regulator evaluates the application, conducts fit-and-proper tests on directors, and may request additional information.
  7. License issuance: Upon approval, the license is granted, and the company can begin token issuance.

The entire process typically takes 3-6 months. Consulting24 provides end-to-end support, from company setup to compliance training.

Banking and Payment Services

Opening a corporate bank account in Georgia is relatively straightforward for licensed token issuers. Major banks such as Bank of Georgia and TBC Bank accept crypto-related businesses with proper licensing. Requirements include:

  • Valid NBG license.
  • AML/KYC documentation.
  • Source of funds for the initial capital.

Payment services for token sales (e.g., accepting fiat from investors) can be integrated through local payment processors. For international payments, consider multi-currency accounts. Consulting24 can introduce you to banking partners familiar with the crypto sector.

For example, a client issuing a stablecoin needed to provide a detailed breakdown of reserve assets to TBC Bank before the account was approved. Having this documentation ready can save weeks.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Applicants often make these errors:

  • Incomplete whitepaper: Missing risk disclosures or tokenomics details can lead to rejection.
  • Underestimating AML requirements: The NBG expects strong KYC procedures, including for token purchasers.
  • Insufficient capital: Some applicants try to use borrowed funds, which the NBG may not accept.
  • Ignoring fit-and-proper tests: Directors with criminal records or poor financial history may disqualify the application.
  • Delaying bank account opening: Without a local account, you cannot deposit capital, stalling the process.

To avoid these, work with experienced advisors like Consulting24. We have seen applications fail due to a single missing document. Prepare early and double-check all submissions.

Another common mistake is assuming the license allows all crypto activities. For instance, operating a separate exchange for third-party tokens requires a different license. Always verify the scope with the NBG or your advisor.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a utility token license in Georgia?

The minimum capital is GEL 100,000 (approximately EUR 32,000 as of 2026). This must be fully paid up and held in a Georgian bank account before the license is issued.

How long does it take to get the Georgia token issuance license?

The typical timeline is 3 to 6 months from application to approval, depending on the complexity of the token model and the completeness of documentation. Consulting24 can help streamline the process.

Can I issue both utility and asset-referenced tokens under one license?

No, the license is specific to the token type. You need separate licenses for utility tokens and asset-referenced tokens. However, you can apply for both simultaneously if your project requires it.

Is the Georgia token issuance license recognized in the EU?

No, it is a national license. For EU-wide recognition, you would need a MiCA license from an EU member state. However, Georgia's license is respected internationally and can facilitate partnerships.

What are the tax benefits for token issuers in Georgia?

Utility token profits are taxed at 5% corporate income tax. Token issuance is generally VAT-exempt. Asset-referenced tokens may have different treatment; consult a tax advisor.

Can a foreign company apply for the Georgia token issuance license?

Yes, but the company must be registered in Georgia as a legal entity (e.g., LLC). Foreign directors are allowed, but they must pass the NBG's fit-and-proper test.

What happens if I fail to maintain the minimum capital?

The NBG may impose fines or revoke the license. It is crucial to monitor capital levels and inject additional funds if needed. Consulting24 provides compliance monitoring to help you stay compliant.

Do I need a local bank account for the license?

Yes, you must open a corporate bank account in Georgia to deposit the minimum capital and receive token sale proceeds. Major banks like Bank of Georgia and TBC Bank accept licensed crypto firms.

Can I conduct an ICO for a global audience with this license?

Yes, the license allows you to issue tokens to the public, including non-Georgian residents. However, you must comply with the laws of the investors' countries. Consulting24 can advise on cross-border compliance.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements after obtaining the license?

You must submit annual AML audits, maintain capital, update the whitepaper if needed, and report suspicious transactions. The NBG may conduct periodic inspections.
