What is the Anjouan Crypto Broker License?

The Anjouan crypto broker license is a regulatory authorization issued by the Anjouan Financial Services Authority (AFSA) that permits companies to engage in crypto-related activities such as operating a crypto exchange, providing crypto wallet services, and acting as a crypto broker. It is part of Anjouan's International Business Companies (IBC) regime, which offers a favorable tax environment and a streamlined licensing process. The license is suitable for businesses targeting global markets without the high costs of European or North American jurisdictions.

Unlike EU member states where MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets) regulation is fully in force with capital tiers of EUR 50,000 to 150,000, Anjouan provides a more accessible entry point. The license is recognized internationally for its cost-effectiveness and efficiency, making it a popular choice for crypto startups and established firms alike.

If you are already operating in another jurisdiction, an Anjouan license can serve as a secondary or backup license. For those comparing with other jurisdictions, Anjouan offers a unique balance of low cost and regulatory legitimacy.

License Type and Regulator

The Anjouan crypto broker license is issued by the Anjouan Financial Services Authority (AFSA), the official regulator for financial services in the autonomous island of Anjouan. The license falls under the International Business Companies (IBC) Act and is specifically designed for crypto activities. There is no separate class for broker vs. exchange; the license covers a broad range of crypto services.

AFSA is known for its pragmatic approach to regulation, focusing on anti-money laundering (AML) compliance and consumer protection without imposing excessive operational burdens. The license is valid for one year and must be renewed annually. Consulting24 advises and coordinates the application process, ensuring all documentation meets AFSA's requirements.

Capital Requirement

The Anjouan crypto broker license does not have a statutory minimum capital requirement. This is a significant advantage compared to EU jurisdictions where MiCA mandates capital of EUR 50,000 for simple custody, EUR 125,000 for exchange, and EUR 150,000 for brokerage. In Anjouan, the capital requirement is determined on a case-by-case basis and is typically set at a nominal amount (e.g., EUR 10,000 to 50,000) to demonstrate financial stability. However, the exact figure is agreed upon during the application process and can be as low as EUR 10,000 for startups.

This flexibility allows businesses to allocate more funds to operations and growth rather than locking them in regulatory capital. Consulting24 advises on the appropriate capital level based on your business plan and risk profile.

Tax Treatment

Anjouan offers a highly favorable tax regime for International Business Companies (IBCs). Companies holding a crypto broker license are exempt from corporate income tax on income derived from outside Anjouan. There is no capital gains tax, no withholding tax on dividends, and no value-added tax (VAT) on services provided to non-residents. The only tax liability is an annual license renewal fee (approximately EUR 2,000) and a small government service fee.

This tax treatment makes Anjouan an attractive jurisdiction for crypto businesses that primarily serve international clients. However, companies must ensure they comply with tax obligations in the countries where their clients are resident. Consulting24 recommends seeking local tax advice to avoid double taxation issues.

Allowed Activities

The Anjouan crypto broker license permits a wide range of crypto-related activities, including but not limited to:

  • Operating a crypto exchange (centralized or decentralized).
  • Providing crypto wallet services (custodial and non-custodial).
  • Acting as a crypto broker (matching buyers and sellers).
  • Issuing and trading crypto assets.
  • Providing crypto payment processing services.
  • Offering crypto staking and lending services (subject to approval).

Activities that are not allowed include gambling-related crypto services and any activity that violates international sanctions. The license is flexible enough to cover most business models, and AFSA is open to discussing new use cases.
