What Is the Spain Crypto License?

The Spain crypto license is a mandatory authorization for any entity providing crypto-asset services in Spain, as required by the transposition of MiCA into national law. The regulator is the Bank of Spain (Banco de España), which oversees CASP registration. Unlike the earlier voluntary register (which was limited to anti-money laundering compliance), the new CASP regime covers full prudential, conduct, and consumer protection requirements. All virtual asset service providers (VASPs) must be registered before offering services to Spanish residents. This includes exchanges, custodial wallet providers, and brokers. The license is valid across the EU under the MiCA passport, making Spain a strategic gateway to the European crypto market.

Under MiCA, Spain applies a single license for all crypto-asset services, but the capital requirement varies by service type. The Bank of Spain also requires a local presence, including a registered office and management. The license is not required for entities that only develop software or provide non-custodial services without handling client funds. For example, a startup building a non-custodial wallet that does not hold private keys may not need a license, whereas a platform that matches buyers and sellers of crypto-assets likely does. The distinction is crucial and often requires legal analysis. Consulting24 can provide an initial assessment based on your business plan.

Who Needs a Spain Crypto License?

Any legal entity that provides one or more of the following services to Spanish clients must obtain a CASP license from the Bank of Spain:

  • Operation of a trading platform for crypto-assets
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for fiat currency or other crypto-assets
  • Custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • Execution of orders for crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • Placement of crypto-assets
  • Receiving and transmitting orders for crypto-assets
  • Providing advice on crypto-assets
  • Portfolio management of crypto-assets

Foreign companies targeting Spanish customers also need to register unless they operate under a MiCA passport from another EU member state. DeFi protocols and NFT platforms may be subject to specific assessments. For example, a decentralized exchange with a governance token may still be considered a trading platform if it facilitates order matching. Additionally, if you are a Spanish company already registered under the old voluntary regime, you must transition to the full CASP license by the deadline set by the Bank of Spain. Failure to do so could result in enforcement actions. Consulting24 can assist with the transition process.

License Type and Regulator

The license is formally called "Crypto-Asset Service Provider (CASP) Registration" under the Bank of Spain. The regulator is the Banco de España (Bank of Spain), which is the central bank and the competent authority for MiCA implementation in Spain. The Bank of Spain works in coordination with the Spanish Securities Market Commission (CNMV) for certain aspects, particularly when crypto-assets qualify as financial instruments. The registration is a single license that covers all crypto-asset services listed in MiCA. There is no separate classification for exchanges versus wallet providers; all services are included in one authorization. However, the capital requirement depends on the specific services you intend to offer, as outlined in the capital requirements section.

The Bank of Spain has published detailed guidelines on the application process, including required documentation and criteria for approval. These guidelines are available on their official website (see authority links below). Consulting24 can help you interpret these guidelines and prepare a compliant application.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, Spain applies tiered minimum capital requirements based on the type of services offered:

  • EUR 50,000 for simple services such as execution of orders, receiving and transmitting orders, and advice
  • EUR 125,000 for exchange services (crypto-to-fiat or crypto-to-crypto) and custody
  • EUR 150,000 for operating a trading platform

These amounts are minimums; the Bank of Spain may require higher capital if the business volume or risk profile warrants. Capital must be held in liquid assets and maintained throughout the license period. For example, if you operate both an exchange and a trading platform, the highest tier (EUR 150,000) applies. It is important to note that the capital must be fully paid up before the license is granted. Some companies choose to raise funds specifically for this purpose. Consulting24 can help you prepare a capital plan that satisfies the regulator.

Tax Treatment

Spain has a comprehensive tax framework for crypto-assets. Corporate income tax for companies is generally 25% (with reduced rates for new companies in certain cases). Capital gains from crypto trading are taxed as income. VAT does not apply to crypto-to-fiat exchanges but may apply to certain services. Additionally, Spain requires reporting of crypto holdings and transactions to the tax authorities (AEAT) through annual declarations. Companies must also comply with the OECD's Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework (CARF) as of 2026. Consulting24 can refer you to tax specialists for detailed planning. Note that tax obligations can significantly affect your net profitability, so early planning is essential.

For example, if your company holds crypto-assets as inventory, the tax treatment may differ from holding them as investments. A tax advisor can help structure your operations to minimize liabilities. Allowed Activities

A Spain CASP license allows the following activities within the EU under MiCA passporting:

  • Operating a crypto-asset trading platform (order book, AMM, etc.)
  • Exchanging crypto-assets for fiat currency or other crypto-assets
  • Custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • Execution of orders for crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • Placement of crypto-assets (underwriting or placing without firm commitment)
  • Receiving and transmitting orders for crypto-assets
  • Providing advice on crypto-assets
  • Portfolio management of crypto-assets

Activities not covered include issuing asset-referenced tokens (ARTs) or e-money tokens (EMTs), which fall under separate regimes. DeFi services may require additional assessment. The license does not permit gambling or financial services outside crypto-assets. For a full list of regulated activities, refer to the Bank of Spain's official guidelines.

If your business model includes novel features such as staking or lending, these may be considered part of custody or portfolio management, depending on how they are structured. Consulting24 can help you map your activities to the regulated categories.

Step-by-Step Process

The application process for a Spain crypto license involves the following steps:

  1. Pre-application assessment: Determine if your business model requires a CASP license and which capital tier applies.
  2. Company incorporation: Set up a Spanish legal entity (usually a limited liability company) with a registered office in Spain. Minimum share capital is EUR 3,000 for an SL.
  3. Documentation preparation: Prepare a detailed business plan, AML/CFT policies, risk assessment, governance framework, IT security policies, and financial projections.
  4. Appoint local representatives: You may need a local compliance officer and a board member resident in Spain.
  5. Submit application: File the application with the Bank of Spain, including all required documents and the application fee.
  6. Review and interview: The Bank of Spain reviews the application, may request additional information, and may conduct interviews with management.
  7. Decision: The regulator issues a decision within 3-6 months. If approved, you receive a CASP registration number.
  8. Post-licensing compliance: Ongoing reporting, audits, and capital maintenance.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between the old Spanish VASP register and the new CASP license?

The old VASP register was a voluntary anti-money laundering registration with limited oversight. The new CASP license under MiCA is mandatory and covers full prudential, conduct, and consumer protection requirements. Capital requirements, governance, and reporting are much stricter under the CASP regime.

How long does it take to get a Spain crypto license?

The timeline from application to decision is typically 3 to 6 months, assuming complete documentation. Preparation can take an additional 2-3 months. Consulting24 can help expedite the process by ensuring your application is complete and accurate.

Can I use my Spain CASP license to operate in other EU countries?

Yes, the CASP license is passportable across the EU under MiCA. Once registered with the Bank of Spain, you can provide services in any EU member state without needing a separate license, subject to notification requirements.

What are the capital requirements for a Spain crypto license?

Capital requirements are tiered: EUR 50,000 for simple services (e.g., advice), EUR 125,000 for exchange and custody, and EUR 150,000 for operating a trading platform. The highest tier applicable to your services must be met.

Do I need a physical office in Spain to get the license?

Yes, you need a registered office in Spain. You may also need local management or compliance officers. Consulting24 can help you set up a virtual office or lease a physical space.

Is it possible to get a Spain crypto license if I am a non-EU company?

Yes, non-EU companies can apply, but they must incorporate a Spanish subsidiary and meet all local requirements. The subsidiary will be the licensed entity. Consulting24 can assist with company formation.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations after obtaining the license?

Ongoing obligations include annual reporting to the Bank of Spain, AML/CFT audits, capital maintenance, and notification of material changes. You must also comply with MiCA's market abuse and transparency rules.

Can I operate a DeFi platform under a Spain CASP license?

It depends on the level of control and intermediation. Fully decentralized protocols with no central operator may fall outside the scope, but hybrid models often require a license. Consulting24 can assess your specific DeFi setup.

How does the Spain crypto license compare to Panama's license?

Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 license with no minimum capital and a faster timeline, but it does not provide EU passporting. Spain's license is more expensive and slower but grants access to the EU market. Your choice depends on your target market.

What happens if I provide crypto services in Spain without a license?

Operating without a license is illegal and can result in fines, criminal penalties, and forced cessation of activities. The Bank of Spain actively monitors the market and can take enforcement actions.
