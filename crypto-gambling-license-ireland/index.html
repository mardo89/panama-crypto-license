What Is a Crypto Gambling License in Ireland?

Ireland does not issue a single 'crypto gambling license'. Instead, operators must obtain two separate authorisations: a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) registration (or, from 2026, a MiCA CASP license) for crypto activities, and a gaming license under the new Gambling Regulatory Authority of Ireland (GRAI), once the Gambling Regulation Bill 2022 is enacted. The bill is expected to become law in 2026, establishing a unified regulator for all gambling, including online and crypto-based betting. For now, gambling licensing is handled by the Revenue Commissioners (for excise licenses) and the Department of Justice (for remote betting licenses). The Central Bank of Ireland (CBI) oversees VASP registration under the Criminal Justice (Money Laundering and Terrorist Financing) Act 2010, which will transition to MiCA CASP licensing by end of 2026.

This dual-regime structure means that a crypto gambling operator must comply with both financial services regulation and gambling law. The practical effect is that you need a strong compliance framework covering AML/CFT, data protection (GDPR), and responsible gambling measures. For example, if you operate a crypto sportsbook, you must verify customer identities, monitor transactions for suspicious activity, and offer self-exclusion tools. The CBI and GRAI will share information, so any compliance gaps in one area can affect the other. Who Needs This License?

Any business that offers crypto-based gambling services - including crypto sportsbooks, casino games using digital assets, or platforms that accept Bitcoin, Ethereum, or stablecoins for wagering - needs both a VASP/CASP registration and a gambling license. This applies to:

  • Online casinos accepting crypto deposits and withdrawals
  • Sports betting platforms using cryptocurrency
  • Fantasy sports and esports betting with crypto payouts
  • Provably fair gaming operators
  • White-label crypto gambling solutions

If your business involves holding or transferring crypto assets for customers, the VASP/CASP requirement is mandatory. Operators based outside Ireland but serving Irish customers may also need to comply. For example, a Malta-based crypto casino targeting Irish players would need to register with the CBI and obtain an Irish gambling license, unless they rely on EU passporting under MiCA (which covers crypto services but not gambling). License Type & Regulator

For crypto activities, the relevant regulator is the Central Bank of Ireland (CBI). Under MiCA (fully effective from 2026), crypto-asset service providers must obtain a CASP license. The license covers services such as custody, exchange, and transfer of crypto assets. For gambling, the future regulator is the Gambling Regulatory Authority of Ireland (GRAI), which will issue licenses for online gambling, including crypto gambling. Until the GRAI is operational, remote betting licenses are issued by the Revenue Commissioners (excise license) and the Department of Justice (bookmaker's license). Capital Requirement

For the CASP license under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement depends on the services provided:

  • 50,000 for custody and transfer services
  • 125,000 for exchange services (fiat-to-crypto or crypto-to-crypto)
  • 150,000 for trading platform (order book matching)

For the gambling license, the Gambling Regulation Bill 2022 proposes a minimum capital of 100,000 for online gambling operators. However, this figure is subject to change. Combined, a crypto gambling operator should expect to hold at least 150,000 - 250,000 in capital. These figures are general guidance, not legal advice. Tax Treatment

Ireland offers a favourable corporate tax rate of 12.5% on trading income. For crypto gambling operators, the key taxes are:

  • Corporation Tax: 12.5% on profits from gambling and crypto activities
  • Betting Duty: 2% of net betting revenue (for remote betting licenses)
  • VAT: 23% on commissions and fees (if applicable; gambling is generally exempt)
  • Capital Gains Tax: 33% on disposal of crypto assets held as investments

Ireland does not impose a specific crypto tax. However, operators must comply with anti-money laundering (AML) regulations and report suspicious transactions. Tax advice from a qualified accountant is essential, as individual circumstances vary. For example, if your platform charges a commission on bets, that commission may be subject to VAT. Allowed Activities

A combined crypto gambling license in Ireland permits the following activities:

  • Accepting crypto deposits (BTC, ETH, USDT, etc.) for wagering
  • Operating online casino games, slots, table games
  • Sports betting and esports betting with crypto payouts
  • Provably fair gaming using blockchain technology
  • Offering crypto-based loyalty tokens or rewards
  • Custody of customer crypto assets (must be segregated)

Prohibited activities include unlicensed gambling, money laundering, and offering gambling to minors. The license does not cover securities or derivatives; those require separate authorisation. For example, if you want to offer crypto futures trading, you would need a MiFID license in addition to the CASP. Banking & Payments

Securing banking is a major challenge for crypto gambling operators in Ireland. Most traditional banks avoid crypto-related businesses. Options include:

  • EMI (Electronic Money Institution) accounts: Providers like Revolut Business or Paysera may accept crypto companies with proper licensing.
  • Payment processors: Specialised gateways like Coinbase Commerce, BitPay, or CoinGate for crypto-to-fiat conversion.
  • Irish credit unions: Some may accept licensed VASPs, but this is rare.

Common Mistakes

Founders often make these errors:

  • Underestimating capital: Attempting to start with less than the minimum capital requirement.
  • Ignoring AML: Failing to implement proper KYC/AML procedures leads to application rejection.
  • Choosing the wrong corporate structure: Using a non-Irish company without a local presence.
  • Applying without a business plan: Regulators require detailed financial projections and risk assessments.
  • Overlooking banking: Assuming a standard business account will suffice.
  • Mixing funds: Not segregating customer crypto assets from operational funds.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need both a CASP license and a gambling license in Ireland?

Yes, if your business involves holding or transferring crypto assets for customers and offering gambling services, you need both. The CASP license covers crypto activities, while the gambling license covers betting and gaming. Consulting24 advises on how to obtain both simultaneously.

What is the minimum capital for a crypto gambling license in Ireland?

For the CASP license, capital ranges from 50,000 to 150,000 depending on services. The gambling license requires at least 100,000. Combined, expect to hold 150,000 to 250,000. These are estimates; exact figures depend on your business model.

How long does it take to get a crypto gambling license in Ireland?

The combined process takes 12-18 months. The CASP application alone takes 6-12 months, and the gambling license (once GRAI is operational) takes a similar timeframe. Delays can occur if documentation is incomplete.

Is Ireland's corporate tax rate really 12.5% for crypto gambling?

Yes, Ireland's standard corporate tax rate is 12.5% on trading income. This applies to profits from both crypto services and gambling. However, betting duty of 2% on net revenue also applies. Consult a tax advisor for your specific situation.

Can I use an existing company from another EU country to apply?

You can, but you will likely need to establish a branch or subsidiary in Ireland with a physical office and local director. The CBI and GRAI expect a substantive presence. Consulting24 can advise on the best corporate structure.

What are the main compliance requirements for a crypto gambling operator?

Key requirements include AML/KYC policies, transaction monitoring, GDPR compliance, responsible gambling tools, and annual audits. You must also segregate customer crypto assets and report suspicious transactions. Consulting24 can recommend compliance specialists.

Is banking difficult for crypto gambling companies in Ireland?

Yes, it is one of the biggest challenges. Traditional banks often reject crypto-related businesses. Options include EMI accounts (e.g., Revolut Business) and specialised payment processors. Consulting24 can introduce you to banking partners.

How does Ireland compare to Panama for crypto gambling licensing?

Panama offers a faster, cheaper option (6,000 flat fee, 2-4 months) with no tax on foreign income, but it does not provide EU passporting. Ireland offers EU market access and regulatory credibility at a higher cost and longer timeline.

What happens if I operate without a license in Ireland?

Operating without the required licenses is illegal and can result in fines, imprisonment, and seizure of assets. The CBI and GRAI have enforcement powers. It is essential to obtain proper authorisation before launching.
