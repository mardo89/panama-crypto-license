What Is the Hungary Crypto License?

The Hungary crypto license is a mandatory registration for businesses providing virtual currency services. It is officially known as a virtual currency service provider (VCSP) registration and is governed by Act LIII of 2017 on the Prevention and Combating of Money Laundering and Terrorism Financing, as amended, and the EU's Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA), which is fully in force from 2025. The license allows companies to operate legally within Hungary and across the EU under the MiCA passport.

Key features include:

  • Regulated by the Hungarian National Bank (MNB)
  • Full compliance with MiCA capital tiers (EUR 50,000 / 125,000 / 150,000 depending on service class)
  • No corporate income tax on crypto-to-crypto transactions (subject to conditions)
  • Access to EU single market via passporting
  • Clear application process with defined timelines

Hungary is not a full-fledged crypto hub like Malta or Lithuania, but its low tax rate and straightforward regulatory approach make it attractive. Consulting24 advises and coordinates your application to ensure you meet all requirements.

Who Needs a Hungary Crypto License?

Any business that performs the following activities in or from Hungary needs a crypto license:

  • Exchange of virtual currency for fiat or other virtual currencies
  • Virtual currency wallet services (custodial and non-custodial)
  • Virtual currency transfer services
  • Virtual currency custody and administration
  • Participation in and provision of financial services related to an issuer's offer or sale of crypto-assets

If your business targets Hungarian customers or has a physical presence in Hungary, you must obtain the license. Even if you are based elsewhere in the EU, offering services to Hungarian residents may trigger a requirement to register or passport under MiCA. License Type and Regulator

The Hungary crypto license is a registration-based authorization, not a full banking license. The competent authority is the Hungarian National Bank (Magyar Nemzeti Bank, MNB). Under MiCA, the license is categorized as a CASP (Crypto-Asset Service Provider) authorization, with the following service classes:

Service ClassMinimum Capital (EUR)
Class 1: Exchange services (fiat-crypto, crypto-crypto)50,000
Class 2: Wallet services, custody, transfer125,000
Class 3: Full services (all of the above plus dealing on own account, underwriting, etc.)150,000

The MNB also requires fit and proper checks on directors and beneficial owners, plus a strong AML/CFT framework. The regulator is known for thorough but fair review processes. Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for a Hungary crypto license depends on the service class:

  • Class 1 (exchange only): EUR 50,000
  • Class 2 (wallet, custody, transfer): EUR 125,000
  • Class 3 (full services): EUR 150,000

Capital must be held in a bank account in Hungary or another EU member state, and it must be fully paid up before the license is granted. The MNB may require additional capital if the business model involves higher risk, such as high transaction volumes or complex products. Tax Treatment for Crypto in Hungary

Hungary offers a favorable tax regime for crypto businesses. Key points:

  • Corporate income tax (CIT) is 9%, one of the lowest in the EU.
  • Capital gains from crypto-to-crypto transactions are tax-exempt for corporate entities (under certain conditions).
  • Fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-fiat transactions are subject to CIT on the profit.
  • VAT: Crypto exchange services are generally VAT-exempt under EU law.
  • Personal income tax for individuals: 15% flat rate on crypto gains.

This tax structure makes Hungary particularly attractive for crypto-to-crypto trading platforms. However, tax treatment can be complex, especially for multi-jurisdictional operations. Allowed Activities Under the Hungary Crypto License

Once licensed, you can conduct the following activities in Hungary and, via passporting, across the EU:

  • Exchange of virtual currencies for fiat money
  • Exchange of virtual currencies for other virtual currencies
  • Transfer of virtual currencies
  • Custody and administration of virtual currencies on behalf of clients
  • Operation of a trading platform for virtual currencies
  • Participation in and provision of financial services related to an issuer's offer or sale of crypto-assets

Activities not covered include issuing stablecoins (unless registered as an asset-referenced token issuer under MiCA) and operating as a central securities depository. The license also does not permit proprietary trading beyond what is allowed under Class 3. Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a Hungary crypto license involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step.

  1. Pre-assessment: We review your business model and determine the appropriate service class and capital requirement.
  2. Company incorporation: Set up a Hungarian company (usually Kft.) with a registered office and appointed directors.
  3. AML/CFT program: Develop a compliant AML policy, risk assessment, and internal procedures.
  4. Documentation: Prepare business plan, financial projections, organizational structure, and fit-and-proper declarations for all key personnel.
  5. Application submission: File the application with the MNB, including all supporting documents and payment of the filing fee.
  6. Review and queries: The MNB reviews the application and may request additional information. Typical response time is 2-4 months.
  7. Approval and licensing: Once approved, you receive the license and can commence operations. Post-licensing compliance includes regular reporting to the MNB.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

After obtaining the license, you must maintain compliance with Hungarian and EU regulations. Key obligations include:

  • Annual AML/CFT audits and reporting to the MNB
  • Regular financial reporting (annual accounts, capital adequacy)
  • Transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting
  • Fit-and-proper reassessments every 3 years
  • MiCA compliance updates (e.g., white paper requirements for issuers)

Failure to comply can result in fines or license revocation. For example, the MNB has imposed fines for inadequate AML controls in the past. Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make these errors when applying for a Hungary crypto license:

  • Underestimating capital requirements: Ensure you have the full capital paid up before application. Some founders try to use loans or crypto assets, which the MNB may not accept.
  • Incomplete AML documentation: The MNB is strict on AML policies; use a professional template tailored to your business model. Generic policies are often rejected.
  • Ignoring beneficial ownership transparency: All ultimate beneficial owners must be disclosed. Hidden ownership structures can lead to immediate rejection.
  • Applying for the wrong service class: Over- or under-classifying services can delay approval. For instance, if you offer both exchange and custody, you need Class 2 or 3, not Class 1.
  • Not preparing for bank account opening: Start banking discussions early. Many founders assume they can open an account quickly, but it often takes months.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Hungary crypto license?

The minimum capital depends on the service class under MiCA: Class 1 (exchange) EUR 50,000, Class 2 (wallet, custody, transfer) EUR 125,000, Class 3 (full services) EUR 150,000. Capital must be fully paid up in a bank account before license approval.

How long does it take to get a Hungary crypto license?

The application process typically takes 3 to 6 months from submission to approval, depending on document completeness and MNB workload. Pre-application preparation can add 1-2 months. Consulting24 advises and coordinates to keep your timeline on track.

Can I passport the Hungary crypto license to other EU countries?

Yes, under MiCA, a Hungary crypto license allows you to provide services across all EU member states through passporting. You must notify the MNB of your intention to passport and comply with local consumer protection rules.

What is the tax rate for crypto businesses in Hungary?

Corporate income tax is 9%, one of the lowest in the EU. Crypto-to-crypto gains are tax-exempt for corporate entities under certain conditions. Fiat transactions are subject to CIT. VAT on exchange services is generally exempt.

Is a Hungary crypto license worth it compared to Panama?

It depends on your target market. Hungary offers EU passporting and a low 9% tax rate but requires EUR 50,000-150,000 capital. Panama costs a flat EUR 6,000 with 0% offshore tax but no EU passport. If you need EU access, Hungary is better; for cost savings, Panama.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements after obtaining the license?

Ongoing obligations include annual AML/CFT audits, regular financial reporting, transaction monitoring, suspicious activity reporting, and fit-and-proper reassessments every 3 years. Consulting24 offers ongoing compliance support to help you meet these requirements.

Can I apply for a Hungary crypto license if my company is already registered in another EU country?

Yes, you can apply as a foreign company by establishing a branch or subsidiary in Hungary. Alternatively, you may passport your existing EU license to Hungary. Consulting24 advises and coordinates the best approach for your situation.

What happens if I operate without a license in Hungary?

Operating without a license is illegal and can result in fines, criminal prosecution, and shutdown of operations. The MNB actively monitors unlicensed activity. It is essential to obtain the license before offering services to Hungarian residents.

Does the Hungary crypto license allow me to issue stablecoins?

No, issuing stablecoins (asset-referenced tokens) requires a separate authorization under MiCA. The Hungary crypto license covers only virtual currency services. If you plan to issue stablecoins, you need to apply for a different regulatory approval.
