What Is a Crypto License in Switzerland and Malta?

A crypto license is an authorisation to operate a virtual asset service provider (VASP) legally. In Switzerland, the Swiss Financial Market Supervisory Authority (FINMA) oversees crypto activities under the DLT Act and the Anti-Money Laundering Act. Depending on the business model, firms may need a FINMA banking licence (for custodial services) or a simpler AML registration. Malta, on the other hand, has a dedicated VFA framework administered by the Malta Financial Services Authority (MFSA). The VFA licence is required for exchanges, wallet providers, and other crypto services.

Both jurisdictions are known for their clear rules and proactive stance toward blockchain innovation. However, the cost and complexity vary. Switzerland is often chosen for its reputation and stable regulatory environment, while Malta offers a more streamlined process and lower capital requirements. The Swiss regime is principle-based, giving firms flexibility, whereas Malta's framework is rule-based with detailed requirements for each licence class. Consulting24 helps you understand which approach aligns with your business strategy.

For example, a Swiss AML registration is sufficient for a non-custodial exchange, but a custodial wallet provider would need a full banking licence. In Malta, the same custodial service would fall under a Class 3 VFA licence, which has its own capital and operational requirements. These distinctions matter when planning your budget and timeline.

Who Needs a Switzerland or Malta Crypto License?

Any business offering crypto-related services to clients in Switzerland or Malta must hold the appropriate licence. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (centralised and decentralised)
  • Wallet providers (custodial and non-custodial)
  • Payment processors using digital assets
  • Token issuers and ICO/STO platforms
  • Asset managers dealing in crypto
  • Brokerage and trading platforms

If your target market is Swiss or Maltese residents, or if you plan to establish a physical presence there, licensing is mandatory. Even if you operate remotely, serving clients in these jurisdictions may trigger licensing obligations. Capital Requirement

Switzerland does not have a statutory minimum capital for AML registration, but custodial services (banking licence) require at least CHF 10 million (approx. EUR 10.5 million). Malta's VFA licence requires minimum capital of EUR 50,000 for Class 1, EUR 125,000 for Class 2, and EUR 150,000 for Class 3 and 4. These figures are in line with MiCA capital tiers. Consulting24 can advise on the exact capital needed for your specific licence class.

For Swiss AML registration, the regulator expects adequate financial resources relative to the business volume, but no fixed minimum. In practice, you should have sufficient funds to cover operational costs for at least six months. Malta's capital requirements must be maintained at all times and can be in the form of share capital or reserves. Tax Treatment

Switzerland treats crypto assets as property for tax purposes. Capital gains for private investors are tax-free, but professional traders and businesses pay corporate income tax (effective rates 12-20% depending on canton). VAT is not applied to crypto transactions. Malta offers a more favourable corporate tax rate of 5% after imputation (effective rate can be as low as 5% for non-residents). Crypto transactions are exempt from VAT. Both jurisdictions have tax treaties to avoid double taxation.

In Switzerland, the tax burden varies by canton. Zug and Zug are popular for their low rates (around 12%). Malta's imputation system means shareholders can claim refunds on tax paid by the company, reducing the effective rate. Consulting24 can connect you with tax advisors in both jurisdictions to optimise your tax structure. For a simpler tax environment, consider Panama, which has no corporate tax on offshore income.

Allowed Activities

Switzerland allows a wide range of activities: crypto trading, custody, payment services, token issuance, and asset management. However, custodial services require a banking licence. Malta's VFA licence permits exchange, wallet, advisory, and portfolio management services, depending on the licence class. Both jurisdictions restrict activities not covered by the licence. In Switzerland, the DLT Act also allows for digital securities issuance and trading. Malta's VFA framework covers virtual financial assets but not e-money or securities, which fall under other regimes. What is the difference between a Switzerland and Malta crypto license?
