What is a Crypto OTC Desk License in El Salvador?

In El Salvador, there is no standalone "OTC desk license". Instead, businesses conducting over-the-counter crypto trades must comply with the Digital Assets Securities Law (DASL), which regulates digital asset service providers. This includes exchanges, custodians, and OTC desks. The regulatory body is the National Digital Assets Commission (Comisión Nacional de Activos Digitales, CNAD). The CNAD oversees registration and supervision of digital asset service providers.

An OTC desk facilitates large-volume trades between institutional clients, often with personalized service and liquidity. In El Salvador, such activity falls under the definition of digital asset services. To operate legally, a company must be registered with the CNAD and meet specific requirements, including a minimum capital, compliance with AML/CFT regulations, and reporting obligations. The registration process is similar to a license, and we refer to it as a crypto OTC desk license for practical purposes.

For example, a typical OTC desk might handle trades of 50 BTC or more per transaction, providing price quotes based on market depth and executing trades off-exchange. In El Salvador, these activities are regulated to ensure transparency and prevent money laundering. The CNAD requires detailed transaction records and regular reporting. Compared to other jurisdictions like Lithuania, where MiCA imposes strict capital tiers, El Salvador's framework is still evolving but offers flexibility.

Who Needs a Crypto OTC Desk License in El Salvador?

Any entity that provides OTC trading services for digital assets, including Bitcoin, Ethereum, and other cryptocurrencies, to clients in or from El Salvador must register with the CNAD. This includes:

  • OTC brokers matching buyers and sellers off-exchange
  • Liquidity providers offering quotes for large trades
  • Institutional trading desks serving funds, banks, or high-net-worth individuals
  • Any business that holds client funds or crypto for settlement
  • Foreign entities targeting Salvadoran clients may also need to register

The law applies to both local and foreign companies providing services within the country. License Type and Regulator

The relevant regulator is the National Digital Assets Commission (CNAD), established under the Digital Assets Securities Law. The registration is called "Registration as a Digital Asset Service Provider" (Proveedor de Servicios de Activos Digitales). This registration is mandatory for OTC desks, exchanges, custodians, and other service providers.

The CNAD issues a certificate of registration, which is valid indefinitely but subject to ongoing compliance. The regulator has the authority to conduct inspections, impose sanctions, and revoke registration for non-compliance. As of 2026, the CNAD has been active in processing applications, though the timeline can vary. The CNAD's approach is similar to other regulators like the Bank of Lithuania under MiCA, but with less prescriptive capital requirements. Capital Requirement

The Digital Assets Securities Law does not prescribe a specific minimum capital for OTC desks in the law itself. However, the CNAD may require a minimum capital based on the type and volume of services. As of 2026, the common expectation is a minimum capital of $50,000 to $100,000, depending on the scope of operations. This capital must be maintained in a bank account or in digital assets as approved by the regulator. For exact requirements, a consultation is recommended.

For comparison, under MiCA in Lithuania, capital requirements are tiered: EUR 50,000 for simple custody, EUR 125,000 for exchange services, and EUR 150,000 for more complex activities including OTC desks. El Salvador's flexibility can be an advantage for smaller operations, but the lack of clarity may require legal guidance.

Tax Treatment

El Salvador has a favorable tax regime for digital asset businesses. Under the Digital Assets Securities Law, income from digital asset services is subject to a reduced corporate income tax rate of 0% for the first three years, and then a standard rate of 30% thereafter. However, there is no specific tax exemption for OTC desks beyond the general corporate tax. Additionally, capital gains from digital assets may be taxed at 10% if realized. Value-added tax (VAT) of 13% applies to services provided to Salvadoran clients.

It is important to note that Bitcoin is legal tender, and transactions in Bitcoin are exempt from capital gains tax. This can be a significant advantage for OTC desks dealing primarily in Bitcoin. However, other cryptocurrencies may be treated differently. Consulting24 recommends engaging a local tax advisor to optimize your structure. For a jurisdiction with a simpler tax regime, consider Panama, which has no capital gains tax on crypto and a territorial tax system.

Allowed Activities

With a CNAD registration, an OTC desk can perform the following activities:

  • Purchase and sale of digital assets directly with clients
  • Matching buy and sell orders for large blocks
  • Providing liquidity and price quotes
  • Custody of digital assets for settlement
  • Facilitating transfers between clients

Activities not explicitly allowed include operating a public exchange (which requires a separate exchange license), offering derivatives or margin trading (subject to additional regulation), and providing investment advice without a separate license. For OTC desks that also want to offer exchange services, a separate exchange license may be needed. It is crucial to define your service scope clearly in the application to avoid regulatory breaches.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a crypto OTC desk license in El Salvador involves the following steps:

  1. Company incorporation: Register a Salvadoran corporation (Sociedad Anónima) with the National Registry of Commerce. This typically takes 1-2 weeks.
  2. AML/CFT compliance: Develop internal policies, appoint a compliance officer, and set up transaction monitoring systems. This includes customer due diligence (CDD) and suspicious transaction reporting.
  3. Document preparation: Prepare business plan, financial statements, background checks on directors, and legal documents. All documents must be translated into Spanish if not already.
  4. Submit application: File the registration request with the CNAD, including all supporting documents and payment of fees. The CNAD may request additional information within 30 days.
  5. Review and interview: The CNAD reviews the application and may request additional information or conduct an interview with the compliance officer or directors.
  6. Approval: Once satisfied, the CNAD issues the registration certificate. The certificate is valid indefinitely but requires annual renewal.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Once registered, OTC desks must comply with ongoing requirements:

  • Annual renewal of registration (fee varies, typically $1,000-$3,000)
  • Submission of audited financial statements within 6 months of year-end
  • AML/CFT reporting to the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) for transactions above $10,000
  • Transaction reporting for large trades (above $10,000) to the CNAD
  • Maintenance of minimum capital as specified in the registration
  • Appointment of a local compliance officer

Failure to comply can result in fines or revocation of registration. Consulting24 provides ongoing compliance support to ensure you meet all obligations. This is general guidance, not legal advice. For a jurisdiction with lighter ongoing requirements, consider Panama, which has no annual renewal fee and simpler reporting.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

When applying for a crypto OTC desk license in El Salvador, avoid these pitfalls:

  • Underestimating the time required for company incorporation (allow 2-3 weeks)
  • Submitting incomplete or poorly translated documents (all must be in Spanish)
  • Ignoring AML/CFT requirements until after application (prepare policies early)
  • Choosing a business name that conflicts with existing registrations (check availability)
  • Assuming the registration allows all crypto activities (e.g., exchange or custody without separate approval)
  • Not budgeting for ongoing compliance costs (annual renewal, audits, compliance officer salary)
