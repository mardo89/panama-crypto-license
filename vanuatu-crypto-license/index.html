What Is the Vanuatu Crypto License?

The Vanuatu crypto license, officially a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) registration, authorizes businesses to offer cryptocurrency services such as exchange, custody, and transfer. It is regulated by the Vanuatu Financial Services Commission (VFSC) under the VASP Act 2023. The regime is designed to align with FATF recommendations while maintaining flexibility for innovators.

Key features include:

  • No minimum capital requirement (though adequate financial resources are expected).
  • Zero corporate tax on offshore income.
  • Fast processing: typically 2-4 months if documents are in order.
  • No requirement for a local director or physical office (but a registered agent is mandatory).
  • License valid for one year, renewable annually.

This license is ideal for businesses that want a credible, regulated status without the heavy costs of EU or US jurisdictions. Consulting24 advises clients on structuring their Vanuatu entity to meet VFSC expectations. For example, a crypto exchange handling both fiat and crypto pairs would need to demonstrate strong AML controls, while a custody provider must show secure storage protocols. The VFSC reviews each application on its merits, so a well-prepared business plan is essential.

Who Needs a Vanuatu Crypto License?

Any entity providing virtual asset services in or from Vanuatu must hold a VASP license. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (centralized and peer-to-peer).
  • Digital wallet providers (custodial and non-custodial).
  • Token issuers and ICO platforms.
  • Payment processors using crypto.
  • OTC trading desks.
  • DeFi platforms if they have centralized control.

Even if your target market is outside Vanuatu, operating through a Vanuatu entity triggers licensing. The VFSC takes a territorial approach: if your business is incorporated in Vanuatu or has its place of effective management there, you must be licensed. License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Vanuatu Financial Services Commission (VFSC), which oversees all financial services including VASPs. The license is a single category covering all virtual asset services, but the VFSC may impose specific conditions based on the business model. For example, a license for a crypto exchange might include a condition to maintain a certain level of liquidity.

The VASP Act 2023 sets out the legal framework. Key points:

  • License is valid for one year and must be renewed annually.
  • Applicants must submit a business plan, AML/CFT policies, and proof of fit and proper management.
  • The VFSC conducts background checks on directors and beneficial owners.
  • Annual renewal requires submission of audited financial statements and a compliance report.

Unlike EU MiCA regimes that have tiered capital requirements (EUR 50,000 to 150,000), Vanuatu has no statutory minimum capital. However, the VFSC expects applicants to demonstrate sufficient financial resources for their operations. Consulting24 coordinates the application, ensuring all documentation meets VFSC standards. Capital Requirement

Vanuatu does not impose a statutory minimum capital requirement for VASP license applicants. However, the VFSC expects the company to have adequate financial resources to cover operational expenses and potential liabilities. In practice, this means demonstrating a reasonable capital base relative to the scale of business. For a small exchange, USD 10,000 might be acceptable, while a larger platform may need USD 50,000 or more.

For comparison, EU jurisdictions under MiCA require minimum capital of EUR 50,000 for simple services (e.g., custody) up to EUR 150,000 for exchanges. Vanuatu's flexibility is a major advantage for startups. Tax Treatment

Vanuatu offers a zero-tax regime for offshore companies. There is no corporate income tax, no capital gains tax, no withholding tax, and no VAT on services provided to non-residents. This makes it one of the most tax-efficient jurisdictions for crypto businesses.

Key tax features:

  • 0% corporate tax on foreign-source income.
  • No stamp duty on share transfers.
  • No exchange controls.
  • Annual government fee of approximately USD 300 for company maintenance.
  • No requirement to file tax returns if no local income.

However, businesses must still comply with tax reporting in their home country. For example, a US-based founder must report global income to the IRS, and a UK resident must comply with HMRC rules on crypto assets. Consulting24 recommends consulting a tax advisor to understand cross-border implications. We often work with tax specialists to structure entities optimally.

Allowed Activities Under the License

The Vanuatu VASP license permits a wide range of activities, including but not limited to:

  • Exchange between virtual assets and fiat currencies.
  • Exchange between one or more forms of virtual assets.
  • Transfer of virtual assets (on behalf of clients).
  • Custody and administration of virtual assets.
  • Participation in and provision of financial services related to an issuer's offer of virtual assets (including ICOs).
  • OTC trading services.

Activities not explicitly covered may require additional approval. The VFSC may restrict certain high-risk activities on a case-by-case basis, such as services involving privacy coins or tokens with unclear legal status. Compliance and Trust Considerations

While Vanuatu offers a light-touch regulatory environment, compliance is taken seriously by the VFSC. Licensees must:

  • Implement strong AML/CFT policies aligned with FATF recommendations. This includes customer due diligence (CDD) for all clients, enhanced due diligence for high-risk customers, and ongoing transaction monitoring.
  • Conduct customer due diligence (CDD) and ongoing monitoring. You must verify identity using official documents and screen against sanctions lists.
  • Report suspicious transactions to the Vanuatu Financial Intelligence Unit within 24 hours.
  • Maintain records for at least five years, including transaction details and identification data.
  • Submit annual audited financial statements and compliance reports to the VFSC.
  • Appoint a compliance officer who is resident in Vanuatu or accessible.

Non-compliance can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. The VFSC conducts periodic inspections. Common Mistakes to Avoid

Many applicants make errors that delay or derail their application. Common pitfalls include:

  • Incomplete or poorly drafted AML policies. For example, using generic templates not tailored to the business model.
  • Lack of clear business plan or revenue model. The VFSC wants to see a viable business with realistic projections.
  • Directors with adverse background or insufficient experience. A director with a criminal record or no crypto experience may be rejected.
  • Underestimating the need for a local registered agent. You must appoint a VFSC-approved agent.
  • Failing to disclose all beneficial owners. The VFSC requires full transparency on ownership structure.
  • Not preparing for the interview. Key personnel may be asked about their role and the business.
