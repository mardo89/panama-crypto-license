What Is the Anjouan Crypto Exchange License?

The Anjouan crypto exchange license is a regulatory authorization issued by the Anjouan Financial Services Authority (AFSA) that allows businesses to operate a cryptocurrency exchange platform. It covers the trading of digital assets, including spot trading, token swaps, and related services. The license is part of Anjouan's broader efforts to attract fintech and blockchain companies by offering a clear legal framework and competitive advantages.

As of 2026, the Anjouan regime is fully operational and has been updated to align with international anti-money laundering (AML) standards. The license is recognized for its efficiency, with a typical processing time of 2-4 months. Anjouan's legal framework is based on the Companies Act and the Financial Services Act, which provide a clear basis for digital asset activities. The license is non-expiring but requires annual renewal. It is important to note that Anjouan is not part of the EU, so it does not benefit from MiCA passporting. However, it remains a strong choice for non-EU markets.

License Type and Regulator

The regulatory body for the Anjouan crypto license is the Anjouan Financial Services Authority (AFSA). The license is classified as a Class B Financial Services License for digital asset activities. This license permits the operation of a cryptocurrency exchange, including trading, order matching, and settlement services.

AFSA oversees compliance with AML/CFT regulations and requires licensees to implement strong internal controls. The license is non-expiring but subject to annual renewal and ongoing supervision. AFSA is a member of the International Association of Financial Services Authorities (IAFSA), which provides some international recognition. However, it is not a member of the European Securities and Markets Authority (ESMA) or other major regulatory bodies. Consulting24 coordinates with AFSA on behalf of clients, handling all regulatory communication and documentation.

The Class B license is distinct from a Class A license, which is for banking or insurance activities. For crypto exchanges, the Class B license is the appropriate choice. AFSA also issues licenses for other fintech activities, such as payment services, but those are separate.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a crypto exchange license in Anjouan is EUR 50,000. This capital must be fully paid up and held in a bank account in the name of the licensed entity. The funds serve as a financial buffer to ensure operational stability and client protection.

For businesses offering additional services like custody or margin trading, higher capital may be required. For example, if you plan to hold client funds in custody, AFSA may require up to EUR 100,000 in capital. Compared to other jurisdictions, EUR 50,000 is relatively low. For instance, under MiCA, capital requirements range from EUR 50,000 to 150,000 depending on services. Panama typically has no minimum capital requirement, making it even more accessible.

Tax Treatment in Anjouan

Anjouan offers a tax-neutral environment for licensed entities. There is no corporate income tax, no capital gains tax, and no withholding tax on dividends or interest. The only tax obligation is an annual license renewal fee, which is typically between EUR 2,000 and EUR 4,000.

This tax regime makes Anjouan highly attractive for crypto exchanges seeking to minimize their tax burden. However, clients must ensure compliance with tax laws in their home country or where their clients are based. Consulting24 recommends consulting with a tax advisor to understand cross-border tax implications. For example, if you are a resident of a high-tax country, you may still be liable for taxes on worldwide income. Anjouan does not have a tax treaty network, so withholding taxes on payments to non-residents may apply in some cases.

It is also important to note that Anjouan does not levy VAT on services, which can reduce operational costs. However, you may need to register for VAT in countries where your customers are located.
