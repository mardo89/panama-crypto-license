What Is the Crypto Wallet Custody License Luxembourg?

The crypto wallet custody license Luxembourg is a regulatory authorization that allows companies to provide custody and administration of crypto assets for clients. Under MiCA, this is classified as a CASP (Crypto-Asset Service Provider) license, specifically covering the service of 'custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients'. The CSSF is the competent authority responsible for licensing and supervision.

This license is essential for any business that holds private keys or controls crypto assets for third parties. It ensures compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) obligations, as well as operational resilience and client asset protection standards. Luxembourg's framework is considered one of the most rigorous in Europe, offering a strong signal of trustworthiness to partners and customers.

Who Needs This License?

Any business that intends to offer custodial wallet services for crypto assets in or from Luxembourg requires this license. Typical applicants include:

  • Crypto exchanges that hold client funds in cold or hot wallets
  • Institutional custodians serving asset managers, pension funds, or family offices
  • Fintech startups developing wallet applications with custody features
  • Banks or traditional financial institutions expanding into digital asset custody

If your business model involves holding private keys on behalf of clients, even if combined with other services like exchange or transfer, you must obtain this license. The CSSF also requires that the entity have a substantive presence in Luxembourg, including a registered office, local management, and adequate staff.

License Type & Regulator

The license is formally a Crypto-Asset Service Provider (CASP) license under MiCA, specifically for the service of 'custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients'. The regulator is the Commission de Surveillance du Secteur Financier (CSSF), which oversees all financial sector participants in Luxembourg.

The CSSF applies a risk-based approach, requiring applicants to demonstrate strong governance, operational resilience, and compliance frameworks. The license is a single passport within the EU, meaning that once licensed in Luxembourg, you can offer custody services across all EU member states without additional licensing.

It is important to note that the CSSF does not issue a separate 'wallet license' - the CASP license covers custody as one of several possible services. You may apply for custody alone or in combination with other CASP services such as exchange, transfer, or advice.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for a CASP license offering custody services is EUR 125,000. This is the second tier of capital requirements (the first being EUR 50,000 for simpler services like advice, and the third being EUR 150,000 for exchange and trading).

This capital must be held as own funds and maintained at all times. It can be in the form of cash or highly liquid assets. The CSSF may require additional capital based on the scale and risk profile of the business. For example, if you also offer exchange services, the higher tier of EUR 150,000 applies.

Tax Treatment

Luxembourg offers a competitive tax regime for crypto businesses. The standard corporate income tax rate is 24.94% (including solidarity surcharge and municipal business tax). However, capital gains from the sale of crypto assets held as investment may be tax-exempt under certain conditions, depending on the holding period and classification.

Value-added tax (VAT) on crypto custody services is generally exempt, as per EU VAT rules for financial services. However, specific advice from a Luxembourg tax advisor is essential, as tax treatment can vary based on the exact nature of the services and the client's profile.

Luxembourg also has an extensive network of double tax treaties, making it attractive for international operations. Consulting24 coordinates with local tax experts to ensure your structure is tax-efficient.

Allowed Activities

With a crypto wallet custody license in Luxembourg, you are authorized to:

  • Hold and administer crypto assets on behalf of clients, including private key management
  • Offer cold storage and hot wallet solutions
  • Provide multi-signature and other security features
  • Facilitate client access to their assets via web or mobile interfaces

If you also hold a broader CASP license, you may additionally offer exchange, transfer, and advisory services. The CSSF requires that each service be clearly defined in the license scope. It is possible to apply for custody only, but many businesses combine it with other services for operational efficiency.

Benefits of a Luxembourg Crypto License

Choosing Luxembourg for your crypto wallet custody license offers several advantages:

  • EU passport: License allows you to operate across all EU member states without additional licensing.
  • Regulatory credibility: CSSF supervision is highly respected, enhancing trust with clients and partners.
  • Stable legal environment: Luxembourg has a well-established legal framework for financial services, including digital assets.

Compliance & Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, you must maintain ongoing compliance with CSSF requirements. This includes:

  • Annual AML/CTF audits and reporting
  • Submission of annual financial statements and regulatory reports
  • Maintenance of minimum capital at all times
  • Notification of any material changes to the business (e.g., new services, change of management)
  • Regular IT security assessments and penetration testing

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Based on our experience, here are common pitfalls when applying for a crypto wallet custody license in Luxembourg:

  • Insufficient local substance: The CSSF requires a real presence in Luxembourg, including local management and staff. A mailbox company will be rejected.
  • Inadequate AML/CTF policies: Your policies must be tailored to your business model and comply with Luxembourg law and EU directives.
  • Underestimating timeline: The process can take 6-12 months. Plan your business launch accordingly.
  • Poor IT security documentation: The CSSF expects detailed security protocols, including key management, disaster recovery, and incident response plans.
