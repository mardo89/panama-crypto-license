What Is a Crypto Wallet Custody License in Seychelles?

A crypto wallet custody license in Seychelles is a regulatory authorization issued by the Financial Services Authority (FSA) under the Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) regime. It permits companies to offer custodial wallet services, meaning they can hold, store, and manage private keys on behalf of clients. This license is part of Seychelles' broader framework for virtual asset services, which also covers exchange and transfer services.

The license is essential for any business that wants to provide secure storage for cryptocurrencies, including hot and cold wallet solutions. It signals to clients and partners that your operations meet international standards for security and compliance. As of 2026, the FSA requires all VASPs to be licensed, with a focus on anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) measures. The regime is aligned with FATF recommendations, and the FSA conducts regular inspections to ensure adherence.

For example, a company offering multi-signature wallet services for institutional investors would need this license. Without it, they risk fines or shutdown. The license also facilitates partnerships with banks and payment processors who require proof of regulation.

License Type and Regulator

The crypto wallet custody license Seychelles falls under the Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) category, regulated by the Financial Services Authority (FSA) of Seychelles. The FSA oversees all virtual asset activities under the Virtual Asset Service Provider Act (VASPA) and related regulations. There is no separate license for custody alone; it is included within the VASP license scope.

The FSA requires all VASPs to register and obtain a license before commencing operations. The regulatory framework aligns with FATF recommendations, emphasizing AML/CFT compliance. As of 2026, the FSA has tightened oversight, requiring annual audits and regular reporting. The FSA also conducts background checks on directors and beneficial owners, including criminal record checks and financial history reviews.

It is important to note that the Seychelles VASP license is not a passportable license under MiCA. If you plan to serve EU clients, you may need a separate license in an EU member state. Consulting24 can advise on multi-jurisdictional strategies.

Capital Requirement

The Seychelles FSA imposes a minimum capital requirement for VASP licenses, including custody services. As of 2026, the minimum paid-up capital is EUR 50,000 (or equivalent in USD). This capital must be maintained throughout the license period and is intended to ensure financial stability and client protection.

Higher capital may be required if your business plans to hold large volumes of client assets or offer additional services like exchange or transfer. The exact amount is determined during the application review. Consulting24 advises on structuring your capital to meet regulatory expectations, including whether to use share capital or a bank guarantee.

For comparison, Lithuania requires EUR 50,000-150,000 depending on services, while Panama has no specific capital requirement for its license. The Seychelles requirement is moderate and achievable for most startups.

Tax Treatment

Seychelles offers a highly favorable tax regime for licensed VASPs. The corporate income tax rate is a flat 15%, but many businesses qualify for exemptions or reduced rates under the International Business Company (IBC) structure. Additionally, there is no capital gains tax, no withholding tax on dividends, and no VAT on crypto services.

However, tax treatment depends on the specific corporate structure and activities. For example, if your company is tax resident in Seychelles, you may be subject to the standard 15% rate, but offshore income can be exempt. The IBC structure allows for tax exemption on foreign-sourced income, which is common for crypto businesses serving international clients.

Allowed Activities

With a crypto wallet custody license Seychelles, you are authorized to perform the following activities:

  • Safekeeping and administration of virtual assets on behalf of clients
  • Management of private keys
  • Wallet creation and management (hot, cold, multi-signature)
  • Incidental services such as transaction monitoring and reporting

Note that the license does not automatically permit exchange services (buying/selling crypto for fiat) or transfer services (sending crypto between wallets). Those require separate authorization or a broader VASP license. If you plan to offer multiple services, you may need a combined license or a separate exchange license.

For example, if your wallet app also allows users to swap tokens, you would need to apply for an exchange license as well. The FSA considers each service separately. Consulting24 can help you scope your license correctly to avoid compliance gaps.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Applicants often make these errors when applying for a Seychelles crypto license:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing AML policies or business plans cause delays. Ensure all documents are comprehensive and professionally drafted.
  • Underestimating capital: Not maintaining the minimum capital throughout operations. The FSA may request proof of capital at any time.
  • Ignoring local presence: The FSA requires a physical office and local director. Using a virtual office may not suffice.
  • Overlooking banking: Not securing a bank account before licensing can delay operations. Start the process early.
  • Mixing services: Offering exchange services without proper authorization can lead to penalties. Apply for the correct license scope.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Seychelles crypto wallet custody license?

The minimum paid-up capital is EUR 50,000 as of 2026. This must be maintained throughout the license period. Higher capital may be required for larger operations or additional services.

How long does it take to get a Seychelles crypto license?

The process typically takes 3 to 6 months from application submission to approval. Delays can occur if documentation is incomplete. Consulting24 helps streamline the process.

Can I offer exchange services with a custody license?

No, a custody license only covers safekeeping and management of private keys. Exchange services require a separate authorization or a broader VASP license. You may need to apply for both.

What are the tax benefits of a Seychelles crypto license?

Seychelles offers a 15% corporate tax rate, but IBCs can be exempt on foreign-sourced income. There is no capital gains tax, no withholding tax on dividends, and no VAT on crypto services.

Do I need a physical office in Seychelles?

Yes, the FSA requires a physical office and a local director. Virtual offices are not sufficient. Consulting24 can arrange a serviced office and local director.

Is the Seychelles VASP license recognized in the EU?

No, the Seychelles license is not passportable under MiCA. If you want to serve EU clients, you may need a separate license in an EU member state like Lithuania or Estonia.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

You must conduct annual AML/CFT audits, submit quarterly or annual reports to the FSA, appoint a local compliance officer, and maintain secure custody infrastructure. Consulting24 offers ongoing support.

Can I use a Seychelles license for a non-custodial wallet?

If your wallet is truly non-custodial (users control private keys), you may not need a license. However, if you provide any key management services, registration may be required. We recommend a consultation.

What happens if I fail to comply with FSA regulations?

Non-compliance can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. The FSA may also impose penalties on directors. Ongoing compliance is critical.

How does Seychelles compare to Panama for crypto custody?
