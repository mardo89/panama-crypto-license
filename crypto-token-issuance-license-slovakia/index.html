What Is the Crypto Token Issuance License in Slovakia?

The crypto token issuance license in Slovakia is a regulatory authorisation granted by the National Bank of Slovakia (NBS) under the transposition of MiCA. It permits a legal entity to offer crypto tokens to the public or to seek their admission to trading on a trading platform. This includes both utility tokens and, under certain conditions, asset-referenced tokens (ARTs) and e-money tokens (EMTs), though the latter two face stricter requirements.

Unlike a general CASP license, the token issuance license is specifically for issuers who do not operate a trading platform or provide custody services. It focuses on the offering and listing of tokens. The license is mandatory for any token sale exceeding EUR 150,000 in a 12-month period, unless exemptions apply (e.g., tokens offered exclusively to qualified investors or for free).

Key features of the Slovak token issuance license include:

  • Regulated by the National Bank of Slovakia (NBS).
  • Full alignment with MiCA requirements as of 2026.
  • Requires a white paper approved by the NBS or a competent authority in another EU member state.
  • Ongoing reporting obligations, including annual updates to the white paper.

Who Needs a Crypto Token Issuance License in Slovakia?

Any entity that intends to offer crypto tokens to the public in Slovakia - or to have tokens admitted to trading on a platform accessible to Slovak investors - must obtain this license. This includes startups launching initial coin offerings (ICOs), established companies issuing security tokens, and decentralised finance (DeFi) projects that tokenise assets.

Exemptions exist for:

  • Tokens offered for free (e.g., airdrops).
  • Tokens offered to fewer than 150 natural or legal persons per member state.
  • Tokens with a total consideration below EUR 150,000 over 12 months.
  • Tokens offered exclusively to qualified investors.

License Type and Regulator

The competent authority for crypto token issuance in Slovakia is the National Bank of Slovakia (Národná banka Slovenska, NBS). NBS acts as the single point of contact for MiCA authorisations and supervises all crypto-asset service providers and token issuers.

The license type is a crypto-asset issuance authorisation under MiCA. It is distinct from the CASP license, which covers services like exchange, custody, and transfer. However, if your business also intends to offer trading or custody, you may need a combined license.

For asset-referenced tokens (ARTs) and e-money tokens (EMTs), the European Banking Authority (EBA) and the European Securities and Markets Authority (ESMA) have additional oversight. In practice, most token issuers in Slovakia apply for the standard token issuance license, which covers utility tokens and security tokens that are not ARTs or EMTs.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for token issuers depends on the type of token and the services offered. For a standard token issuance license (non-ART/EMT), the requirement is EUR 50,000 in own funds. This must be maintained at all times and can be in the form of share capital, reserves, or subordinated loans.

For issuers of asset-referenced tokens, the capital requirement is higher: EUR 350,000 or 2% of the reserve assets, whichever is greater. E-money token issuers face a requirement of EUR 350,000 or 2% of the average outstanding e-money. Consulting24 advises that most token projects fall under the EUR 50,000 tier.

Capital must be held in a bank account in Slovakia or another EU member state. The NBS may also require professional indemnity insurance or a guarantee if the issuer's activities pose higher risks.

Tax Treatment of Token Issuance in Slovakia

Slovakia offers a relatively favourable tax environment for token issuers. As of 2026, the corporate income tax rate is a flat 21% (15% for companies with turnover below EUR 60,000). Capital gains from token sales are generally treated as business income and taxed at the same rate.

Importantly, Slovakia does not impose VAT on the issuance of crypto tokens, as the European Court of Justice has ruled that transactions involving traditional currencies and crypto assets are exempt from VAT. However, advisory and management fees may be subject to 20% VAT.

For individual token holders, gains from the sale of crypto assets held for more than one year are tax-exempt, provided the total annual income from such sales does not exceed EUR 2,400. This can be attractive for token issuers who are also investors. Consulting24 recommends engaging a Slovak tax advisor to structure your token sale efficiently.

Allowed Activities Under the License

The crypto token issuance license in Slovakia permits the following activities:

  • Public offering of crypto tokens (including ICOs, STOs, IEOs).
  • Admission of tokens to trading on a trading platform.
  • Marketing and promotion of token offerings within Slovakia and the EU.
  • Issuance of utility tokens and security tokens (subject to securities laws).

The license does NOT cover:

  • Operating a trading platform (requires a separate CASP license).
  • Custody of client crypto assets.
  • Exchange services (fiat-to-crypto or crypto-to-crypto).
  • Transfer services for crypto assets.

Compliance and Trust: Ongoing Obligations

Holding a token issuance license in Slovakia comes with ongoing compliance duties. You must:

  • Maintain minimum capital of EUR 50,000 at all times.
  • Update your white paper annually or whenever a material change occurs.
  • Report to NBS on a quarterly basis: number of token holders, volume of tokens in circulation, and any complaints.
  • Implement AML/CFT policies, including customer due diligence for token purchases above EUR 1,000.
  • Appoint a compliance officer and a data protection officer (if processing personal data).

Common Mistakes When Applying for a Token Issuance License in Slovakia

Many applicants face delays or rejections due to avoidable errors. Common pitfalls include:

  • Incomplete white paper: Missing risk disclosures, unclear tokenomics, or failure to specify rights attached to tokens.
  • Insufficient capital proof: Not demonstrating that the minimum capital is unencumbered and available.
  • Unclear governance structure: NBS expects a clear organisational chart, roles, and responsibilities.
  • Ignoring AML requirements: Even token issuers must have AML policies, even if they do not handle fiat directly.
  • Choosing the wrong entity type: A sole proprietorship is not acceptable; a limited liability company (s.r.o.) is standard.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a token issuance license and a CASP license in Slovakia?

A token issuance license is specifically for offering tokens to the public or listing them on a trading platform. A CASP license covers services like exchange, custody, and transfer. If your business only issues tokens, the issuance license suffices. If you also operate a platform, you need a CASP license.

Can I use a Slovak token issuance license to offer tokens in other EU countries?

Yes. Under MiCA, a license from the National Bank of Slovakia is passportable across all EU member states. You can offer tokens in any EU country without additional authorisation, subject to notification requirements.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a token issuance license in Slovakia?

For standard token issuers (non-ART/EMT), the minimum capital is EUR 50,000. For asset-referenced tokens, it is EUR 350,000 or 2% of reserves. For e-money tokens, it is EUR 350,000 or 2% of outstanding e-money.

How long does it take to get a token issuance license in Slovakia?

The typical timeline is 3 to 6 months from submission of a complete application. NBS has up to 3 months to review, but delays can occur if additional information is needed.

What are the tax implications of issuing tokens in Slovakia?

Corporate income tax is 21% (15% for small companies). Token issuance is exempt from VAT. Capital gains from token sales are taxed as business income. Individual holders may be exempt from tax on gains if tokens are held over one year and annual sales are below EUR 2,400.

Do I need to prepare a white paper for a token issuance in Slovakia?

Yes. A compliant white paper is mandatory under MiCA. It must include information about the issuer, the project, rights attached to tokens, and risk factors. The white paper must be approved by NBS or another competent authority.

What happens if I issue tokens without a license in Slovakia?

Unauthorised token issuance can result in fines up to 5% of annual turnover or EUR 5 million, and the regulator may order a halt to the offering. It can also damage investor trust and lead to civil liability.

Are there exemptions from the token issuance license requirement?

Yes. Exemptions include offerings to fewer than 150 persons per member state, total consideration under EUR 150,000 in 12 months, tokens offered exclusively to qualified investors, and free tokens (airdrops).

What ongoing obligations apply after obtaining the license?

You must maintain minimum capital, update the white paper annually, report quarterly to NBS, implement AML policies, and appoint a compliance officer. Non-compliance can lead to penalties.
