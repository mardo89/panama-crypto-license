What is a Bermuda Crypto OTC Desk License?

A Bermuda crypto OTC desk license allows a company to operate an over-the-counter trading platform for digital assets. OTC desks facilitate large-volume trades between institutional clients without the liquidity and slippage issues of public exchanges. The license is issued under the Digital Asset Business Act 2018 (DABA) by the Bermuda Monetary Authority (BMA). It covers activities such as trading, brokering, and custody of digital assets on behalf of clients.

Bermuda's regulatory regime is known for its strong standards, including anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) requirements. The jurisdiction is particularly attractive for firms seeking a reputable, well-regulated environment with a clear legal framework for digital assets. As of 2026, Bermuda remains a top choice for OTC desks targeting institutional clients in North America and Europe.

The license is not a simple registration; it involves a rigorous application process that examines your business plan, governance, and financial health. The BMA expects applicants to demonstrate a deep understanding of digital asset risks, including market volatility, cybersecurity, and operational resilience. For example, an OTC desk handling Bitcoin swaps for a pension fund would need to show how it manages counterparty risk and settlement finality.

Who Needs a Bermuda Crypto OTC Desk License?

Any company that provides OTC trading services for digital assets to clients in or from Bermuda must hold a license. This includes:

  • OTC brokers executing large trades on behalf of institutional investors.
  • Market makers providing liquidity in digital assets.
  • Platforms offering matched principal trading or agency execution.
  • Firms that combine OTC trading with custody or advisory services.

If your business model involves facilitating trades between two parties for a fee or spread, you likely fall under the BMA's definition of a digital asset business. Exemptions exist for limited activities, but most commercial OTC desks require a full license. Consulting24 can help determine the correct license class for your operations.

For instance, a startup that only provides a bulletin board for matching buyers and sellers without handling funds may not need a license, but once you take custody or execute trades, the license becomes mandatory. The BMA also considers whether your clients are in Bermuda, even if your company is incorporated elsewhere. If you have a physical presence in Bermuda, you must apply.

License Type and Regulator

The Bermuda Monetary Authority (BMA) is the sole regulator for digital asset businesses in Bermuda. The relevant license is the Class F (Full) Digital Asset Business License, which permits all activities including OTC trading, custody, and exchange operations. There is also a Class M (Modified) license for smaller firms with limited activities, but most OTC desks will require Class F.

The BMA's regulatory framework is principles-based, focusing on risk management, capital adequacy, and client protection. The application process involves a detailed review of the business plan, governance structure, AML/CTF policies, and financial projections. The BMA also conducts fit and proper checks on directors and senior management.

Class F is the most comprehensive license, allowing you to operate an OTC desk, custody client assets, and even issue tokens. If you only want to broker trades without custody, a Class M might suffice, but the BMA encourages full licensing for credibility. Consulting24 advises most OTC desks to pursue Class F to avoid future limitations.

Capital Requirement

The BMA requires a minimum capital of at least 100,000 USD for a Class F license. This capital must be maintained at all times and can be in the form of cash or liquid assets. Higher capital may be required based on the volume of transactions or risk profile. The capital requirement is designed to ensure the firm has sufficient financial resources to cover operational risks and client obligations.

In addition to capital, the BMA may require a surety bond or professional indemnity insurance, depending on the activities. Consulting24 advises clients on structuring their capital to meet BMA expectations while optimising for operational efficiency. For instance, if you plan to trade large volumes, the BMA might ask for 200,000 USD or more. The capital must be held in a bank account in Bermuda or a jurisdiction approved by the BMA.

Tax Treatment

Bermuda has no corporate income tax, capital gains tax, or withholding tax. The tax regime is based on a payroll tax and social insurance contributions. For digital asset businesses, this means that profits from OTC trading are not subject to corporate tax. However, companies must register for payroll tax if they have employees. There is also a stamp duty on certain documents but generally not applicable to trading activities.

Bermuda's tax-neutral environment is a major advantage for crypto OTC desks, as it allows firms to retain more of their trading profits. Consulting24 recommends consulting with a tax advisor to understand the specific implications for your business structure. For example, if you have employees in Bermuda, you will need to pay payroll tax of about 4.75% on salaries above a threshold. No VAT or sales tax applies to digital asset services.

Allowed Activities

A Class F license permits the following activities:

  • Operating an OTC trading desk for digital assets.
  • Brokering trades between buyers and sellers.
  • Providing custody services for digital assets.
  • Issuing and redeeming stablecoins or other digital tokens.
  • Acting as a market maker.
  • Offering advisory services related to digital assets.

The BMA does not permit unlicensed retail exchange services without additional licensing. OTC desks can also engage in proprietary trading, subject to capital adequacy rules. All activities must be conducted in compliance with AML/CTF regulations and the BMA's conduct of business rules. For example, if you want to offer margin trading to institutional clients, you must disclose the risks and maintain additional capital.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The application process for a Bermuda crypto OTC desk license involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application consultation: Discuss your business model with Consulting24 to determine the appropriate license class and structure.
  2. Document preparation: Prepare a detailed business plan, financial projections, AML/CTF policies, governance framework, and personal questionnaires for directors.
  3. Submission: Submit the application to the BMA along with the application fee.
  4. BMA review: The BMA conducts a thorough review, which may include requests for additional information or clarifications.
  5. Interview: Key personnel may be interviewed by the BMA to assess fitness and propriety.
  6. Approval: Once satisfied, the BMA issues the license, subject to any conditions.
