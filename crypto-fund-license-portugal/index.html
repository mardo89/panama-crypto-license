What Is a Crypto Fund License in Portugal?

A crypto fund license in Portugal is a regulatory authorization issued by the CMVM that permits a fund manager to establish and manage an alternative investment fund (AIF) investing primarily in crypto assets. This license is required under the Portuguese Securities Code and the AIFMD regime, transposed into national law. The fund itself is typically structured as a contractual or corporate vehicle, such as a Sociedade de Investimento Colectivo (SICAV) or a Fundo de Investimento Alternativo (FIA).

The license covers both the management company (the AIFM) and the fund it manages. The CMVM oversees the entire lifecycle: authorization, ongoing compliance, and investor protection. As of 2026, the regime integrates MiCA's definitions for crypto assets, ensuring that funds holding crypto are treated consistently with EU standards.

Key features of the Portuguese crypto fund license include:

  • Passporting rights across the EU under AIFMD
  • Flexible fund structures (open-ended or closed-ended)
  • Eligibility for professional and retail investors (with additional requirements for retail)
  • Possibility to delegate portfolio management to third parties

Who Needs a Crypto Fund License in Portugal?

Any entity that manages a collective investment scheme investing in crypto assets and is established in Portugal must obtain a crypto fund license from the CMVM. This includes:

  • Startup fund managers launching a crypto venture capital fund
  • Established asset managers expanding into digital assets
  • Family offices setting up a private fund for crypto investments
  • International fund promoters seeking an EU base for crypto funds

Exemptions exist for funds with fewer than 100 investors and assets under management (AUM) below EUR 100 million (or EUR 500 million for unused closed-ended funds), but even then, registration with the CMVM is required. License Type and Regulator

The crypto fund license in Portugal is categorized under the Alternative Investment Fund Manager (AIFM) regime, regulated by the Comissão do Mercado de Valores Mobiliários (CMVM). The CMVM is the Portuguese securities market regulator, responsible for authorizing and supervising AIFMs and funds. The license is formally an "authorisation as an alternative investment fund manager" under Decree-Law No. 83/2017, which transposes AIFMD. For crypto-focused funds, the CMVM applies additional guidance on valuation, custody, and risk management of digital assets.

There are two main types of AIFM licenses:

  • Full AIFM license: For managers with AUM above EUR 100 million (or EUR 500 million for unused closed-ended funds). Requires compliance with all AIFMD requirements, including capital, risk management, and reporting.
  • Small AIFM (sub-threshold) registration: For managers below those thresholds. Requires lighter compliance but still must register with the CMVM and follow basic conduct rules.

For crypto funds, the CMVM expects the manager to demonstrate expertise in digital assets and have appropriate custody arrangements. As of 2026, MiCA's rules for crypto asset service providers (CASPs) also apply if the fund engages in activities like custody or trading on behalf of investors.

Capital Requirements

Under AIFMD, the minimum initial capital for a full AIFM license in Portugal is EUR 125,000. For small AIFMs (sub-threshold), the requirement is lower: EUR 50,000. However, if the fund manager also holds client assets or provides additional services (e.g., discretionary portfolio management), the capital requirement may increase to EUR 150,000. These figures align with the EU's MiCA capital tiers for CASPs, but for fund managers, AIFMD rules apply.

In addition to initial capital, the AIFM must maintain own funds equivalent to at least 0.02% of AUM above EUR 250 million, up to a maximum of EUR 10 million. The capital must be held in liquid assets and cannot be used for other purposes. Consulting24 advises on the precise capital calculation based on your fund's AUM and activities.

Tax Treatment

Portugal offers a competitive tax regime for crypto funds. The fund itself is generally exempt from corporate income tax on capital gains and income from crypto assets, provided it meets certain conditions (e.g., not engaging in trading as a regular business). Instead, taxation occurs at the investor level:

  • Resident individual investors: Capital gains from crypto funds are taxed at a flat rate of 28%, but can be aggregated with other income and taxed at progressive rates (up to 48%) if elected.
  • Non-resident investors: Generally exempt from Portuguese tax on capital gains, subject to anti-abuse rules and double tax treaties.
  • Corporate investors: Subject to standard corporate income tax (CIT) at 21% (plus municipal surcharges up to 1.5%), with potential participation exemption for qualifying holdings.

Additionally, Portugal does not levy stamp duty or wealth tax on fund investments. The fund manager (AIFM) pays standard CIT on its management fees. Consulting24 recommends a tax consultation to optimize the structure for your target investors.

Allowed Activities

A Portuguese crypto fund license permits the fund manager to perform the following activities:

  • Portfolio management of crypto assets and related instruments
  • Risk management for the fund
  • Administration, including valuation, NAV calculation, and investor services
  • Marketing of fund units to professional investors across the EU (passporting)
  • Delegation of investment management to third parties (subject to CMVM approval)

The fund itself may invest in a wide range of crypto assets, including cryptocurrencies, tokens, stablecoins, and crypto derivatives. It may also engage in staking, lending, and other yield-generating activities, provided these are disclosed in the prospectus and comply with MiCA rules. The fund cannot, however, provide direct crypto custody to third parties (that requires a separate CASP license).

Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining a crypto fund license in Portugal involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step:

  2. Documentation preparation: Draft the fund prospectus, constitutional documents, risk management policy, AML/KYC procedures, and valuation policy.
  3. Capital deposit: Place the minimum capital in a blocked account with a Portuguese bank.
  4. Submission to CMVM: File the application with all supporting documents. The CMVM has 30 days to acknowledge receipt and request missing information.
  5. CMVM review: The regulator evaluates the application within 3-6 months. They may request clarifications or amendments.
  6. License issuance: Upon approval, the CMVM issues the AIFM authorization. The fund can then be registered and launch.
  7. Ongoing compliance: Submit annual reports, conduct AML checks, and maintain capital requirements.

Benefits of a Portuguese Crypto Fund License

Portugal offers several advantages for crypto fund managers:

  • EU passporting: Market your fund to professional investors across all EU member states without additional authorization.
  • Tax efficiency: No capital gains tax for non-resident investors; fund-level exemption for crypto gains.
  • Regulatory clarity: CMVM has a pragmatic approach to crypto, with clear guidance on valuation and custody.
  • Cost-effective: Lower setup and operational costs compared to other EU hubs like Luxembourg or Ireland.
  • Lifestyle and talent: Portugal's quality of life attracts top talent in fintech and blockchain.

Compared to Panama, which offers a flat-fee EUR 6,000 license but without EU passporting, Portugal provides access to the EU single market. For managers targeting European investors, Portugal is often the better choice.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

After obtaining the license, the fund manager must comply with ongoing obligations:

  • Annual audited financial statements and fund reports to the CMVM
  • Quarterly reporting on AUM, risk exposures, and investor composition
  • AML/CFT compliance, including transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting
  • Maintenance of minimum capital and own funds
  • Periodic updates to the prospectus and key investor information documents (KIIDs)

The CMVM conducts periodic inspections and may request additional information. Non-compliance can result in fines or license revocation. Consulting24 provides ongoing compliance support to ensure you meet all regulatory requirements. This is general guidance, not legal advice.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Many applicants make avoidable errors that delay or derail their license application. Common pitfalls include:

  • Inadequate AML policies: The CMVM expects strong AML/KYC procedures tailored to crypto assets, including blockchain analytics.
  • Underestimating capital requirements: Ensure you have the full amount in liquid assets before applying.
  • Poor fund documentation: The prospectus must clearly describe investment strategy, risks, and valuation methodology for crypto assets.
  • Ignoring MiCA overlap: If the fund also provides custody or trading services to third parties, a separate CASP license may be needed.
  • Choosing the wrong structure: SICAV vs. FIA has different tax and regulatory implications; get professional advice.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto fund license in Portugal?

The minimum initial capital is EUR 125,000 for a full AIFM license, or EUR 50,000 for a small AIFM (sub-threshold). Additional own funds may be required based on AUM.

How long does it take to get a crypto fund license in Portugal?

The process typically takes 4 to 8 months from preparation to approval, depending on the complexity of the fund and the CMVM's workload.

Can I passport my Portuguese crypto fund to other EU countries?

Yes, a full AIFM license allows you to market fund units to professional investors across the EU under the AIFMD passporting regime.

What is the tax rate for crypto funds in Portugal?

The fund itself is generally exempt from corporate tax on crypto gains. Non-resident investors are exempt from Portuguese capital gains tax. Resident individuals pay 28% flat or progressive rates.

What activities are allowed under a Portuguese crypto fund license?

The license permits portfolio management of crypto assets, risk management, administration, and marketing of fund units. It does not cover direct third-party custody or trading services.

Do I need a physical office in Portugal to get the license?

Yes, the AIFM must have a registered office and effective management in Portugal. However, we can help you set up a virtual office or co-working space that meets CMVM requirements.

Can I use a Portuguese crypto fund to invest in ICOs or token sales?

Yes, as long as the tokens are classified as crypto assets under MiCA and the fund's prospectus discloses the investment strategy. The CMVM may require additional risk disclosures.

What happens if I don't meet the capital requirements after launch?

The CMVM requires ongoing compliance with capital rules. If AUM increases, you must top up own funds. Failure to do so can result in fines or license suspension.

Is a Portuguese crypto fund license recognized outside the EU?

Recognition depends on bilateral agreements. For non-EU investors, the license adds credibility but does not automatically grant access to local markets. We advise on structuring for global distribution.

How does Portugal compare to Panama for crypto funds?

Panama offers a low-cost EUR 6,000 flat license with no capital requirement, but no EU passport. Portugal is more expensive but provides access to the EU market and a strong regulatory framework.
