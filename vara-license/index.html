What is a VARA License?

The Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA) is the sole regulator for virtual assets in Dubai, excluding the Dubai International Financial Centre (DIFC). A VARA license authorizes a company to provide one or more virtual asset services, such as operating an exchange, providing custody, or facilitating token offerings. The license is mandatory for any business offering virtual asset services in or from Dubai.

VARA's regulatory framework is principle-based and risk-focused. Firms must comply with anti-money laundering (AML), counter-terrorist financing (CTF), and consumer protection rules. The regime is designed to foster innovation while ensuring market integrity. VARA publishes a comprehensive rulebook that details requirements for each activity class, including governance, risk management, and technology standards. As of 2026, VARA has licensed over 30 VASPs, with many more in the pipeline. The regulator also conducts regular thematic reviews and publishes guidance on emerging topics like DeFi and stablecoins.

Who Needs a VARA License?

Any entity that provides virtual asset services in Dubai (mainland or free zones) must obtain a VARA license. This includes:

  • Virtual asset exchanges (centralized and decentralized)
  • Custodians and wallet providers
  • Broker-dealers and market makers
  • Token issuers (including ICOs, STOs, and utility tokens)
  • Payment and remittance services using virtual assets
  • Advisory and portfolio management services involving virtual assets
  • Lending and borrowing platforms
  • Any other service that involves the transfer, exchange, or safekeeping of virtual assets

License Type & Regulator

The regulator is the Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA), based in Dubai. VARA issues a single license type called the Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) license. However, the license scope is defined by the specific activities you apply for. There is no tiered license; instead, the capital requirement and conditions vary by activity. VARA also requires that the licensed entity be a legal person established in Dubai (mainland or a designated free zone such as DMCC or ADGM). The company must have a physical office and a registered address in Dubai.

VARA operates under the supervision of the Dubai World Trade Centre (DWTC) and works closely with other UAE regulators, including the Central Bank and the Securities and Commodities Authority (SCA). For activities that overlap with securities, additional SCA approval may be needed. Consulting24 coordinates with all relevant authorities to ensure a smooth process.

Capital Requirement

VARA imposes minimum capital requirements based on the licensed activities. As of 2026, the requirements are:

  • Exchange services: AED 10 million (approx. USD 2.7 million)
  • Custody services: AED 5 million (approx. USD 1.36 million)
  • Broker-dealer services: AED 2 million (approx. USD 545,000)
  • Advisory services: AED 500,000 (approx. USD 136,000)
  • Token issuance: AED 1 million (approx. USD 272,000)
  • Payment services: AED 3 million (approx. USD 817,000)

Capital must be held in a bank account in the UAE and may be in fiat or certain stablecoins approved by VARA. The capital must be maintained at all times and is subject to audit. VARA also requires that the capital be unencumbered and available to cover operational risks. Tax Treatment

Dubai offers a highly favourable tax environment. The UAE has a 9% corporate tax rate (effective from June 2023), but free zone entities that meet certain conditions can benefit from 0% corporate tax. Virtual asset transactions are generally not subject to VAT (5%) if they qualify as financial services. There is no capital gains tax or withholding tax on crypto transactions.

However, VARA requires licensees to maintain proper accounting records and file tax returns. Free zone entities must also comply with economic substance requirements, which include holding board meetings in the UAE and having adequate physical presence. Allowed Activities

A VARA license allows a range of activities, subject to the scope granted. Permitted activities include:

  • Operating a virtual asset exchange (order book, matching engine)
  • Providing custody and wallet services
  • Brokerage and dealing in virtual assets
  • Market making
  • Issuing virtual assets (including security tokens and utility tokens)
  • Providing advisory and portfolio management
  • Transfer services (remittance, payment)
  • Lending and borrowing of virtual assets (if specifically authorised)
  • Staking and yield services
  • Virtual asset derivatives (with additional approval)

Activities not explicitly authorised are prohibited. VARA also restricts certain activities like algorithmic trading without additional approval. The rulebook requires that each activity be clearly described in the application, and VARA may impose conditions on how the activity is conducted. Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make these errors when applying for a VARA license:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing AML policies or insufficient technical details can delay the process. VARA expects a comprehensive risk assessment and detailed system architecture.
  • Underestimating capital requirements: Ensure you have the required capital in a UAE bank account before applying. Capital must be fully paid up and held in a regulated bank.
  • Choosing the wrong free zone: Not all free zones are recognised by VARA. DMCC and ADGM are preferred. Some free zones may have additional requirements.
  • Ignoring ongoing costs: Budget for annual license fees, compliance salaries, and audit costs. Ongoing costs can be USD 30,000-60,000 per year.
  • Assuming passporting: A VARA license does not automatically allow operations in other emirates or countries. You may need separate licenses for Abu Dhabi or other jurisdictions.
  • Overlooking economic substance: VARA requires a physical office and local staff. Remote operations are not permitted.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a VARA license and a DIFC license?

VARA regulates virtual asset activities in Dubai mainland and most free zones, while the Dubai International Financial Centre (DIFC) has its own regulator, the Dubai Financial Services Authority (DFSA). The DFSA also licenses crypto activities but only for firms operating within DIFC. VARA is the primary regulator for virtual assets in Dubai, and most crypto businesses choose VARA over DIFC due to broader scope and lower costs.

Can I apply for a VARA license remotely?

No, VARA requires the licensed entity to have a physical office in Dubai and a local registered address. You must also appoint a local compliance officer and senior management. However, you can incorporate a company in a free zone like DMCC remotely through authorised agents. Consulting24 can coordinate the entire process remotely, but you will need to visit Dubai for the on-site inspection.

How long does a VARA license last?

The VARA license is valid for one year and must be renewed annually. Renewal requires submission of updated documents, payment of fees, and evidence of ongoing compliance. VARA may also conduct periodic reviews before renewal. The annual renewal cost is typically lower than the first-year cost, but still significant.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements after obtaining a VARA license?

Licensees must submit quarterly regulatory returns, including transaction data and financial statements. They must also conduct annual AML audits and appoint a local compliance officer. VARA may perform on-site inspections and thematic reviews. Non-compliance can lead to fines or license suspension.

Is a VARA license recognised in other UAE emirates?

VARA's jurisdiction is limited to Dubai. For operations in Abu Dhabi, you may need a license from the Abu Dhabi Global Market (ADGM) or the Securities and Commodities Authority (SCA). However, VARA is working on mutual recognition with other UAE regulators. Currently, a VARA license does not automatically allow operations outside Dubai.

What happens if I don't meet the capital requirement?

VARA will not issue the license until the full capital is deposited in a UAE bank account and verified. If you fail to maintain the capital after licensing, VARA may impose penalties or revoke the license. Capital must be unencumbered and available for operational risks.

Can I use a VARA license to offer services globally?

A VARA license only authorises services in or from Dubai. If you want to serve clients in other countries, you must comply with local regulations. Some jurisdictions may accept a VARA license as evidence of regulatory status, but you typically need separate licenses for each market. Consulting24 can advise on multi-jurisdictional strategies.

What is the success rate of VARA license applications?

VARA does not publish official statistics, but industry estimates suggest a success rate of around 60-70% for first-time applicants. Common reasons for rejection include incomplete documentation, insufficient capital, and inadequate AML controls. Consulting24 helps improve your chances by preparing a strong application.

How does the VARA regime compare to MiCA?

MiCA is the EU's comprehensive crypto regulation, effective from 2025. It imposes capital requirements of EUR 50,000 to 150,000 depending on services. VARA has higher capital requirements (up to AED 10 million) but offers a more innovation-friendly environment and lower tax. MiCA allows passporting across EU member states, while VARA is limited to Dubai.

Can I apply for a VARA license if my company is already registered in another jurisdiction?

Yes, but you must set up a separate legal entity in Dubai. VARA does not recognise foreign licenses. Your existing company can be a parent or affiliate, but the licensed entity must be a Dubai-registered company. Consulting24 can help with the incorporation and restructuring.
