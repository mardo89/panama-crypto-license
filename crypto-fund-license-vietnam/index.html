Overview: What Is a Crypto Fund License in Vietnam?

A crypto fund license in Vietnam is not a single, standalone permit. Rather, it refers to the combination of registrations and approvals needed to manage a collective investment scheme that invests in digital assets. Currently, the State Securities Commission of Vietnam (SSC) oversees fund management activities under the Law on Securities, while the State Bank of Vietnam (SBV) regulates payment-related crypto services. The government has signalled plans to introduce a pilot regulatory sandbox for crypto assets, but as of 2026, no formal crypto fund license exists.

For practical purposes, setting up a crypto fund in Vietnam involves either:

  • Registering as a fund management company with the SSC and obtaining a securities business certificate.
  • Applying for a VASP registration if the fund engages in custody or exchange activities (subject to ongoing legal developments).

Who Needs a Crypto Fund License in Vietnam?

Any entity that pools capital from investors to invest in cryptocurrencies, tokens, or blockchain-based assets and charges a management fee or performance fee requires some form of authorisation. This includes:

  • Hedge funds, venture capital funds, and private equity funds focused on crypto.
  • Fund managers offering discretionary portfolio management in digital assets.
  • Staking pools or yield farming funds that operate as collective investment schemes.

If you plan to raise funds from Vietnamese residents or market your fund locally, you must comply with securities laws. Foreign fund managers looking to access Vietnamese investors may also need to establish a local presence or use a licensed fund management company. License Type and Regulator

The primary regulator for fund management in Vietnam is the State Securities Commission (SSC) under the Ministry of Finance. The relevant license type is the Fund Management Company License (Giấy phép thành lập và hoạt động công ty quản lý quỹ). This license allows the holder to establish and manage securities investment funds. For crypto-focused funds, the SSC currently treats digital assets as securities only if they meet the legal definition of securities under Vietnamese law.

Additionally, if the fund involves crypto custody or exchange, the State Bank of Vietnam (SBV) may require a VASP registration under the amended Law on Credit Institutions. As of 2026, the SBV has not issued detailed guidance for crypto VASPs, but a sandbox is expected. Capital Requirements

Vietnam imposes minimum charter capital requirements for fund management companies. As of 2026, the legal capital for a fund management company is typically VND 25 billion (approx. EUR 1 million) for domestic firms. However, for foreign-invested companies, the requirement may be higher or subject to approval by the Ministry of Planning and Investment. For crypto funds, the SSC may apply additional capital buffers based on the risk profile of the assets managed.

If the fund itself is structured as a securities investment fund, the fund must have a minimum capital of VND 50 billion (approx. EUR 2 million) for open-ended funds and VND 10 billion (approx. EUR 400,000) for closed-ended funds. These figures are subject to change and should be verified with local authorities. Tax Treatment for Crypto Funds in Vietnam

Vietnam's tax regime for crypto funds is still evolving. As of 2026, the following general principles apply:

  • Corporate Income Tax (CIT): Standard CIT rate is 20% on net profits. Crypto trading gains are generally taxable as ordinary income.
  • Withholding Tax: No specific withholding tax on crypto distributions, but dividends paid to foreign investors may be subject to 0-10% under double tax treaties.
  • Value Added Tax (VAT): Management fees are subject to 10% VAT. Crypto transactions may be exempt if classified as financial services.
  • Personal Income Tax: Investors are taxed on capital gains at progressive rates up to 35%, though crypto gains are not explicitly defined.

Consulting24 recommends a detailed tax review with local advisors. For a clearer tax environment, many funds choose Panama, which offers territorial taxation (no tax on foreign-source income).

Allowed Activities Under a Crypto Fund License

A fund management company license in Vietnam permits a range of activities, including:

  • Establishing and managing open-ended and closed-ended funds.
  • Portfolio management for individual and institutional clients.
  • Investment advisory services.
  • Custody of securities (but not yet explicitly for crypto assets).

For crypto-specific activities such as direct trading of unregistered tokens, staking, or DeFi participation, the license may not provide explicit coverage. The SSC currently limits fund investments to securities listed on recognised exchanges. Crypto assets that do not qualify as securities may fall outside the permitted scope. Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a fund management company license in Vietnam involves several stages:

  1. Pre-incorporation: Prepare legal documents, including a business plan, charter, and proof of capital. Engage a local law firm.
  2. Company registration: Register with the Department of Planning and Investment (DPI) to obtain an Enterprise Registration Certificate.
  3. License application: Submit the application to the SSC, including details of shareholders, directors, and compliance policies.
  4. Capital contribution: Deposit the minimum charter capital in a blocked bank account and obtain a certificate of capital contribution.
  5. Approval: The SSC reviews the application within 30-60 days. Additional information may be requested.
  6. Post-licensing: Register for tax, open a corporate bank account, and comply with ongoing reporting.

Compliance and Trust Considerations

Compliance is paramount for any regulated fund. In Vietnam, fund management companies must adhere to the Law on Securities, the Investment Law, and anti-money laundering (AML) regulations. Key requirements include:

  • Appointing a compliance officer and a risk management team.
  • Implementing AML/KYC procedures for investors.
  • Submitting annual audited financial statements and periodic reports to the SSC.
  • Maintaining adequate internal controls and record-keeping.

Common Mistakes When Applying for a Crypto Fund License in Vietnam

Many applicants encounter pitfalls that delay or derail their license. Common mistakes include:

  • Underestimating capital requirements: Failing to meet the minimum charter capital can lead to rejection.
  • Incomplete documentation: Missing business plans, shareholder details, or compliance policies.
  • Ignoring local partner requirements: Foreign-owned fund management companies may need a local partner.
  • Misclassifying activities: Offering services that fall outside the license scope, such as direct crypto trading.
  • Poor bank selection: Approaching banks without a clear compliance framework.

The minimum charter capital for a fund management company is approximately VND 25 billion (EUR 1 million). For the fund itself, open-ended funds require VND 50 billion (EUR 2 million) and closed-ended funds VND 10 billion (EUR 400,000). These figures are subject to change.

How long does it take to get a fund management license in Vietnam?

The process typically takes 4 to 8 months, including company registration and SSC approval. Delays can occur if documentation is incomplete or if the SSC requests additional information.

Yes, but foreign-owned fund management companies may face additional requirements, such as a minimum capital contribution and a local partner. The approval process is more stringent and may take longer.
