What is a Crypto Staking License in the Bahamas?

A crypto staking license in the Bahamas is a regulatory approval that permits a company to offer staking services for digital assets. Staking involves locking up cryptocurrencies (e.g., proof-of-stake coins) to support blockchain network operations and earn rewards. The Bahamas Securities Commission (SCB) regulates these activities under the DARE Act, which came into force in 2020 and was updated in 2024 to include staking-specific provisions.

The license is technically a "Digital Asset Business Registration" with a scope that includes staking services. It is not a separate "staking license" per se, but the registration can be tailored to cover staking activities. The SCB classifies staking as a form of "digital asset services" and requires firms to meet capital, compliance, and reporting obligations.

Key features of the Bahamas regime:

  • Regulator: Securities Commission of the Bahamas (SCB)
  • Legal basis: Digital Assets and Registered Exchanges Act (DARE Act)
  • License type: Digital Asset Business Registration (with staking scope)
  • Minimum capital: USD 100,000 (for staking services, as of 2026)
  • Corporate tax: 0% on offshore income; no capital gains tax
  • Timeline: 4 to 8 months for approval

Who Needs a Crypto Staking License in the Bahamas?

Any business that provides staking services to clients or operates a staking pool in or from the Bahamas must obtain a registration. This includes:

  • Staking-as-a-service platforms
  • Custodians offering staking rewards
  • Exchanges that allow users to stake assets
  • Funds or investment vehicles that stake on behalf of investors
  • Blockchain validators operating from the Bahamas

If your company merely holds staked assets as part of its own treasury, you may not need a license, but any service offered to third parties triggers the requirement. The SCB has a broad definition of "digital asset business" and has been actively enforcing compliance since 2024.

License Type and Regulator

The Bahamas Securities Commission (SCB) is the sole regulator for digital asset businesses. Under the DARE Act, there are two main categories: "Digital Asset Business" (full registration) and "Digital Asset Exchange" (for exchange platforms). Staking services fall under the broader Digital Asset Business registration, which covers a range of activities including custody, trading, and staking.

The SCB does not issue a separate "staking license" - instead, the registration scope must explicitly include staking services. Applicants must demonstrate that they have the operational capability, risk management, and capital to handle staking activities.

Key regulatory requirements:

  • Fit and proper test for directors and shareholders
  • Minimum capital of USD 100,000 (for staking)
  • Compliance with AML/CFT obligations
  • Regular reporting to the SCB
  • Annual audit by a SCB-approved auditor

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a digital asset business registration that includes staking services is USD 100,000 as of 2026. This capital must be maintained at all times and can be in the form of cash or liquid assets. The SCB may require higher capital depending on the volume of staked assets or the risk profile of the business.

Capital must be held in a bank account in the Bahamas or with a SCB-approved financial institution. Proof of capital is required at the time of application and must be audited annually.

Tax Treatment in the Bahamas

The Bahamas offers a tax-neutral environment for digital asset businesses. There is no corporate income tax, no capital gains tax, no VAT, and no withholding tax on profits from staking services. This makes the Bahamas highly attractive for staking operations where margins can be thin.

However, businesses must still comply with international tax reporting standards, such as CRS and FATCA, if they have clients from other countries. The Bahamas has also implemented economic substance requirements for certain entities, but digital asset businesses are generally exempt if they are not engaged in traditional financial services.

Allowed Activities Under the License

A Bahamas Digital Asset Business registration with staking scope allows the following activities:

  • Operating a staking pool for proof-of-stake assets (e.g., Ethereum, Solana, Cardano)
  • Offering staking-as-a-service to retail and institutional clients
  • Managing staking rewards and distributing them to clients
  • Providing staking-related custody services
  • Facilitating delegation of staking rights

Activities that are NOT allowed without additional registration include operating a crypto exchange (requires a separate exchange license), issuing stablecoins, or offering derivatives. The SCB prohibits staking of assets that are deemed securities without proper registration under the Securities Industry Act.

Compliance and Trust Considerations

Operating a crypto staking business in the Bahamas requires ongoing compliance. The SCB expects licensees to maintain strong AML/CFT controls, conduct regular audits, and submit periodic reports. Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license.

Key compliance obligations:

  • Appoint a Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO)
  • Conduct customer due diligence (CDD) and enhanced due diligence (EDD) for high-risk clients
  • Screen transactions for suspicious activity
  • Maintain records for at least 5 years
  • Submit annual audited financial statements to the SCB

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Many applicants face delays or rejection due to avoidable errors. Here are common pitfalls:

  • Inadequate capital proof: Failing to show that the minimum capital is fully paid up and held in a Bahamian bank account.
  • Poor business plan: Not providing a detailed plan that clearly describes the staking model, target market, and risk management.
  • Incomplete due diligence: Directors with undisclosed criminal records or adverse financial history.
  • Ignoring AML requirements: Submitting generic AML policies that do not address staking-specific risks.
  • Overlooking substance: The SCB expects the business to have real presence in the Bahamas, including a physical office and local staff.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto staking license in the Bahamas?

The minimum capital requirement is USD 100,000 as of 2026. This must be maintained in a Bahamian bank account or with an SCB-approved institution.

How long does it take to get a Bahamas crypto staking license?

The typical timeline is 4 to 8 months from application submission to approval. The SCB has improved processing times since 2024.

What taxes apply to staking income in the Bahamas?

The Bahamas has no corporate income tax, no capital gains tax, and no VAT on staking income. It is a tax-neutral jurisdiction for offshore businesses.

Can I stake any cryptocurrency under the Bahamas license?

You can stake proof-of-stake assets like Ethereum, Solana, and Cardano. However, staking assets that are classified as securities may require additional registration under the Securities Industry Act.

Do I need a physical office in the Bahamas?

Yes, the SCB expects licensees to have a registered office and a physical presence in the Bahamas. This can be a serviced office provided by a registered agent.

What is the difference between a Digital Asset Business registration and an Exchange license?

A Digital Asset Business registration covers services like custody, staking, and advisory. An Exchange license is specifically for operating a trading platform. Staking services fall under the broader registration.
