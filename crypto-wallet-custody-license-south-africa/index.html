What Is the Crypto Wallet Custody License South Africa?

The crypto wallet custody license South Africa is a regulatory authorization issued by the FSCA under the FAIS Act. It allows a company to provide crypto asset wallet services where the provider holds or controls the client's private keys or crypto assets. This includes custodial wallets, exchange-hosted wallets, and any service where the provider has custody of digital assets.

The license is part of South Africa's broader effort to bring crypto asset service providers (CASPs) into the formal financial regulatory framework. As of 2026, all CASPs must be licensed and comply with AML/CFT obligations under the Financial Intelligence Centre Act (FICA). The FSCA has set a deadline for existing providers to apply, and new entrants must be licensed before commencing operations.

Key features of the license include mandatory registration as a financial services provider (FSP), appointment of a compliance officer, and adherence to fit and proper requirements for key individuals. The FSCA also requires that licensees maintain professional indemnity insurance or a guarantee fund to cover potential client losses. For example, if a wallet provider suffers a security breach resulting in loss of client funds, the insurance provides a layer of protection. This requirement is similar to that for traditional financial advisors.

Who Needs This License?

Any entity that offers custodial wallet services to South African residents or operates from South Africa needs this license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges that hold client funds in wallets
  • Wallet providers that store private keys on behalf of users
  • Brokers or OTC desks that take custody of crypto assets
  • Any business that offers crypto asset custody as a service

Non-custodial wallet providers (where the user controls the private keys) may not require this license, but they should seek legal advice. The FSCA has indicated that any service involving control over a client's crypto assets falls under the definition of a financial service. For instance, a wallet that generates keys on the user's device and never holds them is likely exempt, but if the provider offers recovery services or holds a backup, it may be considered custodial.

License Type and Regulator

The relevant regulator is the Financial Sector Conduct Authority (FSCA). The license is a Category I or II Financial Services Provider (FSP) license under the FAIS Act, depending on the scope of services. For wallet custody, a Category I FSP license is typically sufficient, but if you also provide advice or intermediary services, a higher category may apply.

The FSCA has issued a specific declaration that crypto assets are financial products under the FAIS Act. This means all crypto asset service providers must comply with the same requirements as traditional financial advisors, including disclosure, record-keeping, and conflict of interest management. The declaration also subjects crypto firms to the Code of Conduct for Financial Services Providers.

Capital Requirement

South Africa does not prescribe a specific minimum capital requirement for crypto wallet custody license applicants under the FAIS Act. However, the FSCA expects applicants to demonstrate sufficient financial resources to operate the business and meet solvency requirements. As a general guideline, you should have at least ZAR 1,000,000 (approximately EUR 50,000) in available capital to cover operational costs and compliance obligations.

The exact amount depends on the scale of your operations. The FSCA may request a business plan and financial projections to assess adequacy. For instance, a startup with minimal overhead may need less, while a large exchange handling significant volumes may need more. Tax Treatment

South Africa taxes crypto assets as ordinary revenue, subject to income tax. The South African Revenue Service (SARS) has issued guidance that crypto transactions are subject to normal tax rules. For wallet custody providers, income from fees and spreads is taxable at the corporate income tax rate of 27% (as of 2026).

Value-Added Tax (VAT) on crypto services is still under review. As of 2026, the supply of crypto assets is generally exempt from VAT, but advisory or custody fees may be subject to VAT at 15%. You should consult a tax specialist to confirm your specific obligations. For example, if you charge a monthly custody fee, that fee likely attracts VAT, while the underlying asset transfer does not.

Allowed Activities

With a crypto wallet custody license South Africa, you are permitted to:

  • Hold and safeguard client crypto assets
  • Manage private keys on behalf of clients
  • Facilitate transfers between wallets
  • Provide wallet-related reporting and statements
  • Offer staking services if the client's assets are held in custody

You cannot provide financial advice without an additional license. If you want to advise clients on which crypto assets to buy or sell, you need a Category I FSP license with advice authorization. Trading as principal (proprietary trading) may also require separate authorization. For instance, if your business model involves market-making or using client assets for lending, you may need a different license or additional approvals.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a crypto wallet custody license South Africa involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step.

  1. Company registration: Register a South African company (Pty Ltd) with the Companies and Intellectual Property Commission (CIPC).
  2. FICA registration: Register with the Financial Intelligence Centre as an accountable institution.
  3. Appoint a compliance officer: Engage a FSCA-approved compliance officer to oversee AML/CFT programs.
  4. Prepare documentation: Draft a business plan, risk management plan, AML policy, and conflict of interest policy.
  5. Submit application: File the FSP license application with the FSCA, including all supporting documents and the application fee.
  6. FSCA review: The FSCA assesses the application, may request additional information, and conducts fit and proper checks on key individuals.
  7. License issuance: Once approved, you receive a license certificate and are added to the FSCA's register.

The entire process typically takes 6-12 months. We recommend starting early and engaging professional assistance to avoid delays. Consulting24 has experience with FSCA applications and can help you prepare a strong submission.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, you must comply with ongoing requirements:

  • Submit annual financial statements and compliance reports to the FSCA.
  • Conduct regular AML/CFT training for staff.
  • Maintain records of all transactions for at least five years.
  • Report suspicious transactions to the Financial Intelligence Centre.
  • Renew your FSP license annually and pay the prescribed fees.
