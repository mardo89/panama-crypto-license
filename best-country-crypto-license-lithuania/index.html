What Is the Lithuania Crypto License?

The Lithuania crypto license is a regulatory authorization issued by the Bank of Lithuania under the Law on Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) implementation. In 2026, it is a full CASP (Crypto-Asset Service Provider) license, replacing the old registration regime. It allows companies to offer crypto services legally within Lithuania and across the European Economic Area (EEA) under the EU passport.

Key features include a minimum capital requirement of EUR 125,000 for most services (custody and exchange), a corporate income tax rate of 15%, and a processing timeline of 3-6 months. The license covers activities such as crypto-to-fiat exchange, custodian wallet services, and crypto-to-crypto trading.

Unlike some EU jurisdictions that still rely on transitional regimes, Lithuania fully adopted MiCA by 2026, making its license one of the most strong and future-proof in the region. The Bank of Lithuania has a dedicated fintech team that reviews applications with a focus on AML compliance and business model viability. This license is not a simple registration; it requires a substantive application with detailed policies, a physical office, and a local director.

For founders comparing options, note that Estonia also offers a MiCA-compliant license but with a higher capital threshold for certain services. Lithuania's balance of cost and speed often makes it the preferred choice.

Who Needs a Lithuania Crypto License?

Any business planning to offer crypto-asset services to EU residents should consider Lithuania. This includes startups, established fintech companies, and traditional financial institutions expanding into digital assets. Specifically, you need this license if you:

  • Operate a crypto exchange (fiat-to-crypto or crypto-to-crypto)
  • Provide custodian wallet services
  • Offer crypto custody for third parties
  • Execute orders on behalf of clients
  • Place or receive crypto orders
  • Provide crypto advisory services

If your target market is the EU, Lithuania's license is often the best choice due to its cost-effectiveness and clear regulatory path. Even if you are based outside the EU, you can incorporate a Lithuanian subsidiary and apply for the license to access the single market. The license is also suitable for companies that want to use Lithuania as a gateway to the broader EEA, using the EU passport for services across 30 countries.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Bank of Lithuania (Lietuvos bankas). The license is a CASP (Crypto-Asset Service Provider) license under MiCA. In 2026, this is the only type of crypto authorization available for commercial crypto services. There are no separate categories for exchanges or wallets; all are covered under the CASP umbrella.

The Bank of Lithuania is known for its pragmatic approach and digital-first supervision. It requires applicants to have a physical office in Lithuania, at least one director with a clean criminal record, and strong AML/KYC procedures. The regulator also expects a detailed business plan, risk assessment, and operational policies before approving an application.

Compared to other regulators, the Bank of Lithuania is relatively accessible and provides guidance through its fintech hub. However, it does not compromise on compliance standards, so thorough preparation is essential. The regulator also conducts on-site inspections and may request interviews with key personnel. Unlike some authorities, the Bank of Lithuania publishes a list of licensed entities, adding transparency for clients and partners.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a Lithuania CASP license is EUR 125,000 for services involving custody and exchange. For more limited services (e.g., only order execution), the requirement may be lower at EUR 50,000. The capital must be paid up and held in a Lithuanian bank account. It can be used for operational expenses once the license is granted.

This capital is lower than in many other EU countries (e.g., Malta EUR 730,000, Cyprus EUR 350,000), making Lithuania one of the best countries for crypto licensing in terms of capital efficiency. The capital must be fully paid before the license is issued, and you must maintain it throughout the license period. The Bank of Lithuania may ask for proof of source of funds, so ensure you have clear documentation.

If you are considering other requirements, note that the capital is just one part. You also need to demonstrate operational readiness and a sound business model. For a lighter capital option, Panama has no minimum capital requirement, but the license does not cover EU markets.

Tax Treatment

Lithuania offers a competitive corporate income tax rate of 15% (standard) with a reduced rate of 5% for small companies (revenue under EUR 300,000 and fewer than 10 employees). VAT is not applicable to crypto-to-crypto transactions, but fiat-related services may be subject to VAT. Personal income tax on crypto gains for individuals is 15%.

There are no specific crypto taxes beyond the general framework. Lithuania does not impose withholding tax on dividends paid to EU residents. This tax environment is favorable compared to many other EU jurisdictions. For example, in Estonia, corporate tax is 20% on distributed profits, while Lithuania's 15% applies to retained earnings as well.

However, tax planning is essential. If you structure your operations through Lithuania but have management elsewhere, transfer pricing rules may apply. Consulting24 recommends working with a local tax advisor to ensure full compliance. For a jurisdiction with zero corporate tax on foreign income, Panama is an alternative, but it lacks EU market access.

Allowed Activities Under the License

The CASP license permits the following activities under MiCA:

  • Exchange of crypto-assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for other crypto-assets
  • Custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • Execution of orders for crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • Placing of crypto-assets (underwriting)
  • Receiving and transmitting orders for crypto-assets
  • Providing advice on crypto-assets

All activities are subject to AML/KYC obligations and reporting to the Bank of Lithuania. You cannot engage in activities outside this list without additional authorization. For example, if you plan to offer staking or lending, you may need a separate license under MiCA or local law. The license does not cover derivatives or securities tokens, which fall under MiFID II.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Pre-application preparation: Define business model, prepare AML/KYC policies, and draft internal procedures. This stage typically takes 4-6 weeks. You should also conduct a gap analysis against MiCA requirements.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Lithuanian company (UAB) with a registered office in Lithuania. You will need a local director and a physical address. The registration cost is around EUR 2,000 and takes 1-2 weeks.
  3. Capital deposit: Open a bank account in Lithuania and deposit the minimum capital. The bank will verify the source of funds. This step can take 2-4 weeks due to bank due diligence.
  4. Submit application: File the CASP license application with the Bank of Lithuania, including all required documents (business plan, AML policy, risk assessment, organizational structure). The application fee is approximately EUR 1,500.
  5. Review and interview: The regulator reviews the application (3-6 months) and may request additional information or an interview with management. Be prepared to explain your business model and compliance framework.
  6. License grant: Once approved, the license is issued and you can begin operations. You must notify the regulator of any material changes. Ongoing reporting is required annually.

Opening a bank account in Lithuania for a crypto company can be challenging but is feasible. Major Lithuanian banks (e.g., Swedbank, SEB) are open to crypto businesses with a license. Alternatively, EMI (Electronic Money Institution) accounts or payment service providers like PayrNet or TransferWise (now Wise) are available. Consulting24 can introduce you to banking partners.

For fiat on-ramps, you may need to partner with a payment processor. Lithuania's fintech ecosystem is supportive, with many providers experienced in crypto. However, expect thorough due diligence from banks, including source of funds checks and ongoing transaction monitoring. Some crypto businesses opt for EMI accounts to avoid the stricter requirements of traditional banks.

It is advisable to open a bank account before applying for the license, as the capital deposit is required. However, some banks wait for the license to be granted before opening a full account. Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, you must comply with ongoing AML/KYC requirements, submit annual reports, and maintain the minimum capital. The Bank of Lithuania conducts periodic inspections. You must also appoint a local AML officer and ensure all staff are trained. Failure to comply can result in fines or license revocation.

Ongoing costs include annual compliance fees (EUR 5,000-10,000), accounting, and AML officer salary. You must also report suspicious transactions and conduct regular risk assessments. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Insufficient AML/KYC documentation: The Bank of Lithuania is strict on AML procedures. Prepare detailed policies that cover customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and reporting.
  • Underestimating capital: Ensure you have the full EUR 125,000 available and documented. The regulator may ask for proof of source of funds.
  • Ignoring local presence: A physical office and local director are mandatory. Virtual offices are not accepted.
  • Rushing the application: Incomplete applications cause delays. Use an experienced consultant to review all documents.
  • Not planning for ongoing costs: Budget for annual compliance, accounting, and legal fees. Many startups underestimate these.
  • Overlooking tax obligations: Even with a reduced tax rate, you must file timely returns and maintain proper accounting records.
  • Choosing the wrong service class: If you only offer limited services, you may qualify for a lower capital tier (EUR 50,000). Misclassifying can increase costs unnecessarily.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Lithuania crypto license in 2026?

The minimum capital is EUR 125,000 for most services (custody and exchange). For limited services like order execution only, it is EUR 50,000. The capital must be paid up and held in a Lithuanian bank account before the license is granted.

How long does it take to get a Lithuania crypto license?

The typical timeline is 3-6 months from submission of a complete application. Preparation can take an additional 1-2 months. Delays often occur if AML policies are insufficient or capital documentation is incomplete.

Can I offer services across the EU with a Lithuania license?

Yes, the Lithuania CASP license allows passporting across the European Economic Area (EEA). You can offer services in all EU member states plus Iceland, Liechtenstein, and Norway without additional licenses.

What are the ongoing compliance costs after obtaining the license?

Annual compliance costs typically range from EUR 5,000 to EUR 10,000, including AML officer salary, accounting, and reporting fees. You must also submit annual reports to the Bank of Lithuania and maintain the minimum capital.

Is a physical office required in Lithuania?

Yes, you must have a physical registered office in Lithuania. Virtual offices are not accepted. You also need at least one local director with a clean criminal record.

What activities are allowed under the Lithuania CASP license?

The license covers exchange (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto), custody, order execution, placing, receiving and transmitting orders, and advisory services. Activities like staking or lending may require additional authorization.

How does Lithuania's tax regime benefit crypto companies?

Lithuania offers a 15% corporate income tax rate, reduced to 5% for small companies. Crypto-to-crypto transactions are VAT-exempt. There is no withholding tax on dividends to EU residents. This is competitive compared to many EU jurisdictions.

Can I apply for a Lithuania license if my company is based outside the EU?

What are the main differences between Lithuania and Panama for crypto licensing?

Lithuania offers EU passporting and a regulated environment with a capital requirement of EUR 125,000. Panama has no capital requirement, lower cost (EUR 6,000 flat), and faster setup (2-4 weeks), but no EU market access. Choose based on your target market.
