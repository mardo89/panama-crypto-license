What Is a Crypto Token Issuance License in Vietnam?

In Vietnam, there is no single license called a "crypto token issuance license." Instead, token issuers must comply with several legal frameworks depending on the nature of the token. Utility tokens that do not represent equity or debt may fall outside securities laws, but any token that promises profits or is traded on a secondary market could be deemed a security. The SSC has signaled that it will treat security tokens under the Securities Law, requiring a public offering license (if offered to the public) or a private placement registration. Additionally, the SBV prohibits the use of cryptocurrencies as payment instruments, so tokens designed for payments face severe restrictions.

For practical purposes, a crypto token issuance license in Vietnam is a combination of corporate registration, securities registration (if applicable), and compliance with AML/CFT obligations. The government is also piloting a regulatory sandbox for fintech, including token offerings, but the sandbox has limited capacity and is not a permanent licensing route.

Who Needs This License?

Any entity that intends to issue tokens to Vietnamese residents or raise capital from Vietnamese investors may need to comply with Vietnamese regulations. This includes:

  • Startups conducting initial coin offerings (ICOs) or security token offerings (STOs)
  • Foreign projects that market tokens to Vietnamese citizens
  • Blockchain platforms that issue native tokens used within Vietnam
  • Companies that tokenize assets such as real estate or art for Vietnamese investors

However, due to the lack of a clear licensing pathway, many projects choose to issue tokens from a jurisdiction with a defined regime, such as Estonia or Lithuania, and then offer them to Vietnamese investors under cross-border rules. License Type and Regulator

The primary regulators for token issuance in Vietnam are:

  • State Securities Commission (SSC): Oversees securities offerings, including security tokens. If your token is classified as a security, you need a securities offering license or registration.
  • State Bank of Vietnam (SBV): Regulates payment instruments and virtual currencies. Tokens used as payment are effectively banned.
  • Ministry of Finance (MOF): Coordinates with SSC on tax and financial regulations.

As of 2026, there is no specific "crypto license" category. The government has drafted a Digital Asset Law that may introduce a licensing framework, but it has not been enacted. Therefore, the current approach is to either qualify under existing securities laws or operate through the fintech sandbox (Decision No. 316/QD-TTg). Capital Requirement

Vietnam does not have a statutory minimum capital requirement specifically for token issuance. However, for securities offerings, the issuer must have a minimum charter capital of VND 30 billion (approximately USD 1.2 million) for public offerings, as per the Securities Law. For private placements, the capital requirement is lower but still significant. Additionally, the company must maintain sufficient working capital to cover operational expenses for at least 12 months. Foreign investors may face higher capital requirements due to foreign ownership limits in certain sectors. Consulting24 advises on capital structuring to meet regulatory expectations.

Tax Treatment

Vietnam's tax treatment of token issuance is evolving. As of 2026:

  • Corporate Income Tax (CIT): Standard rate is 20%. Proceeds from token sales are generally treated as taxable income. If the token is classified as a security, capital gains from token trading may be subject to CIT.
  • Value Added Tax (VAT): Token issuance may be exempt from VAT if considered a financial service, but this is not settled. A conservative approach is to apply 10% VAT.
  • Personal Income Tax (PIT): Investors who sell tokens for profit may be subject to PIT at 0.1% of the transaction value (for listed securities) or 20% on net gains (for unlisted).

Tax rulings are case-by-case. Consulting24 recommends obtaining a tax ruling from the General Department of Taxation before launching.

Allowed Activities

Under current Vietnamese law, the following activities are permitted with appropriate licenses:

  • Issuance of utility tokens that do not promise profits or function as payment (subject to legal review)
  • Private placement of security tokens to qualified investors (up to 100 investors per offering)
  • Operation of a token exchange platform under a securities trading license (rarely granted)

Prohibited activities include:

  • Issuing tokens that are used as payment instruments (banned by SBV)
  • Public offering of tokens without SSC approval
  • Using tokens to raise funds from the general public without a license

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Token Classification: Engage legal counsel to determine if your token is a security, utility, or payment token.
  2. Company Incorporation: Register a company in Vietnam with appropriate business lines (e.g., "financial services" or "technology").
  3. Securities Registration (if applicable): File with SSC for a public offering license or private placement registration.
  4. AML/CFT Compliance: Implement KYC/AML procedures and register with the State Bank's AML department.
  5. Tax Registration: Obtain a tax code and register for CIT and VAT.
  6. Sandbox Application (optional): Apply to the fintech sandbox if your project qualifies.
  7. Legal Opinion: Obtain a legal opinion confirming compliance with all applicable laws.
  8. Token Issuance: Launch the token sale after all approvals are obtained.

Banking and Payment Integration

Vietnamese banks are generally cautious about crypto-related businesses. Most major banks (e.g., Vietcombank, Techcombank) will not open accounts for token issuers. However, some smaller banks and fintechs offer payment gateway services for token sales. Additionally, international payment processors like Stripe and PayPal may not support Vietnamese crypto businesses. A common workaround is to use a foreign bank account (e.g., in Singapore or Hong Kong) to receive fiat from token sales, then transfer funds to Vietnam.   • AML/CFT reporting to SBV for any suspicious transactions
  • Tax filings quarterly and annually
  • Maintenance of minimum capital requirements (if applicable)
  • Renewal of licenses (typically annual)

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using crypto as payment: Accepting tokens for goods/services can violate SBV rules.
  • Ignoring AML requirements: Even if no license is required, AML compliance is mandatory.
  • Choosing the wrong corporate structure: A Vietnam company may not be the best vehicle for token issuance.

Frequently asked questions

Is there a specific crypto token issuance license in Vietnam?

No, as of 2026 Vietnam has not enacted a comprehensive crypto law. Token issuers must comply with existing securities, banking, and AML regulations. The SSC and SBV are the main regulators, and a securities license may be required if the token is classified as a security.

What is the minimum capital for token issuance in Vietnam?

For public securities offerings, the minimum charter capital is VND 30 billion (about USD 1.2 million). For private placements, no fixed minimum exists, but the company must have sufficient working capital. There is no minimum capital for utility tokens that are not securities.

How long does it take to get approval for token issuance in Vietnam?

The timeline varies: company incorporation (2-4 weeks), securities registration (3-6 months), and AML setup (4-8 weeks). Total process can take 6-12 months. The fintech sandbox may accelerate approval for selected projects.

What taxes apply to token issuance in Vietnam?

Corporate income tax is 20% on proceeds. VAT may apply at 10% unless exempted as a financial service. Investors may pay personal income tax of 0.1% on transaction value (listed securities) or 20% on net gains (unlisted). Tax rulings are recommended.

Can I issue a utility token in Vietnam without a license?

Potentially, if the token does not promise profits, is not used as payment, and is not offered to the public. However, legal review is essential because the definition of a security is broad. Many projects choose to issue from a clearer jurisdiction to avoid risk.

What activities are prohibited under Vietnamese crypto regulations?

Using cryptocurrencies as payment is banned by the SBV. Public token offerings without SSC approval are illegal. Also, issuing tokens that are clearly securities without registration can lead to penalties.

Is Vietnam a good jurisdiction for token issuance compared to Panama?

Vietnam offers a large market but regulatory uncertainty. Panama provides a clear, low-cost license (EUR 6,000 flat) with no capital requirement and 0% tax on foreign income. Many projects choose Panama for issuance and then expand to Vietnam.

What is the fintech sandbox in Vietnam?

The fintech sandbox (Decision 316/QD-TTg) allows selected companies to test innovative financial services, including token offerings, under relaxed regulatory conditions. However, it has limited capacity and is not a permanent licensing solution.
