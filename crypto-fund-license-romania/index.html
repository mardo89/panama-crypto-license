What Is a Crypto Fund License in Romania?

A crypto fund license in Romania is a regulatory authorization issued by the ASF that allows a fund to invest primarily in crypto assets. Under MiCA, which is fully in force in 2026, crypto fund managers must comply with harmonized EU rules for investor protection, custody, and transparency. The license covers both open-ended and closed-ended funds, provided they meet the definition of a collective investment scheme. This means the fund pools capital from multiple investors and invests in a diversified portfolio of crypto assets, such as Bitcoin, Ethereum, or tokenized securities. The license is essential for any fund that wants to operate legally within Romania and market itself across the European Economic Area (EEA).

Key features include:

  • Regulated status under Romanian and EU law
  • Ability to market fund shares across the EEA via passporting
  • Ongoing reporting and compliance obligations, including quarterly and annual filings
  • Minimum capital requirements based on fund type and size, ranging from EUR 50,000 for small AIFMs to EUR 125,000 for full-scope AIFMs

This license is distinct from a CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) license, which is required for exchange and wallet services. A crypto fund license is specifically for fund management activities, such as portfolio management, risk management, and administration of the fund. It does not cover proprietary trading or providing custody to third parties.

Who Needs a Crypto Fund License in Romania?

Any entity that manages a collective investment scheme investing in crypto assets and is based in Romania (or markets to Romanian investors) needs a crypto fund license. This includes a wide range of fund structures, from small venture capital funds to large hedge funds. Specifically, the following types of entities require licensing:

  • Venture capital funds focusing on blockchain startups and early-stage crypto projects
  • Hedge funds trading crypto derivatives, spot assets, and using use
  • Tokenized real estate or asset funds that issue tokens representing ownership in physical assets
  • Funds of crypto funds, which invest in multiple other crypto funds
  • Private equity funds with a crypto focus, such as those acquiring mining operations or staking infrastructure

If you are a fund manager currently operating without a license, or considering Romania as your base, you must apply for ASF approval. Exemptions exist for small funds below EUR 100 million in assets under management (AUM) that meet certain criteria, such as being closed-ended and not having redemption rights for 5 years. However, most professional funds targeting institutional or retail investors require full licensing. License Type and Regulator

The regulator for crypto fund licenses in Romania is the Financial Supervisory Authority (ASF), which oversees all financial markets, including investment funds. The license type is an AIFM (Alternative Investment Fund Manager) license with a crypto-specific scope, as defined under the EU's Alternative Investment Fund Managers Directive (AIFMD) and MiCA. The ASF is the competent authority for approving fund prospectuses, reviewing applications, and supervising ongoing compliance.

There are two main categories:

  • Full-scope AIFM: For funds managing over EUR 100 million in AUM (or EUR 500 million if the fund is closed-ended and no redemption rights for 5 years). This category requires the full EUR 125,000 minimum capital and extensive reporting.
  • Small AIFM: For funds below these thresholds, subject to lighter requirements, including a lower minimum capital of EUR 50,000 and simplified reporting. However, small AIFMs cannot passport their services across the EEA without additional registration.

Both categories require registration with the ASF and compliance with MiCA's operational and capital rules. The ASF also conducts background checks on key personnel, including fund managers and compliance officers. Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital for a crypto fund manager in Romania is EUR 125,000 for full-scope AIFMs and EUR 50,000 for small AIFMs. These figures are set by the ASF and align with the EU capital tiers for CASPs and fund managers. The capital must be held in liquid form, such as cash or cash equivalents (e.g., government bonds), and maintained throughout the fund's life. It cannot be used for operational expenses or investments; it serves as a buffer against losses and a sign of financial stability.

Additional capital may be required based on the fund's risk profile, AUM, and operational expenses. For example, a fund with high use or illiquid assets may need to hold extra capital. The ASF may also require professional indemnity insurance or a bank guarantee as an alternative to part of the capital. Tax Treatment

Romania offers a competitive tax regime for crypto funds. The standard corporate income tax rate is 16%, but certain fund structures may benefit from exemptions or reduced rates. For example, closed-ended investment funds that distribute at least 90% of their profits to investors may be exempt from corporate tax at the fund level, with tax liability passed through to investors. This is similar to the tax treatment of UCITS funds in other EU countries.

Capital gains from crypto assets are taxed as income, but specific rules apply to fund vehicles. For instance, gains from trading crypto assets within the fund are generally taxed at the fund level, but if the fund is structured as a partnership, gains may be attributed to investors. VAT is generally not applicable to fund management services, as they are considered exempt financial services. As of 2026, Romania has not introduced a specific crypto tax regime, so general tax laws apply. Consulting24 recommends consulting a local tax advisor for your specific structure, as tax optimization can significantly impact net returns. For example, a fund investing in long-term holdings may benefit from lower capital gains rates if held for more than one year.

Allowed Activities

A crypto fund license in Romania permits the following activities:

  • Management of collective investment schemes investing in crypto assets, including portfolio construction, rebalancing, and risk management
  • Portfolio management and risk management for the fund, including setting investment limits and monitoring exposure
  • Marketing fund shares to professional and retail investors across the EEA, subject to prospectus approval by the ASF and notification to host member states
  • Custody of crypto assets, either directly or via a third-party custodian that meets MiCA standards (e.g., qualified crypto custodian)
  • Execution of transactions in crypto assets on behalf of the fund, including trading on exchanges or OTC desks
  • Administration services, such as calculating net asset value (NAV), processing subscriptions and redemptions, and maintaining investor registers

The license does not cover proprietary trading for the fund's own account, exchange services for third parties, or wallet services for retail customers. Those require a separate CASP license under MiCA. The fund must operate strictly within the scope defined in its prospectus, and any deviation requires prior ASF approval. For example, if the fund wants to start lending crypto assets, it must amend its prospectus and obtain approval.

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Pre-application preparation: Define fund structure (e.g., open-ended vs. closed-ended), prepare a detailed prospectus, and engage legal counsel with experience in Romanian fund regulation. This step typically takes 4-6 weeks.
  2. Capital deposit: Deposit the minimum capital (EUR 50,000 or 125,000) in a Romanian bank account in the fund's name. The bank will issue a certificate of deposit, which is required for the application.
  3. Submit application to ASF: File the prospectus, fund rules, personal questionnaires for key personnel (including directors and compliance officers), and proof of capital. The ASF may request additional documents, such as AML policies or a business plan.
  4. ASF review: The ASF has up to 3 months to review the application, with a possible extension of 3 months if clarifications are needed. During this period, the ASF may conduct interviews with key personnel or request changes to the prospectus.
  5. License issuance: Upon approval, the fund is registered in the ASF's public register and can begin operations. The license is valid indefinitely, subject to ongoing compliance.
  6. Ongoing compliance: Submit periodic reports (quarterly and annual) to the ASF, maintain capital, and comply with MiCA transparency rules, including disclosure of holdings and risk metrics.

Banking and Payments

Opening a bank account for a crypto fund in Romania can be challenging due to compliance concerns. Many traditional banks are hesitant to serve crypto businesses, even licensed ones, due to perceived money laundering risks. However, some Romanian banks, such as Banca Transilvania and BRD, accept crypto funds with proper licensing and AML procedures. They typically require a copy of the ASF license, the fund's prospectus, and a detailed business plan.

Alternative options include:

  • EMI (Electronic Money Institution) accounts, such as those offered by PayDo or Moneff, which are more crypto-friendly and offer faster onboarding
  • Payment service providers like Revolut Business or Wise, which allow multi-currency accounts and international transfers
  • Specialized crypto-friendly banks in the EU, such as Bank Frick in Liechtenstein or Clear Junction in the UK, which have dedicated crypto divisions

  • Regular reporting on fund performance, holdings, and risk metrics, submitted quarterly and annually
  • Annual audits by an approved auditor, with the audit report filed with the ASF
  • AML/KYC procedures for investors and counterparties, including ongoing monitoring of transactions
  • Maintaining adequate capital and liquidity, with periodic stress tests
  • Disclosure of conflicts of interest and related-party transactions

Common Mistakes

  • Underestimating capital requirements: Ensure you have the full EUR 125,000 (or 50,000) available and unencumbered. Some applicants try to use borrowed funds, which the ASF may reject.
  • Poor prospectus drafting: The prospectus must meet ASF standards, including detailed risk disclosures and investment strategy. Generic templates often get rejected or delayed.
  • Ignoring MiCA compliance: Even after licensing, ongoing reporting is mandatory. Missing deadlines can result in fines or license suspension.
  • Choosing the wrong bank: Some banks refuse crypto funds; research before applying. Applying to multiple banks simultaneously can improve your chances.
  • Not engaging local experts: Romanian language and legal nuances require local support. Using a translator for legal documents can lead to errors.
  • Overlooking AML requirements: The ASF expects strong AML policies, including transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting. Failure to implement these can delay approval.

Alternatives and Comparison

Romania is one of several EU jurisdictions for crypto fund licensing. Key alternatives include:

  • Panama: A non-EU jurisdiction with a flat EUR 6,000 company setup cost, no capital requirement for funds, and no specific crypto fund license. Suitable for funds targeting non-EU investors. Panama offers flexibility and speed (2-4 weeks for incorporation) but lacks EU passporting and regulatory oversight, which may deter institutional investors.
  • Lithuania: Offers a CASP license under MiCA, but fund licensing is through the Bank of Lithuania. Capital requirement EUR 125,000, similar to Romania, but timeline can be longer (6-12 months) and costs higher due to mandatory local presence requirements. Lithuania crypto license is better for exchange services than funds.
  • Malta: Established hub for crypto funds with a dedicated Virtual Financial Assets Act. Capital requirements start at EUR 125,000, but costs are higher (EUR 30,000+ in legal fees) and timeline 6-12 months. Malta offers strong reputation but is more expensive.
  • Estonia: Previously a popular jurisdiction, but now requires a full CASP license under MiCA with EUR 125,000 capital. Estonia's e-residency program is attractive, but the timeline is similar to Romania. Estonia crypto license is an option for funds that also offer exchange services.

For EU-focused funds, Romania is often the most cost-effective and fastest option. For global funds with non-EU investors, Panama may be simpler. Consulting24 advises on the best jurisdiction for your needs, considering your target market, investor base, and budget.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto fund license in Romania?

The minimum capital is EUR 125,000 for full-scope AIFMs (managing over EUR 100 million AUM) and EUR 50,000 for small AIFMs. Capital must be held in liquid form and paid up before the ASF approves the license.

How long does it take to get a crypto fund license in Romania?

The typical timeline is 4 to 8 months from application to license issuance. This includes 1-2 months for ASF review and 2-4 months for legal and compliance setup. Complex funds may take up to 8 months.

Can I market my Romanian crypto fund to investors in other EU countries?

Yes, a full-scope AIFM license allows passporting across the EEA. You can market fund shares to professional and retail investors in other EU member states by notifying the local regulator, without needing additional licensing.

What activities are not covered by a crypto fund license in Romania?

The license does not cover proprietary trading, exchange services, wallet services for third parties, or providing custody to non-fund clients. Those activities require a separate CASP license under MiCA.

Are there tax exemptions for crypto funds in Romania?

Yes, closed-ended investment funds that distribute at least 90% of profits to investors may be exempt from corporate tax. Capital gains are taxed as income, but specific rules apply. Consult a local tax advisor for your structure.

What are the main compliance obligations after obtaining the license?

Ongoing obligations include quarterly and annual reporting to the ASF, annual audits, AML/KYC procedures, maintaining minimum capital, and disclosing conflicts of interest. Non-compliance can result in fines or license revocation.

Can I use a Romanian crypto fund license to operate a hedge fund?

Yes, hedge funds trading crypto assets are eligible for licensing. The fund must specify its investment strategy in the prospectus, including use of use, derivatives, and short selling, subject to ASF approval.

What happens if I fail to maintain the minimum capital?

The ASF may suspend or revoke the license if capital falls below the minimum. You must restore capital within a specified period or face penalties. Consulting24 advises on capital management to avoid this risk.

Is a local presence required in Romania for the fund?

Yes, the fund must have a registered office in Romania and at least one director or manager resident in the EU. The ASF may also require a local compliance officer. Consulting24 can help with local setup.

How does Romania compare to Panama for crypto fund licensing?

Romania offers EU passporting and regulatory clarity under MiCA, with capital requirements and a 4-8 month timeline. Panama has no specific crypto fund license, no capital requirement, and a flat EUR 6,000 setup cost, but lacks EU market access. Panama is better for non-EU investors.

What documents are needed for the ASF application?
