What Is a Crypto OTC Desk License in Luxembourg?

A crypto OTC desk license in Luxembourg is a regulatory authorization that permits a company to operate an over-the-counter trading desk for crypto assets. Unlike exchange-based trading, OTC desks facilitate direct trades between counterparties, often for large volumes. Under MiCA, this activity falls under the category of "execution of orders on behalf of clients" or "dealing on own account," depending on the business model. The CSSF is the competent authority responsible for licensing and supervision.

This license is essential for firms that want to offer crypto OTC services to institutional clients, high-net-worth individuals, or other businesses. It demonstrates compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) regulations, capital adequacy, and operational standards. Luxembourg's regulatory environment is particularly attractive due to its stability, EU passporting rights, and reputation as a global financial center. For example, a firm executing large block trades for pension funds would use this license to ensure regulatory compliance and client trust.

  • Banks and investment firms diversifying into crypto

License Type and Regulator

The crypto OTC desk license in Luxembourg is a type of Crypto-Asset Service Provider (CASP) authorization under MiCA. The specific service classification is typically "execution of orders on behalf of clients" (Article 3(1)(9) of MiCA) or "dealing on own account" (Article 3(1)(10)). The regulator is the Commission de Surveillance du Secteur Financier (CSSF), which oversees all financial services in Luxembourg.

The CSSF has implemented MiCA requirements, including fit and proper assessments of management, operational resilience, and investor protection measures. The license is passportable across the EU, meaning once obtained, you can serve clients in other member states without additional licensing. This makes Luxembourg an excellent base for pan-European OTC operations. The CSSF also requires firms to have a local presence, including a registered office and at least one director resident in Luxembourg.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for CASPs depends on the type of services provided. For crypto OTC desks, the relevant tiers are:

  • EUR 50,000 for execution of orders on behalf of clients (if no custody of client funds)
  • EUR 125,000 for dealing on own account
  • EUR 150,000 for combined services including custody

These are minimums; the CSSF may require higher capital based on the volume and risk profile. Capital must be held in liquid assets and is subject to ongoing monitoring. Tax Treatment

Luxembourg offers a favorable tax regime for crypto OTC desks. Corporate income tax is generally 24.94% (including solidarity surcharge). However, specific tax treatment depends on the nature of income (trading gains, fees, etc.). Crypto-to-crypto trades may be subject to VAT exemption under EU VAT rules for financial services. Luxembourg has also introduced a specific tax framework for crypto assets, treating them as intangible assets for corporate tax purposes.

It is essential to obtain local tax advice to optimize your structure. Consulting24 coordinates with local tax experts to ensure compliance. Note that tax laws are subject to change; always consult a professional. For example, trading gains from crypto assets held for less than six months may be treated as ordinary income, while longer-term holdings could qualify for capital gains treatment.

Allowed Activities

The crypto OTC desk license in Luxembourg permits the following activities under MiCA:

  • Execution of orders for crypto assets on behalf of clients (matching buyers and sellers)
  • Dealing on own account (trading as principal)
  • Reception and transmission of orders (if ancillary)
  • Providing custody of crypto assets (if included in the license scope)

Activities not covered include operating a crypto exchange (order book matching), which requires a separate license. Additionally, the license does not permit offering derivatives or securities unless the firm holds a relevant MiFID license. The CSSF may impose conditions on the scope of activities. For example, a firm licensed for execution of orders cannot also operate a custody service without amending its license.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining a crypto OTC desk license in Luxembourg involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step:

  1. Pre-assessment: Evaluate your business model, identify the correct service classification, and assess readiness.
  2. Company incorporation: Set up a Luxembourg company (SARL or SA) with a registered office and local management.
  3. Documentation: Prepare a business plan, AML/CFT policies, risk management framework, and operational procedures.
  4. Fit and proper checks: Submit personal questionnaires for directors and shareholders.
  5. Capital verification: Deposit the required capital in a Luxembourg bank account.
  6. Application submission: File the application with CSSF including all supporting documents.
  7. CSSF review: The regulator may request additional information or clarifications.
  8. Approval: Once satisfied, the CSSF grants the license. The entire process typically takes 4-8 months.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a crypto OTC desk license and a crypto exchange license in Luxembourg?

An OTC desk license allows off-exchange matching of buyers and sellers, typically for large volumes, while an exchange license permits order-book trading. Under MiCA, these are separate service classifications. The OTC desk license is suitable for institutional block trades, whereas an exchange license is for retail or continuous trading.

How long does it take to get a crypto OTC desk license in Luxembourg?

The process typically takes 4 to 8 months from application to approval, depending on the completeness of documentation and CSSF review. Pre-incorporation and document preparation can add 1-2 months. Consulting24 can help streamline the process.

What is the minimum capital required for a crypto OTC desk license in Luxembourg?

Under MiCA, the minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for execution of orders (no custody), EUR 125,000 for dealing on own account, and EUR 150,000 for combined services including custody. The CSSF may require higher capital based on risk profile.

Can I passport the Luxembourg crypto OTC desk license to other EU countries?

Yes, once licensed by the CSSF, you can provide services across the EU under the MiCA passporting regime without additional licensing. This is a key advantage of Luxembourg.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations after obtaining the license?

Obligations include annual AML audits, capital adequacy monitoring, transaction reporting to the CSSF, financial statement submissions, and fit and proper updates for management changes. Non-compliance can lead to fines or revocation.

Is it possible to apply for a crypto OTC desk license without a local presence in Luxembourg?

No, you must have a registered office in Luxembourg and at least one director resident in the country. The CSSF requires a local physical presence for supervision purposes.

What activities are not allowed under a crypto OTC desk license?

Operating a crypto exchange (order book matching), offering derivatives or securities (requires MiFID license), and providing custody without authorization are not permitted. The license scope is limited to OTC trading activities.

How does the tax treatment differ for crypto OTC desks in Luxembourg compared to Panama?

Luxembourg has a corporate tax rate of 24.94% and VAT exemptions for financial services, while Panama has no corporate tax on offshore income and no capital gains tax. However, Panama lacks EU passporting and regulatory credibility. The choice depends on your target market.

Are there any restrictions on the types of crypto assets that can be traded under this license?

Under MiCA, only crypto assets that qualify as "crypto-assets" (not securities or e-money) can be traded. Stablecoins and utility tokens are generally permitted, but security tokens may require a MiFID license. The CSSF provides guidance on asset classification.
