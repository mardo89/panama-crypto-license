What is the Crypto Fund License Kazakhstan?

The crypto fund license Kazakhstan is a regulatory authorization issued by AFSA that allows a legal entity to operate a collective investment scheme (CIS) investing primarily in crypto assets. This license is part of the Astana International Financial Centre (AIFC) framework, which operates on English common law principles. The license covers both open-ended and closed-ended funds, and it is designed to attract international fund managers.

Key characteristics include:

  • Regulated by AFSA under the AIFC Collective Investment Schemes Rules.
  • Available for both retail and professional investor funds.
  • Requires a licensed fund manager (often a separate entity).
  • Assets must be held with a qualified custodian.

The license is distinct from a general crypto exchange or payment license. It focuses on pooled investment vehicles, making it ideal for fund managers seeking to raise capital from investors for crypto strategies. For example, a manager launching a Bitcoin-focused hedge fund would need this license to accept investor funds and trade on behalf of the fund. The AIFC regime also allows for umbrella funds with multiple sub-funds, each with its own investment strategy.

The crypto fund license Kazakhstan is essential for any entity that wishes to manage a collective investment scheme investing in digital assets within or from the AIFC. This includes:

  • Venture capital funds investing in blockchain startups.

  • Tokenized real estate or asset funds.
  • Index funds tracking crypto benchmarks.

If you are a fund manager currently operating in a less regulated environment, or if you are launching a new fund, obtaining a license in Kazakhstan can provide credibility and access to international investors. The license also allows the fund to be marketed to professional investors in the AIFC and, through passporting, to other financial centres. For example, a manager with an existing fund in the Cayman Islands might consider Kazakhstan for a new crypto fund to benefit from the tax incentives and regulatory clarity. License Type & Regulator

The crypto fund license Kazakhstan is issued by the Astana Financial Services Authority (AFSA), the independent regulator of the AIFC. AFSA oversees all financial services activities within the AIFC, including fund management, custody, and investment services.

There are two main categories for crypto funds:

  • Recognised Fund: For funds that are already regulated in another jurisdiction and seek recognition in the AIFC.
  • Authorised Fund: For new funds established within the AIFC that require full authorisation.

The applicable rulebook is the AIFC Collective Investment Schemes Rules, which align with international standards such as IOSCO principles. The license is not a MiFID passport but is recognised by several other financial centres. AFSA also requires that the fund manager, if separate, holds a license under the AIFC Asset Management Rules. This dual-license structure ensures that both the fund and its manager meet regulatory standards.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a crypto fund license Kazakhstan depends on the type of fund and whether it is marketed to retail or professional investors. As of 2026, the following thresholds apply:

  • Retail fund: Minimum capital of USD 125,000 (or equivalent in KZT).
  • Professional investor fund: Minimum capital of USD 50,000.
  • Fund manager (separate entity): Minimum capital of USD 50,000.

Capital must be held in liquid assets and maintained at all times. It can be in the form of cash or cash equivalents. The capital requirement is lower than in many EU jurisdictions (which under MiCA require EUR 125,000-150,000 for similar activities), making Kazakhstan attractive for smaller fund managers. For example, a professional fund with USD 50,000 capital can start operations, whereas in Lithuania under MiCA the same activity would require at least EUR 125,000. This difference can be a deciding factor for early-stage fund managers.

Tax Treatment

The AIFC offers a favourable tax regime for licensed entities. Key tax benefits include:

  • Corporate income tax: 0% on income derived from AIFC activities for the first 10 years (subject to extension).
  • Withholding tax: 0% on dividends and interest paid to non-residents.
  • VAT: 0% on most financial services.
  • Capital gains: Exempt for gains on disposal of investments.

These incentives make Kazakhstan one of the most tax-efficient jurisdictions for crypto funds globally. However, fund managers should seek local tax advice to ensure compliance with their home country tax obligations. For example, a US-based manager may still be subject to US tax on global income, but the Kazakhstan entity itself pays no local tax. This structure can be combined with tax treaties to optimize overall tax liability.

Allowed Activities

A crypto fund license Kazakhstan permits the following activities:

  • Establishing and managing collective investment schemes that invest in crypto assets.
  • Accepting contributions from investors in fiat or crypto.
  • Investing in a diversified portfolio of digital assets, including tokens, stablecoins, and crypto derivatives.
  • Engaging in staking, lending, and yield farming as part of the fund's strategy.
  • Issuing fund units or tokens representing ownership.

The license does not cover direct exchange services (buying/selling crypto for fiat) or custody services for third parties, which require separate licenses. However, the fund can use third-party exchanges and custodians. For example, a fund might delegate trading to a licensed exchange in the AIFC and use a qualified custodian for asset safekeeping. The fund's prospectus must clearly outline the permitted investment strategies and risk factors.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process for obtaining a crypto fund license Kazakhstan involves the following steps:

  1. Pre-application consultation: Engage with Consulting24 to review your fund structure and determine the appropriate license category.
  2. Incorporation: Register a legal entity in the AIFC (typically a private company limited by shares).
  3. Appoint service providers: Engage a licensed fund administrator, custodian, and auditor.
  4. Prepare application documents: Including a business plan, fund prospectus, compliance manual, and AML policies.
  5. Submit to AFSA: File the application along with the fee. AFSA will conduct a fit and proper assessment of directors and shareholders.
  6. Review and approval: AFSA may request additional information. The process typically takes 3-6 months.
  7. Post-license compliance: Ongoing reporting, audits, and capital maintenance.

Compliance & Ongoing Obligations

Holding a crypto fund license Kazakhstan requires ongoing compliance. Key obligations include:

  • Annual audit: By an AFSA-approved auditor.
  • Regular reporting: Monthly, quarterly, and annual reports to AFSA on fund performance, NAV, and investor composition.
  • AML/CFT compliance: Implement and maintain AML policies, conduct customer due diligence, and report suspicious transactions.
  • Capital maintenance: Ensure minimum capital is always met.
  • Fit and proper: Directors and senior management must remain fit and proper.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto fund license in Kazakhstan?

The minimum capital is USD 50,000 for professional investor funds and USD 125,000 for retail funds. If a separate fund manager is required, that entity also needs USD 50,000 capital. Capital must be held in liquid assets.

How long does it take to get a crypto fund license in Kazakhstan?

The typical timeline is 3 to 6 months from application to issuance, depending on the complexity of the fund and the completeness of documentation. Pre-application preparation can add 4-8 weeks.

What taxes apply to a crypto fund in Kazakhstan?

Licensed AIFC entities enjoy 0% corporate income tax for the first 10 years, 0% withholding tax on dividends and interest to non-residents, and 0% VAT on financial services. Capital gains are also exempt.

Can I manage a crypto fund from outside Kazakhstan?

Yes, the fund manager can be located outside Kazakhstan, but the fund itself must be established in the AIFC. The manager may need to be licensed if it provides services from within the AIFC. Consulting24 can advise on the optimal structure.

What activities are allowed under the crypto fund license?

The license allows managing collective investment schemes investing in crypto assets, accepting investor contributions, trading digital assets, staking, lending, and issuing fund units. Direct exchange or third-party custody services require separate licenses.

Is the Kazakhstan crypto fund license recognized internationally?

The AIFC is recognized by several financial centres and is a member of IAIS. However, it does not provide an EU passport. Many institutional investors accept AIFC regulation as equivalent to other common law jurisdictions.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

Ongoing obligations include annual audits, monthly/quarterly reporting to AFSA, AML/CFT compliance, capital maintenance, and fit and proper assessments for directors. Non-compliance can lead to fines or license revocation.

Can I convert an existing offshore fund to a Kazakhstan license?

Yes, you can apply for a Recognised Fund license if your fund is already regulated in another jurisdiction. This involves a lighter application process. Consulting24 can assist with the conversion.

How does Kazakhstan compare to Panama for crypto funds?

Panama offers a simpler company setup at EUR 6,000 flat with no specific crypto fund license, but it lacks regulatory clarity for crypto. Kazakhstan provides a dedicated framework with tax benefits, higher credibility, and a longer timeline. The choice depends on your investor base and regulatory needs.

What documents are needed for the application?

Key documents include a business plan, fund prospectus, compliance manual, AML policies, financial projections, and fit and proper declarations for directors and shareholders. Consulting24 helps prepare these documents.
