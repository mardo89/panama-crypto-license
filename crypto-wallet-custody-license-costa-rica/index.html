What Is the Crypto Wallet Custody License in Costa Rica?

The crypto wallet custody license in Costa Rica is a regulatory authorization that allows companies to hold, store, and manage digital assets on behalf of clients. It is designed for businesses that provide custodial wallet services, including private key management and transaction facilitation. The license is issued under the framework of the Ley Reguladora del Mercado de Valores (Securities Market Law) and related fintech regulations, with oversight by SUGEVAL and the Banco Central de Costa Rica (BCCR).

This license is distinct from a general crypto exchange license, as it focuses specifically on custody activities. It requires strong security protocols, anti-money laundering (AML) procedures, and capital reserves. The regulatory intent is to protect consumers and ensure the integrity of digital asset markets. As of 2026, Costa Rica has not adopted MiCA-style rules, but its framework is considered progressive in Latin America. The license is often referred to as a "custodian of virtual assets" authorization and is required for any business holding digital assets on behalf of third parties.

Who Needs This License?

Any business offering custodial digital asset services to Costa Rican residents or operating from Costa Rica must obtain this license. Typical applicants include:

  • Cryptocurrency wallet providers (hot and cold storage)
  • Custodians for institutional investors
  • Platforms offering staking or lending of digital assets
  • Exchanges that hold client funds in custody
  • Decentralized finance (DeFi) protocols with a custodial component

If your business involves holding private keys or managing digital assets on behalf of others, this license is mandatory. Non-compliance can result in fines, operational shutdown, or even criminal liability. Consulting24 advises on whether your specific business model falls under the licensing requirement. For example, a non-custodial wallet provider that does not control private keys may not need the license, but any service that controls client assets likely does.

License Type & Regulator

The crypto wallet custody license in Costa Rica is categorized as a financial technology service under the purview of SUGEVAL and the BCCR. It is not a blanket "crypto license" but a specific authorization for custody activities. The regulator requires applicants to demonstrate technical competence, financial stability, and compliance with AML/CFT standards.

Key regulatory bodies include:

  • SUGEVAL: Primary securities regulator, oversees custody of digital assets classified as securities.
  • BCCR: Central bank, monitors monetary implications and may require reporting.
  • UIF: Financial Intelligence Unit, for AML compliance.

The license type is typically a non-bank financial intermediary authorization, with specific conditions for crypto custody. The regulator may also require a separate registration for any securities-related activities. Consulting24 coordinates with local legal experts to ensure proper classification.

Capital Requirement

Costa Rica imposes a minimum capital requirement for crypto custody license holders. As of 2026, the requirement is generally USD 50,000 for basic custody services, but may increase to USD 200,000 if the company also offers exchange or lending services. Capital must be maintained in a local bank account and can be in fiat or approved stablecoins.

The capital serves as a buffer against operational risks and is subject to annual audit. Consulting24 advises on structuring capital to meet regulatory expectations without overcapitalizing. For instance, a pure custody service may only need the lower tier, while a platform combining custody with exchange features will require the higher amount. The capital must be fully paid up before application submission.

Tax Treatment

Costa Rica does not have a specific crypto tax law, but general tax principles apply. Corporate income tax is 30% on net profits. Capital gains from crypto are treated as ordinary income. VAT (13%) applies to services provided to Costa Rican residents, though cross-border services may be exempt.

Cryptocurrency held as inventory for trading is subject to standard taxation. Custody fees are taxable income. Transfer pricing rules apply for related-party transactions. Consulting24 recommends engaging a local tax advisor to optimize your structure. For example, using a holding company in a lower-tax jurisdiction may reduce overall tax burden, but must comply with substance requirements.

Allowed Activities

The license permits the following activities:

  • Custody and safekeeping of digital assets
  • Private key management
  • Transaction execution on behalf of clients
  • Staking and lending (if explicitly authorized)
  • Conversion between crypto and fiat (requires additional authorization)

Prohibited activities include unlicensed exchange operations, offering securities without registration, and commingling client funds with operational funds. The license does not cover mining or non-custodial software development. If you plan to offer exchange services, you may need a separate license under the Ley Reguladora del Mercado de Valores. Step-by-Step Application Process

The process for obtaining the license involves several stages:

  1. Company incorporation: Register a Costa Rican corporation (Sociedad Anónima) with local directors and registered address.
  2. Document preparation: Draft AML policies, business plan, security protocols, and compliance manual.
  3. Capital deposit: Deposit minimum capital in a local bank and obtain proof.
  4. Application submission: File with SUGEVAL and BCCR, including all supporting documents.
  5. Regulatory review: Regulators conduct background checks and may request additional information.
  6. Approval & licensing: Once approved, pay final fees and receive license certificate.
  7. Ongoing compliance: Submit periodic reports and undergo annual audits.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto wallet custody license in Costa Rica?

The minimum capital requirement is typically USD 50,000 for basic custody services, but can go up to USD 200,000 if additional activities like exchange or lending are included. The capital must be deposited in a local bank account before application.

How long does it take to get a crypto custody license in Costa Rica?

The timeline is typically 4 to 8 months from application submission to approval. Delays can occur if documents are incomplete or if regulators request additional information. Consulting24 helps expedite the process by ensuring all requirements are met upfront.

Can I use a Costa Rica license to serve clients in other countries?

The license is primarily for serving Costa Rican residents or operating from Costa Rica. However, it may be accepted by some international partners as a sign of compliance. For cross-border services, you may need additional registrations in target jurisdictions.

Does Costa Rica require a local director for the license?

Yes, at least one director must be a Costa Rican resident. This director can be a local nominee provided by your incorporation agent. The director is responsible for regulatory communications and compliance oversight.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

Licensed entities must submit annual AML reports, undergo independent audits, and maintain transaction monitoring systems. They must also report suspicious transactions to the UIF. Failure to comply can result in fines or license revocation.

Can I apply for the license if I am a foreign company?

Yes, foreign companies can apply by incorporating a Costa Rican subsidiary. The subsidiary must meet all local requirements, including having a local director and registered address. Consulting24 can assist with the incorporation process.

Is the Costa Rica license recognized under MiCA?

No, Costa Rica is not an EU member and its license does not provide EU passporting. However, it is recognized in Latin America and may be used as a basis for compliance with FATF recommendations.

What activities are prohibited under the custody license?

The license does not cover unlicensed exchange operations, offering securities without registration, or commingling client funds with operational funds. Mining and non-custodial software development are also not covered.

How much does the application cost in total?

Total costs typically range from USD 15,000 to USD 30,000, including government fees, legal setup, and compliance documentation. Exact figures depend on your business scope and the complexity of your application.

Can I switch from a Panama company to a Costa Rica license later?

Yes, you can transition by incorporating a Costa Rican entity and applying for the license. However, the process requires a new application and capital deposit. Consulting24 can help you plan the transition smoothly.
