What Is a Crypto Gambling License in Gibraltar?

A crypto gambling license in Gibraltar is a regulatory authorization that permits an operator to offer gambling services where customers can deposit, wager, and withdraw using cryptocurrencies such as Bitcoin, Ethereum, and stablecoins. The license is issued by the Gibraltar Gambling Commissioner under the Gibraltar Gambling Act 2005 (as amended). It covers both traditional online gambling and innovative blockchain-based gaming products.

The license requires the operator to be a company registered in Gibraltar or a branch of an overseas company. The regulator assesses the applicant's integrity, financial standing, technical systems, and anti-money laundering (AML) controls. Once licensed, operators must comply with ongoing reporting, auditing, and player protection obligations.

Gibraltar is particularly attractive for crypto gambling because of its pragmatic approach to digital assets. The Gibraltar Financial Services Commission (GFSC) also regulates distributed ledger technology (DLT) providers, creating a coherent ecosystem for crypto businesses. However, the gambling license itself is separate from any DLT authorization.

Who Needs a Crypto Gambling License in Gibraltar?

Any business that offers gambling services to customers where cryptocurrency is accepted as a payment method or where the outcome is determined by blockchain technology should consider a Gibraltar license. This includes:

  • Online casinos accepting crypto deposits and payouts
  • Sports betting platforms using digital currencies
  • Poker rooms with crypto wallets
  • Lottery and bingo sites with crypto integration
  • Fantasy sports and esports betting operators
  • Blockchain-based gaming platforms (e.g., provably fair games)

If your target market includes UK, European, or international players, Gibraltar provides a respected license that facilitates banking relationships and player trust. Operators who already hold a traditional Gibraltar gambling license but wish to add crypto functionality may also need to apply for a variation or new license, depending on the nature of the crypto integration.

License Type & Regulator

The Gibraltar Gambling Commissioner is the sole regulatory authority for all gambling activities in Gibraltar. The license types available include:

  • Remote Gambling License: For online casino, poker, sports betting, bingo, and other games. This is the standard license for crypto gambling.
  • Betting Intermediary License: For operators who facilitate betting between customers (e.g., betting exchanges).
  • Hosting License: For companies that provide gambling platforms to other licensees.

For most crypto gambling operators, the Remote Gambling License is the relevant category. The regulator does not issue a separate “crypto” license; instead, the license covers all forms of remote gambling, and the acceptance of cryptocurrency is a permitted payment method subject to AML compliance.

The Gibraltar Gambling Commissioner works closely with the Gibraltar Financial Services Commission (GFSC) on AML and financial crime matters. Operators must also register with the GFSC for DLT activities if they issue tokens or operate a blockchain-based platform beyond simple payment processing.

Capital Requirement

Gibraltar does not prescribe a fixed minimum capital requirement for gambling operators in the same way as some other jurisdictions. Instead, the regulator requires applicants to demonstrate adequate financial resources to run the business and meet player liabilities. As a rule of thumb, the Gibraltar Gambling Commissioner expects operators to maintain sufficient working capital, typically interpreted as at least EUR 100,000 to EUR 500,000 depending on the scale of operations.

This capital can be in the form of cash, bank guarantees, or other liquid assets. The exact amount is determined on a case-by-case basis during the licensing process. For crypto gambling, additional capital may be required to cover volatility risks and to ensure player funds are protected. Tax Treatment

Gibraltar offers a highly favorable tax regime for gambling operators. The key points for 2026 are:

  • Corporate tax rate: 10% on profits derived from Gibraltar (standard rate). However, gambling operators may be subject to a gross profits tax (GPT) instead, which is typically 1% to 2% of gross gaming revenue (GGR), depending on the license type and negotiation with the regulator.
  • No withholding tax on dividends or interest paid to non-residents.
  • No capital gains tax on the sale of shares in a Gibraltar company.
  • No VAT on gambling services (exempt).
  • No stamp duty on share transfers.

For crypto gambling, the tax treatment of cryptocurrency transactions must be considered separately. Gibraltar does not impose a specific crypto tax, but profits from crypto trading or mining may be subject to corporate tax. Operators should seek tailored tax advice.

Allowed Activities

A Gibraltar remote gambling license permits the following activities when using cryptocurrency:

  • Online casino games (slots, table games, live dealer)
  • Sports betting (pre-match and in-play)
  • Poker rooms and tournaments
  • Bingo and lottery
  • Virtual sports and esports betting
  • Peer-to-peer betting exchanges
  • Fantasy sports
  • Provably fair blockchain games

The license does not automatically cover the issuance of utility tokens or security tokens; separate authorization from the GFSC may be required for token offerings. Additionally, operators must ensure that their games comply with Gibraltar's technical standards and fair play requirements.

Banking & Payments

Gibraltar has a well-developed banking sector, but crypto gambling operators may face challenges opening accounts due to the perceived risk. Many traditional banks are cautious about crypto-related businesses. However, Gibraltar-based banks and some international banks with a presence in Gibraltar are more open to licensed operators.

Payment processing for crypto gambling typically involves:

  • Direct cryptocurrency wallets (hot and cold storage)
  • Integration with payment gateways that support crypto-to-fiat conversion
  • Use of stablecoins to reduce volatility
  • Partnerships with licensed crypto payment processors

Operators must implement strong AML procedures, including transaction monitoring and source of funds checks. The Gibraltar Gambling Commissioner expects operators to segregate player funds from operational accounts.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital required for a crypto gambling license in Gibraltar?

Gibraltar does not have a fixed minimum capital requirement. The regulator expects operators to have sufficient financial resources to cover player liabilities and operational costs. Typically, this is interpreted as EUR 100,000 to EUR 500,000, but the exact amount is determined on a case-by-case basis.

How long does it take to get a Gibraltar crypto gambling license?

The application process usually takes 6 to 12 months. The timeline depends on the completeness of your application, the complexity of your operations, and the regulator's workload. Consulting24 can help streamline the process.

Can I accept cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin and Ethereum under a Gibraltar gambling license?

Yes, a Gibraltar remote gambling license allows you to accept cryptocurrencies as a payment method. You must implement strong AML/KYC procedures and ensure compliance with the regulator's guidelines on digital assets.

What taxes will I pay on my crypto gambling profits in Gibraltar?

Gibraltar imposes a gross profits tax (GPT) of 1% to 2% on gambling revenue, rather than corporate income tax. There is no VAT on gambling services, no withholding tax on dividends, and no capital gains tax on share sales.

Do I need a separate DLT license for blockchain-based gambling?

If your platform involves issuing tokens or operates a distributed ledger beyond simple payment processing, you may need to register with the Gibraltar Financial Services Commission (GFSC) as a DLT provider. However, the gambling license itself covers the gambling activities.

Is Gibraltar part of the EU for licensing purposes?

Gibraltar is a British Overseas Territory and is not part of the European Union. However, it has close ties with the EU and follows many EU standards, including AML and data protection. Its license is well-regarded internationally.

Can I operate in the UK with a Gibraltar gambling license?

No, a Gibraltar license does not automatically permit you to target UK players. The UK Gambling Commission requires operators to hold a UK license if they offer services to British residents. Gibraltar license is primarily for international markets.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

Licensed operators must submit annual audited financial statements, technical audits, and AML reports. They must also maintain player fund segregation, responsible gambling tools, and cooperate with the regulator on any investigations.

How much does a Gibraltar crypto gambling license cost in total?

The total initial cost, including application fees, legal fees, company formation, and technical audits, typically ranges from EUR 80,000 to EUR 175,000. Annual fees are EUR 30,000 to EUR 60,000. Exact pricing depends on your specific case.
