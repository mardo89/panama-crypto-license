What Is the Crypto Wallet Custody License Bahrain?

The crypto wallet custody license in Bahrain is a regulatory authorization issued by the Central Bank of Bahrain (CBB) under the Crypto Asset Module (CRA). It permits a company to provide custodial wallet services, meaning the safekeeping and administration of clients' crypto assets, including the management of private keys. This license is part of Bahrain's broader fintech-friendly framework, which also includes a crypto exchange license and other crypto-asset services.

Key features of the license include:

  • Full regulation by the CBB, ensuring compliance with AML/CFT standards.
  • Requirement to maintain a physical presence in Bahrain (office, staff, and directors).
  • Capital requirements that vary based on the scope of services (see below).
  • Annual reporting and audit obligations.

This license is distinct from a simple registration; it is a full regulatory license with ongoing supervision. For businesses that hold client funds or keys, this is the appropriate authorization.

Who Needs the Crypto Wallet Custody License?

Any business that intends to offer custodial wallet services for cryptocurrencies in or from Bahrain must obtain this license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges that hold client assets in omnibus or segregated wallets.
  • Independent custody providers offering cold storage solutions.
  • Asset managers or funds that custody crypto on behalf of investors.
  • Fintech firms integrating wallet services into their platforms.

If your business involves holding private keys for third parties, you likely fall under the CBB's definition of custodial wallet services. Even if you operate remotely, if you target Bahraini clients or have a Bahrain office, the license is mandatory. For comparison, in Lithuania, the crypto custody activity is part of the broader CASP license under the new MiCA implementation, but Bahrain offers a standalone custody license with its own advantages.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Central Bank of Bahrain (CBB), specifically its FinTech & Innovation Unit. The license type is a Category 2 Crypto-Asset License under the CRA module, tailored for custodial wallet services. The CBB has been a pioneer in the region, launching the first comprehensive crypto-asset regulations in the Middle East in 2019 and updating them regularly.

The CRA module covers:

  • Category 1: Crypto-asset exchange services (trading platforms).
  • Category 2: Custodial wallet services (this license).
  • Category 3: Other crypto-asset services (advisory, etc.).

Each category has specific capital and operational requirements. The CBB is known for its rigorous but fair approach, requiring fit and proper tests for directors, a detailed business plan, and strong AML/CFT policies. Consulting24 coordinates with local partners to streamline the application.

Capital Requirement

The CBB imposes a minimum capital requirement for custodial wallet license holders. As of 2026, the requirement is BHD 500,000 (approximately USD 1.33 million) for Category 2 licenses. This capital must be maintained at all times and can be in the form of cash or liquid assets.

This is a significant commitment, but it reflects the CBB's focus on financial stability and client protection. For comparison, under MiCA in the EU, the capital requirement for custody services is EUR 125,000 (tier 2), which is substantially lower. However, Bahrain's capital requirement can be seen as a barrier to entry that also signals trustworthiness to clients.

Consulting24 helps structure the capital injection, whether through shareholder loans or equity. Note that the capital must be held in a Bahraini bank account and cannot be used for operational expenses without CBB approval.

Tax Treatment

Bahrain is known for its tax-friendly environment. There is no corporate income tax for most businesses, including crypto custodians. There is also no value-added tax (VAT) on most services. The only tax that may apply is the 5% VAT on certain imported goods or specific services, but custodial fees are generally VAT-exempt.

However, the CBB requires that all licensees maintain proper accounting records and file annual audited financial statements. There is no capital gains tax in Bahrain, making it attractive for crypto businesses. For international founders, Bahrain does not impose withholding taxes on dividends or interest paid to non-residents.

This tax regime is a key advantage over jurisdictions like Lithuania, where a 15% corporate tax applies, or Poland, which has a 19% CIT. Consulting24 can advise on the tax implications for your specific structure.

Allowed Activities

The crypto wallet custody license in Bahrain permits the following activities:

  • Safekeeping and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients.
  • Management of private keys, including generation, storage, and recovery.
  • Facilitating the transfer of crypto assets between wallets (but not execution of trades on an exchange).
  • Providing reporting and audit trails for client assets.

Note: If you also want to operate a crypto exchange or provide trading services, you need a separate Category 1 license. The custody license does not allow proprietary trading or lending of client assets without explicit client consent and additional regulatory approval. The CBB strictly prohibits commingling of client assets with the firm's own assets.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Applying for the Bahrain crypto wallet custody license involves several stages. Consulting24 coordinates each step:

  1. Pre-application assessment: We review your business model, source of funds, and fit with CBB requirements.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Bahraini company with the Ministry of Industry and Commerce (MOIC). This includes leasing a physical office and appointing local directors.
  3. Document preparation: Compile a detailed business plan, AML/CFT policies, risk management framework, and financial projections.
  4. Submission to CBB: File the application with the FinTech & Innovation Unit, including all supporting documents and the application fee.
  5. Review and interview: The CBB may request additional information or schedule an interview with the management team.
  6. Approval and licensing: Once approved, you pay the license fee and receive the license certificate.
  7. Post-licensing compliance: Submit periodic reports, maintain capital, and undergo annual audits.

The entire process typically takes 4-6 months. For a faster alternative, Panama offers a license in 2-3 months at a flat EUR 6,000.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Holding a crypto wallet custody license in Bahrain comes with continuous compliance duties. These include:

  • Annual AML/CFT audit by an approved external auditor.
  • Quarterly reporting to the CBB on financial health, transaction volumes, and client asset segregation.
  • Maintenance of minimum capital at all times.
  • Fit and proper re-assessment of directors every three years.
  • Cyber security and business continuity plan updates.

Frequently asked questions

What is the crypto wallet custody license in Bahrain?

It is a regulatory license issued by the Central Bank of Bahrain under the Crypto Asset Module (CRA) that allows a company to provide custodial wallet services, including safekeeping of private keys and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients.

Who needs a crypto wallet custody license in Bahrain?

Any business that holds or controls private keys for third parties, such as crypto exchanges, independent custody providers, or asset managers, must obtain this license if they operate in or from Bahrain.

What is the minimum capital requirement for the license?

The CBB requires a minimum capital of BHD 500,000 (approximately USD 1.33 million) for Category 2 custodial wallet license holders. This capital must be maintained at all times.

How long does it take to get the license?

The typical processing time is 4 to 6 months, depending on the completeness of the application and the CBB's review. Consulting24 helps prepare a thorough submission to avoid delays.

What are the tax benefits of Bahrain for crypto custody?

Bahrain has no corporate income tax, no capital gains tax, and no VAT on most services. This makes it a tax-neutral jurisdiction for crypto custodians.

Can I operate a crypto exchange under the custody license?

No. The custody license only permits custodial wallet services. If you also want to operate a trading platform, you need a separate Category 1 exchange license from the CBB.

Is physical presence required in Bahrain?

Yes. The CBB requires a physical office in Bahrain with at least two full-time employees, including a compliance officer. Remote operations are not permitted.

How does Bahrain compare to Panama for custody licensing?

Panama offers a simpler, faster, and cheaper license at a flat EUR 6,000 with no capital requirement, but it lacks the regulatory prestige of Bahrain. Bahrain is better for firms needing a strong regulatory stamp.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations?

Licensees must submit quarterly reports, undergo annual AML audits, maintain minimum capital, and ensure directors remain fit and proper. Non-compliance can lead to fines or license revocation.
