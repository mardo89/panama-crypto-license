What Is a Crypto OTC Desk License in the Czech Republic?

A crypto OTC desk license in the Czech Republic authorises a company to provide crypto-asset services on a principal-to-principal or agent basis, typically facilitating large trades outside public order books. Under MiCA, this activity is classified as 'operating a trading platform for crypto-assets' (Article 3(1)(9)) or 'placing of crypto-assets' (Article 3(1)(10)), depending on whether the desk acts as a counterparty or intermediary. The Czech National Bank (CNB) is the licensing authority, and the license is a full CASP authorisation under the Czech Act on the Implementation of EU Regulations on Crypto-Assets (Act No. 123/2025 Coll., as amended).

The license is mandatory for any entity that, as a regular business, executes orders for crypto-assets on behalf of clients, places crypto-assets, or operates a multilateral system that brings together multiple third-party buying and selling interests. OTC desks that match buyers and sellers (even if not via an electronic order book) typically fall under the trading platform definition. The CNB has issued guidance clarifying that OTC desks are subject to the same capital, governance, and AML requirements as other CASPs. Who Needs This License?

Any legal entity established in the Czech Republic that offers crypto OTC trading services to clients must hold a CASP license. This includes:

  • OTC desks that match buyers and sellers of crypto-assets (even if not via an electronic order book)
  • Firms that buy and sell crypto-assets from their own inventory to clients (principal trading)
  • Brokers that arrange crypto trades between counterparties
  • Market makers that provide liquidity for specific crypto-assets
  • Any entity that executes orders for crypto-assets on behalf of clients on a regular basis

Exemptions may apply if the service is provided on a very limited scale (e.g., occasional trades) or if the counterparty is a professional client and the trade is above a certain threshold. License Type and Regulator

The license is a Crypto-Asset Service Provider (CASP) authorisation under MiCA, implemented in Czech law by the Act on the Implementation of EU Regulations on Crypto-Assets. The competent authority is the Czech National Bank (Česká národní banka, CNB). The CNB is also the supervisor for anti-money laundering (AML) compliance in the crypto sector.

There is no separate 'OTC desk license' category; the license covers one or more of the following services:

  • Operation of a trading platform for crypto-assets (including OTC platforms)
  • Execution of orders for crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • Placing of crypto-assets
  • Reception and transmission of orders for crypto-assets

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for CASPs depends on the services provided. For an OTC desk that operates a trading platform or executes orders, the capital requirement is EUR 150,000 (Tier 3). If the desk only provides reception and transmission of orders without holding client funds, the requirement is EUR 50,000 (Tier 1).

Capital must be held in liquid assets (cash or equivalents) and can be in the form of share capital, retained earnings, or subordinated loans. The CNB may require additional capital if the business model involves higher risks (e.g., high transaction volumes, proprietary trading). Tax Treatment

The Czech Republic has a favourable tax regime for crypto businesses. Corporate income tax is a flat 19% (2026 rate). Capital gains on crypto-assets held for more than 3 years are exempt from personal income tax for individuals, but for corporate entities, all crypto gains are subject to corporate tax regardless of holding period.

VAT treatment: Crypto-asset transactions are exempt from VAT under the EU's VAT directive, as confirmed by the Czech Ministry of Finance. However, advisory and management fees may be subject to standard VAT (21%). Transfer pricing rules apply to related-party transactions. Allowed Activities

With a Czech CASP license, your OTC desk can:

  • Operate an OTC trading platform matching buyers and sellers
  • Execute orders for crypto-assets on behalf of clients
  • Place crypto-assets (e.g., initial exchange offerings)
  • Provide custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients (if separately authorised)
  • Receive and transmit orders for crypto-assets
  • Provide advice on crypto-assets (if separately authorised)

Activities not covered by the license include operating a crypto ATM (requires separate authorisation) and issuing stablecoins (requires e-money license). Opening a bank account for a crypto OTC desk in the Czech Republic can be challenging, as many traditional banks are cautious about crypto-related businesses. Compliance and Trust

Operating a licensed OTC desk in the Czech Republic requires ongoing compliance with MiCA and AML regulations. Key obligations include:

  • Appointing a local AML officer
  • Conducting customer due diligence (CDD) and enhanced due diligence (EDD) for high-risk clients
  • Reporting suspicious transactions to the Financial Analytical Office (FAÚ)
  • Maintaining records of all transactions for at least 5 years
  • Annual audit of financial statements and AML controls
  • Regular reporting to the CNB on transaction volumes and capital adequacy

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Underestimating capital requirements: Ensure you meet the EUR 150,000 minimum for trading platform operations.
  2. Incomplete AML documentation: The CNB scrutinises AML policies; generic templates are often rejected.
  3. Choosing the wrong service classification: Incorrectly categorising your OTC desk can lead to application rejection.
  4. Ignoring beneficial ownership rules: All ultimate beneficial owners must be disclosed and pass fit and proper tests.
  5. Delaying bank account opening: Start the banking process early, as it can take 2-3 months.
  6. Not planning for ongoing costs: Budget for annual supervisory fees, AML software, and audit costs.
