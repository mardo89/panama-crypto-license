What is the Anjouan Crypto Fund License?

The Anjouan crypto fund license is a regulatory authorization issued by the Anjouan Financial Services Commission (AFSC) that allows fund managers to operate a collective investment scheme focused on crypto assets. It is designed for funds that invest in digital currencies, tokens, blockchain projects, and related assets. The license provides a legal framework for fund formation, management, and operation, ensuring investor protection and regulatory compliance.

Anjouan's regulatory environment is based on the Comoros International Companies Act and the AFSC's guidelines for investment funds. The license is often categorized as a 'Class B' or 'International Fund' license, depending on the fund's structure and investor base. Key features include a fast-track application process, confidentiality for fund investors, and a tax-neutral regime. The AFSC requires the fund to appoint a licensed fund administrator and a compliance officer, but the operational flexibility is high compared to more stringent jurisdictions.

License Type and Regulator

The Anjouan crypto fund license is issued by the Anjouan Financial Services Commission (AFSC), the regulatory body for financial services in the autonomous island of Anjouan. The AFSC is a self-regulatory organization that operates under the Comoros International Companies Act and the AFSC's own rules and guidelines.

There are two main types of fund licenses in Anjouan:

  • Class B Fund License: For funds with up to 50 investors, typically professional or high-net-worth individuals. This is the most common for crypto funds.
  • Class A Fund License: For funds with more than 50 investors or those that are publicly offered. This requires more extensive disclosure and compliance.

For most crypto fund managers, the Class B license is appropriate. The AFSC does not impose a minimum capital requirement for Class B funds, but the fund must have sufficient assets to cover operational costs. The regulator expects the fund to have a licensed fund administrator, a custodian (if holding assets), and a compliance officer. Capital Requirements

Anjouan does not impose a statutory minimum capital requirement for crypto fund licenses. However, the AFSC expects the fund to have sufficient capital to cover its operational expenses for at least the first year. This is typically assessed during the application process based on the fund's business plan and budget.

In practice, most funds are set up with an initial capital of at least EUR 10,000 to EUR 50,000, depending on the fund's size and strategy. There is no requirement to maintain a specific amount of capital in a bank account, but the fund must demonstrate financial viability. Tax Treatment for Anjouan Crypto Funds

Anjouan offers a tax-neutral regime for international funds. Funds licensed in Anjouan are exempt from corporate income tax, capital gains tax, withholding tax, and value-added tax (VAT) on income derived from outside Comoros. This is a significant advantage for crypto funds, as they typically generate income from trading, investments, and management fees from global investors.

However, funds must comply with tax reporting requirements in their home jurisdiction and the jurisdictions of their investors. Anjouan does not have a double tax treaty network, so investors should seek advice on their own tax liabilities. The tax exemption applies only to the fund itself; fund managers and service providers may be subject to tax in their country of residence. Allowed Activities Under the License

The Anjouan crypto fund license permits a wide range of investment activities focused on crypto assets. Typical allowed activities include:

  • Investing in cryptocurrencies (Bitcoin, Ethereum, etc.) and tokens.
  • Participating in initial coin offerings (ICOs) and security token offerings (STOs).
  • Trading crypto derivatives and futures (subject to the fund's investment mandate).
  • Staking, lending, and yield farming activities.
  • Investing in blockchain startups and venture capital deals.
  • Holding and managing digital assets in custody.

The fund must operate within the investment strategy disclosed in its offering memorandum. The AFSC does not restrict the types of crypto assets, but the fund must have appropriate risk management and custody arrangements. Activities such as direct lending to retail clients or operating a crypto exchange are not permitted under a fund license. Consulting24 can help you define the fund's scope to ensure compliance.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process for obtaining an Anjouan crypto fund license involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step:

  2. Company incorporation: Register an International Business Company (IBC) in Anjouan (typically within 1-2 days).
  3. Document preparation: Draft the fund's offering memorandum, subscription agreement, and operational policies (AML/KYC, risk management, custody).
  4. Appointment of service providers: Engage a licensed fund administrator, compliance officer, and auditor (if required).
  5. Submission to AFSC: Submit the application package, including corporate documents, fund documents, and due diligence on directors and shareholders.

  7. Post-licensing compliance: File annual returns, audited financial statements, and compliance reports.

While Anjouan offers a light-touch regulatory environment, fund managers must still implement strong compliance measures to attract investors and maintain credibility. Key compliance requirements include:

Investors increasingly expect funds to adhere to international standards such as FATF recommendations. Anjouan itself is not on the FATF grey list, but the Comoros has been under monitoring. Common Mistakes to Avoid

When applying for an Anjouan crypto fund license, avoid these pitfalls:

  • Incomplete documentation: The AFSC requires a full set of fund documents; missing items cause delays.
  • Choosing an unlicensed administrator: The fund must use an AFSC-licensed administrator.
  • Ignoring tax obligations elsewhere: The fund may be tax-exempt in Anjouan, but investors and managers may have tax liabilities in their home countries.
  • Overlooking AML requirements: Even with low regulatory oversight, strong AML policies are essential for credibility.
  • Not planning for banking: Opening a bank account can take time; start the process early.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital required for an Anjouan crypto fund license?

Anjouan does not have a statutory minimum capital requirement. However, the AFSC expects the fund to have sufficient capital to cover operational expenses for at least one year, typically EUR 10,000 to EUR 50,000 in practice.

How long does it take to get an Anjouan crypto fund license?

The application process typically takes 4 to 6 weeks from submission of a complete application. With proper preparation, approval can be obtained within 30 days.

What are the annual costs for maintaining an Anjouan crypto fund license?

Annual costs include the AFSC renewal fee (EUR 2,500-4,000), fund administrator fees (EUR 3,000-8,000), compliance officer fees (EUR 2,000-5,000), and audit fees. Total annual costs range from EUR 7,500 to 17,000.

Is Anjouan a tax haven for crypto funds?

Anjouan offers a tax-neutral regime for international funds, meaning no corporate income tax, capital gains tax, or VAT on offshore income. However, investors and managers may have tax obligations in their home countries.

Can I use an Anjouan fund license to operate a crypto exchange?

No, a fund license does not permit operating a crypto exchange. It is limited to collective investment activities. For exchange operations, you would need a separate license, such as a CASP under MiCA in Lithuania.

What documents are required for the Anjouan fund license application?

Required documents include the fund's offering memorandum, subscription agreement, AML/KYC policies, risk management procedures, corporate documents of the fund (IBC), and due diligence on directors and shareholders.

Do I need to appoint a local service provider in Anjouan?

Yes, the fund must appoint a licensed fund administrator and a compliance officer. These can be local or international firms approved by the AFSC. Consulting24 can help you find suitable providers.

How does Anjouan compare to Panama for crypto fund licensing?

Panama offers a flat fee of EUR 6,000 for a crypto license with no minimum capital, making it cheaper upfront. However, Panama's license is more general, while Anjouan provides a fund-specific framework. Both are tax-neutral. Your choice depends on your fund's structure and target investors.

Can I convert an existing fund to an Anjouan fund?

Yes, it is possible to redomicile an existing fund to Anjouan, subject to the fund's current jurisdiction's regulations. The process involves transferring the fund's assets and liabilities and obtaining AFSC approval. Consulting24 can advise on the feasibility.
