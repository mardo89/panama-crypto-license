What Is a Crypto Exchange License in El Salvador?

In El Salvador, a "crypto exchange license" is not a single document but a registration as a Digital Asset Service Provider (DASP) with the Central Reserve Bank (BCR). The legal basis is the Bitcoin Law (2021) and the DASP Regulation (2023, updated 2025). The registration authorises a company to operate a cryptocurrency exchange, custody digital assets, and facilitate conversions between Bitcoin, USDT, and fiat currency (USD).

Key features of the regime:

  • Regulator: Central Reserve Bank of El Salvador (BCR).
  • Registration type: DASP (Digital Asset Service Provider).
  • No separate exchange license: all crypto services fall under DASP registration.
  • Bitcoin is legal tender; other digital assets are treated as commodities.
  • Zero capital gains tax on Bitcoin profits (as of 2026).

Unlike EU member states under MiCA, El Salvador does not impose tiered capital requirements by service class. Instead, the minimum capital is a flat $100,000 for exchange and custody services. The BCR also requires a local office, a compliance officer, and an AML/KYC programme.

Who Needs a Crypto Exchange License in El Salvador?

Any business that offers the following services in or from El Salvador must register as a DASP:

  • Cryptocurrency exchange (BTC, USDT, ETH, etc.) for fiat or other crypto.
  • Digital asset custody or wallet services.
  • Brokerage, trading platforms, or order-matching systems.
  • Conversion between Bitcoin and US dollars (as per Bitcoin Law).
  • Issuance of stablecoins or tokenised assets (requires additional approval).

Founders targeting the Latin American market, especially those focusing on Bitcoin remittances, peer-to-peer trading, or BTC-backed lending, find El Salvador attractive. The jurisdiction also appeals to companies that want to avoid the complex MiCA capital tiers (EUR 50k-150k) and prefer a flat $100k requirement. License Type & Regulator

Regulator: Central Reserve Bank of El Salvador (BCR). The BCR oversees all DASP registrations and enforces AML/CFT compliance under the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) supervision.

License type: DASP (Digital Asset Service Provider) registration. There is no separate "exchange license" - all crypto activities are covered by this single registration. The BCR issues a certificate of registration after a thorough due diligence process.

Legal framework:

  • Bitcoin Law (Decreto 57, 2021) - grants Bitcoin legal tender status.
  • DASP Regulation (2023, updated 2025) - defines registration requirements, capital, and compliance.
  • AML Law (Ley Contra el Lavado de Dinero) - applies to all DASPs.

Compared to other jurisdictions, El Salvador's regime is simpler: no separate license classes for exchange vs custody. One registration covers all permitted services. This contrasts with Lithuania's two-tier system (exchange and custodian wallet licenses) or Panama's license-free regime (as of 2026, Panama has no specific crypto law, so exchanges operate under general business registration).

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a DASP registration in El Salvador is $100,000 (USD). This capital must be fully paid up and deposited in a Salvadoran bank account. The BCR may require a higher amount depending on the volume and risk profile of the exchange, but $100,000 is the statutory minimum as of 2026.

This is significantly lower than EU MiCA capital tiers (EUR 50,000 for certain services, but up to EUR 150,000 for exchanges with custody). It is also lower than Lithuania's capital requirement of EUR 125,000 for exchange and custodian wallet licenses. Panama has no minimum capital requirement for crypto businesses, but banks often impose their own thresholds for account opening.

Capital can be in the form of cash or liquid assets. The BCR does not accept cryptocurrencies as capital. The funds must remain in the company's account and be used for operational expenses; they cannot be withdrawn without BCR approval.

Tax Treatment for Crypto Exchanges in El Salvador

El Salvador offers a highly favourable tax regime for crypto businesses:

  • Zero capital gains tax on Bitcoin profits (as per Bitcoin Law). This applies to both individuals and companies.
  • Standard corporate income tax of 30% on profits from other digital assets (e.g., USDT, ETH) and non-Bitcoin services.
  • VAT of 13% applies to commissions and fees charged to customers, but not to Bitcoin transactions themselves.
  • No withholding tax on dividends paid to non-resident shareholders (provided the company is not managed in El Salvador).
  • Tax treaty network: limited; El Salvador has treaties with a few countries, but not with major financial hubs.

Because Bitcoin is legal tender, its sale is not considered a taxable event. This is unique among crypto-friendly jurisdictions. For comparison, Panama has no capital gains tax at all (territorial tax system), but lacks a specific crypto law. Lithuania taxes crypto gains as capital gains at 15% for individuals and 15% corporate tax.

Allowed Activities Under the DASP Registration

A DASP registration in El Salvador permits the following activities:

  • Operation of a cryptocurrency exchange (BTC, USDT, ETH, and other digital assets).
  • Custody and wallet services for digital assets.
  • Conversion between Bitcoin and US dollars (mandatory under Bitcoin Law for businesses that accept BTC).
  • Brokerage and trading services.
  • Staking and lending (if not involving securities; securities are regulated separately by the Superintendencia del Sistema Financiero).
  • Issuance of stablecoins backed by USD (requires additional approval from BCR).

Activities NOT allowed without further licensing:

  • Security token offerings (regulated by the SSF).
  • Derivatives and futures on crypto (not explicitly covered by DASP regulation).
  • Banking services (deposit-taking, lending in fiat) - requires a banking license.

Compared to Lithuania, where exchange and custodian wallet licenses are separate, El Salvador's single registration covers both. Panama has no specific allowed activities list, so any crypto service is technically allowed but unregulated.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Company incorporation: Register a Sociedad Anónima (SA) in El Salvador. This requires a local registered address and at least two shareholders (can be foreign). Consulting24 coordinates the incorporation.
  2. Open a corporate bank account: Deposit the minimum capital ($100,000) in a Salvadoran bank (e.g., Banco Agrícola, Banco Cuscatlán).
  3. Prepare documentation: Business plan, AML/KYC policies, compliance officer appointment, source of funds declaration, and organisational structure.
  4. Submit DASP application: File with the BCR, including all supporting documents and the application fee. The BCR conducts due diligence on shareholders and directors.
  5. BCR review and interview: The BCR may request additional information or an interview with the compliance officer. This takes 30-60 working days.
  6. Receive DASP certificate: Once approved, the BCR issues a certificate of registration. The company can then commence operations.
  7. Ongoing compliance: Submit quarterly AML reports, annual financial statements, and maintain a local compliance officer.

Compliance & Trust Requirements

To maintain your DASP registration, you must adhere to strict compliance standards. This includes:

  • AML/KYC programme: Implement customer due diligence (CDD), transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting to the FIU.
  • Local compliance officer: A resident of El Salvador (can be outsourced) responsible for AML compliance.
  • Quarterly reports: Submit transaction volumes, customer numbers, and AML statistics to the BCR.
  • Annual audit: Financial statements must be audited by a local auditor.
  • Data protection: Comply with El Salvador's data protection law (Ley de Protección de Datos Personales).

Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the DASP certificate. Consulting24 provides ongoing compliance support to ensure you meet BCR expectations. This is general guidance, not legal advice.

Common Mistakes When Applying

  1. Insufficient capital proof: The BCR requires evidence that the $100,000 is from a legitimate source. Bank statements and source of funds declarations are mandatory.
  2. Incomplete AML policies: Many applicants submit generic AML manuals. The BCR expects a programme tailored to the exchange's specific business model.
  3. No local compliance officer: The BCR insists on a local resident as compliance officer. Using a foreign officer is a common reason for rejection.
  4. Ignoring Bitcoin Law obligations: If your exchange handles Bitcoin, you must comply with the Bitcoin Law's requirement to accept BTC as payment (if you offer goods/services).
  5. Rushing the application: Incomplete documentation leads to delays. Consulting24 advises and coordinates to avoid these pitfalls.

Frequently asked questions

Is a crypto exchange license required in El Salvador?

Yes, any business offering digital asset exchange, custody, or conversion services must register as a Digital Asset Service Provider (DASP) with the Central Reserve Bank (BCR). This is the equivalent of a crypto exchange license.

What is the minimum capital for a crypto exchange in El Salvador?

The minimum capital is $100,000 USD. This must be deposited in a Salvadoran bank account and proven to be from a legitimate source. The BCR may require more for higher-risk operations.

How long does it take to get a DASP registration in El Salvador?

The process typically takes 2-4 months, including company incorporation and BCR review. The BCR aims to process applications within 60 working days, but delays can occur if documentation is incomplete.

What taxes apply to crypto exchanges in El Salvador?

Bitcoin profits are exempt from capital gains tax. Other digital assets are subject to standard corporate income tax of 30%. VAT of 13% applies to commissions. No withholding tax on dividends to non-residents.

Can I operate a crypto exchange from Panama without a license?

Yes, Panama has no specific crypto license as of 2026. Exchanges operate under general business registration. However, this lacks regulatory clarity and may complicate banking. Consulting24 offers Panama company setup for EUR 6,000.

Does El Salvador accept foreign companies for DASP registration?

Yes, the company must be incorporated in El Salvador as a Sociedad Anónima. Foreign shareholders and directors are allowed. A local compliance officer is required.

What activities are covered by the DASP registration?

Exchange, custody, wallet services, Bitcoin-fiat conversion, brokerage, and staking/lending (non-securities). Security tokens and derivatives are not covered and require separate licenses.

Is El Salvador's crypto license recognised in other countries?

No, the DASP registration is only valid in El Salvador. However, it provides a regulated status that may facilitate partnerships and banking in other jurisdictions, especially within Latin America.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

Quarterly AML reports to the BCR, annual audited financial statements, and a local compliance officer. The BCR may conduct inspections. Non-compliance can lead to fines or revocation.

How does El Salvador compare to Lithuania for crypto licensing?

Lithuania requires separate exchange and custodian licenses with EUR 125,000 capital, 15% tax, and 4-6 months timeline. El Salvador has a single registration, lower capital ($100k), and tax advantages for Bitcoin. Lithuania offers stronger EU banking.
