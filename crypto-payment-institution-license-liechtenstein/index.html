What is the Crypto Payment Institution License Liechtenstein?

The crypto payment institution license in Liechtenstein is a regulatory authorization issued by the Financial Market Authority (FMA) under the Token and Trusted Technology Service Provider Act (TVTG), also known as the Blockchain Act. It allows companies to offer services such as crypto payment processing, custody of digital assets, exchange between cryptocurrencies and fiat, and issuance of tokenized assets. This license is designed for businesses that want to operate as payment institutions with a focus on virtual assets, providing a clear legal status that enhances trust with partners and clients.

Unlike some jurisdictions that treat crypto service providers under general financial services laws, Liechtenstein has a bespoke regime that recognizes the unique nature of blockchain technology. The license is aligned with EU anti-money laundering (AML) standards and the MiCA regulation, which is fully in force in 2026. This makes it a future-proof choice for European operations. License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Financial Market Authority Liechtenstein (FMA), which oversees all financial services, including crypto activities. The license is formally a 'payment institution license' under the TVTG, specifically for trusted technology (TT) service providers that offer payment services involving virtual assets. The FMA applies a risk-based approach, requiring strong AML/KYC procedures, corporate governance, and operational resilience. As of 2026, the license is fully integrated with the EU's MiCA framework, meaning that licensed firms can potentially passport services to other EU member states. The FMA is known for its thorough but fair review process, often requesting additional information on business models and risk management. Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a crypto payment institution license in Liechtenstein is EUR 125,000. This is the standard for payment institutions under the EU's second Payment Services Directive (PSD2), which Liechtenstein applies to crypto payment services. The capital must be held in a bank account in Liechtenstein or another EU/EEA country and be fully paid up before licensing. For firms offering additional services like trading on own account, higher capital may be required. For example, if your business also provides crypto-to-crypto trading as a principal, the FMA may require up to EUR 150,000. Tax Treatment

Liechtenstein offers a favourable tax environment for crypto businesses. The corporate income tax rate is a flat 12.5% on net profits. There is no capital gains tax on the sale of crypto assets held for investment, and no VAT on crypto-to-fiat transactions (subject to specific conditions). However, income from crypto payment services is subject to standard corporate tax. The country has a double taxation treaty network, but it does not automatically exchange information on crypto assets (as of 2026, OECD's CARF is being implemented). For example, if your company earns fees from payment processing, those profits are taxed at 12.5%. Interest income from crypto lending may also be taxable. Consulting24 recommends engaging a local tax advisor to optimize your structure. This tax rate is competitive compared to other European hubs like Switzerland (effective rates around 18-20%) and Malta (35% but with refunds).

Allowed Activities

Under the crypto payment institution license in Liechtenstein, you can perform the following activities:

  • Execution of payment transactions in virtual assets (e.g., sending crypto on behalf of clients)
  • Conversion services between fiat and crypto, or between different crypto assets
  • Custody and administration of private keys on behalf of clients
  • Issuance of tokenized assets (e.g., security tokens) if combined with a prospectus
  • Payment initiation services (e.g., initiating a crypto payment from a client's wallet)
  • Account information services (aggregating crypto holdings from different wallets)

Activities must be conducted in compliance with AML laws and the FMA's guidelines on safeguarding client assets. For instance, a licensed payment institution can offer a crypto debit card where users spend their crypto at merchants, with the license covering the conversion and settlement. Common Mistakes to Avoid

Applicants often make these errors:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing business plan details or AML policies lead to delays. Ensure your business plan includes realistic financial projections and a clear description of your target market.
  • Underestimating capital: Not having the full EUR 125,000 deposited before submission. The FMA requires proof of the deposit.
  • Ignoring local presence: FMA requires a physical office and local management. A virtual office is not sufficient.
  • Poor AML/KYC setup: Using generic templates without customization. The FMA expects tailored policies that reflect your specific business risks.
  • Unrealistic financial projections: Overly optimistic revenue forecasts raise red flags. Base projections on conservative assumptions.
  • Delaying banking: Not starting the bank account opening process early enough. This can hold up the entire application.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Liechtenstein crypto payment institution license?

The minimum capital is EUR 125,000, which must be deposited in a bank account in Liechtenstein or another EU/EEA country before submission. This is a standard requirement under PSD2, applied to crypto payment services. Higher capital may be required for additional activities like trading on own account.

How long does it take to get the license?

The typical timeline is 6 to 12 months from application submission to approval. This includes preparation, FMA review, and any follow-up queries. The FMA has up to 6 months to decide after receiving a complete application, but delays can occur if additional information is needed.

Can I passport the license to other EU countries?

Yes, as Liechtenstein is an EEA member, licensed firms can passport services to other EU member states under the MiCA framework. This allows you to operate across the EU without additional licensing, subject to notification procedures with the FMA and host regulators.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

Ongoing requirements include quarterly transaction reporting, annual audited financial statements, AML/KYC updates, suspicious transaction reports, and maintaining a local AML officer. The FMA may conduct on-site inspections. Non-compliance can lead to fines or license revocation.

Is a physical office required in Liechtenstein?

Yes, the FMA requires a physical registered office and local management. A virtual office is not sufficient. The office must be staffed during business hours and serve as the company's principal place of business. Consulting24 can help you find suitable office space.

What activities are allowed under the license?

Allowed activities include execution of payment transactions in virtual assets, conversion between fiat and crypto, custody of private keys, issuance of tokenized assets (with a prospectus), payment initiation, and account information services. All activities must comply with AML laws and FMA guidelines.

How does Liechtenstein tax crypto businesses?

Corporate income tax is a flat 12.5% on net profits. There is no capital gains tax on crypto held for investment, and no VAT on crypto-to-fiat transactions under certain conditions. Income from payment services is taxable. Consult a local tax advisor for your specific situation.

Can I use a bank account from another country for the capital deposit?

The capital must be held in a bank account in Liechtenstein or another EU/EEA country. The account must be in the company's name and the funds must be fully paid up before submission. Consulting24 can assist with bank introductions.

What are the common reasons for application rejection?

How does Liechtenstein compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Panama does not have a specific crypto license; businesses use existing corporate structures with no capital requirement and 0% tax on foreign-source income. Setup costs EUR 6,000 flat and takes 1-2 months. Liechtenstein offers a regulated license with EU passporting but at higher cost and longer timeline.
