What Is a Crypto OTC Desk License USA?

A crypto OTC desk license in the USA is not a single permit but a combination of registrations and licenses required to legally operate an over-the-counter trading desk for cryptocurrencies. OTC desks facilitate large-volume trades directly between counterparties, often involving institutional clients. In 2026, the US regulatory framework remains fragmented: federal agencies like FinCEN and the SEC oversee certain aspects, while each state has its own money transmission laws.

The core requirements include:

  • FinCEN registration as a Money Services Business (MSB) - mandatory for any business transmitting virtual currency.
  • State Money Transmitter Licenses (MTLs) in each state where you have customers or conduct business.
  • SEC registration if trading digital assets deemed securities (e.g., tokens from ICOs).
  • Compliance with the Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) and Anti-Money Laundering (AML) programs.

Because the US lacks a unified crypto license, many OTC desks choose to operate from a single state or partner with licensed entities. Consulting24 advises on structuring your US operations and can coordinate licensing in other jurisdictions for a global footprint.

Who Needs a Crypto OTC Desk License USA?

Any entity that facilitates the exchange of fiat currency for cryptocurrency, or vice versa, on behalf of customers, and holds customer funds, likely needs US licensing. Specifically, you need a crypto OTC desk license if you:

  • Operate an OTC trading desk that matches buyers and sellers of digital assets.
  • Provide liquidity for large trades, often with institutional clients.
  • Hold customer funds or digital assets in custody during settlement.
  • Offer price quotes and execute trades as a principal or agent.

Exemptions may apply if you only trade your own capital (proprietary trading) and do not hold customer funds. However, most OTC desks serve third parties and thus fall under state MTL requirements. License Type & Regulator

The US crypto OTC desk license is not a single license but a combination of:

  • FinCEN MSB Registration - Federal registration with the Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN) as a Money Services Business. This is required for any business that transmits virtual currency. Cost: USD 0 for registration, but ongoing compliance costs.
  • State Money Transmitter Licenses (MTLs) - Each state has its own regulator (e.g., New York Department of Financial Services, California Department of Financial Protection and Innovation). MTLs require separate applications, bonding, and capital.
  • SEC Registration - If trading digital asset securities, you may need to register as a broker-dealer or alternative trading system (ATS) with the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC).

The primary regulators are FinCEN (federal) and state financial regulators. There is no single national crypto license in the US as of 2026. Consulting24 advises on the specific regulators relevant to your operations and coordinates applications where possible.

Capital Requirement

Capital requirements for a US crypto OTC desk vary by state. Most states require a minimum net worth or capital for MTLs, typically ranging from $25,000 to $500,000 depending on transaction volume and state. For example:

  • New York: $500,000 minimum capital for a BitLicense (if applicable).
  • California: $25,000 - $500,000 based on volume.
  • Texas: $50,000 - $300,000.

Additionally, you must maintain a surety bond in each state, which can be 10-20% of the bond amount. The total capital tied up across multiple states can exceed $1 million. Tax Treatment

Cryptocurrency OTC desks in the USA are subject to federal and state taxes. The IRS treats cryptocurrency as property, so each trade is a taxable event. OTC desks must report gains/losses and issue Form 1099 to customers if they are brokers. Key tax considerations:

  • Corporate income tax: Federal rate 21% (C-corp) or pass-through rates (LLC).
  • State income tax: Varies by state (0% in Nevada, Wyoming; up to 13.3% in California).
  • Sales tax: Not typically applicable to crypto trades, but some states may impose transaction taxes.
  • Reporting: FinCEN requires reporting of suspicious transactions; IRS requires FBAR for foreign accounts.

Allowed Activities

A crypto OTC desk license in the USA permits the following activities, subject to state and federal limits:

  • Buying and selling cryptocurrency as a principal or agent.
  • Providing liquidity for large trades.
  • Holding customer funds in custody (with appropriate safeguards).
  • Executing trades on behalf of institutional clients.
  • Offering price quotes and negotiating bilateral trades.

Activities typically not allowed without additional licenses: operating a public exchange, offering derivatives (requires CFTC registration), or trading securities without SEC registration. Step-by-Step Process

Obtaining a crypto OTC desk license in the USA involves multiple steps. Consulting24 advises on this process and coordinates with local counsel.

  1. Business Structuring: Choose entity type (LLC, C-corp) and state of incorporation. Delaware, Wyoming, or Nevada are common.
  2. FinCEN MSB Registration: File the registration form online. Prepare AML/BSA policies.
  3. State MTL Applications: Identify target states based on customer location. Submit applications with financial statements, surety bonds, and business plans.
  4. Compliance Program: Implement AML/KYC procedures, transaction monitoring, and reporting.
  5. Banking Relationship: Open a bank account for the OTC desk (challenging; see Banking section).
  6. Ongoing Reporting: File periodic reports with FinCEN and state regulators.

Timeline: 6-18 months for multi-state coverage. Consulting24 can streamline the process by recommending a single-state approach or using the Panama license as a faster alternative.

Compliance & Trust

Compliance is the backbone of a US crypto OTC desk. Key requirements:

  • AML/BSA Program: Written policies, risk assessment, independent testing.
  • KYC: Customer identification, beneficial ownership, ongoing monitoring.
  • Transaction Monitoring: Detect suspicious activity and file Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs).
  • Recordkeeping: Maintain records for 5 years.
  • Privacy: Comply with state data protection laws.

Common Mistakes

Avoid these pitfalls when applying for a US crypto OTC desk license:

  • Underestimating costs: Many founders think a single state MTL is enough, but they may need multiple states.
  • Ignoring securities laws: Trading tokens that are securities can lead to SEC enforcement.
  • Poor banking relationship: Failing to secure a bank account before licensing can delay operations.
  • Incomplete AML program: Regulators scrutinize AML policies; a cookie-cutter approach fails.
  • Choosing the wrong state: Some states (e.g., New York) are much more expensive and time-consuming.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a US crypto OTC desk license if I only serve non-US clients?

If you have no US customers, you generally do not need US licensing. However, if you are a US person or entity, you may still be subject to FinCEN registration. Consulting24 advises on structuring to avoid unnecessary US licensing.

Can I use a single state license for the whole US?

No. Most states require separate MTLs if you have customers in that state. Some states have reciprocity, but you typically need licenses in each state where you operate. A common approach is to limit operations to a few states.

What is the difference between a US MSB and a state MTL?

FinCEN MSB registration is federal and covers money transmission broadly. State MTLs are required for each state and include additional bonding, capital, and reporting. Most crypto OTC desks need both.

How long does it take to get a US crypto OTC desk license?

6-18 months for multi-state coverage. Single-state MTLs can take 4-8 months. FinCEN registration takes 1-2 weeks. Consulting24 can help expedite the process.

What are the ongoing compliance costs for a US OTC desk?

Expect $50,000 - $200,000+ annually for AML compliance, legal fees, audits, and reporting. Costs scale with transaction volume and number of states.

Can I operate an OTC desk without a license if I only trade my own capital?

Yes, proprietary trading without holding customer funds generally does not require a license. However, if you provide quotes or match third parties, you likely need licensing. Consult an attorney.

Is Panama a good alternative to a US crypto OTC desk license?

Yes. Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 license with no capital requirement and 0% tax on foreign-source income. It is ideal for international OTC desks serving non-US clients. Consulting24 delivers Panama licenses directly.

What if I want to trade security tokens in the US?

You would need SEC registration as a broker-dealer or ATS. This adds significant cost and complexity. Consulting24 advises on whether to avoid security tokens or use a separate entity.

Do US crypto OTC desks need a BitLicense?

Only if you operate in New York. The BitLicense is a state-specific license for virtual currency businesses. It is expensive and time-consuming. Many OTC desks avoid New York.
