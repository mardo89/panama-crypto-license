What Is the Fastest Switzerland Crypto License?

The fastest Switzerland crypto license refers to the authorisation process under FINMA's framework for financial intermediaries engaging in crypto-related activities. Switzerland does not have a single 'crypto license' but rather a classification system: depending on your business model, you may need a banking license (for deposit-taking), a securities dealer license, or a simpler registration as a financial intermediary under the Anti-Money Laundering Act (AMLA). For most crypto startups, the quickest route is FINMA registration as a DLT (Distributed Ledger Technology) trading facility or a simple AMLA registration, which can be obtained in 3-6 months.

This speed is achieved through Switzerland's clear regulatory guidelines and the absence of a mandatory minimum capital for pure AML registration. However, if your activities involve custody of client assets or operating an exchange, you may need a full license, which takes longer. Consulting24 advises on the fastest path tailored to your specific activities. For a deeper dive into the application process, see our application process guide.

For example, a startup offering a non-custodial wallet might only need AML registration, while a custodial exchange would require a DLT license. The fastest route typically involves the DLT trading facility license, which was introduced in 2021 and has a streamlined process compared to a full banking license.

Who Needs a Swiss Crypto License?

Any business that provides crypto services to Swiss residents or operates from Switzerland must comply with FINMA regulations. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralised and decentralised if offering fiat on/off ramps)
  • Wallet providers (custodial and non-custodial with financial intermediation)
  • Token issuers (ICOs, STOs, security token offerings)
  • Brokerage and trading platforms
  • Asset managers dealing with crypto assets
  • Payment service providers using crypto

If your business involves holding client funds or assets, you likely need a license. Pure software development or non-custodial services may be exempt. Capital Requirement for the Fastest Switzerland Crypto License

Switzerland's capital requirements are tiered by license type. For the fastest route (simple AML registration), there is no minimum capital. For a DLT trading facility license, the minimum capital is typically CHF 500,000. For a full banking license, it is CHF 10 million. These figures are as of 2026 and may be adjusted by FINMA on a case-by-case basis. Consulting24 advises on structuring your capital to meet the requirements efficiently. For example, a DLT license applicant might need to show CHF 500,000 in liquid assets at the time of submission, which can be held in fiat or stablecoins with FINMA approval.

It is important to note that the capital must be fully paid up and maintained throughout the license period. Some applicants use a combination of equity and shareholder loans, but FINMA prefers equity. We recommend consulting a Swiss financial advisor for capital planning.

Tax Treatment for Crypto Businesses in Switzerland

Switzerland offers a favourable tax environment for crypto businesses. Corporate income tax rates are low, typically 12-21% depending on canton. Capital gains from crypto trading are tax-exempt for individuals, but businesses pay tax on profits. VAT is generally not applicable on crypto transactions (except for advisory services). Stamp duty may apply to securities transactions. Switzerland has no withholding tax on dividends for Swiss companies. Consulting24 recommends consulting a Swiss tax advisor for your specific situation. For example, a company in Zug (Crypto Valley) might pay around 12% corporate tax, while one in Zurich pays about 21%.

Additionally, crypto-to-crypto trades are not subject to VAT, which is a significant advantage. However, mining and staking rewards may be treated as income. Proper tax planning can reduce your effective rate further.

Banking & Payment Services for Swiss Licensed Crypto Firms

Opening a bank account for a Swiss crypto company is challenging but possible. Major Swiss banks like UBS, Credit Suisse (now part of UBS), and cantonal banks are cautious. Fintech-friendly banks such as Sygnum, SEBA Bank, and some cantonal banks (e.g., Zuger Kantonalbank) offer services to licensed crypto firms. Payment processing can be done through Swiss payment providers or integrated with crypto-friendly PSPs. Consulting24 advises on banking strategies and introductions to partner banks. Compliance & Trust: Maintaining Your Swiss Crypto License

Once licensed, ongoing compliance is mandatory. This includes:

  • AML/CFT obligations: customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, reporting suspicious activity.
  • Annual audits by an external auditor.
  • Regular reporting to FINMA (financial statements, risk reports).
  • Maintaining adequate capital (if applicable).
  • Data protection and cybersecurity measures.

Non-compliance can lead to fines, license revocation, or criminal liability. Consulting24 provides ongoing compliance support to ensure you meet all requirements. Common Mistakes When Applying for the Fastest Switzerland Crypto License

  1. Choosing the wrong license type: Many applicants apply for a full banking license when a simpler registration suffices, wasting time and money.
  2. Incomplete documentation: Missing AML policies or business plans cause delays.
  3. Underestimating capital requirements: For DLT licenses, having insufficient capital at submission leads to rejection.
  4. Ignoring local presence: FINMA requires a physical office in Switzerland, even for simple AML registration.
  5. Poor AML compliance: Inadequate transaction monitoring or KYC processes can result in license denial.
