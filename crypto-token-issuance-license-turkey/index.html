What Is the Crypto Token Issuance License in Turkey?

The crypto token issuance license in Turkey is a regulatory authorization granted by the Capital Markets Board (CMB) for the public offering and trading of crypto tokens on a platform. It applies to token issuers and platforms that facilitate token sales. The license ensures compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) rules, investor protection, and market integrity. As of 2026, the CMB has established a clear framework under the Capital Markets Law, which includes registration, capital requirements, and ongoing reporting obligations.

This license is distinct from a general crypto service provider license. It specifically covers the issuance and listing of tokens, including utility tokens, security tokens, and stablecoins. The CMB evaluates the token's nature and the issuer's business model before granting approval. The process involves a detailed application, a prospectus (if required), and a minimum capital commitment. For example, a company issuing a utility token for a decentralized application must demonstrate that the token provides access to a service rather than representing an investment. The CMB also requires a legal opinion on the token's classification. Consulting24 advises and coordinates with Turkish legal experts to prepare this opinion.

Compared to other jurisdictions, Turkey's license is more prescriptive, with specific capital tiers and disclosure requirements. This provides clarity but also imposes higher upfront costs. For a lighter alternative, Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 company setup with no capital requirement for token issuance, though it does not provide a local license.

  • Foreign issuers targeting Turkish investors must also obtain the license or work with a licensed platform.

License Type & Regulator

The regulator is the Capital Markets Board of Turkey (CMB), which oversees capital markets, including crypto token issuance. The license is called the "Token Issuance License" or "Crypto Asset Issuance License". It is a category under the Capital Markets Law (amended in 2024 to include crypto assets). The CMB classifies tokens as either capital market instruments (security tokens) or other crypto assets (utility tokens). The license type depends on the token's characteristics.

For security tokens, the issuer must comply with full prospectus requirements, similar to a traditional securities offering. For utility tokens, a simplified registration process may apply, but the issuer still needs to submit a white paper and AML policies. The CMB also requires the issuer to have a legal presence in Turkey (a company registered in Turkey) and appoint a licensed custodian for investor funds. The custodian must be a Turkish bank or an authorized crypto custody provider. Consulting24 can help you identify suitable custodians and legal partners.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement depends on the token type:

  • Utility tokens: Minimum paid-in capital of EUR 50,000.
  • Security tokens: Minimum paid-in capital of EUR 125,000.
  • Stablecoins or asset-referenced tokens: EUR 150,000 or higher, depending on the asset backing.

The capital must be deposited in a Turkish bank account and cannot be withdrawn without CMB approval. It serves as a guarantee for investor protection. For example, a utility token issuer must show that the capital is available for operational expenses and potential refunds. The capital is typically held in a blocked account until the license is granted. For a cost-effective alternative, Panama has no minimum capital requirement, allowing you to allocate funds elsewhere.

Tax Treatment

Turkey's tax regime for crypto tokens is evolving. As of 2026:

  • Corporate income tax: 25% for companies, applied to profits from token sales.
  • Value-added tax (VAT): 18% on platform fees, but token issuance itself may be exempt if classified as a financial service.
  • Withholding tax: 15% on dividends distributed to shareholders.
  • Capital gains: Individuals are taxed at 15-40% on gains from crypto trading, but token issuance is treated as business income.

Allowed Activities

With the crypto token issuance license, you can:

  • Issue tokens to the public (ICO, STO, IEO).
  • List tokens on a CMB-approved platform.
  • Offer tokenized securities or utility tokens.
  • Facilitate secondary trading of issued tokens on your own platform (if also licensed as a trading platform).
  • Provide custody services for tokens (if separately licensed as a custodian).

Activities not allowed include operating a crypto exchange without a separate license, offering derivatives, or issuing tokens that are clearly fraudulent. The CMB prohibits unregistered offerings to Turkish residents. For example, a DeFi protocol cannot offer yield-bearing tokens without a license. Step-by-Step Process

  1. Company setup: Register a Turkish joint-stock company (Anonim Şirket) with the required capital. This takes 2-4 weeks.
  2. Token classification: Determine if your token is a security or utility token with legal counsel. This step is critical for capital and prospectus requirements.
  3. Prepare documentation: Draft a white paper, prospectus (if security token), AML/KYC policies, risk disclosures, and a legal opinion. This takes 4-6 weeks.
  4. Submit application: File with the CMB, including all documents and proof of capital deposit. The application fee is paid at this stage.
  5. CMB review: The CMB reviews the application within 90 days, requesting clarifications if needed. They may ask for additional information on the token's technology or business model.
  6. Approval & listing: Once approved, you can issue tokens and list them on a licensed platform. You must also register with MASAK (Turkey's financial intelligence unit) for AML compliance.
  7. Ongoing compliance: Submit annual reports, audits, and maintain AML procedures. The CMB may conduct inspections.

Compliance & Trust

Compliance is critical for maintaining the license. The CMB requires:

  • AML/KYC procedures aligned with Turkish law (MASAK). This includes customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and reporting suspicious activities.
  • Regular audits by a licensed auditor, submitted annually to the CMB.
  • Reporting of suspicious transactions to MASAK within 24 hours.
  • Investor fund segregation and custody with a licensed custodian.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a utility token and a security token under Turkish law?

Utility tokens grant access to a product or service and are subject to a simplified registration with the CMB, with a minimum capital of EUR 50,000. Security tokens represent an investment in an underlying asset and require a full prospectus, a minimum capital of EUR 125,000, and stricter disclosure rules. The CMB classifies tokens based on their economic function and rights attached.

Can a foreign company apply for a Turkish token issuance license?

Yes, but the issuer must have a legal presence in Turkey, typically a joint-stock company. The foreign parent company can hold shares, but the Turkish entity must be the licensed issuer. Consulting24 advises on the corporate structure and helps incorporate the Turkish subsidiary.

How long does the CMB take to approve a token issuance license?

The CMB has up to 90 days to review the application after submission. However, if they request additional information, the timeline can extend to 3-6 months total. Consulting24 helps prepare a complete application to minimize delays.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements after obtaining the license?

Issuers must submit annual audited financial statements, AML reports to MASAK, and notify the CMB of any material changes. They must also maintain the minimum capital and custody arrangements. Non-compliance can lead to fines or license revocation.

Is there a separate license for operating a token trading platform in Turkey?

Yes, operating a platform that lists and trades tokens requires a separate Crypto Asset Trading Platform License from the CMB. The token issuance license only covers issuance and listing on a licensed platform. Consulting24 can advise on both licenses.

What happens if I issue tokens without a license in Turkey?

Unlicensed issuance is illegal and can result in criminal penalties, including fines and imprisonment. The CMB can also order the cessation of the offering and restitution to investors. Always obtain the license before any public sale.

Can I use the Turkish license to issue tokens to investors in the EU?

No, the Turkish license is only valid for offerings to Turkish residents. For EU investors, you need a license under MiCA in an EU member state, such as Lithuania or Estonia. Consulting24 can help you obtain a MiCA license for EU passporting.

What are the tax implications for token issuers in Turkey?

Issuers pay 25% corporate income tax on profits from token sales. VAT at 18% may apply to platform fees but not to token issuance itself. Withholding tax of 15% applies to dividends. Consulting24 advises on tax planning to optimize your structure.

How does Panama compare to Turkey for token issuance?

Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 company setup with no capital requirement, no local license, and 0% tax on foreign-source income. It is faster (2-4 weeks) and cheaper, but does not provide a regulated license. Turkey offers regulatory clarity and access to the Turkish market at a higher cost.
