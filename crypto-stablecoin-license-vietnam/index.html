Overview: What Is the Crypto Stablecoin License in Vietnam?

Vietnam does not have a single, unified license for crypto stablecoin operations. The term "crypto stablecoin license" refers to the combination of legal permissions and registrations required to issue, manage, or exchange stablecoins within or from Vietnam. As of 2026, the government has been piloting a regulatory sandbox for crypto assets under Decision No. 125/QD-TTg, but it has not yet been fully implemented. Stablecoin issuers must comply with anti-money laundering (AML) laws, the Law on Credit Institutions (if the stablecoin is deemed a payment instrument), and general business registration requirements.

Who Needs a Crypto Stablecoin License in Vietnam?

Any entity planning to issue, trade, or facilitate stablecoins in Vietnam should consider the regulatory requirements. This includes:

  • Stablecoin issuers: Companies creating fiat-backed (e.g., VND-pegged) or crypto-backed stablecoins. For example, a startup issuing a VND-pegged stablecoin for remittances would need SBV approval.
  • Exchanges: Platforms listing stablecoins for trading against VND or other assets. Even if the exchange is based abroad, serving Vietnamese users may trigger local compliance.
  • Payment service providers: Firms using stablecoins for remittances or merchant payments. These often require a payment intermediary license.
  • DeFi protocols: Projects offering stablecoin lending, borrowing, or yield generation. DeFi platforms must ensure their smart contracts comply with local securities laws.

If your business involves any of these activities, you need to ensure compliance with Vietnamese laws. For foreign founders, setting up a local entity is often required, with a minimum charter capital of VND 10-20 billion (roughly EUR 380,000-760,000) depending on the business line. License Type and Regulator

In Vietnam, the regulatory environment is fragmented. The key bodies involved are:

  • State Bank of Vietnam (SBV): Oversees payment services and may classify stablecoins as electronic money or payment instruments. The SBV also manages the fintech sandbox.
  • Ministry of Finance (MoF): Regulates securities and may treat certain stablecoins as securities if they offer returns or are traded on exchanges.
  • Ministry of Information and Communications (MIC): Manages digital assets and cybersecurity, including data protection requirements.

There is no single "stablecoin license." Instead, you may need a combination of a business registration certificate, a payment intermediary license (if applicable), and AML registration with the SBV. As of 2026, the government is still drafting a dedicated crypto law, expected to be passed in 2027. Until then, the sandbox program allows limited testing of crypto activities under SBV supervision. Capital Requirement

Vietnam does not prescribe a specific minimum capital for stablecoin operations. For payment intermediary services, the SBV may impose higher capital thresholds, often exceeding VND 50 billion (EUR 1.9 million). In practice, foreign-owned stablecoin ventures often need to demonstrate sufficient capital to cover operational risks and AML compliance. Tax Treatment

Vietnam's tax treatment of stablecoins is still evolving. As of 2026, the following general principles apply:

  • Corporate Income Tax (CIT): Standard rate of 20% on profits from stablecoin issuance or trading. This applies to both local and foreign-owned entities.
  • Value Added Tax (VAT): Stablecoin transactions may be exempt from VAT if classified as financial services, but this is not settled. Some tax authorities treat crypto transactions as subject to 10% VAT.
  • Withholding Tax: Payments to foreign entities may be subject to 0.5-1% on gross revenue (depending on the nature of the service), plus a 10% VAT equivalent.
  • Personal Income Tax: Individuals earning from stablecoin activities are taxed at progressive rates (up to 35%). Capital gains from crypto are not explicitly defined.

Tax rulings are case-by-case. Consulting24 coordinates with Vietnamese tax advisors to obtain clarity for your specific model. Allowed Activities

Under the current sandbox and general laws, the following activities may be permissible for a stablecoin venture in Vietnam:

  • Issuance of fiat-backed stablecoins (e.g., VND-pegged) with SBV approval. For example, a stablecoin fully backed by VND reserves held at a licensed bank.
  • Exchange of stablecoins for other crypto assets (non-VND pairs). Direct VND conversion is restricted to licensed banks.
  • Provision of stablecoin payment services to licensed intermediaries, such as e-wallet providers.
  • Stablecoin lending and borrowing within a sandbox framework, subject to caps on total value.

Activities that are likely restricted include direct conversion to VND (unless through a licensed bank), retail payment services without a license, and algorithmic stablecoins that could threaten financial stability. Step-by-Step Process

Obtaining a crypto stablecoin license in Vietnam involves the following steps:

  1. Feasibility study: Assess whether your stablecoin model fits Vietnam's regulatory environment. Consulting24 provides an initial assessment, including a review of your business plan and legal structure.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a local entity (e.g., a limited liability company) with the Department of Planning and Investment. This requires a local address, a legal representative, and charter capital of at least VND 10 billion.
  3. AML compliance: Develop an AML/CFT policy, appoint a compliance officer, and register with the State Bank's Anti-Money Laundering Department. This includes implementing KYC procedures and transaction monitoring.
  4. Sandbox application (if applicable): Submit a proposal to the SBV detailing your stablecoin's design, risk management, and testing plan. The sandbox is limited to 12 months and may require a minimum capital of VND 50 billion.
  5. Operational setup: Open a corporate bank account (challenging, as noted below), implement KYC procedures, and deploy your stablecoin technology. You may need to partner with a local bank for fiat backing.
  6. Ongoing reporting: File periodic reports with the SBV and tax authorities, including audited financial statements and transaction data.

For stablecoin issuers, maintaining a VND settlement account is essential for fiat backing. Compliance and Trust

Compliance is critical for stablecoin operations in Vietnam. Key requirements include:

  • Implementing strong AML/KYC procedures aligned with FATF recommendations. This includes customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and reporting suspicious transactions to the SBV.
  • Conducting regular audits and reporting to regulators. Stablecoin issuers must provide proof of reserves (e.g., monthly attestations) if the stablecoin is fiat-backed.
  • Ensuring data protection under the Law on Cybersecurity and the Personal Data Protection Decree. This includes storing user data locally and obtaining consent.
  • Appointing a local compliance officer who is a resident of Vietnam.
