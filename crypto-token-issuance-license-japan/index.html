What Is the Crypto Token Issuance License Japan?

The crypto token issuance license Japan refers to the regulatory approval required to issue tokens (whether utility, security, or payment type) to Japanese residents or from within Japan. The exact licence depends on the token's classification. For security tokens (e.g., STOs), the issuer must register as a Type I Financial Instruments Business operator under the Financial Instruments and Exchange Act (FIEA). For utility tokens that are not securities, the issuer may need to register as a Crypto Asset Exchange Service provider under the Payment Services Act if the token is traded on an exchange. However, if the token is simply issued and not actively traded, a lighter registration may apply. The Financial Services Agency (FSA) is the sole regulator, and it enforces strict investor protection, anti-money laundering (AML), and cybersecurity standards.

Who Needs This License?

Any entity that plans to issue tokens to Japanese residents, or that operates a platform facilitating token sales to Japanese investors, needs this licence. This includes:

  • Startups conducting ICOs or STOs targeting Japanese investors
  • Foreign companies that want to offer tokens in Japan
  • Decentralized finance (DeFi) projects issuing governance tokens
  • Non-fungible token (NFT) platforms if the NFTs are deemed securities

Capital Requirement

Japan imposes minimum capital requirements for crypto token issuance licenses. For a Type I Financial Instruments Business (security tokens), the minimum capital is JPY 50 million (approx. EUR 300,000). For a Crypto Asset Exchange Service Provider, the minimum capital is JPY 10 million (approx. EUR 60,000). However, the FSA may require higher capital based on the scale of operations and risk profile. Capital must be held in liquid assets and cannot be borrowed. Additionally, the company must maintain positive net assets at all times. Consulting24 advises on structuring your capital to meet these requirements efficiently.

Tax Treatment

Japan's tax treatment of token issuance is complex. As of 2026, corporate income from token issuance is generally subject to corporate tax at the standard rate of 23.2% (effective rate may vary). For security tokens, income from issuance may be treated as capital gains or ordinary income depending on the structure. Utility tokens issued as a prepayment for services may be taxed upon issuance. Individual investors face progressive income tax rates (up to 55%) on crypto gains, but corporate issuers are taxed at corporate rates. Consumption tax (10%) applies to certain crypto transactions. The FSA and National Tax Agency have issued guidelines, but the landscape is evolving. Consulting24 recommends engaging a Japanese tax advisor to optimize your tax position.

Allowed Activities Under the License

Once licensed, an entity can:

  • Issue and distribute tokens to Japanese residents
  • Conduct ICOs/STOs in compliance with disclosure rules
  • Operate a token sale platform (if also registered as a crypto exchange)
  • Provide custody of issued tokens
  • Market and advertise token offerings (subject to restrictions)

Prohibited activities include issuing tokens that are clearly fraudulent, misleading investors, or violating AML laws. The FSA also restricts certain marketing practices, such as promising guaranteed returns. The licence does not automatically allow trading on exchanges; separate exchange registration is needed.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process for obtaining a crypto token issuance license in Japan involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application assessment: Determine the correct licence type based on your token's legal classification. Consulting24 can help with this initial analysis.
  2. Company incorporation: Establish a Japanese entity (Kabushiki Kaisha) with a registered office and local directors. Minimum capital must be deposited.
  3. Documentation preparation: Prepare a business plan, AML/CFT policies, risk management framework, financial projections, and token whitepaper. All documents must be in Japanese.
  4. Submission to FSA: File the application through the FSA's online portal. The FSA will review and may request additional information.
  5. Examination and interview: The FSA conducts a thorough examination, including interviews with management. This can take 3-6 months.
  6. Approval and registration: If approved, you receive a registration certificate. Post-licence, you must submit periodic reports and undergo audits.

Some regional banks and digital banks may offer accounts. You will need to provide your FSA registration certificate, proof of capital, and a detailed business plan. Benefits of the Japan License

Obtaining a crypto token issuance license in Japan offers several advantages:

  • Market credibility: Japan is one of the most respected regulatory regimes globally, enhancing trust with investors and partners.
  • Access to a large investor base: Japan has a sophisticated and wealthy investor population interested in digital assets.
  • Clear legal framework: The FSA provides detailed guidance, reducing legal uncertainty.
  • Strong banking relationships: Once licensed, access to mainstream banking improves.
  • Potential for future expansion: A Japan licence can serve as a gateway to other Asian markets.

However, the high cost and long timeline are trade-offs. For many projects, starting in a faster jurisdiction like Panama (EUR 6,000 flat fee) or Lithuania may be more practical initially, then expanding to Japan later.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

After obtaining the licence, the issuer must comply with ongoing requirements:

  • Regular reporting: Submit annual financial statements, audit reports, and AML compliance reports to the FSA.
  • Audits: Engage an external auditor approved by the FSA.
  • AML/CFT: Implement and maintain strong AML/KYC procedures, including transaction monitoring.
  • Cybersecurity: Maintain security standards for token custody and IT systems.
  • Disclosure: Provide ongoing disclosures to token holders, including financial health and material changes.

Non-compliance can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the licence. Consulting24 advises on compliance frameworks and can coordinate with local compliance officers. This is general guidance, not legal advice.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Many applicants face delays or rejections due to avoidable errors:

  • Misclassifying the token: Incorrectly treating a security token as a utility token can lead to enforcement action.
  • Insufficient capital: Failing to meet the minimum capital or not maintaining positive net assets.
  • Poor AML/KYC documentation: The FSA scrutinizes AML policies; generic documents are rejected.
  • Incomplete application: Missing documents or translations not certified.
  • Underestimating timeline: Expecting approval in less than 6 months is unrealistic.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a Type I Financial Instruments Business and a Crypto Asset Exchange Service Provider license?

A Type I FIB licence is for security tokens (STOs) and involves stricter capital requirements (JPY 50M) and full securities regulation. A Crypto Asset Exchange Service Provider licence is for utility tokens traded on exchanges, with a lower capital requirement (JPY 10M) but still subject to AML and operational rules.

Can a foreign company obtain a Japan crypto token issuance license?

Yes, but the company must have a registered branch or subsidiary in Japan with a physical office and local representatives. The FSA requires that the entity be subject to Japanese jurisdiction and oversight.

How long does the Japan crypto license application take?

The process typically takes 6 to 12 months from preparation to approval. The FSA's review alone can take 3 to 6 months. Delays are common if documents are incomplete or if the regulator requests additional information.

What are the ongoing compliance costs after obtaining the license?

Ongoing costs include annual audit fees (JPY 1-3M), compliance officer salaries, AML software, legal fees for reporting, and potential FSA inspection costs. Total annual compliance can range from JPY 5M to 15M.

Is there a minimum capital requirement for issuing utility tokens?

If the utility token is traded on an exchange, the issuer needs a Crypto Asset Exchange Service Provider licence with a minimum capital of JPY 10 million. For simple issuance without trading, there may be no formal capital requirement, but the FSA expects sufficient financial resources.

How are tokens taxed in Japan for the issuing company?

Corporate tax at approximately 23.2% applies to income from token issuance. The timing of taxation depends on whether the token is considered a prepayment or a capital gain. Consult a Japanese tax advisor for specifics.

Can I use the Japan license to issue tokens to investors in other countries?

The Japan licence only covers issuance to Japanese residents. For other countries, you need separate licences or exemptions. However, having a Japan licence can enhance credibility globally.

What happens if I issue tokens without a license in Japan?

Issuing tokens without the required licence is illegal and can result in criminal penalties, fines, and imprisonment. The FSA actively monitors and can shut down unlicensed operations.
