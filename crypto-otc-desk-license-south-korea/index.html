What Is the Crypto OTC Desk License in South Korea?

The crypto OTC desk license in South Korea is effectively a VASP registration for businesses that facilitate large-volume, off-exchange trades of virtual assets. An OTC desk matches buyers and sellers directly, often handling institutional-sized orders without impacting public order books. Under Korean law, any entity that conducts “virtual asset business” - including exchange, transfer, custody, or brokerage - must register with KoFIU.

Key features of the regime:

  • Registration with KoFIU under the Specific Financial Information Act.
  • Mandatory AML/CFT compliance including real-name accounts and transaction monitoring.
  • Partnership with a domestic bank to issue real-name accounts for customers.
  • Annual reporting and ongoing supervision by FSC/KoFIU.

The license is not a “crypto OTC desk license” in name but covers OTC activities as part of VASP registration. As of 2026, the regulatory framework is mature and enforcement is strict.

Who Needs a Crypto OTC Desk License in South Korea?

Any legal entity that provides OTC trading services for virtual assets to Korean residents must obtain VASP registration. This includes:

  • Institutional OTC desks serving hedge funds, asset managers, or high-net-worth individuals.
  • Brokerage firms that match buyers and sellers off-exchange.
  • Foreign entities that target Korean clients - they must establish a local subsidiary and register.
  • Market makers and liquidity providers that execute block trades.

Exemptions are narrow: if you only trade for your own account (not as a service) or provide non-custodial software, you may not need registration. However, any activity that involves holding or transferring customer assets triggers the requirement. Consulting24 advises on whether your business model falls under VASP obligations.

License Type and Regulator

The relevant license is the Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) registration under the Act on Reporting and Use of Specific Financial Transaction Information. The primary regulator is the Financial Services Commission (FSC), with day-to-day oversight by the Korea Financial Intelligence Unit (KoFIU), a division of the FSC.

There is no separate “OTC license” class; OTC services are considered a form of virtual asset exchange or brokerage. The registration is mandatory and non-transferable. As of 2026, the FSC has increased scrutiny on OTC desks due to money laundering risks, requiring enhanced due diligence on counterparties and transaction monitoring.

Capital Requirement

South Korea does not prescribe a specific minimum capital for VASP registration in law, but in practice, the FSC expects a substantial capital base to ensure operational stability and cover potential losses. As of 2026, the effective minimum is around KRW 1 billion (approximately EUR 700,000) for exchange-type services, including OTC desks. This capital must be held in a Korean bank account and may be subject to a lock-up period.

Capital is used to demonstrate financial soundness and to cover liabilities. The exact amount can be negotiated based on business volume and risk profile. Consulting24 advises on capital planning and structuring to meet regulatory expectations without overcapitalizing.

Tax Treatment

South Korea taxes virtual asset gains as “other income” at a flat rate of 20% (plus local surtax of 2%, effective 22%) for individuals, effective from 2025. For corporations, gains are included in corporate income taxed at 9-24% depending on revenue. OTC desks must also pay value-added tax (VAT) on commission fees at 10%.

Key points:

  • Corporate tax: standard CIT rates apply to net profits from OTC activities.
  • Withholding tax: no special withholding on crypto transactions, but reporting is mandatory.
  • Transfer pricing: foreign-related party transactions must be arm’s length.
  • Tax incentives: none specific to crypto; but R&D credits may apply for blockchain development.

Allowed Activities

With a VASP registration, an OTC desk may conduct the following activities:

  • Purchase and sale of virtual assets directly with clients (principal trading).
  • Brokerage of OTC trades between third parties (agency trading).
  • Block trading and large lot execution.
  • Provision of custody for virtual assets incidental to trading.
  • Market making services (if disclosed and compliant with fair trading rules).

Prohibited activities include: operating a public exchange without separate approval, offering derivatives (unless licensed as a financial investment firm), and engaging in unregistered lending or staking. The FSC restricts the use of privacy coins (e.g., Monero) and requires all listed assets to undergo a review process.

Step-by-Step Process

Obtaining a crypto OTC desk license in South Korea involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step with local partners.

  1. Company incorporation: Establish a local subsidiary (usually a stock company). Minimum capital of KRW 100 million (approx. EUR 70,000) is typical.
  2. ISMS certification: Obtain Information Security Management System certification from the Korea Internet & Security Agency (KISA). This covers data protection and cybersecurity.
  3. Bank partnership: Secure a real-name account issuance agreement with a domestic bank. This is the hardest step; banks often require a large deposit and proven compliance.
  4. Prepare documentation: Draft AML/CFT policies, business plan, risk management framework, and organizational chart.
  5. Submit application: File with KoFIU via the e-FIU system. Include all required documents and certifications.
  6. Review and interview: KoFIU may request additional information or conduct an interview with key personnel.
  7. Registration: Upon approval, the company is listed on the FSC’s VASP registry. Begin operations under ongoing supervision.

Total time: 6-12 months. Consulting24 provides a project plan and timeline during initial consultation.

Banking and Payments

Banking is the most challenging aspect of the South Korean crypto OTC desk license. Real-name accounts are mandatory for every customer transaction. As of 2026, only a few banks (e.g., Kookmin, Shinhan, Woori) offer crypto partnership accounts, and they impose strict conditions:

  • Minimum deposit of KRW 10-30 billion (EUR 7-21 million) for the partnership.
  • Comprehensive AML compliance review.
  • Real-time transaction monitoring integration.
  • Separation of customer and company funds.

Compliance and Trust

Operating a crypto OTC desk in South Korea requires a strong compliance framework. Key obligations include:

  • Customer due diligence (CDD) and enhanced due diligence (EDD) for high-risk clients.
  • Transaction monitoring for suspicious activity, with reporting to KoFIU.
  • Record keeping for at least 5 years.
  • Annual external audit and submission of compliance report.
  • Appointment of a compliance officer and a money laundering reporting officer (MLRO).

Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of registration. Consulting24 advises on building a compliance program that meets Korean standards. This is general guidance, not legal advice. Always consult with a Korean law firm for specific obligations.

Common Mistakes

Many applicants fail or face delays due to these errors:

  • Underestimating banking requirements: Not securing a bank partnership letter before filing leads to rejection.
  • Insufficient capital: Having less than KRW 1 billion (EUR 700k) is a red flag.
  • Poor documentation: AML policies must be detailed and tailored to OTC operations, not generic.
  • Ignoring ISMS certification: This is mandatory and takes 3-6 months to obtain.
  • Hiring unqualified compliance staff: KoFIU expects experienced personnel with Korean financial sector background.

Frequently asked questions

What is the crypto OTC desk license in South Korea?

It is not a standalone license but part of the VASP registration under the Specific Financial Information Act. It allows legal entities to conduct OTC trading of virtual assets for Korean residents, subject to AML/CFT compliance and bank partnership.

Who regulates crypto OTC desks in South Korea?

The Financial Services Commission (FSC) and its intelligence unit KoFIU are the primary regulators. They enforce the VASP registration regime and oversee compliance with anti-money laundering rules.

What is the minimum capital for a crypto OTC desk in South Korea?

There is no statutory minimum, but the FSC expects effective capital of around KRW 1 billion (EUR 700,000) to demonstrate financial stability. This is a practical requirement, not a fixed rule.

How long does it take to get a crypto OTC desk license in South Korea?

The process typically takes 6 to 12 months from company incorporation to final registration. Delays often arise from bank partnership negotiations and ISMS certification.

What are the tax implications for a crypto OTC desk in South Korea?

Corporate income is taxed at standard CIT rates (9-24%). Individual gains are taxed at 22% (20% + 2% surtax). Commission fees are subject to 10% VAT. Proper tax reporting is mandatory.

Can a foreign company apply for a South Korean crypto OTC desk license?

Yes, but only after establishing a local subsidiary (e.g., a stock company). The subsidiary must meet all requirements, including bank partnership and ISMS certification.

Is banking difficult for crypto OTC desks in South Korea?

Yes, it is the hardest part. Banks require a real-name account partnership, often demanding large deposits (KRW 10-30 billion) and rigorous AML compliance. Few banks offer this service.

What activities are allowed under the South Korean VASP registration for OTC desks?

Permitted activities include principal trading, brokerage of OTC trades, block trading, custody incidental to trading, and market making. Derivatives and unregistered lending are prohibited.

How does South Korea compare to Panama for crypto OTC licensing?

Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 license with no minimum capital and faster setup (4-6 weeks). South Korea is more expensive (EUR 30,000+), requires higher capital (~EUR 700k), and takes longer. Panama is ideal for early-stage firms.
