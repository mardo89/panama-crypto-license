Regulatory developments that change how, where and how fast a crypto business can be licensed. Every item names the primary source it reports on and links straight to it.

  • European Union 22 July 2026

    ESMA launches supervisory action on crypto custody resilience

    The European Securities and Markets Authority is launching a Common Supervisory Action focusing on the digital operational resilience of Crypto-Asset Service Providers, with a specific emphasis on custody services.

    Source: European Securities and Markets Authority (ESMA)

  • European Union 21 July 2026

    ESMA tells unauthorised crypto firms to wind down EU operations as MiCA transition ends

    ESMA has set out what crypto-asset service providers without a MiCA authorisation must do now that the transitional period has closed: stop onboarding EU clients, wind down in an orderly way, and keep AML controls running throughout.

    Source: ESMA public statement ESMA75-113276571-1710
