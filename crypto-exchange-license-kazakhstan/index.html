What Is a Crypto Exchange License in Kazakhstan?

A crypto exchange license in Kazakhstan is a regulatory authorization granted by AFSA, the financial regulator of the AIFC. It permits a legal entity to operate a digital asset trading platform, matching buyers and sellers of cryptocurrencies. The license is part of the AIFC's framework for fintech and digital assets, designed to attract international investment while ensuring compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) standards.

Key features include:

  • Legal recognition for exchange activities within the AIFC.
  • Ongoing supervision by AFSA.
  • Access to the AIFC's common law legal system and tax incentives.

This license is distinct from a simple registration; it involves a full application and compliance process. Consulting24 advises and coordinates with local legal experts to ensure your application meets AFSA's requirements. The AIFC's regulatory framework is based on international standards, including IOSCO and FATF recommendations, which provides credibility for global operations.

For example, an exchange that holds a Kazakhstan license can more easily partner with international banks and payment providers, as the regulatory oversight is recognized. The license also allows the exchange to list tokens approved by AFSA, which can include major cryptocurrencies and selected utility tokens. The scope of activities is defined in the license, and any expansion requires prior approval.

Who Needs a Crypto Exchange License in Kazakhstan?

Any entity planning to operate a cryptocurrency exchange platform that serves clients within or from Kazakhstan should consider this license. This includes:

  • Centralized exchanges (CEX) offering spot trading, margin trading, or futures.
  • Decentralized exchanges (DEX) if they have a central management or profit motive.
  • Brokerage services that match orders or provide liquidity.
  • White-label exchange operators.

If you are already licensed in another jurisdiction, a Kazakh license may be necessary if you target Kazakh residents or want to benefit from the AIFC's tax regime. For a cost-effective alternative, compare with Panama where the company setup is a flat EUR 6,000, though the regulatory framework differs.

Additionally, businesses that provide crypto-to-fiat conversion services, over-the-counter (OTC) trading desks, or institutional trading platforms may also require this license. The AFSA rulebook defines a Digital Asset Trading Facility broadly to include any system that facilitates the exchange of digital assets. If your platform allows users to place orders that are matched against other users or against the platform's own inventory, you likely fall under this definition.

For startups, it is important to assess whether the license is required at all. If you are only providing wallet services or advisory services, a different or no license may suffice. Consulting24 can help you determine the exact licensing needs based on your business model.

License Type & Regulator

The relevant regulator is the Astana Financial Services Authority (AFSA), operating under the Astana International Financial Centre (AIFC). AFSA grants a "Digital Asset Trading Facility" (DATF) license for crypto exchanges. This is a full financial services license that covers operating a trading platform for digital assets.

Key regulatory points:

  • AFSA is an independent regulator with a rulebook based on international standards (IOSCO, FATF).
  • The license is issued to a company incorporated in the AIFC (usually a Private Company or Special Purpose Company).
  • There is no separate "crypto license" class; it falls under the general financial services regime for digital assets.

Consulting24 advises and coordinates with AIFC-registered agents to handle the licensing process. For a simpler alternative, see our Lithuania crypto license page, which operates under a different EU regime.

The DATF license is divided into two categories: for retail clients and for professional clients. The retail license has stricter requirements, including higher capital and more detailed disclosure obligations. Most new entrants apply for the professional license first, then upgrade as they grow. AFSA also imposes fit and proper tests on all directors and senior management, including background checks and interviews.

Capital Requirement

AFSA requires a minimum capital for Digital Asset Trading Facility license holders. As of 2026, the minimum paid-up capital is approximately EUR 100,000 (or equivalent in KZT, subject to exchange rate). This capital must be maintained at all times and can be in cash or liquid assets.

Additional capital may be required based on the volume of trading or risk profile. The capital serves as a buffer for operational risk and client protection. Consulting24 advises on structuring the capital contribution and ensuring compliance with AFSA's prudential requirements.

The capital must be held in a bank account in the name of the AIFC company, and the source of funds must be documented. AFSA may also require a surety bond or insurance for certain activities. For comparison, EU CASP licenses under MiCA require EUR 125,000 for exchanges, so Kazakhstan's requirement is competitive. However, the capital must be fully paid up before the license is issued, not just committed.

If the exchange plans to offer margin trading or derivatives, the capital requirement may increase to EUR 200,000 or more. Consulting24 can help you determine the exact amount based on your business plan.

Tax Treatment

Companies licensed under the AIFC enjoy a favorable tax regime. Key points:

  • Corporate income tax: 0% for the first 10 years (until 2035) for AIFC-registered entities.
  • No VAT on financial services provided within the AIFC.
  • Withholding tax on dividends and interest: 0% if paid to non-residents.
  • No capital gains tax on disposal of digital assets (subject to conditions).

These incentives make Kazakhstan attractive for crypto exchanges. However, tax treatment depends on the specific structure and activities. Consulting24 recommends consulting with a tax advisor for your situation. For comparison, see Estonia crypto license where corporate income tax is 20% on distributed profits.

It is important to note that the 0% corporate tax applies only to income derived from activities within the AIFC. If the exchange also has operations outside the AIFC (e.g., a branch in another country), that income may be taxed separately. Additionally, the tax holiday is not automatic; the company must apply for it and meet certain conditions, such as having a physical presence in the AIFC.

For employees, personal income tax in Kazakhstan is progressive up to 10%, but AIFC employees may benefit from a reduced rate of 5% for the first 10 years. This can be a significant advantage when hiring talent.

Allowed Activities

With a Digital Asset Trading Facility license, you can engage in the following activities:

  • Operating a platform for the exchange of digital assets against fiat or other digital assets.
  • Providing custody services for digital assets.
  • Matching orders and executing trades.
  • Offering margin trading (with additional approval).
  • Listing digital assets for trading (subject to AFSA approval of tokens).

Activities not permitted without further licensing: banking, insurance, or issuing securities (unless under a separate license). Consulting24 advises on the scope of activities and any restrictions.

AFSA maintains a whitelist of approved tokens that can be traded. Exchanges must apply for each token to be listed, providing a legal opinion and risk assessment. This process can take 2-4 weeks per token. Stablecoins are treated as digital assets and require separate approval. For fiat-backed stablecoins, the issuer must hold a corresponding license.

If the exchange wants to offer staking or lending services, these are considered separate regulated activities and may require an additional license or amendment. Consulting24 can help you structure your business to include these services under the DATF license where possible.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process for obtaining a crypto exchange license in Kazakhstan involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step.

  1. Pre-application: Determine the appropriate corporate structure and prepare business plan, AML/KYC policies, and risk management framework.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a company with the AIFC Registrar. This includes appointing directors, shareholders, and a registered office.
  3. License application: Submit the application to AFSA with all required documents (business plan, financial projections, compliance manual, etc.).
  4. Review and interview: AFSA reviews the application and may request additional information or an interview with key personnel.
  5. License issuance: Upon approval, pay the license fee and receive the license certificate. The license is subject to ongoing supervision.
  6. Post-licensing: Set up banking, implement systems, and commence operations. Annual reporting and audits are required.

Typical timeline: 3-6 months. For a faster process, see application process tips.

During the pre-application phase, it is critical to prepare a detailed business plan that includes projected trading volumes, revenue streams, and risk management strategies. AFSA expects the plan to be realistic and based on market data. The AML/KYC policies must be aligned with FATF recommendations and include procedures for customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting.

The interview with AFSA typically involves the CEO, compliance officer, and money laundering reporting officer (MLRO). They will be asked about their experience, the business model, and how they will ensure compliance. Consulting24 can provide interview preparation and mock sessions.

Banking & Payment Services

Opening a bank account for an AIFC-licensed exchange can be challenging due to compliance concerns. Options include:

  • Local banks in Kazakhstan (e.g., Halyk Bank, Kaspi Bank) but many are cautious with crypto.
  • International banks with presence in the AIFC (e.g., some European banks).
  • EMI (Electronic Money Institution) accounts that support fiat on/off ramps.

Consulting24 advises on banking partners and can coordinate introductions. For a jurisdiction with more established banking relationships, see Lithuania crypto license.

Many exchanges use a combination of a local bank account for operating expenses and an EMI for client funds. The AIFC does not require client funds to be held in segregated accounts, but it is best practice. Some EMIs like CurrencyCloud or TransferWise (now Wise) are open to crypto businesses with an AFSA license. However, banks may still require a personal guarantee from directors or a minimum deposit.

Compliance & Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, you must comply with AFSA's ongoing requirements:

  • Annual AML/CFT audit by an approved auditor.
  • Submission of financial statements and regulatory returns.
  • Maintenance of minimum capital.
  • Reporting of suspicious transactions.
  • Periodic review of policies and procedures.

Failure to comply can result in fines or license revocation. Consulting24 can assist with compliance outsourcing. This is general guidance, not legal advice.

AFSA conducts periodic inspections, both on-site and off-site. They may request transaction data, client records, and compliance reports. It is essential to maintain accurate records for at least 5 years. The annual AML audit must be conducted by an external auditor approved by AFSA. The cost of this audit is typically EUR 5,000-10,000 per year.

Additionally, the exchange must appoint a Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO) who is resident in Kazakhstan or the AIFC. The MLRO is personally responsible for compliance. Consulting24 can help you find a qualified MLRO or train an existing team member.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Applying for a crypto exchange license in Kazakhstan can be complex. Avoid these common pitfalls:

  • Incomplete application: Missing documents or insufficient detail can delay the process.
  • Underestimating capital requirements: Ensure you have the minimum capital available and can prove source of funds.
  • Poor AML/KYC framework: AFSA scrutinizes compliance policies; they must be strong and tailored to your business.
  • Ignoring tax implications: Even with incentives, you must file returns.
  • Choosing the wrong corporate structure: A local director may be required.

Consulting24 advises on best practices to avoid these issues.

Another common mistake is not preparing for the interview. AFSA may reject applicants who cannot clearly explain their business model or compliance procedures. It is also important to have a realistic timeline; some applicants expect the license in 2 months, but 4-6 months is more realistic. Finally, do not assume that a license in another jurisdiction will expedite the process in Kazakhstan. AFSA conducts its own due diligence from scratch.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto exchange license in Kazakhstan?

The minimum paid-up capital is approximately EUR 100,000 (or equivalent in KZT). This must be maintained at all times and can be in cash or liquid assets. Higher capital may be required for margin trading or higher risk profiles.

How long does it take to get a crypto exchange license in Kazakhstan?

The typical timeline is 3 to 6 months from application submission. Pre-application preparation may take 1-2 months. The total process including company incorporation can take 4-8 months.

What is the tax rate for crypto exchanges in Kazakhstan?

AIFC-registered companies enjoy 0% corporate income tax for the first 10 years (until 2035). There is no VAT on financial services within the AIFC, and no withholding tax on dividends or interest paid to non-residents.

Can I operate a crypto exchange in Kazakhstan without a license?

No. Operating a digital asset trading facility without a license from AFSA is illegal and can result in fines, closure, and criminal liability. All exchanges serving Kazakh residents must be licensed.

What activities are allowed under a Digital Asset Trading Facility license?

Allowed activities include operating a trading platform, custody of digital assets, order matching, and margin trading (with approval). Listing tokens requires AFSA approval. Banking and insurance are not permitted without separate licenses.

Does Kazakhstan accept foreign companies for licensing?

The license is issued to a company incorporated in the AIFC. Foreign companies cannot directly hold the license; they must set up a subsidiary in the AIFC. The subsidiary must have a physical office and local directors.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

Ongoing requirements include annual AML/CFT audits, submission of financial statements and regulatory returns, maintenance of minimum capital, reporting suspicious transactions, and periodic policy reviews. Non-compliance can lead to fines or license revocation.

How does Kazakhstan compare to Panama for crypto exchanges?

Panama offers a faster and cheaper setup (EUR 6,000 flat) with no specific crypto regulation, but lacks legal clarity. Kazakhstan provides a clear regulatory framework, tax incentives, and international credibility, but at a higher cost and longer timeline.

Can I use a Kazakhstan license to serve clients in the EU?

No. A Kazakhstan license does not provide EU passporting. To serve EU clients, you need a license under MiCA in an EU member state. However, the Kazakhstan license is recognized in some non-EU countries.

What are the banking options for crypto exchanges in Kazakhstan?

Banking options include local Kazakh banks (cautious with crypto), international banks with AIFC presence, and EMIs. Consulting24 can coordinate introductions. It is advisable to start banking early as it can take 2-3 months.

What is the difference between a retail and professional DATF license?

A retail license allows serving individual clients and has stricter requirements, including higher capital (EUR 150,000) and more disclosure. A professional license is for institutional clients and has lower capital (EUR 100,000) and simpler rules.
