What Is the Offshore Cyprus Crypto License?

The offshore Cyprus crypto license is a regulatory authorization issued by the Cyprus Securities and Exchange Commission (CySEC) under MiCA. It allows companies to provide crypto-asset services such as exchange, custody, and advisory services to clients in Cyprus and across the EU via passporting. The term "offshore" typically refers to a structure where the company is registered in Cyprus but serves non-resident clients, often benefiting from the country's favorable tax regime.

Key features of the license include:

  • Full compliance with MiCA, ensuring EU-wide recognition.
  • Minimum capital requirements: EUR 50,000 for simple services (e.g., advisory), EUR 125,000 for exchange/custody, and EUR 150,000 for combined services.
  • Corporate tax rate of 12.5% on profits, with potential exemptions for foreign-sourced income under certain conditions.
  • CySEC as the primary regulator, known for its rigorous but fair oversight.

Cyprus has a long history as a financial hub, with a legal system based on English common law. This makes it familiar to international investors and provides a stable environment for crypto businesses. The offshore structure is particularly appealing because it allows non-resident companies to minimize tax exposure while accessing the EU market.

Who Needs an Offshore Cyprus Crypto License?

Any business that intends to offer crypto-asset services to clients in Cyprus or other EU member states must obtain a Cyprus CASP license. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralized or decentralized if they have a central operator).
  • Custodial wallet providers.
  • Brokers and dealers in crypto-assets.
  • Advisory firms providing investment advice on crypto.
  • Portfolio managers handling crypto assets.
  • Underwriters and placing agents for crypto-assets.

Offshore structures are particularly attractive for non-EU founders who want to establish a European presence without relocating. The Cyprus license allows you to serve EU clients while maintaining a tax-efficient corporate structure. License Type & Regulator

The regulator for crypto-asset services in Cyprus is the Cyprus Securities and Exchange Commission (CySEC). Under MiCA, CySEC issues a single license type: the Crypto-Asset Service Provider (CASP) license. This replaces the earlier registration-only regime that required no minimum capital. As of 2026, all CASPs must meet the full MiCA requirements, including:

  • Minimum capital as per service class.
  • Fit and proper management.
  • Strong AML/CFT controls.
  • Secure custody of client assets.
  • Transparent disclosure of risks and fees.

CySEC is known for its strict enforcement and high standards. Applicants should expect a thorough due diligence process, including background checks on shareholders and directors. The license is valid indefinitely, subject to ongoing compliance and annual fees. CySEC also requires that the company have a physical presence in Cyprus, including a registered office and local management (or at least a local compliance officer). This ensures that the regulator can effectively supervise the entity.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital for a Cyprus CASP is determined by the type of services offered:

  • EUR 50,000 for simple services such as advisory or reception and transmission of orders.
  • EUR 125,000 for exchange services (crypto-to-fiat or crypto-to-crypto) and custody.
  • EUR 150,000 for a combination of exchange, custody, and other high-risk services.

This capital must be fully paid up and maintained at all times. It can be held as cash or liquid assets. CySEC may require additional capital based on the volume of transactions or risk profile of the business. The capital serves as a buffer to protect clients and ensure operational resilience. For instance, a company offering both exchange and custody services must hold at least EUR 150,000 in capital, and if transaction volumes exceed certain thresholds, CySEC may demand a higher amount.

Tax Treatment

Cyprus offers one of the most attractive corporate tax regimes in the EU:

  • Corporate income tax rate: 12.5% on net profits.
  • Exemptions: Foreign-sourced income may be exempt if the company is not managed and controlled in Cyprus (offshore structure).
  • No withholding tax on dividends paid to non-resident shareholders.
  • VAT: 19% standard rate, but crypto services may be exempt under certain conditions.
  • Capital gains tax: 0% on gains from the sale of securities (including crypto if classified as securities).

Proper tax structuring is essential to maximize benefits. Consulting24 works with local tax advisors to ensure compliance and optimize your tax position. For example, a company that is managed and controlled outside Cyprus can claim that its income is not sourced in Cyprus, thereby reducing its tax liability to zero on foreign-sourced income. However, careful documentation is needed to avoid scrutiny from tax authorities.

Allowed Activities

A Cyprus CASP license permits the following activities under MiCA:

  • Operating a trading platform for crypto-assets.
  • Exchanging crypto-assets for fiat currency or other crypto-assets.
  • Custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients.
  • Reception and transmission of orders for crypto-assets.
  • Providing investment advice on crypto-assets.
  • Portfolio management of crypto-assets.
  • Underwriting and placing of crypto-assets.

Each activity may require additional permissions or higher capital. The license does not cover security tokens or asset-referenced tokens (e-money tokens), which are regulated under separate frameworks. For a full list of permitted activities, consult with CySEC or your advisory team. For instance, if you plan to offer both exchange and custody services, you need to meet the EUR 150,000 capital requirement and have separate policies for each activity.

  8. Approval and licensing: Once approved, you receive the CASP license and can commence operations.

Banking & Payments

Opening a bank account for a Cyprus crypto company can be challenging due to the perceived risk. However, Cyprus has several banks that accept crypto businesses, especially those with a CASP license. Options include:

  • Traditional banks: Bank of Cyprus, Hellenic Bank (with strict compliance requirements).
  • EMI (Electronic Money Institution) accounts: More flexible, often with faster onboarding.
  • International payment gateways: For fiat on/off ramps.

Consulting24 can introduce you to banking partners who understand the crypto sector. Expect enhanced due diligence, including source of funds and business plan review. A licensed entity has a higher chance of approval. For instance, a licensed exchange can open a corporate account with Bank of Cyprus after providing the license, audited financials, and a detailed business plan. EMIs like Revolut Business or Paysera are also options, but they may have lower transaction limits.

Benefits of an Offshore Cyprus Crypto License

Choosing Cyprus for your crypto license offers several advantages:

  • EU passporting: Serve clients across all EU member states without additional licenses.
  • Low corporate tax: 12.5% with potential exemptions for offshore income.
  • Established legal system: Based on English common law, familiar to international investors.
  • Skilled workforce: Access to multilingual professionals in finance and law.
  • Good infrastructure: Modern telecommunications, reliable internet, and a strategic location.
  • No capital gains tax on crypto: If classified as securities, gains are tax-free.

Compared to other EU jurisdictions like Malta or Lithuania, Cyprus offers a balanced mix of cost, speed, and regulatory clarity. For example, while Malta also has a 5% tax rate for certain structures, its licensing process can be slower. Lithuania's license is cheaper but does not offer the same tax benefits. Cyprus strikes a good balance.

Compliance & Trust

Maintaining a Cyprus CASP license requires ongoing compliance with MiCA and AML regulations. Key obligations include:

  • Annual audit by a Cyprus-approved auditor.
  • Submission of periodic reports to CySEC (financial, operational, and AML).
  • Appointment of a compliance officer and AML officer (can be outsourced).
  • Implementation of strong KYC/AML procedures.
  • Secure custody of client assets, with segregation and insurance where required.

Common Mistakes

Many applicants make avoidable errors when applying for a Cyprus crypto license. Common mistakes include:

  • Insufficient capital: Not meeting the minimum capital requirement or failing to maintain it.
  • Poor AML documentation: Submitting generic or incomplete AML policies that do not meet CySEC standards.
  • Lack of local presence: Not having a registered office or local compliance officer, which is mandatory.
  • Underestimating timeline: Expecting approval in a few months when the process typically takes 6-12 months.
  • Ignoring ongoing compliance: Assuming the license is one-time and not preparing for annual audits and reports.

Alternatives: Cyprus vs Panama, Estonia, and Lithuania

When choosing a jurisdiction for your crypto license, consider these alternatives:

  • Panama: Panama offers a flat fee of EUR 6,000 for a crypto license, with no minimum capital and no corporate tax on offshore income. However, it does not provide EU passporting. Ideal for non-EU clients.
  • Estonia: Estonia crypto license is cheaper (around EUR 3,000 application fee) but requires a local presence and has a 20% corporate tax. No passporting outside Estonia.
  • Lithuania: Lithuania crypto license has a lower capital requirement (EUR 125,000) and faster processing (3-6 months), but corporate tax is 15%. Offers EU passporting under MiCA.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Cyprus crypto license?

Under MiCA, the minimum capital is EUR 50,000 for advisory services, EUR 125,000 for exchange or custody, and EUR 150,000 for combined services. This capital must be fully paid up and maintained at all times.

How long does it take to get a Cyprus crypto license?

The process typically takes 6 to 12 months from application to approval. The timeline depends on the completeness of your application and CySEC's workload. Consulting24 helps expedite the process by preparing thorough documentation.

Can I serve clients in other EU countries with a Cyprus license?

Yes, a Cyprus CASP license allows passporting across all EU member states. You can provide services to clients in any EU country without needing additional licenses, subject to notification requirements.

What is the corporate tax rate in Cyprus for crypto companies?

The standard corporate tax rate is 12.5%. Offshore structures may benefit from exemptions on foreign-sourced income if the company is not managed and controlled in Cyprus. No withholding tax on dividends to non-residents.

Do I need a physical office in Cyprus?

Yes, you need a registered office in Cyprus and a local compliance officer or management presence. This can be arranged through service providers. CySEC requires a physical address for supervision.

What activities are allowed under a Cyprus CASP license?

Allowed activities include operating a trading platform, exchange services, custody, order reception and transmission, investment advice, portfolio management, and underwriting. Each activity may have specific capital requirements.

Is it difficult to open a bank account for a Cyprus crypto company?

It can be challenging, but licensed entities have a higher chance. Traditional banks like Bank of Cyprus and Hellenic Bank accept crypto businesses with enhanced due diligence. EMIs offer faster alternatives.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

Ongoing requirements include annual audits, periodic reports to CySEC, appointment of a compliance officer, KYC/AML procedures, and secure custody of client assets. Non-compliance can lead to fines or license revocation.

How does Cyprus compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Panama offers a flat fee of EUR 6,000 with no minimum capital and no corporate tax on offshore income, but no EU passporting. Cyprus has higher costs but provides access to the EU market and a lower tax rate of 12.5%.
