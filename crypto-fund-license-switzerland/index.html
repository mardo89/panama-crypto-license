What Is a Crypto Fund License in Switzerland?

A crypto fund license in Switzerland is a regulatory authorization issued by FINMA that allows a fund management company to operate a collective investment scheme focused on crypto assets. Under Swiss law, crypto funds are typically structured as contractual funds (Anlagefonds) or limited partnerships (Kommanditgesellschaft für kollektive Kapitalanlagen, KKK). The license is required for any entity that manages assets of third parties in the form of a fund, including funds that invest primarily in cryptocurrencies, tokens, or blockchain-based assets.

FINMA classifies crypto assets as either payment tokens (like Bitcoin), utility tokens, or asset tokens. Funds investing in these assets must comply with the Collective Investment Schemes Act (CISA) and related FINMA regulations. The license ensures that the fund manager meets standards of proper business conduct, risk management, and investor protection. As of 2026, Switzerland maintains its own regulatory regime, distinct from the EU's MiCA, offering a tailored approach for crypto funds.

Who Needs a Swiss Crypto Fund License?

The following entities typically require a Swiss crypto fund license:

  • Fund management companies that establish and manage crypto collective investment schemes.
  • Asset managers of Swiss domiciled funds that invest more than a de minimis amount in crypto assets (thresholds set by FINMA).
  • Foreign fund managers who wish to distribute crypto funds to Swiss qualified investors.
  • Blockchain startups launching tokenized funds or decentralized autonomous organization (DAO) structures that qualify as collective investment schemes.

Exemptions may apply for funds offered exclusively to qualified investors with a limited number of participants, but professional advice is essential to determine if an exemption is available. License Type and Regulator

The primary regulator is the Swiss Financial Market Supervisory Authority (FINMA), headquartered in Bern. FINMA oversees all collective investment schemes under CISA. The license type is typically a 'license as a fund management company' (Fondsleitung) or a 'license as a limited partnership for collective investments' (KKK). For crypto funds, FINMA applies its existing framework with additional guidance on custody, valuation, and risk management of digital assets.

FINMA has issued guidelines on the treatment of initial coin offerings (ICOs) and asset tokens, which also apply to fund structures. The license is not a separate 'crypto license' but a standard fund license adapted for crypto assets. As of 2026, FINMA continues to refine its approach, with a focus on anti-money laundering (AML) compliance and technological neutrality.

Capital Requirement

Under Swiss law, a fund management company must have a minimum capital of at least CHF 500,000 (approximately EUR 520,000 as of 2026). This capital must be fully paid up and held in liquid assets. For limited partnerships (KKK), the minimum capital requirement is typically CHF 500,000 as well, but the structure may require additional capital based on the fund's risk profile.

FINMA may require higher capital if the fund's activities or risk exposure warrant it. The capital must be maintained throughout the license period. Consulting24 can help structure your capital to meet regulatory requirements efficiently.

Tax Treatment

Switzerland offers a favorable tax environment for crypto funds. Fund vehicles are generally exempt from corporate income tax at the fund level if they meet certain criteria (e.g., distributing at least 80% of income). Investors are taxed on distributions and capital gains at their personal tax rates. Crypto assets are treated as assets for tax purposes, and gains from trading by professional investors may be subject to income tax, while private investors often enjoy tax-free capital gains.

Switzerland has no capital gains tax for private individuals, but professional traders and fund managers are taxed. Stamp duty may apply on the issuance of fund units. Value-added tax (VAT) is generally not applicable on fund management services. As of 2026, Switzerland continues to refine its tax treatment of digital assets, with guidance from the Swiss Federal Tax Administration (FTA). Allowed Activities

With a Swiss crypto fund license, a fund management company may engage in the following activities:

  • Establish and manage collective investment schemes that invest in crypto assets (payment tokens, utility tokens, asset tokens).
  • Offer fund units to qualified investors (and, under certain conditions, to non-qualified investors).
  • Provide portfolio management and risk management services for the fund.
  • Custody of crypto assets (subject to FINMA's custody guidelines, often requiring a licensed custodian).
  • Engage in staking, lending, or other yield-generating activities within the fund's investment strategy, if disclosed.

Activities not covered include operating a crypto exchange, issuing stablecoins, or providing payment services, which require separate licenses. The fund must adhere to its investment strategy as disclosed in the prospectus.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process for obtaining a Swiss crypto fund license involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step.

  1. Pre-application assessment: Determine the appropriate fund structure (contractual fund or KKK) and ensure eligibility.
  2. Documentation preparation: Prepare a business plan, fund prospectus, organizational regulations, AML policies, risk management framework, and audited financial statements.
  3. Capital deposit: Deposit the minimum capital in a Swiss bank account.
  4. Appointment of key personnel: Ensure the fund manager and board members meet FINMA's fit and proper requirements.
  5. Submission to FINMA: Submit the application with all supporting documents and pay the fee.
  6. FINMA review: FINMA assesses the application, may request additional information, and conducts interviews.
  7. License grant: Upon approval, FINMA issues the license, and the fund can begin operations.
  8. Post-licensing compliance: Ongoing reporting, AML audits, and annual financial statements.

The entire process typically takes 6-12 months. Consulting24 coordinates with Swiss legal partners to ensure a smooth application.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

After obtaining the license, the fund management company must comply with ongoing requirements:

  • Annual financial audits by a licensed auditor.
  • Regular AML reporting and suspicious transaction reporting.
  • Maintenance of minimum capital at all times.
  • Periodic reporting to FINMA on fund activities and risk.
  • Updates to prospectus and regulatory filings for material changes.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make these errors when applying for a Swiss crypto fund license:

  • Inadequate AML framework: FINMA expects strong AML policies tailored to crypto assets.
  • Underestimating capital needs: The minimum CHF 500,000 must be fully paid and maintained.
  • Poor documentation: Incomplete or inconsistent business plans and prospectuses cause delays.
  • Ignoring fit and proper requirements: Key personnel must have clean records and relevant experience.
  • Choosing the wrong structure: Not all fund structures suit crypto assets; professional advice is crucial.
