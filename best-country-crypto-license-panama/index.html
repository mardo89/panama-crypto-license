What Is the Panama Crypto License?

The Panama crypto license is not a traditional license but a registration with the Superintendencia de Bancos de Panamá (SBP) under Law 23 of 2015, as amended. This registration allows companies to legally operate virtual asset service provider (VASP) activities in or from Panama. It covers a range of crypto-related services including exchange, custody, and payment processing.

Panama's framework is unique because it does not impose a minimum capital requirement, making it one of the most accessible jurisdictions for startups and established firms alike. The process is straightforward: incorporate a Panama company, register with the SBP, and comply with ongoing reporting obligations.

For founders comparing jurisdictions, Panama offers a compelling alternative to EU member states where MiCA is fully in force, requiring capital of EUR 50,000 to 150,000 depending on services. Panama's simplicity is a major advantage for those who want to launch quickly without a large upfront investment.

However, Panama is not suitable for firms that need to serve Panama's domestic market directly, as the regulator expects activities to be primarily international. License Type and Regulator

The Panama crypto regime is a registration, not a full license. The regulator is the Superintendencia de Bancos de Panamá (SBP), which oversees financial activities including virtual asset services. The registration is issued under Law 23 of 2015 (as amended by Law 70 of 2019 and subsequent regulations).

There is only one type of registration for VASPs, covering all permitted activities. Unlike some jurisdictions that have multiple license tiers (e.g., exchange vs. custody), Panama's registration is comprehensive. This simplicity reduces administrative burden and costs.

It's important to note that Panama is not a member of the EU, so MiCA does not apply. However, Panama has committed to FATF recommendations and has implemented anti-money laundering (AML) requirements. The SBP expects registrants to have strong AML/KYC policies and a local compliance officer.

Capital Requirement

Panama has no minimum capital requirement for crypto registration. This is a key differentiator from other jurisdictions. Under MiCA, EU member states require capital of EUR 50,000 for simple services (e.g., custody) up to EUR 150,000 for complex services (e.g., exchange and trading). Panama's zero capital requirement means you can allocate your funds to business growth rather than locking them up.

While there is no legal minimum, the SBP may expect a company to have sufficient capital to operate responsibly. Typically, a paid-up capital of USD 10,000-50,000 is recommended to demonstrate financial substance, but this is not a regulatory requirement. Tax Treatment

Panama operates a territorial tax system. Income earned outside Panama is not subject to Panamanian income tax. Since most crypto license holders serve international clients, their income is typically sourced outside Panama and therefore tax-free in Panama. This is a major advantage for founders looking to minimize tax burden.

There is no capital gains tax on crypto transactions in Panama, no VAT on crypto services (if provided to non-residents), and no withholding tax on dividends paid to foreign shareholders. However, income sourced within Panama (e.g., from Panamanian residents) is taxed at 25% corporate income tax.

Founders should still consult with a tax advisor to ensure compliance in their home country. Panama's tax regime is transparent and favorable, but it does not automatically exempt you from taxes elsewhere.

Allowed Activities

The Panama crypto registration permits the following activities:

  • Exchange of virtual assets for fiat currency or other virtual assets
  • Custody and safekeeping of virtual assets
  • Virtual asset payment services (issuance, processing, settlement)
  • Token issuance and distribution (including ICOs/STOs)
  • Operation of a virtual asset trading platform (centralized or decentralized)

Activities that are prohibited include: lending or borrowing of virtual assets (unless separately licensed), derivatives trading, and activities that violate AML laws. The SBP may also restrict services to Panamanian residents if the company does not have a local banking license.

If you plan to offer services that require a MiCA license to EU residents, Panama may not be sufficient. However, for non-EU markets, Panama's registration is widely accepted.

Step-by-Step Process

Obtaining the Panama crypto registration involves the following steps:

  1. Company Incorporation: Register a Panama corporation (Sociedad Anónima) with a local registered agent. This takes 1-2 weeks.
  2. Document Preparation: Prepare AML/KYC policies, business plan, organizational structure, and due diligence on directors and shareholders. Consulting24 assists with templates and review.
  3. Submission to SBP: File the registration application with the Superintendencia de Bancos de Panamá, including all required documents and the application fee.
  4. SBP Review: The SBP reviews the application, which typically takes 4-6 weeks. They may request additional information or clarifications.
  5. Registration Approval: Once approved, the company is registered as a VASP and can commence operations.
  6. Ongoing Compliance: Submit annual reports, maintain AML/KYC records, and appoint a local compliance officer.

Compliance and Trust

While Panama's registration is simpler than a full license, it still requires strong compliance. The SBP expects registrants to implement AML/KYC procedures, appoint a local compliance officer, and submit annual reports. Failure to comply can result in fines or revocation of registration.

Consulting24 helps clients establish a compliance framework that meets SBP standards. We also recommend engaging a local AML officer who understands Panamanian regulations. This is general guidance, not legal advice. Each company should consult with a qualified lawyer.

Trust is built through transparency. Panama's regulatory framework is recognized by FATF, and the SBP is a member of the International Association of Insurance Supervisors (IAIS) and other bodies. While not as strict as EU regulators, Panama's regime is credible for international operations.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make these mistakes when pursuing a Panama crypto registration:

  • Assuming no compliance needed: Even without capital requirements, AML/KYC policies are mandatory. Neglecting them can lead to rejection.
  • Ignoring banking: Without a bank account, you cannot operate. Plan banking early.
  • Not understanding territorial tax: If you have income from Panama sources, you must pay tax. Ensure your business model is international.
  • Choosing a cheap registered agent: A poor agent can delay incorporation and cause compliance issues. Use a reputable firm.
  • Overlooking EU restrictions: If you plan to serve EU clients, Panama registration alone may not be sufficient. Consider a dual-license strategy.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a Panama crypto license and a registration?

Panama uses a registration regime under the SBP, not a full license. This means lower costs and faster processing, but the regulatory oversight is lighter. The registration still requires AML/KYC compliance and annual reporting.

Can I serve EU customers with a Panama crypto registration?

Panama registration does not automatically comply with MiCA. If you target EU residents, you may need a license from an EU member state. However, Panama can be used for non-EU markets or as part of a multi-jurisdictional structure.

How long does the Panama crypto registration take?

Typically 2-3 months from company incorporation to SBP approval. This includes 1-2 weeks for incorporation, 2-3 weeks for document preparation, and 4-6 weeks for SBP review.

Is there a minimum capital requirement for Panama crypto registration?

No, Panama has no minimum capital requirement. However, it is advisable to have sufficient capital to demonstrate financial substance, typically USD 10,000-50,000.

What taxes will I pay in Panama?

Panama has a territorial tax system. Income from foreign sources is tax-free. Income from Panamanian sources is taxed at 25% corporate tax. There is no capital gains tax on crypto and no VAT on international services.

Can I open a bank account for my Panama crypto company?

Yes, but it can be challenging. Consulting24 can introduce you to banks in Panama and other jurisdictions that accept crypto businesses. A strong compliance framework is essential.

What activities are not allowed under Panama crypto registration?

Lending, borrowing, and derivatives trading are not permitted. Additionally, services to Panamanian residents may be restricted. Always check with the SBP for the latest list of prohibited activities.

Do I need a local compliance officer in Panama?

Yes, the SBP requires a local compliance officer to oversee AML/KYC procedures. Consulting24 can help you find a qualified professional.

How does Panama compare to Estonia for crypto licensing?

Panama is cheaper (EUR 6,000 vs. EUR 15,000-25,000) and faster (2-3 months vs. 3-6 months), with no capital requirement. Estonia has a more established regulatory framework and is recognized in the EU. Choose based on your target market.

Is Panama's crypto registration recognized by FATF?

Panama is a member of FATF and has implemented AML standards. However, Panama has been on the FATF grey list in the past. As of 2026, it is not on the grey list, but compliance is taken seriously.
