What is the Crypto Payment Institution License Bahrain?

The Crypto Payment Institution License Bahrain is a specific license category under the CBB's regulatory sandbox and permanent regime. It permits licensed entities to offer crypto payment services to both retail and institutional clients. The license is part of Bahrain's proactive approach to fintech, aiming to provide legal certainty while maintaining high standards of consumer protection and anti-money laundering (AML) compliance.

Key features include a clear scope of activities, mandatory capital requirements, and ongoing reporting obligations. The CBB has designed the regime to be competitive with other global hubs, such as Dubai and Singapore, while offering a stable regulatory environment. As of 2026, the license is fully operational, with several firms already licensed. The CBB regularly updates its rulebook to reflect market developments, ensuring the regime remains relevant. For example, in 2025 the CBB introduced enhanced custody requirements to align with international standards. This license is distinct from the Crypto-Asset Exchange License, which covers a broader range of activities including trading platforms.

Who Needs This License?

This license is essential for any business that intends to provide crypto payment services in or from Bahrain. Typical applicants include:

  • Crypto exchanges offering fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto trading
  • Payment processors facilitating crypto payments for merchants
  • Custodians holding client crypto assets
  • Remittance services using crypto for cross-border transfers
  • Fintech startups integrating crypto into their payment solutions

If your business model involves any of these activities, obtaining the Crypto Payment Institution License Bahrain is mandatory. Consulting24 can assess your specific case and guide you through the suitability process. Even if your target market is outside Bahrain, operating from Bahrain may require this license if you have a physical presence or serve Bahraini residents. The CBB also considers the place of management and control, so offshore structures may still be captured.

License Type & Regulator

The regulator is the Central Bank of Bahrain (CBB), specifically through its Crypto-Asset Module (CRA) under Volume 5. The license type is formally called a "Crypto Payment Institution License" or sometimes referred to as a "Crypto-Asset Service Provider License" for payment services. It is distinct from the "Crypto-Asset Exchange License" which covers a broader range of activities including order matching and trading platforms.

The CBB has a tiered capital requirement based on the services offered. The license is issued for an initial period, typically renewable annually, subject to compliance. The CBB is known for its rigorous but fair oversight, making Bahrain a trusted jurisdiction for crypto businesses. The CBB also operates a regulatory sandbox for innovative fintech solutions, which can be a stepping stone to a full license. However, as of 2026, most firms apply directly for the permanent license given the clear framework.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a Crypto Payment Institution License Bahrain is set by the CBB based on the services offered. As of 2026, the typical minimum capital is BHD 200,000 (approx. USD 532,000) for payment services. This capital must be maintained at all times and can be in the form of cash or liquid assets. The CBB may require higher capital for firms offering additional services such as custody or margin trading.

Capital must be deposited in a local bank account in Bahrain and is subject to regulatory oversight. Consulting24 can help structure the capital requirement efficiently, for example by using a combination of share capital and reserves. It is important to note that the capital cannot be used for operational expenses without CBB approval. Firms must also demonstrate that the capital is unencumbered and freely available.

Tax Treatment in Bahrain

Bahrain offers a highly favorable tax environment for crypto businesses. There is no corporate income tax, no capital gains tax, and no value-added tax (VAT) on most services. The only tax applicable is a social insurance contribution for employees (approx. 12% of salary). This makes Bahrain an attractive jurisdiction for crypto payment institutions seeking tax efficiency.

However, firms must comply with transfer pricing rules if transacting with related parties. Consulting24 recommends a tax review as part of the setup process. Additionally, Bahrain has double taxation treaties with several countries, which can be beneficial for cross-border operations. It is advisable to structure the entity to maximize treaty benefits, subject to substance requirements. The tax regime is stable and has not changed significantly in recent years, providing predictability for businesses.

Allowed Activities Under the License

The Crypto Payment Institution License Bahrain permits the following activities:

  • Exchange between crypto assets and fiat currencies
  • Exchange between one or more crypto assets
  • Transfer of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Facilitating crypto payments for merchants
  • Remittance services using crypto

Activities not covered include issuing crypto assets (requires a separate license) and operating a crypto exchange with advanced features like derivatives (may require additional approval). The CBB may also restrict certain activities for smaller license holders. For example, a license with lower capital may only allow transfer and custody, not exchange. It is important to define the scope of activities clearly in the application to avoid delays.

Compliance & Trust: Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, firms must comply with ongoing requirements:

  • Annual AML/CFT audits
  • Regular reporting to the CBB (financial statements, transaction reports)
  • Maintenance of minimum capital
  • Independent annual audit by a CBB-approved auditor
  • Compliance with the CBB's crypto-asset rules, including cybersecurity standards

Failure to comply can result in fines or license revocation. Consulting24 provides compliance monitoring services to help you meet these obligations. This is general guidance, not legal advice. The CBB also conducts periodic inspections and may request ad-hoc information. Firms must have a local compliance officer and maintain records for at least five years. Common Mistakes When Applying

  1. Incomplete documentation: Missing business plan or AML policies delay the process.
  2. Underestimating capital: Not having the full capital available upfront.
  3. Poorly defined business model: CBB requires clarity on services offered.
  4. Ignoring local partnerships: Not engaging a local compliance officer or legal advisor.
  5. Rushing the application: Submitting without thorough preparation leads to rejection.

Consulting24 helps you avoid these pitfalls by providing a structured application roadmap. Another common mistake is not preparing for the CBB's due diligence on beneficial owners. Ensure all shareholders and directors have clean backgrounds and are prepared to provide detailed information. Also, avoid making assumptions about the scope of activities; clearly state what you will and will not do.
