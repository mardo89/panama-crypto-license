License Type and Regulator

The fastest Lithuania crypto license is a Crypto-Asset Service Provider (CASP) license regulated by the Bank of Lithuania. The Bank of Lithuania is the central bank and financial regulator responsible for supervising crypto-asset activities. Under MiCA, CASPs must comply with strict AML/CFT, capital adequacy, and consumer protection rules. The regulator has a dedicated Fintech team that handles applications and provides guidance.

There is only one license type for all crypto services, but you must specify which services you intend to offer. The license covers:

  • Exchange of crypto-assets for fiat currency.
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for other crypto-assets.
  • Custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients.
  • Transfer services for crypto-assets.
  • Execution of orders for crypto-assets.
  • Placement of crypto-assets.
  • Receiving and transmitting orders for crypto-assets.
  • Advising on crypto-assets.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the capital requirement for a Lithuania CASP license depends on the types of services provided. The minimum initial capital is:

  • EUR 50,000 for services that do not involve custody of client assets (e.g., exchange only).
  • EUR 125,000 for custody services (e.g., wallet provider).
  • EUR 150,000 for services that involve both custody and trading (e.g., exchange with wallet).

These amounts must be fully paid up in cash and held in a Lithuanian bank account. The capital must be maintained at all times. Consulting24 can help you structure your capital and provide proof of funds. Note that the capital can be used for operational expenses once the license is granted, but it must always be available to cover liabilities. For comparison, Panama has no minimum capital requirement, but that comes with less regulatory oversight.

Tax Treatment

Lithuania offers a favourable tax regime for crypto businesses. The corporate income tax rate is 15%, but small enterprises (revenue under EUR 300,000 and fewer than 10 employees) can benefit from a reduced rate of 5% for the first year and 10% thereafter. There is no VAT on crypto-to-crypto transactions or on crypto-to-fiat exchanges. Capital gains from crypto trading are treated as business income and subject to corporate tax.

Lithuania also has a wide network of double taxation treaties, which can reduce withholding taxes on dividends and interest. For personalised tax planning, consult a tax advisor. The tax treatment of crypto staking and lending is still evolving; as of 2026, staking rewards are generally treated as income at market value. Allowed Activities

With the fastest Lithuania crypto license, you can perform the following activities legally:

  • Exchange of crypto-assets for fiat currency.
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for other crypto-assets.
  • Custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients.
  • Transfer services for crypto-assets.
  • Execution of orders for crypto-assets on behalf of clients.
  • Placement of crypto-assets (including initial exchange offerings).
  • Receiving and transmitting orders for crypto-assets.
  • Advising on crypto-assets.

You must apply for the specific services you intend to offer. The license does not cover derivatives or securities tokens, which fall under MiFID II. For such activities, consider a separate investment firm license. Also, if you plan to issue stablecoins or other asset-referenced tokens, you may need a separate e-money license or additional authorisation under MiCA.

Compliance and Trust

Maintaining compliance after obtaining the fastest Lithuania crypto license is crucial. You must:

  • Appoint a local AML officer (or outsource to a service provider).
  • Conduct ongoing customer due diligence (CDD) including enhanced due diligence for high-risk clients.
  • Report suspicious transactions to the Financial Crime Investigation Service (FCIS) within required timelines.
  • Maintain capital adequacy at all times and report any breaches.
  • Submit annual audited financial statements to the Bank of Lithuania.
  • Undergo periodic regulatory inspections and respond to requests promptly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Incomplete Documentation: Missing AML policies or business plan details cause delays. Ensure all documents are translated into Lithuanian if required.
  • Insufficient Capital Proof: Failing to show source of funds or maintain capital. Provide bank statements and source of wealth documentation.
  • Incorrect Service Scope: Applying for services you don't need or missing required ones. This can lead to additional capital demands or rejection.
  • Poorly Prepared Management: Regulator interviews can reveal lack of crypto or compliance knowledge. Prepare your team thoroughly.
  • Ignoring Ongoing Compliance: Post-license, failure to submit reports or maintain AML procedures can lead to penalties.
  • Choosing the Wrong Jurisdiction: Lithuania is fast, but not for everyone. Compare with Estonia or Poland for different regulatory styles.
