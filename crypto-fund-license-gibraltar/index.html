What is the Gibraltar Crypto Fund License?

The Gibraltar crypto fund license is a regulatory authorization issued by the GFSC for fund managers operating collective investment schemes that invest primarily in crypto assets. It is part of Gibraltar's broader Distributed Ledger Technology (DLT) framework, which was introduced to regulate businesses using blockchain technology. The license ensures that fund managers meet standards of probity, competence, and financial soundness.

Funds can be structured as private or public, with different regulatory requirements. The license is essential for any manager offering fund services to investors in or from Gibraltar. It covers both the fund itself and the management company. The regime is based on the Gibraltar Collective Investment Schemes Act and related regulations, which have been updated to address crypto-specific risks such as custody, valuation, and cybersecurity. For example, the GFSC requires that crypto assets be held by a qualified custodian with appropriate insurance or segregation. The valuation of illiquid tokens must follow a documented policy approved by the regulator. These requirements protect investors and reduce operational risk.

As of 2026, the regime is fully aligned with international standards, including IOSCO principles and the FATF recommendations on virtual assets. This alignment makes Gibraltar a trusted jurisdiction for institutional investors. Consulting24 can help you understand whether this license fits your fund structure through a detailed consultation.

Who Needs a Crypto Fund License in Gibraltar?

Any entity that manages a collective investment scheme investing in crypto assets and is established in Gibraltar, or markets such a scheme to investors in Gibraltar, requires a license. This includes:

  • Fund managers operating hedge funds, venture capital funds, or index funds focused on crypto.
  • Asset managers offering discretionary management of crypto portfolios.
  • Structured product issuers that pool investor capital for crypto exposure.
  • Family offices that manage third-party capital in a pooled structure.

Exemptions exist for very small funds (e.g., fewer than 15 investors, no public offering) but are limited. For instance, a fund with 10 investors each contributing at least EUR 100,000 may qualify as a professional investor fund and face lighter requirements. However, the manager still needs to be licensed if it exercises discretion. License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Gibraltar Financial Services Commission (GFSC). The license type is a 'Collective Investment Scheme (CIS) license' with a crypto focus. There are two main categories:

  • Private Scheme: For funds with up to 50 investors, no public offering, and a minimum investment of EUR 100,000 (or equivalent). These funds can be set up more quickly and with lighter disclosure requirements.
  • Public Scheme: For funds offered to the public, subject to full prospectus requirements and ongoing reporting. Public schemes require approval of the prospectus by the GFSC and are subject to stricter conduct rules.

Both require a licensed manager. The GFSC also requires the fund to have a local administrator and custodian (for assets). The administrator handles NAV calculations, investor subscriptions and redemptions, and regulatory filings. The custodian safeguards the fund's assets, including crypto private keys. The regime is aligned with international standards, including IOSCO principles. The GFSC conducts regular on-site inspections and reviews of fund operations. Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a Gibraltar crypto fund manager is typically EUR 125,000 (or equivalent in GBP). This is the initial capital requirement under the EU's MiCA-inspired framework adopted by Gibraltar. However, the exact amount may vary based on the fund's risk profile and the services provided. For example, managers with discretionary management may need higher capital, up to EUR 150,000. The capital must be held in liquid assets (cash or near-cash) and is subject to ongoing solvency requirements. The fund itself does not have a capital requirement, but the manager must maintain its own capital. Consulting24 can confirm the precise figure for your specific case during a consultation. For comparison, Lithuania also requires EUR 125,000 under MiCA, while Panama has no specific capital requirement for fund managers.

Tax Treatment

Gibraltar offers a favorable tax regime for funds. The key features:

  • Corporate tax rate: 10% on profits (standard rate for companies).
  • No capital gains tax: Gains from crypto asset disposals are not taxed.
  • No withholding tax: On dividends or interest paid to non-residents.
  • Exemptions: Qualifying funds may be exempt from tax on investment income and gains if they meet certain conditions (e.g., being a collective investment scheme with a prescribed number of investors).

Fund managers are subject to the standard corporate tax rate on their management fees. Gibraltar is not on any EU blacklist and has a transparent tax system. It has a tax information exchange agreement with the EU and OECD. Always consult a tax advisor for your specific situation. For example, a fund that only holds crypto assets and does not trade actively may qualify for the exemption, reducing its effective tax rate to zero. Allowed Activities Under the License

The crypto fund license Gibraltar permits the following activities:

  • Establishing and managing collective investment schemes that invest in crypto assets (e.g., Bitcoin, Ethereum, tokens).
  • Offering fund units to qualified or retail investors (depending on scheme type).
  • Providing discretionary portfolio management for crypto assets.
  • Holding custody of crypto assets (through a licensed custodian).
  • Valuation, administration, and transfer agency services for the fund.
  • Marketing the fund to investors in Gibraltar and, under certain passports, to investors in other jurisdictions.

The license does not cover direct trading for own account or providing crypto exchange services. Those require separate licenses under the DLT framework. For example, if your fund also wants to operate a trading platform, you would need a DLT exchange license. The fund license is strictly for asset management. Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Pre-application assessment: Consulting24 reviews your fund structure, business plan, and compliance readiness. We identify any gaps in documentation or substance requirements.
  2. Documentation: Prepare the application pack including fund prospectus, manager's business plan, financial projections, and personal questionnaires for key individuals. The prospectus must disclose all material risks, fee structures, and redemption terms.
  3. Submission: File the application with the GFSC, along with the application fee. The GFSC acknowledges receipt and assigns a case officer.
  4. Review: The GFSC conducts due diligence on the fund manager, directors, and shareholders. This may take 2-4 months. The regulator may request additional information or clarifications.
  5. Approval: If satisfied, the GFSC grants the license with conditions (e.g., capital maintenance, reporting obligations, appointment of a local administrator).
  6. Post-licensing: Ongoing compliance including annual returns, audited accounts, and AML/CFT reporting. The GFSC may conduct periodic inspections.

Compliance and Trust

Gibraltar's regulatory framework is built on principles of transparency and investor protection. Fund managers must comply with AML/CFT regulations, conduct regular risk assessments, and appoint a Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO). The GFSC conducts on-site inspections and reviews. Key compliance obligations include:

  • Annual submission of audited financial statements.
  • Quarterly reporting of NAV and investor numbers.
  • Immediate notification of any material changes to the fund or manager.
  • Maintenance of a register of beneficial owners.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Underestimating capital requirements: Ensure you have the minimum capital (EUR 125,000) before applying. Some managers try to apply with lower capital, leading to rejection.
  • Poor documentation: A vague business plan or incomplete prospectus will delay approval. The GFSC expects detailed information on investment strategy, risk management, and fee structures.
  • Ignoring local substance: The fund manager must have a physical office in Gibraltar and at least one director resident. Remote management is not accepted.
  • Not appointing a local administrator: The GFSC requires a licensed administrator for the fund. Some managers try to self-administer, which is not permitted.
  • Overlooking ongoing costs: Annual supervision fees, compliance costs, and audit fees add up. Budget for at least EUR 10,000 per year in ongoing expenses.
  • Assuming fast approval: While the process can take 3 months, many applications take 6 months or more due to incomplete submissions. Plan accordingly.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Gibraltar crypto fund manager?

The minimum capital requirement is typically EUR 125,000, though it may vary based on the fund's risk profile. This capital must be held in liquid assets and is subject to ongoing solvency requirements. Consulting24 can confirm the exact figure for your case during a consultation.

How long does it take to get a Gibraltar crypto fund license?

The process typically takes 3 to 6 months, depending on the complexity of the fund structure and the completeness of the application. Simple private funds may be approved faster, while public funds or those with complex strategies may take longer.

What is the tax rate for crypto funds in Gibraltar?

The standard corporate tax rate is 10%. There is no capital gains tax on crypto disposals, and qualifying funds may be exempt from tax on investment income and gains. No withholding tax applies on dividends or interest paid to non-residents.

Can I manage a crypto fund from Gibraltar without a license?

No, any entity managing a collective investment scheme investing in crypto assets and established in Gibraltar must be licensed by the GFSC. Exemptions exist only for very small funds with limited investors and no public offering.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations for a Gibraltar crypto fund?

Ongoing obligations include annual submission of audited financial statements, quarterly NAV reporting, notification of material changes, AML/CFT compliance, and maintaining a register of beneficial owners. The GFSC may also conduct on-site inspections.

Does Gibraltar accept crypto as payment for fund subscriptions?

Yes, but the fund must have clear policies on accepting crypto. The GFSC expects the fund to value crypto contributions at fair market value and to comply with AML checks. Many funds accept crypto in-kind and convert to fiat immediately.

Is Gibraltar part of the EU for passporting purposes?

Gibraltar is not in the EU but has equivalence arrangements for certain services. Post-Brexit, Gibraltar retains access to UK markets. For EU passporting, funds may use national private placement regimes. Consulting24 can advise on specific marketing options.

Can I use a Gibraltar fund to invest in DeFi protocols?

Yes, but the GFSC expects the fund to have strong risk management and due diligence on DeFi protocols. Investments in DeFi may require additional disclosures in the prospectus. The fund must also ensure compliance with custody requirements for any tokens held.

What happens if I fail to maintain the minimum capital?

The GFSC may impose sanctions, including fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. It is critical to monitor capital levels and notify the regulator if capital falls below the requirement. Consulting24 can help set up monitoring systems.

How does Gibraltar compare to Panama for crypto fund licensing?

Panama offers a faster and cheaper option (EUR 6,000 flat, 1-2 weeks) but without a dedicated fund license. This means less regulatory credibility and potential banking difficulties. Gibraltar provides a regulated framework that enhances investor trust, at a higher cost and longer timeline.
