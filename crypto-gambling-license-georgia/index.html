What Is a Crypto Gambling License in Georgia?

A crypto gambling license in Georgia is a regulatory authorization issued by the Georgian government that permits operators to offer gambling services using cryptocurrencies. Georgia has a well-defined legal framework for gambling, which was updated to explicitly include digital assets. The license is issued by the LEPL Revenue Service of Georgia (the tax authority), which also oversees compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and responsible gambling requirements.

The Georgian model is attractive because it combines a relatively low tax burden with a straightforward licensing process. Unlike some jurisdictions that require a physical presence or local servers, Georgia allows remote gambling licenses, making it a popular choice for international operators. The license covers both fiat and crypto gambling activities, provided the operator complies with AML regulations.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator for gambling in Georgia is the LEPL Revenue Service of Georgia, operating under the Ministry of Finance. The license is officially called an Online Gambling License (also referred to as a remote gambling permit). It covers both fiat and cryptocurrency operations.

There are two main categories:

  • Category A: For casino games, slot machines, and table games (including live dealer).
  • Category B: For sports betting and other fixed-odds wagering.

Operators can apply for one or both categories. The license is valid for 5 years and renewable. The Revenue Service also requires a compliance audit every 2 years. Consulting24 coordinates the application process, ensuring all documentation is complete and submitted correctly.

Capital Requirement

Georgia does not have a statutory minimum capital requirement for gambling license applicants. However, the regulator expects the company to have sufficient financial resources to operate responsibly. In practice, a minimum paid-up capital of EUR 50,000 to 100,000 is recommended to demonstrate financial stability. This capital must be held in a bank account in Georgia or an EU bank account acceptable to the regulator.

For crypto-focused operators, the capital can be held in stablecoins or fiat, but the regulator prefers fiat for transparency. Consulting24 advises clients on structuring their capital to meet regulatory expectations without overcapitalizing.

Tax Treatment

Georgia offers a highly competitive tax regime for gambling operators. The tax is based on a fixed license fee rather than a percentage of revenue, which can be advantageous for high-volume operators. As of 2026, the annual license fee ranges from EUR 10,000 to 20,000 depending on the category and number of games offered.

Additionally, corporate income tax in Georgia is a flat 15%, but gambling companies are exempt from VAT on their services. There is no withholding tax on dividends paid to non-resident shareholders. However, operators must pay a 5% gambling tax on gross gaming revenue (GGR) if they exceed certain thresholds (typically over EUR 1 million in GGR per year). Step-by-Step Application Process

The application process for a crypto gambling license in Georgia involves the following steps:

  1. Company incorporation: Register a legal entity in Georgia (LLC recommended) with a registered address and local director (optional).
  2. Document preparation: Gather business plan, AML/KYC policies, responsible gambling policy, software certifications, and financial statements.
  3. Submit application: File with the LEPL Revenue Service, including the application form, supporting documents, and payment of the government fee.
  4. Regulatory review: The Revenue Service reviews the application and may request additional information. This stage takes 2-4 months.
  5. License issuance: Once approved, the license is issued for 5 years. Annual fees must be paid thereafter.
  6. Ongoing compliance: Submit periodic reports, undergo audits, and maintain AML procedures.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital requirement for a crypto gambling license in Georgia?

Georgia does not have a statutory minimum capital requirement. However, the regulator expects sufficient financial resources. Practically, a minimum paid-up capital of EUR 50,000 to 100,000 is recommended to demonstrate stability. This capital can be held in a Georgian or EU bank account.

How long does it take to get a crypto gambling license in Georgia?

The typical timeline is 3 to 6 months from application to issuance. Delays can occur if documentation is incomplete or if the regulator requests additional information. Consulting24 helps expedite the process by preparing a thorough application package.

What taxes apply to crypto gambling operators in Georgia?

Operators pay an annual license fee of EUR 10,000 to 20,000. Corporate income tax is 15%, but gambling services are VAT-exempt. A 5% gambling tax on gross gaming revenue applies if GGR exceeds EUR 1 million per year. No withholding tax on dividends to non-residents.

Can I operate a crypto casino from Georgia targeting international players?

Yes, the Georgian license permits remote gambling services to international markets. There is no requirement to restrict operations to Georgian residents. However, you must comply with AML laws and ensure your platform is not accessible in prohibited jurisdictions.

Does Georgia accept cryptocurrencies for gambling transactions?

Yes, the Georgian regulatory framework explicitly allows the use of cryptocurrencies for gambling. Operators can accept Bitcoin, Ethereum, and other digital assets. However, they must implement AML/KYC procedures for crypto transactions.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements after obtaining the license?

Licensees must submit periodic reports (financial and operational) to the Revenue Service, undergo a compliance audit every 2 years, and maintain AML/KYC policies. Responsible gambling tools must be active. Failure to comply can lead to fines or license revocation.

Can I use a Georgian company without a local director?
