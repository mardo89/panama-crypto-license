What Is an Offshore Lithuania Crypto License?

An offshore Lithuania crypto license is not a separate license category but a standard Lithuanian VASP registration obtained by a company incorporated in Lithuania but owned or controlled by non-residents. The company is treated as a Lithuanian legal entity for regulatory purposes, but the beneficial owners may be based abroad. This structure is often used for international crypto businesses seeking an EU passport without establishing physical presence in Lithuania.

The license is issued by the Bank of Lithuania under the Law on Prevention of Money Laundering and Terrorist Financing, as amended to align with MiCA. Since 2025, MiCA has fully harmonised crypto asset service provider (CASP) rules across the EU. Lithuania's VASP regime now mirrors MiCA's requirements, including capital tiers based on service type.

Key features of the offshore Lithuania crypto license include:

  • Full EU market access via passporting
  • Regulation by the Bank of Lithuania
  • Minimum capital from EUR 50,000 to EUR 150,000 depending on services
  • Corporate income tax of 15% (with potential exemptions)
  • No VAT on crypto-to-crypto transactions

License Type and Regulator

The offshore Lithuania crypto license is a VASP registration under the Bank of Lithuania. As of 2026, the Bank of Lithuania is the competent authority for crypto asset service providers under MiCA. The license covers several service classes, each with specific capital requirements and operational rules.

The regulator conducts fit-and-proper checks on shareholders, directors, and key personnel. It also reviews the company's AML policies, business plan, and IT security measures. The application process is divided into two stages: preliminary assessment and final authorisation. Consulting24 advises clients on preparing the documentation and liaising with the regulator.

For an offshore structure, the company must have a physical office in Lithuania and at least one local representative (can be a service provider). The regulator expects substance proportionate to the activities.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, capital requirements for crypto asset service providers in Lithuania depend on the service class. As of 2026, the minimum capital tiers are:

  • EUR 50,000 for reception and transmission of orders on behalf of clients (no custody)
  • EUR 125,000 for exchange services (custodial or non-custodial)
  • EUR 150,000 for custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients

These amounts must be maintained as paid-up share capital or a combination of share capital and reserves. The capital must be held in a bank account in Lithuania or another EU member state. For offshore structures, the capital is typically contributed by the parent company or shareholders. Tax Treatment

Lithuania offers a favourable tax regime for crypto businesses. Key points:

  • Corporate income tax (CIT) at 15% on profits. Reduced rate of 5% for small companies (up to EUR 300,000 turnover, fewer than 10 employees) for the first year, then 10%.
  • No VAT on crypto-to-crypto transactions (exempt). VAT applies only to fiat-crypto exchanges and advisory services.
  • Capital gains from crypto held as investment are subject to 15% CIT, but gains from trading as a business are ordinary income.
  • Withholding tax on dividends is 15% (reduced under tax treaties).
  • No withholding tax on interest or royalties paid to EU residents.

Offshore structures should consider substance requirements to avoid being treated as tax resident elsewhere. Consulting24 recommends a tax review with a local advisor to optimise the structure.

Allowed Activities

An offshore Lithuania crypto license permits the following activities under MiCA:

  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets
  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Reception and transmission of orders for crypto assets
  • Execution of orders on behalf of clients
  • Placement of crypto assets (underwriting)
  • Advice on crypto assets

Activities not covered include operating a crypto ATM (requires additional registration) and issuing asset-referenced tokens (ARTs) or e-money tokens (EMTs), which are regulated separately under MiCA. The license does not allow banking or payment services unless combined with other licenses.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process for obtaining an offshore Lithuania crypto license involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step.

  1. Pre-application phase: Business analysis, service scope definition, and preparation of documentation (business plan, AML policy, risk assessment, IT security measures).
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Lithuanian company (UAB) with a registered office and at least one director (can be a nominee). Minimum share capital EUR 2,500 (required for UAB).
  3. Preliminary assessment: Submit a preliminary application to the Bank of Lithuania with key documents. The regulator reviews the fitness of shareholders and management.
  4. Final authorisation: After preliminary approval, submit the full application with detailed policies, procedures, and evidence of capital. The regulator conducts an on-site or off-site inspection.
  5. License issuance: Once approved, the company is entered into the VASP register. The license is valid indefinitely but subject to ongoing compliance.

Benefits of an Offshore Lithuania Crypto License

Choosing an offshore Lithuania crypto license offers several advantages:

  • EU passport: Once licensed, you can provide services across all EU/EEA member states without additional licensing.
  • Cost-effective: Compared to other EU jurisdictions like Malta or Luxembourg, Lithuania's fees and capital requirements are lower.
  • Fast processing: The Bank of Lithuania aims to process applications within 3-6 months, faster than many peers.
  • Pro-crypto regulation: Lithuania has a clear legal framework and is open to innovation.
  • Tax efficiency: Low corporate tax rate and VAT exemptions for crypto-to-crypto trades.
  • Offshore flexibility: Non-resident ownership is permitted, and substance requirements can be met through service providers.

Compliance and Trust

Maintaining compliance is essential to keep the offshore Lithuania crypto license valid. Key obligations include:

  • Annual AML/CFT risk assessment and policy updates
  • Reporting suspicious transactions to the Financial Crime Investigation Service (FCIS)
  • Appointing a local AML officer
  • Audited financial statements (if turnover exceeds EUR 1 million)
  • Ongoing capital maintenance

Note: This is general guidance, not legal advice. Consulting24 recommends engaging a local compliance officer or outsourcing to a licensed AML service provider. Failure to comply can result in fines or revocation of the license.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Applicants often make these mistakes when pursuing an offshore Lithuania crypto license:

  • Insufficient substance: The Bank of Lithuania expects real presence, not just a mailbox. Use a reputable registered office provider.
  • Poor AML documentation: Generic policies are rejected. Tailor your AML program to your business model.
  • Underestimating capital requirements: Ensure capital is fully paid up and held in a compliant account.
  • Ignoring tax obligations: Even offshore, you must file annual returns and pay CIT if profitable.
  • Choosing the wrong service provider: Unqualified advisors can delay or derail the application. Work with experienced consultants like Consulting24.
