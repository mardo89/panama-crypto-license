Overview: What Is a Crypto OTC Desk License in Vietnam?

A crypto OTC desk license in Vietnam is a regulatory authorization that allows a company to operate an over-the-counter trading platform for cryptocurrencies. OTC desks facilitate direct trades between buyers and sellers, often for large volumes, without the need for a public order book. This license is essential for businesses that want to offer liquidity, execute block trades, or provide personalized trading services to institutional and high-net-worth clients.

Vietnam's regulatory environment for crypto is evolving. As of 2026, the State Bank of Vietnam (SBV) and the Ministry of Finance are the primary bodies overseeing crypto activities. While there is no standalone crypto OTC desk license, businesses typically register as a financial services company with specific permissions for digital asset trading. Consulting24 advises clients on the most appropriate corporate structure and licensing pathway.

The OTC desk model is particularly attractive for large traders who value privacy and price certainty. For example, a hedge fund wanting to sell 1,000 BTC can use an OTC desk to avoid moving the market on an exchange. Vietnam's growing fintech ecosystem makes it a strategic location for such services, though the regulatory framework is still being developed.

License Type and Regulator

The primary regulator for crypto OTC desk activities in Vietnam is the State Bank of Vietnam (SBV), which oversees payment-related services, while the Ministry of Finance (MOF) regulates securities and investment activities. As of 2026, Vietnam does not have a dedicated crypto license; instead, crypto OTC desks typically operate under a financial services company license with a scope that includes digital asset trading.

The license type is often referred to as a "Crypto Trading Services License" or "Digital Asset Brokerage License." It is issued after a thorough review of the company's financial standing, compliance policies, and beneficial ownership. The SBV has been increasingly active in issuing guidance on AML and KYC for crypto businesses, which applicants must follow.

Capital Requirement

Vietnam does not have a statutory minimum capital requirement specifically for crypto OTC desks as of 2026. However, the SBV and MOF expect companies to demonstrate sufficient financial resources to cover operational costs and potential liabilities. Typically, a paid-up capital of at least EUR 50,000 is recommended to show financial stability. For larger operations, capital of EUR 100,000 or more may be required. Tax Treatment

Vietnam imposes corporate income tax at a standard rate of 20% on profits derived from crypto trading activities. Value Added Tax (VAT) at 10% applies to service fees charged by the OTC desk. There is no specific crypto tax regime; digital assets are treated as commodities or services for tax purposes. Consulting24 recommends engaging a local tax advisor to ensure compliance with reporting obligations. For many clients, structuring through jurisdictions like Panama may offer more favorable tax treatment, as Panama does not tax offshore income. Vietnam's tax system can be complex for foreign-owned entities, especially regarding withholding taxes on dividends and interest.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process for obtaining a crypto OTC desk license in Vietnam involves several stages:

  1. Company Incorporation: Register a company in Vietnam with a suitable business scope (e.g., financial services, digital asset trading). This requires at least one local director and a registered address.
  2. Document Preparation: Prepare a business plan, compliance manual, AML/KYC policies, and financial projections. The business plan must detail your target market, revenue model, and risk management.
  3. Application Submission: Submit the application to the SBV or MOF, along with supporting documents. The regulator may require notarized translations of foreign documents.
  4. Review and Interviews: The regulator reviews the application and may request additional information or an interview with the directors. This stage can take 1-2 months.
  5. License Issuance: Upon approval, the license is issued, and the company can commence operations. You must then register with the tax authority and open a corporate bank account.
