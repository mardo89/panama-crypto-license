What Is a Switzerland Crypto License?

A Switzerland crypto license is an authorisation from FINMA to conduct regulated activities involving crypto assets. The license falls under several categories depending on the business model: the DLT (Distributed Ledger Technology) Act for tokenised assets, the fintech license for deposit-taking activities, or a banking license for full-scale deposit services. Most crypto projects apply for a DLT trading facility or a securities dealer license under the DLT Act.

The Swiss regime is known for its principles-based regulation, focusing on the economic function of tokens rather than rigid definitions. FINMA categorises tokens as payment, utility, or asset tokens, each with distinct requirements. The license process includes anti-money laundering (AML) compliance through a self-regulatory organisation (SRO) like VQF, and ongoing reporting obligations.

Key features of the Swiss license include:

  • Regulator: FINMA (Swiss Financial Market Supervisory Authority)
  • Legal basis: DLT Act (effective 2021), Banking Act, Financial Services Act (FinSA)
  • AML supervision: Via SRO membership (e.g., VQF, SRO PolyReg)
  • Timeline: Typically 6-12 months
  • Cost: Premium, starting from CHF 100,000+ (EUR 100,000+) depending on complexity

Who Needs a Switzerland Crypto License?

A Switzerland crypto license is required for any business that professionally trades, custodies, or facilitates crypto assets on behalf of clients. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralised and decentralised)
  • Custody wallet providers holding private keys for clients
  • Token issuers conducting security token offerings (STOs)
  • DLT trading facilities operating secondary markets
  • Asset managers investing in crypto assets

Even if your project is based outside Switzerland, if you target Swiss clients or have a Swiss legal entity, FINMA oversight applies. Many projects choose Switzerland for its legal clarity and reputation, especially those dealing with tokenised securities or complex DeFi protocols.

Capital Requirement

Switzerland does not have a fixed minimum capital for all crypto licenses. Instead, FINMA assesses capital adequacy on a case-by-case basis, considering the risk profile and business volume. However, practical guidelines suggest:

  • DLT trading facility: Typically CHF 500,000-1,000,000 (EUR 500,000-1,000,000)
  • Fintech license: CHF 300,000-1,000,000
  • Banking license: CHF 10 million+

The capital must be held in liquid assets and maintained throughout the license period. FINMA may require additional capital for higher-risk activities, such as offering margin trading or derivatives. For example, a DLT exchange with high trading volumes may need CHF 1 million or more, while a simple custody-only service might be acceptable with CHF 500,000. We work with you to prepare a robust capital adequacy assessment.

Tax Treatment

Switzerland offers a favourable tax environment for crypto businesses. Corporate income tax rates are low (11.9%-21% depending on canton) and capital gains from crypto trading are generally tax-free for companies. However, VAT applies to certain services.

  • Corporate tax: Low effective rates, especially in Zug (11.9%)
  • Capital gains: Exempt for professional traders under certain conditions
  • VAT: 7.7% standard; crypto-to-fiat exchanges may be exempt
  • Wealth tax: Applies to individuals, not companies

Swiss tax authorities treat cryptocurrencies as assets, not currency. Tokenised securities follow securities tax rules. Consulting24 coordinates with local tax advisors to optimise your structure, including transfer pricing and withholding tax considerations for cross-border operations.

Allowed Activities

Under a Switzerland crypto license, you can conduct a wide range of activities, including:

  • Operating a DLT trading facility (exchange)
  • Custody of crypto assets for clients
  • Brokerage and order execution
  • Token issuance and distribution (STOs)
  • Asset management for crypto portfolios
  • Payment services using stablecoins

Activities not covered include deposit-taking (requires fintech/banking license) and derivatives trading (requires separate authorisation). The DLT Act also permits decentralised trading platforms under certain conditions, provided the platform operator retains control over key functions. Compliance & Trust

Compliance is paramount in Switzerland. FINMA requires robust AML/KYC procedures, regular audits, and ongoing reporting. Key requirements include:

  • Membership in an SRO (e.g., VQF) for AML supervision
  • Annual financial audits by a licensed auditor
  • Risk management framework covering operational, legal, and reputational risks
  • Data protection compliance under Swiss FADP
  • Regular reporting to FINMA on financial health and suspicious transactions

Failure to comply can result in fines, license revocation, or criminal liability. Consulting24 ensures your compliance framework meets Swiss standards. Common Mistakes

Many applicants make avoidable errors. Common mistakes include:

  • Underestimating costs: Budgeting only for application fees, ignoring legal and capital requirements.
  • Incomplete documentation: Submitting a business plan that lacks detail on risk management or governance.
  • Ignoring SRO requirements: Failing to join an SRO before applying to FINMA.
  • Choosing the wrong license type: Applying for a banking license when a fintech license suffices.
  • Inadequate capital: Not maintaining sufficient liquid capital during the review period.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Switzerland crypto license?

There is no fixed minimum; FINMA assesses on a case-by-case basis. For a DLT trading facility, expect CHF 500,000-1,000,000 (EUR 500,000-1,000,000). The capital must be held in liquid assets and maintained throughout the license period.

How long does it take to get a Switzerland crypto license?

Typically 6-12 months from application to approval. This includes 2-3 months of preparation and 4-8 months of FINMA review. Consulting24 helps expedite the process with thorough documentation.

Can I operate a crypto exchange without a Swiss license?

No, if you target Swiss clients or have a Swiss entity, you need a license from FINMA. Unlicensed operations face fines and criminal penalties. Even foreign exchanges may need a license if they actively solicit Swiss customers.

What is the cost of a Switzerland crypto license?

Total costs typically range from EUR 100,000 to 300,000+, including FINMA fees (CHF 20,000-50,000), legal fees (CHF 50,000-150,000), and SRO membership (CHF 5,000-15,000/year). Exact pricing is confirmed in a consultation.

Is Switzerland part of the EU crypto regime (MiCA)?

No, Switzerland is not in the EU or EEA, so MiCA does not apply. However, FINMA's standards are equally rigorous, and Swiss regulation is recognised globally. The DLT Act provides a bespoke framework for crypto assets.

What activities are allowed under a DLT trading facility license?

You can operate an exchange for tokenised assets, custody client crypto, execute orders, and facilitate token issuance. Deposit-taking and derivatives trading require separate licenses. Consulting24 can advise on scope.

Do I need to be physically present in Switzerland to apply?

No, but you need a registered Swiss company (AG or GmbH) with a local registered office and a director. Consulting24 can assist with company setup and nominee services if needed.

What are the tax benefits for crypto businesses in Switzerland?

Corporate tax rates are low (11.9%-21%), capital gains are often exempt, and VAT on crypto-to-fiat exchanges may be exempt. Switzerland also has no withholding tax on dividends for certain structures.

Can Consulting24 guarantee my Switzerland crypto license approval?

No, we cannot guarantee approval. FINMA makes the final decision. However, we maximise your chances by ensuring complete and accurate documentation, proper AML framework, and compliance with all requirements.

What happens if my application is rejected?

You can reapply after addressing FINMA's concerns, but the application fee is non-refundable. Consulting24 helps identify and fix issues before resubmission. Common reasons for rejection include insufficient capital or weak AML policies.

How does Switzerland compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Switzerland offers higher credibility and a mature ecosystem but costs EUR 100,000+ and takes 6-12 months. Panama costs a flat EUR 6,000 and takes 2-3 months, with no capital requirement and 0% territorial tax. Choose based on your budget and market needs.

Are there any ongoing compliance obligations after obtaining the license?

Yes, you must maintain SRO membership, conduct annual audits, report suspicious transactions, and update FINMA on material changes. Consulting24 offers ongoing compliance support to ensure you meet these obligations.
