What Is a Crypto Gambling License in Bahrain?

A crypto gambling license in Bahrain is a regulatory authorization that permits companies to operate gambling and betting services using digital currencies such as Bitcoin, Ethereum, and stablecoins. The license is issued by the Bahrain Tourism and Exhibitions Authority (BTEA) in coordination with the Central Bank of Bahrain (CBB) for financial compliance. It covers both land-based and online gambling activities, including casino games, poker, sports betting, and lottery, provided they are conducted with cryptocurrencies.

The framework is relatively new, with the first licenses granted in 2023. As of 2026, the regime is fully operational, with clear rules on player protection, anti-money laundering (AML), and responsible gaming. The license is valid for one year and must be renewed annually. Consulting24 advises clients on the application process, compliance setup, and ongoing reporting obligations.

Who Needs a Crypto Gambling License in Bahrain?

Any business that wants to legally offer gambling services to customers in Bahrain or from Bahrain-based servers must obtain this license. This includes:

  • Online casinos accepting crypto deposits and withdrawals
  • Sports betting platforms using digital currencies
  • Poker rooms and skill game operators
  • Lottery and bingo providers
  • Gambling software developers (if they offer white-label solutions)

Even if your target market is international, hosting servers or having a management office in Bahrain requires licensing. The regulator takes a strict approach to unlicensed operators, with fines and potential criminal penalties. If you are considering Bahrain as your base, it is essential to secure the appropriate license before launch.

License Type and Regulator

The crypto gambling license in Bahrain is a single license category issued by the Bahrain Tourism and Exhibitions Authority (BTEA). The BTEA is the primary regulator for all gambling activities in the country, including land-based casinos, online gambling, and lottery. For crypto-specific operations, the BTEA works closely with the Central Bank of Bahrain (CBB) to ensure compliance with financial regulations, particularly AML and counter-terrorist financing (CTF) rules.

The license is not industry-specific to crypto gambling - it covers all gambling verticals. However, operators must apply for a specific endorsement to accept cryptocurrencies. This endorsement requires a separate AML compliance review by the CBB. As of 2026, there is no separate 'crypto gambling license' per se; it is a standard gambling license with a crypto add-on.

Capital Requirement

Bahrain imposes a minimum capital requirement for gambling license holders. As of 2026, the minimum paid-up capital is USD 500,000 for online gambling operators. This capital must be maintained throughout the license period and cannot be withdrawn without regulatory approval. The capital can be held in fiat or cryptocurrency, but the regulator prefers a mix to ensure liquidity.

For operators that also offer land-based gambling, the capital requirement is higher, typically USD 1 million. The capital must be deposited in a Bahraini bank account and is subject to annual audit. Tax Treatment

Bahrain offers a highly favorable tax environment for gambling operators. There is no corporate income tax on gambling revenues, and no value-added tax (VAT) on gambling services. However, operators must pay an annual license fee as described above. Additionally, there is a 5% tax on gross gaming revenue (GGR) for online gambling, which is relatively low compared to other jurisdictions. Land-based casinos face a 10% GGR tax.

For crypto gambling, the tax treatment of cryptocurrency transactions is still evolving. As of 2026, the CBB treats crypto as a commodity for tax purposes, meaning that gains from crypto held as investment may be subject to capital gains tax (0% in Bahrain). However, crypto used for gambling stakes and payouts is considered part of the gambling revenue and is subject to the GGR tax. Consulting24 recommends working with a local tax advisor to ensure full compliance.

Allowed Activities

A crypto gambling license in Bahrain permits the following activities:

  • Online casino games (slots, blackjack, roulette, etc.) using crypto
  • Sports betting (pre-match and live) with crypto stakes
  • Poker and other card games
  • Lottery and instant win games
  • eSports betting
  • Provision of gambling software and platforms to other licensees (B2B)

Operators are not allowed to offer gambling services to residents of countries where online gambling is prohibited. The license also restricts advertising to jurisdictions that permit it. The BTEA requires all games to be tested and certified by an approved testing laboratory for fairness and randomness. Crypto transactions must be recorded on a blockchain-based ledger for audit purposes.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining a crypto gambling license in Bahrain involves several stages. Consulting24 coordinates the entire process:

  1. Pre-application consultation: Assess your business model and determine the appropriate license category.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Bahraini company with the Ministry of Industry and Commerce (MOIC). Minimum capital of USD 500,000 must be deposited.
  3. Prepare documentation: Business plan, AML/CFT policies, responsible gambling policy, financial statements, ownership structure, and technical specifications.
  4. Submit application: File the application with the BTEA, including the crypto endorsement request. Pay the application fee.
  5. CBB AML review: The CBB reviews your AML framework and crypto handling procedures. This may require a physical presence in Bahrain for interviews.
  6. License issuance: Once approved, pay the annual license fee and receive the license. The license is valid for one year.
  7. Post-licensing compliance: Submit quarterly reports to the BTEA and CBB, undergo annual audits, and renew the license annually.

The entire process typically takes 6-12 months. Consulting24 can reduce delays by ensuring all documents are complete and compliant from the start.

Banking and Payment Processing

One of the biggest challenges for crypto gambling operators is finding banking partners. In Bahrain, the CBB has issued guidelines that allow licensed operators to open bank accounts with local banks, provided they have a valid gambling license and a crypto endorsement. However, many international banks remain cautious. As of 2026, several Bahraini banks offer accounts to licensed gambling operators, including Bank ABC and Ahli United Bank, but they require extensive AML documentation.

For crypto payment processing, operators can integrate with licensed crypto payment gateways that are registered with the CBB. These gateways handle the conversion of crypto to fiat and vice versa, and ensure compliance with local regulations. Compliance and Trust

Operating a crypto gambling business in Bahrain requires a strong compliance framework. The BTEA and CBB mandate the following:

  • AML/CFT program: Appoint a compliance officer, conduct customer due diligence (CDD), and report suspicious transactions.
  • Responsible gambling: Implement tools for self-exclusion, deposit limits, and reality checks.
  • Data protection: Comply with Bahrain's Personal Data Protection Law (PDPL) for handling player data.
  • Technical standards: Use certified random number generators (RNGs) and maintain game logs for at least 5 years.
  • Audit: Annual financial audit by a licensed auditor, plus periodic compliance reviews by the BTEA.

Failure to comply can result in fines, license suspension, or revocation. Consulting24 provides ongoing compliance support to ensure your operations remain in good standing. This is general guidance, not legal advice.

Common Mistakes When Applying

Many applicants encounter pitfalls that delay or derail their license. Avoid these common errors:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing business plans, AML policies, or financial statements cause immediate rejection.
  • Underestimating capital requirements: Failing to show the full USD 500,000 capital is a top reason for denial.
  • Ignoring the CBB review: The crypto endorsement is not automatic; prepare for a thorough AML inspection.
  • Choosing the wrong corporate structure: The company must be a Bahraini joint-stock company (BSC) with local directors.
  • Rushing the process: Expect 6-12 months; trying to speed through often leads to mistakes.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto gambling license in Bahrain?

The minimum paid-up capital is USD 500,000 for online gambling operators. This capital must be maintained throughout the license period and is subject to annual audit.

How long does it take to get a crypto gambling license in Bahrain?

The process typically takes 6 to 12 months from application submission to license issuance. Delays can occur if documentation is incomplete or if the CBB AML review is extensive.

What taxes apply to crypto gambling in Bahrain?

There is no corporate income tax or VAT on gambling. However, a 5% tax on gross gaming revenue (GGR) applies to online gambling, and 10% for land-based casinos.

Can I accept multiple cryptocurrencies with a Bahrain license?

Yes, the license allows acceptance of various cryptocurrencies, including Bitcoin, Ethereum, and stablecoins. You must use a CBB-approved crypto payment gateway for conversion and compliance.

Do I need a physical office in Bahrain?

Yes, you must have a registered office in Bahrain. The BTEA may also require a physical presence for interviews during the application process.

Is a crypto gambling license from Bahrain valid internationally?

The license allows you to offer services to players in jurisdictions where online gambling is legal. You are responsible for ensuring compliance with local laws in each target market.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

You must submit quarterly reports to the BTEA and CBB, conduct annual audits, maintain AML/CFT programs, and renew the license annually. Non-compliance can lead to fines or revocation.

Can I convert my existing gambling license to a crypto license?

If you already hold a BTEA gambling license, you can apply for a crypto endorsement. This involves an additional AML review by the CBB and a fee of USD 5,000-15,000.

How does Bahrain compare to Panama for crypto gambling?

Panama offers a cheaper and faster company setup (EUR 6,000, 2-4 weeks) but with lighter regulation. Bahrain provides more credibility and a clearer regulatory framework, which may be better for targeting regulated markets.

What happens if my application is rejected?

If rejected, you may appeal the decision or reapply after addressing the deficiencies. The application fee is non-refundable. Consulting24 can help identify and fix issues before resubmission.
