Overview: What Are El Salvador and Cyprus Crypto Licenses?

In 2026, both El Salvador and Cyprus offer formal licensing for crypto asset service providers (CASPs), but the underlying legal frameworks are fundamentally different. El Salvador's regime is built on the Bitcoin Law and subsequent regulations issued by the Central Reserve Bank of El Salvador (BCR). It is designed to attract crypto businesses with a light-touch, innovation-friendly approach. Cyprus, as an EU member, applies the Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA) through the Cyprus Securities and Exchange Commission (CySEC). This creates a harmonised, high-compliance environment.

An El Salvador crypto license is a registration with the BCR, allowing companies to operate as issuers of digital assets, exchanges, custodians, and payment processors. A Cyprus crypto license is a full CASP authorisation under MiCA, with tiered capital requirements and extensive AML/CFT obligations. The choice between them often comes down to target market, budget, and risk appetite.

Who Needs a Crypto License in El Salvador or Cyprus?

Any business that provides crypto services to residents or clients in either jurisdiction needs a license. This includes exchanges, wallet providers, custodians, payment processors, token issuers, and DeFi front-ends that are not fully decentralised. If your target market is Latin America or you prioritise speed and low cost, El Salvador may be the better fit. If you need to serve European clients and value regulatory clarity and passporting rights, Cyprus is the logical choice.

License Type and Regulator

El Salvador: The regulator is the Central Reserve Bank of El Salvador (BCR). The license is a registration for Digital Asset Service Providers (DASP). There are two main categories: (1) issuers of digital assets, including stablecoins and tokens, and (2) service providers such as exchanges, custodians, and payment processors. The regime is relatively new, with regulations updated in 2025 to align with international FATF standards.

Cyprus: The regulator is the Cyprus Securities and Exchange Commission (CySEC). The license is a CASP authorisation under MiCA. It is a full passportable license, meaning once obtained, you can operate across all EU/EEA member states without additional licensing. CySEC classifies services into: (a) custody and administration, (b) operation of a trading platform, (c) exchange of crypto for fiat, (d) exchange of crypto for crypto, (e) execution of orders on behalf of clients, (f) placement of crypto assets, (g) transfer services, and (h) advice and portfolio management.

Capital Requirement

El Salvador: There is no statutory minimum capital requirement for a DASP registration. However, the BCR expects applicants to demonstrate sufficient financial resources to operate and comply with AML obligations. In practice, a minimum of USD 10,000-50,000 in paid-up capital is recommended to show substance.

Cyprus: Under MiCA, capital requirements are tiered by service class. For custody and administration of crypto assets, the minimum is EUR 150,000. For operating a trading platform or exchanging crypto for fiat, the minimum is EUR 125,000. For other services such as transfer or advice, the minimum is EUR 50,000. These figures are strictly enforced and must be maintained as own funds.

Tax Treatment

El Salvador: El Salvador does not impose capital gains tax on Bitcoin or other crypto assets. Corporate income tax is standard at 30%, but there are no specific crypto taxes. The country has a territorial tax system, meaning income sourced outside El Salvador is generally not taxed. This can be highly advantageous for businesses that operate internationally.

Cyprus: Cyprus has one of the most favourable corporate tax regimes in the EU at 12.5%. Crypto gains are treated as corporate income and taxed accordingly. There is no withholding tax on dividends paid to non-residents, and no capital gains tax on the sale of securities (including crypto, under certain conditions). However, MiCA imposes reporting obligations to tax authorities. A Cyprus company can be highly tax-efficient when structured properly.

Allowed Activities

El Salvador: Licensed DASPs can engage in: issuance of digital assets (including stablecoins and security tokens), operation of crypto exchanges (both fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto), custody and wallet services, payment processing using crypto, and transfer services. There are no restrictions on DeFi or NFT platforms, but they must comply with AML regulations.

Cyprus: A Cyprus CASP license covers all services listed in MiCA: custody, exchange, platform operation, order execution, placement, transfer, and advice. Additionally, Cyprus allows the issuance of asset-referenced tokens (ARTs) and e-money tokens (EMTs) under separate regimes. DeFi protocols that are sufficiently decentralised may fall outside the scope, but most front-ends require authorisation.

Step-by-Step Application Process

El Salvador:

  1. Incorporate a Salvadoran company (Sociedad Anónima) with a local registered address and director.
  2. Prepare AML/KYC policies, risk assessment, and business plan.
  3. Submit application to the BCR with supporting documents, including proof of capital, background checks on directors, and a description of the business model.
  4. BCR reviews and may request additional information. In-person meetings are not required.
  5. Upon approval, pay the registration fee and receive the DASP certificate.
  6. Ongoing: submit annual reports and maintain compliance.

Cyprus:

  1. Incorporate a Cyprus limited company with a local registered office and at least two directors (one may be non-EU).
  2. Appoint a local compliance officer and AML officer.
  3. Prepare a comprehensive application pack: business plan, AML policies, risk management framework, IT security documentation, and capital proof.
  4. Submit to CySEC. The authority conducts a thorough review, including interviews with management.
  5. CySEC may impose conditions or request modifications. Typical timeline is 4-8 months.
  6. Once granted, the license is passportable across the EU. Ongoing reporting is quarterly.

Banking and Payment Processing

El Salvador: Banking relationships for crypto businesses are improving but remain limited. The state-owned Banco de Desarrollo de El Salvador (BANDESAL) offers accounts for crypto companies. Some private banks accept crypto clients, but many still hesitate. Payment processing via Bitcoin is well-established, with the Chivo wallet and other providers. For fiat on/off ramps, partnering with local payment processors is common.

Cyprus: Cyprus has a mature banking sector with several banks willing to onboard licensed CASPs, including Hellenic Bank and Bank of Cyprus. However, due diligence is rigorous. Payment processing for crypto is available through local and EU PSPs. Cyprus also has a growing fintech ecosystem that supports crypto payments.

Benefits of Each Jurisdiction

El Salvador:

  • Fast and low-cost licensing (2-4 months, under USD 25,000).
  • No capital gains tax on crypto.
  • Territorial tax system: income from abroad is tax-free.
  • Progressive government supportive of crypto innovation.
  • Bitcoin legal tender status provides a unique ecosystem.

Cyprus:

  • EU passportable license under MiCA, covering 30+ countries.
  • Low corporate tax rate (12.5%) and extensive double tax treaties.
  • High regulatory clarity and investor confidence.
  • Access to EU banking and payment infrastructure.
  • Well-established professional services ecosystem.

Compliance and Trust Considerations

Compliance is non-negotiable in both jurisdictions. El Salvador requires strong AML/CFT policies aligned with FATF recommendations. The BCR conducts periodic inspections. Cyprus, under MiCA, demands a higher level of compliance: mandatory segregation of client assets, detailed transaction monitoring, and regular audits. Both jurisdictions require the appointment of a local AML officer.

Trust is built through transparency. El Salvador's regime is newer and less tested, which may concern conservative investors. Cyprus's MiCA framework is widely recognised and trusted by banks, partners, and clients. However, the cost of compliance in Cyprus is higher. Common Mistakes When Choosing Between El Salvador and Cyprus

Founders often make the following errors:

  • Underestimating timeline: Cyprus can take 6 months or more; El Salvador is faster but still requires careful preparation.
  • Ignoring passporting needs: If you plan to serve EU clients, a Cyprus license is almost mandatory. El Salvador does not offer EU passporting.
  • Overlooking substance requirements: Both jurisdictions require local presence (office, director, compliance officer). Virtual setups are not accepted.
  • Choosing based solely on tax: While El Salvador has no capital gains tax, the lack of regulatory clarity may deter partners and banks. Balance tax benefits with operational needs.
  • Failing to budget for ongoing costs: Cyprus has higher annual compliance costs (audits, reporting, AML officer). El Salvador's ongoing costs are lower but still exist.

Alternatives and Comparisons: El Salvador vs Cyprus vs Other Jurisdictions

Beyond El Salvador and Cyprus, other jurisdictions may suit your needs. Panama offers a flat-fee EUR 6,000 company setup with no specific crypto license but a favourable tax regime (territorial, no capital gains). However, Panama does not provide a formal crypto license, which may limit banking and partner relationships. Lithuania is another EU option with a lower capital requirement (EUR 125,000) and faster processing than Cyprus, but it does not offer full MiCA passporting (as of 2026, Lithuania is transitioning to MiCA). Estonia has a well-known regime but higher capital (EUR 100,000) and stricter AML requirements.

Frequently asked questions

What is the main difference between an El Salvador and Cyprus crypto license?

El Salvador offers a fast, low-cost DASP registration with no capital gains tax, ideal for Latin American markets. Cyprus provides a full MiCA CASP license with EU passporting, higher compliance, and a 12.5% corporate tax rate. The choice depends on your target clients and budget.

Which jurisdiction is cheaper: El Salvador or Cyprus?

El Salvador is significantly cheaper, with total costs around USD 8,000-25,000 and no minimum capital. Cyprus costs EUR 40,000-100,000+ plus tiered capital of EUR 50,000-150,000. However, the Cyprus license offers EU-wide access, which may justify the higher cost.

Can I passport a Cyprus crypto license to other EU countries?

Yes, a Cyprus CASP license under MiCA is fully passportable across all EU/EEA member states. This means you can operate in 30+ countries without additional licensing, subject to notification procedures.

Does El Salvador require a minimum capital for a crypto license?

No statutory minimum capital is specified, but applicants must demonstrate sufficient financial resources. In practice, a minimum of USD 10,000-50,000 is recommended to show substance and meet AML requirements.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in El Salvador?

The process typically takes 2-4 months from submission to approval, assuming all documents are in order. This is faster than Cyprus (4-8 months) due to a simpler regulatory framework.

What are the tax benefits of El Salvador for crypto businesses?

El Salvador has no capital gains tax on crypto and a territorial tax system, meaning income from outside El Salvador is generally tax-free. Corporate income tax is 30% on local-source income, but many crypto businesses can structure to minimise tax.

Is a Cyprus crypto license recognised by banks?

Yes, Cyprus-licensed CASPs are well-regarded by EU banks, including Hellenic Bank and Bank of Cyprus. The MiCA framework provides regulatory clarity that facilitates banking relationships, though due diligence is still required.

Can I operate a DeFi platform with an El Salvador or Cyprus license?

In El Salvador, DeFi platforms are allowed if they comply with AML regulations and are not fully decentralised. In Cyprus, DeFi front-ends that exercise control may require a CASP license. Fully decentralised protocols may fall outside scope, but legal advice is essential.

What ongoing compliance is required in Cyprus?

Cyprus CASPs must submit quarterly reports to CySEC, undergo annual audits, maintain AML/CFT policies, appoint a local compliance officer, and ensure client asset segregation. Ongoing costs are typically EUR 10,000-25,000 per year.
