What Is a Crypto OTC Desk License in Germany?

A crypto OTC desk license in Germany is a regulatory authorisation from BaFin that allows a company to engage in over-the-counter trading of crypto assets. OTC desks facilitate large trades directly between counterparties, avoiding public exchanges. Under MiCA, this activity falls under the CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) regime, specifically for dealing on own account or execution of orders. Germany has a mature regulatory environment. BaFin requires a physical presence in Germany, a local compliance officer, and adherence to the German Banking Act (KWG) and the Anti-Money Laundering Act (GwG). The license is mandatory for any firm offering OTC crypto trading services to German residents.

For example, a London-based hedge fund wanting to execute a EUR 5 million Bitcoin trade with a German pension fund must use a BaFin-licensed OTC desk. The license ensures transparency, investor protection, and market integrity. Without it, the trade would be illegal and could result in fines or criminal charges. The license also covers related activities like price discovery and settlement, provided they are part of the OTC service.

Who Needs This License?

Any company that operates an OTC desk for crypto assets and serves German clients must obtain a BaFin license. This includes:

  • Proprietary trading firms that buy and sell crypto assets directly.
  • Brokers that match buyers and sellers of crypto assets off-exchange.
  • Market makers providing liquidity for crypto pairs.
  • Investment firms offering crypto derivatives or structured products.

If your business model involves large block trades, customised pricing, or institutional clients, the German OTC desk license is likely required. License Type and Regulator

The relevant regulator is the Federal Financial Supervisory Authority (BaFin). The license type under MiCA is a Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) license for the services of dealing on own account and execution of orders. Germany also requires a separate permission under the KWG for proprietary trading if the firm deals in financial instruments (including crypto). BaFin is known for its thorough review process. Applicants must demonstrate strong AML controls, a suitable business plan, and sufficient capital. The license is valid across the EU under the MiCA passport. BaFin also coordinates with the European Securities and Markets Authority (ESMA) for cross-border supervision. The application is submitted via BaFin's online portal, and the regulator typically assigns a case officer who reviews the submission in detail. The case officer may request additional information or clarifications, which can extend the timeline.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital for a CASP license in Germany depends on the services offered. For an OTC desk involving dealing on own account, the capital requirement is EUR 150,000. This is the highest tier under MiCA, reflecting the risk of proprietary trading. Additionally, BaFin may impose higher capital based on the volume of transactions. Capital must be held in liquid assets and demonstrated at the time of application. It is a key requirement for approval. For example, if your OTC desk expects to handle EUR 10 million in monthly trades, BaFin might require EUR 200,000 or more. The capital must be maintained at all times, and any shortfall must be reported immediately. Capital can be in the form of cash or highly liquid securities, but cannot include crypto assets themselves due to volatility. Consulting24 can advise on structuring your capital base.

Tax Treatment

Germany has a favourable tax regime for crypto assets. Private investors holding crypto for more than one year are exempt from capital gains tax. For businesses, corporate income tax (approx. 15%) plus trade tax (7-17%) applies to profits from OTC trading. VAT is not applicable on crypto-to-crypto trades, but fiat conversions may be subject to VAT. It is advisable to consult a German tax advisor for your specific structure. Consulting24 can recommend tax partners. For instance, a company making EUR 500,000 in annual OTC trading profit would pay roughly EUR 75,000 in corporate tax plus trade tax varying by municipality. Losses can be carried forward to offset future profits. Additionally, Germany does not impose a withholding tax on crypto dividends or interest, but you must report all transactions to the tax authorities. The tax treatment of staking or lending income is different and may require separate licensing.

Allowed Activities

With a German crypto OTC desk license, you can:

  • Buy and sell crypto assets directly with clients (dealing on own account).
  • Execute client orders for crypto assets.
  • Provide crypto-to-crypto and crypto-to-fiat trading.
  • Offer custody services if separately licensed (or as part of CASP).
  • Act as a market maker for crypto pairs.

You cannot engage in activities not covered by your license, such as operating a crypto exchange without additional permissions. For example, running a matching engine for multiple buyers and sellers would require a separate exchange license. Also, offering crypto derivatives may require a MiFID license. The license does not cover lending or borrowing of crypto assets unless explicitly included. If you plan to offer margin trading, you need to ensure it falls under the dealing on own account service. Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto OTC desk license in Germany?

The minimum capital is EUR 150,000 under MiCA for dealing on own account. BaFin may require more based on trade volume. Capital must be held in liquid assets and demonstrated at application.

How long does it take to get a German crypto OTC desk license?

The process takes 6-12 months from application to approval. This includes company setup, documentation, BaFin review, and final approvals. Delays can occur if additional information is requested.

Can I use a German crypto license to serve clients across the EU?

Yes, under the MiCA passport, a German CASP license allows you to provide services in any EU member state without additional licensing. You must notify BaFin of your cross-border activities.

What are the ongoing compliance costs for a German crypto OTC desk?

Ongoing costs include annual audits (EUR 5,000-15,000), AML software (EUR 5,000-20,000), compliance officer salary, and regulatory filing fees. Budget at least EUR 20,000 per year.

Do I need a physical office in Germany to get the license?

Yes, BaFin requires a registered office and local management. You must have a physical presence in Germany, including a compliance officer resident in the country.

What is the difference between a German crypto license and a Panama crypto license?

Panama offers a flat-fee license at EUR 6,000 with no minimum capital, but no EU passport. Germany requires EUR 150,000 capital and costs EUR 50,000-150,000, but provides EU-wide access and higher credibility.

Can I offer crypto custody with a German OTC desk license?

Custody is a separate service under MiCA. You can include it in your CASP license if you apply for the custody service. Otherwise, a separate license is needed. Consulting24 can advise on the scope.

What happens if I operate an OTC desk in Germany without a license?

Operating without a license is illegal and can result in fines up to EUR 5 million or 10% of annual turnover, imprisonment, and forced closure. BaFin actively monitors unlicensed activity.

Does Germany tax crypto-to-crypto trades for OTC desks?

Crypto-to-crypto trades are generally exempt from VAT, but corporate income tax and trade tax apply to profits. Tax treatment depends on the specific transaction and entity structure.

Can Consulting24 guarantee approval of my German crypto license?

No, we cannot guarantee approval. BaFin makes the final decision. We advise and coordinate the application to maximise your chances, but approval depends on your compliance with all requirements.
