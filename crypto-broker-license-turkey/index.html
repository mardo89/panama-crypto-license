What is the Crypto Broker License in Turkey?

The crypto broker license in Turkey, officially referred to as a Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) license, is a regulatory authorization issued by the Capital Markets Board (CMB). It allows companies to legally offer crypto asset trading, custody, and related services to Turkish residents. The license was introduced as part of Turkey's efforts to regulate the rapidly growing crypto market and combat money laundering and terrorist financing.

As of 2026, the CMB has fully implemented a licensing framework that requires all crypto asset service providers to obtain authorization before operating. The license covers activities such as buying and selling crypto assets, providing custody services, and operating a trading platform. Companies that fail to obtain the license face significant penalties, including fines and potential criminal liability.

Who Needs a Crypto Broker License in Turkey?

Any legal entity that intends to offer crypto asset services to customers in Turkey must obtain a CASP license from the CMB. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges (spot and derivatives)
  • Brokerage platforms that facilitate crypto trading
  • Custodians and wallet providers
  • Market makers and liquidity providers
  • Any firm that acts as an intermediary in crypto transactions

Foreign companies targeting Turkish customers also need to establish a local presence and obtain the license. The CMB requires that the entity be incorporated in Turkey as a joint stock company (Anonim Şirket) and have its real office and management in the country.

License Type and Regulator

The regulatory body for crypto asset service providers in Turkey is the Capital Markets Board of Turkey (CMB) (Sermaye Piyasası Kurulu - SPK). The CMB is responsible for issuing licenses, supervising compliance, and enforcing regulations under the Capital Markets Law and the recently enacted Crypto Asset Law (Law No. 7518).

The license type is a single authorization covering all crypto asset services, though additional permissions may be required for specific activities like derivatives trading. The CMB maintains a public registry of licensed providers, which enhances transparency and investor confidence.

As of 2026, the CMB has adopted a risk-based approach, with higher capital requirements for services that involve client funds or complex products. The regulator also requires that all licensed entities become members of the Turkish Capital Markets Association (TCMA) and comply with its rules.

Capital Requirement

The CMB imposes minimum capital requirements for crypto asset service providers, which are based on the type and volume of services offered. As of 2026, the minimum share capital for a crypto broker license is:

  • Basic services (e.g., spot trading, custody): TRY 5,000,000 (approximately EUR 150,000)
  • Advanced services (e.g., derivatives, margin trading): TRY 10,000,000 (approximately EUR 300,000)

These amounts must be fully paid in cash and deposited in a blocked account at a Turkish bank. The capital serves as a safeguard for client assets and operational stability. Additionally, the CMB may require higher capital for firms with large trading volumes or complex risk profiles.

It is important to note that the capital requirement is subject to periodic adjustment by the CMB. Consulting24 provides up-to-date information on capital thresholds during the initial consultation.

Tax Treatment for Crypto Brokers in Turkey

Turkey's tax regime for crypto asset activities is still evolving, but as of 2026, the following principles apply:

  • Corporate income tax: Crypto brokers are subject to the standard corporate tax rate of 25% on their profits.
  • Value Added Tax (VAT): Crypto trading services are generally exempt from VAT, following the European model.
  • Withholding tax: There is no specific withholding tax on crypto transactions, but income from crypto assets may be subject to personal income tax for individuals.

Brokers must also comply with transfer pricing rules and report transactions to the Turkish Revenue Administration. The CMB and the Ministry of Treasury and Finance are working on more detailed tax guidelines, which are expected to be published in 2026.

Allowed Activities Under the License

The crypto broker license in Turkey permits a wide range of activities, including but not limited to:

  • Buying and selling crypto assets for clients (brokerage)
  • Operating a crypto exchange platform (order matching)
  • Custody and safekeeping of private keys
  • Providing crypto-to-crypto and fiat-to-crypto trading pairs
  • Offering margin trading and derivatives (with additional permission)
  • Staking and lending services (subject to CMB approval)

Activities that are explicitly prohibited include issuing unregistered securities, offering anonymous transactions, and facilitating money laundering. The CMB also restricts the use of privacy coins and requires all listed assets to undergo a vetting process.

Brokers must implement strong AML/KYC procedures and report suspicious transactions to the Financial Crimes Investigation Board (MASAK).

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

After obtaining the license, brokers must adhere to ongoing compliance requirements:

  • Regular reporting: Submit periodic reports to the CMB on trading volumes, financial statements, and suspicious transactions.
  • Audits: Annual external audits by a CMB-approved audit firm.
  • Capital maintenance: Maintain the minimum capital at all times; any reduction must be reported.
  • AML/CFT updates: Continuously update AML policies and train staff.
  • IT security: Conduct regular penetration tests and maintain cybersecurity standards.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital requirement for a crypto broker license in Turkey?

As of 2026, the minimum capital is TRY 5,000,000 (approx. EUR 150,000) for basic services and TRY 10,000,000 (approx. EUR 300,000) for advanced services like derivatives. This capital must be fully paid and deposited in a blocked account.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Turkey?

The process typically takes 6 to 12 months from application submission to license issuance. Delays can occur if the application is incomplete or if the CMB requests additional information.

Can a foreign company apply for a Turkish crypto license?

Yes, but the company must first incorporate a joint stock company in Turkey with a physical office and local management. The license is issued to the Turkish entity, not the foreign parent.

What activities are allowed under the Turkish crypto broker license?

Allowed activities include spot trading, custody, exchange operation, margin trading (with permission), and staking. Prohibited activities include issuing unregistered securities and offering privacy coins.

Is there a tax exemption for crypto brokers in Turkey?

Crypto trading services are generally exempt from VAT. Corporate income tax applies at 25% on profits. There is no specific withholding tax on crypto transactions, but personal income tax may apply to individuals.

Do I need a local office in Turkey to get the license?

Yes, the CMB requires that the licensed entity have a physical office in Turkey with a minimum number of employees and a local manager. The office must be operational before the license is granted.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations after obtaining the license?

Ongoing obligations include regular reporting to the CMB, annual audits, maintaining minimum capital, updating AML policies, and conducting IT security tests. Non-compliance can lead to fines or license revocation.

How does the Turkish license compare to a Panama crypto license?

Panama offers a faster and cheaper option (EUR 6,000 flat fee, no capital requirement) but does not provide direct access to the Turkish market. Turkey requires higher investment but grants access to a large local user base and international credibility.
