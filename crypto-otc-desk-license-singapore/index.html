What Is a Crypto OTC Desk License in Singapore?

An OTC desk facilitates large-volume crypto trades directly between parties, bypassing public order books. In Singapore, this activity falls under the Payment Services Act as a Digital Payment Token (DPT) service. The license required is the Major Payment Institution (MPI) license, which authorises the provision of DPT services, including OTC trading, to customers. The regulator is the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS).

Unlike retail-focused exchanges, OTC desks typically serve high-net-worth individuals, family offices, and institutional clients. The MPI license imposes higher capital requirements and stricter compliance obligations compared to a standard payment institution license. As of 2026, the minimum base capital for an MPI license is SGD 250,000 (approximately EUR 170,000).

MAS defines DPT services broadly to include the exchange of any digital payment token for fiat currency or other DPTs. This means that even if your OTC desk does not take custody of client funds, you may still need an MPI license if you facilitate trades. The regulator expects firms to have strong systems in place to monitor transactions and prevent market abuse.

Who Needs This License?

Any business that facilitates the exchange of digital payment tokens (cryptocurrencies) for fiat currency or other DPTs on behalf of clients, particularly in large volumes, needs the MPI license. This includes:

  • OTC desks serving institutional investors
  • Crypto brokerages that match buyers and sellers off-exchange
  • Market makers providing liquidity via OTC channels
  • Any entity that regularly handles DPT transactions exceeding SGD 3 million per month

If your firm only facilitates peer-to-peer transfers without taking custody or executing trades, a Standard Payment Institution (SPI) license may suffice. However, most OTC desks will require the MPI license due to transaction volumes and custody of client assets. For example, a firm executing trades of SGD 5 million per month for institutional clients would clearly need the MPI license. Even smaller volumes may trigger the requirement if the business model involves frequent trading or holding client assets.

License Type and Regulator

The relevant license is the Major Payment Institution (MPI) License under the Payment Services Act (PSA). The regulator is the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS). MAS is the central bank and integrated financial regulator, responsible for licensing and supervising all payment service providers, including crypto OTC desks.

The PSA was amended in 2021 to bring DPT services under its ambit. As of 2026, MAS continues to refine its requirements, focusing on anti-money laundering (AML), countering the financing of terrorism (CFT), and consumer protection. Firms must also comply with the Payment Services (Amendment) Act 2022, which introduced additional measures for DPT service providers, including segregation of client assets and enhanced disclosure requirements.

MAS requires all MPI license holders to have a physical presence in Singapore, including a registered office and at least one executive director who is a Singapore resident. This ensures that the regulator can effectively supervise the entity.

Capital Requirement

The minimum base capital for an MPI license is SGD 250,000 (approximately EUR 170,000). However, MAS may require higher capital based on the volume of transactions and risk profile. The capital must be maintained at all times and can be in the form of cash or liquid assets. There is no specific audit requirement for the capital itself, but the firm must demonstrate ongoing solvency.

For comparison, the EU's MiCA framework (fully in force in 2026) requires capital of EUR 125,000 for OTC services (Class 2), which is lower than Singapore's requirement. However, Singapore's regulatory environment is considered more stable and business-friendly for crypto OTC desks. In practice, many applicants maintain a capital buffer of SGD 500,000 or more to satisfy MAS's expectations and to cover operational risks.

Tax Treatment

Singapore does not impose capital gains tax, which is a significant advantage for crypto OTC desks. However, income from trading activities is subject to corporate income tax at a flat rate of 17%. The Inland Revenue Authority of Singapore (IRAS) treats cryptocurrency as an intangible asset for tax purposes.

For OTC desks, the key tax considerations include:

  • GST (Goods and Services Tax) at 9% applies to fees charged for DPT services, but not to the underlying exchange of DPTs.
  • Withholding tax on payments to non-residents (e.g., for consultancy services) may apply at rates of 10-15% depending on the tax treaty.
  • Transfer pricing rules apply for related-party transactions, requiring documentation to ensure arm's length pricing.

Consulting24 advises clients on structuring their Singapore entity to optimise tax efficiency while remaining compliant. For example, holding the intellectual property of the trading platform in a separate entity can reduce taxable profits.

Allowed Activities

An MPI license for DPT services permits the following activities:

  • Facilitating the exchange of digital payment tokens (cryptocurrencies) for fiat currency or other DPTs.
  • Providing OTC trading services to institutional and accredited investors.
  • Acting as a market maker for DPTs.
  • Providing custody services for DPTs (if separately licensed).

Activities not allowed include: dealing in securities (which requires a Capital Markets Services license), offering derivatives, or providing services to retail clients without additional safeguards. MAS restricts OTC desks to serving only institutional and accredited investors, unless the firm obtains a separate license for retail services. For example, if your OTC desk wants to serve retail clients, you would need to apply for a separate license under the Securities and Futures Act.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Applying for an MPI license involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates the entire process:

  1. Pre-application preparation: Develop a comprehensive business plan, AML/CFT policies, risk assessment, and compliance manual. Engage a law firm to draft legal documents. This stage typically takes 2-4 months.
  2. Submit application to MAS: File the application via MAS's online portal, including all required documents and the non-refundable fee of SGD 10,000. Ensure all information is accurate to avoid delays.
  3. MAS review and queries: MAS will review the application and may request additional information. Respond promptly to avoid delays. Common queries relate to the source of funds, beneficial ownership, and the AML framework.
  4. On-site inspection: MAS may conduct an on-site inspection of your premises and systems. Prepare by having all compliance records and IT systems ready for review.
  5. Approval and licensing: Once satisfied, MAS issues the MPI license. The firm must then comply with ongoing reporting and audit requirements, including quarterly transaction reports and annual audits.

Typical timeline: 6-12 months from submission to approval. For a faster route, consider jurisdictions like Panama (flat EUR 6,000) or Estonia, which Consulting24 delivers directly.

Benefits of Licensing in Singapore

Singapore offers several advantages for crypto OTC desks:

  • Regulatory clarity: MAS provides clear guidelines and a stable regulatory environment, reducing legal uncertainty.
  • No capital gains tax: Profits from trading are not subject to CGT, enhancing net returns.
  • Strategic location: Gateway to Asia-Pacific markets, with strong connectivity to Hong Kong, Japan, and Australia.
  • Strong banking infrastructure: Access to global financial networks and multi-currency accounts.
  • High credibility: An MPI license enhances trust with institutional clients and counterparties.

Compared to other jurisdictions such as Lithuania or Panama, Singapore offers a premium regulatory framework but at a higher cost. For firms targeting Asian markets, the investment is often justified by the access to a sophisticated investor base.

Compliance and Trust

Compliance is critical for maintaining an MPI license. Key requirements include:

  • Appointing a compliance officer and MLRO (Money Laundering Reporting Officer) who are based in Singapore.
  • Conducting regular AML/CFT training for staff, at least annually.
  • Performing customer due diligence (CDD) and enhanced due diligence (EDD) for high-risk clients, including source of funds verification.
  • Reporting suspicious transactions to the Suspicious Transaction Reporting Office (STRO) within 15 days.
  • Annual audit by an external auditor approved by MAS.

Failure to comply can result in fines up to SGD 1 million, suspension, or revocation of the license. This is general guidance, not legal advice. Always consult with a qualified lawyer. Consulting24 helps clients implement strong compliance programs that meet MAS expectations.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Many applicants face delays or rejection due to common pitfalls:

  • Incomplete application: Missing documents or insufficient detail, such as not providing a detailed business plan or risk assessment.
  • Weak AML framework: MAS places heavy emphasis on AML/CFT policies; a generic template is not acceptable.
  • Unrealistic business plan: Overly optimistic projections without supporting data, like projecting millions in revenue within the first year without a clear client acquisition strategy.
  • Ignoring MAS queries: Slow responses can stall the process; aim to respond within 5 business days.
  • Using unlicensed service providers: Ensure all partners, such as custodians or payment processors, are licensed or regulated.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between an MPI license and an SPI license for crypto OTC desks?

The Major Payment Institution (MPI) license is for firms handling transaction volumes exceeding SGD 3 million per month or holding client assets. The Standard Payment Institution (SPI) license is for lower volumes. Most OTC desks need the MPI license due to high trade values.

Can a foreign company apply for an MPI license in Singapore?

Yes, but the company must be incorporated in Singapore as a private limited company. Foreign entities can set up a subsidiary. The directors must include at least one Singapore resident, and the company must have a physical office in Singapore.

How long does it take to get an MPI license for an OTC desk?

The MAS typically takes 6 to 12 months to process an application, depending on completeness and responsiveness. Pre-submission preparation can take 2-4 months. Total timeline from start to approval is usually 8-16 months.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements after obtaining the license?

Ongoing requirements include quarterly transaction reporting, annual AML/CFT audits, maintaining a compliance officer and MLRO, conducting regular staff training, and reporting suspicious transactions. Failure to comply can result in fines or license revocation.

Is there a minimum capital requirement for an MPI license?

Yes, the minimum base capital is SGD 250,000 (approximately EUR 170,000). MAS may require higher capital based on transaction volumes and risk profile. The capital must be maintained in liquid assets.

What is the tax rate for crypto OTC desks in Singapore?

Corporate income tax is 17% on profits. There is no capital gains tax. GST at 9% applies to fees charged for DPT services but not to the underlying exchange of tokens. Withholding tax may apply on payments to non-residents.

Can an OTC desk serve retail clients in Singapore?

Generally, MPI license holders are restricted to serving institutional and accredited investors. To serve retail clients, additional safeguards and possibly a separate license under the Securities and Futures Act are required.

How does Singapore compare to Panama for crypto OTC desk licensing?

Singapore offers a strong regulatory framework and credibility but at high cost (EUR 75k-180k) and long timeline (6-12 months). Panama has no specific crypto license, low cost (EUR 6,000 flat), and fast setup (2-4 weeks), but less regulatory clarity.

What banking options are available for crypto OTC desks in Singapore?

Major banks like DBS and OCBC are cautious but may open accounts for licensed entities. Digital banks and fintech-friendly providers are more accessible. Requirements include a valid license, strong AML policies, and a clear business model.
