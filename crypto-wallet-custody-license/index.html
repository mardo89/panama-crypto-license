What Is a Crypto Wallet Custody License?

A crypto wallet custody license authorises a company to hold, store, and safeguard private keys or digital assets on behalf of clients. Under MiCA, this activity falls under the CASP category known as 'custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients'. The licence is distinct from exchange or trading licences, though many firms combine them.

Key features of a custody licence include:

  • Legal permission to hold client crypto-assets in segregated wallets.
  • Obligation to implement strong security measures (e.g., multi-signature, cold storage).
  • Compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) rules.
  • Regular reporting to the national competent authority (NCA).

In 2026, any EU-based custodian must hold a CASP licence. Non-EU jurisdictions like Panama offer alternative frameworks with lower capital requirements and faster setup times. For example, a Panama company setup costs a flat EUR 6,000 and can be operational in 1-2 months, whereas an EU CASP licence may take 3-6 months and cost significantly more.

Who Needs This License?

The crypto wallet custody license is essential for a wide range of businesses:

  • Wallet providers: Companies offering hosted (custodial) wallets to retail or institutional clients.
  • Exchanges: Platforms that hold client funds in exchange wallets.
  • Asset managers: Firms managing crypto portfolios on behalf of investors.
  • Payment processors: Businesses facilitating crypto payments that temporarily hold funds.
  • Institutional custodians: Specialised firms serving hedge funds, family offices, and pension funds.

If your business model involves taking control of a client's private keys, even temporarily, you likely need this licence. Non-custodial wallet providers may not require it, but many choose to obtain one to offer hybrid services or build trust. For instance, a DeFi platform that offers a non-custodial wallet might still seek a licence to provide custodial staking services, thereby expanding its revenue streams.

License Type and Regulator

Under MiCA, the crypto wallet custody license is a specific CASP authorisation. The regulator varies by EU member state:

  • Estonia: Financial Supervision Authority (FINSA). Estonia transitioned from its earlier VASP regime to MiCA-compliant CASP licensing in 2025.
  • Lithuania: Bank of Lithuania. Lithuania offers a streamlined process for CASP licences.
  • Other EU states: Respective NCAs (e.g., BaFin in Germany, AMF in France).

Outside the EU, Panama is a popular alternative. The regulator is the Superintendencia de Bancos de Panamá (SBP) for banking-related custody, or the Superintendencia del Mercado de Valores (SMV) for securities. Panama does not yet have a specific crypto custody licence, but firms can operate under a general business licence with AML compliance.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for a CASP licence depends on the services offered. For custody and administration of crypto-assets, the requirement is EUR 125,000. If combined with other services (e.g., exchange, trading), higher tiers apply:

  • EUR 50,000 for advisory or reception/transmission of orders.
  • EUR 125,000 for custody, exchange, and execution of orders.
  • EUR 150,000 for placing crypto-assets.

In Panama, there is no specific minimum capital for crypto custody, but a general business licence may require a minimum of USD 10,000 (approximately EUR 9,300). However, banks may impose their own requirements for corporate accounts.

These figures are subject to change; always verify with your regulator or a professional advisor. For instance, if you plan to offer both custody and exchange services in Lithuania, you would need EUR 125,000 in capital.

Tax Treatment

Tax treatment of custodial wallet services varies by jurisdiction:

  • Estonia: Corporate income tax is 0% on retained earnings; only distributed profits are taxed at 20%. Crypto custody fees are subject to VAT at the standard rate of 20%.
  • Lithuania: Corporate tax is 15% (5% for small companies). Crypto custody services are VAT-exempt in Lithuania, as they are considered financial services.
  • Panama: Territorial taxation: only income sourced in Panama is taxed. Corporate tax is 25% on Panamanian-source income. No VAT on services.

Tax planning is crucial. Many firms choose Lithuania for its VAT exemption, while Panama offers territorial tax advantages. Consulting24 advises on tax-efficient structures. For example, a custody service earning EUR 1 million in fees would pay EUR 150,000 in corporate tax in Lithuania, but only on Panamanian-source income if incorporated in Panama.

Allowed Activities

A crypto wallet custody license typically permits the following activities:

  • Safekeeping and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients.
  • Generation and storage of private keys.
  • Transfer of crypto-assets between wallets.
  • Reporting and reconciliation of client holdings.
  • Integration with other CASP services (e.g., exchange, staking) if separately licensed.

Prohibited activities usually include proprietary trading with client assets, lending without specific authorisation, and providing investment advice without a separate licence. Always check the exact scope with your regulator. For instance, in Lithuania, a custody licence does not automatically permit staking; you would need a separate authorisation or a licence amendment.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Obtaining a crypto wallet custody license involves several stages. Here is a typical process for an EU CASP licence:

  1. Pre-application: Define your business model, select jurisdiction, and engage a compliance advisor (like Consulting24).
  2. Company setup: Incorporate a legal entity in the chosen jurisdiction (e.g., a UAB in Lithuania).
  3. Documentation: Prepare a business plan, AML/CTF policies, risk assessment, security protocols, and governance framework.
  4. Capital deposit: Place the required minimum capital in a bank account (e.g., EUR 125,000 for custody).
  5. Submission: File the application with the NCA, including all supporting documents.
  6. Review and interview: The regulator reviews the application, may request clarifications, and often interviews key personnel.
  7. Approval: If satisfied, the regulator grants the CASP licence. This can take 3-6 months.

For Panama, the process is simpler: incorporate a company, obtain a business licence, and register with the Financial Analysis Unit (UAF) for AML compliance. The entire process can be completed in 1-2 months with a flat fee of EUR 6,000.

Banking and Payment Solutions

One of the biggest challenges for crypto custodians is opening a corporate bank account. Many traditional banks are wary of crypto exposure. However, several options exist:

  • Lithuania: Local banks like Swedbank and SEB accept CASP-licensed firms, though due diligence is thorough.
  • Estonia: Banks such as LHV and Coop Pank are crypto-friendly, especially for licensed entities.
  • Panama: Many international banks operate in Panama, but crypto firms face similar scrutiny.
  • EMI solutions: Electronic money institutions (e.g., Paysera, Monese) offer accounts for crypto businesses.

Benefits of a Crypto Wallet Custody License

Obtaining a formal licence offers several advantages:

  • Legal certainty: Operate with full regulatory approval, reducing legal risk.
  • Client trust: Licensed custodians attract institutional clients who require regulated partners.
  • Access to banking: Licensed firms find it easier to open bank accounts.
  • Passporting: An EU CASP licence allows you to serve clients across the EEA without additional licences.
  • Tax benefits: Jurisdictions like Lithuania and Panama offer favourable tax regimes.

For many firms, the licence is a competitive differentiator in a crowded market. For instance, a licensed custodian can charge higher fees than an unlicensed one, as clients are willing to pay a premium for regulatory protection.

Compliance and Trust

Compliance is the backbone of a successful custody operation. Key requirements include:

  • AML/CTF programme: Customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting.
  • Security standards: ISO 27001 certification, cold storage for majority of funds, multi-signature wallets.
  • Audit trail: Immutable records of all transactions and client holdings.
  • Regular reporting: Submit financial and operational reports to the regulator.

Note: This is general guidance, not legal advice. You should engage a qualified legal professional for your specific situation. For example, a custodian holding EUR 10 million in client assets should have a security audit conducted quarterly and maintain insurance coverage against theft or loss.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Many applicants encounter pitfalls. Avoid these common errors:

  • Underestimating capital: Ensure you have the full capital requirement in liquid funds before applying.
  • Poor documentation: Vague business plans or incomplete AML policies lead to rejection.
  • Choosing the wrong jurisdiction: Some regulators are slower or more demanding. Lithuania is often faster than Germany.
  • Ignoring tax implications: Failing to structure for tax efficiency can cost you later.
  • Neglecting banking: Start the bank account process early; it can take months.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a custodial and non-custodial wallet license?

A custodial wallet license is required when you hold private keys for clients. Non-custodial wallets, where users control their own keys, typically do not require a license. However, if you offer additional services like staking or exchange, you may still need a CASP license.

Can I use a Panama company to serve EU clients?

Panama is not in the EU, so a Panama license does not allow you to passport services into the EEA. You would need a separate CASP license in an EU member state to serve EU clients. Panama is suitable for non-EU clients or as a holding company.

How long does the Lithuanian CASP application take?

Typically 2-4 months from submission to approval. The Bank of Lithuania is known for its efficient process. However, delays can occur if documentation is incomplete. Consulting24 can help prepare a complete application to minimise delays.

What are the ongoing compliance costs for a custody license?

Ongoing costs include AML officer salary, audit fees, regulatory reporting, and software for transaction monitoring. In Lithuania, expect annual costs of EUR 10,000-20,000. In Panama, costs are lower, around EUR 5,000-10,000 per year.

Do I need a physical office to obtain a custody license?

Yes, most EU regulators require a physical registered office and a local presence. In Lithuania, you need a local director or compliance officer. Panama requires a registered agent but not necessarily a physical office.

Can I offer staking with a custody license?

Staking is not automatically covered under a custody license. In the EU, staking may require a separate CASP authorisation or a licence amendment. In Panama, there is no specific regulation, but you should ensure your business licence covers the activity.

What happens if I operate without a license?

Operating without a required license can lead to fines, forced closure, and even criminal charges. In the EU, unlicensed CASP activity is illegal under MiCA. In Panama, the UAF can impose penalties for non-compliance with AML rules.
