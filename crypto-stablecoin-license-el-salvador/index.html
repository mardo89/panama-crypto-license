What Is the Crypto Stablecoin License in El Salvador?

The crypto stablecoin license in El Salvador is not a separate permit but is integrated into the Digital Asset Service Provider (DASP) authorization under the Digital Assets Securities Law. This law, enacted in 2023 and fully operational by 2026, regulates all digital asset activities including issuance, trading, custody, and exchange of stablecoins. The regulator is the Comisión Nacional de Activos Digitales (CNAD), which grants licenses to both local and foreign entities.

Key features of the regime include:

  • Legal recognition of stablecoins as digital assets, not securities (unless they represent equity or debt).
  • Issuers must comply with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) rules, including know-your-customer (KYC) requirements.
  • Stablecoin reserves must be held in a qualified custodian, with regular audits.
  • The license allows for the issuance of fiat-backed, crypto-backed, or algorithmic stablecoins, subject to CNAD approval.
  • Issuers must publish regular reserve attestations and undergo third-party audits.

The CNAD has published detailed guidelines for stablecoin issuers, including reserve requirements, disclosure obligations, and operational standards. The license is non-transferable and must be renewed annually. This capital must be maintained at all times and can be in the form of cash or liquid assets. Higher capital may be required depending on the volume of stablecoins issued or the risk profile of the business.

Tax Treatment

El Salvador offers a territorial tax system: income earned within El Salvador is taxed at a standard corporate rate of 30%, while foreign-source income is generally exempt. However, stablecoin issuance may be considered a local activity if the issuer operates from El Salvador. Additionally, there is no VAT on digital asset transactions, and capital gains from crypto are not taxed if the assets are held for more than one year.

Panama also uses a territorial tax system with no corporate income tax on foreign-source income, and no capital gains tax on crypto. This makes Panama highly attractive for stablecoin issuers targeting global markets. Consulting24 can advise on tax structuring for both jurisdictions.

Allowed Activities Under the License

The DASP license in El Salvador permits the following activities related to stablecoins:

  • Issuance of stablecoins (fiat-backed, crypto-backed, or algorithmic).
  • Exchange of stablecoins for other digital assets or fiat currency.
  • Custody and safekeeping of stablecoins.
  • Payment services using stablecoins, including remittances.
  • Staking and lending of stablecoins (if approved by CNAD).
  • Conversion between stablecoins and fiat currency.

Activities not allowed include operating as a bank or taking deposits without a banking license. The CNAD also prohibits the issuance of stablecoins that could be considered securities without proper registration.
