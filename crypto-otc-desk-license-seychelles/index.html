What is a Crypto OTC Desk License in Seychelles?

A crypto OTC desk license in Seychelles permits a company to facilitate large-volume cryptocurrency trades directly between buyers and sellers, outside of public exchanges. The license is issued under the Financial Services Authority (FSA) of Seychelles, which regulates securities and virtual asset services. The primary license type is the Securities Dealer License, which covers OTC trading of virtual assets. This license is essential for businesses that want to offer liquidity, execute block trades, or provide institutional-grade crypto trading services.

Seychelles is known for its business-friendly environment, with no capital gains tax and a simple corporate tax regime. The regulatory framework is designed to balance innovation with investor protection. For OTC desks, the license allows you to operate legally and access banking and payment partners more easily. Unlike some jurisdictions that require a separate license for each activity, Seychelles' Securities Dealer License covers a broad range of virtual asset services, making it a versatile choice for firms that may expand into other areas later.

Key features of the license include: no statutory minimum capital, fast processing times (2-4 months), and low ongoing costs. However, the FSA does require strong AML/CTF policies and annual reporting. Consulting24 advises clients on structuring their operations to meet these requirements efficiently.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Financial Services Authority (FSA) of Seychelles. The relevant license is a Securities Dealer License, which covers dealing in virtual assets as securities. The FSA classifies virtual assets as securities, so OTC trading falls under this regime. There is no separate 'crypto license' per se; instead, the Securities Dealer License is the appropriate authorization. The FSA requires firms to comply with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorist financing (CTF) regulations, and to have a physical office in Seychelles or a registered agent.

The FSA also publishes guidelines on virtual asset services, including requirements for custody, disclosure, and market conduct. Firms must appoint a compliance officer and a money laundering reporting officer (MLRO). The FSA conducts background checks on directors and shareholders, including criminal record checks and financial soundness assessments. Capital Requirement

Seychelles does not impose a statutory minimum capital requirement for a Securities Dealer License. However, the FSA expects firms to maintain adequate financial resources to cover operational risks. As a guideline, most applicants set aside at least EUR 50,000 in paid-up capital, though this is not a fixed rule. The exact amount depends on the scale of your OTC operations. In contrast, a smaller desk with EUR 1 million volume could operate with EUR 30,000. The FSA reviews capital adequacy on a case-by-case basis. Tax Treatment

Seychelles offers a highly favorable tax environment. There is no capital gains tax, and corporate income tax is a flat 15% on profits derived from Seychelles. However, offshore companies (International Business Companies, IBCs) can be tax-exempt if they do not conduct business locally. Most crypto OTC desks are set up as IBCs, paying zero tax on foreign-source income. Additionally, there is no withholding tax on dividends or interest. This makes Seychelles a tax-efficient jurisdiction for crypto trading.

Important: If you or your directors are tax residents in another country, you may still be liable for taxes there. For example, a UK resident director might need to declare Seychelles company profits under UK tax rules. Consulting24 recommends consulting a tax advisor in your home country. The Seychelles tax regime is particularly attractive for firms that generate revenue from non-Seychelles clients, as is typical for OTC desks.

Allowed Activities

Under the Securities Dealer License, you can:

  • Execute OTC trades in cryptocurrencies and tokens
  • Provide liquidity and market-making services
  • Offer custody of virtual assets
  • Facilitate tokenized securities trading
  • Act as a broker between buyers and sellers

Note: The license does not permit operating a public exchange or accepting retail deposits. If you need exchange functionality, consider a separate exchange license or a combined license. The FSA also restricts certain activities like lending or staking unless explicitly authorized. For example, if you want to offer crypto-backed loans, you may need a separate lending license. Frequently asked questions

What is a crypto OTC desk license?

A crypto OTC desk license allows a company to facilitate large cryptocurrency trades directly between buyers and sellers, off-exchange. In Seychelles, it is regulated by the FSA under a Securities Dealer License.

How much does a Seychelles crypto OTC desk license cost?

Total costs typically range from EUR 5,000 to EUR 8,500, including company incorporation and application fees. Exact pricing is confirmed during consultation.

How long does it take to get the license?

The FSA processes applications in 2-4 months, depending on document completeness and due diligence. Consulting24 helps streamline the process.

Is there a minimum capital requirement?

Seychelles does not set a statutory minimum, but the FSA expects adequate financial resources. Most firms maintain at least EUR 50,000 in paid-up capital.

What taxes apply in Seychelles?

Seychelles IBCs pay zero tax on foreign-source income. There is no capital gains tax. Corporate tax is 15% only on local profits.

Can I operate a crypto exchange with this license?

No, the Securities Dealer License covers OTC trading, not public exchange operations. For an exchange, you need a separate license or a combined one.

Do I need a physical office in Seychelles?

Yes, you must have a registered office in Seychelles, which can be provided by a registered agent. A physical presence is not required.

What documents are needed for the application?

You need a business plan, AML/CTF policies, risk assessment, financial projections, and personal documents of directors/shareholders (passport, proof of address, CV).

Can Consulting24 guarantee approval?

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

Annual filings, AML audits, beneficial ownership disclosure, and renewal fees. Consulting24 can help you set up a compliance calendar.

Can I use the license to serve EU clients?

If you actively market to EU clients, you may need to comply with MiCA. Seychelles does not provide EU passporting. Consult a lawyer for cross-border rules.

How does Seychelles compare to Panama?

Both are low-cost, tax-free jurisdictions. Panama is faster (1-2 months) and cheaper (EUR 6,000 flat), but Seychelles has a more established crypto regulatory framework. See our Panama page for details.
