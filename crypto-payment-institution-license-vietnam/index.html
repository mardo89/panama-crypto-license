Overview: What Is the Crypto Payment Institution License Vietnam?

The crypto payment institution license Vietnam is a regulatory authorization issued by the State Bank of Vietnam (SBV) that permits companies to offer payment services involving cryptocurrencies and digital assets. This license is designed for businesses that facilitate the exchange of crypto for fiat, provide crypto payment processing, and manage digital wallets. It is distinct from a general crypto exchange license, as it focuses specifically on payment-related activities.

As of 2026, Vietnam has moved beyond a sandbox approach and now has a formal licensing regime for crypto payment services. The license is part of the country's efforts to regulate digital assets while supporting innovation. Companies holding this license must comply with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) regulations, as well as consumer protection standards. Consulting24 advises clients on the nuances of this license and coordinates the application process with local partners.

The license is not a blanket permission for all crypto activities. It specifically covers payment services, meaning that activities like crypto trading as a principal business or operating a crypto exchange require a separate license. The SBV has published guidelines on what constitutes a payment service, including the transfer of funds, direct debits, and payment instruments. Crypto payment services fall under these definitions when digital assets are used as a medium of exchange. Consulting24 helps you map your business activities to the correct license category.

Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a crypto payment institution license Vietnam is set by the State Bank of Vietnam. As of 2026, the required paid-up capital is VND 50 billion (approximately EUR 1.9 million) for payment institutions offering crypto services. This capital must be maintained throughout the license period and can be in the form of cash or liquid assets.

This requirement is relatively high compared to other jurisdictions, reflecting Vietnam's cautious approach to digital assets. However, for serious payment businesses, this capital level ensures stability and consumer protection. Tax Treatment for Crypto Payment Institutions in Vietnam

Vietnam's tax treatment for crypto payment institutions is evolving. As of 2026, the following tax rules apply:

  • Corporate income tax: Standard rate of 20% on net profits from crypto payment services.
  • Value-added tax (VAT): Crypto payment services are generally exempt from VAT, but fees charged to merchants may be subject to 10% VAT.
  • Withholding tax: Payments to non-residents for services may be subject to withholding tax at rates specified in tax treaties.
  • Personal income tax: Employees of crypto payment institutions are taxed at progressive rates.

It is advisable to consult with a local tax advisor, as the tax environment can change. Consulting24 coordinates with tax experts to ensure your business is tax compliant.

Transfer pricing rules apply if your Vietnamese entity transacts with related parties abroad. The tax authority may scrutinize intercompany fees and profit allocation. Allowed Activities Under the Crypto Payment Institution License

Holders of the crypto payment institution license Vietnam are permitted to engage in the following activities:

  • Facilitating the exchange of cryptocurrencies for fiat currency
  • Providing digital wallet services for storing and transferring crypto
  • Processing crypto payments for merchants
  • Offering crypto-to-crypto exchange services (if ancillary to payment services)
  • Providing remittance services using blockchain technology

Activities strictly prohibited include: operating a crypto exchange as a primary business (requires a separate exchange license), issuing unregistered tokens, and engaging in speculative trading on behalf of customers. Compliance and Trust: AML and Ongoing Obligations

Compliance is a cornerstone of the crypto payment institution license Vietnam. License holders must implement strong AML/CFT programs, including:

  • Customer due diligence (CDD) and know-your-customer (KYC) procedures, including enhanced due diligence for high-risk customers
  • Transaction monitoring and reporting of suspicious activities to the SBV's financial intelligence unit
  • Record keeping for at least 5 years, including transaction records and identification data
  • Appointment of a compliance officer who is a resident of Vietnam
  • Regular audits and reporting to the SBV, including annual AML reports

Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. The SBV conducts periodic inspections.
