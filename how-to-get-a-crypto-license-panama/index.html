What Is a Panama Crypto License?

Panama does not issue a standalone 'crypto license' in the traditional sense. Instead, crypto businesses operate under a general business license combined with a registration with the Financial Analysis Unit (UAF) for anti-money laundering (AML) compliance. The UAF is the regulator responsible for supervising virtual asset service providers (VASPs) under Panama's AML laws. As of 2026, Panama has not implemented MiCA-style capital requirements, making it one of the most accessible jurisdictions for crypto startups.

The licence allows companies to offer exchange services, wallet custody, and other VASP activities. The process is streamlined, with a typical timeline of 4-8 weeks from company formation to operational readiness. For founders comparing options, Panama stands out for its low barrier to entry. Unlike EU member states where MiCA now imposes capital tiers of EUR 50,000 to 150,000, Panama requires no paid-up capital. This makes it a popular choice for early-stage projects and bootstrapped teams. However, the UAF expects a strong AML framework, so preparation is key.

Who Needs a Panama Crypto License?

Any business that provides virtual asset services in or from Panama must register with the UAF. This includes:

  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (centralized and peer-to-peer)
  • Wallet providers (custodial and non-custodial)
  • OTC desks and payment processors handling crypto
  • DeFi platforms that offer custodial services

Founders who want to avoid the high capital requirements of EU jurisdictions (EUR 50,000 to 150,000 under MiCA) often choose Panama. It is also ideal for projects targeting Latin American markets or those seeking a tax-neutral base. License Type and Regulator

The relevant regulator is the Financial Analysis Unit (UAF) of Panama. The registration is not a financial services license but an AML compliance registration under Law 23 of 2015 (as amended). Crypto businesses must also hold a general commercial license from the Panama Public Registry. There is no separate 'crypto license' category; the UAF registration is the key requirement.

This approach means lower upfront costs and less bureaucratic overhead compared to jurisdictions like Lithuania or Estonia. However, the UAF expects strong AML/KYC policies. Consulting24 ensures your application meets all regulatory standards.

Capital Requirement

Panama has no minimum capital requirement for crypto businesses. This is a major differentiator from EU MiCA jurisdictions, which impose capital tiers of EUR 50,000 (for exchange services), EUR 125,000 (for custody), or EUR 150,000 (for combined services). In Panama, you can start with a share capital as low as USD 10,000 (approximately EUR 9,200), but even this is not strictly enforced for UAF registration. The lack of capital requirement makes Panama ideal for bootstrapped startups and early-stage projects.

Note: While no minimum capital is required, you must demonstrate sufficient operational funds to run AML compliance. Consulting24 recommends maintaining at least EUR 10,000 in the company account to cover initial costs.

For comparison, a Lithuania MiCA license now requires EUR 125,000 for custody services, which can be a barrier. Panama removes that hurdle entirely.

Tax Treatment

Panama operates on a territorial tax system. Income earned outside Panama is tax-free. For crypto businesses serving international clients, this means zero corporate tax on foreign-source profits. Local-source income (e.g., from Panamanian residents) is taxed at a standard rate of 25%. However, most crypto exchanges target global users, making Panama a tax-efficient base.

There is no VAT on digital services, and no capital gains tax on crypto transactions. Panama also has no central bank restrictions on foreign currency holdings. This tax regime is a key reason why founders choose Panama over jurisdictions like Lithuania or Malta. For detailed tax planning, consult a local advisor.

If your revenue is entirely from non-Panamanian clients, your effective tax rate can be 0%. This is a powerful advantage for scaling.

Allowed Activities

Under Panama's AML framework, registered VASPs can conduct the following activities:

  • Exchange of virtual assets for fiat currency or other virtual assets
  • Custody and wallet services (both hot and cold storage)
  • Payment processing using virtual assets
  • OTC trading and brokerage services
  • Issuance of stablecoins or utility tokens (subject to securities law review)

Activities that are not allowed without additional licensing include: operating as a securities exchange (regulated by the Superintendence of the Securities Market), banking services, or gambling. DeFi protocols that are fully non-custodial may not require registration, but legal advice is recommended.

If your business model includes token issuance, you may need a separate securities license. Consulting24 can help you determine the scope.

Step-by-Step Process to Get a Panama Crypto License

  1. Company Formation: Incorporate a Panama corporation (Sociedad Anónima) with the Public Registry. Consulting24 handles this within 1-2 weeks.

  4. UAF Registration: Submit the application to the Financial Analysis Unit, including company documents, AML policy, and beneficial ownership details. Process takes 2-4 weeks.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Once registered, you must maintain AML compliance. Key obligations include:

  • Appoint a compliance officer (can be outsourced)
  • Conduct annual AML training for staff
  • Submit periodic reports to the UAF (typically every 6 months)
  • Maintain records of transactions and customer due diligence for 5 years
  • Renew the commercial license annually (small fee)

Non-compliance can result in fines or revocation of registration. Consulting24 offers ongoing compliance support. Note: This is general guidance, not legal advice. Always consult a qualified lawyer for your specific situation.

Common Mistakes When Applying for a Panama Crypto License

Avoid these pitfalls to ensure a smooth application:

  • Incomplete AML policy: The UAF requires a detailed manual covering risk assessment, KYC procedures, and suspicious transaction reporting. Generic templates often get rejected.
  • Ignoring beneficial ownership: All ultimate beneficial owners (UBOs) must be disclosed. Attempting to hide ownership can lead to denial.
  • Using a non-compliant resident agent: Only licensed lawyers or trust companies can act as resident agents. We work with vetted partners.
  • Underestimating banking challenges: Open the bank account early; delays can hold up operations.
  • Not understanding territorial tax: If you have Panamanian clients, you must pay local tax. Plan accordingly.

Frequently asked questions

What is the total cost for a Panama crypto license?

Consulting24's flat fee is EUR 6,000, which covers company formation, registered address, resident agent, and UAF registration coordination. Additional costs may include bank account opening fees (varies by bank) and optional legal opinions. There are no hidden charges.

How long does it take to get a Panama crypto license?

The typical timeline is 4-8 weeks from start to operational readiness. Company formation takes 1-2 weeks, UAF registration 2-4 weeks, and bank account opening 2-6 weeks. Expedited processing is possible if documents are prepared in advance.

Is there a minimum capital requirement for a Panama crypto license?

No, Panama has no minimum capital requirement for crypto businesses. This is a key advantage over EU MiCA jurisdictions that require EUR 50,000 to 150,000. You only need sufficient funds to cover operational costs and AML compliance.

What taxes will my Panama crypto company pay?

Panama uses a territorial tax system. Income earned outside Panama is tax-free. If you have Panamanian clients, local-source income is taxed at 25%. There is no VAT on digital services and no capital gains tax on crypto. Most crypto businesses pay 0% tax on global revenue.

Can I serve EU clients with a Panama crypto license?

Yes, but Panama is not an EU member, so you cannot passport into the EU. EU clients may prefer a regulated EU entity under MiCA. However, many global clients accept Panama registration. For EU-focused projects, consider Lithuania or Estonia.

What activities are allowed under a Panama crypto license?

Allowed activities include exchange services, wallet custody, OTC trading, payment processing, and token issuance (subject to securities review). Prohibited activities include securities exchange, banking, and gambling without separate licenses.

Do I need to visit Panama to apply?

No, the entire process can be handled remotely. Consulting24 coordinates everything via email and video calls. You will need to provide notarised copies of passports and proof of address, which can be done at a local notary.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations?

You must appoint a compliance officer, conduct annual AML training, submit semi-annual reports to the UAF, maintain transaction records for 5 years, and renew your commercial license annually. Consulting24 offers ongoing compliance support.

How does Panama compare to Lithuania for crypto licensing?

Panama is cheaper (EUR 6,000 vs ~15,000-25,000), has no capital requirement, and offers 0% tax on foreign income. Lithuania requires EUR 50,000-150,000 capital and 15% corporate tax, but provides EU passporting. Choose based on your target market.

Can I use a Panama license for an exchange serving US clients?

Yes, but you must comply with US regulations if you have US clients. Panama registration does not exempt you from US securities or money transmitter laws. Consult a US attorney for compliance.
