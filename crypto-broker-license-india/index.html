Overview: What is the Crypto Broker License India?

India does not issue a single 'crypto broker license' but requires compliance with several regulatory frameworks. The key registration is with the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) under the Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA), 2002. As of 2026, all Virtual Asset Service Providers (VASPs), including crypto brokers, exchanges, and wallet providers, must register with the FIU. Additionally, the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has issued guidelines for banks dealing with crypto entities, and the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) regulates crypto products that qualify as securities. The Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY) also oversees certain aspects. This multi-regulatory approach means that obtaining a 'crypto broker license India' is a composite process involving registrations, reporting, and ongoing compliance.

For example, a crypto broker that facilitates trading of tokenized stocks would need both FIU registration and SEBI registration as a stockbroker. Consulting24 helps you identify which registrations apply to your specific business model. The process is not a single license but a bundle of approvals that together allow legal operation. This can be complex, but with expert guidance, it is manageable.

Who Needs a Crypto Broker License in India?

Any entity that facilitates the buying, selling, or exchange of virtual assets for clients in India needs to register as a VASP with the FIU. This includes:

  • Crypto brokers (matching buyers and sellers)
  • Cryptocurrency exchanges (order book platforms)
  • Over-the-counter (OTC) desks
  • Wallet providers (custodial and non-custodial)
  • Payment processors dealing in crypto
  • Any business that transfers virtual assets on behalf of clients

Foreign entities that have Indian customers must also register. For instance, a foreign exchange that allows Indian users to trade must comply with Indian AML laws. Consulting24 advises and coordinates the application for international founders looking to enter the Indian market. Even if your business is based outside India, if you target Indian residents, you need to register.

Capital Requirement

India does not prescribe a specific minimum capital requirement for crypto broker registration under the FIU. However, the Companies Act, 2013 requires a minimum paid-up capital of INR 100,000 (approx. EUR 1,100) for a private limited company. For SEBI-registered entities, capital requirements may apply depending on the category (e.g., stockbroker requires INR 10 million, approx. EUR 110,000). As a general guideline, we recommend a minimum capital of EUR 50,000 to demonstrate financial stability. Consulting24 can advise on appropriate capital based on your business plan.

For example, if you plan to offer margin trading, you may need higher capital to cover risks. While there is no formal capital requirement for VASP registration, having sufficient capital can ease banking relationships and regulatory scrutiny. Consulting24 helps you determine the right amount.

Tax Treatment

India's tax regime for crypto is defined by the Finance Act, 2022. As of 2026, income from the transfer of virtual assets is taxed at a flat 30% (plus surcharge and cess). No deductions are allowed except the cost of acquisition. Additionally, a 1% Tax Deducted at Source (TDS) applies on transactions above INR 50,000 (or INR 10,000 for specified persons). Goods and Services Tax (GST) at 18% applies on brokerage fees and commissions. Consulting24 can connect you with tax advisors for detailed planning.

For example, if your broker earns INR 10 million in fees, you pay 18% GST (INR 1.8 million) and 30% income tax on profits. The TDS is deducted on each trade. This high tax burden makes India less attractive for some founders compared to jurisdictions like Panama with 0% on foreign-source income. Consulting24 advises on tax-efficient structures.

Allowed Activities

With a valid FIU VASP registration, you can engage in the following activities:

  • Brokerage services for buying/selling virtual assets
  • Operating a cryptocurrency exchange
  • Providing custody services
  • Facilitating peer-to-peer trading
  • Offering staking or lending (subject to SEBI if securities)
  • Payment processing using crypto

Activities that are prohibited include offering derivatives (unless SEBI-regulated) and any activity that violates RBI guidelines. Consulting24 advises on the scope of allowed activities. For example, a broker can offer spot trading but not futures without SEBI approval. The FIU registration covers basic VASP activities, but any expansion into securities requires additional licenses.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Company Incorporation: Register a private limited company in India. Consulting24 assists with company setup.
  2. Draft AML/KYC Policies: Prepare a comprehensive AML/CFT policy aligned with PMLA guidelines.
  3. Appoint Compliance Officer: Designate a qualified compliance officer (resident in India).
  4. Submit FIU Application: File the VASP registration application online through the FIU portal with required documents.
  5. Due Diligence: FIU conducts background checks on directors and beneficial owners.
  6. Approval: Upon approval, you receive a registration certificate.
  7. Ongoing Reporting: Submit Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs) and annual returns.

Frequently asked questions

Is there a specific crypto broker license in India?

No, India does not have a single dedicated crypto broker license. Instead, you must register as a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) with the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) under the PMLA. Additional registrations with SEBI may be required if you offer securities-like products.

What is the minimum capital required for a crypto broker in India?

There is no statutory minimum capital for FIU registration. However, the Companies Act requires INR 100,000 (approx. EUR 1,100) for a private limited company. For SEBI-registered entities, capital requirements can be higher, e.g., INR 10 million for stockbrokers. We recommend at least EUR 50,000 for credibility.

How long does it take to get a crypto broker license in India?

The process typically takes 4-8 months. This includes company incorporation (1-2 months), AML policy drafting (2-4 weeks), FIU application (2-4 months), and bank account opening (1-3 months). Delays can occur if documents are incomplete.

Can a foreign company apply for a crypto broker license in India?

Yes, foreign entities can apply, but they must incorporate a subsidiary in India (private limited company) and appoint a resident compliance officer. The FIU registration requires the Indian entity to be the applicant. Consulting24 assists with company setup and registration.

What are the tax implications for crypto brokers in India?

Income from crypto transfers is taxed at 30% (plus surcharge and cess). A 1% TDS applies on transactions. Brokerage fees are subject to 18% GST. No deductions are allowed except cost of acquisition. Consulting24 can connect you with tax advisors for planning.

Is SEBI registration required for crypto brokers in India?

SEBI registration is required if you offer products that qualify as securities, such as tokenized stocks or derivatives. For spot crypto trading, only FIU registration is needed. Consulting24 helps determine if your activities trigger SEBI requirements.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations after obtaining the license?

You must submit Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs) to FIU, maintain records for 5 years, conduct annual audits, provide AML training to staff, and appoint a Principal Officer. Failure can lead to penalties or revocation. Consulting24 offers compliance monitoring.

Can I open a bank account for my crypto brokerage in India?

Yes, but it is challenging. Some private banks like HDFC and ICICI accept crypto businesses after enhanced due diligence. Payment aggregators like Razorpay can also be used. Consulting24 provides introductions to compliant banking partners.

What activities are prohibited under the Indian crypto license?

Prohibited activities include offering unregulated derivatives, violating RBI guidelines (e.g., routing through unauthorized channels), and any activity that contravenes PMLA. Always check with Consulting24 before launching new products.

How does India compare to Panama for crypto licensing?

Panama offers a simpler, cheaper license (flat EUR 6,000, no minimum capital, 2-3 months, 0% tax on foreign income). India has higher costs, longer timelines, and 30% tax, but gives direct access to a large market. Consulting24 delivers directly for Panama and advises for India.
