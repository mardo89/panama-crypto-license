What Is a Crypto Payment Institution License in Indonesia?

The Crypto Payment Institution License in Indonesia allows businesses to offer crypto payment services, including processing crypto-to-fiat transactions, digital wallet services, and merchant payment solutions. It is regulated by the Bank of Indonesia (BI) for payment aspects and Bappebti for crypto asset trading. The license ensures compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) regulations, as well as data protection standards under the Personal Data Protection (PDP) law.

This license is distinct from a crypto exchange license, which focuses on trading platforms. Payment institutions must also adhere to the Indonesian Payment System Blueprint (SPI) 2025 and the Electronic Transaction and Trading System (PSE) regulations. The license is mandatory for any business that facilitates crypto payments to or from Indonesian residents. It covers services like payment initiation, merchant acquisition, and digital wallet issuance. The dual regulatory oversight means you must satisfy both BI and Bappebti requirements, which can be complex but provides a clear legal framework.

For example, a company offering a crypto-based remittance service would need this license to convert crypto to fiat and transfer funds locally. Similarly, a payment gateway that allows merchants to accept Bitcoin must hold this license. Without it, operations are illegal and subject to sanctions.

Who Needs This License?

Any business that facilitates crypto payments in Indonesia requires this license. This includes:

  • Payment gateways that accept crypto and settle in fiat
  • Digital wallet providers supporting multiple cryptocurrencies
  • Merchant service providers enabling crypto transactions at point-of-sale or online
  • Remittance services using crypto for cross-border transfers
  • Payment initiation services that use crypto as a funding source

If you plan to offer any of these services to Indonesian residents, you must obtain the license. International companies targeting the Indonesian market also need to incorporate a local entity and apply. License Type & Regulator

The license is issued by the Bank of Indonesia (BI) as a Payment Service Provider (PJP) license, with additional registration with Bappebti for crypto asset activities. The regulatory framework is based on BI Regulation No. 22/23/PBI/2020 on Payment Services and Bappebti Regulation No. 8 of 2021 on Crypto Asset Trading. BI oversees payment system stability and consumer protection, while Bappebti focuses on crypto asset trading and market integrity.

There are two types of PJP licenses: Principal and Supporting. For crypto payment services, a Principal PJP license is typically required as it allows direct participation in the payment system. The application process involves a fit-and-proper test for shareholders and directors, submission of a business plan, and proof of technical capability. Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a Crypto Payment Institution License in Indonesia is 1 billion IDR (approximately EUR 60,000). This must be deposited in a local bank account and maintained throughout the license period. The capital is used to cover operational risks and ensure financial stability. For businesses offering broader services, such as cross-border remittances or merchant acquisition, the capital requirement may be higher, up to 5 billion IDR.

The capital must be in the form of paid-up capital in the local PT company. It cannot be withdrawn without regulatory approval. Note that the capital requirement is separate from operational costs like office rent and salaries.

Tax Treatment

Crypto payment services are subject to Indonesian corporate income tax at a rate of 22% (2026). Additionally, value-added tax (VAT) of 11% applies to service fees charged to merchants or customers. Crypto transactions may also be subject to capital gains tax if the business holds crypto as an asset. Tax incentives are available for certain fintech companies, such as reduced rates for pioneering industries, but specific eligibility should be assessed.

Foreign companies with a permanent establishment in Indonesia are taxed similarly. Withholding tax on dividends (20%) and interest (15-20%) may apply. Consulting24 recommends working with a local tax advisor to optimize your tax structure and ensure compliance with transfer pricing rules if you have related-party transactions.

Allowed Activities

With a Crypto Payment Institution License, you can:

  • Process crypto payments for merchants and convert to fiat
  • Issue crypto wallets for storage and transactions
  • Convert crypto to fiat and vice versa (limited to payment purposes)
  • Provide crypto remittance services (both domestic and cross-border)
  • Offer payment initiation services using crypto as a funding source

You cannot engage in crypto trading or exchange services without a separate license from Bappebti. Additionally, you cannot offer lending, staking, or other investment products without relevant licenses. The license is strictly for payment services. Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Pre-incorporation: Register a local company (PT) with the Ministry of Law and Human Rights and obtain a Business Identification Number (NIB) through the Online Single Submission (OSS) system.
  2. Prepare documents: Business plan, AML/CTF policies, financial statements, shareholder structure, and organizational chart. All documents must be in Indonesian or translated by a sworn translator.
  3. Submit to BI: Apply for Payment Service Provider license via the BI online system (SPIP). Include technical specifications of your payment system.
  4. Register with Bappebti: For crypto asset activities, register as a Crypto Asset Trader (Pedagang Fisik Aset Kripto) and obtain a certificate from the Crypto Asset Futures Exchange (Bursa Berjangka).
  5. Capital deposit: Deposit minimum capital in a local bank and obtain a certificate of deposit.
  6. Operational readiness: Set up IT systems, compliance team, internal audit procedures, and appoint a compliance officer.
  7. License issuance: BI issues license after review (4-6 months). You must then register with the Financial Services Authority (OJK) for consumer protection.

Compliance & Trust

Compliance is critical. You must implement strong AML/CTF procedures, conduct customer due diligence (CDD) and enhanced due diligence (EDD) for high-risk customers, and report suspicious transactions to the Financial Transaction Reports and Analysis Center (PPATK). Regular audits by BI and Bappebti are required, including annual compliance reports and IT security audits. Failure to comply can result in fines up to 10% of revenue or license revocation.

Data protection under the PDP law requires you to obtain consent for data processing, appoint a data protection officer, and report data breaches. This is general guidance, not legal advice.   • Not engaging local legal counsel early: Local expertise is essential for navigating bureaucracy and cultural nuances.
  • Assuming the license allows all crypto activities: Payment license does not cover trading, lending, or investment services.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a crypto payment license and a crypto exchange license in Indonesia?

A crypto payment license allows you to process payments, wallets, and conversions for payment purposes, regulated by Bank of Indonesia. A crypto exchange license is for trading platforms, regulated by Bappebti. Some businesses may need both if they offer trading and payment services. The capital and compliance requirements differ.

How long does it take to get a crypto payment institution license in Indonesia?

The process typically takes 4-6 months from company incorporation to license issuance. Delays can occur if documents are incomplete or if regulatory reviews are slow. Consulting24 can help streamline the process by ensuring all requirements are met upfront.

Can a foreign company apply for an Indonesian crypto payment license?

Yes, but you must incorporate a local PT company with at least one Indonesian director. Foreign ownership is allowed up to 100% for most sectors, but check with BKPM for restrictions. The company must have a registered office in Indonesia.

What is the minimum capital for a crypto payment license in Indonesia?

The minimum capital is 1 billion IDR (approx. EUR 60,000). This must be deposited in a local bank and maintained. Higher capital may be required for broader services, up to 5 billion IDR. Consulting24 can advise on the exact amount for your business.

What taxes apply to crypto payment businesses in Indonesia?

Corporate income tax is 22%, VAT on service fees is 11%. Crypto transactions may incur capital gains tax. Tax incentives are available for fintech companies. Consult a local tax advisor for specifics and to optimize your structure.

Do I need a physical office in Indonesia?

Yes, you need a registered office address. A virtual office is not sufficient. The office must be a physical location where records are kept and compliance staff work. Consulting24 can help you find suitable office space in Jakarta or other major cities.

Can I offer crypto-to-fiat conversion with this license?

Yes, conversion is allowed as part of payment services. However, operating a crypto exchange requires a separate license from Bappebti. The conversion must be incidental to payment services, not a standalone trading operation.

What are the main compliance requirements?

You must have AML/CTF policies, conduct customer due diligence, report suspicious transactions to PPATK, and undergo regular audits. Data protection under PDP law also applies. You need a compliance officer and internal audit function.
