What Is a Crypto OTC Desk License in Costa Rica?

A crypto OTC desk license in Costa Rica is not a traditional financial license but a registration with the UAF (Unidad de Análisis Financiero) under the VASP regime. It allows a company to legally operate an over-the-counter trading desk for cryptocurrencies, facilitating large-volume trades directly between counterparties without public order books. The registration is mandatory for any entity providing crypto-to-fiat or crypto-to-crypto exchange services, including OTC desks.

Key characteristics of the Costa Rican regime:

  • No minimum capital requirement - you can start with the capital you deem appropriate.
  • 0% tax on foreign-source income - only income sourced within Costa Rica is taxed at a standard corporate rate of 30%.
  • No specific licensing fee - only registration and annual compliance costs.
  • Regulation focused on AML/CFT, not prudential supervision.

This light-touch approach contrasts with the heavier capital and compliance burdens of EU MiCA regimes, making Costa Rica a cost-effective choice for OTC desks targeting non-Costa Rican clients.

Who Needs This License?

The Costa Rica crypto OTC desk license is suitable for:

  • OTC desk operators handling large block trades for institutional clients, family offices, or high-net-worth individuals.
  • Market makers providing liquidity for specific tokens or pairs.
  • Brokerage firms that facilitate off-exchange crypto transactions.
  • International crypto businesses seeking a tax-efficient jurisdiction with a stable legal framework.

It is not ideal for businesses targeting local Costa Rican clients, as domestic income is taxed at 30%. Most applicants are non-resident entities serving global customers. License Type & Regulator

The relevant regulator is the Financial Analysis Unit (UAF) - Unidad de Análisis Financiero - which oversees AML/CFT compliance for VASPs. The registration is classified as a VASP registration under Costa Rica's Law 8204 and its 2024 amendments. There is no separate license for OTC desks; the VASP registration covers all exchange-related activities, including OTC trading.

Key regulatory features:

  • Registration with UAF is mandatory before starting operations.
  • Ongoing AML/CFT obligations: appoint a compliance officer, implement KYC/KYB procedures, report suspicious transactions, and maintain records for 5 years.
  • No capital adequacy or solvency requirements.
  • No specific reporting to the central bank or securities regulator.

This structure is simpler than the EU CASP regime under MiCA, which requires capital of EUR 50,000-150,000 and detailed operational plans. For a comparison, see our page on Costa Rica vs Lithuania.

Capital Requirement

Costa Rica imposes no minimum capital requirement for VASP registration. This is a significant advantage over EU MiCA regimes, which require at least EUR 50,000 for basic exchange services and up to EUR 150,000 for custody or trading platforms. You can capitalise your Costa Rican company with the amount you consider necessary for operational expenses - typically EUR 1,000-10,000 is sufficient.

However, you must demonstrate that the company has sufficient resources to implement AML/CFT controls. A low capitalisation may raise questions during UAF review, but it is not a formal bar. Tax Treatment

Costa Rica offers a territorial tax system: only income sourced within Costa Rica is taxable at the standard corporate rate of 30%. Foreign-source income - including profits from OTC trades with non-resident clients - is taxed at 0%. This makes Costa Rica a tax-efficient jurisdiction for international OTC desks.

Key tax points:

  • No withholding tax on dividends paid to non-resident shareholders.
  • No capital gains tax on the sale of shares in Costa Rican companies (if the seller is non-resident).
  • VAT (IVA) of 13% applies to local services, but OTC services to non-residents are generally exempt.
  • Transfer pricing rules apply if you transact with related parties, but they are less onerous than in the EU.

You should consult a tax advisor to structure your operations optimally. For a deeper dive, see our cost analysis page.

Allowed Activities

Under the Costa Rica VASP registration, you can conduct the following activities related to an OTC desk:

  • OTC trading of cryptocurrencies (both crypto-to-crypto and crypto-to-fiat) with counterparties.
  • Brokerage - matching buyers and sellers without holding client assets.
  • Market making - providing liquidity by quoting bid/ask prices.
  • Exchange services - converting one cryptocurrency to another or to fiat.
  • Transfer services - facilitating the movement of crypto assets between wallets on behalf of clients.

Activities not allowed under a standard VASP registration include:

  • Custody of client assets (requires a separate trust license).
  • Derivatives or margin trading (may require securities license).
  • Operating a public exchange or order book (may require additional registration).

If your OTC desk also provides custody, you may need a separate license. Consulting24 advises and coordinates the appropriate structure for your business model.

Step-by-Step Process

Obtaining a crypto OTC desk license in Costa Rica involves the following steps. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each stage.

  1. Company incorporation - Register a Sociedad Anónima (SA) or Sociedad de Responsabilidad Limitada (SRL) with the National Registry. This takes 2-3 weeks and requires a local registered agent.
  2. Prepare AML/CFT documentation - Draft a risk assessment, AML manual, KYC procedures, and internal controls. These must be tailored to your OTC desk operations.
  3. Appoint a compliance officer - The officer must be a Costa Rican resident or a local legal representative. They will be the point of contact for UAF.
  4. Submit UAF registration - File the VASP registration application with UAF, including company documents, AML manual, and compliance officer details. UAF reviews the application and may request additional information.
  5. Obtain registration certificate - Once approved, you receive a VASP registration certificate. This allows you to start operations.
  6. Open a bank account - With the registration certificate, you can open a corporate bank account in Costa Rica or abroad. Banking for crypto firms is challenging; we recommend exploring digital banks or payment processors.
  7. Ongoing compliance - File annual AML reports, update risk assessments, and maintain records. UAF may conduct periodic audits.

For a detailed timeline and checklist, see our application process page.

Banking & Payments

Banking for crypto OTC desks in Costa Rica is possible but requires careful preparation. Local banks (e.g., Banco Nacional, BAC Credomatic) may open accounts for VASP-registered companies, but they often require a strong compliance track record and a clear business plan. Many OTC desks opt for international payment processors or digital banks (e.g., Mercury, Brex) that are more crypto-friendly.

Key considerations:

  • Present your UAF registration certificate to the bank as proof of regulatory compliance.
  • Prepare a detailed AML policy and demonstrate strong KYC procedures.
  • Be transparent about your transaction volumes and counterparty types.
  • Consider using a payment gateway for fiat settlements if local banking is not feasible.

Benefits of a Costa Rica Crypto OTC Desk License

Costa Rica offers several advantages for OTC desk operators:

  • No minimum capital - lower entry barrier compared to EU MiCA jurisdictions.
  • 0% tax on foreign income - ideal for international clientele.
  • Fast setup - 2-4 months from incorporation to registration.
  • Low ongoing costs - annual compliance fees are modest (approx. EUR 1,000-2,000).
  • Stable legal environment - Costa Rica has a strong rule of law and respects property rights.
  • Privacy - corporate records are not publicly accessible, unlike in some EU countries.

These benefits make Costa Rica a compelling choice for OTC desks that do not need to serve EU clients directly. For EU-facing operations, you may need a full CASP license under MiCA - see our Lithuania crypto license page.

Compliance & Trust

Maintaining compliance is critical for the longevity of your OTC desk. Costa Rica's UAF expects VASPs to adhere to international AML/CFT standards, including FATF recommendations. Key compliance obligations:

  • Conduct customer due diligence (CDD) and enhanced due diligence (EDD) for high-risk clients.
  • Monitor transactions for suspicious activity and report to UAF.
  • Keep records of all transactions and CDD for at least 5 years.
  • Appoint a local compliance officer (can be outsourced).
  • Submit annual AML reports to UAF.

Failure to comply can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of registration. Consulting24 advises and coordinates your compliance framework to ensure it meets UAF expectations. Note: this is general guidance, not legal advice. You should engage a local attorney for specific legal opinions.

Common Mistakes

Avoid these pitfalls when applying for a Costa Rica crypto OTC desk license:

  • Inadequate AML documentation - UAF rejects applications with generic or poorly drafted manuals. Tailor your AML policy to your OTC desk operations.
  • Ignoring local presence requirements - You must have a local registered agent and a compliance officer who is reachable. Some founders try to operate without a local representative, which leads to rejection.
  • Underestimating timeline - UAF processing can take 8 weeks or more. Plan accordingly.
  • Failing to open a bank account - Without banking, you cannot settle fiat trades. Start the banking process early.
  • Assuming no tax filing needed - Even if foreign income is 0%, you must file annual tax returns. Non-compliance can lead to penalties.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a crypto OTC desk license and a regular exchange license in Costa Rica?

Costa Rica does not have separate licenses for OTC desks and exchanges. Both fall under the VASP registration with UAF. However, operating a public order-book exchange may require additional compliance measures. OTC desks typically have lower transaction volumes and fewer clients, so the same registration suffices.

Can I serve EU clients with a Costa Rica crypto OTC desk license?

If you actively solicit EU clients, you may need to comply with MiCA regulations, which require a CASP license in an EU member state. Costa Rica registration alone does not provide EU passporting. You can serve EU clients on a reverse solicitation basis, but this is a grey area. Consult a lawyer.

How long does it take to get a crypto OTC desk license in Costa Rica?

The total process takes 2-4 months: 2-3 weeks for company incorporation, 2-4 weeks for AML documentation, and 4-8 weeks for UAF processing. Delays can occur if UAF requests additional information. Consulting24 advises and coordinates to minimise delays.

What are the ongoing compliance costs for a Costa Rica VASP?

Annual compliance costs typically range from EUR 1,000 to 2,000, including AML reporting, compliance officer fees (if outsourced), and registered agent fees. There is no annual government license fee. Costs may increase if you need external audits.

Do I need to be physically present in Costa Rica to apply?

No, you do not need to be physically present. However, you must appoint a local registered agent and a compliance officer who is a Costa Rican resident. Consulting24 can help you find suitable local representatives.

Is Costa Rica a good jurisdiction for a crypto OTC desk serving US clients?

Costa Rica can be used for US clients, but you must ensure compliance with US securities and money transmission laws. The OTC desk should not solicit US residents without proper registration (e.g., FinCEN MSB). Costa Rica registration does not exempt you from US regulations.

What happens if I do not register with UAF?

Operating a crypto OTC desk without UAF registration is illegal and can result in fines, closure of the business, and potential criminal liability for money laundering. Costa Rica has been increasing enforcement since 2024.

Can I convert my Costa Rica VASP registration to a full license later?

Costa Rica does not currently have a tiered licensing system. The VASP registration is the only regime. If you need to expand activities (e.g., custody), you may need a separate trust license. Consult with a local attorney.

What are the reporting requirements to UAF?

VASPs must submit annual AML/CFT reports, including transaction volumes, suspicious activity reports (SARs), and updates to risk assessments. Reports are filed electronically. Failure to report can lead to sanctions.

How does Costa Rica compare to Panama for an OTC desk?

Both offer 0% tax on foreign income and no minimum capital. Panama has a flat fee of EUR 6,000 for a VASP license, while Costa Rica costs vary. Panama's regulator (SSRP) is more experienced with crypto. Costa Rica may be simpler for small operations. See our Panama page for details.
