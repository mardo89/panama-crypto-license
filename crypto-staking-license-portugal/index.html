What Is a Crypto Staking License in Portugal?

A crypto staking license in Portugal is an authorisation under the EU's Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA), fully in force since 2026. It allows firms to offer staking services-where customers lock their crypto assets to support blockchain network operations and earn rewards-as a regulated activity. The licence is part of the broader CASP (Crypto-Asset Service Provider) regime, which covers custody, exchange, and staking services.

Portugal has implemented MiCA through national legislation, designating the Bank of Portugal (Banco de Portugal) as the primary regulator for CASPs, with the CMVM (Comissão do Mercado de Valores Mobiliários) overseeing certain aspects. The licence is not a standalone "staking license" but a CASP authorisation that includes staking as an allowed activity. Firms must apply for the appropriate service categories under MiCA, which include "providing custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients" and "execution of orders for crypto-assets on behalf of clients," both of which cover staking.

Key features of the Portuguese regime include a minimum capital requirement of EUR 50,000 for staking-only services (lower tier), a corporate tax rate of 21% (plus potential municipal surcharges), and a transparent application process typically taking 6-12 months. The regulator conducts fit-and-proper checks on management and requires strong AML/CFT controls.

Who Needs a Crypto Staking License in Portugal?

Any business that provides staking services to clients in Portugal or across the EU must hold a CASP licence authorising staking. This includes:

  • Staking-as-a-service platforms that pool client assets and delegate them to validators.
  • Validator node operators that offer staking rewards to delegators.
  • Exchanges that offer staking products on their platforms.
  • Wallet providers that integrate staking functionalities.
  • DeFi protocols that intermediate staking (if they have a centralised entity).

If your business merely runs a node with your own funds and does not accept third-party assets, you likely do not need a licence. However, any form of client asset aggregation or reward distribution triggers licensing requirements. The Portuguese regime also applies to non-EU firms targeting Portuguese clients, so passporting under MiCA is essential.

Licence Type and Regulator

The relevant licence is a Crypto-Asset Service Provider (CASP) authorisation under MiCA, implemented in Portugal by Decree-Law 52/2024 (as amended). The primary regulator is the Bank of Portugal (Banco de Portugal), which handles authorisation, supervision, and enforcement. The CMVM has a secondary role for activities involving crypto-assets that qualify as financial instruments (e.g., security tokens), but staking of utility or payment tokens falls under the Bank of Portugal.

The CASP licence covers multiple service categories. For staking, the relevant categories are typically:

  • Providing custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients (includes staking).
  • Execution of orders for crypto-assets on behalf of clients (if staking involves order execution).
  • Placing of crypto-assets (if you offer staking as a placement service).

Each category has its own capital requirement: EUR 50,000 for custody/administration, EUR 125,000 for execution, and EUR 150,000 for placement. Most staking businesses will fall under the first category, but a comprehensive analysis is needed.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, the minimum capital requirement for CASPs is tiered by service category. For staking services, the most common category is "providing custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients," which requires EUR 50,000 in own funds. If your staking service also involves execution of orders (e.g., automated staking with trading), the requirement rises to EUR 125,000. For the highest tier (placing crypto-assets), it is EUR 150,000.

These amounts must be maintained at all times and can be in cash or high-quality liquid assets. The capital serves as a buffer to cover operational risks and potential client losses. It is important to note that the capital requirement is separate from any security deposit or insurance. Tax Treatment of Staking Income in Portugal

Portugal offers a favourable tax regime for crypto activities, but staking income is treated as investment income (capital gains) rather than business income for individuals, unless it constitutes a professional activity. For corporate entities holding a CASP licence, staking rewards are generally treated as taxable revenue at the standard corporate income tax rate of 21% (plus a municipal surcharge of up to 1.5% and a state surcharge for profits above EUR 1.5 million).

Value-added tax (VAT) is not applicable to crypto-asset transactions under current EU guidance, but staking fees may be subject to VAT if they are considered a service. The Portuguese tax authority has issued limited guidance, so professional advice is essential. Key points: Staking rewards are taxable when received; cost basis (e.g., electricity, hardware) may be deductible; and losses can offset gains. For non-resident entities, withholding tax may apply. Always consult a tax advisor for your specific situation.

Allowed Activities Under the Licence

A Portuguese CASP licence authorises a range of activities, including but not limited to:

  • Custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients (including staking).
  • Execution of orders for crypto-assets on behalf of clients.
  • Placing of crypto-assets.
  • Transfer services for crypto-assets.
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for fiat currency or other crypto-assets.
  • Operation of a trading platform.

For staking specifically, the licence allows you to accept client assets, pool them, delegate to validators, and distribute rewards. You may also offer flexible or locked staking products, subject to disclosure requirements. The licence does not permit proprietary trading or lending of client assets without explicit authorisation. All activities must be conducted in accordance with MiCA's conduct of business rules, including transparency, fair treatment, and segregation of client assets.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Pre-application assessment: Define your business model, identify applicable service categories, and prepare a detailed business plan. Consulting24 assists with gap analysis and documentation.
  2. Company incorporation: Set up a Portuguese legal entity (e.g., Lda or SA) with a registered office and local management. This typically takes 2-4 weeks.
  3. Prepare application package: Draft policies (AML/CFT, risk management, business continuity), governance documents, and financial projections. Include fit-and-proper forms for directors and beneficial owners.
  4. Submit application to Bank of Portugal: Pay the application fee and submit the complete dossier electronically. The regulator will acknowledge receipt and start the assessment.
  5. Regulatory review: The Bank of Portugal may request additional information or clarifications. Respond promptly to avoid delays. This phase lasts 3-6 months.
  6. On-site inspection (if required): The regulator may conduct an on-site visit to verify operational readiness.
  7. Decision: If approved, you receive a CASP licence with conditions. If rejected, you have the right to appeal.
  8. Post-licence compliance: Appoint an AML officer, submit regular reports (transaction monitoring, audit reports), and maintain capital requirements.

Banking and Payment Services

Securing a bank account in Portugal for a CASP can be challenging due to perceived risks. However, with a valid licence, most Portuguese banks (e.g., Millennium BCP, Santander Totta, Novo Banco) will open accounts for licensed entities. You will need to provide the licence, AML policies, and proof of source of funds. Consulting24 can introduce you to banking partners who understand the crypto sector.

For payment services (e.g., fiat on-ramp/off-ramp), you may need a separate payment institution licence or partner with an existing provider. Many CASPs use third-party payment processors to handle fiat transactions. Portugal is part of the SEPA zone, so euro transfers are efficient. Crypto-to-fiat exchanges can be conducted through the licensed platform itself, subject to regulatory oversight.

Compliance and Trust Considerations

Operating a staking business under a Portuguese licence requires a strong compliance framework. Key obligations include:

  • AML/CFT: Implement customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting. The Bank of Portugal expects a risk-based approach.
  • Asset segregation: Client crypto-assets must be held separately from the firm's own assets, preferably in cold storage with multi-signature controls.
  • Disclosure: Provide clear information about staking rewards, risks, lock-up periods, and fees to clients.
  • Audit: Annual financial audits by an approved auditor, plus periodic regulatory audits.
  • Data protection: Comply with GDPR for handling personal data.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Underestimating capital requirements: Ensure you have sufficient own funds for the correct service tier. Many applicants fail by applying for the wrong category.
  2. Incomplete documentation: The Bank of Portugal requires detailed policies. Missing documents can delay the process by months.
  3. Ignoring fit-and-proper requirements: Directors and beneficial owners must have clean criminal records and relevant experience. Background checks are thorough.
  4. Not planning for ongoing compliance: Post-licence obligations are significant; budget for an AML officer and regular reporting.
  5. Choosing the wrong jurisdiction: Portugal is ideal for EU-focused staking, but if your target market is outside the EU, other jurisdictions like El Salvador or Cayman Islands may be more suitable. Compare options on our jurisdictions page.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a licence for staking as a service in Portugal?

Yes, if you provide staking services to clients (e.g., pooling their assets and delegating to validators), you need a CASP licence under MiCA. Running your own node with personal funds does not require a licence.

What is the minimum capital for a Portuguese staking license?

For custody and administration of crypto-assets (which covers most staking), the minimum capital is EUR 50,000. If your service also involves execution of orders, it rises to EUR 125,000.

How long does it take to get a crypto staking license in Portugal?

The application process typically takes 6 to 12 months from submission to approval. This includes a 3-month completeness check and a 3-6 month substantive review.

Can I passport my Portuguese license to other EU countries?

Yes, under MiCA, a CASP licence from Portugal allows you to provide services across all EU/EEA member states without additional authorisation, subject to notification requirements.

What is the corporate tax rate for licensed staking firms in Portugal?

The standard corporate income tax rate is 21%, plus a municipal surcharge of up to 1.5% and a state surcharge for high profits. Effective rate is typically around 22.5%.

Is staking income subject to VAT in Portugal?

Currently, crypto-asset transactions are exempt from VAT under EU guidance. However, staking fees may be considered a service and could be subject to VAT. Professional advice is recommended.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements after obtaining the license?

You must appoint an AML officer, submit annual audit reports, conduct transaction monitoring, file suspicious activity reports, and maintain capital requirements. The Bank of Portugal conducts periodic inspections.

Can I use a Portuguese license to offer staking to US clients?

The Portuguese license does not cover US clients directly. You would need to comply with US federal and state regulations separately. The license is primarily for EU/EEA clients.

What happens if I operate without a license in Portugal?

Unlicensed activity is a criminal offence under Portuguese law, punishable by fines and imprisonment. The Bank of Portugal actively monitors and enforces against unlicensed CASPs.
