What Is the Crypto NFT Marketplace License Bahrain?

The crypto NFT marketplace license Bahrain is a regulatory approval issued by the Central Bank of Bahrain (CBB) under its Crypto Asset Module (CRA). This license allows operators to run a platform where users can buy, sell, and trade non-fungible tokens (NFTs) using cryptocurrencies or fiat currency. The CBB has been a pioneer in the region, introducing a comprehensive framework for crypto asset services in 2019 and updating it regularly to cover emerging asset classes like NFTs.

The license is part of Bahrain's broader effort to attract fintech innovation while ensuring investor protection and anti-money laundering compliance. Unlike some jurisdictions that treat NFTs separately, Bahrain classifies them as crypto assets under the same rules, provided they are not securities. This means NFT marketplaces must adhere to the same capital, custody, and reporting standards as other crypto exchanges.

For founders, this license offers a regulated environment that can enhance credibility with users, banks, and institutional investors. Bahrain also benefits from a free trade agreement with the US and strong connectivity to other Gulf markets, making it a strategic base for regional expansion.

Who Needs This License?

Any business operating a platform that facilitates the primary or secondary trading of NFTs for a fee or commission requires the Bahrain crypto license. This includes:

  • NFT marketplaces that allow peer-to-peer trading of digital art, collectibles, music, or virtual real estate.
  • Platforms that mint and sell NFTs directly to users (primary sales).
  • Marketplaces that accept cryptocurrency payments for NFT transactions.
  • Businesses that offer custodial wallets for NFT storage as part of the marketplace service.

If your platform only provides informational services or a non-custodial wallet without facilitating trades, you may not need a full license. However, the CBB has broad definitions, so it is wise to seek professional advice. License Type and Regulator

The regulator for the crypto NFT marketplace license Bahrain is the Central Bank of Bahrain (CBB), specifically through its Crypto Asset Module (CRA). The CBB classifies crypto asset services into several categories, and an NFT marketplace typically falls under Category 3: Operating a Crypto Asset Exchange or a bespoke category for trading platforms.

Bahrain does not have a separate NFT license; instead, the existing crypto asset framework covers NFTs as a type of crypto asset. The license type is a Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) license under the CRA Module. This is a full regulatory license, not a simple registration, and requires ongoing compliance with capital, governance, and reporting rules.

Key features of the license:

  • Issued by the CBB's Fintech & Innovation Unit.
  • Valid for an indefinite period subject to annual renewal and compliance.
  • Allows both spot trading of cryptocurrencies and NFT trading.
  • Requires a physical presence in Bahrain (office and local staff).

Capital Requirement

The capital requirement for a crypto NFT marketplace license Bahrain is set by the CBB's CRA Module. As of 2026, the minimum capital for operating a crypto asset exchange (which includes NFT marketplaces) is USD 500,000 (or equivalent in BHD). This capital must be maintained in liquid assets and cannot be used for operational expenses.

For smaller platforms or those with limited activities, the CBB may consider a lower capital requirement on a case-by-case basis, but USD 500,000 is the standard. The capital must be fully paid up before the license is issued and held in a Bahraini bank account. Consulting24 can assist with structuring the capital and meeting the CBB's solvency requirements.

This capital is higher than many other jurisdictions. For comparison, Estonia's crypto license requires EUR 12,000 to 100,000, and Panama's license is a flat EUR 6,000 with no separate capital requirement. If capital efficiency is a priority, you may consider Panama as an alternative, which we cover in the alternatives section.

Tax Treatment

Bahrain offers a highly favorable tax environment for crypto businesses. There is no corporate income tax on profits from crypto trading or NFT marketplace operations. Additionally, there is no value-added tax (VAT) on crypto asset transactions, including NFT sales. This makes Bahrain one of the most tax-efficient jurisdictions for NFT marketplaces globally.

However, businesses must pay social insurance contributions for employees (approx. 12% of salary) and a small municipal fee. There is also a 5% customs duty on imported goods, but this does not apply to digital services. The absence of capital gains tax and withholding tax further enhances the appeal.

It is important to note that tax residency rules apply: if your company is managed and controlled from Bahrain, it will be considered a tax resident. Consulting24 recommends consulting with a tax advisor to optimize your structure, especially if you have operations in multiple countries.

Allowed Activities

With a crypto NFT marketplace license Bahrain, you are permitted to engage in the following activities:

  • Operating an NFT marketplace for primary and secondary trading.
  • Minting and selling NFTs on behalf of artists or creators.
  • Providing custodial wallet services for NFTs and cryptocurrencies.
  • Facilitating peer-to-peer transfers of NFTs.
  • Accepting cryptocurrency payments for NFT purchases.
  • Offering staking or yield-generating services related to NFTs (subject to additional approval).

Activities that are NOT allowed without a separate license include: operating a crypto ATM, issuing a stablecoin, or offering derivatives based on NFTs. The CBB also prohibits anonymous transactions; all users must be KYC-verified. If you plan to offer margin trading or lending, you may need a separate license under the CBB's rules.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The application process for a crypto NFT marketplace license Bahrain involves several stages. Consulting24 coordinates with local partners to guide you through each step.

  1. Pre-application consultation: Meet with the CBB's Fintech & Innovation Unit to discuss your business model and confirm the license category.
  2. Prepare documentation: This includes a business plan, AML/KYC policies, risk management framework, financial projections, and governance structure.
  3. Submit application: File the formal application with the CBB along with the non-refundable fee.
  4. Review and due diligence: The CBB conducts background checks on directors and shareholders, and may request additional information.
  5. On-site inspection: CBB officials visit your Bahrain office to verify premises, systems, and compliance procedures.
  6. License issuance: Upon approval, pay the issuance fee and receive the license. This is valid for one year initially.
  7. Post-licensing compliance: Submit regular reports, maintain capital, and undergo annual audits.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, you must comply with the CBB's ongoing requirements. These include:

  • Annual AML/CFT audit by an approved external auditor.
  • Quarterly financial reports and capital adequacy submissions.
  • Real-time transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting.
  • Maintaining a physical office with at least one senior manager resident in Bahrain.
  • Annual renewal of the license with payment of supervision fees.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Founders often make these errors when applying for a crypto NFT marketplace license Bahrain:

  • Insufficient capital: Not having the full USD 500,000 capital ready before application. The CBB requires proof of funds.
  • Incomplete AML policies: Submitting generic AML documents without tailoring them to NFT transactions (e.g., identifying NFT-specific risks like wash trading).
  • Ignoring local presence: Trying to operate remotely without a physical office and local staff. The CBB mandates a substantive presence.
  • Overlooking tax residency: Failing to understand that Bahrain tax residency may trigger tax obligations in your home country.
  • Delaying bank account opening: Banks have long due diligence processes; start early.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a crypto license and an NFT marketplace license in Bahrain?

Bahrain does not have a separate NFT license. The CBB's Crypto Asset Module covers all crypto assets, including NFTs. An NFT marketplace license is essentially a Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) license for exchange services.

How long does it take to get a crypto NFT marketplace license in Bahrain?

The typical processing time is 3 to 6 months, but it can take up to 9 months if the application is complex or requires additional information. Consulting24 recommends starting the process early.

What is the minimum capital requirement for an NFT marketplace in Bahrain?

The CBB requires a minimum capital of USD 500,000 for a crypto asset exchange license, which covers NFT marketplaces. This must be maintained in liquid assets.

Can I operate an NFT marketplace in Bahrain without a license?

No. Operating an NFT marketplace without a license is illegal and can result in fines, closure, and criminal penalties. All crypto asset services must be licensed by the CBB.

Is there a physical presence requirement for the Bahrain crypto license?

Yes, you must have a physical office in Bahrain and at least one senior manager resident in the country. The CBB conducts on-site inspections to verify this.

What taxes will my NFT marketplace pay in Bahrain?

Bahrain has no corporate income tax, no VAT on crypto transactions, and no capital gains tax. You only pay social insurance contributions for employees and a small municipal fee.

Can I use a bank account from another country for my Bahrain license?

No. The CBB requires that the minimum capital be held in a Bahraini bank account. You will need to open a local corporate bank account for operational transactions.

Does the Bahrain license allow me to trade NFTs for cryptocurrencies only, or also for fiat?

Both. The license allows trading NFTs for cryptocurrencies and for fiat currency, provided you comply with AML/KYC requirements for all transactions.

How does the Bahrain license compare to Panama for an NFT marketplace?

Panama offers a lower cost (EUR 6,000 flat) and no capital requirement, but lacks the regulatory depth and regional market access of Bahrain. Panama is better for budget-conscious startups, while Bahrain suits those targeting the Gulf region.
