What Is a Crypto License in Panama and the Czech Republic?

A crypto license authorises a business to offer cryptocurrency-related services such as exchange, custody, or trading. In Panama, there is no specific crypto license; instead, companies operate under a general corporate structure with no dedicated regulatory framework for crypto. The Panama company setup (EUR 6,000 flat fee) allows you to engage in crypto activities, but it lacks formal authorisation from a financial regulator. In contrast, the Czech Republic, as an EU member state, has fully implemented the MiCA regulation as of 2026. Crypto asset service providers (CASPs) must register with the Czech National Bank (CNB) and comply with capital and AML requirements. Consulting24 advises and coordinates both routes, helping you choose based on your business needs.

The key distinction is regulatory certainty. A Czech CASP registration gives you a passportable licence across the EU, while Panama offers operational flexibility without a formal licence. Your choice depends on your target market and risk appetite.

Who Needs a Crypto License in These Jurisdictions?

Any business offering crypto services-exchange, wallet, custody, trading, or payment processing-needs to consider licensing. In Panama, the lack of a specific license means that any company can legally operate, but this may limit access to banking and partnerships. In the Czech Republic, a CASP registration is mandatory for EU-based operations. The license is ideal for startups and established firms targeting the European market. Consulting24 advises clients on whether Panama's simplicity or the Czech Republic's regulatory clarity suits their business model.

For example, a crypto exchange serving EU residents must register as a CASP in the Czech Republic to comply with MiCA. A Panama company might be suitable for a crypto fund operating outside the EU. License Type & Regulator

Panama does not have a dedicated crypto regulator. Companies are registered under the general corporate law with the Public Registry of Panama. There is no specific license for crypto activities, which means less regulatory oversight but also less legal certainty. The Czech Republic, under MiCA, requires registration as a Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) with the Czech National Bank (CNB). The CNB oversees compliance with capital, AML, and operational requirements. Consulting24 coordinates the application process with the CNB for clients.

Key differences include: Panama has no formal licensing, while the Czech Republic has a regulated framework with defined obligations. The CNB is a respected EU regulator, and its approval signals credibility. In Panama, you rely on corporate law, which may be sufficient for some business models but insufficient for others.

Capital Requirement

Panama has no minimum capital requirement for crypto companies. You can start with any amount, though a typical paid-in capital of EUR 10,000 is common for credibility. The Czech Republic, under MiCA, imposes tiered capital requirements based on services: EUR 50,000 for simple exchange services, EUR 125,000 for custody, and EUR 150,000 for trading platforms. These funds must be maintained as own funds. Consulting24 advises on structuring your capital to meet these thresholds.

For example, a Czech CASP offering both exchange and custody would need at least EUR 125,000. Panama has no such requirement, making it easier to start with limited capital. However, lower capital may mean less trust from partners. We recommend discussing your capital structure with us.

Tax Treatment

Panama operates a territorial tax system: income earned outside Panama is tax-free. Crypto trading profits from offshore sources are not taxed. However, Panama has no tax treaties with many countries, which may affect withholding taxes. The Czech Republic taxes worldwide income at a flat corporate rate of 19%. Crypto gains are treated as capital gains or business income. VAT is not applied to crypto transactions. Consulting24 recommends consulting a tax advisor for your specific situation.

For instance, a Panama company trading crypto on international exchanges pays no Panama tax, but may face tax in the country of residence of its shareholders. A Czech CASP pays 19% on global profits. If your business is truly offshore, Panama offers tax advantages. If you need EU market access, the Czech Republic's tax regime is predictable.

Allowed Activities

In Panama, any crypto activity is allowed as long as it does not violate AML laws. Common activities include exchange, custody, mining, and ICOs. However, there is no legal framework for licensing, so activities are not formally authorised. In the Czech Republic, CASP registration permits exchange between crypto and fiat, exchange between cryptos, custody, transfer services, and advisory. Activities like derivatives or stablecoin issuance may require additional authorisation. Consulting24 can help define your service scope.

For example, a Panama company can run a crypto exchange without specific approval, while a Czech CASP must adhere to MiCA's service definitions. If you plan to offer complex financial products, the Czech Republic provides clearer rules.

Czech Republic CASP Registration

  1. Incorporate a Czech company (usually a s.r.o.) with minimum capital.
  2. Prepare AML policies, business plan, and organisational documents.

Frequently asked questions

What is the main difference between Panama and Czech Republic crypto licenses?

Panama has no specific crypto license; you operate under a general company structure. The Czech Republic requires CASP registration with the Czech National Bank under MiCA, with capital and compliance obligations.

How much does a Panama crypto company cost?

The flat fee is EUR 6,000, covering company incorporation, registered agent, and initial documentation. Annual costs are approximately EUR 1,000-2,000.

What is the timeline for a Czech Republic crypto license?

The application process typically takes 3-6 months, depending on the completeness of your documentation and the CNB's workload.

Do I need to be physically present in the Czech Republic to apply?

No physical presence is required for the application, but you may need a local director or representative. Consulting24 can help arrange this.

Can a Panama company bank easily?

Banking can be challenging. Some international banks accept Panama companies, but due diligence is strict. We assist with introductions to crypto-friendly banks.

What are the capital requirements for a Czech CASP?

Under MiCA, capital requirements are tiered: EUR 50,000 for exchange only, EUR 125,000 for custody, and EUR 150,000 for trading platforms. These must be maintained as own funds.

Is Panama tax-free for crypto businesses?

Panama has a territorial tax system. Income earned outside Panama is not taxed. However, income sourced in Panama may be subject to tax. Consult a tax advisor.

Does the Czech Republic accept crypto license applications from non-EU residents?

Yes, non-EU residents can apply, but they may need to appoint a local representative. The company must be incorporated in the Czech Republic.

Which jurisdiction is faster: Panama or Czech Republic?

Panama is much faster: 2-4 weeks for company setup. The Czech Republic takes 3-6 months for CASP registration.

Can I operate a crypto exchange with a Panama company?

Yes, Panama does not prohibit crypto exchanges. However, you lack a formal license, which may affect banking and partner trust.

What ongoing compliance is required for a Czech CASP?

Ongoing obligations include AML reporting, annual audits, capital maintenance, and regular filings with the CNB. Consulting24 can provide compliance support.
