Overview: What Are These Licenses?

The Cayman Islands and Cyprus both offer regulatory frameworks for crypto asset service providers, but they differ fundamentally in structure and scope.

Cayman Islands: The Cayman Islands Monetary Authority (CIMA) regulates VASPs under the Virtual Asset Service Provider Act (2020, amended). This is a registration regime, not a full license, but it requires compliance with AML/CFT obligations, capital requirements, and fit-and-proper tests. It is designed for businesses targeting international markets, with no local substance requirements beyond a registered office and compliance officer.

Cyprus: Cyprus, as an EU member state, has fully implemented the Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) regulation as of 2026. Crypto asset service providers (CASPs) must be authorized by the Cyprus Securities and Exchange Commission (CySEC). The regime imposes tiered capital requirements (EUR 50,000, 125,000, or 150,000 depending on services), comprehensive governance, and ongoing reporting. It is passportable across the EU, making it attractive for businesses seeking a European base.

Both jurisdictions have strong AML frameworks, but the Cyprus regime is more prescriptive and harmonized with EU standards, while the Cayman Islands offers greater flexibility for non-EU operations.

Who Needs a Crypto License in These Jurisdictions?

The ideal candidate for each jurisdiction depends on your target market, operational scale, and regulatory appetite.

  • Cayman Islands: Suitable for international crypto exchanges, custodians, and fund managers that do not require EU passporting. It is popular among institutional players and funds due to its tax neutrality and flexible regulatory approach. Businesses that primarily serve non-EU clients or operate in the DeFi space may prefer this regime.
  • Cyprus: Best for companies that need to serve EU customers and benefit from MiCA's single market passport. It is ideal for retail-facing exchanges, wallet providers, and token issuers that want regulatory clarity and credibility within the EU. Startups and scale-ups looking to raise capital from European investors often choose Cyprus.

Capital Requirement

Minimum capital requirements are a key differentiator.

  • Cayman Islands: The VASP Act requires a minimum capital of USD 100,000 (or equivalent in other currencies) for all VASP registrants. This capital must be maintained and evidenced at all times.
  • Cyprus (MiCA): Capital requirements are tiered based on the type of crypto asset services provided:
    • EUR 50,000 for providing custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients.
    • EUR 125,000 for operating a trading platform, exchanging crypto assets for fiat or other crypto assets, or placing crypto assets.
    • EUR 150,000 for all other services, including portfolio management and transfer services.

The Cayman Islands has a single flat requirement, while Cyprus offers flexibility for smaller operations. Both require the capital to be held in liquid assets and fully paid up.

Tax Treatment

Tax efficiency is a major factor in choosing a jurisdiction.

Cayman Islands: No direct taxes: no corporate income tax, capital gains tax, withholding tax, or VAT. The only government levies are annual registration fees (typically USD 500 - 1,500) and stamp duties on certain documents. This makes the Cayman Islands extremely attractive for businesses that want to minimize tax leakage.

Cyprus: Corporate income tax rate of 12.5% on net profits, one of the lowest in the EU. Capital gains tax applies only to disposal of immovable property in Cyprus. VAT of 19% applies to most services, but crypto transactions may be exempt under certain conditions. Cyprus has an extensive double tax treaty network, which can reduce withholding taxes on dividends and interest.

For a crypto business with high margins, the Cayman Islands offers zero tax, but Cyprus provides access to EU markets and treaty benefits. The choice depends on your revenue model and client base.

Allowed Activities

The scope of permitted activities under each regime.

Cayman Islands (VASP Registration)

  • Exchange between virtual assets and fiat currency
  • Exchange between one or more forms of virtual assets
  • Transfer of virtual assets
  • Custodian wallet provider
  • Participation in and provision of financial services related to an issuer's offer or sale of a virtual asset

Cyprus (MiCA CASP Authorization)

  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Operation of a trading platform for crypto assets
  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets
  • Execution of orders for crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Placing of crypto assets
  • Receiving and transmitting orders for crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Providing advice on crypto assets
  • Portfolio management of crypto assets
  • Transfer services for crypto assets on behalf of clients

Both regimes cover core activities, but Cyprus's MiCA framework is more detailed and includes advisory and portfolio management services explicitly. The Cayman Islands regime is broader in some areas but less prescriptive.

Step-by-Step Application Process

While Consulting24 handles the entire process for you, understanding the steps helps you plan.

Cayman Islands

  1. Pre-application: Engage a local registered office and compliance officer. Prepare business plan, AML policies, and financial projections.
  2. Submit application to CIMA with supporting documents (fit-and-proper declarations, source of funds, etc.).
  3. CIMA review and potential requests for additional information (typically 2-3 rounds).
  4. Approval and issuance of VASP registration certificate.
  5. Ongoing compliance: annual AML audit, transaction reporting, and renewal.

Cyprus

  1. Pre-application: Incorporate a Cyprus company (or use an existing one), appoint directors (at least two, one of whom must be a Cyprus resident), and establish a registered office.
  2. Prepare a comprehensive application pack including governance arrangements, risk management policies, and capital evidence.
  3. Submit to CySEC. CySEC has up to 3 months to review, but the process often takes longer.
  4. On-site or remote inspection by CySEC (may be required).
  5. Grant of CASP authorization under MiCA.
  6. Ongoing: quarterly and annual reporting, capital maintenance, and compliance audits.

Banking & Payment Services

Access to banking is a common challenge for crypto businesses.

Cayman Islands: The Cayman Islands has a well-developed banking sector, but many traditional banks are cautious about crypto. Some local banks, such as Butterfield Bank and Cayman National Bank, may open accounts for VASP registrants, but they require thorough due diligence. Alternative payment providers and EMI accounts are available. Expect a longer onboarding process (2-4 months).

Cyprus: Cyprus has a more open banking environment for crypto, especially after MiCA implementation. Several local banks (Bank of Cyprus, Hellenic Bank) and EMIs (e.g., Revolut Business, Monese) accept CASP clients, though compliance checks are rigorous. The EU's Payment Services Directive (PSD2) facilitates open banking. Account opening typically takes 1-3 months.

In both jurisdictions, a strong compliance framework and a clean source of funds are essential. Consulting24 can introduce you to banking partners we have worked with.

Compliance & Trust

Both jurisdictions require strong AML/CFT programs, but the level of detail differs.

Cayman Islands: VASPs must comply with the Proceeds of Crime Act, the Anti-Money Laundering Regulations, and CIMA's guidance notes. Requirements include customer due diligence (CDD), transaction monitoring, suspicious activity reporting, and appointment of a compliance officer. CIMA conducts periodic inspections. The regime is principles-based, allowing some flexibility.

Cyprus: MiCA imposes detailed requirements on governance, risk management, business continuity, and disclosure. CASPs must have a compliance officer, a risk management function, and an internal audit function (or outsource). They must also publish a white paper for certain tokens. CySEC is known for rigorous enforcement. The regime is rules-based, leaving less room for interpretation.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Based on our experience advising clients, here are frequent pitfalls.

  • Insufficient capital evidence: Both regulators require proof that capital is unencumbered and held in liquid assets. Using borrowed funds or crypto assets as capital is often rejected.
  • Poor AML documentation: Generic AML policies copied from the internet are a red flag. Policies must be tailored to your business model and risk profile.
  • Underestimating timeline: Many applicants expect approval in weeks. In reality, both jurisdictions take months. Plan your business launch accordingly.
  • Ignoring substance requirements: While the Cayman Islands does not require physical presence, Cyprus requires at least one resident director. Failure to appoint one can delay or derail the application.
  • Not engaging local experts: Attempting to apply without local legal and compliance support often leads to rejections. Consulting24 coordinates with vetted local partners.

Alternatives: Panama and Other Jurisdictions

If neither the Cayman Islands nor Cyprus fits your needs, consider these alternatives.

Panama

Panama offers a straightforward corporate structure with no specific crypto license regime as of 2026, but businesses can operate under general business law with AML compliance. The cost is a flat EUR 6,000 for company setup, with no minimum capital requirement. Panama is ideal for early-stage projects that want a low-cost, tax-neutral base. However, it lacks regulatory clarity and banking access for crypto. Panama is a popular choice for startups testing the waters.

Lithuania

Lithuania has a crypto license regime under the Bank of Lithuania, with a capital requirement of EUR 125,000 for exchange and custodian services. It offers a fast track (3-4 months) and is part of the EU, but MiCA implementation is ongoing. Lithuania crypto license is a strong alternative for EU-focused businesses that want a simpler regime than Cyprus.

Estonia

Estonia's crypto license (now a full authorization under MiCA) requires a minimum capital of EUR 250,000 (as of 2026) and a physical presence. It is more expensive and slower than Cyprus but offers a reputable digital ecosystem. Estonia crypto license is suited for companies that value the e-Residency program.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a Cayman Islands VASP registration and a Cyprus CASP authorization?

The Cayman Islands VASP registration is a lighter regime focused on AML compliance, with no EU passporting. Cyprus CASP authorization under MiCA is a full license that allows passporting across the EU, with more prescriptive capital and governance requirements.

Which is cheaper: Cayman Islands or Cyprus crypto license?

The Cayman Islands is generally cheaper, with total costs estimated at USD 20,000-40,000 versus EUR 25,000-50,000 for Cyprus. However, ongoing costs in Cyprus may be higher due to more extensive compliance requirements.

Do I need to be physically present in the Cayman Islands to get a VASP registration?

No. The Cayman Islands does not require physical presence. You need a registered office and a local compliance officer, which can be arranged through service providers. Consulting24 can coordinate these arrangements.

Can I passport a Cyprus crypto license to other EU countries?

Yes. Under MiCA, a CASP authorized in Cyprus can provide services across the EU without needing separate licenses in each member state, subject to notification procedures.

What are the capital requirements for a crypto exchange in Cyprus?

For operating a trading platform or exchanging crypto assets, the minimum capital is EUR 125,000. For custody services, it is EUR 50,000. For other services, it is EUR 150,000. These are tiered under MiCA.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in the Cayman Islands?

Typically 3 to 6 months from submission to approval, depending on the completeness of your application and CIMA's workload. Consulting24 can help prepare a strong application to minimize delays.

Is there any tax on crypto profits in the Cayman Islands?

No. The Cayman Islands has no corporate income tax, capital gains tax, or VAT. The only government charges are annual registration fees and stamp duties.

What activities are not allowed under a Cyprus CASP license?

Activities not covered by MiCA, such as fully decentralized finance (DeFi) protocols without a central operator, or non-fungible tokens (NFTs) that are not classified as crypto assets, may not require a license. However, if you provide any of the listed services, you need authorization.

Can I switch from a Cayman Islands VASP registration to a Cyprus CASP later?

Yes, but it would require a new application in Cyprus. There is no direct conversion. You would need to meet all MiCA requirements from scratch. Consulting24 can assist with the transition if your business expands into the EU.
