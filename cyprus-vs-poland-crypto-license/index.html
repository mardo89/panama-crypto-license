What Is a Crypto License in Cyprus and Poland?

A crypto license (or registration) is the regulatory permission to operate virtual asset services such as exchange, custody, or wallet provision. Under MiCA (fully in force from 2026), all EU member states must apply harmonised rules, but national implementation details vary.

In Cyprus, the Cyprus Securities and Exchange Commission (CySEC) regulates crypto asset services under the Investment Services and Activities and Regulated Markets Law, as amended. Cyprus offers a CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) license. In Poland, the Polish Financial Supervision Authority (KNF) oversees a registration system for VASPs (Virtual Asset Service Providers) under the AML Act, though Poland is transitioning to MiCA-aligned licensing. The transition means that by late 2026, all Polish VASPs must hold a full CASP license, aligning capital and compliance requirements with Cyprus.

Both licenses allow you to serve EU clients and passport services across the bloc. However, the maturity of the regime differs: Cyprus has a proven track record with dozens of licensed CASPs, while Poland's system is still evolving.   • Token issuers and ICO/STO platforms launching new assets
  • DeFi platforms interacting with EU users, even if partially automated
  • Payment processors using crypto for merchant settlements
  • Brokers facilitating crypto-fiat conversions

If your target market includes EU clients, obtaining a license in Cyprus or Poland can provide a passportable base under MiCA. Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, capital requirements are tiered by service type:

  • EUR 50,000 for basic services (e.g., order execution, advice)
  • EUR 125,000 for exchange and wallet services (custodial)
  • EUR 150,000 for advanced services (e.g., custody of client assets, trading on own account)

Cyprus applies these tiers strictly. Poland, as of 2026, is expected to adopt the same tiers, though its current registration has no minimum capital. This means a Polish VASP today may need to inject capital when transitioning to a CASP license. Consulting24 can clarify the exact requirements for your specific services. For example, a simple exchange without custody may only need EUR 50,000, while a full-service platform needs EUR 125,000.

Tax Treatment

Cyprus offers a corporate tax rate of 12.5%, one of the lowest in the EU. Crypto gains may be treated as capital gains or trading income depending on activity. VAT on crypto services is generally exempt. Cyprus also has a notional interest deduction on new equity, which can further reduce effective tax.

Poland has a standard corporate tax rate of 19% (9% for small taxpayers with revenue under EUR 2 million). Crypto transactions are subject to CIT/PIT, with specific rules for mining and trading. Poland also applies VAT on certain crypto services, such as advisory fees. Consulting24 recommends a tax advisor for detailed planning. For instance, a crypto exchange in Cyprus might pay 12.5% on profits, while the same business in Poland pays 19%.

Step-by-Step Licensing Process

Cyprus CASP License

  1. Pre-application: prepare business plan, AML policies, risk assessment, and organisational structure (board members, compliance officer).
  2. Submit application to CySEC with required documents: audited financials, shareholder background, source of funds, IT security policies.
  3. CySEC reviews within 3 months, may request clarifications or additional documentation.
  4. Upon approval, pay the license fee (typically EUR 10,000) and commence operations. Post-licensing, CySEC conducts periodic audits.

Poland VASP Registration

  1. Register in the VASP register maintained by KNF via an online form.
  2. Submit AML compliance documentation, identification of beneficial owners, and a description of the business model.
  3. KNF reviews within 2 months; registration is granted if no objections are raised.
  4. For MiCA transition, additional steps may be required in 2026: submit a supplementary application for CASP status, including capital proof and governance documents.

Benefits of Cyprus and Poland

Cyprus: low corporate tax (12.5%), mature regulatory framework with CySEC, fast-track option for licensed firms (expedited review for existing regulated entities), EU passporting, and a supportive ecosystem with legal and audit firms experienced in crypto.

Poland: lower initial costs, faster registration, large local market (38 million population), growing crypto ecosystem with active developer community, and a pragmatic regulator that engages with the industry.

Both offer access to the EU single market under MiCA. Your choice depends on budget, timeline, and tax preferences. For a cost-sensitive startup, Poland may be attractive initially, but the future MiCA compliance costs should be factored in. For long-term stability, Cyprus offers a proven regime.

Frequently asked questions

What is the main difference between Cyprus and Poland crypto licenses?

Cyprus offers a full CASP license under MiCA with a 12.5% corporate tax, while Poland currently has a lighter VASP registration that is transitioning to MiCA. Cyprus has higher costs but a more established framework; Poland is cheaper and faster but less mature. Under MiCA, both will eventually require similar capital and compliance.

Which jurisdiction has lower capital requirements?

Under MiCA, both will apply the same capital tiers (EUR 50k/125k/150k) depending on services. Poland's current registration has no minimum capital, but that will change with full MiCA implementation. Cyprus already enforces the tiers. For a simple exchange without custody, EUR 50,000 is needed in Cyprus; Poland currently requires zero.

Can I passport my license to other EU countries?

Yes, both Cyprus and Poland are EU members. A CASP license from Cyprus or a MiCA-aligned license from Poland allows passporting across the EU, enabling you to serve clients in any member state without additional licensing. You must notify the home regulator of your cross-border activities.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Cyprus vs Poland?

Cyprus typically takes 3-6 months, Poland 2-4 months for registration. Timelines depend on application completeness and regulator workload. Consulting24 can provide current estimates. What are the ongoing compliance costs?

Annual costs include AML officer, audit, legal fees, and regulatory filings. Expect EUR 5,000-15,000 per year for Cyprus and EUR 3,000-10,000 for Poland. Exact figures depend on business size and complexity. For a small exchange, compliance costs may be at the lower end; for a large platform, higher.

Is Poland's crypto license recognized after MiCA?

Yes, Poland is transitioning its VASP registration to a full MiCA CASP license. Existing registrants will need to upgrade, but the process is designed to be smooth. New applicants will apply under MiCA rules from 2026. The KNF has published guidelines for the transition.

Poland is generally more affordable initially, with lower registration costs and no minimum capital currently. However, consider future MiCA compliance costs.
