What Is a Crypto Exchange License in Bahrain?

The Central Bank of Bahrain (CBB) introduced the Crypto Asset Module (CRA) in 2019, updated regularly to align with international standards. Under this module, any entity providing crypto exchange services, including trading platforms, order-matching systems, and custody, must obtain a Crypto Asset Exchange License. This license is a Category 1 or Category 2 license depending on the services offered.

Key features of the Bahrain regime:

  • Regulated by the CBB, a highly respected central bank.
  • License types: Category 1 (full exchange with order book) and Category 2 (broker/dealer or simpler platforms).
  • Minimum capital requirement: BHD 500,000 (approx. USD 1.3 million) for Category 1; BHD 200,000 (approx. USD 530,000) for Category 2.
  • No corporate income tax on profits (0% corporate tax rate for most activities).
  • Timeline: Typically 6-12 months from application to approval.

Bahrain's regime is considered one of the most mature in the GCC, with a strong focus on investor protection and anti-money laundering (AML) compliance.

Who Needs a Bahrain Crypto Exchange License?

Any business that facilitates the exchange of crypto assets for fiat or other crypto assets, operates an order book, provides custody of client assets, or acts as a broker or dealer in crypto assets must be licensed. This includes:

  • Centralized exchanges (CEX) with order books.
  • Decentralized exchanges (DEX) if they have any centralized control or custody.
  • OTC desks that match buyers and sellers.
  • Brokerage platforms that execute trades on behalf of clients.
  • Any platform that holds client private keys or funds.

License Type & Regulator

The Central Bank of Bahrain (CBB) is the sole regulator for crypto asset services. The CBB's Crypto Asset Module (CRA) defines two main license categories:

CategoryServices PermittedMinimum Capital
Category 1Full exchange with order book, matching, custody, and tradingBHD 500,000 (USD 1.33M)
Category 2Broker/dealer, OTC, custody only, no order bookBHD 200,000 (USD 533,000)

Both categories require a physical presence in Bahrain, including a registered office and a senior management team. The CBB also requires appointment of a compliance officer, AML officer, and a board with relevant experience. The regime is principle-based but detailed, with specific rules on client asset segregation, cybersecurity, and market abuse prevention.

Capital Requirements

The minimum capital requirements are set by the CBB and must be maintained at all times:

  • Category 1: BHD 500,000 (approx. USD 1.33 million) - must be fully paid up in cash or liquid assets.
  • Category 2: BHD 200,000 (approx. USD 533,000) - also fully paid up.

Additionally, the CBB requires firms to hold operational capital based on risk profile, which may be higher than the minimum. Capital must be held in a Bahraini bank account and cannot be used for trading or lending. Proof of capital is required at application stage. Tax Treatment in Bahrain

Bahrain offers a highly favorable tax environment for crypto businesses:

  • 0% corporate income tax on profits from most activities (including crypto exchange).
  • No VAT on crypto transactions (Bahrain does not have a general VAT; only a 5% VAT on certain goods/services, but crypto is exempt).
  • No withholding tax on dividends or interest.
  • No capital gains tax.
  • No personal income tax.

However, firms must still comply with transfer pricing rules and economic substance requirements. The CBB also requires audited financial statements. The tax regime is a major draw for exchanges looking to minimize tax leakage. For a jurisdiction with even lower setup costs, Panama offers territorial taxation (no tax on foreign income) at a flat EUR 6,000 company setup fee.

Allowed Activities Under a Bahrain Crypto License

Depending on the license category, the following activities are permitted:

  • Operating a crypto exchange with order book (Category 1 only).
  • Brokerage and dealing in crypto assets (Category 2).
  • Custody of crypto assets (both categories).
  • OTC trading (both categories).
  • Staking and lending (subject to CBB approval).
  • Issuance of stablecoins (requires additional approval under the CRA).

Activities that are strictly prohibited include: unlicensed derivatives, gambling-related tokens, and any service that violates Sharia principles (if the firm markets itself as Sharia-compliant). The CBB also prohibits mixing client assets with firm assets. For a broader range of activities with lower capital, see our jurisdictions page.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a Bahrain crypto exchange license involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application preparation: Engage local legal counsel, prepare business plan, AML/CFT policies, and governance documents.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Bahrain SPC (Single Person Company) or WLL (Limited Liability Company) with a local registered office.
  3. Capital deposit: Open a corporate bank account in Bahrain and deposit the minimum capital.
  4. Application submission: Submit the license application to the CBB with all required documents, including a detailed business plan, risk assessment, and compliance manual.
  5. CBB review: The CBB conducts a thorough review, including background checks on directors and shareholders. This may take 3-6 months.
  6. Interview and site visit: The CBB may request interviews with management and a physical inspection of the office.
  7. License approval: Once approved, the license is issued, and the firm must pay the annual fee.
  8. Ongoing compliance: Submit regular reports, undergo audits, and maintain capital.

Banking & Payments for Bahrain Crypto Exchanges

Opening a bank account in Bahrain for a crypto exchange is challenging but possible. Most traditional banks are cautious, but the CBB has encouraged local banks to serve licensed crypto firms. Options include:

  • Bahraini commercial banks (e.g., National Bank of Bahrain, Ahli United Bank) - require a license and strong compliance history.
  • Digital banks and payment service providers (e.g., Tarabut Gateway, Mamo) - more crypto-friendly.
  • International banks with a Bahrain presence (e.g., HSBC, Standard Chartered) - often require a minimum deposit of USD 500,000+.

Compliance & Trust: Staying on the Right Side of the CBB

Compliance is paramount in Bahrain. The CBB expects licensees to maintain strong AML/CFT controls, including:

  • Customer due diligence (CDD) and enhanced due diligence (EDD) for high-risk clients.
  • Transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting.
  • Annual independent audits.
  • Cybersecurity measures aligned with the CBB's framework.
  • Regular reporting to the CBB on financials, transactions, and compliance.

Common Mistakes When Applying for a Bahrain Crypto License

Many applicants face delays or rejections due to avoidable errors:

  • Insufficient capital proof: The CBB requires evidence that capital is fully paid and held in a Bahraini bank account.
  • Incomplete business plan: A vague or unrealistic plan is a red flag.
  • Poor AML/CFT documentation: The CBB expects detailed policies tailored to the business.
  • Lack of local presence: Directors and senior management must be physically present in Bahrain.
  • Underestimating timeline: Rushing the application leads to mistakes.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto exchange license in Bahrain?

Category 1 (full exchange) requires BHD 500,000 (approx. USD 1.33 million). Category 2 (broker/dealer) requires BHD 200,000 (approx. USD 533,000). Capital must be deposited in a Bahraini bank account and maintained at all times.

How long does it take to get a Bahrain crypto license?

Typically 6 to 12 months from application submission to approval. The CBB conducts a thorough review, including background checks and interviews. Delays can occur if documents are incomplete.

What taxes apply to crypto exchanges in Bahrain?

Bahrain has 0% corporate income tax, no VAT on crypto transactions, no capital gains tax, and no personal income tax. This makes it one of the most tax-friendly jurisdictions for crypto businesses.

Can a foreign company apply for a Bahrain crypto license?

Yes, but the company must be incorporated in Bahrain as a local entity (e.g., SPC or WLL). 100% foreign ownership is allowed. Directors and senior management must be physically present in Bahrain.

What activities are allowed under a Category 2 license?

Category 2 permits brokerage, dealer services, OTC trading, and custody. It does not allow operating an order book or matching engine. For full exchange services, Category 1 is required.

Is a Bahrain crypto license recognized in other countries?

The CBB is a respected regulator, but the license does not automatically passport to other jurisdictions. However, it can facilitate banking relationships and partnerships in the GCC and beyond.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

Licensees must submit annual audited financial statements, regular AML reports, transaction monitoring data, and undergo periodic CBB inspections. A local compliance officer is mandatory.

Can I use a Bahrain license to serve customers in the EU?

No, a Bahrain license does not cover EU markets. For EU access, consider a MiCA license in Lithuania or another EU member state. Consulting24 advises on both options.

What happens if I don't meet the capital requirement?

The CBB will reject the application or revoke the license. Capital must be fully paid up and maintained. If capital falls below the minimum, the firm must notify the CBB and rectify within a specified period.

How does Bahrain compare to Panama for crypto exchanges?

Bahrain offers a regulated environment with zero tax but high capital and cost. Panama has no capital requirement, no crypto-specific license, and a flat EUR 6,000 setup fee, but no regulatory clarity. Choose based on your risk appetite and target market.
