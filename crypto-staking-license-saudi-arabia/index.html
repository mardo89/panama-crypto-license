What Is a Crypto Staking License in Saudi Arabia?

A crypto staking license in Saudi Arabia is a regulatory authorization that allows a company to offer staking services to clients. Staking involves locking up cryptocurrency to support a blockchain network's operations, earning rewards in return. Under Saudi Arabia's 2026 framework, firms must register with the Saudi Central Bank (SAMA) for payment and virtual asset services, and with the Capital Market Authority (CMA) if staking involves securities-like tokens. The license ensures compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) laws, as well as consumer protection standards.

This license is part of Saudi Arabia's broader effort to position itself as a fintech hub within the Middle East, aligning with Vision 2030. It covers activities such as proof-of-stake (PoS) staking, delegated staking, and staking-as-a-service (SaaS) for institutional and retail clients. The regulatory framework is still evolving, but as of 2026, it requires a minimum capital of SAR 500,000 (approximately EUR 125,000) for basic staking services, with higher tiers for more complex operations. For example, a firm offering staking to retail clients may need additional safeguards like segregated client funds and regular audits. The key difference is that Saudi Arabia does not have a single consolidated crypto law; instead, multiple regulators issue guidelines. Who Needs a Crypto Staking License?

Any entity intending to offer staking services to Saudi residents or operate from Saudi Arabia must obtain the appropriate license. This includes:

  • Blockchain startups launching staking pools or platforms that accept client deposits.
  • Exchanges that want to add staking as a service for their users, either as a custodial or non-custodial option.
  • Asset managers offering staking yield products as part of a portfolio.
  • Institutional investors providing delegated staking to clients, such as pension funds or family offices.

If your business model involves holding client funds for staking, you will likely need a full virtual asset service provider (VASP) license. For pure staking without custody, a lighter registration may suffice, but the line is thin. For instance, if you operate a staking pool where clients retain control of their private keys but delegate voting rights, you may still need registration. License Type and Regulator

The primary regulators for crypto staking in Saudi Arabia are the Saudi Central Bank (SAMA) and the Capital Market Authority (CMA). SAMA oversees payment and virtual asset services, while the CMA regulates activities involving securities-like tokens. The license type is typically a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) license with a staking endorsement. For staking that does not involve securities, a SAMA registration may suffice, but if the staked token is deemed a security (e.g., some staking derivatives), CMA approval is required.

As of 2026, the framework is not fully codified into a single law; instead, it draws from the Financial Institutions Law and the Capital Market Law. SAMA has issued guidelines for virtual asset activities, and the CMA has published rules for digital securities. Firms must apply through the Saudi Fintech Sandbox or directly to the respective regulator. The sandbox allows limited operations during the application process, which can be advantageous for testing your business model. Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a crypto staking license in Saudi Arabia is SAR 500,000 (approximately EUR 125,000). This must be deposited in a Saudi bank account and maintained as paid-up capital. For firms offering additional services like custody or exchange, higher capital tiers apply, up to SAR 2,000,000 (EUR 500,000). The capital must be in fiat currency (SAR or equivalent) and cannot be in cryptocurrency. This is to ensure liquidity and solvency.

This requirement is relatively high compared to other jurisdictions. For example, Lithuania requires EUR 125,000 for a CASP license under MiCA, and Panama has no minimum capital requirement for its crypto license. If you are a startup with limited capital, Panama may be more accessible. Tax Treatment

Saudi Arabia does not impose a personal income tax on individuals, but corporate tax applies to foreign-owned entities at a rate of 20% on profits. For crypto staking, the tax treatment is as follows:

  • Corporate income tax: 20% on net profits from staking services.
  • Zakat: 2.5% on assets for Saudi-owned entities (applies to Saudi shareholders).
  • VAT: 15% on service fees charged to clients.

Staking rewards earned by the company are treated as taxable income. There is no specific crypto tax law; general tax principles apply. This means that if you receive staking rewards in cryptocurrency, you must convert them to fiat for tax reporting at the prevailing market rate. Allowed Activities Under the License

A crypto staking license in Saudi Arabia permits the following activities:

  • Proof-of-stake staking for supported blockchains (e.g., Ethereum, Solana, Cardano).
  • Delegated staking where clients delegate tokens to the operator's node.
  • Staking-as-a-service (SaaS) for institutional clients, including automated reward distribution.
  • Management of staking pools and distribution of rewards, with transparent fee structures.

Activities not allowed include operating a crypto exchange without a separate license, offering unregistered securities, and providing lending services. The license may also restrict staking of tokens deemed high-risk by the regulator, such as those with high volatility or unclear legal status. For example, staking of privacy coins may be prohibited. Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a crypto staking license in Saudi Arabia involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step:

  1. Pre-application assessment: Review your business model and determine the correct license type (SAMA or CMA, sandbox or direct).
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Saudi company with the Ministry of Investment (MISA) and obtain a commercial registration (CR).
  3. Capital deposit: Deposit the minimum capital in a Saudi bank account and obtain a certificate of deposit.
  4. Compliance documentation: Prepare AML/CTF policies, business plan, risk assessment, and governance framework.
  5. Submit application: File with SAMA or CMA through the Fintech Sandbox or direct channel, including all supporting documents.
  6. Regulatory review: Respond to queries and provide additional information within specified deadlines (typically 30 days).
  7. Approval and licensing: Receive the license and begin operations, subject to ongoing reporting.

Compliance and Trust

Compliance is critical for maintaining a crypto staking license in Saudi Arabia. Firms must adhere to AML/CTF regulations, including customer due diligence (CDD), transaction monitoring, and reporting suspicious activities to the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU). Regular audits and annual compliance reports are required. The regulator may conduct on-site inspections to verify adherence.

Building trust with clients involves transparent communication about staking risks, reward distribution, and security measures. For example, you should clearly disclose lock-up periods, slashing risks, and fee structures. Consulting24 recommends implementing strong cybersecurity protocols and insurance for custodial assets. Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto staking license in Saudi Arabia?

The minimum capital is SAR 500,000 (approximately EUR 125,000) for basic staking services. Higher tiers apply for additional services like custody or exchange, up to SAR 2,000,000 (EUR 500,000). The capital must be deposited in fiat currency in a Saudi bank account.

How long does it take to get a crypto staking license in Saudi Arabia?

The timeline typically ranges from 6 to 12 months, depending on the complexity of the application and the regulator's workload. Using the Fintech Sandbox may expedite the process, but it still requires thorough preparation.

Can a foreign company apply for a crypto staking license in Saudi Arabia?

Yes, but a foreign company must first incorporate a local entity with the Ministry of Investment (MISA) and have a local sponsor or representative. The license is issued to the Saudi entity, not the foreign parent.

What taxes apply to crypto staking in Saudi Arabia?

Corporate income tax of 20% on net profits applies to foreign-owned entities. Zakat of 2.5% applies to Saudi shareholders. VAT of 15% is charged on service fees. Staking rewards are taxable as income.

Is staking considered a security under Saudi law?

It depends on the token and the staking structure. If the staking involves a token that the CMA deems a security, additional regulations apply. Pure staking of utility tokens may fall under SAMA's purview. Consulting24 can help classify your activity.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

Ongoing requirements include annual AML/CTF audits, transaction monitoring, suspicious activity reporting, and maintaining the minimum capital. The regulator may conduct on-site inspections. Annual compliance costs range from EUR 5,000 to 15,000.

Can I offer staking to retail clients in Saudi Arabia?

Yes, but additional consumer protection measures may be required, such as segregated client funds, clear risk disclosures, and a cooling-off period. Retail staking is permitted but subject to stricter oversight.

How does Panama compare to Saudi Arabia for crypto staking?

Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 fee, no minimum capital, territorial tax (no tax on foreign income), and a 2-3 month timeline. Saudi Arabia requires EUR 125,000 capital, 20% tax, and 6-12 months. Panama is simpler and cheaper for most startups.
