What Is a Crypto Stablecoin License in Cyprus?

A crypto stablecoin license Cyprus is an authorisation granted by CySEC under the MiCA regulatory framework. It allows a company to issue stablecoins (asset-referenced tokens or e-money tokens) and provide related crypto-asset services such as custody, exchange, and transfer. The license is part of the EU's harmonised regime, which replaced national VASP registers with a unified CASP (Crypto-Asset Service Provider) licence. Stablecoin issuers must comply with strict transparency, reserve management, and consumer protection rules.

Cyprus has transposed MiCA into national law, and CySEC is the competent authority for licensing and supervision. The license covers both the issuance of stablecoins and the operation of a trading platform for those tokens. This dual scope makes it attractive for projects that want to combine issuance with exchange services. For example, a company issuing a euro-backed stablecoin can also run a platform where users trade that token against other crypto-assets, all under one license.

The license is distinct from a traditional investment firm licence. It is specifically designed for crypto-asset activities and does not permit deposit-taking or lending without additional authorisation. Who Needs a Crypto Stablecoin License in Cyprus?

Any entity that intends to issue stablecoins or provide crypto-asset services in or from Cyprus needs this license. Specifically, the following activities require authorisation:

  • Issuing asset-referenced tokens (ARTs) or e-money tokens (EMTs) that aim to maintain a stable value.
  • Operating a trading platform for stablecoins.
  • Providing custody and administration of stablecoins on behalf of clients.
  • Exchanging stablecoins for fiat currency or other crypto-assets.
  • Executing orders for stablecoins, including as a broker.
  • Transferring stablecoins on behalf of clients.
  • Providing advice on stablecoins or portfolio management involving stablecoins.

If your project involves a stablecoin that is not classified as an ART or EMT under MiCA (e.g., a pure utility token), different rules may apply. Additionally, if you already hold a CASP licence in another EU member state, you may be able to passport into Cyprus without a full local application, but this requires notification to CySEC.

For projects that are purely non-EU and do not target EU clients, a Cyprus license may still be beneficial for credibility and future expansion. However, if your operations are entirely outside the EU, you might consider Panama for a faster and lower-cost alternative.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator for crypto stablecoin licensing in Cyprus is the Cyprus Securities and Exchange Commission (CySEC). The license type is a Crypto-Asset Service Provider (CASP) licence under the Investment Services and Activities and Regulated Markets Law of 2023 (as amended for MiCA). For stablecoin issuers, additional requirements apply under the Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (EU) 2023/1114.

CySEC is a well-respected EU regulator with a track record of supervising investment firms, funds, and now crypto-asset service providers. It is a member of the European Securities and Markets Authority (ESMA) and the European Banking Authority (EBA), ensuring alignment with EU-wide standards. The CASP licence allows passporting to other EU member states without additional licensing.

CySEC has published detailed guidelines on the application process, including a dedicated application form for CASPs. The regulator expects applicants to have substance in Cyprus, including a local office, at least two directors (one of whom must be a Cyprus resident), and a compliance officer based in the EU. Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, minimum capital requirements are tiered by service class. For stablecoin issuers and CASPs in Cyprus, the following apply:

  • €50,000 for custody and administration services only.
  • €125,000 for exchange services (fiat-to-crypto or crypto-to-crypto) and order execution.
  • €150,000 for operating a trading platform or placing crypto-assets.

If your stablecoin license covers multiple service classes, the highest applicable capital requirement applies. The capital must be held in liquid assets and maintained at all times. Additionally, stablecoin issuers must hold a reserve of assets equal to the value of tokens in circulation, subject to strict custody and audit requirements.

The capital can be in the form of cash or cash equivalents held in a Cyprus bank account. CySEC may also accept certain liquid securities. Consulting24 can advise on the most efficient way to structure the capital deposit, including options for escrow accounts that minimise operational disruption.

Tax Treatment

Cyprus offers a favourable tax regime for crypto businesses:

  • Corporate income tax: 12.5% on net profits, one of the lowest in the EU.
  • Capital gains tax: 0% on gains from disposal of securities (including crypto-assets held as investments).
  • VAT: Exempt for crypto-asset exchange and transfer services under EU VAT rules.
  • Withholding tax: 0% on dividends paid to non-resident shareholders, subject to conditions.

Stablecoin transactions may be subject to specific tax treatment depending on the nature of the token (e.g., e-money tokens vs. asset-referenced tokens). Consulting24 recommends engaging a Cyprus-based tax advisor for your specific structure. The tax authorities have issued guidance indicating that stablecoin issuance may be treated as a supply of services for VAT purposes, but exemptions apply for exchange and transfer.

For international tax planning, Cyprus has an extensive network of double tax treaties. This can reduce withholding taxes on cross-border payments. Consulting24 can coordinate with your tax advisor to optimise your corporate structure.

Allowed Activities Under the License

A crypto stablecoin license Cyprus permits the following activities under MiCA:

  • Issuance of asset-referenced tokens (ARTs) and e-money tokens (EMTs).
  • Custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients.
  • Operation of a crypto-asset trading platform.
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for fiat currency or other crypto-assets.
  • Execution of orders for crypto-assets.
  • Placement of crypto-assets (underwriting).
  • Transfer services for crypto-assets on behalf of clients.
  • Advice on crypto-assets and portfolio management.

Stablecoin issuers must also comply with additional requirements: publishing a white paper, maintaining a reserve of assets, and providing redemption rights to holders. The license does not cover activities that fall under traditional financial regulation (e.g., deposit-taking), which require a banking license. If your stablecoin project also involves lending or staking, you may need a separate authorisation or a different license type.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The application process for a crypto stablecoin license Cyprus involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step:

  1. Pre-application assessment: We review your business model, token classification, and compliance readiness. This includes a gap analysis against CySEC requirements.
  2. Company incorporation: Set up a Cyprus company with appropriate corporate structure and registered office. We assist with director appointments and shareholder documentation.
  3. Documentation preparation: Draft the white paper, business plan, AML/KYC policies, risk management framework, and governance documents. This is the most time-consuming phase, typically 2-4 months.
  4. Capital deposit: Place the required minimum capital in a Cyprus bank account or escrow. We can introduce you to banks that accept crypto-related deposits.
  5. Submission to CySEC: File the application with all supporting documents and pay the application fee. CySEC will issue an acknowledgement of receipt.
  6. Review and queries: CySEC reviews the application and may request additional information or clarifications. This can take 3-6 months depending on complexity.
  7. On-site inspection: CySEC may conduct an on-site visit to verify operational readiness, including office premises, IT systems, and compliance procedures.
  8. Approval: Once satisfied, CySEC grants the license. The company is then registered as a CASP and can commence operations.

Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Holding a crypto stablecoin license Cyprus requires ongoing compliance. Key obligations include:

  • AML/CFT compliance: Implement strong KYC, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting. CySEC expects a risk-based approach.
  • Reserve management: For stablecoin issuers, maintain a fully backed reserve with regular audits. The reserve must be held with a qualified custodian.
  • White paper updates: Keep the white paper accurate and file material changes with CySEC. Any change to the token's design or reserve policy must be pre-approved.
  • Reporting: Submit periodic financial reports, transaction reports, and compliance reports to CySEC. Annual audited accounts are required.
  • Capital maintenance: Ensure minimum capital is always met. If capital falls below the threshold, CySEC must be notified immediately.

Non-compliance can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. Consulting24 provides ongoing compliance support to help you meet these obligations. Common Mistakes to Avoid

Applying for a crypto stablecoin license Cyprus can be complex. Here are common mistakes that delay or derail applications:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing or poorly drafted white papers, business plans, or AML policies are the top reason for queries. Ensure every required document is thorough and professionally prepared.
  • Underestimating substance requirements: CySEC expects a physical office in Cyprus, local directors, and a compliance officer in the EU. Virtual offices are not accepted.
  • Ignoring token classification: Misclassifying your stablecoin as a utility token when it is actually an ART or EMT can lead to rejection. Consulting24 can help with classification.
  • Insufficient capital: Failing to maintain the minimum capital or reserve requirements can result in suspension. Plan for ongoing capital adequacy.
  • Poor banking relationships: Not securing a bank account before applying can delay the process. Start banking applications early.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto stablecoin license in Cyprus?

The minimum capital depends on the services offered. For custody only, it is €50,000. For exchange services, €125,000. For operating a trading platform, €150,000. If you offer multiple services, the highest tier applies. Stablecoin issuers also need a reserve equal to the token value.

How long does it take to get a Cyprus stablecoin license?

The typical timeline is 6 to 12 months from application to approval. This can vary based on the complexity of your business model and the quality of your documentation. Consulting24 can help prepare a complete application to minimise delays.

Can I passport my Cyprus license to other EU countries?

Yes, the Cyprus CASP license allows passporting across all EU/EEA member states. You can provide services in other countries without needing a separate license, though you must notify the local regulator.

What are the tax benefits of a Cyprus stablecoin license?

Cyprus offers a corporate tax rate of 12.5%, one of the lowest in the EU. Capital gains on securities (including crypto) are tax-exempt, and dividends paid to non-residents have 0% withholding tax. VAT is exempt for crypto exchange and transfer services.

Do I need a physical office in Cyprus?

Yes, CySEC requires that you have a registered office and a physical presence in Cyprus. This includes office space, local directors, and a compliance officer based in the EU. Virtual offices are not sufficient.

What activities are not covered by the Cyprus stablecoin license?

The license does not cover deposit-taking, lending, or any activity that falls under traditional banking regulation. If your stablecoin project involves lending or staking, you may need a separate authorisation or a different license type.

Can I apply for a Cyprus license if my company is already registered elsewhere?

Yes, you can set up a Cyprus subsidiary and apply for the license. The parent company can be based anywhere. However, the Cyprus entity must have its own substance and comply with local requirements.

How does the Cyprus license compare to Panama for stablecoin issuance?

Cyprus offers EU passporting and a lower corporate tax rate, but costs €47,000-€90,000 and takes 6-12 months. Panama costs a flat €6,000 and takes 2-4 months, but does not provide EU access. Choose based on your target market.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations after obtaining the license?

You must maintain AML/CFT programs, submit regular reports to CySEC, keep your white paper updated, and ensure capital and reserve requirements are met. Annual audits are required. Non-compliance can lead to fines or license revocation.

No, we cannot guarantee approval. However, our thorough preparation and understanding of CySEC requirements significantly increase the chances. We have a strong track record with over 500 licenses in other jurisdictions.
