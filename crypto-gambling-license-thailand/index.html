What Is a Crypto Gambling License in Thailand?

Thailand does not have a dedicated crypto gambling license. The country's gambling industry is largely illegal under the Gambling Act of 1935, with only state-run lotteries and horse racing permitted. Crypto gambling, whether based in Thailand or targeting Thai players, faces significant legal barriers. The Thai SEC regulates digital asset businesses, but it does not issue licenses for gambling activities. Operators considering Thailand must be aware that running a crypto gambling platform from within the country is not feasible under current law.

Instead, international operators often set up in jurisdictions like Panama, where a company can be formed for EUR 6,000 and obtain a license that covers crypto gambling activities. License Type & Regulator in Thailand

In Thailand, the relevant authorities for digital assets are the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) and the Ministry of Finance. However, they do not issue gambling licenses. The SEC regulates crypto exchanges, brokers, dealers, and fund managers under the Digital Asset Business Decree. Gambling falls outside their scope. The only legal gambling in Thailand is the Government Lottery and horse racing at specific venues.

For a crypto gambling license, you must look to jurisdictions like Panama, where the regulator is the Gaming Control Board (Junta de Control de Juegos). Panama offers a license that covers online gambling and explicitly allows cryptocurrency transactions. Capital Requirement

Capital requirements for a crypto gambling license vary by jurisdiction. In Panama, there is no fixed minimum capital for the company itself, but the Gaming Control Board may require a bank guarantee or a deposit to ensure player funds are protected. Typically, this ranges from EUR 50,000 to EUR 200,000 depending on the scale of operations. In Curacao, the minimum capital is around EUR 50,000. Malta requires a minimum share capital of EUR 40,000 for a Class 1 license.

Thailand does not have a capital requirement for crypto gambling because the activity is not licensed. However, if you operate from another jurisdiction, you must meet that jurisdiction's capital rules. Consulting24 can advise on the specific capital needed for your chosen route.

Tax Treatment

Tax treatment for crypto gambling income depends on where your company is registered. Panama operates a territorial tax system: income earned outside Panama is not taxed. This makes Panama attractive for crypto gambling operators targeting global players. Corporate income tax on Panama-source income is 25%, but most gambling revenue is considered foreign-source if processed through a Panama entity. There is no withholding tax on dividends.

In contrast, Thailand taxes worldwide income for residents. If you were to operate from Thailand, you would face corporate income tax of 20% and potential withholding taxes. However, since Thailand does not license crypto gambling, operating there is not advisable. Consulting24 recommends Panama for tax efficiency and legal clarity.
