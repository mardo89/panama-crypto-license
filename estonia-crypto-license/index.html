Estonia Crypto License 2026: VASP to MiCA CASP

Estonia was the original go-to for crypto licensing — thousands of VASP authorizations were issued here in the early days. The rules have tightened dramatically and MiCA has now replaced the VASP regime with the EU-wide CASP authorization, supervised by the Estonian Financial Supervision Authority. Estonia still appeals for its famous 0%-on-retained-profit tax and digital-first administration.

The end of the easy Estonian VASP

Estonia pioneered crypto licensing in 2017, but a series of reforms (notably in 2022) raised capital, local-substance and management requirements sharply, and the Financial Intelligence Unit became far more selective. Under MiCA, the VASP register is being phased out entirely in favour of CASP authorization under the Estonian Financial Supervision Authority. In practice that means a serious application: real governance, AML systems, qualified staff and capital.

Tax — Estonia's signature advantage

Estonia's distinctive corporate-tax model means 0% tax on retained or reinvested profit; corporate income tax (22% from 2025) is only triggered when profit is distributed as dividends. For a growth-stage crypto company reinvesting earnings, this is highly efficient. Estonia also offers fully digital company administration and e-Residency.

Cost & timeline

Plan for a total project cost in the region of €25,000–€55,000+ (formation, compliance build-out, personnel and the application), plus the MiCA capital. Timelines are typically 5–9 months given the EFSA's thorough review.

Estonia vs Lithuania vs Panama: Estonia and Lithuania are both EU MiCA routes with passporting — Estonia for its tax model, Lithuania for its larger, often-faster ecosystem. Panama is the fast, low-cost, 0%-tax option for non-EU-facing businesses (€6,000, 2–3 weeks).

Who Estonia suits

Estonia fits founders who want an EU-regulated CASP, value the reinvestment-friendly tax model, and can meet the capital and substance bar. If you need to launch quickly and cheaply for a global audience, start with a Panama company and consider Estonia or Lithuania when EU market access becomes the priority.
