What Is the Estonia Crypto License?

The Estonia crypto license is a Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) authorization granted by the Estonian Financial Supervision Authority (Finantsinspektsioon) under the EU MiCA framework. It replaced the previous FIU/VASP register in 2026, aligning Estonia with the harmonized EU regulatory standards. The license allows companies to legally offer crypto services such as exchange, custody, transfer, and advisory services across the European Economic Area (EEA) through passporting.

Key features of the Estonia CASP license include:

  • Full EU passporting rights - operate in any EU/EEA member state without additional licensing
  • Regulated by a reputable authority (Finantsinspektsioon)
  • Minimum capital requirements ranging from €50,000 to €150,000 depending on service types
  • Mandatory central administration and management in Estonia
  • Compliance with MiCA's stringent AML/CFT, consumer protection, and operational resilience rules

This license is ideal for crypto businesses seeking a credible, EU-wide regulatory foundation.

Who Needs an Estonia Crypto License?

Any company offering crypto asset services in or into the European Union must hold a CASP license under MiCA. The Estonia crypto license is particularly suited for:

  • Crypto exchanges (order-book, DEX, P2P) that match buyers and sellers
  • Wallet providers offering custodial or non-custodial wallet services
  • Brokers and dealers executing client orders for crypto assets
  • Transfer services moving crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Advisory and portfolio management firms providing crypto investment advice
  • Token issuers (subject to additional rules under MiCA for asset-referenced tokens or e-money tokens)

If your target market includes EU residents, you need an EU-compliant license. Estonia offers a fast, transparent process compared to larger EU states. For businesses focused on non-EU markets, consider a Panama crypto license (flat €6,000) or other jurisdictions we advise on.

License Type & Regulator

The Estonia crypto license is a Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) license under MiCA. The regulator is the Estonian Financial Supervision Authority (Finantsinspektsioon), which also supervises banks, insurance companies, and securities markets. Finantsinspektsioon is a member of the European Banking Authority (EBA) and the European Securities and Markets Authority (ESMA), ensuring high regulatory standards.

Capital Requirement

As mentioned, the minimum capital for an Estonia crypto license ranges from €50,000 to €150,000 depending on the service classes you apply for. The capital must be paid up in cash and held in an Estonian bank account or a licensed EU credit institution. It must remain unencumbered and available at all times to cover operational risks.

Capital requirements are calculated as the higher of:

  • Fixed minimum (per service class)
  • Quarterly fixed overheads multiplied by 25%
  • Risk-based capital (based on transaction volume and custody assets)

Most startups qualify for the fixed minimum. We help you structure your application to meet these requirements efficiently. For comparison, Lithuania has similar MiCA capital tiers but with slightly different regulatory expectations.

Tax Treatment

One of Estonia's biggest attractions is its unique corporate tax system: 0% corporate income tax on retained and reinvested profits. Tax is only due when profits are distributed (e.g., as dividends) at a rate of 22% on the gross distribution. This means you can reinvest your crypto business earnings tax-free, accelerating growth.

Key tax points for crypto businesses:

  • No withholding tax on dividends paid to non-resident shareholders
  • VAT: Crypto-to-fiat exchange is exempt; crypto-to-crypto may be subject to VAT (currently 20%) but guidance is evolving
  • Personal income tax: 20% flat rate on employment income and capital gains
  • No net wealth tax, no inheritance tax

Estonia also has a wide double taxation treaty network. For detailed tax planning, consult a local tax advisor. Our team can connect you with trusted partners.

Allowed Activities Under the License

The Estonia CASP license permits the following activities (as defined by MiCA):

  • Exchange services: converting fiat currency to crypto assets, or crypto assets to fiat, or between crypto assets
  • Custody and administration: safekeeping and controlling crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Transfer services: transferring crypto assets from one address to another on behalf of a client
  • Execution of orders: concluding contracts to buy or sell crypto assets
  • Placing of crypto assets: marketing and selling crypto assets on behalf of issuers
  • Advice: providing personalized recommendations on crypto asset transactions
  • Portfolio management: managing crypto asset portfolios on a discretionary basis

Activities not covered include issuing asset-referenced tokens (ARTs) or e-money tokens (EMTs), which require separate licensing under MiCA. For those, you may need a credit institution or e-money license. We can advise on these requirements as part of our jurisdictional guidance.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Many applicants face delays or rejections due to avoidable errors. Here are the most common pitfalls:

  • Incomplete AML policies: Generic templates won't pass. Your policies must be tailored to your business model and risk profile.
  • Insufficient capital proof: You must demonstrate that the capital is unencumbered and held in a regulated institution.
  • Weak business plan: Finantsinspektsioon expects realistic financial projections and a clear operational plan.
  • Lack of local substance: You need a physical office in Estonia (can be co-working) and a local compliance officer.
  • Ignoring MiCA transitional provisions: If you held a previous FIU license, ensure you comply with the new CASP requirements.
  • Not engaging experts: DIY applications often fail. Professional guidance saves time and money.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for an Estonia crypto license?

Under MiCA, the minimum capital is €50,000 for custody services, €125,000 for exchange services, and €150,000 for transfer and advisory services. If you offer multiple service classes, the highest applicable capital applies.

How long does it take to get an Estonia crypto license?

The process typically takes 5 to 9 months from start to license issuance. This includes company setup, compliance documentation, and regulatory review by Finantsinspektsioon.

Can I operate in other EU countries with an Estonia crypto license?

Yes, the Estonia CASP license is recognized across the EU under MiCA passporting. You can offer services in any EU/EEA member state without additional licensing, subject to notification requirements.

What are the tax benefits of an Estonia crypto license?

Estonia imposes 0% corporate income tax on retained and reinvested profits. Tax is only due at 22% when profits are distributed as dividends. This makes Estonia highly attractive for growing crypto businesses.

Do I need to have a physical office in Estonia?

Yes, your company must have a registered office and central administration in Estonia. This can be a virtual office or co-working space. We can help arrange this.

Can I apply for the license as a non-EU resident?

Yes, non-EU residents can establish an Estonian company via e-Residency and apply for the license. You must appoint a local contact person or compliance officer, which we can provide.

What services are covered under the Estonia CASP license?

Covered services include exchange, custody, transfer, order execution, placement, advice, and portfolio management of crypto assets. Issuance of asset-referenced tokens or e-money tokens requires separate authorization.

How does the Estonia crypto license compare to the old FIU register?

The old FIU register was a lighter-touch registration with no minimum capital. The new MiCA CASP license imposes stricter capital, governance, and compliance requirements but offers EU-wide passporting and enhanced credibility.

What happens if my application is rejected?

Rejections are rare with proper preparation. If rejected, you can appeal or reapply after addressing deficiencies. Consulting24 has a high success rate and we guide you to avoid common pitfalls.

Can I switch from another EU crypto license to Estonia?

Yes, you can transfer your CASP license from another EU member state under MiCA's passporting rules, but you must notify your home regulator and Finantsinspektsioon. We can assist with the process.
