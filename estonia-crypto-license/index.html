Estonia Crypto License 2026: VASP to MiCA CASP

Estonia was the original go-to for crypto licensing — thousands of VASP authorizations were issued here in the early days. The rules have tightened dramatically and MiCA has now replaced the VASP regime with the EU-wide CASP authorization, supervised by the Estonian Financial Supervision Authority. Estonia still appeals for its famous 0%-on-retained-profit tax and digital-first administration.

The end of the easy Estonian VASP

Estonia pioneered crypto licensing in 2017, but a series of reforms (notably in 2022) raised capital, local-substance and management requirements sharply, and the Financial Intelligence Unit became far more selective. Under MiCA, the VASP register is being phased out entirely in favour of CASP authorization under the Estonian Financial Supervision Authority. In practice that means a serious application: real governance, AML systems, qualified staff and capital.

Capital requirements

As an EU member, Estonia applies the same MiCA own-funds tiers as other member states:

Tax — Estonia's signature advantage

Estonia's distinctive corporate-tax model means 0% tax on retained or reinvested profit; corporate income tax (22% from 2025) is only triggered when profit is distributed as dividends. For a growth-stage crypto company reinvesting earnings, this is highly efficient. Estonia also offers fully digital company administration and e-Residency.

Cost & timeline

Plan for a total project cost in the region of €25,000–€55,000+ (formation, compliance build-out, personnel and the application), plus the MiCA capital. Timelines are typically 5–9 months given the EFSA's thorough review.

Estonia vs Lithuania vs Panama: Estonia and Lithuania are both EU MiCA routes with passporting — Estonia for its tax model, Lithuania for its larger, often-faster ecosystem. Panama is the fast, low-cost, 0%-tax option for non-EU-facing businesses (€6,000, 2–3 weeks).

Allowed activities under an Estonian CASP

As with every MiCA member state, an Estonian CASP authorization is scoped to specific crypto-asset services:

  • Operating a trading platform for crypto-assets
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for fiat and for other crypto-assets
  • Custody and administration of crypto-assets
  • Reception, transmission and execution of orders
  • Placement, advice and portfolio management
  • Transfer services

Substance and management — what changed

The defining feature of modern Estonian licensing is substance. The 2022 reforms and the MiCA transition mean the regulator expects genuine local management, a real office footprint appropriate to the activity, a qualified AML/MLRO function, and demonstrable control over outsourced functions. The era of buying a shelf "Estonian crypto licence" and running it remotely with no presence is firmly over — applications that look like that are declined.

Banking and the e-Residency advantage

Estonia's fully digital administration and e-Residency program make company management unusually smooth — board decisions, filings and signatures are all online. For banking, a regulated Estonian CASP is a credible counterparty for EU EMIs and IBAN providers; as always, expect enhanced due diligence and a solid source-of-funds narrative. Pairing the 0%-on-retained-profit model with disciplined reinvestment can be highly efficient for a scaling platform.

The application process, step by step

  1. Scope & structure — service classes, capital tier, ownership.
  2. Incorporate the Estonian OÜ and fund the MiCA capital.
  3. Build the file — business plan, governance, AML/CFT, IT/custody, key persons.
  4. Establish substance — local management, office and compliance staffing.
  5. Apply to the Estonian Financial Supervision Authority and handle review queries.
  6. Authorization and ongoing supervision.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Assuming Estonia is still the cheap, fast 2019-style VASP — it isn't.
  • Under-resourcing local substance and AML staffing.
  • Mis-modelling tax — the 0% applies to retained profit; distributions are taxed at 22%.
  • Choosing Estonia for the brand when Lithuania may be faster for your service mix.

Who Estonia suits

Estonia fits founders who want an EU-regulated CASP, value the reinvestment-friendly tax model, and can meet the capital and substance bar. If you need to launch quickly and cheaply for a global audience, start with a Panama company and consider Estonia or Lithuania when EU market access becomes the priority.
