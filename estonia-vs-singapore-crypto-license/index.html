What is the Estonia vs Singapore Crypto License Comparison?

This comparison examines two prominent crypto licensing regimes: Estonia's MiCA-compliant license and Singapore's Major Payment Institution (MPI) license under the Payment Services Act. Both are designed to regulate crypto asset service providers (CASPs), but they differ in scope, cost, and operational requirements. Estonia offers a single license covering multiple services under MiCA, while Singapore requires separate licensing for different payment services. Understanding these differences is key to making an informed choice for your crypto business.

For example, a crypto exchange offering custody and fiat-to-crypto trading would need only one license in Estonia, but in Singapore, each service type (exchange, custody, transfer) may require a separate application or a combined MPI license with specific limits. The choice depends on your target market and service scope. Consulting24 can help you map your services to the correct license type.

Who Needs a Crypto License in Estonia or Singapore?

Any business offering crypto-related services such as exchange, custody, transfer, or wallet services must obtain a license in either jurisdiction. In Estonia, this applies to all CASPs under MiCA, including virtual currency exchange, wallet services, and transfer services. In Singapore, the PSA covers digital payment token (DPT) services, including exchange, transfer, and custody. Companies targeting EU markets often prefer Estonia, while those focusing on Asia-Pacific may choose Singapore. Both regimes require a local presence and compliance with AML/CFT regulations.

For instance, a startup building a crypto wallet for EU users would be well-suited for Estonia, whereas a firm facilitating cross-border DPT transfers for Asian clients would likely need Singapore. Consulting24 advises on jurisdiction fit based on your business model and target audience.

License Type and Regulator

Estonia: The regulator is the Financial Supervision Authority (Finantsinspektsioon). The license is a MiCA CASP license, which is a single authorization covering multiple services. It replaced the previous virtual currency service provider registration in 2025. The license allows activities such as exchange, custody, transfer, and platform operation under one umbrella.

Singapore: The regulator is the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS). The license is a Major Payment Institution (MPI) license under the Payment Services Act (PSA). It covers specific payment services, and companies must apply for each service type separately. For example, a firm offering both DPT exchange and custody would need to apply for both service classes under the MPI license.

Both regulators enforce strict AML/CFT requirements and conduct background checks on key personnel. Consulting24 assists in preparing the necessary documentation for both authorities.

Capital Requirements

Estonia (MiCA): Capital requirements are tiered based on service type: EUR 50,000 for simple services (e.g., custody), EUR 125,000 for exchange services, and EUR 150,000 for complex services (e.g., trading platforms). This capital must be maintained as own funds. For example, a company offering only crypto custody needs EUR 50,000, while one operating a crypto exchange needs EUR 125,000.

Singapore (PSA): The minimum capital requirement for an MPI license is SGD 250,000 (approximately EUR 170,000). This must be held as paid-up capital or a combination of capital and other forms of financial resources. Unlike Estonia, Singapore does not have tiered capital based on service type, but the single threshold is higher than Estonia's lowest tier.

Tax Treatment

Estonia: Corporate income tax is 0% on retained profits; only distributed profits are taxed at 20%. This allows tax deferral and reinvestment. Crypto transactions are generally treated as income from business activities and subject to standard corporate tax rules. VAT does not apply to crypto-to-fiat exchanges (subject to specific conditions). For example, if your Estonian company earns EUR 100,000 and retains it, no tax is due until dividends are paid.

Singapore: Corporate tax rate is 17% on chargeable income. Crypto transactions are taxed based on the nature of the business (e.g., trading income, investment income). Capital gains are generally not taxed. GST (VAT) is not applicable to digital payment token transactions as of 2026. For a Singapore company earning the same EUR 100,000, the tax would be 17% on the profit, unless it qualifies as capital gains.

Consulting24 advises on tax structuring to minimize liabilities in both jurisdictions.

Allowed Activities

Estonia (MiCA): Services include exchange between crypto and fiat, exchange between crypto assets, custody and administration, transfer services, and operation of a trading platform. All services can be covered under a single license. This is a major advantage for businesses offering multiple services.

Singapore (PSA): Allowed activities include digital payment token (DPT) exchange, DPT transfer, DPT custody, and facilitating DPT transactions. Each service requires a separate license application. The MPI license covers multiple services but with specific limits. For example, a company offering DPT exchange and custody must apply for both service classes under the MPI license, which may involve separate applications and fees.

Estonia: Lower capital requirements, faster timeline (3-6 months), single license for multiple services, 0% tax on retained profits, and access to the EU single market. Ideal for startups and EU-focused businesses. For example, a DeFi startup can launch quickly with minimal capital.

Singapore: Strong regulatory reputation, access to Asian markets, stable banking environment, and clear tax rules. Higher capital requirement but perceived as a gold-standard license. Frequently asked questions

What is the main difference between Estonia and Singapore crypto licenses?

Estonia offers a single MiCA-compliant license covering multiple services with lower capital (EUR 50,000+) and faster timeline (3-6 months). Singapore requires separate licenses per service under PSA, with higher capital (SGD 250,000) and longer timeline (6-12 months). Estonia is better for EU-focused startups; Singapore suits Asian markets.

Which is cheaper: Estonia or Singapore crypto license?

Estonia is generally cheaper. Application fees range EUR 3,000-10,000, professional fees EUR 15,000-30,000, and minimum capital EUR 50,000. Singapore costs SGD 5,000-100,000 application fee, SGD 50,000-150,000 professional fees, and SGD 250,000 capital. Ongoing compliance is also higher in Singapore.

Can I apply for both Estonia and Singapore licenses simultaneously?

Yes, but it is resource-intensive. Each jurisdiction requires a separate legal entity, compliance framework, and management attention. Consulting24 can coordinate both applications if your business model requires presence in both EU and Asia.

What are the tax benefits of Estonia for crypto businesses?

Estonia offers 0% corporate tax on retained profits; only distributed profits are taxed at 20%. This allows tax deferral. Crypto transactions are treated as business income. No VAT on crypto-to-fiat exchanges. Singapore has a flat 17% corporate tax but no capital gains tax.

How long does it take to get a crypto license in Singapore?

The MAS typically takes 6-12 months to process an MPI license application. This includes a pre-application stage, document review, background checks, and interviews. Consulting24 helps expedite by ensuring complete and accurate submissions.

What are the capital requirements for a crypto license in Estonia?

Estonia's capital requirements are tiered: EUR 50,000 for custody, EUR 125,000 for exchange, and EUR 150,000 for complex services like trading platforms. This capital must be maintained as own funds. Consulting24 can advise on the correct tier for your services.

Is banking easier in Estonia or Singapore for crypto firms?

Singapore generally offers easier banking access for licensed MPI holders, with major banks like DBS and OCBC providing accounts. In Estonia, banking is more challenging but possible through EMIs and crypto-friendly banks like LHV. Consulting24 assists with introductions in both jurisdictions.

Can a non-EU company get an Estonian crypto license?

Yes, but the company must be incorporated in Estonia and have a local presence (e.g., registered office, local director or representative). Consulting24 helps with company setup and compliance. Non-EU founders are welcome.

What ongoing compliance is required for a Singapore MPI license?

Ongoing compliance includes regular AML/CFT reporting, transaction monitoring, annual audits, and maintaining minimum capital. MAS requires periodic submissions and may conduct inspections. Consulting24 provides compliance support packages.

Which jurisdiction has faster processing: Estonia or Singapore?

Estonia is faster, with a typical timeline of 3-6 months, compared to Singapore's 6-12 months. Estonia's MiCA regime has streamlined the process, while Singapore's MAS conducts thorough due diligence. Consulting24 can help prepare applications to minimize delays.

What happens if I operate without a license in Estonia or Singapore?

Operating without a license is illegal and can result in fines, criminal charges, and business closure. Both regulators actively enforce licensing requirements. Consulting24 advises on compliance to avoid such risks.
