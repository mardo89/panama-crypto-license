What Is a Crypto Stablecoin License in Germany?

A crypto stablecoin license in Germany is a regulatory authorization issued by BaFin under the German Banking Act (KWG) and the EU Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA). It allows entities to issue stablecoins - crypto assets designed to maintain a stable value by referencing a fiat currency, commodity, or other asset. The license is part of the broader CASP (Crypto Asset Service Provider) regime, which fully applies as of 2026.

Stablecoin issuers must comply with stringent requirements, including holding a MiCA-compliant license, maintaining adequate capital, and implementing strong governance and risk management frameworks. The license is essential for operating legally within Germany and the EU, providing investor protection and market integrity. For example, a fintech launching a euro-backed stablecoin like EURT must obtain this license to ensure compliance with reserve asset segregation and redemption rights. The license also covers activities such as custody, exchange, and transfer of stablecoins, depending on the scope applied for.

Under MiCA, stablecoins are categorized into asset-referenced tokens (ARTs) and e-money tokens (EMTs). ARTs reference a basket of assets or a single commodity, while EMTs reference a single fiat currency. Each category has distinct requirements, including mandatory white papers for ARTs and compliance with e-money directives for EMTs. Who Needs a Crypto Stablecoin License in Germany?

Any entity that issues stablecoins in or from Germany requires this license. This includes:

  • Fintech startups launching euro-backed stablecoins (e.g., EURT, EURS alternatives).
  • Traditional financial institutions expanding into digital assets.
  • Non-EU firms seeking to offer stablecoins to German or EU residents.
  • Decentralized finance (DeFi) projects that issue algorithmic or asset-backed stablecoins.

Exemptions may apply for very small issuers or those operating under certain thresholds, but most commercial stablecoin projects must seek authorization. For instance, a DeFi protocol issuing a token pegged to gold would need to determine if it qualifies as an ART under MiCA. License Type and Regulator

The primary regulator is the German Federal Financial Supervisory Authority (BaFin), which acts as the competent authority under MiCA. The license type is a CASP license with a stablecoin issuance endorsement. BaFin oversees compliance with capital requirements, governance, and ongoing reporting.

For stablecoins referencing a single fiat currency (e.g., EUR), the issuer may also need a credit institution license under the Capital Requirements Regulation (CRR) if the stablecoin is considered an e-money token. However, under MiCA, ARTs and EMTs have specific regimes. ARTs require a white paper approved by BaFin, while EMTs must comply with e-money directives. The application is submitted via BaFin's online portal, and the regulator typically assigns a case officer who reviews the submission.

Capital Requirement

Under MiCA, stablecoin issuers must meet minimum capital requirements based on the type of stablecoin:

  • E-money tokens (EMTs): EUR 350,000 or 2% of the average outstanding e-money, whichever is higher.
  • Asset-referenced tokens (ARTs): EUR 350,000 or 2% of the reserve assets, with a minimum of EUR 250,000.
  • Other CASP services (if bundled): EUR 50,000 to 150,000 depending on service class.

These figures are subject to change and should be verified with BaFin. For example, an issuer with EUR 10 million in outstanding EMTs would need at least EUR 350,000 capital (2% of EUR 10M is EUR 200,000, so the EUR 350,000 floor applies). For ARTs, if the reserve assets are EUR 5 million, the capital requirement would be EUR 350,000 (2% of EUR 5M is EUR 100,000, but the floor applies). Tax Treatment of Stablecoins in Germany

Germany treats stablecoins as financial instruments for tax purposes. Key points:

  • Corporate income tax: Issuers pay 15% plus solidarity surcharge (approx. 15.8% total) on profits.
  • Trade tax: Varies by municipality, typically 7-17% (e.g., Frankfurt 16.1%, Berlin 14.4%).
  • VAT: Stablecoin issuance and exchange are generally VAT-exempt under EU law (Article 135 of VAT Directive).
  • Investor taxation: Private investors holding stablecoins for over one year may benefit from tax-free gains (if not used for income). However, frequent trading or staking may trigger taxation.

Additionally, reserve assets held in fiat may generate interest income that is taxable. Transfer pricing rules apply if the issuer is part of a multinational group. Allowed Activities Under the License

With a BaFin crypto stablecoin license, you can:

  • Issue stablecoins (EMTs and ARTs).
  • Provide custody and administration of stablecoins.
  • Operate a trading platform for stablecoins.
  • Exchange stablecoins for fiat or other crypto assets.
  • Provide transfer services for stablecoins.
  • Offer staking or lending services (if separately authorized).

Activities must be conducted in compliance with MiCA and BaFin regulations. Additional permissions may be required for ancillary services, such as operating a multilateral trading facility. For instance, if you plan to offer a stablecoin with built-in lending features, you may need a separate license under the German Banking Act. Step-by-Step Application Process

The process involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application (1-3 months): Conduct a gap analysis, prepare business plan, governance documents, AML/KYC policies, and technical documentation (e.g., white paper for ARTs). This includes drafting risk management policies, business continuity plans, and IT security measures.
  2. Submission: File the application with BaFin, including detailed information on ownership, management, financial projections, and reserve management procedures. The application must be in German or accompanied by a certified translation.
  3. Review (3-6 months): BaFin assesses the application, with possible requests for additional information. They may also conduct on-site inspections of the office and interview key personnel.
  4. Decision: If approved, the license is granted with conditions (e.g., reporting obligations). Rejection can be appealed. BaFin typically issues a formal notice with the license number and scope.
  5. Post-licensing: Ongoing compliance, periodic reporting, and annual audits. You must notify BaFin of any material changes, such as new products or changes in management.

Compliance and Trust: Ongoing Obligations

Post-licensing, issuers must maintain:

  • Capital adequacy: Meet minimum capital at all times; notify BaFin of any breaches within 24 hours.
  • Reserve management: Segregate and safeguard reserve assets, with regular audits by an independent auditor.
  • AML/CFT programs: Implement strong KYC, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting. Annual AML audits are required.
  • Reporting: Submit periodic reports to BaFin on financials, transactions, and audits (e.g., quarterly for ARTs, semi-annually for EMTs).
  • White paper updates: Keep the white paper current and notify BaFin of material changes within 30 days.

Common Mistakes in the Application Process

Applicants often make these errors:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing business plans, governance policies, or risk assessments. BaFin expects a detailed risk management framework covering operational, credit, and market risks.
  • Underestimating capital: Failing to meet the minimum capital requirement or not demonstrating that capital is unencumbered.
  • Poor white paper quality: ARTs require a white paper that meets MiCA's content requirements, including a clear description of the reserve mechanism and redemption rights. Many applications are rejected due to insufficient detail.
  • Weak AML controls: Inadequate KYC procedures or lack of transaction monitoring tools. BaFin may request proof of a tested AML system.
  • Unqualified management: Key personnel must have relevant experience and be fit and proper. Background checks are conducted.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between an e-money token (EMT) and an asset-referenced token (ART) under MiCA?

EMTs reference a single fiat currency and are treated similarly to e-money, requiring compliance with e-money directives. ARTs reference a basket of assets or a single commodity and have specific white paper and reserve requirements. The capital requirement for both is EUR 350,000, but ARTs have a minimum of EUR 250,000 if the 2% calculation is lower.

Can I apply for a German stablecoin license if my company is based outside the EU?

Yes, but you must establish a legal entity in Germany or another EU member state. The entity must have a physical office in Germany and be managed locally. Non-EU firms often set up a German subsidiary to apply. Consulting24 assists with company setup and registration.

How long does the BaFin application process take?

The typical timeline is 6 to 12 months, depending on the complexity of the application and BaFin's workload. Delays can occur if additional information is requested. Pre-application preparation can take 1-3 months. Consulting24 helps streamline the process to minimize delays.

What are the ongoing compliance costs after obtaining the license?

Ongoing costs include annual AML audits (EUR 5,000-15,000), compliance officer salary (EUR 80,000-120,000 per year), reporting software (EUR 10,000-30,000 per year), and legal fees for updates (EUR 10,000-20,000 per year). Total annual costs typically range from EUR 50,000 to 150,000.

Is a German stablecoin license recognized in other EU countries?

Yes, under MiCA, a CASP license from BaFin allows you to passport services across all EU member states without additional licenses. You must notify BaFin of your intention to operate in other countries and comply with local marketing rules.

What happens if I fail to meet the capital requirement after licensing?

You must notify BaFin immediately. Failure to maintain capital can result in penalties, suspension, or revocation of the license. BaFin may require a capital restoration plan. Consulting24 provides ongoing capital monitoring services.

Can I issue a stablecoin that pays interest to holders?

Yes, but it may be classified as a security or deposit, triggering additional regulations under German banking law. Interest-bearing stablecoins may require a credit institution license. Consulting24 advises on the legal implications.

Do I need a separate license for custody of stablecoins?

If your license includes custody services, it covers custody of stablecoins. If not, you need a separate CASP endorsement for custody. Many issuers include custody in their application to offer a full suite of services.

What are the white paper requirements for an ART?

The white paper must include details on the issuer, the token's features, the reserve mechanism, redemption rights, and risk factors. It must be approved by BaFin before issuance. The white paper must be updated if there are material changes.
