What is a Crypto Gambling License in Jersey?

A crypto gambling license in Jersey is a regulatory authorisation that allows operators to offer online gambling services using cryptocurrencies. The license is issued by the Jersey Gambling Commission (JGC), which operates under the Gambling (Jersey) Law 2012. The JGC is responsible for licensing, regulating, and supervising all gambling activities in Jersey, including those involving digital assets.

The license covers various gambling verticals such as casino games, sports betting, poker, and lotteries, provided they are conducted using cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin, Ethereum, or stablecoins. Operators must also comply with the Money Laundering (Jersey) Order 2008 and the Proceeds of Crime (Jersey) Law 1999, which require strong AML/KYC procedures.

Jersey's regime is known for its stability and alignment with international standards, making it a preferred choice for serious operators. The JGC conducts thorough due diligence on applicants, including background checks on directors and beneficial owners, to ensure the integrity of the gambling market.

Who Needs a Jersey Crypto Gambling License?

Any business that intends to offer online gambling services to customers using cryptocurrencies and targeting the Jersey market or operating from Jersey must obtain a license from the JGC. This includes:

  • Online casinos accepting crypto deposits and withdrawals
  • Sports betting platforms that use digital currencies
  • Poker rooms and bingo sites with crypto integration
  • Lottery and sweepstakes operators using blockchain technology
  • White-label operators and platform providers servicing crypto gambling sites

Even if your business is based outside Jersey, if you target Jersey residents or use Jersey as your operational hub, you need a license. The JGC also expects operators to have a physical presence in Jersey, typically a registered office and at least one director resident in the island. The regulator for crypto gambling in Jersey is the Jersey Gambling Commission (JGC). The JGC issues two main types of licenses:

The JGC also regulates ancillary services like software providers and game developers. The licensing process involves a detailed application, background checks, and ongoing compliance monitoring. The JGC is known for its rigorous approach, which enhances the credibility of licensed operators.

Capital Requirement for Jersey Crypto Gambling License

Jersey does not have a statutory minimum capital requirement for gambling licenses, but the JGC expects operators to have sufficient financial resources to cover liabilities and operational costs. Typically, applicants must demonstrate a minimum of EUR 100,000 in available funds, though this can vary based on the scale of operations. The JGC assesses financial stability through audited accounts, bank references, and business plans.

For crypto gambling, the JGC may require additional capital to cover potential volatility in cryptocurrency values. Operators must also maintain a separate client account for player funds, which must be protected from business creditors. Tax Treatment for Jersey Crypto Gambling Operators

Jersey offers a favourable tax regime for gambling operators. The standard corporate tax rate is 0% for most businesses, including gambling companies, provided they are not classified as financial services entities. There is no gambling tax or turnover tax on gross gaming revenue (GGR). However, operators must pay an annual license fee to the JGC, which is not a tax but a regulatory charge.

Value Added Tax (VAT) is not applicable in Jersey, as the island is outside the EU VAT area. This can result in significant savings for operators. Personal income tax for directors and employees is capped at 20%. Consulting24 can provide detailed tax planning advice as part of our advisory services.

Allowed Activities Under a Jersey Crypto Gambling License

A Jersey crypto gambling license permits a wide range of activities, including:

  • Online casino games (slots, table games, live dealer)
  • Sports betting (pre-match and in-play)
  • Poker rooms and tournaments
  • Bingo and lottery-style games
  • eSports betting
  • Fantasy sports
  • Providing gambling software and platforms to other operators

All activities must comply with Jersey's AML/CFT regulations and the JGC's technical standards. Operators must ensure that their games are fair and random, and that player funds are segregated and protected. The license does not cover activities that are explicitly prohibited, such as unlicensed betting exchanges or games with no skill element.

Step-by-Step Process to Obtain a Jersey Crypto Gambling License

The application process for a Jersey crypto gambling license involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application consultation: Engage with the JGC to discuss your business model and licensing requirements. Consulting24 can facilitate this initial step.
  2. Submit application: Complete the formal application form, providing detailed information about your company, directors, beneficial owners, and operational plans.

  4. Technical review: Your gambling platform must undergo a technical audit to ensure compliance with JGC standards for fairness, security, and data protection.
  5. License approval: Once all checks are passed, the JGC issues the license, subject to payment of the annual fee.
  6. Ongoing compliance: Licensed operators must submit regular reports, undergo periodic audits, and maintain AML/KYC procedures.

Compliance and Trust: What the JGC Expects

The JGC imposes strict compliance requirements to ensure the integrity of gambling operations. Key expectations include:

  • AML/KYC procedures: Operators must verify customer identity and monitor transactions for suspicious activity, in line with Jersey's AML laws.
  • Player protection: Responsible gambling tools (deposit limits, self-exclusion) must be offered, and advertising must not be misleading.
  • Data security: Personal and financial data must be protected in accordance with the Data Protection (Jersey) Law 2018.
  • Technical standards: Games must use certified random number generators (RNGs) and be tested by approved labs.
  • Reporting: Annual financial statements, compliance reports, and incident reports must be submitted to the JGC.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a Jersey gambling license and a crypto license?

Jersey does not have a separate 'crypto license'. The gambling license issued by the JGC covers all forms of online gambling, including those using cryptocurrencies. Operators must comply with AML/KYC requirements for crypto transactions.

Can I operate a crypto casino in Jersey without a license?

No. Operating any form of gambling without a license from the JGC is illegal and can result in criminal prosecution. This includes crypto casinos that target Jersey residents or are based in Jersey.

How long does it take to get a Jersey crypto gambling license?

The typical timeline is 4 to 9 months, depending on the completeness of the application and the JGC's workload. Pre-application consultation can help speed up the process.

What are the ongoing costs for a Jersey license?

Annual license fees range from EUR 20,000 to EUR 50,000, plus compliance costs such as audits, AML officer salaries, and legal fees. Exact amounts depend on the size of your operations.

Does Jersey have a minimum capital requirement?

There is no statutory minimum, but the JGC expects operators to have sufficient funds, typically around EUR 100,000 or more, to cover liabilities and operational expenses.

Is Jersey a good jurisdiction for crypto gambling startups?

Jersey is best suited for established operators with larger budgets due to the high costs and rigorous due diligence. Startups may find Panama or Lithuania more accessible.

Can I use a Jersey license to target EU markets?

No. Jersey is not an EU member, so its license does not automatically allow access to EU markets. You would need separate licensing in each EU country or use an EU jurisdiction like Malta.

What taxes do I pay in Jersey as a crypto gambling operator?

Corporate tax is 0% for most gambling companies, and there is no gambling tax. However, you must pay an annual license fee. Personal income tax for employees is capped at 20%.

Does the JGC accept cryptocurrencies for player deposits and withdrawals?

Yes, the JGC permits the use of cryptocurrencies, provided the operator implements strong AML/KYC procedures to verify the source of funds and identity of players.
