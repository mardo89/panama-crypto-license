What Are the Dubai and Singapore Crypto Licenses?

In 2026, Dubai's crypto license is issued by the Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA) under the Dubai Multi Commodities Centre (DMCC) or the Dubai Financial Services Authority (DFSA) for the Dubai International Financial Centre (DIFC). The most common license for crypto exchanges is the VASP (Virtual Asset Service Provider) license from VARA. Singapore's crypto license is the Major Payment Institution (MPI) license or Standard Payment Institution (SPI) license under the Payment Services Act (PSA), regulated by the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS). Both regimes require compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) obligations, but their scope and costs differ. For example, VARA's VASP license covers a wider range of virtual asset activities, while MAS's MPI focuses on digital payment token services. Understanding these nuances is critical before applying.

Who Needs a Crypto License in Dubai or Singapore?

Any business that provides virtual asset services - including exchange, custody, transfer, or advisory - needs a license. In Dubai, VARA regulates all virtual asset activities within or from Dubai (excluding DIFC, which is under DFSA). In Singapore, MAS regulates payment services involving digital payment tokens (DPTs). Typical applicants include:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralized and decentralized)
  • OTC trading desks
  • Custodial wallet providers
  • Token issuers and brokers
  • DeFi platforms (if offering exchange or custody)

If you plan to serve retail or institutional clients in either region, you must hold the appropriate license. License Type & Regulator

Dubai: VARA (Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority)

The primary license for crypto businesses in Dubai (mainland and free zones except DIFC) is the VASP license. VARA classifies activities into four categories: Advisory, Broker-Dealer, Exchange, and Lending & Borrowing. Each category has specific requirements. For a full exchange license, you typically need to apply for the Exchange category. VARA also introduced a DeFi-specific framework in 2024, which continues to evolve.

Singapore: MAS (Monetary Authority of Singapore)

MAS issues two license types under the PSA: Standard Payment Institution (SPI) for lower transaction volumes (up to SGD 3 million per month) and Major Payment Institution (MPI) for higher volumes. Crypto exchanges usually require the MPI license, which allows unlimited transaction value but imposes higher compliance costs. MAS also regulates custody under a separate Trust Business license, so if your business includes custody, you may need both licenses.

Both regulators require a local presence, fit-and-proper assessments for directors, and strong AML/CFT policies. Consulting24 can help you prepare the necessary documentation for these assessments.

Capital Requirements

Dubai: VARA requires a minimum capital of AED 500,000 (approx. EUR 125,000) for a VASP license, but higher amounts may be needed for exchange or custody activities (up to AED 2,000,000). The capital must be maintained as paid-up share capital and cannot be withdrawn without approval. For example, a custody service may require AED 2 million, while an advisory service only AED 500,000.

Singapore: MAS requires a minimum capital of SGD 250,000 (approx. EUR 160,000) for an MPI license. For SPI, the requirement is lower at SGD 100,000. Capital must be held in the form of share capital or retained earnings. MAS also requires that the capital be proportionate to the risk profile of the business, so larger operations may need more.

Both jurisdictions require the capital to be unencumbered and available for operational risks. Consulting24 can help structure your capital allocation to meet these requirements efficiently, for instance by using a combination of share capital and a director's loan.

Tax Treatment

Dubai: Zero corporate tax for most free zone companies (including DMCC) until 2026; a 9% corporate tax applies to mainland companies exceeding AED 375,000 profit, but free zones often offer 0% for 50 years. No capital gains tax, no withholding tax. VAT at 5% applies to taxable supplies. Crypto transactions may be VAT-exempt in certain cases, such as when they are considered financial services.

Singapore: Corporate tax rate of 17% on chargeable income, with tax exemptions for new start-ups (up to SGD 200,000 exempt for first 3 years). Capital gains are not taxed. GST (VAT) at 9% applies to taxable supplies, but digital payment tokens may be exempt from GST under the e-services rules. Singapore also has extensive double tax treaties, which can reduce withholding taxes on cross-border payments.

Dubai offers a more tax-friendly environment overall, but Singapore provides a stable, low-tax regime with double tax treaties worldwide. For a business with global clients, Singapore's treaty network may be more valuable.

Allowed Activities Under Each License

Dubai VARA VASP: Allows virtual asset exchange, custody, transfer, advisory, and lending/borrowing within the licensed categories. You can operate a crypto exchange, OTC desk, wallet service, or token issuance platform. DeFi activities are allowed subject to VARA's DeFi framework. For example, a decentralized exchange (DEX) can operate under the Exchange category with additional DeFi-specific conditions.

Singapore MAS MPI: Allows digital payment token services (exchange, transfer, merchant acquisition), but does not cover custody as a standalone activity (custody is regulated under a separate Trust Business license). Token issuance (ICO) is not directly regulated under PSA but may fall under securities laws if tokens are deemed capital markets products. Therefore, a business offering both exchange and custody may need two licenses in Singapore.

If your business requires custody, Dubai's VARA license is more straightforward. If you focus on payment services, Singapore's MPI is suitable.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Dubai VARA VASP

  1. Incorporate a legal entity in a VARA-approved free zone (e.g., DMCC) or mainland with a local partner.
  2. Prepare business plan, AML/CFT policies, risk assessment, and governance documents.
  3. Submit initial application to VARA with supporting documents and fee.
  4. VARA reviews and may request additional information (2-4 months).
  5. If approved in principle, set up physical office, hire compliance officer, and implement systems.
  6. Final approval and license issuance after inspection (total 6-12 months).

Singapore MAS MPI

  1. Incorporate a private limited company in Singapore.
  2. Prepare application pack: business model, compliance manual, AML/CFT policies, financial projections.
  3. Submit application via MAS's online portal with fee.
  4. MAS assesses fit-and-proper of directors and shareholders (6-9 months).
  5. If approved, obtain license and comply with ongoing reporting.

Banking & Payment Integration

Dubai: Crypto-friendly banks are limited but growing. Emirates NBD, RAKBANK, and some digital banks offer accounts for VARA-licensed entities. Payment gateways like Checkout.com and Stripe are available. However, many banks still require a local physical presence and may take months to open accounts. For example, Emirates NBD may require a minimum deposit of AED 100,000 and a personal meeting.

Singapore: MAS-licensed entities have better access to banking. DBS, OCBC, and UOB offer accounts to crypto firms, though due diligence is rigorous. Payment services like PayPal and local e-wallets are integrated easily. Singapore's banking system is generally more open to fintech and crypto, with some banks offering dedicated fintech units.

Both jurisdictions require a local bank account to operate. Consulting24 can introduce you to banking partners in each region. For instance, we have relationships with banks that understand the crypto space and can expedite account opening.

Benefits of Each Jurisdiction

Dubai: Zero corporate tax, no capital gains tax, strategic location between East and West, fast-growing crypto ecosystem with VARA's progressive regulation, and access to the UAE's wealthy investor base. Dubai also offers a 50-year tax holiday in free zones. The cost of living and office space is lower than Singapore, making it attractive for startups.

Singapore: Stable regulatory environment, strong rule of law, excellent banking infrastructure, double tax treaties with over 80 countries, and a skilled workforce. Singapore is a global financial hub with a reputation for compliance and innovation. The government actively supports fintech through grants and sandbox programs.

Your choice depends on whether you prioritize tax efficiency (Dubai) or regulatory stability and banking access (Singapore). For a high-volume exchange with institutional clients, Singapore may be better; for a startup focused on tax optimization, Dubai is ideal.

Compliance & Trust: Ongoing Obligations

Both VARA and MAS require ongoing compliance: periodic reporting, AML/CFT audits, transaction monitoring, and record-keeping. Dubai: VARA requires quarterly and annual reports, plus a compliance officer resident in the UAE. Singapore: MAS requires annual audited financial statements, AML/CFT audit, and notification of any changes in key personnel. Both regulators expect real-time transaction monitoring for suspicious activities.

Non-compliance can result in fines, suspension, or revocation of the license. For example, VARA may impose fines of up to AED 5 million for serious breaches. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Underestimating the timeline: Both processes take 6-12 months; plan ahead. Many applicants assume 3 months and then face delays.
  • Insufficient capital: Ensure you meet the minimum capital and can prove source of funds. VARA and MAS both scrutinize the source of capital.
  • Poor AML/CFT policies: Both regulators scrutinize compliance manuals; generic templates are rejected. You need a tailored policy for your business model.
  • Ignoring local presence requirements: You need a physical office and local staff (compliance officer, director). Virtual offices are not accepted.
  • Choosing the wrong license category: VARA and MAS have specific activity scopes; applying for the wrong one wastes time and money. For example, a custody business in Singapore needs a trust license, not just MPI.

Alternatives: Panama and Other Jurisdictions

If Dubai or Singapore are not the right fit, consider Panama. Panama offers a crypto license at a flat EUR 6,000, with no minimum capital, no corporate tax on offshore income, and a faster timeline (2-3 months). Panama's regulator is the Superintendencia de Bancos de Panama (SBP) for certain activities, but most crypto businesses operate under a general business license with AML registration. Panama is ideal for startups and smaller exchanges that want a quick, cost-effective setup.

Other alternatives include Estonia (EU MiCA compliant, capital from EUR 50,000), Lithuania (EU MiCA, capital from EUR 50,000), and Czech Republic (non-EU but low cost). Frequently asked questions

What is the main difference between Dubai VARA and Singapore MAS crypto licenses?

Dubai VARA covers a wider range of virtual asset activities, including custody and DeFi, while Singapore MAS focuses on digital payment token services. Dubai offers zero corporate tax, while Singapore has a 17% rate but better banking access. Both require 6-12 months for approval.

Which license is cheaper: Dubai or Singapore?

Dubai has lower annual fees (AED 40,000-150,000 vs SGD 10,000-100,000) but higher capital requirements (AED 500,000-2,000,000 vs SGD 250,000). Overall, Dubai may be cheaper for smaller operations, but Singapore offers more banking options.

Can I operate a crypto exchange in Dubai without a license?

No. All virtual asset activities in Dubai require a VARA license. Operating without one can lead to fines, closure, and criminal liability. VARA actively monitors and enforces compliance.

Does Singapore MAS require a physical office?

Yes. MAS requires a registered address and a physical office in Singapore. You also need at least one director who is a Singapore resident. Virtual offices are not accepted.

What is the timeline for a Dubai VASP license?

Typically 6-12 months from application to issuance. The process includes initial review (2-4 months), in-principle approval, office setup, and final inspection. Delays can occur if documents are incomplete.

Can I use the Dubai license to serve clients in the EU?

No. A Dubai license does not allow you to serve EU clients under MiCA. You would need a separate EU license, such as in Lithuania or Estonia, which Consulting24 can help with.

What are the ongoing compliance costs for Singapore MPI?

Annual compliance costs include audit fees (SGD 10,000-30,000), AML/CFT audit (SGD 5,000-15,000), compliance officer salary (SGD 60,000-120,000), and regulatory filing fees. Total can exceed SGD 100,000 per year.

Is Panama a good alternative to Dubai or Singapore?

Yes, for startups. Panama offers a flat EUR 6,000 license, no minimum capital, and 2-3 month timeline. However, it lacks the regulatory prestige and banking access of Dubai or Singapore. It is ideal for early-stage projects.
