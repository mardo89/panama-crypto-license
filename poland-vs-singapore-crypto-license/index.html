Overview: Poland vs Singapore Crypto License

Poland and Singapore represent two distinct approaches to crypto regulation. Poland, as an EU member state, has fully implemented MiCA, which harmonizes rules across the European Union. The Polish Financial Supervision Authority (KNF) oversees crypto-asset service providers (CASPs). Singapore, under the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS), regulates digital payment token (DPT) services via the Payment Services Act (PSA). Both regimes require licensing, but the capital, tax, and compliance burdens differ markedly.

Poland offers a gateway to the EU single market, with a single license valid in all 27 member states. Singapore provides access to Asia's financial hub, with a strong reputation for regulatory clarity. Your choice depends on your target market, budget, and risk appetite. This comparison covers all key factors: license type, costs, capital, tax, timeline, allowed activities, banking access, and compliance obligations.

Who Needs a Crypto License in Poland or Singapore?

Any business providing crypto-asset services in or from these jurisdictions must obtain the appropriate license. In Poland, this includes exchanges, wallet providers, custodians, and brokers dealing with crypto-assets. In Singapore, the scope covers digital payment token (DPT) services, including exchange, transfer, and custody.

Typical clients include:

  • Crypto exchanges (centralized and decentralized)
  • OTC trading desks
  • Wallet providers
  • Custodians and staking services
  • Payment processors using crypto
  • DeFi platforms (if offering custodial services)

License Type & Regulator

Poland: Crypto-Asset Service Provider (CASP) License

Regulated by the Polish Financial Supervision Authority (KNF) under MiCA. The license is a single EU passport for all member states. It covers a range of services: custody, exchange, execution, placement, transfer, and advisory. The KNF requires a detailed application including AML policies, business plan, and governance structure.

Singapore: Major Payment Institution (MPI) License for DPT Services

Regulated by the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) under the Payment Services Act. The MPI license allows multiple payment services, including DPT services. A smaller DPT-specific license (Standard Payment Institution) exists but is rarely used for crypto due to lower transaction limits. MAS imposes strict AML/CFT requirements, including a resident director and compliance officer.

Both regulators require strong AML/CFT policies, governance, and operational resilience. Consulting24 can guide you through the application process for both licenses.

Capital Requirement

Poland (MiCA): Minimum capital tiers by service class: EUR 50,000 for simple custody; EUR 125,000 for exchange; EUR 150,000 for full-service CASPs. These are locked up as share capital and must be maintained throughout the license period.

Singapore (MPI): No fixed minimum capital for DPT services, but MAS requires a base capital of SGD 250,000 for MPI license. Additionally, MAS may impose higher capital based on risk assessment. Typically, companies hold SGD 500,000 - 1,000,000 in capital. This must be in liquid assets.

Poland's requirement is lower for basic services, making it more accessible for startups. Singapore's higher capital reflects its status as a major financial center and the higher operational costs. For comparison, Lithuania requires EUR 125,000 for exchange services.

Tax Treatment

Poland: Corporate income tax (CIT) at 19% (9% for small taxpayers). Crypto transactions are subject to CIT on profits. VAT is generally not applicable to crypto-to-fiat exchanges. However, mining and staking rewards may be taxable. Poland has no specific crypto tax regime; general tax rules apply. Tax on capital gains is treated as income.

Singapore: Corporate tax at 17%, with exemptions for new companies (first SGD 100,000 profit exempt). No capital gains tax. Crypto transactions are treated as barter trade or investment; gains may be taxable if trading is frequent. MAS does not levy GST on DPT transactions under the PSA. Singapore's tax regime is more favorable for long-term holders.

Both jurisdictions offer competitive tax rates, but Singapore's lack of capital gains tax is a distinct advantage for investors. However, Poland's lower corporate tax for small firms (9%) can be attractive for startups.

Allowed Activities

Poland (CASP): All MiCA-defined services: custody, exchange (crypto-to-fiat and crypto-to-crypto), execution of orders, placement, transfer, advisory, and portfolio management. Also allowed: staking, lending, and DeFi services if they involve custody. The scope is broad and includes any service involving crypto-assets.

Singapore (MPI): DPT services: exchange, transfer, and custody. Also allowed: cross-border money transfer, merchant acquisition, and e-money issuance (if combined). Staking and lending are not explicitly regulated but may fall under DPT services if custodial. The regime focuses on payment services; DeFi and lending are less clear.

Poland's scope is broader due to MiCA's comprehensive list. Singapore's regime is narrower but offers clarity for payment-related services. For a more flexible regulatory environment, see Dubai.

Step-by-Step Process for Poland vs Singapore

Poland CASP License Process

  1. Company incorporation in Poland (typically a limited liability company).
  2. Prepare AML/KYC policies, risk assessment, and business plan.
  3. Appoint a local AML officer and board members.
  4. Submit application to KNF with all documents.
  5. KNF reviews (3-6 months) and may request clarifications.
  6. Obtain license and register with the register of CASPs.

Singapore MPI License Process

  1. Incorporate a Singapore company (private limited).
  2. Appoint local directors, compliance officer, and AML officer.
  3. Prepare compliance manual, risk assessment, and audit plan.
  4. Submit application to MAS with fee.
  5. MAS review (6-12 months) including interviews and on-site visits.
  6. Grant of MPI license with conditions.

Banking & Payment Services

Poland: Polish banks are cautious but increasingly open to licensed CASPs. Several banks offer accounts to crypto firms, especially after MiCA. Payment services can be integrated via local PSPs like PayU or Blue Media. However, some banks still refuse high-risk clients, so it is advisable to approach multiple banks. Singapore: Major banks like DBS, OCBC, and UOB have dedicated crypto-friendly units for licensed MPI holders. Banking is easier in Singapore due to MAS's clear regulatory stance. Payment services are strong, with fast integration via PayNow and FAST. Singapore also has a thriving fintech ecosystem with many payment gateways.

Singapore generally offers better banking access for crypto firms, though Poland is improving. For banking challenges, see our blog on crypto banking.

Compliance & Trust Considerations

Both Poland and Singapore require strong AML/CFT compliance. Poland follows the EU's 5th Anti-Money Laundering Directive (5AMLD) and MiCA. Singapore's PSA mandates strict transaction monitoring and suspicious reporting. Non-compliance can result in fines or license revocation.

Key compliance areas:

  • Customer due diligence (CDD) and KYC.
  • Transaction monitoring and screening.
  • Travel rule compliance (for transfers over EUR 1,000 in EU; Singapore has its own travel rule).
  • Annual audits and regulatory reporting.
  • Data protection (GDPR in Poland, PDPA in Singapore).

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Underestimating capital: Singapore's capital may be higher than expected. Plan accordingly and ensure funds are available.
  • Ignoring tax differences: Poland taxes crypto profits as income; Singapore does not tax capital gains. Structure your operations to minimize tax.
  • Poor AML preparation: Both regulators reject incomplete applications. Invest in a strong compliance manual and hire experienced AML officers.
  • Choosing based on cost only: Consider long-term operational costs and market access. Singapore's higher costs may be offset by better banking and tax benefits.
  • Not engaging local advisors: Local expertise is critical. Consulting24 provides coordinated support.
  • Delaying banking applications: Start banking discussions early to avoid delays after license approval.

Frequently asked questions

What is the main difference between Poland and Singapore crypto licenses?

Poland offers an EU-wide passport under MiCA with lower capital requirements (EUR 50,000 for basic services) and faster timeline (3-6 months). Singapore provides access to Asia with no capital gains tax but higher capital (SGD 250,000+ base) and longer timeline (6-12 months). Your choice depends on target market and budget.

Which license is cheaper: Poland or Singapore?

Poland is cheaper overall: first-year costs around EUR 20,000-50,000 vs Singapore's SGD 50,000-100,000. Annual compliance is also lower in Poland. However, Singapore offers tax advantages (no capital gains tax) that may offset costs for high-profit businesses.

Can I serve EU customers with a Singapore license?

No, a Singapore license does not cover EU customers. To serve EU clients, you need a license in an EU member state like Poland, which provides passporting rights under MiCA. Singapore is for Asia-Pacific markets.

What are the capital requirements for a Poland crypto license?

Under MiCA, Poland requires minimum capital of EUR 50,000 for custody services, EUR 125,000 for exchange, and EUR 150,000 for full-service CASPs. These must be maintained as share capital.

Does Singapore tax crypto gains?

Singapore does not have capital gains tax. However, if crypto trading is frequent and constitutes a business, gains may be taxed as income. Long-term holdings are generally not taxed. Consult a tax advisor for your specific situation.

How long does it take to get a Poland crypto license?

The Poland CASP license typically takes 3-6 months from application to approval, assuming complete documentation. The KNF may request additional information, which can extend the timeline. Consulting24 helps streamline the process.

Can I get a crypto license in Poland without a local office?

Yes, you can incorporate a Polish company without a physical office, but you need a registered address and a local AML officer. Virtual offices are acceptable. Consulting24 can assist with setup.

What activities are allowed under a Singapore MPI license?

The MPI license allows digital payment token services (exchange, transfer, custody), as well as cross-border money transfer, merchant acquisition, and e-money issuance. Staking and lending are not explicitly covered but may be allowed if custodial.

Is Poland's MiCA license valid in all EU countries?

Yes, the Poland CASP license under MiCA allows passporting to all 27 EU member states without additional licensing. You can serve clients across the EU from a single license.

What are the ongoing compliance costs for Poland vs Singapore?

Poland's annual compliance costs are around EUR 10,000-20,000, including AML officer, audits, and reporting. Singapore's are higher at SGD 20,000-40,000 due to stricter requirements and higher professional fees.
