What is the Crypto Token Issuance License Kazakhstan?

The crypto token issuance license Kazakhstan is a regulatory authorization granted by AFSA under the AIFC framework. It permits licensed entities to issue digital tokens to investors, conduct token sales, and manage token-related activities. The license is part of the AIFC's Digital Asset Platform (DAP) regime, which covers token issuance, exchange, and custody. This regime was established to attract fintech and blockchain companies by providing a clear legal environment.

Key features include:

  • Issuance of security tokens, utility tokens, and asset-referenced tokens.
  • Compliance with AIFC's Digital Asset Activities Regulations (DAAR).
  • Requirement for a minimum capital of USD 100,000 (or equivalent in KZT) for token issuance activities.
  • Annual reporting and AML/CFT obligations, including submission of suspicious transaction reports.

The license is ideal for projects seeking a reputable, regulated environment for token launches. Kazakhstan offers a 0% corporate income tax on activities conducted within the AIFC, making it a cost-effective choice compared to other jurisdictions. For example, a company issuing utility tokens for a decentralized application can benefit from the tax exemption while maintaining regulatory compliance.

Who Needs This License?

Any entity planning to issue digital tokens to the public or institutional investors in Kazakhstan needs the crypto token issuance license. This includes:

  • Startups conducting a token generation event (TGE) or initial coin offering (ICO).
  • Companies launching security tokens representing equity, debt, or real-world assets like real estate.
  • Platforms issuing utility tokens for access to products or services, such as a blockchain-based gaming platform.
  • Projects conducting an initial exchange offering (IEO) on a regulated exchange within the AIFC.

If you are a foreign entity, you can set up a company in the AIFC and apply for the license. Consulting24 assists with company setup and license application. The license is not required for mere token holdings or peer-to-peer transfers, but any issuance activity triggers the requirement. For example, a decentralized finance (DeFi) project issuing governance tokens would need this license if targeting Kazakh investors.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Astana Financial Services Authority (AFSA), operating under the Astana International Financial Centre (AIFC). The license type is a Digital Asset Platform (DAP) License for token issuance. AFSA categorizes digital asset activities into:

  • Operating a digital asset exchange (trading platform).
  • Providing digital asset custody services.
  • Issuing digital tokens (our focus).
  • Providing digital asset investment services.

For token issuance, the specific license is the Digital Asset Issuance License. It allows the holder to create and distribute tokens to investors, subject to a prospectus or exemption requirements. AFSA also oversees AML/CFT compliance and ongoing supervision. The AIFC applies English common law, which provides legal certainty for international investors. Capital Requirement

The minimum capital requirement for a crypto token issuance license in Kazakhstan is USD 100,000 (or equivalent in local currency). This must be paid up in cash and held in a bank account in the AIFC. The capital serves as a buffer for operational risks and must be maintained throughout the license period. For example, if the company incurs losses, it must top up the capital to remain compliant.

Additional capital may be required if the license also covers other activities (e.g., custody or exchange). The capital can be used for operational expenses but must not fall below the minimum. AFSA may require higher capital based on the scale of issuance. Consulting24 advises on structuring capital to meet regulatory expectations, including whether to use share capital or a combination of capital and reserves.

Tax Treatment

One of the biggest advantages of the AIFC is its tax regime. Entities licensed within the AIFC enjoy:

  • 0% corporate income tax on income derived from AIFC-permitted activities.
  • 0% withholding tax on dividends and interest paid to non-residents.
  • 0% VAT on most financial services.
  • Exemption from property tax and land tax for AIFC-registered companies.

This makes Kazakhstan extremely attractive for token issuers. However, tax treatment depends on the nature of the token (security vs. utility) and the issuer's residency. For instance, security tokens may be treated as securities for tax purposes in the investor's home country. Consulting24 recommends consulting a tax advisor for your specific situation. Allowed Activities

With the crypto token issuance license Kazakhstan, you can:

  • Issue digital tokens, including security tokens, utility tokens, and asset-referenced tokens.
  • Conduct token sales (public or private) to investors, subject to prospectus requirements or exemptions.
  • List tokens on AIFC-registered exchanges (subject to exchange rules).
  • Provide token-related services such as token management and buyback programs.
  • Offer staking or yield-generating products if disclosed and approved by AFSA.

Activities not allowed without separate licenses include operating an exchange, custody of third-party assets, or providing investment advice. The license is strictly for issuance. If you need exchange services, consider an exchange license separately. For example, a project issuing a utility token for a decentralized storage network can use this license, but if it also wants to run a trading platform, it needs a separate DAP exchange license.

Compliance and Trust

Maintaining the license requires ongoing compliance with AFSA regulations. Key obligations include:

  • Annual AML/CFT training and reporting, including submission of suspicious transaction reports.
  • Audited financial statements submitted yearly within 6 months of year-end.
  • Notification of any changes in directors, shareholders, or business activities within 14 days.
  • Maintaining minimum capital at all times, with quarterly capital adequacy reports.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

When applying for the crypto token issuance license Kazakhstan, avoid these pitfalls:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing business plan or token whitepaper can delay approval. Ensure the whitepaper includes clear tokenomics and use of funds.
  • Underestimating capital: Ensure USD 100,000 is readily available and maintained. Some applicants use loans, which AFSA may scrutinize.
  • Ignoring fit-and-proper checks: Directors must have clean records and relevant experience. A past bankruptcy can disqualify.
  • Not engaging local advisors: AFSA expects familiarity with AIFC rules; Consulting24 bridges that gap.
  • Overlooking tax planning: Even with 0% tax, proper structuring is key to avoid future liabilities, especially for foreign investors.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a crypto token issuance license in Kazakhstan?

The minimum capital is USD 100,000 (or equivalent in KZT). This must be paid up in cash and held in an AIFC bank account. It must be maintained throughout the license period. Additional capital may be required if the license covers other activities.

How long does it take to get the license?

The typical timeline is 3 to 6 months from application submission to approval. Delays can occur if documentation is incomplete or if AFSA requires additional information. Consulting24 helps streamline the process by preparing all required documents in advance.

What taxes apply to token issuers in Kazakhstan?

Licensed entities within the AIFC enjoy 0% corporate income tax, 0% withholding tax on dividends and interest, and 0% VAT on financial services. This tax exemption applies only to activities permitted under the AIFC regime. Consult a tax advisor for your specific situation.

Can a foreign company apply for the license?

Yes, but you must first incorporate a company in the AIFC. The AIFC allows 100% foreign ownership. Consulting24 assists with company setup and the license application. The directors and shareholders can be non-residents.

What types of tokens can I issue under this license?

You can issue security tokens, utility tokens, and asset-referenced tokens. The license covers token generation events, initial coin offerings, and initial exchange offerings. However, the token must comply with AIFC's Digital Asset Activities Regulations.

Is the license recognized internationally?

The license is issued by AFSA under the AIFC, which follows international standards like FATF recommendations. It is recognized by many global exchanges and investors, but you may need additional registrations in other countries where you offer tokens.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

You must submit annual audited financial statements, conduct AML/CFT training, report suspicious transactions, and notify AFSA of any changes in directors or business activities. Minimum capital must be maintained at all times. Consulting24 offers compliance support.

How does Kazakhstan compare to Panama for token issuance?

Panama offers a flat fee of EUR 6,000 with no capital requirement and a faster timeline of 1-2 months, but lacks the tax benefits and regulatory depth of Kazakhstan. Kazakhstan provides 0% corporate tax and a common law environment, but costs USD 28,000-60,000 total.

Can I also operate an exchange with this license?

No, the token issuance license only covers issuance activities. To operate an exchange, you need a separate Digital Asset Exchange License from AFSA. Consulting24 can advise on obtaining multiple licenses if needed.

What happens if I fail to maintain the minimum capital?

AFSA may impose fines, suspend the license, or revoke it. You must top up the capital immediately if it falls below USD 100,000. Regular capital adequacy reporting helps monitor compliance.
