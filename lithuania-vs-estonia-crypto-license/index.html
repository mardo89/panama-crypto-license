What Is a Crypto License in Lithuania and Estonia?

Both Lithuania and Estonia offer regulated status for crypto asset service providers (CASPs), but the legal form differs. In Lithuania, the license is a CASP authorization under the Law on Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) implementation, effective fully from 2025. In Estonia, the license is a CASP authorization under the Estonian Money Laundering and Terrorist Financing Prevention Act, aligned with MiCA since 2025. Both are required for offering exchange, wallet, and other crypto services.

Key distinctions: Lithuania issues a single authorization for all services, while Estonia historically had separate licenses for exchange and wallet services, but now under MiCA it is a single CASP license. Both are recognized across the EU.

Under MiCA, a CASP license allows you to passport services across the European Economic Area without additional licensing. This is a major advantage for businesses targeting multiple EU markets. The license also signals to customers and partners that you operate under a strong regulatory framework.

Who Needs a Crypto License in Lithuania or Estonia?

Any business that provides the following services in or from Lithuania or Estonia must hold a CASP license:

  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets
  • Custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients
  • Transfer services of crypto assets
  • Placing of crypto assets (underwriting or placing without firm commitment)
  • Reception and transmission of orders for crypto assets
  • Advice on crypto assets
  • Portfolio management of crypto assets

If your business targets EU clients, operating from either jurisdiction provides passporting rights across the European Economic Area. Even if you are based outside the EU, you can establish a subsidiary in Lithuania or Estonia to serve EU customers. The license is mandatory for any entity that offers these services to EU residents, regardless of where the company is registered.

For example, a crypto exchange based in Singapore that wants to onboard EU users must obtain a CASP license in an EU member state. Lithuania and Estonia are popular choices due to their efficient processes and supportive regulatory environments.

License Type and Regulator

Lithuania: The regulator is the Bank of Lithuania (Lietuvos bankas). The license is a CASP authorization under MiCA. The Bank of Lithuania is known for its thorough application review and ongoing supervision. It has a reputation for being strict but fair, with clear guidelines.

Estonia: The regulator is the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) of Estonia (Rahapesu Andmebüroo). The license is a CASP authorization under MiCA. Estonia was an early adopter of crypto regulation, and the FIU has extensive experience. The FIU is known for a more streamlined process, especially for companies with strong compliance frameworks.

Both regulators are competent and respected. The choice often comes down to speed, cost, and capital requirements. However, the Bank of Lithuania has a slightly more detailed application process, which can lead to longer timelines but also provides more regulatory certainty.

Capital Requirements

Under MiCA, both Lithuania and Estonia apply the same capital tiers based on the services offered. The minimum capital is:

  • EUR 50,000 for exchange services (crypto-to-fiat, crypto-to-crypto) and wallet services
  • EUR 125,000 for custody services
  • EUR 150,000 for full services including trading, advice, portfolio management, etc.

Capital must be held in a bank account in the respective country or an EU bank. Some banks may require higher deposits. Consulting24 assists in meeting these requirements. The capital can be used for operational expenses once the license is granted, but it must always be maintained at the required level. For example, if you start with a tier 1 license and later add custody services, you must top up the capital to EUR 125,000.

It is important to plan your capital allocation carefully. Some businesses choose to start with a lower tier and expand later, which can reduce upfront costs.

Tax Treatment

Lithuania: Corporate income tax is 15% (5% for small companies with fewer than 10 employees and turnover below EUR 300,000). Crypto-to-crypto exchanges are generally not taxable until converted to fiat. VAT is not applicable on crypto transactions. Withholding tax on dividends is 15% (reduced under treaties).

Estonia: Corporate income tax is 0% on retained and reinvested profits; tax is only due upon distribution (20% on gross dividends). This is a major advantage for growing companies. Crypto-to-crypto is not taxable. No VAT on crypto services. Estonia's deferred tax system is often cited as more favourable for startups.

Both countries have tax treaties with many jurisdictions. Consult a tax advisor for your specific situation. For example, if you plan to reinvest profits for several years, Estonia's 0% tax on retained earnings can significantly reduce your tax burden. However, if you expect to distribute dividends regularly, Lithuania's lower dividend withholding tax (15% vs 20%) may be more attractive.

Allowed Activities

Both licenses allow the full range of CASP services under MiCA, including:

  • Custody and administration of crypto assets
  • Operation of a trading platform
  • Exchange of crypto assets for fiat currency
  • Exchange of crypto assets for other crypto assets
  • Execution of orders on behalf of clients
  • Placing of crypto assets
  • Reception and transmission of orders
  • Advice on crypto assets
  • Portfolio management
  • Transfer services

No additional local restrictions beyond MiCA. Both allow passporting to other EU countries. However, Lithuania has a more detailed classification of services in its application form, which can help clarify the scope of your license. Estonia's application is more streamlined but still covers all MiCA services.

It is important to specify the exact services you intend to offer in your application, as adding services later may require a license amendment.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process for both countries is similar but with distinct timelines and documentation requirements.

Lithuania

  1. Company incorporation in Lithuania (or registration of a branch)
  2. Preparation of AML/KYC policies, risk assessment, business plan
  3. Submission of application to Bank of Lithuania with all documents
  4. Payment of state fee (EUR 1,500)
  5. Review by the regulator (typically 3-6 months)
  6. If approved, license issuance and ongoing supervision

Estonia

  1. Company incorporation in Estonia (or registration of a branch)
  2. Preparation of AML/KYC policies, risk assessment, business plan
  3. Submission of application to FIU with all documents
  4. Payment of state fee (EUR 3,000)
  5. Review by the FIU (typically 2-4 months)
  6. If approved, license issuance and ongoing supervision

Consulting24 manages the entire process, including document preparation and liaison with regulators. For both countries, you will need to provide detailed information about your beneficial owners, source of funds, and operational structure. The AML policy must be tailored to your specific business model, covering transaction monitoring, customer due diligence, and suspicious activity reporting.

Banking and Payment Services

Opening a bank account for crypto companies remains challenging in both countries. Lithuania has a more developed fintech ecosystem, with several banks and payment institutions willing to work with licensed CASPs (e.g., Revolut, Paysera, and some local banks). Estonia also has options, but the market is smaller. In both cases, having a local license facilitates banking relationships.

Consulting24 can introduce you to banking partners and assist with account opening. For faster setup, some clients opt for an EMI (Electronic Money Institution) account. However, some EMIs may not accept crypto-related businesses, so it is important to check their policies.

We recommend starting the banking process early, as account opening can take 4-8 weeks. Some banks require a minimum deposit or a certain transaction volume. For example, a Lithuanian bank may require a EUR 10,000 minimum deposit for a corporate account.

Benefits of Each Jurisdiction

Lithuania: Strong fintech ecosystem, more banking options, lower state fee, and a straightforward application process. The Bank of Lithuania is proactive and provides clear guidance. Lithuania also has a growing pool of local compliance and legal talent.

Estonia: Zero corporate tax on retained profits, faster processing (typically 2-4 months), and a well-established e-Residency program for remote management. Estonia's digital infrastructure is world-class, making it easy to manage the company online.

Both offer EU passporting and a stable regulatory environment. Your choice depends on tax strategy, timeline, and banking needs. For example, a startup that plans to reinvest profits for several years may prefer Estonia, while a company that needs quick access to banking may lean towards Lithuania.

Alternatives and Comparisons

Beyond Lithuania and Estonia, other EU jurisdictions offer CASP licenses under MiCA, such as Malta, Cyprus, and the Czech Republic. However, Lithuania and Estonia are among the most popular due to their relatively fast timelines and clear regulations.

For non-EU options, Panama offers a flat-fee license at EUR 6,000 with no capital requirement, but without EU passporting. Other jurisdictions like Dubai and El Salvador are also options for global operations.

Frequently asked questions

What is the main difference between Lithuania and Estonia crypto licenses?

The main differences are tax treatment and timeline. Estonia offers 0% tax on retained profits and faster processing (2-4 months vs 3-6 months). Lithuania has a lower state fee (EUR 1,500 vs EUR 3,000) and more banking options. Both are MiCA-compliant CASP licenses.

Which country has lower capital requirements?

Both have the same capital requirements under MiCA: EUR 50,000 for exchange and wallet services, EUR 125,000 for custody, and EUR 150,000 for full services. The capital must be held in a local bank account and maintained throughout the license period.

Can I passport my license to other EU countries?

Yes, both Lithuania and Estonia issue CASP licenses that allow passporting across the European Economic Area. This means you can offer services in other EU countries without obtaining additional licenses, subject to notification requirements.

How long does the application process take?

In Estonia, the typical timeline is 2-4 months. In Lithuania, it is 3-6 months. The timeline depends on the completeness of your application and the regulator's workload. Consulting24 can help expedite the process by ensuring all documents are in order.

What are the ongoing compliance costs?

Annual compliance costs range from EUR 5,000 to EUR 10,000 in both countries. This includes AML officer fees, audit costs, and regulatory reporting. Some businesses may need to allocate more if they have complex operations or high transaction volumes.

Do I need a local office in Lithuania or Estonia?

Yes, both regulators require a local registered office and a local compliance officer. However, the office can be a virtual office or a serviced workspace. Consulting24 can help arrange these services.

What happens if I don't meet the capital requirement?

If you fail to maintain the required capital, the regulator may suspend or revoke your license. It is important to monitor your capital levels and top up if necessary. Consulting24 provides ongoing compliance support to help you stay compliant.

Can I apply for a license remotely?

Yes, the application process can be done remotely. You will need to submit documents electronically and may need to appear for a video interview with the regulator. Consulting24 manages the entire process remotely.

Which jurisdiction is better for a startup?

Estonia is often better for startups due to its 0% tax on retained profits and faster timeline. However, if you need more banking options and a lower state fee, Lithuania may be a better fit. Consider your specific business model and tax strategy.

How does Panama compare to Lithuania and Estonia?

Panama offers a flat-fee license at EUR 6,000 with no capital requirement, but it does not provide EU passporting. It is a good option for businesses that do not need EU access. For EU market access, Lithuania or Estonia are recommended.
