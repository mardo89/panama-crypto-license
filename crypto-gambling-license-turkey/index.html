What Is a Crypto Gambling License in Turkey?

Strictly speaking, there is no such thing as a crypto gambling license in Turkey. The country does not have a regulatory framework that issues licenses for online gambling, let alone crypto gambling. The only gambling activities permitted are those run by the state, and all others are illegal. This means that any operator offering casino games, poker, sports betting, or any form of gambling with cryptocurrency to Turkish residents is acting illegally.

However, Turkey has taken steps to regulate cryptocurrency trading. The Capital Markets Board (CMB) oversees crypto asset service providers (CASPs) under the Law on Capital Markets, but this regulation covers exchanges, custody, and wallet services, not gambling. The CMB requires CASPs to register and comply with anti-money laundering (AML) and know-your-customer (KYC) rules, but this does not legalize gambling.

For founders looking to offer crypto gambling, the absence of a Turkish license means either operating in a legal gray area (with high risk) or obtaining a license in a jurisdiction that explicitly permits crypto gambling.
