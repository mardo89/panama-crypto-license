Cyprus Crypto License – EU MiCA CASP Authorization

As an EU member state, Cyprus provides passporting rights across the EEA, making it a strategic hub for crypto businesses targeting European markets.

What Is a Cyprus Crypto License?

A Cyprus crypto license, officially a Crypto-Asset Service Provider (CASP) authorization under MiCA, is issued by the Cyprus Securities and Exchange Commission (CySEC). It allows firms to legally offer crypto services such as exchange, custody, and wallet management within Cyprus and across the EU via passporting. Cyprus transitioned from its national CASP registration (Law 191(I)/2021) to the EU-wide MiCA regulation effective 2025/2026. New applicants must comply with MiCA’s harmonised rules, including capital requirements, governance, and AML/CFT obligations.

Key features include:

  • EU passporting: serve all 27 member states from a single license, eliminating the need for multiple national registrations.
  • CySEC oversight: a well-respected EU regulator with a strong track record in investment services, now extended to crypto-assets.
  • English-friendly environment: all regulatory communication, legal frameworks, and documentation are in English, reducing barriers for international firms.
  • MiCA compliance: ensures your business meets EU standards for consumer protection, market integrity, and financial stability.

For firms already holding a CASP in another EU member state, Cyprus offers a smooth relocation or secondary establishment process under MiCA’s passporting rules.

Who Needs a Cyprus Crypto License?

A Cyprus crypto license is ideal for a wide range of crypto businesses targeting the EU market:

  • Exchanges – Centralized or decentralized platforms trading crypto-to-fiat or crypto-to-crypto, requiring a regulated environment to build trust with European users.
  • Custodians – Firms holding client crypto assets securely, needing to comply with MiCA’s strict custody requirements, including segregation of assets and insurance.
  • Wallet providers – Services managing private keys on behalf of users, subject to operational resilience and AML checks.
  • Brokers & dealers – Firms facilitating crypto transactions for clients, often requiring a license to offer fiat on-ramps.
  • Fund managers – Those managing crypto investment portfolios, benefiting from Cyprus’s favorable tax regime and regulatory clarity.
  • Payment processors – Businesses integrating crypto payments for merchants, needing a CASP for conversion and settlement.

If your target market is the EU, a Cyprus license offers the most efficient route. Even non-EU firms can establish a Cyprus subsidiary to access the single market. Consult our requirements page for detailed eligibility criteria, including director residency and substance requirements.

License Type & Regulator

The license is a CASP authorization under MiCA, regulated by CySEC. CySEC has been the national competent authority for investment services since 2001 and now oversees crypto-asset activities under MiCA. The regulator classifies CASPs into three tiers based on the services offered and associated risks:

MiCA introduces additional requirements for white paper publication (for certain tokens) and conflict of interest management.

Capital Requirement

As shown above, minimum capital depends on service tier: €50k, €125k, or €150k. This capital must be fully paid up and held in a Cyprus bank account (or an EU bank with a Cyprus branch). It serves as a buffer for operational risks and is not a security deposit – it can be used for working capital, but must be maintained at all times. CySEC may require additional capital based on the firm’s risk profile, especially for high-volume exchanges or those dealing with complex instruments like derivatives. Capital must be maintained at all times; withdrawals require prior approval from CySEC. For comparison, Estonia requires €12,000 minimum, but without EU passporting. Cyprus is more expensive but offers broader market access and regulatory credibility.

Tax Treatment

Cyprus offers a favourable corporate tax regime that attracts crypto businesses:

  • Corporate income tax: 12.5% – one of the lowest in the EU, applicable on net profits.
  • VAT: 19% standard rate; crypto services may be exempt depending on nature (e.g., exchange of fiat for crypto is generally VAT-exempt).
  • No withholding tax on dividends paid to non-residents (subject to conditions, e.g., not from tax havens).
  • Notional interest deduction (NID): deduction on new equity capital, reducing effective tax rate further.
  • Double tax treaties: over 60 treaties, reducing cross-border taxation on royalties, interest, and dividends.
  • IP box regime: 80% exemption on qualifying intellectual property income, applicable to crypto-related software patents.

Crypto asset gains are treated as taxable income. Consult a tax advisor for your specific structure. Cyprus does not impose capital gains tax on securities, but crypto may be classified differently – typically as trading income. For offshore structures, Panama offers 0% tax but no EU access.

Allowed Activities

A Cyprus CASP license permits the following activities under MiCA:

  • Execution of orders for crypto-assets on behalf of clients.
  • Placing of crypto-assets (underwriting or placing with or without a firm commitment).
  • Reception and transmission of orders for crypto-assets.
  • Custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients.
  • Operation of a trading platform for crypto-assets.
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for fiat currency (e.g., EUR, USD).
  • Exchange of crypto-assets for other crypto-assets (e.g., BTC/ETH).
  • Provision of transfer services for crypto-assets on behalf of clients.

Activities not covered require additional licenses (e.g., investment advice under MiFID II, or credit institution license for lending). Stablecoin issuance may fall under e-money regulations (EMD2) or credit institution rules. CySEC provides guidance on activity classification.

Banking & Payments

Cyprus has a mature banking sector with several banks open to crypto firms, including Bank of Cyprus, Hellenic Bank, and RBC (subject to due diligence). Payment processors like Revolut Business, EMI accounts (e.g., Monese, Paysera), and PSPs like TrueLayer are also available. Challenges: some banks are risk-averse and may require a minimum deposit (e.g., €50k) or a proven track record. We recommend preparing a solid compliance file, including AML policies, source of funds, and business plan. Bank onboarding can take 2–4 months. Alternatives include e-money institutions (EMIs) and payment service providers (PSPs) that offer faster account opening. For crypto-to-fiat exchanges, a EUR-denominated account is essential. Cyprus banks are generally more crypto-friendly than those in Lithuania or Estonia, but still subject to strict KYC/AML.

Compliance & Trust

CySEC enforces strict AML/CFT rules under the 4th and 5th AML Directives, aligned with FATF recommendations. Licensees must appoint a local AML compliance officer (resident in Cyprus), conduct regular audits, and report suspicious transactions to the Cyprus Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU). Ongoing obligations include:

  • Annual AML audit by an external auditor.
  • Periodic reporting to CySEC (quarterly or annually).
  • Transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting (SARs).
  • Record keeping for at least 5 years.
  • Staff training on AML/CFT.

Common Mistakes

Avoid these pitfalls when applying for a Cyprus crypto license:

  • Underestimating capital – choosing the wrong tier can delay approval or require reapplication. Ensure your business model aligns with the tier’s allowed activities.
  • Poor AML documentation – CySEC rejects applications with generic policies copied from other jurisdictions. Must be tailored to your specific services and risk profile.
  • Ignoring substance requirements – you need a physical office in Cyprus (not just a virtual address) and local staff (at least one director or compliance officer).
  • Incomplete business plan – must detail operations, risk management, financial projections (3 years), and marketing strategy. CySEC expects realistic assumptions.
  • Not engaging local experts – DIY approaches often fail due to lack of familiarity with CySEC expectations. We recommend using experienced legal and compliance advisors.
  • Overlooking ongoing costs – budget for annual supervision fees (€5k–€10k), audit costs, and compliance officer salary.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum capital for a Cyprus crypto license?

The minimum capital depends on the service tier: €50,000 for basic services (custody, wallet), €125,000 for custodial exchanges, and €150,000 for full-service platforms. This capital must be fully paid up and held in a Cyprus bank account.

How long does it take to get a Cyprus crypto license?

Typically 4–8 months from application to issuance. This includes company incorporation (2–4 weeks), document preparation (4–8 weeks), and CySEC review (8–16 weeks). Delays can occur if additional information is requested.

Can I passport my Cyprus license to other EU countries?

Yes, under MiCA, a Cyprus CASP license allows passporting to all 27 EU member states plus EEA countries. You must notify CySEC of your intention and comply with host state requirements.

What are the tax benefits of a Cyprus crypto license?

Cyprus offers a 12.5% corporate tax rate, no withholding tax on dividends (subject to conditions), notional interest deduction, and over 60 double tax treaties. Crypto gains are taxed as income, but effective rates can be lower with planning.

Do I need a physical office in Cyprus?

Yes, CySEC requires a physical office in Cyprus (not just a virtual address) with local staff, such as a compliance officer or director. This is part of the substance requirements to demonstrate genuine presence.

Can non-EU companies apply for a Cyprus crypto license?

Yes, non-EU companies can incorporate a Cyprus subsidiary and apply for the license. The subsidiary must meet all local requirements, including having directors and compliance officers resident in Cyprus.

What activities are allowed under a Cyprus CASP license?

Allowed activities include execution of orders, custody, operation of a trading platform, exchange of crypto for fiat or other crypto, and transfer services. Stablecoin issuance may require additional e-money licensing.

How does Cyprus compare to Lithuania for crypto licensing?

Cyprus offers EU passporting and a lower corporate tax rate (12.5% vs 15%), but higher capital requirements (€50k+ vs €2,500). Lithuania is cheaper but without passporting, limiting market access.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations?

Ongoing obligations include annual AML audits, periodic reporting to CySEC, transaction monitoring, SAR filing, staff training, and maintaining a local compliance officer. Annual supervision fees range from €5,000 to €10,000.

Can Consulting24 guarantee approval of my Cyprus license?

No, we cannot guarantee approval. Each application is assessed by CySEC on its merits. We provide expert coordination and guidance to maximize your chances, but outcomes depend on your specific circumstances.
