Cyprus Crypto License 2026: CySEC CASP under MiCA

Cyprus pairs EU CASP status and passporting with an English-speaking legal environment, an attractive tax regime and a deep financial-services ecosystem (built on its large investment-firm and forex industry). Under MiCA, the CySEC authorizes crypto-asset service providers.

Why Cyprus

Cyprus has a mature regulator in CySEC and a large pool of compliance, legal and audit talent from its investment-firm sector. That makes it a practical EU base: passportable, English-friendly, with competitive costs and a favourable corporate-tax regime. Under MiCA it grants standard CASP authorizations.

The application process

  1. Scope services and capital tier.
  2. Incorporate a Cyprus company and fund capital.
  3. Build the file — business plan, governance, AML/CFT, IT/custody, key persons.
  4. Apply to CySEC and complete review.
  5. Authorization and ongoing supervision.

Common mistakes

  • Underestimating CySEC's documentation expectations.
  • Thin local substance or AML staffing.
  • Mis-modelling the tax position — plan it early.

Cyprus vs Panama: Cyprus is a cost-effective, English-friendly EU CASP; Panama is faster and cheaper (€6,000, 2–3 weeks, 0% foreign-income tax) but not EU-regulated. Compare with Malta, Lithuania and the jurisdictions hub.

Who Cyprus suits

Cyprus fits founders who want an EU-passportable CASP with English-language administration, competitive tax and reasonable costs. For a fast global launch, start with Panama.
