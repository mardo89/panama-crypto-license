What Is the Crypto Gambling License South Korea?

The crypto gambling license South Korea is not a single permit but a combination of approvals from the Financial Services Commission (FSC) and the Korea Gaming Commission (KGC). The FSC oversees the Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) registration under the Act on Reporting and Use of Specific Financial Information, while the KGC regulates gambling activities. For crypto gambling operators, the key requirement is obtaining a VASP registration and a separate license from the KGC if the gambling activities target Korean citizens. However, for operators targeting foreign customers, a special license is available under the Korea Tourism Organization (KTO) framework, which allows casinos in designated zones.

As of 2026, the FSC requires all VASPs to report their business and comply with Anti-Money Laundering (AML) obligations. The KGC does not issue new licenses for domestic online gambling, but the KTO license for foreign-only casinos exists. This guide focuses on the KTO license combined with VASP registration, which is the most viable path for crypto gambling businesses. Tax Treatment

South Korea imposes a corporate tax rate of 9-24% (progressive). For casino operations, a special gambling tax applies: 10% of gross gaming revenue (GGR) for foreigner-only casinos under the KTO license. Additionally, VAT of 10% applies to commissions and services. Crypto transactions are subject to capital gains tax (20% on gains over KRW 2.5 million) for individuals, but corporate crypto gains are taxed as ordinary income. Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between VASP registration and KTO license?

VASP registration is mandatory for any crypto business in South Korea, covering AML and reporting obligations. The KTO license is specific to casinos targeting foreign nationals, allowing physical casino operations in designated tourism zones. For crypto gambling, both are required.

Can I operate an online crypto casino targeting Korean citizens?

No, online gambling targeting Korean citizens is illegal. The KTO license only permits operations for foreign nationals in physical premises. Targeting Korean citizens can result in criminal penalties, including imprisonment.

What is the minimum capital for a crypto gambling license in South Korea?

For VASP registration, the FSC requires a minimum of KRW 500 million (approx. EUR 370,000). For the KTO casino license, the minimum is typically KRW 5 billion (approx. EUR 3.7 million) for a full casino, though smaller operations may negotiate.

How long does it take to get a crypto gambling license in South Korea?

The total timeline is 6-12 months. VASP registration takes 3-6 months, and the KTO license takes an additional 3-6 months. Delays can occur if additional documentation is required.

What taxes apply to crypto gambling operators in South Korea?

Corporate tax is 9-24% progressive. A special gambling tax of 10% of GGR applies to foreigner-only casinos. VAT of 10% applies to commissions. Crypto gains are taxed as ordinary income for corporations.

Is it possible to use a foreign bank for the real-name account requirement?

No, the FSC requires a real-name account with a Korean bank for VASP registration. Foreign bank accounts are not accepted. Consulting24 can help you open an account with banks like Shinhan or Kookmin.

What are the ongoing compliance obligations after obtaining the license?

Ongoing obligations include annual VASP reporting, AML audits every two years, KGC inspections, real-time suspicious transaction reporting, and data localization. Consulting24 provides ongoing support.

Can I apply for the KTO license without VASP registration?

No, VASP registration is a prerequisite for any crypto-related business. You must first register with the FSC as a VASP before applying for the KTO license. Both are required for crypto gambling.

What happens if I target Korean citizens with a KTO license?

Targeting Korean citizens is strictly prohibited and can lead to license revocation, fines, and criminal charges. The KTO license only permits operations for foreign nationals.

Is the South Korea license recognized internationally?
