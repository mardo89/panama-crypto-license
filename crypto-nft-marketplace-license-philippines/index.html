What is the Crypto NFT Marketplace License Philippines?

The Crypto NFT Marketplace License Philippines is a regulatory approval from the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP) under the Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) framework. It is required for any platform that facilitates the exchange, transfer, or storage of virtual assets, including non-fungible tokens (NFTs). The license ensures that the marketplace operates with adequate governance, anti-money laundering (AML) controls, and consumer protection measures.

As of 2026, the BSP has updated its guidelines to specifically address NFT marketplaces, recognizing their unique role in the digital economy. This license is not a one-size-fits-all; it is tailored to the activities of the platform, such as primary sales, secondary trading, or custody of NFTs. For example, a marketplace that only hosts primary sales of digital art may have a simpler compliance burden than one that offers custodial wallets and secondary trading. The BSP also requires VASPs to implement the Travel Rule for virtual asset transfers above PHP 500,000, which involves collecting and sharing sender and recipient information.

Who Needs This License?

Any entity operating an online platform that connects buyers and sellers of NFTs in the Philippines must obtain the VASP license. This includes:

  • NFT marketplaces for digital art, music, and collectibles
  • Gaming platforms that trade in-game NFTs
  • Marketplaces for tokenized real-world assets such as real estate or commodities
  • Platforms offering NFT minting and secondary trading
  • Any platform that facilitates fiat-to-crypto or crypto-to-crypto transactions for NFTs

If your platform handles fiat-to-crypto or crypto-to-crypto transactions for NFTs, you fall under BSP regulation. Even if you only facilitate peer-to-peer trading without holding customer funds, licensing is still required. The BSP has clarified that any intermediary that brings together buyers and sellers of virtual assets is a VASP. For example, a social media platform that allows NFT trading would need a license. License Type and Regulator

The license is officially called a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) Certificate of Registration, issued by the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP). The BSP is the central bank of the Philippines and the primary regulator for virtual asset activities under Republic Act No. 9160 (Anti-Money Laundering Act) as amended. The VASP registration is not a full banking license but a specific authorization for digital asset services.

As of 2026, the BSP requires VASPs to comply with the latest AML/CFT guidelines, including the Travel Rule for virtual asset transfers. The regulator also conducts periodic on-site examinations and requires annual audits. The BSP has a dedicated Virtual Asset Unit that handles VASP applications and supervision. Capital Requirement

As of 2026, the BSP does not prescribe a fixed minimum capital for VASP registration. However, applicants must demonstrate sufficient financial resources to cover operational expenses and potential liabilities. Typically, the BSP expects a minimum paid-up capital of PHP 10 million (approximately EUR 170,000) for a standard VASP. For NFT marketplaces with higher risk profiles, such as those offering custody services, the capital may be higher, up to PHP 20 million.

The capital must be held in a Philippine bank account and be freely available. It cannot be borrowed or subject to encumbrances. Tax Treatment

NFT marketplace operations in the Philippines are subject to corporate income tax at 25% (or lower for small enterprises under the CREATE Act, which reduces the rate to 20% for companies with net taxable income below PHP 5 million and total assets below PHP 100 million). Additionally, transactions may be subject to 12% value-added tax (VAT) if the platform charges fees. For NFT sales, the seller may be liable for capital gains tax or income tax, depending on the nature of the asset.

The Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) has issued guidelines on digital asset taxation, treating NFTs as intangible assets. The BIR requires VASPs to withhold taxes on certain transactions and report them. Consulting24 advises on structuring your marketplace to optimize tax efficiency. For a tax-neutral alternative, consider jurisdictions like Panama, which has no corporate tax on offshore income. Lithuania also offers a 15% corporate tax rate, which may be lower than the Philippine rate for larger profits.

Allowed Activities

With a VASP license, your NFT marketplace can engage in the following activities:

  • Facilitating primary sales of NFTs by creators
  • Operating a secondary marketplace for NFT trading
  • Providing NFT minting services

  • Providing escrow services for NFT transactions

Step-by-Step Application Process

The process to obtain a Crypto NFT Marketplace License Philippines involves several stages. Consulting24 advises and coordinates each step:

  1. Pre-application consultation: We assess your business model and prepare a compliance roadmap. This includes a gap analysis of your current operations against BSP requirements.
  2. Company incorporation: Register a Philippine corporation with the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC). You need at least five incorporators, a resident director, and a corporate secretary who is a Philippine citizen.
  3. AML program development: Draft policies, risk assessment, and internal controls. This includes customer due diligence (CDD), transaction monitoring, and suspicious transaction reporting.
  4. Document submission: File the VASP application with the BSP, including business plan, financial statements, AML manual, and organizational structure.
  5. BSP review and interview: The BSP evaluates the application and may conduct interviews with key personnel, such as the compliance officer and CEO.
  6. Approval and registration: Upon approval, the BSP issues a Certificate of Registration. You must then register with the Anti-Money Laundering Council (AMLC) and comply with ongoing reporting.

Throughout the process, Consulting24 liaises with local legal and compliance partners. The total timeline is typically 4-8 months, depending on the completeness of your application and the BSP's workload.

Compliance and Trust

Operating a licensed NFT marketplace builds trust with users and partners. Compliance with BSP regulations includes:

  • AML/CFT policies, including customer due diligence (CDD) and transaction monitoring. You must verify the identity of users for transactions above PHP 500,000 or any suspicious activity.
  • Travel Rule compliance for transfers above PHP 500,000. This requires collecting and sharing sender and recipient information with counterparties.
  • Regular reporting to the BSP and Anti-Money Laundering Council (AMLC). Reports include suspicious transaction reports (STRs) and annual compliance reports.
  • Annual audit by an external auditor approved by the BSP.
  • Appointment of a resident compliance officer with relevant experience.

The BSP does not set a fixed minimum capital, but expects applicants to demonstrate sufficient financial resources. Typically, a paid-up capital of PHP 10 million (around EUR 170,000) is expected. Higher capital may be required for custodial services.

Can I operate an NFT marketplace without a license in the Philippines?

No. Operating without a VASP license is illegal and can result in fines, closure, and criminal liability. The BSP actively monitors unlicensed VASPs and has taken enforcement actions. It is essential to obtain the license before launching.

Prohibited activities include dealing in privacy coins (e.g., Monero, Zcash), offering lending or staking services without SEC approval, and providing derivative products. The BSP also prohibits anonymous transactions and requires compliance with the Travel Rule.

Do I need a local director or compliance officer?

Yes. The BSP requires that the VASP have at least one resident director who is a Philippine citizen or permanent resident, and a compliance officer who is based in the Philippines. This ensures local accountability and regulatory oversight.
