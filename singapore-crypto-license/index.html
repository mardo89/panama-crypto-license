What Is the Singapore Crypto License?

The Singapore crypto license refers to the Digital Payment Token (DPT) service licence under the Payment Services Act (PSA), administered by the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS). It covers activities such as buying, selling, or exchanging DPTs; facilitating DPT transfers; and providing custodial services for DPTs. The licence is part of Singapore's broader regulatory framework aimed at mitigating money laundering, terrorist financing, and other financial risks while fostering innovation.

Unlike some jurisdictions with lighter regimes, Singapore imposes stringent requirements on applicants, including fit and proper criteria for directors and shareholders, strong AML/CFT policies, and a local presence. The MAS expects applicants to demonstrate operational readiness and compliance before approval. As of 2026, the DPT licence is fully operational, and the MAS continues to refine its approach to digital assets. The MAS has also issued guidelines on stablecoins and digital payment tokens, ensuring that licensed entities adhere to high standards of consumer protection and financial stability.

For founders evaluating whether Singapore is the right fit, it is important to understand that the MAS regime is one of the most respected globally but also one of the most demanding. Who Needs a Singapore Crypto License?

Any business that provides any of the following services in or from Singapore must obtain a DPT licence:

  • Digital payment token exchange (e.g., running a crypto exchange or OTC desk)
  • Transfer of digital payment tokens (e.g., remittance services using crypto)
  • Custodial services for digital payment tokens (e.g., wallet providers)
  • Facilitating the issuance of digital payment tokens (e.g., ICO platforms)

Exemptions exist for entities that only provide services incidental to other regulated activities, but these are limited. For example, a company that develops blockchain software but does not handle customer funds may not need a licence. However, if you are a crypto startup or established fintech planning to offer DPT services to Singapore residents, you likely need the licence. The MAS also requires that any entity marketing DPT services to Singapore residents, even from abroad, may be deemed to be carrying on business in Singapore and thus require licensing.

License Type and Regulator

The regulator is the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS), the country's central bank and integrated financial regulator. The licence type is the Digital Payment Token (DPT) service licence, which is part of the Payment Services Act (PSA). The PSA also covers other payment services like e-money issuance and money transfer, but the DPT licence specifically addresses crypto-related activities.

MAS has a tiered approach to capital requirements based on the type and volume of services. The DPT licence is a single licence covering all DPT activities, but applicants must specify which services they intend to provide. There is no separate class for small vs. large firms; however, MAS may impose additional conditions based on risk assessment. For instance, a firm handling high transaction volumes may face higher capital requirements and more frequent audits.

MAS also regulates crypto derivatives and securities tokens under the Securities and Futures Act (SFA), which is a separate licensing regime. If your business involves tokenised securities or derivatives, you may need both a DPT licence and a capital markets services (CMS) licence. Capital Requirement

The base capital requirement for a DPT licence is SGD 250,000 (approximately EUR 170,000 as of 2026). However, MAS may require higher capital based on the volume and risk profile of the business. The capital must be maintained at all times and can be in the form of cash or liquid assets. This is a significant barrier for smaller startups, making Singapore more suitable for well-funded ventures.

MAS also requires that the capital be held in a Singapore bank account, and the funds must be unencumbered. The capital serves as a buffer against operational risks and ensures that the licensee can meet its obligations. During the application process, MAS may ask for proof of capital availability, such as bank statements or letters of credit.

Tax Treatment

Singapore has a territorial tax system, meaning income sourced in Singapore is taxed, while foreign-sourced income may be exempt if certain conditions are met. For DPT service providers, income from trading, exchange fees, and custody fees is generally taxable at the corporate income tax rate of 17%. However, Singapore does not impose capital gains tax, so long-term holdings may not be taxed.

Goods and Services Tax (GST) at 9% applies to taxable supplies, but digital payment tokens may be exempt from GST under certain conditions. The Inland Revenue Authority of Singapore (IRAS) has issued guidance on the tax treatment of crypto transactions, including that DPT exchanges may be subject to GST on the spread or commission. It is important to consult a local tax advisor to optimize your tax position and ensure compliance with IRAS requirements.

For businesses that also operate in other jurisdictions, transfer pricing rules may apply. Consulting24 recommends engaging a Singapore-based tax advisor with crypto expertise to structure your operations efficiently.

Allowed Activities

Under the DPT licence, the following activities are permitted:

  • Exchange of digital payment tokens for fiat currency or other DPTs
  • Transfer of DPTs (e.g., facilitating peer-to-peer transfers)
  • Custodial services for DPTs (safekeeping and administration)
  • Facilitating the issuance of DPTs (e.g., helping companies issue tokens)

Activities not covered include dealing in non-payment tokens (e.g., utility tokens that do not function as payment), derivatives, and securities offerings (which fall under the Securities and Futures Act). The MAS strictly regulates the scope, and any expansion of services requires prior approval. For example, if a DPT licensee wants to offer staking services, it may need to apply for a variation of licence or obtain a separate approval.

MAS also prohibits certain activities, such as offering used trading to retail investors without proper safeguards. Licensees must also comply with MAS notices on technology risk management and cyber hygiene. Consulting24 can help you define your service scope accurately to avoid regulatory breaches.

Step-by-Step Application Process

The application process for the Singapore crypto license involves several stages:

  1. Pre-application consultation: Engage with MAS to discuss your business model and licensing requirements. This is optional but highly recommended.
  2. Prepare documentation: Draft business plan, AML/CFT policies, risk assessment, governance structure, and financial projections. Key documents include a detailed description of services, target market, and compliance framework.
  3. Submit application: File through MAS's online portal with the required fee of SGD 10,000 (non-refundable).
  4. Review and due diligence: MAS reviews the application, conducts background checks on key individuals (directors, shareholders, and compliance officers), and may request additional information.
  5. On-site inspection: MAS may visit your premises to verify operational readiness, including IT systems and physical office.
  6. Approval and licensing: If satisfied, MAS issues the licence, often with conditions such as reporting requirements or restrictions on certain activities.

The timeline is 9-18 months. Consulting24 advises clients on each step, helping to prepare strong documentation and respond to MAS queries efficiently. We also assist with the pre-application consultation by preparing a briefing memo that addresses potential concerns.

Banking and Payment Services

Opening a bank account in Singapore for a crypto business is challenging due to enhanced due diligence by banks. Many traditional banks are cautious about crypto-related entities. However, some digital banks and fintech-friendly banks may accept DPT licensees. MAS has encouraged banks to provide services to licensed entities, but it is not guaranteed. Banks often require a copy of the licence, a detailed business plan, and proof of AML/CFT policies.

Alternative payment services like stablecoin rails or payment gateways may be used for fiat settlement. Some crypto-friendly payment processors offer multi-currency accounts that can receive fiat from exchanges. Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

Once licensed, DPT service providers must comply with ongoing requirements:

  • Annual AML/CFT audits by an external auditor
  • Regular reporting to MAS on transactions, suspicious activities, and financial health (e.g., quarterly returns on transaction volumes and customer complaints)
  • Maintenance of minimum capital and liquid assets at all times
  • Compliance with MAS notices on technology risk, cyber hygiene, and consumer protection, including regular penetration testing and incident reporting

Failure to comply can result in fines, licence suspension, or revocation. For example, in 2024, MAS revoked the licence of a DPT provider for failing to maintain adequate AML controls. Consulting24 emphasizes that this is general guidance, not legal advice. We recommend engaging a local compliance officer and legal counsel to ensure ongoing adherence. The annual compliance cost can range from SGD 30,000 to 80,000, depending on the complexity of operations.

Common Mistakes in the Application

Many applicants face delays or rejection due to:

  • Incomplete documentation: Missing or poorly drafted AML/CFT policies, such as not covering all risk scenarios or lacking a clear customer due diligence process.
  • Unclear business model: Vague descriptions of services or revenue streams that do not align with the DPT licence scope.
  • Weak governance: Lack of experienced directors or inadequate board oversight, such as not having a dedicated compliance officer with relevant experience.
  • Underestimating capital needs: Not maintaining sufficient capital during the process or failing to provide proof of capital availability.
  • Ignoring MAS feedback: Failing to address queries promptly or adequately, leading to prolonged review times.

Frequently asked questions

What is the Singapore crypto license?

The Singapore crypto license is the Digital Payment Token (DPT) service licence under the Payment Services Act, regulated by the Monetary Authority of Singapore. It is required for businesses providing DPT exchange, transfer, or custodial services in or from Singapore.

How much does a Singapore crypto license cost?

The total first-year cost ranges from SGD 110,000 to 290,000, including a non-refundable application fee of SGD 10,000, legal and consultancy fees, compliance setup, and ongoing annual compliance. Exact pricing depends on the complexity of the application.

How long does it take to get a Singapore crypto license?

The timeline is typically 9 to 18 months from application to approval. Delays can occur if the application is incomplete or if MAS requests additional information. Pre-application consultation can help streamline the process.

What is the minimum capital requirement for a DPT licence?

The base capital requirement is SGD 250,000 (approximately EUR 170,000). MAS may require higher capital based on the volume and risk profile of the business. The capital must be maintained at all times in liquid assets.

Can I apply for a Singapore crypto license if I am not based in Singapore?

Yes, but you must have a local presence, such as a registered office and a local director or compliance officer. MAS requires that key personnel be based in Singapore. Consulting24 can help you establish the necessary local infrastructure.

What activities are allowed under the DPT licence?

Allowed activities include DPT exchange (fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto), DPT transfers, custodial services, and facilitating DPT issuance. Activities like derivatives trading or securities offerings require separate licences under the Securities and Futures Act.

Is the Singapore crypto license recognized in other countries?

The licence is specific to Singapore and does not provide passporting rights. However, being regulated by MAS enhances credibility globally and may facilitate partnerships with banks and exchanges in other jurisdictions.

What are the tax implications for a DPT licensee in Singapore?

Corporate income tax is 17% on Singapore-sourced income. No capital gains tax applies. GST at 9% may apply to certain services, but DPT transactions may be exempt. Consult a local tax advisor for specific guidance.
